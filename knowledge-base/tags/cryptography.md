# Tag: cryptography

- `INS-260323-B47B` NIST's post-quantum encryption standards use lattice-based problems in ~1000 dimensions where finding the closest vector is computationally infeasible for both classical and quantum machines.
- `INS-260323-8815` Quantum computers don't break encryption by trying all keys — they exploit superposition and the quantum Fourier transform to find periodic patterns in modular arithmetic, which reveals prime factors.
- `INS-260411-E951` Cryptographic quorum keys requiring multi-party consensus to unlock substrate-level AI capabilities offer stronger containment than software-only constitutional constraints.
- `INS-260325-A1B9` Digital encryption relies on the difficulty of factoring large numbers -- a task that takes digital computers centuries but quantum computers could do almost instantly.
