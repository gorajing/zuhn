Derived from Hassabis interview with Cleo Abram (the Hardest Problem AI Ever Solved, 2026). Hassabis explicitly ranked bad-actor weaponization and rogue agentic AI as existential-grade medium-term risks (3-4 year horizon), framed deepfakes/misinformation as a separate immediate-term issue, and called for international cooperation among frontier labs and AI safety institutes.

Hassabis is the leading-indicator candidate here. He's the only frontier-lab CEO who's publicly invoked CERN-style coordination at this level of specificity. The prediction tests whether his framing diffuses into the broader frontier-lab discourse within 3 years, or whether it stays an outlier.

Falsification readings:
- (a) Discourse stays at voluntary-corporate framing (industry pledges, Frontier Model Forum, RSPs) without invoking treaty/international precedent — implies coordination doesn't escalate from voluntary to formal
- (b) Hassabis was an outlier rather than a leading indicator — his views don't propagate to other frontier-lab leadership
- (c) Urgency timeline shifted further out than 3 years — capability advances slower than Hassabis expected, so the political pressure for international structure also delays

The specific CERN/IAEA framing is what makes this prediction distinct from the general 'AI safety discourse will exist.' It tests for a structural shift in framing, not just continued discussion.
