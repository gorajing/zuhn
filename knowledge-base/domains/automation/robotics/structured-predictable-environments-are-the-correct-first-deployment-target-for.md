The deployment roadmap for humanoid robots follows the same logic as any complex system entering the real world: start where variance is low, feedback is fast, and errors are recoverable. Manufacturing floors, warehouses, and security patrol routes have defined physical layouts, predictable task sequences, and finite exception states. This lets robots accumulate training data and improve rapidly while limiting the blast radius of failures.

This principle applies broadly to any AI system deployment. The instinct to showcase capability in the hardest problem (domestic assistance in an unstructured home) is a marketing move, not a product strategy. The correct sequencing is structured → semi-structured → unstructured, each stage funded by demonstrated ROI at the previous stage. BMW's deployment of Figure 02 in X3 production is the model: a controlled, high-volume, economically legible use case that builds operational credibility before expanding scope.
