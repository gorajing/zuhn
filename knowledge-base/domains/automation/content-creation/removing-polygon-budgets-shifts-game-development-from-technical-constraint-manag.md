Unreal Engine 5's Nanite technology uses hierarchical triangle clusters streamed on demand, effectively removing the polygon budget that previously forced developers to constantly trade artistic fidelity against performance. The Matrix demo rendered 7 million assets across 7,000 buildings with millions of polygons each — a scene impossible under traditional constraints.

This pattern generalizes beyond gaming: when a tool eliminates a hard technical ceiling rather than merely raising it, the workflow transforms from constraint management to creative expression. The developer time previously spent on LOD optimization, polygon counting, and texture tricks gets redirected to world-building and storytelling. This is why constraint removal (not just constraint relaxation) produces step-function productivity gains in creative tooling.
