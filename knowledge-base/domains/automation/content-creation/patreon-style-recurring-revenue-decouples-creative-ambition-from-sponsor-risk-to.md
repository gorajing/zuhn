Destin explicitly describes the supersonic baseball cannon as a project that was 'gigantic' with 'too much risk' for any sponsor to underwrite. His solution was building Patreon support over time — convincing his wife, offering signed baseballs to every patron, and gradually accumulating enough recurring revenue to fund projects on his own timeline without sponsor obligations. He frames it as escaping a 'calendar/schedule-based model' for content creation.

This reveals a structural advantage of creator-funded models: they shift the risk calculus entirely. A sponsor evaluates ROI on a single video; a patron base amortizes risk across the creator's entire output. This means patron-funded creators can pursue projects with high variance in outcome (might go viral, might not) and long timelines (months of engineering before a single video) that are systematically underserved by the sponsor model. The tradeoff is slower scaling — Destin spent four months signing baseballs — but the creative freedom compounds over time.
