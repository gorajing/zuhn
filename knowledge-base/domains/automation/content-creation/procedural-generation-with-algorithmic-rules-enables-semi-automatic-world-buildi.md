The Matrix demo's city — 260km of roads, 512km of sidewalk, 7,000 buildings — was procedurally generated based on algorithmic rules rather than manually placed. This semi-automatic approach freed designers to focus on fine-tuning the world and working on narrative rather than placing every object.

This represents a productive middle ground in automation: neither fully manual (too slow at scale) nor fully random (lacks coherence). The designer encodes intent as rules and constraints, and the system generates within those boundaries. This pattern applies broadly — from content generation to infrastructure provisioning — wherever the output space is too large for manual creation but too quality-sensitive for pure automation.
