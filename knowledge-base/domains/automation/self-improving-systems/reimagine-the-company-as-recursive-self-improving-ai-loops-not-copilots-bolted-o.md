A YC group partner argues most companies are organized like Roman legions (nested hierarchy, humans as the conduit for information up and down) and that AI breaks that model. Copilots that make engineers 20-30% more productive are 'a more powerful engine on the old way of working' (Pete Koomen's framing). The reframe: each part of the company becomes a recursive self-improving loop — a sensor layer (customer emails, support tickets, code changes, cancellations, telemetry), a policy/decision layer (what it can do, what needs human permission, what to log), a tool layer (deterministic APIs / skills / code), a quality gate (evals, safety filters, human review for high-risk), and a learning mechanism that picks up what didn't work and loops back. Run with minimal human intervention, the company 'self-improves while you sleep.' This is a near-exact external description of Zuhn's own architecture (ingest -> rules -> scripts/MCP -> health/benchmark CI -> learning mechanisms), validating that thesis from YC's center of gravity.
