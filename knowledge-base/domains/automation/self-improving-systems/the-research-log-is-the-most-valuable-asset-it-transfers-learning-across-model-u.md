Karpathy himself said 'the research log might be the most valuable asset,' and this insight extends far beyond ML. In AI Andy's content pipeline, every prompt change is logged alongside the performance data that caused it. After a week there are 7 iterations of prompt evolution; after a month, 30. Each entry records what changed, why (which data pattern triggered it), and what happened after.

The strategic value: when a smarter model comes out next month, you hand it the research log and it picks up exactly where the last model left off. Unlike manual tweaking where institutional knowledge lives in someone's head, the research log externalizes the entire optimization history. This transforms prompt engineering from a one-time artisanal effort into a compounding asset. The same principle applies to any system where you're iterating on prompts or configurations — structured logs of what was tried, what worked, and what didn't are more valuable than the current best prompt.
