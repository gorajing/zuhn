Copeland: think of everything as an experiment — hypothesis, structured activities, evaluation criteria decided in advance, then a keep-or-move-on call. This makes decisions feel less trapdoor and less over-committed; spending too long designing the perfect version yields insight slower than something scrappier; the teams that do this well are always running something and willing to kill what isn't working. Directly corroborates the KB's autoresearch / change-measure-keep-or-discard self-improvement loop.
