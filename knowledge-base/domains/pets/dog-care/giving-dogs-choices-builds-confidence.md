Giving dogs agency through small choices throughout the day is a
consistently recommended enrichment strategy. Examples include: letting
the dog choose which toy to play with (offer two), which direction to
turn on a walk, whether to approach a new person or hang back, and
whether to rest or engage in activity. Dogs that regularly get to make
choices show increased confidence, reduced anxiety, and better
adaptability to new situations. This approach is particularly effective
for fearful or anxious dogs, as it gives them a sense of control over
their environment. The key is respecting the dog's decision rather than
overriding it -- if they choose to avoid something, that choice should
be honored.
