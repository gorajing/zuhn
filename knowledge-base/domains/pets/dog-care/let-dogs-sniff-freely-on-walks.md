One of the most consistently cited ways to keep dogs happy is letting
them sniff freely during walks rather than rushing through a route.
Dogs process their environment primarily through scent -- a short
"sniff walk" where the dog leads and explores at their own pace
provides more mental stimulation than a longer fast-paced walk. Mental
stimulation through scent work tires dogs out more effectively than
physical exercise alone. This is a zero-cost, immediate change any dog
owner can make. Even dedicating a portion of each walk as "sniff time"
(letting the dog choose direction and pace) has measurable effects on
reducing anxiety and improving overall behavior at home.
