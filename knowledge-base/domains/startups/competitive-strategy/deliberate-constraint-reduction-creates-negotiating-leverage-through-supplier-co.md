Most retailers treat shelf space as inventory to fill — more products means more potential sales. Costco inverts this by radically constraining selection to roughly 4,000 SKUs. This artificial scarcity transforms the power dynamic with suppliers: instead of Costco needing products to fill shelves, suppliers need Costco's limited slots to access its affluent, high-volume membership base.

This leverage allows Costco to dictate terms — lower wholesale prices, custom packaging (like reengineered cashew containers saving 24,000 pallets annually), and product modifications. When no supplier meets Costco's standards, it creates Kirkland Signature alternatives (25% of sales). The lesson generalizes beyond retail: deliberately constraining what you offer can increase your negotiating power more than expanding selection, because you become a curator rather than a commodity platform.
