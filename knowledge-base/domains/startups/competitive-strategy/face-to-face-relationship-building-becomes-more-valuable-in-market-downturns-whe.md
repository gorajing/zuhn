In the sneaker market downturn, a toxic dynamic emerged: shops became known for bait-and-switch, promising one offer over the phone and lowballing after sellers showed up. This destroyed trust across the entire market. Resellers now prefer attending conventions where multiple buyers compete, rather than driving to individual shops where they might be cheated.

For Leo, conventions serve dual purposes: restocking inventory and building brand reputation. Each honest transaction creates a future supply relationship -- a seller who has a good experience knows he can bring future shoes to Leo rather than waiting for the next event. This in-person relationship building is more valuable than ever precisely because the overall market trust has collapsed. The principle generalizes: in any market where trust erodes (gig economy, B2B services, freelancing), operators who invest in face-to-face reputation building gain disproportionate advantage.
