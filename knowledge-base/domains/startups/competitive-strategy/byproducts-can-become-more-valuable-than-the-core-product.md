Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen was literally waste — a byproduct of splitting water molecules to replace lost electrons. Yet this 'waste product' fundamentally transformed Earth's atmosphere and enabled the entire kingdom of aerobic life.

In startups, the most valuable thing you build is often not what you set out to build. Slack was a byproduct of a failed game studio. Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature. AWS started as internal infrastructure. The lesson is to pay attention to what your system produces incidentally — the side channel may be carrying the real signal. This argues for keeping close feedback loops with users who may be deriving value from aspects of your product you consider secondary.
