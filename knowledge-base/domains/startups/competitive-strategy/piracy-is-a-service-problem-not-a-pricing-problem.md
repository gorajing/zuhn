At its peak in the 2010s, BitTorrent accounted for a third of all internet traffic and 95% of music downloads were pirated. What killed piracy wasn't enforcement or morality - it was convenience. Streaming services offered a faster, easier, safer way to access content. Piracy collapsed not because people wanted to pay, but because paying became the path of least resistance.

Now streaming services are reversing course: raising prices, fragmenting content across platforms, adding ads to paid tiers, restricting account sharing, and degrading recommendations. Each of these individually is minor, but collectively they're recreating the convenience gap that piracy originally filled. The services knew this day would come - they just needed to pretend it wouldn't long enough to build their subscriber bases.
