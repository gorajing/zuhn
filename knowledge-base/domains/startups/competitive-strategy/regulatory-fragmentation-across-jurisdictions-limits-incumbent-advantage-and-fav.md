Unlike Google or Uber, which could expand globally with relatively uniform products, real estate tech faces fundamentally different legal frameworks in every market. Real estate law, title systems, transaction processes, and regulatory requirements vary enormously by country and even by state or municipality.

This fragmentation pattern is a strategic signal for investors and founders: industries with high regulatory variance across jurisdictions will produce multiple regional winners rather than one global dominant player. For startups, this means the classic 'copy and localize' playbook from consumer tech actually works — you can build a successful company by adapting a proven proptech model to your specific regulatory environment. For investors, it means the TAM for any single company is capped by jurisdictional boundaries, but the total number of investable opportunities is much higher.
