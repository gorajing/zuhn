The EV transition follows network-effect dynamics that work in reverse for the incumbent technology. Gas stations require a minimum volume of customers to remain profitable. As EV adoption increases and gasoline demand drops, marginal stations close. Each closure makes owning a gas car slightly less convenient, pushing more buyers toward EVs, which causes more closures.

This means the transition from combustion to electric won't be gradual — it will be slow until a tipping point, then rapid. Different countries will hit this tipping point at different times based on EV penetration, geography, and charging infrastructure density. Automakers who wait too long to transition will find the market has flipped beneath them. Over 25% of global car sales are already EVs as of 2025, suggesting several markets are approaching this inflection.
