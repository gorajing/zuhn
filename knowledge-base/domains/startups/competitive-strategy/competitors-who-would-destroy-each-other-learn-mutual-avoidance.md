When two army ant swarms encounter each other, they either pass through ignoring each other or both move away. The evolutionary logic is stark: army ants that fought other army ants destroyed themselves and were selected out of existence.

This is a powerful model for competitive strategy. When two companies with identical destructive capabilities go to war (price wars, talent poaching, feature-for-feature copying), mutual destruction is the likely outcome. The survivors in any ecosystem are those who learned to differentiate or avoid direct symmetric conflict. This explains why mature markets often develop tacit non-aggression — not from collusion, but from evolutionary selection against suicidal competition.
