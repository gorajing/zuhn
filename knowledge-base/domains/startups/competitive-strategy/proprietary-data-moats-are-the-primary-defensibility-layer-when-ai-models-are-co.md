As access to powerful AI models becomes democratized through open-source releases and API access, model architecture alone ceases to be a differentiator. The defensibility question shifts entirely to data: Are you collecting data that others can't easily replicate? Does your system improve as more users engage? Does your advantage compound over time?

This creates a specific strategic imperative for AI-native founders: design for data accumulation from day one. Every user interaction should feed back into making the system smarter and harder to replicate. Harvey's fine-tuning on legal domain data, trained in partnership with actual law firms, exemplifies this — the product gets better with every contract drafted and every case summarized, creating a moat that no generic model can easily replicate regardless of parameter count.
