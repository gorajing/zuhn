Early photosynthetic organisms weren't efficient, but they were the only game in town for converting inorganic carbon to organic carbon. As they proliferated, they flooded the atmosphere with oxygen — a byproduct that actually made their own core enzyme (RuBisCo) work worse. Paradoxically, this environmental change also eliminated most competing metabolic strategies that couldn't handle oxygen.

For startups, this maps directly to platform dynamics: an adequate first mover that generates network effects or reshapes market expectations creates an environment where latecomers face structural headwinds the first mover never did. The lesson isn't 'ship garbage early' — it's that the threshold for dominance is adequacy plus environmental reshaping, not optimality. Founders should ask: does my product change the competitive landscape in ways that disadvantage alternatives?
