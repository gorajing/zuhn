Patel observed that many consumer electronics startups with excellent products still failed. The problem wasn't the product—it was the business model. Framework's innovation isn't just modular hardware; it's a business model where the product belongs to the user, not the company. Users can repair, upgrade, and customize their laptops.

This creates a fundamentally different relationship with customers: instead of planned obsolescence driving repeat purchases, genuine user empowerment drives loyalty and word-of-mouth. Framework has doubled revenue year-over-year in a tough environment for consumer electronics startups. The lesson: in established markets, the real disruption often comes from the business model, not the technology.
