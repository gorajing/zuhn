The video opens by calling photosynthesis a process that 'rather sucks' — it's inefficient, complex, and relies on a fundamentally flawed enzyme. Yet no competing energy-capture mechanism has displaced it in 450 million years. This is because photosynthesis didn't just become the dominant strategy — it reshaped the entire environment (oxygenating the atmosphere), making the ecosystem itself dependent on its outputs.

This maps directly to platform strategy: a product that reshapes its ecosystem (creating dependencies, standards, and infrastructure around its outputs) becomes nearly impossible to displace regardless of technical superiority of alternatives. The key insight is that dominance comes not from being the best solution, but from being the solution that other systems co-evolved to depend on. Windows, QWERTY keyboards, and TCP/IP all follow this pattern.
