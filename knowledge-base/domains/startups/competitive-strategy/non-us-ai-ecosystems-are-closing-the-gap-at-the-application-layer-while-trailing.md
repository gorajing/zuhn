Multiple signals in the data — Accel's Globalscape report showing non-US ecosystems closing the gap at the application layer, the €33B Japan-Europe tech alliance since 2019, and Europe's push in applied AI with thousands of startups across strategic verticals — point to a bifurcation in global AI competition.

At the infrastructure and foundation model layer, US dominance remains strong due to compute concentration and talent density. But at the application layer, the advantages shift toward domain expertise, regulatory knowledge, local distribution networks, and industry relationships. This creates a strategic opening for founders outside the US: rather than competing on model capabilities, they can build AI-native solutions in verticals where local context is a genuine moat. The growing cross-border capital flows suggest investors are recognizing this pattern.
