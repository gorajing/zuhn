By stocking only 4,000 unique items compared to Walmart's 140,000, Costco turns scarcity of shelf space into a negotiating weapon. Suppliers compete aggressively to be one of only one or two choices per category, willingly lowering prices and even re-engineering products (e.g., redesigning cashew containers to save 24,000 pallets/year).

This is counterintuitive in an era where platforms compete on selection breadth. Costco proves that radical curation — choosing the single best option per category — can generate more value through supplier leverage than offering every option. The constraint also simplifies operations (stocking, shopping experience) and eliminates choice paralysis for customers.
