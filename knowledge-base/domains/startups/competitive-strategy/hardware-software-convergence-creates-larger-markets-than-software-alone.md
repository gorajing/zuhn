A non-obvious pattern across 60 years of tech history: silicon chips enabled computers, computers enabled software, networking hardware enabled the internet, and the iPhone enabled mobile apps. In each case, a physical hardware breakthrough created the platform on which software companies could thrive. This challenges the pure-software thesis that dominated ZIRP-era thinking. Chamath's 'Platformization Conjecture' argues the trifecta is a new bit platform + a new atom platform + sustained above-average rates. Looking forward, he identifies two atom-level shifts: marginal cost of energy approaching zero (solar, wind, natural gas) and the shift from CPUs to GPUs making compute costs approach zero. Founders should look for opportunities at the intersection of hardware and software breakthroughs rather than pure software plays.
