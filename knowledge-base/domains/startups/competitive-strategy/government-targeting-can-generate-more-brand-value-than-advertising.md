When the Pentagon designated Anthropic a 'supply chain risk,' prediction markets initially dropped the company's projected IPO value by ~14%, then fully rebounded within days—recognizing the designation was legally narrow and commercially trivial. Meanwhile, Anthropic climbed from #120 to #1 on the App Store, and enterprise customers began switching to Claude specifically because a company 'willing to walk away from a massive contract on ethical grounds' seemed more trustworthy with their data. The attack became the best marketing the company ever received. This dynamic is not unique to Anthropic: public adversarial pressure from a credible, disliked opponent can signal integrity to customers in ways that advertising cannot. The asymmetry matters—if the government's case is legally weak, the downside (losing ~5-10% of revenue from government-adjacent contracts) can be outweighed by the upside of reputation gains in the much larger commercial market.
