Under Armour grew at double digits for 13 straight years, passed Adidas as #2 US sportswear brand, and had endorsements from Brady, Curry, Phelps, and The Rock. Their shoes (Hovr, Apollo) were 'mind-blowingly light and comfortable' — but aesthetically hideous. Revenue is a lagging indicator: by the time sales peaked, consumers had already decided the brand wasn't cool. The company then suffered expense trips to strip clubs, federal accounting investigations, failed pivots, and CEO turnover. The core lesson: in any consumer category where products are publicly displayed (fashion, cars, electronics), aesthetics is the primary purchase driver. Performance is table stakes; looks close the deal.
