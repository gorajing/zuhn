Direct competitors declare intent and invite immediate response from incumbents. Plausible alternatives, by contrast, spread behind the scenes — giving buyers room to test, wait, and rethink commitments without forcing a confrontation. This is especially potent in infrastructure markets where architectural choices lock in years of cost and performance tradeoffs.

Groq never needed to replace Nvidia GPUs outright. It only needed to make 'Nvidia or nothing' feel false. Once Google demonstrated it could reduce internal GPU dependence for certain workloads, the psychological threshold was crossed — not because the alternative scaled across the industry, but because it existed at all. For founders, this reframes competitive strategy: you don't need to beat giants at their own game, you need to design situations they cannot safely ignore.
