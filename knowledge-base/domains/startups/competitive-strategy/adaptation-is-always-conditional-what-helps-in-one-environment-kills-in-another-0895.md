Carroll's icefish example is a powerful illustration of conditional advantage. These fish evolved antifreeze proteins and eliminated red blood cells entirely to survive in sub-freezing Antarctic waters. Losing red blood cells reduced blood viscosity in extreme cold — a net positive there. But in any other ocean, the same adaptation is instantly lethal.

This maps directly to competitive strategy: a company's deepest moat in one market can become its biggest liability when the environment shifts. The lesson is that adaptations (or strategies) should never be evaluated in the abstract — only relative to the specific conditions they operate in. What made you dominant yesterday might be what kills you tomorrow if the environment changes.
