Andreessen Horowitz's hire of David Booth as Partner and Head of Ecosystem represents a deliberate strategy to industrialize network effects in venture capital. Rather than treating relationships as informal deal flow channels, the firm is investing in connectivity as core infrastructure.

This mirrors a broader pattern where the most successful platforms win not through superior individual transactions but through network density that compounds over time. For VC specifically, this means the value of being in a16z's portfolio extends beyond capital to include systematized access to talent, customers, and co-investors. Other firms competing purely on check size or deal terms face a structural disadvantage against a network that grows more valuable with each new node added.
