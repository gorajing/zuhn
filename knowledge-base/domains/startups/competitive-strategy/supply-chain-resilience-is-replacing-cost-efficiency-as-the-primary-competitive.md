For decades, startup orthodoxy held that offshoring to the cheapest supplier was table stakes. The 2024-2025 tariff waves have inverted this logic: startups with single-source dependencies on China or any one geography are now carrying massive unpriced risk. Founders who pre-built flexible supply chains with redundant suppliers across India, Mexico, and Southeast Asia are absorbing tariff shocks that are existential for cost-optimized competitors.

This represents a structural shift in what constitutes a moat. The operational messiness of managing multiple suppliers across geographies—once a pure cost center—is becoming a competitive advantage. Startups like Mason and Aigen are already treating supply chain agility as a first-class design constraint, not an afterthought. The implication for founders: resilience engineering belongs in your product architecture, not just your ops playbook.
