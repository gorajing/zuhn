Under Armour's wholesale strategy reveals the asymmetric nature of retail relationships. It took years to convince retailers to stock UA products: retailers are 'cautious creatures who want to minimize every risk of being stuck with unsellable goods.' Under Armour earned their trust gradually through premium pricing (higher per-unit profits for retailers), athlete endorsements (demand signals), and low discount rates (pricing integrity).

But this trust is fragile. Retailers want guarantees that products will sell quickly at maximum markup with minimal returns. When Under Armour's brand heat cooled, retailers had no loyalty -- they simply ordered less or demanded deeper discounts. The wholesale channel that powered UA's growth became the channel that amplified its decline. This is why Nike and Adidas maintain both wholesale and direct-to-consumer channels, and why all major brands invest in outlet stores to clear excess inventory without contaminating the full-price wholesale channel.
