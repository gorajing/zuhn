Despite VS Code's dominance as a general editor, platform-specific IDEs persist because their deep integration (refactoring, debugging, build tools) is fundamentally more reliable than bolted-on extensions. WebStorm's React refactoring 'just feels way more reliable' than VS Code extensions attempting the same task.

This suggests a durable competitive strategy: when a horizontal platform dominates, vertical players survive by going deeper than the platform's extension model allows. The trade-off is clear — you pay ($69/year for JetBrains) for reliability and polish that free extensions approximate but don't match. This mirrors broader SaaS dynamics where vertical solutions coexist with horizontal platforms.
