The conventional wisdom that accumulating proprietary data creates an unassailable competitive advantage is increasingly challenged. Datasets can be replicated, scraped, or synthesized, and features built on data alone are vulnerable to fast followers with better architectures.

True defensibility emerges from system-level design: feedback loops where each user interaction improves the product in ways that are structurally difficult to copy. This shifts the strategic question from 'how do we collect more data?' to 'how do we design systems where usage itself compounds into advantage?' The insight applies beyond AI to any platform business where network effects are often confused with data quantity.
