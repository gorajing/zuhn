Masayoshi Son's Vision Fund systematically offered startups far more money than they requested — if you asked for $100M, he'd push $500M. This created a cascading effect: companies with excess capital pursued aggressive growth-at-all-costs strategies (like WeWork), competitors were intimidated by the sheer checkbook size and often didn't even try to compete, and companies that refused the money faced the threat of Son funding their rivals instead. While this approach can create dominant market positions quickly, it also inflates valuations beyond sustainable levels and incentivizes spending over profitability. The strategy works in winner-take-all markets but produces spectacular failures when the underlying business doesn't support network effects or natural monopoly dynamics.
