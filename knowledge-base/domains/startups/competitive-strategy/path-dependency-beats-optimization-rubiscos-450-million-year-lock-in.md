RuBisCo is objectively bad at its job — it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts that plants must spend additional energy to neutralize. Yet it remains the sole carbon-fixation enzyme on Earth after 450 million years because plants 'went all-in' on the RuBisCo strategy early.

The parallel to technology and business is direct: QWERTY keyboards, x86 architecture, JavaScript — all have well-known flaws, yet the ecosystem built around them makes replacement costlier than accommodation. The strategic lesson is that when competing against an entrenched incumbent, superior performance alone is insufficient. You need to either offer a completely orthogonal value proposition or wait for an environmental shift so dramatic that the incumbent's compensating mechanisms fail. Plants literally changed Earth's atmosphere and RuBisCo still couldn't be replaced — that's how strong path dependency is.
