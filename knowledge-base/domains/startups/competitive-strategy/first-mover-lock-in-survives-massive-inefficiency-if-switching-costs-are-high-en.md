RuBisCo is the most abundant protein on Earth (~40 billion tons) despite being spectacularly bad at its job—it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts that plants must expend energy to neutralize. Plants compensate by simply producing enormous quantities of it rather than evolving a replacement.

This mirrors how first-mover technologies and business strategies persist far beyond their optimality window. Once an ecosystem builds dependencies around a suboptimal solution (QWERTY keyboards, x86 architecture, legacy enterprise software), the switching cost exceeds the inefficiency cost. Startups competing against entrenched incumbents should recognize that 'better' alone doesn't win—you need the incumbent's switching costs to drop or the inefficiency to become existential, not merely annoying.
