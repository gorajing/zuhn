Emperor Justinian's single greatest achievement wasn't military conquest — it was compressing 1,528 Latin law books into the Digest (533 CE), accompanied by the Institutes as a teaching curriculum. This codification allowed Roman legal principles to survive the fall of both halves of the empire and become the foundation of European civil law that persists today.

The lesson for organizations is that codified, teachable knowledge systems outlast everything else — armies, territories, even the civilization itself. Companies that invest in documentation, playbooks, and formalized decision frameworks create something that compounds across generations of employees. The Digest wasn't just a reference document; it was a complete educational curriculum (the Institutes) designed to transmit understanding, not just information. That distinction — between a wiki nobody reads and a genuine onboarding system — is the difference between knowledge that dies with individuals and knowledge that becomes institutional.
