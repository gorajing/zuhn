The three SaaS moats that once guaranteed defensibility — implementation complexity, workflow lock-in, and data gravity — are becoming irrelevant. Foundation models can integrate, retrain, and migrate data across platforms with near-zero switching costs. This means startups compete not only with other startups but with their own customers, who have access to the same APIs and fine-tuning infrastructure.

The new defensibility lives in relationship depth rather than technical opacity. VCs now hunt for moats built on enterprise knowledge (learning from proprietary workflows), trust (sustained confidence through observability and audit trails), and model traceability. Founders who embed within enterprise ecosystems through partnership rather than fighting them are the ones investors prize. This is a fundamental reorientation from building walls to building bridges.
