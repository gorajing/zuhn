The article's final characteristic — difficulty to copy — is positioned as the capstone because without it, all other value proposition qualities become temporary. Beyond Meat's advantage isn't one recipe; it's the accumulated R&D, proprietary processes, and brand positioning that together create a barrier competitors can't easily replicate.

This maps to the broader moat literature but adds a nuance: defensibility should be designed into the value proposition from the start, not bolted on after product-market fit. Spotify's personalization algorithms, Stripe's developer ecosystem, and Canva's template marketplace all represent value propositions where the delivery mechanism itself becomes harder to replicate over time. The practical takeaway is to evaluate value propositions not just on current customer value but on the rate at which they accumulate defensibility through usage.
