The Concorde's anticipated dominance of premium air travel caused airlines to abandon first class development and focus on creating business class as the new top tier on subsonic planes. When the Concorde failed commercially, the business class innovation it had catalyzed remained—permanently reshaping the industry. This pattern generalizes: incumbents often make irreversible strategic adaptations in response to perceived disruption threats, and those adaptations persist even if the threat never materializes. For startups, this means that even an unsuccessful competitor can leave lasting structural changes in a market. The shadow of disruption is itself a market force.
