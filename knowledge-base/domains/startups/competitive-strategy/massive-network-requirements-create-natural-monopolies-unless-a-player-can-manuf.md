The express shipping industry is a natural oligopoly of FedEx, UPS, and DHL because the business requires hundreds of planes, dozens of hub airports, thousands of routes, and continental-scale sorting infrastructure just to offer competitive service. No startup can build this incrementally — you need near-complete coverage from day one or your service is useless.

Amazon's entry into delivery illustrates the only viable disruption path: generating enough captive demand (1M+ packages/day) to justify building the network for yourself, then potentially opening it to others. This pattern recurs in network-dependent industries. Telecom incumbents weren't disrupted by new telcos but by tech companies (Google Fi, Amazon's delivery network) whose core business generated enough traffic to bootstrap their own infrastructure. The lesson for startups: if your industry requires massive networks, either find a niche that doesn't need full coverage, or build captive demand in another business first.
