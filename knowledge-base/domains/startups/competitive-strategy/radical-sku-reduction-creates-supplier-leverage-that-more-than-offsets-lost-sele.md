Conventional retail wisdom treats selection breadth as a competitive advantage — more choice means more customers served. Costco inverts this by stocking only 1-2 options per category across ~4,000 total SKUs. This creates a winner-take-all dynamic where suppliers compete fiercely for the sole Costco slot, driving prices down and willingness to customize up (e.g., reengineering cashew containers to save 24,000 pallets/year).

The deeper insight is about where leverage accumulates in supply chains. Breadth distributes your purchasing power across many suppliers, giving each one alternatives. Radical curation concentrates purchasing power, making you the single most important customer for each supplier. This principle applies to any platform or marketplace: constraining the supply side can paradoxically increase your value to suppliers while lowering costs for buyers.
