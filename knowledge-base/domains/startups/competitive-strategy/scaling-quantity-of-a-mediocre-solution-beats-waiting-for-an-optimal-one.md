When early organisms needed carbon fixation, RuBisCo was a poor enzyme for the job. But instead of evolving a better one, organisms simply produced massive quantities of the mediocre version. This volume-first strategy let them dominate before any competitor could develop a superior alternative.

This maps directly to startup strategy: shipping a mediocre product at scale often beats perfecting a product in isolation. The volume creates network effects, ecosystem dependencies, and switching costs that make the 'better' competitor irrelevant by the time it arrives. Amazon's early site was ugly, Craigslist still is — but they scaled distribution of a good-enough solution before anyone could build something better. The strategic insight is that quantity creates its own quality through ecosystem lock-in.
