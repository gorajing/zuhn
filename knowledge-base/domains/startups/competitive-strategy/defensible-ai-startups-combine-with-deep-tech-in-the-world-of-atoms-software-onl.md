Hassabis's founder advice: software-only AI wrappers are systematically vulnerable — every foundation model update obsoletes some layer of them. The defensible play is interdisciplinary: AI plus a deep technology area (materials science, drug discovery, climate modeling, robotics, advanced manufacturing). The moat comes from the world of atoms — physical manufacturing, regulatory hurdles, domain expertise that doesn't fit into a foundation model's pretraining set, and slow-moving feedback loops.

The team-shape implication: interdisciplinary teams with ML expertise plus the other domain (ideally same person, or co-founder pairs) are the right structure. Hassabis warns deep tech is a ~10-year journey, so founders must plan for AGI arriving in the middle of that journey — meaning the defensible play also needs to leverage AGI when it arrives, not be obsoleted by it. For Zuhn's editorial scoring framework: this is the structural test. A startup whose value proposition is 'we use Claude to do X' is on the wrong side; a startup whose value proposition is 'we built specialized infrastructure (data, hardware, domain models, distribution) that AGI will plug into' is on the right side.
