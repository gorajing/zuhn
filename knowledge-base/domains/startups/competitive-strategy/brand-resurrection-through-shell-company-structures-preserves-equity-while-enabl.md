Nokia's 2017 comeback architecture split responsibilities across three entities: Nokia retained brand ownership and licensing, HMD Global (founded by ex-Nokia executives) handled product development, marketing, and sales, while Foxconn provided R&D and manufacturing. This asset-light model allowed a major brand to re-enter the market without the capital expenditure of rebuilding manufacturing facilities.

This pattern is increasingly relevant as brand equity becomes separable from operational capability. Companies with strong brand recognition but depleted operational capacity can structure returns through licensing arrangements and operational partners, preserving the most valuable asset (brand recognition) while outsourcing the most capital-intensive activities.
