Sequoia identifies a clear pattern: vertical agents trained end-to-end on domain-specific tasks using RL and synthetic data are achieving superhuman performance. Expo's AI outperformed every human hacker on HackerOne. Traversal's AI troubleshooter beats the best human troubleshooters. Meter's networking agent exceeds human networking engineers.

The strategic implication for founders is that deep domain understanding combined with end-to-end agent training creates a powerful moat. Sequoia sees two paths companies are taking: orchestration with rigorous testing/eval, or agents tuned on end-to-end tasks. The latter, following the same pattern as OpenAI's Deep Research, appears to be producing the most impressive results. For the application layer, this means the race is between startups going deep in verticals versus foundation models going broad.
