Hormozi contrasts Chick-fil-A (privately held, minimal debt, one location at a time, missionary founder) with Boston Market (franchise model, Wall Street money, rapid expansion, mercenary investors). Boston Market outgrew Chick-fil-A for years but eventually went bankrupt due to talent dilution and unsustainable infrastructure. The tactical application is '100 golden BBs, not one silver bullet' — each quarter, add one improvement to each business function that compounds permanently rather than being replaced. Growth is a lagging indicator of quality; treating it as the goal creates 'growth for growth's sake' which fragments focus. Elon Musk applies the same principle by only entering markets where he can be an order of magnitude better than existing solutions — he abandoned a chocolate venture because no version was 10x better than what existed.
