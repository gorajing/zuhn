Army ants are not individually impressive — they're smaller and weaker than many of their prey. Yet they consistently win against larger, stronger opponents like wasps because they attack as a coordinated unit with overwhelming numbers. The wasps might kill a few, but are quickly swarmed.

This maps directly to competitive strategy: a well-coordinated team executing together can defeat a competitor with individually stronger talent or resources. The key variable isn't individual capability but collective coordination speed and commitment. Startups that move as a unified force on a single vector often outcompete larger, fragmented incumbents.
