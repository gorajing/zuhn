As iPhone quality improved and resale values climbed, a thriving secondary market emerged on Amazon and eBay. Each new iPhone now competes not just against Samsung and Google, but against last year's still-excellent iPhone at a steep discount. The average upgrade cycle stretched from 2 years to 4 years between 2018-2019.

This is a structural inevitability for any company selling durable goods that improve incrementally. The 'product quality trap' means success breeds its own competitor. Apple's response — embracing longer lifecycles through better batteries and extended software support — is counterintuitive but strategically sound, because fighting durability would destroy the brand equity that sustains premium pricing.
