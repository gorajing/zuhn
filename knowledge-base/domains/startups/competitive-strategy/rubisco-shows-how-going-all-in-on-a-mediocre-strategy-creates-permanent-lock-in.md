RuBisCo, the enzyme that fixes carbon in photosynthesis, is so inefficient that it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, creating toxic byproducts plants must waste energy processing. Yet plants produce 40 billion tons of it at any given moment because they went 'all-in' on this strategy 450 million years ago.

This maps directly to technology and business: early architectural decisions that are 'good enough' become load-bearing walls that can never be replaced. The cost of switching grows exponentially as more systems depend on the original choice. The lesson isn't to avoid commitment — RuBisCo-using organisms dominated the planet — but to recognize that your earliest technical choices will outlive every optimization you layer on top. Choose foundations deliberately, because you'll be producing 40 billion tons of workarounds for whatever you pick.
