Graham's 'run upstairs' principle is a concrete competitive strategy: when a small startup is chased by a large incumbent, it should seek difficult technical terrain where the incumbent's size becomes a liability rather than an advantage. At Viaweb, when choosing between two equally valuable features, they always built the harder one — not despite the difficulty but because of it.

This inverts the typical optimization mindset. Most people seek the path of least resistance, but in startup competition, difficulty is your friend. Patents and legal protections are unreliable (large companies will work around them or simply violate them and dare you to sue), but genuine technical difficulty creates barriers that money alone cannot overcome. The strategy is fractal — it applies both to the overall company mission and to every micro-decision along the way.
