NFX distinguishes between two strategic approaches to AI in gaming. Incumbents will naturally add AI incrementally — lowering production costs, increasing development speed, and deepening existing game mechanics. This is valuable but evolutionary, not revolutionary.

The disruptive path belongs to AI-native founders who start with AI capabilities and ask what new forms of engagement are possible. This means new interfaces, new forms of play, new player types, new network effects, and new viral paths — not just better versions of existing game formats. The parallel to previous platform shifts is strong: Zynga didn't emerge from EA, and Roblox didn't come from Blizzard. The founders most likely to win are those who can reimagine what a 'game' even means when AI is the core medium rather than an add-on feature.
