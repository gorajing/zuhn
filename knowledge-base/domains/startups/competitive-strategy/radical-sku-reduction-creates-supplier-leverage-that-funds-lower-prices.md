By stocking only 1-2 options per product category instead of dozens, Costco concentrates its entire purchasing volume into those few items. Suppliers compete fiercely for those limited slots, accepting lower margins and even re-engineering products (like the cashew container redesign that saved 24,000 pallets/year) to win or keep Costco's business.

This is counterintuitive because conventional retail wisdom says more selection attracts more customers. But the constraint creates a triple benefit: (1) massive per-SKU volume gives negotiating leverage, (2) simplified stocking reduces operational costs, and (3) reduced choice paradox actually improves the shopping experience. When the retailer can credibly threaten to replace you with a single competitor or its own Kirkland brand (25% of sales), suppliers have no choice but to offer their best terms.
