Sculley explicitly noted that smartphone technology had become so commoditized that it was possible to build a high-quality phone and differentiate primarily through design — hiring Robert Brunner, the former head of Apple's product design who originally hired Jony Ive. The underlying Snapdragon and MediaTek processors, displays, and camera modules were available to anyone.

This is a recurring pattern across hardware industries: once the core technology becomes a commodity, the barriers to entry drop dramatically and competition shifts to design, brand, distribution, and price. The incumbents who built their advantage on proprietary technology find themselves vulnerable to nimble entrants who can assemble equivalent products from the same commodity supply chain. The key insight is recognizing the moment when commoditization has progressed far enough to enable this kind of entry.
