Amazon's labor model relied on high turnover as a feature: constantly refreshing the workforce prevented unionization and kept wages low. But in many markets, they've literally exhausted the local labor pool of people who haven't already worked there and left.

This is a general principle for companies that treat workers as interchangeable: there's a finite pool of humans in any geography. Once you've burned through enough of them, recruitment costs spike and quality drops. The same model that enabled rapid scaling creates its own ceiling.
