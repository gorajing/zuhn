The video highlights that nearly all domesticable animals (cattle, horses, sheep, goats, pigs, camels, donkeys) are native to Eurasia, leaving the Americas with only the llama. Most animals fail domestication for specific reasons: hippos eat people, zebras are too ornery, elephants take 22 months to breed. This wasn't a failure of American civilizations — it was a resource lottery.

The startup parallel is market infrastructure. Two equally talented founding teams in different markets face radically different ceilings based on available 'domesticable' resources: payment rails, developer talent pools, regulatory environments, existing APIs to build on. The lesson isn't 'pick a better market' — it's that resource advantages compound over time, so early infrastructure investments (or choosing markets with better existing infrastructure) create permanent structural advantages that no amount of execution can overcome.
