GoPro had everything Silicon Valley and Wall Street valued: category creation, first-mover advantage, strong brand (became the generic name for action cameras), and actual profits. But the action camera market was inherently small and niche. Smartphones improved. Competition commoditized the hardware. GoPro launched 9 generations and 20 models in 10 years, making each upgrade trivial. The prevailing belief that they could 'always branch out' never materialized. Lesson: creating a category is not the same as owning a viable long-term business. Market size is the ceiling, and no amount of brand strength changes the addressable market.
