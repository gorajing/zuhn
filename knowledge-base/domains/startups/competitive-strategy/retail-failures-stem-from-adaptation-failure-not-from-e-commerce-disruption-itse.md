The narrative of an 'Amazon-driven retail apocalypse' oversimplifies what actually happened. Sears tried to do everything poorly, J.C. Penney lost touch with its customers, and Toys-R-Us dismissed the internet. Meanwhile, physical retailers like Ross, Lush, Aldi, Dollar General, and Costco grew during the same period by offering distinctive value propositions that e-commerce couldn't easily replicate.

This matters because 'disruption' narratives often paralyze incumbents into believing their entire category is doomed, when the real threat is strategic stagnation. Companies that died didn't lose to Amazon specifically — they lost to their own failure to give customers a reason to choose them. The lesson for startups entering 'disrupted' markets: look for incumbents blaming external forces for internal failures, because that gap between narrative and reality is where opportunity lives.
