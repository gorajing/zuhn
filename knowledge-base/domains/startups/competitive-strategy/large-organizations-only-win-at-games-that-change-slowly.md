For most of history, bigger organizations won because they could marshal more resources and impose more discipline. But this advantage assumed a slowly-changing competitive environment where opponents couldn't rewrite the rules mid-game through new technology.

The critical insight is the qualifying condition: large and disciplined organizations win *at games that change slowly*. When the pace of change accelerates, the overhead of coordination, protocol, and politics that large organizations always carried becomes fatal rather than merely annoying. Small groups can pivot faster, adopt new technology sooner, and iterate without committee approval. This means competitive strategy must now account for the rate of environmental change as a primary variable, not just market share or resource depth.
