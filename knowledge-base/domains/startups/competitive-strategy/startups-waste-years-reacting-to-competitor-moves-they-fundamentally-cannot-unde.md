When Google Maps launched free navigation in 2009, Waze panicked and tried to anticipate Google's next moves based on their historical patterns (open APIs, US-UK-Germany rollout). Waze pivoted to Latin America to build assets where Google hadn't yet expanded. This cost them a full year of lost focus on the US market.

The deeper insight is that internal dynamics at large companies — VP politics, promotion incentives, reactions to other competitors — are completely invisible to outsiders. What looks like strategic intent is often organizational inertia or internal politics. Bardin's rule: until you own 20% of a market, no competitor is even aware you exist. Spending energy on competitor strategy before that threshold is literally negotiating with yourself — creating imaginary constraints from an opponent who doesn't see you. The only productive focus is your users and your core product.
