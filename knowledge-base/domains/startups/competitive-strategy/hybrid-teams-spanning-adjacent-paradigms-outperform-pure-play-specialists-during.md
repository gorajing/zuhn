NFX observes that the first wave of web3 games was built primarily by 'crypto people' approaching gaming from a monetary perspective — and they largely failed at creating sustainable products. The standout teams instead have experienced game designers, storytellers, and live ops specialists at their core, augmented by blockchain engineering talent.

This pattern holds because established-paradigm expertise encodes decades of hard-won knowledge about user psychology, engagement mechanics, and operational complexity that can't be skipped. The new paradigm contributes genuinely novel capabilities but doesn't invalidate the domain knowledge.

The hiring implication is counterintuitive during hype cycles: when everyone is hiring for the hot new skill, the scarce and more valuable talent is the veteran who understands the underlying domain deeply enough to apply new tools wisely. This applies to AI transitions today — teams with deep domain expertise augmented by AI capability will likely outperform pure AI-native teams in established verticals.
