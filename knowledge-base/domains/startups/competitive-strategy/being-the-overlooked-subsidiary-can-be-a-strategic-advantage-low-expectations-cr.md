Yum Brands under David Novak was laser-focused on beating McDonald's in China. KFC and Pizza Hut were the chosen warriors for this international arms race -- Taco Bell was excluded because Mexican food was a hard sell even to Americans, let alone Chinese consumers. This omission lowered expectations and pressure on Taco Bell to produce massive profits or rapidly scale.

The result was transformative. Emil Brolick had six years to methodically build the product foundation -- chicken quesadillas, crunch wrap supreme, Mountain Dew Baja Blast, the value menu. Greg Creed had nine more years to build the marketing and pricing house on that foundation. Neither faced the constant corporate interference that plagued KFC and Pizza Hut. When those two brands regressed domestically, corporate attention shifted to fixing them -- again leaving Taco Bell alone to execute. The pattern suggests that in multi-brand portfolios, the unfavored brand sometimes has the greatest long-term advantage precisely because it's left alone to build.
