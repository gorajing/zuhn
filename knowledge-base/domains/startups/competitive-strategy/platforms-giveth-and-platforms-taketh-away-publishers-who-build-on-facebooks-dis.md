Facebook's evolution: social network → engagement platform → ad machine. Zuckerberg opened to developers (like Apple's App Store), publishers flooded in as engagement drivers, Facebook's algorithm boosted their content. Then Facebook shifted priorities — deprioritizing publisher content to favor personal connections and paid distribution. BuzzFeed and VICE went from millions of organic impressions to paying to reach their own audiences. The lesson: building your business on someone else's distribution channel is renting, not owning. When the landlord changes terms, you have no recourse.
