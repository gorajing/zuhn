Stealth mode creates an inherent tension: the secrecy that protects against competitors also blocks the customer feedback loop that validates product-market fit. The article acknowledges startups risk 'developing products that miss the mark' without feedback, but frames this as a manageable tradeoff rather than the existential risk it often is.

The case studies reveal a survivorship bias — we hear about SpaceX and LinkedIn succeeding from stealth, but not the hundreds of stealth startups that built the wrong product in isolation. For most startups, the real competitive moat comes from execution speed and customer intimacy, not secrecy. The startups that succeeded from stealth (SpaceX, LinkedIn) had founders with exceptional domain expertise and networks that partially substituted for broad market feedback.
