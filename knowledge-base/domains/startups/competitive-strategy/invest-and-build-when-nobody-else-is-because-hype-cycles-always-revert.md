Doyle has been investing since 1999 and has seen multiple technology cycles. In 2022, when everyone was investing in AI, he deliberately was not, because he recognized the hype curve pattern. His rule: if everybody's investing in the same thing, you probably shouldn't be.

The corollary for founders: the best time to enter a market is during the trough of disillusionment, not the peak of hype. Companies started during hype peaks face inflated valuations, overcrowded markets, and unrealistic expectations. Companies started during troughs have cheaper talent, less competition, and lower bars for impressive results. Timing market entry to be contrarian—building when others are fleeing—is one of the most reliable strategies across 27 years of tech investing.
