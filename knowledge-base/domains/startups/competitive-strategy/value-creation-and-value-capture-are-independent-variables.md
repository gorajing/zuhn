Thiel contrasts the US airline industry ($195B revenue, ~zero cumulative profit over 100 years) with Google ($50B revenue, massive profits) to show that X (value created) and Y (percentage captured) are completely independent. Airlines create more value for society but capture none of it due to perfect competition, while Google captures enormous value through monopoly position.

This framework reframes startup strategy: founders should obsess not just over building something valuable, but over the structural dynamics that allow value capture. The entire history of science and the first Industrial Revolution (textile mills, 1780-1850) show that even decades of 5-7% annual efficiency gains produced no wealth for innovators when hundreds of competitors existed. Startups must design for capture from day one by targeting monopoly characteristics: proprietary technology (10x better), network effects, economies of scale, and branding.
