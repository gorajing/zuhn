The historical pattern across SEO slop, Facebook app scams, early iPhone app store spam, crypto ICOs, and the emerging AI generation-slop wave is consistent: the companies that participate in the slop war all burn out (turkey graph, up-then-dead) while the underlying platform (Google, Facebook, Apple, Ethereum, the AI model providers) accrues the durable value. The strategic implication is that when a competitor is spamming, matching their spam produces more tokens consumed and a more valuable platform, not a more valuable startup. The winning move in any slop war is to leave the war — find a retention-oriented metric that the slop producers cannot game and build toward that instead. Symmetric escalation is the trap.
