Wang reframes Chinese innovation: the Silicon Valley model is Steve Jobs in a garage producing an Apple computer. The Chinese model treats the production process itself as innovation. With 50+ major automotive makers and 6-7 smartphone brands competing fiercely, the iteration speed is the advantage. American automakers take 6 years from concept to street; Chinese competitors do it in 18 months.

China has 70 million manufacturing workers building sophisticated electronics daily, solving new problems before breakfast. This creates a practice-based innovation loop: you cannot innovate on products you do not make. When R&D is separated from manufacturing, you lose the feedback loop. Wang argues 'Made in China' is becoming a quality signal the way 'Made in Japan' once did. Detroit, Intel, Boeing, and Tesla have all suffered from strategic missteps unrelated to China — the American manufacturing base looks broken from within.
