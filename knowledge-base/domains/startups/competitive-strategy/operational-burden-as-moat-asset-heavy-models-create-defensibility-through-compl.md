Companies like Pacaso deliberately take on the burden of property management for fractional vacation homes, despite it being operationally intensive. This isn't a weakness — it's a moat. When a platform manages the physical asset (maintenance, scheduling, legal entities), it creates switching costs, accumulates proprietary operational knowledge, and deters competitors who want to stay asset-light.

This echoes the broader principle that constraints which limit a business also protect it. The essay notes that 'asset-heavy inventory is going to be a huge competitive advantage' in the lifestyle segment. Founders often instinctively avoid operational complexity, preferring pure software plays. But in markets where the physical world is the product (real estate, logistics, healthcare), the willingness to get hands dirty with atoms — not just bits — creates the strongest defensibility. The key is having 'the right DNA' for it from the start, not bolting it on later.
