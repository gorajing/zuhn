3G Capital's turnaround of Burger King is a textbook PE execution: take private, cut costs, franchise everything, improve margins, relist. Operating margins went from 15% to 69%. Revenue shifted from $2.5B to $1B (lower, but almost all profit). The company went from 1,500 corporate stores to 52. On paper, stunning.

But average store revenue stayed frozen at $1.18M per year for five years -- no change in actual customer demand. The new products were deliberately operationally simple (rearrangements of existing ingredients), the broilers were replaced to save on gas despite potentially ruining the signature flame-grilled taste, and marketing became so broad ('we welcome everyone') that it said nothing. The PE playbook optimized the financials while hollowing out everything that would drive long-term growth. Wall Street was pleased by the margin story, but customers experienced a brand that became stale, unopinionated, and forgettable.
