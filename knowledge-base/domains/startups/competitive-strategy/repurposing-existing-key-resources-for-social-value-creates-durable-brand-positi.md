LVMH's pivot achieved three objectives simultaneously: it responded to a humanitarian need, kept factories operational (avoiding production shutdown risks), and permanently repositioned the brand in consumers' minds. The cost was raw materials for sanitizer; the return was a shift in brand perception that advertising budgets of tens of millions couldn't reliably purchase.

The strategic principle is that key resources are often underutilized because companies define them narrowly. A perfume factory is really a chemical mixing and bottling operation. The trigger question 'how might we use one of our key resources to create a new value proposition?' forces founders to see their assets at the capability level rather than the product level. This reframing is valuable far beyond crisis situations—it's the foundation of platform thinking and resource-based diversification.
