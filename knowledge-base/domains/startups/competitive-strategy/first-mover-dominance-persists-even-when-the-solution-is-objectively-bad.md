The video opens by calling photosynthesis something that 'actually rather sucks—complicated, inefficient and confusing.' Yet it has been the dominant energy conversion system for 450 million years, powering nearly all life on Earth. No competing biological mechanism has ever displaced it.

This challenges the common startup assumption that a better product inevitably wins. Photosynthesis created a self-reinforcing moat: it changed the atmosphere (adding oxygen), which made the environment hostile to alternative approaches while simultaneously creating dependencies (aerobic respiration) that require its continued operation. First movers that reshape their environment don't just gain market share—they redefine the game so thoroughly that 'better' alternatives can't even get a foothold.
