Between 2013-2014, Google acquired Boston Dynamics (robotics), DeepMind (AI/machine learning), Nest (smart home/IoT), and multiple smaller robotics companies in rapid succession. Rather than acquiring revenue streams or market share, each purchase added a distinct capability layer — perception, intelligence, physical manipulation, and consumer hardware. The Motorola Mobility deal further illustrates this: Google bought it for $12.5B, sold the hardware business for $2.9B, but retained the patent portfolio to protect Android. This reveals an acquisition philosophy focused on assembling technological building blocks rather than buying businesses, treating M&A as R&D acceleration rather than revenue consolidation.
