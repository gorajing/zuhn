Tasch and Calacanis's framework: 
• **Bucket 1 — Eroding terminal value (melting iceberg)**: Tools agents replace (Marquetto, Outreach, Atlassian, Medallia). If you also have debt, you're a candidate for handing keys to creditors. 
• **Bucket 2 — System-of-record bounded value**: Tools agents tolerate because they hold the data of record (Workday, Salesforce CRM, ERP). Positive but slow-growth NPV. Workday's 3-year-then-5-year contracts buy ~8 years of forced retention but don't change terminal value. 
• **Bucket 3 — Agent-leveraged growth**: Tools agents actively amplify with multiplier effects (Twilio, Cloudflare, Salesforce headless if executed well). Increasing returns as agents fan out, can re-ignite SaaS growth.

The ServiceNow analyst-call evidence: drilly questions about whether agent revenue is 'really real or just bundling' — the market has internalized that agent acceleration is the only signal that justifies SaaS premium. Without it, you're priced like a real company on real cash flows, no Pixie Dust premium. For Zuhn editorially: this is the right model for evaluating any SaaS company in 2026. Don't ask 'is it growing?' — growth from system-of-record renewals is misleading. Ask 'which bucket is it in, and is the trajectory between buckets?'
