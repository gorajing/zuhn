OpenAI's own head of startups warns that most AI companies are 'just demos' — feature-level AI capabilities get replicated quickly as foundation models improve and API costs drop. The real survival filter is pricing discipline combined with structural moats.

The three durable moats identified — proprietary data, distribution, and trust — share a common trait: they compound over time and resist copying. A startup with unique training data, an embedded distribution channel, or deep customer trust has advantages that don't evaporate when a competitor ships a similar feature. This framework is especially useful for evaluating which AI startups will survive the current wave versus which are riding temporary capability gaps.
