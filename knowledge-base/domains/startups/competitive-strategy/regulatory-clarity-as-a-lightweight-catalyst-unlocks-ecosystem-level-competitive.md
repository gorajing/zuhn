The stablecoin case illustrates a powerful pattern: instead of building complex infrastructure directly, authorities can catalyze entire ecosystems by providing just three things — a clear definition (what qualifies as a digital USD), simple requirements (full reserves, auditable finances, KYC'd founders), and legal protection (trademark enforcement). This is remarkably low-lift governance that enables high-impact outcomes.

This pattern applies beyond stablecoins to any domain where governments or platforms want to foster innovation while maintaining standards. The key insight is that regulatory ambiguity is the bottleneck, not lack of government capability. Founders are already motivated and building — they just need clear rules of engagement. When those rules arrive, the competitive advantage accrues not to any single company but to the entire ecosystem and the sovereign entity that enabled it.
