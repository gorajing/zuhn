Argentine ants are 2-3mm with weak mandibles — individually unremarkable compared to fire ants or carpenter ants. Yet they built the largest society on Earth by going all-in on reproduction (one queen per 120 workers, 60 eggs/day) and cooperation across colonies.

This maps directly to competitive strategy: startups that ship fast, hire aggressively, and maintain tight internal alignment can overwhelm larger, more talented but siloed competitors. The Argentine ant lesson is that the winning strategy isn't building the strongest individual unit — it's maximizing throughput while eliminating internal friction. Amazon's leadership principles and high-volume hiring, or Chinese tech companies' 996 culture outpacing Western competitors, echo this biological pattern.
