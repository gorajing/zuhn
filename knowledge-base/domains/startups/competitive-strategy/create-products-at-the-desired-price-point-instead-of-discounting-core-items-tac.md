Burger King's story contrasts sharply with Taco Bell's approach, both covered in Modern MBA episodes. Burger King discounted the Whopper so frequently that the product lost all perceived value -- customers would never pay full price because they knew a deal was always coming. McDonald's did the same with Big Mac promotions. Both chains destroyed their own pricing power.

Taco Bell took the opposite approach: 'We don't discount core menu items. If we need a $1 price point, we create products at $1.' A 79-cent cheese roll-up or 99-cent bean burrito are purposefully designed to be profitable at those prices. When Taco Bell wants to promote value, customers get these dedicated items, not discounted Crunch Wraps. This preserves the perceived value and pricing power of core items while still serving price-sensitive customers. The result: Taco Bell has some of the highest store-level profit margins in the entire restaurant industry, ahead of McDonald's, Burger King, and KFC.
