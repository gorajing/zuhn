Academic research by Glennon (2023) established that H-1B restrictions cause multinationals to increase foreign affiliate employment, particularly in R&D-intensive roles in China, India, and Canada, at a rate of 0.4 foreign hires per visa rejection. This pattern is now repeating at scale: Alphabet is plotting millions of square feet of new office space in Bangalore, while OpenAI and Anthropic have recently set up Indian operations. India's global capability centers are projected to grow from 1.9 million to 2.5 million employees by 2030. The economic damage is compounding — those workers would otherwise spend their salaries on American services, creating domestic economic multipliers. For startups, this means the talent landscape is permanently shifting: the best AI engineers will increasingly be accessible through Indian offices rather than US immigration, reshaping where companies need to build engineering presence.
