Osterwalder's expected return axis evaluates not just revenue size but business model robustness — recurring revenues, long-term growth potential, scalability, and protection from competition. This means two businesses with identical current revenue can sit at very different positions on the return axis based on structural quality.

This reframes the goal of business model innovation from simply finding revenue to designing defensible systems. An initiative that validates customer demand but lacks moats or scalability has not fully reached the upper-right corner. The implication for founders is that validation should extend beyond product-market fit to include testing whether the economic model itself is structurally sound and defensible.
