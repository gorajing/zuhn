Board membership pays extremely well for minimal time commitment - often hundreds of thousands for quarterly meetings. This compensation structure attracts and retains directors who are incentivized to maintain smooth relationships rather than exercise challenging oversight.

The fundamental misalignment: directors who challenge management risk losing lucrative board seats (and seats on other interlocked boards). Directors who go along keep collecting fees across multiple boards. Natural selection produces boards optimized for agreeableness rather than oversight, which is exactly backward from the original governance design.
