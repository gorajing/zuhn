Kate describes Anthropic's dual sales motion. The primary buyer has been developers consuming the API, which manifests through CPOs and CTOs who build with Claude. But a second buyer has emerged: the Chief AI Officer (or equivalent), who manages what Anthropic internally calls 'model gardens' — the portfolio of AI models an enterprise provisions and manages.

The CAO's internal customers are the CTOs and CPOs. So Anthropic works with both groups but in fundamentally different ways: the CAO cares about model governance, security, compliance, and portfolio strategy; the CTO/CPO cares about capability, performance, and developer experience.

Kate estimates the majority of the Fortune 500 now has someone responsible for model management, even if the exact title varies. This is a rapidly crystallizing buyer persona.

For an Anthropic sales candidate, understanding both buyer types and their distinct concerns is essential. A deal might require winning the CAO on governance and safety (where Anthropic's RSP and constitutional AI are differentiators) while simultaneously winning the CTO on model performance and developer experience (where Claude's capabilities matter).
