In January 1994, before Netscape even existed, Microsoft engineer J Allard sent a memo arguing Mosaic's adoption curve looked exponential and represented an existential opportunity. Bill Gates held an internet offsite on April 5, 1994 (one day after Netscape's incorporation) where he declared it a core Microsoft company value that 'exponential phenomena cannot be ignored.' The human brain is poorly wired to understand compounding — even Marc Andreessen initially pivoted Netscape toward the 'information superhighway' rather than the open web. The lesson for incumbents: when a technology is clearly compounding, the correct response is to reorganize the entire company around it, not to place defensive side-bets that approximate the future. Microsoft's willingness to rewrite Windows 95 on the fly to embed a browser is what let them survive the internet transition.
