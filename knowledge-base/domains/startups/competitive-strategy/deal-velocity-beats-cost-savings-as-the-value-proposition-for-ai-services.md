Crosby crystallized their value proposition as 'deal velocity' rather than cheaper legal services. Their fastest-growing customers like Cursor and Clay are signing dozens of contracts daily. The cost of slow contract review isn't the legal fee; it's delayed revenue from deals stuck in negotiation. Crosby's approach of predicting which terms to concede upfront can eliminate entire negotiation rounds, saving weeks per deal.

This reframes how to sell AI-enabled services: don't compete on cost (which commoditizes quickly), compete on speed of outcomes. The billable hour model is inherently misaligned with this goal, which is why Crosby bills per document and invests in predicting deal complexity at time zero. By understanding that AI's ability to explain contract changes persuasively reduces negotiation rounds, they're selling acceleration rather than labor replacement.
