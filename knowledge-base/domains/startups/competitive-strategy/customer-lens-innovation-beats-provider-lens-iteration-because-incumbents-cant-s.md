The existing VCs couldn't conceive of the platform model because they had never been entrepreneurs — they had never been customers of their own product. They looked at VC from the provider side and saw a perfectly functioning business (the sushi boat analogy), while entrepreneurs experienced a fundamentally broken relationship with information asymmetry and lack of support.

This pattern appears across industries: incumbent auto companies couldn't build compelling EVs because their executives weren't software-native; financial institutions couldn't see fintech opportunities because they weren't frustrated end-users. The structural advantage of coming from the customer side is that you can see pain points that providers have rationalized away as features. Once founders with the customer perspective enter an industry, incumbents who haven't been their own customers are structurally unable to respond because they literally can't see what's broken.
