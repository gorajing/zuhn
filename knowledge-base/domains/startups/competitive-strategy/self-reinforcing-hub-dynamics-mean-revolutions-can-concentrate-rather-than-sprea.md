Every previous economic revolution—agriculture, urbanization, industrialization—eventually spread across the world. Graham argues startups may break this pattern because the forces that drive concentration (community expertise, investor proximity, peer networks) are stronger than the forces that drive dispersal (no geographic market need, government policy ineffectiveness).

This creates a winner-take-all dynamic at the ecosystem level. The best startups produced by any aspiring hub get 'sucked away' to established centers, creating a drainage effect that actually weakens emerging ecosystems. For founders and investors, this suggests that proximity to an established hub remains a meaningful competitive advantage, and that the decentralization of startup culture may be far slower than the pace of communication technology would suggest.
