Bacteriophages demonstrate a powerful strategic principle: when bacteria evolve resistance to phages, they must give up their antibiotic resistance. This catch-22 dynamic applies broadly to competitive strategy — the most devastating attacks come in pairs where the defense against one requires vulnerability to the other.

In business, this manifests as multi-vector competition. A startup might simultaneously compete on price and ecosystem lock-in, forcing incumbents to choose between margin erosion (matching price) or platform erosion (ignoring the ecosystem play). The key insight is that the two attack vectors must be mechanistically linked — defending against one must structurally require exposure to the other, not just stretch resources thin.
