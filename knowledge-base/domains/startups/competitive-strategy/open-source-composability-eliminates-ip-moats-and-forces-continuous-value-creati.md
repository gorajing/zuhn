Web3's open-source and composable nature creates a competitive environment fundamentally different from web2. Sushiswap's vampire attack on Uniswap demonstrated that any product can be forked instantly, and users can migrate with their data. This eliminates two of the most powerful web2 moats: proprietary technology and high switching costs.

The strategic implication extends beyond web3. This is a preview of what happens in any market where code transparency and data portability become norms—competitive advantage shifts entirely from defensive moats (IP, data lock-in, network effects from proprietary graphs) to offensive capability (speed of innovation, community trust, continuous value delivery). Products must earn their users every day rather than relying on friction to retain them. For product strategists in any domain, this suggests that regulatory trends toward data portability and open APIs will create similar dynamics in web2, making community-first strategies increasingly relevant even outside crypto.
