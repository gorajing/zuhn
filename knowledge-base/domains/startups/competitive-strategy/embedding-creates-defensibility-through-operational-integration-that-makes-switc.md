Embedding as a durability factor works by integrating your product into customer operations so deeply that replacing it requires significant time, money, and organizational disruption. The key metrics are customer retention rate, net revenue retention, and net revenue expansion through cross-selling — each additional product sold deepens the embed.

This is particularly powerful in B2B where becoming the 'database of record' creates a structural lock-in that transcends any individual feature comparison. The strategic implication is that product roadmaps should prioritize integration depth (APIs, workflows, data dependencies) over feature breadth, because each integration point increases the cost of switching. Workday, Oracle, and SAP exemplify this — they persist not because they're the best product, but because they're woven into organizational DNA.
