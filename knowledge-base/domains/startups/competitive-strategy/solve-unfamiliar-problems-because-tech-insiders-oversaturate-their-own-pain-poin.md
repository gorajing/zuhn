Katz challenges Silicon Valley dogma: the popular belief that founders should solve their own problems creates a saturation of solutions for tech workers' problems while massive industries like insurance go untouched. Two outsiders with no insurance background built Commulate to 8-figure ARR.

The key: the early skepticism ('I'm taking this call as a favor, we don't need your services') is the signal, not the noise. That company is now one of their largest customers. When industry insiders say they don't need help, it often means they've accepted broken processes as normal. Outsiders see the brokenness clearly because they have no inherited assumptions about 'how things are done here.'
