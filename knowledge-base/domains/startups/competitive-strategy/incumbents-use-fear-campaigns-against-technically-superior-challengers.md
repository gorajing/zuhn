When Edison recognized that his DC system was technically inferior to Tesla's AC, he launched a fear campaign rather than improving his product. He paid schoolboys to steal neighborhood pets, then publicly electrocuted them using AC current to 'prove' it was too dangerous for homes. He also ensured the electric chair used AC current to cement the association between AC and death.

This pattern recurs throughout technology history: incumbents with inferior technology invest in FUD (fear, uncertainty, doubt) rather than innovation. The strategy buys time but ultimately fails because technical superiority compounds while fear campaigns have diminishing returns. Tesla's counter-demonstration — shooting AC current through his own body at the 1893 World Fair — was a single dramatic act that neutralized years of Edison's propaganda.
