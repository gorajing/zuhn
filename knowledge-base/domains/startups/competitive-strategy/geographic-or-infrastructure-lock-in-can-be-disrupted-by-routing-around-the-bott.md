The video shows multiple examples of routing around Russia's airspace chokepoint. Finnair flew over the North Pole (adding 4 hours but avoiding Anchorage stops entirely). Wow Air calculated that routing around Russia to Delhi added only 45 minutes versus the direct path. The Cold War-era Anchorage hub, while inefficient, still halved London-Tokyo travel time versus the pre-polar route. The lesson for competitive strategy is that chokepoint controllers often overestimate their leverage. Challengers who map the actual cost of workarounds frequently discover the penalty is tolerable—especially for cost-focused competitors whose customers trade convenience for price. This applies broadly: when an incumbent controls a critical resource, the disruptor's first move should be to quantify the exact cost of the alternative path, because it's usually smaller than the incumbent assumes.
