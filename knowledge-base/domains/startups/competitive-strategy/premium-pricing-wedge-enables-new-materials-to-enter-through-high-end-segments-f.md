Real Graphene's graphene-enhanced batteries add approximately 30% to the battery component cost. Rather than waiting for costs to drop to parity, the company's strategy is to enter through premium device segments where the value proposition (3x faster charging, 2.5x battery lifecycle) justifies the price premium. The CEO stated: 'trickling down from the higher end first.'

This is the classic top-down market entry pattern seen with lithium-ion (laptops before phones), LED lighting (commercial before residential), and electric vehicles (Tesla Roadster before Model 3). The pattern works because premium segments provide revenue to fund manufacturing improvements that drive costs down for mass-market entry. For materials startups, this means the go-to-market question isn't 'when will costs match incumbents' but 'which premium segment values the performance improvement enough to pay a premium now.'
