Traditional startup evaluation heavily weights gross margins as a proxy for business quality—high margins signal scalability and pricing power. But in the AI era, this heuristic breaks down. A company can show strong margins while having zero defensibility if its AI capabilities are easily replicated, and conversely, a lower-margin business with deep network effects and proprietary data may be far more durable.

The framework from Immerman and George argues that speed earns the right to build walls—move fast to capture usage and love, then convert that into structural advantages like network effects, scale economies, switching costs, brand, and unique data. The key insight is that margin optimization is a second-order concern; the first-order question is whether the business is accumulating compounding advantages that become harder to replicate over time.
