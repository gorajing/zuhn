Business model innovation is difficult partly because it requires configuring many interdependent elements simultaneously — a combinatorial design problem. Pattern libraries reduce this complexity by providing pre-validated configurations that can be studied, adapted, and combined.

This parallels how software design patterns (Gang of Four) made architecture more accessible by codifying recurring solutions. The 9 INVENT patterns with 27 flavors represent a similar codification effort for business models. The practical implication is that entrepreneurs should invest time studying existing patterns before attempting to design novel models — most 'innovative' business models are recombinations of known patterns applied to new contexts.
