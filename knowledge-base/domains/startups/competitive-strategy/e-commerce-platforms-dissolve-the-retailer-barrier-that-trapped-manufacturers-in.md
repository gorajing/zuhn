Historically, Shenzhen manufacturers were trapped as white-label suppliers—they built the products but retailers captured the brand premium. Companies like Anker, DJI, and OnePlus broke this pattern by selling directly through Amazon and other e-commerce platforms, where algorithmic recommendation rewards product quality over brand legacy. This represents a structural shift: when distribution was controlled by physical retailers, proximity to consumers mattered more than proximity to production. E-commerce inverted this, making production-side advantages (speed, cost, iteration) directly monetizable. The pattern generalizes—any industry where intermediaries extract value by controlling access to end customers is vulnerable to platform-driven disintermediation.
