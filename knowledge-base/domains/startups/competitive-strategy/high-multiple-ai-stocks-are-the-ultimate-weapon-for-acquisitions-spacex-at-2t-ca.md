The 20VC team's structural observation: future SpaceX public shareholders will be valuing a $20B revenue company at $2T (100x revenue). At those multiples, paper acquisitions of 10-15x-revenue companies are obvious arbitrage. Same logic applies to Anthropic going public into peak AI FOMO — the moment to IPO is when relative desirability is at its maximum because you can use stock as currency for future roll-ups.

The corollary for incumbent SaaS: if your stock multiple compresses (Figma down 87% from peak, Adobe under pressure), your M&A currency evaporates exactly when you most need to acquire AI-native capability. This creates a positive-feedback loop where high-multiple AI leaders accumulate further capability via acquisition while incumbents lose the ability to defensively acquire. For Zuhn editorially this is the operational form of the model-is-not-the-moat thesis: it's not just that incumbents lose to new entrants, it's that the high-multiple new entrants can buy the incumbent's customers and tech directly with paper currency that the incumbents can't match. The race-to-IPO is partly a race to weaponize multiples.
