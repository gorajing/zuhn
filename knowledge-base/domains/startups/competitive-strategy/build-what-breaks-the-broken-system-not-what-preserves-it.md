Roy Lee identified that LeetCode-style coding interviews are a broken system—candidates memorize 300-600 riddles that have nothing to do with actual engineering work. Instead of accepting this, he built a tool that exposed the absurdity by making the memorization game trivially easy to cheat.

The underlying insight: when a system is fundamentally broken (testing memorization instead of ability), the disruption comes from making the broken metric worthless, forcing the market to adopt better approaches. Lee then parlayed this into Cluely, a broader AI assistance tool, showing how contrarian market insight can be the foundation for larger product visions.
