The forward-deployed engineer (FDE) model, first popularized at Palantir, places builders directly inside customer environments to ship solutions against real problems in real-time. This creates a tight feedback loop that traditional sales engineering or professional services cannot match — the builder sees the problem firsthand and can iterate immediately.

However, the same proximity that makes FDEs powerful also makes bad FDE hires uniquely destructive. A poor fit doesn't just waste internal salary — they burn through enterprise customer goodwill and budgets at the point of highest trust. The role requires a rare combination of technical depth, customer empathy, and autonomous judgment. Startups adopting this model should treat FDE hiring with more rigor than typical engineering roles, not less, because the blast radius of failure is directly customer-facing.
