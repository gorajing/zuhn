The video contrasts cow and elephant populations to make a striking point: cows are neither smart nor strong, but their usefulness to humans (meat, milk, leather, labor) made them one of the most 'successful' large mammals on earth by population. Elephants are smarter, stronger, and more capable — but their 22-month gestation and independence from human systems left them endangered.

This is the platform strategy lesson distilled to biology. Companies that align themselves as essential infrastructure for dominant platforms (AWS marketplace, Shopify apps, iOS ecosystem) achieve scale that independently brilliant companies cannot. Stripe succeeded not by being the smartest payments company, but by being the most useful to developers building on web platforms. The survival advantage isn't intelligence or strength — it's indispensability to the dominant system. Founders should ask: 'What is the cow strategy for my market?' — how do I make my product so useful to the dominant platform that my growth becomes a side effect of theirs?
