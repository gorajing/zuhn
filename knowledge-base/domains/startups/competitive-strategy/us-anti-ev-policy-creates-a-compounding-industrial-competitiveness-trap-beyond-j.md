Battery cells, power electronics, electric motors, and battery management systems are shared components across EVs, drones, robots, and consumer electronics. Manufacturing these at scale drives down costs and up quality through learning curves — the same dynamic that made Asian electronics dominant.

When a country artificially suppresses EV demand through tariffs and political hostility, it doesn't just lose the car market. It prevents domestic manufacturers from achieving the production scale needed to compete in adjacent technology markets. This is the 'platform technology' trap: the US is simultaneously trying to lead in drones and robotics while kneecapping the single largest demand driver for their shared component base.
