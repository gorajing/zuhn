Insurance incumbents had no CTOs, no product managers, no engineers. Their backend was Guidewire, billing was outsourced, implementation was Accenture. Every product change required coordinating with multiple third parties, making iteration cycles measured in quarters or years rather than days. Hippo, built natively on AWS/Stripe/Intercom, could ship in days what incumbents needed months to spec.

This is a generalizable pattern for identifying disruption opportunities: look for industries where the dominant players treat technology as a cost center rather than a core competency. When the entire technology function is outsourced, the incumbent has no muscle memory for rapid iteration. Combined with regulatory filing requirements across 50 states, every change becomes a bureaucratic exercise. For founders evaluating markets: the gap between 'has technology' and 'is a technology company' is where the opportunity lives.
