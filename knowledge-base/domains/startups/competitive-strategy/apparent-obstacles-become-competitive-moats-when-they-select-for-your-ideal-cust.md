The $60-120 annual fee seems like a barrier to customer acquisition, but it functions as a demographic filter: Costco members average nearly $100,000 in household income. Business owners, who make up one-third of members, account for two-thirds of sales. The fee triggers sunk-cost psychology that increases visit frequency and reduces price-comparison shopping.

This principle applies broadly to product design and go-to-market strategy. Strategically placed friction can be more valuable than frictionless access when it selects for customers with higher lifetime value, lower churn, and stronger word-of-mouth. The key insight is that the obstacle doesn't just filter — it transforms the filtered customer's relationship with the brand. They become invested advocates rather than passive browsers.
