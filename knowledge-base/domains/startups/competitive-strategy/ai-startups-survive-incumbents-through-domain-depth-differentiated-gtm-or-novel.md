Krieger identifies three specific moats for AI startups facing competition from Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google: (1) deep domain expertise like Harvey in legal, where understanding the domain is as important as the model; (2) differentiated go-to-market with specific customer knowledge that platform companies can't easily replicate; and (3) completely new interaction paradigms that incumbents can't easily copy.

The companies that thrived on Anthropic's platform — Cursor, Lovable, Bolt — succeeded because they pushed the limits with earlier models, hit walls, and were ready when new capabilities emerged. This suggests a fourth meta-strategy: build at the bleeding edge of model capabilities so you're first to exploit each capability jump. The worst position is building a thin wrapper that any competitor or the platform itself can replicate.
