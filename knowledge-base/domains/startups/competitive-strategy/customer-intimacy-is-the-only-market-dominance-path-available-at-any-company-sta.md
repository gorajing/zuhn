Michael Treacy and Fred Wiersema's HBR framework identifies three paths to market dominance, each requiring a specific company organization: best price (logistics focus, like Walmart/Amazon), best product (R&D focus, like Apple), or best overall solution (customer intimacy). The first two require massive capital investment in logistics infrastructure or R&D capability.

Customer intimacy — the path followed by luxury brands and hospitality — requires only humility and manners. This makes it the only viable default strategy for startups at any stage. Wufoo demonstrated this: a 10-person remote team with $118K in total funding achieved a 29,000% return to investors by being fanatically customer-intimate. The implication is that startups should stop trying to compete on price or product sophistication and instead compete on the depth of their relationship with users.
