When Microsoft acquired Nokia's device division, a 30-month non-compete clause prevented Nokia-branded phones from being released. Rather than being purely destructive, this window allowed ex-Nokia executives to found HMD Global, secure manufacturing partnerships with Foxconn, and plan a coordinated market re-entry with proper marketing infrastructure.

This pattern applies broadly: forced dormancy periods—whether from non-competes, regulatory holds, or contractual lockouts—can be leveraged as strategic planning windows. The key is whether the displaced team uses the time to simply wait or to actively rebuild with fresh architecture and partnerships.
