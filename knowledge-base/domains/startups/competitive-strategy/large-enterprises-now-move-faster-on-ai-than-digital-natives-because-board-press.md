Maggie shares a counterintuitive observation: she changed her mind on the assumption that large enterprises can't move fast. OpenAI now sells to oil, gas, energy, airlines, and government — sectors traditionally seen as slow. The UK Ministry of Justice piloted and bought within a couple of months.

The dynamic: when boards demand an AI strategy and CEOs feel competitive pressure, large enterprises bypass their usual lengthy evaluation cycles. Meanwhile, digital-native companies that are culturally more experimental are paradoxically slower because they're busy piloting and testing everything.

The sales implication: for AI companies, the largest enterprise deals may actually close faster than mid-market deals right now, because the urgency is top-down (board-driven) rather than bottom-up (user-driven). This inverts the typical startup wisdom of 'start small, move upmarket later.'

For Anthropic sales strategy, this suggests that going after traditional enterprises (financial services, healthcare, government) who face board-level AI pressure may yield faster sales cycles than pursuing tech companies who want to carefully evaluate every model option.
