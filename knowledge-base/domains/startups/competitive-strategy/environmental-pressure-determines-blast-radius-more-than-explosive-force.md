The Tsar Bomba — humanity's most powerful weapon — creates a 10km fireball in open air but only a 1km bubble at the bottom of the ocean before being crushed. Same explosive force, radically different outcomes based purely on environmental pressure.

This maps directly to market disruption: a powerful new product or technology has its blast radius determined more by the structural resistance of the market than by the product's inherent capability. Heavily regulated industries, entrenched distribution networks, high switching costs, and strong network effects are the 'thousand atmospheres of pressure' that can crush even genuinely superior technology. This is why the same innovation can reshape one market while barely registering in another — the medium determines the blast radius, not the bomb.
