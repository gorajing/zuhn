The video describes photosynthesis as 'complicated, inefficient, and confusing' — yet this process enabled early photosynthetic organisms to take over the entire planet. The inefficiency didn't matter because no competitor existed with a better alternative, and the sheer volume of output (flooding the atmosphere with oxygen) reshaped the environment to favor organisms that had already adapted to it.

This parallels first-mover advantage in markets: products like Craigslist, PHP, or early social networks succeeded not through elegance but through being first and scaling. The environmental reshaping is key — photosynthesis changed Earth's atmosphere in ways that benefited photosynthesizers and harmed competitors, just as dominant platforms reshape market conditions to favor incumbents. The implication for founders: launch an inefficient-but-working solution early, then let scale create defensive moats rather than perfecting before launch.
