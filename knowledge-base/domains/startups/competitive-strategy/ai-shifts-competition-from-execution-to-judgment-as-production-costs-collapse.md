AI systems amplify whatever strategic direction they're pointed at — good judgment gets amplified into outsized results, while poor judgment accelerates failure. This inverts the traditional startup advantage where scrappy execution could compensate for mediocre strategy.

The implication is profound: founders and leaders must invest disproportionately in decision-making frameworks, market understanding, and strategic clarity rather than execution speed. The ability to correctly identify what to build, for whom, and when becomes the scarce resource, while the actual building becomes increasingly commoditized. Teams with clear thinking and poor execution will outperform teams with excellent execution and muddled strategy.
