Multiple ant species developed highly specialized defensive adaptations against army ants: some evolved workers with oversized square heads solely to block nest entrances, while leafcutter ants maintain soldiers 100x the mass of workers whose primary purpose is colony defense. These aren't improvised responses — they're pre-built infrastructure.

For startups, this means competitive moats must be built before competitors arrive, not in response to attack. Companies that wait until a well-funded competitor enters their market to start building defensibility are like ant colonies without specialized soldiers — they'll be overwhelmed. The cost of maintaining defensive capability (platform lock-in, network effects, switching costs) is the price of survival.
