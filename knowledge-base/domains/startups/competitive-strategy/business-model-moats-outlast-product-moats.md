Samsung can copy the iPhone's hardware and software features, but replicating Apple's App Store ecosystem with its hundreds of thousands of applications, developer relationships, and user habits is a fundamentally different challenge. This illustrates why product innovation alone is an increasingly unreliable source of long-term competitive advantage.

The implication for founders is to evaluate competitive strategy at the business model level, not just the product level. When assessing defensibility, the question isn't 'can someone build a similar product?' (they almost certainly can) but 'can someone replicate the entire system of relationships, incentives, and structural advantages?' Business models that score well on multiple dimensions — switching costs, recurring revenue, externalized labor, cost structure advantages — create compounding moats that are qualitatively different from product differentiation.
