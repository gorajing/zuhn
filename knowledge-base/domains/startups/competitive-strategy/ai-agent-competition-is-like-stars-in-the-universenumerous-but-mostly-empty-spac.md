Crivello draws a vivid analogy: there are trillions of stars in the universe, yet the universe is 99.9% empty space. AI agents are the same—there seem to be new companies launching every week on Twitter, yet when Lindy is out in the market selling, they rarely encounter competitors.

This means the perceived competition is mostly noise. The AI agent market is so enormous and so early that even hundreds of companies are barely scratching the surface. For founders considering entering the space, the question shouldn't be 'is it too crowded?' but 'can I serve a specific segment better than anyone else?' The market will absorb far more companies than currently exist.
