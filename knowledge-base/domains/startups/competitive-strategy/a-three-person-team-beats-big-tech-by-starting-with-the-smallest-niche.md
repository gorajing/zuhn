Lang is blunt: it would be foolish to believe a 3-person team can just show up and be better than companies with thousands of employees after one year of work. The realistic path is starting with a very small, specific niche where you can deliver dramatically better value, then expanding over time.

Mangomint started with salon and spa scheduling—a niche within a niche. Every town in America has these businesses, but no major tech company was building specifically for them. This gave Mangomint a foothold where their small team's focused attention was actually a competitive advantage over generalist solutions. The pattern is universal: find the niche too small for incumbents to care about, dominate it, then expand.
