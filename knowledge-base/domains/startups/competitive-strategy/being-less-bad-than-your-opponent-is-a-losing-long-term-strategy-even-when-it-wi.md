Smith captures this with the devastating observation that progressivism's only reliable selling point became 'We're not Donald Trump.' While technically sufficient to win some elections, this purely oppositional positioning allowed internal quality to degrade — progressive governance tolerated urban decay, permissive crime policy, and institutional capture because there was no affirmative standard to be measured against, only a negative one. This pattern maps directly to competitive strategy: companies that define themselves primarily as 'not the incumbent' (not Oracle, not Salesforce, not legacy) can win initial market share but eventually hollow out because they lack an internal compass for what good looks like. The strongest competitive positions are built on affirmative value propositions that would remain compelling even if the competitor disappeared.
