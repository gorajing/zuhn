RuBisCo evolved when Earth's atmosphere had minimal oxygen, so its inability to distinguish O2 from CO2 was irrelevant. But as photosynthetic organisms succeeded, they flooded the atmosphere with oxygen — their own byproduct — making RuBisCo's design flaw catastrophic. Plants literally poisoned their own key enzyme's operating environment through success.

This is a precise analogy for companies whose early architectural decisions become liabilities at scale. A startup's database choice, pricing model, or organizational structure optimized for zero-to-one can become the biggest drag during one-to-many. The cruel twist is that success itself changes the environment in ways that undermine the strategy that created it. The antidote is recognizing which early decisions are load-bearing versus which are environmental assumptions that will shift.
