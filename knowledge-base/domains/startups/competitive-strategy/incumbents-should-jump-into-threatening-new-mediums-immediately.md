TV networks and newspapers both made the mistake of grudgingly putting content online while still hoping audiences would come back to the original medium. This defensive posture consistently fails because it prioritizes preserving existing business models over serving customer behavior. By the time incumbents fully commit, insurgents have already captured the audience and built the new distribution infrastructure.

The pattern is universal across disruption cycles: record labels vs. digital music, taxis vs. ridesharing, retail vs. e-commerce. The strategic implication is clear — if a new technology is powerful enough to cause genuine anxiety in your organization, treat that anxiety as a signal to aggressively adopt rather than resist. The companies that survive platform shifts are those that cannibalize their own revenue streams before competitors do it for them.
