China's industrial strategy follows a repeatable pattern: the CCP designates an industry as strategic priority, selects one or two national champions, provides unlimited subsidies, then uses aggressive below-cost pricing until market dynamics shift permanently. This playbook has already captured steel, solar panels, commercial drones, and EVs.

For founders and investors, this means competing head-to-head on cost with Chinese firms in hardware-heavy industries is a losing strategy. The viable counter-strategies are either regulatory moats (tariffs, export controls), moving up the value chain to areas where software and design matter more than manufacturing scale, or focusing on markets where trust and data sovereignty create natural barriers. Understanding this dynamic is essential for any startup considering hardware or clean-tech markets.
