Historical pattern: platform owners either compete with you directly (Microsoft vs Lotus 123) or tax you at variable rates (Facebook changing its take rate at whim). The VisiCalc-to-Lotus-to-Excel progression shows that when a platform owner controls 95% of the market and your product is core to the platform's value prop, you're toast.

The AI era is structurally different: there are 5+ major model providers plus open-source alternatives, meaning no single platform can drown you. The risk shifts from existential (will they build my product?) to economic (will they change pricing?). Additionally, many AI applications orchestrate work across multiple model providers, making single-vendor dependency less likely. The Dan Rose 'gold bricks' insight applies: big companies won't pursue opportunities that are '100 feet away' when they have gold bricks at their feet.
