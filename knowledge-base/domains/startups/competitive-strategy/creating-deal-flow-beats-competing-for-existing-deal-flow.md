Graham distinguishes YC's competitive dynamics from traditional VCs: VCs play a zero-sum game competing for a fixed amount of deal flow, while YC creates new deal flow by encouraging hackers who would otherwise take jobs to start companies instead. This means YC competes more with employers than with other investors.

This is a powerful strategic principle beyond venture capital. Any business that can expand the total market rather than fight for share within it enjoys fundamentally different — and usually superior — economics. The insight also explains why YC's growth doesn't directly threaten VC returns: YC enlarges the pie of fundable companies, which actually benefits later-stage investors who get higher-quality, better-prepared deal flow.
