By offering only one or two options per product category instead of dozens, Costco creates a winner-take-all dynamic among suppliers. Brands compete fiercely to be selected, accepting lower margins and even re-engineering products (like redesigning cashew containers to save 24,000 pallets annually) to win or keep the slot.

This is a counterintuitive strategy: conventional retail wisdom says more selection attracts more customers. But radical curation concentrates purchasing volume into fewer suppliers, creating leverage that translates directly to lower prices. The lesson generalizes beyond retail — any platform or marketplace that curates aggressively can extract better terms from the supply side, as long as the demand side trusts the curation quality. Costco's Kirkland Signature brand (25% of sales) serves as the ultimate leverage tool: if no supplier meets their standards, they just make it themselves.
