In the 1890s, Asa Candler pioneered what may be the first systematic free-trial customer acquisition program. He contacted every pharmacy, obtained their top customer lists, and sent free Coca-Cola coupons. The explicit logic was that a free trial would create 'customers for life' — aided by the drink's addictive chemicals (caffeine, sugar, and initially cocaine).

This strategy worked in concert with another pivotal decision: repositioning Coke from a patent medicine to a refreshing beverage for everyone. By abandoning the shrinking medicinal market (which faced increasing scrutiny) and targeting the universal market of 'anyone who wants a tasty drink,' Candler massively expanded the addressable market. The combination of broad market positioning + free trials + habit-forming ingredients created a growth engine that competitors selling similar products couldn't match. Modern parallels exist in freemium software (habit formation through daily use), social media (dopamine-driven engagement loops), and subscription services (free month trials banking on inertia).
