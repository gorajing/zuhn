The traditional startup playbook of building defensible businesses through proprietary software is breaking down. AI coding tools, open-source proliferation, and rapid feature replication mean any software advantage can be cloned in weeks rather than years. This forces founders to rethink where real moats come from.

The new defensibility stack centers on assets that compound independently of code: proprietary data flywheels, deeply embedded customer workflows, network effects, regulatory capture, or brand trust. Startups that rely solely on technical complexity as their barrier will find that complexity commoditized by AI tooling. The winners will be those who use software as a delivery mechanism for harder-to-replicate advantages like unique data assets or switching-cost-heavy integrations.
