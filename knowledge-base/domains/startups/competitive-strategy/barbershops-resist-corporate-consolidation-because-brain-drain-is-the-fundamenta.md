Regis Corporation assembled 3,000+ locations (SuperCuts, MasterCuts, Cost Cutters) through M&A and blanketed every mall and strip with multiple brands for an illusion of choice. But it failed because barbering has uniquely low barriers to entry — anyone can start with scissors, no formal training required. Brain drain is the fundamental constraint: talented barbers leave chains for independence where they keep 100% of earnings. Regis designed low-end, walk-in concepts specifically to combat this (commoditized haircuts where individual talent doesn't matter), but upscale independents still coexist alongside no-frills shops. The optimal strategy for barbers: find the sweet spot between quality and speed to capture the majority who want 'good enough, fast enough, fair price.'
