Credentials are fundamentally a prediction mechanism—they exist because large organizations cannot cheaply or immediately measure the performance of individual recruits. When the economy consists of many small organizations competing in a market, the market itself acts as the measurement system: it takes every organization and keeps just the good ones, which approaches taking every person and keeping the good ones as organizations shrink.

This explains why startup founders don't care about college pedigree—the market will judge the company directly within months. It also explains why credentialism persists in government and large corporations: they still can't measure individual contribution well. The strategic implication is that any economy wanting to reduce credentialism should focus on enabling small organization formation rather than trying to reform credential-granting institutions.
