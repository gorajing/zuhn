One of the five native advantages NFX identifies for fintech-enabled marketplaces is the ability to subsidize product or marketing through bundling. When a marketplace embeds lending, insurance, or payment services alongside its core transaction, the combined customer lifetime value exceeds what any single-service competitor can achieve.

This LTV surplus can be redirected into customer acquisition, creating a structural advantage: the bundled marketplace can outspend incumbents on acquiring users because each user is worth more. Square exemplifies this by expanding beyond SMB payments into multiple financial services, simultaneously increasing engagement, boosting LTV, and reducing multi-tenanting. For founders, this means the fintech layer isn't just a revenue add-on — it's a weapon for winning the acquisition war against better-funded but narrower competitors.
