Bolt's initial expansion strategy was intuitive: nearby European countries. Launching in 10 cities simultaneously (Netherlands to Finland to Serbia) nearly bankrupted the company. The pivot was radical: throw away geographic biases and rank the world's top few hundred cities by population, availability of alternatives, unemployment rate, and tax/regulatory friendliness for drivers.

African cities dominated the top of the list -- large populations, massive need for transport alternatives, high unemployment (millions eager to join the platform). Despite having no experience in Africa and superficially low income levels, the volume was so vast and economies growing so quickly that the economics worked. Bolt went from zero African presence to 50%+ of business from emerging markets in just 6 months. The lesson: first-principles market analysis often reveals that your best opportunities are where you'd least expect them.
