Graham observed that even the strongest founders in YC's first batch were 'surprisingly frightened' of competitors, comparing the reaction to hypochondria — magnifying threats into monsters when you lack experience. His reframing is direct: if you're currently leading and building faster than anyone else, the number of competitors is irrelevant.

This maps to a broader pattern where startups die from internal causes (not getting enough done) rather than external threats. The mundane failure to ship consistently is deadlier than any competitor. This suggests founders should spend less energy on competitive analysis and more on execution velocity, treating competitor fear as a cognitive distortion to be managed rather than a signal to be acted on.
