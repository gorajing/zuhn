Lenny identifies 20 distinct differentiation strategies ranging from lowest price to story-driven branding. The real insight emerges from the examples: Trader Joe's combines price + quality + service + unique supply. Tesla combines product experience + service + problem-solving + limited supply + delight. No dominant company wins on a single axis.

This framework is useful as both an offensive and defensive tool. Offensively, it helps founders identify which 3-4 axes to combine for a unique position. Defensively, it reveals that competitors who match you on one axis but not your full combination aren't true threats. The alternative path — not differentiating and just scaling faster (Uber vs. Lyft) — is explicitly called out as viable but is a pure execution and capital race with no structural moat.
