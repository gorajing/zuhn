Mercury never stole a customer from Silicon Valley Bank until SVB failed. The canonical AI startup strategy is greenfield over brownfield: target new companies choosing their first system, or existing companies hitting inflection points (like outgrowing QuickBooks and needing multi-entity ERP). Trying to sell 'AI Mailchimp' to existing Mailchimp customers is extremely hard.

However, the dynamic flips for labor-replacement and walled garden businesses. Software that does the job of labor can go after brownfield because the value proposition is fundamentally different — you are not asking someone to switch software, you are offering to handle work they could never economically staff. Incumbents are simultaneously strong in this era because everyone agrees AI is a good idea (unlike cloud, where incumbents dismissed it). The battle is about who captures greenfield faster.
