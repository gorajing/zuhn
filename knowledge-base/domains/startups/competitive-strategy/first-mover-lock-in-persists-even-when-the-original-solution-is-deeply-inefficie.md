RuBisCo is the most abundant protein on Earth despite being catastrophically inefficient — it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts that plants must spend additional energy neutralizing. Plants compensate by simply producing enormous quantities of it rather than evolving a better enzyme.

This mirrors technology markets where first-movers with inferior solutions (QWERTY keyboards, x86 architecture, JavaScript) become so deeply embedded in ecosystem dependencies that the cost of replacement exceeds the cost of working around their flaws. The lesson for competitive strategy: once your solution becomes load-bearing infrastructure that others build on top of, efficiency matters far less than entrenchment. The entire biosphere reorganized around RuBisCo's limitations rather than replacing it — and the same happens with platform technologies.
