Apple Silicon's M1 chip demonstrated that co-locating CPU, GPU, memory controller, ML engine, and I/O on a single die (system-on-chip) consistently outperforms Intel machines where each component is individually more powerful but physically separated on a motherboard. The energy and latency costs of shuttling data between discrete components overwhelm the raw capability advantage of each individual part.

This principle extends beyond chips: startups with tightly integrated teams shipping a unified product often outperform larger competitors with individually stronger but siloed departments. The bottleneck in complex systems is usually communication overhead between components, not the capability of any single component. When evaluating any system architecture — technical or organizational — measure total throughput, not peak component specs.
