Early-stage investors asking 'what if Google builds your thing' is rightly mocked as unsophisticated — it's unanswerable and irrelevant pre-PMF. But there is a definite stage where this becomes the central strategic question. Microsoft Dynamics quietly took CRM customers from Salesforce. Microsoft Teams damaged Slack enough to force an acquisition. AWS had a near-decade monopoly before Azure emerged as a serious competitor. These aren't hypotheticals.

The key insight is that incumbents compete primarily through bundling — Microsoft could default-roll Teams to every Office customer while Slack had to sell, upsell, and contract each deal individually. Founders should reframe this not as a threat but as empowering: competing against big players often means bringing a genuinely better product to market. DoorDash competing against Amazon on product delivery works because Amazon's product discovery has degraded (spam, fake reviews, misleading ads) while DoorDash's local-store fulfillment provides inherent quality guarantees. The incumbents are often more vulnerable than they appear.
