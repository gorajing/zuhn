Veritasium's evolutionary simulations show that in environments with limited resources, the replicators that dominate consistently share the same traits: high replication rate (20% vs 17% average), low death rate, and low mutation rate (1% vs 3.73% average). The winning strategy isn't the most complex or innovative -- it's the one that copies itself most faithfully and most quickly.

This maps directly to competitive strategy: in markets with finite customer attention and budget, the company that can replicate its value proposition fastest (through distribution, content, sales, partnerships) while maintaining quality (low mutation rate = brand consistency) and survival (low death rate = unit economics) will dominate. Growth rate is not just one advantage among many -- it's the dominant one.
