The billionaire retirement paradox has a corporate governance dimension: leaders who refuse to step down often block necessary strategic pivots. They optimize for the business environment they mastered rather than the one that exists. Boards are reluctant to push out iconic founders/leaders even when performance suffers.

The cost of leadership overstay is difficult to measure because it's counterfactual - you can't know what a successor would have achieved. But the pattern of iconic leaders presiding over decline (GE's Welch-era strategies, Boeing's post-merger culture) suggests that succession timing is one of the most impactful and most neglected strategic decisions.
