By offering only one or two choices per category instead of dozens, Costco transforms each product slot into a winner-take-all competition among suppliers. Brands will lower prices and re-engineer products (like redesigning cashew containers to save 24,000 pallets/year) just to be the chosen option in Costco's limited selection.

This is counterintuitive — conventional retail wisdom says more selection attracts more customers. But radical reduction creates a virtuous cycle: fewer SKUs → concentrated volume per item → massive supplier leverage → lower costs → lower prices → higher membership renewal. The constraint also simplifies operations (stocking, logistics, store layout) and reduces decision paralysis for shoppers, turning a limitation into a multi-dimensional advantage.
