Hormozi teaches that differentiation for a new business should be compressed into a single positioning sentence structured as: 'I help [specific avatar] achieve [specific outcome] in [specific timeframe] through [unique mechanism].' The unique mechanism is the critical differentiator — it is your proprietary name for your method, process, or framework that makes your approach feel distinct even if the underlying tactics are similar to competitors.

This works psychologically because it gives prospects a new category to file you under rather than comparing you directly against existing options. When someone says 'I help busy executives lose 20 pounds in 90 days through the Metabolic Reset Protocol,' the named mechanism ('Metabolic Reset Protocol') creates the perception of a proprietary system. This single sentence also serves as a forcing function for clarity: if you cannot fill in all four blanks compellingly, your offer needs more refinement before you start selling.
