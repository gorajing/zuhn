The video presents a hierarchy of control that explains seemingly irrational tech company behavior. At the bottom sit plugins and extensions — fundamentally dependent on a larger company's indifference. Websites sit above, at the mercy of browsers. Apps depend on operating systems. OS depends on hardware. At the top, owning the hardware means owning everything above it.

This framework explains why Amazon sells Kindle and Echo at zero profit, why Microsoft and Amazon both attempted phones despite long odds, and why Facebook has invested billions in VR headsets. These aren't failed product bets — they're strategic moves to reduce existential dependency. A company at a lower level can be destroyed overnight by a decision from the level above (Apple rejecting apps, Google deprioritizing websites). The framework also reveals why the most dangerous competitive threat isn't a better product at your level but a company above you deciding to absorb your functionality — the 'Sherlocking' pattern where OS makers copy popular app features into the platform itself.
