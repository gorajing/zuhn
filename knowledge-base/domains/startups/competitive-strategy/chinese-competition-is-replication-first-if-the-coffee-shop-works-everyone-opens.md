Jin describes a fundamental difference in competitive dynamics: in the US, if someone opens a coffee shop, the neighbor opens a bagel shop (differentiation). In China, if the coffee shop works, everyone opens the same coffee shop (replication). This creates extraordinarily intense competition on execution, price, and speed rather than positioning.

For startups competing with or in Chinese markets, this means defensibility through branding or positioning is nearly worthless. What matters is execution speed, cost structure, and the ability to iterate faster than replicators. This also explains why Chinese companies often win on price globally — they've been forged in a domestic market where identical competition is the norm, not the exception.
