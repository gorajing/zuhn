Sam Harris argues the Democratic Party allowed trans activists (~0.5% of adults) and other far-left factions to define party positioning on cultural issues, costing them millions of votes including record losses among Latinos and Black voters who nominally aligned with Democratic economic interests. The mechanism is that organizations optimize for the loudest internal voices rather than the largest external audience.

This maps directly to startup strategy: companies that let power users or vocal community members dictate product direction often alienate the broader market. The key signal is when your brand becomes associated with positions that even your target customers find alienating. Harris cites polling showing the strongest predictor of swing voters choosing Trump was agreement with 'Harris focused more on cultural issues like transgender issues rather than helping the middle class' — the brand association, not the policy specifics, drove defection.
