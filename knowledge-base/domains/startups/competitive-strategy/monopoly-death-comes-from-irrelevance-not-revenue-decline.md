Paul Graham noticed Microsoft was dead not from their balance sheet but from a young founder's blank stare — the founder simply didn't understand why anyone would fear Microsoft. This reveals a crucial pattern: market dominance ends as a cultural shift before it shows as a financial one. When startups stop designing around a dominant player's moves, stop inviting them to demo days, and stop even thinking about them as competition, the monopoly is functionally over regardless of current revenue. This has direct implications for competitive strategy — monitoring whether new entrants fear you is a more leading indicator than quarterly earnings.
