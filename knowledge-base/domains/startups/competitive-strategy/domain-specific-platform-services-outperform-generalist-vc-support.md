a16z learned that general startup research produced negligible value, while domain-specific efforts — like testing every AI foundation model and publishing comparative findings for portfolio companies — accelerated company timelines by months. The same vertical specialization applies to talent: AI researchers require completely different sourcing pools, compensation structures, and closing strategies than full-stack engineers.

The actionable framework is to identify one domain where your portfolio naturally clusters and build proprietary research or talent maps exclusively for that vertical. This creates a flywheel where deep domain expertise attracts more companies in that vertical, which generates more proprietary data, which deepens the expertise. The critical caveat is that companies must eventually own these capabilities themselves — a16z helped Databricks hire 100+ people early, but the enduring value was Databricks building their own recruiting muscle.
