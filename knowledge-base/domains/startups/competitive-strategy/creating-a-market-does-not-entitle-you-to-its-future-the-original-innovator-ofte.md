GoPro's collapse is a masterclass in the first-mover trap. They created the action camera category and dominated it completely, but that dominance bred complacency. They believed they could set the tempo because they owned the market, so they launched 20 models in 10 years. But improvements between generations became trivial, the use case never expanded beyond extreme sports, and there was no reason to buy the latest at full price.

When GoPro tried to diversify (media business, failed drone), they executed poorly and squandered capital, momentum, and credibility. Insta360 studied all of this and did the opposite: started in an adjacent niche, invested in software over hardware, expanded the use case beyond extreme sports, and maintained disciplined product releases. The market creator provided the roadmap of what not to do, and the fast follower used it. Even if you create the market, you are not entitled to its future.
