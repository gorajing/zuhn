Otwell uses a brilliant music analogy: if Metallica starts catering to Taylor Swift fans, they lose their core fans. If Swift changes her sound, she loses hers. The same applies to software products. Sideline critics who shout suggestions will always dangle another carrot—'oh thanks for that, but I also don't like this'—and never actually become users.

Laravel grew from a few downloads per day to a quarter million by serving its core community, not by chasing every critic. The lesson: identify your core users, understand what they love, and double down on it. Smart people will always try to tell you how to change your product. Following their advice can be a trap if they're not representative of your actual users.
