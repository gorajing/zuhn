The auto industry's core problem isn't technology - it's affordability. Americans can't responsibly afford car ownership but also can't afford not to own cars in car-centric infrastructure. The industry responded to this squeeze by building more expensive vehicles with higher margins rather than affordable alternatives.

This reveals a broader market failure: when an industry becomes simultaneously essential and unaffordable, technological innovation becomes irrelevant to the core problem. Electric vehicles, autonomous driving, and connected cars are solutions to problems that matter less than the basic question of whether people can afford to buy them.
