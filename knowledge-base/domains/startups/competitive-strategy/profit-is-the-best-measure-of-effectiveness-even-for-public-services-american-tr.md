US transit agencies serve fewer riders than international counterparts yet cost more, with annual operating losses in the billions. In contrast, Tokyo Metro, Berlin BVG, and Hong Kong MTR operate profitably — they publish annual reports, share progress, and treat profit as a forcing function for efficiency. Profitable systems invest surpluses in upgrades and staff bonuses; unprofitable American systems burn through billions and ask for more. The argument that 'transit is a public service, not a business' fails when every world-class system treats it as both. Systemic corruption in US transit is documented across agencies and decades.
