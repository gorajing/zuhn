In any industry where large incumbents hold massive datasets and distribution (real estate marketplaces, mortgage platforms, construction software), AI improvements will make their existing platforms incrementally better. Startups cannot win this incremental game.

The winning startup strategy is to skip the copilot phase entirely and deliver autopilot-level complete solutions. Rather than helping a lawyer write demand letters faster, generate the entire letter. Rather than helping architects analyze floorplans, generate complete as-built drawings without a site walk. This works because incumbents are structurally unable to eliminate the workflows they built their businesses around — they can't cannibalize their own value chain. The race becomes: can you reach distribution faster than incumbents can innovate past incremental improvement?
