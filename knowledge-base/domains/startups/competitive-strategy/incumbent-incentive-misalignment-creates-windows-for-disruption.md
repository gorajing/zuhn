Graham identifies a crucial mechanism behind incumbent failure: when leaders are driven by annual bonuses rather than equity, they maximize this year's revenue by squeezing existing business models harder rather than investing in adaptation. Building new things takes too long for bonus cycles, so they lobby, litigate, and enforce instead of innovate.

This insight is directly actionable for founders evaluating competitive landscapes. When you see an industry where incumbents are aggressively litigating or lobbying against technological change, examine their executive compensation structure. If leadership is bonus-driven, the company is structurally incapable of adapting — they'll keep extracting until disrupted. This creates a reliable signal for where startup opportunities exist: industries where technology has shifted but incumbents' incentive structures prevent them from following.
