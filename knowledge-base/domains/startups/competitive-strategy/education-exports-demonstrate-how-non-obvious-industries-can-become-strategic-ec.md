Australia hosts 875,000 international students in a country of 25 million — 1 in every 28 people is an international student. Chinese students alone contribute over $10 billion annually. This isn't accidental; it's the result of positioning quality anglophone universities within geographic and timezone proximity to the world's fastest-growing middle class demand.

The startup parallel is powerful: the most defensible business advantages often come from serving a need that others don't recognize as a market. Australian universities aren't just educational institutions — they're export businesses with sticky revenue, high margins on international fees, and network effects (alumni clusters create demand pipelines). Identifying where your existing assets can serve an adjacent, underserved demand — especially one others categorize differently — is a repeatable strategic pattern.
