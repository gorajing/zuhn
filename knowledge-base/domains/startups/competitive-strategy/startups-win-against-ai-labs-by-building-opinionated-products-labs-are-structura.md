Despite dozens of consumer product launches from OpenAI and Google in 2025 — group chat, Pulse, Atlas, Sora social, Stitch, Gems, Opal, Doppel — almost none gained traction beyond Notebook LM. The a16z team attributes this to a structural problem: at big companies, mid-career PMs get promoted by building safe features that extend core metrics, not by shipping risky opinionated products that might fail or create legal/compliance headaches.

This structural inability to be opinionated is a durable startup advantage. Startups also benefit from being multi-model (labs are definitionally first-party only), not having compute allocation tensions (labs must split between training and inference), and being able to build specialized interfaces rather than stuffing everything into a chat box. The critical caveat: startups must differentiate beyond text-in/text-out, where the big labs' frequency advantage is nearly unbeatable. Opinionated visual tools, workflow-specific UIs, and multi-modal products are where startups can steal users.
