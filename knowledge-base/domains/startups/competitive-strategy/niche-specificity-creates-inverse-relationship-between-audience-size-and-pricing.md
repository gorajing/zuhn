Hormozi demonstrates a concrete pricing ladder: generic 'time management' sells as a $19 PDF, 'time management for salespeople' becomes a $199 product, 'time management for outbound salespeople' hits $1,999, and 'outbound time management system for garden and power tools reps' commands $10,000. This 500x price increase comes from three compounding effects: the buyer's certainty that the solution is built exactly for their situation, the elimination of competition (who else serves that specific niche?), and the buyer's ability to calculate concrete ROI against their specific revenue numbers. The counterintuitive lesson is that a smaller addressable market can yield higher total revenue because conversion rates and prices both increase faster than the audience shrinks. This is the 'blue ocean' effect — you're the only boat in a pool full of drowning prospects. For broke entrepreneurs, this is especially powerful because you don't need thousands of customers; you need 5-10 customers paying premium prices.
