Multi-tenanting—where buyers and sellers use multiple competing platforms simultaneously—is the silent killer of marketplace defensibility. When switching costs are low, users discover on one platform and transact on another (or offline) to avoid take rates.

By embedding financial services like insurance, financing, or payments directly into the transaction, marketplaces make themselves structurally necessary rather than merely convenient. Airbnb's $1M host insurance policy wasn't just a feature—it was a lock-in mechanism that made hosting through Airbnb fundamentally safer than listing elsewhere. Lyft owns its own insurance entity for the same reason. The financial layer becomes the moat, not just the matching algorithm.
