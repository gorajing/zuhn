The conventional wisdom is that competing against a dominant incumbent is suicidal, but this framing misses a crucial nuance: if the incumbent hasn't solved the core technical problem that would unlock the next order of magnitude in value, the market is still contestable. SpaceX revolutionized launch costs with partial reusability but hasn't achieved full reusability — the breakthrough that would drop costs to ~$100/kg and unlock exponential company creation in space.

This pattern generalizes beyond space. In any market where the incumbent's solution is a 'stopgap' rather than a complete solution to the fundamental problem, there's room for a focused competitor who can solve the remaining piece. The key is identifying that the unsolved piece is genuinely the binding constraint on market growth, not just a nice-to-have improvement. Stoke Space exemplifies this: they didn't try to out-SpaceX SpaceX on existing capabilities — they attacked the specific unsolved problem of second-stage reusability.
