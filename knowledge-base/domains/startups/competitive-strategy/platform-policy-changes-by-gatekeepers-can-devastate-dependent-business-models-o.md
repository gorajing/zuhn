Apple's decision to require opt-in ad tracking on iOS didn't just inconvenience Meta — it fundamentally degraded the precision of Facebook's ad targeting, which was the core engine of its business model. This contributed directly to Meta missing earnings forecasts and the subsequent historic crash.

This is a structural lesson for any company whose revenue depends on another company's platform: your business model exists at the pleasure of your platform provider. Meta had over $100B in annual ad revenue and it still couldn't protect itself from a single policy change by Apple. For startups, this means building on someone else's platform (iOS, Android, AWS, etc.) requires explicit contingency planning for the scenario where the platform changes rules against you. The strongest competitive moats are ones you control end-to-end.
