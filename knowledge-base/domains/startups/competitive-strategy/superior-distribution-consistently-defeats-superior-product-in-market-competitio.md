Investors and founders alike observe that companies with solid products regularly get beaten by competitors with superior distribution. This isn't an edge case — it's a consistent pattern across industries. The reason is structural: product advantages are increasingly ephemeral (especially in AI where capabilities converge quickly), while distribution advantages compound over time through network effects, community trust, and channel ownership.

This reframes how founders should allocate their time and resources. Rather than pouring all energy into product differentiation, the winning strategy is to achieve product parity and then invest disproportionately in distribution. The moat isn't your feature set — it's your ability to consistently reach and convert customers through channels your competitors can't easily replicate. This is why investors now prioritize go-to-market strategy evaluation alongside product assessment.
