The traditional sequence—build the technology, then figure out how to sell it—is the most expensive possible approach to product development. World-class teams run technology R&D and business R&D (customer desirability, business viability) in parallel, iterating fast between capabilities and customer validation.

This principle applies beyond startups. Established industrial companies like Siemens and Honeywell regularly build sophisticated solutions that nobody wants to buy because they skipped the cheap validation step. The whole point of developing great value propositions is that you test and iterate fast in the cheapest possible way before committing to the expensive engineering work.
