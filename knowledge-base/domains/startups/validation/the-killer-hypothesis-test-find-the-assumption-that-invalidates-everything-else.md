Entrepreneurs naturally gravitate toward testing what's easy or what they're excited about building. But hypothesis prioritization should follow a strict dependency chain: if customers don't care about the problem, it doesn't matter whether you can build the solution or deliver it profitably.

The Assumptions Map framework makes this explicit — you extract hypotheses from your value proposition and business model, then rank them by how much risk they carry. The hypothesis at the top isn't the one you're least sure about in isolation; it's the one that, if falsified, renders every downstream assumption moot. This is the scientific method applied to entrepreneurship: you don't test peripheral predictions before the core theory.
