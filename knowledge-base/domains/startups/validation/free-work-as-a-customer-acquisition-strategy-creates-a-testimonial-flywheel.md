The conventional wisdom is that giving away work for free devalues your offering. Hormozi flips this: free work at the start is a deliberate investment in social proof. Each free client becomes a testimonial source, a referral channel, and a case study. The testimonials reduce the trust gap for the next prospect, making each subsequent sale easier than the last.

This creates a flywheel: free work → testimonials → easier paid sales → more testimonials → higher prices. The critical nuance is that this only works if you explicitly ask for testimonials and referrals in exchange for the free work. Without that agreement upfront, you're just doing charity. The free period should be time-bounded and framed as a founding member deal, not an indefinite offer.
