The quality of business model innovation depends directly on the quality of evidence collected from the market. When compliance processes prevent teams from talking directly to customers or putting prototypes in front of real users, the evidence they gather is necessarily filtered, delayed, and less actionable.

This creates a hidden cost that rarely appears in corporate innovation metrics. Teams may still produce deliverables and check boxes, but the insights drawn from compliance-constrained research are fundamentally weaker. The gap between 'we surveyed customers through an approved channel after 6 months' and 'we had 20 direct conversations this week' is enormous in terms of signal quality and iteration speed.
