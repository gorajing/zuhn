Todd Jackson's 'four P's' framework (persona, problem, promise, product) gives founders a structured diagnostic when PMF stalls. The ordering matters: most founders instinctively change the product when stuck, but often the real issue is targeting the wrong persona, solving the wrong problem, or positioning with the wrong promise. The framework forces founders to check upstream assumptions before investing engineering effort.

This connects to a broader pattern in startup failure — building the right thing for the wrong person, or the right thing with the wrong framing. Examples like Vanta, Lattice, and Persona all navigated Level 1 by getting the persona and problem right before optimizing product. The four P's framework is essentially a structured pivot decision tree that prevents the common failure mode of endless product iteration without questioning foundational assumptions.
