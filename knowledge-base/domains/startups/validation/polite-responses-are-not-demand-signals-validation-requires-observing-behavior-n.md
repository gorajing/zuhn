Founders routinely mistake polite encouragement for validation. Someone nodding along and saying 'that's interesting' creates a false sense of progress that can sustain months of misguided building. The Mom Test framework exists specifically to counter this: ask questions that reveal truth rather than comfort.

The fix is structural, not motivational. Replace opinion-seeking conversations with behavior-observing experiments. Can you get someone to sign up, pay a deposit, or switch from their current solution? These actions have friction, which is exactly what makes them reliable signals. The gap between stated intent and actual behavior is one of the most expensive blind spots in early-stage startups.
