Graham relays that Sequoia partner Greg McAdoo specifically looks for 'proxy for demand': what are people doing right now, using inadequate tools, that demonstrates they need what you're building? This is fundamentally different from the typical pitch approach of citing market size numbers ('$5 billion TAM').

The proxy-for-demand framework works because it provides concrete behavioral evidence rather than abstract projections. Graham compares market-size pitches to a beginning actor telling his parents how much Tom Hanks makes — technically true but irrelevant to the actual challenge. The strongest form of this evidence is the founder's own need (Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all grew from founder needs), followed by needs of people you know personally. This hierarchy maps to credibility: the closer the evidence is to direct experience, the harder it is to fake and the more investors trust it.
