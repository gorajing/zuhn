Most founders fail at product-market fit because they jump to their solution too quickly. The PM-Fit Logic Template prescribes a rigid sequencing: define one specific customer segment, articulate their job to be done, identify the obstacle, map dominant alternatives and their shortcomings, and quantify consequences — all before mentioning your own product.

This inverted structure works because by the time you introduce your solution, it feels like the obvious next step rather than a pitch. The 80/20 split between problem and solution forces intellectual honesty about whether a real, painful, high-priority problem exists. Investors respond to this because they're tired of decks that jump to features without establishing who cares and why.
