Despite having sober expectations about launch awareness, Standard Capital was surprised by overwhelming application volume -- Dalton had to schedule three times as much reading time as planned. The demand came from pure word-of-mouth among founders who were excited about a genuinely different process.

This validates a pattern they'd coached thousands of founders on at YC: launch expectations are usually too high, and it takes repeated launches for awareness to build. But in Standard Capital's case, the product (a better Series A process) was addressing such acute pain in a well-connected community that organic spread vastly exceeded expectations. When you fix something genuinely broken for a tight-knit community, the launch problem solves itself.
