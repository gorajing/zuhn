Despite these being organizations that actively use Business Model Canvas and Lean Startup methodologies, the self-assessments revealed that scaling testing practices was one of the weakest areas across the board. Only one organization reported meaningful experience in this dimension.

This gap is significant because testing is the mechanism that converts innovation theater into actual validated learning. Organizations can spread canvas usage and design thinking skills more easily because those are primarily communication and ideation tools. But systematically testing business hypotheses requires operational changes — budget allocation for experiments, tolerance for failure, metrics infrastructure, and decision-making frameworks that act on test results. The testing gap suggests most corporate innovation programs plateau at the ideation stage without building the experimental infrastructure needed to validate ideas.
