The scientific community pursued two entirely different methods to redefine the kilogram: (1) counting silicon atoms in a perfect sphere to fix Avogadro's constant, and (2) using a Watt Balance to fix Planck's constant. Neither approach alone would be sufficient — each provides a check on the other. Only when both converge within ~20 micrograms of agreement would the redefinition proceed.

This is a powerful validation pattern for startups and product development: instead of perfecting one signal (e.g., only user interviews, or only A/B tests), pursue two fundamentally different approaches to the same question. If they converge, confidence is high. If they diverge, you've discovered something important about your assumptions.

The key insight is that the two approaches must be genuinely independent — they derive from different physical constants and use different experimental apparatus. Two variations of the same method don't provide the same epistemic guarantee as two truly orthogonal approaches.
