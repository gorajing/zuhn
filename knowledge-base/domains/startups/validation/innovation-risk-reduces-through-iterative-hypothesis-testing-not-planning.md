At the start of any innovation initiative, risk and uncertainty are at their maximum. The instinct to reduce this through more analysis, bigger plans, or consensus-building is a trap — these activities feel productive but don't actually reduce uncertainty about whether customers want what you're building or whether the business model works.

The alternative is to identify the riskiest assumptions embedded in your idea, convert them into testable hypotheses, and design cheap experiments to gather real evidence. Each experiment either builds evidence toward desirability, feasibility, and viability — or reveals that a pivot is needed before more resources are committed. This experimental approach transforms innovation from a high-stakes bet into a managed learning process.
