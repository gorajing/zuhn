A common A/B testing failure mode is measuring the wrong behavior. The article highlights that high sign-up counts (a call-to-action) do not validate willingness to pay (a hypothesis). This mismatch produces confident but wrong conclusions — teams believe they have validated demand when they have only validated curiosity.

The remedy is to design calls-to-action that are isomorphic to the hypothesis. If you are testing willingness to pay, the CTA must involve a financial commitment (even a fake checkout). If you are testing feature preference, the CTA must involve feature engagement, not just page visits. This discipline prevents the most common source of false positives in lean experimentation.
