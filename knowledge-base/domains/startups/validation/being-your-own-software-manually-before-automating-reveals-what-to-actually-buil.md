Stripe delivered 'instant' merchant accounts by having founders manually sign up merchants behind the scenes. Viaweb built online stores for merchants by hand to learn how the software should work. This 'be your software' approach lets you launch faster and builds deep understanding of the problem space that you can't get any other way.

The insight is that manual execution before automation is not a compromise — it's a superior product development methodology. When you finally do automate, you know exactly what to build because you've done it yourself hundreds of times. The feedback loop is near-instantaneous: you discover missing features in the middle of doing the work, implement them immediately, and continue. This is fundamentally different from speculative product development where you guess what users need. It would be frightening to solve users' problems manually, but far less frightening than the more common case of having something automatic that doesn't solve anyone's problems.
