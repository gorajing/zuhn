The Business Model Canvas organizes its 9 blocks into four validation dimensions: Desirability (customer segments, value props, channels, relationships), Viability (revenue streams, cost structure), Feasibility (key resources, activities, partnerships), and Adaptability (industry forces, market forces, key trends, macroeconomic forces). Most startups obsess over desirability—product-market fit—while treating the other three as afterthoughts.

The adaptability dimension is especially undervalued. A business model that works today but can't survive regulatory shifts, technology changes, or macroeconomic disruption is a ticking time bomb. Tesla's model works not just because people want electric cars, but because it's positioned to benefit from tightening emissions regulations and declining battery costs. Founders should stress-test their models against all four dimensions before committing resources.
