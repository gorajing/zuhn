Business plans and business model canvases serve fundamentally different purposes. A canvas is a prototype for exploring whether a model could work; a plan is an implementation document for a model you've proven works. Writing a plan first means spending months refining the execution details of assumptions you haven't tested.

The practical workflow is: iterate through dozens or hundreds of one-page canvases, test the riskiest assumptions in each, and only write a business plan once you have market evidence that a particular model works. This reframes the business plan from a starting document to a graduation document—you earn the right to write one by surviving canvas-stage validation.
