Graham identifies medium selection as a powerful lever for getting through the ugly-duckling phase. When you're drawing in a notebook, it's easy to tell yourself it's just a sketch; when you're carving stone, every mark feels permanent. Digital media's advantage isn't just technical—it's psychological, because low upfront commitment makes it safe to produce bad early work.

John Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this: Quake's crude technical constraints meant quick gameplay hacks weren't far from the official product, launching many careers. When commercial quality rose, modding became prohibitively expensive. Minecraft and Roblox reversed this by making crudeness the aesthetic, so innovative gameplay concepts became the primary value. The principle generalizes: any tool or medium that closes the gap between early-stage and final-stage work removes a psychological barrier to starting.
