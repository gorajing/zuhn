Graham's core methodological insight is that YC functions as a natural experiment in talent evaluation because the feedback loop is tight: invest based on founder assessment, observe market outcome within a year. This is fundamentally different from large organizations where individual contribution is hard to isolate and career outcomes unfold over decades, allowing credential bias to persist unchallenged.

This has implications beyond hiring. Any domain where feedback loops are slow and diffuse will tend to overweight proxies (credentials, brand names, social proof) over substance. Founders building evaluation systems — whether for talent, technology, or strategy — should deliberately seek environments or create mechanisms that compress feedback loops to expose which signals are genuinely predictive versus merely comfortable.
