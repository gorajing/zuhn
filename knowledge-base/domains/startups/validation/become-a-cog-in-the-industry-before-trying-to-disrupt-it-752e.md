Adora Cheung literally got a job at a cleaning company before building Homejoy. The training itself was useful, but the real payoff was understanding why local cleaning companies couldn't scale — their booking, scheduling, and operations were deeply inefficient in ways only visible from inside.

This contradicts the common advice that disruption requires being an outsider unconstrained by industry norms. The truth is more nuanced: you need outsider thinking combined with insider knowledge. A brief immersion (weeks, not decades) gives you the operational details — cost structures, workflow bottlenecks, customer pain points — that let you identify where technology can create 10x improvements rather than incremental ones.
