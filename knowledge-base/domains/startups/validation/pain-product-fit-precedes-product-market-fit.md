The authors distinguish 'pain-product fit' from product-market fit as a prerequisite milestone. Pain-product fit means you've confirmed through direct customer conversations that your solution addresses a mission-critical organizational pain — not just a nice-to-have. Without this, you're either selling very little, selling tomorrow's churn, or burning cash on high CAC with slow LTV.

The practical test is looking ICP customers in the eye, asking them not to lie, and posing well-designed questions about whether they'd invest in your solution. The authors note they're consistently staggered by how many founders skip this step entirely — comparing it to releasing pharmaceuticals without clinical trials. This validation-first approach can compress the typical 3-year PMF search and save millions in wasted spend.
