Harvard Innovation Labs developed a comprehensive 91-slide framework covering the path from $0 to $1M in B2B revenue and product-market fit. The core insight is that PMF is not a light switch—it's a gradient with measurable checkpoints including initial customer discovery, first paying customer, repeatable sales motion, and scalable acquisition.

Founders who internalize this graduated model make better resource allocation decisions at each stage. Pre-PMF spending on growth is the most common way startups die, while under-investing in distribution post-PMF leaves money on the table. The framework helps founders correctly diagnose which stage they're actually in rather than prematurely declaring PMF based on early enthusiasm or vanity metrics.
