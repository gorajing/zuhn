Generic encouragement is easy to discount and eventually becomes noise. Graham argues that the critical support structure for ambitious projects is not cheerleaders but fellow practitioners — people working on their own similarly ambitious projects who have the domain expertise to recognize genuine promise in rough early work. This is why university departments, research labs, and startup ecosystems like YC work: they concentrate people who can provide calibrated optimism rather than empty support.

The implication for founders is that seeking out 'supportive' people is the wrong optimization. The right optimization is finding people whose judgment you trust enough that their encouragement carries signal. These are almost always people doing similar work themselves, because only they have the pattern-matching ability to distinguish a genuinely promising ugly duckling from a project that's just ugly.
