Healthcare and deep-tech ventures default to optimizing technology risk because that's where founders' expertise lies. Scientists are comfortable asking 'how will the body metabolize this?' but rarely ask 'who will pay for this and how will it be adopted?' BioCity found that too many projects absorbed enormous funding while carrying fundamental flaws in market assumptions about adoption, usefulness, and business model viability.

The shift toward stratified medicine and companion diagnostics means there are no more single blockbuster cures — making market risk validation even more critical. The era of 'if you cure cancer, market risk doesn't matter' is over. Ventures must now understand end users, payers, adoption pathways, and how their solution interacts with existing treatments. BioCity addresses this by forcing early-stage companies through a structured 12-week program that systematically challenges business model assumptions with real customer evidence.
