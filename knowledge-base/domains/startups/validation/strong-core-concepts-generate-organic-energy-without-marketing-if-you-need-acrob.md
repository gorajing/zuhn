Raphael Ouzan described feeling 'incredible energy even in the Zoom rooms' when early teams came together on A.Team. This wasn't manufactured excitement — it was the natural response of talented people finally getting what they'd been missing. The NFX essay frames this as a validation principle: if you need marketing acrobatics to know your concept is working, it probably isn't.

This is a useful corrective to the lean startup orthodoxy of running endless A/B tests and measuring conversion funnels. For marketplace concepts specifically, the right core idea creates a self-perpetuating cycle almost immediately. Supply gets excited because they can finally work the way they want. Demand gets excited because they can access talent they couldn't before. That mutual excitement is observable and unmistakable. The caveat is that this applies specifically to the core concept validation stage — scaling still requires rigorous measurement and iteration.
