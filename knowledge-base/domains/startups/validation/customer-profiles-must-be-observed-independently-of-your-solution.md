When building customer profiles, teams instinctively filter customer jobs, pains, and gains through the lens of their existing product or idea. This creates a confirmation-bias loop where you only see problems your solution already addresses, missing the deeper motivations that actually drive purchasing decisions.

The fix is to approach customer research like an anthropologist — deliberately set aside your solution and use techniques like the Five Whys to uncover root motivations. This separation is structural, not just attitudinal: the Value Proposition Canvas physically divides the customer profile (circle) from the value map (square) precisely because what you observe about customers and what you design into your product are fundamentally different activities requiring different mindsets.
