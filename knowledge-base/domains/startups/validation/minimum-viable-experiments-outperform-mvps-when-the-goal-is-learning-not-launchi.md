The article distinguishes a Minimum Viable Experiment (MVE) from an MVP by flipping the question from 'What can we launch?' to 'What can we learn?' MVE tactics — landing pages, fake-door tests, concierge prototypes — validate demand without writing full product code. This is especially powerful when time or funding is limited.

The key insight is that many MVPs fail not because they ship too little, but because they ship without a clear hypothesis. Teams over-engineer products that don't answer the right questions. An MVE forces clarity on what you're actually testing before you invest in building, preventing the common trap of consuming resources without yielding learning.
