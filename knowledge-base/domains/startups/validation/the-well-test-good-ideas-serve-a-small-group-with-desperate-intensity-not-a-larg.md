Graham introduces a powerful visual framework: plot all potential users on the x-axis and intensity of need on the y-axis, then invert. Companies are holes in this surface. Startups must choose between digging broad and shallow (many people, mild interest) or narrow and deep (few people, desperate need). Nearly all good startup ideas are narrow and deep — 'wells.'

The critical validation question becomes: 'Who wants this so much that they'll use a crappy version one made by a two-person startup they've never heard of?' If you can't name those people, the idea is probably bad. Facebook started with just Harvard students who wanted it intensely. Microsoft started with a few thousand Altair owners who had no alternative. The well must also have a path outward — Facebook could expand from Harvard to all colleges to everyone — but the starting point must be deep, not broad.
