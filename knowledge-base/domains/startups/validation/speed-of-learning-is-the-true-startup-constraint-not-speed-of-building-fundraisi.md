Maurya's core thesis inverts the common startup narrative: the constraint was never building speed, and AI making building faster actually makes the real constraint more visible. What separates winners from the noise is contrarian insights about customers that nobody else sees.

Facebook wasn't the first social network. Google wasn't the first search engine. Tesla didn't build the first electric car. They won because their founders uncovered secrets about what customers wanted but didn't know how to get. The same applies to AI startups — winners won't have the best AI technology, they'll have the deepest understanding of customer problems that AI can uniquely solve. Strategic differentiation through customer insight is the only durable advantage when everyone has access to the same AI building tools.
