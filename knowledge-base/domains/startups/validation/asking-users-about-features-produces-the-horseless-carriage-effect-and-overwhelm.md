Shear warns that asking about features creates a dangerous trap: a real person with a real problem asking for a specific feature is nearly impossible to say no to. But this produces the 'horseless carriage effect' — users describe incremental improvements to their current workflow rather than revealing the underlying problem that could lead to a transformative solution.

The antidote is to stay as far away from features as possible during early interviews. Instead, focus entirely on current behavior: how do you do X today? What tools do you use? What's frustrating? This surfaces the problem space without anchoring on solutions. Twitch's broadcasters asked for polls, scrolling text, and ban list management — none of which Twitch built. Instead, Twitch addressed the underlying goals: making money (subscription program), video stability, and global access.
