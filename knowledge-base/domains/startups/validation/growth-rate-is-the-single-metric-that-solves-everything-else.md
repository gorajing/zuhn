Seibel is emphatic: for typical Silicon Valley investors, growth is the number one metric for determining whether you'll raise money. Not team, not past experience, not fancy investors — growth. He repeats this across fundraising, PR, and operations sections because it's the universal solvent.

Having growth means investors find you instead of the reverse. It means press wants to write about you. It means top talent wants to join. It means your fundraising meetings have urgency and FOMO. Seibel's tactical advice flows from this: schedule all investor meetings in one compressed week (to create FOMO), get press coverage (so investors hear about you from others), and above all, show consistent weekly or monthly growth numbers. The growth rate is simultaneously the proof that you've built something people want and the leverage that makes everything else possible.
