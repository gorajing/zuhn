NFX explicitly encourages founders to ask: 'How long would it take for a user to do the same task as your core product, with ChatGPT+?' This is a powerful validation heuristic because it forces founders to articulate what their product does that a general-purpose model cannot. If the answer is 'about the same time,' the startup has no moat.

This framework is time-sensitive because the capabilities of generalist LLMs are rapidly expanding. A product that passes the litmus test today may fail it in 12 months as foundation models improve. The implication for founders is that defensibility must be continuously re-evaluated, and the strongest positions combine specialized training data, workflow integration, and domain-specific UX — not just model performance.
