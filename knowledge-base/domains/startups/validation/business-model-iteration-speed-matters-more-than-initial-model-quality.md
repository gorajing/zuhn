The case study describes a team that iterated their business model four times in 12 weeks using structured innovation sprints, ultimately delivering an evidence-based presentation to their project sponsor. This speed was enabled by having access to pre-built e-learning modules, workshops, and structured workflows rather than designing processes from scratch.

This reinforces a core lean startup principle but adds a structural dimension: iteration speed is not just about mindset but about having reusable toolkits — experiment libraries, progress mapping templates, and milestone frameworks. The infrastructure for iteration matters as much as the willingness to iterate. Teams without this scaffolding default to slower cycles because each iteration requires reinventing the testing methodology.
