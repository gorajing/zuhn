The video explicitly calls oxygen 'the most useful useless byproduct in the history of useless byproducts.' Plants split water molecules to replenish lost electrons — oxygen is just waste. Yet this waste product enabled the entire aerobic ecosystem, including every animal that has ever lived. The byproduct became more consequential than the intended product (glucose for the plant).

For startups, this is the platform insight: your core product serves your direct customers, but the byproducts of your process — data exhaust, APIs, community, infrastructure — may enable entirely new ecosystems worth more than the original product. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure byproduct. Google's ad platform emerged from search infrastructure. When evaluating your business, audit what you're throwing away. Your 'oxygen' might be someone else's atmosphere.
