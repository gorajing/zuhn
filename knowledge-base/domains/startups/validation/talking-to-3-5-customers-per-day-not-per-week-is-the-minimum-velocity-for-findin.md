Kim is 'still surprised' that founders talk to only 3-5 customers per week. His standard: 3-5 customer conversations per day, with commitments to follow up within 24 hours on any request, and feature shipping within a week on 2-week sprint cycles. This creates a tight feedback loop where evening customer calls become next-day builds.

The failure pattern he identifies: founders with engineering backgrounds over-index on building (spending time on product before validating with customers), while outward-facing founders over-index on networking (attending conferences and speaking events instead of building strong product). Both types neglect the core loop. Kim's directive: deprioritize everything except talking to customers and building product until PMF is found. Nothing else matters in the early days.
