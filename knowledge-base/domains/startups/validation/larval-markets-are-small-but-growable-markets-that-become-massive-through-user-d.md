Paul Graham introduces the concept of 'larval markets' — markets that are small but contain the seeds of massive growth. Apple in 1976, Facebook at Harvard, and Google's early web search all started as things a tiny group wanted that eventually everyone would want.

The key insight is that evaluating startup ideas by current market size is misleading. What matters is whether there's a path from the small initial market to a huge one. The best founders are 'living in the future' — at the leading edge of some change — and building for themselves and their peers. Their personal need is the larval form of mass demand. This reframes market analysis from 'how big is this now?' to 'what change is making this grow?'
