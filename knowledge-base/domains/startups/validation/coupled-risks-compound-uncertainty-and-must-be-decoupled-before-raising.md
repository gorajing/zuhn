Not all risks are independent. A single founder magnifies both execution and hiring risk simultaneously. Unproven demand can mask poor product fit, making both layers appear unresolved at once. These coupled risks are dangerous because they create non-linear uncertainty — if one fails, the other collapses too. Experienced investors specifically look for coupling during due diligence and will either reduce check size or pass entirely when they find tightly bound risks. The practical implication is that founders should map risk dependencies before fundraising and prioritize decoupling the most dangerous pairs. Proving you can separate team risk from product risk (by shipping with a complete founding team) or market risk from economic risk (by showing retention before optimizing unit economics) is often more valuable than peeling any single layer in isolation.
