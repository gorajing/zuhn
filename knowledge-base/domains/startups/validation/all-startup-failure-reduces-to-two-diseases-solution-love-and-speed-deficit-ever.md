Misti Cain's framework is deliberately reductive: every failure autopsy she's seen as an investor and mentor traces back to either building a solution the founder loved rather than solving a problem the market had, or moving too slowly to discover the mismatch. Co-founder conflict, funding failure, and recession impact are symptoms that compound these two root causes, not independent failure modes.

This is a powerful counterweight to complex post-mortem narratives. When founders list 15 reasons their startup failed, Cain's framework forces a harder question: did you fall in love with your solution, or did you move too slowly to learn it was wrong? The Butterfield example is key — he failed twice with game solutions he loved before accidentally discovering Flickr and Slack from problems his teams actually had.
