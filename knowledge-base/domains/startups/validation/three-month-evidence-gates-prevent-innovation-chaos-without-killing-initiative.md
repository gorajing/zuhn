A common objection to democratizing innovation is that it creates chaos — too many projects, no focus, wasted resources. Strategyzer's answer is an evidence-based investment process: anyone can start, but within three months innovators must demonstrate evidence that customers actually face the problem they're solving. No evidence, project terminated. This combines startup speed and flexibility with corporate structure and accountability.

This approach solves two problems simultaneously. First, it removes the political gatekeeping that kills good ideas before they're tested — you don't need executive sponsorship to start. Second, it prevents zombie projects that persist on enthusiasm alone without market validation. The three-month window is short enough to limit resource waste but long enough for meaningful customer discovery. It's essentially lean startup methodology formalized as corporate policy, with the crucial addition of a hard kill criterion rather than the ambiguous 'pivot or persevere' decision that often lets weak projects linger.
