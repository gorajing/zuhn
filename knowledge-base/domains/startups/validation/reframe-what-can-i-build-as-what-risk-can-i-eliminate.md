The default entrepreneurial impulse — 'what can I build?' — smuggles in a dangerous assumption: that building is the right next step. It anchors you on solution space before you've adequately explored problem space and risk space.

The reframe to 'how do I break this into smaller chunks of risk' is a fundamentally different cognitive operation. It forces you to articulate what you're actually uncertain about, separate those uncertainties into independent testable claims, and then find the cheapest possible way to resolve each one. Many of the most important business risks can be tested without building anything at all — through interviews, landing pages, letters of intent, or market data analysis.
