The water company analogy illustrates a subtle trap: even commodities with universal demand require distribution channels and differentiation to grow. Many founders conflate PMF with traction, but PMF only means the product is wanted — you still need efficient channels to reach buyers. The practical implication is that PMF validation and go-to-market planning should run in parallel, not sequentially. Founders should begin experimenting with acquisition channels before declaring PMF, so they can invest capital efficiently the moment demand is confirmed.
