One of Graham's most practical techniques for surviving early work is reframing the entire endeavor as a way to learn rather than a way to produce. Under this frame, even if the project truly fails, you've gained knowledge — and if the problem is sharply defined, failure itself is knowledge (a disproven theorem, a structural member that breaks under stress). This is not mere positive thinking; it changes what counts as a successful outcome. The reframe works because it lowers the emotional stakes without lowering the intellectual engagement. You're still working just as hard, but you've removed the binary pass/fail judgment that causes people to quit during the ugly early phase.
