Emmett Shear describes three groups Twitch interviewed: existing Justin TV broadcasters, competitor platform broadcasters, and non-broadcasters. The non-users surfaced entirely different blockers — computer performance, time constraints, strategic concerns about revealing practice sessions. These were the barriers preventing the market from growing.

Critically, existing users had already self-selected past these barriers, so they could only report incremental feature requests. Competitor users revealed switching costs. But non-users revealed why the market wasn't bigger — the actual ceiling on growth. Twitch's biggest strategic moves (building broadcasting into Xbox/PlayStation, partnering with broadcasting software companies) came from non-user feedback, not feature requests from existing users.
