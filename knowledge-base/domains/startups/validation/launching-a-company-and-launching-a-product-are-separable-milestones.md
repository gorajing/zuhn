Graham explicitly separates company launch from product launch, noting that several of YC's most promising startups hadn't shipped products but were 'definitely launched as companies.' The YC model treats Demo Day as a company launch event — founders present to investors with clear direction, resolved team dynamics, and momentum, even if the product itself needs more build time.

This distinction matters tactically for founders deciding when to fundraise. Waiting for a finished product before engaging investors can mean missing funding windows or running out of runway. The company-launch milestone — having a clear vision, committed team, removed obstacles, and investor-ready narrative — is independently valuable and fundable. Founders should sequence these as separate goals rather than treating product completion as a prerequisite for all external engagement.
