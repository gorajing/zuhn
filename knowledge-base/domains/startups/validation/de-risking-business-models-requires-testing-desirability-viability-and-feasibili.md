The innovation risk axis in Osterwalder's framework is grounded in evidence accumulation across desirability (do customers want this?), viability (can this generate sustainable profit?), and feasibility (can we build and deliver this?). Ideas with no evidence sit at the far left; those rigorously tested across all three dimensions move right.

This three-lens approach prevents a common failure mode where founders validate one dimension and assume the others follow. A product customers love but that cannot generate margins (desirability without viability) or a profitable model that cannot be technically delivered (viability without feasibility) both fail. The framework forces explicit attention to all three, and the non-linear path acknowledges that invalidating any one dimension may require pivoting back to re-test.
