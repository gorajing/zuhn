Stanley Tang describes DoorDash's origin: after interviewing 150-200 small business owners about delivery pain points, they built paloaltodelivery.com in about an hour — a static page with PDF menus and their personal cell phone number. No backend, no drivers, no dispatch system. When the first call came, they personally drove to pick up and deliver the food. They used Square for payments, Google Docs for order tracking, and Apple's Find My Friends to track drivers.

The framework is explicitly experimental: treat startup ideas as hypotheses to test, not products to build. The one-hour launch eliminated the biggest risk (building something nobody wants) and simultaneously created a learning machine — every delivery taught them about the actual logistics process, every customer conversation revealed real needs. This hands-on operation informed what the eventual automated system should look like, making the 'real' product better than if they'd designed it from first principles.
