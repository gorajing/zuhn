Rob Fitzpatrick's Mom Test principle highlights a fundamental flaw in how most teams validate ideas: asking people about your idea triggers social desirability bias. Customers will praise your concept to avoid conflict, producing false positives that waste months of development.

The counter-intuitive fix is to never talk about your idea at all during early validation. Instead, ask about their actual behavior, past spending decisions, and existing workarounds. This principle extends beyond startups — any domain requiring honest feedback (product development, UX research, even medical interviews) benefits from asking about concrete past behavior rather than hypothetical future intentions.
