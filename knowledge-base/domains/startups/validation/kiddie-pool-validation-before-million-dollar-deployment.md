Before committing to covering a massive reservoir with 96 million shade balls at ~$0.34 each, the LA water department ran the simplest possible experiment: three inflatable kiddie pools filled with reservoir water — one in sunlight, one with a tarp, one with shade balls. The shade balls 'knocked out the problem immediately.'

This is the gold standard of validation: find the cheapest, fastest test that can definitively falsify your hypothesis. The kiddie pool test cost virtually nothing but provided the same signal as a million-dollar pilot program. In product development, this translates to testing the core mechanism in isolation before building the full system. The key insight is that the test doesn't need to simulate scale — it only needs to test the mechanism.
