Many teams fall into what the article calls the 'A/B test death spiral' — endlessly tweaking buttons, headlines, and email timings while ignoring that users simply don't want what's being offered. The diagnostic signals are clear: Day 1 retention below 10% in daily-use categories, Day 7 below 5% after multiple experiments, and zero cohorts that flatten across any segment, device, or geography.

When these patterns persist, no amount of notification optimization, discount campaigns, or UI redesign will move the needle. The fix isn't at the edges but at the center — changing the problem you're solving or the user you're solving it for. Successful pivots often come from studying the best 1% of retained users or discovering an entirely different use case the product accidentally serves. The hardest part is emotional: letting go of months of invested roadmap to face the truth that some curves simply don't bend.
