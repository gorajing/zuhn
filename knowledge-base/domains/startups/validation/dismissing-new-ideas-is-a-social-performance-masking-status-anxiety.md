Graham identifies a 'sinister' motivation behind idea dismissal: status anxiety. When someone attempts something ambitious, observers consciously or unconsciously hope for failure because success would change the relative hierarchy. This isn't just individual psychology — in some cultures it becomes institutionalized as tall-poppy syndrome.

Silicon Valley partially solves this through aligned incentives: investors, colleagues, and community members benefit from your success, so encouragement becomes self-interested rather than purely altruistic. Over time, this instrumental optimism has calcified into genuine cultural norm. The implication for founders is structural: choose environments where people's incentives align with your success, because you cannot talk people out of status anxiety — you can only make your success their success too.
