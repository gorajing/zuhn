Fireship's side hustle is a perfect case study in distribution-masked failure. With a YouTube channel as a free marketing engine, he got thousands of signups. But at 0.3% conversion to a $6.90 product, the unit economics are fatal: you need 300 free users for every $6.90 sale. Without the free YouTube distribution, customer acquisition costs would immediately exceed lifetime value.

This is a critical counterweight to the 'just launch and grow' advice. Having an audience doesn't mean having product-market fit. Fireship's honest admission: he had two additional sales after the initial launch. The free distribution created the illusion of traction (signups!) while masking the reality (nobody will pay). If your own warm audience won't convert, the product isn't solving a problem worth paying for — no amount of marketing fixes that.
