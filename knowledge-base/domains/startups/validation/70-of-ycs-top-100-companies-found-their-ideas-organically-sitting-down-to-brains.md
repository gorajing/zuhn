Analysis of YC's top 100 companies by valuation shows that at least 70% got their startup ideas organically — through work experience, personal frustration, or domain expertise — rather than by sitting down to deliberately brainstorm. The reason is structural: when people try to think of startup ideas from scratch, they tend to converge on the same superficially plausible tar pit ideas that thousands of others also generate.

For those who want to play the long game, three strategies increase organic idea flow: become an expert in something valuable (working at a startup is excellent for this), build interesting things even if they're not clearly businesses (Replit started this way), and maintain exposure to frontier problems in your field. For those who need ideas now, the speaker offers seven recipes ranked by likelihood of producing good ideas, with 'start from your team's expertise' at the top and 'look for broken industries' at the bottom.
