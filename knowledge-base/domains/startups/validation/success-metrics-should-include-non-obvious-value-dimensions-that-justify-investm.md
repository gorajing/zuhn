Two years after opening, St Helena's tourism numbers showed modest growth — on target but not transformative. If judged solely on visitor arrivals and economic impact, the airport looked like a questionable investment. But the documentary's most powerful segment is about Eli, a premature baby with respiratory distress syndrome who was medevac'd to Cape Town in five hours. Without the airport, the month-long wait for the ship would likely have been fatal.

This reframes the ROI calculation entirely. The airport's value wasn't just tourism economics — it was the elimination of a mortality risk that came with geographic isolation. For startups with long payback periods, this is critical: identify and track the non-obvious value dimensions early. When your primary metric is growing slowly, having compelling secondary proof points (lives saved, risks eliminated, options created) keeps stakeholders invested through the trough of disillusionment.
