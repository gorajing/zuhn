Shear presents a counter-intuitive prioritization framework: Justin TV broadcasters complained loudly about uneditable ban lists, uneditable highlight titles, and lack of troll management. But they were using the product despite these issues, which meant these were annoyances, not dealbreakers.

Competitor broadcasters, by contrast, cited video stability, revenue sharing, and multi-person streaming — problems so severe they refused to switch. This comparison revealed the true priority hierarchy. The insight generalizes: complaints from retained users are revealed preferences about what's tolerable. The real signal comes from comparing what retained users endure versus what drives competitor users and non-users away. Twitch focused resources on the latter and grew dramatically.
