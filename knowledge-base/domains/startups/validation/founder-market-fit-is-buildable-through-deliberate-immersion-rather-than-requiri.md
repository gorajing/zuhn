The conventional framing of founder-market fit suggests you either have it or you don't—you lived the problem or you're an outsider. But the article argues fit exists on a spectrum and can be deliberately cultivated. Three specific strategies emerge: bringing on a co-founder with insider domain knowledge to complement your skills, embedding yourself in the target community through events, forums, and direct customer conversations, and building public credibility through thoughtful content, prototypes, or published customer research.

The key insight is that time spent in the trenches transforms inexperience into a distinct advantage, because earned insights carry more weight with investors than inherited ones. Founders who visibly demonstrate their learning journey—through blog posts, demos, or shared interview notes—create tangible evidence of deepening fit that VCs can evaluate, turning a weakness narrative into a growth story.
