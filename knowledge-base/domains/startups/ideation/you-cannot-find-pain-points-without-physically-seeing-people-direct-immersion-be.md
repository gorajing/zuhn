Loh's biggest product breakthrough came not from analysis but from physically visiting 50+ cities, giving math talks in public parks, carrying his own AV equipment. This direct contact with thousands of parents and students revealed the win-win-win ecosystem he built (middle schoolers learning to think, high schoolers building EQ through teaching, drama students finding stable part-time work).

He extends this principle broadly: he rides overnight buses to understand different demographics, teaches in disadvantaged schools to see real challenges, and takes improv comedy classes to improve communication. Each physical immersion generated ideas that pure reasoning could not. The lesson for builders: your most important product insights come from putting yourself in uncomfortable proximity to the people you want to serve. Theoretical empathy is insufficient; embodied experience is required.
