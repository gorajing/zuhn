Oxman's team keeps returning to a core insight when asking 'what does nature want?': nature wants to increase information while finding order (decreasing entropy). This maps to biological systems that grow increasingly complex and organized over time.

As a design principle, this suggests building products and systems that accumulate information and utility over time rather than degrading. It's the difference between a building that wears out (entropy increases) and a living structure that adapts and improves (information increases). For software and knowledge systems specifically, this resonates strongly — the best systems become more valuable as they accumulate information.
