Graham observes that the most transformative products were initially dismissed as toys: microcomputers, airplanes, cars, and early social networks. This dismissal is actually a useful filter because it explains why the opportunity remains available — serious people overlooked it precisely because it didn't look serious.

For founders evaluating ideas, this inverts the usual validation heuristic. Instead of seeking approval from experienced operators, a more reliable signal is users who love it combined with observers who scoff. YC explicitly weights this pattern when evaluating investments: user engagement plus perceived triviality equals high conviction.
