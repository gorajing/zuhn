Graham describes the Silicon Valley evaluation style as a deliberate polarity switch: instead of the default mode of listing reasons an idea won't work, you challenge your imagination to find ways it could. This isn't lower standards—it's a fundamentally different cognitive operation. The approach is battle-tested at Y Combinator, where power-law distribution of outcomes makes it painfully obvious when you dismiss a future giant. The trick is that optimistic evaluation is extremely lucrative in environments with asymmetric upside, and lucrative tricks tend to spread. This suggests the rest of the world will eventually adopt this evaluation posture.
