Apple started because Wozniak wanted to build microcomputers and HP wouldn't let him. Yahoo began as Filo's personal link collection. Lotus originated from a program Kapor wrote for a friend. Graham argues that measured by total market cap, this 'build stuff for yourself' model may be more fruitful than deliberate ideation.

The implication is counterintuitive: the best way to generate million-dollar startup ideas is to do what hackers enjoy doing anyway—cook up amusing hacks with friends. This works because personal projects start from genuine problems the founder understands deeply, the motivation is intrinsic rather than financial, and the founder-market fit is organic rather than manufactured. It also aligns with Graham's observation that startups need multiple founders who were already friends.
