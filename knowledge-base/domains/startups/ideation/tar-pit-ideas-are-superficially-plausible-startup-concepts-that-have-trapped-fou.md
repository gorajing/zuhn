YC has seen the same set of startup ideas applied batch after batch for decades. These 'tar pit ideas' share a common pattern: they center on a widespread problem that lots of potential founders encounter, and the solution seems obviously buildable. The classic example is 'app to coordinate plans with friends' — everyone has this problem, you can immediately imagine the app, yet in 20 years nobody has made it work.

The danger is that these ideas are tantalizing from a distance because they're so superficially plausible. The speaker's first piece of advice: just Google it. An astonishing number of founders skip the step of searching for who has tried this before. If you want to work on a suspected tar pit idea, find the people who tried before, talk to them, and understand what the hard structural barrier actually is. It's not that tar pit ideas are necessarily impossible — they're common ideas that are dramatically harder than they appear.
