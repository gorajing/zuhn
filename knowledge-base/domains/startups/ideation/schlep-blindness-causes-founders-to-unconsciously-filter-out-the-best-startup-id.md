Paul Graham identifies 'schlep blindness' as a cognitive blind spot where founders unconsciously filter out ideas involving tedious, painful work — dealing with banks, regulations, fraud, broken infrastructure. This isn't a conscious decision; people don't weigh 'fix payments vs. build recipe site' and choose the recipe site. They simply never see the payments opportunity because their unconscious mind shrank from the complications.

This means the standard advice to 'look for problems' is insufficient. You can stare directly at a massive problem and still not see it as a startup idea if the solution path involves enough schleps. The practical antidote is to reframe the question: instead of 'what should I build?' ask 'what do I wish someone else would solve for me?' This bypasses the unconscious filter by separating problem identification from the intimidation of solving it.
