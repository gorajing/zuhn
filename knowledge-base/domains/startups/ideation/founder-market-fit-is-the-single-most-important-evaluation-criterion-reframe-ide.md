Among the 10 evaluation questions for startup ideas, the speaker singles out founder-market fit as the single most important. The example is PlanGrid: Tracy knew construction, Ralph was an excellent developer — the team was obviously right for building an iPad app for construction blueprints.

The strategic reframe is to stop searching for abstractly good ideas and instead search for ideas where your team has an unfair advantage. It doesn't matter if something would be a great startup for someone else if your team isn't the right one to build it. The speaker notes it's bizarre how many YC applications come from founders who are legitimate experts in one field but apply with an idea in a completely different space. The Resi founders exemplified this correctly: they only searched for ideas at the intersection of real estate and fintech because that's where they had deep expertise, and quickly found an excellent idea with automatic founder-market fit.
