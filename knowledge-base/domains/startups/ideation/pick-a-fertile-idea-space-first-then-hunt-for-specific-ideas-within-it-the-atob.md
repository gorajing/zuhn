Different idea spaces have wildly different hit rates. Over the past decade, fintech infrastructure and vertical enterprise SaaS produced billion-dollar companies at astonishingly high rates, while consumer hardware and social networks had orders-of-magnitude lower success rates. The Fivetran example shows why this matters: by shopping for ideas within the data analysis space, they put themselves in position to 'bump into' a good idea even after two failed attempts.

The AtoB case study is the most instructive. The founders were young without deep domain expertise but chose trucking because it was a big, under-disrupted industry — a fertile hunting ground. They literally drove to truck stops, talked to drivers, interviewed founders of existing trucking companies, and systematically built a mental map of where good and bad ideas lived. After exploring many potential ideas, they landed on fuel cards. The speaker emphasizes that this approach is replicable by anyone — most founders just won't do it because it sounds like too much work.
