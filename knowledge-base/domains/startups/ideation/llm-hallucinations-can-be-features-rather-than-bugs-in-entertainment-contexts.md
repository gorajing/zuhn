NFX poses a provocative design question: what experiences would allow hallucinating LLMs to be 'easily forgiven or better yet, a feature?' This reframes AI's biggest perceived weakness as a potential strength in the right context. Games have always relied on unpredictability, randomness, and surprise — hallucinations fit naturally into that design space.

Similarly, they ask what 'slow motion experiences captivate players, even when waiting for inference or image generation?' This suggests that the latency problem isn't purely technical — it's a design problem. Turn-based games, narrative experiences, and collaborative world-building can all absorb inference delays gracefully. The broader insight is that product-market fit sometimes comes from designing around constraints rather than waiting for them to be solved. Death by AI and AI Dungeon succeeded precisely because they embraced AI unpredictability as core gameplay.
