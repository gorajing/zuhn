Most successful startups originate from a founder's personal frustration rather than detached market analysis. When you experience a problem firsthand, you understand its nuances, edge cases, and emotional weight in ways that no survey or report can capture. Salary.com emerged because Kent Plunkett needed reliable compensation data for his own hiring decisions. Uber was born from frustration with unreliable taxis.

This pattern suggests that the starting question should not be 'What's a big market?' but 'What problem do I face where people are already paying for bad solutions?' The existence of inferior paid alternatives validates both demand and willingness to pay, while your personal experience gives you an unfair advantage in designing something better.
