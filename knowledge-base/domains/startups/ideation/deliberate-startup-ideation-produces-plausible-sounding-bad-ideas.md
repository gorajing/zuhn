Paul Graham argues that conscious effort to generate startup ideas produces a uniquely dangerous category: ideas that are bad but plausible-sounding, causing founders to waste significant time before realizing the flaw. Apple, Yahoo, Google, and Facebook all started as side projects — none were originally meant to be companies.

The alternative is to cultivate a mind where ideas form unconsciously by (1) learning deeply about things that matter, (2) working on genuinely interesting problems, and (3) collaborating with people you like and respect. Living at the leading edge of some technology makes ideas that seem prescient to others feel obvious to you. The implication is that idea generation is a byproduct of authentic intellectual engagement, not a discrete activity.
