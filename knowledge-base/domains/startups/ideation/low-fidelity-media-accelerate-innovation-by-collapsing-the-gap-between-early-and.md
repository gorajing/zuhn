John Carmack's observation about game mods illustrates this principle perfectly: Quake's crude technical limitations meant quick gameplay hacks weren't far from the official game's quality, spawning a golden age of modding and launching careers. As commercial game quality rose, the gap between prototype and polished product became too large, killing experimentation — until Minecraft and Roblox deliberately re-embraced crude aesthetics, making innovative gameplay the overriding value again.

The generalized insight is that the psychological barrier to starting is proportional to the perceived gap between where you'll start and where you need to finish. Digital media's advantage over physical media isn't primarily the software — it's that you can start something new with minimal upfront commitment. This suggests that when choosing tools, formats, or platforms for new projects, creators should deliberately bias toward mediums that let them produce something presentable quickly, even if the ceiling is lower.
