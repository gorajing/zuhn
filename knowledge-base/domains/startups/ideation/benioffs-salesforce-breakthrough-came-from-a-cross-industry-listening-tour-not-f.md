After leaving Oracle, Mark Benioff didn't immediately start building. He embarked on a deliberate listening tour, engaging with people across different industries and backgrounds. It was this breadth of exposure — not deep enterprise software expertise — that produced the catalytic question: 'What if we sold enterprise-level software like Amazon sells books?'

This pattern aligns with Gregersen's broader research on innovation: the most transformative questions come from cross-pollinating experiences across domains. Living and working in five different countries taught Gregersen himself that assuming knowledge in unfamiliar contexts leads to misunderstanding, while genuine curiosity in those contexts leads to breakthrough insights. The implication for founders is that structured exposure to adjacent or unrelated industries is a more reliable source of novel ideas than deeper specialization.
