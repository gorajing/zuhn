Graham proposes a simple career heuristic: look at whether the most successful people in a field are nerds — people who care about the work for its own sake. In fields like politics, crime-adjacent business, or patent trolling, earnestness appears to be irrelevant or even a handicap. In technology and startups, it's increasingly the dominant factor.

This heuristic is useful precisely because it's observable from the outside. You don't need insider knowledge to assess whether a field's leaders seem genuinely fascinated by the work versus motivated by status or financial extraction. The nerd-density test gives you a quick read on whether your natural earnestness would be an asset or liability.
