Hormozi argues that broke entrepreneurs should not chase trends or analyze markets abstractly. Instead, the strongest starting point is the intersection of three personal filters: Pain (problems you have personally experienced and understand viscerally), Profession (skills you already get paid for or have deep competence in), and Passion (topics you would voluntarily spend time on without compensation).

The key insight is that this trilemma acts as a credibility and persistence filter simultaneously. Pain gives you authentic customer empathy, profession gives you execution capability, and passion gives you the endurance to push through the inevitable grind of a bootstrapped business. Ideas that only hit one or two of these filters tend to stall — you either lack the skill to execute, the empathy to sell, or the motivation to persist.
