Graham highlights a critical founder trap: conflating personal interest with market demand. His example of the woman starting an 'outdoor portal' because she liked the outdoors illustrates how founders project their own preferences onto the market. The correct business for someone who likes the outdoors might be data recovery — there's zero connection between what you enjoy and what creates wealth.

This connects to his broader argument that users are the only real proof of wealth creation. VCs watch for 'techno-weenies' obsessed with interesting problems rather than user happiness. The practical test is simple: treat your startup as an optimization problem where the metric is number of users. Get version 1.0 out as fast as possible, because until you have users to measure, you're optimizing based on guesses — the startup equivalent of premature optimization.
