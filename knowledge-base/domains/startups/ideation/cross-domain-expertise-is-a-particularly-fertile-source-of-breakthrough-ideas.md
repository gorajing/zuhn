Graham notes that domain expertise fueling crazy new ideas can come from another field entirely, and that such crossovers tend to be particularly promising. This aligns with the broader pattern that paradigm constraints are field-specific — an outsider with deep expertise in a different domain can see structural possibilities that insiders literally cannot conceive because their cognitive building blocks are paradigm-locked.

This has direct implications for team composition and idea evaluation. When evaluating seemingly crazy proposals, the question isn't just 'does this person have relevant expertise?' but 'does this person have deep expertise in any rigorous domain?' A physicist proposing a biological insight or a mathematician suggesting a business model may be operating from genuine cross-domain pattern recognition that bypasses the target field's paradigm constraints. The most valuable ideas may come from people who are simultaneously expert and outsider.
