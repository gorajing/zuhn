The central paradox of corporate reinvention is that the urgency to change is lowest precisely when the capacity to change is highest. When a company is profitable and dominant, stakeholders resist disruption of the status quo. But waiting until the core business declines means fewer resources, less talent retention, and diminished market credibility to launch something new.

Amazon exemplifies the counter-pattern: Bezos constantly challenges the company to pursue new growth opportunities alongside improving existing operations. This dual mandate — optimize the present while inventing the future — requires leadership that can hold both time horizons simultaneously. Companies that wait for crisis to innovate find they've already lost the key resources, customer trust, and organizational energy needed to build the next thing.
