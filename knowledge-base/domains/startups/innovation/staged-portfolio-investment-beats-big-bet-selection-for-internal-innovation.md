Bayer's Catalyst Fund invested €10,000 each in 74 projects from 400+ proposals. After four months of testing, 29 received ~€90K second-round funding based on evidence presented to senior leaders. Of those, 17 showed feasibility and got €100-300K continuation funding, with 5 launching to market. This 400→74→29→17→5 funnel mirrors venture capital portfolio dynamics.

The key insight is that leaders cannot identify winners on day one. The filtering mechanism must be evidence from real experiments, not executive preference. Training decision-makers to evaluate based on evidence rather than gut feel about ideas was a critical enabler. This approach converts innovation from a high-stakes guessing game into a systematic portfolio operation.
