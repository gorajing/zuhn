Scientists and engineers often resist business strategy work because it feels foreign to their training and identity. BioCity discovered that combining the Business Model Canvas with lean startup methodology — challenging assumptions by forming hypotheses, designing experiments, gathering data, and revising — maps directly onto the scientific method these founders already use daily. The tools 'look and feel a lot like scientific discovery.'

The result was transformative: scientists who previously felt the business world was inaccessible suddenly felt empowered to conduct customer interviews and validate market assumptions. The key insight is that the barrier wasn't intellectual capability or willingness — it was framing. By presenting business validation in the language of experimentation rather than the language of MBA strategy, BioCity removed the psychological barrier that kept technical founders from engaging with market risk.
