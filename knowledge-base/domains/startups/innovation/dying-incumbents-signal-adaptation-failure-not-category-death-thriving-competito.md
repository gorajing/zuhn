The 'Retail Apocalypse' narrative attributed store closures to Amazon making physical retail obsolete. But the reality is more nuanced: Sears tried to do everything poorly, Toys-R-Us dismissed the internet, J.C. Penney alienated its customer base. Meanwhile, in the exact same market conditions, Costco, Ross, Aldi, Lush, and Dollar General were growing.

This is a recurring pattern across industries. When disruption hits, observers blame the disruptor for killing the category when the real story is that weak players who failed to adapt are being cleared out. The survivors aren't necessarily copying the disruptor — Costco explicitly does the opposite of Amazon. Recognizing this distinction matters for strategic decision-making: the correct response to disruption is rarely to copy the disruptor but to find a differentiated position that leverages your unique strengths.
