When a business model is unproven, the traditional planning toolkit — sales forecasts, growth projections, multi-year financial models — produces fiction dressed as analysis. The numbers look precise but are built on unvalidated assumptions about customer behavior, pricing, and market size.

The alternative is to treat each assumption as a hypothesis and run cheap, fast experiments to test them. Each experiment either validates an assumption (proceed) or invalidates it (adapt or pivot). This continuous learning loop lets the model evolve toward product-market fit rather than committing resources to a plan that was wrong from day one. The key discipline is keeping bets small enough that any single failure is cheap.
