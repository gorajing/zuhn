The article lists six timing signals for innovation software investment, but the most revealing is 'leadership requires clear ROI on innovation initiatives.' This reframes the purchase decision from an operational problem (too many ideas) to a strategic one (can't prove innovation creates value).

This distinction matters because idea volume is a vanity metric — having thousands of submitted ideas means nothing if none are validated or executed. The real pain point is the accountability gap: innovation teams that cannot connect their activities to business outcomes lose executive sponsorship and budget. Software that provides progress tracking dashboards and data-backed validation evidence solves the actual problem.
