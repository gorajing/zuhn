Photosynthesis splits water molecules not to produce oxygen, but because chlorophyll desperately needs replacement electrons after losing its own to the electron transport chain. Oxygen is literally discarded as waste. Yet this 'useless byproduct' is what enabled the evolution of all aerobic life, fundamentally transforming Earth's biosphere.

This pattern recurs in technology: SMS was a trivial byproduct of cellular signaling protocols that became a multi-billion dollar communication platform. Slack emerged from a failed game company's internal tool. The lesson for builders is to pay attention to what your system produces incidentally—the waste streams, the side effects, the logs nobody reads. The most transformative output of your core process might be something you're currently throwing away.
