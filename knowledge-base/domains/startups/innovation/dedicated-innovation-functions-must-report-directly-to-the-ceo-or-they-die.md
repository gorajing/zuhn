Best Buy's Strategic Growth Office — 50 experienced staff 100% dedicated to innovation — succeeded because its leader Corie Barry reported directly to Joly. The function identified opportunities, prototyped solutions, ran experiments, and scaled winners.

This confirms the ambidextrous organization thesis: the part running the business cannot simultaneously innovate. When innovation reports through operational leaders, it gets deprioritized because quarterly execution always feels more urgent. Direct CEO reporting provides the legitimacy and resource protection needed to survive. Notably, Corie Barry went on to become CEO, signaling how seriously Best Buy treated this function.
