Revenue Loop was dismissed in 1998 during the dot-com advertising bubble, yet by 2006 'everyone is focused on this type of approach.' The bubble created a temporary economic environment where inefficiency was profitable — advertisers overpaid so much that optimizing toward real value would have hurt revenue. Once the bubble burst and advertising rates normalized, the algorithm that maximized actual transaction value became obviously correct. This pattern recurs: during AI hype cycles, cloud spending bubbles, or any period of inflated unit economics, innovations that optimize for real efficiency get dismissed because waste is temporarily rewarded. Founders building efficiency-oriented products during bubbles should expect initial rejection and plan for a longer adoption timeline that extends past the correction.
