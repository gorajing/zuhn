McKinsey Academy's approach stands out by explicitly using behavioral science to drive habit change — not just teaching frameworks but engineering the conditions for participants to apply them on the job. This includes simulations, reflection loops, progress tracking tied to business impact, and strong coaching elements.

Most corporate training fails not because the content is wrong but because it never changes behavior. The distinction between knowledge transfer and behavior change is the critical gap in innovation education. Programs that incorporate practice loops, accountability structures, and measurement of on-the-job application address the actual failure mode of executive education: people learn concepts but revert to old patterns within weeks. The ROI difference is not incremental — it's categorical.
