Graham draws a sharp distinction between Silicon Valley's optimism and naivety. Skeptics cite impostors like Theranos, but Silicon Valley investors actually weren't fooled by Theranos — journalists were. When measured by revenue outcomes, the optimistic evaluation framework outperforms the default dismissive one.

The key insight is that this optimism isn't innate character but a cultural technology that evolved through practice. YC partners develop it by evaluating thousands of strange ideas every six months under power-law pressure where missing a winner is catastrophically expensive. This means any organization or individual can cultivate the same skill: switch polarity from 'why this won't work' to 'how could this work,' then let that practice compound. The lucrative nature of this trick means it will inevitably spread beyond Silicon Valley.
