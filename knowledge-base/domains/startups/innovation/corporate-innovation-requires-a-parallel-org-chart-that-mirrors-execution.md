Osterwalder argues that innovation cannot be a side project grafted onto existing roles. Instead, companies need a full parallel org chart: a Chief Entrepreneur mirrors the CEO, a Chief VC mirrors the CFO, and a Chief Risk Officer enables rather than blocks experimentation. This dual structure ensures that exploration of new business models receives the same organizational seriousness as execution of current ones.

The key insight is structural symmetry — each function that sustains the existing business (budgeting, risk management, resource allocation) needs an innovation counterpart with different operating logic. The CFO optimizes known returns; the Chief VC funds cheap experiments and doubles down on evidence. This prevents the antibodies of the existing organization from killing nascent ideas before they can prove themselves.
