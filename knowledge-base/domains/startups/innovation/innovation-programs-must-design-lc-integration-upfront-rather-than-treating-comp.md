The conventional approach treats Legal & Compliance as a gate that innovation teams must pass through — a downstream checkpoint. This creates an adversarial dynamic where innovation teams try to move fast and L&C tries to ensure safety, with neither understanding the other's constraints.

The alternative is to design the innovation program itself to include L&C from the start — at both the design and delivery phases. Pharmaceutical industry examples show that when L&C professionals are brought in as collaborators rather than gatekeepers, they can actually help teams move forward faster by identifying compliant paths to experimentation that teams wouldn't discover on their own. This reframes L&C from obstacle to innovation accelerator.
