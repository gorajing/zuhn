Amazon's creation of AWS demonstrates that a company's internal infrastructure — built to serve its core business — can become the basis for a fundamentally different value proposition. Rather than treating IT infrastructure as a cost center, Amazon recognized it as a key resource capable of generating recurring revenue with margins far exceeding its razor-thin e-commerce operations.

This pattern is generalizable: any company with scale-dependent internal capabilities (logistics, data processing, identity verification) should evaluate whether those capabilities could serve external customers. The critical insight is that the new business model doesn't compete with the original — it creates an entirely new market category. The Business Model Canvas approach of mapping key resources to potential value propositions provides a structured method for identifying these opportunities.
