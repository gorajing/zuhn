The framework emphasizes that a well-designed innovation portfolio is deliberately heavy on the left (high-risk, unvalidated) side. This is not a sign of poor discipline — it is the mathematically necessary structure given that most initiatives fail during testing. Organizations that maintain too few early-stage initiatives starve their future growth pipeline.

This maps directly to venture capital portfolio theory applied inside a single company. The implication is that killing individual initiatives is healthy and expected, but having too few initiatives in the funnel is the real strategic failure. Companies should budget for and expect high attrition rates in their innovation portfolio rather than trying to pick winners upfront.
