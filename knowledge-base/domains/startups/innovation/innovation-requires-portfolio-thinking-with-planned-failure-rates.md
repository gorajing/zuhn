The venture capital model of portfolio investing applies directly to corporate innovation. Out of ten innovation projects, expect only two to succeed big, three to perform moderately, and five to fail outright. This is not pessimism — it is the mathematical reality of exploring uncertain territory.

Companies that demand high success rates from every initiative inadvertently kill exploratory projects before they can find product-market fit. Amazon exemplifies this by explicitly embracing failure as inseparable from invention, which enabled them to discover entirely new growth engines like AWS. The practical implication is that innovation budgets and governance structures must be designed around portfolio economics, not individual project ROI.
