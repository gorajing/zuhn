One of the most effective techniques for getting through the ugly-duckling phase of ambitious projects is deliberate genre miscategorization. You tell yourself a painting is 'just a sketch,' new software is 'just a quick hack,' or a startup is 'just an experiment.' This lets you apply a lower evaluation standard to early results, reducing the psychological cost of producing imperfect work. Once the project has enough momentum and form, you quietly promote it to its true category.

This technique is amplified by choosing media that support fast iteration and low upfront commitment. Digital tools beat physical ones partly because starting something new carries almost zero sunk cost. The gap between early work and final work should be as narrow as possible — not because quality doesn't matter, but because a smaller gap makes the lame phase shorter and less psychologically punishing. Minecraft mods outproduced AAA game mods precisely because the crude aesthetic eliminated the quality gap that paralyzed would-be creators.
