The article reveals a three-tier framework for selecting innovation training: hands-on team programs (Strategyzer, IDEO U) for spreading practical skills, academic executive programs (MIT, Harvard, Stanford) for strategic depth at senior levels, and custom transformation programs (BCG, McKinsey) for enterprise-wide cultural change.

The non-obvious insight is that many organizations default to the most prestigious or expensive option, when the real decision should be driven by where the bottleneck sits. A team that can't prototype and validate ideas doesn't need a Harvard executive certificate — they need tactical tools. Conversely, a company with good execution but no innovation strategy won't benefit from another design thinking workshop. Mismatching program type to maturity level is one of the most common ways organizations waste L&D budgets.
