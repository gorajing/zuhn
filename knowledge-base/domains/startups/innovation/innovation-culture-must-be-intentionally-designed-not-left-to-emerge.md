Most companies treat culture as an emergent byproduct of hiring and office norms, but Strategyzer argues culture should be designed with the same rigor as a business model. The Culture Map provides a systematic framework for making culture conversations tangible and actionable rather than fuzzy.

This matters because culture directly determines whether a company can execute on its strategy. A culture that accidentally rewards risk-aversion will kill innovation regardless of how much leadership talks about wanting it. Intentional design means identifying the specific behaviors, enablers, and blockers that shape how people actually work — then systematically reinforcing what serves the company's goals.
