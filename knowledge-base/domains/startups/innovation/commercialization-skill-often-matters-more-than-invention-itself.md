The video draws an explicit parallel between Edison and Steve Jobs: neither invented the core technology they're famous for, but both excelled at improving, packaging, and selling it. Edison built on 22 prior light bulb inventors; Jobs built on existing MP3 players, smartphones, and GUIs. Both founded massive companies (General Electric, Apple) from commercialization rather than pure invention.

This creates a structural disadvantage for pure inventors like Tesla, who often lack the business acumen or interest to capture the value their inventions create. The pattern suggests that founding a company or securing strong licensing terms is as important as the invention itself — Tesla's AC motor designs power virtually every home on Earth, yet he died in relative obscurity and poverty.
