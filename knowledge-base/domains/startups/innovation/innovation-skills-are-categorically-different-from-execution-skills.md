Execution skills optimize for delivering projects on time and on budget within known parameters. Innovation skills optimize for navigating uncertainty — designing business models, identifying risky assumptions, running experiments, and pivoting based on market evidence. These are not different points on the same spectrum; they are fundamentally different cognitive modes.

This distinction matters because organizations that try to run innovation with execution mindsets kill promising ideas by demanding premature certainty. Conversely, applying innovation mindsets to established operations creates unnecessary chaos. The practical implication is that companies need to deliberately build innovation capability as a separate discipline, with different metrics, different timelines, and different tolerance for failure than their core operations.
