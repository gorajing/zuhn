Photosynthesis splits water not because plants need oxygen, but because chlorophyll desperately needs a replacement electron after losing one to the transport chain. Oxygen is literally discarded waste — yet it enabled the entire animal kingdom and transformed Earth's atmosphere. The most consequential output of the process was never the point.

This pattern recurs in business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game, Flickr emerged from an abandoned MMO, and AWS grew from Amazon's internal infrastructure needs. The insight for builders is to systematically audit your waste streams and side effects. What are you producing as a byproduct that others might find transformative? Rather than only optimizing your primary output, periodically inventory what you're discarding or ignoring — that's where asymmetric value often hides.
