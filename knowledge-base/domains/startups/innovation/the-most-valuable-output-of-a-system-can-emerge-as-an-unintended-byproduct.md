Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy. Oxygen was merely the leftover from splitting water molecules — a 'useless byproduct' as the video puts it. Yet this waste product became the foundation for all aerobic life on Earth, arguably the most consequential chemical output in biological history.

This pattern recurs across technology and business. Slack began as an internal tool for a failed game company. AWS started as Amazon's excess infrastructure capacity. YouTube's recommendation algorithm became more valuable than the video hosting it supported. The insight is that systems optimized for one output frequently generate byproducts with outsized value — but only if someone recognizes and captures that value. Designing systems with visibility into their side-effects, not just their intended outputs, increases the surface area for serendipitous discovery.
