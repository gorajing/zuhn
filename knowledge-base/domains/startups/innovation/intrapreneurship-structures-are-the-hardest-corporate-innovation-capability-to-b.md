Self-assessments from the summit showed that intrapreneurship structures received the lowest scores of any category. No organization had cracked the problem of creating strong institutionalized structures, processes, and dedicated incentive systems for business model innovation projects.

This suggests that the structural challenge of intrapreneurship is fundamentally different from the cultural or leadership challenges. Even organizations that had executive support and widespread canvas usage couldn't translate that into formal systems that consistently spawn and support internal ventures. The implication is that intrapreneurship requires purpose-built organizational architecture — separate incentive systems, dedicated funding mechanisms, and protected team structures — rather than bolting innovation onto existing corporate processes.
