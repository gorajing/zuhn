A single innovation initiative, no matter how well-designed, has uncertain odds of success. Organizations that pursue ideas one at a time face a compounding problem: each failure creates pressure to abandon the innovation effort entirely, while each success creates complacency. Neither dynamic produces sustained innovation capability.

The portfolio approach changes the math fundamentally. By running multiple ideas through design and testing simultaneously, organizations increase the probability that at least some will succeed while also generating cross-pollinating insights between projects. Leaders must learn to evaluate and invest across this portfolio — advancing promising experiments, killing underperforming ones, and maintaining enough diversity to hedge against market unpredictability. This is innovation management as a discipline, not innovation as a lucky break.
