Graham distinguishes between having lower standards and switching polarity entirely. Lowering standards means you still evaluate from a critical frame but set a lower bar—this produces mediocrity. Switching polarity means you treat each new idea as a challenge to your imagination: can you find a way this could work?

This is the practiced skill of YC partners who review thousands of ideas per batch in a power-law outcome environment. The cost of false negatives (missing the next Airbnb because the idea sounds stupid) vastly exceeds the cost of false positives. This asymmetry makes optimism not naive but mathematically rational. Silicon Valley's cultural edge is that this optimistic evaluation has become a social custom, not just an individual technique.
