Photosynthesis exists to produce G3P (and from it, glucose) for the plant's own energy needs. Oxygen is literally called a 'useless byproduct' in the process — it's what's left over when water molecules are split to replenish electrons in Photosystem II. Yet this 'waste' enabled the entire animal kingdom, including humans.

This pattern recurs in technology and business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game, Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature, and AWS grew from Amazon's internal infrastructure needs. The implication for builders is to pay attention to what your core process discards or produces incidentally — those byproducts may be more valuable to others than your primary output is to you. Designing systems with observable, accessible byproducts increases the surface area for serendipitous value creation.
