When you frame a new project as a serious endeavor from the start, you hold early results to finished-work standards and become discouraged. By deliberately calling it 'just a sketch' or 'just a quick hack,' you give yourself permission to produce ugly first versions. Once the project gains momentum, you can sneakily convert it into something more serious.

This technique is amplified by choosing media that allow fast iteration with low upfront commitment — digital over physical, notebooks over carved stone. Patrick Collison extends this further: making the tenuousness a feature ('yes, it's ridiculous, I'm just seeing how far this naive approach gets') turns the early awkwardness into a source of creative freedom rather than shame. YC itself was started in this spirit.
