Most organizations default to measuring innovation programs purely on value creation—revenue, profit, new products launched. The Ecosystem Map framework argues this is dangerously incomplete. Culture change—encompassing leadership support, organizational design, and innovation practice—is equally critical because it determines whether promising ideas can actually be leveraged into sustained growth.

Without culture change, innovation remains fragile and personality-dependent. Teams lack time and legitimacy, new ventures can't access corporate capabilities, and decisions get made on gut feeling rather than evidence. The dual-axis assessment forces organizations to acknowledge that an innovation lab producing ideas (high value creation) but operating in a hostile culture (low culture change) is building on sand.
