Osterwalder and Pigneur draw a sharp line: an innovation engine is explicitly NOT a place where you write business plans. Its core function is decreasing risk and uncertainty through experimentation—prototyping, testing, gathering evidence cheaply and quickly using Lean Startup and Customer Discovery methodologies.

This distinction matters because business plans for novel ventures are exercises in fiction—they project certainty where none exists. The Business Model Canvas and Value Proposition Canvas serve as architectural blueprints that can be rapidly prototyped and tested with real customers. The key insight is that innovation is only expensive when done wrong. When you use systematic experimentation to reduce market risk before scaling, the cost profile changes dramatically. The expensive gamble isn't innovation itself—it's committing resources to unvalidated assumptions.
