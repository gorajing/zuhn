The Chief Internal Ambassador addresses the fundamental paradox of corporate innovation: new ventures need the existing company's resources (customers, brand, salesforce, patents) to succeed, but the existing company's immune system tends to reject innovation efforts that threaten established processes. A dedicated ambassador with trust on both sides can negotiate access to these assets without triggering organizational antibodies.

This role recognizes that innovation teams isolated from the parent company lose their primary competitive advantage over startups — existing assets and distribution. But innovation teams too tightly coupled to the parent get suffocated by existing processes and priorities. The ambassador creates a selective membrane rather than either a wall or an open door, allowing beneficial resource flows while protecting the innovation team's autonomy.
