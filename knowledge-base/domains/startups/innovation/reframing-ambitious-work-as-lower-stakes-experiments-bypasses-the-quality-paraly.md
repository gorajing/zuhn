Graham identifies a specific psychological maneuver used by successful creators: deliberately miscategorizing ambitious work as a lower-stakes format. You start a painting as 'just a sketch,' software as 'just a quick hack.' This isn't self-deception — it's a strategic lowering of the evaluation standard applied to early output, which prevents quality paralysis before the work has enough substance to judge fairly.

The technique works because the transition from sketch to real project happens gradually through accumulated momentum. By the time you're invested enough to care about quality, you've already pushed past the fragile early phase where most projects die. Patrick Collison extends this further: making the tenuousness itself a feature, turning implausibility into a kind of playful challenge rather than a source of anxiety.
