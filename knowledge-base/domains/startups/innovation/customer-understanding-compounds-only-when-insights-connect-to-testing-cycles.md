Many teams treat customer research as a phase that precedes building, rather than a continuous loop that feeds experimentation. The article's emphasis on 'understanding to action' highlights a common failure mode: teams build rich customer profiles but don't systematically convert those insights into testable experiments.

The compounding effect only works when each cycle of learning feeds the next: customer insight generates a hypothesis, the hypothesis becomes an experiment, the experiment produces new learning, which refines the customer profile. Breaking this chain at any point — especially the insight-to-experiment link — means research effort doesn't compound. This is why tools that explicitly connect customer profiles to testing activities outperform disconnected research documentation.
