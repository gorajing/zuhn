A persistent misconception about deeptech is that it means pure hardware. The article argues the strongest teams blend atoms and bits: cloud-first biotech, ML-powered chemistry, synthetic biology platforms. Companies like Ginkgo Bioworks (organism engineering platform), Twelve (CO2-to-chemical conversion), and Tesla (hardware-software integration) exemplify this hybrid approach that creates multiple layers of defensibility.

This has strategic implications for how founders architect deeptech companies. Rather than building a physical product, the winning move is building a platform where the physical science becomes programmable through software. This creates network effects, data moats, and scalability that pure-hardware plays lack, while the physical science component provides barriers to entry that pure-software plays cannot match.
