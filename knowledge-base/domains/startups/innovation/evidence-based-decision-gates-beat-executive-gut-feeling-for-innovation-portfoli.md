A critical failure mode in corporate innovation is when senior leaders override the testing and evidence-gathering process to champion pet projects or kill uncomfortable findings. This is an innovation practice problem—the third pillar of culture change alongside leadership support and organizational design.

The fix isn't removing leadership judgment entirely but building a system where decisions are made based on strength and quantity of evidence. This requires both skill development (teams learning rigorous testing methods) and cultural acceptance (leaders trusting the process). When identification of winning ideas is made on partial evidence and gut feeling, even well-funded innovation programs produce inconsistent, unrepeatable outcomes.
