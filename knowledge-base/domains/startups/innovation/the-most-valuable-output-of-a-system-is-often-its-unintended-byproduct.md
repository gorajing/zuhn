Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen was literally waste — a 'useless byproduct' from splitting water molecules to harvest electrons. Yet this discarded output became the most consequential chemical transformation in Earth's history, enabling all aerobic life.

In startups and product development, the pattern recurs: Slack was a gaming company's internal chat tool, Flickr was a game feature, and AWS was Amazon's internal infrastructure. The insight is structural — when you build a system optimized for one output, the side effects of that system often contain untapped value. Deliberately auditing your byproducts (data exhaust, internal tools, intermediate outputs) is a repeatable discovery mechanism, not just serendipity.
