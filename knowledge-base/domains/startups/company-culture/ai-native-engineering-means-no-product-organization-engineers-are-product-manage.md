Foroughi's structural choice: AppLovin has no product organization. Engineers are imaginative, audit AI-generated code, and align directly to KPIs — model accuracy and revenue growth. The reason this works: when the engineering layer can both ship and measure, the PM-as-translator role becomes redundant. PMs exist to translate user needs into specs because traditional engineers couldn't do that translation. AI-native engineers can.

The required engineering profile: imagination, product sense, ability to audit code (not write all of it), and direct understanding of business KPIs. AppLovin's 400 engineers in the core ad business produce ~$10M EBITDA each — a ratio possible only because the org doesn't have a translation layer eating productivity.

For builders: this is one of the strongest current arguments for org redesign. If your engineers cannot self-direct against business KPIs, you have an engineering training gap, not a missing PM org. Fix the gap; don't add the org. For Zuhn editorially: this maps onto the broader trend that role hierarchies built around 'who has authority to translate' (PMs, project managers, middle managers) are systematically vulnerable to AI tools that make the underlying translation work cheap. The companies with highest revenue per employee will be those that collapsed translation layers fastest.
