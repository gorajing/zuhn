Foroughi's structural argument: tech has normalized stock-based compensation as a ~3% of cap table annual burn, which produces a downward spiral when stock prices fall. Stock down 66% means SBC burn is now 10% of cap table — incredibly hard dilution to recover from. AppLovin solved this by limiting SBC to top 10-15% of company; everyone else gets cash comp plus optional ESP discount-buy. The annual SBC is $300M against a $150B market cap (0.2%), genuinely unburdensome.

The valuation principle: if a company generates $1B in cash and gives out $1B in equity, then either dilutes or uses cash to buy back the equity, the real cash generation is zero. Cash flow as reported is misleading. Cash flow minus SBC is the actual economic generation. For investors and operators, this is the single number to watch.

For founders: structure compensation deliberately. Don't give equity to people who can't take risk on the stock — they'll be unhappy when it falls and motivated by anchor effects when it rises, both of which distort decisions. Keep equity concentrated where it provides actual incentive alignment. For Zuhn editorially: this is a useful frame for evaluating any tech company — the public 'cash flow positive' narratives often hide significant SBC burn that would zero out the cash generation.
