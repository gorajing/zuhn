Periods of elevated M&A in software typically happen when organic growth slows for large public companies and they turn to acquisitions to maintain revenue expansion. For startups, this is a structural tailwind: it increases exit optionality and can elevate valuations for companies with strategic fit even if they lack the scale for an IPO.

For founders evaluating exit strategy, high M&A environments favor building for acqui-hire readiness — strong teams, defensible technology, and strategic positioning relative to potential acquirers. The risk is that acquisition multiples compress if the wave ends before you are ready. For investors, this environment validates backing companies with clear M&A appeal, not just IPO trajectory.
