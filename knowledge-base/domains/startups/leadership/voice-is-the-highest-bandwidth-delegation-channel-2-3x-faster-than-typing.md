Most people delegate with their thumbs on their phone, which is the worst method. Typing on a computer is better but still slow. Voice is the optimal channel — you can talk 2-3x faster, do it while walking between meetings, and capture ideas in real time.

Swanson would walk between meetings at Thumbtack and voice-note takeaways to his assistant: pre-draft these emails, follow up with this person. By the time the next meeting started, all action items were delegated. The pattern for super-delegators at Athena: send voice notes out, receive text messages back (because listening to voice notes is slow for the recipient). This creates an asymmetric communication protocol optimized for both sender and receiver.
