Naval's co-founder exemplifies this by spending days deep-diving into obscure GitHub projects and niche research papers — not the obvious AI model training work, but adjacent fields like micro weather forecasting with unusual ML algorithms. He then contributes a genuine code tweak or asks a considered question, building authentic relationships with people who are tinkering at the edges unnoticed.

This approach works because it's genuine curiosity, not a recruiting pitch. The founder finds weird loners working on side projects and only then attempts to recruit them. Most attempts fail, but the ones that succeed yield people no conventional recruiter could have surfaced. The key insight is that taste in people is domain-specific — makers recognize makers, builders recognize builders — which is why outsourcing to HR or recruiters fundamentally cannot work for finding outlier talent.
