Jensen Huang deliberately avoids the standard executive information diet of dashboards and polished summaries. Instead, he reads raw updates, listens to engineers explain problems in technical language, and attends seminars to learn new concepts firsthand. This dramatically increases his workload but prevents a compounding failure mode: when leaders only consume summaries, they inherit every blind spot of the summarizer.

This approach enables detection of 'weak signals' — small anomalies or strange patterns that precede major shifts. CUDA and AI were both spotted as opportunities through this kind of ground-level awareness. The tradeoff is brutal cognitive load, but the payoff is a leader whose strategic vision is grounded in reality rather than a filtered version of it. For founders, the actionable lesson is to maintain at least some direct channels to raw information, especially in domains where you're making bet-the-company decisions.
