Carlson describes a pivot mid-interview with Putin: he started by trying not to 'get rolled,' interrupting Putin's history lecture. Then he realized 'I'm not here to prove I'm a great interviewer -- I want to know who this guy is.' By letting Putin talk freely, even through what seemed like a tiresome history recitation, Carlson got the actual answer: Putin's worldview, his framework, his reasoning.

This is a transferable leadership and information-gathering principle. Aggressive questioning puts people into defensive mode where they give only 'shallow talking points.' Creating space for extended, uninterrupted expression reveals the deep roots of how people actually think. For founders doing customer discovery, hiring interviews, or partner negotiations, the instinct to control the conversation often destroys the very information you're seeking.
