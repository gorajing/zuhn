Dave Gray's Culture Map framework recommends starting with behaviors rather than outcomes because behaviors are what people experience daily — they're what gets discussed at lunch or after work. By asking 'What does a great day look like?' and 'What does a bad day look like?', facilitators surface concrete patterns rather than abstract aspirations.

This behavior-first approach forces specificity. Instead of vague complaints like 'lack of teamwork,' participants describe scenes: 'People agree in meetings but leave and do their own thing.' This granularity makes the gap between current and desired culture actionable, because you can design enablers and remove blockers for specific behaviors rather than hoping outcomes emerge from platitudes.
