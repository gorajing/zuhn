Graham identifies the 'pointy-haired boss' as a manager who stopped programming, and traces the root cause to corporate structures where advancement requires leaving technical work. His solution isn't to become a better manager — it's to avoid the structural incentive entirely by working at startups where peer consensus replaces hierarchy.

This is a structural argument, not a personal development one. The problem isn't that people lack management skills; it's that traditional organizations create a false choice between career advancement and staying technical. Startups dissolve this by operating as flat peer groups where founders and early employees advance through contribution, not title escalation.
