Chris Voss redefines empathy as pure understanding without agreement, liking, or compassion. He demonstrated this with Egyptian Muslim witnesses in terrorism trials — by stating 'you believe there's been a succession of American governments for 200 years that are anti-Islamic' without agreeing or disagreeing, he built enough trust for every single witness to testify. The key insight: being unafraid to articulate someone's worldview signals strength, not weakness.

This applies directly to any leadership context — investor negotiations, team disagreements, customer conflicts. The practical framework: state the other side's position accurately and completely, then shut up. You never need to say 'I agree' or 'I'm on your side.' The act of demonstration is itself the bridge. Most people conflate empathy with sympathy, which makes them either avoid it (fearing concession) or fake it (appearing disingenuous). Tactical empathy sidesteps both failure modes.
