During his brief ouster from OpenAI, Altman observed that his executive team operated the company effectively even when he couldn't communicate with them directly. He frames this as validation of the often-repeated CEO advice that your most important job is hiring and developing your team.

This reframes organizational resilience away from the founder's personal indispensability. A company approaching AGI-level stakes needs to function under uncertainty and stress without single-point-of-failure dependence on any individual — including the CEO. The test isn't whether the founder can heroically save the company, but whether the team they built can sustain operations autonomously.
