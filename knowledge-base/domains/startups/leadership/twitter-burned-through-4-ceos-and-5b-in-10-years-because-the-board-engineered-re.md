Twitter was like Game of Thrones: politics, hidden agendas, plots, and schemes. The board frequently engineered regime changes. Two constants persisted: the board's chaos and a founder (Dorsey) whose desire to return kept destabilizing leadership. Twitter regressed further from viability with each CEO cycle. Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, and TikTok all built walled gardens for engagement while Twitter remained a 'wild west' where controversy drove virality over quality. Multiple acquisition offers (Google, Disney, Salesforce) fell apart because the problems ran deeper than price. Musk eventually bought it because the board had given up hope of independent survival.
