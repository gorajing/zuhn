Zuckerberg reveals that what keeps him up at night isn't strategy — it's people dynamics. He has enough conviction in Meta's direction that strategic decisions don't stress him. But when there's tension within his core leadership group of about 30 people, everything becomes difficult.

His operating rhythm reflects this priority: weekly Monday meetings with the top 30 that are often deliberately unstructured, working through diverse issues together to build shared intuition and camaraderie. He spends a disproportionate amount of time fostering this tight core group. The insight for any leader is that investing in team cohesion isn't soft management — it's the highest-leverage activity because a cohesive team can push through hard challenges, while a fractured team cannot execute even clear strategies.
