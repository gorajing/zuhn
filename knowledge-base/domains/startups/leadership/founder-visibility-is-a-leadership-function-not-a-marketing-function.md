The article reframes founder visibility from a marketing or ego concern into a leadership function. When a founder's credibility doesn't travel externally, the internal environment warps: teams question strategy, hiring stalls, and board trust erodes — not from failure, but from persistent misrecognition.

The practical framework offered is 'designing authentic visibility' through five channels: curated progress signals (product updates with reasoning), founder narrative construction (linking expertise to company trajectory), interface optimization (deck, homepage, LinkedIn as thinking proxies), feedback loop engineering (mentors and operators who echo strengths publicly), and teaching-as-signal (sharing learnings to demonstrate depth). None of these require performative behavior — they're about making what's already true externally legible.
