The Founder Mode concept, popularized by Paul Graham and covered by the WSJ, argues that the conventional wisdom to 'hire great people and get out of their way' fails at certain stages. Founders who remain obsessively hands-on — reviewing details, skipping hierarchy, staying close to the product — often scale better than those who prematurely delegate.

This reframes a long-stigmatized management behavior as a feature, not a bug. The key insight is that founder context and pattern recognition are irreplaceable assets; abstracting them away through layers of management degrades decision quality. The implication for founders: don't let professional management orthodoxy push you into a role that strips your competitive advantage.
