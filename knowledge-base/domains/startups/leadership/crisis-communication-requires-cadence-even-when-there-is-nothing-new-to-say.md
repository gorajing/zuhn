Unity CEO Tomer Bar-Zeev explicitly advised communicating with teams 'even when you don't have anything new to say,' because employees seek belonging and stability more than information. Many CEOs communicate at the onset of a crisis then go silent, which employees interpret as either indifference or hidden bad news.

The insight is that communication cadence is a trust-building mechanism independent of content. A weekly all-hands where the CEO says 'nothing has changed, here's what we're watching' is more stabilizing than sporadic updates packed with information. This maps to broader organizational design: predictable rhythms (standups, retros, 1:1s) create psychological safety not because of what's discussed, but because the forum reliably exists. For founders, the practical rule is: set a communication schedule at crisis onset and never skip it, even when you feel you have nothing to add.
