Ginni Rometty's hiring philosophy evolved over 40 years from speed-hiring to hiring experts to finally prioritizing willingness to learn above all else. Her reasoning: experts with 30 years in one domain have low odds of adapting when the industry shifts. At IBM, only 2 out of 10 employees had future-relevant skills when she became CEO.

The practical implication is to test for curiosity and learning capacity in interviews rather than credential-checking. When IBM hired both external 'shiny object' experts and retrained internal employees, the winners were not one group or the other — they were the individuals from both groups who were willing to learn from each other. This reframes hiring as building skills rather than buying them.
