Corcoran's observation that 'Slack doesn't build culture, shared memory does' captures why so many remote teams feel disconnected despite heavy tool adoption. Digital communication handles information transfer well but fails at creating the emotional bonds that make people sacrifice for each other and stay through hard times. A story from a dinner lasts longer than any company memo.

The prescription is specific: quarterly in-person gatherings structured around one unforgettable activity, supplemented by small repeating rituals. The unforgettable activity creates shared stories that become team lore — inside jokes, references, and memories that bind people together across geographic distance. Capturing short recap videos converts moments into persistent culture artifacts. The insight extends beyond remote teams: any organization where members don't naturally collide needs engineered shared experiences to build the trust substrate that makes collaboration work.
