The French Revolution illustrates a recurring pattern: the National Assembly began as moderate constitutional monarchists, but the Jacobins pushed toward increasingly radical republicanism. The radicals then consumed each other — Robespierre, architect of the Terror, was himself guillotined. The final outcome under Napoleon and subsequent monarchs landed close to the original 1789 vision of constitutional monarchy.

This pattern appears in startup contexts too. Founding teams with moderate product visions get pressured by radical pivots or aggressive growth factions. The extremists often burn out or are removed, and the company returns to something resembling the original vision. The insight is that moderate positions have staying power precisely because they're sustainable, but they require surviving the radical middle phase. Leaders should expect radicalization pressure and plan for organizational resilience rather than trying to prevent the cycle entirely.
