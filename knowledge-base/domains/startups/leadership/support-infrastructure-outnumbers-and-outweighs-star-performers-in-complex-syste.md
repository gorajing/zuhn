In the nervous system, neurons get all the credit for thinking and decision-making, but glial cells outnumber them roughly 10 to 1 and constitute about half the brain's mass. These support cells anchor neurons to blood supply, provide immune defense, create cerebrospinal fluid, and insulate signal pathways with myelin sheaths. Without this support infrastructure, neurons cannot function at all.

This maps directly to organizational design: the visible performers (engineers, salespeople, executives) depend on a much larger support layer (ops, HR, IT, QA, documentation) that is systematically undervalued. Companies that cut support roles to invest in more 'star' talent often see system-wide degradation, just as demyelination diseases destroy nervous system function despite neurons themselves being intact. The ratio matters: high-performing systems invest disproportionately in the invisible scaffolding.
