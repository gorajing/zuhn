Vikrama Dhiman's 3 W's framework decomposes PM career growth into three dimensions: (1) what you produce—the tangible artifacts and outcomes, (2) what you bring to the table—your unique perspective, skills, and domain knowledge, and (3) what your operating model is—how you work, collaborate, and make decisions.

The framework's diagnostic power lies in identifying which dimension is lagging. A PM who produces excellent artifacts and brings deep domain expertise but has a chaotic operating model will plateau. Similarly, someone with a great operating model and strong outputs but nothing distinctive to offer won't be selected for the hardest problems. The framework suggests that career plateaus are rarely about 'not being good enough' generically—they're about specific dimensional gaps that can be targeted.
