Twitter's board was the one constant across all four CEO tenures, and it was consistently destructive. The board blessed Ev's coup against Jack, then backed Jack's scheme to replace Ev with Dick, then allowed Dick to flounder before bringing Jack back. Each transition came with a complete strategic reset: Jack's SMS focus, Ev's product-driven growth, Dick's business-minded reactionary approach, Jack's second coming with ephemeral content.

No strategy had time to mature. The board optimized for short-term optics (who looked most CEO-like for an IPO) rather than long-term execution. Meanwhile, Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, and TikTok were all building coherent long-term product visions under stable leadership. The lesson is that board-level instability is more damaging than any individual CEO's mistakes, because it prevents the organizational learning and strategic compounding that drives long-term success.
