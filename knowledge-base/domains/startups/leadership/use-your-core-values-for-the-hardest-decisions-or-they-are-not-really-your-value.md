When LucidLink faced a funding crisis with dried-up capital markets and a short runway, every advisor told Thompson to lay off everyone except core people and protect the IP. Instead, he and his co-founder looked at each other and realized that advice didn't align with their core values. If they didn't use their values for the hardest decision of their lives, those values were meaningless.

They kept all 25 people, asked everyone to take deep pay cuts, and told them exactly what was going on. It was during this crisis—with the whole team intact and committed—that they found product-market fit. The lesson: values aren't posters on the wall. They're the decision framework you use when everything is on the line. And sometimes the values-driven path produces better outcomes than the 'rational' one.
