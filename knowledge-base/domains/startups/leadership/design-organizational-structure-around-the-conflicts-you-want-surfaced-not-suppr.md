Most organizational design focuses on efficiency — who reports to whom, how work flows, how decisions get made. But a more powerful lens is conflict routing: which disagreements does the structure naturally surface, and which does it suppress? Every org structure creates information channels that amplify certain tensions while muting others.

A product-organized structure surfaces conflicts between product teams competing for resources but may suppress conflicts between engineering quality and shipping speed. A functional org surfaces craft-quality debates but may suppress customer-facing trade-offs. The key insight is that leaders should deliberately choose which conflicts they want to hear about — the ones most critical to the company's success — and design reporting structures, meeting cadences, and escalation paths to ensure those specific tensions reach decision-makers. Suppressed conflicts don't disappear; they compound silently until they become crises.
