The Strategyzer model positions the leader's role not as the source of business model innovation but as the architect of conditions that produce it. Instead of dictating strategy, effective leaders use structured assessment questions to guide their teams toward discovering weaknesses and opportunities themselves.

This is a meaningful distinction because it scales better—a leader who provides answers becomes a bottleneck, while a leader who provides the right questions creates a distributed innovation engine. The 9-mechanic framework gives this abstract principle concrete scaffolding: leaders can direct attention to specific business model components without prescribing the solutions for each.
