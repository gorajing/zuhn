The video notes that during the Truman administration, the President refused to leave the Oval Office during what was believed to be an attack, and that subsequent presidents have echoed the 'captain goes down with the ship' sentiment. The logic is counterintuitive from a pure asset-protection standpoint — the president is replaceable via the line of succession — but morale is not fungible. Seeing leadership share risk creates a sense of national unity that keeps the social contract intact. For startups in crisis (layoffs, product failures, existential threats), the founder's visible presence and shared vulnerability has outsized impact on team cohesion. The decision to retreat to safety (golden parachutes, blame-shifting, going silent) is individually rational but organizationally corrosive.
