During Geneva Hospital's COVID-19 transformation, the Head of Operations, Surgical bloc manager, Patient flow manager, and projects department each took ownership of their units' transformation. The Team Alignment Map made their individual commitments visible to everyone else.

This distributed-but-aligned model outperformed centralized command because each unit head understood their domain's constraints better than any coordinator could. The alignment tool's value was making cross-unit dependencies explicit — when the surgical bloc committed to freeing beds, patient flow could plan intake accordingly. The chain of commitments replaced the need for constant top-down coordination.
