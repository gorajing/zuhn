Goldberg describes how Trump administration job interviews literally test for willingness to affirm falsehoods ('Who won the 2020 election?'). This creates a binary filter: candidates are either 'so lacking in integrity that they're willing to lie' or 'so deluded that they actually believe the correct answers.' Neither type can provide the honest feedback a leader needs.

This pattern is directly applicable to startup leadership. When founders surround themselves only with people who think 'every brain fart is brilliant,' they lose the organizational capacity to recognize and correct errors. The critical insight is that this isn't just about missing feedback — it's about structural selection. The hiring process itself systematically excludes exactly the people who would provide corrective information. Every yes-man hired makes the next correction harder, creating an accelerating decay in decision quality.
