Rometty nearly turned down a major promotion because she felt she wasn't ready. Her husband's observation — 'do you think a man would have answered it that way?' — combined with the principle that growth and comfort never coexist became a career-defining reframe. She learned to look for nervousness as evidence of learning rather than a signal to retreat.

This is not about recklessness but about reframing the internal critic. When offered a stretch role, instead of cataloging what you don't know, stack what you can do and recognize that discomfort means the role will grow you. The mentor's follow-up — 'don't ever do that again' — underscores that hesitation in the face of growth opportunities has a real cost.
