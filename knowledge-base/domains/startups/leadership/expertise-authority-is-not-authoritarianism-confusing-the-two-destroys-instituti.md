Harris identifies a paradox in how we value authority in science and expertise. People confuse respecting expert knowledge with blind credentialism or establishment loyalty. This confusion fuels both anti-expert populism ('they're all shills') and expert arrogance ('trust us without question').

For founders and leaders: build cultures that respect domain expertise while maintaining healthy skepticism. The key signal is whether experts can explain their reasoning transparently, not whether they have the right credentials. Institutions rebuild trust through demonstrated competence and transparency, not by demanding deference.
