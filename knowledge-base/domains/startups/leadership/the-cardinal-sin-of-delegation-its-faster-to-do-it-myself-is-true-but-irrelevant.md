The number one blocker to delegation is the belief that it will be faster or better to do it yourself. The insight is that this belief is actually true — but it's a trap. The only way to build leverage is by absorbing the activation energy of teaching someone else. The second mistake is churning assistants every 6-12 months instead of compounding the relationship.

Swanson has worked with his primary assistant Marne for a decade. She now knows everything about him and operates like a 'little sister.' The compounding effect of long-term delegation relationships creates leverage that's impossible to replicate with frequent turnover. This applies equally to AI assistants — the investment in building context and preferences pays dividends over time.
