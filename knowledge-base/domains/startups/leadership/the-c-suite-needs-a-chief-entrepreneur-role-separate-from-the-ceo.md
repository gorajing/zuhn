Osterwalder proposes that CEOs excel at growing companies within known business models but systematically underinvest in future growth engines. The Chief Entrepreneur role exists to lead exploration of completely new value propositions and business models while the CEO manages the existing business.

This insight connects to the broader pattern that organizational structure determines organizational behavior. When innovation reports to the CEO, it will always lose budget battles to the existing business because the CEO's incentives are tied to current performance. A peer-level Chief Entrepreneur with independent authority and budget can protect long-term exploration from short-term optimization pressure.
