When an executive fails but has given great effort, the instinct is to demote rather than fire — it feels humane for both parties. But Horowitz shows this ignores the third perspective: every other employee in the company. A demoted VP of Sales still holds 1.5% equity while engineers who outperform them hold 0.1-0.2%. Former peers lose respect for someone they now outrank. The demoted person becomes unmanageable by rising talent. The cultural message becomes 'effort without results gets rewarded with golden parachutes.' The key insight is that the compassionate-seeming option for one person can be the toxic option for the organization. The exception is when someone was promoted internally without outsized equity — then demotion may work because the compensation mismatch doesn't exist.
