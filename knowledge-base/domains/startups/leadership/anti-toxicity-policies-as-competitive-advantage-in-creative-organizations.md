Osterwalder explicitly notes that Sutton's 'No Asshole Rule' is 'very important at Strategyzer,' revealing that successful innovation companies treat behavioral standards not as HR niceties but as strategic infrastructure. Toxic individuals destroy the psychological safety required for creative risk-taking, idea sharing, and honest feedback — all prerequisites for innovation.

The competitive advantage is indirect but powerful: organizations with explicit anti-toxicity norms retain creative talent longer, generate more diverse ideas, and maintain the trust required for idea meritocracy to function. This creates a reinforcing loop — good culture attracts good people who further strengthen the culture — making it increasingly difficult for competitors to replicate.
