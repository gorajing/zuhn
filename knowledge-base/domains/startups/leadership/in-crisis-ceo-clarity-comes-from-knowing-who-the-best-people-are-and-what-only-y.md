Tenev's crisis decision tree is deceptively simple: (1) Are the best people awake? (2) Do they need anything? (3) What can only I do? During GameStop, the CEO-only tasks were public communication about a complex situation and raising billions of dollars overnight. Everything else was delegated to known experts.

The prerequisite is crucial: this clarity requires pre-crisis intimacy with the organization. You must already know who the best people are for each type of problem, which requires the kind of deep operational engagement that founder-mode CEOs maintain. A CEO who doesn't know their organization's talent map cannot effectively delegate during a crisis and will either try to do everything themselves or make poor delegation choices.
