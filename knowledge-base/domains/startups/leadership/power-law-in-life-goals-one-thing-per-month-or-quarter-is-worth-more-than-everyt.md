Swanson argues there's a power law in business, venture, and life. Each month or quarter, there's typically one thing that if accomplished is worth more than everything else combined. Most people set too many goals and use the long list to procrastinate on the most important one.

The practical framework: identify the one foundational thing (find your partner, start the business, fix your health), go all-in on it, then layer secondary goals on top. If your calendar doesn't reflect your highest goals when you audit it weekly, you're not doing it right. This connects to the delegation framework — once you know your one thing, decompose everything else into delegatable subtasks.
