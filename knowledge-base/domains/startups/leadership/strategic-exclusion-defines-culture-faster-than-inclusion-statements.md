Brian Armstrong's Coinbase memo banning political debate and Tim Cook's 'leakers do not belong here' email both demonstrate that culture is most powerfully defined by drawing clear lines about what is unacceptable. Armstrong's memo caused 5% of the company to self-select out, which was the intended effect — it filtered for mission-alignment. This contrasts with typical culture-building through positive value statements, which are often too vague to drive behavioral change. The tactical implication for founders is that a single, clearly-enforced exclusion creates more cultural clarity than a dozen aspirational slides. However, this approach requires accepting real talent loss as a feature, not a bug.
