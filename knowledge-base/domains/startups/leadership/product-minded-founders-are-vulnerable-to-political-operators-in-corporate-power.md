Ev Williams drove Twitter from 500K to 175M users, shipped hashtags, retweets, lists, and native advertising -- arguably the most productive era in Twitter's history. Yet he was ambushed and replaced because he failed to recognize that Jack Dorsey was systematically weaponizing employee frustrations, cultivating board relationships, and positioning himself as the visionary founder.

The pattern is clear: product excellence is necessary but insufficient for CEO survival. Ev hired friends for key roles (nepotism concerns), was slow to make operational decisions, and chose not to punish Jack's public misrepresentations out of fear of damaging the company. His inability to play corporate politics -- combined with a board that prioritized IPO speed over product growth -- made him an easy target. The lesson extends beyond Twitter: any founder who treats leadership as purely a product role while ignoring organizational politics is building on unstable ground.
