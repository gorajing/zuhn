Most founder comeback narratives emphasize dramatic moments — boardroom coups, investor standoffs, public ultimatums. Page's story offers a radically different template: a decade of quiet competence accumulation where each product success incrementally rebuilt the case for founder leadership.

The critical insight is that Page used the observation period productively. Watching Schmidt's tenure taught him delegation, org-scale communication, and team-building — the exact skills whose absence got him sidelined. By the time he returned, he wasn't the same leader who'd been removed. This suggests that founder 'exile' periods, when used for genuine skill development rather than resentment, can produce leaders far more capable than they were before being displaced.
