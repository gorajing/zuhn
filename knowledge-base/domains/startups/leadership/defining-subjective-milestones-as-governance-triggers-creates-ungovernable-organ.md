OpenAI's structure gave the nonprofit board responsibility for determining when the company achieved AGI, defined as 'a highly autonomous system that outperforms humans at most economically valuable work.' This is inherently subjective and carries enormous financial stakes: the AGI determination could theoretically exclude certain technologies from Microsoft's commercial license.

This creates a governance time bomb. As AI capabilities advance, the board faces increasing pressure from all sides — researchers wanting to slow down, investors wanting to commercialize, and employees caught between. The subjective nature of the trigger means reasonable people can disagree about whether it's been reached, turning a technical question into a political one. Any organization that embeds subjective, high-stakes milestones into its governance structure is designing for eventual deadlock or coup.
