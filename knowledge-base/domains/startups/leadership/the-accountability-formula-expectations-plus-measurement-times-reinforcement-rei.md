Most leaders stop at expectations (job descriptions, SOPs) and maybe measurement (KPIs, reports). But reinforcement is the multiplier — without it, the equation zeroes out. Reinforcement means: what happens AFTER you receive the measurement? Do you applaud, high-five, redirect, or ignore?

The weight study proves this: people who weighed themselves daily during December (measurement only, no diet or exercise) lost 1.9 lbs. Those who didn't weigh themselves gained 4.9 lbs. The scale doesn't yell — it's neutral feedback that changes behavior. Leaders should be the scale. Self-assessment is the highest form: have employees fill out rubrics rating themselves, then present to you. This shifts the dynamic from 'you pointing out problems' to 'collaborating on deficits they already see.' The reinforcement must be positive — encourage toward better behavior rather than threaten away from bad behavior. Negative reinforcement works short-term but creates avoidance, disloyalty, and turnover.
