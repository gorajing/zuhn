Genghis Khan broke from Mongol tradition by promoting commanders based on battlefield performance rather than clan lineage. This single organizational innovation — combined with absorbing skilled lower-class members of conquered tribes while stripping power from existing elites — allowed him to rapidly scale a loyal, competent fighting force from a relatively small tribal base.

The pattern is directly transferable to startups: organizations that hire and promote on demonstrated capability rather than credentials, tenure, or connections build stronger teams faster. The Mongol case is extreme but illustrative — they went from one of the smallest steppe groups to controlling 11 million contiguous square miles, largely because their talent pipeline wasn't bottlenecked by aristocratic gatekeeping.
