The Culture Map's garden analogy is more than a metaphor — it's a systems decomposition. Outcomes are the harvest (what you want culture to produce), behaviors are the plants (daily actions), enablers are fertilizer and expertise (what helps behaviors thrive), and blockers are weeds and pests (what prevents desired behaviors). This decomposition matters because it makes culture designable.

Most culture initiatives fail because they target outcomes directly ('we need more innovation') without mapping the behavioral chain that produces them. The enabler-blocker layer is where actual organizational design happens — it's where leaders examine how rewards, processes, unwritten rules, and their own actions create the conditions for specific behaviors. By making these layers explicit and separate, teams can identify which specific components to change rather than hoping culture shifts through willpower or sloganeering.
