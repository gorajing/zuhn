Organizations develop shorthand for cultural problems — phrases like 'people are lazy' or 'there's no accountability' become reflexive explanations that everyone nods along to without actually diagnosing anything. The Culture Map framework combats this by requiring participants to tell stories with specific examples, describing scenes rather than summarizing sentiments.

This distinction between evidence and opinion is critical for culture work. When someone says 'People agree to something in a meeting, but leave and do their own things,' that's a diagnosable behavior with identifiable enablers and blockers. The abstract version — 'lack of teamwork' — is unfalsifiable and therefore unchangeable. Demanding narrative specificity is a forcing function that converts culture complaints into design problems.
