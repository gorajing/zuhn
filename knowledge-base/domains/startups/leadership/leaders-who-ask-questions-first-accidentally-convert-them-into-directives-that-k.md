Hal Gregersen's research reveals a counterintuitive dynamic: when a leader asks a question in a group setting, the power differential transforms that question into a perceived directive. Team members hear 'the boss wants us to think about X' rather than 'here's an open exploration.' This effectively narrows the solution space to whatever the leader's question implies.

The fix is what Gregersen calls 'reflective quietness' — leaders consciously choose silence and observation at the start of meetings, letting others surface questions first. This creates genuine psychological safety for divergent thinking. Only after diverse perspectives have emerged should the leader add their own questions. The timing of a question matters as much as its content.
