When you tell a salesperson 'your calls suck,' they walk away knowing what you don't want but having no idea what you do want or how to get there. Ironic process theory predicts they'll actually think more about the bad behavior, not less — like telling an anxious person to stop worrying. They regress.

The fix: state neutral facts ('Last week your close rate was 20% below normal'), ask what they think about it, ask what they'd do differently, support them in fixing it, then reinforce the corrective behavior when it appears. Reinforce sticking to the script, not closing the deal — because the script leads to closes. Give one behavior change at a time, like starting a fitness program with just 'no alcohol and no dessert' rather than a complete lifestyle overhaul. Wait for that habit to stick before adding the next. Most leaders dump 17 items on a performance review — overwhelming people who then change nothing. Simple, sequential, positively reinforced changes compound into transformation.
