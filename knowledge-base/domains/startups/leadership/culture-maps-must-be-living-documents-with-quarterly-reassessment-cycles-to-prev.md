Alex Osterwalder warns that you can't just slap phrases on a wall and expect employees to enact them. The Culture Map framework addresses this by treating the map as a living document that requires regular reassessment — ideally quarterly — where teams evaluate whether the culture has actually moved from current to desired state.

The reassessment protocol asks specific diagnostic questions: Have the enablers you designed actually produced the behaviors you wanted? Have blockers been removed? This creates a feedback loop that prevents the common failure mode where culture initiatives produce impressive workshop artifacts that are forgotten within weeks. Integration into existing company processes — placing maps in meeting rooms, referencing them during decisions — keeps the map operational rather than aspirational.
