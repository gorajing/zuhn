Bukele's strategy mirrors a pattern seen across politics and business: instead of competing on the terms set by incumbents, he redefined what the competition was about. He ran against the system — the two established parties, the corruption, the old way of doing things — which let him absorb dissatisfied voters from both sides simultaneously.

This is structurally identical to how disruptive startups position themselves. They don't say 'we're a better version of the incumbent' — they say 'the incumbent category is broken.' Bukele dressed like a Silicon Valley CEO, campaigned on social media instead of TV, and skipped debates entirely. Each of these choices reinforced the frame that he was something fundamentally different, not just a better politician. The lesson: when you can credibly position yourself as outside the system, you can capture demand from everyone frustrated with the status quo — which in a broken system is nearly everyone.
