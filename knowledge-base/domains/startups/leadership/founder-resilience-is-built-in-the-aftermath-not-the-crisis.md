Drawing from his own experience (the OpenAI board crisis), Altman distinguishes between the crisis itself -- where you get support, adrenaline, and can function under pressure -- and the 'fallout after.' He notes that 'the emotional toll gets easier as you go through more bad things' even though the challenges get objectively harder and higher stakes.

Critically, he observes there's very little guidance available for founders on this rebuilding phase: 'I've never actually found something good to point founders to about not how you deal with the crisis on day zero or day one, but on day 60 as you're just trying to rebuild after it.' This gap in founder education suggests the recovery period is both the most important and most under-discussed aspect of founder resilience.
