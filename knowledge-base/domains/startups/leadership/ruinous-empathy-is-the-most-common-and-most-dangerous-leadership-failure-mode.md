Kim Scott's 2x2 framework maps leadership behavior along two axes: caring personally and challenging directly. The sweet spot is radical candor (high on both). But the vast majority of mistakes happen in the 'ruinous empathy' quadrant — where leaders genuinely care but avoid giving hard feedback because they don't want to hurt feelings.

The Alex story illustrates this perfectly: 10 months of softened feedback ('great start, maybe a little better') led to termination. When fired, Alex asked 'Why didn't you tell me?' — revealing that the leader's 'kindness' was actually a betrayal of trust. The team suffered too, as top performers contemplated leaving because they had to compensate for uncorrected poor work. Ruinous empathy doesn't just fail the underperformer — it punishes everyone who depends on them.
