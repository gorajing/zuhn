Gawande draws a sharp distinction: teachers deposit knowledge or skill but do not necessarily see your performance in an ongoing way. A coach circles back, observes you in real conditions, and helps you walk through the process of change and improvement over time.

His executive coach asked to record meetings and discovered Gawande was talking 70% of the time — something he would have guessed was impossible. The coach suggested starting meetings with questions instead of statements and setting clear action steps. The surgical coach taught him to count to 30 before intervening when trainees struggled with bleeding, breaking his micromanagement habit. The key is that coaching works not through content transfer but through the feedback loop: agree on goals, observe real performance, identify gaps between intention and reality, iterate.
