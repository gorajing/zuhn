Kaz Nejatian's core operating principle is stewardship over status — focusing on what you are doing and in service of what, rather than what title you hold. Early in his career, when asked what he wanted to do, he would name a job title. His mentor pointed out this was hollow.

The parallel he draws is revealing: people who optimize for happiness (a state) don't find it, while people who optimize for service end up deeply happy. This maps directly to the founder-mode philosophy — founder-mode CEOs hold themselves responsible for outcomes not processes. Professional managers care more about what things look like than what they are, because their power source is management itself. The practical test: are you pursuing a role for its title, or for the work it lets you do?
