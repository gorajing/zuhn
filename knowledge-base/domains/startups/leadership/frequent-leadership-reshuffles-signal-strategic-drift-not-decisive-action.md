Samsung's leadership pattern reveals a common corporate failure mode: treating leadership changes as strategy. Three new CEOs in 2021 across major divisions, the removal of the chip division head in May 2024, and emergency measures like six-day executive work weeks are symptoms of an organization that lacks a coherent long-term direction and substitutes personnel changes for strategic clarity.

Each reshuffle resets institutional knowledge, disrupts ongoing initiatives, and creates a period of cautious decision-making as new leaders establish themselves. For startups and established companies alike, the lesson is that leadership stability correlates with strategic coherence — frequent changes at the top usually mean the board or controlling family doesn't have a clear vision and is hoping the right person will magically produce one. The most effective turnarounds typically come from giving one leader a clear mandate and sufficient time, not from musical chairs.
