Lex frames jiu-jitsu as more than a sport for tech leaders like Zuckerberg — it's a humility practice. When you run a company used by billions, very few experiences genuinely humble you. Jiu-jitsu does this efficiently: no amount of wealth or status protects you on the mat. A smaller, more skilled practitioner will submit you every time.

Quoting Miyamoto Musashi — 'think lightly of yourself and deeply of the world' — Lex suggests that this physical humbling translates directly into better leadership. Leaders who regularly experience being a beginner, being physically dominated, and having to tap out develop a different relationship with failure and learning. This complements the more detailed insights from the full Zuckerberg podcast about willingness to be embarrassed and rapid feedback loops in physical disciplines.
