The explore-exploit tradeoff is one of the most fundamental tensions in business strategy. Osterwalder proposes a structural solution: redefine the CEO as 'chief execution officer' focused on optimizing the proven business model, and create a parallel 'chief entrepreneur' role at the same organizational level focused 100% on innovation. This isn't just delegation — it's architectural separation of two fundamentally different operating modes.

Best Buy's Hubert Joly exemplified this by creating a strategic growth office staffed with top talent focused solely on innovation, separate from the operational management structure. The key insight is that innovation requires protected organizational space — when it competes for attention with execution in the same person's calendar, execution always wins because its returns are more immediate and measurable. Steve Ballmer's Microsoft is the canonical cautionary tale: extraordinary execution of Windows and Office, catastrophic failure to explore mobile, cloud, and social.
