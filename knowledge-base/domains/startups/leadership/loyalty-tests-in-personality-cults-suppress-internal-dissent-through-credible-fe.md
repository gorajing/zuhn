Harris describes how Republican Congress members who privately wanted to impeach Trump after January 6th didn't because they feared physical violence against themselves and their families. JD Vance and Elon Musk 'bending the knee' by asserting belief in claims they likely don't hold illustrates how loyalty tests function: the point isn't the belief itself but the public demonstration of submission.

This pattern appears in any organization with a dominant founder or leader: the loyalty test replaces competence as the hiring/promotion criterion, principled people either leave or self-censor, and the organization's decision quality degrades. The key diagnostic is whether members publicly assert things they privately disbelieve—this is the signature of a loyalty-test culture, not a truth-seeking one. The antidote is institutional structures that make dissent safe, which is exactly what peaceful transfer of power provides in democracies.
