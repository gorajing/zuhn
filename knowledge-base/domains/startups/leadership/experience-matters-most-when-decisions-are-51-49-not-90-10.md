David Solomon pushes back on Silicon Valley's bias toward slope over experience, arguing that Goldman Sachs' management committee succeeds because of deep judgment forged through decades of practice. His framework: intelligence must clear a 'smart enough' threshold, but beyond that, the human elements (connection, resilience, determination) and battle-tested judgment matter far more.

The key insight is about decision difficulty distribution. If a decision is 90-10, anyone can make it and it won't reach the CEO. The decisions that actually arrive are 51-49 calls where experience provides the marginal edge. When things are going well, experience doesn't visibly matter. It matters precisely during the bumps, crises, and ambiguous situations where you need someone who has seen something similar before.
