Naval argues that trying to fill predefined roles is a trap borrowed from large company HR practices. When you interview marketing candidates and hire the best from that set, you're optimizing within a constrained pool rather than optimizing for genius. If you find someone truly exceptional who doesn't fit any current need, hire them anyway — great people identify problems and get involved regardless of their job description.

This inverts the standard hiring funnel. Instead of role → candidate pool → best fit, it becomes genius spotted → hire immediately → let them find their highest-leverage contribution. The constraint is keeping them interested, but the upside of having a genius on the team far outweighs the awkwardness of not having a neat job description. Small companies should never apply large-company HR frameworks like rigid roles and compensation brackets.
