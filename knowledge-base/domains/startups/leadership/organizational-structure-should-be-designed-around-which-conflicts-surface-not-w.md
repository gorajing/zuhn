Every organizational structure creates information channels that amplify certain types of conflicts while muting others. A functional org (engineering, sales, marketing) surfaces technical debt and specialization conflicts but suppresses product-level coordination problems. A product-line org does the reverse. The insight is that there is no conflict-free structure — the question is which conflicts you need to hear about given your current strategic priorities. Leaders should explicitly choose their structure based on which tensions are most dangerous to ignore, and then build supplementary mechanisms (cross-functional reviews, skip-levels, internal customer feedback) to partially compensate for the conflicts their structure naturally suppresses. This reframes reorgs from 'fixing dysfunction' to 'rotating which problems get executive attention.'
