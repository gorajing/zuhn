Most organizational design focuses on efficiency, reporting lines, and functional grouping. But a more powerful lens is to design for the conflicts you want to hear about. Every org structure creates natural escalation paths — some tensions surface to leadership while others get resolved (or suppressed) lower in the hierarchy. The conflicts that reach you are the ones you can actively manage.

This reframe suggests that when restructuring teams, the key question isn't 'what's the most efficient grouping?' but 'what trade-offs do I want to be making?' If product-engineering conflicts matter most, organize so those tensions surface naturally. If customer-vs-efficiency trade-offs are critical, structure reporting so those conflicts escalate. The org chart is essentially a filter for which problems reach decision-makers.
