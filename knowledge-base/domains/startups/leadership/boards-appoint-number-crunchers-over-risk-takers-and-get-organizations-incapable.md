Hershey, Kellogg, General Mills, and Kraft Heinz all replaced creative leaders with 'numbers guys' between 2011-2014. The new CEOs publicly derided intuition-based decision-making and legacy brand protection. The result: a corporate monoculture where executives see spreadsheets not humans, boards appoint optimizers over inventors, and organizations are built to hit quarterly targets but entirely incapable of creating anything new. Those who optimize for short-term metrics rise up the ladder; those who swing for home runs are seen as reckless.
