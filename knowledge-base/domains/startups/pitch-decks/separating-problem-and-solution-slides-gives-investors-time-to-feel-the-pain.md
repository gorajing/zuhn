Merging 'Problem' and 'Solution' onto a single slide is a common mistake that shortcuts the emotional and cognitive arc investors need to travel. When the solution immediately follows the problem statement, investors haven't had time to fully accept the severity or prevalence of the problem—so the solution feels less compelling than it deserves.

Separating them creates a deliberate pause: investors sit with the problem, potentially nodding along or mentally validating it from their own experience. Only then does the solution arrive, landing with more force. This is a structural application of the persuasion principle that agreement precedes acceptance—get the 'yes, that's a real problem' before asking for 'here's why our solution works.'
