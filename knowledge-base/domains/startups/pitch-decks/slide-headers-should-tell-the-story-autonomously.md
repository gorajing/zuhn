Most pitch decks use generic section headers—'Team,' 'Market,' 'Problem'—that require the founder to be present to interpret them. This creates a single-channel communication dependency: the deck alone conveys almost nothing.

Descriptive headers like 'A Team That's Built and Exited Together' or 'A $3B Market Growing 12% a Year' transform a navigation label into a claim. The slide header becomes the argument; the slide body becomes the proof. This means the deck works as a standalone artifact when shared via email, forwarded to partners, or reviewed without a live pitch—dramatically expanding the deck's persuasive surface area across the entire investor decision process.
