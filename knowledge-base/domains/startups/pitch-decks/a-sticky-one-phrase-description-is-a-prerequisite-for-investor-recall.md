Professional investors at large Demo Days or pitch events experience genuine pitch blur — not from inattention but from volume. The first sorting step isn't 'was this good?' but 'do I remember what this was?' A concrete, distinctive phrase anchors the memory.

Graham notes the phrase serves a dual function: it helps investors recall you, and the exercise of crafting it forces founders to achieve the focus that good startups require. If you can't construct a compelling one-sentence description, it often signals insufficient product focus — not just a communication gap.

The Hollywood 'x meets y' format and startup 'x of y' format both work by borrowing established mental models. This reduces cognitive load on investors: they understand the category immediately and can focus evaluation on whether you execute it well.
