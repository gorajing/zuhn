Watts and Strogatz's groundbreaking 1998 paper showed that in a network of 8 billion nodes where everyone knows their 100 nearest neighbors, the average path between any two people would be 40 million steps. But rewiring just 3 out of every 10,000 connections to be random shortcuts drops the average to six steps -- while preserving the local clustering that makes communities functional.

For founders and networkers, this means the ROI on 'shortcut' connections (people outside your normal circle -- attending random events, accepting unexpected invitations) is astronomically higher than deepening existing cluster connections. Steve Strogatz directly connects this to the 'strength of weak ties' finding: people disproportionately get jobs through acquaintances, not close friends. The actionable lesson: systematically put yourself in situations where shortcut links can form.
