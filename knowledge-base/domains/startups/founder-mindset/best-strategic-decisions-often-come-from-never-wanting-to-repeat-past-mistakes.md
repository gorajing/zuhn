When asked how he knew to acquire massive GPU capacity before the AI wave, Zuckerberg admitted it wasn't foresight about AI — it was trauma from being GPU-constrained while trying to catch up to TikTok with Reels. His operating principle was 'always have enough capacity to build something you can't see on the horizon yet,' which led him to double the GPU order beyond what Reels required.

This pattern — where what looks like strategic genius was actually a defensive reaction to past pain — appears repeatedly in his career decisions. Not selling Facebook for $1B wasn't sophisticated financial analysis; it was personal conviction that he'd just build another similar company anyway. The broader lesson is that founder 'intuition' is often just pattern-matching from operational scars. The best founders don't predict the future — they build enough slack into their resource allocation that they're prepared when the future arrives. This is a powerful argument for systematic over-investment in infrastructure capacity.
