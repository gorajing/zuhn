The biggest killer of ambitious projects isn't failure—it's never starting. When you label something as 'real work,' you apply finished-work standards to embryonic output and get discouraged. The reframing trick works because it genuinely changes what standards you apply.

Patrick Collison extends this further: making the tenuousness a feature ('yes, it's ridiculous, I'm just seeing how far this naive approach goes') creates psychological permission to explore. This pairs well with choosing media that allow fast iteration and low upfront commitment, creating a virtuous cycle where quick results sustain motivation through the ugly phase.
