The widely-cited statistic that the average millionaire has 7 income streams is technically true but causally backwards. Hormozi argues people observe the end state (wealthy people with diversified holdings) and prescribe it as the starting strategy, when in reality those people concentrated intensely on one opportunity first.

The analogy of filling cups is powerful: spreading 10% attention across 7 opportunities means none ever 'overflow' into profitability. A competitor putting 100% into one cup will always outpace you. The sequence matters — you make wealth through concentration (one avatar, one product, one channel) and preserve wealth through diversification. Trying to start diversified is arrogant because it assumes your divided amateur attention can compete with someone's focused effort. This applies beyond business to skill acquisition, career development, and any domain where depth beats breadth early on.
