Arthur didn't invent a new category. He scrolled the App Store's top grossing list, noticed affirmation apps were everywhere with identical features, and built the same thing targeted at Nordic countries with seasonal depression — warm UI, cozy mascot, quotes acknowledging dark winters. The top three competitors were literally identical (same quotes, same design, same everything), meaning there was proven demand with no differentiation.

This approach eliminates idea risk almost entirely. The market is already validated — you just need a sharper angle. His previous AI startup failed because they never talked to users and ignored market fit. This time, he started with known demand and layered on a specific niche. The pattern: find a category with lazy incumbents, identify an underserved segment with a real pain point, and build the same core product with positioning that speaks directly to them.
