Stanford founders once told Dalton they were taught that all consumer startups suck and only B2B SaaS works — as if someone could give them slots A, B, C, D and if they filled them correctly, success pops out. This 'MBAification' of startup thinking comes from business schools originally designed to train middle managers for General Motors. In that world, there is a guidebook — how to get in, what to do, how to get promoted.

Startups are the polar opposite: extremely high-risk, irregular, plans change constantly, and luck plays a huge role. There's no video game cheat code. The people who made it through the maze gritted through it — no one gave them an easy hack. Even a satirical guidebook for writing a number-one single only worked once (Chumbawamba's Tubthumping). Rules and best practices won't save you. You still have to do something extremely hard at the limit of your abilities.
