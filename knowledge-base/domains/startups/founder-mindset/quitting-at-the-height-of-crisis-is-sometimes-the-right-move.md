Dohmke quit his job at Bosch during the height of the 2008 financial crisis. This seems crazy, but the timing worked in his favor: while everyone else was focused on stability, he had less competition for freelance development work as the iPhone SDK had just launched and only Apple could build native apps.

The broader pattern: economic downturns reduce the number of people willing to take entrepreneurial risks, which means less competition for early adopters, talent, and market attention. The founders who start during crises often build more disciplined companies because they never had the luxury of excess capital.
