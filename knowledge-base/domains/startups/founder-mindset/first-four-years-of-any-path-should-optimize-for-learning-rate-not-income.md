Hormozi frames both the college path and the career path as fundamentally the same choice: invest in learning capacity now to earn later. The college student pays $1,000/week to learn; the career starter gets paid minimum wage to learn. In both cases, the money flow is secondary to skill acquisition rate. Hormozi himself took minimum wage at a gym after leaving a white-collar consulting job because the learning rate was higher.

This reframes the college decision from 'education vs. money' to 'which path has higher learning velocity for my specific situation?' The person who treats their first four years as a professional student — whether formally enrolled or not — and invests evenings in additional skill-building will dramatically outperform someone optimizing for immediate income. The practical move: spend money on 1-on-1 time with skilled practitioners, courses, and deliberate practice rather than lifestyle inflation.
