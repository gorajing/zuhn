When asked about OpenAI's master plan, Altman is blunt: 'There is no master plan beyond making great models and shipping good products.' He describes having 'unwavering confidence' in their ability to build products people love and great models, but takes tactical steps only one or two at a time. The products they'll build next year they're 'probably not even thinking about right now.'

He contrasts this with people who describe brilliant backwards-chained strategies: 'I have never seen those people really massively succeed.' This is particularly relevant for AI companies where the capability frontier shifts so rapidly that long-range product planning becomes obsolete within quarters. The implication for founders: deep conviction in direction combined with tactical nimbleness beats detailed multi-year roadmaps.
