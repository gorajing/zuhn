The mathematical asymmetry of losses versus gains means a 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover. This makes mistake-avoidance disproportionately powerful for wealth accumulation — each avoided catastrophic behavior preserves the base on which future returns compound.

For founders, this translates to: fix your burn rate before optimizing revenue, eliminate co-founder conflict patterns before seeking new partnerships, and remove process bottlenecks before adding new initiatives. The compounding math favors subtraction over addition at every stage.
