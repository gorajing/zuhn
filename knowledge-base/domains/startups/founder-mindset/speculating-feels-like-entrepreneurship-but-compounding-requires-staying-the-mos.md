The speculator confuses boldness with restlessness: crypto, NFTs, day trading, new courses — never sticking with anything long enough for compounding to work. Codie Sanchez spent her 20s jumping from asset management to fashion startup to consulting to side hustles, building nothing. 'Shiny doesn't compound.'

The Barber & Odean study is definitive: among 66,000 individual investors, the most active traders underperformed the market by almost 7% per year. The least active investors performed best. The speculator trades movement while the builder profits from time. The remedy is the '12-month commitment contract': pick one business or opportunity (not the sexiest — the one actually making money), commit in writing for 12 months, and refuse every shiny object until it expires. Compounding only works if you stay.
