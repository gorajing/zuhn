Graham observes that after four years of working with founders through Y Combinator, relentless resourcefulness can surprisingly often be taught — not to everyone, but to many. The key insight is that large institutions and schools actively suppress this trait; the recipe for success in big companies involves obedience and alliance-building, which is antithetical to resourcefulness.

This has a selection implication: the pool of potential founders is larger than it appears because many capable people have been shaped by environments that discouraged the very quality they need. Young people who have always been under authority are particularly likely to have this latent capacity. The practical takeaway is that founder potential should be evaluated not by current behavior but by how someone responds when constraints are removed.
