Max wasted 3 months hoping someone would validate his ideas. The
counterintuitive realization that changed everything: instead of
seeking confirmation, hunt for reasons why something WON'T work. If
you cannot find a reason after genuinely trying, you may have
something real. This falsification mindset is the opposite of what
most founders do — most founders fall in love with their idea and
seek confirming evidence. Max failed 10 times before landing on
Gumloop. Each failure was fast because he was actively trying to
disprove the idea rather than defend it. The speed of iteration is
directly proportional to your willingness to kill ideas. The longer
you spend hoping, the longer you delay finding the idea that
actually works.
