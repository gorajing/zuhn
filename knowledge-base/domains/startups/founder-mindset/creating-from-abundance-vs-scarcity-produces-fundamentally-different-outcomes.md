Hormozi relays a conversation with a friend who sold his company for $200 million, who observed that '99% of people are plugged into the matrix' — operating from lack and desire to extract money from others. The rare alternative is creating 'just for the sake of making it, like art,' which produces qualitatively different results. Hormozi cites his own book as an attempt to create from this space.

This maps to research on intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation: intrinsically motivated creators make different decisions about quality, generosity with information, and long-term thinking. They optimize for the work itself rather than the transaction, which paradoxically often produces better commercial outcomes. The practical application is a diagnostic question before any creative or business endeavor: 'Am I making this because I need something from the audience, or because I want to give something to them?' The answer shapes everything from product design to marketing tone to pricing strategy.
