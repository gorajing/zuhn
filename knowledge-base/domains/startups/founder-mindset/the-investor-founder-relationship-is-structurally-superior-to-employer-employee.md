Graham argues the employer-employee relationship retains 'master-servant DNA' that creates resentment on both sides — employees feel infantilized, bosses find it easier to do things themselves. His controlled experiment (doing identical work at Yahoo post-acquisition vs. his own startup) proved the relationship structure itself changed his behavior, pushing buttons he'd 'forgotten he had.'

The investor-founder model preserves all the pain of hard problems but removes the resentment. A startup is 'one's own' in two critical senses: creatively and economically. This dual ownership is what open source demonstrates at scale — people working for love on their own projects routinely surpass those working for money on assigned ones. The implication is that more economic relationships should be structured as investments rather than employment.
