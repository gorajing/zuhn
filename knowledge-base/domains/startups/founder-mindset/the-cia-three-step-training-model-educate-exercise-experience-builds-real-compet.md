CIA's training model follows three steps in rapid succession. First, classroom education of a skill or concept. Second, immediately after, exercises and role-plays to practice the skill in controlled conditions. Third, students are deployed into the real world to apply the skill where they operate alone and are the only ones who know if they succeeded or failed.

The power of this model lies in two elements: the tight feedback loop (learn-practice-apply within hours, not weeks) and the psychological ownership of knowing your own results. When you return to the classroom, it is up to you whether to seek help or celebrate — building both self-reliance and institutional loyalty. This three-step model is directly applicable to any high-stakes skill development.
