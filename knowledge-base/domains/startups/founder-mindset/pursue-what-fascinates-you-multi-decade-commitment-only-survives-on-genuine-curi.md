Playter credits Marc Raibert's most lasting lesson: pursue your curiosity, do something because you love it, don't worry if it's going to make money. This philosophy translated into a company with remarkably low attrition — people come and stay because the pursuit itself is fulfilling.

The roboticist happiness effect he describes (among the happiest PhDs he's met) connects to the physical, tangible nature of the work — bringing things to life. For founders choosing problems to work on: the ability to sustain multi-decade commitment depends on genuine fascination with the problem domain, not just the business opportunity.
