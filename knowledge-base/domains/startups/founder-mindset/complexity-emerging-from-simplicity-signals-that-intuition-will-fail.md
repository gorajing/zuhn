The numbers 26 and 27 differ by just 1, yet their Collatz sequences diverge dramatically: 26 reaches a maximum of 40 and takes 10 steps, while 27 climbs to 9,232 (higher than Everest in the altitude metaphor) and takes 111 steps. The seed 9,663 reaches 27 million. No amount of intuition about 'nearby' inputs helps predict behavior.

This is a pure demonstration of sensitive dependence on initial conditions in a discrete system. For founders and decision-makers, the lesson is that in complex systems (markets, networks, user behavior), similar-looking starting conditions can produce radically different outcomes. When you're operating in a domain with this property, small experiments and rapid iteration beat grand strategic planning, because your predictions about what will happen are fundamentally unreliable.
