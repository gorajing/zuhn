Shade balls exist specifically to block sunlight from reservoirs, yet even in their tightest possible packing configuration, they leave 9% of the surface exposed. This isn't an engineering failure — it's a mathematical certainty of sphere packing geometry. Light still penetrates through the cracks.

For founders and operators, this is a critical mental model. Every coverage strategy — market coverage, test coverage, risk mitigation, customer support — has an irreducible gap determined by the shape of the solution itself. You can optimize placement all you want, but the geometry of your approach sets a hard ceiling. Recognizing these structural gaps early lets you either design complementary approaches for the remaining coverage or honestly communicate limitations rather than promising 100% coverage that geometry forbids.
