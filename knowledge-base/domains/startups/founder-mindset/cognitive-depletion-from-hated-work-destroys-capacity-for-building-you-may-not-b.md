The 'trapped employee' isn't lazy — they're cognitively depleted. Research on cognitive depletion shows that repetitive tasks that violate your values drain your brain's actual capacity. You can't learn new skills, execute long-term plans, or build on nights and weekends because you literally don't have the processing power left.

Codie Sanchez stayed for 12 years in a seven-figure finance job she hated. The fix isn't 'just quit' — it's building an exit ramp. Calculate 6 months of basic expenses as your 'freedom fund.' Set up automatic transfers every paycheck. Once you have a few months saved, everything changes — you're there by choice. Write down your 'liberation day' and build toward it. The shift from trapped to choosing transforms not just your finances but your cognitive capacity to think about money differently.
