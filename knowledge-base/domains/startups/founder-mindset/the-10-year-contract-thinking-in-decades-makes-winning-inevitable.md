Hormozi's core mental model: people overestimate what they can accomplish in a year and underestimate what they can accomplish in a decade. He asks new employees: 'Would you sign a contract for zero income for 10 years if on year 10 you add two zeros to your net worth?' Almost everyone says yes. His response: 'Then act like it.' The shift from optimizing for fast money to making decade-scale bets changed his trajectory from twice-bankrupt to $100M.

The mechanism: decade-thinking eliminates the temptation to cash in current potential for short-term gains (taking $100K/year job vs $35K/year internship that 10x's earning potential in 3 years). It reframes every decision through 'what would make it unreasonable that I DON'T 10x in 10 years?' This naturally leads to reasonable, compounding bets rather than high-variance gambles. His summary: 'Money loves speed, wealth loves time, poverty loves indecision.'
