The conventional wisdom of 'work-life balance' assumes a steady-state life. Hormozi argues this is wrong for people trying to escape constrained circumstances — balance is a luxury of the already-established. Instead, he proposes 'seasonal thinking': accept radical imbalance for a defined 36-month window, with the explicit understanding that this is temporary.

The framework extends beyond personal finance into startup strategy. Early-stage founders who try to maintain balance often fail to achieve escape velocity — the minimum threshold of effort/capital/traction needed to become self-sustaining. The key nuance is that this must be time-bounded and intentional, not an open-ended grind. Hormozi also introduces 'family-unit balance' as distinct from 'individual balance' — partners can specialize (one on career, one on childcare) so the family as a unit is balanced even when individuals aren't.
