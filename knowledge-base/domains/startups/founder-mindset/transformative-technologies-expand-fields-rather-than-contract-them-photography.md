The essay traces photography from the 1839 daguerreotype through calotype mass printing, the Kodak Brownie consumer camera, 35mm street photography, Polaroid instant film, and smartphone cameras. Each leap didn't replace prior ecosystems — it layered on top while spawning entirely new ones: photojournalism, darkroom culture, consumer film labs, instant print shops, and eventually billion-user image platforms.

The mechanism is specific: a transformative technology changes what's possible, which changes what people attempt, which creates demand for things that didn't exist before, which creates businesses and markets to meet that demand. This cascade pattern has repeated for nearly 200 years in photography alone.

For founders, this means the correct response to AI disruption is not to defend existing territory but to explore the newly created territory — which history shows is almost always larger than what came before.
