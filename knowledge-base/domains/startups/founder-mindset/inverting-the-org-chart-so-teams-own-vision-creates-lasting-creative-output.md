Paananen's founding insight came from observing that successful game companies tend to shift control from game developers to upper management over time, killing creative output. Supercell explicitly inverted this: individual game teams ('cells') own the vision, leadership exists to serve them, and the company name itself comes from these teams being 'super cells.'

The culture was tested immediately: after raising Series A partly on the promise of their first Facebook game Gunshine, they killed it and pivoted the entire platform strategy from Facebook to mobile. Rather than treating this as a failure, the team lead gathered the whole company to share learnings, and Paananen brought champagne. This tradition -- celebrating kills with champagne -- reframed failures as experiments: 'Sometimes the hypothesis proves true, sometimes false, but in either case you learn something new.' The result: Clash of Clans, a game industry veterans said 'why would anyone play this,' grew from 10K to 100M DAU in months.
