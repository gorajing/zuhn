The essay identifies a emerging pattern where workers maintain corporate personas while building parallel systems of actual value — developers shipping products in the afternoon, marketers running agencies from corporate desks, consultants automating deliverables to free time for side projects. The critical psychological shift is from identity ('I am a Growth Hacker') to utility ('this role funds my real work').

This reframe resolves the cognitive dissonance that makes corporate work psychologically corrosive. Once you stop expecting meaning from the role, you can extract maximum value from its infrastructure: steady income, health insurance, professional network, enterprise tools, and schedule predictability. The founder mindset isn't about quitting — it's about seeing employment as a resource to be optimized rather than a purpose to be fulfilled.
