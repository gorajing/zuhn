Reid Hoffman challenges the 'founder as superperson' myth by arguing that no one scores 10/10 on every entrepreneurial skill. Instead, great founders demonstrate the ability to navigate apparent paradoxes: being both persistent and flexible, holding both belief and fear, focusing both internally and externally. The key isn't resolving these tensions permanently but developing the judgment to know when to lean 90% one way versus 80% the other.

This reframes founder evaluation from a checklist of skills to a meta-capability: adaptive judgment under uncertainty. Hoffman uses the metaphor of 'crossing uneven ground in a fog' — you can't pre-plan the exact path, but you can develop the pattern-recognition to adjust your balance with each step. This is why he looks for 'infinite learning curve' in founders — each entrepreneurial situation is unique, and the ability to learn and adapt the new pattern matters more than mastering the last one.
