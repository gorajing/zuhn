The math of earning vs owning reveals a stark asymmetry. Earning $200K/year requires a full decade of saving 100% of after-tax income to reach $1M liquid net worth — obviously impossible with living expenses. Meanwhile, building a business generating $250K/year in owner-independent profit can sell at a 4x multiple, achieving $1M net worth as soon as the business reaches that profitability threshold.

This reframes the millionaire goal from 'how do I earn more salary' to 'how do I build something with equity value.' The key variable isn't income level — it's whether time works for or against you. With earned income, time erodes wealth through taxes and expenses. With owned equity, time compounds value through business growth and valuation multiples. This is why Hormozi emphasizes that the fastest path to wealth is ownership, not employment — even at identical income levels.
