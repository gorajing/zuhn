Stoute illustrates this with his entry into the beauty business through Carol's Daughter (later sold to L'Oreal). He knew nothing about beauty as an industry, but he identified a problem he could solve. The fearless move was entering the space despite not understanding all its elements, embracing ignorance rather than being paralyzed by it.

The framework distinguishes between reckless fearlessness and strategic fearlessness. Strategic fearlessness means: acknowledge what you don't know, find freedom in saying 'I don't know,' and then systematically learn what you need while executing. This builds 'muscle memory' — the capacity to enter new domains increases with each successful entry. The advantage of not knowing an industry is that you're unburdened by assumptions incumbents take for granted. But this only works when paired with genuine humility and willingness to learn, not arrogance about being an outsider.
