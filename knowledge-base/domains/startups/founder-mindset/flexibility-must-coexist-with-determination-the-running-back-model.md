The 'don't give up on your dreams' framing of determination is actively harmful in startups because the environment is too unpredictable for rigid goal pursuit. The running back metaphor captures the correct synthesis: absolute commitment to forward progress combined with moment-to-moment willingness to change direction entirely.

Daniel Gross exemplifies this — he applied to YC with a bad ecommerce idea, immediately pivoted when told to try something else, went through two more ideas, and landed on Greplin with only days of work before Demo Day. The speed of his pivoting wasn't indecisiveness; it was tactical flexibility in service of the strategic goal. This suggests that founders should hold their vision loosely while holding their ambition tightly.
