Altman reframes the AGI question: maybe we never build AGI, but if AI makes humans dramatically more productive and creative, that's still an enormous win. He describes being excited about a world where AI is 'an extension of human will and an amplifier of our abilities.'

This is a pragmatic framing for founders and builders: don't bet everything on the AGI timeline. Build tools that make humans 10x more productive today. The chess analogy is instructive — AI didn't kill chess, it made it more popular. Humans plus AI tools may remain more interesting than AI alone.
