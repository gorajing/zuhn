Brodeur-Urbas worked at Microsoft and found it actively unhelpful for his startup. The only value was the logo on his resume providing default credibility. Everything he does at Gumloop is the exact opposite of how big tech works.

His stronger claim: the years between 21-23 with no responsibilities are irreplaceable, and spending them logging into a 9-to-5, fixing tickets, and logging off is wasting your most valuable asset—time with no obligations. The common 'I'll learn at big tech first' plan is cope—most people who go to big tech get golden handcuffs and never leave. The learning that matters for startups can only happen by actually building a startup.
