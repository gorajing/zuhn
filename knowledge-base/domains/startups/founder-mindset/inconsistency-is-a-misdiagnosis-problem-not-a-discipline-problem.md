The standard framing treats inconsistency as a character flaw requiring more discipline, accountability systems, or motivation. Hormozi reframes it as a cognitive error: ambitious people encounter the inevitable difficulty of a pursuit and interpret it as evidence they're on the wrong path, when it's actually evidence they're on any path at all.

This reframe shifts the intervention from 'how do I force myself to keep going' to 'how do I correctly interpret the signals I'm receiving.' Once you understand that difficulty is path-invariant — present in every worthwhile pursuit — the urge to restart dissolves. The fix isn't more willpower; it's better pattern recognition about what difficulty actually means.
