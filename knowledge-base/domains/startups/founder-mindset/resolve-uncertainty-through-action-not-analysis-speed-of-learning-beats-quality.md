The Pensive founder left a 10-year career at a recognized Korean educational institution to go to the US because he concluded that 'resolving uncertainty quickly is much more important than feeling anxious about it.' This manifested in concrete behaviors: going to meet property managers during rainstorms, waking at 5 AM to source food market ingredients, building and killing products in rapid succession.

The underlying principle is informational: in genuinely uncertain environments (new markets, new technology, new geography), the quality of your mental model is bottlenecked by real-world data, not thinking time. Each failed pivot generated more actionable information than months of market research could have. After 8 pivots, the founders had a visceral understanding of what 'founder-market fit' felt like versus what 'interesting idea' felt like.

This is the action-oriented corollary to the lean startup method — it's not just about MVPs, it's about making your entire life an information-gathering operation where the cost of a failed experiment is always lower than the cost of not knowing.
