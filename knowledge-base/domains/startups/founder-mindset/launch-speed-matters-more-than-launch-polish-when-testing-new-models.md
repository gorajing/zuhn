Graham describes a pattern of speed over polish at every stage: deciding to start YC during a single walk home, recruiting co-founders within days, launching the Summer Founders Program fast because 'students would already be making plans,' and moving to California in a last-minute decision with barely enough time to get space — founders had to avoid touching wet-painted walls at the first dinner.

Yet the structure established in that rushed first summer 'is still almost identical to what it was.' This suggests that speed doesn't just help you beat competitors — it helps you discover the right structure through real-world contact faster than deliberation would. Graham notes they 'did what we're always telling startups to do: we launched fast.' The constraint of time forced decisions that turned out to be nearly optimal.
