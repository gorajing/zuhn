Graham shares that after nearly a decade running YC, he learned to keep a 'completely open mind' about which startups in each batch would become stars. Some founders arrived confident they would ace YC just as they'd aced every prior test. Others arrived wondering what mistake had caused their admission. Neither attitude predicted outcomes.

He draws a parallel to military recruits, where swaggering newcomers are no more likely to prove tough than quiet ones — because the actual tests are so different from anything in their previous experience. Smartness is easy to evaluate in 10 minutes ('hit a few tennis balls over the net'), but toughness and ambition — the traits that matter most — are nearly impossible to predict. This has a practical implication: if you're merely unsure whether you can do it, uncertainty alone shouldn't stop you. The only way to find out is to try. Fear is not a signal of inability.
