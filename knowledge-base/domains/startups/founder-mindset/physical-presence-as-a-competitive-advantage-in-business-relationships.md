Stoute built 'getting on the plane' as a core company value because he observed a consistent pattern: physical presence created outsized returns in business relationships. The act of traveling to meet someone face-to-face — even for a 15-minute conversation — communicates commitment and respect in a way that calls and emails cannot replicate.

The compounding effect is powerful: mediocre ideas presented in person often advance further than better ideas pitched remotely, because the in-person dialogue opens unexpected directions and builds trust. When competing ideas are roughly equal, the person who made the effort to show up wins the prioritization. This is especially relevant in an era where remote communication is default — the willingness to physically appear has become a stronger differentiating signal precisely because fewer people do it.
