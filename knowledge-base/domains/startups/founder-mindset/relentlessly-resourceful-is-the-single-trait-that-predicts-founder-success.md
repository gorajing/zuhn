Graham distills founder evaluation to a single axis: hapless vs. relentlessly resourceful. Bad founders, regardless of intelligence, get overwhelmed by events and give up. Good founders have 'a healthy respect for reality' but find ways to make things happen anyway. This is distinct from mere stubbornness — it includes adaptability and reality-testing.

The practical value of this frame is its diagnostic power. When evaluating founders (or potential co-founders, or hires for early-stage roles), you can ask: when this person hit obstacles, did they find creative paths around them, or did they get stuck and rationalize why it wasn't possible? The trait is observable in how people describe past challenges. Relentlessly resourceful people tell stories about unexpected pivots and creative solutions; hapless people tell stories about external forces that prevented success.
