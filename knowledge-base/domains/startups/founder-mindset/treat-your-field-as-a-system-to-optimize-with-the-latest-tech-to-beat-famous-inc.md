De Moor frames the cybersecurity AI race through the 1575 Battle of Nagashino: Oda Nobunaga, a minor warlord who treated warfare as a system to be optimized and used the very latest guns, defeated the Takeda clan whose famous cavalry was thought invincible. He argues the same dynamic now holds — those with AI will win regardless of incumbent reputation. The transferable startup principle is that reputation, accumulated craft, and historical dominance are liabilities, not protection, when a new technology resets the rules: incumbents over-trust the skills that earned their status, while the upstart who systematizes the problem and adopts the frontier tooling compounds an advantage. The actionable read for founders is to look for arenas where incumbents are coasting on prestige and a new capability lets you treat the whole contest as an optimization problem they aren't running.
