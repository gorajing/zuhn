Most competitive dimensions (capital, talent, looks, IQ) are positional goods with one winner. 'Caring the most' is different: it's an attitude that any actor can claim without displacing others, and customers reliably detect it. Itzler argues that in a world where most operators optimize for spreadsheet outcomes, visible effort and emotional investment becomes an undervalued asymmetric moat. The practical move for founders without capital, credentials, or networks is to compete where the crowd is thinnest — on how much they obviously, visibly care about the customer's outcome.
