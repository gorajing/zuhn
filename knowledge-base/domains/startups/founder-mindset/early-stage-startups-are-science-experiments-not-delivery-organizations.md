The instinct to build process, hierarchy, and delivery cadences is often triggered by funding events, team growth, or investor pressure — before the underlying business model is validated. This is precisely backwards. Pre-PMF, the highest-value activity is rapid hypothesis testing: find what's true about the market, not what can be reliably shipped.

Founders who conflate operational maturity with progress often over-invest in infrastructure, headcount, and execution rigor before they've found something worth executing on. The correct posture is to hold organizational structure loosely and ruthlessly prioritize learning velocity over output velocity. This is uncomfortable because it produces less visible 'work' — but it's the only mode that generates the signal needed to make the delivery investment worth anything.
