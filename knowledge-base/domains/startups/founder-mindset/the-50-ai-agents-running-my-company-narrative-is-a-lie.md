Brodeur-Urbas runs an automation platform processing 4M workflows daily and directly calls out the Twitter narrative of effortless AI automation. Most people claiming 'I work one hour a week and make $10M' are lying—they're selling hope, not truth. The actual path to building with AI is not shortcutting the hard work.

The irony: the person selling the 'skip the hard work' course is the one doing the hard work of marketing. They found a way to print money by selling a fantasy. Real AI automation requires deep understanding of specific business problems, careful orchestration, and constant iteration. Gumloop's success comes from letting domain experts (marketers, salespeople, ops people) automate their own work—not from magic AI agents that run themselves.
