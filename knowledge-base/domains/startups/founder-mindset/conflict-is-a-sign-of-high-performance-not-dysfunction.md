Stoute reframes conflict as a positive organizational signal: it arises when two high-performing people share the same goal but see different paths to achieve it. The key conditions are right intent (perfection, success, growth) and mutual respect (challenging ideas without disrespecting people).

The practical framework: put the company at the center, remove ego, and ask what is the best idea for the organization regardless of who proposed it. Leaders must model this by framing conflict explicitly as positive and demonstrating that disagreement does not equal poor performance. Organizations that can process conflict smoothly and extract the best ideas from competing perspectives consistently outperform those where disagreement is suppressed.
