Hormozi argues that early-career workers often make the mistake of optimizing for salary when they should be optimizing for learning. He illustrates this with a candidate who demanded an extra $3,000/year for a YouTube editor role, missing that the skills and network gained from the position would far exceed any salary premium available elsewhere.

The core insight is that earning capacity compounds — a 2-3x increase in your ability to generate income from skill investment in your 20s will produce returns that dwarf the $10-25K salary differences people negotiate for. The people willing to pay a premium for your current skills have nothing to add to your development; the ones who pay less but operate at a higher level are the ones who accelerate your growth.
