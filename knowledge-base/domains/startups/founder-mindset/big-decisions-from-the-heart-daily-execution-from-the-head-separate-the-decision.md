Ahana's framework for irreversible decisions (leaving the US, starting a company, pivoting the business model): make the big decision with your heart — find what truly fulfills you. But once that decision is made, put your heart aside entirely. It's all brain, discipline, and data from there. You cannot allow yourself to re-question the decision on bad days.

This creates an asymmetric emotional architecture: the 'why' is emotional and purpose-driven (it sustains you through crying at 11pm in the office), while the 'what' and 'how' are purely rational. Most founders either make emotional decisions AND execute emotionally (leading to constant second-guessing), or try to make purely rational decisions about inherently emotional life choices (leading to regret and lack of conviction). The separation is the key: heart for direction, head for execution. This contradicts the purely data-driven founder archetype while also contradicting the purely passion-driven one.
