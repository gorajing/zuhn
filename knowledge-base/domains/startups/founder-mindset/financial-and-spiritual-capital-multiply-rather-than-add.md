Itzler's mental model frames business as two axes that multiply: the financial side (revenue, profit, valuation) and the spiritual side (culture, customer care, philanthropy, integrity). Because the relationship is multiplicative, a large financial number paired with zero spiritual capital equals zero, and vice versa. The practical implication is that pursuing revenue at the expense of culture or ethics doesn't just come with a moral cost — it mathematically caps the long-run outcome. Founders should treat spiritual capital as a coefficient on financial capital, not as a soft concern to address later once the business is 'successful'.
