Teddy Atlas grew up as a doctor's son but ran with street kids, did robberies, and got into constant fights. He realized that 'you don't have to be poor to be poor' -- his father's total dedication to medicine meant emotional absence at home, and the resulting hunger for family and belonging drove Teddy toward dangerous substitutes.

This maps directly to founder psychology and team dynamics. Many driven founders come from materially comfortable backgrounds but experienced emotional deprivation -- absent parents, lack of validation, disconnection. The hunger that drives startup intensity often isn't financial but emotional. Recognizing the actual source of your drive (connection-seeking vs. money-seeking vs. validation-seeking) determines whether that drive can be sustainably channeled or will eventually become self-destructive.
