Grind culture tells you that if you're not bleeding, you're not trying hard enough. The martyr says 'I'm doing this for my family' when really they can't let go of control, or they're scared to stop because stopping means what they're doing might not be working. Codie Sanchez lived this — bragging about 80-hour weeks in finance until her back gave out.

The data is clear: burnout leads to reduced productivity and efficiency. A 10% productivity drop for a high earner means tens of thousands lost annually. Burning out five years early costs ~$300K/year on average. The remedy is a 'suffering audit': write down everything you're doing for others, ask who actually asked you to do it, and check if it's creating wealth or just being busy. Most martyrs find 70% of their sacrifices are self-imposed. Apply the 3Ds — delegate, delete, or do it differently.
