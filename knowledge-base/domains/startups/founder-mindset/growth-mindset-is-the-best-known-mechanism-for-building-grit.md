Duckworth acknowledges a significant gap: while grit is the strongest predictor of success, science knows remarkably little about how to build it. The best idea she's encountered is Carol Dweck's growth mindset — the belief that the ability to learn is not fixed but changes with effort.

Dweck's research shows that when kids learn about neuroplasticity — how the brain literally changes and grows in response to challenge — they become much more likely to persevere when they fail. The mechanism is reframing failure from a permanent identity ('I'm not smart enough') to a temporary state ('I haven't learned this yet'). For founders, this connects directly to the startup grind: the belief that you can learn and improve through effort is what sustains perseverance through the inevitable failures of building a company. Grit without growth mindset is just stubbornness; growth mindset without grit is just optimism. The combination is what drives sustained performance.
