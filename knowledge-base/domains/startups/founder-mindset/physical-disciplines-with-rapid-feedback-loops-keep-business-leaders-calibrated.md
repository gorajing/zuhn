Zuckerberg identifies a fundamental problem in business: the feedback cycle is so long (months to years for products, especially hardware) that leaders lose calibration on what can be forced through with grit versus what requires finesse or redirecting.

Physical disciplines like jiu-jitsu, surfing, and MMA provide compressed feedback loops where you learn instantly if you're trying to force something that won't work. You can't push through a wave going the wrong direction, and you can't muscle through a submission against a skilled opponent. This rapid physical feedback trains an intuition that directly transfers to business — knowing when to grind and when to adapt is the differentiator between leaders who burn out teams on lost causes and those who redirect energy effectively.
