This is the fundamental tension in all startup advice: you must simultaneously be impatient and ambitious — feel like your hair is on fire — while also holding the idea that doing great work takes a long time. Founders constantly struggle with this apparent contradiction, and many give up because they can't reconcile the two.

The practical approach is to make it serial rather than simultaneous. When starting a company, lean into the urgency — move fast, ignore how long it will take. Once committed and building, shift to the patient mindset — accept that hard problems won't yield to speed alone. You have to get to that second phase. The founders who can't make this transition either never start (too patient) or quit too early (too impatient).
