McConaughey critiques both extremes: 'dream it, you can do it' is a half-assed horrible thing to tell someone if not paired with practical action. Equally, 'hope means nothing' misses that dreams provide necessary direction. The key insight is that siloing either one fails — if you only dream, you live in illusion; if you only grind, you lack direction.

He learned pragmatism first from his parents, who taught him to work up the ladder, and then earned the right to dream by building a foundation of competence. The parenting lesson is significant: the happiest moment for a parent is when a child doesn't ask permission to chase a dream but announces they're doing it — because that demonstrates the child has built enough pragmatic foundation to have the courage to leap.
