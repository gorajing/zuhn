Bornich built 1X (humanoid robots) and says the grind narrative is real—there will be many dark days. But the founders who survive are the ones who chose a problem exciting enough to make the dark days worth it. His specific cheat code: 'pick a problem that really excites people. We do humanoid robots. It's really freaking cool.'

This isn't just about passion—it's a strategic advantage. Exciting problems attract better talent, generate more organic attention, and create intrinsic motivation that survives external setbacks. The founder who finds their problem genuinely exciting will outlast the one who chose a problem because of market size or investor interest.
