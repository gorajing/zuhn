Brianna Wiest's insight: 'Human beings are guided by comfort — they reject what doesn't feel familiar, even if objectively better.' This explains why founders stay in draining jobs, keep bad clients, or endlessly polish portfolios instead of cold-pitching. The brain interprets any unfamiliar situation as a threat, even positive ones.

The antidote is not dramatic life overhauls (which shock the nervous system and trigger rebound). Instead: 15 minutes a day on your dream business. One rough draft of a cold pitch. One small step. Confidence comes from making promises to yourself and keeping them. Slowly, the new behaviors become your comfort zone, and what was once terrifying becomes normal.
