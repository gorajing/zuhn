Hormozi introduces the concept of a 'season of no' — not a permanent lifestyle change, but a finite period where you say no to everything that isn't the single priority. This framing matters psychologically: telling yourself 'this is just for right now' makes extreme focus sustainable in a way that 'I must always be disciplined' does not.

The key insight is that true prioritization means two things literally cannot be equally important. If you spend 30 minutes on A and 30 minutes on B, you've said they're equally important regardless of what you claim. If something is the priority, everything else gets a no. This is the operational definition of prioritization — not ranking things, but actively refusing everything below #1.
