YC tends to fund real startups — teams that lean toward building and shipping rather than performing. As a result, the most impactful advice YC gives is not about building better products (these teams already excel at that) but about sales, marketing, and revenue generation — precisely the activities these founders would naturally avoid.

This is a general principle about advice: its value is inversely proportional to how likely you were to do it anyway. 'Only hire A-players' is useless — no one was planning to hire bad people. But 'you, the deeply technical founder, should personally sell your product' — that's the kind of uncomfortable, counter-instinct advice that actually changes outcomes. When evaluating advice, ask whether it pushes you toward something you'd naturally resist.
