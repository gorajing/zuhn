Hormozi argues that if you could have only one advantage in business, it should be a 'starving crowd' — customers with acute, urgent pain who are actively seeking a solution. This is more valuable than a superior product, better marketing, or stronger execution. A mediocre hot dog stand outside a stadium after a game will outsell a gourmet restaurant in an empty neighborhood.

The framework for evaluating markets includes: growing (vs. shrinking), easy to target (can you find and reach them?), pain-driven (vs. desire-driven, since pain motivates action more reliably), and willingness to pay. This inverts the common startup advice to 'build something people want' — instead, find people who desperately want something and build that. The distinction matters because demand-first thinking eliminates the hardest part of business: convincing people to care.
