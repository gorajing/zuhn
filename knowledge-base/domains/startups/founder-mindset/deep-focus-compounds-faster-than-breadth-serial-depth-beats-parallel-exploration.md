Hormozi argues that the biggest returns come from going deep rather than wide. The compounding effect of expertise, reputation, and systems only kicks in after sustained focus in a single area. Early diversification resets the compounding clock each time.

This maps directly to the startup principle of staying focused on one product and one market until achieving clear product-market fit. The temptation to diversify is strongest right when compounding is about to accelerate — making premature pivots one of the most expensive mistakes founders make.
