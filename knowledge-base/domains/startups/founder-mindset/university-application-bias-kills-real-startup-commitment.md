Rakhmetzhanov identifies a destructive pattern among high school student founders: they build products and launch them but always with a safety net of university applications. This split focus prevents the total commitment that separates successful startups from side projects.

His own journey was the opposite—he dropped out of 11th grade and relocated to San Francisco at 18. When he chased Paul Graham to his car at YC, it wasn't a calculated move for a university essay—it was genuine desperation from someone who had burned all bridges. That authenticity and total commitment is what attracted PG's investment. The lesson: half-measures in entrepreneurship produce half-results.
