Senkut's career is a chain of people telling him he couldn't do the next thing: stuck in Turkey, wrong university, not an engineer, never been a VP, never done venture capital. At every stage, someone tried to put him in a box. His response was always to burn bridges and do whatever it takes.

The lesson for founders: people will constantly tell you it's not going to work. The difference between success and failure is whether you internalize those limitations or use them as fuel. You still control your destiny even when no one believes in you. The path is harder, but it means nobody else is dictating your trajectory.
