Hormozi defines commitment as 'the elimination of alternatives,' using marriage as the clearest analogy: you eliminate all other potential partners. Applied to business, if you enter a partnership agreeing to pursue no other ventures, that constraint is the commitment. The insight reframes the common failure mode where people 'commit' to a new goal while keeping all their old options alive.

This maps directly to how founders and operators should evaluate their own seriousness: look at your calendar and your credit card statement. Those reveal your true commitments regardless of what you say your goals are. 'We vote with our dollars about the things we care about.' The gap between stated priorities and revealed priorities (time and money allocation) is the single best diagnostic for why someone isn't making progress. If someone wants to commit, ask them what they're willing to eliminate — if the answer is nothing, they haven't committed.
