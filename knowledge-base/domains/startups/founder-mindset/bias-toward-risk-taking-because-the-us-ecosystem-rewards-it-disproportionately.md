Misra dropped out of a machine learning PhD to join a startup. People thought he was crazy. Looking back, it was the right decision. His insight: especially in the US, you can take risks and actually get rewarded for them. People don't bias enough toward risk.

The corollary: if you just do what everyone else is doing, you end up where everyone else is going. Misra came from India where the career path was more prescribed. The US's unique ecosystem—venture capital, failure tolerance, talent mobility—means the expected value of risk-taking is higher here than anywhere else. Captions grew to $500M and tens of millions of users because Misra repeatedly chose the riskier path.
