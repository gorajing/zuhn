Hormozi illustrates with a snowblower analogy: invest $10K in the S&P and earn $1K (10% return). Or spend $10K on a snowblower that takes you from 3 driveways/day to 20, multiplying income far beyond 10%. His recommendation: allocate 15% of pre-tax income to market investments AND another 15% to education and skill development.

The insight is that human capital returns are asymmetric and front-loaded. Early in a career, a $5K course or tool that increases earning capacity by $20K/year represents a 400% annual return — impossible to achieve consistently in markets. The compounding also differs: market returns compound on capital, but skill returns compound on earning capacity, which itself grows. The day you stop investing in skills is the day you decide your current income is your peak. However, this principle weakens as wealth grows — at some point, passive capital deployment becomes more scalable than personal skill development.
