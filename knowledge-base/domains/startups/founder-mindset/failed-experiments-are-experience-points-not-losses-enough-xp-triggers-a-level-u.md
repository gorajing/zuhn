When broke people invest money in something that fails (a Shopify store, an ad campaign, a course), they feel like they're back at zero. Hormozi reframes this using video game mechanics: you're at your previous position plus one experience point. With enough accumulated experience points, you level up — and each level requires more XP (and more money) to reach the next.

This reframe serves two practical purposes. First, it removes the catastrophic framing that makes people afraid to try anything after a first failure. Second, it explains why the first $100K takes 5 years while subsequent milestones come faster — you've banked enough experience points that your 'bets' become increasingly informed. The key insight is that the bar to start has never been lower (no need to mortgage a house), but there is still a bar, and getting over it requires treating every failed attempt as progress rather than regression.
