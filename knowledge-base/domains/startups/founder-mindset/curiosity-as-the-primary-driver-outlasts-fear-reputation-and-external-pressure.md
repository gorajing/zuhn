Carlson describes sitting in Molotov's old office in the Kremlin, 'blown away' not by the political significance but by the raw historical reality of the place. His motivation for the entire endeavor was 'to bring more information' -- not to score points, not to advance a career, not to prove anything. He explicitly notes he wasn't nervous because 'I'm 54, my kids are grown, I believe in God.'

The insight for anyone building a long career is that curiosity is the only motivation that doesn't decay with time. Fear of failure fades as you accumulate successes and failures. Career ambition peaks and plateaus. Reputation management becomes exhausting. But genuine curiosity about how things work and who people are actually deepens with age and experience. Building your work around curiosity rather than these other motivators creates a sustainable engine.
