Hormozi identifies impatience as the primary reason new entrepreneurs overpay or start from scratch unnecessarily. The character trait of success isn't hustle — it's the willingness to say 'I can take six months because in the next decade of my life there's no rush.' This patience unlocks dramatically better deal structures.

The math is stark: his second gym cost $250,000 to open and made no more money than his first ($40,000 investment), while his fifth gym cost zero dollars out of pocket and paid for itself in 30 days. The difference wasn't skill or market conditions — it was patience and deal discipline. Most people won't do this because finding good deals requires sustained effort without immediate gratification, which filters out the majority of competitors. The paradox is that the 'lazy' approach of waiting for the right deal requires more discipline than the 'hardworking' approach of grinding to build from scratch.
