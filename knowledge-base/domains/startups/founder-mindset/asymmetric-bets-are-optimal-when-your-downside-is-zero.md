Hormozi argues that the biggest mistake ambitious young people make is risk-aversion when their actual downside is zero — you can always get another job, move back home, or start over. The math is simple: if the downside is returning to your current baseline and the upside is life-changing, every bet is positive expected value.

This inverts conventional wisdom about 'being responsible.' The truly irresponsible move is playing it safe when you have decades of earning potential and zero obligations. The asymmetry only shrinks as you age, accumulate dependents, and build a lifestyle to protect — making early-career the optimal window for maximum variance strategies.
