The natural instinct during a major business failure is to see it as a setback. Ahana's experience inverts this: the -47% EBITDA marketplace disaster created financial discipline the team didn't have before. They learned to grow 100% year-on-year while staying profitable — a capability that didn't exist before the failure. Coming out of crisis gave them more confidence than they'd have had without it.

This connects to a deeper pattern: you cannot develop crisis capability without experiencing crisis. The Open Secret team jumped on quick-commerce early precisely because they weren't in 'victim mode' wallowing after the marketplace failure — they were constantly looking for the next opportunity. Resilience isn't about surviving failure; it's about the specific capabilities that failure installs. The counterweight here is to the failure-avoidance mindset: some of the most valuable company capabilities are only accessible through experiencing and recovering from significant failures.
