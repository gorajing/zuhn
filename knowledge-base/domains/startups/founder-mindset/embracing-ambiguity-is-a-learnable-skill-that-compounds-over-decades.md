Thompson wasn't a storage expert when he joined DataCore as employee #20 to help them expand in Asia Pacific. He had to represent the company as a storage expert while honestly not being one. Through many uncomfortable moments of being slightly out of his element, he gradually became the expert.

Starting LucidLink at 57 wasn't despite his age—it was because of the decades of ambiguity tolerance he'd built. Each uncomfortable situation expanded his comfort zone slightly, and those expansions compounded. The lesson for young founders: every uncomfortable moment of operating beyond your expertise is building a skill that will serve you for decades.
