Gary's observation about why 'the world is so tight right now': we've collectively stayed in a negative conversation for 10 years because our newsfeeds have been optimized for political outrage and decline narratives. His counter: his newsfeed isn't politics, isn't 'my parents had it better,' isn't outrage — he actively curates for optimism and constructive problem-solving. The people who listen to his podcast chose to follow him and the host — they self-selected into offense and optimism — and that choice is available to everyone with the same phones. The actionable teaching: audit your information diet and peer relationships explicitly, subtract the negative inputs, and add people who are trying to figure it out. Leisure and escapism are fine as side dishes but when they become the main meal (Saturday morning Netflix binges driven by anxiety about Monday), that's a symptom that your life isn't going well and the fix is re-engaging the work, not deeper escape.
