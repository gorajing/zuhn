Jeff Kaplan spent three years in solitary writing — eight hours a day, 170 rejection letters in one year, deepening depression and alcoholism. He describes the painful realization that our culture's emphasis on 'never give up' can become toxic. Sometimes a large group of people (in his case, literary magazine editors across the US) are telling you it's time to quit.

The crucial insight is that closing one door is often required for another to open, but the next calling is unknown when you make the decision to walk away. Kaplan threw all his manuscripts in a dumpster — not because he found something better, but because holding onto them would tempt him to try again. That clean break created the space for 6,000 hours of EverQuest, which became his real preparation for game design. The lesson: sometimes strategic quitting is the bravest and most productive decision.
