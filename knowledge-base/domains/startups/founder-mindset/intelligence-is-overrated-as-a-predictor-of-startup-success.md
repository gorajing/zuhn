Graham observed from YC's earliest-stage investing that determination consistently outpredicted intelligence for startup outcomes. The 'genius founder' narrative persists because it's more compelling for PR and gives observers an excuse for their own inaction ('I'm just not smart enough').

This has direct implications for founder self-assessment and investor evaluation. Many smart people achieve nothing because they lack the willfulness-discipline balance to push through the unglamorous middle of building a company. Conversely, moderately intelligent but fiercely determined founders often outperform because startups are not pure problem-solving — they're a gauntlet of heterogeneous challenges where persistence matters more than brilliance at any single task.
