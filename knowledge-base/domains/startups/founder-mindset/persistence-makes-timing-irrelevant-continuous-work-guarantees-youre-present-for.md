Hormozi presents timing as a visual model: plot opportunity windows on a timeline. People who try to time their effort to coincide with those windows almost always miss them. But someone who works continuously across the entire timeline hits every window by default. The conclusion: you can time everything perfectly if your intention is to never stop.

This inverts the common startup advice about market timing. Starting IS the perfect condition — it creates readiness for opportunities that can't be predicted. He extends this with a skill-accumulation argument: opportunities multiply with skill, so the person with the broadest skill base has access to the maximum number of potential opportunities. The practical implication for founders is to stop waiting for ideal conditions and instead focus on continuous skill development, which expands the opportunity surface area over time.
