Kate specifically used the word 'scrappy' to describe founders, and Graham unpacks this as the diminutive form of belligerent — someone who manages to be both threatening and undignified simultaneously. He argues this is exactly what one should want to be in any kind of work: if you're not threatening, you're probably not doing anything new, and dignity is merely a sort of plaque.

This is a powerful observation because most professional environments actively train scrappiness out of people. Corporate culture rewards composure, deference to hierarchy, and polished presentation. The startup world inverts this completely — scrappiness signals intensity and resourcefulness. The outsider's surprise at this quality reveals just how culturally aberrant the startup world is from conventional professional norms.
