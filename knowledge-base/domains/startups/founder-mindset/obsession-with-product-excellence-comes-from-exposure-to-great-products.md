Meniri's life changed when he walked up Fifth Avenue and discovered the Apple Store. The iPod ad, the beautiful product design, the 24-hour access to computers—this encounter with Apple's obsession with excellence made him want to learn design and become a product founder.

The broader lesson: great founders are often created by formative encounters with excellence. Meniri didn't have education or network—he had exposure to what great products felt like. This suggests that the bottleneck for producing more great founders isn't education or capital—it's exposure to excellence. Making beautiful, well-designed products accessible to people who wouldn't otherwise encounter them could be one of the highest-leverage interventions for producing the next generation of founders.
