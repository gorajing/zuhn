YC's biggest lesson for Max: build something amazing and investors
email YOU. The entire fundraising, hiring, and networking problem
collapses into a single variable — product quality. Networking events
are a distraction: "no one who's building something amazing is at
those events." The same applies to hiring: Gumloop's best hires were
customers who quit their jobs to join because they already loved the
product and saw the vision. And fundraising: when the product speaks
for itself, investors come inbound. This is not "if you build it they
will come" naivety — it is a deliberate strategy to make the product
so good that it becomes its own marketing, recruiting, and fundraising
engine. The prerequisite is that the product must genuinely be
exceptional, not just good.
