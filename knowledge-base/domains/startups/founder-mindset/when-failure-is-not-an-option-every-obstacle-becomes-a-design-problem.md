Meniri came from Algeria, where geopolitical restrictions meant he couldn't even travel. When he finally reached NYC, he had no education, no network, and no option to fail. He learned design by using the free 24-hour computers at the Apple Store. Every decision he doubts, but a 'stubborn persona' keeps him going.

The key insight: having no safety net changes your relationship with obstacles. With a backup plan, every hard challenge triggers a cost-benefit analysis of whether to continue. Without one, challenges become purely design problems—how do I solve this?—not decision problems—should I keep going? This is why immigrant founders disproportionately succeed: the question 'should I quit?' simply doesn't exist in their framework.
