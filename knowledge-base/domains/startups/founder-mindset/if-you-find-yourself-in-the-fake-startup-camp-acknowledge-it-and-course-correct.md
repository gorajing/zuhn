The core message of the real-vs-fake framework isn't to shame anyone — it's a diagnostic tool. If you're not taking a real shot, you're guaranteed to lose because startups are extraordinarily hard even when you are fully committed. The problem with fake isn't that these founders lack talent or ambition; it's that their energy is directed at performing startup rather than doing startup.

The fix is straightforward but uncomfortable: take an honest inventory. Are you building product or building appearances? Are you solving customer problems or taking investor meetings? If you're in the fake camp — and many smart, well-intentioned people end up there — do something about it. Strip away the performance, get back to building, and invest your grit in the highest-leverage activities rather than the most visible ones.
