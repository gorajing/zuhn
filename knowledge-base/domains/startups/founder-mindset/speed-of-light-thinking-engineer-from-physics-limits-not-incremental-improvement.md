Jensen describes a methodology he started 30 years ago called 'speed of light' thinking. The speed of light is his shorthand for the physical limits of what's possible — applied to memory speed, math speed, power, cost, time, manufacturing cycle time, everything. Every design decision at NVIDIA is tested against these theoretical limits.

He explicitly rejects continuous improvement as a starting point. When someone says 'it takes 74 days and we can do it in 72,' Jensen strips it back to zero and asks: 'Why 74 days? If built from scratch with today's technology, what would physics allow?' Often the answer is 6 days. The remaining gap between 6 and 74 may contain legitimate compromises, but now you know what they are. This mindset — 'as complex as necessary, but as simple as possible' — drives NVIDIA's engineering culture.
