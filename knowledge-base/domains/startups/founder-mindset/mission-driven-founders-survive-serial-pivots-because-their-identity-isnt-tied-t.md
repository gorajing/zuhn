Pensive's founder went through 8 pivots — from voice cloning to food market logistics to property management AI — before landing on AI-powered education. What sustained him through serial failure was not attachment to any single idea but a deeply personal mission around education rooted in his own experience navigating Korean and American education systems.

The insight is structural: founders who anchor identity to a domain-level mission (e.g., 'solve learning') rather than a product hypothesis (e.g., 'build an AI grader') can pivot freely because each pivot is a different approach to the same mission, not an identity crisis. This is why he distinguishes 'mission-driven founders' from 'idea-driven founders' — the former can sustain 8 pivots, the latter usually burn out after 2-3.

Practically, this means the founder selection question isn't 'do you have a good idea?' but 'do you have a problem you'd spend 10 years on regardless of which specific solution works?'
