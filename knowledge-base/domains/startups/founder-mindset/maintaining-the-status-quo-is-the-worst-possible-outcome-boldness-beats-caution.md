After going through painful organizational changes where key team members left and the culture shifted dramatically, the Moddoc founder developed a framework for decision-making under uncertainty: 'What is the absolute worst thing? Even if it's the worst, it couldn't be worse than death. Even if it is the worst, if it is only that bad, there are times when you need to be bold.'

His deepest regret was not the bold moves that failed, but the moments of hesitation: 'I really regret the moments when I wasn't bold enough. Maintaining the status quo is truly the absolute worst thing to do.' This reframes risk: the real danger isn't change -- it's inaction. The startup graveyard is full of companies that saw the need for change but moved too slowly.
