Paul Graham observed that the most successful YC founders were 'fire-and-forget' — give them a lead and they'd close it without babysitting. The least successful were hard to talk to, not because they lacked intelligence, but because they filtered advice through pre-existing decisions rather than processing it with fresh eyes.

This reveals that conversational resourcefulness — the willingness to chase down all implications of what someone tells you, including inconvenient ones — is a subset of general resourcefulness. Founders who can't do this conversationally also fail to chase down funding, users, and new ideas. The practical test: when you give someone advice, do they get a 'glazed over look' (internal rationalization process) or do they engage with logical responses like 'we tried that' or 'users told us otherwise'?
