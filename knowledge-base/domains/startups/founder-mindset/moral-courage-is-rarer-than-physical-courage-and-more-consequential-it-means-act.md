RFK Jr recounts his father's emphasis on moral courage as distinct from and harder than physical courage on battlefields or football fields. His book 'Profiles in Courage' documented politicians who chose principle over career, and most were destroyed by that choice.

The Anne Frank test his father posed to his children — would you hide her at risk to your own life? — is easy to answer hypothetically but reveals a deeper truth: most people choose self-preservation over moral action when actually tested. For leaders, the question is whether you build organizations and personal habits that make the morally courageous choice the default, or whether you only discover your answer in the crisis moment.
