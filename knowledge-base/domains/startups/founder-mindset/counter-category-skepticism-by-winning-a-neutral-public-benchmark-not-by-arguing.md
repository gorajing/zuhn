Many security researchers insisted fully autonomous hacking was impossible. Instead of debating, XBOW entered its bot on HackerOne — a neutral platform where ethical hackers compete to find real vulnerabilities for bounties — and within weeks became the #1 hacker in the US, then #1 in the world, on real open-source web applications rather than toy benchmarks. The founder lesson is that when your category faces a credibility wall ('this can't be done'), the highest-leverage move is to win on an independent, adversarial, public stage where the result is unambiguous and not self-reported. A leaderboard position the audience already trusts does more to convert skeptics than any demo or deck, because it removes the suspicion that the founder cherry-picked the conditions.
