Bezos traces his problem-solving mentality directly to his grandfather's ranch in Texas, where he spent every summer from age 4 to 16. His grandfather was 'so resourceful' that he made his own veterinary needles from wire, bought a broken $5,000 D6 bulldozer and spent an entire summer fixing it, and never 'picked up the phone and called somebody.' Every problem was solved with persistence and ingenuity.

This childhood imprinting created the foundational belief that you can solve problems yourself if you're persistent and creative enough — the same mindset that led to building Amazon from scratch. The lesson is not 'never ask for help' but rather 'default to figuring it out yourself first.' Building cranes to move gears that were too heavy to lift is the physical-world version of the startup principle: resourcefulness over resources.
