JC started Fuse at 19 and never went to college. His argument: being young provides naivete (you don't know enough to be discouraged) and stamina (you have the energy to sustain extraordinary intensity). Both of these advantages naturally decline with age and experience.

The college question is telling: 'Why do I need to convince an admission officer to let me sit in a classroom for 4 years to get a piece of paper that says I can potentially do something, versus just do something and prove you can do it?' Starting as early as possible maximizes your chances because even failures in your teens and early twenties are recoverable—as long as you don't die or go to jail.
