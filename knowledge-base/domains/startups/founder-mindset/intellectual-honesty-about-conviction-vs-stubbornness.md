Simons spent 7 years building StackBlitz before Bolt's breakthrough. He candidly admits the company was about to go out of business. The question he constantly asked himself: 'Am I not giving up because people say you shouldn't, or because I actually have conviction?'

This is the existential founder question. There's no external signal that reliably tells you the answer. But Simons suggests intellectual honesty with yourself is the only path. If you're persisting out of habit, sunk cost, or social pressure, that's stubbornness. If you're persisting because you genuinely see something others don't, that's conviction. The 7-year journey to Bolt's success shows both the danger and the reward of this distinction.
