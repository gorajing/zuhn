The PayPal mafia is the famous example — Thiel started Palantir and Founders Fund, Musk did Tesla and SpaceX, Hoffman started LinkedIn, Levchin started Affirm — but this pattern repeats with every breakout company. Early Google employees became COOs at Facebook (Sandberg), Dropbox (Woodside), and the CEO/leadership of Stripe, Gusto, and dozens of others. Pinterest and Instagram founders came from Google.

The practical takeaway for career strategy: where you choose to work early — and who you work alongside — matters more than your specific role. These networks create collaboration across companies for decades because everyone knows and trusts each other. The implication is that joining a small, fast-growing company is not just a job choice but a network investment. The relationships formed under the pressure of rapid scaling become the most valuable professional asset you'll ever build.
