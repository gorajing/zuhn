The chocolate bar illusion works because each piece along the cut is slightly shorter, but the cut itself makes the shrinkage hard to notice. The animation version actively conceals the loss during transitions. This is structurally identical to how organizations lose track of value.

When you reorganize teams, rebrand metrics, or change reporting periods, each transition creates a seam where small losses become invisible. Quarterly revenue looks continuous across a reorg, but the context switch cost is absorbed into the seams. Customer counts look stable through a product pivot, but the cohort quality degraded at every transition. The discipline is to measure across the seams, not between them — compare the total area before and after the cut, not just the apparent shape.
