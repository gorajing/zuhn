Horowitz offers a nuanced critique of the popular 'founder mode' concept. The half that's right: founders should stay deeply engaged with their product and not fully delegate to professional managers who lack context. The half that's dangerously wrong: using 'founder mode' as justification to skip building management infrastructure, ignore organizational design, or operate as a one-person bottleneck at scale.

This matters because the founder mode narrative has become a convenient excuse for founders who resist the uncomfortable work of building organizational capability. Horowitz has seen both failure modes—founders who over-delegate to hired executives and lose their company's soul, and founders who refuse to develop any management layer and become the single point of failure. The right answer is a synthesis: maintain founder-level engagement and taste while building the organizational machinery to execute at scale.
