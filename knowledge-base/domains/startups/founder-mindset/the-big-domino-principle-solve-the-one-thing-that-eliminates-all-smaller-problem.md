Hormozi describes his core problem-solving philosophy: in dating, fitness, and business, he looks for the single thing that, if solved, makes all other problems disappear. In dating, that big domino is becoming someone worth being with — which eliminates the need for profile optimization, messaging hacks, and posturing tactics. The principle generalizes: most people spend time on dozens of micro-optimizations because the big domino requires confronting harder truths (you're not good enough yet, your product doesn't solve a real problem, your business model is broken). The 100 tiny tweaks feel productive but produce marginal gains. The big domino feels uncomfortable because it usually involves fundamental personal or structural change, but it's the only move that transforms the entire game. This maps to startup strategy where founders optimize landing pages when the real issue is product-market fit.
