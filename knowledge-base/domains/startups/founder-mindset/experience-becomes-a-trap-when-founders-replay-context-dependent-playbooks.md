Success rewires founder brains to attribute outcomes to skill rather than context — timing, market tailwinds, and competitor missteps often played larger roles than founders acknowledge. This creates a dangerous pattern: founders lift their entire previous playbook (hiring cadence, pricing model, launch strategy, management style) into a new venture that operates under fundamentally different dynamics. The article calls this 'fighting the last war.' The antidote is treating past experience as hypotheses to test rather than proven strategies to deploy. Every scar-tissue-driven decision should come with the question: 'Is this what THIS startup needs, or is this what my LAST startup needed?'
