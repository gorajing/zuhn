Michael describes a B2B founder who used AI to identify a company's biggest problem, decided it was too hard to solve, then spent four months searching for easier problems — and found nothing important. This pattern maps to SAT test-taking advice: if the problem seems too hard, you're probably doing it wrong, skip it and find an easy one. But startups are not SATs.

Everything that is valuable is hard. The hard problem was the real opportunity. Running from difficulty is one of the most common founder failure modes because it feels like strategic thinking — 'I'm looking for better product-market fit' — when it's actually avoidance. The willingness to sit with a hard problem and grind through it, rather than endlessly searching for the easy version, is what separates companies that matter from those that don't.
