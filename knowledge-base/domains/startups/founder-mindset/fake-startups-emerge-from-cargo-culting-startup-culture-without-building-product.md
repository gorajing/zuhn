The cartoonish extreme of a fake startup: no code written, no product shipped, no customers, but the founders are on 30-under-30 lists and have raised money. It's performance art — Hamilton on stage versus actually being there. The reason Google worked wasn't the pastel-colored balls and free lunch; it was that they built a really good search engine.

More charitably, many founders slip into this pattern unintentionally. They consume startup culture on Twitter, observe what everyone else seems to be doing, and cargo-cult the visible outputs: rent an office, hire people, take investor meetings. They genuinely believe this is what a startup looks like because no one has told them otherwise. The infrastructure gets built without the core insight that all of it is supposed to serve one purpose: building something people want.
