Shamgunov spent 10 years at SingleStore, growing it from $7M to $20M+ run rate with a $1.5B valuation. Then he noticed: the operational work had reduced, and removing him from the organization wouldn't materially change outcomes. Combined with a craving for learning, this was his signal to start Neon.

This is a rare and honest framework for founder departure. Most founders leave because of conflict, burnout, or failure. Shamgunov left because of success—he'd completed his contribution. The lesson: founders should regularly ask 'would this organization do just as well without me?' When the answer becomes yes, staying is about ego, not impact.
