Alex Karp warns that Silicon Valley's biggest existential threat isn't competition or regulation — it's a political horseshoe effect. If AI simultaneously eliminates white-collar jobs (affecting educated, Democratic-leaning constituencies) while concentrating wealth among a small number of tech leaders who don't visibly support the military or the broader public, both parties will converge on one thing: nationalization.

Karp draws the analogy to Hollywood's rating system: Hollywood realized that if they didn't self-regulate, Washington would do it for them — badly. Silicon Valley needs the same reckoning. The wealth tax is already a derivative of this dynamic — everyone knows it won't help the poor, it'll just punish the rich, and that's politically sufficient. Karp's advice: tech leaders need to physically go to Iowa, go to DC, explain to political leaders why they earn hundreds of millions while a soldier from Iowa doesn't have the best technology. Then cross the hall and explain to progressive Democrats why their constituents won't have jobs. Then imagine what happens when you leave the room.
