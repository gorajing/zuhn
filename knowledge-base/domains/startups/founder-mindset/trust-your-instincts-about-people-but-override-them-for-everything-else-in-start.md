Graham draws a sharp line: your life hasn't prepared you for startup business decisions (they're counterintuitive like skiing), but all your social interactions have prepared you to judge people. The characteristic mistake is suppressing people instincts because someone 'seems impressive.' Founders consistently report 'I knew there was something wrong about that guy, but I ignored it.'

The specific trap for technical founders is assuming distasteful behavior is normal for business. Graham's advice: pick co-founders and partners the way you'd pick friends. Work with people you genuinely like and respect, and who you've known long enough to see how they behave when interests are opposed. This is 'one of those rare cases where it works to be self-indulgent.'
