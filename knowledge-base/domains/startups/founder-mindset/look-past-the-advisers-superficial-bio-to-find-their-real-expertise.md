When Dalton was a new YC partner, founders asked him about entertainment and music because of his visible background. But his actual most valuable expertise was in hiring/firing, growth, developer tools, and infrastructure — things you'd discover by noticing he was a programmer into open source, not from his surface-level bio. The fix: look at someone's portfolio ('Dalton has funded a lot of open source companies — he probably has opinions about that'), ask directly what categories they've invested in, and lean into their operating past over their investing past. One powerful question: 'How have you seen other companies in our category die?' This surfaces pattern-matched wisdom from dozens of similar companies. Don't ask advisers to be oracles — even Peter Thiel knows nothing about your specific business.
