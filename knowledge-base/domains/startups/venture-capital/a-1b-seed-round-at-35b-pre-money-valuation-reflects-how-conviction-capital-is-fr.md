AMI's $1.03B seed round at a $3.5B pre-money valuation sets a new record for European seed funding. The participants — Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, HV Capital, Bezos Expeditions — are not tourists. This is concentrated conviction capital from experienced technology investors who believe the LLM scaling paradigm is cresting and world models represent the next architectural leap.

The pattern matters beyond AI. When investors of this caliber write nine-figure checks at seed, it reflects a specific thesis: the paradigm window is open now, the founding team is irreplaceable (Yann LeCun is not replicable), and waiting for proof points will mean paying 10x more for the same position. The staged funding model assumes uncertainty; mega-seed rounds assume the question is not 'will this work?' but 'who will own it?'

For founders, this is a signal about how conviction capital behaves at paradigm inflection points. The implication is not 'raise as much as possible' but rather: if you can articulate a genuine paradigm shift and assemble a team with unique credibility to execute it, the constraint is not capital availability — it's founder clarity.
