Revenue-based financing—where repayment is structured as a percentage of monthly revenue rather than fixed debt service—has historically been niche, but the market data now shows it entering mainstream adoption. $5.8B in 2024 with ~70% annual growth suggests it is tracking toward $10B+ by 2026. Simultaneously, venture debt reached $27.8B in 2025, indicating the broader non-dilutive capital market is maturing.

For SaaS founders specifically, RBF is structurally aligned with their business model: repayments flex with revenue, so there's no cash flow crisis during slow months. The tradeoff versus equity is that RBF has a repayment cap (typically 1.3–1.5x the advance) and works best when the business has predictable MRR. Founders at $20K–$100K MRR are often in the ideal window—enough revenue to qualify, not so much that equity financing would be dilutive at high valuations.
