The conventional advice for breaking into VC focuses on deal sourcing and network-building, but the deeper skill is calibration — knowing what great looks like. Studying late-stage founders (those who have survived and scaled) provides a ground-truth reference point for founder quality that early-stage analysis alone cannot supply. Without that calibration, early-stage assessments tend to rely on proxies (pedigree, storytelling ability) rather than genuine signal. An investor who has deeply studied what excellence looks like at scale can reverse-engineer those traits in pre-traction founders — a core edge in competitive dealmaking.
