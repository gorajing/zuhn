Frank, the VC panelist, shared staggering concentration metrics: approximately two-thirds of all venture capital is now flowing into AI-related companies, and two-thirds of that AI funding concentrates in the Bay Area. This double-filter means roughly 44% of all global venture capital flows through one metropolitan area for one technology category.

This creates a self-reinforcing dynamic where talent, capital, and deal flow concentrate geographically, making it increasingly difficult for AI startups outside the Bay Area to compete for funding. The implication for founders is stark: if you're building an AI company and not in the Bay Area, you're competing for roughly one-third of the already-allocated AI capital pool. For investors, the concentration raises portfolio risk concerns as a single geographic and thematic bet dominates venture portfolios.
