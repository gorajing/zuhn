When First Round Capital found female-founded startups outperformed by 63%, they likely thought they were publishing research on startup trends. They were actually measuring their own selection filter. Because the sample was limited to their portfolio, any performance gap among subgroups reflects differential selection criteria, not differential founder ability or market dynamics. This is a generalizable caution for any organization that draws conclusions from self-selected samples: the findings describe the selector, not the world. Investors, hiring managers, and admissions committees who study 'what predicts success in our population' are studying their own biases as much as the underlying signal.
