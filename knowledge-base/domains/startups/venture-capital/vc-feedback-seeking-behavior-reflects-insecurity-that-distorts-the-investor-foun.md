Kyle Harrison's 'Trough of Feedback' essay names an underappreciated dynamic: many VCs are deeply insecure about their social standing with founders and optimize their communications to be liked rather than useful. This produces a systematic bias toward vague, non-committal feedback ('great team, but not the right fit') that protects the VC's reputation while leaving founders with no actionable information. The perverse outcome is that the most honest feedback often comes from VCs who passed early and have nothing to lose, while VCs actively engaged with a company give the least useful criticism. Founders should actively seek out 'brutal honesty' from trusted investors and weight pleasant consensus feedback heavily downward.
