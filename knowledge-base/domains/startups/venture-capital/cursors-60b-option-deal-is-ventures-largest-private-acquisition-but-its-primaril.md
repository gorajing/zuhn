The 20VC team's deconstruction: Cursor has ~$3B ARR but bad gross margins because they don't own a model and depend on others' compute; xAI has $20B+ in Colossus data center investment but 'no revenue,' having failed to sell GPU capacity at scale. The deal works because their P&Ls cancel — Cursor's $3B in negative gross margin offsets some of xAI's $18B burn, and the combined entity looks like a real frontier AI lab with revenue.

The option structure (exercised after SpaceX IPO with $10B walk-away) reveals the deeper logic: SpaceX is preparing for a $2T IPO and the X.AI overhang has been a story problem in road shows. The option says 'we have a plan to fix this' to public investors without actually paying today. For Zuhn editorially: this is the canonical example of how AI deals get done at this stage — not 'this vertical AI app is worth $60B as a standalone' but 'we can construct a story where two flawed businesses become one less-flawed business.' Don't read transaction prices as market clearing for capability — read them as architecture diagrams of what each side needs.
