The PC market took from 1975 to 2008 (33 years) to sell its first billion cumulative units. Smartphones sold 1 billion units in 2013 alone, growing at 4x the PC industry's maximum growth rate. This multiplicative acceleration pattern — not linear improvement — suggests that each new platform benefits from the infrastructure, literacy, and distribution networks of its predecessor. For founders timing market entry, the implication is that new platform shifts will feel impossibly fast relative to the previous generation's adoption curve, and waiting for 'enough market size' means you're already late.
