Smartphones didn't just displace one product category — they simultaneously collapsed markets for point-and-shoot cameras, camcorders, MP3 players, portable gaming systems, GPS devices, netbooks, and print media. The key insight is that convergence doesn't need to match specialized quality; it only needs to cross a 'good enough' threshold while offering the convenience of a single carry. This has strategic implications: if you're building a specialized hardware product, you must either dramatically exceed smartphone capability in your domain or offer something a smartphone physically cannot do. The smartphone-plus-internet combination is the real disruption vector — it's not just the hardware convergence but the software ecosystem that makes each function continuously improve.
