Facebook's user decline in developing markets (India, Africa, Latin America) wasn't driven by existing users churning — it was driven by new internet users bypassing Facebook entirely and going straight to TikTok. This is a fundamentally different dynamic than the Western market decline, where older users stayed while younger ones left.

This pattern suggests that platform disruption in global markets follows demographic replacement rather than conversion. The incumbent doesn't lose its existing users — it fails to capture the incoming generation. This has profound implications for platform defense strategy: retention programs are insufficient if you're not winning new user acquisition against emerging competitors. It also means that developing market growth, often cited as the backstop for mature-market decline, can evaporate simultaneously when a global competitor captures the next cohort.
