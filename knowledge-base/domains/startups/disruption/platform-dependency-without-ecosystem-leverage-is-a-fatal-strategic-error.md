Nokia's fatal strategic error was choosing Microsoft's mobile OS over Android when they finally entered the smartphone market. Despite having pristine build quality, superior cameras, and excellent hardware, the dominant user complaint was 'there were no apps.' The app ecosystem—not the device hardware—determined consumer choice.

This illustrates a durable principle in platform economics: once network effects establish a dominant ecosystem, choosing an alternative platform requires not just superior hardware but a credible path to ecosystem parity. Nokia had neither. The lesson extends beyond mobile: any hardware company choosing a minority software platform bets its survival on that platform achieving critical mass—a bet that should require extraordinary justification given the base rate of platform failures.
