The default response to a competitor raising funding is panic and strategic pivoting. Seibel's counter-practice: the first diligence step is using the competitor's product personally. This reveals in minutes whether the competitor is building slop, building craft, or building something orthogonal. Without product use, all competitive data (funding news, press, user-count claims) is indirect and manipulated. With product use, the actual quality and direction becomes immediately clear. The implication is practical: before any competitive response (pivot, feature add, pricing change, messaging change), spend at least one day as the competitor's user. If the competitor is shipping slop, the correct response is to double down on craft, not to match. If the competitor is shipping craft, the correct response is different and possibly more urgent — but at least it is informed.
