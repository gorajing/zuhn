Ben and David explicitly adopted Buffett and Munger's too-hard pile after their Berkshire series. For Acquired, Hollywood is a too-hard pile: every TV/film adaptation offer sounds great until they trace the implications, and the answer is always "we should just make another episode." The discipline is to decline a whole category rather than re-litigate each opportunity.

The key mechanic is that the too-hard pile only works if the yes pile is genuinely awesome. Acquired can reject Hollywood because publishing another episode to a compounding subscriber base has absurdly high expected value — their opportunity cost is so high that entire industries fall into "too hard" without remorse. This is the opposite of the standard founder instinct to say yes to optionality and figure it out later.

Apply this by naming the categories you are permanently out of and enforcing it at the calendar level. Every Hollywood-adjacent meeting Acquired declines is a month not spent being seduced down a rabbit hole with nothing to show. The win isn't the no itself — it's the elimination of the evaluation cost.
