M-PESA's success in Kenya was enabled by Safaricom's dominant market position. In the US, the competitive telecom and fintech landscape means payment apps fragment across Chase Pay, Google Pay, Apple Cash, Venmo, Zelle, and others — the hard part isn't building the app, it's hitting critical mass. African telecom monopolies solved this coordination problem by default: one dominant carrier meant one dominant payment platform with near-universal reach.

This is a counterintuitive case where monopoly structure produces better consumer outcomes than competition. The insight for platform builders is that winner-take-all dynamics in payment networks mean that early distribution advantages (especially through existing infrastructure like SIM cards) matter more than product quality. M-PESA didn't need to be the best app — it needed to be the only app people already had access to through their carrier.
