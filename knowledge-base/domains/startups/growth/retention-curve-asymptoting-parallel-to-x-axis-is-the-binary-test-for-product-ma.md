Alex Schultz argues retention is the single most important thing for growth. The diagnostic is simple: plot the percentage of users who are monthly active against their days since acquisition. If this curve asymptotes to a line parallel to the x-axis, you have product-market fit for some market subset. If it slopes down and intercepts the x-axis, your product will eventually lose all users regardless of acquisition velocity.

This framework was validated at Facebook's scale (predicting 1-year advertiser value to 97% accuracy with just 90 days of data) and at startup scale (applicable with as few as 10,000 users). The key insight is that this is a binary test -- either the curve flattens or it doesn't -- and no growth hacking can compensate for a curve that doesn't flatten. Schultz identifies this as the number one problem he sees in both Facebook's internal new products and the startups he advises: teams pursue growth tactics when they don't actually have product-market fit.
