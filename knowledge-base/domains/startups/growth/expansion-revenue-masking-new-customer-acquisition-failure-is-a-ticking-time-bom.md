As growth decelerated post-pandemic, many SaaS companies began leaning heavily on existing accounts through price increases, bundled features, and add-on modules to maintain reported expansion numbers. This masked a critical weakness: net new logo growth was slowing and sales cycles were stretching.

The distinction matters enormously. Expansion revenue from genuine deeper workflow adoption — customers integrating more data, automating more processes — is healthy and durable. But expansion used as a substitute for failing acquisition creates a ticking time bomb. When cohort performance eventually degrades, the damage compounds: enterprise software has already penetrated easy-to-reach early adopters, so winning now requires displacing incumbents or selling to far more price-sensitive segments. Investors who relied on a 'reacceleration thesis' — that growth dips were temporary — have been proven wrong across the board.
