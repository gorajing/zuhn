The prediction market growth story is fundamentally a distribution story. Rather than convincing millions of users to create new accounts, link bank details, and learn a new interface, platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket benefited from integration into Robinhood, Coinbase, DraftKings, and FanDuel — platforms where users already have funded accounts and trading habits.

This unlocks a key startup principle: for novel financial products, the fastest path to scale is often becoming a feature within existing platforms rather than building a standalone destination. The 100M+ retail accounts accessible through these partnerships represent distribution leverage that would take years and billions in marketing spend to replicate organically. Founders building in fintech or adjacent regulated markets should prioritize distribution partnerships over direct-to-consumer acquisition once regulatory clarity is achieved.
