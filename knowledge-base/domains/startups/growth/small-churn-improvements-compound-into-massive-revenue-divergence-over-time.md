The math on churn compounding is brutal and non-intuitive. Dropping monthly churn from 5% to 4% means you retain an extra percentage point of MRR every single month. Over 24 months, the cumulative difference between these two scenarios is enormous—far larger than most founders intuit from the seemingly small 1% gap.

This makes churn reduction arguably the highest-leverage activity for early-stage SaaS companies. Unlike acquisition (which requires ongoing spend), churn improvements compound permanently on your entire existing revenue base. A founder who models this explicitly in a simple dashboard can see the compounding effect visually, which reframes churn from a 'we should work on that someday' problem into an urgent strategic priority.
