Hale frames growth as a simple interaction between two variables: conversion rate and churn. A 1% improvement in either produces identical growth effects, but reducing churn is consistently cheaper and easier — yet companies routinely assign their best people to conversion and their B-team to retention.

Wufoo grew for 5 years with zero advertising spend, entirely through word-of-mouth, by obsessively focusing on the churn side of the equation. The company's growth curve was driven by an almost imperceptible gap between new users and downgrades. This insight challenges the default startup instinct to pour resources into acquisition. Marketing spend, Hale argues, is 'a tax you pay because you haven't made your product remarkable enough' for word-of-mouth growth.
