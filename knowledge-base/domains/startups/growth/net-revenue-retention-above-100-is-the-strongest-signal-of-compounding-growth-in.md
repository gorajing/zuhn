Net Revenue Retention measures how much revenue you retain from existing customers after accounting for expansion, upsells, and downgrades. An NRR above 100% means your existing customer base is growing even with zero new logos — this is the definition of a compounding business.

Slack's S-1 reported 132% NRR; Zoom exceeded 140%. These numbers reflect products where usage naturally expands: teams add seats, usage grows across departments, and customers upgrade as they hit workspace limits. For investors, high NRR proves both product-market fit and efficient growth mechanics. The distinction between Gross Revenue Retention (stability without expansion, benchmark ~90%) and NRR (the full picture including expansion, top quartile 120-140%) helps founders diagnose whether they have a retention problem, an expansion problem, or both.
