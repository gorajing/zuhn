Each case study in the article includes a 'why copycats fail' section revealing that the visible features of a growth loop are not what makes it work. Substack's loop works because of creator ownership (email list control, monetization), not just the publishing interface. Twitch succeeds because of audience liquidity on both sides, not just creator tools. ChatGPT's loops work because OpenAI layered usage, creation, and distribution as interconnected systems, not standalone features. Tripadvisor took years of relentless SEO optimization to hit velocity. This pattern suggests growth loops function as genuine moats precisely because the system dynamics are invisible to outsiders who only see the surface-level implementation. The implication for competitive strategy is that you should never copy a competitor's growth loop — instead, find your own behavioral flywheel.
