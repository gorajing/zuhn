The article presents a measurement framework treating the growth loop as a machine to be diagnosed rather than a dashboard to be monitored. The five metrics are: (1) K-factor (viral coefficient — users generated per user), (2) Cycle time (how fast the loop completes one revolution), (3) Loop drop-off (where users start but don't finish the loop), (4) Loop retention (whether users complete the loop more than once), and (5) Reinvestment rate (what percentage of output feeds back into the loop). The key insight is that these metrics must be read together as a system. A high K-factor with poor retention means the loop has novelty but not depth. Fast cycle time with high drop-off means the loop is tight but broken at a specific step. This systems-thinking approach to growth measurement is more actionable than tracking isolated metrics like DAU or conversion rate.
