The annual pre-pay technique works by collecting 12 months of revenue upfront while acquisition costs are paid once. The surplus cash between collection and service delivery can be immediately reinvested into acquiring more customers. This creates a self-reinforcing loop: more annual customers → more upfront cash → more marketing spend → more annual customers.

This technique is especially powerful for bootstrapped or capital-efficient startups because it eliminates the dependency on external funding for growth. The key is offering a discount meaningful enough to incentivize annual commitment (typically 15-20%) while ensuring the upfront cash exceeds customer acquisition cost. Dozens of founders have used this to transform negative cash-flow businesses into self-funding growth engines without raising additional capital.
