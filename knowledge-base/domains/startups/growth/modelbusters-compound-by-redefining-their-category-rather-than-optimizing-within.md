a16z identifies 'modelbusters' as companies that defy standard growth models by fundamentally redefining their markets rather than competing within established categories. These companies don't just grow faster—they change what the market even looks like, making traditional competitive analysis irrelevant.

The key trait is that modelbusters don't optimize for incremental advantages; they create new rules. This has implications for both founders (aim to redefine categories, not just win them) and investors (the best returns come from companies that make existing evaluation frameworks obsolete). The compounding effect means early category redefinition creates widening gaps that competitors can never close through mere optimization.
