When core metrics are declining, companies instinctively hire growth leaders expecting a turnaround. Elena Verna argues this is a category error: growth teams optimize and amplify existing product-market fit signals through experimentation and channel development. They cannot fix fundamental product problems, competitive displacement, or eroding PMF.

The practical implication is sequencing — address the root cause (product quality, positioning, competitive response) before layering growth. Premature growth hiring wastes the hire's credibility and the company's runway. The prerequisite checklist is clear: strong PMF, at least $1M ARR, enough user data for meaningful experiments, and self-serve revenue for PLG companies.
