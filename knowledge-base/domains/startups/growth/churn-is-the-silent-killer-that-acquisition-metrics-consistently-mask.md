The article frames churn as the metric that 'quietly erodes growth even when acquisition looks strong.' This is a well-documented but persistently ignored dynamic in SaaS: teams celebrate new logo wins while the revenue base underneath leaks. A company adding 50 customers per month at $100 ARPU but churning 5% monthly will plateau at roughly $100k MRR regardless of how long they keep acquiring — the churn drag creates a mathematical ceiling.

The projection model makes this visible by showing churned MRR as a separate line item each month. When founders see the cumulative churn number growing alongside their acquisition spend, the emotional calculus shifts. Retention investment stops feeling like a defensive expense and starts looking like the highest-leverage growth lever available. This is especially true past $50k MRR where the absolute dollar impact of each churn percentage point becomes material.
