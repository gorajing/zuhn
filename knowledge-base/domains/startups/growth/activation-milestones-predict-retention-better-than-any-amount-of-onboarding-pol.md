Retention starts and often ends in the first session. Calm found users who set a reminder on Day 1 retained 3x better. Facebook discovered that adding 7 friends in 10 days predicted long-term retention and rebuilt their entire onboarding around that single behavior.

The pattern is consistent across categories: there's usually one activation milestone that separates retained users from churned ones. Rather than building comprehensive feature tours or polished tutorial flows, the highest-leverage move is to dig into retained-user behavior data, find what they all did early, and make that single action the entire focus of the first experience. Everything else — removing form fields, reducing decision fatigue, surfacing core value — serves getting users to that milestone faster.
