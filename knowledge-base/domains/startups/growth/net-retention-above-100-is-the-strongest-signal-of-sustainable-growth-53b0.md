Gross retention caps at 100%—the best you can do is keep every customer. Net retention factors in upsells and expansions, so it can exceed 100% even with some churn. A net retention of 120% means that a cohort of customers acquired a year ago now generates 20% more revenue than when they started, purely from expansion.

This metric matters more than acquisition velocity because it reflects whether your product delivers increasing value over time. High net retention means your growth compounds: even if you stopped acquiring new customers entirely, revenue would still grow. It also directly informs how much you can rationally spend on CAC—if customers expand over time, a higher upfront acquisition cost becomes justifiable. Investors scrutinize this metric because it separates businesses that grow through brute-force sales from those with genuine product-market fit and pricing power.
