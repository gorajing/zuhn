Vendict's customers report cutting security questionnaire response time by 92%, transforming a weeks-long process into hours. This level of improvement flips the traditional switching cost dynamic: normally, the pain of changing processes deters adoption, but when the existing process is painful enough and the new tool delivers a 10x+ improvement, the cost of NOT switching becomes the dominant factor.

This is why NFX emphasizes 'instant customer love' — the product solves a problem on day one, which is the prerequisite for overcoming decades-old process inertia. The lesson for AI founders is that merely being better is insufficient; the improvement must be so dramatic that it creates its own adoption momentum. The 92% benchmark suggests that roughly an order-of-magnitude improvement is the threshold where product-led growth kicks in naturally.
