Lenny attributes his growth from ~500K to 1M subscribers largely to Substack's recommendation feature, where over 5,000 other newsletters now recommend his. The chart shows a clear hockey-stick inflection when the feature launched. However, he emphasizes that this mechanism only works if other writers genuinely find your content worth recommending—making it fundamentally a word-of-mouth amplifier rather than an independent growth channel.

This reveals a critical dynamic in platform-mediated growth: the best platform features create multiplicative effects on existing quality signals rather than additive effects. For founders building on platforms, the strategic implication is to invest first in making something genuinely worth recommending, then position to benefit from platform distribution features when they arrive. Being early and excellent when a platform ships a recommendation feature is a compounding advantage that latecomers can't easily replicate.
