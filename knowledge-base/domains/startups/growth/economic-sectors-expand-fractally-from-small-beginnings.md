Graham draws a parallel between steam power's trajectory in the British economy and Internet startups today. Steam was a sliver of the economy when Watt began his work, but successive waves of innovation expanded that sliver until it dwarfed the original whole. The mechanism is a positive feedback loop: the most dynamic sector offers the best opportunities, which attracts the most ambitious people, whose work expands the sector further.

Even if a sector remains a minority of total GDP, its cultural and economic influence radiates outward. The most dynamic part of the economy sets norms for salaries, work culture, dress, and operating principles across all industries — not just through prestige, but because the principles driving the most dynamic sector tend to actually work better. This means tracking where ambitious talent concentrates is a leading indicator of which principles will eventually become mainstream.
