Churn behaves like a cliff, not a revolving door. Email win-backs get less than 5% click-through, reactivation discounts see 10-20% redemption, and actual sustained usage reactivation is under 10%. Even successfully reactivated users reset to a worse retention curve than their first time.

This means the highest-ROI retention investment is always upstream: shortening time-to-value, smoothing the second-session experience, and catching friction with heatmaps or NPS drop-offs. The question should never be 'how do we get them back' but 'why did they leave and how do we stop the next one.' Network-effect products are the only real exception, where teammates can pull dormant users back organically.
