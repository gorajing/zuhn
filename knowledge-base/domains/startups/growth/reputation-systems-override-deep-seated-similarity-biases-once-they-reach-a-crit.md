Airbnb's research found that humans naturally trust people similar to them in age, location, and geography — a well-documented social bias. Below three reviews, reputation signals don't change this behavior at all. But above ten reviews, high reputation completely beats high similarity. This has profound implications for marketplace design: the critical early challenge isn't just getting supply listed, but getting supply reviewed past the ten-review threshold where trust dynamics fundamentally shift. This means marketplaces should invest aggressively in seeding early reviews — Airbnb gave employees travel credits specifically to leave reviews for new hosts. It also means that review density, not just review scores, is a key marketplace health metric. The ten-review threshold functions as a phase transition in trust, unlocking demand from demographics the host would otherwise never reach.
