Cheung's growth framework is deliberately sequential: pick one channel, execute fully for one week, evaluate. If it works, keep executing until it caps out. If it doesn't, move on. This stands in contrast to the common approach of running Google ads, content marketing, and partnerships simultaneously with fractional effort on each.

The reasoning is that partial effort on multiple channels produces ambiguous signal — you can't tell if a channel failed because it's wrong for your business or because you didn't execute well enough. Full dedication for a compressed time period gives you conviction about the result. She also emphasizes revisiting failed channels later, since your business context changes (Homejoy couldn't afford Google ads early on but could later when unit economics improved). Channels aren't permanently good or bad — they depend on your current position.
