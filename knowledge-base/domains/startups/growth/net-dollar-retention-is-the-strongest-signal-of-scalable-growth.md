Net Dollar Retention measures whether your existing customer base generates more revenue over time through upsells, cross-sells, and usage expansion — net of churn and downgrades. Best-in-class SaaS companies achieve 110-130%+ NDR, meaning the business grows even if it stops acquiring new customers entirely.

VCs prize NDR because it's a compounding engine. A startup with 120% NDR doubles its existing-customer revenue roughly every 3.8 years without a single new sale. This reveals product-market fit at the deepest level — customers aren't just staying, they're buying more. The tactical playbook includes premium plan tiers, usage-based pricing that scales with customer success, proactive customer success teams that identify expansion opportunities, and baking expansion triggers directly into the product experience.
