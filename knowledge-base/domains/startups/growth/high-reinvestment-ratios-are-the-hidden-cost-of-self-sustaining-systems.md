In the Calvin Cycle, 3 molecules of RuBP are processed through carbon fixation and reduction to produce 6 molecules of G3P. But 5 of those 6 must be recycled to regenerate the starting 3 RuBP molecules. The net yield is just 1 G3P per full cycle — an 83% reinvestment rate.

This pattern appears everywhere in growth systems. SaaS companies reinvest 60-80% of revenue into customer acquisition and retention just to maintain growth rates. Content creators spend most of their output on audience maintenance rather than audience expansion. The Calvin Cycle reveals a fundamental truth: self-sustaining systems are expensive to sustain. The visible output (1 G3P, net new revenue, audience growth) dramatically understates the total energy the system requires. Founders who budget only for the net output without accounting for regeneration costs will starve the cycle.
