General Catalyst's thesis that roll-ups could be bigger than SaaS challenges the prevailing startup orthodoxy of building software products. The argument centers on a $6T+ services market (legal, IT, customer service, property management) that is fragmented, inefficient, and now vulnerable to consolidation through a combination of acquisitions and applied AI.

The model works by acquiring traditional service businesses at low multiples, then applying AI to dramatically improve margins and scalability. Legal, IT services, customer service, and property management are already proving this works. Unlike SaaS, which requires building demand for a new product, roll-ups capture existing revenue streams and optimize them. For founders and investors, this represents a strategic shift: instead of competing in crowded SaaS markets, the larger opportunity may be in acquiring and transforming legacy service businesses with AI-powered operational improvements.
