When you're a tiny team (1-2 people), the temptation is to try five growth strategies at once. Adora argues this produces false negatives — you'll conclude channels don't work when really you just didn't invest enough effort to find out. By concentrating a full week on one channel, you get a much cleaner signal.

This also applies to revisiting failed channels later. Homejoy couldn't afford Google ads early on because established competitors like Merry Maids had higher margins and could outbid them. But after Homejoy improved unit economics, those same paid channels became viable. A channel that fails today may succeed when your business fundamentals change — but you need the clean initial signal to know what you're revisiting.
