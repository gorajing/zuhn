There's a measurable generational shift in purchasing behavior: younger consumers increasingly choose products based on the founder's story, the company's values, and its stated mission — not just features or price. This creates a structural opening for small, founder-led startups to compete with larger players by leading with authenticity and purpose.

The practical implication is that mission isn't just marketing — it's a retention and community-building mechanism. Startups that clearly articulate what they stand for (sustainability, mental health, financial inclusion) are converting customers into community members who have emotional investment beyond the product. This is harder for large companies to replicate because it requires genuine founder presence and consistent values alignment, which scales poorly in corporate structures.
