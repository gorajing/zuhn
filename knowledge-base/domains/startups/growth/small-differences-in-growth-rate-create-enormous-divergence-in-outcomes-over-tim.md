In epidemic modeling, the difference between a transmission rate that produces a growth factor of 1.15 versus 1.05 seems marginal — both are 'growing.' But 1.15^30 is about 66x while 1.05^30 is about 4.3x. Over the same period, a 10-percentage-point difference in daily growth rate produces a 15x difference in outcome. This extreme parameter sensitivity is a defining property of exponential systems.

For startups, this means the difference between 5% and 10% weekly growth isn't '2x better' — it's the difference between reaching 1M users in 3 years versus reaching 1M in 18 months, which often means the difference between running out of money and achieving escape velocity. This is why YC obsesses over weekly growth rate as the single most important metric: tiny improvements in the exponent create massive divergence in where you end up.
