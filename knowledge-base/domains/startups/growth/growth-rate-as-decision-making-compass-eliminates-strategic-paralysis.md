Graham argues that startups should pick a target growth rate (5-7% weekly at YC) and use it as a compass for all decisions. Should you attend a conference? Hire someone? Add a feature? The answer is whatever gets you your target growth rate. This transforms the bewilderingly complex problem of starting a company into an optimization problem — a narrow focus that programmers find satisfying and effective.

The key insight is that hill-climbing via weekly growth targets almost never leads to local maxima in practice, because the act of forcing founders to take action every week is more valuable than any amount of strategic deliberation. Nine times out of ten, sitting around strategizing is procrastination. Founders' intuitions about which hill to climb are usually better than they realize, and most good ideas in startup-space are adjacent to even better ones.
