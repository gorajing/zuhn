Lessons from Grubhub and Pinterest demonstrate that marketplace growth hinges on solving the supply side first. Grubhub methodically onboarded restaurants city-by-city before investing in consumer acquisition, while Pinterest seeded content and curated boards to ensure new users found valuable pins immediately.

The asymmetry exists because consumers evaluate a marketplace in seconds based on available inventory, but suppliers will tolerate low demand if onboarding is frictionless. This means founders should concentrate resources on supply density in a narrow vertical or geography before expanding demand channels. Premature demand-side spending on a thin-supply marketplace burns capital and creates negative first impressions that are hard to reverse.
