Adora provides one of the clearest pivot heuristics in startup literature: set an optimistic-but-realistic growth plan (e.g., doubling users weekly from 1→2→4→8), and if you hit 3-4 consecutive weeks of no growth or negative growth while executing hard, something fundamental is broken.

Critically, she distinguishes this from the common founder myth of 'just stick with it for 3 years and it'll work.' Real growth curves are jagged — you'll have lulls — but the overall trend should be up. The pivot signal isn't any single bad week; it's sustained flatness despite genuine effort. And pivoting doesn't mean starting from scratch — Homejoy was the 13th iteration, each building on lessons from the last. The key qualifier is 'while executing hard' — if you're not putting in full effort, the lack of growth tells you nothing about the idea.
