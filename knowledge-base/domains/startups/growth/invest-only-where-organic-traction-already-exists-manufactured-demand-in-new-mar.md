Waze repeatedly tried to open specific cities or countries through deliberate investment and business development, and it never worked. Meanwhile, markets that showed organic adoption responded explosively to additional investment. France became one of Waze's strongest markets by accelerating organic trends; Germany, despite being a comparable market with similar investment, never gained traction because there was no organic foundation.

This principle has a specific operational implication: don't pursue business development or partnerships until you can point to an organic trend you're accelerating. The metaphor is 'pouring gasoline on fire' — gasoline without fire produces nothing. For network-effect businesses especially, organic traction is the signal that the network has reached minimum viable density. Trying to skip that step through marketing spend or partnerships puts your destiny in someone else's hands and burns resources without building durable growth.
