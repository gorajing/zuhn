James Pastan (Head of Growth, Framer) defines activation as sign-up → first meaningful outcome that correlates to a downstream business metric. Compound metrics taken literally define negative product pathways (Facebook's seven-friends-in-ten-days would justify blocking users until seven friends). At Framer the metric is publishing an active site — but zero edits before publishing, or a site nobody visits, isn't activation. Setup enables aha; only aha + habit (they come back) is activation. Extends the KB's metric-design and feedback-loop principles into activation.
