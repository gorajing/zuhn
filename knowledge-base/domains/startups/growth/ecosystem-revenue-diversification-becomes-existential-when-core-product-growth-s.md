Apple's deliberate decision to stop reporting unit sales marked a strategic narrative shift: the company reframed its value proposition from 'we sell more phones each year' to 'each user generates more revenue over time.' This was necessary because public markets price stocks on growth expectations — flat unit sales, even at 200M+ premium devices, signal stagnation to investors.

The ecosystem play (Apple Music, iCloud, Apple Watch, AirPods, streaming video) converts one-time hardware buyers into recurring revenue streams. Higher ASPs ($1,100-$1,400) extract more per transaction while services extract more over time. This two-pronged approach — charge more per device AND monetize the installed base — is the playbook for any hardware company hitting saturation. The critical insight is that this pivot must happen proactively while the core business is still strong, not reactively when decline is already visible.
