First-time founders typically build the product they want, then scramble to find customers. Repeat founders invert this — they start with distribution mechanics, ICP testing, and channel validation before writing much code. As Justin Kan puts it: first-time founders are obsessed with product, second-time founders are obsessed with distribution. This isn't anti-product; it's recognizing that a product without a distribution engine is just a prototype. The practical shift is treating messaging, positioning, and early funnels as week-one priorities, not marketing problems to solve after launch. Founders who internalize this can short-circuit months of post-launch floundering.
