Most marketplace founders evaluate customer acquisition channels in isolation—if a channel's CAC exceeds the revenue from a single transaction, it's discarded. But fintech-enabled marketplaces fundamentally change this math by capturing more value per transaction through bundled financial products (insurance, financing, concierge services).

This creates a compounding advantage: higher ARPU at the same CAC means more channels become profitable, which means faster growth, which means more data for underwriting models, which means better financial products, which means higher ARPU. OpenDoor bundling renovation services and Ribbon bundling bridge financing are examples where the financial product isn't just revenue—it's the growth engine that funds customer acquisition competitors can't match.
