Traditional marketing funnels are linear: you push users in at the top, some convert, most don't, and the moment you stop spending, growth stops. Growth loops fundamentally change this by making each user's action (sharing, inviting, creating content) the trigger for acquiring the next user. The key architectural difference is that loops are self-reinforcing — output feeds back into input — while funnels are consumptive. This means loops get stronger over time while funnels hit diminishing returns. The practical implication is that startups should audit their user behavior for naturally occurring loop-like patterns before investing in paid acquisition scaling.
