Figma's freemium model was the enabling infrastructure for its entire bottom-up motion. By letting designers start for free, the product could spread virally within organizations before any budget conversation occurred. The permission layer — manager approval, IT review, procurement contracts — was bypassed entirely at the adoption stage and only re-engaged at the monetization stage, when the product had already proved its value.

The key design insight is that freemium must be genuinely useful at the free tier, not artificially crippled. A crippled free tier forces users back into the permission layer before they have evidence of value, defeating the purpose. Figma's tiered pricing structure was designed so that individuals got real value for free, teams paid for collaboration features, and enterprises paid for admin controls — each upgrade trigger was natural rather than coercive.
