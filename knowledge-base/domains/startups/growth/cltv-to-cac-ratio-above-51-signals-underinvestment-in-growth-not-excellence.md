Most founders celebrate a high CLTV to CAC ratio, but ratios significantly above 3:1 — especially 5:1 or 7:1 — often reveal under-spending on customer acquisition. If every customer returns 5-7x their acquisition cost, the company likely has room to invest more aggressively in growth channels while still maintaining healthy economics.

This is particularly common in B2B SaaS with long-term contracts and low churn where organic growth masks the opportunity cost of not scaling paid acquisition. The optimal strategy is often to deliberately move the ratio closer to 3:1 by reinvesting in growth, especially when the market opportunity is large. VCs look for this signal as evidence that a founder understands capital deployment, not just capital efficiency.
