Gross retention measures how many customers or how much revenue you keep from a cohort — it caps at 100%. Net retention adds upsell revenue back in, meaning it can exceed 100% even with some churn. A company with 90% gross retention but 120% net retention is growing its existing customer base by 20% annually without spending a dollar on acquisition.

This distinction fundamentally changes how much you should invest in customer acquisition. If net retention is above 100%, every customer you acquire becomes a compounding revenue stream. If it's below 100%, you're on a treadmill — constantly needing new customers just to maintain current revenue. The metric directly informs whether CAC payback periods are sustainable and whether the business model works at scale.
