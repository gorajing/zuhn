In exponential systems, outcomes are determined by the exponent, not the base. A community spreading a virus at 15% daily versus 5% daily does not produce a 3x difference — over 30 days it produces a difference of orders of magnitude. The same math applies to startup growth: a company growing 3% weekly versus 1% weekly will be ~4.5x larger after a year. This extreme sensitivity to the growth rate has two implications. First, optimizing the rate deserves disproportionate attention because small improvements create enormous downstream value. Second, any analysis that treats growth rate as fixed is fragile — even modest interventions (social distancing in epidemics, conversion optimization in startups) that shave a few percentage points off the rate can fundamentally alter the trajectory.
