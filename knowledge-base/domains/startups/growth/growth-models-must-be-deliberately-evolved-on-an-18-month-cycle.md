Elena Verna prescribes a specific cadence for growth model evolution: layer new channels on top of existing ones, expect diminishing returns from current tactics, and allocate 20-25% of resources to exploring new growth loops. New channels take 6-18 months before showing meaningful results, so exploration must start well before current channels plateau.

This counters two common failure modes: complacency (riding one channel until it dies) and panic-switching (abandoning a working channel for the next shiny thing). The 20-25% allocation creates a portfolio approach — the majority of resources optimize proven channels while a meaningful minority builds the next generation. The 18-month cycle gives enough time to validate new loops without betting the company on unproven channels.
