Meet-Ting's scheduling coordination has a built-in viral mechanism: when a user schedules a meeting through the AI, every guest encounters the product. Growth is embedded in the product's core function rather than requiring separate marketing spend. This is a textbook example of product-led growth where the use case itself is the distribution channel.

This pattern — where the product's core action naturally reaches non-users — is one of the most powerful and capital-efficient growth mechanisms available to startups. Calendar and email tools are especially well-suited because coordination inherently involves multiple parties. The key insight for founders is to evaluate whether their product's core interaction naturally involves people outside the current user base, and if so, to design the experience so that exposure feels valuable rather than spammy.
