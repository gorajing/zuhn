A common trap in early-stage startups is calculating CAC using only paid media spend, which dramatically understates the real cost of acquiring customers. A complete CAC calculation includes paid media, content production and SEO costs, marketing tools and CRM platforms, sales team salaries and commissions, outsourced agencies, and even onboarding tools for PLG motions.

This matters because an artificially low CAC inflates the CLTV/CAC ratio, creating a false sense of healthy unit economics. When investors or CFOs dig deeper and apply fully loaded CAC, the ratio often drops significantly. Founders who proactively present fully loaded CAC demonstrate financial sophistication and avoid painful due diligence surprises that can derail funding rounds.
