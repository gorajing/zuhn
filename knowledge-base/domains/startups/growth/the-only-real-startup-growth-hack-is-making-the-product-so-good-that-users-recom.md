Graham repeatedly found that founders wanted to discuss launch timing, getting influential people to talk about them, and securing media exposure — all of which are test-hacking approaches to growth. The actual answer, which founders found shockingly simple, is that you get lots of users by making the product genuinely great, at which point users recommend it to friends and growth becomes exponential.

This is a powerful counter to the growth-hacking industry. While tactical optimizations have their place, they're secondary to the core question: is your product good enough that people spontaneously tell others about it? If not, no amount of launch-day optimization or influencer outreach will produce sustainable growth. The founders who took years to grasp this weren't stupid — they were well-trained test hackers encountering their first unhackable test.
