The investor contrast was between older thematic investing, where a firm could hold a theme for long periods, and the current AI cycle, where the premise itself may need to be refreshed every few months. The key question is not just whether a category matters, but when the product-market window opens for a particular startup.

For founders, this means static positioning decays quickly. For investors, it means a memo from last quarter may have the right category but the wrong timing, evaluation criteria, or buyer assumption.
