The article notes that most CFOs are type-A personalities motivated to win rather than to optimize. This is a behavioral insight with practical leverage: loss-framed arguments (we are falling behind) tend to outperform gain-framed ones (we could grow) for risk-averse decision-makers, and CFOs are structurally positioned to be risk-averse.

Data like '75% of high-growth companies have a dedicated innovation division' and '40% of Fortune 500 companies will disappear in 10 years' are more persuasive not because they are more rigorous but because they activate the competitive instinct. For innovation advocates, the implication is to lead with the competitive benchmark, then back it up with the financial framing—not the other way around. FOMO opens the door; the option-value argument closes it.
