Most attempts at all-in-one platforms fail because building quality across many product surface areas is genuinely hard. PostHog's success with analytics, experimentation, feature flags, session replay, error tracking, data warehouse, and surveys in one platform is notable precisely because it's rare. The key insight is that the value isn't just convenience—it's workflow continuity. Being able to follow an issue from session recording through analytics impact to feature flag fix to A/B test to survey feedback is qualitatively different from stitching together separate tools. That end-to-end traceability is the holy grail that creates defensibility. For founders, this suggests the all-in-one bet can pay off enormously if you have the execution discipline, but most companies should probably not attempt it.
