Most companies default to incremental improvement of their existing business model without consciously choosing that allocation. An honest assessment of the current innovation portfolio — mapping each project on a spectrum from pure exploitation to pure exploration — reveals the actual strategy being executed, which often diverges dramatically from the stated one.

This assessment step matters because cognitive biases and organizational incentives naturally pull resources toward safe, incremental work. Without a clear baseline showing the current portfolio mix, strategy conversations become abstract debates rather than concrete reallocation decisions. The assessment creates accountability: if you claim to value breakthrough innovation but 90% of your portfolio is incremental improvement, the gap between aspiration and reality becomes undeniable.
