Graham compares containing parental ambition to keeping heroin out of a prison: naive solutions won't work because you're fighting one of the most powerful forces in human nature. Parents will push their scruples to the limit for their children, especially when other parents do the same. This means any credentialing system, no matter how well-designed, will eventually be gamed.

The deeper insight is about system design philosophy: when facing a force this powerful, don't try to build better walls (more rigorous credentials, more corruption-proof admissions). Instead, make the walls matter less by shifting to direct measurement. This is a general principle for designing robust systems—if a pressure is irresistible, don't resist it; redirect it or make the thing it's pressuring irrelevant. Graham's solution of encouraging smaller organizations achieves this by making credentials unnecessary rather than unhackable.
