New Story's evolution from purely philanthropic funding to a model where families pay for homes and land reveals a critical scaling insight: philanthropy and government subsidies are excellent for proving risky models and funding innovation, but they cannot scale to serve 1.6 billion people. The transition requires building systems (better financing, lower construction costs via 3D printing, digital payments via crypto) that make the product affordable enough for the market itself.

This mirrors venture capital logic applied to social impact — use subsidy capital to de-risk and prove the model, then shift to self-sustaining economics. The key enabling innovations are cost reduction (3D printing drops home costs to $6K-$10K) and financial infrastructure (Bitcoin/Lightning enables digital mortgages for unbanked populations). Founders in any deep-tech or infrastructure space can learn from this: use grant/philanthropic capital for the expensive proving phase, but design from day one for eventual market sustainability.
