Alexander highlights organizations like the Liberal Gun Club and Conservative Animal Welfare Foundation as examples of cross-factional presence. While these minority-faction branches may seem small, they provide access to policymakers who would never meet with the opposition. This 'embassy model' of maintaining presence across faction lines is a structural advantage.

For startups and organizations, the lesson generalizes: building relationships across competing ecosystems, political alignments, or industry factions creates optionality. When power shifts, you already have trusted voices in the new power structure. This requires resisting the urge to purge members who don't fit the dominant faction's identity — the short-term cohesion gain is outweighed by the long-term loss of cross-factional reach.
