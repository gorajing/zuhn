Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen is explicitly described as a 'useless byproduct' of splitting water molecules during the light-dependent reactions. Yet this waste product became the foundation for all aerobic life on Earth, fundamentally reshaping the planet's atmosphere and enabling entirely new kingdoms of organisms.

This pattern repeats in business: AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure byproduct. Slack emerged from a failed gaming company's internal chat tool. The insight is that systems optimized for one output often generate secondary outputs that prove more transformative than the original goal. Founders should actively monitor what their core process produces as waste — it may be the real opportunity.
