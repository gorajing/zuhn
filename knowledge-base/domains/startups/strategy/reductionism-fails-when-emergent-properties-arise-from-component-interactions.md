Steven Strogatz argues that while reductionism has been phenomenally successful across every branch of science, the great frontier is reassembling parts to understand wholes. We don't understand the immune system, consciousness, or the economy well precisely because their important properties emerge from interactions between components, not from the components themselves.

For builders, this means that understanding each feature, team member, or market segment in isolation gives a dangerously incomplete picture. The Millennium Bridge engineers understood lateral forces, walking frequencies, and structural resonance individually — yet missed the catastrophic feedback loop that emerged from their interaction. This suggests that when designing complex products or organizations, dedicated effort must go into modeling interactions and feedback loops, not just perfecting individual components. Testing in isolation will systematically miss the most dangerous failure modes.
