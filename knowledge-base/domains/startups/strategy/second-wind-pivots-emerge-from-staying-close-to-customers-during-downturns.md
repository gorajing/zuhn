The article identifies a specific pattern in startup turnarounds: the pivots that rescue companies from the year 5-7 plateau consistently come from founders who went back to direct customer contact — calling major customers personally, testing new segments hands-on, and listening to PM proposals that emerged from customer-facing work. This is 'founder mode' applied to crisis recovery.

The mechanism is that during growth plateaus, the default organizational response is to retreat into strategy — hiring consultants, running offsites, analyzing spreadsheets. But the actual signal about where the next growth vector lives is in the gap between what customers need and what the company currently provides, and that signal only transmits through direct contact. This suggests that maintaining founder-customer channels even during high-growth periods is a form of strategic insurance, not a failure to delegate.
