Netanyahu describes Israel's economic transformation through two mechanisms. First, dozens of free market reforms that unleashed entrepreneurial energy. Second, and more uniquely, a permanent investment in conceptual products through military service — soldiers in intelligence units become knowledge workers who then become knowledge entrepreneurs after service.

This created a self-reinforcing innovation cycle: military needs drive R&D, which produces technically skilled people, who start companies, which attract global investment (Nvidia building a supercomputer in Israel, Intel investing $25 billion in a new plant). The model has broadened from telecoms to medicine, agritech, biology — 'everything is becoming technologized and in Israel everybody is dealing in everything.' The lesson for any region or organization: the most durable innovation ecosystems are built on permanent talent pipelines, not one-time investments.
