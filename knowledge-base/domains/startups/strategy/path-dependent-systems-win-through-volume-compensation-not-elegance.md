RuBisCo, the enzyme responsible for carbon fixation in all plants, is so inefficient that it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts that plants must spend additional energy to neutralize. Yet plants never evolved a replacement — they simply produce enormous quantities of it (an estimated 40 billion tons on Earth at any time) to compensate for its poor performance.

This is a powerful model for understanding path dependency in technology and business. QWERTY keyboards, JavaScript, TCP/IP — many dominant standards won not through superior design but through early adoption followed by ecosystem lock-in. The lesson for founders and engineers: the first adequate solution that achieves scale often beats the elegant latecomer. Optimization of the incumbent usually wins over replacement, because the switching costs compound with every dependent system built on top of it.
