Nejatian discovered this pattern by studying company histories obsessively (he read the US Postal Service Act from the 1700s when assigned to run Shopify Shipping). Union Pacific Railway made more money selling land and homes along rail routes than carrying cargo. Google monetizes through ads served on search results. Shopify evolved from SaaS to a merchant services company (payments, tax, billing, ads).

Silicon Valley consistently misunderstands first-derivative businesses because the LTV/CAC is harder to calculate — cohorts bake differently in SMB and consumer compared to enterprise. This is why both Shopify (Ottawa) and HubSpot (Boston) succeeded outside the Valley: Silicon Valley is allergic to SMB because it doesn't understand cohort dynamics. The strategic implication: look for the first derivative of your core product, and be willing to build it outside conventional ecosystem thinking.
