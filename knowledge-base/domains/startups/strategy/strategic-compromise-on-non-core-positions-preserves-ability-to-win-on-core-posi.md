The Democrats' historical successes involved deliberate strategic retreat: 'safe, legal, and rare' on abortion, civil unions as a stepping stone to marriage equality. These compromises weren't moral failures — they were strategic moves that preserved resources and credibility for the fights that mattered most. The current progressive refusal to entertain any compromise on any position simultaneously weakens their hand on every issue.

This maps directly to startup and organizational strategy. Companies that try to win on every front — every feature, every market segment, every positioning battle — end up winning none. The discipline of strategic compromise means identifying which positions are core to your mission and which are negotiable, then trading the negotiable ones for advantage on the core ones. It requires honest assessment of which battles you can actually win versus which ones feel righteous but drain resources from higher-impact fights.
