When markets collapsed in 2022, Brex and Ramp faced the same decision: accept reality or defend the old price. Ramp took the mark-down, reset expectations, and regained the ability to raise capital and operate without the burden of justifying a fantasy valuation. Brex chose to defend its $12.3B number by cutting costs internally rather than repricing externally.

The counterintuitive lesson is that taking a down round can be a power move, not a sign of weakness. By repricing to match fundamentals, a company removes the valuation overhang that constrains every subsequent decision. Defending an inflated price creates a doom loop: you can't raise new capital without admitting the old price was wrong, but you also can't grow aggressively without new capital. Founders who treat valuation as ego rather than strategy end up optimizing for optics while competitors optimize for outcomes.
