Destiny asserts that history moves in one direction — social progress is the default trajectory, and reverting to past norms (like shotgun marriages) is unrealistic. Shapiro pushes back hard: the entire 20th century was dominated by moral regression — Nazism, Stalinism, Maoism — all of which were regressions from the liberal democratic progress of the 19th century.

This has practical implications beyond political philosophy. Anyone building long-term strategies (investment theses, product roadmaps, institutional design) tends to assume that current trends will continue — that technology adoption only accelerates, that social liberalization only deepens, that markets only become more efficient. But history shows that discontinuities and reversals are not edge cases; they're recurring features of human civilization. Building for resilience against regression is at least as important as optimizing for continued progress.
