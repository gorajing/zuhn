Capital One evaluated Brex on current fundamentals: a profitable, scaled corporate spending platform with real enterprise customers. Building this from scratch would have cost more time and money than the acquisition price. From their perspective, $5.15B was a disciplined strategic purchase, not a venture-style bet.

VCs evaluated the same transaction against the $12.3B peak and their fund return expectations. For a late-stage fund that needs 3-5x returns, buying at $12.3B and exiting at $5.15B is a capital loss regardless of how impressive the business is. This framework incompatibility explains why startup exits generate such contradictory narratives — the same number is simultaneously a bargain and a disaster depending on the buyer's math.
