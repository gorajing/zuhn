The reframe is that incumbents don't lose because they're dumb or slow — they lose because attention is finite and the day-to-day of running an operating business leaves no bandwidth for deep learning of a new substrate. Startups inherit 'time to learn' as an endowment. This is also why incumbents who say 'we're AI-first' while maintaining the same org chart almost always fail: they're using the words without paying the attention cost. The harder observation is that genuine adoption requires firing or re-leveling people, which incumbents avoid because the political cost exceeds the short-term productivity cost — until a competitor who started AI-native makes the comparison obvious.
