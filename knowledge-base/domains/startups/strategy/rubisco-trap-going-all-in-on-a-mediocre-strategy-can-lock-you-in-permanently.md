RuBisCo is the most abundant protein on Earth (~40 billion tons) despite being terrible at its job — it confuses O2 for CO2 roughly half the time. Plants compensate by producing massive quantities rather than evolving a better enzyme, because their entire metabolic architecture depends on it.

This is a precise analog for startups and tech platforms that scale on an adequate-but-flawed core architecture. Once you've built your entire ecosystem around a mediocre foundation, the cost of redesigning exceeds the cost of compensating. The lesson isn't 'get it right the first time' — early RuBisCo was optimal for its environment. The lesson is that your early architectural choices become permanent once you scale, even if the environment changes dramatically. Recognize the moment before lock-in when redesign is still cheaper than perpetual compensation.
