When Microsoft dominated the PC era, it enjoyed margins that were practically impossible to replicate in mobile or cloud. The psychological and organizational anchoring to those margins created a structural barrier to reinvention — any new business looked inadequate by comparison, making it easy to dismiss or underfund.

This is a generalizable pattern beyond Microsoft: dominant incumbents struggle not because they lack resources or talent, but because their success calibrates expectations and culture around a specific business shape. Nadella's insight was accepting that Microsoft would never dominate like it used to, and redefining success around growth in new directions rather than recapturing past glory. Leaders navigating platform transitions must explicitly reset organizational expectations about what 'good' looks like in the new paradigm.
