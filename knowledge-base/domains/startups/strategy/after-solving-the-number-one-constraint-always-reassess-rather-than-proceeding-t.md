Hormozi applies theory of constraints thinking to show that fixing the number-one bottleneck changes the entire system state. Problems that seemed critical before may vanish (because they were downstream symptoms) while entirely new problems may surface as the new binding constraint. If you had pre-committed to tackling items 2-5 in order, you'd be solving problems that no longer exist.

This maps to a broader strategic principle: strategy is not a static ranked list but a dynamic reassessment loop. The correct cadence is solve-one → reassess → reprioritize → solve-one, never solve-one → solve-two → solve-three from an aging backlog. The paid-ads team example illustrates this perfectly: fixing the pixel tracking issue might resolve conversion rate problems downstream, making the planned conversion optimization work unnecessary.
