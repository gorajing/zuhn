RuBisCo, the enzyme responsible for carbon fixation in photosynthesis, frequently grabs oxygen instead of CO2 — failing at its core job up to 50% of the time. Yet plants produce an estimated 40 billion tons of it rather than evolving a better alternative, because the entire metabolic architecture depends on it.

This mirrors technology and business lock-in: QWERTY keyboards, x86 architecture, legacy codebases. When a system achieves dominance early (plants colonized Earth using RuBisCo), the surrounding infrastructure co-evolves to compensate for its flaws rather than replace the core. The lesson for founders: your early architectural choices become permanent. Optimize for correctness at the foundation, because you'll be patching around bad core decisions forever.
