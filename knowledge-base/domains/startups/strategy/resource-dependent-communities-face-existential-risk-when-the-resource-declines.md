Barrow's economy depends heavily on North Slope oil — Prudhoe Bay holds North America's largest oil deposit and drives both direct employment and the government jobs that oil tax revenue funds. As oil supplies deplete and the world shifts to renewables, the town faces an economic cliff. The mayor explicitly acknowledges the threat but notes that tourism — the typical pivot for oil-dependent regions like the UAE and Norway — requires hotels, transportation, and tourist infrastructure that simply don't exist.

This is a pattern visible across resource-dependent communities worldwide: the same isolation that makes extraction profitable also makes diversification nearly impossible. The infrastructure built to serve extraction (airports for workers, not tourists; housing for employees, not visitors) doesn't transfer cleanly to alternative industries. For startups, this maps to the danger of building a business entirely dependent on a single platform, customer, or technology trend without investing in adjacent capabilities before the core revenue stream declines.
