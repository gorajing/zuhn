Photosynthesis converts only about 1-2% of incoming solar energy into chemical energy — an objectively terrible conversion rate. RuBisCo fails half the time. The whole system requires elaborate workarounds for its own byproducts. Yet this 'complicated, inefficient and confusing' system has been the dominant energy platform on Earth for 450 million years with no competitor displacing it.

The parallel to startup strategy is direct: a mediocre product that reaches market first and builds network effects (in biology, the entire food chain depending on it) becomes nearly impossible to displace, regardless of how suboptimal it is. The time spent optimizing before launch is almost always less valuable than the ecosystem lock-in gained by shipping. Plants didn't wait for a better enzyme — they shipped RuBisCo, scaled it massively, and built workarounds for its failures. This is exactly what successful startups do with technical debt.
