Constantine moved his capital east for structural reasons: the eastern provinces were wealthier, more urbanized (inheriting Greek city-states), and more compact — making them easier to administer and tax. The Eastern Empire's survival for a millennium after the West's collapse wasn't about superior military strategy; it was about better positioning relative to trade routes, agricultural wealth, and defensible geography.

For startups, this maps to market selection and positioning. Companies that establish themselves in rich, growing markets with natural structural advantages (network effects, regulatory moats, proximity to customers) outlast those that try to brute-force their way through hostile territory. The West's mistake was spreading too thin across impoverished, hard-to-govern provinces. The East's advantage was concentration in a compact, wealthy, well-connected geography. Market selection is destiny.
