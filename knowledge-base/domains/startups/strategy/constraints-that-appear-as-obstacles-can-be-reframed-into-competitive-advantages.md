Costco turns nearly every apparent disadvantage into a strategic filter. The membership fee selects for affluent households (average income ~$100K). The warehouse layout forces wandering through aisles, increasing impulse purchases. Bulk quantities attract business owners who make up one-third of members but two-thirds of sales. Even the lack of decoration signals 'we spend money on low prices, not aesthetics.'

This is the same principle behind IKEA's self-assembly model making customers proud of their furniture, or how exclusive clubs use high barriers to create belonging. The pattern for founders: before removing friction from your product, ask whether that friction is accidentally filtering for exactly the customer segment you want. Not all barriers are bugs — some are the feature that makes the business model work.
