The nuclear bubble in the trench doesn't simply expand or simply collapse — it oscillates. It overextends, loses pressure, gets pushed back by the water, re-expands with remaining energy, gets pushed back again, and eventually is chopped into smaller bubbles that drift upward harmlessly.

This maps to how disruption plays out in structurally resistant markets. A well-funded startup enters with explosive force, overextends beyond sustainable reach, gets pushed back by incumbents and market realities, tries again with remaining capital and momentum, and often fragments into smaller niche plays. The oscillation pattern — not a clean explosion or clean failure — is the default dynamic. Recognizing this pattern helps founders calibrate expectations: the first expansion wave is almost certainly not the final state, and surviving the recompression phase is where the real strategic challenge lies.
