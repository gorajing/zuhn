RuBisCo is the most abundant protein on Earth (~40 billion tons) despite being terrible at its job — it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time in modern atmospheric conditions. Plants compensate by producing massive quantities rather than evolving a better enzyme, because their entire metabolic architecture depends on it.

This maps directly to startup strategy: early architectural decisions that are 'good enough' become load-bearing walls. Slack built on PHP, Twitter on Ruby on Rails, Facebook on PHP — all suboptimal choices that persisted because the switching cost exceeded the inefficiency cost. The lesson isn't to avoid early commitment, but to recognize that your first adequate solution will likely become permanent infrastructure. Choose early foundations with that permanence in mind.
