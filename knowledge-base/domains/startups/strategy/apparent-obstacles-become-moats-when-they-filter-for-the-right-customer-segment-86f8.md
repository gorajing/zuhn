Costco turns three apparent disadvantages into compounding advantages. The mandatory membership fee ($60-$120) selects for households earning ~$100,000 average income. The warehouse layout that seems confusing forces customers through more aisles, increasing discovery and basket size. The bulk quantities attract business owners—one-third of members who drive two-thirds of sales.

This pattern applies broadly to startup strategy: friction that seems like a barrier to growth may actually be filtering for your highest-value segment. The key diagnostic is whether the friction correlates with willingness to pay and customer lifetime value. If your 'obstacle' systematically selects for customers who spend more, stay longer, and advocate louder, removing it would actually destroy value. The counterintuitive move is to lean into the friction rather than smooth it away.
