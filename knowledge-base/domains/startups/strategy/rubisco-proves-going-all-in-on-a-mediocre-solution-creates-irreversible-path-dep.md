RuBisCo, the enzyme that fixes carbon in photosynthesis, was evolved in a low-oxygen atmosphere and works poorly in today's oxygen-rich air — misfiring up to 50% of the time by grabbing O2 instead of CO2. Yet plants produce 40 billion tons of it rather than evolving a replacement, because every downstream pathway depends on it.

This mirrors technical debt in startups and legacy systems: once you scale massively around a suboptimal core (a database schema, a pricing model, a key hire), the cost of replacement exceeds the cost of workarounds. The lesson is that 'good enough at scale' creates its own gravity well. The time to choose the right foundation is before scaling, not after — because post-scale, you're stuck compensating for the original compromise forever.
