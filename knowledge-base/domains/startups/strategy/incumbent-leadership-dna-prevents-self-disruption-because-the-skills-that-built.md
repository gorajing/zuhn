Wand references Andy Grove's 'Only the Paranoid Survive' to explain why incumbent leadership fails at self-disruption. The core insight: people at the top of large organizations got there by consistently making the right decisions for the existing business model. Their 'entire DNA is wired to a certain thing.' Disruption requires someone to make choices that are counter to what got the CMO, CEO, and CFO promoted in the first place.

This is deeper than just resistance to change — it's a selection effect. The organizational hierarchy filtered for people whose judgment pattern-matches to the old model. You're asking a $30B company CEO to make choices that contradict the very instincts that earned them the role. Wand's counter-strategy at Hippo: weight decisions by domain expertise multiplied by conviction rather than seniority, and actively cultivate independent thinkers who are comfortable being challengers. Without this, he warns, 'natural atrophy' turns any growing company into the next incumbent.
