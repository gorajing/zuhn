When YC told New Story to double their target from 50 to 100 houses in 100 days, it forced them to fundamentally rethink their go-to-market approach rather than just working harder. They ended up delivering 115. The key mechanism is that bold goals change the solution space you explore — incremental goals let you optimize existing approaches, while 2x goals force you to find entirely new ones.

This creates a self-reinforcing cycle: bold vision attracts bold people (investors, employees, partners) who would never engage with a 'safe' target. The gravitational pull of normalcy constantly drags founders toward average goals, so maintaining audacity is an active choice, not a personality trait. The practical implication is that if your goals don't make some advisors uncomfortable, they're probably not ambitious enough to unlock the talent and capital you need.
