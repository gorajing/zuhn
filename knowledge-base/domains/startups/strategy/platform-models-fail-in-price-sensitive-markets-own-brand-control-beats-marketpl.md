After Series B, Open Secret tried to become 'Whole Foods for India' by onboarding 150+ better-for-you brands across online and offline. The result: 50 crores revenue at -47% EBITDA, burning cash rapidly. The pivot to a Trader Joe's own-brand model transformed the business: 2 years later, 170+ crores revenue at 4% profitable EBITDA — a 3x revenue growth with a 51-point EBITDA swing.

Three structural reasons own-brand wins in India: (1) Affordability — when purchase frequency is high and price elasticity is higher, own-brand lets you make premium products affordable; (2) Quality control — when your brand promise is 'unjunked' (no maida, no trans fat, no artificial preservatives), controlling your own supply chain lets you keep that promise; (3) Unit economics — marketplace margins are thin by nature. This directly contradicts the Silicon Valley default of 'build the platform, not the product.' In price-sensitive, high-frequency consumer markets, controlling the product is the only path to sustainable margins.
