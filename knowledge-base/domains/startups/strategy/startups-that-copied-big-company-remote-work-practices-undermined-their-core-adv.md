Michael makes a nuanced distinction: large companies are inherently remote because once you pass a certain headcount, the probability that your three closest collaborators sit near you approaches zero. The remote-vs-in-person debate was always really about startups, where colocation is one of the few structural advantages a small team has over a large organization.

The COVID experiment forced startups to go remote involuntarily. What was surprising was that many startups -- teams explicitly trying not to be like big companies -- voluntarily adopted big-company remote practices and kept them. This amounted to copying the operational playbook of exactly the type of organization startups are supposed to out-execute. Some positive norms did stick: Zoom meetings and real-time chat became normalized, which made certain types of collaboration more efficient.
