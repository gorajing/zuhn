RuBisCo evolved when Earth had abundant CO2 and almost no oxygen. It was mediocre at carbon fixation even then, but it was the only game in town. Plants compensated by producing massive quantities rather than evolving a better enzyme. When oxygen levels rose and RuBisCo started grabbing O2 instead of CO2 half the time, plants developed elaborate workaround pathways (C4, CAM) rather than replacing the enzyme.

This mirrors startup dynamics: early platform choices, APIs, and architectural decisions become load-bearing infrastructure. The ecosystem builds compensating mechanisms around the flaws (plugins, middleware, migration tools) rather than replacing the core. The lesson isn't to avoid early commitment — RuBisCo's organism conquered the planet — but to recognize that compensating for a locked-in mediocre choice is the norm, not the exception. Budget for workarounds, not replacements.
