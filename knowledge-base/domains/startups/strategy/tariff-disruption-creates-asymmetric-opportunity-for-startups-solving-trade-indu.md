Flexport's doubling of its duty-drawback business demonstrates a pattern: when regulation creates universal pain, the companies that abstract away complexity capture outsized value. The tariff wave is generating demand for supply chain simulation tools, AI-driven policy tracking, trade finance platforms, alternative component sourcing, and domestic manufacturing automation.

This is the classic 'sell pickaxes in a gold rush' play, but with a geopolitical twist. VCs are already shifting due diligence to reward tariff-resilient or tariff-solving startups. The window is time-sensitive because the specific tariff landscape will evolve, but the meta-pattern—turning regulatory complexity into product opportunity—is evergreen. Founders should ask: does my startup help others navigate the friction, or am I just absorbing it?
