The Mongols had never seen a castle before their conquests began, yet they became expert siege warfare practitioners by interrogating prisoners. They adopted gunpowder (likely introducing it to Europe), built naval fleets to attack Japan, and forcibly relocated administrators, artists, and specialists throughout their empire to fill capability gaps.

This 'acquire don't build' approach to capability development is a powerful startup strategy. Rather than investing years in building domain expertise from scratch, organizations can acquire it through hiring domain experts, acquiring smaller companies with specialized knowledge, or deeply studying competitors. The Mongols show that the speed advantage of absorption over invention can be decisive — they conquered more territory in 25 years than Rome did in 400.
