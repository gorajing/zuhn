Levie argues that technology disruptions don't just affect the tech industry — they cascade into every vertical, forcing enterprises to rebuild their technology stacks. Retail needs multi-channel commerce software because consumers now shop across phone, web, and store. Healthcare needs telehealth and connected records as the business model shifts from procedure-based billing to wellness outcomes. Media companies need big data and marketing automation as distribution shifts from linear theatrical releases to on-demand targeting of specific audience segments.

The insight is that these vertical opportunities are often invisible to horizontal software companies because they require deep domain understanding. A movie studio needing predictive analytics to target 30 million potential viewers out of 3 billion internet users represents an unpredictable mashup of industries that no generic analytics platform is optimized for. This creates a durable pattern: every time an industry undergoes business model disruption, there's a corresponding wave of vertical enterprise software startups that understand the domain deeply enough to build the specific tools those enterprises need.
