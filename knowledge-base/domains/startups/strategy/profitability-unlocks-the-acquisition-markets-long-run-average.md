Graham notes that public acquirers are 'like property management companies run by madmen' — sometimes overpaying enormously, sometimes completely uninterested. Over a five-year window, you're likely to hit a favorable cycle. But you can only wait if your company is self-sustaining through its own revenues.

This creates a direct link between profitability and exit optionality. Startups burning cash are at the mercy of investor patience and market timing. Profitable ones can play the long game, treating the acquisition market like a volatile asset that reverts to mean. The strategic implication: even if you plan to sell, building a profitable business isn't the conservative choice — it's the one that maximizes your expected acquisition outcome.
