Frebel describes writing papers with simulation-focused colleagues who study the same phenomena she observes. Despite both being astronomers with shared interests, 'it was almost like we're speaking two different languages.' Theorists and observers approach problems with different mental models, different vocabularies, and different standards of evidence.

But her most successful and enjoyable papers came from exactly these difficult cross-disciplinary collaborations — the ones where both parties invested time to find common ground. This directly parallels the challenge any organization faces when trying to integrate engineering, design, business, and research perspectives. The lesson: if even scientists in the same field struggle with this, cross-functional communication in any organization requires deliberate investment, not just goodwill. And the payoff for pushing through the communication barrier is disproportionately high.
