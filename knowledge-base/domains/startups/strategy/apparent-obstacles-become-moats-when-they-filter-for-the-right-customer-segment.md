Traditional business advice treats friction as the enemy of growth. Costco demonstrates that strategically placed friction can be a powerful customer-selection mechanism. The $60-$120 membership fee filters for households with ~$100K average income. The bulk-only format attracts business owners (one-third of members generating two-thirds of sales). The confusing warehouse layout forces wandering that increases basket size.

Each apparent obstacle simultaneously accomplishes two things: it reduces operational costs (no fancy decor, no back rooms, no small-unit packaging) while selecting for the exact customer profile that maximizes lifetime value. This is the same principle behind IKEA's self-assembly model making customers feel proud of their purchases rather than annoyed. The pattern suggests founders should ask not 'how do we remove all friction?' but 'which friction selects for our ideal customer while repelling unprofitable ones?'
