Graham advises startups to try to make as little money as possible—not as a charity, but as a competitive moat. If you can compress a billion-dollar industry into fifty million dollars of total revenue and own all of it, you've built an unassailable position. Craigslist did this to classified ads; free alternatives did it to paid content.

This works because incumbents face the innovator's dilemma at extreme ratios. A company making billions cannot rationally pivot to a model that generates 5% of current revenue, even if it's the future. The startup has no legacy revenue to protect. This is why the most dangerous competitors aren't the ones trying to out-earn you—they're the ones making your revenue model obsolete.
