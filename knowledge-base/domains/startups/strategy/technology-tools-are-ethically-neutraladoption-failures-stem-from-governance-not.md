The FTX collapse illustrates a recurring pattern in technology adoption: a tool designed for transparency and decentralization was used to propagate fraud because centralized actors operated without checks and balances. The technology itself continued functioning as designed—Bitcoin and Ethereum kept working throughout the crisis.

This framing matters for any startup building powerful tools. The ethical burden falls not on the tool's design but on the governance structures surrounding its deployment. Founders building platforms that handle user assets or data must proactively design governance constraints—boards of directors, proof-of-reserves, on-chain transparency—rather than assuming good intentions will suffice. The absence of regulation is not permission to operate without internal controls.
