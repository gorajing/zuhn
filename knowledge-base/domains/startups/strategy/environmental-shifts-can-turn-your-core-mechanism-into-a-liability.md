RuBisCo was perfectly adapted to early Earth's CO2-rich, oxygen-poor atmosphere. But photosynthesis was so successful that it flooded the atmosphere with oxygen — its own waste product. Now RuBisCo grabs oxygen instead of CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic phosphoglycolate that plants must spend energy to neutralize. The winning strategy literally created the conditions for its own degradation.

This is a precise model for platform risk and market-reshaping disruption. A dominant company can change its market so thoroughly that its core competency becomes a liability. Newspapers built readership that attracted advertisers, then the internet they helped popularize destroyed their distribution monopoly. The question for any dominant strategy is: what environment am I creating, and will my approach still work in that new environment?
