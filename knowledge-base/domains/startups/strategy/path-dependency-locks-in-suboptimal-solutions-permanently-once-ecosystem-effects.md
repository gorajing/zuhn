When early photosynthetic organisms evolved RuBisCo, atmospheric CO2 was abundant and O2 was scarce — the enzyme worked well. But the success of photosynthesis itself flooded the atmosphere with oxygen, creating the exact conditions that made RuBisCo fail. The organism's own dominance reshaped the environment in a way that permanently locked in the suboptimal design.

This is a precise analogy for platform lock-in: a startup's early technical choices work fine at small scale, but as the product succeeds and the ecosystem (APIs, integrations, user habits) co-evolves around those choices, the cost of switching becomes astronomical. The system's own success creates the conditions that make its flaws permanent. Recognizing when you're in a 'RuBisCo moment' — where your growth is about to lock in a current limitation — is one of the most valuable strategic instincts a founder can develop.
