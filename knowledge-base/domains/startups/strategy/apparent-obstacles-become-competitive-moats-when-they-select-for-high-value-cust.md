Conventional business wisdom says reducing friction increases conversion. Costco demonstrates that strategically placed friction can be a feature, not a bug. The membership fee performs triple duty: it filters for affluent customers (average household income ~$100K), triggers sunk-cost psychology that discourages comparison shopping, and generates the majority of revenue independent of margins.

This principle extends well beyond retail. Any business can ask: what barrier could we introduce that would repel low-value customers while making high-value ones feel invested? The key insight is that the barrier must deliver genuine value on the other side — Costco's prices really are dramatically lower. A paywall without corresponding value is just friction; a paywall with genuine value behind it is a moat that deepens with every renewal cycle.
