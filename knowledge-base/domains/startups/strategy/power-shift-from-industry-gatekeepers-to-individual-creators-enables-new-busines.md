The music industry's historical structure gave labels monopoly control over distribution, branding, and customer relationships. Digital disruption shifted that power back to artists — but only those who developed business competency could capitalize on it. Zoo Labs exists precisely to close this capability gap.

This pattern generalizes beyond music. In any industry where gatekeepers lose their distribution monopoly (publishing, education, media), the first wave of independent practitioners who combine craft excellence with business discipline will capture outsized value. The window is temporary — eventually the market matures and new intermediaries emerge — making speed of business capability adoption a competitive advantage.
