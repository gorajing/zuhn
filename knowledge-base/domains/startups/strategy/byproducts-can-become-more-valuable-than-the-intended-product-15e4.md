Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for energy storage. Oxygen was literally a waste byproduct of splitting water molecules. Yet this 'useless' byproduct reshaped Earth's entire atmosphere and enabled the evolution of all aerobic life — a transformation infinitely more significant than the glucose production it was designed for.

In startups, the parallel is striking: Slack began as an internal tool for a failed game company. Flickr was a feature of an abandoned MMO. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure. The lesson isn't just to watch for valuable byproducts — it's that the byproduct's value often dwarfs the original product's because it serves a broader ecosystem need that wasn't visible from within the original problem frame.
