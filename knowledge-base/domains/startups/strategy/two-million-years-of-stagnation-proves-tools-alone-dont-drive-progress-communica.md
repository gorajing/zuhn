Early humans used tools for nearly two million years with minimal advancement. Fire accelerated things by enabling cooking (better nutrition, larger brains) and extending productive hours, but the true inflection point came around 50,000 years ago when advanced language enabled humans to communicate information 'effectively and down to the last detail.'

This allowed flexible cooperation in large groups — the capability the video identifies as humanity's true differentiator. The strategic implication is that investing in communication infrastructure (documentation, shared context, knowledge management) compounds more reliably than investing in better tools alone. A team with mediocre tools but excellent communication will outinnovate a team with superior tools but poor knowledge sharing.
