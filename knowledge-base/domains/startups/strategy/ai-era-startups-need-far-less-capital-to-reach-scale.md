The article argues that AI has fundamentally changed the economics of building startups. Tasks that required a 30-person team can now be accomplished by 5 people using tools like ChatGPT, Notion AI, and Zapier. No-code and low-code platforms eliminate the need for full engineering teams, allowing founders to go from idea to MVP without raising millions.

This operational leverage shift is structural, not cyclical. It means the minimum viable capital to reach profitability has dropped dramatically for software businesses. The implication is that the traditional VC model — which was designed for an era when scaling required massive upfront capital investment — is increasingly mismatched with how modern startups actually operate. Communities like Indie Hackers and ecosystems around MicroAcquire are proving this model at scale.
