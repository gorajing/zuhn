Glycolysis perfectly illustrates the bootstrap paradox: you need ATP to make ATP. The cell must invest 2 ATP molecules to destabilize glucose enough to split it, only then earning 4 ATP for a net gain of 2. This mirrors startup economics where you must spend capital (hiring, infrastructure, marketing) before generating revenue.

The deeper lesson is about what happens with those returns. The cell doesn't stop at the 2 ATP profit — it feeds the byproducts (pyruvate, NADH) into further stages that generate 17x more energy. Similarly, the most successful resource allocation strategies don't optimize for immediate ROI but for enabling downstream compounding. The 2 ATP from glycolysis are almost irrelevant compared to the 34 ATP the electron transport chain produces from glycolysis's byproducts.
