In most technology markets, speed of execution and product quality determine winners. Energy infrastructure inverts this: the binding constraint is regulatory approval, not engineering capability. A decade-long approval timeline for major transmission projects means that companies with permits in hand have a moat that no amount of capital or technical talent can overcome in the short term.

This pattern generalizes beyond energy. Any market where regulatory timelines exceed technology cycles creates structural advantages for incumbents and early movers. For startups, this means the strategy isn't to build better grid technology—it's to navigate permitting faster, acquire companies with existing approvals, or build solutions that avoid the transmission bottleneck entirely (like on-site generation for data centers).
