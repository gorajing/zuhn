RuBisCo is the most abundant protein on Earth (~40 billion tons) and is catastrophically inefficient — it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts plants must actively detoxify. Yet plants went 'all-in' on the RuBisCo strategy hundreds of millions of years ago when atmospheric CO2 was high and O2 was low. When the environment changed (O2 rose), redesigning the core enzyme was impossible because the entire metabolic architecture depended on it.

This maps directly to startup strategy and platform architecture. Early technical or strategic choices that achieve market dominance become load-bearing walls — even when clearly suboptimal. The winning move is not to pick the perfect architecture upfront (impossible) but to recognize which early decisions will become irreversible and invest disproportionate design effort there. Plants couldn't swap RuBisCo; they evolved elaborate workarounds (C4 photosynthesis, CAM metabolism) instead. Companies do the same with legacy systems.
