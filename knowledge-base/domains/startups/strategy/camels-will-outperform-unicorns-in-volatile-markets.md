The unicorn narrative — billion-dollar valuations backed by massive VC rounds — is losing its shine as many celebrated unicorns turn out to be unprofitable and overextended. The article introduces 'camels' as the counter-archetype: startups that grow deliberately, maintain profitability, and survive market downturns intact.

This framing reflects a deeper shift in what startup success means. When interest rates are high and capital is expensive, the ability to survive without external funding becomes a competitive advantage. Camels don't just endure downturns — they can acquire distressed competitors and capture market share while VC-backed companies are cutting staff and pivoting. The camel model is particularly compelling because it aligns founder incentives (ownership, autonomy, sustainability) with business fundamentals in a way that the unicorn model never did.
