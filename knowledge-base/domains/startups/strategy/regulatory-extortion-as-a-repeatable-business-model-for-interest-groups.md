SEIU's strategy follows a clear pattern: identify an industry (hospitals, then dialysis, then tech), propose legislation that sounds progressive but is designed to be ruinous, then either extract concessions for withdrawal or win the vote and capture the earmarked revenue. The union leader explicitly described this as a deliberate investment strategy with measurable ROI.

This is a generalizable threat model for any industry operating in jurisdictions with citizen-initiated legislation. The defense costs are inherently asymmetric — $100M to defeat vs $5M to propose — and the attacker can simply try again next cycle. For startups especially, this represents an underappreciated category of regulatory risk: not regulation by lawmakers who can be lobbied, but regulation by interest groups exploiting democratic mechanisms as leverage tools.
