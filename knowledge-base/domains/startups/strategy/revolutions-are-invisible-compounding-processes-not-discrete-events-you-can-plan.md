Evidence from Franchthi Cave shows humans selectively breeding snails for size over 13,000 years ago — not to start a revolution, but because bigger snails meant more calories. Agriculture emerged the same way: plant more of what grows well, repeat. The 'revolution' label is retrospective; participants were just optimizing locally.

This has direct implications for startup strategy. Founders who try to 'start a revolution' often build solutions in search of problems. The more reliable path mirrors the agricultural pattern: find what people already want more of, make it incrementally easier to get, and let the compounding effects reshape the system. The revolution becomes visible only in retrospect. Today's subtle, irrevocable decisions — like which AI workflows people adopt — will be remembered as revolutions by future historians.
