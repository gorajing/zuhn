The guide recommends running startups like a hedge fund manager by diversifying across cloud providers. Use AWS Activate credits ($5K-$100K) in year one, shift inference workloads to Google Cloud's $350K two-year program in year two, then test GPU pricing on providers like Scaleway. This multi-cloud arbitrage can provide 2-3 years of heavily subsidized infrastructure.

The key enabler is modular architecture designed from the start for portability. The pitfall is that migration costs (engineering time, downtime risk, data transfer fees) can erase savings if your systems are tightly coupled to one provider. The right approach is designing movable pieces, not rebuilding the house each time. This reframes cloud architecture decisions as capital allocation decisions.
