After Boeing discontinued the 757 in 2004, a clear capability gap emerged: no aircraft existed between 230-280 seats with 4,000+ mile range. Airlines needed this exact category for transatlantic routes between smaller markets. The gap was so obvious that the next entirely new plane from Boeing (the 797) was near-certain to fill exactly this space.

This pattern is powerful for opportunity identification in any industry. When an incumbent discontinues a product line, existing customers don't disappear — they adopt worse substitutes and accumulate unmet demand. The gap becomes more obvious over time as workarounds strain. For founders, systematically scanning for recently discontinued products with loyal user bases is a reliable source of startup ideas. The demand is pre-validated and the timing signal is the growing pain of inadequate substitutes.
