Most strategic discussions about business models remain qualitative—leaders discuss strengths and weaknesses in general terms without a consistent framework for comparison. The 9-mechanic assessment approach forces granularity: each component of the business model gets an independent score, and the resulting profile visualizes where the model is strong versus vulnerable.

The key insight is that no business model achieves a perfect score, and awareness of specific weak points is itself a strategic advantage. A company that knows it scores poorly on switching costs but well on recurring revenue can make targeted investments rather than spreading resources across unfocused 'innovation' initiatives. This structured approach also enables before-and-after comparisons when testing business model extensions, turning business model design from an art into a measurable discipline.
