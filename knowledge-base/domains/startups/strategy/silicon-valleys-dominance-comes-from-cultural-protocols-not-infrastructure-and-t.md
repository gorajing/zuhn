Many cities have tried to create 'the next Silicon Valley' by replicating measurable infrastructure—VCs, universities, coworking spaces, incubators. This approach fails because the real engine is a set of shared psychological protocols: non-zero-sum thinking, credentialism-free meritocracy, habitual speed, thinking big, impact as the status marker, paying it forward, radical information sharing, and celebrating founder-heroes.

These protocols are self-reinforcing: they attract like-minded people globally, creating a feedback loop that strengthens the network. But they're extremely difficult to transplant because any new location already has its own cultural protocols that change slowly. The recommended approach is to start with a 'white-hot center' of 5-8 trusted CEO nodes and grow the network organically over 5+ years, rather than trying to engineer culture top-down.
