World War II's expansion by both Axis powers was driven more by desperate resource needs than ideological conviction. Germany needed agricultural land and raw materials; Japan needed food and fuel that its island geography couldn't provide. The ideological frameworks (Lebensraum, Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere) were constructed around these material needs, not the other way around.

This pattern recurs in business: companies frame aggressive expansion in terms of mission and vision, but the underlying driver is often resource scarcity — runway burning, market windows closing, or dependency on a single revenue stream. Understanding this helps founders distinguish between genuine strategic vision and survival-driven expansion dressed up as strategy. The test: would you still expand this way if resources were abundant?
