Tim's mentor told him (too late) that 'your first release should be absolutely embarrassing.' The team spent months perfecting the mobile app before putting it in the wild. When they finally launched, there were still many issues and features that needed changing — all that polish was wasted on the wrong things.

This directly challenges quality-first engineering culture. The counterweight: in early-stage startups, quality without user direction is waste. Release something broken in days, iterate based on real feedback every few days. The embarrassment of a bad release is orders of magnitude less costly than the embarrassment of spending two years building something nobody wants.
