Kurzgesagt draws a direct parallel between Age of Exploration colonization and lunar development: Phase 1 is scouting (plant a flag, gather data, leave), Phase 2 is a dependent outpost (small team, reliant on home base for supplies, doing groundwork), and Phase 3 is a self-sufficient colony generating its own economic value through exports.

This maps cleanly to startup market entry. Phase 1 is customer discovery and small experiments — you're just proving the territory exists. Phase 2 is an MVP with a small team that still depends heavily on existing revenue or funding — you're building infrastructure and learning what local resources (distribution channels, partnerships, talent) you can harvest. Phase 3 is product-market fit with self-sustaining unit economics. The key insight is that each phase has different success criteria and trying to skip phases (jumping straight to colony without outpost learnings) reliably fails.
