The Great Filters concept — barriers that most civilizations never cross — maps precisely onto startup mortality. Companies face sequential filters: finding product-market fit, surviving the funding gap, scaling operations, navigating market shifts, and maintaining culture through growth. Like civilizations, most startups are eliminated at one of these barriers, and the overall survival rate is the product of passing each one. A company with 70% odds at each of five major filters has only a 17% chance of clearing all five. This framing is more useful than generic 'most startups fail' advice because it directs attention to identifying which specific filter you're approaching next and concentrating resources on crossing it. The civilization-scale insight also applies: if most companies in your space are dying at the same stage, that stage is likely a structural Great Filter for the category, not just bad execution.
