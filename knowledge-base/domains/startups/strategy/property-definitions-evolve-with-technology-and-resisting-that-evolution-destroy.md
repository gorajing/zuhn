Paul Graham argues that property is not an inherent, unchanging concept but rather a pragmatic convention: something counts as property when treating it as property works for society. Hunter-gatherers didn't treat land as property; we do because it works for our economy. Digital content is undergoing the same shift — the arrival of networks made data move like smells, freely and uncontainably.

This has direct implications for startup strategy. Founders building in regulated or IP-heavy industries should recognize that incumbents fighting to preserve obsolete property definitions (through litigation, lobbying, or DRM) are creating a strategic opening. The incumbents are trying to make people 'breathe through tubes' on a planet with atmosphere. Startups that build business models aligned with how information actually moves — rather than how incumbents wish it moved — capture the value the old guard is leaving on the table.
