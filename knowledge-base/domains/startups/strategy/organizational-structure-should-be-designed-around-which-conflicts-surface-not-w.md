Most organizational design optimizes for efficiency and harmony, but this creates blind spots where critical conflicts go unheard. The insight is to flip this: decide which conflicts are most important for your business to hear — customer needs vs. engineering constraints, short-term revenue vs. long-term product quality, speed vs. reliability — and then structure teams so those tensions naturally surface to decision-makers.

This is counterintuitive because it means deliberately creating friction points. A company that separates sales and product into siloed organizations with different KPIs will naturally surface the tension between customer demands and product vision. The key is ensuring these conflicts reach people who can make trade-off decisions, rather than being resolved (or suppressed) at lower levels where the full strategic context isn't visible.
