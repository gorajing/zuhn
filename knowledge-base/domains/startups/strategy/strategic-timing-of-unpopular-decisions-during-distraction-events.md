On June 14, 2018, while Russian soccer fans celebrated their World Cup opening match victory over Saudi Arabia, Putin quietly announced raising the retirement age from 60 to 65 for men and 55 to 63 for women. The timing was clearly strategic — maximize distraction, minimize immediate protest momentum.

Despite the timing, it still produced Putin's largest-ever short-term approval collapse (15 points) and trust fell to 33% by January. The lesson for any leader implementing unpopular changes: strategic timing can blunt initial reaction but cannot neutralize fundamental opposition to changes that affect people's core economic interests. The distraction buys days, not months. This applies equally to startup pivots, pricing changes, or organizational restructuring — if the change genuinely hurts your stakeholders, no amount of timing optimization substitutes for genuine value alignment.
