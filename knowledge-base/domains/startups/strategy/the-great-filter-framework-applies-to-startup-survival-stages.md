The Great Filter concept maps cleanly onto startup evolution. Just as civilizations face barriers at specific developmental stages (origin of life, multicellularity, intelligence, technology that doesn't self-destruct), startups face discrete kill zones: pre-PMF (most die here), first scaling attempt (unit economics break), organizational complexity transition (~50-150 people), and market maturation (disrupted by next wave). Each filter eliminates the majority.

The strategic insight is that knowing which filter you're at changes everything about resource allocation. Pre-PMF, you conserve cash and iterate fast. At the scaling filter, you invest heavily in infrastructure and hiring. At the organizational filter, you invest in culture and process. The Fermi framing also suggests a disturbing possibility: maybe the filter ahead (e.g., AI disruption of your market) is harder than the one behind you. The absence of large successful companies in your space might not mean the opportunity is untapped — it might mean there's a filter nobody has passed yet.
