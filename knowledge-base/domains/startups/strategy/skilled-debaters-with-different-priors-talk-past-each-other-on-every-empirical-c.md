Throughout the 2.5-hour debate, Shapiro and Destiny agree on many factual premises (education matters, two-parent households correlate with outcomes, marriage rates are declining) but consistently reach opposite conclusions. Shapiro interprets declining marriage as a moral regression requiring cultural restoration. Destiny interprets it as an economic consequence of delayed career starts and rising costs, requiring systemic intervention.

This reveals a fundamental limitation of evidence-based argumentation: when two parties have different priors about human nature (are people primarily self-correcting agents vs products of their environment?), they can share all the same data and still diverge completely. For anyone building products, teams, or strategies, this means: persuasion through evidence only works when the audience shares your foundational assumptions. Otherwise, you need to surface and negotiate the priors themselves.
