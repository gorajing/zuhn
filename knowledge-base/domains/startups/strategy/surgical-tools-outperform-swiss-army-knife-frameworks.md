The BMC deliberately excludes competition, problem validation, and metrics—not because they don't matter, but because a tool that tries to do everything does nothing well. The surgeon analogy is precise: you wouldn't want a brain surgeon using a swiss army knife.

This principle extends beyond business canvases. When designing any analytical framework or decision tool, the temptation is always to add more dimensions. But each addition dilutes focus and makes the tool harder to master through repetition. The better architecture is a suite of integrated single-purpose tools, each optimized for a specific type of decision.
