The jet stream blows at 110+ mph and can cut a full hour off a transatlantic flight, saving $5,103 in fuel per flight at $1.89/gallon. On extreme days, winds exceed 200 mph and a 777 can approach the sound barrier. No amount of engine tuning, weight reduction, or route micro-optimization within still air could produce comparable savings.

This maps directly to startup strategy: identifying and aligning with powerful external forces (market tailwinds, platform shifts, regulatory changes, demographic trends) produces gains that dwarf internal optimization. A mediocre product riding a massive tailwind often outperforms an excellent product fighting headwinds. The strategic question isn't 'how do we fly faster?' but 'where is the jet stream today?' — and the answer changes daily, just like the North Atlantic Tracks are republished every morning.
