The video explains why the gene, not the individual or species, is the unit of natural selection. A unit must satisfy three criteria: (1) it makes near-identical copies of itself, (2) it exhibits traits affecting environmental interaction, and (3) those traits affect survival probability. A single nucleotide is too small (no selectable trait). A chromosome is too large (gets broken up during reproduction). The gene is the sweet spot.

For businesses, this framework asks: what is your replicating unit? For a SaaS company, it might be the individual user workflow that gets shared. For a marketplace, it's the successful transaction pattern. For a content business, it's the format/template that gets copied. Optimizing at the wrong level (company-level metrics vs. the actual replicating unit) leads to the same confusion as thinking evolution operates on species rather than genes.
