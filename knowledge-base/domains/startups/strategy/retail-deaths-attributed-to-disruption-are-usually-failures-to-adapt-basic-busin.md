The narrative of Amazon single-handedly destroying retail is convenient but misleading. Sears tried to do everything and did it all poorly. Toys-R-Us dismissed the internet. J.C. Penney lost touch with its customers. Meanwhile, companies like Costco, Aldi, Dollar General, and Ross thrived during the same period by executing distinctive strategies well.

This pattern recurs across industries: disruption doesn't kill healthy companies—it kills companies that were already declining but protected by market inertia. The 'Retail Apocalypse' was really a 'Mediocrity Apocalypse.' The implication for founders: when a new technology appears to threaten your market, the real question isn't 'how do we defend against the technology?' but 'have we been serving our customers well enough that they'd miss us if we disappeared?'
