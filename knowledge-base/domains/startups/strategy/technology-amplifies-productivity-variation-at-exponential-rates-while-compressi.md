As technology advances, it increases individual productive capacity at exponential rates, meaning the most skilled people can generate orders of magnitude more value. A high school student today can write software worth millions, while their peers scoop ice cream. This creates wider income variation.

However, the same technology democratizes consumption. Mass production makes high-quality goods cheap enough for everyone. A $30 Timex keeps better time than a $220,000 Patek Philippe. The rich drive the same cars, wear the same clothes, and eat the same food as the middle class. Brand is the last remaining differentiator — 'the residue left as the substantive differences between rich and poor evaporate.' For founders and investors, this means the real leverage of technology is in creating wealth through productivity, not in gaining exclusive access to better goods.
