Every major cloud and SaaS provider fights to lock in founders early because switching costs compound over time. This isn't charity — it's customer acquisition at scale. AWS, GCP, and Azure all know that a startup trained on their platform during the credit period is unlikely to migrate once real workloads are running. Algolia is explicitly noted as being 'sticky' with painful migration paths.

Understanding this dynamic gives founders leverage in two ways. First, you can negotiate extensions and additional tranches when showing growth, because vendors want you hooked. Second, you can use competing credit offers as leverage in vendor negotiations. The founders who treat credits as negotiable contracts rather than fixed perks extract significantly more value. Growth makes you valuable to vendors — if you're scaling usage, many will happily extend credits to keep you on their platform.
