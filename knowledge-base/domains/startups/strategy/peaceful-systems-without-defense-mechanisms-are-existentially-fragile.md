The Indus Valley Civilization is one of history's most striking examples of a cooperative system that lacked defensive infrastructure. Across 1500+ archaeological sites, researchers found almost no evidence of warfare or weapons — yet the civilization disappeared around 1750 BCE, with conquest being one of three leading theories for its decline.

This pattern recurs in business: companies that optimize purely for product and internal cooperation (like early BlackBerry or Xerox PARC) without building competitive moats get overrun by more aggressive entrants. The lesson isn't to become militaristic, but that any system — civilization or company — needs some investment in resilience against external threats, even during periods of prosperity.
