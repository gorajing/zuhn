Doi challenges the startup culture that glorifies funding rounds as achievements: 'I see funding as an obligation, not necessarily as a success.' TwoSpace deliberately avoided external funding for its entire life, which forced critical discipline: revenue is not king, profit is king; user count is not king, cash flow is king. Without someone bankrolling growth, you have to very quickly innovate and build product offerings that have margin and are scalable. The counterpoint to the 'raise fast, grow fast' narrative: when you're not relying on someone to bankroll you, you learn to build sustainable businesses from month one. The funded competitor's approach — spend $1M to acquire users who generate no revenue, then figure out monetization later — is a luxury that also breeds laziness about unit economics. Doi's first $100 went to branded hoodies, not growth hacking.
