Photosynthesis is described as a process that 'rather sucks' — it's complicated, inefficient, and confusing. RuBisCo fails at its core task roughly half the time. Yet photosynthesis became the dominant energy conversion system for life on Earth, not because it was elegant, but because it was first and sufficient.

This pattern repeats across technology and business: QWERTY keyboards, TCP/IP, JavaScript — all widely acknowledged as suboptimal but unassailable because they achieved critical mass. The strategic implication is that optimizing a solution before shipping it is often the wrong move. The competitive advantage of being adequate and deployed vastly exceeds the advantage of being optimal and theoretical. Speed to market with a working solution beats delayed perfection.
