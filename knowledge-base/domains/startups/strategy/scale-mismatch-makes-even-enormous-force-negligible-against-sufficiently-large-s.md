A 100-megaton nuclear weapon — the most destructive device humans have ever conceived — leaves the Moon's orbit essentially unchanged. The scale mismatch is so extreme that even orders-of-magnitude increases in bomb yield would remain irrelevant. This isn't a problem of insufficient force; it's a category error in approach.

Startups face this pattern when competing head-on against incumbents in their core market, or when trying to shift consumer behavior through brute-force marketing. The insight isn't 'try harder' — it's that direct force against a sufficiently massive system is structurally futile. The only viable strategies involve either finding leverage points where small forces create disproportionate effects (like how a tiny gravitational tug over millennia actually can shift orbits), or redefining what 'success' looks like entirely.
