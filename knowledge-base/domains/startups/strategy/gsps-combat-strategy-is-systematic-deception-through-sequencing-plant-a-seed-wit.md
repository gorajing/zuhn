GSP describes his signature strategy: use the jab — his longest weapon — as a 'decoy' and 'distraction.' After tagging the opponent once or twice, their 'short-term memory' makes them flinch at the jab, creating an opening for the takedown. He starts the jab deliberately low and outside the opponent's field of vision, creating a false sense of security about the distance. The key insight: 'you plant a seed in your mind and then I can get in easier.'

This maps directly to competitive strategy: establish a credible threat in one domain (like a jab) to create openings in another domain (like a takedown). The opponent allocates defensive resources to the visible threat, leaving them exposed to the real attack. For founders: sometimes the most effective competitive move isn't direct — it's establishing credibility in one area to create openings in the area you actually care about. The sequencing matters: the setup must be real enough to demand a response.
