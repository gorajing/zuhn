Vitalik makes a subtle but crucial distinction: technical feasibility of running a node is one aspect, but culture is equally important. People need to feel that independent verification is 'a very ingrained responsibility.' He notes this culture is difficult to cultivate and once lost is 'fairly difficult to try to get back.' The two reinforce each other: a culture that values running nodes will push for protocol changes that make it easier, and easy node operation makes that culture sustainable.

This principle applies broadly to any system that depends on distributed participation — open source projects, democratic institutions, peer review in science. The technical infrastructure enables distributed participation, but the cultural norm of actually participating is what prevents consolidation. Many competing blockchains subsidize node operators, but Vitalik questions what happens when subsidies disappear — suggesting that subsidy-dependent participation is fragile compared to culturally-motivated participation.
