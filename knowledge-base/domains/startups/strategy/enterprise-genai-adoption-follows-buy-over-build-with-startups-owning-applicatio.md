A survey of 500 executives reveals that buy-over-build is accelerating in enterprise GenAI adoption, with off-the-shelf tools like Claude, ChatGPT Enterprise, and Copilot dominating adoption and driving measurable ROI. The market is stratifying clearly: startups lead in horizontal, departmental, and vertical applications, while incumbents hold infrastructure positions. Coding tops departmental spend, and healthcare leads verticals.

This stratification pattern matters for startup strategy. It suggests that the AI application layer is where startups have the strongest competitive advantage, particularly in specialized vertical and departmental use cases where incumbents are slow to customize. For founders, this validates building AI-native applications rather than competing on infrastructure, and for investors, it highlights that the real value capture in enterprise AI may be in the application layer rather than the foundation model race.
