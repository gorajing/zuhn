Animal welfare advocates have secured over 3,000 corporate pledges globally — McDonald's cage-free, European retailers ditching low-welfare chicken breeds, Costco going 100% cage-free. This happened because the decision-makers at retailers and restaurants are consumer-facing and respond to public opinion, whereas legislation must pass through Agriculture Committees dominated by industry lobbyists who refuse to even invite opposing witnesses.

Meanwhile, billions of VC dollars have flowed into cultivated meat while humane-technology VC gets perhaps $10 million a year. In-ovo sexing — which has already spared 200 million male chicks from being ground up alive — was kickstarted with roughly $10 million in philanthropic money. The ratio between the moonshot path and the incremental corporate-campaign path is extreme: the campaigns have delivered real animals spared now while the moonshots remain contingent on scaling breakthroughs that may never arrive.

This generalizes: in a neglected cause area, the highest-leverage interventions are usually the ones that already have a playbook and just need more dollars poured into execution. Moonshots attract attention and capital because they feel transformational, but neglected incremental work is where the marginal dollar does the most good.
