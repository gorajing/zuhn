The gap between 'great workshop' and 'nothing happened after' is one of the most common failures in organizational strategy work. The article highlights that canvas tools create tangible outputs — filled-in frameworks with sticky notes, annotations, and visual maps — that persist beyond the session.

This matters because verbal agreements and meeting notes rapidly decay in shared understanding. A completed Business Model Canvas hanging on a wall serves as a persistent, shared reference that team members can point to, update, and hold each other accountable against. The tangibility of the output is not incidental to the tool — it is the mechanism by which workshop insights survive contact with daily operations.
