In a block and tackle system, each additional rope adds one unit of force — linear growth. In a compound pulley system, each stage doubles the previous stage's output: 1→2→4→8. Both achieve the same 8:1 mechanical advantage, but the compound system does it with fewer pulleys.

This maps directly to business strategy: stacking leverage mechanisms (automation on top of software on top of distribution) compounds their effect rather than adding linearly. The insight is that *how you arrange* your resources matters more than *how many* you have. Archimedes pulling an entire warship by himself wasn't about strength — it was about arrangement.
