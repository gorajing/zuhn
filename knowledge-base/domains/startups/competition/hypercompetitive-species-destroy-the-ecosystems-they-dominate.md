Kurzgesagt argues that species competitive enough to take control of their planet necessarily destroy it while competing for resources — runaway chain reactions in every ecosystem that, once started, cannot be fixed. This maps directly to competitive startup dynamics: the growth-at-all-costs mentality that wins market share can destroy the underlying ecosystem (burning through customer trust, collapsing supplier economics, or triggering regulatory backlash). The Great Filter version of this is absolute — 100% of civilizations powerful enough to alter their atmosphere make it uninhabitable. The startup version is softer but structurally identical: the traits selected for in hypergrowth (aggression, speed, resource extraction) are anti-correlated with long-term sustainability.
