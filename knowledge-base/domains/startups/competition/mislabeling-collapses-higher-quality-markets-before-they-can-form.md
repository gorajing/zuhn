Cooks Venture and other pasture-raised chicken startups went bankrupt despite clear consumer demand for higher-welfare, outdoor-raised chicken. The reason wasn't production cost but information asymmetry at the point of sale: factory-farmed chicken can be labeled 'all-natural,' which surveys show consumers interpret to mean the chickens were raised outdoors. When pasture-raised chicken sits on the shelf at $2 more next to an 'all-natural' product that seems equivalent, the premium market cannot clear.

Compare this to the egg sector, where 'cage-free' and 'pasture-raised' have enforced legal definitions. Pasture-raised eggs are now a rapidly growing premium segment, and Vital Farms has built a real brand on the welfare story. The only structural difference between the two markets is labeling enforcement.

The lesson for founders in any quality-differentiated market (food, cosmetics, sustainable goods, handmade, AI-training-data provenance): your business model depends on labeling regulation you don't control. If the law allows competitors to claim your differentiator vaguely, your market cannot form at scale regardless of product quality. Fighting for precise labeling definitions may be a higher-leverage business activity than improving the product itself.
