When teams skip structured customer research, they accumulate assumption debt: beliefs about customer needs that feel right but haven't been tested. Each untested assumption compounds risk — features built on wrong assumptions don't just fail to add value, they consume resources that could have gone toward validated opportunities.

The article frames this as the core cost of guessing: time building unwanted features, money invested in misdirected solutions, and opportunities lost while heading in the wrong direction. The fix isn't more research bandwidth — it's converting assumptions into explicit, testable hypotheses before committing resources. This is the Jobs-to-be-Done principle applied to risk management: understand what the customer is actually trying to achieve before investing in a solution.
