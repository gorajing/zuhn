When early organisms evolved photosynthesis, oxygen was literally toxic waste — a byproduct of splitting water to harvest electrons. These organisms had no use for it. Over billions of years, this 'waste' accumulated in the atmosphere and became the essential substrate for aerobic respiration, enabling all complex animal life including humans.

In product development, the most valuable output is frequently not what you set out to build. Slack emerged from a failed game company's internal chat tool. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure. The pattern suggests that teams should pay close attention to what their process produces incidentally, not just what it's designed to produce. The throwaway feature, the internal tool, the data exhaust — these byproducts deserve scrutiny as potential primary products.
