Walter identifies a specific cognitive trap PMs fall into: believing that because their project is strategically important to the company, other teams will naturally agree it should be prioritized. At YouTube, a partner team had a long backlog of competing requests, and general appeals to strategic importance would have failed.

The fix was concrete: walking the partner team through a detailed forecast model showing specific contribution to their metrics, and even recommending projects they could deprioritize. This level of preparation treats influence as a resource-allocation negotiation rather than a status assertion. The pattern applies broadly — whenever you need something from a team with competing priorities, you must do the work of showing why your request serves their goals, not just yours.
