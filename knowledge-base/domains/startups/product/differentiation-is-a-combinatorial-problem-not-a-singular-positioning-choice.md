Traditional positioning advice tells founders to find their one differentiator. But Lenny's framework reveals that differentiation is combinatorial — the most successful companies layer multiple dimensions. This reframes the product strategy exercise: instead of asking 'what makes us unique?' ask 'which 3-4 of these 20 axes can we credibly own together?'

The combinatorial framing also explains why copying is hard. A competitor can match your price or your quality, but matching your specific combination of price + unique supply + customer service + local identity requires rebuilding their entire business model. Each additional axis in your combination exponentially increases the difficulty of replication.
