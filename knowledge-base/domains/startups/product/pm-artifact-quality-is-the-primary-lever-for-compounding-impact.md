Dhiman frames PM artifacts—PRDs, product notes, design briefs—as the core output through which product managers demonstrate proficiency and create leverage. These documents are not bureaucratic overhead; they are the PM's primary tool for multiplying team effectiveness.

The insight reframes artifact creation from a checkbox exercise to a diagnostic and growth tool. If a PM is unsure what to focus on next in their career, Dhiman recommends scrutinizing recent artifacts and asking how they could be improved. This makes artifact quality both the leading indicator of PM skill and the most direct improvement lever available at any career stage.
