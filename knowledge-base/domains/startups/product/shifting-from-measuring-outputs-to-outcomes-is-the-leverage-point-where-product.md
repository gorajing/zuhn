The outputs-to-outcomes shift is one of product management's most cited but least practiced principles. Outputs measure what you ship (features, releases, story points); outcomes measure what changes as a result (user behavior, revenue, retention). Teams optimizing for outputs often fall into the build trap—shipping more without verifying impact.

The practical implementation requires changing how teams set goals (from 'launch feature X' to 'increase metric Y'), how they evaluate success (from deployment to adoption), and how they decide what to build next (from roadmap commitments to experiment results). This is especially critical for startups where every engineering cycle spent on low-impact features is an existential resource misallocation.
