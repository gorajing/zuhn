As AI tools dramatically reduce the cost and time to build software, the traditional moat of engineering talent and technical execution erodes. What remains scarce is taste — the ability to create aesthetic, intuitive products that users choose over functionally equivalent alternatives. Rex Woodbury argues this shift means startups should invest disproportionately in design and UX rather than engineering headcount.

This has practical implications for hiring, resource allocation, and competitive strategy. Founders who treat design as a core competency rather than a polish layer will have structural advantages in crowded markets where every competitor can ship features at similar speed. The pattern echoes historical shifts where manufacturing commoditization made brand and design the differentiator (e.g., consumer electronics post-2007).
