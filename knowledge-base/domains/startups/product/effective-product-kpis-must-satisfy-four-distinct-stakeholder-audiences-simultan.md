Most product teams design KPIs for themselves — they optimize for granular, actionable, leading indicators that help them make daily decisions. The problem is these metrics are opaque to executives (who need aggregate business health signals), stakeholders (who need progress-toward-goals signals), and customers (who need to see value delivered).

A robust KPI framework solves for all four simultaneously: the same underlying data should roll up into views appropriate for each audience. This requires intentional metric architecture — not just choosing what to measure, but designing translation layers between operational metrics and communicable ones. The failure mode is teams that maintain parallel metric systems for internal vs. external audiences, which creates misalignment and trust deficits.
