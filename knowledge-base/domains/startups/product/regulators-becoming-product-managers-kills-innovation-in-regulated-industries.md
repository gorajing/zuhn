The Libra/Diem experience reveals a pattern that extends beyond crypto: when regulatory oversight crosses from setting boundaries into directing product decisions, innovation dies. Regulators have legitimate roles in defining what's permissible, but they lack market feedback loops, user empathy, and iteration speed. When they become de facto product managers, the result is a product designed for compliance rather than users.

This insight applies to any startup operating in regulated industries (fintech, health tech, edtech). The strategic response is to design products that satisfy regulatory requirements as constraints rather than features — build for users within the regulatory box rather than building for regulators. Alternatively, choose jurisdictions or market segments where regulatory burden is lighter, establishing traction before engaging heavier regulatory environments.
