The standard technology adoption curve assumes users weigh costs against benefits and gradually adopt. Zimbabwe's mobile money adoption shattered this model. When the alternative to EcoCash was waking at 2 AM to queue at ATMs for a maximum withdrawal of one or two US dollars — or making a dangerous multi-day journey to send money to family — the 'consideration phase' collapsed to zero.

This has direct implications for startup timing and market selection. Products that are 'nice to have' in stable markets become 'must have' during crises. The key insight is that the depth of the pain, not the sophistication of the solution, determines adoption velocity. M-PESA's technology was relatively simple — SIM card identification, text-based transfers, a network of 100,000 human agents for cash conversion — but it solved an existential problem, which is why Zimbabwe's economy became 90% digital transactions by 2017.
