Graham argues that while young founders are worse at made-up ideas (which require experience to predict others' needs), they have a structural edge in organic ideas. Being at the forefront of technology adoption means they encounter new categories of brokenness first — the gap between what a new technology could do and what it currently does.

This reframes the conventional wisdom about founder experience. The advantage of youth isn't naivety or energy — it's proximity to the frontier. A 22-year-old using the latest tools daily will feel friction points that a 40-year-old industry veteran literally cannot perceive because they haven't adopted those tools yet. The most valuable startup ideas live in the intersection of 'just became possible' and 'clearly broken.'
