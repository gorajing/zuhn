Leyden observes that at every civilizational inflection point, the people most invested in the dying system resist change with existential intensity. The Civil War is the most extreme example: the South fought to the death rather than abandon slavery when the manufacturing economy demanded free labor. The 1930s America First movement resisted the New Deal transformation.

This pattern means that extreme polarization and political conflict are not signs that something is going wrong — they are the predictable signature of a major transition underway. The old system's defenders are passionate precisely because they correctly perceive that their world is being dismantled. Understanding this pattern helps builders maintain conviction during turbulent periods.
