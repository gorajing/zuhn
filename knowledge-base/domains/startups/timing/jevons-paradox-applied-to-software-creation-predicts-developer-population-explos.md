Replit's billion-developer thesis rests on the Jevons Paradox from economics: when a resource becomes more efficient and cheaper to use, total consumption increases rather than decreases. Applied to software, this means AI won't eliminate developers — it will create an explosion of new builders as every small business owner, student, and knowledge worker can create custom tools for the cost of a few API tokens.

The evidence is already emerging. Replit's platform saw a 34x year-over-year explosion in projects by Q2 2023, and the company now targets non-technical workers (sales staff, marketers, small business owners) rather than competing for professional developers. This reframes the AI coding market: the biggest opportunity isn't making existing developers faster, but enabling the next 990 million people who have ideas but couldn't previously build software. Companies positioning for this expanded market may capture more long-term value than those optimizing for the existing developer population.
