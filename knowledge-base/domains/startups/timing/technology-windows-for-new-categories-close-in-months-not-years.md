NFX presents compelling data on technology window compression. The social network window opened with Friendster's beta in October 2002 and effectively closed with Facebook's launch in February 2004 — just 11 months. The mobile social window ran from WhatsApp (February 2009) to Snapchat (July 2011) — about 30 months. After these windows closed, even superior products with talented teams couldn't gain traction.

This pattern carries a brutal implication for founders: timing isn't just important, it's existential. The AI games window has 'just cracked open,' suggesting roughly 36 months of opportunity. This means founders who are still perfecting their concept when the window closes will lose to those who shipped imperfect products within the window. The mobile gaming window was notably shorter (3 years) because foundational technologies arrived pre-packaged on phones — suggesting that when AI infrastructure matures rapidly, the AI games window could compress even further.
