Michael identifies a critical failure mode in tech thinking: people who are directionally correct about a trend (self-driving, AGI) often fail to account for how much timeline uncertainty matters for practical decisions. A founder who believed self-driving would arrive in 2017 and one who believed it would arrive in 2025 both held 'correct' views -- but should have built entirely different companies.

The same dynamic applies to AGI: even if every 'wacky prediction' about AI capabilities turns out to be true, the difference between an 18-month and a 10-year timeline should fundamentally alter startup strategy, hiring plans, and even personal life decisions. Michael recounts people delaying having children based on AGI timeline predictions -- revealing how timeline-insensitive thinking about technology trends can lead to costly real-world mistakes.
