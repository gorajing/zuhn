When a rule operates for thousands of years, it becomes so deeply embedded in culture, institutions, and individual psychology that people follow it unconsciously even after conditions have changed. The corporate ladder, prestige of large employers, credentialism, and management-as-advancement are all artifacts of the bigger-is-better era.

The arbitrage opportunity is precise: identify which inherited assumptions about career paths, organizational structure, and prestige hierarchies are now vestigial, then build strategies that exploit the gap between outdated consensus and current reality. This is especially valuable because the deeply-rooted nature of these traditions means most people will be slow to update, extending the window of opportunity for those who recognize the shift early.
