Founders tend to focus on controllable variables: product quality, team, business model, fundraising. But timing is a contextual variable that operates independently. A venture entering a market too early faces insufficient demand, missing infrastructure, or regulatory resistance. Too late, and incumbents have built moats that make customer acquisition prohibitively expensive.

This creates a strategic implication: founders should explicitly assess environmental readiness as a separate validation track. Regulatory trends, technology adoption curves, infrastructure availability, and competitive density all signal whether the timing window is open. Bill Gross's research on startup success factors found that timing was the single largest predictor of outcomes, ahead of team, idea, business model, and funding — making it the most important factor that founders most frequently ignore.
