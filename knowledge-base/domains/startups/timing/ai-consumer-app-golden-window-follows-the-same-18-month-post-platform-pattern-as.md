NFX draws a direct historical parallel between the iPhone app explosion and the current AI wave. After the iPhone launched in 2007, the breakthrough consumer apps (WhatsApp, Uber, Instagram, Snap, DoorDash) emerged in a golden period starting roughly 18 months later and running for about 5 years. They argue AI is following the same trajectory: founders need time to deeply understand the product and design possibilities before building truly native applications rather than incremental improvements.

This timing insight has practical implications for founders and investors. Being too early means building before the design patterns are understood; being too late means competing against established AI-native players. The framework suggests the current period (2024-2025) is the equivalent of 2008-2009 in mobile—early experiments are informing what works, and the true breakout applications are just beginning to emerge. The key is distinguishing between AI wrappers (lightweight apps on top of LLMs) and genuinely AI-native products that couldn't exist without the technology.
