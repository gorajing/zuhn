Pre-revolutionary France was one of Europe's wealthiest nations yet couldn't service its debt because the nobility and clergy — those with the most resources — were tax-exempt. King Louis XVI tried multiple reform ministers but couldn't overcome the structural resistance of those who benefited from the status quo.

This maps directly to platform and marketplace dynamics. Companies fail not because their ecosystem lacks value, but because they can't capture revenue from their most powerful participants. When your biggest users or partners have structural exemptions from your monetization model, you're France in 1789 — rich on paper, bankrupt in practice. The fix requires structural redesign (like the National Assembly abolishing feudal privileges), not incremental reform within the existing framework.
