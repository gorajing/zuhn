Huang uses the phrase 'zero-billion-dollar market' for a category that has no current revenue but that NVIDIA believes will become large. Automotive (15 years ago, when CTOs told him 'cars can't tolerate the blue screen of death'), CUDA (a decade before the machine learning market existed), data center (started 17 years before AI workloads), and now Omniverse (currently ~40 customers) all followed this pattern.

The strategic logic: 'When the market emerges, it's very likely there aren't that many competitors shaped that way.' Competing in an existing market means fighting incumbents on their terms. Positioning in non-consumption means the market forms around your existing capabilities. By the time others see the opportunity, the moat — developer ecosystems, architectural compatibility, distribution — is already built.

This is a counterintuitive corollary to 'PMF.' Huang is explicitly saying: don't wait for the market to prove itself. Reason about where it must go from first principles, then stand near that tree for years. You won't catch the apple before it hits the ground, but you'll be the first one to pick it up.
