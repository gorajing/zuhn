Paul Graham shows that in 1892, only 20% of American millionaires inherited their wealth — less than today. The 1982 Forbes 400, dominated by heirs and real estate/oil deals, was the product of a specific historical phase where J.P. Morgan-era financiers consolidated thousands of companies into oligopolies, making it nearly impossible to start new ones.

This reframing matters for founders and investors: the structural conditions favoring startups (low costs, accessible technology, available capital) aren't a bubble but a return to baseline. The oligopoly era was propped up by regulation, high tax rates, and economies of scale that technology has since eroded. Expecting a return to corporate dominance misreads which period was normal.
