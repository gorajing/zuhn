Matt Hagger built Scatter, one of the first smartphone photo-sharing apps, before Instagram existed. Despite getting early Android access from Google and raising over $1M, the app stores were too new, the smartphone ecosystem was unstable, and distribution was nonexistent. Instagram later executed the same idea with perfect timing.

The counterweight lesson: being right about the future is not the same as being ready for the present. If the technology infrastructure, distribution channels, and addressable market don't yet exist, you become an expensive R&D experiment that better-funded, better-timed competitors will learn from and surpass. Product-market fit requires market readiness, not just product readiness.
