Naval's observation about 1999 dot-com failures perfectly illustrates this: Webvan, Kozmo, and Pets.com all crashed, but Instacart, Postmates, DoorDash, and Chewy succeeded with essentially the same ideas a decade later. The difference wasn't the idea — it was that smartphones, GPS, mobile payments, and logistics infrastructure now existed as 'shoulders of giants' to stand on.

This has direct implications for startup timing decisions. Before pursuing an innovation, map the 'innovation stack' it depends on. If you're trying to bootstrap too many layers simultaneously, you're likely premature. The first version of a new technology should look 'surprisingly like the last version of an old technology' — true disruption appears incremental at the time and only looks revolutionary in retrospect.
