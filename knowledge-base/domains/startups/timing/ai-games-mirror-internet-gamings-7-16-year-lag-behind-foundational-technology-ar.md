NFX draws a direct historical parallel between the internet era and the AI era for gaming. The first web browser arrived in 1993, but foundational internet games like Runescape, World of Warcraft, and Minecraft didn't emerge until 2001-2009 — a 7-16 year gap. The bottleneck wasn't imagination but convergence: bandwidth, video processing, and memory all had to reach critical thresholds simultaneously.

AI games face the same multi-dependency convergence problem. Inference speed, inference cost, model quality, memory management, and generated media quality (image, video, voice, music) must all reach viable levels together. Philip Rosedale's SecondLife story illustrates this perfectly — he recognized the vision in 1995 but spent four years building prerequisite codec technology before the platform could support his ambition. This suggests AI game founders should be building foundational tools now rather than waiting for all pieces to arrive.
