The post identifies three failure patterns: overspending on marketing before proving product-market fit, underestimating CAC and churn, and being unable to explain unit economics to investors. The first is the root cause of the other two — once marketing spend is accelerated, founders discover real CAC and churn numbers in the worst possible moment (with depleting runway). The correct sequencing is: stabilize churn → validate payback period → then scale acquisition. A financial model forces this discipline by making the relationship between CAC, churn rate, and runway explicit before capital is deployed.
