GOAT's path to product-market fit began not with a better sneaker marketplace but with an insight revealed by fraud: buyers couldn't trust that what they purchased was real. By inserting authentication as a core product feature, GOAT converted a market failure (counterfeits) into a durable moat. This pattern — finding PMF by solving a trust deficit the existing market had normalized — recurs across successful marketplace businesses. The strategic implication is to look not just at what users want to buy, but at what they're afraid of when they try to buy it. Removing that fear often unlocks a larger market than the original product pitch.
