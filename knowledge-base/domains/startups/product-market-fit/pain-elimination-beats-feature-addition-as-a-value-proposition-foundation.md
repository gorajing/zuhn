The Value Proposition Canvas distinguishes between customer jobs (what people are trying to do), pains (what makes those jobs hard), and gains (desired outcomes). Apple Pay's clarity came from anchoring entirely on pains: the payment process was slow, awkward, and left users financially exposed through card skimming and data breaches.

Rather than pitching Apple Pay as a new capability, Apple framed it as a relief from existing suffering. This is a fundamentally different positioning — it doesn't require convincing customers they need something new, only that the old thing was bad. The bar for adoption is lower because the problem is already felt.

This insight has immediate application in product strategy: before listing features or benefits, identify the specific named pain your product eliminates. If you can't articulate that pain sharply, your value proposition will feel vague no matter how good the product is.
