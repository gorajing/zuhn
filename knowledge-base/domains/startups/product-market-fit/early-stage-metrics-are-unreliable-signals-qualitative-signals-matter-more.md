Butler's discussion of signals vs. hard metrics captures a common founder trap: over-indexing on quantitative dashboards when sample sizes are too small for the numbers to mean anything. A 10% retention rate looks terrible in absolute terms but might represent the 5 most passionate early adopters in a new category — the exact cohort you need to understand deeply before scaling.

The practical alternative is to treat early metrics as conversation starters, not conclusions. When a metric looks bad, the question is whether the underlying users are disappointed or simply not the right users yet. High-intensity qualitative signals — users who would be devastated to lose the product, users who evangelize without being asked — are more predictive of PMF than aggregate retention curves. This is why Figma invested in designer advocates rather than optimizing vanity metrics.
