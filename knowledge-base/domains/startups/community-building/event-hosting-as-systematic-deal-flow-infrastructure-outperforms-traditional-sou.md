Venture capital firms are discovering that community and event hosting isn't a marketing expense — it's core infrastructure for deal sourcing and conviction building. By hosting events, firms interact with founders months or years before they formally raise, building relationships and pattern-matching intuition that cold inbound can never provide. This creates a compounding moat: the best founders preferentially share ideas with people they already know and trust, so firms embedded in communities see deals earlier and with more context. The implication extends beyond VC — any business that depends on sourcing high-quality opportunities (recruiting, partnerships, M&A) can build similar systematic community infrastructure rather than relying on transactional outreach.
