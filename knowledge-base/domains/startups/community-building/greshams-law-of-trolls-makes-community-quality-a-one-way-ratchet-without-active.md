Graham identifies a Gresham's Law dynamic in online communities: trolls are willing to participate in high-quality forums, but thoughtful contributors flee troll-infested ones. This asymmetry means community quality degrades in only one direction unless actively defended. Reddit, Slashdot, and Digg all followed this trajectory.

This has direct implications for anyone building community products. Quality is not self-sustaining — it requires active moderation, cultural norm enforcement, and technical mechanisms that raise the cost of low-effort contributions. The implication is that community investment should be front-loaded: establishing norms early with a small committed group is far easier than rehabilitating a degraded culture.
