Scott notes that most ACX book review entries trend toward nonfiction on technical topics, and he wants to counteract this without abandoning meritocracy. His solution: reserve no more than 25% of finalist slots for nontraditional categories (fiction, poetry, pre-1900 books). The explicit cap is key — it makes the intervention legible and bounded rather than open-ended.

This is a transferable design principle for any community curation system. Pure meritocracy in creative contests often produces genre homogeneity because the loudest or most technically confident voices dominate. But overcorrecting with quota-style mandates destroys participant trust. A small, explicit, capped affirmative action mechanism threads this needle: it signals that the curator values diversity, quantifies exactly how much weight that value carries, and leaves most slots to open competition. The 25% threshold seems roughly calibrated to 'noticeable but not dominant.'
