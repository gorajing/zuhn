The Freeman, Staudenmaier, Zisser, and Andresen study on psychiatric conditions among entrepreneurs and their families provides clinical grounding for what many in the startup world experience anecdotally. Entrepreneurs show elevated rates of depression, ADHD, bipolar disorder, anxiety, and substance use — often co-occurring. This has important implications: (1) the traits that make founders effective (high energy, risk tolerance, pattern recognition, obsession) often co-occur with clinical conditions; (2) investor and board pressure that exacerbates these conditions may be destroying founder performance rather than improving it; (3) venture-backed companies should treat founder mental health as an operational risk. For founders themselves, recognizing that their psychological challenges are both common and partially structural — not personal failures — is actionable for self-management and help-seeking.
