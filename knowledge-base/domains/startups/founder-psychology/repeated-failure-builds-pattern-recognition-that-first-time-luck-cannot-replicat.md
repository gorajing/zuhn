Eman Venture Labs explicitly prefers founders who have failed three or four times because each failure compresses the learning curve toward success. The 95% startup failure rate means most founders will fail — the question is whether they extract signal from that failure.

This directly counters the Silicon Valley narrative of the visionary genius who succeeds on attempt one. Failure is not just acceptable — it is the primary mechanism through which founders develop the judgment to recognize timing, market fit, and execution traps. The emotional cost is real (Hagger describes depression after Scatter's failure), but that pain becomes 'armor' and 'weaponry' for future ventures.
