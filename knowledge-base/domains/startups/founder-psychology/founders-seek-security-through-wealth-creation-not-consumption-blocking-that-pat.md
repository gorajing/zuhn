Graham reveals that his own motivation for founding a startup was not consumption but security — making enough money to stop worrying about money. This reframes founder motivation from greed to risk management. The startup path is actually a high-variance strategy for achieving the same goal (financial security) that a tenured professorship achieves through low-variance means.

This has a structural consequence: in societies where outsized wealth is impossible, seniority systems become proportionally more important. People who would have been founders instead optimize for institutional tenure. The variation in wealth and the prevalence of seniority systems may be inversely proportional — suppress one and the other expands to fill the gap. This suggests that egalitarian societies don't eliminate status competition; they redirect it from productive risk-taking to organizational politics.
