Since failure is inherent in any new experiment -- new job, product, hobby, or relationship -- pre-commitment to failure scenarios is a practical skill. This means giving thought before starting to what you will do if it does not work out and how to minimize the downside while maximizing the upside.

Combined with building feedback mechanisms that provide early honest signals about whether things are going well, this creates a framework where failure becomes informative rather than catastrophic. The key operational elements are: define success markers upfront, secure trusted sources of honest feedback (remarkably hard to find), and have a pre-planned exit or pivot strategy. Edison's framing -- 'I've succeeded in showing what doesn't work' -- is only possible when failure was anticipated and instrumented from the start.
