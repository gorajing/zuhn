Tobi Lütke's framing: 'I'm an eight, which I almost already said by talking about my motivational source being dissatisfaction or the shittification of everything which I want to fix at least in my little nook of the world. Eights are basically conspired against in companies because they are pain in their asses. They just say a thing — they say like this is bullshit, if our shit does not smell better than other people's shit, therefore this is just like exactly what it looks like and no amount of fancy dressing changes what it is. They are dangerous to everyone else's careers around them. They usually don't get the promotions, they usually leave — what can happen is they start companies because they often see things.' The Apple-Steve-bringing-back-experiment: 'It created the boundary conditions for financiers, capital, boards to actually give founders a longer run rate. And the results were pretty good because now you have diversity with executives — you have someone who no one can get rid of who just calls bullshit bullshit. That turns out to be really really good for companies.' Tobi's hiring filter: 'Shopify is remarkably high on eights because I really seek them. I fundamentally like this kind of discussion.' The corollary for hiring: if you're building a company that needs to break conventions or face uncomfortable truths, deliberately recruit eights even though they're politically inconvenient. Standard professional-management hiring processes filter eights out because they're high-friction in interviews. You need an explicit override on standard hiring criteria — specifically optimize for 'will call bullshit even when costly to themselves.' The corollary for self-knowledge: if you're an eight (most founders are), understand that you'll always be socially expensive in conventional company contexts and that founding/starting is often the path that lets your traits compound rather than waste. The corollary for VCs: the founders most likely to build great companies are the ones whose previous employers found them frustrating to work with — that's a feature, not a bug. Standard 'team chemistry' assessments penalize the trait that produces outsized outcomes.
