Kim's growth framework is concrete: when your company is growing quickly, you don't have time to organically learn the skills you need. The solution is Bezos-style working backwards — define what your company needs to look like in 12-24 months, then find 3-5-10 people who have already navigated that transition and interview them to find the general pattern of greatness.

This is not about finding one mentor — it's about pattern matching across multiple experienced operators to discover what capabilities, team structures, and leadership upgrades are required at your next stage. The CEO's job reduces to three things: get the strategy right, get the right teams onboarded, and allocate resources. Everything else is noise that distracts from the stage-appropriate version of these three responsibilities.
