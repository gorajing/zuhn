Dalton and Michael identify a pervasive founder failure mode: spiraling after reading a fundraising announcement on Twitter. The spiral takes multiple forms -- sadness ('why not us?'), rage ('how dare this terrible product raise money?'), magical thinking ('if they can raise, we can too'), or pivot impulse ('we should do what they're doing'). All of these are self-defeating.

The emotional reaction consumes energy, distorts strategy, and changes nothing about the founder's actual probability of success. People on Hacker News and Twitter spend more time worrying about what YC funded than YC itself does. Learning to quiet this emotional response is a critical founder skill -- not because the feelings aren't valid, but because acting on them leads to worse outcomes.
