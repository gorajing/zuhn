When asked whether AI is in a bubble, Dalton says no, because current AI tools' actual impact on business is barely being felt. Adoption is so early in its cycle that even if all AI development stopped today, the existing technology would drive enormous improvements for years as businesses actually implement and integrate what already exists.

This reframes the bubble question: the risk isn't that AI doesn't work (it clearly does), but rather that the gap between capability and adoption is so vast that it creates both opportunity and mispricing. For founders, this means the near-term opportunity is less about pushing the frontier of AI capabilities and more about deploying existing AI capabilities into the vast majority of businesses and workflows that haven't adopted them yet.
