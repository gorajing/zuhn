Fireship frames the passion test explicitly: if Bukowski's poem about going all the way doesn't describe how you feel about your project, it might not be worth your time. Then he immediately confesses: 'I'd much rather spend my time making content on fireship than I would trying to build up this half-assed voice cloning tool.'

This is a counterweight to the 'persist through everything' startup narrative. The honest answer to 'would I rather be doing something else?' is the most important diagnostic a founder can run. Fireship's side hustle was technically profitable and running on autopilot, but the lack of passion meant he wouldn't do the grinding work (organic marketing, feature development, user research) needed to grow it. A zombie project that runs on autopilot is the best-case outcome for a founder without passion — the worst case is years of half-hearted effort.
