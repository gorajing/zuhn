The article surfaces a critical distinction in founder motivation that VCs use as a primary filter: the difference between founders chasing a market opportunity and founders who are genuinely obsessed with the problem. The litmus test is simple—would you still pursue this idea if it took 10 years to work? Founders driven by obsession iterate faster, see insights others miss, and push through friction that would stop opportunity-driven founders.

This has practical implications for both self-selection and storytelling. If you find yourself primarily motivated by market size or financial upside rather than the problem itself, that misalignment will eventually surface—either in your pitch (where VCs detect it quickly) or in year three when things get genuinely hard. The obsession signal is difficult to fake because it manifests in how founders talk, hire, and make product decisions, not just in what they claim during a pitch.
