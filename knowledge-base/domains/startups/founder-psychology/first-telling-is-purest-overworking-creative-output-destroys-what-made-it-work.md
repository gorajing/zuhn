Kreischer describes a recurring pattern in his comedy craft: a joke told spontaneously for the first time lands perfectly, then he tries to 'razzle dazzle' it into a 15-minute bit, and the joke dies. His deaf child joke was simple and perfect — Louis CK loved it — but Kreischer's attempts to add gun control material and other layers destroyed it.

This maps to Hemingway's 'write drunk, edit sober' and the broader creative principle that the first unfiltered expression captures something authentic that subsequent editing can remove rather than improve. The practical lesson for any creative endeavor — product launches, pitch decks, writing, design: capture the first instinct, then be extremely careful about what you add. The default should be subtraction, not addition. As Kreischer learned: 'that first time — that's when the joke comes out purest.'
