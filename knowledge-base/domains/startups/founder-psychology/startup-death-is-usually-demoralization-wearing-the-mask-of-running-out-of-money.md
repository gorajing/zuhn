Graham makes a subtle diagnostic distinction: while startups technically die by running out of money, the real pathology is demoralization causing loss of focus. Smart founders don't fail from stupidity — they fail because the psychological weight of a startup grinds them down, causing them to lose the relentless focus that survival requires.

His prescription is prophylactic awareness: understand that starting a startup is a 'huge moral weight' and consciously guard against being ground down, the way you'd consciously bend your knees when lifting a heavy box. This frames founder resilience not as heroic grit but as ergonomic self-management — a practical, repeatable discipline rather than an innate trait.
