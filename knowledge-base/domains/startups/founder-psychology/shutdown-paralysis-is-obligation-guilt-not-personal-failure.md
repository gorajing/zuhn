Dalton observes that by the time founders are considering shutdown, most have processed the personal-failure emotion but remain stuck on obligation guilt toward employees and investors. His reframe: the obligation was never to win — it was to work hard, try smart, act ethically, and run the experiment you said you'd run. Investors hedge across portfolios and don't stake their careers on any single bet. Employees are better served by being freed to pursue the next thing in their career than by being strapped into a zombie. Recognizing this produces visible relief in founders, which is diagnostic — the guilt was the trap, not the mechanics.
