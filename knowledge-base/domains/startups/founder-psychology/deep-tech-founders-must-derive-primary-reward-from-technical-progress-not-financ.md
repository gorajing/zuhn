The multi-year valleys in infrastructure startups eliminate the standard reward systems of consumer or SaaS startups (growing user counts, revenue milestones, valuation step-ups). Founders who have only these external scaffolds collapse emotionally during the dead years. Founders who have internalized reward systems tied to technical validation (does the chip tape out correctly? does the algorithm produce a novel result? did we solve a problem nobody had solved?) sustain through the same period. This is not a platitude — it is a selection criterion that predicts who survives the category. Chamath explicitly names this as a self-knowledge requirement: know where you get energy, and if you get it from difficult non-obvious accomplishments, deep-tech is viable; if you need market validation on shorter cycles, the category will break you.
