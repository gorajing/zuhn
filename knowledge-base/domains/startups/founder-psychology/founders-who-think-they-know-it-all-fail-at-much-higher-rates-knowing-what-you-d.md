VanDusen identifies founder hubris as a top-10 failure pattern: the confidence required to start a business becomes fatal when it prevents hiring expertise in marketing, consumer research, finance, sales, design, and customer service. 'Those who know what they don't know and hire or retain experts are much more likely to succeed.'

The practical escape hatch: even if you cannot afford full-time hires, retaining consultants gets you expert knowledge at a fraction of the cost without ongoing salary overhead. The counterweight here is about intellectual humility as a survival skill — the founders most likely to succeed are those who accurately map their own incompetence zones and fill them with people who know better.
