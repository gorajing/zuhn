The founder's actual job is to point the way forward and believe harder than anyone else. A founder who hedges across five pivots signals to employees and investors that they have no clue, which destroys the trust needed to execute anything. Michael and Dalton note counterintuitively that founders with strong conviction in a wrong direction often succeed — they mobilize people, generate real feedback, and recalibrate along the way. The failure mode isn't wrong direction; it's no direction. If you truly have no idea what to do, the problem isn't picking — it's that you've lost whatever edge you had.
