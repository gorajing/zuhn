Dalton reframes the zombie-continuation decision as an opportunity-cost calculation, not a financial one. Continuing to run the zombie is not free just because payroll is covered — the founder is spending their most valuable years on something with bounded upside. The real return from shutting down is the optionality to pursue the next thing, which for many founders leads to a materially better outcome (he cites an example of someone who shut down and landed at a frontier AI lab). This is the frame that most effectively breaks the obligation loop because it makes continuation itself an active decision rather than a default.
