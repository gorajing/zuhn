Urban specifically calls out self-starter careers — arts, entrepreneurship — as the domains most vulnerable to non-deadline procrastination. Nothing happens until you've gone out and done the hard work to get momentum going. There are no deadlines at first because nothing's happening.

This creates a cruel catch-22 for founders: the phase that most needs the Panic Monster (zero-to-one, before customers or investors create external pressure) is precisely when it's absent. Smart founders solve this by manufacturing deadlines — demo days, launch dates, public commitments, co-founder accountability — creating artificial Panic Monsters before organic ones exist.
