DigiCash's eCash was described by insiders as a 'technically perfect product' that solved credit card security and fee problems. Bill Gates personally offered $100M to integrate it into every copy of Windows 95. Chaum declined — and declined every offer that followed — driven by perfectionism, paranoia about partners' intentions, and the assumption that better offers would come.

By 1998, DigiCash was bankrupt. The pattern is instructive: Chaum's failure wasn't technical or market-based — there was genuine demand from the world's most powerful tech company. It was a founder psychology failure. His inability to trust partners, close deals, or accept 'good enough' terms destroyed a product that could have become the global digital payment standard a decade before Bitcoin. For founders, this is a cautionary tale about how perfectionism and paranoia can be more lethal than competition or bad timing.
