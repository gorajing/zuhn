Horowitz says the problem with story-writing is 'it doesn't feel like work — like what are you doing today? I'm working on my story. That doesn't sound like work.' Because it lacks the legible markers of productivity (meetings attended, code shipped, customers visited), founders and CEOs systematically under-invest in it. But it's one of the most important parts of the job. This is a specific case of the general pattern where high-leverage work looks unlike work. The fix: force yourself to do it in long-form written format because writing imposes the discipline you can't fake in a slide or verbal pitch. Horowitz notes he still writes the a16z story 'every once in a while' decades in.
