John Kim identifies the most common first-time founder mistake: choosing ideas based on market trends or opportunity rather than personal alignment with the problem. The result is predictable — after 2-3 years, founders realize 'whatever you do is harder than you think, it will take longer, it will be harder' and they quit because the motivation was external.

The ten-year test is Kim's filter: can you imagine yourself doing this at greater scale for a decade? This is not about passion — it's about durability of commitment when everything goes wrong. Smart people who pick trendy ideas fail at higher rates than average people deeply aligned with boring problems, because intelligence doesn't compensate for motivational collapse.
