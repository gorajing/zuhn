Ek pushes back on the media narrative of founders as preternaturally confident operators who understood their path from day one. His view: every really successful entrepreneur has had at least three near-death experiences where they didn't know if the company would exist the next week. He explicitly says Spotify's journey was an emotional roller coaster, drove enormous weight gains and hair loss, and that had someone told him in advance how hard it would be, he would never have started. The practical implication is twofold: first, recalibrate the baseline — if you haven't had near-death moments, you may not be swinging hard enough; if you have, you're not broken, you're at par. Second, be suspicious of founder narratives that read as linear and confident, because the real path almost always includes periods where the founder was running on fumes and luck. Use this to set expectations for yourself and to read between the lines when evaluating other founders.
