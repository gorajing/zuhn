Dalton identifies a cultural shift he calls the 'casinoification of modern capitalism.' Young people today are implicitly trained to believe wealth comes from sports gambling, crypto speculation, and zero-dated options. Many know more people who made real money from short-term bets than from long-term building.

This creates a fundamental mismatch with startup reality. When all your reference points for wealth creation are casino-like — fast, binary, luck-driven — advice about sustained multi-year effort feels wrong. The long-term startup advice literally doesn't land because it contradicts lived experience. This is particularly dangerous because it makes founders pivot too quickly, abandon hard problems too early, and treat Series A raises as finish lines rather than starting points.
