Dalton's closing advice ties the patience-urgency paradox together: you need to have fun. He hasn't seen people sustain effort on hard challenges over many years when they're not enjoying the work. Conversely, founders having fun do literally crazy things — sustained intensity that looks unsustainable from the outside but works because intrinsic motivation carries it.

This isn't soft advice — it's a structural requirement. If building a great company takes 5-15 years of sustained hard work (and the evidence says it does), then enjoyment isn't a nice-to-have. It's load-bearing infrastructure. Taking pride in your work, being excited about the problems, finding the day-to-day stimulating — these aren't luxuries. They're the fuel source that makes multi-year commitment possible.
