stance: Musk-type founders are literally addicted to intensity

Isaacson observed that Musk doesn't just tolerate high-stakes intensity -- he needs it. When Tesla is finally producing enough cars and rockets are landing successfully, Musk doesn't relax; he surges into a new crisis (buying Twitter, pushing Starship timelines). This isn't self-sabotage -- it's the operational mode that produces the most output from this type of founder.

The nuance is that this pattern emerges from childhood trauma (Musk's 'demon mode' traces directly to his father's psychological abuse) but becomes a productive engine when channeled toward cosmic-scale missions. The warning for founders is that this archetype is both the most productive and the most destructive -- Musk vomits from stress, channels his father's phrases during breakdowns, but also moves entire industries. Understanding whether you're this archetype (and whether you have the mission to justify the destruction) is essential self-knowledge.
