For chronic conditions, real-world outcomes are dominated by adherence — patients who miss doses or drop therapy entirely. A biohybrid implant that produces the drug continuously and is titrated by onboard electronics makes non-adherence structurally impossible once the device is implanted. Commercially this reshapes the value capture: payers can price against real-world effectiveness rather than ideal-use efficacy, and the product moat shifts from formulation/patent to device implantation and servicing. The tradeoff is that device failure modes (extrusion, biofouling, battery/lead integrity) now directly substitute for missed-dose failure modes.
