The prototype hardware cost is reported as under $980 for low-volume acquisition excluding the tunable laser and laptop, with practical implementations expected to replace the tunable laser with cheaper laser diodes or LEDs. It scans about 12.5 cm2 in roughly 15 minutes and targets settings where conventional pathology infrastructure is unavailable. This points to a startup pattern: when model capability is temporary, durable advantage may come from controlling the measurement surface, generating task-specific data, validating the escalation workflow, and making the deployment economics work in places incumbents underserve. The caveat is that this is proof-of-concept hardware plus retrospective tissue arrays, not a clinical deployment.
