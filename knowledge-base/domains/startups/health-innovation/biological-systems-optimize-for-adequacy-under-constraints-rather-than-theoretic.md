The video opens by calling photosynthesis 'complicated, inefficient and confusing' — yet it's the foundation of virtually all life on Earth. The Calvin Cycle requires 9 ATP and 6 NADPH to process just 3 CO2 molecules, yielding only 1 net G3P. RuBisCo wastes roughly half its reactions on the wrong substrate. By any efficiency metric, photosynthesis is mediocre.

Yet it works reliably across wildly diverse conditions — deserts, oceans, arctic tundra. This is a lesson for product builders: systems that are reliably adequate under messy real-world constraints consistently outcompete systems optimized for peak theoretical performance. In healthcare startups particularly, the EHR that works 'well enough' across 10,000 edge cases beats the elegant system that fails on case 47. Design for robustness and constraint-tolerance, not benchmark performance.
