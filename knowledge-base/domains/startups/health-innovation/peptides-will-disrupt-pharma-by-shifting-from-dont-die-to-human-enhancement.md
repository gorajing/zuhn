Current pharmaceuticals are largely 'don't die' interventions — take a statin to avoid a heart attack. Peptides represent a different category entirely: human enhancement compounds that increase energy, improve sex drive, reduce inflammation, and improve gut health. Early results from users are promising, though rigorous research is still needed.

The structural disruption comes from the fact that peptides are not patentable. They can be produced cheaply, which means the entire pharma business model of patent-protected monopoly pricing does not apply. If peptides prove even modestly effective at the population level, they could redirect massive healthcare spending away from expensive patented drugs toward cheap, unpatentable compounds — a threat the pharmaceutical industry has strong incentives to suppress.
