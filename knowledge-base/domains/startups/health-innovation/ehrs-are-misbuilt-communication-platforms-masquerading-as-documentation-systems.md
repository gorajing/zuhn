During ICU rounds, a neurologist co-founder scrolling through a major EHR system to find a medication change said 'we communicate through this thing' — revealing that the EHR has become healthcare's de facto communication platform despite never being designed for that purpose. It was built for documentation and insurance billing, which explains why providers spend hours after 12-16 hour shifts doing charting at home. This architectural mismatch creates the burnout crisis and the care gaps the speaker's company addresses. The insight reframes the healthcare AI opportunity: rather than building better EHR features, the winning approach is building a communication-first layer that sits on top of the EHR, surfacing real-time data and social context that the flat EHR structure fundamentally cannot represent.
