Doudna uses a powerful analogy: CRISPR gives us the ability to cut and paste DNA, but we are still inventing the postal system to deliver those edits. Different organs contain many cell types, and diseases like Alzheimer's affect only specific cells within the brain. Getting CRISPR molecules to exactly those cells — and not bothering others — requires understanding each cell type's surface 'zip code' molecules.

This is the rate-limiting step for expanding gene therapy beyond blood diseases. Scientists are studying how viruses naturally target specific cells and trying to repurpose that mechanism for CRISPR delivery. This delivery problem represents a massive opportunity space for biotech startups — whoever solves targeted in-body delivery unlocks gene therapies for neurological diseases, organ-specific conditions, and potentially preventive genomic medicine.
