Wales reveals that the hardest question Wikipedia faces is not accuracy but scope: what deserves an article? The classic Bic pen example illustrates the spectrum — the Bic pen product line (yes, it has history and significance) versus Lex Fridman's specific Bic pen (no, it's one instance with no known history). The German Wikipedia metaphor of 'the rear nut of Ulifook's bicycle wheel' shows where the limit lies.

Biographies of living persons are the highest-risk area. Wikipedia must balance completeness against potential harm — a private person who is a crime victim probably shouldn't have a standalone article. The term 'notability' itself is problematic because it implies judgment of worth when the real question is verifiability: are there enough reliable sources to write an informative entry? For anyone building knowledge platforms: the inclusion/exclusion decision is the most consequential design choice, and it must vary by content type.
