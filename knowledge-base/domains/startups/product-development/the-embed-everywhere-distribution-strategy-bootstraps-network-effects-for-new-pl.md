YouTube's most consequential product decision was building embeddable video from day one. The logic was simple: a new brand with a new domain needs existing platforms and foundations that people already know to drive adoption. You could embed a YouTube video on Craigslist, eBay, MySpace, or any platform that allowed HTML insertion. The breakthrough came via MySpace: users started putting YouTube videos in their profiles. Visitors would see one friend with a YouTube-logoed video, then another, then another over several days. Eventually curiosity drove click-throughs to YouTube itself, where they discovered more videos and started uploading their own. This viral loop cost exactly zero dollars in marketing. The critical design choice: the embedded player prominently displayed the YouTube logo, making every embed a brand impression. This is a powerful pattern for any content platform: make your content embeddable everywhere with your brand attached, and other platforms' distribution becomes yours.
