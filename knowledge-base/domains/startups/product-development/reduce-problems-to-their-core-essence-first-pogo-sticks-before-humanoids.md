Marc Raibert's foundational insight was that the fundamental problems of legged locomotion — balance, dynamic stability, energy efficiency — could be studied with a single-legged pogo stick. If you solve balance for a spring-mass system, you can extrapolate to machines that gallop like horses.

This principle applies broadly to product development and engineering: resist the temptation to tackle the full complexity of your problem immediately. Find the simplest embodiment that preserves the essential physics/dynamics of your challenge. Solve that first. The path from one-legged bouncing to humanoid backflips took decades but was built on that simplifying foundation.
