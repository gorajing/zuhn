Adora describes an 'honesty curve' where your mom will always praise your product, friends will be somewhat honest, and random free users won't bother giving real feedback. But when money is involved, the curve inverts: your mom feels guilty criticizing something she paid for, but random paying customers will absolutely tell you if they didn't get their money's worth.

This has a critical implication for early-stage product development: the common advice to 'get users first, monetize later' actually handicaps your feedback loop. The fastest path to product-market fit isn't maximizing free user count — it's finding even a handful of people willing to pay, because their feedback will be the most honest and actionable signal you can get.
