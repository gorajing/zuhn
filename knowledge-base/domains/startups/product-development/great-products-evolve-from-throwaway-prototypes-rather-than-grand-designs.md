Paul Graham observes that most dominant technologies grew organically from small throwaway programs rather than being architected as grand systems from the start. Perl began as report-generation utilities before becoming a full language. Unix grew from a small project while the big-budget Multics stagnated.

This maps directly to product development: trying to build something comprehensive from day one leads to overwhelm, sterile designs, and 'shopping mall rather than real downtown' outcomes. The better path is to solve one small problem well, attract early users, and let their feedback drive evolution. The throwaway origin also ensures the product solves a real problem — if it wasn't useful as a quick hack, it won't be useful as a big system either.
