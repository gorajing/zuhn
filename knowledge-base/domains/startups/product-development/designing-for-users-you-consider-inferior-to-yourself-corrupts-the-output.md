Graham notes that C, Lisp, and Smalltalk were created by designers who used them, while Cobol, Ada, and Java were created for others. The pattern extends to architecture: few US housing project architects expected to live in their designs. When you design for people you consider less sophisticated, condescension leaks into every decision.

This isn't just about empathy — it's about information quality. Designers who use their own products get continuous, high-fidelity feedback. Designers who don't must rely on abstracted user research, which filters out the subtle irritations and delights that separate good from great. The strongest version of user-centered design is making yourself the user.
