Jon Noronha tested GPT-3 in 2020 for summarization and slide generation, found it inadequate, and dismissed language AI. Two years later, seeing Stable Diffusion and DALL-E outputs on Twitter provided the aha moment. The visual output was inherently viral and magical in a way that text improvements were not. Image generation was 'the next clip art,' a 30-year gap in presentation innovation.

Ironically, image model excitement led them to revisit language models, which had dramatically improved through instruction tuning. The combination of visual generation (making output look professional and appealing) and language generation (structuring content and text) together produced the product breakthrough. The lesson for AI product builders in visual domains: start with the visual because it provides the emotional hook and virality, then layer in the language capabilities.
