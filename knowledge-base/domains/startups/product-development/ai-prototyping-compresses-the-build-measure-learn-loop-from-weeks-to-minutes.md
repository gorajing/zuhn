Historically, building even a basic interactive prototype required significant engineering time, which rationed how many ideas a product team could explore. AI prototyping tools like Bolt and v0 can produce functional multi-page prototypes in under 10 minutes from a text prompt or Figma screenshot. This collapses the cost of the 'build' phase in build-measure-learn nearly to zero.

The strategic implication is that engineering capacity is no longer the bottleneck for early-stage validation. Instead, the constraint moves upstream to hypothesis generation and customer access. Teams that still ration prototyping as if it's expensive will be outpaced by teams that treat prototypes as disposable and iterate on customer feedback rapidly. This also means PMs need stronger discovery skills (customer interviews, experiment design) since the excuse of 'we couldn't build it fast enough to test' evaporates.
