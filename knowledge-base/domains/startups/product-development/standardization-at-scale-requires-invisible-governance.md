Mohenjo Daro and Harappa — cities hundreds of miles apart — used identically sized bricks, perpendicular street layouts, and centralized drainage systems. This level of standardization across a geographically dispersed civilization implies powerful coordination mechanisms, yet archaeologists have found no palaces, no monumental royal architecture, and no clear evidence of kings or authoritarian rule.

This is a powerful model for modern product organizations: the strongest governance is often invisible. APIs, design systems, coding standards, and infrastructure conventions that 'just work' across teams are far more indicative of organizational health than top-down mandates. The Indus Valley suggests that standardization can emerge from shared protocol rather than centralized command — a pattern seen in successful open-source ecosystems and platform companies.
