When Sweeney released ZZT, he included the same level editor he used to build the game. This created an ecosystem where players could create and share their own content. 30 years later, people still approach him at industry events saying they grew up making ZZT levels. This pattern -- shipping tools alongside the product -- became foundational to Epic's entire business model through Unreal Engine.

The strategic insight is that by serving both creators and consumers, Epic weathered every financial downturn: when games weren't funding the business, the engine was, and vice versa. For any product company, the question isn't just 'what product do we sell' but 'can we also ship the tools that let others create on our platform?' This transforms a one-time product sale into a persistent ecosystem. The creator tools become both a moat and a stabilizer against market volatility.
