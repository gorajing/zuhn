When asked about expanding into project management territory (like Linear or Confluence), Heo firmly rejects horizontal expansion. His argument: as a PM's job title suggests project management, but their practical work involves front-end planning. Manifast must keep this vertical narrow for long-term success. A horizontal business covering many areas may generate short-term sales but is not sustainable.

Instead of building competing features, Heo's strategy is to align protocols with existing PM tools and improve compatibility — letting the product become a collaborative agent that integrates with workflows rather than replacing them. This is a disciplined approach to SaaS product strategy that prioritizes depth over breadth, especially relevant in AI-era markets where integration via MCP/APIs makes collaboration between specialized tools more viable than monolithic platforms.
