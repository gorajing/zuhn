Paul Graham draws an analogy between designers and doctors: users report symptoms (feature requests, complaints) but cannot diagnose their underlying needs. A doctor who only treats symptoms is negligent; a designer who only builds what users request is equally misguided.

This doesn't mean ignoring users — the measure of good design is still how well it works for them. The paradox resolves by staying close to users (constant calibration, like Jane Austen reading drafts aloud) while maintaining the judgment to interpret their needs rather than transcribe their wants. The customer is always right about whether the result works, but rarely right about what the result should be.
