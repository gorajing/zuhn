The article argues that the original MVP compact — users tolerate rough edges in exchange for early access — has broken down. Today's users are conditioned by polished products and don't distinguish between 'this is a test' and 'this company doesn't get it.' A clunky MVP damages brand before it generates learning.

This creates a paradox: the faster you ship something ugly, the faster you burn your first-impression opportunity. The practical implication is that even lean launches need investment in clean UX, intuitive flows, and trust-building design. The bar for 'minimum' has simply risen, and teams that ignore this collect churn data instead of insight data.
