At Homejoy, Cheung's team initially interviewed every cleaning professional candidate by phone and in person, with a 3-5% acceptance rate. This was extremely labor-intensive, but over time they discovered which specific questions were reliable predictors of on-platform performance. Only then did they build an online application form with those validated questions.

The principle is that automation encodes assumptions, and premature automation encodes wrong assumptions. By doing the work manually first, you build an empirical understanding of which variables matter. This applies beyond hiring — to customer onboarding flows, quality scoring, matching algorithms, and any process where you don't yet know the decision criteria. The manual phase is not inefficiency; it's a learning investment that makes the eventual automation dramatically more effective.
