Michelin's evolution from tire manufacturer to tire-service provider illustrates servitization: instead of just selling rubber, they offered tire maintenance, performance monitoring, and support services. This shifts the customer relationship from a one-time purchase to an ongoing partnership, making price comparison with competitors much harder.

The principle applies broadly to any company selling a product that risks commoditization. The strategic move is to identify the customer's higher-order need — Michelin customers don't want tires, they want safe, efficient mobility — and then build services around that need. This creates switching costs, recurring revenue, and a deeper understanding of customer behavior that feeds back into product innovation. For startups, the lesson is to think past the product to the ongoing relationship: what service wrapper would make your product indispensable rather than interchangeable?
