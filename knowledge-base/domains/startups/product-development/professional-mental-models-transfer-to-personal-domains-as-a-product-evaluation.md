Throughout the guide, Lenny evaluates baby products through a PM lens — comparing apps on 'UX, functionality, and reliability,' describing baby care advice as 'tactical, there's a system to it, lots of acronyms, and you'll see immediate impact,' and framing a cardboard cutter as transforming a 'package-opening workflow.' This isn't just cute language — it reveals how deeply internalized professional mental models become powerful evaluation tools in unrelated domains.

This has implications for content strategy and trust-building. Reviews from domain experts applying their frameworks to adjacent areas (a PM reviewing baby products, an engineer reviewing kitchen tools, a designer reviewing travel gear) often produce more structurally useful evaluations than generic consumer reviews. The professional lens provides systematic evaluation criteria that most consumers lack, making these cross-domain applications uniquely valuable.
