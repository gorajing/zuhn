The Proven-Better-New framework inverts the typical startup narrative of disruption and innovation. PROVEN: copy what existing successful products do — you do not have the right to change it, you are not good enough to change it. BETTER: only counts if 10 out of 10 current users confirm it's better (not what you think is better — that's just 'new'). NEW: the narrow innovation layer, where you can experiment but must be honest that it's a gamble.

Zynga Poker exemplified this: copied every other poker game pixel-for-pixel (proven), added no-download (better, confirmed by 50% user loss at download step), added real friend photos (new, got lucky it worked). This framework is a counterweight to the 'disrupt everything' mentality. Most innovation is naive, and founders who think their changes are 'better' are usually just being naive. True discipline is copying what works and innovating only where you have true signal.
