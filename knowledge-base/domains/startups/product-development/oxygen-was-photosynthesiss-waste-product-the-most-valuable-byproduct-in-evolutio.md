In photosynthesis, when Photosystem II needs to replace an electron that's been excited away, it splits water (H2O) and steals an electron. The leftover hydrogen ions and oxygen are literally waste products. Yet oxygen became the foundation for all aerobic life on Earth — arguably the most consequential 'byproduct' in history.

This pattern recurs constantly in product development. Slack was a byproduct of a failed game. Flickr emerged from a failed MMO. AWS was internal infrastructure that became Amazon's most profitable business. The insight isn't just 'pay attention to byproducts' — it's that the waste output of solving your core problem may be more valuable than the solution itself. This reframes how founders should think about what their systems produce: every output stream, including the ones you're discarding, deserves evaluation as a potential product.
