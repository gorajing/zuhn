This single 4.5-hour tutorial has accumulated 48 million views over 8 years — a sustained acquisition engine that costs nothing to maintain after creation. freeCodeCamp built its entire brand and community on this model: give away the most comprehensive version of educational content for free, and the resulting audience creates a self-sustaining ecosystem of contributors, donors, and brand recognition.

The key insight for product builders is that comprehensiveness is the moat, not exclusivity. A tutorial that covers everything from installation to OOP in one sitting eliminates the need for learners to piece together fragmented resources. This 'definitive guide' strategy works across domains — the content that aims to be the only resource someone needs on a topic tends to capture disproportionate long-tail traffic and trust.
