Fast food chains had all retreated to nostalgia marketing, celebrity endorsements, and international expansion to cover up domestic decline. Then Popeye's launched a chicken sandwich so good that people waited hours, stores sold out daily, and the product lived up to the viral hype. The sandwich was years in the making under PE ownership (the same RBI that ran Burger King poorly). Popeye's had always been an anomaly: charges for sauces, sketchy locations, poor service, small menu — but food quality so good people tolerate everything else. The insight: even in the most mature, commoditized markets, genuine product innovation resets the competitive landscape.
