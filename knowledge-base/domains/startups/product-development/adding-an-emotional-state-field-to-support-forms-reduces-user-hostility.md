Wufoo added an 'emotional state' dropdown to their support form, expecting no one would use it. Instead, it was filled out 75.8% of the time — nearly matching the browser type field at 78.1%. Users were saying: how I feel about this problem is as important as the technical details.

The surprising byproduct was behavioral change. Text analysis showed measurable decreases in all three written indicators of strong negative emotion: exclamation marks, curse words, and all-caps usage. Simply having a sanctioned outlet for emotions made people more rational in their actual problem descriptions. This is a low-cost, high-impact UX pattern applicable to any support interface.
