Coning argues that startups should stop chasing frontier model access and instead invest in earned insight — deep contextual knowledge of specific workflows, situations, or markets. Even last-generation models work fine when given the right context. The example is Claude Code skills: small snippets of context about how to do a task that dramatically improve model performance.

For founders, this means the strategic investment is in understanding your customer's workflow deeply enough to give AI the right context — not in AI engineering. The CRM for Thai restaurant owners built with Lovable wins not because of model quality but because the builder understands the workflow. This is especially relevant for emerging markets where context is scarce and AI systems lack local knowledge.
