Wales identified the key precondition for Wikipedia's success: 'we all pretty much know what an encyclopedia entry should be like.' An entry on the Eiffel Tower should have pictures, history, location, something about the architect. This shared understanding meant contributors didn't need detailed coordination — they could independently chip in knowing their contribution would fit.

This is a powerful principle for any platform or community: the simpler and more universally understood the output format, the easier it is to enable decentralized contribution. Open source software works similarly — developers share understanding of what good code looks like. For product builders: if you can't describe what a contribution should look like in one sentence that everyone agrees with, your platform will struggle with coordination costs.
