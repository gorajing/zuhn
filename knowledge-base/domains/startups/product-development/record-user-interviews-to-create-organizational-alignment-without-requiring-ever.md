Shear describes recorded interviews as 'magic' for organizational buy-in. When a founder tells their team 'I talked to users and we should build X,' people don't trust the interpretation. But playing back the actual recording where a user describes their frustration or explains their workaround has disproportionate persuasive power. The emotional weight of a real person's voice expressing a real problem bypasses the skepticism that secondhand summaries trigger.

Recording also solves a practical problem: taking notes during an interview is disruptive and makes the interviewer feel disengaged. When you're free from note-taking, you can focus on the conversational flow and pursue unexpected threads with 'tell me more' follow-ups. Shear emphasizes that the most valuable insights come from these detective-mode moments when a user says something unexpected and you dig deeper — moments that email interviews completely miss because they lack the interactive feedback loop.
