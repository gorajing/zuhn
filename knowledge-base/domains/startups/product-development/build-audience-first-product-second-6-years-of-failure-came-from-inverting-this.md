After 20+ failed businesses, the author identified her core mistake: she never had an audience to iterate with. The correct order is: (1) build an audience rallied around a mission, (2) survey them to find their biggest problems, (3) build the cheapest possible MVP (even a Google form), (4) iterate with their feedback until people are begging for a broken product, (5) only then build the real product.

The sign of product-market fit is unmistakable: you can't sleep because you're fulfilling demand, not because you're hustling for customers. If you're asking 'do I have PMF?', you don't. This inverts the typical founder sequence of idea-then-product-then-customers. The audience-first approach eliminates the most common failure mode: building something nobody wants. It also means your first 10 MVPs will fail — but each costs so little time and money that failure is data, not disaster.
