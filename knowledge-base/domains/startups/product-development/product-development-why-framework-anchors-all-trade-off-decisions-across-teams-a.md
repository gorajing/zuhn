Jawbone's product development centers on what they call 'Whys' — explicit articulations of the user problem being solved that, once solved, people can't live without. For UP24, this distilled to five specific statements about real-time data flow, contextual guidance, and ongoing encouragement. These Whys then became the reference point for every trade-off decision: when hardware engineers debated electrode materials affecting reliability, sourcing, and signal performance, the Whys determined which trade-off won.

The framework also serves as a creative constraint that channels innovation rather than limiting it. Rahman describes the concepting phase as identifying 'hero experiences' — the most important problems to solve — and then building cross-functional pods (product experience + hardware engineering + software engineering + data) that own specific themes. The Whys stay posted on walls during 2.5-hour daily cross-functional calls so that every trade-off can be evaluated against the fundamental purpose. This prevents the common failure mode where each discipline optimizes locally at the expense of the whole system.
