When early photosynthetic organisms evolved water-splitting to replenish electrons, oxygen was literally garbage — a 'useless byproduct' as the video frames it. Yet this waste product transformed Earth's atmosphere and enabled the entire animal kingdom. The original 'product' (glucose for the plant) matters far less to the planet than the 'waste.'

This pattern recurs in business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game, Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature, and AWS grew from Amazon's internal infrastructure. When building systems, actively monitor what your process produces as side effects. The throwaway output your users or team finds unexpectedly useful may be the real product waiting to be recognized.
