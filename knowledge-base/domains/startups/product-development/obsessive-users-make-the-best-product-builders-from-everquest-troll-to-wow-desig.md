Jeff Kaplan was known on EverQuest for his passionate, detailed — and extremely profane — feedback rants to the developers. But buried in the profanity was deep product insight: specific, actionable suggestions from someone who had invested 6,000+ hours in the product and understood it better than most designers.

Blizzard recognized this. Several Blizzard employees were secretly playing in Kaplan's guild (including Rob Pardo, lead designer of Warcraft III, and Allen Adham, Blizzard's founder). They spent six months having lunches with Kaplan — what he thought were casual friend meetups were actually extended interviews. When the WoW quest designer position opened requiring a creative writing degree, it was tailor-made for him.

The hiring principle: the most obsessive, passionate users — even the ones flaming you on forums — may be your best potential hires. They care enough to articulate what's wrong, and their deep domain knowledge is impossible to teach.
