Graham argues that 'the most important part of design is redesign' and that users, while valuable for feedback, create powerful inertia against change. Every change risks breaking existing users, so the more users you have, the harder redesign becomes. Committees make this worse — even two people sharing an interface creates enough friction to freeze it.

The counterintuitive prescription is to deliberately limit your user base in the early stages. Choose users carefully, grow their number slowly, and use the window of small user count to iterate aggressively. This mirrors the lean startup insight but goes further: it's not just about learning from users, it's about preserving your freedom to redesign. 'Having users is like optimization: the wise course is to delay it.'
