Gamma spent two years pre-AI with middling traction because 95% of users dropped off at the blank page. They initially saw AI as solving their activation metric problem by filling the blank page. But the deeper insight was that AI didn't just help users get started with Gamma; it solved the universal problem that makes presentations intimidating: the daunting process of outlining, structuring, designing, and formatting from scratch.

When users could go from a vague idea to a fully worked-out rough draft, their job shifted from creating to editing. This isn't just a UX improvement; it's a fundamental change in the user's relationship with the task. The company failed to appreciate this distinction until launch, when exponential growth showed that they had addressed a universal pain point, not just fixed a funnel metric. This is why Gamma reached $50M ARR with just 30 people.
