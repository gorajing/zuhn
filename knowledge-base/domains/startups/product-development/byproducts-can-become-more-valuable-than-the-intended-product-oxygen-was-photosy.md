When Photosystem II splits water to replace lost electrons, it produces hydrogen ions and oxygen as byproducts. The oxygen was, from the plant's perspective, useless waste. Yet this 'waste' product became the foundation for the entire animal kingdom, fundamentally transforming Earth's atmosphere and enabling complex multicellular life. The video calls it 'the most useful useless byproduct in the history of useless byproducts.'

This pattern recurs in business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game company. Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure. The skill isn't just building products — it's recognizing when a side effect of your core process is more valuable than the process itself. Founders should actively audit their byproducts: internal tools, data exhaust, community effects, and workflow artifacts. The thing you're throwing away might be the thing the market actually wants.
