The Biz4Kids process used three distinct feedback layers: first playing the story back to kids for reaction, then having kids interact with actual comic panels to critique both story and visuals, and finally sharing publicly with Kickstarter backers as co-creators. Each stage surfaced different problems.

This maps directly to product development: internal review catches structural issues, prototype testing with target users reveals usability and engagement problems, and public feedback uncovers market-fit questions. The critical insight is that these aren't redundant — skipping any layer means an entire category of failure modes goes undetected. The shift from passive consumption (reading back) to active manipulation (crafting text bubbles) in the second feedback round is particularly powerful for surfacing deeper engagement issues.
