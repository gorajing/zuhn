Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen was explicitly described as 'the most useful useless byproduct in the history of useless byproducts' — a waste gas that plants needed to expel through stomata. Yet this byproduct enabled the entire kingdom of aerobic life, fundamentally reshaping Earth's atmosphere and making complex multicellular organisms possible.

In product development, some of the most transformative pivots come from recognizing that a byproduct has more value than the core product. Slack emerged from a failed game's internal communication tool. Flickr was a byproduct of a game's photo-sharing feature. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure. The pattern suggests that teams should systematically audit their byproducts — the data exhaust, the internal tools, the secondary outputs — because adjacent ecosystems may value these waste products more than the primary customers value the intended product.
