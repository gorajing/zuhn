When photosynthesis first evolved, oxygen was literally waste — a dangerous byproduct that early anaerobic organisms had to tolerate. Over geological time, this 'waste' became the single most important atmospheric gas for complex life, enabling the entire animal kingdom.

This pattern recurs in business: Slack started as an internal tool for a failed game company. AWS began as Amazon's internal infrastructure. YouTube's recommendation algorithm — a 'byproduct' of the video platform — became more valuable than the hosting itself. When building products, pay close attention to what your system produces incidentally. The secondary outputs, data exhaust, community effects, or workflow patterns your users develop may contain more value than the feature you designed.
