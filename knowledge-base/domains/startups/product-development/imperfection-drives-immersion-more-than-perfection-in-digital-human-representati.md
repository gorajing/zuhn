A counterintuitive finding from Meta's codec avatar development: photorealism that feels authentic comes from preserving flaws, not eliminating them. Freckles, skin color variations, wrinkles, and facial asymmetry are what make a digital representation feel like a real person rather than a mannequin.

This principle extends beyond VR to any product attempting to represent humans digitally. The uncanny valley exists precisely because too-perfect representations feel wrong. Most communication is non-verbal — expressed through subtle eye movements, micro-expressions, and the unique physical characteristics of individual faces. Products that try to beautify or standardize human representation sacrifice the very signals that create authentic connection and trust.
