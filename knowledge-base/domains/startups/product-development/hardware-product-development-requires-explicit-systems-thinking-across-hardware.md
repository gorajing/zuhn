Jawbone discovered that building at the intersection of hardware, software, and data creates fundamental organizational friction because each discipline operates with different iteration speeds and quality standards. Hardware teams take 16 weeks for tooling and can't hack solutions; software teams are accustomed to rapid iteration and A/B testing; data science teams need sustained signal quality to generate insights.

The resolution came through forcing these disciplines to learn from each other: hardware teams learned to move faster, software teams learned to resolve experiences before shipping rather than iterating in production, and data science informed both with evidence-based decisions. This cross-pollination is painful but produces products that competitors organized around a single discipline cannot replicate. The 'system' isn't just the product architecture — it's the organizational design that produces it.
