Kevin Hale's core practice at Wufoo was making every team member do customer support shifts. The mechanism is simple: after the second or third time an engineer gets the same support call, they stop what they're doing and fix the underlying bug. This eliminates the broken feedback loop where engineers are divorced from the consequences of their code.

The results were measurable: a 10-person team supported 500,000 users with 7-12 minute response times during business hours. Jared Spool's research backs this up — there's a direct correlation between time spent directly exposed to users and design quality, with a minimum threshold of 2 hours every 6 weeks. Wufoo's team was getting 4-8 hours per week. One documentation redesign driven by support insights reduced customer support volume by 30% overnight.
