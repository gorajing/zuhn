Most product teams operate with an implicit theory of value: 'customers want this feature because it helps them do X.' The Value Proposition Canvas makes that theory explicit and testable by forcing you to name the customer job (what they're trying to accomplish), the pains (frustrations, risks, obstacles), and the gains (desired outcomes, unexpected delights).

The discipline is in the specificity. 'Customers want easier payments' is not a pain — it's a wish. 'Customers fear their card data will be skimmed at a point-of-sale terminal' is a pain. The more specific the pain, the more clearly you can evaluate whether your product actually addresses it.

For early-stage product development, running this exercise before writing any code forces the founding team to be explicit about their theory of value and surface disagreements early. It also produces a vocabulary for communicating value to customers that matches their own language rather than internal engineering terminology.
