Graham observes that side-by-side comparisons of finished programs in different languages are misleading. A language that produces beautiful finished code may require agonizing trial-and-error to get there — like marble, which looks great finished but is hopeless for iterating. The real test is the development experience: can you explore, make mistakes, and refine?

This principle extends beyond programming languages to any tool or platform. Metrics that only measure output quality miss the creative process entirely. A tool that enables rapid prototyping and iterative refinement (like oil paint replacing fresco) may produce better outcomes than one optimized for pristine final results, because it keeps the maker engaged and responsive to discoveries along the way.
