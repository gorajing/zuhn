A common instinct is to map every customer pain to a pain reliever and every gain to a gain creator, attempting comprehensive coverage. This produces unfocused products that address everything superficially and nothing deeply, leaving customers still unsatisfied.

The best value propositions make explicit tradeoffs — identifying the highest-priority jobs, pains, and gains, then performing exceptionally well on just those. This requires the discipline to say no to real customer needs that aren't your highest-priority targets. The strategic value is in the deliberate exclusion: what you choose not to address is as important as what you do address.
