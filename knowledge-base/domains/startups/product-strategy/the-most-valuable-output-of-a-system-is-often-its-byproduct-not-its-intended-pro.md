Photosynthesis evolved to fix carbon into glucose for plant energy and structure. Oxygen is literally the waste dumped out through stomata. Yet this 'useless byproduct' became the foundation of all aerobic life, fundamentally reshaping Earth's atmosphere and enabling the entire animal kingdom. The plant never optimized for oxygen production — it optimized for sugar.

This pattern repeats in technology and business. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure byproduct. Slack emerged from a failed game's internal chat tool. The lesson for builders: systematically audit what your process discards or treats as secondary. The throwaway output of your core loop may be more transformative than the thing you're actually optimizing for. Design systems that make byproducts visible rather than silently discarding them.
