Viaweb deliberately used their own product to build their company website, even though it wasn't an online store, because they wanted to experience what their users did. This dogfooding practice created a tight feedback loop where every internal frustration became a product improvement opportunity.

This pattern extends beyond simple 'eat your own cooking' advice. When your marketing site IS your product demo, you're simultaneously stress-testing the product and showcasing its capabilities. Any limitation visitors notice is one you've already felt internally. Modern SaaS companies that maintain separate marketing sites built on different stacks lose this advantage — they can ship a broken user experience without ever encountering it themselves.
