When building vertical AI products, founders face a critical strategic choice: optimize a single task in a value chain (floorplan analysis, lead scoring) or own the control point that gates the entire workflow. Control points are 'need-to-haves' that facilitate the actual transaction rather than merely accelerating it.

The distinction matters because time-saving tools are easily grafted onto incumbent platforms, while control-point owners become the platform themselves. NFX illustrates this with EvenUp (generates entire demand letters, not just helps write them faster) and TailorBird (generates complete as-built plans, not just analyzes floorplans). The strategic test: are you the bigger idea, or the feature? Start with an incredibly valuable wedge at a control point, then expand — but never stay so specialized that you get bundled away.
