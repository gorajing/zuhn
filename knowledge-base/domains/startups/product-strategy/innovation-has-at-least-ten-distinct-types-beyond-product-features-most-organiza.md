Keeley's Ten Types of Innovation framework reveals that product innovation is just one of ten categories, alongside profit model, network, structure, process, product performance, product system, service, channel, and brand innovations. Most companies default to product-feature competition, which is the most easily copied and lowest-margin form of innovation.

The strategic implication is significant: companies should audit which innovation types they're investing in and deliberately diversify. Some of the most defensible innovations — like business model innovation (Costco's membership model) or network innovation (Apple's App Store ecosystem) — don't involve building a better product at all. This typology is a practical diagnostic tool for innovation portfolio reviews.
