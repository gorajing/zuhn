In mature web2 companies, the PM role was progressively unbundled into user researchers, data scientists, UX designers, product marketing managers, and more. Web3 reverses this trend because the work—on-chain analysis, tokenomics design, community engagement, partnership formation, and launch marketing—is so technically interdependent that splitting it across specialists creates coordination overhead that exceeds the benefit of specialization.

This pattern mirrors what happens in any nascent industry: when the problem space is poorly understood and rapidly changing, generalists outperform specialists because the cost of handoffs exceeds the benefit of depth. The implication for founders is that hiring a versatile generalist PM who can operate across functions will outperform building a traditional product org structure. For PMs considering web3, this means the most valuable skill is rapid context-switching and learning velocity, not deep expertise in any single PM discipline.
