NFX's AI spectrum categorizes companies from Level 1 (AI-Enhanced: using AI for workflow efficiency) through Level 5 (AI-First: impossible without generative AI). Levels 1-3 represent incremental applications—using AI to speed up existing processes, lock in PMF, or add intelligence layers on top of existing products. Levels 4-5 represent transformational applications—where AI is the product itself or where the entire company couldn't exist without AI.

The strategic insight is that most founders gravitate toward Levels 1-3 because they're safer and more legible to existing market logic. But the venture-scale outcomes concentrate at Levels 4-5, particularly in Level 4's 'AI leapfrogging' zone where AI solves problems in industries that cloud/SaaS never reached. The spectrum serves as a self-diagnostic: founders can locate where their product sits and ask whether they're aiming high enough. A company at Level 2 using AI as a 'locking mechanism' for existing traction is defensible but unlikely to be transformative. A company at Level 5 building something literally impossible without AI has the highest ceiling but the most uncertainty.
