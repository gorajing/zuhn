The Jobs to Be Done framework typically gets reduced to functional tasks, but the article emphasizes that the emotional and social layers are where true differentiation lives. The NWSL example is telling — Angel City FC's prom night theme has nothing to do with soccer quality, yet it drives fan loyalty by fulfilling desires for community, identity, and shared experience.

This has direct implications for product development prioritization. Most roadmaps are dominated by functional improvements (faster, cheaper, more features), but the highest-leverage investments may be in how the product makes users feel and how it shapes their social identity. The practical challenge is that emotional and social jobs are harder to discover through standard user research — they require deeper ethnographic understanding of customer context.
