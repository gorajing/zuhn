The video makes the observation that every major aircraft development is driven by fuel efficiency and cost reduction, not passenger experience improvements. Airlines consistently find it easier to sell cheaper tickets than premium comfort. This is why the 787 Dreamliner's impact was enabling long-haul budget airlines and new thin routes, not revolutionizing the passenger experience.

This principle extends to any market where the core product is commoditized — ride-sharing, cloud computing, shipping. Once the baseline product is 'good enough,' the competitive axis collapses to price. Startups in commoditized markets should obsess over cost structure innovation rather than marginal experience improvements. The exception is when you can create an entirely new category of experience (not incremental comfort), but that's a different strategic move than competing within the existing frame.
