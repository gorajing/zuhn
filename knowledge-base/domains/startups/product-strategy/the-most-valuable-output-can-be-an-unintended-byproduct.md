Photosynthesis evolved to capture solar energy and fix carbon for plant growth. Oxygen was literally garbage — 'the most useful useless byproduct in the history of useless byproducts.' Yet this waste product transformed Earth's atmosphere and enabled the entire animal kingdom.

This pattern recurs in business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game studio. Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature. AWS started as internal infrastructure. The mechanism is that solving hard problems for yourself often produces capabilities or outputs that are more valuable to others than the original goal was to you. Founders should pay attention to what their process discards or treats as secondary — it may be their real product.
