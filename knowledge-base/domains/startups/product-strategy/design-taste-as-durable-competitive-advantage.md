Steve Jobs demonstrated that elevating design from frivolous extra to core company principle creates a moat that competitors struggle to copy even when they recognize its importance. Apple's competitors 'now know better' but still can't replicate the advantage because design taste is cultural and deeply embedded—it's not a feature you bolt on.

This insight applies beyond product aesthetics. Design thinking as an organizational value shapes hiring, prioritization, and what 'done' means. Companies that internalize this create compounding returns: each well-designed product raises the bar for the next, attracting talent and customers who expect that standard. The lesson for founders is that design conviction at the CEO level isn't optional decoration—it's a strategic weapon.
