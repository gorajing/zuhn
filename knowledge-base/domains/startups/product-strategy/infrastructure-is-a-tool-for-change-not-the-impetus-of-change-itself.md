St Helena spent hundreds of millions on an airport expecting it to catalyze tourism growth, but two years in there was no tourism explosion. The airport functioned — flights landed reliably, no diversions occurred — yet the island remained a niche destination because flights were expensive, infrequent, and indirect for non-South Africans.

This mirrors a common startup failure mode: building the platform before building demand. The documentary explicitly states 'the airport is a tool for change, not the impetus of change itself.' The island still had to sell itself one-by-one to tourists and investors. Infrastructure removes a constraint but doesn't create pull. Founders who build technically excellent products and then wonder why growth is slow are making the same error — confusing removing a bottleneck with creating momentum.
