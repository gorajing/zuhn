Chandra distinguishes 'big S' strategy as fundamentally different from the tactical 'small s' process. Big S takes a 5-10 year view, is led by design and research rather than product management, and focuses on envisioning distinct future scenarios through concept prototypes rather than analyzing current data. It can run in parallel with small s work without conflict.

The rationale for design leadership is that product managers are trained to optimize within constraints — they naturally anchor to what's feasible today. Designers and researchers are better equipped to imagine discontinuous futures because their methods (concept cars, scenario prototyping) are explicitly designed to suspend current constraints. This mirrors how automotive companies use concept cars not as product plans but as strategic provocations that pull the organization toward a future vision.
