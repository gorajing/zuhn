Baxley observes that companies like Disney operate efficiently with small teams because their vision ('happiest place on earth') is so clear and well-internalized that individual contributors can make aligned decisions without constant check-ins. The vision acts as a distributed decision-making framework, eliminating the need for heavy process, extensive documentation, or large management layers.

This insight connects team size directly to vision clarity. Large teams are often a symptom of vague vision — you need more people to coordinate because no one independently knows the right answer. The implication for founders is that investing in crystallizing and communicating vision isn't soft leadership theater; it's a structural efficiency gain that directly reduces headcount requirements and speeds up execution.
