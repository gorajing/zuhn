The Wayback Machine exercise reveals something subtle: as companies morph from single-product to multi-product suites, certain messaging elements remain constant while others change. The constant elements represent the company's durable platform identity — the overarching problem space they own. The changing elements represent individual product positioning that adapts as the portfolio grows.

For early-stage founders, this means the earliest messaging work isn't about describing a product — it's about staking a claim on a problem space broad enough to support multiple products. Message-market fit at the platform level should come before any individual product-market fit. This is a practical diagnostic tool: if your current messaging can only describe one product, your positioning may be too narrow for eventual platform expansion.
