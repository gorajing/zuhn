Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen was literally waste — the video calls it 'the most useful useless byproduct in the history of useless byproducts.' Yet oxygen became the foundation for all aerobic life, including every animal on Earth. The byproduct dwarfed the original product in planetary impact.

This pattern recurs in business: Slack started as an internal tool for a failed game company. AWS began as Amazon's internal infrastructure. YouTube was a dating site. The strategic implication is that teams should actively monitor what their systems produce incidentally — data exhaust, internal tools, community artifacts — because the byproduct-to-primary-product inversion is one of the highest-leverage pivots available. Don't just build for the intended output; instrument and observe what else your system generates.
