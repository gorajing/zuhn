Step 1 of Changuel's 4-step delight model emphasizes identifying both functional and emotional motivators during user research. Spotify's Discover Weekly succeeded not because it was technically superior to other recommendation engines, but because it tapped into the emotional motivator of musical identity—users felt the playlist 'understood' them. This applies equally to B2B: products like Linear and GitHub succeed partly because they make developers feel competent and fast, not just because they have better feature sets. The practical implication is that user research should explicitly probe for emotional states (frustration, anxiety, pride, belonging) alongside task completion metrics. Teams that skip this step build functionally complete but emotionally inert products that lose to competitors who nail the feeling.
