Step one of pricing is defining value from the customer's perspective: a Gamma user wants quality decks, not visibility into how many API calls were made under the hood. Technical pricing in tokens or calls maps to your cost but not to their perceived value, and it overwhelms buyers. Stripe groups AI value into four frameworks — automation (saving time/cost), augmentation (same headcount, higher quality output), enhanced service (access to proprietary data/capability like fraud detection from payment volume), and improved results (direct bottom-line impact, e.g. Intercom charging per ticket resolved without a human).

The charge metric should then sit on a spectrum from consumption-based (API calls — easy to implement, aligns to your cost, but hard to tie to value) through workflow-based (images generated, documents summarized — aligns to the product) to outcome-based (qualified leads, candidates hired — aligns to customer ROI). Outcome pricing is easier to sell but harder to attribute; consumption is easier to implement but harder to align to value. The evidence that this matters: 53% of hypergrowth companies offer clear value-based pricing the customer understands, versus only 26% of low-growth peers.
