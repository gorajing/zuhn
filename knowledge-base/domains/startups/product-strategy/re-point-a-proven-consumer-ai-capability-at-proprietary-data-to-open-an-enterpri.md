The entire pitch is 'exactly the same idea, only now it goes to your private data.' That is a generalizable opportunity-discovery heuristic: a capability that has already been validated and adopted by millions in consumer tools (web deep research, chat, summarization, document generation) carries a pre-proven user behavior. Re-pointing it at a company's proprietary corpus inherits that validated demand while addressing data that the consumer version structurally cannot reach.

This lowers product risk on two axes at once: the interaction pattern is de-risked by consumer adoption, and the data is one the incumbent consumer providers won't touch (it lives behind enterprise auth, in SharePoint/Jira/Notion). The remaining work is integration and trust, not inventing a new behavior.

As a sourcing lens for builders and investors: scan widely-adopted consumer AI features and ask 'what would this be worth aimed at a company's internal knowledge?' The strongest candidates are capabilities whose value scales with how scattered and proprietary the underlying information is.
