Two distinct cohorts are emerging in SaaS. The first consists of horizontal tools with moderate differentiation and limited workflow lock-in — they rely on marketing-led acquisition, serve broad use cases, and integrate lightly. Their AI features are easily copied across competitors within quarters, and net retention trends toward or below 110%.

The second cohort is embedded in mission-critical workflows where operational, financial, or regulatory stakes are high. Customer processes adapt to the platform rather than the reverse. Their NRR stays above 120% because customers integrate more data, not just add seats. These companies behave like embedded infrastructure — CrowdStrike and Cloudflare still command 20x+ multiples because security and connectivity are non-discretionary. The strategic implication: founders should optimize for depth of workflow integration over breadth of feature coverage.
