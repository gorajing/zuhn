RuBisCo fixes carbon roughly half the time and produces toxic phosphoglycolate the other half. Plants evolved an entire photorespiration pathway just to clean up this mess — converting the toxic byproduct into usable glycine and Calvin cycle intermediates. The cleanup infrastructure is nearly as complex as the productive pathway itself.

This maps precisely to fast-growing startups: rapid customer acquisition generates support debt, fast shipping generates technical debt, aggressive hiring generates culture debt. Each of these is a 'toxic byproduct' of an otherwise productive core process. The companies that scale successfully are the ones that build dedicated cleanup systems (SRE teams, refactoring sprints, onboarding programs) proportional to their throughput, rather than hoping the byproducts will resolve themselves.
