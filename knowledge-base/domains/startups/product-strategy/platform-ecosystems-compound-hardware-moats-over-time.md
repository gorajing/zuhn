The iPhone launched in 2007 without an App Store. When it opened in 2008 with 500 apps, it grew to 1.8 million by 2019. This platform layer transformed a hardware product into an ecosystem play — users stay for the apps, developers build for the users, creating a flywheel that competitors cannot easily replicate.

This pattern reveals that the initial product innovation is often just the entry point. The real long-term moat comes from the platform layer built on top. Hardware specs can be matched; a mature ecosystem with millions of apps and developers cannot. Founders should think about their product's platform potential from day one, even if the platform launches later.
