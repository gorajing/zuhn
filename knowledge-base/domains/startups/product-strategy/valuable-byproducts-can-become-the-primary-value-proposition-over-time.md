Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen was a toxic waste product that nearly wiped out anaerobic life in the Great Oxidation Event. Yet oxygen became the foundation for all complex animal life — the byproduct eclipsed the primary product in planetary significance.

This pattern recurs in business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game, Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature, and AWS grew from Amazon's internal infrastructure needs. Founders should systematically audit what their systems produce incidentally — data exhaust, internal tools, intermediate artifacts — because the byproduct often has a larger addressable market than the intended output.
