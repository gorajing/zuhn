Daniel Ek recounts that Spotify's move into audiobooks and eventually podcasts was catalyzed not by a strategic memo but by discovering that music labels in Germany were quietly uploading audiobook catalogs to the platform to get more revenue from their rights. This unsanctioned behavior revealed that users cared less about the 'music' framing and more about consuming audio content generally — the platform primitives (discovery, ubiquity, freemium, search) were format-agnostic. The operational lesson: when sophisticated actors start gaming your platform for purposes you didn't design for, that's a product expansion signal stronger than any focus group. Set up observability to catch these emergent behaviors, and treat them as cheap experiments someone else already ran for you. Most companies suppress these as abuse; the best ones ask whether the 'abuse' is actually a better product.
