Plant breeders and crop geneticists can find statistical trait associations, but experimentally mapping regulatory consequences across species and stresses is expensive. The model creates a product-shaped wedge: predict which variants and transcription factors deserve scarce assays. That is more defensible than an AI wrapper around plant-science literature because it sits at a real measurement bottleneck.
