The framing of PMF as a milestone to 'achieve and move past' is a trap. Markets shift, competitors emerge, customer needs evolve, and a product that fit perfectly at Series A may partially fit at Series B. Companies that stop the customer feedback cadence after achieving PMF tend to experience gradual drift—still technically profitable but losing the urgency signal that made them successful. Treating PMF maintenance as an ongoing operational discipline—regular customer interviews, churn analysis, usage pattern monitoring—is what separates companies that compound versus those that plateau.
