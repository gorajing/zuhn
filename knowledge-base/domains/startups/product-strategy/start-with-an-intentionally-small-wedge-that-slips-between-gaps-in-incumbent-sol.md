Levie argues that enterprise startups must start intentionally small — not accidentally small. The strategy is to find a discrete problem that falls between the cracks of existing enterprise solutions, deliver an incredible user experience for that narrow use case, and then expand either up-market to larger customers or horizontally to adjacent use cases. Zen Payroll (now Gusto) exemplified this by targeting only the payroll management pain point for small startups, plugging into existing infrastructure rather than rebuilding everything, and making the process dead simple. Incumbents initially dismissed it as 'only for small businesses' — but that dismissal was the strategic advantage. The wedge creates a foothold that compound over time as the startup builds out more services and capabilities. The critical requirement is that the initial wedge must be significant enough that customers actually want to solve it with a discrete tool, not so small that it's trivial.
