Live ops — the practice of continuously publishing new content, items, events, and pricing experiments into a live product — is what Shalel calls 'one of the deep secrets of the game industry.' At Plarium, live ops drove competition between free and paid players, kept engagement high, and if removed from any successful game, would eliminate approximately 40% of revenue.

The mechanism is a compounding growth loop: live ops increases customer lifetime value, which means you can afford to acquire more customers, which generates more revenue to reinvest in live ops. Most non-game companies either don't understand the concept or don't commit the dedicated teams it requires. It cannot be done with a two-person operation — it needs proper investment — but the long-term payback is enormous. SaaS, marketplace, and consumer companies could apply this by treating their product as a living service with regular content drops, rotating promotions, and fresh engagement hooks.
