Most strategy evaluation devolves into 'which area has the biggest potential impact?' — a single-axis comparison that ignores execution risk. Chandra's four-dimension framework adds three critical reality checks: How confident are we in the impact estimate? Do we know specifically what actions would drive results? Are those actions something we're uniquely positioned to do?

This framework naturally filters out 'strategy theater' — big ambitious bets that sound impressive but have no clear execution path. A pillar with moderate expected impact but high certainty, clear levers, and unique differentiation is often a better strategic choice than a moonshot with unclear mechanics. The uniqueness dimension is particularly valuable because it forces teams to confront whether their strategy is defensible or just something any competitor could copy.
