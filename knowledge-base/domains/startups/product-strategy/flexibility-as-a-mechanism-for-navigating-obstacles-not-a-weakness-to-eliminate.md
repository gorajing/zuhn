The archer's paradox demonstrates that an arrow's flexibility is not a deficiency but the exact mechanism enabling accuracy. A perfectly rigid arrow striking the bow's riser would deflect unpredictably. Instead, the arrow's ability to flex allows it to snake around the obstruction while maintaining its trajectory toward the target.

This maps directly to product and organizational strategy: teams that build controlled flexibility into their systems — pivotable architectures, modular designs, adaptable processes — navigate market obstacles more effectively than rigid ones. The key qualifier is 'controlled' — Byron Ferguson doesn't use any random arrow, he precisely calibrates the flex (spine) to match his bow. Flexibility without calibration produces chaos, not accuracy.
