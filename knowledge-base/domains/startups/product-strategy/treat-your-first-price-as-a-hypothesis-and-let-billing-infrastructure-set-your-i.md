Your first price is a hypothesis, not a commitment, because the product is changing every week and premium features keep becoming standard. The data backs frequent iteration as a growth signal: hypergrowth companies (100%+ YoY) changed pricing 3+ times in the last two years, 49% of high-growth companies changed it 3+ times, versus only 22% of low-growth companies — static pricing correlates with a static product. The behavioral prescription is to prioritize speed over precision: put a reasonable price point out, then iterate by interviewing churned customers (price problem → fix pricing; fit problem → fix product), interviewing upgraders, and running A/B tests to find the optimum.

The non-obvious constraint is infrastructure: if every pricing change costs three to four months of engineering effort, fast iteration is impossible regardless of intent, so the billing system you choose effectively sets your maximum iteration cadence. 84% of AI leaders agree fast pricing adaptation is a key competitive advantage — but that capability is gated by tooling, which is why teams treat pricing-system flexibility (caps, notifications at 50/70/90%, rate limiting, tiered/dynamic/dimension-based rating) as a strategic build, not an afterthought.
