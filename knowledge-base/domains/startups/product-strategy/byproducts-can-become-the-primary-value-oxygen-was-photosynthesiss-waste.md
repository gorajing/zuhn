Photosynthesis splits water molecules to replace electrons lost during photoexcitation of chlorophyll. The oxygen released is, from the plant's perspective, a useless waste product — a 'useless byproduct' as the source puts it. Yet this waste product is what enabled the entire animal kingdom, aerobic respiration, and complex multicellular life.

This pattern is remarkably common in technology platforms: Slack started as an internal tool for a game company, AWS was Amazon's internal infrastructure sold as a byproduct, and YouTube's recommendation algorithm (a byproduct of search) became more valuable than the search feature itself. Founders should systematically audit their system's byproducts — data exhaust, internal tools, intermediate artifacts, community effects — because the most valuable output may be something the system produces incidentally rather than intentionally. The oxygen lesson suggests that byproduct value often dwarfs primary-product value by orders of magnitude.
