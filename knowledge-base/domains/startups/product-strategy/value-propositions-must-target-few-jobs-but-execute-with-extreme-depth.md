The instinct to address every customer need is one of the most common product strategy mistakes. The article uses KinetX's laser focus on deep-space navigation as an extreme example — by becoming indispensable in one narrow domain, they made themselves irreplaceable rather than merely useful.

This principle maps directly to startup execution: spreading a value proposition across many jobs creates mediocrity everywhere. The companies cited (Stripe on payment simplification, Canva on design democratization) all found their power by being dramatically better at one thing rather than incrementally better at many things. The practical implication is that the prioritization exercise — choosing the top 3 jobs from a list of 40-50 — is itself the most consequential product decision a company makes.
