Every investor has subconscious doubts during a pitch. The default is that those doubts remain unspoken and unaddressed, creating latent objections that kill deals in committee. Stebbings' counterintuitive approach: add a slide explicitly listing 2-3 reasons an investor should NOT invest, then address each with data.

This works through two psychological mechanisms. First, it demonstrates self-awareness, which signals maturity and trustworthiness — the opposite of the overconfident founder who pretends risks don't exist. Second, it gives you control over which doubts investors focus on. Instead of them fixating on a concern you haven't prepared for, you guide them to objections where you have strong answers. When Stebbings led with 'Can we get 15%+ ownership?' and showed deals averaging 12.5% with 50% year-over-year improvement, he turned the biggest risk into a growth narrative.
