The guide's central thesis is that the hardest part of fundraising isn't pitching—it's knowing who to pitch. Angels write $25K-$500K checks and tolerate pre-traction risk; VCs deploy $500K-$100M+ but demand scalability metrics; accelerators provide $50K-$500K with mentorship; crowdfunding requires marketing muscle rather than investor relationships.

This framing reorients fundraising strategy from pitch optimization to investor selection. A founder with strong traction pitching angels leaves money on the table; a pre-revenue founder pitching Series A VCs wastes months. The actionable framework is to map your current stage, traction, and capital needs to the investor type whose check size, risk tolerance, and value-add align with where you actually are—not where you aspire to be.
