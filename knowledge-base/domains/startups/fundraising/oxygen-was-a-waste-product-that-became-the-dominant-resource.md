Early photosynthetic organisms produced oxygen as a literal waste product of water splitting. This 'pollution' was so toxic to anaerobic life that it caused the Great Oxidation Event, one of the largest mass extinctions in Earth's history. Yet that same waste product became the foundation for aerobic respiration, enabling the evolution of all complex animal life.

For founders, this is a vivid reminder that byproducts of your core process may contain more value than the primary output. Slack was a byproduct of a failed game. Flickr emerged from a failed MMO. The pattern repeats: what you discard or treat as secondary may be the actual market opportunity. The implication is to pay close attention to what your process generates incidentally, especially if users or systems start depending on it in unexpected ways.
