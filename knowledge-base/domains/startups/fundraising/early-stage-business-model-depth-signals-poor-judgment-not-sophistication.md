A VC speaking at YC described investing in experienced founders whose business model he expected to change three times before they got it right. The business model at the early stage is just guesses about things outside the founders' expertise.

Spending precious pitch minutes on business model details does double damage: it wastes time that could showcase the problem and solution (things you actually know deeply), and if the model seems spectacularly wrong, it becomes the thing investors remember instead of your actual innovation. Show you've thought about monetization briefly, then move on.
