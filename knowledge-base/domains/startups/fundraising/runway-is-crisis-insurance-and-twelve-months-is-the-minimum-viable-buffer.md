Levine reframes the standard advice about maintaining 12-18 months of runway: it's not about operational comfort or investor expectations, but about crisis preparedness. When runway drops below a critical threshold, the founder's time allocation shifts almost entirely to fundraising, which means they can't simultaneously execute a crisis recovery plan.

This creates a vicious cycle: the crisis degrades metrics, which makes fundraising harder, which consumes more founder time, which further degrades metrics. Adequate runway breaks this cycle by giving the founder the temporal space to actually fix the underlying problem — whether that's a cost restructuring or a pivot — before needing to convince investors that the fix is working. The insight is that runway management in good times is itself a form of crisis management.
