Growth investors evaluate companies through a financial lens that maps directly to DCF inputs: revenue growth rates, margin expansion trajectories, capital efficiency, and risk-adjusted returns. When founders present valuations without this framework, negotiations devolve into anchoring battles where each side asserts a number without shared analytical foundation.

A founder-built DCF model transforms the dynamic. Instead of arguing whether the company is worth $50M or $100M, both parties can examine specific assumptions—Is 40% revenue growth sustainable? Will gross margins reach 70%? What's the right WACC for this risk profile? This shifts the conversation from adversarial to collaborative, and founders who can fluently discuss sensitivity analysis around their key assumptions signal operational sophistication that itself commands premium valuations.
