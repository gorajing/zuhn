The conventional VC narrative focuses on picking winners, but the pro-rata trap reveals a deeper failure mode: finding the winner and then slowly losing your stake. First Round Capital's $510K Uber seed investment returned ~$2.5B, but could have been 10x larger had they maintained ownership. Meanwhile Lowercase Capital, which aggressively followed on and bought secondaries, turned a similar early position into $1B+ returns with ~4% ownership at IPO. The math is stark — a 10% seed stake that dilutes to 2% by exit turns a $100M outcome into $20M, an $80M gap from inaction alone. This reframes VC skill: identification is necessary but insufficient without the structural capacity to maintain conviction through capital.
