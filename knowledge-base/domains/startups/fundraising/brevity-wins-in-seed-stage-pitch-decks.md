Across 16 unicorn pitch decks, a clear pattern emerges: the decks that closed rounds were remarkably short. Intercom raised $600K with 8 slides, Tinder with 10, Airbnb with 12. The decks that tried to be comprehensive ended up saying nothing memorable.

This suggests seed fundraising is fundamentally about conviction transfer, not information transfer. A founder who can distill their entire thesis into 8-12 slides demonstrates the clarity of thinking investors are betting on. The constraint of brevity forces the founder to pick their single strongest insight and build everything around it, which is exactly the kind of focused execution VCs want to fund.
