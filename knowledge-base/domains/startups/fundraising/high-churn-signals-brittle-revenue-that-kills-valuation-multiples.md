From an investor lens, churn isn't just a retention number—it's a judgment on company viability. VCs model durability alongside growth, and high churn signals that the foundation for scale is brittle. It lowers expansion potential, damages net revenue retention, and tells investors that future growth will require ever-increasing acquisition spend to compensate for losses.

This changes the company narrative from 'high-leverage growth engine' to 'leaky bucket with a sales addiction.' No investor pays a premium for a business that must perpetually outrun its own losses. The implication for founders is that retention work isn't just operational hygiene—it directly impacts the story you can tell in fundraising and the multiples you can command. Low churn is literally worth more in valuation terms than equivalent top-line growth.
