The startup support ecosystem has three distinct tiers that map to company maturity. Venture studios actively co-build companies from scratch, providing teams and operational support for pre-product founders. Incubators nurture early-stage ideas with patience and minimal equity pressure. Accelerators compress 3-6 months of scaling activity for startups that already have product-market traction.

Applying to the wrong tier creates a double penalty: rejection wastes application effort, and acceptance into a mismatched program means receiving resources optimized for a different stage. An ideation-stage founder in an accelerator faces impossible scaling pressure; a scaling startup in an incubator stalls when it needs acceleration. The diagnostic question is simple: Do you have an MVP with traction? If yes, accelerator. Prototype only? Incubator. Just an idea? Venture studio.
