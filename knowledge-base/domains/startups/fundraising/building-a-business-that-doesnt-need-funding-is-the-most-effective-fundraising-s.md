Parker Conrad describes pitching 60 VC firms and getting rejected by all of them for his first company. The turning point came when a VC told him he needed to 'be the Twitter guys' — have something so compelling that the pitch doesn't matter. Conrad took this as motivation to start Zenefits specifically because it seemed like a business he could build without raising money at all, with enough inherent cash flow to be self-sustaining.

The paradox: businesses that don't need venture capital are exactly the ones investors love to fund. Marc Andreessen reinforces this with the Steve Martin principle — 'be so good they can't ignore you' — and adds that raising venture capital is actually the easiest thing a startup founder will ever do compared to recruiting engineers, selling to enterprise customers, or driving viral growth. If fundraising feels hard, it's a signal that the harder challenges ahead will be even more difficult. This reframes fundraising difficulty not as a problem to solve through better pitching, but as diagnostic information about the business itself.
