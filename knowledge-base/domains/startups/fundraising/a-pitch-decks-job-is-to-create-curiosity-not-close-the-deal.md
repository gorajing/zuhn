Many founders treat a pitch deck like a business plan summary, trying to answer every possible investor question upfront. This misunderstands the deck's function in the fundraising process. The deck is a filtering and invitation tool—its job is to make investors want to learn more, not to pre-empt all due diligence.

A deck that creates well-structured curiosity—where the logic is clear but the details invite questions—gives founders a meeting to attend. Investors who have unanswered questions they're excited about are more likely to request time than investors who feel they've already understood the full picture. Clarity on the core thesis combined with strategic openness on details is the optimal configuration.
