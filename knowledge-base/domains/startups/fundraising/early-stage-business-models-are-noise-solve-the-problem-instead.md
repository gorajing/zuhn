A VC who spoke at Y Combinator described investing in experienced founders whose business model he knew was wrong — expecting it to change three times before they got it right. If even repeat founders with top-tier VC backing have bad early business models, spending pitch time on yours is doubly wasteful.

The opportunity cost is severe: every minute spent on guesses about monetization is a minute not spent on the problem you're solving and what you've built — the areas where you actually have expertise and credibility. Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model can become the only thing investors remember about you, pushing out the genuinely compelling parts of your story.
