The article surfaces a fundamental inversion in startup fundraising dynamics: at the angel and seed stage, the best deals are oversubscribed, meaning founders select which investors to accept. Harry Stebbings frames the key LP question as whether an investor is 'an aspirational source of capital that the very best founders consistently choose.' Louis Beryl emphasizes asking 'what is your right to invest in a deal?'

This means the primary skill for angels isn't evaluation but positioning. Packy McCormick illustrates one positioning strategy: offering founders storytelling and distribution to reach potential hires and customers. The implication for founders is equally important—understanding that top angels are competing for allocation gives founders negotiating leverage they often don't realize they have. The best fundraising outcomes come when founders recognize this dynamic and select investors who add genuine strategic value beyond capital.
