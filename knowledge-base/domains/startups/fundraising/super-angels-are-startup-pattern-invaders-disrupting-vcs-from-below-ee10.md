As the startup landscape shifts from an obelisk (few startups, most surviving) to a pyramid (many startups, high early failure rate), the fastest-growing volume is at the earliest stages. But Graham observes that existing investors can't easily move to capture this — their risk tolerance is 'deeply imprinted' in procedures and personnel.

This creates a classic disruption pattern: incumbents are structurally unable to serve the growing low end of the market, so new entrants fill the vacuum. The implication is that the biggest opportunities in startup investing are for new funds and individual angels who can operate comfortably at higher risk levels, not for established firms trying to do smaller deals.
