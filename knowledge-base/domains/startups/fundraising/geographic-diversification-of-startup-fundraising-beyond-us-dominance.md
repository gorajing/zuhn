In this curated list, the US accounted for only 30% of actively raising startups, with Spain at 13%, UK at 12%, and the remaining 45% distributed globally. While selection bias from a European-leaning publication matters, the pattern aligns with broader trends of startup ecosystem maturation outside Silicon Valley.

For investors, this suggests that limiting deal flow to US-based startups means missing the majority of early-stage opportunities. For founders outside the US, the data suggests that being non-US is less of a fundraising barrier than it once was, particularly at pre-seed and seed stages where local market knowledge can be an advantage rather than a limitation.
