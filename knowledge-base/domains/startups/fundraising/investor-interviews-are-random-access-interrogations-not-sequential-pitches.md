Paul Graham reveals that YC's 10-minute interview format is deliberately designed for random-access questioning, not sequential pitches. The partners need to test two things: whether founders truly understand their domain and whether they're being honest. A rehearsed pitch provides only indirect evidence of both; direct Q&A provides conclusive evidence quickly.

The worst advice is to 'take control of the interview.' When founders deflect questions to deliver prefabricated talking points, they signal either that they can't handle unscripted inquiry or that they're hiding something. The best preparation isn't rehearsing a pitch — it's talking to users so deeply that no question can stump you. This principle extends beyond YC: any investor conversation where you're tested on specifics rewards genuine domain mastery over polished presentation.
