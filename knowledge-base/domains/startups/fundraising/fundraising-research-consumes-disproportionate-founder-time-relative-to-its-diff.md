The article opens with a pointed observation: founders spend six weeks building investor spreadsheets and another month on poorly targeted cold emails. This is a resource allocation failure, not an information scarcity problem. The investor landscape is well-documented; what's missing is founders' awareness of aggregated resources and a systematic approach to using them.

This pattern—spending time on research that feels productive but isn't—is a broader founder failure mode. The fundraising version is particularly costly because fundraising has a time decay problem: investor interest cools, market windows close, and runway burns regardless. Every week spent building a spreadsheet from scratch is a week not spent in meetings. The meta-lesson is that founders should aggressively seek pre-built resources and templates for any non-core activity, reserving their original thinking for product and strategy decisions that actually require it.
