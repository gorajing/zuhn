Nathoo illustrates the mechanics: $100K invested on a SAFE with a $5M cap, followed by a Series A at $20M valuation, means the early investor gets shares at roughly 1/4 the Series A price — buying approximately 4x more shares per dollar than Series A investors. This is the investor's reward for early-stage risk.

The dangerous trap is raising too much on SAFEs without modeling cumulative dilution. Nathoo's example: $2M raised on SAFEs with a $6M cap means those investors will own ~25% at conversion. Add a Series A investor wanting 20%, and founders have given away 45% before accounting for option pools. The math can shock founders who viewed each SAFE in isolation. The advice is pragmatic — some money at a low cap is infinitely better than no money — but founders must trace the full dilution path forward rather than celebrating each check in isolation.
