Even in capital-intensive sectors like biotech or deeptech where large rounds are structurally necessary, the smartest founders stage their spending by creating internal deployment gates. They might raise $40M but only release $8M at a time, tied to technical validation, regulatory progress, or commercial traction milestones.

This approach injects 'founder-side discipline' into a system designed to burn capital. It preserves the kill-switch optionality to stop, pause, or pivot before the full budget is consumed. The key insight is that capital should map to specific de-risking milestones, not headcount growth or runway extension. This converts abundance back into a forcing function rather than a sedative.
