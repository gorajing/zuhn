Investors use different valuation methods depending on stage and context, and no single method captures the full picture. A pre-seed founder might blend Berkus (progress-based), Scorecard (market-relative), and Risk Factor Summation (risk-adjusted) to triangulate a range. A Series A company might use Comparable Transactions, light DCF, and the VC Method.

The strategic value of triangulation goes beyond accuracy — it shifts the negotiation dynamic. When a founder presents only one number, the investor can attack the method's weaknesses. When a founder presents a range derived from multiple approaches, the conversation moves to which assumptions are most reasonable, which is a much stronger position. The overlap zone between methods becomes the natural negotiation anchor.
