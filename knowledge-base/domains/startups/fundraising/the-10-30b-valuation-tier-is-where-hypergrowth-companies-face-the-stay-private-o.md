The $10-30B tier contains the highest concentration of companies on the list — over 30 entries including Perplexity, Discord, Deel, and Notion. The article notes these companies 'could go public at any moment' but 'choose not to because private markets now offer enough capital and liquidity.' This creates a new founder decision framework: IPO is no longer the default endpoint but one option among several, including continued private growth, secondary sales, and direct listings. For founders approaching this tier, the strategic question shifts from 'when do we IPO?' to 'what specific advantage would public markets give us that we cannot get privately?' — and increasingly the answer is nothing.
