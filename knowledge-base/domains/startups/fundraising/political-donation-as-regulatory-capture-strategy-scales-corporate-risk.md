FTX's political strategy was sophisticated: Sam personally donated $50M to Democrats (including $5M to Biden), while colleague Ryan Salame donated $23M to Republicans, hedging partisan risk. Sam lobbied for crypto licensing frameworks that would disadvantage competitors like Binance. Reports suggest the SEC was prepared to grant FTX 'no action relief' — essentially permission to break rules. The alleged connection between Caroline Ellison's father (MIT professor) and SEC head Gary Gensler (former MIT professor) added another layer. But this regulatory capture strategy created a dangerous dependency: FTX built its entire compliance posture around political relationships rather than actual controls. When the collapse came, the political donations couldn't prevent DOJ investigation. The lesson for startups: regulatory capture feels like risk mitigation but actually amplifies risk by creating a false sense of immunity that encourages increasingly reckless behavior.
