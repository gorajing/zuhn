Most venture funds reserve 40-60% of committed capital for follow-on investments, but the actual availability depends on how much has been deployed, whether the investment period is still open, and if recycling rights exist. A fund might promise pro rata support, but if it's past its investment window or already deployed reserves into other winners, those rights are empty.

The five diagnostic questions founders should ask: What percentage is reserved for follow-ons? How much is discretionary vs. earmarked? Does the investment period cover your likely next round? Do recycling rights exist (often capped at 120% of committed capital)? Can they lead insider rounds or only participate? These questions reveal whether support is structurally possible, not just promised.
