Most fundraising scams collapse under basic verification. The due diligence checklist is straightforward: search Crunchbase and AngelList for deal history, run WHOIS lookups on email domains (recently registered domains or free email services are red flags), verify team members appear on the firm's official website, and speak directly with founders from their portfolio.

The key insight is that real investors expect founders to run due diligence on them. An investor who discourages verification or pressures for speed before sharing basic documentation is signaling illegitimacy. This checklist should be run before sharing any sensitive documents — pitch decks, cap tables, financial projections, or customer lists. Using secure document-sharing platforms with access tracking adds another layer of protection.
