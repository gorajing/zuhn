Professional investors see hundreds of pitches; memorability drives which ones get follow-up. The tactical goal is to plant a descriptive phrase — 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' — that survives the blur of pitch day. The format ('the X of Y') is secondary to the discipline of generating it.

The deeper value is diagnostic: the exercise of compressing to a phrase forces founders to confront whether they have a focused wedge or a fuzzy everything-for-everyone product. If you can't find the phrase after honest effort, the product strategy may need narrowing before the pitch language does. This makes the soundbite exercise as useful in product development as in investor communication.
