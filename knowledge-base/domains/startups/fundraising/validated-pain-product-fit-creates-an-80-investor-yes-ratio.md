Co-author Chris Tottman, a founding GP at Notion Capital with $1B+ AUM across 200+ portfolio companies, claims that founders with validated pain-product fit can achieve what he calls an '80% Yes Ratio' from investors. This contrasts sharply with the typical fundraising experience where founders burn months pitching to skeptical VCs.

The mechanism is straightforward: investors are buying confidence in market demand. When a founder can demonstrate through customer development data that their ICP has a mission-critical pain and is willing to pay to solve it, the fundraising conversation shifts from 'will this work?' to 'how fast can you scale?' This reframes fundraising preparation not as pitch deck optimization but as customer validation rigor — the same work that accelerates product-market fit also de-risks the investment thesis.
