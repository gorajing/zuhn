The Berkus Method assigns up to $500K each across five categories: the idea, team strength, product progress, launch readiness, and strategic relationships. This framing reveals that at the earliest stages, investors are essentially pricing how much uncertainty you've eliminated, not how big the opportunity could be.

This has tactical implications for how founders spend their time pre-fundraise. Rather than perfecting pitch decks or financial projections, the highest-leverage activities are concrete risk-reduction moves: getting a working prototype with user feedback, recruiting an advisor with industry connections, securing a letter of intent from a potential customer, or demonstrating technical feasibility. Each visible risk reduction directly translates to valuation improvement in a way that narrative alone cannot.
