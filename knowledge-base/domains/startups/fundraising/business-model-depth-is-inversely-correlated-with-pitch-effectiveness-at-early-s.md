A VC speaking at YC described investing in a company whose business model he knew was wrong and would change three times—the founders were experienced with prior exits, and their model was still crap. He invested anyway because that's expected at this stage.

The implication is profound: spending precious pitch minutes on business model details is worse than neutral. If the model seems spectacularly wrong, it pushes the things you want investors to remember out of their heads. They'll remember you as 'the company with the boneheaded plan for making money' rather than 'the company that solved that important problem.' Talk about what you know deeply—the problem and the product—not your guesses about monetization.
