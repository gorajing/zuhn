VC decision-making is not a clean analytical process. It is shaped by heuristics, anchoring to other investors' judgment, and fear of missing out on deals that appear to have momentum. The 'who else is in?' question is not just curiosity — it functions as a proxy for quality assessment when conviction is uncertain, which it usually is at early stages.

For founders, this means the fundraise is partly a momentum engineering problem. Getting one credible lead investor to commit creates the social proof that moves other investors who were on the fence. This is why fundraising sequencing matters: approach your most likely converts first, not necessarily the most prestigious firms. The artificial scarcity and momentum created by early commitments is a legitimate and commonly used tool — understanding this dynamic converts a naive 'merit will win out' posture into a more effective strategy.
