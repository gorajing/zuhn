Graham cites Sequoia's Greg McAdoo looking for a 'proxy for demand' — evidence that people are already struggling with inadequate tools to do what your product enables. This reframes user stories from nice-to-have narrative devices into the hardest evidence an early-stage startup can present.

The hierarchy of story strength is revealing: best are founder-need stories (Apple, Microsoft, Google all grew from personal needs), next are stories about people you know personally. This ranking reflects credibility and depth of understanding — founders who built for their own pain can describe the problem with an authenticity that's hard to fake. Even preliminary data points stick in investor minds more than market size claims, because specific numbers about your users signal real traction while TAM figures signal wishful thinking.
