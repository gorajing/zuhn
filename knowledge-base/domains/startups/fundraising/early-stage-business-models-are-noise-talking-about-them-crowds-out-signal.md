A top VC told YC founders that a company he just funded had a business model he expected to change three times before they got it right — and he invested anyway. This reveals that smart investors treat early business models as disposable. They're evaluating the team and the problem, not the monetization plan.

The deeper issue is opportunity cost: your pitch time is finite and precious. Every minute spent on speculative revenue projections is a minute not spent on the problem domain where you actually have expertise and credibility. Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model becomes the memorable thing about your pitch, crowding out the substantive work you've done. You become 'the company with the boneheaded money plan' instead of 'the company that solved that important problem.'
