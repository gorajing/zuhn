When VCs ask about market size, saying 'it's a $50B industry' is a red flag, not an answer. Top-down sizing demonstrates nothing except the ability to Google an industry report. Bottom-up sizing—counting specific potential customers in your initial wedge, multiplying by realistic annual contract values, and mapping expansion paths—shows you understand your actual addressable opportunity.

For example: '80,000 independent clinics in the US, we're starting with the 10,000 that have in-house billing, at $8K/year that's an initial reachable TAM of $80M, expanding into adjacent verticals from there.' This approach blends ambition with credibility. It shows you've mapped a path to scale rather than just chasing a big number, and it demonstrates the strategic thinking VCs look for in founders who can actually execute a go-to-market plan.
