When VCs evaluate pricing, they're looking for proof that the founder understands their business economics. The CAC:LTV ratio is the core metric: LTV at least 3x CAC puts you in a healthy range, while below 1:1 means pricing or retention is broken. But it goes deeper — a low-ACV product with high CAC is a red flag signaling unsustainable growth (burning capital to acquire users who won't pay it back). Smart pricing improves all three investor metrics simultaneously: value-based pricing increases LTV, scalable models improve CAC efficiency, and together they shorten payback periods. Founders who can articulate how their pricing connects to these economics stand out in fundraising.
