The article cites that startups entering markets valued over $1 billion are 60% more likely to raise funding, and 88% of investors rank market size as the most important early-stage factor. This reveals a structural truth about VC economics: fund math requires outlier returns, so VCs rationally filter first on market ceiling rather than team quality or product elegance. For founders, this means market selection is upstream of every other fundraising decision—a brilliant product in a small market will lose to a mediocre product in an enormous one. The practical implication is that founders should frame their TAM narrative expansively (using TAM-SAM-SOM) and connect to secular trends like AI or climate tech before discussing product specifics.
