The perception that government grants are impractical for startups—too slow, too bureaucratic, near-zero odds—is largely wrong for Phase I programs. Approval rates in the 15–20% range are competitive but not prohibitive; by comparison, top-tier VC funds accept far less than 1% of companies they see. The difference is that founders don't apply to government grants because they don't perceive them as a real path.

The payoff structure is also unusually favorable: Phase I awards are typically $50K–$150K, Phase II can reach $500K+, and none of it requires repayment or equity. For deep tech, biotech, and hardware startups in particular, SBIR and equivalent EU programs (EIC Accelerator offering up to €2.5M) represent a funding source that is specifically sized for early-stage capital needs. The application process is real work, but so is fundraising—and the dilution cost is zero.
