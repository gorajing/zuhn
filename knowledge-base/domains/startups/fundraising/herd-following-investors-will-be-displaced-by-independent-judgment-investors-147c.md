Graham challenges the assumption that '15 big hits per year' is a cosmological constant, arguing that the limiting factor is the number of good founders — not technology or ideas — and that this number could grow 10-50x. If big hits go from 15 to 50-100 annually, the best pickers will have dramatically more winners to find.

The corollary is devastating for mediocre VCs: they'll still get leftovers but pay higher prices for them as valuations rise across the board. This creates a barbell outcome where top firms make more money than ever while average firms get squeezed. The practical implication is that investor differentiation through genuine selection skill becomes the only sustainable competitive advantage — brand, network effects, and deal access all become commoditized as the market grows.
