Among non-dilutive options, crowdfunding (Kickstarter, Indiegogo) occupies a unique niche because it simultaneously raises capital and validates market demand. Pebble Technology raising $10M+ on Kickstarter didn't just fund their smartwatch — it proved a massive customer base existed before manufacturing began. No grant, loan, or RBF arrangement provides this dual signal.

For product-based startups, this dual validation is strategically powerful. A successful crowdfunding campaign gives you capital, a customer list, market proof, and media attention in a single move. It also de-risks subsequent funding conversations — whether with VCs, lenders, or strategic partners — because you have concrete evidence of demand rather than projections. The limitation is that it works best for consumer products with compelling stories, not B2B SaaS or deep tech where the customer isn't the general public.
