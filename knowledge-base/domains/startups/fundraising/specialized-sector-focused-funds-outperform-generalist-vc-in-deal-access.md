The newsletter highlights several fund raises with sharp specializations — a former Citi Battery VC raising $378M specifically to help startups secure enterprise deals, Mayfield's $100M AI incubator, and niche-focused funds like Halo Capital and Wischoff Ventures. This pattern reflects a broader market shift: as capital itself becomes commoditized, the differentiation moves to what surrounds it. Founders increasingly choose investors who can open specific doors — enterprise procurement teams, regulatory navigators, or technical talent networks. For founders raising capital, this means evaluating investors primarily on their operational value-add in your specific vertical rather than brand name or check size. For the VC ecosystem, it signals continued bifurcation between large platform funds and highly specialized boutique funds, with the undifferentiated middle getting squeezed.
