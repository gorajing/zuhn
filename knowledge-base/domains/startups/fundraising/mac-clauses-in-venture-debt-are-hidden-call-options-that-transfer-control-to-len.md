Material Adverse Change clauses are among the most dangerous provisions in venture debt term sheets because they give lenders broad discretion to declare a default if the business 'deteriorates significantly.' The problem is that 'significantly' is often poorly defined, creating an asymmetric option: lenders can invoke MAC during any downturn to renegotiate terms, demand immediate repayment, or seize collateral (which often includes a blanket lien on all company assets, including IP).

This matters because MAC events correlate with exactly the moments when a startup can least afford forced repayment — missed revenue targets, delayed fundraising, key customer losses. Founders should push for highly specific MAC triggers rather than vague language, negotiate cure periods that allow time to remedy issues, and ensure their board and existing investors understand the implications. The negotiation principle: every undefined term in a debt agreement will be interpreted against you when things go wrong.
