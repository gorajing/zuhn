Smith raises a geopolitical dimension often missing from AI investment analysis: the unprecedented concentration of capital in OpenAI (potentially $190B+ from Nvidia, Microsoft, Amazon, SoftBank, Middle Eastern sovereigns, and public markets) means that OpenAI's failure wouldn't just hurt investors — it could cause an unwarranted chill in U.S. AI investment broadly, creating an opening for Chinese companies like DeepSeek to seize leadership.

This is a systemic risk argument. When a single company becomes synonymous with an entire technology wave, its failure gets misread as the technology failing rather than just one company failing. The resulting capital flight can be disproportionate to the actual setback, creating real strategic consequences at the national level. Diversified investment across multiple AI companies would be more resilient even if individually less exciting.
