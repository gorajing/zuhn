Scott Kupor of a16z and multiple other VC authors emphasize a truth that most founders miss: rejection rarely means your startup is bad. Internal fund dynamics—where a firm is in its deployment cycle, existing portfolio conflicts, LP mandate constraints, partner disagreements, and thesis evolution—drive most 'no' decisions.

This insight is immediately actionable for founders. Rather than internalizing rejection as a signal about their company, founders should treat fundraising as a matching problem. Understanding that VCs operate under structural constraints reframes the process: it's about finding the investor whose fund timing, thesis, and portfolio gaps align with your company, not about being 'good enough' for any given firm. Kupor notes that even strong internal advocates lose partner votes for reasons entirely unrelated to the startup's merit.
