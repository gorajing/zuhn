Jason Lemkin's 'third way' framework challenges the false binary that founders typically face: either take institutional VC (and accept the growth treadmill, board dynamics, and dilution that comes with it) or bootstrap entirely (and accept slower growth, capital constraints, and personal financial risk). The single-round path captures benefits of both: external capital validates the business and funds early hiring, while the absence of follow-on rounds keeps the founder in control of exit timing and scale ambitions.

This path is particularly well-suited to capital-efficient software businesses — especially those with strong unit economics, low customer acquisition costs, and recurring revenue — where the primary need is operational runway rather than massive growth capital. The key discipline is defining in advance what 'enough' looks like: enough revenue to be sustainable, enough runway to reach profitability, and a clear plan for how the single round gets deployed to achieve those thresholds rather than funding indefinite growth.
