When startup exit multiples decline — meaning acquirers and public markets pay less relative to revenue or growth metrics — the effects cascade through the venture ecosystem. VCs need larger ownership stakes or lower entry prices to hit return targets, which puts downward pressure on startup valuations and increases dilution for founders.

This dynamic forces adaptation at every level: founders must demonstrate capital efficiency rather than growth-at-all-costs, VCs must be more selective and price-disciplined, and the path to meaningful exits increasingly requires genuine profitability rather than narrative-driven valuation expansion. For founders raising capital, understanding where exit multiples sit in the current cycle is essential for negotiating fair terms and setting realistic expectations about what outcomes their investors actually need.
