Photosynthesis charges the thylakoid like a battery — protons accumulate inside, creating a concentration gradient. Rather than letting protons diffuse randomly, the system forces them through a single channel: ATP synthase. This constrained release converts the gradient's potential energy into ATP with remarkable efficiency.

In fundraising and startup strategy, this maps to the principle of building concentrated proof (traction, user stories, technical validation) before channeling it through one high-stakes moment — a pitch, a demo day, a product launch. Spreading effort evenly across many low-stakes interactions dissipates energy. The biological model shows that the accumulation phase (building the gradient) and the release mechanism (the constrained channel) are equally important. A huge gradient without ATP synthase is wasted potential; a perfect enzyme without a gradient has nothing to convert.
