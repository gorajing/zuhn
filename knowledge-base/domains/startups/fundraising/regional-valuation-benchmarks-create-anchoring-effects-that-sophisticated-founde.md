The Scorecard Method starts with the average pre-money valuation for startups at your stage in your region, then adjusts based on weighted criteria like team (25%), market size (20%), product (18%), and sales channels (15%). A weighted score of 110% against a $3M average yields a $3.3M valuation.

The anchoring psychology here is powerful. The entire calculation pivots on which 'average' you choose as the baseline. A founder building a remote-first AI startup could legitimately reference San Francisco SaaS benchmarks rather than their local market's average. The key is having a defensible reason for the anchor you choose — similar customer base, comparable technology, same investor pool. Angel groups that use this method are particularly susceptible to well-researched anchor selection because the method explicitly builds on the baseline rather than deriving value independently.
