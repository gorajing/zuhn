A common piece of founder advice is that VCs don't look at financials for early-stage companies, so building a detailed model is wasted effort. This is misleading. While VCs don't underwrite projections as literal forecasts, they use financial models to evaluate whether founders understand their cost structure, what assumptions drive growth, and how capital will be deployed. A founder who can't walk through their model is a red flag.

The practical implication: a startup financial model for fundraising doesn't need to be a 5-year DCF. It needs to show the logic: what does one dollar of marketing spend produce in revenue, how many customers are needed to reach profitability, and what drives the key ratios. VCs use this conversation to test operational thinking, not to value the company. Founders should build a lightweight 18-24 month model and be prepared to defend every assumption.
