Many founders instinctively try to minimize their competitive landscape, thinking it makes their startup look more unique. The opposite is true—VCs aren't scared of competition; its absence is actually a red flag. It either means the market doesn't exist, or the founder hasn't looked hard enough.

The winning approach is to name the key players (legacy incumbents, fast-growing startups, adjacent tools), then clearly articulate your specific angle of attack: a niche they're ignoring, tech that's 10x faster, a different go-to-market play, or insight from an untouched space. Weak comparisons like 'we're Uber but for X' or 'nobody is doing this' signal shallow thinking. Demonstrating that you've mapped the landscape and found a strategic entry point shows you're both realistic and strategic—you're not trying to win the whole market on day one, but you've found the right place to start.
