Professional investors hear so many pitches that they blur together. The first competitive threshold isn't getting a term sheet — it's being one of the startups an investor remembers after the event. A well-crafted descriptive phrase ('the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' was Viaweb's) plants an anchor in memory that other details can attach to.

Graham notes this is also a forcing function for founders: if you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, your plans may not be focused enough. The exercise of writing the soundbite is diagnostic — it surfaces whether there's a clear, defensible core to the company.

This reflects a broader principle about communication under information overload: the unit of memorable communication isn't the argument or the slide — it's the phrase. Anchoring everything else to one sticky phrase is how communicators survive competitive attention environments.
