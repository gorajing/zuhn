Professional investors hear enormous volumes of pitches that blur together. Before any deep evaluation happens, the first filter is simply: do they remember you? A descriptive phrase — typically 'the X of Y' in startups or 'X meets Y' in Hollywood — serves as a cognitive anchor that survives the blur.

The exercise of finding this phrase is valuable beyond pitching. If you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, it may indicate insufficient focus in your actual plans. The phrase should be launched clearly but apparently casually near the beginning of your talk. This is a specific instance of the broader principle that compression forces clarity — the constraint of brevity reveals whether you truly understand what you're building.
