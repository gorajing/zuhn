The guide explicitly warns against several investor red flags: unrealistic promises, excessive control demands, lack of industry experience, hidden fees, aggressive liquidation preferences, and poor reputation. The underlying principle is that capital is not fungible—the terms, expectations, and behavior attached to each dollar vary enormously.

This inverts the typical founder mindset of 'any money is good money.' Founders who accept capital from investors demanding disproportionate board seats or equity (beyond the 10-30% stage-appropriate ranges the guide outlines) sacrifice long-term control for short-term runway. The practical discipline is to conduct reverse due diligence: talk to other founders in the investor's portfolio, scrutinize term sheets for hidden provisions, and be willing to walk away from capital that comes with structural damage attached.
