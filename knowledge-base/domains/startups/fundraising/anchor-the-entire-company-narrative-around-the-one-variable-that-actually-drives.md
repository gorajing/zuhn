SpaceX's entire strategy becomes legible once you understand that cost per kilogram to orbit was their north star. Reusability, vertical integration, and launch cadence all trace back to that single constraint. This is a generalizable pattern: great companies find the variable that actually matters and then make every major decision a function of improving it.

For founders, this means resisting the temptation to present a multi-axis story. A pitch that optimizes for speed, cost, reliability, and market size simultaneously gives investors nothing to anchor to. The discipline of collapsing complexity into a single governing metric forces strategic clarity that compounds over time—because each decision can be evaluated against one criterion.

The practical exercise: before writing your next pitch, ask 'what is the one number that, if we moved it by 10x, would make everything else obvious?' If you can't answer that cleanly, the core insight isn't sharp enough yet.
