There is a critical distinction between startups that use AI as a feature and those built so that AI is the structural foundation. VCs in the current era are specifically screening for this difference. A company that bolts GPT onto an existing workflow is fundamentally different from one that reimagines the workflow around what AI uniquely enables.

This distinction shapes both product strategy and fundraising narrative. Founders need fluency in explaining how their AI model works, what data it's trained on, how it performs, and what makes it improve over time. The narrative must match the complexity of the product. Mistral's philosophical commitment to open-weight models and Harvey's deep domain integration with legal experts both demonstrate products that are structurally dependent on AI — not just enhanced by it. Investors respond to this inevitability framing because it signals a fundamentally larger opportunity.
