Founders often frame non-dilutive funding as 'free money' compared to equity rounds, but every funding type carries real costs. Grants demand compliance reporting and consortium participation. Revenue-based financing takes a fixed percentage of top-line revenue, which can be painful during revenue dips. Venture debt means interest payments that compound if cash flow stalls.

The mental model should be: all capital costs something. Running realistic cash flow projections and ROI estimates for every dollar raised — regardless of funding type — is what separates founders who scale sustainably from those who trade one form of financial pressure for another. Funders themselves prefer startups that think like CFOs rather than optimists chasing the cheapest-looking capital.
