The biggest fear investors have with early-stage startups is that founders built from a priori theory rather than observed need. Sequoia's Greg McAdoo calls the antidote the 'proxy for demand' — evidence that people are already doing something with inadequate tools, proving latent demand for your solution. Concrete user stories provide this evidence in a memorable, emotionally resonant format.

The hierarchy of story strength is revealing: best is your own need (Apple, Microsoft, Google all started here), next is needs of people you personally know, and weakest is abstract market research. This hierarchy exists because personal stories are hardest to fake and carry the most conviction. The 'proxy for demand' concept also generalizes beyond fundraising — it's a powerful validation framework for any product decision: don't ask 'would people want this?' but rather 'what are people already doing badly that proves they need this?'
