Paul Graham observed that among YC-funded companies, a dozen founders retained board majority after Series A — and these tended to be the strongest companies. This creates a self-reinforcing dynamic: the best startups have leverage to demand founder-friendly terms, which then normalizes those terms for the next generation of strong founders.

This has practical implications for how founders should interpret VC pushback on board control. If a VC claims founder control 'never happens,' that's either outdated information or a negotiation tactic. The pattern suggests that founders who can credibly retain control should treat it as a default ask, not a concession — because the precedent is set by exactly the caliber of company VCs most want to fund.
