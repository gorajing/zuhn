Dalton Caldwell's Standard Capital applied the YC batch model to Series A investing: a single application deadline, first interviews within a week, in-person second meetings, and final decisions two days later. The total founder time investment was roughly four hours (application plus two meetings), compared to the months-long traditional process of deck editing, back-channeling, and multiple VC pitches.

The process generated far more applications than expected, with high-quality companies self-selecting in. Founders who thrived were those who hadn't yet pitched other investors and simply wanted an efficient path to funding. This suggests the traditional Series A process creates enormous waste, and a structured, time-bound alternative can attract top-tier founders precisely because it respects their time.
