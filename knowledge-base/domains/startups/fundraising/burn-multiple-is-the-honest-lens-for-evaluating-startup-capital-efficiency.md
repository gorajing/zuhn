Growth rate alone can be misleading — a startup burning $5M to add $2M in ARR (2.5x burn multiple) tells a very different story than one burning $3M for the same growth. In tighter capital markets where cheap funding isn't available to subsidize customer acquisition, burn multiple has become the go-to lens for whether a company is building sustainable value or just buying revenue.

The benchmark is roughly 1.5x for enterprise SaaS, meaning each dollar of new ARR costs $1.50 in burn. Combined with other unit economics signals — LTV:CAC of 3:1+, CAC payback under 12 months, gross margins above 70%, and NDR above 120% — burn multiple helps investors see whether a company's financial house is in order. Companies with disciplined financials earn buyer trust, which translates directly into higher exit valuations and faster deal velocity.
