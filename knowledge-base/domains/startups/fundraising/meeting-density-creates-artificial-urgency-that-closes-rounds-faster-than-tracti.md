The founders argue fundraising success splits 50/50 between company strength and process execution. The process half is dominated by one variable: meeting density. When investors see you're simultaneously in conversations with multiple other investors, the 'why now?' question answers itself — if they don't act, someone else will take the deal. This applies even when MRR isn't growing at extreme rates (300%+ MoM).

This is counter-intuitive because most founders believe fundraising is purely a function of metrics and traction. In reality, a founder with moderate traction but excellent process management (dense meeting scheduling, parallel conversations, strategic information sharing about round progress) can outperform a founder with better metrics but a slow, sequential approach. The practical implication: don't spread investor meetings across months. Batch them into 2-3 week sprints.
