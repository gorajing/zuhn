Even with steep revenue growth (5x+ annually), investor interest can never be fully counted on. Something about your business can always spook investors regardless of growth metrics. Graham advises founders to write down precisely what they'll do to survive without more money and precisely when they'll switch to that plan.

The dependency trap works like a feedback loop: the more you depend on future fundraising, the falser that assumption becomes. A startup that's default dead with high expenses and slow growth becomes unappealing to the very investors it's counting on. The combination of high burn, mediocre growth, and no backup plan is the classic death spiral Graham sees repeatedly.
