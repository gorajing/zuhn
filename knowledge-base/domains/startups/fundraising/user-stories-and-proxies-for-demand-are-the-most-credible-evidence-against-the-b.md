Graham identifies the core anxiety driving early-stage investor skepticism: that the startup built something based on theory rather than real demand. Market size statistics don't address this — they describe a potential future, not present human need. A user story about a specific person using an inadequate workaround demonstrates that the problem exists today, independently of the startup.

Sequoia's concept of a 'proxy for demand' captures this well: what are people doing right now, with clunky tools, that proves they need what you're making? Even better is when founders are solving their own problem — a large fraction of famous startups (Apple, Yahoo, Google) originated this way, and investors know it. This shared origin story carries its own credibility.

The actionable implication: before pitching, founders should collect 2-3 specific user stories that show the problem is real and the workarounds are painful. These outperform any slide about market size.
