The founders who were most successful in Standard Capital's process were those who hadn't yet entered the traditional fundraising game at all. They hadn't built pitch decks, hadn't started back-channeling with VCs, and hadn't run any competitive process. They simply applied and said 'if we get in, we're doing it.'

This challenges the conventional wisdom that pitching VCs is a critical founder skill. Dalton notes that while some VCs argue pitch-deck skills are essential, he wouldn't put that in the top five most important skills for building a great company. The implication is that the traditional fundraising process selects for pitching ability rather than company-building ability, and structured alternatives can correct this misalignment.
