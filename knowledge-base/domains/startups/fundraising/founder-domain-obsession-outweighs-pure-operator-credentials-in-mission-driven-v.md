NFX highlights fidu's founders' obsession with improving LatAm education as a primary investment reason, noting the CEO's journey from classroom teacher to policy advisor to entrepreneur. The founding team combines 10+ years of education experience, Stanford/Columbia credentials, and prior entrepreneurial success — but NFX leads with mission obsession, not credentials.

This reflects a pattern in mission-driven verticals where the sales cycle is long, the buyers (school administrators) are risk-averse, and the market requires patient penetration. Founders who entered the space out of genuine frustration with the status quo are more likely to persist through the slow early years and make product decisions that earn trust with conservative institutional buyers. For founders targeting similar verticals (healthcare, government, education), demonstrating authentic domain obsession — not just market-size slides — is a critical fundraising signal.
