The $0-to-$100M-in-9-months story is not a template — it's an extreme case study of what happens when multiple compounding advantages stack: the founders had Microsoft-level enterprise relationships, the cybersecurity sector was flush with capital post-high-profile breaches, and Sequoia/Index co-signing the deal opened enterprise doors immediately. The insight is not 'move fast' but rather 'the variance in startup velocity is enormous based on founder network and sector timing.' For aspiring founders, this highlights the asymmetric returns from working at a marquee company in a high-stakes domain before founding: the credibility transfer alone can compress a 3-year go-to-market into months. Conversely, it cautions investors against extrapolating this growth rate as a general benchmark.
