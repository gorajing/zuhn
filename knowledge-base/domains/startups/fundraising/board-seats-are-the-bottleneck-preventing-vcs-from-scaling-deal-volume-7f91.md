Paul Graham identifies a structural flaw in VC economics: the cost of a board seat forces VCs to demand 20%+ ownership at series A, which means the investment amount is set by the investor's model rather than the company's needs. This creates a principal-agent misalignment where VCs push excess capital on startups.

The first VC firm to break ranks — letting equity percentage float with the market instead of anchoring to their ownership model — would instantly attract the best deal flow. This is a classic case where an industry's legacy financial model has drifted from market reality, and the correction creates outsized opportunity for whoever moves first.
