In early-stage investing, most startups lack revenue, traction, or even a finished product. This forces investors to evaluate the founder rather than the business. Founder-market fit—whether the founder has lived the problem, understands the market from the inside, and would keep building even without funding—has become the primary filter before product-market fit is even possible to assess.

This represents a meaningful shift in VC evaluation frameworks. Rather than betting on a pitch deck or market sizing, investors are making judgment calls about personal conviction, earned insight, and execution track record. A great deck secures the meeting, but founder-market fit closes the deal. Founders preparing for seed rounds should lead with their personal alignment to the problem rather than market analysis slides.
