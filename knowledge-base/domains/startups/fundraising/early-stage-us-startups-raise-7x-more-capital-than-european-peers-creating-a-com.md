Zapflow data from 2013-2023 shows US startups raised 2-3x more than European counterparts across all funding stages, even during global market corrections. At the extreme, early-stage US companies raised 7x more for equivalent products. This gap isn't just about money—it translates to better talent leverage, more runway to iterate, and less equity dilution.

The compounding effect is the critical insight: two teams building identical products in Berlin vs. Boston diverge not linearly but exponentially, because the Boston founder's capital advantage enables faster learning cycles, better hires, and stronger market positioning at every subsequent round. This reframes US expansion from a growth lever to a funding strategy—the question isn't whether to enter the US, but when the cost of staying out exceeds the cost of going in.
