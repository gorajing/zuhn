A top VC told YC founders he invested in a company whose business model was wrong, expecting it to change three times before they got it right. If experienced founders with millions in funding still have a wrong business model, yours is probably wrong too.

The damage is twofold: time spent on business model guesses displaces time spent on your actual strengths (the problem and what you've built), and a spectacularly wrong model can become the only thing investors remember about you. Show you care about revenue, but keep it to one sentence and redirect to substance.
