WeWork's Series D deck is described as the most useful deck in the collection specifically because it illustrates what happens when narrative detaches from fundamentals. The slides read like a storytelling masterclass, yet the business behind them was hollow. WeWork raised $355M on that disconnect.

This represents a critical asymmetry in fundraising: the skills that make a great pitch deck (compelling narrative, bold vision, emotional resonance) are orthogonal to the skills that make a great company (unit economics, operational discipline, honest self-assessment). Founders and investors alike should treat extraordinary storytelling ability as a yellow flag that demands deeper diligence, not as a green light. The WeWork case suggests that the better the narrative, the harder you should look at the numbers.
