Most founders treat VC intro calls as one-directional pitches, missing that the Q&A portion is actually their biggest differentiation opportunity. Questions like 'What does a great founder-investor relationship look like to you?' or 'How do you support portfolio companies during difficult quarters?' signal that you're evaluating fit, not just seeking capital.

This works because it flips the power dynamic. A founder who asks thoughtful, researched questions demonstrates confidence, long-term thinking, and selectivity — all traits VCs associate with successful founders. The key constraint is that questions must be un-Googleable; asking something answered on their website signals the opposite of preparation. Focus on questions about their working style, involvement philosophy, and how they've handled adversity with portfolio companies.
