SpaceX doesn't open with rocket engineering. It opens with cheaper access to space, more frequent launches, and the downstream markets those unlock. This inversion—outcomes first, technology second—is not a presentational trick but a strategic signal: it tells investors that the founders understand the economic destination, not just the engineering journey.

When founders lead with technology, they implicitly ask investors to complete the 'so what' step themselves. Most won't. Leading with outcomes removes that friction and also positions the company against a market transformation rather than a product category, which is a much larger addressable story.

This principle transfers directly: before any pitch or strategy doc, write one paragraph describing the world five years from now if you succeed completely. That paragraph should come first. Everything else—technology, team, milestones—is evidence for why that future is achievable.
