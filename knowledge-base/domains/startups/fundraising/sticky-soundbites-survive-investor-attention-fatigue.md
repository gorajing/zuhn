Professional investors hear so many pitches that they all blur together. The first filter isn't quality judgment — it's simply which ones they remember at all. A descriptive phrase that sticks ('the x of y' pattern) ensures you survive this cut. Viaweb used 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' — immediately graspable, memorable, and positioning.

This exercise also serves as a forcing function for strategic clarity. If you can't describe your startup in one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite isn't just a presentation trick — it's a litmus test for whether you've achieved the conceptual compression that makes a startup idea transmissible. Launch it early in your talk, apparently casually, so it anchors everything that follows.
