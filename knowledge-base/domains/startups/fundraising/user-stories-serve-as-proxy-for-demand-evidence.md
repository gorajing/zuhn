The biggest fear investors have when evaluating early-stage startups is that you've built something based on a priori theories rather than real need. Sequoia specifically looks for 'proxy for demand'—evidence that people are already doing something with inadequate tools that shows they need what you're making.

The hierarchy of user stories matters: stories about your own needs are strongest (Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all started this way), followed by people you know personally, then general market observations. Experienced investors recognize this pattern and weight founder-as-user stories heavily. Another strong demand signal is when people already pay a lot for an inferior solution—it's easy to convince investors demand exists for a cheaper alternative that preserves what made the original popular.
