Graham identifies a dangerous measurement trap: the only easily trackable metric for YC batches — post-Demo Day fundraising rate — has zero or even inverse correlation with the metric that actually matters (whether the batch contains a massive winner). This is because outlier-producing batches necessarily include more unconventional, harder-to-fund startups.

He calculates that if YC is taking the mathematically optimal amount of risk, only about 30% of startups should successfully raise after Demo Day. Yet their actual rate was 94% in the last measured batch. Graham deliberately avoids tracking this number because measuring it would trigger Goodhart's Law — optimizing the metric would make them more conservative, which is the opposite of what produces outlier returns.
