The conventional narrative about investor competition focuses on valuation increases, but Graham identifies that the structural changes matter more. Angel rounds of ~$600k on convertible notes at $4M caps meant founders gave up ~13% versus 30-40% in traditional series A rounds. Beyond dilution, founders retained board control (no VC board seats), avoided option pool dilution requirements, and experienced graceful degradation if fundraising stalled rather than the all-or-nothing collapse of a failed partner meeting. The lesson for founders is to evaluate funding offers on the full deal structure—dilution, control provisions, speed, and downside risk—not just headline valuation. When new investor competition emerges, the structural terms often shift before prices do.
