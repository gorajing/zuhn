a16z's Speedrun program illustrates how top-tier accelerators have evolved beyond simple capital deployment. With $1M checks, $5M in cloud and service credits, visa support for international founders, and access to a 500+ peer cohort, the program bundles a comprehensive founder support package. Since 2023, it has funded 150+ startups with $180M deployed.

This bundling strategy reflects a fundamental shift in accelerator competition: when capital is abundant, the differentiator becomes everything around the capital. Credits reduce burn rate without dilution, visa support removes existential risk for international founders, and peer networks create compounding value through knowledge sharing and deal flow. Founders evaluating accelerators should weight these non-capital benefits heavily, as they often exceed the value of the check itself in the first 18 months.
