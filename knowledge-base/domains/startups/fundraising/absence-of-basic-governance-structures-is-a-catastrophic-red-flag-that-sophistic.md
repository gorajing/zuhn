FTX raised over $2 billion in institutional capital with no board of directors. This wasn't a subtle red flag—it was the most elementary governance structure missing entirely. Yet sophisticated investors, caught up in growth metrics and market narrative, repeatedly failed to flag it.

This reveals a dangerous pattern in venture capital: in bull markets, governance due diligence gets subordinated to growth and momentum signals. Founders and investors both benefit from treating governance structures as non-negotiable checkboxes rather than nice-to-haves. For founders, proactively establishing governance—even when not required—signals maturity and reduces key-man risk. For investors, the absence of basic controls should be a hard disqualifier regardless of the financial trajectory.
