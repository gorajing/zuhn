The problem slide has one job: make investors believe people will pay to have this problem solved. The mistake founders make is presenting a logical argument (market research, survey data, industry statistics) when what's needed is an emotional one. Investors need to step into the customer's shoes and feel the frustration, loss, or inefficiency.

Revolut's problem slide works because everyone has experienced opaque foreign exchange fees — it's visceral and relatable. A slide describing 'fragmented international payment infrastructure' describes the same reality but triggers no empathy. The emotional version converts; the analytical version informs.

This applies to sales, product design, and any communication where the goal is behavior change rather than knowledge transfer. Logic earns permission; emotion earns commitment.
