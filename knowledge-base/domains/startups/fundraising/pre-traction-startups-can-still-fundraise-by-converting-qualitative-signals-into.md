Most startup advice focuses on metric-driven fundraising, but the reality is that pre-seed and many seed rounds happen before meaningful financial data exists. In these cases, investors evaluate TAM credibility, team strength, and competitive differentiation — but the most effective founders also surface leading indicators that bridge the gap between vision and traction.

A growing waitlist demonstrates demand validation. Deep engagement metrics (session length, return frequency) prove the product delivers value even at small scale. Early willingness-to-pay signals — even from a handful of design partners — carry outsized weight because they represent the hardest evidence possible at that stage. The key insight is that these qualitative signals must be presented with the same rigor as financial metrics: tracked over time, benchmarked against comparable companies, and tied to a narrative about what they predict for the business model.
