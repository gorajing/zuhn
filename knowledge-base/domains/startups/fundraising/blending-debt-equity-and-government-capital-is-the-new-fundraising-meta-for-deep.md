The US government has deployed trillions through the CHIPS Act, Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, and Inflation Reduction Act to incentivize domestic production in semiconductors, energy, and infrastructure. Chamath argues that companies which creatively tap into multiple capital pools — debt, equity, and government grants — may represent the biggest winners of the next decade. This is a departure from the VC-centric funding model of the 2010s SaaS era. For deep-tech and hardware founders, the implication is that fundraising strategy itself becomes a competitive advantage: understanding government procurement, grant applications, and debt structuring is now as important as pitching VCs. The winners won't just have the best technology — they'll have the most sophisticated capital stack.
