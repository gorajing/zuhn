Early-stage investors aren't looking for finished companies or smooth presentations — they're pattern-matching for raw talent and real insight. When founders try to appear more established than they are (corporate language, fake confidence about unknowns, pretending expertise they don't have), they obscure the very signal investors need to evaluate.

The practical asymmetry matters: technical founders underestimate how practiced investors are at reading the tells of performance versus authenticity. Candor about unknowns reads as self-awareness and intellectual honesty. Overclaiming reads as a warning flag. The heuristic: be direct about what you haven't figured out, don't go out of your way to surface it, but don't pretend either.
