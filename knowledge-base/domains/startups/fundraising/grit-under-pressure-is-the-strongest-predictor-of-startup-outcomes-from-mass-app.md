After analyzing 8,000 YC applications, conventional filters like idea quality, market size, résumé depth, and early traction proved to be weak signals for predicting startup outcomes. The strongest indicator was grit under pressure, which led evaluators to redesign their screening process to probe resilience from multiple angles rather than over-indexing on initial metrics.

This has profound implications for both founders and investors. For founders, it suggests that demonstrating perseverance through adversity matters more than polishing pitch decks or inflating traction numbers. For investors, it argues for evaluation frameworks that stress-test founders rather than optimize for surface-level markers. The finding also challenges the common VC practice of pattern-matching on pedigree and market sizing as primary filters.
