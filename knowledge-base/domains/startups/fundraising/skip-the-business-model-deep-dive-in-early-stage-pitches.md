A top-tier VC Paul Graham quotes explicitly said a company he just invested in had a business model that would probably change three times. He invested anyway. This is the norm, not the exception. Investors at the seed stage are betting on the problem and the founders, not the revenue model.

The danger of over-explaining the business model is twofold: it wastes the limited time that should be spent on the problem and the product, and if the model seems spectacularly wrong, it displaces the memory investors form of you. You become 'the company with the bad business plan' rather than 'the company solving X.'

The right move is to signal that you've thought about monetization (one slide, one sentence) and then move on. Show you're not naive, but don't defend guesses as facts.
