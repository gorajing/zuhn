Graham traces a causal chain: cheaper startups → founders need less money → founders have leverage → investors get less equity and control. Series A ownership has already crept from 40% to 20%, and he predicts the line will collapse further.

This isn't a cyclical market fluctuation but a structural shift driven by technology reducing startup costs. The implication is that any investor strategy premised on large ownership stakes is fighting a losing battle against the underlying economics. Smart investors should front-run this by voluntarily offering better terms now rather than being forced to later.
