The funding rounds highlighted span Singapore (Tyme Group $250M Series D, AppWorks Demo Day), Europe (Bnewable €40M, ETF Partners €285M, Drifter World SEK 36M), and emerging markets (Uzbekistan, Sri Lanka, Thailand VC roles). This geographic spread signals a structural shift in where venture-backed innovation happens.

For founders, this means both more diverse funding sources and more competition from global peers. The presence of VC analyst and associate roles in places like Tashkent and Colombo indicates institutional infrastructure being built in markets that were previously informal. This decentralization creates arbitrage opportunities for founders who can build in lower-cost markets while accessing global capital.
