YC observes a common pattern: after investing, individuals (especially celebrity investors) request an official advisor title plus additional common stock in exchange for 'advisory services' like making introductions. This is a freebie extraction. The investor already has financial incentive to help the company succeed — their investment appreciates when the company does well. Granting additional shares dilutes existing founders for help the investor should be providing anyway as a stakeholder. The pattern is especially prevalent with celebrity investors who leverage their social capital to extract extra equity for introductions they'd make regardless. Founders should treat every investor as a de facto advisor without any official title or additional compensation. If someone needs extra equity to be motivated to help a company they already invested in, that's a red flag about their alignment.
