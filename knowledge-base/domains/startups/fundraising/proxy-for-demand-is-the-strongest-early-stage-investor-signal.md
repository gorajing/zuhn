Sequoia's Greg Mcadoo explicitly names 'proxy for demand' as a key signal: what are people doing right now, with inadequate tools, that proves they need what you're building? This is far more persuasive than market sizing because it's concrete and observable rather than speculative.

The hierarchy of demand evidence runs: your own need (Apple, Microsoft, Google all started this way), needs of people you know personally, and then observable workarounds in the market. Each level is less direct but still powerful. The underlying investor fear at the early stage isn't that you can't build it — it's that you've built something based on a priori theories rather than observed need. Stories about specific users with specific problems directly counter this fear in a way that TAM slides never can.
