The conventional pitch deck was designed for a world where you had one shot in a conference room. It tries to pack valuation, market size, team, traction, and product into 12-15 slides viewed once. This format fights against how investment decisions actually happen — which involves multiple touchpoints, evolving conviction, and social proof accumulation.

The pitch cycle model breaks investor engagement into phases: initial hook (before the meeting), discovery (the meeting itself), follow-through (async materials post-meeting), and social proof (references, co-investors). Each phase requires different content optimized for that moment. Founders who think in terms of a cycle rather than a deck can tailor each touchpoint to move the investor to the next stage rather than trying to close conviction in a single artifact.
