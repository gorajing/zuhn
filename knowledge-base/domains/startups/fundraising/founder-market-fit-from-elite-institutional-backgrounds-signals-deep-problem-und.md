NFX explicitly highlights the Veriti founders' backgrounds in Unit 8200 (Israel's elite intelligence cyber unit) and Check Point as a primary reason for investment. This goes beyond generic 'domain expertise' — these founders spent 6-13 years embedded in the actual institutions where cybersecurity problems are most acute and advanced. They didn't just study the problem; they lived it at the highest level.

For fundraising strategy, this suggests that the most compelling founder-market fit narratives aren't about adjacent experience or academic knowledge, but about having operated inside the institutions that define the problem space. The founders also validated their understanding by interviewing dozens of CISOs before building — combining institutional depth with customer discovery. This pattern of 'deep insider knowledge + systematic external validation' is particularly powerful for enterprise and security startups where buyer trust is paramount.
