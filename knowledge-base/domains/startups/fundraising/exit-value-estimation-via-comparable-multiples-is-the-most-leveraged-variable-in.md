In the VC Method formula, exit value is the numerator from which everything else derives. VCs typically estimate exit value using revenue forecasts multiplied by comparable company multiples (e.g., 10x ARR for SaaS, different multiples for other industries). A shift from 8x to 12x ARR on the same revenue projection changes the exit value by 50%, which flows directly through to pre-money valuation.

This makes comparable company selection the highest-leverage point in any valuation negotiation. Founders who come prepared with a curated set of comparable exits — emphasizing companies in their specific niche that achieved premium multiples — can materially shift the math in their favor. Conversely, investors will anchor on lower-multiple comps to justify lower valuations. The negotiation is often less about the discount rate or round terms and more about which comparable companies both sides agree are relevant.
