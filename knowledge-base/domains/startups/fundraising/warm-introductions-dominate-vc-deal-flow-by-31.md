The VC deal pipeline is heavily skewed toward referral-based sourcing. Three-quarters of funded deals arrive through warm introductions from angel investors, portfolio founders, or peer VCs. Cold emails rarely work unless traction is undeniable. This creates a structural advantage for founders who invest in relationship-building before they need to raise.

The practical implication is that fundraising preparation should start 6-12 months before you actually need capital. Building relationships with angel investors, startup advisors, and operators in your space creates the referral pathways that VCs trust. Engaging with a VC's content (blog, podcast, newsletter) is another indirect warming strategy. The founders who treat networking as a fundraising prerequisite consistently outperform those who start cold-emailing when runway gets short.
