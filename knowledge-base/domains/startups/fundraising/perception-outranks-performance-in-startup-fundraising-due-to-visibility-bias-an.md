The article points to a well-documented but under-discussed dynamic: investors pattern-match on legible signals rather than underlying substance. When a founder with half your traction raises twice the round, it's not necessarily because they're ahead — it's because their signal made it through the investor's filtering system.

This is reinforced by research on visibility bias and the halo effect, where perceived competence generates compounding advantages: credibility grants space, which grants resources, which generates more credibility. The implication for founders is that fundraising strategy must include signal design as a first-class activity, not an afterthought. Product updates with backstories, roadmap decisions with reasoning, and wins with context are the specific tactical outputs that translate traction into investable narrative.
