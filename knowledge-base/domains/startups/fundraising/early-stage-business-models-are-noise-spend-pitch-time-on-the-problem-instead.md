Paul Graham recounts a VC who invested in a company despite knowing their business model was wrong — he expected it to change three times before they got it right. These were experienced repeat founders who'd just raised millions, and even their model was bad. At the earliest stages, business models are just guesses about domains that probably aren't the founders' expertise.

The opportunity cost is what kills you: every minute spent on speculative revenue projections is a minute not spent on the problem you're solving and the product you've built — the areas where you actually have deep knowledge and can sound genuinely smart. Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model can become the thing investors remember about you, pushing your actual strengths out of their heads. Mention monetization briefly to show you've thought about it, then move on.
