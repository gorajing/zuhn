First Round Capital's data showing 92% of VCs prioritize team aligns with Reid Hoffman's philosophy of investing in people over businesses. The key insight is that VCs aren't just evaluating current capability—they're predicting adaptability. A startup will almost certainly pivot from its original thesis, so what matters is whether the team can navigate that pivot successfully. The ideal configuration isn't three brilliant generalists but complementary specialists (technical depth, go-to-market expertise, operational skill) who collectively eliminate single points of failure. For founders, this means co-founder selection and early hiring should optimize for coverage gaps, not resume prestige.
