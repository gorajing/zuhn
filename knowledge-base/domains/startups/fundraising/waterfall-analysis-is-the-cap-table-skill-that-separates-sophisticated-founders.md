A waterfall analysis calculates how proceeds from a liquidity event flow to each shareholder class based on liquidation preferences, participation rights, and seniority stacking. VCs run these models routinely, but most founders don't—which creates a dangerous information asymmetry at the negotiating table.

The practical impact is significant: a 1x non-participating liquidation preference behaves very differently from a 1x participating preference with a 3x cap across exit scenarios. At a modest exit, participating preferences can consume most of the upside that would otherwise flow to common shareholders (founders and employees). Founders who model waterfalls at $50M, $100M, $250M, and $500M exit values before signing a term sheet can identify which terms actually matter for their likely outcomes and negotiate accordingly. This is also critical for retaining talent—if employee option holders can see that liquidation preferences eat most of a realistic exit, the equity compensation story falls apart.
