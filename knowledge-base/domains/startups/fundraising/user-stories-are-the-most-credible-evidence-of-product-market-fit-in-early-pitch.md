The core investor anxiety about early-stage startups is a priori product development: founders building based on theory rather than validated need. User stories counter this directly by providing 'proxy for demand' — evidence of what people are doing today with inadequate tools.

Sequoia's Greg McAdoo framed this as looking for behavior that demonstrates need before the product exists. The hierarchy of story credibility: your own need that drove the product (founders of Apple, Google, Yahoo built for themselves), then close personal contacts, then broader user research. Founder-need stories get extra attention because experienced investors know this pattern produces the most motivated builders. The specificity of the story — a named person, a concrete workaround — is what makes it stick.
