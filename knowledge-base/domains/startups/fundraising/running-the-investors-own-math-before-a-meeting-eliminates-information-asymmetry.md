Most founders treat valuation discussions as persuasion exercises—telling a compelling story and hoping for a high number. But VCs are running a deterministic model: exit value × probability ÷ required return = what they'll pay today. When founders understand this, negotiations shift from vibes to math.

Concretely, if a founder knows the investor targets 10x and comparable exits in the space are $150M, the implied pre-money at seed is calculable. Walking into a meeting with that math already done means the founder can proactively address the investor's return concerns, challenge unrealistic discount rates, or show how their specific exit path justifies a higher valuation. This collapses the information asymmetry that typically favors the investor.
