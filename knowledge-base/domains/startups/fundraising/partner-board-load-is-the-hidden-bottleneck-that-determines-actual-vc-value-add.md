Every investor promises value-add, but the real constraint is human bandwidth. A partner managing 8-10+ active board seats cannot provide deep strategic engagement to any single company. Studies show that active monitoring and governance correlate with better innovation and exit outcomes, but this requires actual time investment — monthly KPI reviews, quarterly strategy sessions, and timely talent search support.

Private VCs with tighter portfolios and direct carry incentives consistently show the highest monitoring activity, while public VCs with larger portfolios delegate oversight to external board members. Founders should ask specific questions during diligence: How many boards does my partner sit on? How often do they meet with portfolio founders? Who else supports those relationships? Then proactively propose a cadence (monthly KPIs, quarterly deep-dives) rather than waiting for ad-hoc engagement.
