The core investor fear at the early stage is that you've built something based on theory rather than real need. Abstract market statistics do nothing to address this fear — they describe the ceiling of a market that may never materialize for your product.

User stories directly counter the fear. If you can show that specific people are already doing something painful (paying too much, using 5 spreadsheets, manually doing what your product automates), you've demonstrated latent demand without needing a single projection. Sequoia's heuristic — 'proxy for demand' — captures this: observable behavior under constraint is the best leading indicator.

Founder-need stories (Apple, Google, Yahoo all started this way) are the strongest variant because they're credible by default. The founder lived the problem. That authenticity is hard to fake and experienced investors weight it heavily.
