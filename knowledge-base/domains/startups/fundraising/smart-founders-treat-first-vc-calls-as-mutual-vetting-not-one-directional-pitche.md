The highest-signal move a founder can make on a first VC call is to flip the script and ask evaluative questions back. Specifically, asking 'Can I speak to a founder you've backed?' is a power move that accomplishes multiple things: it shows confidence, demonstrates that you're evaluating investors as seriously as they're evaluating you, and reveals how the VC reacts under mild pressure.

Other high-value questions include asking about their thesis in your space (to gauge if you're a strategic bet or a one-off), and asking for specific examples of post-investment support (since every VC claims to be 'hands-on'). This mutual vetting mindset—treating the call as the start of a multi-year partnership rather than a transaction—consistently lands well with serious investors. It signals that you're choosing a partner, not just raising money, which is exactly the founder mentality VCs want to back.
