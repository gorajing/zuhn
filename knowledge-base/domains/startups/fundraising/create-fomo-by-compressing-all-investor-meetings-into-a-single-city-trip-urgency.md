With two payrolls left in the bank, Linq's CEO Elliot flew to San Francisco and compressed all investor activity into a 48-hour window. The results cascaded: two scheduled meetings became seven. Overnight emails brought new meeting requests. The biggest customer signed on the spot ('We're ready to buy'). A VC at Bain Capital said 'Can you come back later?' after one handshake — fully convinced.

The mechanism is simple: scarcity and time pressure. VCs see other VCs competing. Nobody examines the data room when there's urgency. They all start improving terms to avoid missing the deal. One investor even helped Elliot negotiate against himself because he wanted to be a long-term partner. The forcing function of a single day in a city is 'great — you can't waste any time, you don't worry about what to do, you just go do all the things.' Elliot went from no lead investor to receiving competing term sheets within days.
