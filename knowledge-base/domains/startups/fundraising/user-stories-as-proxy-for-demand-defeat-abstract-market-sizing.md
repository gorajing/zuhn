Greg McAdoo from Sequoia looks for what he calls the 'proxy for demand' — evidence that people are already doing something with inadequate tools that shows they need what you're making. This is far more convincing than top-down market sizing, which Graham compares to a new actor citing Tom Hanks's salary as evidence of career potential.

The strongest form of this is when the founders themselves are the users — Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, and Google all grew from founders' own needs. The next best is stories about people you know personally. These concrete, specific narratives stick in investors' minds and directly counter the existential risk that your product is built on untested a priori theories about what the world needs.
