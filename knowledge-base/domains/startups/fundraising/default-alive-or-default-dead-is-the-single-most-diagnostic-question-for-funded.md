Paul Graham argues that the single most important question for any startup past its first 8-9 months is whether it's 'default alive' — meaning it will reach profitability on its current trajectory without additional funding. Half of founders don't know the answer, which is itself a red flag.

The framework forces founders to separate facts from hopes. Rather than vaguely assuming things will work out, it makes you explicitly state 'we're default dead but counting on investors to save us' — a statement that should trigger alarm bells. Starting to ask this question too early is far less dangerous than starting too late, because late recognition leads to the 'fatal pinch': default dead + slow growth + not enough time to fix it.
