The 'pipeline myth' — that there aren't enough qualified women founders — persists despite women earning 27-35% of STEM degrees globally and female-only teams doubling their deal share over the past decade. The real bottleneck is a compounding structural loop: fewer exits → less investor confidence → fewer repeat women founders → fewer visible success stories → back to fewer exits.

European studies show female serial founders raise more funding and achieve better outcomes, but there simply aren't enough of them because earlier-stage hurdles and exit barriers filter them out. Meanwhile, roughly 75% of US VC firms have zero women partners, meaning inbound deal flow is blind to female entrepreneurs before they even reach the pitch stage. The fix isn't 'more women in STEM' — it's breaking the cycle at the network and access layer: gender-smart accelerators, intentional outreach sourcing, and follow-on support at Series A+ where women-led companies most often stall.
