The fundraising environment has shifted from growth-at-all-costs to capital efficiency as the primary evaluation lens. Burn multiple — calculated as net burn divided by net new ARR — gives investors a single number that captures how efficiently a startup converts cash into revenue growth. A multiple under 1x signals exceptional efficiency, while anything over 2x raises red flags regardless of growth rate. This represents a fundamental shift: a startup growing 3x year-over-year with a 3x burn multiple is now less fundable than one growing 1.5x with a 0.8x burn multiple. Founders who optimize only for growth without tracking burn efficiency are speaking a language investors no longer prioritize.
