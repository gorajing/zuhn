A VC who spoke at YC described investing in experienced founders whose business model he expected to change three times before they got it right. If veterans with millions in funding have a wrong business model, early-stage founders certainly will too.

The tactical implication is clear: time spent on business model details displaces time you could spend on your actual strengths — the problem you understand deeply and the product you've built. Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model actively damages recall — investors remember you as 'the company with the boneheaded money plan' instead of 'the company that solved that important problem.' Mention you've thought about monetization, then move on.
