Founders resist narrow descriptions because early-stage products have many possible futures. But as a description broadens toward 'could be anything,' its information content approaches zero. Investors hear phrases like 'collaboratively leverage the value of information' as meaningless boilerplate and mentally discard them.

The fix is to treat your pitch like incremental development: start with a gripping but perhaps overly narrow description ('easy web-based database'), then flesh it out. At every point you need a 'working description' in the investor's head — a concrete mental model they can evaluate. Your goal isn't to describe everything the product might become, but to be compelling enough to warrant a follow-up conversation.
