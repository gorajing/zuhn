A common founder mistake is treating valuation as the primary metric of a good fundraise. In reality, aggressive liquidation preferences (like 2x participating) can mean VCs take double their investment before anyone else gets paid in an exit. Board control and voting rights determine who makes key company decisions post-funding. A higher valuation can also set dangerous expectations that lead to painful down rounds later.

The best founders negotiate the full term sheet with a startup lawyer, understanding that the interplay between valuation, liquidation preferences, board composition, and anti-dilution provisions determines the actual economic outcome. A slightly lower valuation with clean 1x non-participating preferences and founder-friendly board structure often produces better outcomes than a headline number that comes with extractive terms.
