Investors and founders commonly evaluate returns against the initial investment amount, but most venture positions involve follow-on capital. A $500K seed check that becomes $2M total deployed across seed, Series A, and Series B pro-rata has a very different return profile than it appears when evaluated against just the initial check.

This layering effect means the actual multiple on total invested capital is often 3-4x lower than the multiple calculated on just the first check. It also means the risk profile evolves — early dollars are highest-risk/highest-return, while follow-on dollars at higher valuations carry lower return potential but also lower absolute risk.

The practical discipline is to always track and model cumulative capital at risk, including follow-on investments, interim distributions, and dividends, to get an accurate picture of position-level returns. This prevents the common cognitive bias of anchoring to the initial investment amount when the true exposure has grown substantially.
