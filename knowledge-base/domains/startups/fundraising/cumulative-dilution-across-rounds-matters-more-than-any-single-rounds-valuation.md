Most founders negotiate each funding round in isolation — celebrating a high Series A valuation without modeling how Series B and C dilution will compound on top. A founder who raises $1M at Seed, $5M at Series A, and $15M at Series B might end up with far less equity than expected because each round's dilution multiplies against the previous rounds.

The practical implication is that founders need a multi-round cap table model before their first raise. Understanding that a 20% dilution at Seed followed by 25% at Series A and 20% at Series B leaves you with roughly 48% of your original stake (not 35% as naive addition suggests, but the compound math still erodes significantly) changes how you negotiate each round. It also reveals why raising too much too early at inflated valuations can create down-round risk that's worse than taking slightly more dilution at a realistic valuation.
