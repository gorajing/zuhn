The article identifies six binary disqualifiers for VC funding: capital-intensive low-scalability models (gyms, consulting), sub-$1B addressable markets, non-standard legal structures, founders holding under 70% equity post-seed, zero traction or track record, and founder temperament misaligned with VC's growth expectations. The critical insight is that these are structural, not presentational — no amount of pitch deck polish overcomes them. This means the highest-ROI pre-fundraising activity is honest self-assessment against these criteria, not pitch refinement. Founders who fail multiple criteria should redirect energy toward alternative funding (revenue, grants, angels) rather than optimizing cold outreach to VCs.
