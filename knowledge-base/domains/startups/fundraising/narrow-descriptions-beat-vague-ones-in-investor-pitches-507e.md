Founders resist narrow descriptions because their product could become so much more, but as you approach a description of 'anything,' the information content approaches zero. Investors will discard sentences like 'a system to collaboratively leverage information value' and wait impatiently for you to say what you actually built.

The fix is to treat your pitch like incremental development: start with a gripping but perhaps overly narrow description, then flesh it out. At every point you should have a 'working description' in the investor's head — a concrete mental model they can evaluate and remember. Your goal isn't to describe everything your system might become, but to convince investors you're worth a second conversation.
