Graham cites a VC who invested in a company despite knowing their business model was 'crap' — because at the early stage, business models are guesses about domains that aren't the founders' expertise. Spending scarce pitch minutes on guesswork crowds out the things founders actually know well: the problem and the solution.

Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model becomes the memorable thing about your company. Investors will remember 'the company with the boneheaded monetization plan' instead of 'the company that solved that important problem.' The opportunity cost of business model slides is measured in lost credibility on your actual strengths.
