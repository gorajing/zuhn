Analysis of a $13B acquisition highlights that outsized exits are driven not just by product quality but by the defensibility of go-to-market strategy and revenue scaling mechanics. Companies that build compounding distribution advantages—through network effects, deep integrations, or high switching costs—create acquisition premiums that pure-technology plays cannot match.

This has practical implications for founders: investing early in GTM defensibility (customer lock-in, data moats, ecosystem positioning) often yields better exit outcomes than continuous product innovation alone. For investors, evaluating the sustainability and scalability of revenue engines should weigh as heavily as technical due diligence in investment decisions.
