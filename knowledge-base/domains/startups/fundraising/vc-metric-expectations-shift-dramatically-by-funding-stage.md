Pre-seed and seed investors prioritize TAM, early retention, and CAC as proxies for product-market fit potential. By Series A, the bar shifts to ARR ($3-5M+), Net Dollar Retention above 100%, and CAC payback under 12 months — proving the growth model is repeatable. Series B+ investors focus on profitability signals: gross margins (70%+ for SaaS), sustainable YoY revenue growth exceeding 100%, and disciplined burn rates.

This progression means founders must consciously evolve their dashboards and optimization focus as they advance. A seed-stage founder obsessing over gross margin is misallocating attention, just as a Series B founder who can't articulate burn rate efficiency will lose investor confidence. The practical implication is to build your metric stack forward — instrument the next stage's KPIs before you need them, so you have trend data ready when those conversations begin.
