Net Revenue Retention is uniquely valuable as a valuation input because it simultaneously communicates pricing power, customer satisfaction, and product stickiness — information that growth rate alone cannot convey. A company growing 50% through new customer acquisition with 85% NRR tells a fundamentally different story than one growing 50% with 115% NRR.

Best-in-class companies in the $1M-$10M ARR range consistently land between 100-110% NRR. This benchmark matters because NRR below 100% signals quiet contraction that buyers will heavily discount, while NRR above 110% signals organic expansion that compounds without additional sales cost. Founders should note that NRR calculation errors are common and buyers' diligence models will correct them — meaning inflated or deflated NRR gets caught, making accurate self-assessment critical.
