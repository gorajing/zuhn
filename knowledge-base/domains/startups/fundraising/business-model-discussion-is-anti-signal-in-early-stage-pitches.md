A VC speaking at YC described investing in experienced founders whose business model he knew was wrong—and expected it to change three times. Smart investors at the early stage aren't evaluating your revenue projections; they're evaluating whether you're solving an important problem and can build.

Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model creates negative anchoring. Investors will remember you as 'the company with the boneheaded monetization plan' rather than 'the company that solved that important problem.' The business model is guesswork outside your expertise; the problem and product are where you sound smartest.
