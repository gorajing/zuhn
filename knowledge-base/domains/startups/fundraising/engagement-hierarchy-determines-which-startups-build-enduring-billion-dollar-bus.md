Greylock Partners' framework on engagement as the key to building billion-dollar businesses presents a hierarchical model where companies must progress users through escalating levels of engagement. This isn't just about DAU/MAU ratios — it's about creating compounding loops where deeper engagement generates network effects and switching costs.

The hierarchy suggests that many startups fail not because they can't acquire users, but because they optimize for the wrong level of engagement. A startup focused on top-of-funnel metrics without a clear path to deeper commitment will hit a growth ceiling. The most enduring companies — the ones that reach and sustain billion-dollar valuations — are those that architect their product to pull users through each level of the hierarchy, creating self-reinforcing engagement loops that competitors cannot easily replicate.
