Most founders either give the exact same pitch to every investor or try to completely reshape their story per meeting. Both approaches fail. The optimal strategy is keeping your core narrative intact while shifting emphasis to match each VC's known priorities — their portfolio patterns, public writing, and stated thesis.

This requires pre-call research: check the firm's portfolio, the specific partner's background, deals they've led, and content they've published. If a VC has written about retention mattering more than growth and your product has strong retention metrics, lead with that. If they bet on technical founders, don't downplay your product depth. The pitch stays honest but the spotlight moves to what this particular investor is primed to value.
