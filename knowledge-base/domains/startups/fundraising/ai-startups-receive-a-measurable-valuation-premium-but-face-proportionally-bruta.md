AI startups captured 41.7% of all US seed-stage investment in 2025, with median seed valuations of $19M compared to $15M for the broader market — a 27% premium. This creates a two-sided dynamic: AI founders get higher valuations but compete against a massive concentration of similarly positioned companies for VC attention.

For non-AI founders, the challenge has inverted. The default VC question is no longer 'how big is your market?' but 'why can't AI just do this?' — forcing every non-AI startup to articulate a defensibility thesis against AI substitution. Meanwhile, AI founders face a different problem: differentiation within a crowded category where 'we use AI' is table stakes. The strongest answer to 'what's your moat?' now requires proprietary data, network effects, or switching costs — not 'great UX' or 'better prompts.'
