Static unit economics give investors a snapshot, but monthly trend data tells a story. When CLTV rises faster than CAC over consecutive months, it signals compounding efficiency — often indicating product-channel fit or pricing leverage. This accelerating ratio is the kind of trend line VCs use to value startups.

For Series A preparation, a chart-ready narrative showing monthly ratio improvement demonstrates both operational rigor and growth sustainability. It also surfaces problems early: if CAC is creeping up or retention is slipping, monthly tracking catches it before it becomes a fundraising liability. The discipline of monthly tracking itself signals to investors that a founder has command over their metrics rather than cherry-picking favorable quarters.
