The core economic trade-off of venture debt is paying interest (9-14% floating rate) plus small warrant coverage (0.5-2% equity) instead of selling 15-25% of the company in a priced round. For a startup that can reliably service the debt, this math is overwhelmingly favorable. A company raising $5M in venture debt at 1% warrant coverage gives up roughly 10-20x less equity than raising the same amount through a Series A.

However, this comparison only holds when the startup can actually repay. The hidden cost is the fixed obligation — interest payments during the interest-only period (6-18 months) followed by full amortization. If growth stalls or the next round is delayed, those fixed payments drain runway faster than equity would have. The optimal use case is 20-40% of the last equity round, deployed toward initiatives with clear ROI rather than general survival runway.
