Standard Capital's model asks founders to name their own Series A valuation and then refuses to negotiate. Founders who matched their valuation to actual traction (high traction = higher acceptable valuation, lower traction = lower valuation) were the ones who got funded. Those who set unrealistic valuations based on competitor rounds or market hype were simply rejected without a counteroffer.

This approach doubles as a signal-filtering mechanism. Founders who hadn't thought carefully about valuation often admitted they were just copying numbers they'd heard from other rounds. The model reveals that at a given valuation, the bar scales linearly -- a company asking for 120M post needs to look twice as good as one asking for 60M post. Many founders were shocked that Standard would reject rather than negotiate, revealing how deeply embedded the negotiation expectation is in VC culture.
