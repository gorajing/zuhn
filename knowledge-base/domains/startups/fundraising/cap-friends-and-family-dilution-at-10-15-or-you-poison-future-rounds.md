When founders give away 20% for $100K, they anchor their valuation at $500K. If they haven't achieved significant traction by their next raise, they face a brutal dilemma: raise at a modest increase (giving VCs little upside incentive) or accept a down round (signaling failure). Either outcome damages fundraising momentum.

The 10-15% cap is a practical ceiling that preserves enough ownership flexibility for pre-seed and seed rounds while still giving early backers meaningful upside. This constraint also forces founders to be disciplined about how much they raise—tying the amount directly to specific milestones rather than vague runway extension. Equity is the most expensive currency a founder has, and its price only increases over time; spending it conservatively early compounds into massive ownership preservation later.
