Photosynthesis is remarkably inefficient — only about 3-6% of incident solar energy ends up stored in glucose. The electron transport chain, Calvin Cycle, and multiple intermediate energy carriers (ATP, NADPH) each extract a toll. Yet this multi-step approach exists because converting a photon directly into a sugar molecule is thermodynamically impossible in a single reaction.

This maps directly to startup value chains and fundraising pipelines. Each conversion step (impression → click → signup → activation → revenue) loses most of the input, but the full transformation from 'stranger' to 'paying customer' requires the intermediate steps. Trying to skip steps (asking for purchase on first contact) is like trying to convert photons directly to glucose — thermodynamically infeasible. The insight is to optimize each step's conversion rate rather than lamenting the overall funnel loss.
