VCs process dozens of pitches and form snap judgments early. The founder story — why you specifically are building this, what lived experience gave you the insight, how close you've been to the problem — is the single most important element because it's evaluated first and colors everything after.

This means the ROI on rehearsing your opening is dramatically higher than polishing slide 12. Frame your journey so the insight feels obvious and inevitable. If the problem comes from lived experience or a previous role gave you a front-row seat to the market gap, lead with that. Investors want to know where conviction comes from. Practice the first 4-5 slides (problem, solution, traction, market, model) until transitions are smooth and you can handle interruptions without losing your thread.
