SAFEs and convertible notes are deceptively simple instruments. They don't appear on the cap table as equity until conversion, which makes it easy for founders to accumulate them without modeling their downstream impact. When a priced round triggers conversion—typically at a discounted or capped valuation—the resulting dilution can shock founders who assumed their ownership percentage was stable.

The fix is proactive modeling: before signing any SAFE or note, founders should run a fully diluted scenario showing what ownership looks like post-conversion at various Series A valuations. This means treating unconverted instruments as if they've already converted when making equity decisions. Tools like Carta and Pulley automate this, but even a basic spreadsheet model that includes pending SAFEs in the fully diluted count prevents the most painful surprises.
