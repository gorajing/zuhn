At each funding round, investors typically request an option pool refresh to ensure the company can attract talent through the next growth phase. The standard ask is 15-20% of fully diluted shares. What many founders miss is that this pool expansion is usually carved out pre-money, meaning existing shareholders (primarily founders) absorb the dilution rather than sharing it with new investors.

This is a meaningful negotiation point. A 15% option pool refresh on a pre-money basis at Series A can cost founders several percentage points of ownership compared to a post-money expansion. Sophisticated founders negotiate the pool size based on a bottoms-up hiring plan rather than accepting an arbitrary percentage. If you can demonstrate that you only need a 10% pool to cover 18 months of planned hires, you preserve meaningful founder equity. The key is arriving at the negotiation with a detailed hiring forecast that justifies a smaller pool.
