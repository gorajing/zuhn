Y Combinator's 2018 SAFE update introduced post-money valuation caps as the default, replacing pre-money caps. Under a pre-money cap, the valuation excludes the capital being raised, meaning investors don't know their final ownership until the round closes. Post-money caps include all SAFEs and convertible instruments, so investors know exactly how much equity they'll receive regardless of additional funding.

This shift benefits investors with transparency but increases founder dilution risk. Every additional SAFE dollar raised under a post-money cap directly dilutes the founder's slice rather than expanding the pie. Founders must understand this distinction because most standard SAFE templates now default to post-money caps, and failing to negotiate this point means accepting the more founder-unfriendly structure without realizing it.
