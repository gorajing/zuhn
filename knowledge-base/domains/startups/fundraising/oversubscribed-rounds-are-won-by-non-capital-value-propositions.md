The article illustrates that in competitive YC deals co-invested with Lightspeed, a16z, First Round, and Peak XV, small angel investors earned allocations not through capital but through distribution capability. One reader committed to co-investing for 24 months specifically because of the media value demonstrated through a portfolio company launch.

This confirms the power dynamic has inverted in hot markets: founders are selecting investors, not the reverse. The implication for anyone seeking allocation in top-tier deals is that they must develop a clear, demonstrable non-capital value proposition. This could be distribution (media reach), technical expertise, customer introductions, or operational support — but it must be concrete and measurable. Vague claims of 'adding value' are insufficient; the VC Corner team succeeds because they can point to millions of views generated for specific portfolio companies.
