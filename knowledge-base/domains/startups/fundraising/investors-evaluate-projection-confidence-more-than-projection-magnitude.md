The article notes that 'investors don't just look at how fast you're growing — they look at how confidently you can repeat it.' This reframes what a projection model is actually for in a fundraising context: it's not a prediction tool, it's a credibility tool. The founder who says 'here's our baseline trajectory, and here's what changes if churn improves by one point' demonstrates mechanical understanding of their business.

This matters because VCs see hundreds of hockey-stick projections. What differentiates a fundable deck is the founder's ability to decompose growth into its component drivers and show sensitivity to each one. A five-input model makes this trivially easy — you can walk an investor through three scenarios in five minutes and show exactly which lever drives the most variance. That analytical fluency signals operational maturity far more than the absolute numbers on the slide.
