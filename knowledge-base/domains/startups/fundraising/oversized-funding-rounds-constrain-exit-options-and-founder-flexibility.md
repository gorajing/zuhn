When VCs invest at high valuations, they create a floor below which no exit is acceptable to them — even if founders would happily sell at that price. Graham's competitor raised $20M while his company spent only $2M total; the competitor couldn't accept a $50M acquisition that made Graham's team wealthy because their investors would have lost money at that valuation.

The practical lesson is that capital efficiency preserves optionality. Every dollar of unnecessary funding raises the bar for what counts as a successful outcome. Founders who raise only what they need retain the ability to take mid-range exits, pivot cheaply, or simply operate longer without pressure. The instinct to raise as much as possible is often the precise mistake that eliminates the most likely paths to founder success.
