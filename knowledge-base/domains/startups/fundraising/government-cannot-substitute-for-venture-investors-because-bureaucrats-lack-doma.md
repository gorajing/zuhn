Graham identifies two critical qualities of effective startup investors: (a) deep personal experience in technology business that helps them pick winners and provide useful advice, and (b) personal financial stake that ensures genuine attention and engagement. Bureaucrats are structurally the opposite on both dimensions—they lack domain expertise and bear no personal financial risk from bad investments.

This isn't just a talent problem but a structural one. Even large corporations with in-house VC groups typically require a reputable private VC to lead deals, acknowledging that institutional decision-making is fundamentally misaligned with the risk-taking and rapid judgment required in startup investing. The implication is that government programs should focus on creating conditions that attract private investors rather than trying to replace them.
