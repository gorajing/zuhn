Most VC funds deploy capital over a 2-3 year investment period after raising, then shift to reserving capital for follow-ons in existing portfolio companies or begin raising a new fund. This means a fund with a 2019 or 2020 vintage is effectively closed to new investments by 2025, regardless of the firm's brand or stated thesis.

Founders routinely waste weeks cold-emailing VCs who are simply out of dry powder. Checking fund vintage before outreach is a trivially easy filter that eliminates a large fraction of dead-end contacts. Public sources like Crunchbase, PitchBook, and fund announcements in press make vintage verifiable in minutes.

The practical rule: unless a fund raised within the last three years or has publicly announced a new vehicle, treat them as unable to invest and move on. This single filter dramatically improves outreach efficiency.
