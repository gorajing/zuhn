Large-scale data on founder ownership across VC-backed companies shows that equity dilution follows predictable trajectories rather than being random. Founders who don't model their ownership path before raising often end up with surprisingly little equity by Series B or C, even in successful companies. This makes proactive cap table planning and understanding standard dilution benchmarks a critical founder skill, not a nice-to-have.

The implication is that founders should treat equity retention as a first-class strategic variable alongside valuation. Knowing the typical ownership percentages at each stage helps founders negotiate from data rather than gut feel, and decide when alternative funding mechanisms (revenue-based financing, strategic rounds) might preserve more long-term value.
