The venture capital industry's obsession with headline valuations and brand-name portfolio companies obscures the fundamental math that drives LP returns: ownership percentage at exit. A 1% stake in a $10B company returns $100M — impressive in isolation, but potentially insufficient to return a large fund. Meanwhile, a 15% stake in a $700M exit returns the same $105M and likely represents a far more capital-efficient bet.

This insight has direct implications for both fund managers and founders. For VCs, it argues for concentrated portfolios with meaningful ownership rather than spray-and-pray approaches that dilute across dozens of small checks. For founders, understanding that sophisticated investors think in ownership terms — not valuation terms — reframes negotiation strategy. The best investors aren't those who offer the highest valuation; they're those whose ownership-driven incentives align them most closely with the founder's success.
