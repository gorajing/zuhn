High valuations create a trap that goes beyond the commonly discussed difficulty of raising the next round. Liquidation preferences, participating preferred stock, and ratchets pile up from big early rounds, crushing exit flexibility. A modest or even substantial acquisition might return nothing to the founding team after preferences are paid out.

This creates the 'zombie startup' phenomenon — companies that are alive on paper but dead in spirit, where employees lose motivation after calculating that their equity is underwater, and founders feel cornered with no viable path forward. The article argues you've essentially 'priced yourself out of viable exits,' making the cap table structure as dangerous as the valuation number itself. Founders should insist on clean preferences and understand exactly what they're trading when accepting investor-friendly terms.
