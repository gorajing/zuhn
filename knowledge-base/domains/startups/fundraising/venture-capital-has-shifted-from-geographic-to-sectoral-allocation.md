Startups in upstate New York — far from traditional tech corridors — have raised from over 75 different investors across the U.S. and globally, spanning Boston, San Francisco, Los Angeles, Ireland, and Saudi Arabia. The capital flowed because the ideas were strong and the pitch was tight, not because of proximity.

Modern async tools (Zoom, Notion, Loom, Slack) have collapsed the information asymmetry that once made in-person meetings essential for investor conviction. A well-crafted memo or recorded demo now travels faster than a cross-country flight. This means fundraising strategy should optimize for narrative clarity and investor-market fit rather than geographic proximity. The remaining geographic bias in VC is rapidly shrinking as remote deal-making becomes the norm rather than the exception.
