Dalton and Michael note a counterintuitive pattern: companies make the most progress when runway is low, because that's the moment founders stop doing half-measures and get radical. Zombies typically have years of runway, which is precisely the condition under which incrementalism wins over radical change. The overfunded unicorn cohort is disproportionately represented in the zombie population. This inverts the common assumption that more capital is safer — beyond a threshold, excess capital removes the pressure that would force good decisions and locks in bad product assumptions under a veneer of optionality.
