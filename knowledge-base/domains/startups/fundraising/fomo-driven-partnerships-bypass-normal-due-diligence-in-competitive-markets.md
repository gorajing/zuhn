Both Walgreens and Safeway entered partnerships with Theranos despite having only a two-page Johns Hopkins summary (which described a meeting, not actual device testing) as proof the technology worked. Walgreens' internal team raised concerns, but executives overrode them because they feared competitors would secure the deal. This pattern — where competitive anxiety creates urgency that short-circuits verification — is especially dangerous in markets where incumbents face potential disruption. The antidote is requiring independent technical validation proportional to the claim's magnitude, regardless of competitive pressure.
