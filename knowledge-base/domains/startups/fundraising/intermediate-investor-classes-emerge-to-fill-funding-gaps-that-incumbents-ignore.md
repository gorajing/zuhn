For decades, angels invested $20-50k each (totaling ~$200k rounds) while VCs invested $1-5M in series A rounds. The gap between $200k and $1M was exactly where most YC startups needed funding (~$400k). Super-angels filled this gap by pooling outside capital like VCs but making fast decisions like angels, typically investing ~$100k each. This pattern—new financial intermediaries emerging to serve ignored market segments—repeats across industries whenever incumbents leave a structural gap. Founders should watch for similar gaps in adjacent services (legal, recruiting, infrastructure) as signals of where new startup-friendly players will appear.
