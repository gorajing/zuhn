Fundraising cycles have roughly doubled since 2021, from 3-4 months to 6-9 months on average. More critically, the nature of diligence has deepened — what used to take a week now takes 1-2 months, covering org structure, security practices, customer contracts, and founder personal backgrounds. Term sheets that seemed solid fall through after deep diligence reveals issues.

This has practical implications for runway planning: founders need to start fundraising much earlier or maintain more runway buffer. The median time between Seed and Series A also grew 30%+ since 2024. Combined with median seed rounds hitting $4M at $20M post-money, the expectations attached to that capital are substantial. Preparation quality — complete data rooms, practiced answers to the 40 standard questions, clean cap tables — now separates founders who close in 6 weeks from those who stall for 9 months.
