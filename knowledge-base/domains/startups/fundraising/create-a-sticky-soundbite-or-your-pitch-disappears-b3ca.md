Graham frames memorability as a filtering problem: investors hear so many pitches they all blur together, so the first cut is simply being remembered. The mechanism is a compressed descriptive phrase — 'the x of y' pattern — launched apparently casually near the beginning of your talk. Viaweb's was 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce.'

But Graham adds a deeper insight: if you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite exercise isn't just marketing — it's a diagnostic tool for strategic clarity. A startup that can't be described memorably in one phrase may be trying to do too many things, which is itself a red flag for investors and founders alike.
