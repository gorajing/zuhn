Silence after a VC intro call is the most common outcome, not rejection. VCs juggle dozens of meetings and interest fades for reasons unrelated to your startup. The instinct to chase or send frequent check-ins signals desperation and actually decreases your chances.

The effective pattern is: send a thank-you within 24 hours, then wait 3-4 weeks before sending a short milestone-driven update ('We just crossed 10K MAUs and hired a senior product lead — let me know if you'd like to reconnect'). This approach works because it demonstrates continued execution rather than continued neediness. Add unresponsive VCs to your investor update list and let compounding progress do the convincing. Many eventual investments come from VCs who initially passed but were won over by consistent milestone updates over months.
