Silicon Valley handshake deals are notoriously vague and prone to failure because verbal commitments lack specificity about terms, timing, and follow-through. YC's Handshake Deal Protocol addresses this by creating a structured framework that transforms informal agreements into clear, actionable steps.

This matters for founders because misunderstood handshake deals waste time and damage relationships. The protocol doesn't eliminate informality — it channels it into a format where both sides know exactly what was agreed. For early-stage founders especially, having a recognized protocol to reference reduces the power asymmetry in investor conversations.
