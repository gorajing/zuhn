Q3 2025 venture data reveals an interesting divergence: humanoid robotics leads in number of deals while AI leads in total dollar volume. This split reflects fundamentally different capital deployment strategies — robotics investors are making many smaller bets across the category expecting high variance in who cracks manufacturing and go-to-market, while AI investors concentrate massive capital in proven winners.

This pattern has implications for portfolio construction and sector timing. Hardware categories like robotics typically see broad early-stage experimentation before rapid consolidation, while software AI is already in the consolidation phase with a few dominant players absorbing most capital. Founders in robotics have more funding entry points but face hardware-specific risks; AI founders face a winner-take-most dynamic where being second-best may not attract follow-on capital.
