Every founder wants to raise at the highest possible valuation, but without modeling how that valuation plays out for investors — through ownership dilution, future funding requirements, and realistic exit scenarios — high pricing becomes a trap. An inflated Series A valuation that doesn't map to believable fund-level returns creates pressure for a down round when the next raise comes.

The fix is simple but rarely practiced: before entering a pricing conversation, founders should model what their raise amount implies for investor MOIC at different exit scenarios, including a base case with the exit multiple cut by 30%. This creates defensible valuations — the kind that hold up in partner meetings — rather than aspirational ones that collapse at the next market correction.
