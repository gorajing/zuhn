The article identifies a specific failure pattern: deals that survive business diligence but collapse during confirmatory legal review. Cap table confusion, unreviewed SAFE/note terms, unclear option pools, missing C-Corp verification, legacy shareholder issues, and ambiguous convertible notes are cited as the primary culprits. These are all fixable problems that become unfixable once a term sheet creates time pressure.

The practical implication is that founders should treat legal housekeeping as fundraising preparation, not post-term-sheet cleanup. Verifying C-Corp status, reviewing all classes of stock, cleaning up legacy shareholder issues, and ensuring convertible notes have clear terms should happen before the first pitch meeting, not during confirmatory diligence when discovering problems creates maximum damage to deal momentum and investor confidence.
