Horowitz: 'the mistake people make in fundraising is they go what does Ben want to hear — does he want to see a graph like this, does he want to know about market size — that's very hard to figure out and you usually get it wrong.' The correct question is 'how would I convince myself to join or invest in this company — let me make that argument.' Why: the argument that convinces you is also the argument you won't get talked out of in a pitch. The worst thing that can happen in fundraising is you get talked out of your idea — 'why the hell aren't you doing this? Oh, I hadn't thought of that. Well, don't do that.' This also doubles as partner selection — you want investors who want to invest in what you're actually doing, not the performance you put on.
