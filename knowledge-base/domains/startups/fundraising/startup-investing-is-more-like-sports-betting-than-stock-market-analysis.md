Some founders expect startup capital allocation to work like public markets, where companies have P/E ratios and can be valued through quantitative analysis. Dalton points out this expectation breaks completely at the early stage: 'Where are the value investors in startups?' Finding the stuff with the lowest valuation and highest revenue is a demonstrably bad way to invest in startups.

Instead, startup investing is more like a sports bet -- soon the game will be played, most bets will lose, and some will win big. It's closer to the NFL draft than a stock trade. You can question why a team drafted a certain player, but ultimately you're a person on a comment board, not someone whose job is on the line to make good decisions. This reframe helps founders stop expecting 'fair' allocation and start focusing on what they can control.
