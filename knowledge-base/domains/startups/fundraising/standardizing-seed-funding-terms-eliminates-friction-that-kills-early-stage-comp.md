Paul Graham describes pre-YC seed funding as 'very haphazard' — founders got money from a friend's rich uncle with deal terms that were 'often a disaster.' Neither investors, founders, nor lawyers knew what documents should look like, as evidenced by Facebook's early history as a Florida LLC.

YC's core initial insight was becoming 'a standard source of seed funding' — not just providing capital but providing a repeatable, known-good structure. This matters because bad terms create landmines that explode later (equity disputes, control issues, unclear rights), and the friction of negotiating bespoke deals from scratch deterred many potential angel investments from happening at all. The lesson extends beyond venture capital: any market with high transaction costs and information asymmetry benefits enormously from standardization.
