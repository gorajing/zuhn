Graham identifies the core investor anxiety at early stage: that founders built something based on a priori theories rather than actual need. Specific user stories are the antidote. Sequoia calls this 'proxy for demand' — evidence of what people do today with inadequate tools that proves they need what you're making.

The hierarchy of story strength matters: your own need is strongest (Apple, Microsoft, Google all grew from founder needs), followed by needs of people you know personally. Abstract market-size arguments ('$5B TAM') are weakest. This inverts the typical pitch deck structure where TAM slides come early. The insight is that investors pattern-match on founder-problem authenticity, not market arithmetic.
