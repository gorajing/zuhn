Early-stage traction is inherently limited, so VCs are evaluating two things simultaneously: the validation signal (does the market want this?) and the velocity signal (how fast are you learning and moving?). But there's a third, often overlooked dimension: the quality of your traction narrative.

Founders who flood conversations with vanity metrics or present numbers without context actually undermine their credibility. One strong KPI—clearly explained with context about what it means and where it's heading—is more powerful than a scattered dashboard of half-truths. Pre-revenue founders can still win this question by talking about what they've validated: an MVP getting strong feedback, users returning without prompts, or waitlist conversion rates. The key is confidence and precision in how you present whatever signal you have, combined with intellectual honesty about what you haven't yet proven.
