Founders resist narrow descriptions because their product could become so much more. But as Paul Graham points out, as you approach a description of something that could be anything, the information content approaches zero. Investors will discard vague statements like 'collaboratively leverage the value of information' as meaningless boilerplate.

The better approach is successive approximation: start with a gripping but perhaps overly narrow description, then flesh it out. Like incremental development, maintain a 'working description' in the investor's head at every point. The goal isn't to describe everything the system might become — it's to convince investors you're worth talking to further.
