The article notes that convincing investors to back stealth ventures 'requires strong networks and compelling storytelling' since these companies can't rely on media attention. This fundamentally changes the fundraising dynamic — the evaluation shifts from traction-based to trust-based.

This creates a structural advantage for repeat founders and those with deep investor networks, while disadvantaging first-time founders who most need stealth protection for novel ideas. The implication is that stealth mode is paradoxically most accessible to founders who least need it (experienced operators with networks) and least accessible to those who might benefit most (first-timers with truly novel, easily-copied ideas).
