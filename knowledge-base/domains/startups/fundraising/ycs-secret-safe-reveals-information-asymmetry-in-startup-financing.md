The existence of a 'secret' dimension to YC's SAFE instrument highlights how standardized startup financing documents can contain subtle provisions that sophisticated parties understand but first-time founders may miss. When an instrument becomes industry-standard, founders often sign without deep legal review, assuming the standard terms are uniformly fair. This creates a false sense of security—the very standardization that makes SAFEs accessible also makes their nuances easier to overlook. Founders should treat even standardized instruments as requiring careful legal analysis, particularly around conversion mechanics, pro-rata rights, and MFN clauses.
