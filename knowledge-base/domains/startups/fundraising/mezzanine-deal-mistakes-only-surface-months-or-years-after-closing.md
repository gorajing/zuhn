Each party in a mezzanine transaction tends to evaluate terms in isolation: founders focus on the cash interest rate, CFOs model runway without PIK compounding effects, and lenders quote target IRRs without stress-testing how fees, repayment structure, and equity upside interact under different scenarios. The result is that everyone believes they negotiated well, but the actual economics only become clear when the deal resolves.

This is a specific instance of a broader pattern in complex financial instruments: when multiple variables compound and interact over time, human intuition consistently fails to predict outcomes. The fix is end-to-end cash-flow modeling that forces all assumptions into the open and shows how changing any single variable propagates through the entire structure. This principle extends beyond mezzanine to any multi-variable financial commitment where terms interact nonlinearly.
