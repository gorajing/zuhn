September 2025 marked the strongest IPO wave since 2021, but with a fundamentally different character. The companies successfully going public — Klarna, StubHub, Netskope — share common traits: proven scale, paths to profitability, and realistic valuations relative to their 2021 peaks.

This signals a durable shift in what public markets will accept. The 2021-era playbook of IPO-ing on revenue growth alone is dead. Founders building toward an exit need to internalize that the IPO window is now a profitability filter, not just a growth showcase. Companies that raised at inflated private valuations without corresponding unit economics will face painful down-rounds or remain private indefinitely.
