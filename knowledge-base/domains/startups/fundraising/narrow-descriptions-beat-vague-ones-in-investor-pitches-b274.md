As startup descriptions approach generality — 'a system to allow people to collaboratively leverage the value of information' — their information content approaches zero. Investors discard such sentences as boilerplate and grow impatient waiting for the actual explanation.

The better approach is successive approximation: start with a description that's gripping but perhaps overly narrow, then flesh it out. This mirrors incremental development — at every point you have a 'working description' in the investor's head. A web-based database is concrete enough to evaluate; a collaborative information leverage platform is not. The goal isn't to describe everything your system might become, but to convince investors you're worth a second conversation.
