At early stages, valuation multiples and peer comparisons provide sufficient justification for fundraising. But as startups mature into growth rounds, pre-IPO, or M&A, professional investors demand intrinsic valuation grounded in actual future performance. A DCF model forces founders to defend assumptions about revenue trajectories, margins, and capital efficiency rather than hiding behind narrative.

This transition marks a fundamental shift in the founder-investor dynamic: storytelling yields to financial modeling. Founders who cannot articulate their valuation through the lens of discounted future cash flows will either accept lower valuations or lose deals to competitors who can. The DCF framework also serves as an internal tool for scenario planning and understanding how operational levers like CAC, churn, and CAPEX affect long-term enterprise value.
