A VC who spoke at YC described investing in experienced repeat founders whose business model he expected to change three times before they got it right — and he invested anyway. The business model at seed stage is a collection of guesses about areas where founders typically lack expertise. Dwelling on it displaces discussion of what you actually know: the problem space and what you've built.

Worse, a spectacularly wrong business model becomes the thing investors remember about you, crowding out the genuinely impressive parts of your presentation. The mere mention of revenue planning shows you've thought about it; deep elaboration suggests you don't understand what matters at your stage. Smart investors are pattern-matching for problem-solving ability and technical insight, not spreadsheet projections.
