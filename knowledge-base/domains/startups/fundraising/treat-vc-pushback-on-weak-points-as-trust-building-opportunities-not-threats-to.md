VCs deliberately stress-test the weakest parts of your business — that's their job. Many founders instinctively get defensive or try to spin problems into strengths, which backfires because investors pattern-match this to founders who can't face reality.

The counter-intuitive move is to lean into vulnerability with structure: 'That's something we're still figuring out — here's how we're thinking about that risk and what we're doing to de-risk it.' This signals self-awareness, intellectual honesty, and operational maturity. VCs aren't expecting perfection at the intro stage; they're evaluating whether you have the clarity and temperament to navigate inevitable challenges. A founder who can calmly discuss risks is far more investable than one who pretends risks don't exist.
