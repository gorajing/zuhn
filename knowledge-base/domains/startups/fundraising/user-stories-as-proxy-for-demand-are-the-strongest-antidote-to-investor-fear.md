Sequoia's Greg Mcadoo calls it the 'proxy for demand' — evidence that people are currently using inadequate tools to solve the problem you're addressing. This is more convincing than TAM slides because it demonstrates pull rather than projected push. The hierarchy of user stories is clear: your own need (Apple, Microsoft, Google all started this way), then needs of people you know personally, then observed behavior of strangers.

The underlying investor psychology is that early-stage evaluation is primarily about risk elimination, not upside calculation. The existential risk is building something nobody wants. A single vivid story about a specific user struggling with a specific problem does more to eliminate that fear than any amount of market research, because it's concrete, verifiable, and shows you understand the problem domain from the inside.
