Professional investors experience pitch fatigue—after enough presentations, they blur together. The first filter is simply being remembered, and the mechanism for that is a descriptive phrase that sticks. In Hollywood it's 'x meets y'; in startups it's 'the x of y' or 'the x y,' like Viaweb's 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce.'

Graham adds a meta-insight: the exercise of compressing your startup into one compelling phrase is diagnostic. If you genuinely cannot do it, the problem isn't your communication skills—it's that your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite forces strategic clarity because a muddled strategy cannot produce a crisp phrase. This makes it both a communication tool and a strategy-testing tool.
