Most leaders assume they hear about the most important conflicts in their organization. In practice, org structure heavily filters which conflicts become visible. Siloed teams suppress cross-functional tensions. Hierarchies with too many layers dampen signals before they reach decision-makers. Cultures that punish disagreement create silence around the most consequential problems.

The actionable inversion: instead of asking 'how do we resolve conflicts,' ask 'what conflicts should we be hearing that we aren't?' Flat structures, cross-functional ownership, and explicit escalation norms are all design choices that shape which tensions surface. A well-designed org ensures that the conflicts that threaten the company's core mission are the ones that reach leadership fastest.
