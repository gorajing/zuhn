The dominant approach to scaling technical companies imports an organizational template (often from Google, Meta, or enterprise SaaS) and then fills the roles with candidates. This approach optimizes for predictability and legibility to external observers (VCs, enterprise buyers, press). It does not optimize for output. The counter-pattern observed at NVIDIA, SpaceX, Tesla, xAI, and Groq: start with a few people of extreme technical mastery and let organizational structure emerge around the specific shape of what they're building. This produces unusual org topologies (Jensen's 80 direct reports, Elon's multi-company threading) that look chaotic to conventional management thinking but consistently outperform on technical throughput. The implication for a technical founder: resist pressure to hire conventional VPs early. Resist pressure to adopt standard reporting structures. Every org decision should be justifiable in terms of what it enables technically, not what it matches externally.
