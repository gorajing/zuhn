The best-performing modern technical companies deviate sharply from conventional org charts. Jensen Huang at NVIDIA runs with 60-80 direct reports organized around project priorities rather than functional VPs. Elon Musk operates across 5-7 companies simultaneously on work that would consume an ordinary executive's entire day. Both patterns reject the standard VP-of-engineering/CTO/CFO/COO layering. The claim is that conventional org charts were never designed for efficiency — they were designed for promotion optics and legibility to enterprise buyers, reinforced by HR-consulting vendors with economic incentives to sell structure. At technical companies, those same structures accelerate the employees who maneuver and kiss rather than the ones who build. The practical implication: when designing a high-agency technical company, treat any step toward traditional layering as a debt to be justified rather than a default.
