Most organizational structures inadvertently filter out conflict information before it reaches senior leadership — managers smooth over disagreements, teams avoid escalating problems, and cultures punish conflict-surfacing. The result is that leaders make decisions based on sanitized information, often blind to serious tensions within their organizations. The more productive approach is to design explicitly for conflict visibility: creating escalation paths that don't penalize the messenger, structuring meetings to surface rather than resolve disagreements prematurely, and treating surfaced conflicts as valuable signals rather than organizational failures. Leaders who hear about conflicts early can intervene strategically; those who hear about them after they've exploded cannot.
