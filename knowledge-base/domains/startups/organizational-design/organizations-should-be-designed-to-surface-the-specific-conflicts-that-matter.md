Most organizations are implicitly structured to suppress conflict: hierarchy, silos, and social norms around harmony all push disagreement downward and inward. The result is that critical conflicts—misaligned incentives, product-market fit debates, technical risk disputes—get resolved invisibly at low levels or never get resolved at all, surfacing only as failures.

The reframe here is that organizational design should ask: which conflicts do we *want* to hear about at the leadership level? Customer dissatisfaction versus engineering timeline? Revenue targets versus product quality? Cross-functional priority disputes? Deliberately structuring escalation paths, review cadences, and incentive systems to make these specific conflicts visible is a leadership design choice—not a sign of dysfunction, but of maturity. Unhealthy organizations are not conflict-free; they're ones where the wrong conflicts surface.
