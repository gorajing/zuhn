Tenex pays engineers a flat base and then 'rounds up' each quarter based on the story points they completed and got accepted, mirroring how the firm itself bills clients per story point. The logic: AI decouples effort from output, so paying for time (which rewards slowness) or salary (which rewards showing up) no longer maps to the value created. Output-based pay makes the engineer a residual claimant on their own productivity gains — the faster and better they work with AI, the more they earn. The speaker is explicit that this is one model, not a universal prescription, and that it only works alongside a quality counterbalance and elite hiring. The transferable principle is broader than software: when a new technology radically changes how much output a unit of labor can produce, the compensation model built for the old output ceiling becomes a brake. He invites leaders in any function to ask how they'd pay people differently if output were no longer bounded by hours.
