SpaceX is deliberately private while Tesla is public—the difference reflects their missions. Tesla needs public capital markets to fund massive manufacturing scale-up, and its mission (accelerating sustainable energy) is compatible with profitability. SpaceX's mission (Mars colonization) requires spending enormous sums on projects with uncertain timelines and no near-term return, which public market shareholders would not tolerate. Musk stated SpaceX won't IPO until regular Mars flights are achieved. This reveals a strategic framework: match your corporate structure to your mission timeline. If your mission requires sustained investment in speculative, long-horizon projects, private ownership preserves decision-making freedom. If your mission aligns with profitable growth, public markets provide the capital needed to scale. The choice of corporate structure is itself a strategic tool, not just a financing decision.
