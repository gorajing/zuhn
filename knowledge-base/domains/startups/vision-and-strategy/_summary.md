# Topic: vision-and-strategy

> 5 insights

- `INS-260410-EF31` [high] Meta survived nine existential threats by framing itself as a 'human connection company,' not a 'social network' or 'social app' — letting the product form change while the mission stayed fixed.
- `INS-260410-1941` [high] Microsoft publicly committed to OS/2 with IBM while quietly nurturing Windows and Mac applications as parallel hedges, so when OS/2 failed, Windows was ready to inherit the entire PC ecosystem.
- `INS-260410-00F0` [high] 'Organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful' was genius because it was broad enough to scale for 25+ years, altruistic enough to recruit generational engineering talent, and tautologically monetizable — anyone who organizes information for hundreds of millions of people can monetize intent.
- `INS-260405-0D9A` [high] Structure your roadmap so each milestone lowers the cost or risk of the next, making progress compound rather than just accumulate.
- `INS-260405-F2C7` [high] Vision should pull milestones forward, not justify why you missed them—keep the two layers structurally separate in your pitch.
