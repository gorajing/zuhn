Brooke and his co-founder Gil spent months doing nothing but sales calls from 10am to 9pm. They A/B tested different pitches, heard the same questions repeatedly, and built an exhaustive mental model of customer concerns and what resonates.

By the time they hired their first salesperson, they could provide a complete playbook: these are the questions that always come up, these are the answers that work, this is what you should never say, this is what resonates most. Without those months of relentless founder-led sales, the first seller would have been set up to fail. The lesson: founder-led sales isn't just about getting early revenue—it's about building the institutional knowledge that makes sales scalable.
