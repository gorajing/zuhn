Rather than agonizing over the 'right' price before having any customers, Hormozi advocates a graduated approach: start at zero, then raise the price after each cohort. Customer 1-2 are free, customers 3-5 pay a low price, customers 6-10 pay more, and so on. Each price increase is justified by the growing body of proof (testimonials, case studies, results) from previous customers.

This solves two problems simultaneously: it eliminates the pricing paralysis that stops founders from launching, and it creates a natural price discovery mechanism driven by actual market feedback. If you raise the price and people still buy, you haven't hit the ceiling yet. If conversion drops, you've found your current price point. This is empirical pricing rather than theoretical pricing.
