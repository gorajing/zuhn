Duffy provides a tactical toolkit for countering FOMU. Professional services: if you have a PS organization, include implementation services as part of the proposal — structure the deal as a win-win rather than giving services away free. This reduces the buyer's fear that deployment will fail.

Warranties: Arcus Labs offers a $1M warranty — if they don't stop the attacks, they pay $1M. This directly addresses the 'what if it doesn't work?' fear. Not every company can offer this, but some form of outcome guarantee significantly reduces FOMU.

Peer relationships: the strongest FOMU antidote is a referral from someone the buyer trusts. Ask existing happy customers to introduce you to peers at their level in other companies. That introduction is '100X better than any other lead' because trust transfers — the champion candidate has already heard from a trusted peer that your solution works.

Start small: million-dollar deals still exist, but it may be easier to start smaller and land with a deliberate plan to expand. Go slow to go fast.

Bring experts early: involve your legal and finance teams in conversations early to be 'as compromising as possible' and help the champion navigate internal procurement.

For Anthropic: the safety brand, responsible scaling policy, and enterprise security certifications are all FOMU-reducing assets that competitors can't match.
