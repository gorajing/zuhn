Sudheesh proposes four universal buyer archetypes that transcend specific titles. Gatekeepers (InfoSec, compliance) are measured on protecting the business — they buy efficiency (doing the same thing at lower cost), not new capabilities. Business users (revenue drivers, customer support) are directly responsible for moving the business needle — they can find new dollars if you demonstrate productivity or ROI gains. Enablers (HR, IT) make others productive — they value learning and improvement above all. Leaders (2-3 levels from frontline) are measured on growth — they care about efficiency in the denominator.

Each archetype determines how the product evangelizes itself. Gatekeepers are empowered users who need clear ROI (Okta as example). Business users create demand through usage — every user becomes a demand generation machine because they share with colleagues (Miro as example). Enabler decisions are made by few people and can't be appealed — if HR picks Workday, you use Workday. Leader decisions are powerful but risky — 'fly close to the sun, and if you don't deliver, you may not get back in.'

For AI sales, Claude might be all four: a gatekeeper tool (content moderation), a business user tool (sales acceleration), an enabler tool (HR automation), and a leader tool (strategic analysis). Each framing requires a different pitch, different buyer engagement, and different proof points.
