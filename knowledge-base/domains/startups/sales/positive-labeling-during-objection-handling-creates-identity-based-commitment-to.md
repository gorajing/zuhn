The 'Associate' step in Hormozi's framework assigns the prospect a positive identity label — 'that's a really smart question,' 'you're just like our best customers who ask that.' This label serves double duty: it immediately defuses the objection by making the prospect feel validated rather than combative, and it creates a stored identity the salesperson can invoke at close time. A prospect labeled as a 'family man' during a spouse objection can later be closed with 'because you care about your family, this is the right move.' This works because people are motivated to act consistently with identities they've accepted — a well-documented principle in behavioral psychology that transfers directly into sales conversations.
