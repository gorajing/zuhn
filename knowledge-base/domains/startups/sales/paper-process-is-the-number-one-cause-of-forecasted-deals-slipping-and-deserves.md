Whyte explains why many organizations have evolved from MEDDIC to MEDDPICC — the 'P' for paper process addresses the most common source of forecast misses. Once you're the vendor of choice, the selling is ostensibly done, but the paper process (NDAs, security reviews, legal negotiations, procurement approval, contract signing) is where deals slip weeks or months.

The paper process includes: who signs, who must approve before signing, what happens if someone goes on holiday, what security/privacy reviews are needed, and what could go wrong. In cloud/SaaS sales especially, data privacy, infrastructure security, and integration requirements create lengthy paper processes.

Anecdotally, Whyte shares that the head of procurement at Siemens told a MEDDIC consultant that he prefers buying from vendors who use MEDDIC because they ask the right questions, run professional processes, and make procurement efficient for both sides.

For Anthropic enterprise deals, the paper process is especially critical given AI-specific considerations: data processing agreements, responsible AI clauses, security certifications, and compliance reviews that are unique to frontier model deployment. Mapping these early prevents late-stage surprises.
