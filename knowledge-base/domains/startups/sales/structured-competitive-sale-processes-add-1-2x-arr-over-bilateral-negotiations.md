The process through which a SaaS company sells matters as much as its metrics. A structured competitive sale — where multiple qualified buyers are brought to the table simultaneously — consistently adds 1-2x ARR to the final multiple compared to a bilateral negotiation with a single buyer. On a $5M ARR business, that process gap alone represents $5-10M in additional enterprise value.

This finding challenges the common founder instinct to quietly negotiate with one interested acquirer. The competitive dynamic creates information asymmetry that favors the seller: each buyer must price in the risk of losing the deal, which pushes offers upward. Fewer than 5% of private SaaS deals reach the 10-12x range, and nearly all of them involve a managed competitive process. Founders planning exits should budget time and resources for running this process properly rather than optimizing purely on metrics.
