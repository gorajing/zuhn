Harvard Business Review research and Hormozi's own data from Allen (4,000+ appointments/day) show that responding to a lead within 60 seconds creates a 391% increase in conversion likelihood. Beyond 5 minutes, close rates drop by 80%. Half of all prospects simply buy from the first business that responds, regardless of quality.

This means the infrastructure around sales -- automated notifications, always-available reps, call-now buttons in funnels -- matters more than the pitch itself. For enterprise sales job seekers, this translates to: when a prospect expresses interest via email, LinkedIn, or inbound, responding within minutes (not hours) signals competence and captures the narrow window of maximum motivation. The operational discipline of speed-to-lead separates top performers from average reps.

The practical implication is that sales organizations should invest in response infrastructure before sales training. If you can 4x your business by reducing response time, that dwarfs any realistic improvement to close rates through better scripting.
