PolyAI's CEO Nicola, despite being a researcher by training, built the entire sales engine himself. Wen explains this worked because Nicola was deeply technical — a competitive programmer — so when customers made requests, he could instantly filter what was technically feasible versus impossible. A typical non-technical salesperson would bring back every customer request, many of which would be engineering dead ends.

This is especially critical for companies selling novel technology where no established playbook exists. Seasoned sales leaders 'don't necessarily know what your product is and what your technology is' — they still need to learn how to sell something that isn't a commodity yet. The founder's dual technical-commercial knowledge creates a natural filter that prevents the classic pathology of sales teams over-promising and engineering teams under-delivering.
