Tyler Bosmeny references Everett Rogers' technology adoption lifecycle to reframe early-stage rejection. Only 2.5% of companies — the innovators segment — will even consider buying from an unproven startup with no revenue. This means reaching out to 200 companies to find roughly 5 potential buyers is normal, not a failure signal.

This framing is liberating because it makes the task clear: early sales is a numbers game of finding that small innovative segment. Rather than being demoralized by a 97.5% rejection rate, founders should see it as the expected cost of finding those rare early adopters who are wired to try new things. The implication is that insufficient outreach volume, not poor product, is often the real bottleneck for pre-revenue startups.
