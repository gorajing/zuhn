Whyte cites data from iSEEit (a MEDDIC implementation SaaS) showing that over 80% of deals that were won had direct engagement with the economic buyer, while over 80% of deals that were lost or slipped did not. He states bluntly: 'If you aren't engaged with the economic buyer, you are order-taking, not selling.'

The economic buyer is the person with overall authority — they can say yes when others say no, create budget out of thin air, and their engagement has the single biggest impact on deal velocity, deal size, and forecast accuracy. Critically, the economic buyer is NOT the champion; they are more senior.

Whyte's tactical recommendation for engaging the economic buyer uses multi-level selling: before even the first meeting with your prospect, have your boss write to the prospect's boss — no ask, just opening communication: 'Our teams are meeting next week. We've helped companies like you do X, Y, Z. I wanted to open this line of communication.' This establishes executive-to-executive rapport early and creates a channel for ongoing value-add touchpoints.

At Anthropic, this translates to ensuring your VP or CRO has a relationship with the prospect's C-suite from day one, not just when the deal needs executive sponsorship to close.
