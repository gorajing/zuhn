The speaker argues that whether an interview is 30 minutes or an hour, the first 2-3 minutes are disproportionately important due to the psychological power of first impressions. The opening question — whether 'tell me about yourself,' 'what led you here,' or 'why sales?' — is the candidate's chance to take control.

The optimal answer structure: brief background (school, prior work), then immediately pivot to why sales excites you (performance-based career, effort-reward correlation), and then why this specific company (demonstrate you understand their value proposition and believe they're positioned to win in their market). The key phrase: 'Being in the right industry is great, but you want to be on the right horse in the right industry.' This approach demonstrates preparation, intentionality, and the kind of strategic thinking that mirrors what you'd do as an SDR researching prospects.
