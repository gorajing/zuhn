Shelby's core framework: leverage means the specific pain points that will motivate each individual to buy. The same fitness program can be sold as weight loss, muscle gain, health recovery, or prevention -- and using the wrong leverage with the wrong person means the pitch fails even though the product is identical.

The process: question-based discovery gathers 'ammo' (specific pain points), which you pocket for later. When you transition to the solution, you only address the specific leverage points that person cares about. Nothing else. Keep it simple. The metaphor is 'sell the sizzle, not the steak' -- nobody cares about what's included; they care about what changes in their daily life.

In enterprise sales, this is discovery excellence. Two companies may both need your CRM, but one is motivated by sales rep attrition and the other by pipeline visibility. If you pitch pipeline visibility to the company hemorrhaging reps, you lose. The leverage determines the pitch, and the pitch determines the close.
