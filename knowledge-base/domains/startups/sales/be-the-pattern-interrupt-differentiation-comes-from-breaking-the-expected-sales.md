Every prospect has heard thousands of pitches. Their brain has a pattern: detect sales pitch, activate resistance, tune out. The pattern interrupt breaks this cycle by doing something unexpected. Instead of 'I'd like to walk you through our platform,' try 'I've thought about your specific problem at length and have a very specific perspective on solving it.'

Shelby connects this to personal branding: you don't need millions of followers. A friend with 6,000 followers gets introduced to everyone because his content is genuinely excellent and specific. He's a pattern interrupt in a sea of generic content. Similarly, a sales rep who has done real research, has a genuine perspective, and communicates differently from every other vendor email stands out.

In enterprise sales, the pattern interrupt might be: sending a personalized Loom video instead of a templated email, leading with a contrarian insight about their industry instead of a product pitch, or being the one rep who says 'I don't think our product is right for you right now, but here's what I'd recommend.' Authenticity is the ultimate pattern interrupt.
