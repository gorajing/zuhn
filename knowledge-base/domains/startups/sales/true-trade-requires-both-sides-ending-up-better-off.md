The very concept of trade that sales derives from is that by exchanging goods, both sides end up better off. When a founder sells something to a customer that doesn't produce any real benefit but collects money anyway, that's not trade — it's closer to extraction. You might hit your 15% month-over-month growth target, but your customer didn't win.

This matters practically, not just ethically. Building a business on transactions where only you benefit creates churn, bad word-of-mouth, and a fragile revenue base. The founders who win long-term are the ones who structure deals so both sides genuinely end up better. This is the foundational principle underlying all the tactical sales advice: if you're not trying to help people, why are you doing this business?
