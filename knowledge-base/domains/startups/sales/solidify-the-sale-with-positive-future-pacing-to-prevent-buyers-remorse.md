Most salespeople stop connecting after the credit card runs. This is a critical mistake. The moment after saying 'yes' is when buyer's remorse is highest. Shelby's technique: positively future-pace the customer by having them envision the transformation. 'A lot of people love texting me when they hit their first [milestone]. Would you mind keeping me in the loop?'

This does three things: (1) the customer mentally rehearses following through and achieving results, (2) you shift from salesperson frame to accountability partner frame, and (3) you create a commitment device where they've told you they'll report back. Each of these independently reduces cancellations.

In enterprise sales, the equivalent is: immediately after contract signature, schedule the implementation kickoff, walk through the success metrics timeline, and say 'I'm personally invested in making sure you see [specific outcome] by [date]. Let's set up a 90-day check-in now.' This transforms the relationship from vendor to partner.
