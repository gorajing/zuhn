A fundamental asymmetry exists in sales psychology: prospects believe almost nothing the salesperson says but believe almost everything they themselves say. This means the goal is never to tell prospects they're a good fit — it's to ask questions that lead them to conclude it themselves.

This inverts the typical sales training approach of building stronger arguments. Instead, the strongest closers ask more questions and make fewer statements. Hormozi calls this 'breadcrumbing to the logical solution' — each question moves the prospect one step closer to concluding they should buy, but the conclusion feels self-generated. This also explains why 'do you have any questions?' is the worst question in sales: it literally hands conversational control to the prospect and invites objections.
