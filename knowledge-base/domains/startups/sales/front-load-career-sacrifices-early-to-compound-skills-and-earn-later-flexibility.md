The AE reflects on his career arc: in his early years, he worked 7am to 7pm in the office, hit the gym for 90 minutes, went home, and prospected on LinkedIn until bed. No family, no kids, maximum available hours poured into building skills and pipeline. Now at 42 with two kids, he cannot work that schedule -- but he doesn't need to because those early investments compounded.

The skills from thousands of sales conversations, the relationships with major account decision-makers, the reputation within his company -- all of these were built during the high-investment years. Now he can work more efficiently because pattern recognition replaces brute-force effort.

He explicitly chose the production (individual contributor) path over management because it offered more autonomy, higher earning potential through direct sales, and better alignment with his entrepreneurial mindset. This is a strategic career choice: management offers one growth path, but staying in production at the enterprise level offers another that many people don't consider.
