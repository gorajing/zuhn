Hormozi describes a specific escalation pattern for pricing: start free, then 20% of target price, then 40%, 60%, 100%, and eventually 500% — but only when demand at each tier exceeds your capacity. The critical mistake most people make is offering free value with strings attached ('I'll give you X in exchange for Y'), which signals low confidence and misaligned incentives.

The strategy works because of three possible outcomes when you give to someone ahead of you: (1) nothing happens (most likely), (2) they notice your work ethic and offer a different opportunity, or (3) they want exactly what you gave. The key insight is that this is a volume play, not a single-shot gamble. Most of Hormozi's team was hired through this exact funnel. The math is simple: if 5 out of 100 respond positively, you've built a client base or career opportunity from pure generosity — but it requires doing the work 100 times without expectation, which filters out most people.
