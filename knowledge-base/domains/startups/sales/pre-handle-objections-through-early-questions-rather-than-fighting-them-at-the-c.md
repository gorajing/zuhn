The veteran identifies that amateur salespeople face a barrage of objections at the closing stage: 'I need to talk to my wife,' 'I don't have the budget,' 'I need to check with my business partner,' 'I've seen other vendors.' All of these could have been addressed earlier through deliberate questions.

The technique: weave qualification questions throughout the conversation, not just at the beginning. 'Have you put a budget aside to solve this problem?' 'Have you talked to your wife about purchasing this?' 'Have you already reviewed other vendors?' 'Have you bought software like this before?' Each question pre-handles a potential objection by either resolving it or surfacing it when there's still time to address it.

For enterprise sales: the principle is identical but the stakes are higher. 'Who else needs to weigh in on this decision?' asked in the second call prevents a surprise stakeholder vetoing the deal in month five. 'What does your procurement process look like?' asked early prevents the discovery that legal review takes 90 days when you're forecasting 30. Pre-handling in enterprise means mapping the entire decision-making landscape before you invest heavy resources.
