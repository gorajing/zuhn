The vlog strips away the glamour: enterprise sales is 80% managing existing relationships and 20% new business pursuit. A typical day includes financial reviews of existing engagements, invoicing discussions with back-office teams, delivery team alignment meetings, customer check-ins on active projects, and new business meetings -- all conducted over Microsoft Teams from a home office or a commuter car.

The reality check is important: when this AE says he sells to the Fortune 100, people imagine boardroom presentations. The actual work is standing in his kitchen eating five scrambled eggs between calls, driving to physical therapy while on a meeting, and managing his calendar with military precision to fit family obligations around an 8am-to-9pm workday.

For aspiring enterprise AEs: this is what you're signing up for. The compensation is excellent ($300K+ is achievable) precisely because the sustained intensity and relationship management burden is significant. The AEs who thrive are the ones who find genuine satisfaction in the operational rhythm -- solving customer problems, navigating organizational complexity, building long-term partnerships -- not just the thrill of closing.
