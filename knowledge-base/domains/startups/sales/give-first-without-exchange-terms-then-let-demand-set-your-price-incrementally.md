Hormozi distinguishes between two approaches to early-career value creation. The wrong way: offering free work 'in exchange for' something (testimonial, introduction, opportunity) — this signals delusion and undervalues the recipient's time. The right way: do exceptional work, give it freely without conditions, and accept that the most likely outcome is nothing. But across 100 attempts, a few recipients will recognize your work ethic and create unexpected opportunities.

He demonstrates this with his own origin story: starting a nonprofit (the Free Training Project) where clients donated $500 to charity instead of paying him. After 12 weeks of proven results, he simply asked existing clients to pay him instead. Everyone agreed without resistance because the value was already demonstrated. This is a pricing ladder: free → 20% of target price → 40% → 60% → 100% → 500%, only advancing when demand exceeds supply at each level. The key psychological insight is that people who have experienced your value firsthand have zero price resistance — the sale is already made before you ask.
