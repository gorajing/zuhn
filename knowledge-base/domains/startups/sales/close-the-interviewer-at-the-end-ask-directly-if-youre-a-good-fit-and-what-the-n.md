The speaker identifies the end-of-interview questions as the second most important moment (after the opening 2-3 minutes). Having five questions prepared but asking only three prevents going over time and seeming like someone who will pepper the manager with questions all day.

The power move is the fifth question: closing the interviewer. 'Based on our discussion today, do you think I'd be a good fit for the SDR role on your team? And if so, what are the next steps?' If the interview went well, many managers will start affirming enthusiastically. Even if they keep it close to the chest, you've demonstrated that you can ask for the close — which is literally the job you're interviewing for. Combined with a follow-up email within 30 minutes (not end of day, because many managers make decisions within the hour), this creates a complete sales cycle within the interview itself.
