Bosmeny distinguishes between giant consumer conferences (CES, E3) and the small industry-specific conferences — executives in a hotel in Milwaukee for a couple days — that are actual sales goldmines. The key is preparation: get the attendee list weeks or months in advance, email every potential buyer to schedule meetings, and arrive with your entire day booked in 30-minute blocks.

Many of Clever's initial partnerships and customers came from this approach. The reason it works so well is density: you compress what would normally take months of cold outreach into a single exhausting, energizing day of back-to-back conversations with qualified prospects who are already in learning mode. The conference does the hardest part of sales — getting your buyers physically present and mentally open to new solutions — and your job is simply to maximize the time by doing the upfront scheduling work.
