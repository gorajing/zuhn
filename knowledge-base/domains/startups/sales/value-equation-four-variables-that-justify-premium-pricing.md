Hormozi's Value Equation has four levers: increase the dream outcome, increase perceived likelihood of achievement, decrease time delay, and decrease effort and sacrifice required. When all four are optimized, the gap between what you charge and what the customer perceives they receive widens dramatically — making even high prices feel like a bargain.

This reframes pricing from a cost-plus calculation to a value-delivery optimization problem. A gym membership that guarantees results in 6 weeks with minimal lifestyle disruption is worth 10x more than one offering the same equipment with no structure. The practical implication is that before raising prices, founders should audit which of the four variables they can improve rather than simply adding features.
