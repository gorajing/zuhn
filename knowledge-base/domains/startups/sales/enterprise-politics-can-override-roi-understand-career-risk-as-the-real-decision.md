Connor shares a revelation from selling Oracle's cloud suite: you can spell out a perfect ROI case for migrating from on-premise to cloud, button up every financial metric, and still lose the deal. Why? Because the VP of IT who has been the on-premise expert for 20 years sees the migration as an existential career threat. If they move to cloud, their unique expertise becomes irrelevant.

This is organizational politics at scale, and it barely exists in SMB sales. Enterprise deals have stakeholders whose personal incentives actively conflict with the business's best interests. The 30-year veteran who built the legacy system, the department head who championed the competitor's product three years ago, the new executive who wants to make their mark with a different vendor.

For enterprise AEs: the technical and financial business case is just the starting point. You must map the political landscape: who benefits from the status quo, who benefits from change, whose career is at risk, and whose career is enhanced. Then structure your approach to neutralize threats and amplify allies. This is why multi-threading is essential -- you need to understand the full political map, not just one person's perspective.
