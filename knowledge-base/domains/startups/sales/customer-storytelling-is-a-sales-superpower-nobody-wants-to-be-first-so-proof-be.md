Maggie emphasizes that enterprise prospects are risk-averse — 'nobody wants to be the first.' Storytelling bridges this gap by converting early wins into industry-specific credibility. When OpenAI launched their Moderna partnership (estimating they could reduce time to FDA approval for cancer drugs), it unlocked the entire life sciences vertical. Morgan Stanley's wealth advisor use case unlocked financial services.

The mechanics: capture stories early, get logo rights and case studies, deploy them both externally (for prospects) and internally (engineers and PMs love hearing how people use their product). Customer wins/losses Slack channels are essential — at Slack, Webflow, and OpenAI, the 'customer losses' channel is where real learning happens, with executives like the COO swarming in to understand what went wrong.

This insight also applies to customer success strategy: 'A deal isn't won when the deal is signed. A deal is won when you have a deployed, happy customer singing your praises.' It costs 5x more to acquire a new customer than retain an existing one. Customer success should be a core strategy, not a side function — and churn is brand damage, not just lost revenue.

For an Anthropic sales role, this means building a personal library of Claude deployment stories segmented by industry and use case, and leading every customer conversation with relevant proof points.
