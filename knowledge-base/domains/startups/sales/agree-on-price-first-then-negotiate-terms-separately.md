Hormozi's mentor taught him a two-phase negotiation framework: first agree on the total price, then separately negotiate the terms of payment. This is powerful because sellers anchor on the headline number — if you agree to their price, they feel they've won, making them more flexible on terms.

In practice, this means you might agree to pay $625K for a business, then negotiate seller financing for 70% of it over 3-5 years, with a bank loan covering most of the rest and only $50K out of pocket. The seller gets their asking price (eventually), and you get a cash-flowing asset with minimal upfront capital. This tactic works across all negotiation contexts but is especially powerful in business acquisitions where the total deal size makes terms far more important than sticker price.
