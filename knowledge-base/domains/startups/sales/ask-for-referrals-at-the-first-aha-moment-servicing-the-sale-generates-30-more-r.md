The veteran's most impactful advice: most salespeople never go back to their buyers after the sale. They're onto the next deal. But the post-sale relationship is more valuable than the original transaction. The specific technique: identify the customer's first 'aha moment' -- the first call where things click, the first measurable result, the first milestone. In that moment of maximum satisfaction and gratitude, ask: 'Is there one or two other people just like you that you think we could serve with our product?'

This single practice can increase sales volume by 30%. The timing is critical: ask when gratitude and enthusiasm are peaked, not weeks or months later when the glow has faded. The phrasing matters too: 'just like you' makes the referral feel natural (they're helping someone similar), and 'one or two' sets a specific, manageable expectation.

For enterprise AEs: referrals and introductions from satisfied customers are the highest-quality pipeline source. A warm introduction from a VP at Company A to a VP at Company B bypasses months of cold outreach. Build referral requests into your customer success cadence at the specific milestone where value has been demonstrably delivered.
