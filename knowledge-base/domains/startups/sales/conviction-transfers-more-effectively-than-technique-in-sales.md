Hormozi defines selling as 'a transference of belief over a bridge of trust' — requiring both genuine conviction and earned trust. Hot streaks in sales rarely come from suddenly remembering better scripts; they come from renewed belief in the product. Cold streaks happen when a refund request, bad review, or difficult customer erodes that belief.

The practical framework: conviction is a continuum, not binary. Someone who believes 'balls to bone' in something — even something others think is crazy — creates a gravitational pull that makes others question their own skepticism. This is why the most effective sales training isn't technique-based but conviction-based: get salespeople to deeply understand and believe in the transformation they're offering.

This also explains why the 'person who cares the most about the prospect wins the sale' — genuine care is a form of conviction that prospects can sense. If you care more about their well-being than they do, your certainty will make them question their own excuses.
