Sudheesh uses waterfall types as metaphors for deal dynamics. Plunge waterfalls: the deal moves slowly and peacefully until it reaches the point of fall — then massive revenue. This is the traditional large enterprise motion requiring patience, infrastructure, multi-touch attribution, and field marketing. It's worth the investment but requires staying power.

Slide waterfalls: deals close quickly but you won't see another from that customer for a long time. This is extremely dangerous without renewal infrastructure and customer success — the sales team moves on to new opportunities, and by renewal time the customer churns because nobody paid attention. 

Cascade waterfalls: you land and get more and more as you expand scope, use cases, and organizational footprint. This is the most attractive pattern but requires the entire team — product marketing, field marketing, customer success, and product managers. Product managers in particular can move the needle in cascade deals.

The organizational implication: if your deals naturally follow a cascade pattern, you need customer success teams, community, and inside sales for renewals. If they follow a plunge pattern, you need patient field marketing and experienced enterprise reps. Mismatching your team structure to your deal pattern is one of the most expensive mistakes in sales org design.
