Every sales interview includes some variant of 'tell me about a time you faced rejection' or 'how do you handle losing a deal.' The question isn't really about the rejection — it's a proxy for coachability, self-awareness, and long-term durability in a high-rejection role.

The winning pattern is: acknowledge the emotional reality of rejection (showing self-awareness), then pivot to what you analyzed and changed as a result (showing growth mindset). Candidates who claim rejection doesn't bother them seem dishonest. Candidates who dwell on the unfairness seem fragile. The sweet spot is honest acknowledgment plus systematic learning — which is exactly the disposition that predicts success in iterative, high-volume SaaS sales.
