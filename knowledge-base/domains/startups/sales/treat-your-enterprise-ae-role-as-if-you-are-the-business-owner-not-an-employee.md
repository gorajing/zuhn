This 16-year enterprise AE veteran describes his daily reality: calls from 7:30am through 4:30pm, family duties until 7:30pm, coaching calls at 8pm, follow-up work until 10:30pm, weekends spent preparing for the following week. He chose the individual contributor path over management specifically because the autonomy to run his territory like his own business appeals to his entrepreneurial nature.

His core philosophy: 'Would the business still run if you stopped working?' Most sales reps don't ask themselves this question. The top performers never fully disconnect -- not because they're workaholics, but because they understand that enterprise relationships are their business. A customer email at 6pm that gets a response in 10 minutes versus 14 hours communicates everything about how seriously you take the relationship.

For someone entering enterprise sales: understand that the early years require disproportionate time investment. When you don't have a family, you have more discretionary hours. Use them to build skills, relationships, and pipeline that compound over time. The 42-year-old version of you will have earned the flexibility to work differently, but only if the 25-year-old version invested the hours.
