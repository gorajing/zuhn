Hormozi argues that the highest-performing salespeople redefine what winning means: instead of measuring 'did they buy,' they measure 'did I help them make a decision and own their power.' This means you can win every single sales conversation because the metric is fully within your control.

When you genuinely don't care whether someone buys — because your goal is helping them decide, not buying — the pressure evaporates. This isn't a trick or affirmation; it structurally changes the dynamic. You can afford to be more direct, ask harder questions, and challenge their excuses because you're not protecting a fragile outcome. The prospect senses this lack of neediness and trusts you more.

The 10-80-10 rule reinforces this: 10% will never buy, 10% will always buy, and 80% are genuinely undecided. The undecided 80% respond to the salesperson who is most aligned with their interests — which is the one who genuinely doesn't need the sale. This is the same dynamic as the negotiation principle that the person who can walk away has the most power.
