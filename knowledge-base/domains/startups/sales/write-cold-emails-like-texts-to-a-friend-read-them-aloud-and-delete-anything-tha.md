Epstein's seven principles of effective cold email copy center on one meta-principle: be human. Use emotional language ('I'd love to,' 'thanks, you have no idea how much this helped'). Be informal ('hey' not 'Hello Mr. Epstein'). Even typos and no capitalization can help because they signal a real human wrote this. The read-aloud test is the final filter: if you'd feel awkward saying something to someone's face, rewrite it.

Critical tactical advice: don't start by opening a Google Doc and writing a template with placeholder variables. Instead, open your actual email editor, put a specific person's name in the 'to' field, and write that email to that person. Then you can reverse-engineer the template. The order matters because starting with templates produces robotic copy that triggers spam filters (both technological and human).

For enterprise sales: every single email is a brand impression. The 'just following up' email is a brand impression that says 'I have nothing new to offer.' Instead: 'I was talking to [customer in similar role] and they mentioned [specific insight] -- thought this might be relevant to the [specific initiative] you mentioned.' Human, specific, value-adding.
