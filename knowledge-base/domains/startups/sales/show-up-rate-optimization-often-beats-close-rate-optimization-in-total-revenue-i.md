Most sales organizations obsess over closing technique while ignoring that a massive percentage of booked meetings never happen. Hormozi argues that a 20% increase in show-up rates and a 20% increase in close rates are mathematically equivalent in revenue impact -- but show-up rates are far easier to improve.

The optimal reminder sequence from Allen's data: 24 hours before (personalized -- show you've done prep work for their specific situation), morning of (simple confirmation), and 1 hour before (voice memo or video preferred over text). Personal touches like 'I looked over your site and found three things we can help with immediately' make prospects feel guilty about no-showing because you've clearly invested effort.

For enterprise AEs, this means treating calendar management as seriously as deal strategy. Sending a brief, personalized agenda before every call, confirming the morning of, and making the prospect feel that canceling would waste real preparation you've done on their behalf.
