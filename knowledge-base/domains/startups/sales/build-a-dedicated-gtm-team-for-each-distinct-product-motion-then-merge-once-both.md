When OpenAI hypothesized that ChatGPT Enterprise would have a distinct buyer and sales motion from their API product, they made a deliberate choice to build a dedicated go-to-market org from scratch rather than bolt it onto the existing API sales team. The early team was fewer than 10 people with no SDRs, SCs, CSMs, or even a working Salesforce instance — but this lean, focused structure gave them speed, clarity, and deep customer focus.

The result was what they believe was the fastest-growing enterprise application in history. But the story doesn't end there: once both motions were proven, they collapsed the two orgs into a single unified GTM team of 500 people. Everyone got new roles, new workflows, new managers, and had to learn the opposite product. This was painful but drove faster execution, less duplication, and better customer experience. The key lesson: in AI, old organizational playbooks don't always apply — design around products and customers, not legacy structures.
