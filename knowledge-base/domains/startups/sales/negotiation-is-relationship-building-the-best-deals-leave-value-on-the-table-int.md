Hormozi reframes negotiation from a zero-sum extraction game to a relationship-building exercise. The conventional wisdom of 'getting the best deal' optimizes for a single transaction, but business is a repeated game where the same counterparts show up again and again.

Intentionally leaving value on the table signals trustworthiness and creates goodwill that compounds across future interactions. The counterparty remembers being treated fairly and brings more opportunities, referrals, and favorable terms in subsequent dealings. This is the negotiation-specific application of the broader goodwill-as-asset principle.
