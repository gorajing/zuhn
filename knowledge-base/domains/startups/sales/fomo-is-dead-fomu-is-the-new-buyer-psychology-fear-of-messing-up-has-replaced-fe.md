Richard Duffy presents stark data from Ebsta and Pavilion across millions of sales interactions: pipeline dropped 47% quarter-on-quarter, average deal value dropped 32%, win rates decreased significantly, sales cycles lengthened 32%, and the number of people involved in deals increased 33% on both sides. CFOs are now the de facto economic buyer even for non-financial purchases.

The psychological shift: the last decade was driven by FOMO — VCs didn't want to miss out, buyers didn't want to miss out, budgets were plentiful, everyone wanted the latest tools. That's been replaced by FOMU — fear of messing up. Champions are afraid to put their name on your solution because if it fails, their career suffers. This leads to indecision and status quo as the default.

Duffy's response framework: (1) Spend more time in discovery finding 'company-level pain' not just individual pain; (2) Differentiate between 'cool,' 'want,' and 'need' — many people will think your tech is cool, maybe want it, but do they NEED it?; (3) Deliver proven metrics that provide direct ROI, but ROI alone isn't enough — you must also de-risk FOMU through warranties, professional services, relationships, and starting small with a plan to expand.

For AI sales: FOMU is particularly acute because AI deployments carry reputational risk ('what if the model hallucinates on our customers?'). Anthropic's safety brand directly addresses FOMU in ways competitors can't.
