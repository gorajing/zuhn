BAMFAM is a deceptively simple discipline that separates professionals from amateurs. The process: at the end of every call, schedule the next one. Don't say 'I'll send you some times' -- say 'I've got Tuesday at 8am, I just sent the calendar invite to your inbox, can you add it to your calendar right now?'

The commitment lock is the key addition: 'Could you do me a favor? If for whatever reason something comes up, could you text me beforehand so I can fill that slot with someone else looking to discuss our product?' This does two things: (1) gets a micro-commitment that makes no-showing feel like breaking a promise, and (2) subtly communicates that your time is valuable and in demand.

For enterprise sales: the principle extends to every interaction. After a discovery call, book the demo. After the demo, book the technical review. After the technical review, book the executive sponsor meeting. Never leave the momentum of a good conversation to the entropy of email scheduling. Calendar discipline is pipeline discipline.
