The video identifies a common enterprise paradox: you have a champion who is genuinely excited and wants to buy, but they keep having the same high-level value conversations call after call without progressing. The problem isn't interest -- it's that they don't know what the next steps actually are because they've never bought software at this organization before.

The solution is being prescriptive: 'Here's how this process typically plays out with organizations like yours. We've worked with many similar companies. Step 1: we'll need an architecture and performance call with IT. Step 2: we bring in an implementation partner to discuss resourcing and timelines. Step 3: we'll need executive sponsorship to authorize the security review.'

This prescriptive approach works because: (1) it shows you've done this many times before (credibility), (2) it gives the champion a roadmap they can follow (enablement), (3) it surfaces blockers early instead of at the end of a 6-month cycle (risk mitigation), and (4) it positions you as a trusted advisor, not a vendor waiting for instructions. Joint execution plans formalize this into a shared document with dates, owners, and milestones.
