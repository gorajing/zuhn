Duffy's Snap case study provides a detailed champion-building blueprint. Nick (head of security engineering at Snap, 375M active users) was identified as a potential champion early. The progressive testing process: Multiple discovery calls (customer speaks 60%+ of the time, tracked by call recording). Customized demos using their language and workflow. Introduced M1 metrics (proof points from Adobe, Airbnb) early — not product features. Before starting a POV, agreed on success metrics (M2) and mapped the decision process (who else needs to be involved?).

The champion confirmation checkpoints: Nick has power and influence (confirmed by tenure and impact at Snap). Nick shares information under NDA (who to talk to, what problems need solving). Nick provides access to other key stakeholders and shares why this matters to him personally.

The critical failure point: the deal nearly died when Arcus pushed too hard to close by quarter-end. 'You can impact the customer's pace but you can't control it. Trying to control their pace will backfire.' They had to rebuild trust by resetting to the customer's timeline and being authentic about the mistake.

Nick's advice (3 days before the presentation): (1) Major spending is hyper-scrutinized — the economic buyer has shifted to CFO; (2) Drive toward desired outcomes with hyper-optimized metrics; (3) Bucket solutions into 'need' vs 'want' — even within your product, some capabilities are needs and some are wants.
