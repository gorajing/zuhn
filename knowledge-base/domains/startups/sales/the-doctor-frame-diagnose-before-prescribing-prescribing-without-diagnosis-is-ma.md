The doctor frame is the most powerful frame in sales because it inverts the normal power dynamic. Instead of the salesperson chasing the buyer, the buyer is a patient seeking expert diagnosis. The language: 'Is it okay if I ask you a few questions to see where you're at, so I can figure out what might help you? I want to make sure I prescribe the right solution.'

This frame gives you permission to ask probing questions, positions you as an authority figure, and makes the eventual recommendation feel like expert advice rather than a sales pitch. The key principle: prescribing without diagnosis is malpractice. You would never trust a doctor who prescribed medication before asking about your symptoms.

The proof this works: when the veteran gets cold-called and someone asks 'what's the price?' and he gives it, and they say 'okay I'll call back' -- that's an amateur who lost frame. The professional would say 'before I give you a price, let me ask a few questions to make sure this is actually the right fit for you.' This reframes price from a commodity lookup to a diagnostic outcome.
