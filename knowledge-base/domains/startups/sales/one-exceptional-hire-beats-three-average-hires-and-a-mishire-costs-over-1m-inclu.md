Maggie frames hiring as the highest-leverage activity in scaling, citing Sam Altman: 'great companies will always have great people.' At OpenAI, even when they needed to scale from 200 to ~1000 people in one year, they held the bar. Her three principles: (1) Hire for clear, measurable objectives — candidates must articulate exactly how their skills advance the mission, not just express excitement about AGI; (2) Build teams, not just talent — 50 top performers who can't collaborate create bottlenecks, so hire like assembling puzzle pieces; (3) Maintain a high bar — one exceptional hire beats three average hires, and exceptional people refer other exceptional people.

The cost of a mishire: time to hire + time to ramp + time in front of customers not closing + cultural disruption + severance + starting over = >$1M and ~1 year wasted. Common pitfalls include the 'logo trap' (confusing big company brand with startup readiness — you need people who can build the machine, not just operate it), promoting too early (your best AE isn't necessarily your best sales manager), and hiring too senior too early (you don't need a CRO when you have two AEs).

Her red flags: blaming others for failures, job hopping without progression, primarily motivated by titles/money, and lack of humility. What she looks for: 'chaos translators' who thrive in ambiguity, versatile generalists, deep collaborators, and people with a growth mindset.
