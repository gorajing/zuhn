One of the most common early-stage sales mistakes is reaching for discounts as a primary weapon. Founders think that if the customer isn't buying, the price must be too high. But if a customer doesn't understand why your product would help them, a lower price doesn't create understanding — it creates suspicion.

The real issue is almost never price. It's that the customer hasn't been shown how the product solves a problem they actually care about. When you discount before establishing value, you're signaling that even you don't believe the product is worth what you're asking. The fix is to back up and do the problem-solving work: understand their business, identify their pain, and connect your product to a real outcome they care about.
