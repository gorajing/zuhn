Pete Kazanjy from YC makes the case that sales before product-market fit is a completely different activity from sales after PMF. Pre-PMF sales requires vision (selling what the product will become), credibility (being the expert who built it), constant experimentation, and a tight feedback loop with the engineering team. These are founder skills, not sales rep skills.

Technical founders often think they need a 'business co-founder' to handle sales, but their domain expertise and genuine conviction are actually their biggest sales advantages. Sales isn't a dark art of psychological tricks -- it's fundamentally about helping people solve problems, and engineers are great at that.

For anyone looking to enter enterprise sales at a startup: understand that the founders should be selling initially. As an early sales hire, your role is to extend founder-led sales, not replace it. The best early enterprise sales hires learn the founder's conviction and domain expertise deeply enough to carry it authentically into conversations.
