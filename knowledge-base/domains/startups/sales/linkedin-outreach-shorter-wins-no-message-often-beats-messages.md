Parsa has A/B tested LinkedIn outreach extensively. Findings: (1) no-message connection requests get higher accept rates than messages, because messages trigger spam-detection heuristics in recipients. (2) First message after acceptance must be under 80 characters — short and curiosity-piquing. (3) LinkedIn Premium subscription magnitude-reduces block risk (you're a paying customer, the platform is less aggressive). (4) 6-7 message sequence beats 2-3, but each individual message must be short and act-like-a-friend, not act-like-a-salesperson. (5) Lead magnets (communities, guides, value-add content) beat sales pitches in follow-ups — 'hey, here's something I think you'd find interesting.'

Application: when designing LinkedIn outreach campaigns, default to no-message connection requests. Plan a 6-7 message sequence post-accept where each message is <80 characters and provides incremental value (related video, useful insight, link to community). Don't ask for the meeting until the relationship has signal. For Memric specifically: the audit motion's CIO outreach should follow this pattern — no-message connection, then 6-message sequence redirecting to Memric Brief Substack or principle-library downloads, not direct meeting requests.
