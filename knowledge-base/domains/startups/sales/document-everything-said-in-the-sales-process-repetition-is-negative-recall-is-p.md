The veteran identifies a common failure: sales reps don't document what was said during the call. When the deal is handed to onboarding or customer success, the customer has to repeat everything from scratch. This is a binary swing: making a customer repeat themselves is extremely negative, but recalling what they told a previous rep is extremely positive.

The 5-minute investment after each call to log names, pain points, team structure, decisions made, and next steps creates compounding value. The onboarding rep can say 'So you talked to Steve and Jim, and you decided on X -- I think we can help you with [specific goal] by following steps one, two, three.' The customer feels known, valued, and confident they're in competent hands.

For enterprise AEs managing complex multi-stakeholder deals over 6-12 months: meticulous CRM notes are your memory. You're juggling 10-15 accounts simultaneously. The rep who can reference a conversation from three months ago -- 'You mentioned in our March call that Sarah in procurement was concerned about X' -- builds trust that generic follow-ups never can.
