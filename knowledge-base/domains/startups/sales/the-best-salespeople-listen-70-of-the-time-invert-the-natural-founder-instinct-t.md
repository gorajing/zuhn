Bosmeny shares that after shadowing world-class salespeople, he found they were soft-spoken, world-class listeners — nothing like the Don Draper stereotype. They spent 70% of calls listening and asking questions like 'tell me about your problem' and 'what would your ideal solution look like,' leaving only 30% for their own talking.

Founders naturally do the opposite: they're so passionate and excited about their product that they talk 70% of the time, showing off every feature. Bosmeny's team at Clever used UberConference's talk-time analytics to enforce this discipline. When a rep said a call went great but the analytics showed they dominated the conversation, it was a reliable signal the deal was actually weaker than perceived. The core insight is that sales is fundamentally about understanding someone's problem deeply enough to help, which requires listening, not pitching.
