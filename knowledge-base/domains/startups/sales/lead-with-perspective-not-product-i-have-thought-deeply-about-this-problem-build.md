Peter An's coaching data from listening to over a thousand founder sales calls: nine out of ten founders open with 'I'll walk you through everything in the product today.' This is a harbor tour -- stop to stop to stop, overwhelming and impersonal. The prospect can watch a recorded video for that.

The reframe is devastatingly simple: 'I've thought through this problem at length and have a very specific perspective on solving it.' This signals domain expertise, unique thinking, and confidence. When you close your first 5-10 customers, most will say 'I bet on your way of thinking' -- not 'I loved your feature set.'

This is especially powerful when your product is early and incomplete. Instead of apologizing for missing features with 'we can build that,' try: 'We don't have that right now, but when we do, we're thinking about approaching it this way. Is not having that today a showstopper for you?' This maintains roadmap clarity while building confidence in the vision -- which is what enterprise buyers are actually purchasing.
