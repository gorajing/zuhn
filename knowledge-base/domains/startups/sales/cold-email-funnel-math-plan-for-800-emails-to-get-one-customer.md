Aaron Epstein from YC walks through a realistic B2B cold email funnel: 800 sent emails produce 400 opens (50%), which produce 40 responses (10%), which produce 10 demos (25%), which produce 1 customer (10%). This means if you're sending 'a handful of emails every week,' you're not even in the game.

The implications are profound: you need to be sending 50+ emails per day, manually at first so you learn what works before scaling. And these conversion rates will decrease as you scale because you'll exhaust your most ideal prospects first. If your current manual conversion rates are bad, fix them before scaling -- automation only amplifies whatever your current performance is.

For enterprise sales outreach: this math reframes expectations. Prospecting is a volume activity with a learning curve. The founders should be sending these emails (recipients take them more seriously). Block dedicated hours daily for outbound. Track conversion rates at each stage obsessively to identify where your funnel leaks and focus improvement there.
