The Sales Medic Group breaks MEDDIC metrics into two tiers: M1 (proof-point metrics from existing customers) and M2 (prospect-specific metrics uncovered through discovery). The critical insight is that M1 unlocks M2 — you cannot do effective discovery if the prospect doesn't trust you yet.

The common mistake Whyte identifies: sellers spot an opportunity, open their laptop, and immediately tell the customer how bad their current situation is ('telling them how ugly their baby is'). This destroys trust. Instead, the M1 approach says: 'Here's a customer similar to you. Before working with us they had these problems. Now they have these results — conversion rate improved X%, spend decreased Y%.' This builds credibility and makes the customer willing to open up about their own specific pain points.

For Anthropic sales specifically, this means building a library of customer ROI stories segmented by industry and use case. When meeting a new enterprise prospect, lead with 'here's what Company X achieved with Claude' before asking about their specific challenges. The M1-to-M2 progression is what separates consultative selling from product pushing.
