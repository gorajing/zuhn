Cost-plus pricing leaves money on the table because your costs have no relationship to customer value. Competitive-parity pricing lets incumbents set your ceiling. Value-based pricing starts from the customer's perspective: how much time, money, or risk does your product save or create?

ChatGPT at $20/month illustrates the asymmetry — it delivers thousands of dollars in time savings for knowledge workers. The gap between price and perceived value is what drives adoption velocity and reduces churn. The practical challenge is quantifying value, which requires understanding your customer's workflow deeply enough to measure before/after impact. Start with a single clear value metric (hours saved, revenue gained, errors prevented) and price as a conservative fraction of it. Simplicity in tier structure matters too — too many options create decision paralysis and reduce conversion.
