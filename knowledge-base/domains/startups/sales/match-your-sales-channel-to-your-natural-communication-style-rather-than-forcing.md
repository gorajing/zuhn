Naval redefines selling beyond face-to-face persuasion to include writing, PR, recruiting, fundraising, and inspiring people. This broader definition matters because many technically-minded founders dismiss sales as outside their capability when they're actually already doing forms of it.

A strong engineer who writes well can use content and online communication as their sales channel. One who excels one-on-one can focus on recruiting and fundraising. The key insight is that 'learning to sell' doesn't mean becoming a stereotypical salesperson—it means identifying which form of persuasion and communication you're naturally suited for and deliberately developing that specific channel.
