Copeland's v0 example: the hypothesis 'the best way to experience the product is the product' (custom apps per target company) produced very high open rates and zero conversions. The reason is altitude: in a startup the buyer and decision-maker are the same person; upmarket those roles diverge and the senior budget owner cares the product solves a business problem, not the product itself. Developer love is real but community/marketing drive it better than outbound; outbound should leverage PLG learnings to aim higher. Corroborates the KB's deal-size-dictates-motion and PLG-and-sales-are-complements principles.
