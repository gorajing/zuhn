Traditional sales thinking casts the salesperson as the hero pushing their product against competitors. Dunford's framework—aligned with research from The Challenger Sale—repositions the salesperson as the buyer's guide through a complex decision landscape. This shift is substantive, not cosmetic: it means helping buyers understand their options clearly, even when that involves acknowledging where competitors are strong.

This approach works because enterprise buyers are deeply skeptical of vendor bias. A salesperson who demonstrates willingness to help the buyer make the right decision—rather than just the decision to buy—earns a level of trust that closes harder deals. It also means helping internal champions navigate their own organizations, enabling the buyer to become an advocate who can sell internally. Dunford calls this 'empowering champions within client businesses.'
