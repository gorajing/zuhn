The Oracle veteran breaks the sales cycle into two halves: motivation (getting the buyer interested and seeing potential) and enablement (making it actually happen). In SMB, motivation is easy because decision-makers are on the first call and companies move fast. But enablement is hard because they lack resources, dedicated IT teams, and implementation bandwidth.

Enterprise flips this completely. Motivating an enterprise buyer is extremely difficult: they're slow, have established processes, think primarily about risk mitigation, and the people who champion initiatives are staking their careers on the outcome. But once an enterprise organization commits, they have budgets, teams, and infrastructure to enable execution.

For someone transitioning to enterprise sales: the biggest mental shift is patience and strategic thinking. You can't just 'be enthusiastic and demo well' like in SMB. You need to understand organizational dynamics, identify who actually has power, navigate political currents, and help your champion build an internal business case that survives scrutiny from people you may never meet.
