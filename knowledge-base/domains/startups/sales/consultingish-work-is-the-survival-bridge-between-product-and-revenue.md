Graham identifies a practical survival strategy for startups in the fatal pinch: slide partway down the spectrum from pure product to consulting. Startups have better programmers and deeper domain expertise than most customers — assets that are wasted if the only sales conversation is 'buy our product.' By asking 'what do you need that you'd pay a lot for?', founders can monetize their actual advantages immediately. The key constraints are: charge a lot, get paid quickly, keep the IP, avoid hourly billing, and build precisely defined derivative products rather than open-ended custom work. In the best case, this consultingish work reveals the 'thing that doesn't scale' that defines the company's real product-market fit.
