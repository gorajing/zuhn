From conducting over 100 SDR interviews, the speaker observed that candidates without cold calling experience but with strong character traits quickly surpass those with extensive experience but weaker traits. The three traits that constitute a 'slam dunk candidate' are: being a hard worker (showing up early, staying late, high effort), being coachable (taking feedback and visibly applying it), and being a team player (helping others succeed when you start performing well).

This maps directly to what a hiring manager actually needs: someone who will make their life easier, take coaching without resistance, and contribute to the team hitting its KPIs. The cold call roleplay in interviews isn't really testing cold calling skill — it's testing coachability. They give feedback mid-roleplay specifically to see if the candidate can adapt in real time. Understanding this reframes the entire interview from 'demonstrate sales skill' to 'demonstrate character.'
