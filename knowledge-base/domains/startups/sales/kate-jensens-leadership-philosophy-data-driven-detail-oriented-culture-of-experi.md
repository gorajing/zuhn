Kate's leadership approach at Anthropic reveals what the organization values in sales talent. She describes herself as 'very much in the details' and 'obsessed with dashboards,' looking at forecast, product metrics, and pipeline data daily. But her philosophy goes beyond analytics: she places deliberate bets and makes quick decisions on whether they're working.

Her most revealing insight: 'Those who are closest to the business have the best ideas, and that's typically the ICs.' She gives examples: an AE wanted to test a new vertical, another tested a new pricing strategy, another tested a pilot motion — she ran experiments on all three, all were wildly successful, and they changed how Anthropic sells today.

This culture — user-first, data-driven, experiment-friendly, vulnerability-encouraging — is what she learned from Gina (Stripe) and Claire, and what she's replicating at Anthropic. The sales team is 'brand new and learning the industry from scratch,' which means curiosity and adaptability matter more than domain expertise.

For an Anthropic sales candidate, this signals that the ideal profile is someone who proposes experiments backed by data, learns rapidly, shares failures openly, and treats customers as research subjects whose feedback directly shapes go-to-market strategy.
