Kausas pivoted away from a health tech idea after a full year, not just from the idea but from the entire approach—including his co-founder relationship. At Pylon, he and his co-founder built the product themselves, getting to their first customer in 14 days and $120K ARR in 3 months.

The speed difference is stark: a year of building something he couldn't fully control vs 14 days to first customer when building it himself. His blunt advice: 'stop being lazy and learn how to build yourself.' When founders can build, they iterate in hours instead of weeks, understand every nuance of the product, and sell from genuine knowledge rather than marketing scripts.
