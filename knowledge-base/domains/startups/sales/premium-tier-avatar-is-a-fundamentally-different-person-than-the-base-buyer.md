A common mistake when creating premium tiers is imagining the current $100 buyer deciding to spend $1,000. Hormozi argues this is wrong — the $1,000 buyer is a different person entirely. They have different financial capacity, different pain intensity, different accessibility requirements, and different decision-making frameworks.

This means the premium offer design process must start by defining who the premium avatar is: someone with the money, who feels the pain acutely, and is easy to reach through channels different from where you find base buyers. Only after identifying this distinct avatar should you design the offer components, delivery model, and value narrative specifically for their worldview — not as an upgrade path from the cheaper option.
