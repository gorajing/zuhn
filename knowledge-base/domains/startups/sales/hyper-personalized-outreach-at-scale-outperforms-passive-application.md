Hormozi's job-search advice is actually a sales methodology in disguise. The framework: research 5 target companies deeply, message 4 people at each company on LinkedIn with personalized notes, submit a custom cover letter referencing specifics from their website, then physically show up to introduce yourself. This is consultative selling applied to employment.

The meta-insight is that the application process IS the interview. By demonstrating unusual effort in how you apply, you're providing proof of how you'll perform on the job. This transfers directly to B2B sales, fundraising, and partnership development — any context where showing up differently than everyone else creates asymmetric attention. The 'prove it before they hire you' approach eliminates the trust gap that resumes alone can't bridge.
