Most sales training fails because feedback is delayed. A manager listens to a 30-minute role-play, then lists 10 things done wrong at the end. This violates the fundamental learning principle: trainability is inversely correlated with feedback delay. The faster the correction, the fewer repetitions needed.

Hormozi's method: work on one specific skill per session. When the rep says the wrong thing, stop immediately, demonstrate the correct version, have them repeat it 5-6 times in rapid succession until it's drilled in. Simultaneously give positive reinforcement (nodding, 'great keep going') in real time for correct behavior. Set expectations upfront: 'I'm going to stop you 30-40 times, that means we're doing it right.'

This applies directly to personal sales skill development: record your calls, identify the ONE thing that would improve close rates the most, drill it obsessively until it becomes automatic. Don't try to fix 10 things at once.
