Peter An identifies a pattern he sees in nearly every founder sales call: with 2 minutes left, the seller asks 'can I show you one more thing?' This is dangerous because feature-stuffing the first demo overwhelms prospects without actually advancing the deal. In enterprise sales with 8-30 decision-makers, you can't close in one call anyway.

The reframe: 'There's so much more we could dive into, but let's spend some time on next steps and prepare for a deeper dive. You mentioned [specific topic] is really important -- I want to surface that in call number two.' This does three things: (1) leaves something to be desired (creating pull), (2) demonstrates you were listening, and (3) the next-steps discussion itself can take 5-10 minutes in enterprise organizations.

For enterprise AEs: every call should end with crystal-clear next steps, not a rushed feature tour. The ability to close a call by saying 'let me set up a deeper session with the right people from both our teams to address [specific concern]' is worth more than any feature demo you could squeeze in.
