Kevin Egan (CRO of Clickhouse, former CRO of Atlassian, VP Sales at Slack/Dropbox/Salesforce) gave Maggie a career-defining lesson: 'Pick the rocket ship, not the seat.' On a rocket ship, your career goes places you could never have imagined. If you fixate on the captain's seat, you miss the ride entirely. People want to know what you saw, what you built, what you learned — not what your title was.

Maggie lived this: she went from senior director at Webflow (leading 50 people) to individual contributor at OpenAI (GTM was 7 people, no title). Many VCs and mentors told her not to do it. Over 40 of the people who turned down similar opportunities at OpenAI have since reached back out wanting to join.

Her framework for evaluating companies: are they category creators (Slack, ChatGPT) or category disruptors (Webflow, Eventbrite, Clay)? Creators need to move fast enough that people love the product before competition arrives and incumbents copy them. Disruptors need a product that's leaps and bounds ahead before incumbents catch up. Alex Rampell's quote: 'Can the startup acquire distribution before the incumbent acquires innovation?'

For someone targeting Anthropic: this is directly applicable. A role at Anthropic during this phase — even if the title is modest — positions you at the center of the most consequential technology transition in decades.
