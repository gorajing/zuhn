Tyler Bosmeny observed that the top 1% of salespeople spend remarkably little time talking on calls. Their goal is 30% talking, 70% listening. They ask questions like 'Why did you agree to take my call?' and 'How do you solve this problem today?' to deeply understand the prospect's needs.

This is counter-intuitive for founders who are proud of their product and want to describe every feature. But the data backs it up — Clever used call analytics tools that showed talk-time ratios, and Bosmeny could predict deal likelihood just from how much the salesperson talked. The less they talked, the more likely the close. This works because understanding the customer's problem better than they do is what creates trust and urgency.
