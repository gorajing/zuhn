The default founder impulse is to monetize every touchpoint. Claire's team did the opposite: ten-plus emails before launch focused only on problem-framing and idea-sharing. No buttons, no pitches. The release email then converted at outsized rates because expectation had been compounding for weeks — readers had already accepted the problem as real and were actively wondering what the solution looked like. The underlying principle: the conversion power of a sales email scales with the trust debt you built and then cashed in at once. Premature selling bleeds that balance continuously. This only works when the pre-launch nurture is genuinely useful; filler content teaches readers to ignore you.
