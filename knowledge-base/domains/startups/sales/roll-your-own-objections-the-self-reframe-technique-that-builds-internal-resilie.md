Shelby identifies a powerful meta-skill: when you practice reframing other people's objections in sales (turning 'I can't afford it' into 'what's the cost of not solving this?'), you develop the ability to reframe your own limiting beliefs. The internal dialogue becomes: 'I hear you, internal voice saying I'm not good enough. Can I challenge that belief for a second?'

The process: acknowledge the negative feeling as real (don't suppress it), then consciously reframe it. Fear becomes data. Rejection becomes filtering. 'Maybe she just got served divorce papers' -- even if untrue, giving yourself context around rejection prevents it from becoming personal and cumulative.

This is particularly relevant for someone transitioning into enterprise sales from a non-sales background. The imposter syndrome, the rejection sensitivity, the self-doubt -- these are internal objections. Apply the same reframe technique: acknowledge the feeling, challenge the belief, output a positive action. Sales doesn't just build income; it builds psychological resilience.
