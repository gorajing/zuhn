YC has funded thousands of companies, yet Dalton can't recall seeing many die because they did too much consulting. The real consulting boogeyman — a big company wanting your startup to build completely bespoke software with no reusable value (like Disney asking for a white-label social network) — is extremely rare. Yet founders dismiss opportunities as 'consulting' constantly.

The reframe: deep integrations are deep moats. Long sales cycles are moats. Early-stage companies should trust their ability to productize over time and treat these engagements as learning opportunities. Palantir's lack of competition exists partly because too many startups shy away from anything involving that kind of deep integration work. If Palantir figured out how to make it scalable, so can others. The gray area between consulting and product is where many great enterprise businesses are built.
