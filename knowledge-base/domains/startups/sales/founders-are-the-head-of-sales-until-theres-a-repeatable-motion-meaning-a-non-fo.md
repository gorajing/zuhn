Maggie draws a hard line: founders are the head of sales until the motion is repeatable. A repeatable motion means: you know your ICP, you know your pitch, you know where leads come from, and a non-founder can move deals predictably through the funnel. Until those conditions are met, hiring a head of sales or CRO is premature.

She also warns against starting with enterprise customers, citing Slack's Uber experience. In 2015, Uber wanted to buy Slack for 5-6K people when Slack's largest customer was ~1000. Slack offered a small pilot. Uber ignored the suggestion and onboarded 2,461 users overnight, crashing Slack's systems. The technical failure didn't matter — the reputational damage took three years to overcome, as enterprises questioned whether Slack was 'enterprise-ready.'

The corrective: define your ICP, create a target list based on existing happy customers, start outbound on day one (warm intros don't scale — you must sell to strangers), and avoid enterprise until your product can truly deliver at that scale. 'The right customer at the wrong time is still the wrong fit. Wait until you can truly deliver.' Four years later, Slack won Uber back — when the product, infrastructure, and support were ready.
