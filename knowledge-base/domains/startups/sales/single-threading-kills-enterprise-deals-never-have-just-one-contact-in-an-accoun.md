Connor shares the story of his most damaging early enterprise deal: he was working with a single IT champion who presented themselves as the decision-maker. Five months of weekly cadences, demos, everything according to plan. Then overnight, his champion went completely silent. The deal was lost to a competitor championed by the CFO -- a person Connor had never spoken to because his single contact assured him finance 'wasn't important to the decision.'

The damage was compounded: he'd pulled in solutions engineers, architects, and consultants, all of whom had invested significant time based on his assurance the deal was real. When it collapsed, his internal credibility was damaged, meaning those resources were less willing to prioritize his next deal.

The lesson is absolute: by the 3rd to 5th call in an enterprise cycle, if your champion cannot or will not bring other stakeholders to the table, the deal is likely dead. Build multi-threading into your discovery workflow as a non-negotiable step. 'We will not move forward with a custom demonstration until we understand who all the stakeholders are and have access to them.'
