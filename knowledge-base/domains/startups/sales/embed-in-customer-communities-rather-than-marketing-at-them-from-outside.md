The HVAC example reveals a powerful distribution pattern: instead of running ads targeting HVAC owners, the founders joined their Facebook groups, shared trade-specific memes, became group admins, and built authority from the inside out. Similarly, another founder didn't just buy podcast ads — they brought the host on as an advisor, converting a media channel into an organic, trusted pipeline.

This approach works because it transforms the founder from an outsider selling something into an insider providing value. The resulting pipeline feels authentic rather than transactional, which matters enormously in trust-dependent B2B sales. The tradeoff is that this approach is slower to start but creates a distribution asset that no amount of competitor marketing spend can replicate. It also requires genuine customer empathy — you can't fake being an insider in a community you don't understand.
