Maggie created and ran OpenAI's pilot program, which became a critical competitive differentiator. When OpenAI built a dedicated pilot team that treated pilots as real customer engagements rather than 'flip the switch, good luck' free trials, deal sizes increased approximately 5x.

Her pilot recipe has four non-negotiable elements: (1) Executive buy-in upfront — confirm that if the pilot succeeds, the customer has budget and authority to convert. Without this, you invest months only to discover the IT manager you worked with had no purchasing power; (2) Repeatable playbook — Anthropic/OpenAI has a documented playbook across hundreds/thousands of pilots and asks customers to follow it. Deviations are near-non-starters; (3) Clear success metrics — define pre and post-pilot surveys and ROI metrics upfront so both sides agree on what success looks like; (4) Executive awareness — the customer's executives must know the pilot is happening, otherwise even a brilliant pilot won't convert.

The team composition during the pilot is equally important: 'We give our customers a taste of what it is like to work with us.' This partnership experience becomes its own selling point. The contrarian view: most companies see pilots as cost centers. OpenAI treats them as the highest-conversion sales motion.
