Kazanjy reframes the demo from 'showing off your product' to 'scripting a great movie.' The structure: open by recapping who the main character is (the prospect's user) and the problem they face. This proves you listened during discovery. Then show a narrative where the main character solves their problem using your product -- with every feature appearing because it serves the story, not because it exists.

At Optimizely, they invested weeks building a feature that let them demo A/B testing directly on the prospect's own website instead of a dummy site. When marketers saw changes happening on their actual landing page, their eyes lit up. That's a magic moment -- something surprisingly easy or delightful that makes the prospect feel 'this was built for me.'

For enterprise AEs: never do a generic demo. Use their logo, reference their specific workflow, name their team members, and structure the demo as their success story. The personalization signals that you understand their world deeply enough to actually solve their problem, which is the real purchasing decision.
