Every salesperson experiences hot streaks where everything seems to click. Most attribute it to luck or superstition ('my lucky socks'). But hot streaks aren't magic -- they're periods where your tone, pacing, transitions, question timing, and frame control are all slightly but measurably different from your baseline.

Hormozi's advice: when you're on fire, record everything. Then analyze those recordings obsessively. Where did you pause? How did you transition to the close? When did your tone shift? How did you handle early objections? How did you frame the ask? Document every detail and build a 'monster checklist' that recreates the conditions of your best performance.

This is the athlete's film study approach applied to sales. In enterprise sales, this means religiously recording all calls (Gong, Chorus, etc.), tagging your best conversations, and identifying the specific patterns -- questions asked, timing, energy management -- that correlate with closed deals versus losses.
