Naval identifies a hidden ambiguity in the phrase 'working for yourself': the surface reading is 'you're your own boss,' but the deeper reading is that your labor becomes a form of self-expression — an externalization of who and what you are. This is much harder to achieve and much more valuable when found.

Most people optimize for the first reading (escaping a boss) without achieving the second (finding work that is genuinely expressive of identity). The difference matters because the first produces mere freedom from constraint, while the second produces meaning. This insight is practically useful as a diagnostic: if your self-employment still feels like work, you may have the organizational form right but the content wrong.
