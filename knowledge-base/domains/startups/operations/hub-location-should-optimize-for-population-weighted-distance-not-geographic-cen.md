FedEx's Memphis SuperHub demonstrates a counterintuitive principle: the optimal hub location isn't the geographic midpoint of your territory but the population-weighted center. Memphis sits near Wright County, Missouri — the statistical average location of every US resident. This means FedEx minimizes total delivery time across all customers, not just distance to the farthest points.

This principle generalizes beyond logistics. Any business placing physical infrastructure — warehouses, data centers, service depots — should weight placement by demand density rather than geographic coverage. A hub that's 'central' on a map but far from population clusters wastes resources serving empty space. The same logic applies to digital infrastructure: CDN edge nodes are placed near population centers, not evenly distributed across geography.
