Formula 1 teams maintain five identical sets of shipping containers (sea kits) containing chairs, tables, appliances, and garage infrastructure. These are shipped by ocean freight months in advance and rotate between flyaway race venues in a daisy-chain pattern — as each race finishes, its kit ships to the next unserved destination.

This is a classic inventory pre-positioning strategy applicable to any business with recurring multi-location operations. The cost of duplicating relatively cheap equipment (5x containers) is trivially small compared to the cost of air-freighting everything on tight timelines. The principle generalizes: when you face a recurring time constraint, ask whether duplicating the cheap inputs eliminates the need to rush the expensive ones.
