Toyota's Production System succeeded through multiple interlocking principles: autonomation, production leveling, cross-functional teams, a culture of stopping to fix problems, and the elimination of *excess* (not all) inventory. Most manufacturers adopted only the most visible and shareholder-friendly element — slashing inventory — while ignoring the supporting pillars that made it resilient.

This is a general pattern: when organizations copy a successful system's outputs without its foundations, they get the fragility of tight coupling without the resilience mechanisms. The 2020-2021 shortage cascade demonstrated this at global scale — the auto industry's skeletal inventory approach meant a single disruption (chip supply) cascaded into rental car shortages in Alaska, thousands of miles from the original bottleneck.
