Southwest's rolling schedule (8-12 flights per hour, evenly spaced) eliminates the idle time between banks that legacy carriers accept. Once a plane lands, it immediately loads and departs — no waiting to synchronize with a bank. This means Southwest needs fewer total planes to operate the same number of flights. The principle extends beyond airlines: any capital-intensive business can compete on cost by maximizing the productive hours of its most expensive assets rather than optimizing for network effects or premium pricing. The tradeoff is that rolling hubs cannot offer the short connection times that attract premium connecting traffic, so this strategy works best when your customer base is price-sensitive point-to-point travelers rather than connection-dependent business travelers.
