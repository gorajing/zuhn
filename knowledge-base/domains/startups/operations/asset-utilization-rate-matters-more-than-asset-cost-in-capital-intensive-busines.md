A single Ryanair 737 might fly 8 legs in a day (Brussels-Copenhagen-Brussels-Prague-Brussels-Nimes-Brussels-Treviso-Brussels), with only 30-45 minutes between landing and next takeoff. This extreme utilization means each aircraft generates far more revenue-hours per day than a legacy carrier's equivalent plane sitting at a hub gate for 1-2 hours between flights.

The insight generalizes beyond aviation: in any capital-intensive business, the utilization rate of your most expensive asset often matters more than its purchase price. Non-reclining seats, no seatback pockets, and boarding-pass-at-home policies aren't just cost cuts—they're turnaround-time optimizations that increase daily flight cycles. Every operational decision flows backward from the question 'does this keep the expensive asset productive?' This reframes cost optimization from penny-pinching to throughput maximization.
