Scott's CRM journey at GTMFI: started with HubSpot ('good but clunky and expensive'), moved to Attio ('amazing — but it's a Ferrari, and we didn't need a Ferrari'), then moved again to Clarify.ai ('a Honda — fit for where we actually are'). The principle: matching the tool to the stage matters more than picking 'the best.' Attio is engineering-friendly and a delight to use, but if you're a 5-person company doing 50 deals/quarter, the depth of Attio's architecture is paying for value you can't yet absorb. HubSpot's plug-and-play marketplace wins for 80% of business users who want pre-built integrations and don't want to architect. The deeper insight: over-tooling early is a procrastination mechanism — operators tell themselves 'once we have this powerful CRM set up, the deals will follow' when actually the deals come from talking to customers and the CRM is just a way to keep track. Pick the cheapest, simplest tool that solves your current bottleneck, and upgrade only when you can name the specific feature gap that's actually blocking you. Nathan Lippi's parallel insight from the GTM Engineering Secrets episode: he loves Attio's engineering feel but runs his own business in a spreadsheet because HubSpot's plug-and-play wins for 80% of use cases and Attio's flexibility costs more setup time than he can spare. Right-sizing is the meta-skill.
