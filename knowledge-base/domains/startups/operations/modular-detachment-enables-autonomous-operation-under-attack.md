The presidential motorcade's 'secure package' — the cluster of vehicles directly protecting the president — is designed to detach from the broader 40-50 vehicle formation and operate as a fully independent unit under attack. It carries its own medical staff, security detail, counter-assault team, and communications.

This mirrors a critical principle in system design and startup operations: your core capability should never depend on your full infrastructure being intact. When things go wrong, you need a pre-defined minimal viable unit that can function autonomously. Startups that architect their operations with a detachable core (essential team, critical systems, key customer relationships) can survive disruptions — funding crises, key employee departures, infrastructure failures — that would destroy organizations where every function depends on the whole.
