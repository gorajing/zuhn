The US Antarctic Program routes all operations through McMurdo Station as a central hub. Large C-17 cargo planes fly from Christchurch to coastal McMurdo (5 hours). From McMurdo, specialized LC-130 ski-planes serve inland stations like South Pole (2 hours), while a 995-mile compacted snow road handles oversized or bulk cargo via tractor convoys (40 days). Each transport mode is selected for the constraints of its leg—intercontinental range, soft-snow landing capability, or sheer volume at low cost. This layered architecture is far cheaper than attempting any single mode end-to-end. The pattern applies broadly: startups operating in constrained environments (regulatory, geographic, technical) should decompose their delivery chain into legs and optimize each independently rather than seeking a universal solution.
