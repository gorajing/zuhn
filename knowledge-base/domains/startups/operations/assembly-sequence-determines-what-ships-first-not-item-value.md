When F1 teams pack their three priority pallets for air freight, they don't prioritize by value or fragility. They pack the items needed earliest in the assembly sequence: wall paneling, core electrical systems, and IT/communications equipment. These form the skeleton that everything else attaches to.

This inverts the common instinct to protect or prioritize the most expensive assets. In any operation with sequential assembly or deployment, the bottleneck is whatever blocks downstream work. Shipping your most valuable component first is pointless if it can't be installed until the infrastructure it depends on arrives. The lesson for startups: when planning any rollout or migration, map the dependency graph and sequence shipments/deployments by what unblocks the most downstream work, not by what feels most important.
