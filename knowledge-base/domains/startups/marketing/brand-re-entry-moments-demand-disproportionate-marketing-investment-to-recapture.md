When Nokia planned its 2017 return, they invested in contracting MOTHER—one of the UK's largest independent advertising firms with clients like Google, Microsoft, and Coca-Cola—for a full marketing blitz. This reflects a critical insight: brand re-entry is asymmetric. You don't get a second chance at a comeback narrative.

The video explicitly notes that companies frequently 'screw up major opportunities like this because they just don't get the marketing and advertising right.' The re-entry window is narrow—consumers will briefly pay attention to the comeback story, and the advertising must be memorable enough to convert that fleeting attention into purchase consideration. Under-investing in this moment means the brand returns to obscurity permanently.
