The traditional purchase funnel was a multi-step linear process the buyer walked through: a Google search created awareness, opening many tabs and reading reviews built consideration, a comparison spreadsheet drove evaluation, and finally purchase. Each stage was a touchpoint where a brand could intervene — SEO, content, reviews, retargeting.

AI shopping collapses all of these into a single consultative chat thread. The LLM synthesizes awareness, comparison, and evaluation in milliseconds, and the customer reaches a recommendation without ever visiting the brand's site during the earlier stages. The speaker's verdict: this is undeniably preferable for customers (it compresses days of work into one query) and therefore not reversible.

The strategic consequence for brands and marketers: you are no longer convincing the customer at each funnel stage — you are convincing the AI agent that assembles the recommendation. The brand's website, look-and-feel, and time-on-site (which the funnel optimized for) become far less relevant because the customer isn't spending time there. What matters is whether the agent surfaces your product when it synthesizes the answer.

This is the demand-side framing of the same shift the SEO→agent-optimization insight covers on the supply side: when an agent mediates the funnel, the optimization target moves from the human's attention to the agent's reasoning.
