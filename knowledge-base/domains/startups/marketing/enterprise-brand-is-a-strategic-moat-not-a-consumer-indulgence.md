The default playbook in B2B enterprise is to dismiss brand as a consumer concern and focus on features, deployments, and references. McDermott argues the opposite: brand is DNA, it's what you're trying to convey to a global buyer base in the simplest way possible, and the reason consumer brands feel bigger than enterprise brands is that enterprise CEOs have systematically underinvested — not that the opportunity isn't there.

ServiceNow's approach is instructive: a multi-year brand ambassador relationship with Idris Elba, commercials aimed at positioning ('AI in every corner of the Enterprise'), and a tagline ('the world works with ServiceNow') that grounds the value proposition in a story any buyer can repeat. McDermott's stated ambition is to make ServiceNow a top-10 global brand — note that this is an enterprise-only company with no consumer surface area at all. The operating theory is that if your product genuinely serves the world's largest companies, you should be able to compete with consumer brands for mental real estate.

The implication for B2B founders: budget brand as a first-class line item, not a residual of the marketing budget. The enterprise brands that already do this (Salesforce, HubSpot, Stripe) get disproportionate pipeline returns precisely because competitors have abdicated the space. And brand is one of the few enterprise assets that compounds without a marginal cost per customer.
