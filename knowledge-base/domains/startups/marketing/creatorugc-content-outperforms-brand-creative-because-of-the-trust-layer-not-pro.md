Creator and user-generated content (UGC) has become a top e-commerce priority — marketplace advertisers (Amazon, Walmart, TikTok Shop) run constant creator-content engines, and DTC brands partner with agencies specifically to source creators. The mechanism the panelist identifies is not production quality (brand content is often higher-production) but the trust layer: consumers trust a credible-seeming person's recommendation more than the brand's self-promotion.

This matters precisely because of the companion insight on GenAI collapsing production cost. If production quality is now cheap and abundant, quality stops being the differentiator — and trust/authenticity becomes the scarce input. Creator content's edge is that it's perceived as authentic peer testimony, which brand-produced content (no matter how polished, no matter how AI-accelerated) structurally cannot replicate.

The open tension the panel raised: as the internet fills with AI-generated 'creator-style' content, does the trust layer erode? If consumers learn that creator-style content is often synthetic, the authenticity premium could collapse — possibly pushing high-consideration purchases back toward in-store tactile experience. So the trust layer is an asset to borrow now but one whose half-life depends on how visibly AI saturates the channel.

For operators: invest in genuine creator relationships over synthetic creator-style content; the former compounds trust, the latter risks poisoning the channel's authenticity premium for everyone.
