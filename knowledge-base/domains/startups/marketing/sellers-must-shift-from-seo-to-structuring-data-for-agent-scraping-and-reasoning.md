When a human browsed, the seller optimized for human attention: brand look-and-feel, persuasive messaging, time-on-site, SEO keyword ranking. When an agent scrapes 30 product pages and synthesizes a recommendation, those signals largely stop mattering. What matters is whether the agent can quickly extract the critical product information and find an explicit, reasoned differentiation to justify recommending you.

The concrete shifts the speaker names:
1. Structured data and clean DOM — the agent must scrape critical product attributes quickly; messy or JS-buried data loses.
2. Explicit differentiation — vague brand messaging ('premium quality, trusted since...') gives the agent nothing to reason on; specific, comparative differentiation ('X% more Y than competitor category') gives it a reason to surface you.
3. Matching specific queries — customer queries are now hyper-specific ('housewarming gift for a friend in this area, this age group, who likes these hobbies'), so product descriptions must carry the specific attributes that match such queries.

The speaker's emphasis: 'the industry has not fully captured the gravity of this.' This is an early, actionable edge — most sellers are still optimizing for the human funnel. The reframe is from SEO (search engine optimization, ranking for human clicks) to what's effectively AEO (agent/answer optimization, being reasoned-about and surfaced by an LLM). The transferable principle: when the consumer of your content changes from human to agent, optimize for machine extraction and reasoning, not human persuasion.
