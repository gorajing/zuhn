The pre-LLM personalization stack ran on demographic and behavioral proxies: age, gender, country, and view/click history fed a model that predicted what you'd click next. The claim here is that LLMs change the substrate — the platform can now infer the goal behind a session (planning a Europe summer trip, shopping for a graduation) from semantic signals, and recommend against that intent rather than against a demographic bucket.

Why this is directional and not just incremental: demographic personalization and intent personalization rank inventory differently. A demographic model shows a 28-year-old woman what other 28-year-old women bought; an intent model shows whatever serves the Europe-trip goal regardless of demographic cohort. As intent-reading gets cheaper, the demographic approach systematically mis-ranks for any user whose current goal diverges from their cohort's base rate — which is most users most of the time.

For anyone doing growth/marketing: the diagnostic is whether your targeting is keyed on who the user *is* or what they're *trying to do right now*. The latter is the defensible position as LLM intent-inference commoditizes.
