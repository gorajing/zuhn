Across the 58 companies in a16z Speedrun Cohort 005, the overwhelming pattern is vertical AI agents: AI recruiter (Dex, Jooba, Ezra), AI auditor (Bead AI), AI consultant (Presia), AI supply chain (Sourcerer), AI fundraising (Metal), AI mining (URSA). Very few are building horizontal platforms or infrastructure. This represents a strategic bet that the value capture in AI happens at the application layer, not the model layer.

This pattern suggests that founders and a16z believe the moat in AI is domain expertise and workflow integration, not model capability. The implication for builders is clear: pick a vertical, understand its workflows deeply, and build an agent that replaces a specific expensive human function. The companies showing early revenue (Presia at $270K ARR in 3 months, Shockwave at $2.3M revenue) validate that vertical specificity accelerates go-to-market.
