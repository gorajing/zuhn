The software industry has followed a clear pricing evolution: on-premise licenses → SaaS subscriptions → usage-based fees. AI is forcing the next step toward outcome-based pricing, where the customer pays for results rather than access or consumption. This shift is driven by two forces: AI agents can now complete discrete tasks with measurable outcomes, and customers increasingly resist paying per-seat when AI replaces the humans who previously occupied those seats. The companies leading the AI agent 'land grab' are racing to claim tasks with clear, measurable outcomes precisely because those tasks support this pricing model. The winner in any vertical will likely be the one that bundles enough outcome-priced tasks to create an AI workforce platform with economies of scale.
