Horowitz predicts significant M&A activity driven by AI disruption. Unlike previous technology cycles where only certain sectors were disrupted, AI threatens every company and every incumbent simultaneously. The response is not primarily internal R&D but acquisition — companies need to 'acquire the DNA of the future' because they need to fundamentally reconstruct how they work to survive.

This creates a favorable dynamic for AI startups: even companies that don't achieve massive independent scale have acquisition value because incumbents desperately need AI capabilities embedded in their operations. The M&A wave is structural, not cyclical, because the threat is universal and the capability gap between AI-native startups and traditional incumbents is difficult to bridge through organic development alone.
