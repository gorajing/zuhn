The article argues that while the market is flooded with generic AI chatbots, writing assistants, and design tools, enormous white space remains in vertical-specific applications. A legal AI content generator or a real estate listing assistant would provide more value than yet another general writing tool because they encode domain-specific knowledge, compliance requirements, and workflow patterns.

The key strategic insight is that competitive advantage in AI startups increasingly comes not from the AI itself (which is commoditizing) but from three differentiators: clear niche focus, unique proprietary data sources, and deep integrations that make the tool indispensable within an existing workflow. Healthcare, legal, and financial verticals are especially promising because they combine high demand with regulatory barriers that create natural moats—though they also require human oversight to manage accuracy risks in sensitive domains.
