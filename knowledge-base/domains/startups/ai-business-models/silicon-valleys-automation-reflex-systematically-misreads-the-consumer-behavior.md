Bill Gurley's observation, endorsed by Gary: there are countless websites charging $6 for passport photos making tens of thousands a month even though AI can do the same task free, because consumers still default to 'go to website, pay, get thing.' Silicon Valley operators who live near the frontier systematically underestimate this lag and talk themselves out of opportunities the 99.9% of people who've never heard of Bill Gurley would happily pay for. The actionable arbitrage is building in the gap between technical possibility and consumer habit change, particularly brand-driven discovery, which Gary argues explodes in importance during the transition period before defaults shift to 'just ask the main AI.'
