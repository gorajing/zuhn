Ron from Open Router shared that after studying 100 trillion tokens of inference usage, they found that inference is rapidly becoming the dominant operating expense for AI companies. Companies that were spending $1,000/week on inference suddenly spend $1,000/hour when their apps go viral. Open Router estimates 60-80% of operating costs for their top-ranked companies come from inference.

This reframes how founders should think about unit economics. While inference costs are high and growing with agentic usage, the counterbalance is that these companies operate with dramatically fewer people — some doing $10-100M in revenue with just 5 people. The net effect is a fundamentally different cost structure where compute replaces headcount as the primary expense. Founders building AI-native businesses need to treat inference budgeting with the same rigor as headcount planning in traditional startups.
