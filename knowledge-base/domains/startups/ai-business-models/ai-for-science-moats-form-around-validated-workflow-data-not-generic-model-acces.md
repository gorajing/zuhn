The system runs across four LLM backends and still depends on chemistry-specific validation, MLFF protocols, benchmark manifests, and diagnostic histories. The paper also notes that hosted LLM services can change, so frozen configurations and reproducibility artifacts matter.

For AI businesses in scientific domains, the moat is the compounding validated workflow layer: failure traces, measurement adapters, domain schemas, and reference checks. Generic access to capable models is structurally temporary unless it is tied to proprietary or hard-won domain feedback.
