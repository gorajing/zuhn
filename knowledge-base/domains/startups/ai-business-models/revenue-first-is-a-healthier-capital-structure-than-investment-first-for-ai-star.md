The default sequencing of seed-round-then-traction was a response to the cost of building anything at all. Now that two people can ship a paying product in 30 days, the order should be reversed: generate revenue first, then decide whether to raise to accelerate. Companies that raise before revenue often optimize for investor narrative rather than customer reality, which produces worse companies. The healthier path in the AI era is to validate commercially first and treat outside capital as an optional accelerant, not a prerequisite.
