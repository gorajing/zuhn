Gary Vee predicts the AI economy will produce two simultaneous dynamics that look contradictory but aren't. On one side, vibe-coding and cheap tooling create a long tail of micro-businesses where kids build monetizable mini-apps without developers, analogous to how social media made everyone a publisher in 2008. On the other, supercalers (Meta, Microsoft, Google, Apple, plus Chinese equivalents) plus ~25 extreme-scale winners absorb disproportionate value. The squeeze happens in the middle: mid-market agencies like VaynerMedia and the 30-year SaaS run (Adobe, Salesforce, ServiceNow) walk into a buzzsaw as foundation model companies absorb their functionality. The implication for operators is positioning: either go hyper-small and own a niche that benefits from AI leverage, or accept you're competing against the supercalers.
