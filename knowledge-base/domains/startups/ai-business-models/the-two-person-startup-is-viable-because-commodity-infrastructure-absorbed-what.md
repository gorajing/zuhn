Pre-2020 startups needed large teams because they had to build their own infrastructure: databases, payment systems, servers, authentication, analytics. Now these are commodity services, and foundation models have commoditized the next layer (intelligence itself). The result is that the only work left with real differentiation is (1) turning an idea into a working product and (2) finding and closing customers. Two people — one builder, one go-to-market manager — can cover both. Larger teams at this stage are not leverage, they are overhead that slows down iteration. Founders should resist the instinct to hire before revenue.
