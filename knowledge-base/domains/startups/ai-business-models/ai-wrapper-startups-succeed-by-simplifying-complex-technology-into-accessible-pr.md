The rise of AI wrappers represents a familiar pattern in technology: the abstraction layer often captures more value than the infrastructure it sits on. AI wrappers take foundational models and package them into vertical-specific tools that solve concrete problems without requiring users to understand the underlying technology.

This mirrors historical patterns where middleware and application layers captured outsized value relative to infrastructure providers. The key insight for founders is that technical moats in AI may matter less than distribution moats and domain expertise. A wrapper that deeply understands healthcare workflows or legal document processing can be more defensible than the model it uses, because switching costs come from workflow integration, not model performance.
