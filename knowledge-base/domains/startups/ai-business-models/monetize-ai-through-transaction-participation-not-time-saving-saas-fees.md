Selling time-saving AI tools creates a pricing ceiling: customers pay based on hours saved, which commoditizes quickly as competitors offer similar automation. The superior model uses AI-driven efficiency to unlock participation in higher-value downstream activities — embedding marketplaces, fintech, or transaction facilitation into the workflow.

TailorBird illustrates this: by generating complete as-built plans instantly, they don't just save architects time — they enable landlords to fill buildings faster and make more money. The value capture shifts from 'we saved you X hours' to 'we helped you close Y more deals.' Companies that connect increased efficiency directly to revenue growth can command pricing tied to value created rather than time saved, fundamentally changing the unit economics of AI-powered vertical software.
