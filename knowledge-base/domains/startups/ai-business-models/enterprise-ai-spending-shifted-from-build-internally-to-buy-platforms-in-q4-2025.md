Q4 2025 saw $23.6 billion in enterprise AI-related deals, driven primarily by CIOs who tried and failed to build AI capabilities internally and are now purchasing platforms with autonomous digital labor built in. Salesforce's Agentforce is the canonical example — enterprises want agents that plug into their existing stack rather than science projects that require ML teams to maintain.

This buy-over-build shift has two major implications. First, it validates the platform play for AI-native startups — the market is ready to pay for packaged agentic capabilities rather than raw model access. Second, it signals that the copilot era was a transitional phase. Enterprises experimented with AI assistants, found them useful but insufficient, and are now demanding autonomous systems that execute rather than suggest. The spending trajectory suggests this is not a temporary spike but a structural shift in how enterprises allocate software budgets.
