Angela Strange at a16z makes the case that the next generation of financial services infrastructure winners will be 10x larger than current category leaders. The expansion comes from two sources: software categories themselves are merging (KYC + transaction monitoring + customer service become a unified risk platform), and software can now consume labor budgets that previously went to humans for tasks that were either undesirable or chronically understaffed.

This creates a massive TAM expansion for AI-native platforms. The early movers are already proving it: some mortgage servicers increased margins from 5% to 50% by adopting new platforms. The competitive dynamic is stark — companies that fix their infrastructure plumbing gain compounding advantages while competitors take 2-3 years to catch up. For founders, the formula is: deeply understand an archaic corner of banking or insurance, rearchitect the platform from the ground up for AI, unify fragmented data, and capture both software and labor spend.
