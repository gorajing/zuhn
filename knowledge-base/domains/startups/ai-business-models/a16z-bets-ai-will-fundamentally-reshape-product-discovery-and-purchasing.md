a16z's new investment thesis centers on AI transforming the entire commerce stack — from product discovery through purchasing. Rather than incremental improvements to existing e-commerce, this envisions AI fundamentally changing how consumers find, evaluate, and buy products.

For founders, this signals where smart money is flowing: AI-native shopping experiences, personalized product recommendation engines, and tools that help retailers leverage AI for merchandising and customer engagement. The thesis suggests the winners won't be existing e-commerce giants adding AI features, but startups building AI-first commerce experiences from scratch.
