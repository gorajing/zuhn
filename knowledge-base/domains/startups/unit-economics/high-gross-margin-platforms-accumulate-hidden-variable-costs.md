Ek pushes back on the assumption that UGC platforms enjoy clean high-gross-margin economics that improve with scale. At peak, Meta reportedly had around 100,000 content moderators — costs that feel fixed but actually scale with content volume and grow as adversaries get more sophisticated (AI-generated spam forces more sophisticated AI moderation, and so on). He estimates that starting a social media company today might cost an order of magnitude more per user than it did at Facebook's IPO because the ad platform, moderation tooling, and trust-and-safety bar have all risen. The operational lesson for anyone evaluating platform business models: don't trust the 'platonic form' of a 95% gross margin software business when the product involves user-generated content. Model the variable costs of moderation, ad ops, and safety as a function of both scale AND adversarial sophistication — they compound, and the 'fixed cost' framing will mislead you into over-promising margin expansion.
