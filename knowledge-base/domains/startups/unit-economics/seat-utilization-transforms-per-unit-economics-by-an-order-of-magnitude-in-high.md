Walmart's Learjet 45 costs ~$11,600 for a 2,900-mile day trip. With one passenger, the break-even salary is ~$1M/year. At full capacity of nine, it drops to $111K — less than an average Walmart store manager's salary. The economics shift from 'luxury for C-suite' to 'tool for middle management.'

This pattern generalizes to any high-fixed-cost, low-marginal-cost asset: private infrastructure, SaaS platforms, manufacturing equipment. The first unit of utilization is always the most expensive. Businesses that can aggregate demand against fixed-cost assets unlock economics unavailable to those who can't. The strategic question isn't 'can we afford this?' but 'can we fill it?'
