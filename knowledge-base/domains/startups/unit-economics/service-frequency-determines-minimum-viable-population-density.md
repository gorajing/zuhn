A Starbucks in Manhattan needs roughly 6,000 people within a 300-foot radius because coffee is a daily purchase. An Apple Store in Connecticut needs ~500,000 people within a 10-mile radius because laptop purchases are infrequent. This 80x population difference maps directly to purchase frequency, not just price. For startups, this means your addressable market density matters as much as total TAM — a product used weekly needs far fewer users per geography than one used annually. You can reverse-engineer viable launch markets by estimating your service frequency and the minimum population density needed to sustain operations, rather than chasing the largest possible market.
