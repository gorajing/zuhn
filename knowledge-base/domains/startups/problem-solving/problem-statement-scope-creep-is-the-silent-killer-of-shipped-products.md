Rachitsky describes building an Airbnb host dashboard where the original problem — reducing host response time — kept getting lost as scope expanded. The solution was ritualistic re-anchoring: every design review starts with the problem statement, every stakeholder update reviews it, and before finalizing designs you explicitly ask whether the current direction solves the original problem.

This is Step 3 of the framework ('keep coming back to the problem') and it's the step most teams skip. Initial alignment feels like the hard part, but maintaining alignment through weeks or months of execution is where most projects drift. The problem statement and success metrics together form a decision filter: does feature X increase the chances of hitting the defined success criteria? If not, cut it. Without this discipline, dashboards become classic 'silver burrito' problems where every stakeholder adds their interpretation until the product is bloated and unfocused.
