Before discovering shade balls, LA's water department explored increasingly complex novel solutions: floating tarps, PVC pipe trampolines, shredded pipe debris fields. Each custom solution introduced new problems (bird perches, petri dish conditions, structural challenges). The breakthrough came when Dr. Bryan White found an existing product — 'bird balls' — already manufactured for keeping waterfowl off mine tailings ponds and airport retention ponds.

The existing product had already solved the manufacturing, material safety, UV resistance, and wind stability problems. By transferring it to a new application rather than inventing from scratch, they skipped years of R&D and got a battle-tested solution. This pattern appears repeatedly in innovation: the best solution to your problem often already exists in an adjacent market. The research effort should go into finding existing solutions before designing new ones.
