When Apple Music, Tidal, Google, and Amazon all entered music streaming with essentially the same catalog, Spotify retained market leadership largely through its free tier. Ninety percent of revenue came from paid subscriptions, meaning the free tier was a net loss. But it served as a remarkably efficient acquisition funnel — users got hooked on the product for free, then converted to paid to remove ads and get offline access. Competitors offering only paid tiers faced a much higher barrier to initial adoption. The counterintuitive lesson is that in commodity markets where the core product is undifferentiated (same songs everywhere), the conversion mechanism becomes the competitive moat. Record labels actually forced Spotify to deliberately worsen the free tier to drive more conversions, which paradoxically made the free-to-paid funnel even more effective as a business model.
