Topline growth metrics (user count, subscription signups, daily active users) admit many adversarial optimization paths: more push notifications, deceptive email ('problem with your account'), manipulative annual-subscription flows that assume users will forget to cancel. These tricks reliably move the graph up while providing no user value. Retention metrics are structurally different — they require the user to return voluntarily, which means the product must have actually solved something. The practical rule: if your organization's primary metric can be moved with dark patterns, you are designing a company that will end up with a turkey-shaped revenue curve. Pick retention or time-to-value or problem-solved-rate as the anchor metric, and optimize topline growth only after that number is healthy. The inverse ordering systematically produces slop.
