Paul Graham argues that startups are made of people, and the average age of startup founders and early employees falls in the 25-29 bracket. He points to how this demographic shifted Silicon Valley's center of gravity from the peninsula to San Francisco — founders who personally preferred suburban life forced themselves to move to SF because that's where the talent was.

This reframes startup ecosystem development from a top-down policy problem to a bottom-up talent attraction problem. The implication is that cities should optimize for livability factors that attract young people (walkability, cultural scene, affordable housing) rather than startup-specific incentives like tax breaks or accelerator programs. The youth percentage becomes a leading indicator: if it's rising, the startup ecosystem will likely follow.
