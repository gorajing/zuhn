Linear acquisition channels like paid ads, outbound, and content marketing hit diminishing returns because each new customer requires roughly the same marginal effort. Growth loops break this constraint by making each user's activity recruit additional users automatically.

The Rows.com example illustrates the power of stacking loops: usage-based (share a sheet), collaboration (invite teammates), user-generated (template gallery), and referral (program incentives). Each loop type feeds the others—a shared template attracts new users who then collaborate and share their own templates. The compounding effect means the cost of acquisition decreases over time rather than increasing.

The key insight is that growth loops are not a scaling-stage luxury—they should be architected into the product from the GTM fit stage. Waiting until scaling to bolt on virality mechanics is far harder than designing them in when you're establishing repeatable motions.
