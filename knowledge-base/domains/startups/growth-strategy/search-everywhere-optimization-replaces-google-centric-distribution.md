The article frames the biggest SEO mistake of 2025 as over-reliance on Google for traffic. As AI-generated answers reduce clicks to traditional search results, brands need presence across multiple discovery surfaces: email newsletters (owned audience independent of algorithms), YouTube (high-intent visual learners), LinkedIn and Reddit (active advice-seeking communities), and AI chat interfaces themselves.

This is essentially a platform risk argument applied to search. The same logic that warns against building entirely on someone else's platform applies to building entirely on Google's organic results. The concept of 'search everywhere optimization' means showing up not just on traditional search engines but across social platforms, marketplaces, and AI-powered interfaces. The brands that survive the AI search transition will be those that built direct audience relationships before their Google traffic disappeared.
