The 4-stage model (problem-solution fit → PMF → GTM fit → scaling) implies that acquisition strategies are not interchangeable across stages. At problem-solution fit, your 'channel' is literally your phonebook—personal network outreach to validate that the product works. Trying to run paid campaigns or build content funnels at this stage doesn't just waste money; it generates vanity metrics that mask whether you've actually solved a real problem.

At product-market fit, the goal shifts to proving repeatable value delivery through retention and early referrals, not acquisition volume. MedAsk's approach of spending just $200 on Google Ads to get signal, then leveraging satisfaction data to pitch partners, shows the discipline of matching spend to stage.

The framework's power is in what it tells you NOT to do. Most startup failures in GTM come from premature scaling—deploying Stage 4 tactics at Stage 2. The model gives founders a diagnostic: if you can't identify which stage you're in, you're probably misallocating resources.
