In 1998, Viaweb paid a PR firm $16,000 per month because getting mentioned in magazines and newspapers was the primary way to acquire users. There were no effective ways to get found online — their own SEO advice listed seven search engines that mattered, none of which was Google (incorporated that same September).

This is a powerful reminder that distribution channels are not permanent infrastructure but temporary windows. The PR-driven acquisition that Viaweb depended on became nearly irrelevant within five years as Google search and later social media emerged. Founders who over-index on mastering the current dominant channel without building direct relationships or brand equity risk having their growth engine disappear overnight when the platform landscape shifts.
