Physicians Choice's Amazon subscription base approaches $100M/year — customers who bought once, liked the product, and now receive it automatically every month without comparison shopping. This changes the fundamental nature of the business: predictable revenue, predictable inventory needs, predictable cash flow. You are not starting from zero each month.

The product selection filter should be: do people need to buy this again? Is there a daily use case? Will they run out and need more? If the answer is no, you are building a business that must constantly hunt for new customers — 'a treadmill where if you stop running, the business stops.' Subscriptions also create switching friction: to change brands, customers must cancel, find something new, set it up, and trust it works. Most people do not bother unless genuinely disappointed. The difference between hunting for new customers and compounding on a recurring base is 'the difference between having a job and having an asset.'
