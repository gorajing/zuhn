Tudor spent years shipping ice to the Caribbean with almost no sales because people did not understand the product. His breakthrough was giving ice away for free to bartenders so they could sell iced drinks alongside room-temperature ones at the same price. Customers overwhelmingly preferred cold drinks, and as Tudor himself wrote: 'A man who has drank his drinks cold at the same expense for one week can never be presented with them warm again.'

This is the purest form of product-led growth: remove the friction of understanding by letting the product demonstrate its own value. The free trial is not a discount — it is an investment in creating irreversible preference. Tudor's insight applies directly to any product where the value is experiential rather than intellectual.
