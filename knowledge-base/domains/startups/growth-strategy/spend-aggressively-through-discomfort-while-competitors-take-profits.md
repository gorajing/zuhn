Physicians Choice spends $70M/year on advertising in 2026, including $3-4M/month on Amazon ads alone. Their first year, they did $3M revenue and lost $1M. The philosophy: every dollar of profit not reinvested is a dollar a competitor could use to catch up. Most brands hit a revenue milestone and start extracting profits, buying luxuries, getting comfortable — and that is exactly when they become vulnerable.

The math works because of customer lifetime value. Break even on the first sale is acceptable when the customer comes back for the second, third, and tenth purchase. The profit comes from repeat purchases, not the initial acquisition. Wanting every ad dollar to immediately return profit means accepting a small lifestyle business. Domination requires spending at a level that 'feels uncomfortable' because competitors will not match it. The Amazon bestseller badge creates a flywheel: being #1 helps you stay #1 because customers trust the badge, which drives more sales, which reinforces the position.
