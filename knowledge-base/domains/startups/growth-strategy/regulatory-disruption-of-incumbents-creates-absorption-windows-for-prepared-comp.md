The Airbnb regulatory crackdown in Korea (requiring lodging business licenses that most studio/officetel owners lacked) created a massive displacement event. EncoWithers saw this coming in August 2024 and spent over a year preparing: validating the hypothesis that displaced hosts would seek alternatives, building the product, and positioning for the absorption.

The key is that preparation preceded the opportunity by over a year. 'Timing and luck always come — they keep passing by, and the difference is whether I see it or not.' This pattern recurs: regulatory shifts create predictable displacement waves, and the winners are those who prepared infrastructure before the wave hits, not those who react after.
