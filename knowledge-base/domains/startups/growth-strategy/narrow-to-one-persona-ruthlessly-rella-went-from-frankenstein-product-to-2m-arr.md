Natalie's biggest mistake was modeling after Notion's 'for everyone' positioning before having the resources to support it. Trying to serve influencers, talent managers, in-house teams, and content creators simultaneously created feature confusion, high churn, and no clear identity. Influencers said 'love it' but never paid.

The pivot: ask 'who has the biggest problem AND is willing to pay?' — not just who says they need it. Social media managers had deep enough pain that they used Rella despite bugs. After narrowing to that single persona, the team could build coherent features, craft targeted messaging, and grow fast. The lesson: start as small as possible with one persona, prove you can serve them excellently, then expand. Stated interest is noise; credit card willingness is signal.
