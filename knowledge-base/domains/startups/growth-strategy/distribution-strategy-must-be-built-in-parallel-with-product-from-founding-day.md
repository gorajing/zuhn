The traditional startup sequence of build-first, sell-later has been replaced by a parallel execution model. Elite startups now hit $1M ARR in under 9 months with burn multiples below 1.0, meaning they generate more revenue than they burn. This is only possible because founders treat distribution as a core product feature from day one, not as a problem to solve after achieving product-market fit.

The Gong vs. Clari example illustrates this perfectly — comparable products, but Gong's aggressive distribution execution made them the category definer while Clari became perceived as the discount alternative. In AI especially, early buzz means nothing without sustained distribution execution. The implication for founders is clear: your first hire conversation should include distribution strategy, and your first sprint should include channel experiments.
