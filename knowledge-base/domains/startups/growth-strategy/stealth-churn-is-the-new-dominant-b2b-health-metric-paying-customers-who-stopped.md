Calacanis's reframe: he used to consider MAU/WAU/DAU 'the dumbest metric' for B2B and demanded revenue. Now he wants the opposite — he wants engagement metrics growing faster than revenue. Why? AI accelerates the gap between mental churn and contract churn. He himself has been paying for Netflix for years but watches YouTube; he's paying for OpenAI but moved his team to Claude four months ago; people are 'stealth churning' off Canva, Adobe, Figma while the contracts roll over.

The operational diagnostic: if your revenue is growing but your engagement isn't, you're hiding behind annual contracts. The cliff is approaching. Conversely, if engagement is growing faster than revenue, that's the strongest possible signal that your product is winning in the AI era. For builders: rebuild executive dashboards around engagement-to-revenue ratio, not just revenue. For investors: demand engagement disclosure in diligence, not just ARR. For Zuhn editorially: this is direct ammunition for Issue 2 (attribution) — the proxy metrics that worked in the cloud era (revenue, retention, NPS) systematically lag in the AI era because AI tools deliver value through usage intensity that contracts don't capture.
