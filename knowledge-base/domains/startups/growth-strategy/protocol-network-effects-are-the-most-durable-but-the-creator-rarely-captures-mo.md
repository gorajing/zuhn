Protocol network effects demonstrate a paradox: they're among the most defensible network effects (fax protocol and TCP/IP persist despite superior alternatives existing) yet the protocol creator usually captures very little of the value generated. Ethernet's creator 3Com built a successful company, but the vast majority of value flowed to the ecosystem of Ethernet-compatible product manufacturers.

The manual identifies specific levers for value capture: maintaining ownership of tokens (as in crypto networks), controlling addressing/identity/naming systems, or retaining authority over prioritization. Bitcoin's value capture works because the protocol and the token are inseparable. The implication for founders is that if you're building a protocol or standard, the adoption strategy matters more than the technology — VHS beat Betamax through marketing and market niche selection, not technical superiority. Design value capture mechanisms into the protocol from day one, because retrofitting them after adoption is nearly impossible.
