Naval explicitly states his wealth was not generated by one big year or one giant payout, but by consistently creating businesses, investments, and opportunities that pile up over time. This mirrors the mathematical reality of compounding: many small positive-expectation bets compound faster than waiting for a single large one.

This has direct implications for startup founders and investors. The strategy of building multiple revenue streams, equity positions, and capability assets — even if individually modest — creates a portfolio effect that is both more resilient and more likely to produce wealth than betting everything on a single venture. The compounding also applies to skill and reputation: each project builds on the last, creating accelerating returns that look like luck to outside observers but are actually systematic accumulation.
