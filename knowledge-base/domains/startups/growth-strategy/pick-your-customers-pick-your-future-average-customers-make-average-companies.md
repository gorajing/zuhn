Founders under pressure to grow default to the broadest acquirable audience. This reliably increases short-term user counts and decreases long-term positioning options, because the company's culture, product priorities, and messaging drift toward serving the median customer. The median customer wants cheaper, more average, less demanding output — and once the company is organized around that customer, it cannot easily pivot to a higher-value segment. The counter-strategy: pick the customers whose standards match the ambition you have for the company. A specialist wine business capping at 130,000 subscribers and $30M revenue deliberately refuses growth beyond its quality ceiling. This is not anti-growth — it is pro-position. The practical rule: in the first 2 years, customer selection matters more than customer volume, because the customers you acquire today shape what you are allowed to become.
