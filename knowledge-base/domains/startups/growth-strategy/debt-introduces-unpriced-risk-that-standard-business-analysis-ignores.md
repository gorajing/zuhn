Ramsey presented an insight to Vanderbilt MBAs that stunned them: in public markets, we routinely risk-adjust returns using beta (a 2.0 beta fund's 20% return gets discounted vs. a 0.8 beta fund's 11%). But in business and real estate, nobody applies this math. The standard analysis is primitive: borrow at 2%, invest at 8%, pocket 6% — ignoring inflation, taxes, and critically, the risk premium of leverage. Ramsey's personal experience validates this: he went bankrupt in his 20s from overleveraging real estate with short-term notes. Warren Buffett's formulation captures it: 'any number multiplied by zero equals zero.' Over a long enough time horizon, even small leverage risk eventually realizes. This is why Ramsey Solutions survived the 2020 pandemic — 'bring on a pandemic, I got cash.'
