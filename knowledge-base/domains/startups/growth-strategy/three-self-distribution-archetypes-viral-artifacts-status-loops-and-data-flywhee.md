---
Viral artifacts are outputs that spread awareness without additional effort — every MidJourney image shared on Discord or ElevenLabs voice clip posted on TikTok is simultaneously content and marketing. The tactic is to build watermarking, share buttons, and attribution into outputs by default so every use generates discovery.

Status loops occur when features give users something to show off — Clay operators flex their 'intelligence stacks' on Twitter, and Notion AI early adopters showcased AI-generated notes as aspirational. The tactic is to design features that produce artifacts users are proud to display professionally.

Data flywheels emerge when more usage makes the product better — Duolingo's learner feedback improves its models, Grammarly's corrections train better writing suggestions. The tactic is to ensure every feature emits structured data that feeds back into system improvement. The critical insight is that these three types compound at different rates and serve different strategic purposes, so founders should choose deliberately based on their ICP rather than hoping organic growth 'just happens.'
