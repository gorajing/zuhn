Samsung's pattern across multiple industries was consistent: enter a market others had pioneered, learn the trade through persistence, and iterate until achieving market leadership. They became the world's largest memory chip producer (1993), largest TV manufacturer (2006), and largest smartphone manufacturer (2011) — none of which they invented.

This challenges the Silicon Valley emphasis on first-mover advantage. In hardware and manufacturing, the ability to observe existing solutions, invest heavily in R&D ($4B in 2014 alone), and execute incremental improvements at scale can be more valuable than originating the category. The prerequisite is patient capital and willingness to absorb years of being a second-tier player, which Samsung's conglomerate structure uniquely enabled.
