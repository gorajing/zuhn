The Latin American startup ecosystem's growth is driven by what insiders call 'mafias' — deliberately small networks organized through WhatsApp with 6-10-20 people sharing tips, knowledge, and connections. These networks work precisely because they're small: trust is maintained, information quality stays high, and coordination is tight enough that members know each other deeply rather than superficially.

This mirrors early Silicon Valley's structure and offers a replicable pattern for ecosystem building anywhere. The key insight is that these networks provide both practical value (where to live, how to navigate local bureaucracy) and strategic value (introductions, deal flow, market intelligence). Each network is intentionally scoped — Chilean women in Mexico City, Latin American entrepreneurs expanding to Mexico, etc. — creating high-context groups where relevance is guaranteed. The energy of small groups collaborating is what gives participants an edge and helps their businesses move fast rather than slow.
