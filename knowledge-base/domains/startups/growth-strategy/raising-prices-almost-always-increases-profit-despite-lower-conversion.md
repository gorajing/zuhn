Hormozi demonstrates the math: if a product costs $500 to deliver and sells for $1,000 (50% margin), doubling to $2,000 triples profit per sale from $500 to $1,500. Even with a 35% conversion drop, total profit doubles — and you serve fewer customers with lower operational costs. He routinely tests 4-5x price differences, not timid 10-20% increases, because pricing for non-commodity products is far more inelastic than founders assume.

The compounding threat of not raising prices is equally important: $100 in 2017 is equivalent to $79 in purchasing power by 2024. A business running 20% margins that doesn't adjust prices has zero profit after 7 years of inflation alone. Warren Buffett raised See's Candies prices 50 years consecutively — sometimes 17% in a single year — generating over $1 billion in cumulative profit. The emotional barrier is hearing 'no' more often, but the math overwhelmingly favors higher prices.
