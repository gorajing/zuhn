RuBisCo evolved when atmospheric oxygen was negligible and worked 'well enough' at fixing carbon. When oxygen levels rose, it started grabbing O2 instead of CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts. Yet plants doubled down — producing enormous quantities and evolving workaround pathways rather than replacing the enzyme.

This mirrors technical debt in startups: once a core system is deeply integrated, organizations build elaborate workarounds (C3/C4/CAM photosynthesis pathways) rather than rewriting. The lesson is that 'good enough first' strategies create path dependencies that compound. The earlier you recognize a core component will be permanent infrastructure, the more its initial design quality matters.
