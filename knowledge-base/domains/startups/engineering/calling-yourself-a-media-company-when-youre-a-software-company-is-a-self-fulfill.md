Yahoo called project managers 'producers' and divisions 'properties' — media industry language that shaped how the company actually operated. Programmers became translators of product manager specs rather than creative problem-solvers. This wasn't just branding; it determined org structure, hiring priorities, and who had decision-making power. The contrast with Google is stark: at roughly the same headcount (~500 people), Google programmers asked 'what should we do?' about search gaming, while Yahoo programmers built what was spec'd. The deeper lesson is that corporate identity is performative — the label a company chooses for itself reshapes incentive structures, hiring filters, and cultural norms until the label becomes true. Yahoo didn't just call itself a media company; it became one, and media companies don't build great software.
