There's a structural asymmetry in how companies reward value creation: a salesperson who closes a billion-dollar deal gets commissions, bonuses, and prestige, while an intrapreneur who builds a billion-dollar new business line often gets a standard salary bump at best. This makes entrepreneurship outside the company far more attractive.

The fix requires designing explicit career tracks where internal venture builders can earn outsized rewards proportional to the value they create. Without this, large companies face a permanent adverse selection problem — their most entrepreneurial talent leaves to capture upside elsewhere, leaving behind people who optimize rather than create.
