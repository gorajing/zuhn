Dario's framework implies a radical shift in what makes employees valuable. If AI handles 90% of what employees do today, the remaining 10% — judgment calls, taste decisions, strategic direction, AI orchestration — becomes the entire job description. Writing 90% of the code means nothing if the job is only 30% code; the rest is design, debugging, communication, and leadership.

This reframes hiring from 'who can execute tasks fastest' to 'who can make the best decisions about what AI should do and evaluate whether it did it well.' The experienced-developer paradox is instructive: senior devs were actually 19% slower with AI tools despite believing they were faster, likely because their established workflows conflicted with AI-augmented ones. The winners won't be the best coders — they'll be the best directors of AI coding. This has immediate implications for interview processes, performance reviews, and team structure.
