Graham argues that Google's 'Don't be evil' wasn't just PR — it was a competitive advantage in hiring. Technology companies win by attracting the most productive people, and those people preferentially choose employers with visible ethical commitments. The Patent Pledge operates on the same mechanism.

This has direct implications for startup strategy: voluntary ethical constraints that seem costly are actually investments in employer brand. The most productive engineers and designers care about what their work enables and what their employer stands for. A company that publicly commits to not weaponizing patents against small competitors signals a culture that values building over litigating — exactly the culture top builders want to join. The constraint is the feature.
