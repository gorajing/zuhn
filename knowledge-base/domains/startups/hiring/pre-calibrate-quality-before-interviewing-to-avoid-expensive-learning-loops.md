Ben Silbermann describes a specific tactic: before hiring for any role (especially ones outside your expertise like finance or marketing), find people who are de facto world-class in that discipline and ask them what key traits they look for, what questions they ask, and where the next great person in their field is working right now.

This matters because learning what 'good' looks like through the interview process itself is extremely expensive — expensive in founder time and in candidate time. It also leads to costly hiring mistakes. The pre-calibration approach is especially critical for non-technical roles where technical founders lack intuition. Silbermann also suggests using quantitative reference questions ('Is this person top 1%, 5%, or 10% of people you've worked with?') to create forced scarcity that produces materially more useful signal than generic reference checks.
