Graham notes that the same qualities making fierce nerds effective — competitiveness, impatience, independent-mindedness — also make them annoying in conventional settings. His prescription is to seek out organizations and work where getting the right answer matters more than preserving social harmony, which in practice means small groups working on hard problems.

This is a hiring and organizational design insight. Many companies optimize for cultural harmony and screen out abrasive candidates, inadvertently filtering their most potent problem-solvers. The solution isn't to tolerate toxicity universally but to create specific environments — small, hard-problem-focused teams — where directness and competitiveness are assets. Startups naturally provide this environment, which partly explains why 7 of the 8 richest Americans are fierce nerds who built companies.
