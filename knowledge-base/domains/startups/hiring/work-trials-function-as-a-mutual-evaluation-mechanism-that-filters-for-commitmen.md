Matias Woloski (Auth0) acknowledged that requiring significant time investment means losing some candidates, but argued that for startups where commitment is critical, willingness to invest time is itself a valuable filter. Sahil Lavingia (Gumroad) takes this further by giving trial candidates access to company financials, culture, and internal dynamics — making the trial as much about the candidate evaluating the company as the reverse.

This reframes the common objection that work trials are too demanding. The 'cost' of the time investment is actually doing useful work: filtering out candidates who aren't deeply interested while giving serious candidates the transparency they need to commit with confidence. Matt Mullenweg explicitly called it a 'two-way street' that enables confident decisions on both sides.
