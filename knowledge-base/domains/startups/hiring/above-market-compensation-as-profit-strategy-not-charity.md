Costco's average wage of $21/hour is double the US retail average, with better health insurance and retirement benefits. Rather than a charitable gesture, this is a calculated investment: Costco employees are three times more productive than the industry average. The math is straightforward — 2x the cost for 3x the output is a 50% efficiency gain.

This challenges the common startup instinct to minimize labor costs, especially in operational roles. High compensation attracts better talent, reduces turnover (which is extremely expensive in retail), and creates employees who genuinely care about the customer experience. The lesson extends to any business with high employee-customer interaction: paying above market isn't generosity, it's arbitrage on the gap between compensation and productivity.
