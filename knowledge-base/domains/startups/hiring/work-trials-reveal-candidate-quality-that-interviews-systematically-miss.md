Multiple founders independently converged on the same realization: interview performance and job performance are weakly correlated. Matt Mullenweg (Automattic) noted they were being influenced by charm and speaking manner that had no bearing on actual work. James Hawkins (PostHog) found it 'frequently surprising how someone performs relative to what we thought in the interviews.'

The mechanism is that interviews test a narrow skill — presenting yourself — while work trials test the actual skill set needed for the role. Linear found this especially critical for assessing whether someone is 'truly a builder' with good taste and judgment, qualities that are nearly impossible to evaluate in a conversational interview format. The implication is that companies relying solely on traditional interviews are systematically selecting for interview skill rather than job skill.
