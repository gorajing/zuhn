The AI skills divide has created a paradoxical labor market: record numbers of CS graduates and widespread tech layoffs coexist with extreme scarcity of specialized AI talent commanding salaries up to $500K. Mid-career engineers without AI specialization find themselves increasingly sidelined.

This winner-take-most dynamic has strategic implications for startups. Competing for elite AI talent against Big Tech's compensation packages is often futile, so startups must either find AI talent before they become 'prodigies' (bet on potential), build training pipelines that upskill existing engineers, or architect products to minimize dependence on scarce AI expertise. The bifurcation also suggests that the traditional career path of generalist software engineering is under structural pressure.
