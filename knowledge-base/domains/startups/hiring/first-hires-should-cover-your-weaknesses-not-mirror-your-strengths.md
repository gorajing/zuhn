Corcoran's advice — 'Decide what you're good at, hire the opposite' — sounds like common wisdom, but the implementation detail matters. She frames it not as general team diversity advice but as a specific early-stage survival strategy: your first three hires should each eliminate a specific risk that your personal skill gaps create.

This is counterintuitive because founders naturally gravitate toward hiring people they understand and can evaluate — which means people like themselves. Clones feel comfortable in interviews and create cultural harmony, but they replicate blind spots instead of covering them. The practical framework is to list your strengths and weaknesses honestly, then only hire from the weaknesses column, tying each hire to a concrete risk they neutralize. When cash is tight, barter equity or trade favors — the key is getting blind spot coverage early, not waiting until you can afford perfect hires.
