Patrick Collison argues that the first 10 employees are disproportionately important not just for their individual contributions, but because each one brings along roughly 10 more people who share their values and working style. This creates a compounding cultural effect where early hiring mistakes propagate exponentially.

This reframes early hiring from 'find someone good enough to ship code' to 'choose the archetype of the next 100 people.' It means founders should think about what 90 additional people they want those first 10 to attract, and optimize hiring criteria accordingly. The implication is that speed-of-hire in the earliest days is almost always the wrong optimization — getting the cultural DNA right matters far more.
