The speaker's co-founder describes AI as 'one of those crazy mirrors where any one of your attributes it makes 10 times larger — if you're a great engineer AI makes you great, if you're not it makes you sloppier.' This directly contradicts the common assumption that AI commoditizes engineering skill and levels the playing field. Instead, AI is a multiplier on a pre-existing base: it amplifies good judgment and good taste, but it also amplifies carelessness, producing more bad code faster. The operational consequence is that Tenex makes hiring 'incredibly difficult for ourselves so that everything else is easy' — including, critically, so that an output-based comp model doesn't get gamed by people willing to cut corners. The actionable takeaway: as AI raises the variance of individual output, invest the saved time at the front of the funnel (hiring) rather than the back (review and remediation), because a high enough hiring bar makes downstream incentive problems tractable.
