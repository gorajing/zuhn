The same 1/(1-n) formula applies in reverse for employee grants: if a hire improves average company outcome by factor i, they're worth n = (i-1)/i in equity. A hire who improves outcomes by 20% is worth up to 16.7% equity at breakeven. After subtracting salary costs (multiply annual salary+overhead by 1.5 to convert to stock equivalent) and a profit margin, you arrive at the actual offer.

This approach replaces the common practice of using VC-supplied benchmark tables or gut feel with a principled calculation. While Graham acknowledges the numbers require estimation, the framework forces founders to explicitly quantify what they believe a hire is worth to the company. It also reveals why early employees taking lower salaries is so important—every dollar of salary directly reduces the equity that could be offered to them.
