The Roman Empire's military shift from citizen-soldiers to paid mercenaries is often cited as a key factor in its decline. Citizens fought because they had a stake in Rome's survival; mercenaries fought for pay and had no inherent loyalty to the institution. When external pressure mounted, mercenary armies fragmented rather than holding together.

This maps directly to modern organizational dynamics. Companies that aggressively hire mercenary talent — people optimizing for compensation rather than mission — find that their organizations become brittle under stress. When a downturn hits or a competitor offers more, mercenary employees leave. The lesson isn't to underpay people, but to ensure your organization cultivates genuine buy-in alongside competitive compensation. Culture-fit hiring and equity alignment aren't just HR platitudes — they're institutional resilience strategies with 2000 years of historical validation.
