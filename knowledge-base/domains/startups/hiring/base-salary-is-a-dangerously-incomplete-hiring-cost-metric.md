When founders say 'we're hiring an engineer at $150K,' they're quoting perhaps 60-80% of the actual cost. Payroll taxes, health benefits, dental, vision, bonuses, and annual raises compound into a fully-loaded cost that can be dramatically higher than the number discussed in the offer letter.

This gap is especially dangerous because it compounds across the entire team. If you're underestimating per-hire cost by 25% across 10 hires, you've miscalculated your burn by the equivalent of 2-3 additional salaries. The fix is modeling fully-loaded compensation from day one — treating every hiring decision as a total-cost commitment, not a base-salary line item. This is particularly critical pre-fundraise, where investors will scrutinize whether your runway model reflects reality or wishful thinking.
