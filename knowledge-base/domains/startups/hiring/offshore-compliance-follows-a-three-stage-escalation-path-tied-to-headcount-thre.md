The compliance path for offshore hiring has three distinct stages, each appropriate for different scales. Starting with contractors is cheapest and simplest — you pay them like any vendor. As headcount grows, an Employer of Record (platforms like Deel or Remote.com) prevents misclassification risk by formally employing workers with proper taxes and benefits. At critical mass, setting up a local legal entity becomes cost-effective despite the upfront complexity.

The key operational insight is that these transitions are not optional — they are compliance boundaries. Staying on contractor arrangements too long creates legal exposure (worker misclassification), while jumping to entity setup too early wastes resources. The thresholds are country-specific, so companies need legal and tax counsel for each market. This staged approach lets startups begin offshoring immediately with minimal overhead while building toward a permanent international presence.
