Every time Sequoia helps a portfolio company hire a VP of Engineering, they ask one question: who are the five smartest peers you respect in this role? Every answer goes into their CRM, and they've done this for over ten years across roles, stages, and geographies.

The result is a talent map that works like PageRank for people — individuals who are repeatedly nominated by other high-performers surface to the top, often years before they become publicly visible. The critical insight is that the delay is the feature: most firms abandon this practice before the signal compounds. This is a powerful example of how boring, consistent data collection creates irreplaceable proprietary assets over time.
