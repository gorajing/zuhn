a16z's core investment principle — evaluate on magnitude of strength — applies directly to hiring and talent evaluation. The rule is binary: never pass on world-class talent because of a fixable weakness, and never invest in non-world-class talent just because they lack obvious flaws. This reframes evaluation from risk-minimization to upside-maximization.

The practical exercise is to spend the first 20 minutes of any evaluation meeting discussing only strengths before allowing any weakness discussion. This counteracts the natural human bias toward pattern-matching on flaws, which produces safe but mediocre selections. The same principle explains why unclear monetization or unproven go-to-market shouldn't be disqualifying — those are fixable weaknesses, not fundamental talent deficiencies.
