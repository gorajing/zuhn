Horowitz draws a sharp line between two CEO archetypes: those who try to coach everyone up, and those who relentlessly seek out people who are already exceptional. The latter consistently build better companies. This is counterintuitive because most leadership advice emphasizes developing your team, but at the CEO level, the highest-leverage activity is talent selection, not talent development.

This connects to his concept of managerial leverage—the CEO's time is the company's scarcest resource. Every hour spent trying to fix a low performer is an hour not spent finding someone who would transform the role. The 'sad truth about developing executives,' as Horowitz has written separately, is that most executive skills are developed over decades and can't be coached in months. Recognition of this reality is what separates pragmatic CEOs from idealistic ones.
