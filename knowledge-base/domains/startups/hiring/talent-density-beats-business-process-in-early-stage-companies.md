Larry and Sergey's hypothesis was radical: hire all the smartest people, point them at measurable problems, and figure out the business stuff later. The results 'won't be perfect, but they'll be optimal.' This was experimentally verified by Google's success.

This challenges the MBA worldview where strategy, process, and org design come first. For early-stage startups, the implication is to over-index on talent quality and under-index on process. The 'other stuff that business schools think business consists of' can be learned or hired for later, but raw intellectual horsepower in the founding team is irreplaceable. The key constraint is measurability—this only works when you can objectively evaluate output.
