High turnover destroys team efficiency through constant onboarding, knowledge loss, and disrupted workflows. Offshore hires — particularly in regions where competitive-salary roles with international companies are highly valued — tend to have longer tenure than U.S. equivalents in comparable back-office roles.

More importantly, this creates a retention flywheel: adding offshore capacity reduces the workload and burnout on local team members, which makes them stay longer too. The compounding effect means that the ROI of offshore hiring extends well beyond the direct salary savings — it stabilizes the entire team's tenure curve. This is most pronounced in finance and accounting where the U.S. talent shortage makes domestic retention especially difficult.
