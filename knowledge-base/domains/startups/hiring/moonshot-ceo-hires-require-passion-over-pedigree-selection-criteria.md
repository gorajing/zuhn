When describing the ideal first CEO for Halo, Vasco Pedro explicitly deprioritized conventional qualifications in favor of someone who 'cares so deeply about what they do, it's to the point where others that don't care as much might think they're a bit weird.' This filtering heuristic — selecting for intensity that appears socially abnormal — is a pattern seen across successful moonshot companies. The logic is sound: breakthrough products require leaders who will persist through the long trough of technical uncertainty where rational actors would pivot or quit. The 'weirdness' test is actually a proxy for intrinsic motivation depth — mercenary executives optimize for career optics, while missionaries optimize for the problem itself. Combined with Vasco's requirement for 'solid entrepreneurial instincts' and comfort with investors, the profile is specifically a missionary who can operate in a mercenary world.
