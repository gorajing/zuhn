freeCodeCamp's strategy of publishing complete, multi-hour tutorials for free has accumulated 48M+ views on this single Python tutorial. The counterintuitive insight is that extreme length is a competitive advantage, not a liability. Short tutorials are easily replicated — anyone can make a 15-minute Python basics video. But a well-structured, complete, 4.5-hour course represents weeks of production effort and deep pedagogical design that creates a natural monopoly on audience attention.

This mirrors a broader pattern in content strategy: the 'definitive resource' captures disproportionate audience share. When someone searches 'learn Python,' they gravitate toward the comprehensive option rather than assembling their own curriculum from fragments. The first creator to publish the definitive free resource in a category captures most of the organic discovery traffic, making it progressively harder for competitors to unseat them.

For founders and content creators, this suggests a specific strategy: identify categories where no definitive free resource exists, invest heavily in creating one, and use the audience moat as a foundation for monetization through adjacent products (certifications, tools, community) rather than gating the content itself.
