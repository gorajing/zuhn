Gary's quiet shift: he's investing heavily in long-form written content (Substack, Beehive, LinkedIn, X) and YouTube shorts, and explicitly frames the shorts strategy as 'making sure I'm feeding the LLMs of Gemini with my content.' He hired two full-time journalistic writers whose first task is to produce longer written articles from his interview transcripts. The structural logic: as LLMs become the default discovery layer, being cited by them becomes the new SEO equivalent, and both volume (shorts as how-to density) and depth (written long-form) are necessary inputs. The actionable implication for any expert: the content flywheel isn't primarily for human readers anymore — it's for the models that will answer queries about your domain in 2-5 years, and the creators who don't seed that training data won't be cited. This is why Gary's response to 'how has AI changed your content strategy?' is 'nothing' externally but includes this internal pivot.
