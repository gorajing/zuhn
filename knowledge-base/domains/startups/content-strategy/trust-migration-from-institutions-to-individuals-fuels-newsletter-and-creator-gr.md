The 2024 Edelman Trust Barometer reveals a striking inversion: 64% of respondents worry journalists are purposely trying to mislead them, while 74% trust 'someone like me' to tell the truth—on par with scientists. This trust gap is driving consumers toward newsletters, independent creators, and niche experts as primary information sources.

This isn't a temporary trend driven by any single platform or political cycle. It reflects a structural shift in how people evaluate information credibility—favoring perceived authenticity and domain expertise over institutional authority. For anyone building content or audience, this means the barrier to becoming a trusted source has never been lower, but the expectation for genuine expertise and transparency has never been higher. The creators who thrive will be those who combine deep niche knowledge with consistent, authentic communication.
