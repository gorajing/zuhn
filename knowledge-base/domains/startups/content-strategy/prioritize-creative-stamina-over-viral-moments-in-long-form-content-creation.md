Lenny shifted from 80% curiosity-driven / 20% audience-driven topic selection to 100% curiosity-driven, because he learned that individual viral posts come and go but longevity is what compounds. After 5+ years of weekly publishing, he frames this as playing 'infinite games'—optimizing for how long you can sustain the work rather than how well any single piece performs.

This is counter-intuitive because most content strategy advice emphasizes audience research and data-driven topic selection. But for solo creators where the person IS the brand, burning out on topics you don't care about kills the business. The implication is that creator-founders should treat their own energy and curiosity as a scarce resource to be protected, not overridden by analytics.
