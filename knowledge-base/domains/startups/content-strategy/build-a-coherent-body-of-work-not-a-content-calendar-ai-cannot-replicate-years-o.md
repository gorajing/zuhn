Most content advice optimizes at the piece level: this headline, this hook, this thumbnail. The piece-level optimization treats each output as substitutable, which makes the creator substitutable. The body-of-work advice optimizes at the corpus level: across 200 essays, is there a coherent philosophy a reader could describe? Can a new reader predict what position you'll take on an unfamiliar topic? Have your recurring themes developed over time? This corpus-level coherence is what AI cannot replicate — not because AI cannot write a coherent essay, but because coherence across 200 pieces requires a through-line that emerges from a specific human's evolving thinking. The Zuhn Intelligence Brief positioning implication: Issue 1 (Retrieval Trap) and Issue 2 (AI's DoubleClick Moment, planned) should be designed to compound — each brief should strengthen and extend the arguments of previous briefs rather than function as standalone pieces. Over 50+ briefs, the coherent corpus becomes the moat; individual briefs are not.
