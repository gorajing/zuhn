Graham offers a concrete editing process: write your first draft naturally, then review each sentence asking 'Is this the way I'd say this to a friend?' Replace anything that fails with what you'd actually say in conversation. For drafts too far gone for sentence-level fixes, explain the whole piece to a friend and replace the draft with what you said.

The key insight is that this filter becomes internalized with practice. After enough passes, you start hearing the 'clank' of unnatural sentences as you write them. The technique also extends to phonetic quality—reading aloud catches rhythmic awkwardness even when the content is fine. This transforms writing quality from a taste judgment into a mechanical process.
