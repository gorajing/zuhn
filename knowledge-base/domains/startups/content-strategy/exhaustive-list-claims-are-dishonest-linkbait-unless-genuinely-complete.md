Graham identifies a specific dishonesty in list formats: claiming exhaustiveness when you haven't earned it. Titles like 'The 7 Secrets of Success' trigger a reflexive challenge — readers must check whether they already know all 7, creating an irresistible pull. This is the same psychological mechanism as a whodunit.

The distinction matters for content creators building long-term trust. Using 'The' before a number is fine if you genuinely believe you've catalogued everything. But most such titles are linkbait, and audiences eventually learn to distrust writers who over-promise completeness. The honest alternative is framing lists as partial: 'Some things I've learned about X' signals intellectual humility while still delivering value.
