The framing Lenny uses to introduce Claire Vo is telling: 'unlike many people online, she doesn't only talk about using AI—she lives and breathes it.' This distinction—between practitioners who share their learning in real time versus commentators who analyze AI from a distance—is becoming a primary credibility signal for audiences trying to evaluate whose guidance to follow.

As AI content has proliferated, audiences have developed sensitivity to the gap between people who use AI to do their actual work and people who use AI as a topic. The former accumulate tacit knowledge that surfaces in demos, edge-case handling, and honest failure stories. The latter tend toward evergreen takes and hype cycles. For anyone building a media brand or thought-leadership platform in the AI space, the signal quality of their hands-on practice is the most durable differentiator.
