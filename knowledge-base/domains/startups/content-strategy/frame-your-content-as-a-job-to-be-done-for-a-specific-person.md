Lenny categorizes successful newsletters by the job they perform: help me parent better, help me cook better, help me understand tech, inspire me, entertain me. His own newsletter's job is helping people build and grow their product and career. The framework forces clarity — you must identify a specific person and articulate the concrete job you're doing for them.

This is the jobs-to-be-done framework applied to content, and it works because it shifts focus from the creator's identity ('I'm a PM who writes') to the reader's outcome ('this helps me get better at work'). It also naturally guides topic selection, editorial decisions, and what to cut — anything that doesn't serve the job gets removed.
