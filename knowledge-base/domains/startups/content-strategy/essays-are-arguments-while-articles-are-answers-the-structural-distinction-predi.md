The structural distinction: articles start with the conclusion the writer already believes and package it for the reader. Essays start with uncertainty and work toward a position the writer did not hold when they began. The writer changes their mind through the writing process; the reader witnesses that change. AI can write articles reliably — it packages existing knowledge from training data into readable prose. AI cannot write essays, because essays require the writer's situated point of view, the lived experience that produces opinions worth writing, and the genuine cognitive work of changing one's mind through the act of composition. For Zuhn Intelligence Brief and any similar strategic writing output, the durability versus AI replacement depends on which form the piece is operating in. A brief that restates known positions (an article disguised as an essay) loses to AI. A brief that genuinely works through a new position (an essay) does not.
