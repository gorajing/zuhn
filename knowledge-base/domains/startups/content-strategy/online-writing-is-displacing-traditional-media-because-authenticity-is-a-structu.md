Graham's newspaper friend identified the critical distinction: print media executives believed their readership decline was cyclical, but it was actually structural. The structural advantage of online writing is authenticity — people writing what they genuinely think, rather than 'mystery meat cooked up out of scraps of pitch letters and press releases.' Readers can sense the difference even without understanding the PR mechanism behind it. This insight has proven prophetic — the pattern has only accelerated with podcasts, newsletters, and social media. For content strategists, the implication is clear: authentic voice is not a nice-to-have but the fundamental competitive advantage against institutional content production.
