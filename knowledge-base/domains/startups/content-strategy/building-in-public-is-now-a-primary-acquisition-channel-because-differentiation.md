Verna attributes Lovable's first couple million ARR to the CEO being very public — provocative, open about what worked and didn't, transparent about hires. Her claim: when product becomes a commoditized layer, trust and differentiation come from the visible team, making social brand one of the strongest acquisition investments and 'builds in public' a near-resume requirement for early hires. Reinforces the KB's owned-audience/trust-capital and content-as-attention-surface principles.
