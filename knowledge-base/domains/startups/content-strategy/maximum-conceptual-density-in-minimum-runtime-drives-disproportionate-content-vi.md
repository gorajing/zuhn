The Egg delivers an entire metaphysical framework — reincarnation, universal identity, cosmic purpose, the meaning of suffering — in 8 minutes and 905 words. It has 35M+ views. This is a masterclass in content compression: every sentence advances the narrative or deepens the concept, with zero filler. The pattern generalizes to content strategy: the highest-performing content tends to have the highest idea-per-minute ratio, not the longest runtime. This is because attention is the scarce resource, and audiences reward creators who respect it. For startups and creators, the implication is to ruthlessly compress: find the minimum viable narrative that delivers the full emotional or intellectual payload. If an idea needs 45 minutes to explain, either the idea isn't distilled enough or the format is wrong.
