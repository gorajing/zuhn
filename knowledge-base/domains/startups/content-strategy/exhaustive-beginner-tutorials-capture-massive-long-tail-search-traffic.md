This single video accumulated 18M+ views over 8 years by being the definitive answer to 'C programming tutorial for beginners' — a high-intent, evergreen search query. Rather than splitting content into a 30-part playlist that fragments discovery, the monolithic format becomes a one-click commitment for motivated learners.

For content creators and developer-tools companies, this reveals a counterintuitive strategy: instead of optimizing for algorithmic recommendation via short, frequent uploads, create one exhaustive resource that dominates a specific high-intent query. The compounding effect is significant — the video likely generates more total watch-hours than a fragmented series would, because each new cohort of CS students finds and completes it as a single unit. This is the content equivalent of building a moat through comprehensiveness rather than frequency.
