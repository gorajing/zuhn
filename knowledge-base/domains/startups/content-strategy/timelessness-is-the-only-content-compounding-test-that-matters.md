Acquired's rule is that every subject must be an institution that will still matter in five years, and every episode must be 80% as relevant then as it is at release. This sounds obvious but has sharp consequences: they killed the Fed episode (too newsy), they kill episodes that don't have a throughline (Bell Labs), and they avoid anything where the story is still unfolding in ways that will date the analysis.

The payoff is that their back catalog still drives listens years later. Costco, LVMH, Hermès, Rolex — all keep compounding, which means each new release creates lift across the whole catalog rather than being consumed and discarded. This is the media-business version of the durable revenue base that lets them invest in quality without fearing a single bad episode sinks the business.

Apply this test before you commit research time: if this piece will be worth 2% of its value in a month, either drop it or find the timeless angle underneath the news hook. The Google series worked because it was timeless AND timely — but the timeless was the anchor.
