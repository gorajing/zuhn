HubSpot's strategy of publishing massive volumes of blog posts targeting high-volume keywords—even topics unrelated to their core product—worked for years. But when Google's algorithm updates began favoring people-first content and AI summaries started answering simple queries directly, their traffic plummeted. The content that buried answers under SEO padding and long intros was exactly what AI filters out.

Chegg's case is even more severe: their entire value proposition (homework help and study solutions) became automatable by ChatGPT. Students got the same answers for free, instantly. This illustrates a critical strategic distinction—HubSpot's problem was declining visibility (fixable by pivoting strategy), while Chegg's was existential replacement (their core offering became commoditized). Any business whose primary value is answering generic informational queries is now competing directly against AI itself.
