Lenny Rachitsky grew to 500K subscribers without tricks — his core thesis is that quality plus consistency explains nearly all content growth. He observes that 99% of content is mediocre, so the bar to stand out is achievable through sheer effort. He spends a median of 10 hours per post, with some taking hundreds, and sees a strong correlation between time invested and performance.

The implication is radical: any time not spent making the content itself better is wasted. Optimizing websites, crafting brand strategies, or building growth plans actively hurts growth by stealing time from the only thing that matters. Substack's value proposition is precisely this — it handles everything so the writer can focus exclusively on writing. This maps to a broader startup principle: do the thing that matters and ruthlessly cut everything else.
