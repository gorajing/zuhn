This single 4-hour tutorial has 13M+ views — roughly 2-3x what most paid C++ courses achieve in total enrollments. FreeCodeCamp's strategy of giving away exhaustive, high-quality content for free creates a paradox: by not charging, they build an audience asset more valuable than course revenue.

The mechanism is commitment-based trust. Anyone who watches 4 hours of free content has self-selected as genuinely interested and now associates the creator with generosity and competence. This creates a defensible moat — competitors offering shorter or paid alternatives can't replicate the trust built through that time investment. For any content creator or startup, the implication is clear: your best marketing asset might be giving away your most comprehensive work for free, not gating it.
