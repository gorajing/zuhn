Hormozi observed that U.S. presidential candidates who spoke at the lowest grade level consistently won elections — framing campaigns as massive brand exercises where comprehension breadth determines reach. He applied this to his own marketing and saw a 50% increase in email conversion by simply lowering the reading grade level without changing the substance.

The counterargument — 'I don't want to talk down to my audience' — is a false dichotomy. Simpler language helps experts understand faster and beginners understand for the first time. Nobody rejects clear communication. The practical test: run your emails through a reading grade analyzer and iterate until they're below fifth grade. Match analogies to your audience's experience — mechanics get car analogies, realtors get house analogies, broad audiences get universal experiences like food and sleep. This is Feynman's principle applied to marketing: if you can't explain it to a third grader, you don't understand it well enough.
