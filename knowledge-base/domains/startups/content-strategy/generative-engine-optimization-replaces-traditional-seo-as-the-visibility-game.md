Traditional SEO focused on getting your page to position #1 so users would click through. Generative Engine Optimization flips this: the goal is to be the source that AI systems pull from when constructing their synthesized answers. This means structuring content with clear headings, FAQ sections, definitions, and summaries that are easy for LLMs to parse and quote.

The practical shift is from keyword density to topical authority. Instead of targeting dozens of keyword variations with separate pages, you build comprehensive topic clusters that demonstrate deep expertise. Content must be factually accurate, well-sourced, and formatted for both human readers and machine parsing. The new success metric isn't click-through rate—it's 'reference share,' how often AI tools cite or summarize your content across platforms.
