Lara's 4-3-2-1 framework pins cadence at four posts per week explicitly because daily posting burns out founders and stops. Content is a compounding asset: the value comes from the accumulated library and the audience that builds trust through repeated exposure, not from any single viral hit. A four-year library at four posts per week is ~800 pieces — sufficient for algorithm visibility and depth — while a six-month library at daily cadence is ~180 pieces and already abandoned. The decision rule is to set the cadence that survives your worst week, not your best week, because content strategy dies from inconsistency, not from too-low volume.
