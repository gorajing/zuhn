Hormozi frames his current output (450 pieces per week, Guinness World Record for fastest-selling non-fiction) as not disconnected from but directly caused by his early cringe-worthy content. The causal chain: willingness to be bad in public produces iteration, iteration produces skill, skill enables scale. Creators who try to skip to the volume phase without tolerating the bad-output phase hit a wall because they haven't built the taste or muscle memory that makes high volume sustainable. Practical implication: if you want to produce at scale eventually, the investment you need to make now is tolerance for bad output, not better tools or systems.
