Hormozi's team found that simply cutting the first three seconds of a video — removing the preamble before the real hook — took it from 40,000 views to 780,000 views (19x improvement). This echoes David Ogilvy's principle that writing the headline spends 80 cents of your advertising dollar.

The hook formula that consistently works across their top YouTube videos is proof-promise-plan. This applies across all four advertising channels: warm outreach (open and respond rates), cold outreach (response rates), paid ads (click-through rates), and content (watch-through rates). A hook improvement from 1% to 5% CTR is a 5x business multiplier with zero additional spend. Advanced advertisers allocate a disproportionate amount of their time to the first frame and first impression because it both increases consumption of the rest and changes how everything that follows is perceived — they demonstrated this with conference speakers where changing just the 1-2 minute intro changed the entire session's net promoter score.
