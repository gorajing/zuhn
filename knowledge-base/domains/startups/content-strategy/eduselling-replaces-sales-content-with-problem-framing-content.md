Lara Costa's LinkedIn posts never said 'buy Claire.' They explained why most AI-generated content fails, why founders struggle with distribution, why similar tools produce similar output. Readers arrived at the question 'is there a better way?' on their own — at which point Claire appeared as the answer. This is structurally different from feature marketing: the reader does the persuasion work, so resistance collapses. The principle generalizes: any content that frames the problem vividly enough forces the reader into implicit solution-seeking, and whichever product is in their peripheral vision at that moment wins the mental default. Feature lists do the opposite — they trigger skepticism because the seller is doing the work.
