The speakers explicitly analogize the agentic-web moment to the responsive-design era: at some point you had to adapt your website for mobile, and if you didn't, the competition did and users went there instead. They argue the same dynamic is now forming around agents—you must optimize not just for human usability and SEO discoverability, but for how agents discover and act on your app (via LLMs.txt for content discovery and Web MCP for action exposure).

The strategic claim is about timing and option value, not certainty. Web MCP is barely usable and its spec is churning weekly, so this is a 'start experimenting so you're ready' bet rather than a build-it-all-now mandate. But the asymmetry mirrors past platform shifts: the cost of early, cheap experimentation is low, while the cost of being unprepared when agent-driven browsers arrive—and competitors are already agent-legible—is losing a new traffic channel. For founders, the takeaway is to treat agent-readiness as an emerging distribution surface and place small, early bets before it's table stakes.
