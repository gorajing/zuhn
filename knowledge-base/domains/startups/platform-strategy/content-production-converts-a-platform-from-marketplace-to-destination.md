Amazon's move into original content production (movies and TV series) is not primarily about entertainment revenue — it's about transforming the perceived identity of the Prime subscription. A logistics and retail subscription competes on speed, price, and selection. A destination that produces culturally resonant shows competes on identity and belonging.

This matters because it changes the substitution calculus. A competitor can match two-day shipping. No competitor can replicate Amazon's specific catalog of original content. Original production is therefore a defensibility play masquerading as an entertainment play.

For platform founders, the implication is that producing proprietary content — whether editorial, educational, or entertainment — is a way to graduate from commodity infrastructure to destination brand. The content doesn't need to be the primary revenue driver; its job is to make the platform irreplaceable in a way that pure utility cannot.
