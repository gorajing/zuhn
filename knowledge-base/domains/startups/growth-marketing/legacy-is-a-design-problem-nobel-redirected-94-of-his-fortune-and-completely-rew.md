When Nobel read his premature obituary calling him 'the Merchant of Death,' he saw exactly how the world would remember him. His response was decisive: he redirected 94% of his fortune (equivalent to $340M today) to create prizes for those who 'conferred the greatest benefit on mankind.' The result was total legacy transformation.

When people on the street were asked about Alfred Nobel, every single person said 'Nobel Prize.' Not one mentioned dynamite unprompted. As one interviewee observed: 'He took control of his legacy. He decided he wanted it to be a positive thing.' The lesson for founders and leaders: legacy is not a passive byproduct of your work -- it's an active design problem. What you build in your final act can completely overwrite what came before. But it requires genuine commitment of resources (94% of wealth), not just PR gestures.
