Conventional wisdom says keep onboarding short. Coconote tested the
opposite and found that doubling their onboarding to 15 screens
increased trial starts by 16%. The longer flow incorporated more
personalization questions, social proof elements, and investment
steps that built commitment before the paywall. Additionally, they
discovered that requiring login before the paywall caused a 10%
drop-off — users who had not yet seen the value proposition would
abandon at the friction of creating an account. Moving login to
after the paywall eliminated this leak. The principle: onboarding
is not a barrier to minimize but a conversion tool to optimize.
Each screen should build investment and intent so that by the time
users reach the paywall, they have already decided they want the
product.
