With Allen (scheduling software), Hormozi initially sold directly to small business owners but hit a wall: they needed leads before they could use a lead-management tool. The breakthrough was pivoting to sell to marketing agencies, each of which controlled 50+ small businesses. Then he went up another level, pitching to people with large agency audiences, where a single pitch could generate a thousand customers. Two webinar pitches drove the company to $1.7M/month within 6 months. This layered distribution strategy (end user -> agency -> audience owner) demonstrates how each level of intermediary multiplies reach exponentially. The same pattern appeared with Prestige Labs, where gym owners served as the exclusive distribution channel for supplements, creating a moat no other supplement company could replicate.
