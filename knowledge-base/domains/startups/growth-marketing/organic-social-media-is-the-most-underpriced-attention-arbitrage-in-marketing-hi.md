Throughout marketing history, reaching audiences required paying gatekeepers — newspapers, cable companies, billboards, direct mail. Social media broke this model entirely: distribution is free, and the only cost is content creation. Yet most businesses dramatically under-produce content relative to this opportunity.

The practical implication is that businesses should treat social media content production as a core operational function, not a side activity. The barrier isn't budget — it's volume, consistency, and craft. Mr. Beast spending $150K on thumbnail testing exemplifies the 'science of the art' — treating creative as a disciplined, measurable process rather than casual self-expression.
