Selling water to someone who just drank has no signal about whether your water is good — they're simply not thirsty. Lead magnets should create or amplify deprivation adjacent to the core offer, not satisfy it. A 'reveal a problem' diagnostic (free website speed test, posture analysis, financial audit) makes invisible problems visible and quantifies their cost, intensifying urgency as time passes. A 'free trial' creates deprivation by giving buyers a taste of a better state, then removing it. A 'one step of many' solves the first step of a multi-step process, leaving the remaining steps as unsatisfied needs. The common mechanism is that you must leave the buyer deprived of something your paid offer supplies — otherwise the lead magnet cannibalizes the sale. Hormozi's rule: solve a problem that reveals another problem solved by your core offer.
