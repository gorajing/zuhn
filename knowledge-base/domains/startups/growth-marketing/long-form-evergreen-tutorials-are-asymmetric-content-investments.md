This single 4.5-hour video has accumulated 48 million views since 2018 — roughly 6 million views per year, compounding as Python's popularity grew. The cost to produce it was finite (one instructor, one recording session), but the return is essentially perpetual because beginner Python content never goes stale.

For developer-facing startups or tools, this pattern suggests that investing in one truly comprehensive, well-structured tutorial yields dramatically more cumulative attention than a stream of shorter content pieces. The key properties are: (1) covers a topic exhaustively so it becomes THE reference, (2) targets an evergreen skill with growing demand, and (3) is structured for progressive consumption so viewers stay engaged across hours.
