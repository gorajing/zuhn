The conversation surfaces a fundamental shift in how companies are marketed: you can no longer just announce products and report financials. Since Steve Jobs, company marketing has become inseparable from a person — it's Tesla or Elon, Palantir or Alex. This creates a premium on founders willing to be that character.

However, the character must be authentic. a16z's approach was to figure out 'who is this person and what are they good at that they can sustain over time.' TED talks are the anti-pattern: beautifully manicured, formulaic presentations that work for a minute but don't build a real brand. The key test is sustainability — anyone can be coached through a single polished appearance, but a personal brand requires showing up consistently as yourself. If it's not actually you, it won't accrue to the brand over time.
