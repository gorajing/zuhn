Operators reflexively reject 'free work' because it feels like devalued labor, but the correct framing is to compute it as a customer acquisition cost. If a free service costs $25 in hard labor cost, has a $250 fair market value to the buyer, and converts 1 in 4 qualified prospects to paying customers, the effective CAC is $100 — cheaper than most paid acquisition channels for high-ticket B2B services. The rigor comes from (1) only extending the free work to qualified prospects (budget, authority, need, timing), and (2) comparing the implicit CAC against the alternative cost of acquiring the same customer through ads. Hormozi's personal history: he trained clients for free for a year to build testimonials, and when he ran out of capacity, nearly all of them converted to paying clients. The objection 'freebie seekers' is solved by qualification, not by refusing to give value.
