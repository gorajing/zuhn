As organizations scale, brand leaders face enormous pressure to compromise their core message. Real-world problems — 'accepting this order at full quality means a loss,' 'lowering ingredient quality saves money' — get conflated with brand essence conversations. Kwak's critical insight: separate the message from self-interest. Operational problems and brand essence are different problems that require different conversations.

The largest beauty company CEO Kwak visited had their core message displayed like the Ten Commandments: 'We exist for the betterment of our customers' lives.' Companies that can articulate their essence in one slogan can course-correct when they drift. Companies that lack such a slogan tend to organize around hit products instead, and when hits fade, there's no identity to fall back on. The Lego case study illustrates the principle: when Lego diversified into films, theme parks, and jewelry, they nearly failed. Recovery came from returning to one core identity — bricks — and building everything (including the IP business with Star Wars) on that foundation.
