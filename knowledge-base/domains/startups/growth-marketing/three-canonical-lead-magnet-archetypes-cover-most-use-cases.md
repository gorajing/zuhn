Lead magnets decompose into three archetypes with different psychological mechanisms. (1) Reveal a problem: diagnostics like website speed tests, posture assessments, financial audits — used when buyers don't know they have the problem or don't realize its severity. Best when the problem worsens over time (termites, taxes, posture) because that builds urgency automatically. (2) Free trial: a taste of the full product or service, restricted by time, volume, or feature set. Creates deprivation through the 'remove it' mechanism — buyers get used to having it and feel loss when it's taken away. Best when the product has habituation dynamics. (3) One step of many: solve the first step in a multi-step process (first laser hair removal session of 8, first coat of paint of 4). Best when the core offer is inherently sequential and one step is obviously insufficient. Physical products, software tools, information, and services are the four delivery mechanisms — any archetype can be delivered through any mechanism.
