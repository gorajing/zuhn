The shift from search engines to AI assistants as the primary discovery layer represents one of the most significant distribution changes in a decade. When AI models cite only 2-7 sources per query — compared to Google's ten blue links — the competitive dynamics change dramatically. Being source number 8 means complete invisibility rather than just lower click-through rates. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) requires fundamentally different strategies than traditional SEO: structured data, authoritative sourcing, clear factual claims, and content formats that LLMs can easily parse and attribute. For startups, this means rethinking content strategy from 'rank for keywords' to 'become a cited authority that AI models reference.' Early movers in GEO will capture disproportionate discovery as the transition accelerates.
