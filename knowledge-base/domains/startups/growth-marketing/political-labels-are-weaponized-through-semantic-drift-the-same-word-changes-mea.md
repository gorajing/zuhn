Pakman traces how 'liberal' used to mean what 'progressive' means now, but was weaponized into a smear, forcing him to adopt 'progressive' as his self-descriptor. Meanwhile, the socialist-leaning left now uses 'liberal' to mean 'not left enough.' The term 'leftist' is currently transitioning from neutral to negative. These labels evolve month-by-month and are used strategically to either start or stop conversation.

For founders and brand builders, this reveals a critical dynamic: the language you use to position yourself is subject to semantic drift driven by adversarial actors. A term that resonates positively today may become toxic tomorrow. The defense is the same one Pakman uses: explicitly define your terms each time rather than assuming shared meaning, and be willing to update your vocabulary when terms get captured. Brand positioning requires constant linguistic maintenance.
