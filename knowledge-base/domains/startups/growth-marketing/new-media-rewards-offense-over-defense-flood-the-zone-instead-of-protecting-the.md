Ben Horowitz describes a16z's existential crisis: the NYT leaked early fund results, misinterpreted them, and there was no way to correct the record. That experience made a16z hyper-defensive about information control for years. But the physics of media have completely changed. In the old world, 8 channels (NYT, WSJ, CNN, etc.) had permanent monopoly on narrative. What they published was top of Google, uncorrectable, and permanent.

In the new world, Marc and Ben could go on 30 podcasts — all with bigger audiences than those publications — and talk about something more interesting, erasing the old narrative from collective memory. The strategic implication is stark: old media strategy (say nothing, protect the brand, don't upset anyone) will kill you in new media. New media rewards being interesting, being direct, flooding the zone with content. a16z committed fully: they stopped caring about old media channels and went all-in on new media. You cannot play half-and-half because the defensive posture of old media is actively toxic in the new environment.
