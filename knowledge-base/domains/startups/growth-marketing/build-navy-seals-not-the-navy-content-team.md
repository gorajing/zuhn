Coconote's content strategy was built around a small, tight team of
5-10 content creators — not a large roster. The founders met with
each creator 1:1, sharing specific data on what content converted
and what did not. This feedback loop meant creators got better over
time, compounding their effectiveness. A large team creates the
opposite dynamic: quality dilutes, communication overhead grows,
and each creator gets less direct feedback. The metaphor is apt —
build Navy SEALs (small, elite, high-trust) not the Navy (large,
bureaucratic, standardized). This approach also keeps costs
manageable while maximizing output quality per creator.
