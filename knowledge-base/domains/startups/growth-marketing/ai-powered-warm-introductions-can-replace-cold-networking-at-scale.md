Johnson identifies a real problem: access to opportunities (internships, funding, partnerships) is heavily gatted by warm networks. Someone with 'dad's golf buddy' gets the investment banking internship over equally qualified candidates without connections. Series uses AI agents to make warm introductions based on matching needs, removing the human bottleneck.

The platform is differentiated because it has no follower counts or connection numbers—it's purely agent-to-agent matching. This is a fundamentally different approach from LinkedIn or traditional social networks. The 3.1M raised in 14 days (largest round from any current Ivy League startup) suggests investors see the potential for AI to restructure how professional connections happen.
