Arthur's single biggest takeaway after 57 days: 'Only optimize your onboarding. Try to spend nearly 90% of your time on the onboarding. More than 80% of conversions are during the onboarding. That's way more important than the app itself.' This sounds counterintuitive, but the data backed it up repeatedly.

He A/B tested onboarding variants using PostHog and RevenueCat experiments. Removing just three personal questions killed conversions entirely, while removing a different screen ('no-pact' variant) significantly boosted both completion and trial starts. Adding a progress bar improved onboarding completion from 74% to 83%. Adding commitment psychology (tap-to-commit before the paywall, borrowed from Duolingo/Headway) improved conversions. Breaking the paywall into multiple screens with one piece of information each outperformed a single dense paywall. These are small changes with outsized revenue impact.
