Cost-plus pricing guarantees you won't sell at a loss but ignores willingness to pay — dangerous in software where marginal costs are near zero. Market-based pricing assumes your product is interchangeable, surrendering any differentiation premium. Value-based pricing flips the question: if your product saves a company $50K/year, charging $5K isn't aggressive — it's a bargain. The challenge is you need deep customer insight and value proof, but the payoff is pricing power that compounds over time. Early-stage founders default to competitor matching because it feels safe, but safe pricing attracts the wrong customers and leaves expansion revenue on the table.
