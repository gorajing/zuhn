Marketing is not the creative artistic thing people think it is — it's rooted in the scientific method. Run an experiment every day. Spent $10 and got 10 customers? That's $1 per customer. Next day: $20 and 4 customers — worse. Day three: $300 and 3,000 customers — that's less than $0.10 per customer. Triple down on day three's channel.

The company that wins is the one that spends the least to acquire each customer. This is a counterweight to the 'brand building' and 'creative marketing' narratives that dominate startup advice. Until you find a channel where cost-per-acquisition approaches zero, don't invest significant resources in marketing. Keep experimenting daily until something goes viral, then run that machine until your bank account is dry. Data beats intuition; experimentation beats planning.
