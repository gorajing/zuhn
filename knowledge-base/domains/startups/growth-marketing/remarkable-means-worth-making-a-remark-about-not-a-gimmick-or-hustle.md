Godin's Purple Cow concept redefines 'remarkable' away from gimmicks: it literally means 'worth making a remark about.' People spread ideas that raise their own status or strengthen affiliations — they recommend your product because it makes them look good to their friends, not because you ran a referral campaign. His Parisian bakery example crystallizes this: using only flour, water, salt, and starter (same as every other bakery), Poilane built a brand so remarkable that people fly from Japan to buy four loaves and fly home. The test for any business: how many new customers did you get yesterday from direct referrals? If the answer is 'not many,' you need a better story and a better product, not more ad spend.
