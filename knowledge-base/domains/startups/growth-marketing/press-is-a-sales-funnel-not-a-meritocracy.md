Justin Kan dismantles the myth that press coverage happens through journalistic meritocracy. His tactical framework treats it as a conversion funnel: identify a newsworthy story type (product launch, fundraising, milestone, stunt), get warm introductions through entrepreneurs who were recently covered by your target reporters, invest the reporter's time through face-to-face meetings (leveraging sunk cost), and prepare memorized bullet points that structure the conversation into the story you want written.

The warm intro mechanism is particularly elegant: asking a recently-covered founder to introduce you to their reporter costs the founder nothing (they don't need anything more from that reporter), and the reporter receives a pre-vetted lead from a trusted source. Kan also emphasizes that press is not a scalable acquisition channel — it's a bootstrap to your first 100-1000 users at best. The moment you treat it as your growth engine, you've misunderstood its role. PR firms cost $5-20K/month and can only help with contacts and logistics, never with identifying what's actually interesting about your company.
