Andreessen reveals a personal principle from Horowitz: say everything you think, but say it in long form. Every time Andreessen got into serious public trouble, it was from a tweet — something too short to carry context. Every time a public figure gets 'blown to smithereens,' it's from something short that was misinterpreted. The 140-character tweet or 5-second TV soundbite strips context and invites misinterpretation.

The strategic insight: long-form content (podcasts, essays, YouTube) functions as a reputational shield specifically because it provides space for full explanation. Even highly controversial positions rarely blow up when they're surrounded by 90 minutes of reasoning. Short tweets are actually oral culture (emotional, bursty) even though they look written. Long podcasts are actually written culture (abstract, logical) even though they sound oral. For founders: your most controversial and important ideas should be expressed in the longest form possible, never the shortest.
