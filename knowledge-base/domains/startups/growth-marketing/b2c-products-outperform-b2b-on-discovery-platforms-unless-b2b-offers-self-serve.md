Discovery platforms like Product Hunt attract early adopters who want to try products immediately. B2C products inherently match this behavior — broad appeal plus low friction to value. B2B products face a structural disadvantage because their value propositions are narrower and often require demos, sales calls, or enterprise onboarding.

The workaround is self-serve onboarding: B2B products that let users experience value without human intervention can capture the same discovery-platform momentum as B2C. This insight extends beyond Product Hunt — any channel where users expect immediate gratification (Product Hunt, Hacker News, social media virality) will favor products that minimize time-to-value. The implication for B2B founders is that investing in frictionless onboarding isn't just a UX decision — it's a distribution strategy.
