Figma's growth into organizations like Microsoft followed a predictable pattern: one or two highly engaged ICs became internal advocates who presented the product to their managers and eventually to procurement. Figma's GTM role was not to close these deals but to equip the champion — giving them language, case studies, and social proof to make the internal case.

This shifts the design of sales enablement entirely. Rather than building materials for salespeople, bottom-up GTM teams should build materials for champions: ROI templates, comparison guides, implementation playbooks that a non-salesperson can use in an internal meeting. The leverage point is the moment when a champion decides to advocate; everything before that is product and community work, and everything after that is champion enablement.
