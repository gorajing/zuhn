Justin Kan lays out a tactical press playbook that demystifies media coverage. The key mechanism: ask founders who were recently covered to introduce you to their reporter. This works because the entrepreneur has nothing to lose (they don't need anything more from that reporter), and the reporter trusts the source's judgment by transitive property. Kan would write his ideal story as bullet points, memorize them, then steer the conversation to hit every point.

The deeper insight is that press is not a meritocracy — it's a relationship network with specific mechanics. Kan recommends giving yourself at least a week of lead time, pushing for face-to-face meetings over email (sunk cost drives commitment to write), and maintaining a calendar of story beats spaced at regular intervals. PR firms ($5K-$20K/month) can only help with contacts and logistics, never with identifying what's actually interesting about your company. That editorial judgment must come from the founder.
