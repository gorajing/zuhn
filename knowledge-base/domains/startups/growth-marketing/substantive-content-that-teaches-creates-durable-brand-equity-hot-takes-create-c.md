a16z's content strategy revealed a clear hierarchy: substantive pieces on how to construct a company, manage people, and think about promotions generated lasting value and deep entrepreneur loyalty. Meanwhile, commentary pieces like 'are we in a bubble' generated temporary chatter but no lasting brand equity.

The lesson for content strategy is to prioritize 'book-worthy' ideas — content that teaches something durable — over reactive commentary. Ben Horowitz's blog posts became chapters of 'The Hard Thing About Hard Things' specifically because they addressed timeless operational challenges. The firm explicitly differentiated between 'snacks' (tweet-length ideas padded into longer forms) and genuine ideas that could stand the test of time. Some things are just a snack and snacks should remain snacks.
