A16z's new media strategy treats each platform as a fundamentally different medium, following McLuhan's principle that the medium is the message. They hired platform-native experts for each channel: an 18-year-old for Instagram (growing 35% month-over-month), another 18-year-old for video production. These people grew up on the platforms and understand the vibe, taste, and spirit natively.

The common mistake is creating one piece of content and crossposting it everywhere. This fails because each platform has different reward mechanisms: X is where the tech world's opinion leaders live (AI researchers, crypto influencers, founders); TikTok and Instagram have mass reach but different emotional registers; Substack and long-form YouTube serve 'written culture' depth. For a16z, the target is founders — not the world — so platforms that reach 90% of founders matter more than platforms reaching 4% of the general population. They built 'launch as a service' (guaranteed viral announcements) and a 'new media fellowship' (2,000 applicants, 65 selected) to find people who are both online-native enough to have taste and professional enough to execute.
