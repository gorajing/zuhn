Johnson didn't just raise $3.1M—he did it in 14 days as a 21-year-old Yale junior, making it 'the largest round of any startup currently in the Ivy League.' The unconventional approach (a Yale student building an AI social app, raising the biggest Ivy League round) created a story that generated its own momentum.

The lesson: especially for young or unknown founders, the fundraise itself should be remarkable enough to tell a story. Conventional approaches (deck → meeting → term sheet) work for established founders. Unknown founders need the raise itself to be newsworthy. This creates a virtuous cycle: the story generates press, press generates investor interest, and interest drives competitive dynamics that close rounds faster.
