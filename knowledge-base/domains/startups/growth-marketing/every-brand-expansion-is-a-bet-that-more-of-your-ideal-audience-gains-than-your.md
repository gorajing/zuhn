Hormozi uses the small-town band analogy: when a local band goes mainstream, some original fans say 'they sold out.' That audience loss is real and unavoidable. But the band made a calculated bet that the broader audience gained would exceed the niche audience lost. Every brand expansion — new content topic, new collaboration, new product line — carries this same risk calculus. Going from 'taco content' to 'food content' to 'restaurant content' widens reach but dilutes specificity.

The practical discipline is twofold: (1) Don't let 5 mean comments stop you from gaining 500 new aligned followers — the negativity from lost audience is always louder than the quiet approval of gained audience. (2) Evaluate each new pairing against your ideal customer profile, not your current audience. Your current audience may include people who aren't your ideal customer, and losing them while gaining ideal customers is a net positive trade even if the numbers look flat. This reframes audience churn from failure to strategic pruning.
