When the user and the buyer are different people, your marketing
must reach the buyer. Students were Coconote's users, but parents
were frequently the ones paying for the subscription. Some of their
most effective early viral content featured the angle "my mom just
changed my life" — showing a parent introducing Coconote to their
student. When other moms saw this content, they would buy it for
their own kids. This created a viral loop through the buyer persona
rather than the user persona. The broader principle: always map
who uses your product versus who pays for it. If they are different
people, you need content and messaging that reaches and persuades
the buyer, not just content that excites the user.
