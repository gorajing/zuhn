Sandy Lee's algorithm observation from running channels through 2024-2026: 'In 2026 they changed the whole YouTube algorithm, especially this year because now AI is learning the algorithm instead of people manually doing it.' What this means in practice: the algorithm has gotten dramatically better at clustering viewers by interest signals from their actual viewing patterns, then matching content to the right cluster. The implication for growth: every subscriber teaches the algorithm what kind of viewer wants your content. High-fit subscribers (people whose viewing history matches your target customer) reinforce correct targeting. Low-fit subscribers (your mom, your friend) confuse the targeting. Sandy's exact example: 'Let's say my mom likes just watching K-drama, and all of a sudden she's watching AI automation. Then it tells YouTube — hey, people who watch K-drama also like AI automation because of my mom. Then what that means is whoever was watching K-drama, they're showing my AI automation content to them, and they don't click. Impression goes down and eventually I don't perform as well.' The growth-tactic implication: the 2010s tactic of 'launch your channel by getting friends and family to subscribe' is now actively harmful. The correct 2026 approach: (a) Don't promote to off-target friends and family. (b) Run highly targeted distribution to viewers whose history matches your ICP. (c) Even better, optimize for HIGH WATCH TIME from on-target viewers over total subscriber count. The same principle applies far beyond YouTube to any recommendation-system-driven platform (TikTok, Instagram Reels, podcast platforms, even Amazon listings). The algorithm sees who engages with you and concludes who you're for. Don't pollute the signal. The corollary for any product launch: the early users of your product teach the recommendation system who your product is for. If you onboard low-fit users in the early weeks, you'll get recommended to more low-fit users. Be selective with your beta cohort precisely because the algorithm is watching.
