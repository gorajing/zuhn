    fastest growing brand: 'At the end of 2023, our head of growth and I were
    like, oh my god, we need to post every single day. So for literally the

    realize — if this video hit with this hook, how many iterations of that hook
    can we create to reach more audiences? And then you keep going. And then you
    start creating an army of affiliates to do even more content. Now we have
    25,000 plus affiliates working for us, creating millions and millions of
    different iterations on a monthly basis to see what's working. The learning
    system is pretty insane, right? It's like a machine.' The architecture has
    four parts: (1) FOUNDER ORIGINATION: founder personally posts daily multiple
    times early on, develops hook intuition, validates that hooks can work. (2)
    HOOK ITERATION: once a hook hits, generate every variation imaginable. Test
    which audience segments respond to which hook framings. (3) AFFILIATE ARMY
    ACTIVATION: recruit customer-creators (nano-influencers who use the product)
    as paid affiliates. Each affiliate makes content on their own time in their
    own voice. (4) TRACKING & LEARNING SYSTEM: every piece of content tagged for
    performance. Hook variations tracked across affiliate diversity. The system
    learns at scale far faster than any internal creative team. Why this works
    at scale that internal teams can't match: (a) Volume — 25,000 affiliates ×
    10 posts/month = 250,000 monthly creative experiments. No internal team
    produces this. (b) Diversity — 25,000 different voices, contexts, audiences.
    Hook variations get tested across demographics no internal team would
    design. (c) Authenticity — affiliates are actual customers, content reads as
    real. (d) Cost — affiliates earn commissions on sales, so the cost is
    ~variable rather than fixed creative-team payroll. (e) Selection — only
    affiliates who produce performing content stick around; the system naturally
    filters for high-converting voices. The corollary for any brand: stop trying
    to create great content internally. Build a system to recruit, train
    (lightly), pay (commission-based), and track customer-creators. The internal
    team's job shifts from content creation to system design, hook discovery,
    and affiliate recruitment/operations. The model generalizes beyond DTC
    physical products: SaaS companies could build affiliate armies, course
    creators do this with student-affiliates, B2B services could activate
    customer-advocates. The pattern is: don't manufacture content centrally;
    build a platform that lets your customers manufacture it for you while you
    track and amplify what works.

So for literally the first half of 2024, we were posting every single day, multiple times a day, trying to figure out what's working until finally a video hit. And we realize — if this video hit with this hook, how many iterations of that hook can we create to reach more audiences? And then you keep going.
