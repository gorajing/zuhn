Technical founders often model themselves after mythologized versions of companies like Google or Stripe — believing these companies succeeded purely on product quality with zero marketing effort. This myth is dangerous because it gives founders permission to skip distribution entirely.

The reality: Google is one of the best advertising and sales companies in the world. Stripe is a top-tier enterprise payments company. PostHog invests heavily in developer marketing. If no one knows your technology exists, it's game over. The best advice for technical founders (the ones YC tends to fund) is precisely the advice they wouldn't naturally take: invest in sales, marketing, and telling the world about what you've built. The most useful advice is the kind you never would have done on your own.
