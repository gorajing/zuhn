Herjavec distinguishes between two types of media leverage: digital content (social media, newsletters, podcasts) which creates awareness and reach, versus traditional media (books, television, mainstream press) which creates credibility and authority. His own experience with Shark Tank illustrates how TV exposure created a credibility multiplier that no amount of blog posts could replicate.

For founders, this suggests a layered media strategy rather than going all-in on digital. Publishing a book, landing mainstream press coverage, or appearing on established media platforms creates a credibility foundation that amplifies everything else. The compounding effect is key — traditional media credentials don't decay the way social media posts do, and they serve as trust signals in investor meetings, sales conversations, and partnership negotiations for years afterward.
