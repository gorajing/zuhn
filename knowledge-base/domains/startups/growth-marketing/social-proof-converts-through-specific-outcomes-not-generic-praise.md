Social proof can lift conversions 20-30% when placed strategically, but the format matters enormously. 'Love the product!' from 'Happy Customer, Stealth Mode' converts essentially zero. Compare to: 'We cut onboarding time from 10 days to 2, and support tickets dropped by half — Jamie Talbot, VP Product @ Fieldly.' The second works because it maps directly to a promised benefit with a real person staking their reputation on the claim.

The hierarchy of proof: logo strips (6-10 recognizable brands near the hero, never in the footer), count-based proof for pre-logo-stage startups ('Trusted by 2,300 revenue teams'), outcome-specific testimonials with full name/title/company, and investor logos placed near the final CTA. Each layer reduces a different type of perceived risk. Critical rule: never display logos without permission — 'aspirational' brand logos destroy trust instantly when called out. If you lack logos, count-based credibility signals ('Used by 40 YC startups') work because they reduce fear of being first, not because they signal scale.
