Ek says the prevailing wisdom that internet companies should 'go global day one' is wrong in 99.9% of cases. Spotify found empirically that a user needed roughly eight exposures to the brand before they'd sign up, which meant the density of exposures per unit of geography was the key lever — not total reach. They attacked small markets (Sweden, later US college cities) to get above that density threshold. Ek says definitively that Spotify would not have survived without the label-imposed constraint that forced this geographic staging. For founders: treat the constraint to start narrow as a feature, not a bug. Pick a niche — geographic, demographic, or vertical — where you can saturate awareness and hit the repeated-exposure threshold, even if it feels like you're leaving the biggest market on the table. The alternative is spreading thin enough that nobody hears about you eight times.
