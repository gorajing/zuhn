After filing their YC application, Beller went back every few days and updated it with their latest revenue number. When YC reviewers logged in, they saw: on this date, revenue was X. On this date, higher. On this date, higher still. Revenue was going from $200/month to $800 to $2,000 to $3,000.

Beller believes this growth trajectory—visible in real time—is why they got into YC. The hack is simple but powerful: don't present a static number. Present a trend. Any evaluator (investor, customer, partner) responds more to visible momentum than to any single data point, no matter how impressive. This applies beyond YC to any pitch or sales situation.
