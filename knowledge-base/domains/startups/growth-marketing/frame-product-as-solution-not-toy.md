Views and conversion are not the same thing. Coconote had a video
that framed the product as a fun, novel toy — it went massively viral
with 41 million views and 4.5 million likes. But conversion was
terrible because viewers saw entertainment, not a tool they needed.
Videos that positioned Coconote as a study solution — framing it
around the problem of missing key details in lectures — converted
far better even with fewer views. The lesson: toys generate
curiosity clicks, solutions generate intent. When creating content,
always lead with the problem your product solves, not how cool or
novel it is.
