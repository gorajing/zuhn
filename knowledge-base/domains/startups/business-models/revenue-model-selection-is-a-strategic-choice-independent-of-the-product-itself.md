The book identifies three primary revenue architectures — transaction (one-time), recurring (subscription), and usage-based — and demonstrates how the choice between them fundamentally shapes business dynamics. Skype's freemium model works because marginal cost per free user is near zero, creating a massive funnel for premium conversion. Red Hat's open-source-plus-support model turns a free product into recurring enterprise revenue.

This reframes pricing from a tactical decision ('what should we charge?') into a strategic one ('what revenue architecture best fits our value delivery and customer behavior?'). A product that fails under one revenue model might thrive under another. Founders should prototype multiple revenue models early rather than defaulting to the most obvious one, since the revenue model constrains growth mechanics, unit economics, and the type of customer relationships you can sustain.
