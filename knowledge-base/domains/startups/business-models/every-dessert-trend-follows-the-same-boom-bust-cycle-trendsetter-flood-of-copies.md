Crumbl grew from 1 store to 1,000+ in 4 years. But cookies have been around for centuries — Mrs. Fields went from 780 stores to near-extinction. The pattern repeats every decade: a trendsetter elevates a commodity product, copycats flood the market selling virtually the same thing, supply exceeds demand, consumer fatigue sets in, and the market crashes. The underlying economics are favorable (cookies cost cents to make, sell for dollars), but the business model is walk-in dependent and location-sensitive. Mrs. Fields proved you can't franchise your way out of a fad ending. Crumbl's challenge is the same: sustaining novelty and virality as copycats converge.
