The standard PE playbook — buy competitors, achieve majority share, standardize, raise prices — fails when the core asset walks on two legs. Barbershops, law firms, consulting, and other talent-centric service businesses share this trait. Regis spent 40 years and hundreds of millions trying to consolidate barbering and ended up retreating. The key variables: barriers to entry (low = immune to consolidation), capital requirements (low = talent can self-fund), customer relationship (personal = follows the talent, not the brand). This is the anti-pattern to every successful PE rollup in history.
