Costco's business model inverts the traditional retail equation. While most retailers profit from markup on goods, Costco caps markup at 15% (11% average vs Walmart's 24%) and instead generates 75% of revenue from membership fees. This creates a powerful incentive loop: the company's primary goal becomes impressing members enough to renew at $60-120/year, which means every pricing decision favors the customer.

This structure has a compounding effect — 90% annual renewal rates prove the alignment works. The insight generalizes beyond retail: any business that shifts primary revenue from per-transaction extraction to recurring relationship fees will naturally optimize for long-term customer value. This is why subscription models in SaaS, media, and services often produce better customer outcomes than transactional alternatives.
