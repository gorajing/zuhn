Ben Horowitz frames the crime problem as having only three real options: high crime (under-enforcement), harsh punishment (Singapore/El Salvador model), or intelligence-based deterrence. The third path uses technology — Flock cameras, drones, gunshot detection, AI orchestration — to make getting caught near-certain.

The Chinese proverb 'certain punishment means no punishment' captures the logic: when everyone knows crimes will be solved, the incentive structure shifts and crime drops without needing harsh sentences. Las Vegas demonstrates this with 90%+ murder clearance rates (vs. 47% national average), 75% reduction in police shootings of suspects, and community support. The key insight is that intelligence-based policing is simultaneously more humane, more effective, and cheaper than the alternatives.
