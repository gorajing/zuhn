The four pandemic pivots share a common pattern: none of them were incremental adjustments. LVMH repurposed perfume factories for sanitizer, IBM made permanent WFH for 75,000 employees, Spotify shifted from intermediary to content creator, and Banzai acquired a company to completely reinvent its value proposition. Under normal conditions, each of these moves would have faced years of committee review and risk analysis.

This suggests that founders should artificially impose existential constraints during strategic planning—asking 'what would we do if our primary revenue channel disappeared tomorrow?' The answers generated under simulated pressure tend to be more creative than those generated during comfortable brainstorming sessions. The key insight is that organizational inertia is the real enemy of innovation, and only sufficient pressure overcomes it.
