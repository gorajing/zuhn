Costco derives 75% of its revenue from membership fees, which fundamentally reshapes its priorities. Every pricing decision is evaluated against whether it might cost a $60-$120 renewal next year, making short-term margin extraction irrational. This contrasts with Amazon Prime, where the optional subscription creates loyalty but doesn't restructure the underlying business model — Amazon still profits primarily from transactions.

This pattern generalizes beyond retail: any business that makes participation fees mandatory rather than optional shifts its optimization target from transaction extraction to experience quality. The key mechanism is that the fee becomes the product, and everything else becomes marketing for renewal. This is why Costco self-imposes a 15% max markup — it's not generosity, it's rational behavior when your real product is the membership itself.
