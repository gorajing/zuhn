The ultimate irony of Casper's story: the company that proudly declared you'd never need to visit a mattress store found its fastest-growing sales channel was... retail stores. Retail sales grew 74% year-over-year, accounting for 20% of revenue, with average orders 15% higher ($820 vs online). Meanwhile, DTC online sales grew only 20%.

Casper, Everlane, and Away — all poster children for DTC disruption — ended up opening stores and begging to get on big-retailer shelves. The lesson: online-only growth has a ceiling. There are only so many people you can reach through Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and podcast ads before the waves become exhausting and ineffective. Physical retail provides discovery, trust (touch the product), and incremental reach that digital channels cannot fully replace. Disrupting an industry doesn't mean the disrupted model was wrong — sometimes it means you didn't understand why it existed.
