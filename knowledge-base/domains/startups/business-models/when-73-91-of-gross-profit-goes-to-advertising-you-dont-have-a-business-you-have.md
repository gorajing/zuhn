Casper's unit economics told the entire story: buy a mattress for $400, sell for $700, make $300 gross margin, then spend $300 on advertising and $300 on admin, losing $200 per mattress. In 2017 they spent 91% of gross profit on ads, improving only to 60% by 2020 — still catastrophic. The company lost money every single year: -$73M (2017), -$92M (2018), -$93M (2019), -$90M (2020).

The structural problem: online advertising costs exploded as thousands of DTC startups and big brands competed for the same demographics on Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube. Facebook CPCs rose 93% in 2019 alone — meaning every $1 in ad spend now required $2 for the same result. Casper's business model required permanently cheap customer acquisition that was never coming back. The lesson is brutal: if your unit economics depend on an external cost (advertising) staying cheap forever, you don't have a sustainable business.
