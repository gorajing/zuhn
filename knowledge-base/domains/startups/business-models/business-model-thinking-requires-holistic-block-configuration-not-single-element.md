Companies typically focus on one or two business model elements — most commonly product, service, technology, or price. This narrow optimization misses the compounding effects that emerge when multiple building blocks are intentionally configured to reinforce each other.

The architecture metaphor is instructive: south-facing windows aren't inherently superior — they're superior in the pattern of 'maximize natural light in living areas.' Similarly, a freemium pricing model isn't inherently good — it's powerful when combined with specific channel strategies, network effects, and customer segment targeting. The pattern is the unit of design, not the individual block.
