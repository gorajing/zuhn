Airlines commoditized travel into speed and efficiency. Cruises can't compete on speed, so they made the journey the product: bowling alleys, go-karts, comedy shows, rock climbing, ice skating, casinos, and unlimited food/drinks. This transforms a logistics disadvantage (slow) into a product advantage (contained resort experience). Cruises outprofit airlines despite massive capital costs because they extract revenue from every on-board activity, and passengers are captive customers for days. The strategic lesson: when you can't win on the dominant industry metric (speed), redefine the value proposition entirely.
