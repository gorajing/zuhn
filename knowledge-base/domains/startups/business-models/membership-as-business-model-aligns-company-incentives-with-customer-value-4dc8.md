Most retailers profit from product margins, creating a fundamental tension: they want to charge customers as much as possible while appearing affordable. Costco inverts this by making membership fees the primary revenue source (75% of profit). This means every pricing decision is evaluated against its impact on membership renewal rates (90% annually), not short-term margin.

This creates a rare structural alignment where the company genuinely benefits from giving customers the lowest possible prices. The self-imposed 15% markup cap is not charity—it is the rational strategy when $60-120 in annual fees dwarfs the pennies gained from price increases. This model suggests that for businesses with repeat customers, shifting revenue from per-transaction margins to subscription fees can transform adversarial pricing dynamics into cooperative ones.
