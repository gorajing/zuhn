Costco derives 75% of its revenue from membership fees, which fundamentally restructures its incentive system. Unlike Amazon Prime, where the subscription is optional and the core business model remains per-transaction margin, Costco's mandatory membership means its survival depends entirely on members renewing. This creates a powerful feedback loop: the company must consistently deliver value (low prices, high quality) because raising prices saves pennies today but costs $60-120 in lost renewals.

This pattern extends beyond retail. Any business that shifts primary revenue from per-transaction margins to access fees inverts its optimization function — from 'how do I extract maximum value per interaction' to 'how do I make the relationship so valuable that customers never leave.' The 90% renewal rate validates this alignment. The key insight is that optional premium tiers (like Prime) create loyalty without fundamentally changing the business model, while mandatory access fees restructure the entire company around retention.
