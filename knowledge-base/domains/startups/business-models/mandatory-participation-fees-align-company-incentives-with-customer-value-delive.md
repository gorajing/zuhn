Costco derives 75% of its revenue from membership fees, not product sales. This creates a structural incentive where raising prices gains pennies today but risks losing $60-120 in annual renewals. The 15% maximum markup rule is not charity — it's rational self-interest under a membership model.

This contrasts sharply with traditional retail where margin optimization and customer value are inherently in tension. The membership model resolves this tension by making customer satisfaction the direct revenue driver. Amazon Prime creates loyalty but doesn't fundamentally restructure incentives the same way because Amazon still profits primarily from product sales. The distinction matters: Costco's model makes price increases irrational, while Amazon's model merely makes them less attractive.
