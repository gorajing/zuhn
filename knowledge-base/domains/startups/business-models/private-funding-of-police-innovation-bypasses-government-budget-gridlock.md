A striking pattern emerges: 100% of San Francisco's latest policing innovations were privately funded. In Atlanta, the police foundation contributes about $30 million per year. Ben Horowitz personally bought an ice machine and espresso machine for Vegas 911 dispatchers, cutting call wait times from 5 minutes to under 30 seconds.

The model works because police budgets are almost entirely headcount with zero flexibility for technology investment. Adding less than 1% to a police budget through private funding can completely transform capability. The playbook: private funding covers years 1-2, then the city rolls the cost into its budget. Companies like Lowe's fund their local police departments because employee safety is worth more than the cost. This public-private partnership model is spreading nationally.
