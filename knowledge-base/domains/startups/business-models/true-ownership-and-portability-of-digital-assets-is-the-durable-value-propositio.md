NFX argues that the core web3 gaming evolution has two pillars: NFTs as true ownership of virtual goods (currently locked in platform walled gardens like Steam), and tokens as portable in-game currencies. The earn element is a byproduct, not the core value proposition.

In web2, a Counter-Strike knife worth thousands of dollars exists only on Steam's servers — the platform controls what you can do with it and it can never leave that ecosystem. Blockchain-based ownership means the asset lives in the user's wallet, and other developers can read its characteristics from the chain to enable cross-game utility. This breaks down the walled gardens that currently fragment the gaming economy.

However, the execution challenges are substantial: interoperability standards don't exist yet, regulatory uncertainty around tokens persists, wallet UX remains friction-heavy, and balancing economies with externally-tradeable assets adds enormous complexity. The thesis is directionally sound but the timeline to mainstream adoption remains highly uncertain.
