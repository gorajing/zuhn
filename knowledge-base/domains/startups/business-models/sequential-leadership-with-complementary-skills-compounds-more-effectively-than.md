Taco Bell's 15-year turnaround wasn't driven by a single visionary -- it was powered by two leaders with complementary skills deployed in the right sequence. Emil Brolick (2000-2006) was product-oriented: he mandated portable, on-the-go formats, ditched fork-and-knife entrees, launched iconic products, and optimized speed and accuracy in operations. He built the foundation.

Greg Creed (2006-2015) was an old-school marketer who took that foundation and built the house: he created the 'Think Outside the Bun' positioning, developed the anti-discount pricing framework, experimented with packaging and bundling, and leveraged Doritos Locos Tacos into Taco Bell's biggest cultural moment. Emil couldn't have done what Greg did (he lacked the marketing instinct), and Greg couldn't have done what Emil did (he needed the operational foundation to be in place first). The lesson: great companies often need leadership sequencing rather than leadership genius -- the right skills at the right stage of company evolution.
