The 1970s introduced stock-based CEO compensation to 'align' executive and shareholder interests. CEO pay went from 20x average employee (1965) to 300x+ today, almost entirely in stock-linked compensation. This created a powerful incentive to maximize stock price by any means.

The alignment is destructive because stock price and business health diverge in predictable ways: buybacks boost price while reducing investment, layoffs boost margins while losing capability, and financial engineering creates the appearance of growth without substance. The incentive structure makes this rational for individual executives even as it systematically weakens companies.
