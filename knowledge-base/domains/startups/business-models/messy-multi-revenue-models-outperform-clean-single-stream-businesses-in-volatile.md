eDreams pioneered approaches that didn't fit the 'pure OTA' model investors preferred: combining low-cost and traditional carriers via web-scraping, adding trains to a flight engine, replacing airline commissions with service fees, building a parallel media business, and creating a hybrid OTA-Meta model. Each innovation looked messy individually but collectively created multiple revenue streams and competitive moats.

The deeper lesson is about optimizing for reality over narrative. Clean business models are easier to pitch but may be more fragile. When eDreams shifted from airline commissions to service fees (after airlines zeroed out agent commissions), they turned a crisis into alignment — now they were incentivized to find the best flights for customers, not the highest-commission ones. Founders should be suspicious when they're simplifying their model to please investors rather than to serve customers.
