Movies are boom-bust lottery tickets: tens of millions to produce, no guarantee of return, and you need to spend as much on advertising as filming. Good movies aren't always hits — wrong timing, genre fatigue, crowded schedules. The only way to survive is deep pockets and continuous production. The Big Five are all subsidiaries of diversified conglomerates (Comcast, Disney, Sony, Warner Bros Discovery, Paramount). Lionsgate survives as a rare independent by focusing on franchises (Hunger Games, John Wick, Saw) and being more aggressive on original content while the Big Five have retreated to superhero sequels and nostalgia remakes. The gap between safe sequel economics and risky original content is where independent studios find oxygen.
