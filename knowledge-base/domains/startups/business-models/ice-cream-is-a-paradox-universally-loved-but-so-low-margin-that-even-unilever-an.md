Ice cream should be a great business: proven unit economics, simple inputs, infinite flavor possibilities. But reality: Unilever assembled the world's largest ice cream portfolio (Ben & Jerry's, Magnum, Klondike, Good Humor, Cornetto) and grew revenue just 4% across 20 years. Nestle had the same experience and sold off its ice cream. The structural problem: it's a weather-dependent, low-margin CPG category where even conglomerates with unrivaled economies of scale resort to shrinkflation. Private equity keeps supercharging indie ice cream brands (Van Leeuwen, Jeni's) under the bet they'll become the next Haagen-Dazs, but the underlying economics haven't changed.
