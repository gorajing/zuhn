Kodak co-invented the digital camera and had a product customers loved, yet went bankrupt because they never found a viable business model for the digital age. This pattern recurs across industries: teams obsess over product-market fit while treating unit economics, customer acquisition cost, and revenue model as afterthoughts.

The lean startup framework demands parallel validation of both value proposition and business model. Even companies like Twitter and WhatsApp that initially scaled without revenue ultimately needed either a sound business model or an acquirer willing to provide one. For established companies the challenge is even harder because they carry legacy cost structures from previous business models. The practical implication is that every experiment roadmap should include business model experiments — testing willingness to pay, channel economics, and cost structure — alongside desirability experiments.
