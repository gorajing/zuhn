British Airways generates roughly equal revenue from 14 first-class seats as from 122 economy seats on the same flight. This 80/20 pattern—where a small premium segment subsidizes the commodity tier—appears across industries from SaaS (enterprise vs. self-serve) to media (subscribers vs. ad-supported). The strategic implication is that economy passengers exist primarily to fill capacity, not to drive profit. Businesses architected around this pattern can tolerate significant churn in their commodity tier as long as the premium segment remains intact. This also means that product investment should disproportionately flow toward the premium experience, since that's where marginal revenue impact is highest.
