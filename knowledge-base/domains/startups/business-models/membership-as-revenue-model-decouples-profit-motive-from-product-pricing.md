Costco's core insight is structural: by funding operations through membership fees rather than product markups, it eliminates the adversarial tension between the company's profit motive and the customer's desire for low prices. Every dollar Costco extracts through margin today risks a $60 or $120 membership cancellation next year — so the rational move is to keep prices as low as possible.

This creates a flywheel that is hard to replicate via loyalty programs bolted onto transactional models. Amazon Prime triggers sunk-cost loyalty but does not change Amazon's core incentive to extract margin from each sale. Costco's mandatory membership means the business model itself is built on customer satisfaction rather than transaction volume.

For founders, this is a template worth studying: identify whether your revenue mechanism is aligned or misaligned with the outcome your customer wants. A subscription that funds operations from recurring fees, rather than per-unit margin, can enable dramatically better unit economics for the customer while building stickier retention.
