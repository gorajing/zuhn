Costco generates 75% of its revenue from membership fees, which fundamentally changes its incentive structure compared to traditional retailers or even Amazon Prime. Because renewal is the primary revenue driver, Costco self-imposes a 15% maximum markup (vs Walmart's 24% or Home Depot's 35%), passing savings to customers rather than extracting margin.

This creates a virtuous cycle: low prices drive 90% renewal rates, which fund operations, which enable even lower prices. The key distinction from optional programs like Prime is that membership isn't a loyalty add-on—it IS the business model. This means every pricing decision is evaluated against its impact on renewal, not just immediate revenue. For founders designing revenue models, the lesson is that mandatory access fees can create stronger alignment than transaction-based models, but only if the fee genuinely gates access to differentiated value.
