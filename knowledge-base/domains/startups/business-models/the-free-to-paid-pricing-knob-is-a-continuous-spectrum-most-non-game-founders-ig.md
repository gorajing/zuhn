Gaming companies treat the boundary between free and paid as a knob they continuously turn — experimenting with how much value to give away free, where to introduce conversion pressure, and crucially, setting different positions for different user segments. Facebook and Google do similar tuning with ad frequency. Yet most non-game SaaS and consumer founders set a single static pricing tier and rarely revisit it.

The best games keep the core fun element free to maximize retention, then layer monetization on top in ways that don't degrade the core experience. The key operational insight is that you should have multiple monetization pain points rather than one, and you should be running continuous experiments on where conversion pressure sits for each user cohort. This creates optionality that compounds — higher LTV enables more aggressive acquisition, which starts a growth loop.
