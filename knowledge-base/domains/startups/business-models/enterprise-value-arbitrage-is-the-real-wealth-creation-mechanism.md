Hormozi illustrates the wealth alchemy: at $900 permanent CAC and $1,200 annual revenue per customer, a 10x revenue multiple means each customer represents $12,000 in enterprise value versus $900 in cost. Critically, this enterprise value growth is unrealized and therefore untaxed. He contrasts this with a bottom-line-valued business doing the same revenue but only getting a 7x EBITDA multiple ($21M vs $120M). This explains why the wealthiest founders lived in high-tax California — they understood that building enterprise value dwarfs optimizing income tax strategy. The insight reframes business building from 'how do I maximize take-home pay' to 'how do I maximize the arbitrage between acquisition cost and enterprise value multiple.'
