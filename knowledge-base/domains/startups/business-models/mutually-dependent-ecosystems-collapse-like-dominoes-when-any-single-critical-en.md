The esports ecosystem was a chain of mutual dependencies: publishers support competitive play, leagues organize tournaments, teams fund players, players draw viewers, viewers attract sponsors, and sponsors fund everyone. The entire chain assumed all participants would keep investing until broadcast rights materialized.

But different publishers had different commitments. Riot controlled everything for League of Legends. Nintendo refused to invest. EA and Ubisoft had one foot in and one foot out. When publishers reduced support for specific titles, the domino effect was swift: no publisher support meant weaker competitions, which meant less viewership, which meant fewer sponsors, which meant teams couldn't pay players, which meant talent left for streaming careers. The ecosystem had no entity that was independently sustainable -- every participant's business model depended on all others continuing to invest. This fragility is present in any multi-stakeholder ecosystem (app stores, marketplace platforms, media partnerships) where no single entity can generate value alone.
