David Haber at a16z identifies a critical distinction: AI that reinforces a business model (driving revenue) versus AI that merely reduces costs. When AI drives revenue, there's no ceiling on how much customers want to adopt it.

Two case studies illustrate this. Eve operates in plaintiff law where attorneys work on contingency — AI doesn't erode billable hours but enables them to take on more clients and win more cases. Salient applies voice agents to loan servicing — beyond just replacing call center staff, the agents actually drive better collection rates, directly reinforcing the lender's business model. The key insight for founders: look for industries where AI doesn't just automate existing work but expands the capacity to generate revenue.
