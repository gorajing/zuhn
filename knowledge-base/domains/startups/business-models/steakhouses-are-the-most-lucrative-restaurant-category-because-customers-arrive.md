Most restaurants compete on creativity and uniqueness. Steakhouses thrive on conformity: every one serves NY strip, porterhouse, filet mignon, mashed potatoes, Caesar salad, and chocolate cake. Customers don't come for innovation — they come for the experience of splurging on a known quantity. Side dishes (mashed potatoes, fries, asparagus) are cheap inputs heavily marked up, and no one questions $10-15 for sides at a steakhouse. Alcohol adds to margins. The counter-intuitive insight: in premium dining, predictability IS the product. Customers pay more because they know exactly what they're getting.
