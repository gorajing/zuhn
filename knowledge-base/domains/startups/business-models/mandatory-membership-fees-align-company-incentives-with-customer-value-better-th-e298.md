Costco derives 75% of its profit from membership fees, not product markups. This creates a fundamentally different incentive structure than traditional retail: raising prices would gain pennies today but risk losing $60-120 annual renewals. The self-imposed 15% max markup rule is a direct consequence of this model.

This contrasts with Amazon Prime, which is optional and self-selecting — it creates loyalty but doesn't restructure the business model. Costco's mandatory membership means every operational decision is filtered through 'will this make members renew?' The 90% renewal rate validates that the alignment works. For founders designing business models, the lesson is that the payment structure itself can be the strategy, not just a monetization layer on top of one.
