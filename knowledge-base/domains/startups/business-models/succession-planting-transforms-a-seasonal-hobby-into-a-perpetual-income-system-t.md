A single 100 sq ft bed with one crop per year earns about $200. The same bed with four successions earns $800 -- identical land cost, identical soil cost, only the planning changes. The Paris rule: a bed is never empty. The moment one crop comes out, another goes in -- not eventually, but that day.

This requires thinking in weeks rather than seasons: plant radishes (ready in 25-30 days), start lettuce indoors 3 weeks later, transplant lettuce when radishes come out, sow carrots 4 weeks before lettuce finishes. A well-managed quarter acre (20 beds) can generate $6,000-$12,000 annually, or $13,200 through a 20-member CSA at $30/week for 22 weeks. The deeper insight is that this is a systems thinking problem: the hobby gardener asks 'what should I plant this spring?' while the market gardener asks 'what will this bed be producing every week from March through November?'
