Esports had every ingredient that should have produced a viable business: massive viewership (rivaling NBA and NFL for younger demographics), passionate engagement, concentrated millennial/Gen Z audience that marketers were desperate to reach, and Fortune 500 brand sponsors. The plan was simple: grow viewership to critical mass, sell broadcast rights for billions (like NFL and NBA), and distribute that cash to teams.

But the broadcast payday never came. Unlike traditional sports where broadcast deals generate billions in recurring revenue that funds the entire ecosystem, esports broadcast deals were never meaningful enough to offset costs. Twitch itself still can't turn a profit. Without this anchor revenue stream, the entire ecosystem depended on a fragile chain of mutual dependencies: publishers support games, which attract teams, which hire players, which draw viewers, which attract sponsors. All it took was one entity quitting to bring everything down. The lesson is stark: audience is necessary but not sufficient. Without a proven monetization mechanism that scales with viewership, growing your audience just grows your burn rate.
