The theater analogy reveals the BMC's deeper logic: the audience (customers) pays for the play (value proposition), but there's no play without backstage infrastructure (resources, activities, partners), and the theater only survives if ticket revenue exceeds backstage costs. This isn't a list of nine independent factors—it's a causal chain.

This systemic view changes how you use the canvas. Instead of filling blocks independently, you should trace the causal links: what value proposition justifies what revenue stream, what infrastructure enables that proposition, and whether the cost structure supports the whole system. Incoherent business models almost always have a broken link somewhere in this chain.
