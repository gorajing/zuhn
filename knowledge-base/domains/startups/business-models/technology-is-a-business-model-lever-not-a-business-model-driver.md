The contrast between Walmart/Tesco and the music industry reveals a critical distinction. Walmart successfully added e-commerce as a new distribution channel to its existing business model — technology reinforced what already worked. The music industry, by contrast, was upended by digitalization and failed to find sustainable new business models around the same technological shift.

The difference isn't technological capability but business model thinking. Technology creates possibilities; the business model determines whether those possibilities create or destroy value for you. This means every technology assessment should start with 'how does this change our value proposition, channels, or revenue model?' rather than 'should we adopt this technology?' The companies that get this backwards either miss technological waves entirely or adopt technology without a viable economic model around it.
