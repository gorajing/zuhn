Ryanair operates only 737s and EasyJet only the A320 family. This seemingly restrictive choice cascades through the entire organization: every pilot can fly every plane, every mechanic knows every system, every ground crew handles identical configurations. The savings aren't just additive—they compound because scheduling becomes simpler (no aircraft-type constraints), spare parts inventory shrinks, and training pipelines standardize.

This maps to a broader startup principle: radical product simplification doesn't just cut direct costs, it eliminates the hidden coordination overhead that grows superlinearly with variant count. The constraint that feels limiting actually becomes a competitive weapon because competitors managing multiple variants bear invisible complexity taxes across every operational function.
