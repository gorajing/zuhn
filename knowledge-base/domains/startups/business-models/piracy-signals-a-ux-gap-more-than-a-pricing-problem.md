Copyright holders fixate on lost revenue from piracy, assuming users are motivated by getting something for free. But as iTunes demonstrated, people willingly pay for digital content when the purchasing experience is convenient enough. A significant component of piracy is simply that it offers a better user experience — no DRM restrictions, no geographic blocks, no forced scheduling, instant access to any content.

This reframing has profound strategic implications. When your users are defecting to an illegal alternative, the correct response is not more enforcement but better product design. Every friction point in your legitimate offering — sign-up walls, device restrictions, content delays, poor search — is pushing users toward piracy. The businesses that win against piracy are those that make the legal path easier than the illegal one, as Spotify and Netflix later demonstrated. Treating piracy as a UX signal rather than a theft problem leads to fundamentally different and more effective strategic responses.
