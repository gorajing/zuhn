The industries that have seen prices rise fastest relative to wages—healthcare, education, housing—share a common trait: misaligned incentives among participants. Medical providers are incentivized to overbill insurance, universities have no accountability for student loan outcomes, and financial intermediaries extract value without improving experience.

Fintech-enabled marketplaces can break these deadlocks by bringing the financial layer on-platform, eliminating the intermediary whose incentives diverge from the end user. This is why Opendoor (instant home liquidity), Lambda School (income-sharing tuition), and Ribbon (bridge financing for homebuyers) target industries where the financial structure itself is the source of friction. The opportunity is largest where NPS scores for existing financial services are lowest.
