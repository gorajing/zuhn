Movies are inherently boom-bust: a few hits subsidize many flops, and the only way to survive is deep pockets and the ability to keep drawing lottery tickets. But Lionsgate found a structural hedge in library syndication. When they acquired Artisan Entertainment for $220M, they gained 8,000+ titles including Terminator 2, Total Recall, and Reservoir Dogs.

These old titles generate steady licensing revenue year after year -- streaming platforms need deep content libraries and will pay for bulk access to classic films. Modern MBA explicitly compares this to SaaS revenue: recurring, predictable, and growing as more streaming platforms compete for content. This syndication revenue cushioned Lionsgate during years when new films flopped. The lesson is powerful for any content business: every piece of content you create becomes a permanent asset in your library, and the aggregate value of that library compounds over time as more distribution channels compete for access. It's the content equivalent of building a SaaS business alongside a services business.
