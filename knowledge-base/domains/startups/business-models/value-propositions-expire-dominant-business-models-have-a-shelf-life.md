Microsoft's Windows licensing model was enormously profitable for decades, but the shift to mobile and cloud made per-device licensing increasingly obsolete. The company's pivot to Office 365 subscriptions and free Windows 10 upgrades illustrates how even the most dominant business models have a finite shelf life.

The key lesson is temporal: companies must begin experimenting with successor models while the current one is still generating revenue. Waiting until decline is visible means you're already behind. The yoghurt-in-the-fridge metaphor is apt — by the time you notice it's expired, it's already been deteriorating for a while. This applies to any business built on a specific delivery or monetization mechanism that could be undermined by platform shifts.
