Gig economy saturation follows basic supply-demand dynamics: each new driver entering the platform reduces the order flow for existing drivers. When the influx comes from displaced workers with no alternatives rather than people seeking supplemental income, the supply increase is permanent and price-insensitive.

Simultaneously, the customer base is shrinking as consumers pull back on discretionary spending. More drivers competing for fewer orders drives per-driver earnings below sustainability. The gig platforms themselves benefit from oversupply (shorter wait times, lower prices) but individual drivers are trapped in a deteriorating economics.
