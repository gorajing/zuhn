Heathrow operates at 650 of 657 maximum daily flights, meaning growth can only come from replacing low-value flights with high-value ones. A 76-seat domestic Dash 8 generates ~$2,400 per visit while a 345-seat transatlantic 747 generates ~$31,700 — over 13x more revenue using the same runway slot. This creates a structural incentive to eliminate domestic routes in favor of long-haul international ones, which is exactly what Heathrow has done (only 8 UK destinations vs. 8 in China alone).

This pattern applies broadly to any capacity-constrained platform: when you can't add more slots, you optimize the value per slot. Marketplaces, ad platforms, and SaaS products with usage limits all face this same dynamic. The uncomfortable implication is that serving your broadest user base often conflicts with maximizing revenue once you hit capacity.
