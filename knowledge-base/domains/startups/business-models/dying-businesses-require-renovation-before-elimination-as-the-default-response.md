When businesses fall from the profitable top-right to the struggling bottom-left of the Execution Engine, the framework explicitly warns against defaulting to elimination. Instead, the primary response should be renovation, improvement, or reinvention — moving the business back toward the top-right corner. Elimination is one option among several, not the automatic response.

This challenges the common Silicon Valley bias toward 'killing your darlings' and starting fresh. Existing businesses carry embedded value in customer relationships, operational knowledge, distribution channels, and brand equity. A renovation approach preserves these assets while addressing the root causes of decline. The portfolio map makes this visible by showing the movement trajectory a business needs to follow, rather than presenting decline as a binary alive-or-dead state.
