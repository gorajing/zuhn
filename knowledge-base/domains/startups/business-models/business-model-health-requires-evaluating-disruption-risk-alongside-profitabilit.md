Traditional business assessment focuses almost exclusively on financial metrics like revenue, margins, and growth. Osterwalder and Pigneur argue this creates a dangerous blind spot: a highly profitable business can be on the verge of disruption from technology, market shifts, or regulatory changes — Kodak's film business being the canonical example.

The Portfolio Map forces dual-axis evaluation, revealing that a business in the top-left quadrant (high profit, high disruption risk) is actually more vulnerable than one in the bottom-right (low profit, low disruption risk). This reframes strategic priorities: instead of solely chasing profit optimization, companies must simultaneously invest in making business models more sustainable and harder to disrupt.
