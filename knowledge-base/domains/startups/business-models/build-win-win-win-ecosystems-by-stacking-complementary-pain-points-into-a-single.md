Po-Shen Loh's education business stacks three pain points into one ecosystem: (1) middle school students need engaging math education that teaches thinking, (2) math-talented high schoolers need EQ development and mentorship experience, (3) drama/acting graduates need flexible paid part-time work to support their creative careers. Each group's participation solves the others' problems.

The key architectural insight is that it took him 8 years to discover this stacking — and the breakthrough came from improvisational comedy classes he took to improve his own communication. The improv connection revealed the drama-student pain point, which completed the triangle. This suggests that multi-sided ecosystem design often requires the founder to personally inhabit each stakeholder's world rather than theoretically model it.

The scaling mechanism is also notable: this model can reach 100,000 high schoolers teaching 1 million middle schoolers because the high schoolers are motivated by genuine skill development (EQ + coaching from professional actors), not just altruism. The discipline test is: 'Can I explain to their parent why this is the best use of their child's time?' If yes, it's genuinely win-win.
