The traditional SaaS per-seat pricing model assumes value scales with human headcount. But when AI agents handle CRM updates, customer support, and HR workflows, companies need fewer seats while consuming more compute. Klarna dropping Salesforce and Workday illustrates this — they replaced human-operated SaaS with AI automation, making per-seat fees nonsensical.

This creates a strategic imperative for SaaS founders: design pricing around outcomes or usage from day one. Companies like OpenAI already charge per token, not per user. SaaS businesses that cling to per-seat models will face churn as customers realize they're paying for empty chairs. The winners will price on value delivered — API calls processed, workflows automated, insights generated — rather than bodies in seats.
