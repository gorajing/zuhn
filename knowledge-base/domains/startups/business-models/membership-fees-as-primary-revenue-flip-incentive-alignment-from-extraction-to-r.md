Costco derives 75% of its profit from membership fees, not product markup. This fundamentally changes the company's optimization function: raising prices on any item generates pennies today but risks losing a $60-$120 annual renewal. The result is a self-imposed 15% maximum markup (vs Walmart's 24% or Home Depot's 35%), which creates a virtuous cycle where low prices drive renewals, which fund the ability to keep prices low.

This model is structurally different from Amazon Prime, where membership is optional and self-selecting — it creates loyalty but doesn't change the underlying business model. Costco's mandatory membership makes the fee the business model itself, not an add-on. The 90% renewal rate validates that customers perceive the value exchange as fair, creating predictable revenue that shareholders love as a low-risk, long-term investment.
