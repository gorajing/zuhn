Chris Best frames Substack as 'decentralized at the layer that actually matters — ownership.' Writers own their subscriber lists. Readers pay creators directly. There's no algorithmic middleman deciding who sees what. This achieves the stated goals of the web3 creator economy movement — creator sovereignty, direct monetization, censorship resistance through portability — without tokens, wallets, or chain infrastructure.

This insight challenges the assumption that decentralization requires decentralized infrastructure. What creators actually need is not trustless consensus mechanisms but rather portable relationships and direct economics. Substack writers can export their entire subscriber list and leave at any time — that's more meaningful ownership than an NFT membership pass on a chain controlled by a different set of intermediaries. The lesson generalizes: when evaluating decentralization claims, ask which layer is actually being decentralized and whether that's the layer users care about.
