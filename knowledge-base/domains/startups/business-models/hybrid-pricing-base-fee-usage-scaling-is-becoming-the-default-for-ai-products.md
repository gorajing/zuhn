Neither pure subscription nor pure usage-based pricing fits AI: a flat subscription lets power users (5-10% who consume ~80% of compute) burn your margins, while pure usage scares customers away from experimenting because they can't predict their invoice. The hybrid model solves both — a base fee establishes a committed customer relationship and predictable revenue floor, while a usage/scaling fee on top lets customers experiment freely and pay for the value they actually derive, without alienating either light or heavy users.

Stripe's data shows this shift is already dominant and accelerating: hybrid pricing went from 6% of AI companies in 2024 to 41%, a 7x increase, and 56% of AI company leaders now use it. Reference customers — Intercom, Lovable, Eleven Labs, OpenAI, Anthropic — all run hybrid pricing. Notably, even legacy SaaS companies report moving to hybrid the moment they add LLM/AI features, because flat SaaS pricing starts eroding margins once variable compute costs enter the product.
