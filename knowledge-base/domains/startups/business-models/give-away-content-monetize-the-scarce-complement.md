Graham outlines the two viable paths when content becomes free: give it away and monetize indirectly (concerts, t-shirts, advertising), or embody it in physical objects people will pay for. The key economic insight is that when one complement becomes free, demand increases for its paired scarce resource.

This framework applies far beyond publishing. In software, open-source projects monetize through hosting, support, and enterprise features. In AI, models are commoditizing while data curation, fine-tuning expertise, and application layers retain value. The strategic question is always: what's the scarce complement to the thing that just became free?
