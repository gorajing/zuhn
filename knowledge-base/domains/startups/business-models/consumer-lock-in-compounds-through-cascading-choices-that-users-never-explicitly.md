The video highlights a subtle but powerful mechanism: nobody consciously 'chose' to be locked into Apple's ecosystem. They chose to buy an iPhone, which required choosing an App Store, which led to buying apps, which created switching costs, which made leaving increasingly expensive. Each subsequent choice feels voluntary but is actually constrained by the initial decision.

This cascading lock-in model explains why tech ecosystems are stickier than traditional brand loyalty. A grocery store customer can switch to a competitor with zero cost. An iPhone user switching to Android loses purchased apps, learned workflows, iCloud data, and device interoperability. For startup founders building platforms, this means the most durable moat isn't any single feature but the accumulation of small dependencies that make leaving progressively harder. The ethical tension is real: this lock-in simultaneously delivers genuine convenience (integrated experience) and genuine harm (reduced choice).
