Costco makes 75% of its revenue from membership fees, not product markups. This fundamentally changes its optimization function: instead of maximizing per-item margin, it maximizes renewal probability. Every pricing decision filters through 'will this impress customers enough to renew at $60/year?' — making price increases irrational since a few cents today costs $60 next year.

This creates a durable moat because competitors optimizing for product margin literally cannot match Costco's prices without changing their entire business model. Amazon Prime creates loyalty but doesn't shift the core revenue model — Amazon still profits from product sales. The distinction matters: Costco's constraint is structural, not strategic, which is why it holds even under competitive pressure.
