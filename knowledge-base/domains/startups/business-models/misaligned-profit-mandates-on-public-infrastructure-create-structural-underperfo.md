SNCF operates 14,000 daily trips (800 high-speed) for 66M people because French taxpayers cover about half the operating cost per journey. Amtrak runs 300 daily trips for 319M people while being structured as a for-profit, government-subsidized corporation — an inherent contradiction. The mandate to be profitable prevents the investment needed to become profitable. This pattern appears in startups when founders choose a business model that structurally conflicts with their growth requirements — for example, charging enterprise prices while targeting SMBs, or requiring profitability from a marketplace before achieving liquidity. The business model must be designed to support the investment horizon the product actually needs.
