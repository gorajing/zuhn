The mattress industry's 10-year replacement cycle creates a structural ceiling that no amount of marketing can overcome. Casper's growth decelerated from 43% (2017-2018) to 23% (2018-2019) to 18% (2019-2020) despite continued aggressive advertising. With first customers arriving in 2015, true retention wouldn't be measurable until 2025.

This is the market reality trap: venture capital and VC-fueled advertising can create the illusion of a high-growth technology company, but Casper sells mattresses — a physical product in a slow, constrained market. The Silicon Valley narrative of exponential growth simply doesn't apply to industries with inherently long purchase cycles. The $3B+ poured into DTC companies by venture capitalists was predicated on growth patterns that the underlying markets could never sustain.
