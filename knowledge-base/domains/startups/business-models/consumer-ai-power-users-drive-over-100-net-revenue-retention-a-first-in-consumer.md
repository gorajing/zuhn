A paradigm-breaking metric has emerged in consumer AI: top companies achieve greater than 100% net revenue retention. This was previously considered impossible in consumer software, where churn always exceeded expansion. The mechanism is usage-based pricing layered on subscriptions — users can exceed their monthly quota and pay more, either by upgrading tiers or purchasing additional tokens/usage.

This separates the exceptional consumer AI companies from merely good ones. The implication is that AI may fundamentally be a power user story — the depth of value and monetization is so much higher per user than in pre-AI consumer products that power users aren't just edge cases, they're the business. Everyone else may be 'just traffic.' For founders, this means designing products with deep engagement loops that reward heavy usage, not just optimizing for broad shallow adoption.
