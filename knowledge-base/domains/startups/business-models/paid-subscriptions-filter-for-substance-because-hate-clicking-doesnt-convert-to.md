Ad-supported platforms optimize for attention regardless of sentiment — hate-clicks and rage-shares generate the same revenue as genuine engagement. Subscription models invert this dynamic entirely: nobody pays monthly for content that merely provokes them. This creates a natural selection pressure toward substance.

Substack's growth to 5M paid subscriptions demonstrates this at scale. The payment gate doesn't just fund creators — it restructures the entire incentive landscape. Writers who succeed on subscriptions must deliver consistent value, not just viral moments. This principle extends beyond publishing: any business model where customers vote with recurring payments will naturally drift toward quality, while ad-supported models will drift toward engagement maximization regardless of user welfare.
