Facebook's entire value — the content, connections, conversations — is created by users while the company simply maintains the platform. IKEA shifts assembly labor to customers. Red Hat builds a billion-dollar business on top of open-source community work. This pattern of externalizing value creation is described as a 'least publicized weapon of mass destruction' in business model design, and for good reason.

The leverage is extraordinary: instead of linearly scaling headcount with value produced, you create a structure where value scales with participation. This fundamentally changes the cost curve. The key insight is that this isn't parasitic — it works when the arrangement is mutually beneficial. Users get a platform, community gets distribution, customers get lower prices. The business model designer's job is to find arrangements where externalized work creates genuine value for the worker too.
