Garrett Langley proposes a concrete solution to the police staffing crisis: create a Teach for America equivalent where college graduates serve 2-4 years in policing roles (including civilian/analyst positions without guns) in exchange for student debt relief. The staffing shortage is entirely cultural — nothing structural has changed about the job, only the social stigma.

The compounding damage is severe: departments lowered standards, some (Memphis) hired criminals who then committed homicides. Early retirement skyrocketed during social unrest and never recovered. Atlanta went from 60% to 75-80% staffed, but the gap is all inexperienced 21-year-olds who won't know how to solve homicides for 20 years. Higher-tech policing also makes the career more attractive to educated candidates.
