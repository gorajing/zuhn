The portfolio framework explicitly positions new business models at the bottom of the profitability axis — they are not yet established and cannot be expected to match existing businesses on financial metrics. This has a critical implication for how organizations allocate resources and evaluate performance.

Companies that hold new ventures to the same profitability standards as mature businesses systematically kill innovation. The portfolio map makes this visible by showing that the goal for new models is upward and rightward movement over time, not immediate top-right positioning. This creates a visual language for organizational ambidexterity — the ability to manage existing businesses for profit while simultaneously nurturing new models that aren't yet profitable but may be more sustainable long-term.
