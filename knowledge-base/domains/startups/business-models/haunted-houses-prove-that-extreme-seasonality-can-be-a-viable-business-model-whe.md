Haunted houses are a $300M industry with 1,200 US locations, concentrated in Ohio (cheap land, abundance of space). The economics: $250K+ to build, only 4-6 weekends in October to recoup, no alternative use the other 10 months. No bank will lend to such a risky seasonal venture, so the industry is 100% bootstrapped independents. The winning formula: own the land (avoid year-round rent), build yourself (the Ohio operators came from construction), commit to a single cohesive theme (not four separate haunts), and innovate annually to drive repeat visits. The constraint that makes it work: you can't franchise or scale a haunted house because the experience IS the specific physical space.
