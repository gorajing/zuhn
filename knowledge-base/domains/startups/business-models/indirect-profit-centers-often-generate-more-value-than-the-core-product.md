McDonald's only operates 15% of its restaurants directly but owns nearly all their buildings and land. Franchisees pay 8-15% of revenue as rent on top of normal royalties — meaning McDonald's profits whether individual locations do or not. After buying heavily during the 2008 recession, McDonald's accumulated $30B+ in tax-deductible real estate assets.

This pattern — the 'hidden profit center' — recurs across industries. Printers sell at cost to profit from ink. Costco uses low prices funded by membership fees. The strategic insight is that when competition commoditizes your core product, the winning move is often to shift your profit center to something adjacent that competitors don't think to optimize. Identifying where the real margin lives (vs. where customers think it lives) is a fundamental strategic skill.
