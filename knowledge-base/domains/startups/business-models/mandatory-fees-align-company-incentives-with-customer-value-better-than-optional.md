Costco generates 75% of its revenue from membership fees, not product markup. This fundamentally changes its incentive structure: raising prices saves pennies today but risks losing $60-120 annual renewals. Amazon Prime, by contrast, is optional and self-selecting — it creates loyalty but doesn't restructure the underlying business model.

This pattern generalizes beyond retail. Any business that derives primary revenue from access fees rather than per-transaction margins becomes structurally aligned with customer satisfaction. The membership model forces the company to continuously prove its value, creating a virtuous cycle where retention drives investment in lower prices, which drives higher retention. The 90% annual renewal rate at Costco is evidence this alignment works.
