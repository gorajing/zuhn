One featured startup reframes trade-in programs not as standalone resale but as a payment option at checkout, letting shoppers turn unused goods into instant discounts. Early traction shows +45% average order value and 5-10% revenue penetration, suggesting this framing meaningfully changes consumer behavior.

The insight is that the $3,000+ of unused goods per household represents latent purchasing power, but only if the friction of selling is eliminated. By embedding trade-in into the existing checkout flow rather than requiring a separate marketplace interaction, the startup collapses what was a multi-step, multi-platform process into a single transaction. This is a pattern worth watching: reframing an existing behavior (resale) as a new payment primitive rather than a separate activity.
