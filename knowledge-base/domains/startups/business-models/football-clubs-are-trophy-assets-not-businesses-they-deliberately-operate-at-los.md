The football club business model looks like a flywheel: win trophies → grow fanbase → increase monetization → buy better players → win more. But the economics are a trap: clubs continuously bankrupt themselves to sustain on-field success, hoping that winning will eventually generate enough future revenue to offset today's costs. Or owners intentionally run losses for decades, treating the club as a trophy asset to flip for billions in appreciation. Player salaries consume all revenue. The 'business of football' is really the business of vanity ownership — similar dynamics in professional sports leagues worldwide.
