The 'Flappy Bird era of SaaS' framing captures a real dynamic: AI tooling has lowered the cost of building and distributing SaaS products to near zero, creating a Cambrian explosion of point solutions that achieve rapid adoption but lack the stickiness needed to sustain ARR. Distribution is no longer the bottleneck; retention and workflow integration are.

For founders, this implies that virality without structural lock-in is a vanity metric. The defensible SaaS businesses will be those embedded in mission-critical workflows, accumulating proprietary data flywheels, or building network effects that competitors cannot easily replicate. The playbook of 'get viral, raise money, figure out retention' is increasingly fatal — the market punishes this cycle faster than Series A timelines allow for correction.
