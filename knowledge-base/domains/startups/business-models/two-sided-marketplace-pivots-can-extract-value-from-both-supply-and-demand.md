Daniel Ek's own description of Spotify shifted from 'a free library of music' to 'a technology-driven layer between fans and artists' operating as a two-sided marketplace. Labels now pay Spotify for playlist inclusion and higher rankings. Artists can pay to promote merch and ticket sales through the platform. This represents a fundamental business model evolution: the same entity that users pay to access music is simultaneously the entity that artists must pay to reach those users. This mirrors how Google evolved from organic search to a system where businesses pay for visibility, or how Amazon charges both shoppers (Prime) and sellers (marketplace fees). The risk is that optimizing for both revenue streams creates conflicts of interest — playlist rankings may reflect who pays most rather than what listeners actually want, which could erode the trust and user experience that built the platform in the first place.
