Photosynthesis evolved to produce glucose for energy storage. Oxygen was literally waste — a toxic byproduct of splitting water molecules to harvest electrons. Yet this 'waste' product oxygenated Earth's atmosphere and enabled the evolution of all aerobic life, making it arguably the most consequential chemical output in biological history.

This pattern recurs in business: Slack was a byproduct of a failed game company. Flickr emerged from a failed multiplayer game. AWS started as internal infrastructure. The insight for founders is to pay attention to what their process produces incidentally, not just what it's designed to produce. The byproduct that seems worthless in your current frame may be transformative in a different context. Deliberately auditing your 'waste outputs' is an underutilized innovation strategy.
