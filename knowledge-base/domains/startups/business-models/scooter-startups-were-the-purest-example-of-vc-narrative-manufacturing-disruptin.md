Bird became the fastest company in history to reach $1B valuation. The playbook: dump hardware without permission (Uber model), price below transit to manufacture usage, race competitors for city coverage. No moat in hardware (Chinese commodity), software (QR code scanner), or brand. VCs named it 'micromobility' and pointed to Chinese dockless bikes as proof — which collapsed from oversaturation a year later. Bird went bankrupt. Most scooters ended in landfills. Cities now cap deployment, fine improper parking, and require permits. The lesson: not every inconvenience is a market. Walking was not a problem that needed disrupting.
