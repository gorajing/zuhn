In fast-moving markets, product features and technology advantages are rapidly copied or made obsolete. Companies that have survived and thrived through multiple economic cycles—like Salesforce—share a common trait: they invested in the design of their overall business model, not just their product.

This reframes the startup competition question from 'how do we build a better product?' to 'how do we design a system of value creation, delivery, and capture that is structurally difficult to replicate?' The 9-mechanic assessment framework suggests business models have multiple independent dimensions that can each be strengthened, meaning the compounding effect of improvements across all dimensions creates a moat that single-dimension competitors cannot match.
