NFX identifies a consistent 4-phase evolution pattern across vacation rentals, real estate, and food delivery: (1) horizontal lead generation (Craigslist), (2) vertical lead generation (HomeAway), (3) vertical transactional (Airbnb), and (4) tightly managed marketplace (Pacaso). Each phase moves closer to owning the actual transaction.

The key driver is that moving closer to the transaction enables embedding financial services — insurance, lending, payments — which dramatically improves unit economics. A marketplace stuck at the lead-gen phase captures a fraction of the value compared to one that owns the full transaction. This pattern suggests founders should plan their roadmap around progressive transaction ownership rather than trying to leap directly to phase 4.
