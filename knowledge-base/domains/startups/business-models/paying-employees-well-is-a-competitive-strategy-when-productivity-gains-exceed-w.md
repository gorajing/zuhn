Costco's average wage of $21/hour is double the U.S. retail average, supplemented by better health insurance and retirement benefits. Rather than eroding margins, this investment produces employees who are reportedly three times more productive than the industry average. The math works: 2x the cost for 3x the output is a 50% efficiency gain.

This challenges the common startup assumption that labor costs are simply expenses to minimize. When higher wages reduce turnover (which is extremely expensive in retail), increase expertise, and improve customer experience, the 'cost' is actually an investment with measurable returns. The key qualifier is that this works in contexts where individual employee productivity meaningfully varies and where retention compounds skill — it's not universally applicable but is often underestimated.
