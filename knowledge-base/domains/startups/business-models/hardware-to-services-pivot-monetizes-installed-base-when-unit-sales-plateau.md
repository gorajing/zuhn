Apple recognized that used iPhones still generate revenue through App Store purchases, Apple Music, iCloud, and Apple Video subscriptions. Google pays an estimated $9 billion annually just to be the default search engine — revenue that flows regardless of whether the iPhone is new or used. This makes every active iPhone a revenue-generating node in Apple's ecosystem.

This is the 'in-app purchase model applied to hardware' — the device becomes a gateway rather than the product itself. For any company facing hardware commoditization, the playbook is clear: build ecosystem stickiness (Apple Watch, AirPods, iPad), then layer services on top. The installed base becomes the moat, and the hardware margin becomes less important than lifetime ecosystem value.
