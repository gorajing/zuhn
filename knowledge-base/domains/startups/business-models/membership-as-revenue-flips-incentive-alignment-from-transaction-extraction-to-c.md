Costco derives 75% of its profit from membership fees, not product markups. This fundamentally changes its incentive structure: raising prices on any item gains pennies today but risks losing a $60-$120 annual renewal. The result is a self-imposed 15% maximum markup (11% average), far below Walmart's 24% or Home Depot's 35%.

This contrasts sharply with Amazon Prime, which creates loyalty through sunk-cost psychology but doesn't fundamentally restructure the company's revenue model — Amazon still profits primarily from transactions. The distinction matters for founders: a loyalty program bolted onto a transactional model creates stickiness, but a membership-as-revenue model creates genuine incentive alignment. The 90% renewal rate is the evidence that customers perceive the alignment as real.
