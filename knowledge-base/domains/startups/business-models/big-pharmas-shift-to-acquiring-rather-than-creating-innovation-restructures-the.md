The pharmaceutical industry is undergoing a structural shift where large companies' internal research has failed to deliver blockbuster drugs, leading them to outsource innovation by acquiring promising startups at later stages. While this creates acquisition opportunities for startups, it also means the expensive, risky early-stage R&D now falls on smaller companies with far fewer resources.

This restructuring makes market validation even more critical for healthcare startups. They can't afford to burn through limited capital on technology development alone — they need to simultaneously prove market viability to become attractive acquisition targets. The shift also means startups must understand not just their end users but also the acquisition criteria of potential pharma buyers, adding another layer of market risk that must be validated early.
