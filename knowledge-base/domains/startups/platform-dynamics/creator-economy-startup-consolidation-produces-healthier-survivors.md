After years of explosive growth that peaked with 11 unicorns in 2021, the creator economy startup landscape has contracted significantly. Fewer new startups are launching for creators, largely because the previous wave raised too much capital too quickly and couldn't sustain growth.

However, a larger percentage of startups from recent cohorts remain operational compared to those from earlier years. This pattern mirrors classic market cycle dynamics: overcrowded markets with easy capital produce many weak players, while post-correction environments reward the survivors with less competition and more focused opportunities. The implication for founders is that entering a post-hype market with disciplined capital deployment can be more advantageous than riding the initial wave.
