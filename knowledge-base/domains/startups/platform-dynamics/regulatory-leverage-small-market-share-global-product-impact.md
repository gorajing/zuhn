The EU battery law demonstrates the 'Brussels Effect' — a regulatory body governing ~450M consumers can dictate global product design because manufacturers won't absorb the cost of parallel product lines. Apple won't build one iPhone for Europe and another for everywhere else. This pattern applies broadly: any sufficiently large market that sets strict standards effectively exports those standards worldwide. For startups, this means monitoring EU regulatory pipelines is a form of competitive intelligence — upcoming mandates signal forced industry redesigns that create windows for new entrants with compliant-by-default architectures.
