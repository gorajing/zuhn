Network effects create compounding defensibility: the more users on a platform, the more valuable it becomes to each individual user, which attracts more users, creating a flywheel that competitors cannot easily replicate with features or price alone. This is why VCs treat them as a primary investment signal — a business with genuine network effects gets harder to displace over time rather than easier.

There are multiple types: direct (social networks), indirect (marketplaces), data (ML-powered products that improve with scale), and protocol (communication standards). The most durable businesses combine more than one type. For founders, the actionable implication is to design for network effects from the start — retrofitting them is nearly impossible. If your product does not get better as more people use it, you are building a product, not a platform.
