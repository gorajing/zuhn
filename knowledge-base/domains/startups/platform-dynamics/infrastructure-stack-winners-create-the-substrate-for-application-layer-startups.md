The article describes a recurring pattern: infrastructure layers compete intensely, a few winners consolidate, and the resulting cheap/reliable substrate spawns an explosion of application-layer companies. This played out in cloud (AWS consolidated → SaaS boom), AI (foundation models consolidating → AI app layer emerging), and is beginning in space (launch consolidating → space applications nascent).

For founders, the implication is that timing your entry relative to the stack matters enormously. Building applications before infrastructure is settled means fighting infrastructure instability. Building infrastructure after consolidation means fighting incumbents with billions. The sweet spot is building applications just as the infrastructure layer beneath you stabilizes — you get reliable substrate without the premium pricing that comes once the opportunity is obvious to everyone.
