Real estate digitization illustrates a repeatable pattern: Phase 1 (2005-2015) was information access — Trulia and Zillow put property data online, empowering consumers. Phase 2 (2015+) is transaction transformation — companies like Opendoor and Ribbon reimagine how buying and selling actually work. Phase 3 is operational — tech-enabled construction, maintenance, and management.

Each phase depends on the previous one: you can't build alternative transaction models until consumers have information parity with brokers, and you can't optimize operations until transaction data flows digitally. This sequencing pattern applies broadly — in healthcare, fintech, and logistics, the same information→transaction→operations progression holds. For founders, this means timing your entry to the current phase of an industry's digitization, not jumping ahead to Phase 3 when Phase 1 hasn't completed.
