NASA's PhoneSat project (2009) used commercial smartphones as satellite onboard computers because consumer-market scale had driven GPS, accelerometers, radios, and processors below $1,000 — cheaper than any bespoke satellite component. This pattern recurs whenever a consumer market reaches sufficient scale: the volume economics make purpose-built alternatives irrational. The implication for startups is that riding consumer hardware curves can unlock institutional markets that were previously cost-prohibitive. Instead of building specialized hardware, founders should ask what consumer-grade components already exceed the performance threshold for their target use case.
