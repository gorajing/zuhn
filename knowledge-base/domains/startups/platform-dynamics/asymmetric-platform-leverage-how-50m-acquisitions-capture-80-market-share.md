Google acquired Android for $50M in 2005 — a rounding error compared to the $12.5B Motorola deal or even the $1.65B YouTube purchase. By 2013, Android ran on 211 of 261 million smartphones sold globally (80%+). The key mechanism was open-sourcing the OS, which converted every phone manufacturer (Samsung, HTC, Huawei, Xiaomi) from a potential competitor into a distribution channel for Google's services. This inverted the traditional acquisition calculus: instead of buying market share directly, Google bought a platform that made others' market share work for them. China's entire smartphone industry was enabled by this single decision, creating billions of search and ad impressions Google would never have reached through hardware alone.
