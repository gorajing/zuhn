Amtrak operates on 21,300 miles of track but owns only 730 miles. On routes like the California Zephyr, Union Pacific and BNSF own the rails and naturally prioritize their own freight trains, causing Amtrak's on-time rate to drop as low as 31%. This is a structural problem, not an operational one — the track owners have no competitive incentive to accommodate a tenant who has no alternative. The startup parallel is clear: building on a platform you don't control (someone else's API, marketplace, or distribution channel) creates an asymmetric dependency where the platform owner's priorities will always override yours when resources are constrained.
