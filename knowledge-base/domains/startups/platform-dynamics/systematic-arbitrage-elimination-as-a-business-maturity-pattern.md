Airlines evolved their loyalty programs through two critical pivots. First, they switched from distance-based to revenue-based earning: instead of 1 mile = 1 point (exploitable via cheap long-haul routings), they moved to 1 dollar = 5 points, making it mathematically impossible to earn more in points than you spend. Second, they moved from fixed to dynamic redemption charts: a flight that costs more in cash now costs more in points too, eliminating the arbitrage of redeeming points on high-demand days when cash prices were high but point prices were fixed.

This two-sided arbitrage elimination is a pattern that repeats across platforms. Early platforms tolerate power-user exploits to drive adoption, then systematically close loopholes once scale is achieved. Uber surge pricing, Airbnb's smart pricing, and app store commission tiers all follow this arc. For founders: design your system knowing you'll close loopholes later, but build the data infrastructure to detect them from day one.
