Graham notes that silence is a '100% accurate predictor of death' for YC startups, and proposes that the causal arrow may run both ways: not just that dying startups go silent, but that staying in regular contact actually prevents death. The weekly YC dinner functions as a 'mini Demo Day' — a recurring deadline that forces founders to have something to show, which in turn forces them to make progress.

This is an organizational design insight with broad applicability. The mechanism is simple: regular external check-ins create micro-deadlines that combat the demoralization-inaction spiral. When founders feel demoralized, the natural instinct is to withdraw and stop communicating — but this withdrawal removes the very accountability pressure that could pull them out of the spiral. The practical implication is that founders should deliberately build accountability structures (co-working with other startups, regular investor updates, public building) that make silence impossible.
