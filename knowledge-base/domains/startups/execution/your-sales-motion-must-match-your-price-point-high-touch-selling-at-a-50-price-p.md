Bosmeny references Christoph Janz's 'five ways to build a $100M business' framework to illustrate that price point and sales motion are inextricably linked. If customers pay $100K/year, you need 1,000 of them and can afford high-touch enterprise sales. If they pay $10/year, you need 10 million and must invest in self-service, marketing funnels, and referral mechanisms instead.

The trap is founders using the high-touch, conference-and-call approach Bosmeny describes — which works brilliantly for enterprise sales — on a $50/month product. The math simply doesn't work: you cannot fly to conferences and do 30-minute demos for a product that generates $600/year per customer. This forces a strategic decision early: either raise your price to support a sales motion, or invest in marketing and self-service to support a low price. The worst position is the middle, doing neither well.
