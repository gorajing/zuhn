Graham found that having kids forced him onto a fixed schedule, eliminating the ability to let work 'spill promiscuously' through his entire life. Despite having less total time, he adapted and still got enough done. Work, like love, finds a way — when there are only certain times it can happen, it happens at those times.

This validates the broader principle that constraints can preserve or even enhance productivity. Parkinson's Law in reverse: when you can't work all the time, you work more intentionally during the time you have. For founders and creators worried about losing productivity to life commitments, the evidence suggests that focused blocks with hard stops can replace sprawling unstructured time without catastrophic output loss.
