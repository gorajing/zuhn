The F-14 chief engineer's formula ('gather a tight group of people, give them very little money, and very little time') echoes across the collection: Ford's obsession with value ('the public should always be wondering how it is possible to give so much for the money'), Bentley and McIlroy's 'the key to performance is elegance, not battalions of special cases', and Steve Jobs' 'focusing is about saying no.'

The mechanism is that abundance creates optionality, and optionality creates decision paralysis and scope creep. Constraints force prioritization — when you can only do three things, you're forced to identify which three matter most. This is why startups consistently out-innovate large companies in specific domains despite having a fraction of the resources. The constraint isn't a handicap to overcome; it's the active ingredient that produces focus.
