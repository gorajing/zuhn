Cuban identifies a powerful litmus test for entrepreneurial readiness: if you have to ask what business to start, you're not ready. People who are genuinely ready to start a business already have the idea burning inside them because they've been consumed by curiosity about a specific problem or industry.

The corollary is equally important: preparation is the bridge between not-ready and ready. Cuban advocates for learning obsessively about an industry while still employed — nights, weekends, lunch breaks — making calls to test if people will actually pay, saving money to reduce downside risk. The leap doesn't have to be blind. But the readiness signal is internal: you know you're ready when you can't stop thinking about how a specific business should work. If you need someone else to tell you what to build, you haven't done enough preparation yet.
