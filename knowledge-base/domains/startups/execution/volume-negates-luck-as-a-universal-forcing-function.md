The principle 'volume negates luck' reframes success from a probabilistic event into an engineering problem. If each cold outreach has a 1% success rate, doing 10 attempts gives you a 10% chance — but doing 1,000 makes success virtually certain. This applies across domains: job applications, sales calls, investor pitches, content creation.

The insight is particularly powerful because it removes the psychological burden of any single attempt mattering. When you internalize that no individual rejection costs you anything (you're already at zero), the only variable is throughput. This creates an asymmetric payoff structure: losses are bounded at zero, but a single win can be transformative. The practical application is replacing hope-based strategies with volume-based ones and measuring inputs rather than outcomes.
