Nota's trajectory is a masterclass in timing endurance. Founded in 2015 around AI model lightweighting, they ran four beta services with cold market reception ('Do I really have to pay money to use it?'). The CEO describes 2022-2023 as the hardest period -- after making the product and feeling the market's indifference.

The key belief was simple but non-obvious: as more AI models emerge, the need to optimize them for existing hardware will only grow. They were proven right -- new models appearing daily created exactly the demand they predicted. From 1 billion won in 2023 to 10 billion won two years later, then IPO. The lesson isn't 'wait long enough and you'll succeed' but rather 'if the technological need is real and growing, persistence through timing mismatch is a valid strategy.' The CEO's framing: 'This technology will definitely be necessary. I just don't know exactly when.'
