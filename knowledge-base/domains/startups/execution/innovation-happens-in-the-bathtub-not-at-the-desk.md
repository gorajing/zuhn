Utley quotes the history of innovation as 'the bed, the bus, and the bathtub'—moments when we're not actively thinking about work. Churchill was literally in the bathtub when dictating national addresses. The insight: creative breakthroughs happen during unfocused time, not during structured work sessions.

AI's transformative potential isn't replacing desk work—it's enabling people to develop ideas whenever they emerge. With a contextually aware AI assistant available 24/7, the gap between having an insight and developing it into something actionable shrinks to near zero. The founders and creators who build their workflow around this—capturing ideas in real time and developing them through AI—will dramatically outproduce those still tied to structured work sessions.
