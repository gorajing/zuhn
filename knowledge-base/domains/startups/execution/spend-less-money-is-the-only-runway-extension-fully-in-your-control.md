Seibel's operations advice is blunt: the number one problem companies have is spending too much money. His prescription is equally direct — pay yourself less, get a crappier office, and download your bank statement every month to review every line item. If you're the CEO and you don't know your expenses, you're not doing your job.

This matters because runway extension through cost reduction is the only lever that doesn't depend on anyone else. You can't control whether investors write checks. You can't control whether users show up. But you can absolutely control what you spend. Seibel's own company Social Cam grew to over 20 million downloads with just three people (the co-founders, zero employees). Instagram sold for a billion with under 20 people. The examples reinforce the point: hire slow, spend less, and extend the runway that gives you time to find product-market fit.
