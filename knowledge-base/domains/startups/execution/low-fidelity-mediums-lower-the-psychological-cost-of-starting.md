Graham identifies medium selection as a key tactical lever for overcoming the fear of early work. A sketch in a notebook feels inherently less committal than carving in stone—the same idea carries different psychological weight depending on how it's expressed. Digital media's advantage isn't primarily its software capabilities but that it reduces upfront commitment, letting you start something new with minimal psychological overhead.

John Carmack's example of game mods illustrates this perfectly: Quake's crude technical limitations meant quick gameplay hacks weren't far from the official game, spawning entire careers. When commercial game quality rose, modding became psychologically prohibitive—until Minecraft and Roblox deliberately embraced crude aesthetics, once again making innovative gameplay the primary value. The pattern suggests that the most productive creative environments are those where the gap between prototype and product is deliberately minimized.
