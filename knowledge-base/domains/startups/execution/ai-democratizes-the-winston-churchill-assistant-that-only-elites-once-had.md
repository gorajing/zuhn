Utley uses Winston Churchill dictating speeches from the bathtub as the ultimate example of context-rich assistance. Churchill's assistant knew his voice, his intent, his audience, and could produce drafts that needed only direction, not creation from scratch. This capability was previously available only to the most powerful people in the world.

Now, with AI tools that can absorb your context, writing style, and intent, 'the poorest villager in Palo Alto' has access to this same capability. The shift isn't about AI writing for you—it's about AI understanding your context deeply enough that you can direct rather than produce. This is technically possible today, but most people haven't set up their AI tools with enough context to realize it.
