Hormozi's final pillar — 'when to start' — is counterintuitively 'now, especially when busy.' The conventional wisdom is to wait for a clear schedule before taking on new challenges. Hormozi argues this is a trap because free time expands to fill available space with low-value activities. You'll never feel 'ready' because Parkinson's Law ensures your current commitments will always fill your available bandwidth.

When you're already busy and add something important, it forces genuine prioritization. Low-value activities get eliminated by necessity rather than willpower. This is a constraints-breed-creativity argument applied to time: scarcity of time forces efficiency gains that abundance never would. The practical application for founders and operators is to stop treating busyness as a valid reason to delay strategic initiatives — the busy period is actually the optimal time to start because it forces you to cut what doesn't matter.
