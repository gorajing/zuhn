Google's first office at 165 University Ave illustrates a key startup principle: physical environment shapes work habits. When employees are surrounded by restaurants and street life, they stay after conventional hours, eat together, discuss ideas, and return to implement them. This organic extension of the workday drives velocity.

The fact that Google and Facebook only moved to office parks when forced by space constraints—not by choice—reinforces that founders intuitively understand this. The office park is where you end up, not where you start. Early-stage startups should optimize for environments that blur the line between work and life, because that blur is where breakthrough iteration happens.
