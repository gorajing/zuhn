Ambitious people frequently interpret boredom or frustration as evidence they're in the wrong field, triggering a cycle of starting and abandoning pursuits. Hormozi argues this misinterpretation is the primary mechanism behind inconsistency — the belief that the 'right' path should feel exciting consistently.

In reality, every path to significant outcomes passes through extended valleys where the work is repetitive, unrewarding, and invisible. Recognizing this as structural rather than personal eliminates the false signal that pushes people to restart. The competitive advantage goes to those who stay in the valley while others are busy restarting at the exciting beginning of something new.
