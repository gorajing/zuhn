Block's CTO describes a deliberate organizational restructuring: they moved from a GM structure (where Square, Cash App, and Tidal operated as independent units) to a centralized functional organization. This wasn't arbitrary; the GM structure kept valuable AI capabilities locked within silos and prevented the cross-pollination and unified policies needed for AI transformation.

The CTO acknowledges organizational design is contextual: the GM structure made sense when giving Cash App autonomy was the priority. But in an era of seismic weekly shifts in AI, having singular organizational focus and the ability to unify engineering standards, share platform teams, and drive technical excellence centrally became essential. The lesson: org structure should change with the strategic imperative, and AI transformation requires centralization that earlier growth phases may not have needed.
