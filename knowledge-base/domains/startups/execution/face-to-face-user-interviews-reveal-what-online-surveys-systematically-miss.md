At Apple, when building the first generation of AirPods, the team didn't do online surveys. They invited users to small interview rooms for face-to-face conversations. The reason: online surveys produce garbage data because people click the first option to get to the end. Nobody wants to give real feedback through a form.

More importantly, face-to-face interviews reveal what you didn't think to ask. Customers may care about D when you assumed they care about A, B, and C. If D isn't on your survey, you'll never discover it. Creati AI applied this principle to grow to 10M downloads—understanding what results users actually care about, not what the team assumed they'd care about.
