Sequoia partners challenge the room to connect their data flywheels to specific business metrics. When hands go up for 'having a data flywheel' but fewer can identify what metric it moves, the verdict is clear: 'Either you don't have a data flywheel or it just doesn't matter.'

This extends to their framework for evaluating AI companies: they distinguish 'vibe revenue' (feels good but isn't durable) from real behavior change, and insist on inspecting adoption, engagement, and retention metrics rather than topline revenue. Similarly, they note that current gross margins may be poor (high inference costs) but the trajectory matters: cost per token is down 99% in 18 months, and companies successfully moving from selling tools to selling outcomes can increase pricing. The flywheel must tie to one of these concrete improvements.
