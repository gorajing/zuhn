Xiaoyin initially dismissed services as non-technical and unsexy. But competitors who offered human support during virtual conferences (at $20K+ premiums) were winning deals. Conference organizers wouldn't trust pure software for events critical to their business — they needed a human on call. The lesson: for certain business types, services aren't overhead but a scalable revenue amplifier. As long as the service margins are healthy and the offering scales, the services-plus-software model can be superior to pure SaaS.
