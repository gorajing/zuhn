Hormozi distinguishes between stored energy (savings) and potential energy (income-generating capacity). He tracked monthly income rather than bank balance, and was willing to spend $2K from savings if it could turn his $5K/month income into $6K/month — because over a year that's an additional $12K. The wealthiest people he knows think exclusively in terms of skill acquisition.

The progression follows a clear ladder: first you buy skills with your own time and money (learning to sell, market, operate). Once your skills generate excess income, you start buying other people's time — effectively purchasing their 10 years of skill development by hiring them. Eventually you generalize from being good at specific skills to being good at managing people who have those skills. This is the universal path from broke to wealthy: specialize in skills, demonstrate mastery through results, then generalize to people management.
