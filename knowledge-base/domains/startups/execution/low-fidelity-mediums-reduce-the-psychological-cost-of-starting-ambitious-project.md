The conventional explanation for prototyping emphasizes speed and efficiency, but Graham identifies a deeper mechanism: low-fidelity mediums work because they lower the psychological barrier to starting. Calling something 'just a sketch' or 'just a hack' lets you judge initial results by a lower standard, bypassing the paralyzing fear of producing something lame. Once the project gains momentum, you can 'sneakily convert it to something more.'

John Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this — Quake's crude technical limitations meant quick gameplay hacks weren't far from the official game, spawning entire careers. When Minecraft and Roblox made crudeness the explicit aesthetic, innovative gameplay concepts became the dominant value, and single-author mods began outperforming professional teams. The practical lesson: deliberately choose mediums where the gap between early work and final work is small.
