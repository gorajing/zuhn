Anna Goldie describes the adoption challenge: TPU designers each owned specific blocks and had to personally accept responsibility for choosing an AI-generated layout over their own work. The AI layouts looked alien (curved donut shapes vs. human-preferred aligned patterns), and the stakes were enormous -- a bad layout could mean a failed tape-out costing millions.

The team showed data every single week for two years. They also made customers feel like co-creators by having the TPU team develop the cost functions the AI optimized against. Interestingly, the strongest resistance came not from the designers whose jobs were at risk (they were convinced by data), but from researchers whose prior methods were being outperformed -- 'there's something painful about pouring your soul into methods and then people from outside your field outperform using simpler approaches at scale.' This is a universal pattern when AI enters established domains.
