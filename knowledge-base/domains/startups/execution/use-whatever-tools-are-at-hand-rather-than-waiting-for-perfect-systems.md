The Geneva Hospital team used a physical whiteboard with paper post-its for alignment and Trello for task tracking — not because these were optimal tools, but because they were immediately available. The Strategyzer team explicitly notes 'we dived straight into the content by using whatever materials were at hand.'

This principle applies broadly: the cost of delayed alignment always exceeds the cost of imperfect tooling. A team aligned around hand-drawn plans will outexecute a team waiting for the perfect project management system. The hospital generated 32 actionable tasks in a single session precisely because they didn't waste time on tool selection.
