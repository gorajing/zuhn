In the Calvin Cycle, three rounds of carbon fixation produce six G3P molecules, but five of those six are immediately consumed to regenerate the three RuBP molecules needed to restart the cycle. Only one G3P—roughly 17% of gross output—becomes available product.

This regeneration ratio maps directly to operational overhead in any production system. Engineering teams spend most of their capacity maintaining existing systems, not shipping new features. Sales organizations reinvest most revenue into pipeline regeneration. Content creators spend 80%+ of effort on distribution, not creation. Teams that budget only for gross output without accounting for regeneration overhead will chronically under-deliver. The fix: measure net output (what actually ships to customers) separately from gross output, and plan capacity around the net number.
