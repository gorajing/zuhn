Web2 PM career ladders reward strategic thinking and long-term vision-setting as the path to seniority. But in web3 and other nascent markets where new protocols launch daily and fundamental paradigms shift in weeks, the half-life of any strategic vision is shorter than the time needed to execute on it. Jason Shah observed that no web3 PM writes vision documents—vision lives in technical whitepapers by protocol architects.

This is not anti-strategy; it is a recognition that in high-entropy environments, the feedback loop from execution is more strategically valuable than upfront planning. The PM who ships, observes market response, and ships again will converge on the right strategy faster than one who plans extensively before acting. This principle generalizes beyond web3 to any market in its early rapid-evolution phase: the correct ratio of execution to vision is inversely proportional to the rate of market change. As markets mature and the rate of paradigm shifts slows, the traditional balance reasserts itself.
