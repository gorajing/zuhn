To get a risk-averse Fortune 100 to fund an unprecedented 'pie-in-the-sky' project, Northwestern Mutual broke it into six-week sprints, each ending in a tangible, independently productizable deliverable (a data-finder, a metadata-enrichment proof, a pivoting bot). This gave leadership continuous visibility, control, and the ability to pull the plug at any point — deliberately engineered to eliminate sunk-cost bias ('we already paid a million dollars, let's just finish'). Each phase's output also fed the next, making the research self-propelling. Critically, the incremental build doubles as a build-vs-buy hedge: even if they ultimately adopt a vendor like Databricks Genie, they now hold benchmarks, know what 'good' looks like, and can drill past a fluffy vendor demo. The work pays for itself as a buying-decision input even if the final agent is never built — which is precisely what justified quarter-over-quarter reinvestment.
