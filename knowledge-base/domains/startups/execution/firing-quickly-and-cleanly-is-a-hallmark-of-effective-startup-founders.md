Someone at YC once said 'you're not a real founder until you've had to fire somebody.' This reflects the observation that startups inevitably hire people who don't work out, and the founder's response to this situation reveals their professional maturity. The specific best practices are revealing in their directness: fire quickly (don't let bad employees linger — good employees may quit if toxic ones stay), communicate with clear direct statements without apologizing or rationalizing, pay all wages and accrued vacation immediately (legal requirement), cut off all digital and physical access immediately (YC has seen fired founders hold GitHub passwords hostage), and repurchase any unvested shares right away. The key insight is that procrastination on firing has only downside — it's always easier to rationalize keeping someone than to have the difficult conversation, but the cost of delay compounds in lost productivity, cultural damage, and potential security risks.
