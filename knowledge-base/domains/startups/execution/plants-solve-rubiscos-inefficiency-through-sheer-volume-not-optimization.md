When RuBisCo proved too error-prone to replace, plants adopted a brute-force strategy: flood the system with so much of the enzyme that the net output is sufficient despite per-unit failure rates. At any given time, roughly 40 billion tons of RuBisCo exist on Earth — making it likely the most abundant protein on the planet.

This maps directly to startup execution strategy. When your core conversion process is inefficient but replacement is impractical (legacy code, entrenched workflows, regulatory constraints), scaling input volume can be more pragmatic than optimization. Top-of-funnel flooding compensates for leaky mid-funnel conversion. The key insight is that this isn't lazy — it's the mathematically rational response when the cost of fixing the core exceeds the cost of compensating with volume.
