Merge did extensive user interviews and competitive analysis before building anything. Ding says product-market fit 'mostly happened before we even started the company.' They deeply understood why competitors had failed, what customers would actually pay for, and why Merge would be better.

This inverts the 'move fast and break things' orthodoxy. For Merge's B2B integration market, spending 9 months understanding the landscape before building was faster than the build-iterate cycle would have been. The key: deeply understanding why existing solutions failed and what would make customers switch. When they finally built, the product was already validated. The research wasn't just about what to build—it was about understanding the competitive landscape well enough to know exactly why they'd win.
