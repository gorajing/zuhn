Misra was pursuing a PhD in machine learning when he realized the startup he was building on the side had more potential. He dropped out despite social pressure to complete the degree. The curriculum he'd already covered in India made much of the US education redundant anyway.

The insight: credentials have diminishing returns once you've learned the core skills. The opportunity cost of spending 3-5 more years in academia when a viable startup opportunity exists is enormous. This is especially true in fast-moving fields like AI where the market window for new ideas is measured in months, not years.
