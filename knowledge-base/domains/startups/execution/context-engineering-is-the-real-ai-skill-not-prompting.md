Utley distinguishes between prompting ('write me a sales email') and context engineering ('write me a sales email using our brand voice guidelines, referencing this customer call transcript, pulling from our product specs'). Most people expect AI to read their mind, then complain the output sounds generic.

The test for good context engineering: write down your prompt and all documentation, then give it to a human colleague. If they couldn't produce what you want, neither can AI. Everything implicit must become explicit—brand voice, audience, constraints, reference materials. This is the difference between AI as a toy and AI as a genuine force multiplier.
