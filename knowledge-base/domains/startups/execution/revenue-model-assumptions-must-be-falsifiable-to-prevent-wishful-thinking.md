The most dangerous failure mode for early-stage founders is operating on assumptions that feel reasonable but are never tested. When churn assumptions are wishful thinking, or when CAC climbs while ARPA stays flat, a well-structured model exposes these contradictions immediately—the payback calculation breaks visibly, and you can't hide from the math.

This is a design principle for any planning tool: make assumptions explicit and ensure the outputs degrade visibly when inputs are wrong. If your model can accommodate any set of assumptions without producing obviously broken outputs, it's not a model—it's a comfort blanket. The best revenue dashboards are the ones where reality forces uncomfortable conversations early, before the runway disappears.
