Kausas frames the persistence question with brutal simplicity: if you feel like you should stop, you probably should. The only founders who should continue are those who literally can't stop themselves—there's something nagging inside that won't let them quit.

This inverts the standard 'never give up' advice. Most founders should give up sooner. The ones who shouldn't are the ones for whom quitting isn't a real option—not because of sunk costs or ego, but because of an uncontrollable internal drive. Kausas considered giving up many times but something inside him wouldn't allow it. That involuntary persistence, not willpower, is the real signal.
