Ding's framework: 'A lot of people think if you come up with a really good idea, that's all you need. But you need to understand the competition—who has done this before, have they been successful, if not why not, and why would you be more successful.'

This is straightforward advice that most founders ignore. They get excited about an idea and start building before understanding the competitive landscape. Merge's approach—deep competitive research before writing code—ensured they understood exactly what they were building and why it would win. The result: fast product-market fit and $75M raised, because the strategy was sound before a single line of code was written.
