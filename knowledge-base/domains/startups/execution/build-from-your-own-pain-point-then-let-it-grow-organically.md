Dohmke and his co-founders built Hockey App because they needed it for their own freelance mobile development business. Before Hockey App, distributing beta builds required emailing files, dragging them into iTunes, and explaining complex installation steps. They built the tool for themselves, and it turned into a real company.

The pattern: when you solve your own problem, you have perfect founder-market fit. You understand every nuance of the pain because you feel it daily. The product evolves naturally because you're both the builder and the user. Hockey App grew organically because the problem was universal among mobile developers—Dohmke just happened to be the one frustrated enough to build the solution.
