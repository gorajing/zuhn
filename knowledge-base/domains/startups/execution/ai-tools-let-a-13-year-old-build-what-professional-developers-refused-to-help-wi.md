Goldstein had an idea for a general AI agent but every developer he contacted refused to help—probably because of his age. So he built it himself using AI coding tools, supplemented with weekly coding lessons. He went from idea to launched product to first paying customer (via Stripe) as a 13-year-old building after school.

This is the most dramatic evidence yet that AI coding tools have fundamentally changed who can build software products. The barrier to entry isn't programming skill anymore—it's the will to build something and the taste to identify what's worth building. When a middle schooler can ship a product that gets paying customers, the old gatekeeping mechanisms of CS degrees and professional experience are effectively obsolete.
