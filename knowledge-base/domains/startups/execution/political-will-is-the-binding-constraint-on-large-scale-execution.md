China's high-speed rail network equals the rest of the world's combined—not because China has better engineering (much of the technology was licensed from Japan and Europe) or more capital, but because it placed rail at the top of government priorities. Meanwhile, California's comparable 520-mile project will take roughly the same decade China used to build 18,000+ miles, largely due to fragmented political will, environmental reviews, land acquisition disputes, and competing priorities.

This generalizes beyond government: in any organization, the binding constraint on ambitious execution is rarely resources or knowledge—it's the willingness to make a single priority truly paramount and accept the tradeoffs. Startups that try to do five things adequately lose to competitors that do one thing with total commitment.
