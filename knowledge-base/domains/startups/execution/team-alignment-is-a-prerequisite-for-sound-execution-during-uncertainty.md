During the high-uncertainty phase of building a new business, teams often assume they're aligned when they're not. Each member may have different mental models of priorities, success criteria, and next steps. The Team Alignment Map makes commitments, resources, and dependencies explicit, preventing the silent misalignment that causes wasted effort.

This is especially critical during experimentation: when a team is running multiple tests and iterating rapidly, the cost of misalignment compounds. One person optimizing for feature completeness while another optimizes for speed-to-learning creates friction that slows the entire operation. Explicit alignment doesn't eliminate disagreement — it surfaces it early enough to resolve.
