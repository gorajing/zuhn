Unlike traditional products where features A, B, and C interact in predictable ways, AI products powered by large models have unbounded possibility spaces. Luma AI discovered that users generated content the team never imagined. There's no test suite that covers all possible uses of a generative model.

This fundamentally changes the product development loop: instead of build → test → ship, AI products must build → ship → learn. The emphasis shifts from pre-launch quality to post-launch learning velocity. Jain argues this is why Luma could build a $200M company—by shipping fast, learning from users, and iterating faster than competitors who tried to perfect products before launch.
