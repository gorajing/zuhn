Bent argues that early AI use was technical (specific prompt patterns and tricks), but effective AI use today is more like managing a capable colleague. You need to provide context, set expectations, check work, and know when to give latitude vs when to be prescriptive.

This reframing has massive implications for hiring and training. Companies looking for 'AI skills' should be looking for people who are good managers, communicators, and coaches—not people who know technical prompt engineering tricks. The step-by-step playbook approach that worked last year is already obsolete. The skill that endures is the ability to collaborate with an intelligent but imperfect partner.
