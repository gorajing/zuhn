The trick of calling ambitious work 'just a sketch' or 'just a hack' isn't mere self-deception — it structurally changes the stakes. When you're drawing in a notebook vs. carving stone, the cost of a bad start is genuinely lower, and you get initial results faster. Digital mediums amplify this by collapsing the gap between early work and final work, making iteration nearly free. John Carmack's observation about game mods illustrates this perfectly: Quake mods thrived because the crude baseline meant quick hacks weren't far from production quality, and Minecraft later recreated this by making crudeness the aesthetic.

The practical principle is that tool selection for ambitious projects should optimize for iteration speed and low commitment over capability. The best starting medium is the one where producing a terrible first version feels cheapest.
