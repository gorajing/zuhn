RuBisCo is objectively terrible at its job — it confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, creating toxic byproducts that plants must expend additional energy to neutralize. Yet plants never replaced it. Instead, they went all-in on the strategy, producing staggering quantities to compensate for per-unit inefficiency.

This maps directly to startup execution. Founders often stall searching for the elegant, efficient solution when a crude-but-functional approach deployed at scale would capture the market. The organism that first developed RuBisCo became the dominant life form on Earth not because its enzyme was good, but because it shipped first and scaled relentlessly. Technical debt compounds, but so does market position — and sometimes the debt is worth carrying forever.
