The Venus terraforming plan spans thousands of years but succeeds conceptually because each phase creates a habitable intermediate state. After freezing: settlers can walk the surface with pressure suits. After importing water: the planet has continents, islands, and frozen oceans. After cyanobacteria: you only need an oxygen mask and regular clothes. Each phase is independently valuable, not just a stepping stone.

This directly maps to product and company building. Multi-year technical visions fail when Phase 1 delivers zero value until Phase 5 completes. They succeed when each phase ships something usable. The terraforming plan never requires inhabitants to wait for completion — they can settle during any intermediate state. This is the difference between 'we need to build everything before anyone can use it' and 'each layer unlocks new capabilities even if the full vision isn't realized.'
