The inversion framework applied to startups means doing a thorough pre-mortem before a launch or major decision: list every way this could fail, then build specific guardrails for each failure mode. This is higher-leverage than ideation sessions about growth because failure modes are more predictable and enumerable than success paths. Most startups die from known causes (running out of cash, co-founder conflict, no market need) rather than from exotic risks. By turning Munger's 28 poverty behaviors into a startup context — '28 ways to guarantee your startup fails' — founders can create a practical avoidance checklist that prevents the most common deaths.
