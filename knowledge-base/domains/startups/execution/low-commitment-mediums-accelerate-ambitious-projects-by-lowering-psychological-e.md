Graham identifies a practical technique for navigating the lame early phase: deliberately choose media and framing that minimize upfront commitment. Calling a painting 'just a sketch' or software 'just a quick hack' creates psychological permission to produce imperfect early versions. Once the project gains momentum, you can quietly convert it into something more serious.

This has a deeper structural implication: the gap between early work and finished work in a given medium directly affects how many ambitious projects get started. John Carmack's observation about game mods illustrates this — Quake's crude graphics meant quick hacks weren't far from the official game, spawning careers. When commercial quality raised the bar, modding declined until Minecraft's deliberately crude aesthetic restored it. The practical takeaway is to bias toward tools and formats where version 0.1 doesn't look catastrophically different from version 1.0.
