Most credit programs start their expiration clock the moment you activate, not when you apply or get approved. This means founders who activate during the prototype phase burn through free months before their real workloads begin. The playbook is to get approvals in place early (many programs take weeks to process and require partner referrals) but trigger activation only at launch, first user onboarding, or pilot stage.

This timing discipline extends to every category: don't activate Notion Business Plan credits when three co-founders are still in Google Docs, don't trigger ElevenLabs' 12-month voice credits while still iterating on the product concept, and don't start Linear's 6-month free period before you have a team to onboard. A simple tracking dashboard with expiry dates prevents the common founder mistake of leaving $20K+ in unused credits because they forgot a deadline.
