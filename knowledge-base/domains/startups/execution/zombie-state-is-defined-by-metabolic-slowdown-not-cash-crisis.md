Dalton describes zombie startups as companies whose metabolism is slowing — the cell is aging. Symptoms: low energy, stalling, waiting for external events, longer dev cycles, and the creep of quarterly planning rituals. The telling sign: when the planning meeting becomes the most exciting thing happening at the company, not the product or user growth. Founders can miss this because the cash position and headcount look fine; the problem is qualitative, a shift in the kind of work being done. This matters because it means runway-based triggers will miss the condition entirely — a well-funded zombie can persist for years while destroying option value.
