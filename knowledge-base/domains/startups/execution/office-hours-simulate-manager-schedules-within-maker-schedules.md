Graham's practical solution to the maker-manager conflict is the 'office hours' pattern: designating specific chunks at the end of the working day for all meetings, ensuring they compress the day but never interrupt it. This transforms meetings from random interruptions into predictable boundaries.

The key design choice is placement at the end of the day rather than the beginning or middle. End-of-day meetings can only compress available time, never fragment it. A signup system that clusters appointments reinforces this by preventing gaps between meetings that would create unusable slivers of time. This pattern is directly applicable to any technical founder or engineering leader who must balance maker work with advisory or management responsibilities.
