Jain's hierarchy of product outcomes: worst is apathy (nobody cares), second worst is universal happiness (nothing left to build). The best state is dedicated users with a thousand things to complain about—that means engagement, expectations, and a roadmap of what to build next.

This is especially true for AI products: with large models, users can do anything, and there's no physical scenario where you can test all capabilities. You literally don't know what your users will do—some generate anime, others generate tomatoes rolling down hills. You must ship, observe what people actually do, see where it succeeds and fails, and iterate. Pre-launch testing is fundamentally insufficient for AI products.
