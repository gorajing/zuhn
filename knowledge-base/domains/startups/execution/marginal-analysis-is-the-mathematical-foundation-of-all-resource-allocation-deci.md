Calculus 1 explicitly teaches marginal cost and marginal revenue as derivatives of total cost and total revenue functions. The profit-maximizing output is where marginal revenue equals marginal cost — the point where producing one more unit would cost more than it earns. This isn't abstract theory; it's the mathematical formalization of every scaling decision a founder makes.

When deciding whether to hire the next engineer, run the next ad campaign, or add the next feature, you're implicitly computing derivatives. The total value of your company matters less than the marginal value of the next action. Founders who think in marginals avoid both under-investment (stopping before marginal returns are exhausted) and over-investment (continuing past the point where marginal costs exceed marginal returns). The calculus framing makes this intuition precise and actionable.
