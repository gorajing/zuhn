Graham identifies a concrete technique: deliberately framing ambitious work as something less exacting (a sketch, a hack, an experiment) to lower the psychological cost of starting. This works on two levels—you judge initial results by a lower standard, and you get results faster because low-commitment mediums allow rapid iteration. Once the project gains momentum, you can 'sneakily convert it' to something more serious. Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this: crude mediums like early Quake modding or Minecraft/Roblox shift the value axis from polish to innovation, allowing individuals to outperform professional teams. The shift from physical to digital media amplifies this effect by reducing upfront commitment to near zero.
