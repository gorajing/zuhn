Paul Graham argues that every self-made wealthy person occupies a position with both measurement and leverage. Measurement without leverage is piecework — you can work faster but only improve earnings 2-3x. Leverage without measurement means your contribution gets averaged with everyone else's, eliminating the link between effort and reward.

This framework explains why CEOs, movie stars, hedge fund managers, and startup founders get rich while equally talented people in large companies don't. The practical implication is stark: if your job feels safe, you almost certainly lack leverage. The presence of downside risk is actually a positive signal — upside must be balanced by downside, so the possibility of failure indicates the presence of leverage.
