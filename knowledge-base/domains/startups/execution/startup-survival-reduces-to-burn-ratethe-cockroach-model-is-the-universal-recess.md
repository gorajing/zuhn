Graham argues that making a startup recession-proof is identical to what founders should do anyway: minimize operating costs. The immediate cause of death in startups is always running out of money, so the cheaper you are to run, the harder you are to kill. This is the 'cockroach of the corporate world' strategy.

This collapses the perceived tension between 'normal times strategy' and 'recession strategy' into a single principle. A startup running lean doesn't need to pivot its approach when the economy turns—it's already optimized for survival. The insight also carries an implicit critique of growth-at-all-costs models: companies that require abundant capital to survive are structurally fragile regardless of economic conditions.
