Graham learned from Viaweb (after ~20 fallen-through deals) that the most useful skill was not getting hopes up about pending deals. The practice is to treat each deal as a background process you ignore until it terminates — don't track it, don't depend on it, don't let it occupy mental bandwidth.

This is a double-edged insight: depending on deal closure damages morale when deals fall through (which they usually do), AND it paradoxically makes deals less likely to close. Desperation is detectable and repulsive in negotiation. The emotional detachment isn't just self-protective — it's strategically optimal. This pattern generalizes beyond deals to any external dependency a founder can't control: press coverage, partnership discussions, even fundraising rounds.
