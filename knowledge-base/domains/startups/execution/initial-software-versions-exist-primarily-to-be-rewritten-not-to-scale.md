Graham argues that startups should deliberately ignore scalability, internationalization, and heavy-duty security in their first version. The reasoning is counterintuitive: the pain of retrofitting these later is real, but the bigger pain is building something too rigid to evolve into what users actually want.

This creates an asymmetry that favors startups over big companies. Large organizations face institutional pressure toward over-engineering from the start, producing rigid initial versions. Startups can be 'irresponsible' and ship light v1s that remain malleable. The rewrite isn't a failure of planning — it's the expected outcome of successful experimentation with product-market fit.
