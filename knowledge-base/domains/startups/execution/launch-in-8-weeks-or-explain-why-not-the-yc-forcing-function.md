Seibel frames launch speed as a non-negotiable forcing function. When he asks a company how long until launch, his follow-up is always 'why does it take longer than two months?' He doesn't care what the product is or where the team is in development — two months is the ceiling.

The reasoning is straightforward: every day spent iterating without user feedback is wasted iteration. Founders systematically overweight the risk of launching something imperfect and underweight the cost of building in isolation. Seibel has seen this pattern across hundreds of YC companies — the ones who launch fast and iterate with real feedback consistently outperform those who polish in private. The MVP isn't the product; it's the minimum artifact needed to begin the learning loop.
