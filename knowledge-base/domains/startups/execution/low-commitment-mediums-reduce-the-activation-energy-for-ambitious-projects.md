The psychological trick of framing ambitious work as a lower-stakes format — a sketch, a hack, an experiment — exploits the gap between your standards for different work types. You'll start a notebook sketch you'd never start as a formal painting. Once the project has momentum, you can silently upgrade its status.

This also explains why digital mediums accelerate innovation: they reduce upfront commitment compared to physical mediums. The gap between early work and final work shrinks, making it psychologically easier to begin. Game modding followed this exact pattern — Quake's crude aesthetics let single authors compete with professional teams, a dynamic later amplified by Minecraft and Roblox.
