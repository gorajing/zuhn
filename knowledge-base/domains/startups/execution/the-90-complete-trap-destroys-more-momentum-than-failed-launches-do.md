Founders are naturally drawn to beginnings—brainstorms, roadmaps, new initiatives. But momentum compounds at the point of completion, not initiation. The final 10% of any project is where feedback happens, where customers convert, and where the team sees real impact. When that last stretch is perpetually deferred, nothing lands and nothing compounds.

The insidious part is cultural. When a team tolerates half-finished work once, it becomes the norm. Five 90%-done features feel like progress but generate zero learning, zero customer feedback, and zero growth signal. The prescription is simple: before starting anything new, close an existing loop. Ship the feature, send the follow-up, push the experiment live. Finishing is the discipline that separates teams that feel busy from teams that actually move.
