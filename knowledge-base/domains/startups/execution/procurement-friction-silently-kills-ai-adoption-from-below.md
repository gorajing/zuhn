Every company in the study found that procurement bottlenecks were silently undermining their AI strategy. Employees who hit approval walls didn't stop using AI — they used personal ChatGPT accounts, which means company data flowing through unmonitored channels with no governance.

The tactical solutions vary but share a common principle of removing friction: Duolingo gave every employee $300 for AI experimentation. Zapier assigned a dedicated PM to fast-track approvals through procurement, legal, and engineering. Intercom's CTO told managers to give employees dedicated tinkering time after 'no time' emerged as the top excuse. Shopify provides access to multiple tools (Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, Cursor, etc.) and maintains a shared library of prompts and agents. The insight is that the real risk isn't approving tools too fast — it's that your employees are already using AI, just without your knowledge or governance.
