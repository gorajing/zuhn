Graham draws a sharp distinction between strategic direction and tactical execution: 'Make something people want' is the destination, but 'Be relentlessly resourceful' is how you get there. This framing suggests that most founder failure happens not at the vision level but at the execution level — in the daily confrontation with novel obstacles.

The practical implication is that founders should tape 'be relentlessly resourceful' to their mirror as a daily operating principle. It reframes every obstacle from a potential stopping point to a puzzle requiring creative solution. This also suggests that founder coaching and self-development should focus more on building adaptive problem-solving habits than on refining vision or strategy.
