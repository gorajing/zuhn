The article identifies a specific organizational failure pattern: hiring decisions get made in three separate silos. Founders approve headcount during investor calls, finance signs off on raises without knowing HR's pipeline, and HR queues offers without visibility into runway constraints. Each decision seems reasonable in isolation, but the compound effect is that 'runway dies in spreadsheets.'

This is a systems problem, not a people problem. The fix isn't better communication — it's shared infrastructure. When all stakeholders operate from the same model with the same assumptions, hiring decisions naturally incorporate financial constraints. The insight generalizes beyond headcount: any critical resource allocation that spans multiple functions needs a single source of truth, or each function will optimize locally while the company suffers globally.
