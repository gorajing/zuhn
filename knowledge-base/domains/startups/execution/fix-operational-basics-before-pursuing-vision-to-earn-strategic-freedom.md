When Hubert Joly joined Best Buy, he resisted the typical CEO playbook of announcing a grand vision. Instead, he fixed uncompetitive pricing, a broken website, slow shipping, and poor customer experience. This exploit-first approach earned stakeholder trust and strategic freedom.

The insight maps to the explore-exploit continuum: turnaround situations demand exploit-mode execution before explore-mode innovation. A board member taught Joly that quick operational wins create the political capital needed for bigger strategic bets later. The deliberate sequencing — not the choice itself — is what matters.
