Graham and John Carmack both observe that the gap between early work and finished work in a given medium determines how much experimentation happens. When Quake mods were crude enough that a quick hack wasn't far from the official game, innovation flourished. When commercial quality rose, mod creation became a full-time job and innovation collapsed — until Minecraft's deliberately crude aesthetic reopened the space.

The principle extends beyond games: calling something 'just a sketch' or 'just a hack' lets you bypass the quality filter that would otherwise prevent you from starting. Digital media's advantage isn't primarily its features but that it reduces upfront commitment. This suggests that when choosing tools and frameworks for new projects, optimizing for low switching costs and fast first results matters more than optimizing for the best possible final output.
