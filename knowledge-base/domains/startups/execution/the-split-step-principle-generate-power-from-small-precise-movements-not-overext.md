Stevens explains that the fundamental movement in his throws is a small, controlled hip split that generates power without overextension. 'A lot of people try to go here and they overcommit and now this foot can't take that step.' The key is that power comes from precision, not effort — a controlled split where one leg generates force while the other maintains balance.

This maps directly to startup execution: overcommitting resources to a single initiative leaves you unable to pivot when conditions change. The better approach is controlled, precise movements that generate maximum effect while maintaining the ability to take the next step. It is the principle of calculated aggression — being decisive without being reckless, committing enough force to be effective without losing the ability to redirect.
