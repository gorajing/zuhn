Ross Ulbricht had the concept for an anonymous marketplace well before Silk Road launched, but it remained 'just an idea in his mind' because no anonymous payment system existed. Credit cards left traceable logs linked to bank accounts. The convergence of Tor (developed by the US Navy for anonymous browsing, providing buyer/seller anonymity) with Bitcoin (launched 2009, providing payment anonymity) in 2010 created the precise conditions for Silk Road to exist. Neither technology alone was sufficient — anonymous browsing without anonymous payments, or vice versa, wouldn't have enabled the marketplace. This convergence pattern repeats across legitimate markets: the iPhone required touchscreen technology + mobile broadband + app distribution to converge; Uber required smartphones + GPS + mobile payments + always-on connectivity. The strategic implication is that founders should watch for convergence moments rather than single-technology breakthroughs, and that timing depends on when the last critical enabling technology matures, not the first.
