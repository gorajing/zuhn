One of the most effective tricks Graham identifies is re-labeling. Beginning a painting as 'just a sketch' or a new software tool as 'just a quick hack' lowers the psychological bar for starting. Once the project has momentum, you can quietly convert it to something more serious without ever having confronted the full weight of the ambition upfront.

This works best with media that have low upfront commitment costs — notebooks over stone, digital tools over physical ones. The low-commitment medium makes the low-stakes framing more believable, and faster initial results reinforce the loop. John Carmack's note about Minecraft and Roblox illustrates this at scale: when the entire aesthetic of a medium is 'crude,' the gap between early and finished work collapses, and participation explodes.

The deeper point is that perfectionism is often not a standard for quality but a gatekeeper for starting. Strategies that route around the gatekeeper without lowering final standards are genuine competitive advantages.
