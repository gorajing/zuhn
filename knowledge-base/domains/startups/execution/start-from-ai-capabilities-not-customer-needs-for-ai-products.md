This challenges the canonical product development advice. The argument: AI capabilities are advancing so fast that customers can't imagine what's possible. Starting with customer needs leads to incremental improvements on existing workflows. Starting with capabilities leads to entirely new categories of products.

The evidence: chat interfaces emerged because they were the obvious first UX for LLMs, but they're both universal and a dead end. The real innovation is coming from products that create structured, purpose-built experiences around AI capabilities—like Elicit for research or Runway for video. The best AI products don't ask 'what does the user want?' but rather 'what can we now do that was previously impossible?'
