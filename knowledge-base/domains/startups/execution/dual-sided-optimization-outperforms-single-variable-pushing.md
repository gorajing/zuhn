The supersonic baseball cannon achieves Mach 1.38 not by maximizing pressure alone, but by simultaneously pressurizing behind the ball (750+ PSI nitrogen) AND pulling a vacuum ahead of it in the barrel. This dual-sided approach is what makes supersonic velocity achievable with manageable pressures. A single-variable approach — just more pressure — would require dangerously high PSI and still fight atmospheric drag.

This maps directly to execution: most teams focus on 'pushing harder' (more marketing spend, more features, more hires) when removing friction on the output side (reducing onboarding steps, eliminating approval bottlenecks, simplifying deployment) often yields equal or greater gains at lower cost. The compounding effect of doing both simultaneously is non-linear — the cannon doesn't go twice as fast, it goes qualitatively past a threshold (the sound barrier) that neither approach alone could reach.
