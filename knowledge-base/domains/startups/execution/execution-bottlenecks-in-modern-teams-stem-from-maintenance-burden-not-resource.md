Modern teams spend the majority of their engineering cycles maintaining existing infrastructure, updating dependencies, fixing regressions, and managing technical debt. The actual percentage of effort that goes toward building new features is surprisingly small in most organizations.

This means the performance gap between teams is not about who has more resources but about who has minimized their maintenance burden enough to maintain high deployment velocity. Teams that ruthlessly reduce maintenance overhead — through better architecture, automation, and willingness to deprecate — ship faster than teams with 10x the budget but sprawling legacy systems. The strategic implication is to optimize for deployment speed as the primary metric rather than feature count or team size.
