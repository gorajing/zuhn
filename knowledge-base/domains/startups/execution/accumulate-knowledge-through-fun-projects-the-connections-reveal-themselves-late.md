Sweeney's path to building one of the most important game engines in history was not planned. He built a text editor that became a game. He learned vector calculus in engineering school without knowing he'd use it for 3D graphics. He built a compiler for fun. He learned about eigenvalues without seeing the connection to search algorithms. Each piece of seemingly unrelated knowledge became a 'karate kid' moment when the real challenge arrived.

The practical principle is that breadth of knowledge accumulated through genuine curiosity creates option value that structured career planning cannot. The key conditions are: (1) follow genuine interest, not strategic calculation; (2) actually build things, don't just study them; (3) trust that the connections will emerge. This is the antithesis of the credential-focused, resume-optimized approach -- and it produced one of the most successful companies in gaming history.
