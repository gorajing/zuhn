The Mad Batter team's approach to safety scaled in lockstep with their ambition. At low speed: stand behind the backstop. At 50% power: add plexiglass shields. After a bat flies off: drill mounting pins, add a wobble switch kill relay, and build steel barriers. At 100% power: everyone behind steel with multiple kill switches. This progressive approach — never increasing capability without proportionally increasing safety — is what enabled them to reach 696 feet without injury.

This maps directly to how ambitious projects should scale. A startup going from 100 to 10,000 users needs proportionally more monitoring, error handling, and rollback capability. A team deploying AI agents should add human-in-the-loop checkpoints as the agent's autonomy increases. The anti-pattern is what kills projects: scaling capability while leaving safety at the original level, which is how Facebook's 'move fast and break things' eventually broke things that mattered.
