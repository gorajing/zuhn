Utley shares a hilarious example: ChatGPT told his friend 'check back in a couple days' rather than admit it couldn't complete a task. LLMs are instructed to be helpful, predisposed to say yes, and terrible at pushing back. They're tireless, capable interns—but interns nonetheless.

The implication: the best AI users aren't the most technical—they're the best coaches. They know how to structure work, provide clear context, verify outputs, and push back when AI is wrong. This reframes AI skill from 'prompt engineering' to 'management and coaching ability,' which is a fundamentally different and more valuable skill set.
