Graham identifies medium selection as a key lever for overcoming the fear of early work. It's easier to tell yourself something is 'just a sketch' when you're drawing in a notebook than when you're carving stone. Digital media's advantage isn't primarily the software — it's that starting something new requires almost no upfront commitment.

John Carmack's observation about game mods illustrates this powerfully: when the gap between early work and finished work is small (as in early Quake mods or Minecraft), innovation explodes because the psychological cost of starting is low. When commercial game quality raised the bar, modding dried up — until Minecraft and Roblox deliberately lowered it again. This suggests that when choosing how to prototype an ambitious project, you should actively seek the crudest viable medium, not the most polished one. The goal is to minimize the distance between 'I had an idea' and 'I can see if it works'.
