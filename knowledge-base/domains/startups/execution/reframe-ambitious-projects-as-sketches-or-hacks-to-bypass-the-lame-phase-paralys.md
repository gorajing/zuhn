Graham identifies a specific trick used by successful creators: they start by explicitly categorizing their work as a lower-stakes format — a sketch, a quick hack, an experiment. This isn't just humility; it's a deliberate psychological reframe that makes the gap between current quality and final quality feel acceptable.

Patrick Collison extends this further — making the tenuousness itself a feature, almost a practical joke. YC embodied this spirit. The technique works because it changes your internal standard of comparison. You're no longer measuring a 20%-complete ambitious project against a finished one; you're measuring a sketch against other sketches. Once momentum builds, you can sneakily convert the hack into something serious. The key insight is that the medium matters: choose formats with low upfront commitment and fast iteration cycles so the distance between early work and final work feels crossable.
