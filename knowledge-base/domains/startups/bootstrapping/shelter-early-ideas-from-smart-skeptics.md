Smart, experienced people will tell you why your idea will not work.
Their reasoning is usually sound — but based on different products,
different markets, or different execution approaches. Conventional
wisdom said students would not pay $99/year and that short-form video
would not convert for SaaS. Both turned out to be wrong for Coconote.
The founders' framework: imagine your idea like a seed. Early on it is
fragile and needs shelter from harm so it can sprout. Expose it to
harsh criticism too early and it dies before it has a chance to prove
itself. A better question than "why won't this work?" is "what happens
IF it works?" That framing preserves the optionality of the idea
while still allowing you to plan for success.
