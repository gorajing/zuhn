Lenny started his newsletter as an experiment with no clear business plan. Six months in he still thought he wanted to start a company and viewed the newsletter as a distraction. But by committing to publishing at least one post every single week for the entire year (64 posts total), the signal became undeniable — subscriber growth, paid conversions, and community engagement all pointed to the newsletter as his real work.

This contradicts the common startup advice to validate ideas quickly with structured experiments. Sometimes the validation only emerges from sustained, consistent execution over many months. The weekly cadence created a compounding flywheel: each post attracted subscribers, who shared it, which attracted more subscribers. The clarity came not from analysis but from doing the work long enough for the market to respond unmistakably.
