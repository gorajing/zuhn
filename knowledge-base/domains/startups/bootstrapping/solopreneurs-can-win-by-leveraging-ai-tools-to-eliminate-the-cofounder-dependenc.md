The conventional wisdom that startups need cofounders is being challenged by a new generation of AI-augmented solopreneurs. Tools for code generation, design, customer support, and marketing automation allow a single founder to operate at the capacity of a small team. This shifts the calculus of early-stage company building.

However, solopreneurs still face real constraints: fundraising bias toward teams, burnout risk, and the absence of complementary perspectives in decision-making. The playbook for solo success involves ruthless prioritization, heavy automation of repeatable tasks, and building in public to create community-driven feedback loops that substitute for cofounder debate. The AI tooling advantage is time-sensitive as it levels the playing field for everyone simultaneously.
