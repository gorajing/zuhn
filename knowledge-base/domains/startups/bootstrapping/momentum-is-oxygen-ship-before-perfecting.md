Get traction before perfecting anything. Coconote shipped a basic product
that "sort of works" and charged for it from day one. The results: 45 days
to $100K ARR, 4 months to $1M, 5 months to $2M. Momentum compounds —
revenue brings more capital, capital brings more confidence, confidence
enables more investment in growth. The opposite is also true: stalling
to perfect the product before charging creates a death spiral of
uncertainty and dwindling resources. The founders describe momentum as
literal oxygen for a startup. Without it you suffocate. With it,
everything becomes easier — recruiting, fundraising, morale, and
decision-making. Ship the imperfect thing and iterate with real revenue.
