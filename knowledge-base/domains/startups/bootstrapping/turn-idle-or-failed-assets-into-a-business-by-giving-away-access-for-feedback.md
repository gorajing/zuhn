RunPod started in 2022 when its founders had GPU rigs in a basement left over from failed crypto mining. Rather than write off the sunk cost, they prototyped what became RunPod and posted on Reddit: 'anyone want to use these GPUs for free? Just give us feedback on it.' That feedback loop became the company, which has been revenue-generating ever since and now reports $120M in ARR.

Two transferable moves sit inside this story. First, a failed asset is not necessarily a dead one — idle capacity in a depressed market (here, GPUs after a crypto bust) can seed an entirely different business if you find demand for the same underlying resource. Second, giving the resource away for free in exchange for feedback solves the cold-start problem of an infrastructure product: you get real workloads, real bug reports, and an engaged community before you have a polished product. The presenter explicitly tied the company's current Reddit/Discord engagement back to this origin, framing community feedback as a durable operating principle rather than a one-time launch tactic.
