Hormozi's personal example: a single personal training client paying $45,000/month in cash freed him from needing any business revenue, letting him reinvest 100% of the gym's earnings into growth. Tesla followed the same pattern — $250K Roadster funded Model S development, which funded Model 3.

The key insight is that 'unscalable' is a feature, not a bug, in early stages. The 100% margin on 1:1 services provides the cash flow runway that eliminates the need for outside funding. Cap the time commitment (5-10% of your hours), charge 10x your normal rate, and direct every dollar of business revenue into scaling. The limiting belief that 'selling time is what poor people do' ignores that everyone has an hourly rate — the goal is maximizing it, not pretending it doesn't exist.
