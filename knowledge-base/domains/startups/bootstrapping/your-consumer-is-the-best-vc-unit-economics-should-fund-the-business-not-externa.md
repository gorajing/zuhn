Ahana's hardest lesson: capital cannot solve your problems, only your unit economics can. After burning through Series B money on the marketplace model, Open Secret went back to basics — in food, the most important metric is repeat purchases. On quick-commerce, 80% of their revenue now comes from organic or repeat sources. The consumer is literally funding the business through repurchases.

This is an important distinction from consumer tech (where network effects justify burning capital to build supply then demand). In consumer product businesses, there is no network effect — your product either earns repeat purchases or it doesn't, and no amount of capital changes that. External funding should only accelerate initiatives once the core unit economics work. The counterweight to 'raise more to grow faster' is: if your customer won't fund your business through repeat purchases, more capital just delays the inevitable failure.
