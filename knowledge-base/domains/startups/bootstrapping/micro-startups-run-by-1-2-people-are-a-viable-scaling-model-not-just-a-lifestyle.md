The convergence of three trends — AI coding assistants like GitHub Copilot, no-code platforms like Bubble and Webflow, and global freelance talent marketplaces — has created a new category of viable business: the micro-startup. These are companies run by one or two people with strong margins, clear niches, and no external pressure to raise or scale prematurely.

This isn't just bootstrapping rebranded. The key difference is that the ceiling has risen dramatically. What used to be a lifestyle business generating modest revenue can now be a high-margin SaaS or productized service competing with funded teams. The strategic implication is that founders should seriously evaluate whether they need to raise at all, since staying lean with these force-multiplier tools may produce better unit economics than hiring a team and taking dilution.
