Hormozi presents a mental model: $250/month at 9% compounding for 40 years equals approximately $1M. This creates a simple gamification layer — save $500/month and you're building toward $2M, save $1,000/month and it's $4M. The genius is in the simplicity: instead of complex financial planning, you have one number to optimize.

This reframe transforms saving from a deprivation exercise into a scoring system. When you skip Fiji water and save an extra $50/month, you're not 'sacrificing' — you're adding $200K to your end score. The mental model also makes lifestyle inflation visible: that $600/month car lease isn't just $600, it's roughly $2.4M in foregone wealth. By mapping every recurring expense to its compound equivalent, every spending decision becomes a clear trade-off against a concrete future number.
