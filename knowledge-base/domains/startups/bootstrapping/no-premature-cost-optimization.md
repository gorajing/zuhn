Coconote used startup credits for LLM API costs early on and did not
worry about unit economics until they had significant traction. The
founders' philosophy: do not let cost anxiety prevent growth. Premature
cost optimization is a form of premature optimization — you are solving
a problem you do not yet have while neglecting the problem you do have
(getting traction). Almost everything gets cheaper at scale: API costs
come down with volume agreements, infrastructure costs decrease per
unit, and negotiating leverage increases with revenue. The time to
optimize costs is after you have proven demand and have real usage
data to optimize against. Before that, every dollar and hour spent
on cost optimization is a dollar and hour not spent on growth.
