Answer This did over $2M ARR with two full-time employees (the founders) plus a few contractors, and Ayush attributes a large part of that leverage to an internal AI ops agent that handles work which would normally consume founder time or require hiring: processing 100+ emails a day, closing 400+ support tickets, updating the CRM after meetings, and collecting user feedback across channels. Beyond doing tasks, it makes the business instantly queryable ('what's the status of this lead', 'what are the open issues for this customer'), replacing the need to operate many disconnected apps to get answers. The strategic implication for bootstrappers is that operational headcount — historically the first hires as a company grows — is increasingly substitutable with a single self-extending agent, which changes the math on how lean a revenue-scale company can stay. This is presented as a single founder's experience rather than a proven general law, so the honest framing is that it raises the achievable leverage ceiling, not that every startup will replicate $2M-per-two-people; the transferable move is to ask which recurring ops work could be queue-fed to an agent before it justifies a hire.
