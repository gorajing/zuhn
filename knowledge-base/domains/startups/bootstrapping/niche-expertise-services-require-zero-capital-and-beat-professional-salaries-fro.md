The business has near-zero fixed costs: no office (use libraries), no equipment, no inventory, no employees at the start. The only asset required is a verifiable test score and a structured method. This makes the downside effectively zero — if it doesn't work, you've lost a few months of evenings.

The opportunity cost comparison is stark: four years of college costs tuition plus four years of forgone income, and the median post-college salary is well below what this model generates. Even at half capacity (25 students instead of 50), you'd earn $550/day — still a strong income with half the day free. Hormozi frames this as a vehicle problem, not a talent problem: the same effort applied to the wrong model (1-on-1 at $60/hr) produces a fraction of the result. Choosing the right business model is itself the highest-leverage decision.
