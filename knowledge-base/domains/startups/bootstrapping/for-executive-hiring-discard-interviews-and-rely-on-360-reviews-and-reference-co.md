stance: Senior executives are professional interviewers

The more senior the hire, the better they are at interviewing — by definition. At the C-suite level, the interview itself becomes nearly useless as a signal. Two alternative approaches generate much better signal:

1. Ask candidates to share their 360 reviews from their last company (offer to share yours in return). Ten honest reviews from colleagues reveal strengths and weaknesses that no interview can surface.
2. Do reference calls until answers start to sound the same — that convergence point is where you have real signal. Frame references as 'I'm probably going to hire this person, now tell me what they're bad at' to create safety for honest feedback.

Nearly half of executive hires don't work out within 12-24 months, largely because the interview process bears no resemblance to the actual work. Project-based evaluations or trial periods (like Weebly's two-week exec trials) are more predictive.
