Coconote was acquired by Quizlet, and the founders learned a hard lesson
about information management during the process. Acquisition deals can
fall apart in the final week — premature sharing of deal progress with
team members or family creates enormous anxiety and noise that serves
no one. When a deal collapses after you have told everyone, the
emotional damage compounds: the team loses confidence, family members
worry, and the founder must manage everyone's emotions on top of their
own disappointment. A great leader shelters others from unnecessary
anxiety. Keep acquisition talks compartmentalized to the smallest
possible circle until the wire actually hits the bank account. Only
then is the signal clear enough to share broadly.
