Hu citing Jack Dorsey's framework from Block: future companies will have three employee archetypes. (1) Individual Contributor (IC) / builder operator — directly makes and runs things; not limited to engineers, applies to ops, support, sales, everything; everyone shows up to meetings with working prototypes, not pitch decks. (2) DRI — directly responsible individual focused on strategy and customer outcomes; not classic management, but one person with one outcome and no hiding. (3) AI founder — still builds, coaches, leads by example; the founder must show what massive capability gains look like, not delegate AI strategy.

Why middle management disappears: in pre-AI orgs, you needed middle managers to route information up and down — the intelligence layer didn't exist. In AI-native orgs, the intelligence layer (the closed loops, queryable artifacts, agent observability) does the routing. Every layer of human routing is direct speed loss.

For builders: when designing an AI-native org, design for these three archetypes. Hire ICs who can work end-to-end on prototypes. Hire DRIs who own outcomes alone. Founders must lead by example. For Zuhn editorially: this is the most concrete operational picture of post-AI org design from a credible practitioner, and the empirical test is straightforward — do AI-native winners actually have this shape? Companies like AppLovin (no PM org, no CRO/CMO, no middle layers) and Replit (only builders + sales) provide early confirmation.
