NFX observes that financial infrastructure in many non-US markets — Latin America, Asia, Europe — is significantly less developed, creating more friction in customer experiences and business processes. This friction gap represents larger economic opportunity: when the baseline experience is worse, the value captured by digitizing and embedding financial services is proportionally greater.

Examples include Nuvocargo (digitized logistics with embedded insurance and credit in Latin America), LaHaus (online real estate with embedded finance), and Kavak (used car marketplace). In some cases, lighter regulatory burdens further reduce barriers to combining marketplaces with financial services. For founders evaluating market entry, this suggests that fintech-marketplace models optimized for developed markets may actually be more valuable when deployed in emerging economies where the delta between status quo and digital-first is largest.
