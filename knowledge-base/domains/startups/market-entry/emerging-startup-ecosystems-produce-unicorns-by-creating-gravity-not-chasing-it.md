In 2013, only four ecosystems globally had produced unicorns. By 2020, that number exploded to over 80 distinct regions. This isn't passive diffusion — companies like Develocraft in Gdańsk actively catalyzed their local ecosystem by launching tech conferences, coding academies, and talent attraction programs. They created gravity rather than chasing it elsewhere.

The strategic implication for founders in non-hub cities is that ecosystem creation is itself a competitive moat. Lower burn rates, less talent competition, and eager local government support create structural cost advantages. The founder who builds the ecosystem becomes the central node in a local network — a position that compounds over time through deal flow, talent pipelines, and community loyalty that hub-based competitors cannot replicate.
