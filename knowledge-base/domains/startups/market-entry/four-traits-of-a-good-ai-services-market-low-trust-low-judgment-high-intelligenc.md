YC's screen for AI-services markets has four non-obvious traits. Low trust means the work is already outsourced and the customer cares only about the final product, not how it was produced — you're displacing a vendor, not asking anyone to change behavior, so you show up where the budget already lives. Low judgment at the task level means the work can be decomposed into steps that are mostly automatable, with human judgment concentrated in a few hand-off points; if every piece needs a human exercising real judgment, it can't scale. A high intelligence threshold means the overall work is genuinely hard — hard enough that models-plus-humans are required to deliver an outcome the customer will accept — which keeps it defensible. Counterintuitively, regulation is good: regulated industries (tax, audit, insurance, mortgages, FDA submissions) carry higher expectations and legal accountability that raise the bar and the moat. The known-good markets include tax, audit, insurance, mortgages, and parts of healthcare and logistics, but founders shouldn't limit themselves to the obvious ones discussed publicly.
