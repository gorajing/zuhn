An ACX grantee (still in stealth) is building a CPG company developing snake food to replace conventional feeder rodents at scale. The framing reveals a classic neglected-market pattern: feeder mice and rats are among the most numerous farmed mammals in the US, yet almost nobody is working on alternatives. The opportunity exists precisely because it's unglamorous and niche.

This follows the effective altruist framework of impact = scale × neglectedness × tractability. The scale is surprisingly large (millions of rodents), neglectedness is extreme (near-zero competitors), and tractability is plausible (nutritional science for reptile food is well-understood). For founders, this is a template for finding high-impact opportunities: look where large-scale problems persist specifically because the space lacks prestige or visibility.
