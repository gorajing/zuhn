Toyota's first US export, the Toyopet Crown, sold only 300 units because the car overheated, lost power, and consumed excessive fuel on American highways — conditions that never existed on shorter Japanese roads. The company had projected success based on domestic performance without physically testing in the target environment. This failure led Toyota to adopt 'Genchi Genbutsu' (go to the source) as a core export philosophy — sending teams to live in target markets and gather firsthand data before product launches. The principle generalizes: any company expanding internationally must validate assumptions against real local conditions, not extrapolate from home-market success.
