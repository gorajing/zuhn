The Delve example illustrates a powerful go-to-market pattern: a startup hit growth mode but stalled when prospects demanded SOC 2 compliance. Traditional platforms meant endless tasks, tied-up engineers, and lost deals while competitors closed. Delve compressed audit-readiness from months to 15 hours, directly converting a blocked deal into a closed one.

This is a template for identifying high-value wedge opportunities: find the compliance or certification bottleneck that blocks revenue for fast-growing companies. The ROI is immediately quantifiable (deals closed vs. deals lost), the pain is acute (competitors are literally stealing your customers while you wait), and the switching cost is low (you'll try anything when deals are dying). Any regulatory or certification requirement that takes months and requires engineering resources is a potential automation wedge.
