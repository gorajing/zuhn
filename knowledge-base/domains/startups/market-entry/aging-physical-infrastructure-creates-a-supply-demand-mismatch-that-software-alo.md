The US housing stock is aging (median age rose from 31 to 37 years in a decade) while consumer preferences shift dramatically — median years renting before buying doubled from 2.6 to 6 years. This dual mismatch can't be solved by information marketplaces alone; it requires companies that physically repurpose existing supply.

This is a generalizable pattern for market entry: when you see aging physical infrastructure colliding with shifting demand, the winning startups will be operationally complex (managing physical assets, logistics, renovation) rather than pure software aggregators. The trap is building what looks like a tech company but is actually a lease arbitrage business without defensible moats. The winners build network effects or vertical integration that compounds over time, making operational complexity itself the moat.
