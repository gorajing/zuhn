Anker's engineers learned about PowerIQ fast-charging technology before western competitors specifically because of informal knowledge networks within Shenzhen's engineering community. This illustrates a frequently underestimated advantage of industry clusters: information about emerging capabilities, component availability, and technical breakthroughs travels through informal social networks faster than through formal business channels. Western companies relying on trade shows, supplier announcements, and published specifications receive the same information weeks or months later. This creates a systematic first-mover advantage for cluster-proximate firms that compounds over time—each product cycle where the local firm ships first builds brand recognition and market share that the distant competitor must overcome. The pattern applies broadly: Silicon Valley's advantage in software isn't just talent density but information velocity about what's technically possible.
