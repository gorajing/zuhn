Graham argues that startups require only two types of people present at founding: nerds who build and rich people who fund. Every other participant—lawyers, recruiters, designers—arrives after the reaction starts. This 'limiting reagent' framing explains why cities like Miami (rich, few nerds) and Pittsburgh (nerds, few rich) both fail to generate startups despite having one ingredient.

The practical implication is that ecosystem-building efforts should focus exclusively on attracting and retaining these two groups rather than building physical infrastructure, offering tax incentives, or creating government programs. The essay uses chemistry metaphor deliberately: you can have excess of every other reagent, but without both limiting ones, the reaction simply doesn't occur.
