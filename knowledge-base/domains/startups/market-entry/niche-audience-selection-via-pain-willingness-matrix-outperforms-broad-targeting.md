Most early-stage founders spread too thin by targeting large addressable markets. The 2x2 matrix forces a disciplined narrowing: segment your ICP into sub-niches, then score each on how severe the pain is and how readily they'll pay for a solution. The quadrant where both are high is your beachhead.

This mirrors Airbnb's early strategy of targeting budget-conscious conference attendees at SXSW rather than all travelers. Landing 100 deeply satisfied customers in a tight niche creates testimonials, referrals, and learnings that generalize — whereas 100 lukewarm users across segments teach you nothing actionable. The matrix also surfaces which niches to explicitly avoid (low pain, low willingness to pay) before you waste acquisition spend.
