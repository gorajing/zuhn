Latin America has high mobile adoption but schools remain incredibly offline, creating friction with families, higher costs, and less teaching time. This gap between consumer mobile adoption and institutional digitization is the exact window where vertical software companies can achieve rapid adoption.

This pattern repeats across emerging markets and industries: the infrastructure layer (mobile phones, internet connectivity) arrives before the application layer (purpose-built software). Founders who identify industries where end-users are mobile-native but workflows are still paper-based can ride the adoption curve rather than fight it. fidu's rapid expansion to 1,000+ schools across 10 countries in a short time validates that the market was ready for digitization — the bottleneck was purpose-built software, not willingness to adopt.
