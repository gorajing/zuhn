YC W26 shows concentrated bets in healthcare (~10% of batch) and legal tech (~4%), both domains defined by heavy regulation. Healthcare companies target AI prior authorizations, autonomous primary care, dental operations, and drug discovery. Legal tech follows the rapid unicorn trajectories of Harvey and Legora, with founders explicitly pattern-matching to those outcomes.

The strategic insight is that regulatory complexity, traditionally seen as a barrier, has become a deliberate moat strategy in the AI era. When AI commoditizes the technical layer, the companies that win are those that can navigate regulatory approval, build clinical validation, earn institutional trust, and achieve compliance — all things that take time and domain expertise regardless of how good the underlying AI is. This creates a natural defense against both incumbents (who move slowly) and new entrants (who lack domain knowledge). The pattern suggests founders should actively seek regulated domains rather than avoid them.
