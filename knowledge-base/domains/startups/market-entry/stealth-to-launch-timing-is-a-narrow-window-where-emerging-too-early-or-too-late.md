The article identifies three launch readiness signals: market-ready product validated by trusted users, IP protections in place, and a prepared go-to-market strategy. What's notable is that stealth startups lack the continuous market calibration that public startups get, making their timing decision higher-stakes.

Clubhouse's invite-only beta illustrates a hybrid approach — maintaining partial stealth while generating demand signals. This 'controlled visibility' model may be the optimal middle ground, giving founders enough market signal to time their launch while maintaining enough secrecy to build anticipation and protect core innovations.
