A featured startup targets the European SME succession crisis — thousands of profitable companies at risk of closure because aging founders have no succession plan. Their solution enables employee buyouts with financing, software, and advisory services, and they've already built a €70M pipeline with two LOIs signed.

This is noteworthy because it represents a structural demographic opportunity rather than a technology trend. The market is large (€20B serviceable), predictable (founder ages are known), and underserved (traditional M&A advisory ignores small deals). The employee-buyout model is particularly elegant because it preserves institutional knowledge and company culture while solving the liquidity problem for retiring founders. It's a category that's invisible to Silicon Valley but represents real, revenue-generating businesses at risk of disappearing.
