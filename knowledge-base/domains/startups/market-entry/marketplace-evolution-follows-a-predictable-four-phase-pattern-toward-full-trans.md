Across hospitality, food delivery, real estate, and automotive, marketplaces have followed an identical evolutionary path: (1) horizontal lead generation (Craigslist), (2) vertical lead generation (HomeAway, Trulia), (3) vertical transactional (Airbnb, Redfin), and (4) tightly managed with fintech (Opendoor, Fair.com).

This pattern is powerful for founders because it's predictive. If you can identify which phase an industry's dominant marketplace is in, you can anticipate the next disruption vector. Industries still in phase 1 or 2 represent the largest greenfield opportunities. Each phase transition captures more of the transaction value, increases take rates, and improves user experience—which is why later-phase companies tend to be worth more than earlier-phase ones in the same category.
