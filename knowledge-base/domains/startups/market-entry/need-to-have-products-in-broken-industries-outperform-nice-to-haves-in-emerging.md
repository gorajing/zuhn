Latin America has multiple broken industries — education, insurance, logistics, finance — that are simultaneously large and high-impact. The strategic advantage of targeting these sectors is that they represent need-to-haves rather than nice-to-haves. When people desperately need a working solution for something essential, they'll push through trust barriers and adoption friction faster than they would for a convenience product.

This creates a different founder calculus than in mature markets. In the US, founders might succeed with a slightly better version of an existing product. In LatAm and similar emerging markets, the opportunity is to provide the first working version of something essential. The proven-market aspect is crucial — you're not creating demand, you're serving demand that existing institutions have failed to meet. This also means the societal impact is genuine, not a marketing afterthought, which can itself become a trust signal.
