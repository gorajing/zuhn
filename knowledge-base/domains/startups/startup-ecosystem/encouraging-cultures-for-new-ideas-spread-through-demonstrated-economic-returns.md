Silicon Valley's encouragement of strange ideas by unknown people wasn't built on moral superiority. It was built on the observation that dismissing ideas is dangerous when outcomes follow a power-law distribution — missing a single needle in the haystack is painfully obvious in retrospect. Optimism became urgent because the cost of false negatives was catastrophically high.

Once this optimism proved economically superior, it spread as custom. Now it just 'seems like what one does with startups,' a social norm that was once a calculated strategy. This is how cultural evolution works: practices that generate economic surplus get codified as customs and eventually as values.

The implication for builders: you can deliberately construct micro-environments with these incentives rather than waiting for the culture at large to evolve. Research labs, university departments, and accelerators work because they create the same incentive structure — everyone benefits from others' success, making encouragement rational rather than sycophantic.
