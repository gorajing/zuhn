Y Combinator's practice of funding overlapping startups reflects a portfolio theory that large markets support multiple winners and that early competition validates demand rather than splitting it. In AI and SaaS this is especially pronounced given the low marginal cost of software and the speed at which AI capabilities are commoditizing. For founders, this has two implications: YC backing is not exclusivity protection, and being in a crowded YC cohort niche may be a signal of market validity rather than a red flag. For investors, it means YC graduation from a crowded vertical requires additional diligence on differentiation.
