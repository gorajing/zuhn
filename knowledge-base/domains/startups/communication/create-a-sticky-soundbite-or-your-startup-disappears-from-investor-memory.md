Paul Graham frames memorability as a survival problem: professional investors hear enough pitches that they all blur together, so the first cut is simply being one they remember. The mechanism is a descriptive phrase — usually 'the X of Y' or 'the X Y' — launched casually near the beginning of your talk.

But the exercise of crafting this phrase has a diagnostic function beyond marketing. If you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, it may indicate insufficient focus in your actual plans. This mirrors the narrow-description principle: the ability to be concise is evidence of clarity of thought, not a limitation on ambition. Viaweb calling itself 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' didn't limit what it could become — it gave investors a working mental model to build on.
