Graham frames the soundbite not as marketing fluff but as a compression algorithm for investor memory. Professional investors hear hundreds of pitches that blur together. The first cut isn't quality—it's memorability. A phrase like 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' creates a compact, retrievable mental model that survives the blur.

This has a useful diagnostic property: if you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, your plans may lack sufficient focus. The soundbite exercise forces strategic clarity. This connects to information theory—the ability to compress without losing essential meaning is a test of whether the underlying signal is coherent or just noise.
