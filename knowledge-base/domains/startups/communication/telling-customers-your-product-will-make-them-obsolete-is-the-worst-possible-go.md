Smith's central thesis is that AI has possibly the worst sales pitch in history: 'Our product might destroy humanity AND it will definitely make you unemployable.' He compares it to a door-to-door salesman pitching a microwave that cooks your food perfectly but also paralyzes your family, then suggesting you'll be fine on welfare. The extinction risk part is actually defensible as 'our version is safer,' but the economic obsolescence messaging is pure own goal.

This has immediate implications for any startup communicating about transformative technology. The lesson extends beyond AI: never lead your messaging with how your product threatens your customer's livelihood or identity. Even if economic displacement is a real consequence, foregrounding it poisons adoption. The companies that win will be those that frame AI as augmenting human capability, not replacing it — regardless of what they privately believe about the long-term trajectory.
