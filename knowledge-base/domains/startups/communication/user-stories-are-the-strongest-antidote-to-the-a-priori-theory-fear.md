The biggest fear investors have with early-stage startups is that the founders built something based on their own theories about what the world needs, with no actual demand. Sequoia calls this the 'proxy for demand' — what are people doing right now, with inadequate tools, that proves they need what you're making?

The hierarchy of story strength is clear: best is your own need (Apple, Microsoft, Google all grew from founder needs), next is the needs of people you know personally, and after that comes observed market behavior like people paying high prices for inferior alternatives. Each level closer to direct experience is more credible. This pattern extends beyond fundraising — in any persuasion context, concrete user narratives outperform abstract market analysis because they're harder to dismiss and easier to remember.
