Graham cites Sequoia partner Greg McAdoo's concept of 'proxy for demand' — observable behaviors where people use clumsy workarounds that signal latent need for your product. This is more convincing than market size claims for a simple reason: market size numbers are abstract and investors hear them constantly, while specific user pain stories are concrete and memorable. The strongest variant is when the founders themselves are the frustrated users (Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all grew from founder needs). The hierarchy is: your own need > needs of people you know personally > needs of abstract user personas. Each level down loses credibility because it's further from observable evidence and closer to a priori theory about what the world needs — which is investors' biggest fear about early-stage startups.
