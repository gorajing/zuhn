Graham recommends launching a descriptive phrase early in your pitch that sticks in investors' heads—patterns like 'the X of Y' (Viaweb was 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce'). Professional investors hear so many pitches that they blur together; the first cut is simply being remembered.

But the deeper insight is that this exercise serves the founder as much as the investor. Sitting down to compress your startup into one compelling phrase forces you to confront whether your plans are sufficiently focused. If you can't do it, that's diagnostic information about strategic clarity, not just a presentation problem. The soundbite constraint functions as a forcing function for strategic thinking—similar to how writing forces clarity of thought that mere thinking doesn't.
