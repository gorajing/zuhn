Despite architecting most of Google's defining product bets, Page is routinely underattributed in popular narratives because he avoided public communication. The media credited Schmidt as the stabilizing force, not because Page's contributions were invisible, but because Page never narrated them. In the absence of a founder's own story, observers construct a simpler one.

This has become even more pronounced in the current era where founders like Musk, Altman, and Chesky treat public narrative as a core strategic function. The lesson is not that founders must become celebrities, but that distribution applies to legacy just as it applies to product. If you don't actively shape how your contributions are understood, the default narrative will flatten your role. For technically-minded founders who resist self-promotion, this represents a genuine strategic vulnerability.
