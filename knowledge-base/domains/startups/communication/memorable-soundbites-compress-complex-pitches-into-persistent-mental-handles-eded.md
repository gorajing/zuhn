Professional investors hear so many pitches that they blur together. The first filter isn't whether they understood you — it's whether they remember you at all. A compact descriptive phrase like 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' (Viaweb's handle) creates a persistent mental anchor that survives the blur.

Graham frames this as a focusing exercise too: if you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite isn't decoration on top of a clear strategy — it's a diagnostic for whether you have one. This principle applies broadly to any situation where you're competing for attention in a crowded field: job applications, product launches, even internal project proposals. The constraint of compression forces clarity of thought.
