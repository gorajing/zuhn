Greg McAdoo of Sequoia calls this 'proxy for demand'—observable behavior that proves people need what you're building. This is fundamentally different from top-down market sizing. A $5B market number is unfalsifiable abstraction; a story about a specific user jury-rigging spreadsheets to solve the problem you address is concrete evidence of pull.

The hierarchy of proof Graham outlines is: best is your own need (Apple, Microsoft, Google all started this way), next is needs of people you know personally, then broader user stories. Each level trades directness for scale but maintains the critical property of being grounded in observed behavior rather than projected demand. Experienced investors weight this heavily because the #1 startup failure mode is building something nobody wants.
