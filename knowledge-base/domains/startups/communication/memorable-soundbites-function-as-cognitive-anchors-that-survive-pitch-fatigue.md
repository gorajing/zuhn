Professional investors hear enough pitches that they all blur together. The first cut for getting funded is simply being remembered, and the mechanism is a sticky descriptive phrase launched early in the presentation—apparently casually but deliberately crafted.

The format varies by industry ('x meets y' in Hollywood, 'the x of y' in startups), but the function is the same: a cognitive anchor that lets investors recall and discuss you later. Graham notes this is also a useful diagnostic—if you can't describe your startup in one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite test doubles as a strategy clarity test.
