PG recommends constructing a 'soundbite' — a phrase of the form 'the X of Y' (Viaweb was 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce') — and planting it early in the pitch. Investors hear many pitches and what survives is often a single memorable hook. But the deeper value of this exercise is diagnostic: the difficulty of finding a sharp phrase often reveals strategic ambiguity in the underlying plan. If the startup is trying to be too many things, no honest compression is possible. The effort to find the phrase forces founders to confront the question of what they actually are at their core, which is a prerequisite for good strategy, not just good communication.
