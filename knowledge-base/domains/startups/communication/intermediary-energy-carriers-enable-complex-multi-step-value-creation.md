Photosynthesis doesn't convert sunlight directly into glucose — it first converts photon energy into portable energy currencies (ATP and NADPH) through the light-dependent reactions, then uses those currencies in a completely separate process (Calvin Cycle) to build carbohydrates. This decoupling is what makes the system adaptable.

The same principle applies to business architecture: companies that build intermediate value stores — whether data platforms, API layers, or modular capability stacks — gain flexibility to serve multiple use cases from a single energy-capture mechanism. Direct coupling (sunlight → glucose, or customer → product) is simpler but brittle. The intermediary layer is where optionality lives.
