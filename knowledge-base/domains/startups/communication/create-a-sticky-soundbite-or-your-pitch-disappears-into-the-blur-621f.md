After hearing dozens of pitches, investors experience a blur effect where presentations merge together. The first cut isn't quality—it's memorability. A compressed analogy phrase ('the X of Y') serves as a cognitive handle that lets investors recall and discuss your startup later.

This exercise also functions as a forcing function for strategic clarity. If you can't describe your startup in one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The phrase should be launched 'clearly but apparently casually' near the beginning of the talk—early enough to frame everything that follows, casual enough to not seem rehearsed. The Hollywood 'X meets Y' and startup 'the X of Y' patterns persist because they leverage existing mental models to compress novel concepts.
