Graham argues that the first cut in fundraising is simply being remembered. After dozens of pitches, investors retain only a few, and a sticky descriptive phrase acts as a cognitive handle that survives the forgetting curve. Viaweb's 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' compressed a complex product into an instantly graspable analogy.

But Graham adds a deeper insight: if you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite exercise is therefore diagnostic — it reveals whether you have a clear enough identity to pitch effectively. This connects to the narrow-vs-vague principle: a good soundbite is necessarily narrow and concrete, which is exactly what makes it stick. The forcing function of compression serves both communication and strategic clarity.
