When rising oxygen levels caused RuBisCo to produce toxic phosphoglycolate, plants didn't evolve a better carbon-fixing enzyme. Instead, they evolved photorespiration pathways to convert the toxic byproduct into useful amino acids (glycine) and Calvin cycle intermediates. They also evolved C4 and CAM photosynthesis as spatial and temporal workarounds.

This pattern appears everywhere in technology and organizations: successful systems accumulate compensatory layers rather than undergoing ground-up redesign. The workaround approach succeeds because the flawed component is load-bearing — too many other systems depend on its specific interface and behavior. For founders, this reframes technical debt: the question isn't whether to accumulate it, but whether your workaround layers are sustainable and well-understood.
