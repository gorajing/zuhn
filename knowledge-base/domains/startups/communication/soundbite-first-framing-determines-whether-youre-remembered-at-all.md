After hearing dozens of pitches, investors' memories compress each startup into a single impression. If you don't control what that impression is, you're leaving it to chance. Graham's advice is to craft a phrase like 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' (Viaweb's) and deploy it early in the talk, apparently casually.

The exercise of creating this phrase also serves as a strategic forcing function. If you can't describe your startup in one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. This connects to the narrow-vs-vague principle: the soundbite is the ultimate compression of your value proposition. It's not just a presentation tactic — it's a test of strategic clarity. Companies that can't be described simply often haven't achieved the focus needed to win.
