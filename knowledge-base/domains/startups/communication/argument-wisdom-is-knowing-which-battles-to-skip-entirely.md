Seo distinguishes between intelligence (ability to respond to any argument) and wisdom (knowing which arguments to respond to). Elite debaters apply two filters to every potential point of contention: (1) Is this disagreement necessary to resolve in order to make progress on the larger issue? (2) Even if not necessary, would challenging it help advance the overall dispute?

This is a resource allocation problem. In any complex disagreement — board meetings, strategy sessions, team retrospectives — there are dozens of contestable claims. Responding to every wrong or offensive statement scatters your attention and turns the conversation into an unruly mass. The wise approach is to let non-essential points go, even when they're wrong, and concentrate energy on the arguments that actually move the needle.

For founders and leaders, this is particularly critical. Every meeting contains provocative tangents that feel urgent to address but don't advance the core decision. The discipline of asking 'does challenging this help us make progress?' before engaging is a force multiplier for productive conversations.
