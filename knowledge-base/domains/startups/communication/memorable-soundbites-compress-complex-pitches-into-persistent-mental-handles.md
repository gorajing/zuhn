Professional investors hear enormous volumes of pitches that blur together. Graham argues the first survival filter is simply being remembered, and the mechanism is a single descriptive phrase that sticks — typically in 'the X of Y' format. Viaweb's was 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce,' which instantly communicates product category, ambition level, and user experience philosophy in five words.

But Graham adds a deeper insight: the exercise of crafting this phrase is itself strategically valuable. If you genuinely cannot describe your startup in one compelling phrase, that's diagnostic — your plans may lack sufficient focus. This makes the soundbite exercise a cheap strategy test. The best positioning phrases work because they leverage existing mental models (everyone knows Microsoft Word) to explain something new, reducing the cognitive load on the listener while simultaneously anchoring your startup in a category they already value.
