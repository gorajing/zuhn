Graham observes that professional investors hear enormous volumes of pitches, and after a while they all blur together. The first cut in evaluation isn't quality—it's simply being remembered. A descriptive 'soundbite' that encapsulates your startup serves as a cognitive anchor.

The Hollywood formula is 'x meets y'; the startup version is 'the x of y' (Viaweb was 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce'). Graham recommends launching this phrase early in the talk, clearly but with apparent casualness. Beyond its tactical value in pitches, the exercise of distilling your startup into one compelling phrase functions as a strategic forcing function. If you can't do it, your plans may not be sufficiently focused—the inability to compress signals unclear thinking, not just poor communication.
