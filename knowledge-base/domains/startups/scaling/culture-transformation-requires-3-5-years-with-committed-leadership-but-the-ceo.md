When asked about transformation timelines for mid-sized companies, Osterwalder refuses to give a universal answer but identifies leadership commitment as the single variable that matters most. With full CEO and board commitment, significant cultural transformation is achievable in 3-5 years. Ping An, a Chinese banking and insurance conglomerate, transformed from a top-500 to a top-10-15 global company within a decade through sustained commitment to innovation culture.

The implication is that culture transformation timelines are not primarily a function of company size, industry, or existing culture — they're a function of leadership commitment intensity. This reframes the question from 'how long does culture change take?' to 'how committed is leadership?' It also means that consultants or innovation leaders who promise culture transformation without first securing genuine CEO commitment are setting themselves up for failure. The binding constraint is always at the top.
