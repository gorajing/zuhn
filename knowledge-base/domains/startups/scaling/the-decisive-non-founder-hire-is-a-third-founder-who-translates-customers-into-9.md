Epstein: most early companies have two founders (engineering/product, GTM); the real later hire is a program manager / chief of staff who becomes 'almost the third founder' — the first person who can translate between customers and the people building the product, converting an 18-month vision into 90-day shippable scope. When you're five people every founder word is gold and customers over-promise to your face; fight the urge to put your smartest people in front of customers — the conversations belong to people with customer empathy who define the value. Adds a specific scaling-hire principle to the KB's org-design and founder-led-sales cluster.
