Rahman identifies cross-team communication as the single biggest ongoing challenge at Jawbone's 500-person, 7-office scale. His solution is deliberately tedious: 2.5-hour daily calls for the UP3 product with the entire team from materials, sourcing, manufacturing, design, sensor, firmware, and mechanical engineering all sitting through each other's updates. The point isn't efficiency — it's forcing everyone to hear how their trade-offs affect other disciplines.

The specific example is telling: electrode material decisions for UP3's new sensing system had cascading effects on reliability, sourcing, and signal performance that no single team could optimize in isolation. Without forced cross-functional communication, these teams 'weren't talking to each other' and the trade-offs were being made in silos. When teams are small (5 people around a table), this happens naturally. The organizational challenge is recreating that natural cross-pollination at scale, and Rahman's answer is that there is no substitute for putting everyone in the same room — even virtually — and making them share.
