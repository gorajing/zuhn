Rather than evolving a more efficient carbon-fixation enzyme, plants simply produce staggering quantities of RuBisCo — an estimated 40 billion tons active on Earth at any time, making it the most abundant protein on the planet. The strategy works: despite ~50% error rates, sheer volume ensures enough carbon gets fixed.

This maps directly to growth strategy: sometimes the correct move is not to optimize conversion rates or unit economics, but to massively increase top-of-funnel volume. Early-stage companies that obsess over per-unit efficiency when they should be flooding the market with attempts often lose to competitors who accept waste but achieve coverage. The key insight is knowing which regime you're in — optimization matters when you've saturated volume, but premature optimization at low volume is the equivalent of trying to evolve a better RuBisCo when you could just make more of it.
