The neo-Assyrian Empire's downfall wasn't primarily military — it was administrative. They extended control beyond what their road and communication infrastructure could support. Combined with a tax-and-labor burden that made subjects welcome invaders ('Hey nomadic invaders! Come on in; you seem better than the last guy'), the empire became ungovernable.

This maps directly to scaling failures in organizations. Companies that expand into new markets, hire aggressively, or acquire competitors without proportionally scaling their coordination infrastructure (communication tools, management layers, cultural integration) hit the same wall. The Mesopotamian pattern also highlights that over-extraction from your base (heavy taxes, forced labor, mandatory military service) actively turns your foundation into a liability — your own people become the attack surface.
