Ben Horowitz credits Andy Grove, legendary Intel CEO, with a deceptively simple insight: if you're the leader of an industry, the growth of that industry depends on you. No one else will do it. Grove took this seriously at Intel, actively working to grow the entire semiconductor market rather than just competing for share within it.

a16z has applied this principle by investing heavily in policy work (crypto regulation, AI policy, American Dynamism) — not just to benefit their portfolio companies, but to ensure the US technology ecosystem remains competitive globally. The question they ask is not just 'how does a16z win' but 'how does the country win technologically, how do we compete with China, how do we remain relevant for the next hundred years.' This is the difference between optimizing within a system and expanding the system itself.
