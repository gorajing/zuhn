When Facebook needed to build data centers, Schroepfer didn't pretend the existing team could figure it out. He hired people with actual data center and network design experience. The approach wasn't 'we'll figure it out ourselves'—it was 'go hire a team to understand this new area and then help us get good.'

This pattern repeated across every major challenge at Facebook: consumer hardware, AI research, billion-dollar acquisitions. The consistent lesson is that founders and leaders who scale successfully are the ones who can honestly assess their knowledge gaps and bring in people who fill them, rather than letting ego prevent them from admitting what they don't know.
