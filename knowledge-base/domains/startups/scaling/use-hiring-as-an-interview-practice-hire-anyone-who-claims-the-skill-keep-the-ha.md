Conventional wisdom says your first 20 hires define your culture and must be best-in-class. Pincus calls this a myth that doesn't serve founders. Early Zynga hired anyone who claimed PHP skills, ran 2-hour standup calls with people across countries he'd never met, and kept less than half. But this process found Justin Waldron, an 18-year-old on his mom's couch who became a 'savant' and gamechanging early employee.

The counterweight: the careful, deliberate hiring process optimizes for known quality but filters out unknown gems. You can't identify a JW through interviews — you can only find them by giving people a chance and seeing who delivers. Speed of hiring matters more than precision of hiring in early-stage companies, because the cost of a slow hire (months of lost execution) exceeds the cost of a bad hire (fire them and move on).
