The conventional narrative focuses on product-market fit as the great filter, but the article presents a compelling counter-pattern: the years 5-7 growth plateau kills more funded startups than the initial search for PMF. This is when CAC spikes, growth stalls, investors go quiet, and acquisition offers arrive at valuations that feel like defeat. Most companies sell at this point.

The structural reason is that the skills that got you to PMF — scrappy building, founder intuition, small-team intensity — actively work against you at scale. The plateau demands a completely different operating mode: systematic cost management, disciplined reorgs, and the emotional stamina to rebuild from a position of accumulated exhaustion. Founders who recognize this phase transition as a predictable lifecycle event rather than a personal failure are more likely to navigate it successfully.
