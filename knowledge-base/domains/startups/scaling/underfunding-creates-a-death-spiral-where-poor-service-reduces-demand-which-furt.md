Amtrak is trapped in a classic death spiral: insufficient funding prevents infrastructure improvements, degraded infrastructure causes slow and unreliable service, poor service drives away riders, and low ridership justifies continued underfunding. This pattern appears repeatedly in startups — a product that's 'good enough to exist but not good enough to grow' enters a slow decline. The key insight is that incremental improvements within the spiral rarely generate enough momentum to escape it. France's SNCF broke this dynamic by having taxpayers cover ~50% of operating costs, essentially providing the external capital injection needed to maintain service quality above the threshold where ridership becomes self-sustaining.
