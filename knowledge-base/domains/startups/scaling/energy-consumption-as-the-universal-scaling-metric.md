Kardashev's key insight is that energy consumption is a more honest metric of civilizational capability than any cultural, political, or technological self-assessment. Between 1800 and 2015, population grew 7x while energy consumption grew 25x — the energy multiplier reveals the true scaling factor.

This maps directly to organizational scaling: a startup's real capability isn't its headcount or ARR but its total resource throughput — compute consumed, data processed, capital deployed, attention captured. Two companies with identical revenue can have wildly different resource throughputs, and the one consuming more resources per unit of output is either scaling faster or burning inefficiently. The Kardashev lens suggests tracking resource consumption curves as a leading indicator of capability growth, and watching for the characteristic exponential inflection that precedes phase transitions (Type 0→1, seed→growth, etc.).
