LIGO's original design could detect gravitational waves from only a relatively small volume of space and found nothing. Through iterative improvements to laser power, mirror quality, suspension systems, and noise reduction, it now reaches over 1,000 times more space than the original. They detect events roughly once every 3 days, having logged 294 detections.

This demonstrates that iterative refinement of a fundamentally sound architecture can yield exponential gains. The same principle applies to scaling products: rather than rebuilding from scratch, systematically improving the sensitivity of your core system -- conversion funnels, recommendation algorithms, distribution channels -- compounds over time into dramatically larger reach.
