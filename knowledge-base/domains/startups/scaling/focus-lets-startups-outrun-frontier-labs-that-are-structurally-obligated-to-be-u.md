Kilpatrick — speaking from inside one of the largest labs — argues there is more startup opportunity now, not less, despite models doing more than ever. The reason is structural: 'focus is the superpower of startups. If you can focus you can do anything.' Big companies with many products and many users have 'an obligation to do a bunch of things' and 'actually can't focus in one domain.' A startup with vertical expertise knows the customers and the ecosystem and can outrun even the best model labs in that niche.

This is a scaling principle, not just a startup pep talk: the diseconomy is one of scope, not headcount. The same breadth that makes Google's distribution powerful prevents it from going deep in any single vertical. Two compounding tailwinds reinforce the startup case — coding agents let small teams close the gap on incumbents' established codebases by shipping faster, and the agentic primitive is a new product category where differentiated risk appetite can win a risk-tolerant user cohort. The actionable read for a founder: pick a domain narrow enough that a scaled incumbent's breadth obligation actively works against them, and treat focus as the moat itself.
