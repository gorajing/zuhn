Costco's average wage of $21/hour is double the US retail average, plus superior health insurance and retirement benefits. The conventional view treats this as a luxury — a feel-good policy affordable only because the membership model generates margin elsewhere. But the data suggests it's actually a profit driver: Costco employees are three times more productive than the retail average. The math is clear — 2x the cost for 3x the output is a 50% efficiency gain. This works because higher wages reduce turnover (which is enormously expensive in retail), attract higher-caliber workers, and create organizational loyalty that translates into better customer service. The insight for startups is that compensation isn't a line item to minimize — it's a lever where spending more can produce disproportionate returns, particularly in roles where individual productivity variance is high.
