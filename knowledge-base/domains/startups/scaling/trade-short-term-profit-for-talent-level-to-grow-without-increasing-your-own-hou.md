The founder working 2-3 hours/week with a comfortable $6M business who wants $100M faces a clear tradeoff: you cannot change nothing and expect different results. The path that preserves family time requires sacrificing short-term profit to bring in high-level talent who lead growth on your behalf. You can make up profit in the long term; you cannot make up family time.

Hormozi's revelation: when he sold Gym Launch, every single person on the private equity buyer's team was better than every person on his team. The buyer would make more money on the company than the founder did. The lesson: business at the highest level is 'find world-class people and get out of their way.' Small businesses must win on character since they can't compete on vision scale, meaning the founder's temperament, reliability, and emotional control become the primary talent-attraction mechanism.
