Waymo's $16B raise at $126B valuation is backed by concrete operational metrics: 15M rides in 2025 (3x growth), 400K+ paid rides per week across six US cities, and a 90% reduction in serious injury crashes over 127M miles. The planned expansion to 20+ additional cities including Tokyo and London in 2026 signals the transition from proving technology to executing commercial rollout.

The significance lies in the shift from speculative valuations to metrics-backed scaling. Sequoia's framing of Waymo's 'compounding data advantage' suggests a flywheel where more miles driven improve the model, which improves safety stats, which builds public trust, which enables expansion to new cities. The involvement of top-tier investors (a16z, Sequoia, Tiger Global) at this scale indicates institutional conviction that autonomous driving has crossed the deployment threshold.
