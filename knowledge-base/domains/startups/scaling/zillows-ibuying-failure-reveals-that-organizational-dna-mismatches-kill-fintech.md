Zillow's iBuying collapse is a defining case study in organizational DNA mismatch. Despite 8,000 employees and dominant market position, Zillow failed because iBuying requires fundamentally different competencies than running a software marketplace. Their Zestimate was designed for consumer estimates (1% error is fine) but was repurposed for home purchasing decisions where 1% error at 5,000 homes per quarter meant massive losses.

The deeper lesson is structural: they doubled purchase volume every quarter before understanding how cohorts matured, operated at near-zero margin betting on cross-sell revenue that was overstated, and lacked the financial risk management culture that companies like Opendoor built from day one. NFX's takeaway is that mature marketplaces should ask 'partner or build?' when entering fintech — as Amazon did with Affirm — rather than assuming resources and scale can compensate for missing operational DNA.
