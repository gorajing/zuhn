The article's example shows that simply shifting a Head of Sales hire from Q1 to Q2 — not eliminating the hire, just delaying it — extended cash runway by 3 months. This delay created enough breathing room to raise capital on better terms rather than under time pressure.

Most founders think about hiring in binary terms: hire or don't hire. But the timing dimension is often more impactful than the headcount decision itself. A single quarter delay on a $200K+ fully-loaded senior hire preserves $50-60K in burn, which at typical startup burn rates can meaningfully shift runway. This makes hire timing a first-class variable in financial planning, not an afterthought. The implication is that founders should model hire start dates as adjustable inputs in their financial plans and explicitly evaluate the runway impact of shifting each hire by one quarter.
