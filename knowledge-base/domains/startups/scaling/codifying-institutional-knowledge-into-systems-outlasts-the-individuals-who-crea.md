Emperor Justinian commissioned the Corpus Juris Civilis, which compiled centuries of Roman legal precedent into a single, organized body of law. This codification outlived the Byzantine Empire itself — it became the foundation of European civil law and still influences legal systems today, nearly 1500 years later.

This is the strongest historical argument for documentation and knowledge codification in any organization. Most companies treat documentation as a chore, but Justinian's example shows it's potentially the most enduring output an organization can produce. The specific pattern is: take accumulated institutional wisdom that exists as oral tradition and scattered precedent, and compress it into a systematic, accessible format. For startups, this means playbooks, decision frameworks, and process documentation aren't overhead — they're the artifacts most likely to outlast your current team, your current product, and potentially even your company.
