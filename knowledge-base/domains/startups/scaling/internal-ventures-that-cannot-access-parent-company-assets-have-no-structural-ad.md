The letter's most actionable claim is that the execution engine MUST support the innovation engine with corporate assets—brand credibility, existing customers, distribution channels, and resources. This is what distinguishes internal ventures from startups, and without it, corporate innovation teams are just startups with more bureaucracy and less equity upside.

This creates a clear diagnostic for corporate innovation programs: if your internal ventures cannot access existing customer relationships for testing, leverage brand credibility for market entry, or utilize corporate resources and infrastructure, you have not actually created an innovation engine. You've created an internal incubator with all the disadvantages of corporate life and none of the advantages. The ambidextrous organization only works when both arms are connected to the same body.
