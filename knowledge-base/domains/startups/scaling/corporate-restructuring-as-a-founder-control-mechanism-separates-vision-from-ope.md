When Page created Alphabet in 2015, the public narrative framed it as organizational innovation. But the deeper function was resolving the founder's central dilemma: how to maintain strategic control without being consumed by operational complexity. By making Google a subsidiary with its own CEO, Page moved himself above the execution layer entirely.

This restructuring gave Page control over capital allocation, moonshot prioritization, and strategic direction while insulating him from quarterly operational demands. The structure is essentially a mechanism for founder permanence — it allows the original vision-holder to outlast any individual operational leader. Founders approaching scale should recognize that org structure design is itself a strategic tool for preserving founder influence, not just an efficiency optimization.
