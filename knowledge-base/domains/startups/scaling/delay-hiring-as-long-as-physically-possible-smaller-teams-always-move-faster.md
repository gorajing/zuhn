The author's framework: never hire because you raised money or have cash in the bank. Hire only when it's literally the only way to scale — when you're desperate and dying without this person. Then hire people who are obsessively committed and can do the work of multiple people.

Smaller teams work faster because there's less communication overhead. This is a direct counterweight to the 'build a world-class team' startup advice that assumes team size equals capability. The uncomfortable truth: really hardworking people don't want to work for not-hardworking people. If you can't match their intensity, you won't attract them. The three biggest reasons companies fail are: (1) running out of cash, (2) co-founder breakup, and (3) not solving a real problem. Premature hiring accelerates all three.
