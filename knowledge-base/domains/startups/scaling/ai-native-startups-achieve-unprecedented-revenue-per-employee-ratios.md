The data from Ben Lang's tracking of tiny teams in tech reveals a pattern: AI-native startups are reaching revenue milestones in months that previously took years with much larger teams. Cursor hit $100M ARR in 21 months with 20 people, Bolt reached $20M ARR in 2 months with 15 people, and Midjourney hit $200M ARR in 2 years with just 10 employees.

This isn't just about cost savings—it's a structural shift in how value gets created. When AI handles code generation, customer support (up to 80% of routine tasks per IBM), marketing automation, and data analysis, each human team member can focus on high-leverage strategic work. The implication for founders is clear: optimize for revenue-per-employee as a core metric rather than headcount growth.

However, these examples cluster heavily in software and AI products. Hardware, biotech, and regulated industries still require significant human capital, suggesting this pattern has domain-specific boundaries.
