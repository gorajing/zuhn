The immune system operates in escalating tiers: macrophages handle most infections alone, neutrophils are called as backup, and only when both fail does the expensive adaptive immune system (dendritic cells, T cells, B cells, antibodies) activate. Each escalation takes more time but brings more specialized and powerful capabilities. The first layer resolves ~90% of threats without ever needing the costly second layer.

This is directly applicable to organizational scaling. Customer support tiers, security incident response, code review processes, and sales qualification all benefit from this pattern. The key insight is that the cheap first layer must be genuinely capable of resolving most cases independently — it's not just a filter but a functional responder. Organizations that skip tiers and deploy expensive resources for every issue burn out their specialists and waste capacity that should be reserved for truly complex problems.
