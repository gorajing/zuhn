The article presents a nuanced counterpoint to the 'replace everything with AI' narrative. McKinsey's finding that hybrid AI-human approaches are 20% more successful suggests that the optimal startup structure isn't minimum headcount—it's the right ratio of human judgment to AI automation. Enterprise sales, regulatory compliance, and customer relationship building remain domains where human skills are irreplaceable.

Practically, this means founders should map their business functions on an automation spectrum: routine tasks (customer FAQ, data entry, code scaffolding) go to AI, while strategic tasks (enterprise deal closing, compliance navigation, creative direction) stay human. The 75% of consumers who still prefer human support underscore that over-automating customer-facing functions carries real business risk.

The hybrid model also extends to team structure—keeping a small core team while outsourcing specialized functions through networked partnerships, creating flexibility without permanent overhead.
