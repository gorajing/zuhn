Costco's average wage of $21/hour is double the U.S. retail average, supplemented by superior health insurance and retirement benefits. Rather than eroding margins, this produces employees who are three times more productive than competitors' workers. The math is straightforward: paying 2x for 3x output is a 50% improvement in labor cost-efficiency.

This challenges the common startup instinct to minimize labor costs during scaling. Costco demonstrates that above-market compensation creates a virtuous cycle: better pay attracts better workers, who deliver better service, which drives renewals and revenue, which funds continued high wages. The lesson for scaling companies is to model employee compensation as an investment with measurable returns rather than a fixed cost to minimize — especially in roles where individual productivity varies widely.
