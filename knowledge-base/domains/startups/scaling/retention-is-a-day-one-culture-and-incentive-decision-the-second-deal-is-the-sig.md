Josh Epstein (CRO, Coder; built HashiCorp Global Accounts 0→$175M+ through IPO) argues retention is a culture problem decided on day one: hire to close-and-worry-later and that culture permeates. The balance comes from structure — CS paid on something other than booked revenue, support not comped on customer spend, one document (problem, value hypothesis, buyer) following every deal pre- to post-sale. The first deal always closes (demo, referral, rapport); the second is the signal value landed. Pattern '7 over 3': a customer is ~7x more valuable after three years. Extends the KB's retention/expansion and incentive-design principles.
