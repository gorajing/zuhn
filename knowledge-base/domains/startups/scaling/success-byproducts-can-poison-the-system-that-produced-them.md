Plants' light-dependent reactions split water and release oxygen as waste. This oxygen gradually accumulated in the atmosphere and began competing with CO2 at RuBisCo's active site, causing the enzyme to produce toxic phosphoglycolate instead of useful products. Plants now spend significant metabolic energy detoxifying their own success byproduct.

This pattern repeats in scaling systems. Rapid hiring creates cultural debt. Viral growth attracts spam that degrades the product. Technical debt from shipping fast eventually slows shipping to a crawl. The insight is that growth byproducts are not merely neutral waste — they actively degrade the core mechanism that produced the growth. Founders who only monitor their growth engine without monitoring its exhaust get blindsided by self-poisoning.
