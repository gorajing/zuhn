Costco's warehouse stores have no back rooms, no decorations, and deliberately confusing layouts. Conventional retail wisdom would call these customer experience failures. Instead, customers interpret the bare-bones environment as proof that Costco prioritizes low prices over appearances, similar to how IKEA's DIY assembly makes customers proud of their furniture.

This mirrors a broader pattern: when constraints are authentic byproducts of a genuine value proposition (not manufactured scarcity), customers read them as credibility signals. The confusing layout also serves a functional purpose — it forces customers to wander through aisles, increasing exposure to products. The lesson for startups is that polishing away every rough edge can actually erode trust if your brand promise is built on efficiency or value. Sometimes the constraint IS the feature.
