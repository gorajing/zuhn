Most companies slow down as they scale because teams become interdependent — waiting for approvals, shared resources, or other teams to finish their work. Plarium avoided this by creating dedicated teams for every game and every direction within each game, each operating as a semi-autonomous studio with its own targets and market opportunity.

Three elements made this work: first, each studio knew exactly who they were competing against and what market opportunity they were pursuing, which created intrinsic motivation. Second, the incentive structure gave everyone upside in what they were building, aligned with the broader organization. Third, teams had authority to move at their own pace without waiting for headquarters approval. The overall organization was large, but the individual product teams stayed small and fast. This is essentially the 'small teams loosely joined' pattern, but with the critical additions of clear competitive context and aligned incentives that kept speed high without sacrificing strategic coherence.
