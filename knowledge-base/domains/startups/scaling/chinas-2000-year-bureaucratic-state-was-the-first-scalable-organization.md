China was the first modern state — centralized government with a corps of bureaucrats who could execute the wishes of that government. This structure survived dozens of dynasty changes, foreign conquests (the Mongol Yuan), civil wars, and natural disasters. The key was that the bureaucratic apparatus persisted even when the ruling family changed.

This is the original solution to the 'bus factor' problem in organizations. Startups that depend entirely on founder charisma face the same fragility as pre-bureaucratic kingdoms. The Chinese model shows that organizational durability comes from institutionalizing processes and creating a professional class that maintains continuity regardless of who sits at the top. The tension is that bureaucracy also creates resistance to change — the same stability that let China persist for 2,000 years also contributed to its inability to adapt to industrialization.
