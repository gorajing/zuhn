There's a scaling law at work in corporate innovation: as organizations grow, the stakes of any public misstep increase (reputation, brand, market cap), which drives more robust compliance processes. But innovation requires speed, direct market contact, and tolerance for small failures — all of which compliance processes are designed to prevent.

This means the organizations that most need innovation (large incumbents facing disruption) are structurally the worst equipped to execute it through conventional compliance frameworks. The solution isn't to weaken compliance but to create parallel processes — innovation-specific compliance tracks that maintain appropriate risk management while enabling the speed that experimentation demands.
