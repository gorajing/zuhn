The participation criteria for Strategyzer's peer group reveal a maturity model for enterprise innovation adoption: organizations must have been 'working systematically with it at organization or department level for over 1 year' and have a 'senior position championing and managing the process.' This implies a progression from individual champion → departmental adoption → systematic organizational integration.

The 'Organize' theme—designing structures to implement tools and processes—sits between adoption and scaling in their framework, suggesting it's the critical bridge. Many organizations stall at the champion-driven phase because they never build the organizational scaffolding (dedicated teams, budget lines, reporting structures, career paths) that makes innovation practice self-sustaining rather than personality-dependent. The explicit separation of 'Organize' from 'Scale' suggests these are distinct challenges that organizations commonly conflate.
