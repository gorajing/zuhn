Airwallex's revenue composition reveals a deliberate platform strategy: business banking (34%), payments (30%), spend management (20%), and infrastructure licensing. Each segment individually competes with focused players — Mercury in banking, Stripe in payments, Ramp in spend management — but the combination creates a unified financial stack that no single competitor replicates.

The infrastructure licensing segment is particularly revealing. When fintech companies integrate Airwallex directly into their own products, revenue becomes embedded and recurring with high switching costs. This pattern — starting as a payments tool and expanding into a full financial operating system — demonstrates how cross-border infrastructure naturally pulls companies into adjacent financial services. The 60%+ gross margins (unusually high for money movement) confirm that the platform economics are fundamentally different from single-product competitors.
