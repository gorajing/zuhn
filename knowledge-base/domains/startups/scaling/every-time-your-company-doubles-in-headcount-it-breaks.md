Beller states that every time a company doubles in headcount, it breaks. The processes, communication patterns, and management structures that worked for 10 people don't work for 20. What worked for 20 doesn't work for 40. Postscript grew to 270+ people and experienced this repeatedly.

The insight isn't that scaling is hard—everyone knows that. The insight is that the breakage is predictable and should be expected. Companies that plan for these transitions (new processes at 10, 20, 40, 80, 160 people) handle them much better than those surprised by the dysfunction. The discipline of focusing on a core product, core user, and core use case 'breeds excellence' precisely because it creates stability while everything else is breaking.
