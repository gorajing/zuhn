The moon base transitions from outpost to colony not when it stops needing Earth supplies, but when it begins exporting value back — rocket fuel, Helium-3, precious metals. Without export economics, funding cuts kill the project. This is the difference between a cost center and a profit center.

The same principle applies to new business units, product lines, or geographic expansions. A team that merely sustains itself without generating surplus value for the broader organization remains permanently vulnerable to budget cuts. True self-sufficiency requires creating something the parent system wants to buy. This is why internal startups fail when they optimize for independence rather than developing export products — technology, processes, revenue — that make the mothership dependent on them in return.
