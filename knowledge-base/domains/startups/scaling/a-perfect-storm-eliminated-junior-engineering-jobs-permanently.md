Three forces created a perfect storm: (1) Post-2021 mass layoffs proved companies could cut 20-30% of workforce and still function. (2) CS graduates have doubled or tripled in the past 10-15 years, flooding the market with supply. (3) AI tools made individual developers dramatically more productive, reducing headcount needs.

Berkeley graduates are applying to 1,000 positions and hearing back from only 2—not getting interviews, just acknowledgments. This isn't a cyclical downturn—it's a structural shift. Employers are choosing fewer, AI-native workers over larger teams of traditional developers. The implication for founders: you need fewer engineers than you think, and the engineers you do need have fundamentally different skills than traditional hires.
