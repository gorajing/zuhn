Samsung manufactures memory chips, AMOLED displays (98% market share), processors, and then assembles these into their own consumer products — phones, TVs, appliances. This vertical integration means they profit whether competitors succeed or fail. Apple's original iPhone ran on Samsung chips, meaning Samsung earned revenue from its biggest rival's flagship product.

This dual position as both supplier and competitor creates compounding advantages: component manufacturing generates deep technical knowledge that improves end products, end product development reveals what components need to improve, and the revenue from supplying competitors funds R&D that benefits Samsung's own products. For startups considering vertical integration, Samsung demonstrates that controlling critical components in your supply chain isn't just about cost savings — it's about building an information and capability moat that widens over time.
