Hu's structural reframe: most companies historically run as open-loop control systems — make a decision, execute it, don't systematically measure the outcome and adjust. Open loops are inherently lossy. The new company shape is closed-loop: continuously monitor output, adjust process to better meet the goal, self-regulating via intelligence at the center.

The operational requirement: every important process needs to be captured by an intelligent closed loop. Concrete examples: AI notetakers on every meeting, minimize DMs/emails (illegible to AI), embed agents in communication channels, build custom dashboards for revenue/sales/engineering/hiring/ops/everything. The whole organization should be legible to AI — meaning every important action produces an artifact the intelligence at the center can learn from.

For builders: this is the operational form of 'every company will be an AI company.' The retrofit is brutal because legacy processes are designed for human-routing of information, not for AI-driven feedback loops. Greenfield startups have a structural advantage. For Zuhn editorially: this is the most actionable framework for what 'AI-native' actually means — replace open-loop processes with closed-loop ones, instrument the entire company for AI legibility, and use the intelligence layer as the routing/coordination substrate.
