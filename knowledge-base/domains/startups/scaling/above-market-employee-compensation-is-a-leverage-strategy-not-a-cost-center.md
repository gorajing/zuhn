Costco's average wage of $21/hour is double the U.S. retail average, supplemented by superior health insurance and retirement benefits. The result: employees three times more productive than competitors. This isn't charity — it's arbitrage on the relationship between compensation and output.

While Amazon 'ruthlessly sacrifices everything for lower prices' including worker treatment, Costco treats compensation as a crucial ingredient in its model. Higher wages reduce turnover (which is enormously expensive in retail), attract better talent, and create employees who genuinely advocate for the company. In a low-margin business where operational efficiency is everything, the compounding effect of retained, motivated workers is a structural advantage that's difficult for competitors to replicate because it requires long-term thinking that quarterly earnings pressure discourages.
