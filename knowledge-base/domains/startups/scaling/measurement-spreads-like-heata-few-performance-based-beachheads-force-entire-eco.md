Graham identifies a contagion mechanism in compensation: once startups and professional service firms (consulting, law, finance) began paying young employees market rates in the late 1970s-80s, it created a 'yuppie' phenomenon that forced every other organization to follow. If smart, driven young people can earn more by starting companies or joining performance-based firms, legacy employers must raise pay to retain talent.

This is a powerful strategic insight for founders thinking about talent markets. It means that even a small number of performance-oriented competitors in a talent market can reshape compensation norms across an entire industry. The implication for countries worried about competitiveness: rather than reforming credentialing institutions, create conditions where energetic young people can get paid market rate for their work.
