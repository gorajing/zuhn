Hormozi illustrates this with his own trajectory: from running individual gyms to licensing his gym launch playbook to hundreds of operators. The direct service model caps out at operator bandwidth and geographic constraints. The licensing model removes both — you package the system, methodology, and brand into a replicable unit that others execute.

The deeper insight is about leverage type transitions. A service business trades time for money at a premium rate. A licensing business trades intellectual property for royalties at near-zero marginal cost. This also increases enterprise value multiples because recurring licensing revenue with high margins is valued far above one-time service revenue. The practical question for any service founder is: what part of my delivery is systematizable enough to become the product itself?
