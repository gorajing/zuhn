The nuclear explosion creates seismic waves that shake the entire Moon at magnitude 7 regardless of where you stand — there's no 'far enough away.' The Moon is simply too small for distance to attenuate the shock. On Earth, the same explosion's seismic effects would be localized. The body's size determines whether disruptions remain local or become inescapable.

This directly maps to organizational scaling. In a 5-person startup, every crisis — a key departure, a lost client, a product failure — shakes the entire organization with full force. There's no department boundary or management layer to absorb the shock. This isn't a flaw of small teams; it's physics. The strategic implication is that small teams must either prevent shocks entirely (impossible) or build tolerance for constant, full-body disruption. As organizations grow, they gain natural shock absorption — but also lose the universal awareness that comes from everyone feeling everything.
