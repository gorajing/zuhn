Mati (ElevenLabs, 300+ employees) describes his leadership team as exactly half-and-half: people who have done it before and are growing with the role, and people who never did anything like this but have grown incredibly. Anton (Lovable) takes a nuanced approach: for functions that need to get done predictably (sales, legal, finance), he hires for experience; for everything else, high-slope generalists who can be moved around the company. The key insight is that this isn't compromise — it's structural. Experienced operators bring playbooks for known problems; high-slope generalists adopt new tooling, create new processes, and handle the problems that don't have playbooks yet. Mati specifically embeds engineers across non-engineering teams (growth engineers in growth, operations engineers in ops) to help automate processes — extending the generalist principle into organizational design.
