Costco's average wage of $21/hour is double the U.S. retail average, paired with superior health insurance and retirement benefits. The conventional view treats this as a moral choice that sacrifices margins. But Costco's employees are three times more productive than the industry average, meaning the cost per unit of output is actually lower despite the higher per-hour wage.

This inverts the common startup assumption that labor costs should be minimized. The mechanism works through multiple channels: higher wages attract better talent, reduce turnover costs, increase institutional knowledge, and boost motivation. For scaling companies, this suggests that compensation strategy should be evaluated on output-per-dollar rather than cost-per-hour. The 'premium wage as cost reduction' strategy requires high-volume operations where productivity differences compound — which is exactly the environment most scaling startups face.
