While Amazon and many retailers treat labor as a cost to minimize, Costco pays an average of $21/hour — double the US retail average — plus superior health insurance and retirement benefits. The return: employees three times more productive than the industry norm.

This challenges the common assumption that labor cost minimization maximizes profit. Higher wages reduce turnover (which is extremely expensive in retail), attract more capable workers, and create employees who genuinely care about the customer experience. In Costco's membership-driven model, employee quality directly impacts the renewal decision. The compounding effect — better workers → better experience → higher renewal → more membership revenue → ability to maintain high wages — creates a virtuous cycle that cost-cutting competitors cannot easily replicate.
