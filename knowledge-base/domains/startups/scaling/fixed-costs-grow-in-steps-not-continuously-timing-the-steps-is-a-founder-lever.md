Unlike variable costs that scale linearly with revenue, fixed costs (salaries, office space, software licenses, compliance certifications) remain flat until a threshold forces a step-up — hiring another engineer, upgrading infrastructure, or adding a new office. The founder's lever is deciding when to trigger each step.

Premature step-ups burn runway faster without proportional revenue gain. Late step-ups risk service quality and growth bottlenecks. The financial model should explicitly map out when each fixed-cost step is triggered and what revenue milestone justifies it. This turns hiring plans and infrastructure decisions from gut-feel choices into model-driven timing decisions, which is especially critical for venture-backed startups where runway is finite.
