Costco pays its 245,000 workers an average of $21/hour—double the U.S. retail average—plus superior health insurance and retirement benefits. Rather than eroding margins, this investment yields employees who are three times more productive than the industry norm. The math works because high-throughput warehouse operations amplify per-worker productivity differences: a 3x productivity gain on a 2x wage premium is a clear net positive.

This contradicts the common startup instinct to minimize labor costs, especially in operations-heavy businesses. The mechanism is well-documented in economics as 'efficiency wages'—above-market pay reduces turnover (cutting hiring and training costs), attracts higher-caliber workers, and increases effort and loyalty. For Costco, productive, experienced employees also deliver better customer experiences that reinforce membership renewals. The lesson: in businesses where employee quality directly impacts customer experience, treating compensation as an investment rather than an expense can be the profit-maximizing choice.
