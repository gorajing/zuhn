Counter-intuitively, the Caplan thruster makes the Sun live longer by harvesting its mass. Lower-mass stars burn their fuel more slowly, so removing material extends the Sun's lifespan by billions of years. This maps to a powerful scaling insight: extracting value from a system to power its evolution can extend rather than deplete it. Companies that 'harvest' their legacy revenue to fund transformation often find the legacy business becomes more sustainable — leaner, more focused, slower-burning. The key insight is that extraction and preservation aren't always opposed. Strategic reduction of a system's mass (scope, complexity, burn rate) can extend its functional lifetime while simultaneously powering the next phase of growth.
