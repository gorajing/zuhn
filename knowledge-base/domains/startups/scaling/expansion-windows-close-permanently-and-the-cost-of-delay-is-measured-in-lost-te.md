The universe's accelerating expansion means the set of reachable galaxies shrinks continuously. Every second of delay permanently reduces the maximum territory any civilization could ever claim. This isn't a recoverable loss — once a galaxy crosses the cosmological horizon, no amount of future technology can retrieve it.

This maps directly to competitive dynamics in expanding markets. When a market is growing and fragmenting simultaneously, the window to establish presence in adjacent segments closes as competitors consolidate and network effects harden. Unlike the cosmic case, market horizons aren't absolute physics — but the principle that expansion windows close permanently and acceleration compounds the cost of delay is a powerful mental model for prioritizing speed in land-grab phases.
