Variable costs scale smoothly with revenue, but fixed costs—salaries, office space, compliance certifications—increase in lumps. You don't need half an engineer; you either hire one or you don't. This step-function nature means there are windows where your fixed cost base can support more revenue than you're currently generating, and moments where one additional hire tips you into a significantly higher burn rate.

The strategic implication is that timing these steps is one of the few cost levers founders fully control. Hiring one quarter too early when revenue growth stalls can burn months of runway. Conversely, delaying an infrastructure investment too long can bottleneck growth. The skill is in reading leading indicators—pipeline growth, support ticket volume, system load—to time the step-up just before it becomes a constraint, not after the constraint has already cost you customers or before revenue justifies the expense.
