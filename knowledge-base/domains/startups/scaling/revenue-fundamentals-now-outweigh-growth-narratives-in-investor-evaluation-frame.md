Dealroom's seventh annual VC ranking fundamentally restructured its methodology: two-thirds of an investor's score now comes from real revenue milestones achieved by portfolio companies, not fundraising rounds or valuation marks. This rewards investors who back companies that generate actual business results rather than those skilled at raising subsequent rounds at higher prices.

This shift in how the industry measures investor quality has cascading effects on founder behavior. When the VCs themselves are evaluated on portfolio revenue, they incentivize founders to prioritize sustainable economics over growth theater. Early-stage conviction — backing founders at Seed and Series A before valuations inflate — is specifically rewarded, creating a feedback loop that favors genuine company-building over momentum investing.
