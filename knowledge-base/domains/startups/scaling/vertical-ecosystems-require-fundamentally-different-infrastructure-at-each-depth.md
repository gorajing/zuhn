The ocean is structured into discrete zones (sunlit, twilight, midnight, abyssal, hadal), each with radically different conditions. Organisms adapted to one zone would die instantly in another. The food chain itself transforms at each layer — from photosynthesis-driven at the surface to marine-snow-dependent in the deep to chemosynthetic at hydrothermal vents.

For scaling organizations, this is a reminder that what works at one stage often fails catastrophically at the next. A startup's surface-level strategies (move fast, rely on founders, informal processes) break under the pressure of scale, just as surface organisms can't survive at depth. Each growth layer requires rebuilding core infrastructure — not just extending what worked before. The companies that scale successfully are the ones that recognize these phase transitions and redesign rather than stretch.
