The essay identifies an emerging model called 'The Mullet': a small upfront presence in SF or other hubs for accessing ideas, capital, and ecosystem benefits, combined with a large remote engineering workforce for building across platforms (web, mobile, etc.). This mirrors what happened in film/TV, where screenwriting and financing stayed in Hollywood but production moved to Atlanta, Portland, and Canada.

This model resolves the tension between needing hub network effects (which are 3-7X multipliers) and needing large engineering teams (which are prohibitively expensive in hubs). The key insight is that not all roles benefit equally from network density—leadership, fundraising, and strategy roles gain most from hub proximity, while engineering execution can be distributed without losing the network multiplier on the company's trajectory.
