The article's case study is instructive: a seed-stage SaaS CEO was pressured by her board to hire 5 sales reps, but modeling 30 new customers/month at $120 ARPU with 4.5% churn showed they'd only reach $90k MRR in 6 months — not enough to support the headcount. She hired 2 instead, invested in pricing optimization, and beat revised projections within 3 months.

This illustrates a critical anti-pattern in startup scaling: hiring ahead of revenue based on board pressure or pipeline excitement rather than modeled capacity. The MRR projection acts as a constraint check — it forces the question 'can our revenue trajectory actually support this burn increase?' before the commitment is made. The best operators treat headcount decisions as bets that must be validated against their most conservative revenue scenario, not their optimistic one.
