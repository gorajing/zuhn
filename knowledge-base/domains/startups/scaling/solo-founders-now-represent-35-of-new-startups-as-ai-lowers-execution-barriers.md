Sam Altman's prediction of one-person billion-dollar companies is backed by growing evidence. According to Carta, solo founders accounted for 35% of the 2024 startup class—a significant increase driven by AI tools that replace entire departments. BuiltWith generates $14M/year with a single employee, while Testimonial and Seats.aero hit $1.5M ARR as solo operations.

The enabling stack includes AI coding assistants (GitHub Copilot), no-code platforms (Bubble.io, FlutterFlow), AI-powered customer support, and automated marketing tools. This effectively means a single founder with AI tooling can cover engineering, support, marketing, and analytics—functions that previously required 5-10 hires minimum.

The strategic implication is that the bar for 'fundable' team size is dropping, but investors should evaluate whether a startup's solo structure is intentionally lean or just under-resourced for its growth stage.
