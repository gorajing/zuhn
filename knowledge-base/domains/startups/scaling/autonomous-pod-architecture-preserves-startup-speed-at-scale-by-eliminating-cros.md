PostHog's architecture demonstrates how sub-50-person companies can ship extraordinary product breadth. Each pod of 2-6 people operates as a micro-startup: setting their own goals, running their own reviews, building their own roadmap, and managing their own deployment schedule. The critical insight is that this isn't just about speed — it's about resilience. If one pod stalls, the others keep shipping. If a decision fails, the blast radius is local. This structure requires strong generalists who think beyond job titles and a culture of async transparency that does the work hierarchy normally handles. The tradeoff is worth it: you get department-level output without department-level coordination costs.
