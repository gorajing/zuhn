The 2025 emphasis on 'revenue retention or bust' reflects a post-ZIRP market correction where SaaS companies can no longer grow through expensive customer acquisition alone. Net revenue retention (NRR) measures whether existing customers are spending more over time through upsells, cross-sells, and expansion—effectively growing revenue without incremental sales and marketing spend.

High NRR (above 120%) serves as a compound growth engine: if your existing base grows 20%+ annually before any new sales, you have a resilient business that can weather downturns. For founders, this means product and customer success investment should precede aggressive sales hiring. The companies that survive tightening markets are those whose customers naturally expand, not those burning cash to replace churning ones.
