The standard retail playbook treats labor as a cost to minimize. Costco inverts this by paying $21/hour (double the U.S. retail average) with superior health insurance and retirement benefits. The result is employees who are three times more productive than the industry average — a 2x cost for 3x output is a clear mathematical win.

This challenges the common startup assumption that lean staffing and competitive (read: low) compensation optimize for runway. When employee output scales non-linearly with compensation and retention, underpaying is the expensive choice. The mechanism works through reduced turnover costs, institutional knowledge retention, and intrinsic motivation. The critical qualifier is that this works when employees directly impact customer experience and operational efficiency — which describes most roles in most companies.
