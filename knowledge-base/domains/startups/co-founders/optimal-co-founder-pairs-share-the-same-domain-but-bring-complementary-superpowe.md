Ben and Marc Andreessen have worked together for 30 years with a clear division: Marc provides star-level talent and ideas nobody else has, while Ben structures organizations around that talent for execution. They argue about direction and timeframe, but by the time they decide, they've already worked through the conflict productively.

The key insight is that optimal partnerships are not about diversity of domain but diversity of function within a shared domain. Same game, different strengths. This means co-founder matching should optimize for people who care about the same problems but attack them from fundamentally different angles. Partnering with someone who does exactly what you do creates redundancy, while partnering across entirely different domains creates communication overhead that erodes the partnership's advantage.
