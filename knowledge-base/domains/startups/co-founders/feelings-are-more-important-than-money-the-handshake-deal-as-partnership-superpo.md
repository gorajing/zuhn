Kreischer describes the turning point in his partnership with Tom Segura: a business deal where money was causing friction. One of them said 'our feelings are more important than the money, so let's just split it.' They operate their joint ventures on a handshake deal — no contracts, no lawyers.

This sounds naive but has practical logic: when the total value of a partnership exceeds what either could produce alone (and Kreischer explicitly says 'I couldn't do it without him, he couldn't do it without me'), protecting the relationship is the highest-ROI decision. The parallel to band splits is direct — many creative partnerships destroy more value in the fight over money than the money was worth. The handshake deal works because both parties have skin in the game and asymmetric information about each other that makes trust more efficient than legal enforcement.
