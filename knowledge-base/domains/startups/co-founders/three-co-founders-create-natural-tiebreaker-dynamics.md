Tom notes that with two co-founders who both have strong opinions, disagreements can become gridlocked. Adding a third co-founder creates a natural resolution mechanism — the third perspective breaks ties and forces more nuanced discussion. This contrasts with the common advice to keep founding teams small. The key insight is not about voting but about having a third viewpoint that reframes the disagreement and often reveals solutions neither of the original two positions considered.
