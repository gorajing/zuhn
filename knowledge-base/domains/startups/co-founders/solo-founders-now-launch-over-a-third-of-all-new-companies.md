Solo founders now launch over a third of all new companies, marking a structural shift in how startups get built. AI tools have compressed the capabilities that previously required a technical cofounder — design, prototyping, coding, and even legal review can now be handled by a single person augmented with AI. Combined with strong founder communities that provide peer support and accountability, the traditional two-or-three-founder model is no longer a prerequisite for building serious companies. This has implications for equity structures, decision-making speed, and the types of products that get built — solo founders can move faster on opinionated visions without consensus overhead.
