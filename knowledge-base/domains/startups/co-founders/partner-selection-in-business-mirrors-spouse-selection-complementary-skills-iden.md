Hormozi explicitly frames marriage as a partnership and applies business partnership logic: the ideal partner has complementary skills (one sells to the public, the other sells to the team) but shares the same strategic goal. The success metric is the delta between individual capability and combined capability — how much better are they together versus apart. This reframes co-founder evaluation: instead of asking 'are they talented?' ask 'does our combined output exceed what we'd each produce independently?' Most relationships (business and romantic) fail this test — one partner is a net liability, and the other would be better alone. The implication for co-founder selection is to quantify the partnership delta before committing, and to treat misaligned long-term goals as a dealbreaker regardless of complementary skills.
