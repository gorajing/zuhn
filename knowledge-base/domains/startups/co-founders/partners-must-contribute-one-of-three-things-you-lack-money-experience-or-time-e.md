After nine failed partnerships before finding one that worked, Hormozi distills his framework to three tests: does this potential partner have (1) money you don't have, (2) experience you don't have, or (3) time and energy you don't have? Critically, they must also clearly contribute that resource — having experience isn't enough if they're not actively deploying it.

The deeper insight is about why people partner unnecessarily: insecurity. Hormozi admits his biggest partnership losses came from being 'afraid to do it on my own.' The equity cost is enormous — splitting 50/50 on day one before anything has happened is giving away your most expensive asset (equity) at the worst possible time. The test should be: would you work a full shift just to hand this person half the output? If the answer is no, you don't need a partner — you need confidence.
