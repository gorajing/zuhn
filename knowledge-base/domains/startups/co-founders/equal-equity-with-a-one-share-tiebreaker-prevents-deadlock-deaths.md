Michael Seibel identifies two failure modes in equity splits. First, large disparities (like 60/40 or 70/30) create a toxic dynamic where the minority co-founder feels it's 'your company, not ours' — reducing their ownership mentality and willingness to endure hard times. Second, perfectly equal 50/50 splits create deadlock when co-founders disagree on critical decisions, and Seibel says they see this kill companies a lot.

The solution is elegantly simple: split equity as close to equal as possible, but give the CEO one extra share. This makes both co-founders feel like true equals in ownership while establishing an agreed-upon tiebreaker mechanism in writing from day one. The one-share difference is symbolically negligible but structurally critical — it's an agreement made during the honeymoon period that prevents paralysis during the inevitable storms.
