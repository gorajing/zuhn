Graham's observation that most big startup successes have two or three founders, and that those founders must genuinely like each other, is backed by the statistical pattern YC has observed across hundreds of companies. The sock-and-dog metaphor is vivid: startup pressure doesn't create relationship problems, it reveals and amplifies existing ones.

The Justin.tv example (Emmett Shear and Justin Kan, friends since second grade) suggests that the depth of pre-startup relationship correlates with resilience under pressure. This has practical implications for team formation: optimizing for complementary skills at the expense of genuine interpersonal bond is a mistake. The ability to argue without unresolved tension — to disagree productively — requires a foundation of trust that takes years to build.
