Naval identifies a recurring pattern across Apple (Jobs/Wozniak), Microsoft (Gates/Allen), and Google (Page/Brin): successful tech companies consistently pair a builder with a seller. This pattern is so reliable that venture capital investors are essentially trained to look for it as a prerequisite for funding.

The implication is that if you're a solo founder strong in only one dimension, your most important early hire or co-founder search should target the complementary skill. The rare individuals who can do both—like Elon Musk or Larry Ellison—become industry-defining figures precisely because they collapse this two-person requirement into one.
