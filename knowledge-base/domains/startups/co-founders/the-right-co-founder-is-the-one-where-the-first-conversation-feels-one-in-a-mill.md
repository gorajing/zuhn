Katz met his co-founder Michael toward the end of college. Their first lunch was 'one of those one-in-a-million conversations' where they immediately connected on vision, complementary skills, and energy. Katz was going into product management at Asana; Michael reached out to understand what product work was like.

The lesson: don't force co-founder relationships through structured matching or accelerator programs. The best partnerships have an unmistakable quality from the first interaction. If you haven't had that one-in-a-million conversation yet, keep meeting people rather than settling for a merely adequate co-founder. The co-founder relationship will be tested more intensely than any other—you need the foundation of genuine chemistry.
