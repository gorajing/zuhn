Most startup equity splits happen in a single conversation early on, often defaulting to equal splits regardless of actual contribution differences. Data-driven approaches—using frameworks that weight factors like time commitment, capital contribution, domain expertise, and role criticality—produce more defensible and fair outcomes.

This matters because equity disputes are among the top reasons cofounders part ways, and early-stage cap table problems compound as the company grows. Tools like Carta's equity management platform have made it easier to model scenarios and benchmark against industry data, but the key insight is that the conversation itself needs to be structured around measurable inputs rather than relationship dynamics or social pressure to split equally.
