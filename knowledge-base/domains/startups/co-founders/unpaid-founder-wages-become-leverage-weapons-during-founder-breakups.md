YC has observed a recurring pattern in founder breakups: when founders haven't paid themselves, the fired founder's lawyer discovers the wage violation and uses it as leverage to extract vesting acceleration. The departing founder says 'you broke the law by not paying me, but if you give me shares I'm not entitled to, I'll sign a release.' The remaining founders, eager to end the conflict, agree — and now a disgruntled ex-founder owns equity while contributing nothing, effectively making the remaining team work partly for someone who was fired. Carolynn Levy frames founder wages as a 'marital prenup' — not romantic, but essential protection. The fix is simple: set up a payroll service, pay at least minimum wage, and eliminate this attack vector before it's needed. One YC company that skipped payroll taxes for 3 years faced a 'huge expensive disaster,' and in extreme cases people can go to jail for payroll tax evasion.
