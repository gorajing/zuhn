Dalton and Michael observe a striking asymmetry in co-founder breakups: they never hear 'my co-founder is an A-plus person, they just didn't have the right skills.' What they always hear is 'my co-founder is a living nightmare and I can't take it anymore.' Skill mismatch is an error but not a fatal one — because almost everything in startup life is learned on the job anyway.

The implication is that co-founder selection should weight relationship history far more than resume complementarity. Have you had a fight with this person before? Have you seen how they handle stress? Do they attack or retreat under pressure? Understanding your co-founder's stress response pattern is more valuable than knowing their technical stack. Founders who have weathered disagreements before joining forces have a relationship that's been pressure-tested, which is worth more than any skill on a resume.
