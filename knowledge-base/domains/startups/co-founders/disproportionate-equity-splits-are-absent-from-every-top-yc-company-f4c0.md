Carolynn Levy states explicitly that among YC's highest-valued companies, there are zero instances of significantly disproportionate equity splits. YC views unequal splits as a red flag signaling deeper problems: one founder may not be fully committed, may be overvaluing past contributions or credentials, or the team may lack honest communication about expectations.

The key mental shift is looking forward rather than backward. If all founders are committing equally to the future, pre-formation contributions (the idea, early code, an MBA) shouldn't drive allocation. The idea alone has zero value — execution is everything. Equal equity also creates psychological alignment: everyone feels they have equal skin in the game, which matters enormously during the grinding middle years of a startup.
