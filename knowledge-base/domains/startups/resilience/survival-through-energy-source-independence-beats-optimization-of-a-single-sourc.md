In the rogue Earth scenario, surface civilization dies because it depends entirely on an external energy source — the sun. The survivors are those who tap geothermal heat from Earth's core and nuclear energy — power sources that are intrinsic to the planet itself. This maps cleanly to organizational resilience. Companies dependent on a single external platform (an app store, a social media algorithm, a dominant customer) are solar-dependent — one perturbation in the external system and they freeze. Companies that build internal engines of value creation — proprietary technology, owned distribution, deep customer relationships — carry their energy source with them. The lesson isn't to avoid external energy entirely (Earth thrived on solar for billions of years), but to develop internal sources in parallel so survival doesn't require the external one to persist.
