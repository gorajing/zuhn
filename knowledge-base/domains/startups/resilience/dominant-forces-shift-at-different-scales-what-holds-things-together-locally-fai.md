In the Big Rip scenario, gravity holds galaxies together even as intergalactic space expands — until expansion accelerates past the threshold where gravity can compensate. Then galaxies tear apart, then solar systems, then atoms. The binding force that works at one scale becomes irrelevant at the next.

This is a precise analogy for startup scaling. The forces that hold a 10-person team together (personal relationships, shared context, informal communication) break down at 50 people. The structures that work at 50 (small teams, weekly all-hands) fail at 500. Each scale transition requires identifying which binding force is about to become insufficient and replacing it before the organization tears apart. The lesson isn't just 'things change at scale' — it's that the specific force maintaining cohesion has a quantifiable breaking point.
