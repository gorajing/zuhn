Graham concedes that there is no total order of artistic works or people's ability to judge them, but argues there is definitely a partial order. You can't rank Bellini vs. Leonardo definitively, but you can reliably say both are better than a random child's drawing.

This partial-ordering insight has immediate practical value for startups, hiring, and product development. Teams often get paralyzed debating whether option A or B is better when both are clearly in the top tier. The productive move is to recognize that good-enough quality discrimination is achievable and sufficient — you don't need perfect taste to have meaningfully good taste. This also means quality bars in organizations should focus on distinguishing clearly-bad from clearly-good rather than trying to precisely rank options in the same tier.
