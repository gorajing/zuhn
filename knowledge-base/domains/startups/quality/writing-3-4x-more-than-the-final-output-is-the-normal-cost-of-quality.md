Paul Graham reveals that he writes three to four words for every one that survives in a final essay, with heavier cutting at the beginning where he hasn't yet found direction. This rewrite ratio isn't a sign of failure — it's the standard cost of quality.

This has direct implications beyond writing. Any creative or intellectual work — product specs, architecture docs, pitch decks — benefits from the same discipline. The first draft is exploratory, not precious. Planning to discard most of your initial output paradoxically produces better results faster, because you stop trying to get it right on the first pass and instead focus on discovering what 'right' actually looks like through iteration.
