Graham contrasts two creation modes: the 'defend a position' approach (start with a thesis, argue for it) versus the 'river' approach (at each step, flow toward what's most interesting). The river metaphor is precise — the Meander river winds all over the place, but its path is the most economical route to the sea. Similarly, an essay that meanders is actually finding the optimal path through idea-space.

This maps directly to product development. The river approach means you can change the question when you hit a wall, backtrack when a thread runs dry, and discover destinations you couldn't have planned. The defend-a-position approach locks you into your initial thesis and makes you 'bluster through obstacles and hand-wave across swampy ground.' The real problem isn't that you miss subtleties — it's that you can't change the question.
