Andrews reveals the early growth engine of Diary of a CEO: none of the team had podcasting experience, and they had zero credibility. Their entire strategy was volume-based outbound: message 1,000 potential guests every single week. At roughly 1% conversion, that meant 10 yeses per week. The mindset was explicitly 'it's a numbers game — if we keep doing this and do not quit, we will win.' This pure persistence approach, combined with improving content quality over time, drove growth from 8,000 to 13 million subscribers. The lesson is counterintuitive for content creators who focus on algorithmic optimization: sometimes the growth bottleneck isn't your content strategy but your willingness to do the unglamorous outreach work at massive scale.
