The recommended sound design process for video production follows a
specific order: (1) Start with temp music or score to establish the
emotional tone and pacing of each scene -- this guides all subsequent
sound decisions. (2) Add creative sound effects and foley, matching
sounds to on-screen actions while considering the emotional intent
rather than pure realism. (3) Apply final audio treatment -- EQ,
compression, reverb, and mixing to blend all layers into a cohesive
soundscape. Working in this order prevents the common mistake of
designing detailed sound effects that clash with the music added later.
The score sets the emotional framework, SFX fills the visual gaps, and
treatment unifies everything.
