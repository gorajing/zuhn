When a video is posted, the platform runs a multimodal analysis (computer vision on frames, audio fingerprinting on speech, metadata parsing) to build a 'topic mapping' and a fit score. It then shows the video to roughly 200 users — mostly non-followers — it predicts are the best match. If the sample reacts well, the platform escalates to 2,000, then 20,000, then 200,000. If reaction is neutral, it re-samples another 200. If negative, it stops almost immediately.

This means reach is a funnel with a single catastrophic gate: the first 200. Creators should treat every pre-publish decision (topic selection, hook, visual framing, title, thumbnail, opening three seconds) as optimization for a 200-person audition, not for 'everyone who might see it.' Anything that blurs the fit score — generic topics, broad avatars, multi-niche bouncing — corrupts that sample and locks the post out of further distribution.
