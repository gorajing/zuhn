A video is classified through three parallel channels: computer vision on the frames (what objects, faces, scenes, text-on-screen appear), audio fingerprinting on the speech (what words are spoken, in what context), and metadata parsing (caption, hashtags, creator history, location). These signals combine into a single contextual topic mapping.

The tactical consequence is that keyword-stuffing captions or hashtags to imply a topic does nothing if the visuals and audio contradict it. Worse, inconsistency between the three signals likely weakens classification confidence and degrades fit score precision. This aligns every creative choice: the hook must match the topic, the B-roll must match the topic, the spoken script must match the topic, and the caption is a minor reinforcer — not a steering wheel.
