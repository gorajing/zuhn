Yoon Seung-rim was already in psychological counseling for anxiety and depression when a close cinematographer casually said over drinks that staff were worried she was 'coming down.' The remark triggered a spiral — fear that her directing skills really were declining, that opportunities would dry up. But within that fear, anger surged: 'You're telling me my directing skills are ending?'

She channeled that anger into a simple choice: go all out. She accepted both aespa's Armageddon and IVE's Haeya — projects with completely opposite concepts requiring different creative languages. She slept every other day, filmed 40-hour stretches, and worked roughly 1 day off in 120 days. The result: both MVs won Best Music Video at Korea's two biggest awards ceremonies — nearly unprecedented for different artists in the same year. The pattern: crisis creates the pressure that forces expansion beyond comfortable boundaries. The first step isn't calm reflection — sometimes it's allowing anger to override the paralysis of doubt.
