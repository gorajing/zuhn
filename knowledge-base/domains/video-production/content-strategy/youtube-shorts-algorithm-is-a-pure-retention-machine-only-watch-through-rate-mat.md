The creator's core insight is that YouTube's algorithm for Shorts is brutally simple: it measures retention (how long people watch before swiping) and nothing else. If viewers stay, it pushes to more people. If they swipe early, distribution stops. This means the first 3 seconds (the hook) matter more than the remaining 90% of the video combined — a 30-second video where people leave at second 3 means 90% of your content is never seen. The retention psychology framework has three parts: (1) Hook — first 3 seconds, use audio + visual + text hooks simultaneously to hit the viewer's brain before their thumb can scroll, (2) Body — fast pacing, frequent cuts, small zooms, no dead space, (3) Reward — most powerful or outrageous moment at the very end, cut immediately after payoff. You can verify this by checking your audience retention graph in YouTube analytics.
