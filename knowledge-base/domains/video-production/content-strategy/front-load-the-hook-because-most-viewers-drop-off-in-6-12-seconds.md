Reeve's viral statue video was edited in ~20-25 minutes on his phone in CapCut with a 200-quid lapel mic — deliberately low production. The leverage was structural, not technical. His platform analytics showed median view time of just 6-12 seconds before drop-off, so the single most important move is front-loading the hook: if viewers don't 'buy' the hook they're gone before any of your content lands.

Two cheap multipliers he flags as underrated: captions (a clear retention boost) and music. With AI music generation he now produces the video first, then auditions completely different genres until one fits, editing music sections to time up with speech sections — reversing the usual constraint of matching speech to a found track. Music is 'relatively quiet but makes a massive difference to the feeling.' The meta-lesson is that virality is mostly unpredictable practice and iteration (things he expects to hit don't, and vice versa), so volume plus a disciplined hook beats polishing any single piece.
