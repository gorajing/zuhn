Kallaway's engagement formula has four independent requirements that all must be present: (1) topic relevance — the content solves a problem the ideal viewer actually has; (2) non-obvious and tactically implementable information — the viewer learns something new and can act on it; (3) high absorption — the delivery is clear enough that the viewer actually understands what to do; and (4) short implementation distance — the viewer can take a small action and get a meaningful result.

Missing any single factor breaks the chain. A relevant, novel, clear insight that requires a six-month project to implement will not drive engagement. A tactical, fast-to-implement tip on an irrelevant topic will not either. These four also double as conversion levers for turning viewers into buyers — they form the structure of content that builds both audience and revenue simultaneously.
