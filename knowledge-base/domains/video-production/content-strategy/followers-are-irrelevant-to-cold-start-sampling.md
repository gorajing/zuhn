The algorithm deliberately biases the initial sample toward non-followers because it already assumes followers will like the video — their signal is less informative. To discover whether a video can travel, it needs evidence that strangers will watch and engage. This is why large accounts can post flops and small accounts can post viral hits: the sampling mechanism is fundamentally impartial to your existing audience size.

For creators, this has two implications. First, 'build a following' as a distribution strategy is broken — followers help with monetization, sponsor leverage, and off-platform flywheels, but not with algorithmic push. Second, every new post is cold-start: your last viral video does not buy you distribution on the next one if the new video's sample data comes back weak. The only compounding asset in sampling is a sharpened fit score built through consistent topic-avatar matching, not follower count.
