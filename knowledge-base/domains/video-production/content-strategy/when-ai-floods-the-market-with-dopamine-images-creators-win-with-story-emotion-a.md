Yoon observes that a flood of dopamine-stimulating AI-generated imagery has already started — soon no one will be able to tell what's real and what's fake. But ironically, as bizarre and provocative content becomes commonplace, what creators can uniquely provide becomes incredibly clear: stories about emotions, values, meaning, and philosophy.

Her own award-winning works (Armageddon, Haeya, Jeon Somi's Extra MV) were all made with VFX and AI technology, but contain her personal stories and authentic emotional core. She credits this authenticity — not technical execution — for why the public recognizes and pays attention to her work in a flashy market. The principle: technology raises the floor of production quality to the point where technical skill alone is table stakes. The differentiator becomes the human substance — the specific worldview, emotional truth, and meaning that only a particular creator can embed.
