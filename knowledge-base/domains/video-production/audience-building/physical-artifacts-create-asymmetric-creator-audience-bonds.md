Rather than discarding 10,000 shade balls after filming, Veritasium announced signing each one and mailing them to Patreon supporters worldwide — explicitly acknowledging the 'horrendous' shipping costs as a thank-you investment. This transforms a disposable prop into a unique physical token that supporters will keep, display, and talk about.

Physical artifacts activate psychological ownership and reciprocity in ways digital perks cannot. A signed shade ball is conversation-starting, Instagram-worthy, and impossible to replicate — it's proof of participation in a specific cultural moment. The shipping cost signals genuine sacrifice, reinforcing the reciprocal bond. For creators, the lesson is that repurposing production artifacts into supporter rewards creates outsized loyalty per dollar compared to standard digital tier rewards.
