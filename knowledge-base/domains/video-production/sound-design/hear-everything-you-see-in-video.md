The core principle of sound design for video is that every visual
element should have an audio counterpart. Nothing is silent -- even an
empty room has ambient sound (room tone, HVAC, distant traffic). When
viewers see something on screen but hear nothing, the result feels
uncanny and amateur. This applies beyond literal sounds: sound can
represent thoughts, feelings, and even camera movements. The inverse
is also true -- not everything needs to sound the way it looks.
Creative sound design means choosing sounds that serve the emotional
intent rather than strict realism. Tools range from Pro Tools
(professional standard) to Audacity (free, beginner-friendly).
