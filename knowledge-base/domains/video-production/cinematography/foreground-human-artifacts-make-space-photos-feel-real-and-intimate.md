Hank Green repeatedly flags that his favorite Artemis images are the ones that include part of the Orion capsule — the duct-taped cables, switches, the window frame — rather than the clean telephoto shots. The juxtaposition of a fragile human-made 'little submarine out in the ocean of everything' against the Earth or the Moon provides both emotional anchoring and scale. A pure image of Earth could be a stock photo; an image of Earth through a spacecraft window cannot.

The principle generalizes to any visual storytelling: a subject in isolation is often less compelling than the same subject framed by something relatable and tactile. Put a person's hand in the shot. Show the tripod leg. The 'impurity' is what makes the image feel true.
