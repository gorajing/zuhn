Loh visited a high-poverty rural school where kids without phones or internet access showed extraordinary mathematical thinking—because they made their own games for fun. These kids, driven by authentic curiosity, developed problem-solving abilities that wealthy, well-resourced students often lack.

The insight: the environment that produces the best thinkers isn't the one with the most resources—it's the one that cultivates genuine curiosity. Credential-driven learning (studying to pass tests, building resume items) produces narrow skills that AI can replicate. Curiosity-driven learning produces the adaptive thinking that remains uniquely valuable.
