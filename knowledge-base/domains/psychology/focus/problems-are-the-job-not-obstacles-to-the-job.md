Burkeman identifies a meta-problem: we deal with both the actual problem AND our indignation that problems exist. We believe we should have reached a stage where problems are behind us. But problems are just areas where limited capacities meet reality — another word for that is 'meaningful activity in life.'

A friend of Burkeman's realized the problems at work weren't obstacles to her job — they WERE the job. Her value was precisely her ability to deal with unforeseen challenges. A job with no problems would be boring and vulnerable to automation. Developing a 'taste for having problems' removes the second layer of suffering and lets you engage directly with what matters.
