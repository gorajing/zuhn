Epictetus's role ethics recognizes that we simultaneously occupy many roles: parent, child, spouse, friend, professional, citizen. None can be suspended — even while working, you remain a parent and spouse. The critical insight is that physical and emotional energy are finite resources shared across all roles.

Spending energy on non-actionable concerns (doomscrolling news you cannot influence) directly trades off against being a good parent, friend, or colleague. This reframes the Stoic 'focus on what you control' not as passive acceptance but as active energy conservation — protecting your capacity to perform well in the roles that genuinely need your effort. The regret comes when people realize this trade-off only near the end of life.
