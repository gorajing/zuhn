Loh's analogy is devastatingly clear: using AI to do school writing is like driving a car for exercise. The output (traveling a mile) is achieved, but the purpose (building physical fitness) is completely defeated. School assignments exist not to produce the output but to develop the student's cognitive capabilities.

LLMs are powerful precisely because of the 'L'—language. Students who outsource language skills to AI will grow up unable to think logically, becoming dependent on others' outputs. For professionals who already have these skills, AI is a legitimate productivity tool. For students still developing them, it's cognitive atrophy.
