Niksen is the Dutch concept of the art of doing nothing: staring out the window, watching branches sway, listening to birds. It is not meditation and not a forced practice. It is a voluntary pause where you simply stop all busyness and look around.

Cytowic describes it as throwing a circuit breaker. When cognitive load reaches tilt, niksen lowers the level of engagement so you can start fresh. The brain did not evolve to be on 24/7. It needs periods of downtime to restore itself. Silence is described as an essential nutrient because for most of our evolutionary history, the world was quiet. Even three minutes of niksen is enough to achieve this reset effect.
