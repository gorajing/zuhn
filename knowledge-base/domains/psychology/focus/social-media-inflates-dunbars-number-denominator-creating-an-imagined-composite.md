Robin Dunbar's research shows our hardware supports roughly 5 intimate relationships, 15 close friends, 50 casual friends, and 150 meaningful contacts. This hasn't changed in 10,000 years. But social media exploded the denominator of loose connections into the thousands.

The psychological damage isn't just 'more comparison.' Our primate brains perform an unconscious conflation: Bob's intelligence, Ted's wealth, Frank's fitness, Jane's discipline, and Tony's generosity merge into a single composite figure -- an imagined friend who exceeds us in every dimension. This is a biological bug, not a character flaw. The Nightfall metaphor is apt: like the civilization that went insane upon seeing 30,000 stars for the first time, we weren't designed to process this many social signals. The only antidote, repeated across 3,300 years of wisdom traditions, is refusing to play the comparison game entirely.
