After a week of deliberate focus experimentation, Lucas's top recommendation was: don't work on anything before your most important work. The first thing you devote focus to gets absolute prime real estate in your brain with no competition from other inputs or contexts.

He previously would get up at 5am and work on YouTube — but that 'YouTube work' included checking email, analytics, comments, editing, and Photoshop. By the time he clocked into his day job, his brain was already fragmented across a dozen contexts. During the challenge, he committed to a single focus per morning session (e.g., if editing, only editing software — nothing else). The result: he solved a major project issue that had been stalling for months, got praise from leadership, and the team sentiment shifted from nervous to excited — all within one week.
