The brain contains two distinct pleasure circuits. The wanting/reward system (dopamine-based) is diffused widely throughout the brain, very easy to trigger, but impossible to satiate. This is the hedonic treadmill: as soon as we get something we want, we want something else. The liking/pleasure system (opioid-based) is smaller, harder to trigger, but can be satiated.

Tech products primarily exploit the wanting system through variable intermittent reinforcement, the same mechanism as slot machines. You scroll, occasionally get a hit of something interesting, and keep scrolling for the next one. The rewards come infrequently and unpredictably, which is exactly what keeps the insatiable wanting system engaged. This is why turning off the phone is the most effective but hardest intervention.
