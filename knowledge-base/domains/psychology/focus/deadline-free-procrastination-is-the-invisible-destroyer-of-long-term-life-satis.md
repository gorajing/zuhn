Tim Urban distinguishes two kinds of procrastination. Deadline-based procrastination is contained — the Panic Monster activates near the deadline, forcing last-minute action. The system is ugly but functional. Deadline-free procrastination — affecting career ambitions, health, relationships, creative pursuits — has no such safety net. Without external deadlines, the Instant Gratification Monkey rules indefinitely.

This is why people who seem functional (they meet work deadlines) can still feel like spectators in their own lives. The frustration isn't about failing to achieve dreams — it's about never even starting to chase them. The implication is that artificial deadline creation (public commitments, accountability structures, scheduled reviews) may be the most important productivity intervention for self-directed goals.
