Neuroscience research shows that heavy social media users have both hypersensitive reward pathways and underdeveloped prefrontal cortex with fewer neural connections between the cortex and reward centers. This means the technology is not merely taking advantage of immature self-control — it is actively preventing that self-control from developing.

This creates a vicious feedback loop: use weakens the brake system, which increases use, which further weakens the brake system. The implication is that early intervention matters enormously — the longer a child uses social media during this developmental window, the harder it becomes for their brain to develop the self-regulatory capacity needed to moderate use.
