Kotler cites research showing that programmers in flow who get interrupted by something as minor as a text alert take a minimum of 15 minutes to re-enter flow, with no guarantee of return. This is asymmetric damage: a 5-second interruption can destroy an hour of productive state. The implication is that distraction management is not about willpower — it's about pre-commitment. Turning off notifications before a work block isn't optional hygiene; it's the highest-leverage productivity intervention available. This also means that open-office plans and Slack-always-on cultures are systematically destroying their most valuable cognitive output.
