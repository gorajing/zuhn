Burkeman's practical technique: ask 'what version of this am I willing to do in this moment?' and keep lowering the bar. Not speaking to 1,000 people? How about 15 minutes in a small setting? Almost always, there exists some level at which you're genuinely willing to take the next step.

The key is accepting that this level might be embarrassingly small. But that's fine — the point is movement, not magnitude. This approach works because it sidesteps the binary thinking that keeps people stuck (either I do the big scary thing or I do nothing). It also builds the neural pathways gradually, which is how real behavioral change works according to the neuroscience of habit formation.
