Bonanno recommends converting the flexibility sequence into self-talk prompts that can be used in real-time distress. For the flexibility mindset: 'It is going to be okay' (optimism), 'I have some tools to work with this' (coping self-efficacy), 'What do I need to do to get through this?' (challenge appraisal). For the flexibility sequence: 'What is happening now? What do I need to do?' (context sensitivity), 'What tools do I have in my toolbox?' (repertoire).

The power of framing these as questions rather than statements transforms self-talk from motivational affirmation into an active problem-solving dialogue. You are asking yourself questions and generating answers, which forces engagement with the specific problem rather than wallowing in generic distress. This is particularly effective because during crises, people tend to zoom out to catastrophic big-picture thinking — self-talk questions pull attention back to the solvable problem at hand.
