Cytowic advocates for 'niksen' — the Dutch art of doing nothing. Unlike meditation, niksen isn't a structured practice. It's simply stopping, looking up, watching branches sway, listening to birds, for even three minutes. This throws the brain's 'circuit breaker' and lowers the engagement level from overload back to baseline.

The neuroscience supports this: the brain evolved in a quiet world where change was rare. It was not designed for 24/7 stimulation. Silence is an 'essential nutrient' because the brain needs periods of low-stimulus downtime to restore itself. Practical implementation includes adjusting screen settings (blue-yellow tritanopia filter, reduced brightness), maintaining consistent sleep schedules, and avoiding stimulating content before bed.
