Every contemplative tradition Hughes cites prescribes the same operating conditions — stillness, silence, present-moment attention, minimal input. Modern media systems are engineered to do the opposite: maximize novelty, trigger cortisol, exploit variable-ratio reinforcement, and keep the nervous system in mild alarm. Someone who defaults to a modern attention diet has effectively opted out of the preconditions for the mental states these traditions considered basic human equipment. The implication is not 'go off-grid' but 'treat the defaults as actively adversarial' and explicitly carve out conditions (no phone, unscheduled time, boredom tolerated) as a deliberate practice rather than expecting them to happen on their own.
