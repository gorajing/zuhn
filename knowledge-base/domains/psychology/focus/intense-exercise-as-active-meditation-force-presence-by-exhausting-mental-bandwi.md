Beyond the well-documented biochemical benefits of intense exercise (endorphins, dopamine, serotonin, BDNF, cortisol reduction), there is a consciousness-level benefit: when you train hard enough, you literally lack the mental bandwidth to engage in low-vibration thoughts. You cannot catastrophize about the future while doing heavy squats.

This reframes exercise from a health obligation into a consciousness tool. The goal shifts from calories burned to minutes of forced presence achieved. Aim for 30-60 minutes of intensity high enough that worry becomes impossible, 2-4 times per week. The type doesn't matter — weights, running, yoga — as long as it demands full mental engagement.
