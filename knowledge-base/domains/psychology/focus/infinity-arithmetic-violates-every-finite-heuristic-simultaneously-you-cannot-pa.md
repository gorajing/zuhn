Hilbert's Hotel demonstrates that infinity defies subtraction, addition, and division simultaneously. It's not that one rule breaks — every arithmetic intuition fails at the same time. This is qualitatively different from a situation where one heuristic is wrong and the others hold.

When you encounter a domain where all your rules break simultaneously — a new market that violates pricing, distribution, AND product intuitions; an AI system that breaks your assumptions about speed, cost, AND capability — patching individual heuristics is futile. You need to recognize the phase transition and rebuild from axioms. The sign that you've crossed into such a domain is precisely that multiple independent intuitions all fail at once. One broken heuristic is a bug. All broken heuristics is a paradigm shift.
