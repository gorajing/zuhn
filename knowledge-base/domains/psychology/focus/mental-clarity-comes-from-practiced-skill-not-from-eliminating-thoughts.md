The popular interpretation of 'empty mind' as thought suppression is wrong. Musashi's mushin (no-mind) is achieved through such extensive practice that correct responses emerge automatically. The swordsman doesn't think about technique - technique flows from deeply ingrained patterns.

This reframes the relationship between thinking and doing: expertise isn't about removing thought but about converting conscious decisions into automatic responses through practice. The master's 'empty mind' is actually the most full - packed with trained responses that no longer require conscious processing.
