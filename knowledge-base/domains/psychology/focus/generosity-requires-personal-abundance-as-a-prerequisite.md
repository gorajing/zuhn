There is an important distinction between 'humble' kindness (self-sacrificing, depleting) and 'generous' kindness (overflow-based, sustainable). The Korean concept of kindness often maps to humility -- lowering yourself for others -- but the more admirable model is generosity from abundance, like Bill Gates funding infrastructure in Africa. Nobody dismisses that as weakness because the giver's strength is obvious.

The practical framework: kindness should be redefined as sharing your overflow with others once you can adequately care for yourself. Trying to help others when your own mental, physical, or financial health is unstable risks mutual destruction. This is not selfishness but structural wisdom -- you cannot sustainably pour from an empty cup. Build capability first, and the resulting ease will naturally turn outward because humans are fundamentally social animals.
