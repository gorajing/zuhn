The process-over-outcome principle appears across domains: athletes perform better when focused on technique than score, writers produce better work when focused on daily practice than bestseller lists, entrepreneurs build better companies when focused on customer value than valuation.

The mechanism is straightforward: process is immediately actionable (I can improve my technique right now), while outcome is not (I cannot directly cause myself to win). Focusing on what you can control (process) produces steady improvement, while fixating on what you can't directly control (outcome) produces anxiety that degrades performance.
