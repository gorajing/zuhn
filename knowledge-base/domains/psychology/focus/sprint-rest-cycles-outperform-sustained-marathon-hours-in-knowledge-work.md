Naval argues that claims of sustained 80-120 hour work weeks are largely status signaling, not genuine high-output cognition. The brain breaks down under prolonged load, degrading idea quality. Effective knowledge workers instead sprint intensely while inspired and passionate, then rest and reassess before the next burst.

This lion-hunting metaphor reframes productivity away from time-on-task metrics toward output-per-cycle. The practical pattern is a 'marathon of sprints' — each sprint driven by genuine inspiration, each rest period allowing pattern recognition and creative recombination that continuous grinding prevents. This aligns with research on deliberate practice and ultradian rhythms.
