The brain operates within a fixed energy limit fueled by ATP. Most of that energy goes to maintaining physical structure by pumping sodium and potassium ions across membranes, leaving very little for actual thought. Listening to one person speak consumes roughly half of available bandwidth. Listening to two simultaneously is impossible.

This has profound implications for how we structure work and attention. Even passive scrolling consumes significant bandwidth despite feeling effortless. The Oscar fiasco where the wrong best picture was announced happened because the presenter took a photo and tweeted at the exact moment he should have been handling the next envelope, flooding his working memory. Attention is not expandable; it can only be allocated more wisely.
