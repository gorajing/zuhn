Hormozi inverts the common self-help framing of 'become the person who does X.' He argues identity is not a prerequisite for behavior but a consequence of it. You don't need to 'become a runner' before running — you run repeatedly and eventually the label 'runner' attaches to you as lagging evidence of accumulated reps.

This directly challenges the identity-first approach popularized by James Clear's Atomic Habits. Hormozi's framing removes the psychological barrier of 'I'm not that kind of person yet' which often serves as a sophisticated procrastination mechanism. The practical implication is immediate: stop waiting to feel like the person who does the thing, and simply do the thing. The identity will catch up. This reframes the 'who you need to become' question from an existential prerequisite into a natural byproduct of consistent action.
