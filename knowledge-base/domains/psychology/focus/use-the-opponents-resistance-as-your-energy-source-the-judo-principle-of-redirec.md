Travis Stevens demonstrates his signature seonagi technique: press the opponent until they resist, and the moment they push back, redirect that energy into the throw. 'I press them until they press back hard enough where we go from this angle to up, and then I can slide.' The key is that the opponent's own force creates the conditions for the throw.

This is a universal principle applicable far beyond martial arts. In negotiations, let the other side commit to a position and use their commitment against them. In product development, let competitors invest in a direction and use their momentum to find the counter-position. In debates, let someone fully commit to an argument before revealing the flaw their own logic created. The principle is: never generate all the energy yourself — create conditions where others generate the energy you redirect.
