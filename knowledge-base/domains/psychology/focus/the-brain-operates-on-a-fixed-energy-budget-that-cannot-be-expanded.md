The brain runs on ATP (adenine triphosphate), and most of its energy goes to maintaining physical structure — pumping ions across membranes. Very little is left for actual thought. This budget is fixed; no intervention can expand it. Listening to one person speak uses roughly half your bandwidth, making simultaneous listening impossible.

This has radical implications for how we design our days. Even passive scrolling consumes cognitive bandwidth because the brain is a change detector — any novel stimulus demands processing. The Oscar Best Picture fiasco (wrong winner announced because the presenter tweeted at a critical moment) illustrates how easily working memory floods. Attention isn't a skill to improve; it's a finite resource to protect.
