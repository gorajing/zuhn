Urban's concept of the Dark Playground captures a specific psychological state: engaging in leisure activities when you know you should be working. The leisure isn't restorative because it's unearned. Instead of relaxation, you experience guilt, dread, anxiety, and self-hatred simultaneously with the 'fun' activity.

This reframes the procrastination problem. The procrastinator isn't actually enjoying themselves — they're in a worse state than if they were doing the hard work. The Dark Playground is a lose-lose zone: no productive output AND no genuine rest. Recognizing this in real-time ('I'm in the Dark Playground right now') can break the spell, because the Monkey's promise of easy fun is revealed as a lie. Either do the work or take genuinely earned rest — the middle ground is toxic.
