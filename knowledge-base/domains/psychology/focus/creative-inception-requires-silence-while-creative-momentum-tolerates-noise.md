Graham distinguishes two modes of thinking: continuing an existing project (editing, debugging, iterating) versus starting something new. The first tolerates ambient noise because the mental scaffolding is already in place — you know what you're doing and just need to execute. The second requires complete quiet because you're building that scaffolding from scratch.

This has practical implications for how knowledge workers structure their environments and schedules. Protecting silence for inception phases matters more than protecting it for execution phases. Many people optimize their environment for average productivity rather than peak creative moments, which means they sacrifice the rarer but higher-value generative thinking sessions.
