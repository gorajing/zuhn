Manson observes we are drowning in options, information, commitments, and obligations — most of which we accepted without deliberation. The reflex is always to add: add a morning routine, add a supplement, add a course. But the highest-leverage move is subtraction.

This aligns with the broader pattern across multiple domains: in personal growth (stop low-frequency inputs before adding practices), in product development (find the smallest AI insertion point), in business (narrow your audience before expanding). The universal principle is that constraint drives clarity, and removal reveals what matters.
