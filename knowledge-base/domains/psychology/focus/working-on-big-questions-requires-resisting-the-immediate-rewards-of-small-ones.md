Graham uses Newton as a personal reproach — a reminder that the biggest obstacle to great work isn't lack of ability but the comfortable gravitational pull of small problems. Small questions are 'pleasantly familiar' and offer immediate rewards, even bigger rewards in your own lifetime. But this is the route to 'well-deserved obscurity.'

The insight is structural: the reward gradient actively pushes talented people away from important work. Paradigm shifts are separated from us by 'what will later seem a surprisingly thin wall of laziness and stupidity.' The wall isn't thick — it's just that the incentives on the near side are so comfortable that few bother to push through. This reframes ambition not as wanting more, but as willingness to forgo the immediate dopamine of solvable problems.
