One of the most counter-intuitive aspects of ADHD is that sufferers can achieve laser-focus on things they find genuinely interesting or exciting—sometimes exceeding the focus capacity of neurotypical people. This hyper-focus exists because dopamine is released naturally when someone encounters stimuli they find rewarding or curious, temporarily normalizing the default-mode/task-network anti-correlation.

This reframes ADHD from 'broken attention' to 'broken attention gating.' The machinery for focus is intact; what's impaired is the ability to voluntarily direct that machinery toward non-preferred tasks. Huberman's personal strategy of deliberately deluding himself into finding boring material fascinating illustrates this—by artificially engaging the curiosity/desire-to-know circuit (likely involving dopamine release), he could bootstrap focus on any subject. This has implications beyond ADHD: for anyone, finding genuine curiosity or reframing tasks as interesting may be more effective than pure discipline for sustaining attention.
