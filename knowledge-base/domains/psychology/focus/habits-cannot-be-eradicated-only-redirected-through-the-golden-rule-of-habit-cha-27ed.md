The habit loop consists of three parts: a cue (trigger), a routine (behavior), and a reward. Once these neurological pathways form, they persist permanently. Willpower-based approaches try to suppress the entire loop, which study after study shows fails.

The golden rule of habit change says: keep the same cue, deliver the same reward, but insert a new routine. This works because you are not fighting the entrenched neurology; you are redirecting it. You also cannot change all three parts at once. The new neural pathways build up gradually over time, and rushing the process is counterproductive.
