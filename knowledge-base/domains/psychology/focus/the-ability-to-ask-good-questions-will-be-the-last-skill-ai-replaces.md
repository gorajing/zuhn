Anandkumar argues that even as AI tools get dramatically better at execution, you still need to tell AI what to do. The ability to describe tasks, define problems, and ask the right questions remains a human skill. She begins classes by asking questions, not writing equations—because the ability to question is more fundamental than the ability to compute.

The practical implication: young people shouldn't fear AI replacing their skills. They should develop the one skill AI can't replace—curiosity that drives them to ask questions no one else is asking. A student who intuitively asks 'how noisy is this sensor? Let me test with candles of different sizes' is demonstrating exactly the kind of practical questioning that AI cannot yet generate from scratch.
