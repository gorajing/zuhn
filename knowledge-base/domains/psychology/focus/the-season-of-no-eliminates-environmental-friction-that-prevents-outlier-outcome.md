Hormozi describes the 'Season of No' as a transitional period where you're not successful enough for the peer group you aspire to, but you've outgrown your current one. The solution is radical elimination: no social gatherings, no entertainment, no drinking, no fantasy football — nothing that decreases the likelihood of hitting your goals. His personal version was working 5am-9am, his day job 9-5, then 5pm-9pm again on his own business — leaving zero unstructured time. The psychological mechanism is environment design through deprivation: by making work the only available activity, motivation becomes unnecessary because there are no competing alternatives. This connects to his broader framework that motivation comes from deprivation (hungry → eat, broke → earn), and that surrounding yourself with people who have what you want creates productive dissatisfaction. The uncomfortable truth is that this makes you 'odd' — and expecting normal behavior while pursuing outlier results is contradictory.
