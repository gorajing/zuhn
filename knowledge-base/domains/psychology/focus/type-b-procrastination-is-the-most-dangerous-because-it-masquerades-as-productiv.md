Paul Graham identifies three types of procrastination: doing nothing (type A), doing less important things (type B), and doing more important things (type C). Type B is the most dangerous because it doesn't feel like procrastination—you're 'getting things done,' just the wrong things. Any productivity system focused on crossing items off a to-do list is actively misleading if the to-do list itself is a form of type-B procrastination.

This maps directly to the startup context: founders who spend all day on email, meetings, and operational errands feel busy but aren't advancing the company. The litmus test is Hamming's question: 'What's the best thing you could be working on, and why aren't you?' Nearly everyone's to-do list is itself a form of type-B procrastination, because it substitutes the feeling of completion for actual progress on what matters.
