Jordan Jonas describes missing a moose with his only arrow on Alone and noticing how quickly he accepted the failure rather than dwelling on it. He attributes this to years of putting himself in uncomfortable situations — from Siberian wilderness to competitive survival — which built a muscle for rapid failure acceptance.

The key insight is that acceptance speed is trainable. When Jonas missed the moose, instead of moping, he immediately began building a fence and funnel system to create another opportunity. The faster you process failure emotionally, the sooner you can redirect energy toward adaptive action. This applies directly to startup execution, creative work, and any domain where iteration speed determines outcomes.
