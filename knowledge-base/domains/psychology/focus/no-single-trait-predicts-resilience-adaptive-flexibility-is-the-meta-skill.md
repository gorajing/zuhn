Bonanno's research program reveals a paradox: over 100 studies confirm that most people are resilient after trauma, and many individual factors (positive thinking, problem-solving, social support) correlate with resilience. But each factor has only small effects — none strongly predicts who will actually be resilient in the next adverse event.

The resolution is that situations vary enormously, and every coping strategy has both costs and benefits. Even reliable strategies (mindfulness, social support) only work sometimes. What predicts resilience is not any fixed trait but adaptive flexibility — the ability to assess the current situation, select an appropriate strategy from your repertoire, monitor whether it is working, and switch strategies if it is not. This is a meta-skill rather than any particular coping behavior.
