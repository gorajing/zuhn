Lucas's Thursday stumble crystallized this insight: after a morning of focused work, he opened his laptop to order something for a thumbnail and ended up checking email, YouTube stats, and responding to a business deal. Twenty minutes of 'quick checks' destroyed his focus for the rest of the day. The thoughts didn't go away — they competed with his main work for the rest of the afternoon.

His mental model: once you open a door, it's open. You can't close it again. Every input you allow — email, podcast, social media, news — takes a permanent slice of bandwidth. So the strategy is delay: the longer you can go without letting extra stuff in, the more present you can be for everything before that. He also recommended pen and paper over screens whenever possible, since screens are input hubs (notifications, colors, icons) while paper has zero inputs. Even just using a $1 mini composition book for notes instead of a phone preserves mental clarity.
