Over 100 research studies have confirmed the resilience trajectory — most people recover well from adverse events. But the resilience paradox is that no single trait strongly predicts who will be resilient. Positive thinking, problem-solving, distraction — each correlates with resilience but only weakly predicts it for any individual in any specific future situation.

The reason: situations vary enormously, and every strategy has costs and benefits that shift by context. Even your best coping mechanisms fail sometimes. This means resilience is not a fixed trait but an outcome of a process — adaptive flexibility. The ability to assess the current situation, choose from a repertoire of strategies, monitor feedback, and switch approaches when something isn't working. This is fundamentally learnable.
