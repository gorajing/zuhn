Graham points out that spending a morning on the sofa watching TV would immediately register as a danger sign—like drinking alone. But spending the same morning at your desk cycling between news, email, and web browsing feels productive because the posture, location, and tool are identical to actual work.

This camouflage effect means internet distraction can persist for months or years before you notice the pattern. Graham himself describes gradually losing mornings to email-news-email loops without realizing it because the problem was intermittent—like a software bug that only appears sometimes. The insight suggests that any productivity system must account for distractions that are disguised as work, not just obvious time-wasters.
