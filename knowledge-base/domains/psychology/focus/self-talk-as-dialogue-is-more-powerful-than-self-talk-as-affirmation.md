Most positive self-talk research focuses on declarative statements: 'I'll get through this,' 'I can cope.' These map to the flexibility mindset and serve a motivational purpose. But Bonanno identifies a more powerful mode: interrogative self-talk that walks through the flexibility sequence.

For context sensitivity: 'What's happening now? What do I need to do?' For repertoire: 'What tools do I have in my toolbox?' For feedback: 'Is this working? Should I try something else?' This shifts self-talk from monologue to dialogue — almost like having a coach in the room asking questions. The distinction matters because questions engage active problem-solving while affirmations only address motivation. Combining both modes covers the full flexibility system.
