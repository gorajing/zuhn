The freeCodeCamp tutorial structures its 4.5 hours as a series of mini-projects that each introduce 2-3 new concepts within a working application. A mad libs game teaches string concatenation and input, a guessing game teaches while loops and conditionals, a translator teaches dictionaries and functions. Each project is completable in minutes, providing frequent dopamine hits of accomplishment.

This maps to research on situated cognition — knowledge encoded in context of use is more retrievable than abstract knowledge. It also leverages the Zeigarnik effect: starting a project creates an open loop that motivates completion. The implication extends beyond programming to any skill acquisition: structure learning as a sequence of progressively complex projects rather than a concept taxonomy followed by exercises.
