Anandkumar observes that students with wrong intuitions often learn faster than students with no intuitions. The wrong intuition provides a framework to correct, while starting from nothing requires building the entire mental model from scratch.

This has implications for AI collaboration: when working with AI, having an imperfect hypothesis about what you want is much more productive than having no idea at all. You can use AI to test and refine your wrong intuition far faster than you can use it to generate a hypothesis from scratch. The lesson: don't wait until you have the right answer to start—start with your best wrong guess and iterate.
