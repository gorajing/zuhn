The fundamental flaw with screen time limits is that they assume the harm is proportional to minutes of use. In reality, even brief sessions on social media trigger dopamine spikes followed by dopamine deficits that drive persistent craving and mental preoccupation throughout the day.

Children carry the virtual world with them long after closing the app — wondering who liked their post, what followers they gained, what conversations they are missing. This means a 30-minute time limit can produce all-day psychological impact. The addictive design intentionally creates this persistent engagement, making moderation strategies structurally inadequate against engineered compulsion.
