Hormozi draws on Jerry Seinfeld's writing process — locking himself in a windowless room with only a notepad and pen, where he doesn't have to write but can't do anything else. Writing (or any deep work) occurs as a default behavior. The implication is that most people fail at focus not because they lack willpower but because they haven't designed their environment to make distraction impossible.

Three specific tactics: switch phone to grayscale (reduces consumption ~30%), turn off all push notifications so you pull information on your schedule, and use a 'no one can reach me' mode. The timer technique — estimating how long a task should take, running a physical timer, and pausing it whenever you're distracted — reveals the gap between perceived and actual working time. This audit alone often shocks people into recognizing how little focused work they actually do.
