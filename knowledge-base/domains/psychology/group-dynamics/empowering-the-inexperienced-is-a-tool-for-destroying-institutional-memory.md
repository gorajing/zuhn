Paine describes the mechanics of the Red Guards vividly: Mao told teenagers educated on Mao-worship that they were now in charge. With no life experience and no investment in the existing order, they attacked the educational system from the bottom up, starting with teachers they personally resented. The result was ruinous for production and gutted the educational system — every teacher who had 'given hard grades' and 'may have actually known something' was gone.

The mechanism generalizes beyond communism. Any leader who wants to bypass an entrenched expert class can do it by deputizing people with no investment in the existing institutional order. The inexperienced are easier to manipulate because they lack the context to recognize when they're being used, and the psychological payoff of sudden authority is intoxicating.

This is a lens for reading any revolutionary moment — whether in politics, corporate restructuring, or online mob dynamics. When you see experienced people being displaced by newly empowered amateurs, ask who benefits from the destruction of institutional memory.
