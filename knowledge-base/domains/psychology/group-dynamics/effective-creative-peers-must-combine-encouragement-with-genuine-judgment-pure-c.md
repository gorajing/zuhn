Graham makes a subtle distinction that most advice about 'surrounding yourself with supportive people' misses. Pure encouragement becomes noise — you habituate to it and discount it. What you actually need are people with the judgment to distinguish genuinely promising early work from truly bad work, and the most reliable source of such judgment is people attempting similar ambitious projects themselves.

This is why university departments and research labs produce disproportionate creative output: they naturally concentrate people with both the domain expertise to evaluate early work and the personal investment in similar projects that makes their encouragement credible. The practical implication is that when building your creative environment, optimize for the quality of judgment in your peers, not the quantity of encouragement. One colleague who can accurately see promise in your rough draft is worth ten who tell you everything is great.
