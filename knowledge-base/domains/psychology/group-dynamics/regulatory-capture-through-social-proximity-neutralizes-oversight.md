Harry Markopolos submitted detailed mathematical proof of fraud to the SEC five times over nearly a decade. The SEC opened and closed an investigation in 11 months, finding insufficient evidence — while Madoff was simultaneously serving as an advisor to the commission. This isn't mere incompetence; it's structural. When the regulated entity is embedded in the regulator's social graph — providing advice, sharing professional networks, occupying prestigious positions — the cognitive cost of accusing them becomes prohibitively high. The implication for any domain with oversight (corporate boards, peer review, industry self-regulation) is that proximity between watchdog and watched is itself a risk factor. Effective oversight requires genuine social distance between the overseer and the overseen.
