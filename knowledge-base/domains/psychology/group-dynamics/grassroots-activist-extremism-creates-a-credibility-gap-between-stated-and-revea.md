Smith highlights the sanctuary city paradox: Democrats claim they support deporting criminal illegal immigrants, but progressive activists in blue cities systematically obstruct the federal programs (like Secure Communities) needed to accomplish this. Voters observe this gap between stated and revealed preferences and rationally conclude that Democrats don't actually mean what they say about enforcement.

This is a fundamental organizational credibility problem. In any organization — political party, company, movement — if the base or frontline workers visibly contradict leadership's stated positions, external stakeholders will discount the stated positions entirely. They'll infer the organization's true preferences from its members' actions, not its leaders' words. This is why companies that claim to value customer service but don't empower support staff lose credibility, and why startups that claim to move fast but have layers of approval processes are seen as bureaucratic regardless of their messaging.
