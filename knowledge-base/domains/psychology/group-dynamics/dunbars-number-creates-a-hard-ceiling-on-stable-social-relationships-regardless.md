Dunbar's Number estimates the maximum number of people with whom one can maintain stable social relationships — typically 100-230 — based on neocortex size. The video contextualizes this powerfully: a YouTube video with just 230 views has already been seen by more people than any individual can truly know at one time. At 100,000 views, it surpasses the total number of people you'll ever meet in a lifetime (meeting 2-3 new people per day for ~78 years).

This has direct implications for community building, team management, and platform design. Communities that exceed Dunbar's Number fragment into sub-groups whether intentionally designed that way or not. Social media creates an illusion of vast networks, but the cognitive bandwidth for genuine reciprocal relationships hasn't scaled. Leaders managing organizations beyond 150 people are managing structure and culture, not relationships — a fundamentally different skill set.
