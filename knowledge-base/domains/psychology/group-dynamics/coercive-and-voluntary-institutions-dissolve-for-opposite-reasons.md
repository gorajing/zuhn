Paine flags this as one of Gorbachev's most revealing strategic mistakes: he genuinely believed the two blocs were symmetric, so unilateral de-escalation on his side should trigger mirroring on the Western side. Instead, the moment Soviet coercion lifted, Eastern Europe fled toward the West as fast and as far as possible (her quote: 'all our former satellites by compulsion cast off from us as fast and as far as possible'), while Western Europe voluntarily tightened its bonds via the Maastricht Treaty and NATO expansion.

The underlying principle is that the cohesion of an institution depends entirely on whether members are there by choice or by force. Coercive institutions — empires, forced marriages, non-compete-enforced employee bases, customer bases locked in by switching costs — have zero persistence once the coercion ends. Voluntary institutions — free trade blocs, chosen communities, customers who stay because they actually prefer the product — persist and even compound without continued enforcement effort.

The practical test for any leader, manager, or founder: if I removed the enforcement mechanism tomorrow, how many of my members/customers/allies would still be here next month? The answer is the real strength of the institution. Everything above that is borrowed time.
