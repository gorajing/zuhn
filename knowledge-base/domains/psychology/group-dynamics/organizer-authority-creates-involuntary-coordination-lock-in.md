Scott Alexander identifies a subtle group dynamics problem: once you become the meetup organizer, people respect you, coordinate around you, and avoid taking initiative to avoid stepping on your toes. This means passive handoff (just stopping) kills the group, and quiet offers don't work because people won't volunteer unprompted.

The prescribed solution — literally banging something loudly and publicly asking 'anyone want to become a regular organizer?' — works because it breaks the deference pattern through an explicit public transfer of permission. This is a generalizable insight about leadership transitions in informal groups: the incumbent must create a visible, unambiguous moment where initiative-taking is not just permitted but requested. Without this ceremony, the group's coordination equilibrium locks around the original organizer even after they disengage.
