NFX observes that many LatAm founders view startup building as a form of public service — a way to transform the region's socio-economic landscape. This shared noble vision creates collaboration patterns that go beyond typical founder networking: founders actively help each other, operators cross-pollinate between companies, and investors participate in ecosystem building rather than just deal-making. The mechanism is that a shared mission lowers the coordination cost of network formation. When everyone believes they're building something bigger than their individual company, information sharing increases, talent circulation accelerates, and the ecosystem develops faster than economic incentives alone would predict. This mirrors how Silicon Valley's early culture of openness and mutual support (before it became purely transactional) catalyzed its initial growth.
