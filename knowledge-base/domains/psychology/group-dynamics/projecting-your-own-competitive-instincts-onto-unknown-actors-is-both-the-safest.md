The video explicitly acknowledges this tension: the Dark Forest logic may be a product of our 'primitive brain that evolved in the context of gruesome competition' rather than an accurate model of the universe. This is a profound meta-insight — the framework we use to evaluate threats is itself shaped by the competitive pressures that made us threatening. In group dynamics and negotiations, the same pattern appears: parties who assume hostility create defensive postures that provoke the very hostility they feared (security dilemma). The Kurzgesagt framing suggests that maturity — whether for a species or an individual — involves recognizing when your threat model is a projection of your own nature rather than evidence about the other. The practical tension is real: lowering your guard enables cooperation but also enables exploitation, and there is no risk-free way to distinguish which situation you're in.
