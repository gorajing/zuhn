Kurzgesagt's framing of social distancing is notable: 'stay at home to protect those who need to be out — from doctors to cashiers. You depend on all of them; they all depend on you.' This reframes sacrifice from abstract altruism to concrete reciprocity. You're not just 'doing the right thing' — you're holding up your end of a mutual insurance pact.

This framing pattern applies to any collective action problem: open-source contribution, paying taxes, maintaining code quality in shared codebases, or even team norms. Abstract moral appeals ('do it because it's right') consistently underperform reciprocal framing ('you need them to do this, they need you to do this'). The key psychological lever is making the interdependence visible and personal, not theoretical.
