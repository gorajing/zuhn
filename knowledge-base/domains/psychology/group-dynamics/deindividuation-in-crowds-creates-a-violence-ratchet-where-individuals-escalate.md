When people enter a crowd with a shared identity and goal, they undergo deindividuation — they stop thinking as individuals and begin acting as part of a collective. This creates a violence ratchet: individuals don't escalate to the average behavior of the crowd, they escalate to match the leaders and most aggressive actors. The mechanism is a wordless peer pressure combined with reduced sense of personal consequence, since police make far fewer arrests proportional to offenders during mass events.

This has direct implications for any system involving group dynamics — from online communities to organizational culture. The implication is that the behavioral ceiling of a group is set by its most extreme tolerated members, not by its median. Moderation strategy should therefore focus on the most visible escalators rather than trying to shift average behavior.
