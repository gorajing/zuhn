Graham argues that audiences function as incipient mobs. The reactions that spread person-to-person in a crowd are disproportionately the more visceral kind — he uses the analogy of low notes traveling through walls better than high ones. Good speakers exploit this dynamic, using emotional momentum to sweep audiences along.

This effect breaks down below roughly 10 people, where the setting still feels conversational. But beyond that threshold, the group dynamic takes over. The practical implication is that talks are structurally better for motivation and emotional persuasion than for idea transmission. Anyone designing a communication strategy should match the medium to the goal: writing for ideas, small groups for discussion, large talks for motivation.
