Smith identifies a repeating pattern in progressive-led cities: liberals build functional public goods (libraries, parks, transit, walkable neighborhoods), then progressives demand those institutions absorb functions they weren't designed for — libraries become homeless shelters, transit becomes unsafe, parks become open drug markets. Portland exemplifies this: it went from a celebrated liberal city to having the second-highest crime rate in America, with 40% of fires caused by unchecked vagrancy.

The key insight is that this is a parasitic relationship, not mere mismanagement. Progressive ideology specifically demands that public institutions tolerate behaviors that degrade them — open drug use, theft under $950, defunding police. Each demand is framed as compassion, making it politically difficult for liberals to refuse. The host institutions slowly die as the moderate citizens who fund and use them withdraw. Crucially, 'liberalism seems to have evolved no defense against this' — the tolerance that defines liberalism makes it structurally vulnerable to being exploited by its own radical flank.
