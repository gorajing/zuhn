Wendover makes the striking point that COG plans are ultimately about preserving democratic principles — separation of powers, judicial review, popular sovereignty — not about saving any particular person. A survivor who decides they're in 'the Kingdom of California' is, for practical purposes, no longer in the United States. This maps broadly: companies, communities, and open-source projects persist only as long as participants believe in the shared identity. When a startup loses its founding narrative — through a pivot, acquisition, or leadership change — it can retain all its structural assets yet lose the thing that made people show up. Legitimacy is the ultimate non-fungible asset.
