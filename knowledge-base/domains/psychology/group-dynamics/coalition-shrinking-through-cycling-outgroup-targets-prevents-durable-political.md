Smith describes MAGA as a 'roving Eye of Sauron' that cycles through racial targets — Black Americans, Jewish Americans, Hispanics, Indian immigrants, East Asians — to keep base engagement high. Leaders stir up fear, then back off when pushback mounts, but must find new targets to maintain the emotional energy. Each cycle permanently alienates a group that might otherwise have been recruited.

This is a structural problem, not a leadership failure. Any movement that derives energy primarily from outgroup fear faces this dynamic: the fear must be refreshed with new targets because sustained focus on one group triggers organized defensive responses. But cycling through targets means every group eventually gets attacked. In a diverse democracy, this arithmetic is fatal — there simply aren't enough members of any single demographic to form a durable majority. Trump's 2024 coalition (including meaningful Black and Hispanic gains) represented coalition-building, but the movement's core incentive structure works against maintaining those gains.
