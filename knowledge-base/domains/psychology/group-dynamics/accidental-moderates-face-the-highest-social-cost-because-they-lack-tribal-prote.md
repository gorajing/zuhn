The common framing of moderates as cowardly fence-sitters inverts reality for accidental moderates. Ideologues on either extreme enjoy the psychological comfort and social protection of belonging to a committed group. Intentional moderates may indeed be cowardly — choosing the safe middle to avoid controversy. But accidental moderates, who arrive at their positions through independent reasoning, get attacked from both directions.

This dynamic creates a perverse incentive structure: the most intellectually honest position is the most socially punished one. It explains why many independent thinkers either self-censor or cluster into small communities of other independent thinkers rather than engaging broadly. Graham's note that 'having heretical opinions doesn't mean you have to express them openly' acknowledges this cost while implicitly arguing the thinking itself matters more than the signaling.
