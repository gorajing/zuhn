Unconditional encouragement is counterproductive because you quickly learn to discount it. What you need are colleagues who are themselves working on ambitious projects and can therefore distinguish genuine promise from genuine failure in early-stage work. This is why university departments and research labs are so effective — they naturally collect people with the judgment to evaluate early work accurately.

This kind of environment doesn't require institutions. Ambitious people naturally coalesce given the chance, but actively seeking out others attempting new things dramatically accelerates the process. The key selection criterion is not optimism but discernment — people whose encouragement you can actually trust because they understand the domain.
