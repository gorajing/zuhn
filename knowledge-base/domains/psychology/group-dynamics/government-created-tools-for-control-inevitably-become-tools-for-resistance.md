The US Naval Research Laboratory created Tor to protect intelligence communications, and DARPA further developed it. The deliberate decision to release it publicly — reasoning that more users would provide better cover for intelligence officers — created the infrastructure for the entire dark web ecosystem. This pattern recurs throughout history: GPS was military technology that became consumer navigation, the internet itself was a defense project. The paradox deepens when you consider the government still partially funds Tor while simultaneously prosecuting dark web criminals. This reveals a fundamental tension in state-sponsored technology: the utility of a tool for its intended purpose often requires public access, which guarantees unintended uses.
