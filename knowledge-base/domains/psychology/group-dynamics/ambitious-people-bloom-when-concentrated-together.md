Graham argues that ambition is the most malleable component of determination, and that most people are 'practically malnourished' when it comes to ambition because they've never been around sufficiently ambitious peers. The nuclear reactor analogy is apt: ambitious people's energy gets absorbed by normal social environments but amplifies when reflected by other ambitious people.

This has design implications at every scale. Individually, it means deliberately seeking ambitious peer groups is one of the highest-leverage environment changes you can make. Institutionally, it means concentrating talent (elite universities, YC batches, research labs) creates non-linear returns. Graham explicitly criticizes European policies that level universities to equality, arguing this disperses the catalytic effect of ambitious peer concentration.
