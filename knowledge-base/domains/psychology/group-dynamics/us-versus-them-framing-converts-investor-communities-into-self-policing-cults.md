OneCoin investors joined 'OneLife', used secret hand symbols, participated in WhatsApp groups that dismissed critics as 'haters', and framed regulatory action as the establishment attacking a revolution. Many continued investing even after the FBI indictment and the founder's disappearance — reportedly $1 million per week still flowed in. The community had transitioned from an investment vehicle to an identity group, where questioning OneCoin meant questioning one's own judgment, community, and identity.

This is a general pattern: any investment community that develops identity markers (hand signals, insider language), information isolation (private groups, dismissing outside sources), and persecution narratives ('they want to shut us down because we threaten the system') has crossed from investment into cult territory. The key diagnostic is whether members can articulate a falsification condition — what evidence would convince them they were wrong? If no such condition exists, the community is operating on faith, not investment thesis.
