When organizations face criticism for misconduct but experience no actual consequences, the rational institutional response is escalation rather than reform. The ICE pattern illustrates this clearly: after the killing of Renee Good drew widespread criticism, agents became more aggressive rather than more cautious — one told a protester 'Have y'all not learned from the past couple days?' and another kicked over memorial candles.

This pattern transfers to any organization with power asymmetry and weak accountability mechanisms. The key insight is that criticism without consequences actually emboldens bad actors by proving the system won't enforce limits. For founders building organizations, this means accountability mechanisms must have teeth from day one — cultural norms and public disapproval alone cannot constrain behavior when power is concentrated.
