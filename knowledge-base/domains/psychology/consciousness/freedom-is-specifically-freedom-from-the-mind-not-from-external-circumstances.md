Kapil Gupta argues that the only reason any human being is not free is because of his mind — it creates anxieties, conflicts, rules, and tortures. Naval confirms this from personal experience: after achieving material freedom (wealth, travel, relationships), his baseline level of peace barely changed because his internal experience remained the same. Each desire fulfilled simply spawned the next desire.

This reframes the entire self-improvement project. Rather than optimizing external circumstances (which produces diminishing returns past basic needs), the leverage point is understanding how the mind generates suffering. The distinction matters because circumstance-changing is an infinite treadmill — the mind will always manufacture new problems from new circumstances — while understanding the mind's mechanics can produce permanent resolution.
