Donald Hoffman's user interface theory describes perception as an evolutionary design meant for survival rather than accuracy. Your senses do not reveal reality — they hide it behind icons useful enough to keep you alive. The brain processes billions of signals per second, sorting chaos into order and randomness into patterns, translating everything into a 3D hallucination that is just consistent enough to work.

This is supported by sensory evidence: some animals see infrared, bats use echolocation, synesthetes see colors when they hear music. Even within human experience, time stretches or shrinks depending on attention, the mind fills gaps and fabricates continuity. Waking life is not the opposite of dreaming — it is simply the most persistent dream, reinforced by collective trust in shared language, memory, and physical laws.
