Before internal representation, an organism can only pursue what it currently perceives. Once it can maintain a model of something absent — food that moved out of sight, a predator that was seen earlier — it gains memory, object permanence, and the ability to sustain goals through distractions. Human babies develop object permanence around 8 months; chickens within days of hatching.

This capability is the gateway to time perception, delayed gratification, and eventually theory of mind. It's also directly relevant to AI system design: current LLMs operate largely without persistent internal representations between contexts, which may explain why they struggle with sustained goal pursuit and long-horizon planning despite impressive language capabilities.
