The Egg's central conceit — you are every human who ever lived — functions as an extreme empathy device. Most empathy frameworks ask you to imagine being in someone else's shoes, which preserves the self/other boundary. The Egg eliminates that boundary entirely: harming another person is harming a past or future version of yourself. This reframe is psychologically powerful because self-interest is the most reliable human motivator, and routing altruism through self-interest removes the friction of charitable reasoning. The insight applies beyond philosophy — in negotiation, conflict resolution, and leadership, the ability to genuinely internalize that the other party's experience is as real and consequential as your own changes decision-making from zero-sum to positive-sum.
