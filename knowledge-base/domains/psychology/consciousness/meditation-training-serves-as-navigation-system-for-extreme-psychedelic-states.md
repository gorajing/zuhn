Sam Harris describes how his extensive meditation training helped him during a 5-gram psilocybin trip by enabling him to surrender to the experience through cutting through the sense of self. However, he emphasizes that at peak intensity, there was no recollection that mindfulness was even an option — the experience completely overwhelms all intentional practice.

This maps to research showing psilocybin decreases activity in the default mode network (associated with self-referential thinking), which is the same network meditation targets. A recent study combining a 5-day silent retreat with psilocybin found the mindfulness group showed dramatically better outcomes at 4-month follow-up on measures of life appreciation, self-acceptance, and reduced death anxiety — while also minimizing negative experiences. The implication is that meditation and psychedelics are synergistic tools operating on the same neural substrate, but at vastly different intensities — Harris compares meditation to discovering fire while 5 grams of mushrooms is being hurled into the sun.
