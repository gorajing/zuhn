Jung's shadow is commonly understood as the 'dark side' - repressed anger, selfishness, sexuality. But the shadow equally contains repressed positive qualities: ambition suppressed because it feels arrogant, creativity suppressed because it feels impractical, assertiveness suppressed because it feels aggressive.

The person who prides themselves on being 'nice' has often suppressed healthy assertiveness into their shadow. The person who prides themselves on being 'practical' has suppressed creative capacity. Shadow integration means reclaiming all of these - both the difficult and the powerful - rather than maintaining a sanitized self-image that requires constant energy to sustain.
