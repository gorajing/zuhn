The drive to explain everything treats mystery as a deficiency to be corrected. But genuine engagement with irreducible mysteries (why anything exists, what consciousness is) produces a quality of experience - awe, wonder, humility - that explanation would eliminate rather than enhance.

This isn't an argument against science or inquiry but a recognition that some questions have more value as questions than they would have as answers. The sense of wonder at existence is itself a meaningful experience that complete explanation would replace with mere information.
