Al-Khalili explains that the present moment has a measurable 'thickness' in human experience. There is up to a third of a second of perceptual latency between an event occurring and our conscious awareness of it. Beyond that, we experience music and conversation as a continuum by stitching together episodic memories of the recent past with anticipation of the near future.

This has practical implications for interface design, music production, and any system that relies on human perception of simultaneity. What we call 'now' is really a constructed window that blends past memory, current sensation, and future anticipation — closer to a rolling buffer than a point in time. Understanding this can improve how we design real-time feedback systems and creative workflows.
