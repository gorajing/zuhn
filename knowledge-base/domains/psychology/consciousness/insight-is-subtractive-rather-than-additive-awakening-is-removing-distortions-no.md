Hughes's sharpest point: 'awakening is not adding new ideas, it is removing noise.' The contemplative traditions that converge on this framing (Upanishads, Zen, Christian apophatic mysticism, Dzogchen) all treat insight as via negativa — stripping away the accumulated filters until what is left is what was already there. This matters practically because nearly every commercial spirituality product operates by the opposite logic: here is another book, another course, another teacher, another practice stack to add. If the theory is correct, the additive approach is actually blocking what it claims to deliver. The test is whether a given practice produces more inner clarity and fewer assumed beliefs, or more.
