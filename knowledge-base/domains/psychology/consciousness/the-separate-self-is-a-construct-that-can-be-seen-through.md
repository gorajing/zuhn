Siddhartha saw the self as 'not solid at all.' Lao Tzu described the softening of the separate will. Jesus spoke of losing your life to find it. Rumi dissolved the lover into the beloved. Ramana asked 'Who am I?' and found no searcher. The claim isn't that you have no experience — it's that the thing which seems to own experience is itself a construction. This matters practically: if the boundary of self is constructed, much of what it defends (reputation, identity, continuity) rests on something that can be questioned. The felt solidity of 'me' is not evidence of its metaphysical solidity, just as the felt flatness of the earth was not evidence of its shape.
