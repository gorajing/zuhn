Analytical thinking has diminishing returns that can become negative. Early reflection produces genuine insight. Continued deeper analysis reaches a point where additional thought generates more questions than answers, more uncertainty than clarity, and more anxiety than peace.

This isn't anti-intellectual - it's recognition that some domains (meaning, identity, purpose) resist analytical resolution. The person who keeps digging for a definitive analytical answer to 'what is the meaning of life?' will find only more questions. At some point, the appropriate move is to stop analyzing and start living - to let action replace analysis.
