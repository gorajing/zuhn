Al-Khalili subscribes to compatibilism: the philosophical position that free will and determinism are not mutually exclusive. His argument is pragmatic — even in Einstein's block universe where all of time already exists, we are embedded within the system and can never predict the future in advance. Since we cannot access deterministic outcomes before they occur, our experience of making choices is functionally real.

This framework is useful for anyone grappling with AI determinism, decision fatigue, or the tension between data-driven prediction and human agency. It suggests that even in highly predictable systems, the inability of agents to see their own future states preserves meaningful choice. The insight applies directly to AI alignment: a deterministic agent can still be said to 'choose' if it cannot compute its own future states.
