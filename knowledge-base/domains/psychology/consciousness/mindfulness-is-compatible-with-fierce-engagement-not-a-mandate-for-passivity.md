Harris directly addresses critics who tell him a mindfulness teacher shouldn't express strong political opinions. He argues this conflates a 'style of communication — an anodyne religious or new agey communication and a pseudo ethic around being as inoffensive as possible' with the actual goal of spiritual life. He can be nondualistically mindful while telling someone to take their head out of their ass.

The deeper point is that genuine meditative insight — recognizing the nature of mind prior to concepts, cutting through the illusion of self — has no necessary connection to passivity or conflict avoidance. Harris notes he studied with extraordinary meditation masters who 'didn't know a damn thing about most of what I talk about on this podcast,' and it's good they weren't in charge of cyber defense. Meditation that makes you unable to take civilizational problems seriously means you're 'managing your own stress well, but you're no good to us.' What's needed are people who understand both their own minds and the world.
