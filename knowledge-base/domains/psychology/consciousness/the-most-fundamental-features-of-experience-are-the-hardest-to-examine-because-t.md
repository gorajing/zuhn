The most fundamental aspects of experience (that we are conscious, that things appear a certain way, that time seems to flow) are nearly invisible because they form the constant background of all experience. We notice changes and differences, not constants. Consciousness is the ultimate constant.

Philosophers call this the 'phenomenological background' - the structures of experience that are so ever-present they become invisible. Making these visible through deliberate philosophical attention produces the 'weird' feeling described in the video because you're noticing something that was always there but never attended to.
