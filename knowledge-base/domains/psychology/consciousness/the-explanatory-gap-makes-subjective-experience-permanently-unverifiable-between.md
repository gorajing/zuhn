The explanatory gap describes our inability to bridge objective physical descriptions and subjective conscious experience. You can teach an entity every cell, pathway, and chemical involved in pain perception, and it could pass a biology exam on the subject, yet still never actually feel pain. This has profound implications for how we evaluate claims about consciousness in any system — biological or artificial. It means behavioral tests and knowledge tests are categorically insufficient to verify inner experience, which is why the 'hard problem of consciousness' remains unsolved despite centuries of neuroscience progress.

This matters practically when evaluating AI consciousness claims: passing tests about experience is fundamentally different from having experience. Any system that demonstrates knowledge about qualia without necessarily possessing it exposes this same gap.
