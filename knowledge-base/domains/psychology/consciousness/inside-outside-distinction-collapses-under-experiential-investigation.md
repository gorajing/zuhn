Harris demonstrates this through a simple exercise: open your eyes and notice that the visual world 'appears in consciousness' in exactly the same way thoughts do with eyes closed. The inside/outside boundary — the feeling that the world is 'out there' and you are 'in here' — is a conceptual construction, not something found in raw experience. This has practical relevance for anyone building mental models of reality: the subject-object split we take as fundamental is actually a learned abstraction. Understanding this can reduce the grip of anxious self-referential thinking, since the 'self inside the head looking out' is itself just another appearance in the field of experience.
