Johnson's description of 5-MeO-DMT — 'you experience raw consciousness and raw intelligence,' 'release all attachment, all preconditions, all want, all desire, release self, ego, control' — is phenomenologically rich but metaphysically overloaded. The charitable reading is to separate two claims: (1) the experiential claim that subjective awareness can persist while the self-model and agentic control system are temporarily offline, and (2) the interpretive claim that what remains is 'raw reality.'

The first claim is falsifiable and interesting — it constrains theories of consciousness by showing that self-model and awareness are dissociable. The second claim is not falsifiable and almost certainly wrong: the state is still being produced by a brain on a specific serotonin agonist. Treat these reports as data about perceptual architecture, not about cosmology. The user-facing implication: when someone reports a psychedelic 'revelation' about reality, ask what it would predict that could be tested.
