Comfortable ideas confirm existing beliefs and self-concept. Transformative ideas threaten them. This creates a systematic bias against the most valuable insights: the mind's defense mechanisms resist ideas that would require fundamental self-revision.

The practical heuristic: intellectual discomfort is information. Ideas that provoke defensive reactions, dismissal, or anxiety are more likely to contain something genuinely new and potentially valuable than ideas that slide in comfortably. The resistance itself is a signal pointing toward growth.
