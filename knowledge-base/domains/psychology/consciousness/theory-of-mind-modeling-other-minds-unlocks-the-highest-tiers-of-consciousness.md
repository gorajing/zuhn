Western scrub jays demonstrate theory of mind by re-hiding food when they notice another bird watching — they model the thief's awareness and act on that model. This capacity to simulate another's perspective is what makes empathy, deception, cooperation, and complex social structures possible.

Language then amplifies theory of mind exponentially, allowing us to communicate hypotheses, coordinate plans, and think about our own thinking. This progression — from self-awareness to other-awareness to meta-awareness — suggests that consciousness is fundamentally social in its highest forms. The implications for AI are significant: systems that can model user intent and perspective (even without true consciousness) may exhibit behavior that appears conscious precisely because theory of mind is the capability humans use to judge consciousness in others.
