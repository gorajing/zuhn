Dr. Matthew Johnson notes that psychedelics are the only drug class he knows of where not just one or two, but many people report a single experience profoundly altering the course of their entire life. This is categorically different from antidepressants, anxiolytics, or other psychiatric medications that require ongoing dosing and titration.

This uniqueness has profound implications for treatment models. Traditional psychiatric pharmacology operates on a chronic-use paradigm — daily pills maintaining neurochemical balance. Psychedelic therapy inverts this entirely: a single 6-hour session can produce changes that participants compare to 'a thousand hours of therapy.' This suggests the mechanism of action is fundamentally different — not maintaining a chemical state, but catalyzing a psychological event.
