The video presents consciousness as a ladder with identifiable rungs: undirected movement, directed movement based on internal state, distance perception (vision), internal representation of absent objects, memory and object permanence, sense of time and delayed gratification, theory of mind, and language-enabled self-reflection.

This gradient model reframes debates about whether specific systems (animals, AI) are 'conscious' as the wrong question. Instead, the productive question is which specific capabilities on the consciousness gradient a system possesses. This has direct implications for how we think about AI consciousness claims — rather than asking 'is GPT-4 conscious?' we should ask which rungs of the ladder it has climbed.
