The meditation follows a via negativa structure: start with breath, expand to body, notice sounds, then systematically drop each anchor until you're left with awareness itself. This subtractive approach — finding truth by removing what is false — is more reliable than additive approaches that pile on concepts, models, or frameworks.

This principle transfers directly to debugging, decision-making, and product design. When a system is confusing, removing components until clarity emerges (subtractive debugging) often works faster than adding monitoring or logging. In product design, the question 'what can we remove?' frequently produces better results than 'what should we add?' Charlie Munger's inversion principle and via negativa in Nassim Taleb's work operate on the same logic.
