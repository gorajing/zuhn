The standard assumption is that consciousness emerges from complex processing in brains. But Annaka Harris argues this assumption may be wrong — consciousness could be more like gravity, a basic feature of the universe that affects everything rather than arising only in special configurations of matter.

Evidence from plant behavior supports taking this seriously: pea seedlings navigate Y-mazes toward water by sensing moisture and sound, dodder vines detect light wavelengths through leaves to choose optimal hosts. These behaviors involve sophisticated information processing without brains. If consciousness is more basic than we assume, felt experience may accompany processing in many more systems than neuroscience currently acknowledges.
