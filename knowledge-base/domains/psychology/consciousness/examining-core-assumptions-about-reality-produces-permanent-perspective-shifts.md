Philosophical inquiry into fundamental assumptions (Is the self real? Is free will an illusion? Is consciousness produced by the brain?) produces perspective shifts that are irreversible in the sense that you cannot un-know the question even if you reject the answer.

These shifts are disorienting but valuable: they reveal that much of what feels like bedrock reality is actually assumed framework. Once the framework is visible as a framework rather than invisible as 'just how things are,' you gain the ability to choose which frameworks serve you rather than unconsciously inheriting them.
