Laplace's demon is the thought experiment that if you knew every particle's position and velocity in the universe, you could predict the entire future. Classical mechanics implies this level of determinism. But Carroll argues this is a thought experiment, not a practical reality — the memory required to know all your brain's molecules would exceed your brain's capacity.

Compatibilism resolves the tension: at the microscopic level, physics may be deterministic. But since you cannot know the microstates, the best model of human behavior treats people as agents making choices. This is not a comforting illusion — it is the most accurate description available given our necessarily incomplete information. Everyone already does this, and it is the right thing to do.
