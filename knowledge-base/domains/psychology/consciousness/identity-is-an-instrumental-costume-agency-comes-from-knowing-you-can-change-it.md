Bach describes stage 5 (self-authoring) as the realization that identity in interpersonal interaction is a costume. Your values are not fixed endpoints but instruments for achieving a world you prefer. This is not fraudulence — it is the recognition that everyone is wearing a costume whether they know it or not, and wisdom is having agency over yours.

He illustrates this through Burning Man, where costumes become genuine self-expression rather than social compliance uniforms. The deeper point is that most people are locked into identities chosen by circumstance — birthplace, upbringing, social group. True adult development means reverse-engineering your own identity construction and choosing it deliberately. This directly applies to career pivots, creative work, and any situation where you need to break free from an outdated self-concept.
