Cigarettes went from universally normal (ashtrays in every home) to socially stigmatized over approximately a century, through a combination of spreading knowledge and eventual legislation. But legislation followed customs — it couldn't have led. This means cultural protection is always lagging.

The critical problem is the mismatch in rates. If society needs ~100 years to develop antibodies to each new addiction, but technology produces new addictive things every few years, the gap between 'what's addictive' and 'what we have customs to manage' keeps widening. The people who adopt early become 'canaries in the coal mine' — their suffering eventually teaches others, but only after significant damage.
