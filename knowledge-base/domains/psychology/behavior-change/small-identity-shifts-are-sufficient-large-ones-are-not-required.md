This aligns with James Clear / BJ Fogg tiny-habits research: the size of the change matters less than its consistency and its alignment with identity. Bayer's retrospective — 'sometimes it was by a hair' — suggests that people overestimate how large an internal shift needs to be to produce visible behavioral change. The practical implication is to resist the temptation to plan massive identity overhauls and instead isolate the smallest sustainable shift in thought/feeling/action and repeat it.
