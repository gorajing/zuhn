Multiple companies in the study converged on the same discovery: peer demonstration beats formal training programs. At Roblox, Peter Yang shares AI workflows in Slack and screen-shares AI output during meetings to solve problems in real time. Whoop's Head of Product responds to PMs bringing problems with 'Want me to show you how I solve this with AI?' and demonstrates live.

This works because it activates social proof at the moment of relevance — the colleague isn't in a training session learning abstract capabilities, they're watching someone solve the exact kind of problem they face daily. The behavior change literature supports this: people adopt new tools when they see peers (not authorities) succeeding with them in contexts they recognize as their own.
