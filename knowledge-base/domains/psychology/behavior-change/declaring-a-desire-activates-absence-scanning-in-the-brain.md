Bayer's observation is that clarity about wanting something (money, relationship, spiritual depth) immediately makes the brain notice the gap — empty bank account, disconnection, low support. This is not a motivational failure; it is how attention orients to salience. The practical consequence is that goal clarity without a paired state-management practice systematically generates the exact emotions (anxiety, desperation, inadequacy) that degrade execution. Any goal-setting system that lacks a deliberate counter-move for absence-scanning will drift toward demotivating outcomes.
