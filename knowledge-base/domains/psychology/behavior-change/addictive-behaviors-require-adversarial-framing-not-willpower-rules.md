Graham tried setting rules like 'only use the internet twice a day' and found they always collapsed—some legitimate need would arise, creating a crack that widened back into old habits. He concludes that addictive things must be treated as if a sentient adversary in your head is constantly generating plausible justifications.

This insight reframes self-control from discipline to system design. Instead of relying on moment-to-moment willpower decisions (which the 'adversary' always wins eventually), you design environments where the undesired behavior requires a visible, deliberate act. The practical takeaway is to stop making rules about frequency or duration and instead restructure your physical or digital environment so that the default path leads to productive work.
