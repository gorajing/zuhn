Creative paralysis stems from applying final-product standards to first attempts. The hack is to redefine what you're making: a sketch isn't judged like a painting, a quick hack isn't judged like production software. Once the project has momentum, you can 'sneakily convert' it to something more ambitious.

This works even better when paired with medium selection. Digital tools, notebooks, and rapid-iteration environments reduce the perceived commitment of starting. John Carmack's observation about game mods illustrates this perfectly: when the medium is crude enough (Minecraft, early Quake), innovative gameplay ideas can emerge because creators aren't paralyzed by production-quality expectations. The gap between early work and final work should be as small as possible to minimize the courage required to begin.
