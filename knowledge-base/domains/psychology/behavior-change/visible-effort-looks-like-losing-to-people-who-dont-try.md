Hormozi describes meeting someone who bragged about outsourcing content to two hours a week. The person was performing nonchalance as a status move, but what it actually signaled was losing. The underlying dynamic: people who have never cared about anything deeply cannot acknowledge that caring works, because doing so would force them to face their own avoidance. So they mock effort as a defensive move. The takeaway for the effortful: interpret mockery of your effort as information about the mocker, not about you. It's a signal they're protecting an internal story that would collapse if effort visibly paid off.
