Changuel highlights the habituation effect as a critical challenge: features that initially delight users quickly become expected. What was once a 'wow' moment (like Spotify's year-end Wrapped) becomes something users demand and complain about if it's missing or worse than last year. This maps directly to hedonic adaptation from psychology—humans rapidly return to baseline satisfaction after positive experiences. The practical implication is that delight cannot be a one-time project or quarterly initiative. Teams need a pipeline of delight experiments, and the 10% allocation in the 50-40-10 rule should be treated as a recurring investment, not a one-off. Apple's reaction effects in video calls are cited as a cautionary example of delight that backfired when it triggered unexpectedly, showing that even well-intentioned delight features require careful calibration.
