The video identifies three specific intervention points for chronic loneliness. First, attention: are you selectively focusing on negative interactions while filtering out neutral or positive ones? Second, interpretation: are you adding hostile meaning to ambiguous social signals, assuming the worst about others' intentions? Third, behavior: are you declining invitations, avoiding social situations, or pushing people away preemptively?

This three-level framework is powerful because it shows why surface-level advice ('just put yourself out there') fails. If you force behavioral change (attending events) without addressing the attentional and interpretive distortions, you'll attend the event, perceive hostility that isn't there, and use the experience as confirmation that socializing is painful. Effective intervention means questioning your own narrative at each level before it compounds into the next.
