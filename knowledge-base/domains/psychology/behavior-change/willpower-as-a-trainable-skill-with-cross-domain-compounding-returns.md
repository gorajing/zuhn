Osterwalder's interest in willpower research — specifically McGonigal's work — reveals a pattern among high-performing entrepreneurs: they treat willpower not as a fixed trait but as a developable skill with cross-domain returns. The key insight is the transfer effect — willpower gains in exercise or diet spill over into professional discipline, creative output, and strategic patience.

This positions willpower as a meta-capability rather than a domain-specific skill. For founders and knowledge workers, investing in willpower training (through structured habits, environmental design, and progressive challenge) may yield disproportionate returns because improvements compound across every area requiring sustained effort. The fact that an innovation thought leader prioritizes this over pure business reading is itself informative.
