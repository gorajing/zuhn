Graham identifies a concrete technique used across creative fields: deliberately recategorizing new work as a less exacting type. A painting becomes 'just a sketch,' software becomes 'just a quick hack.' This lowers the internal standard you judge early results against, making the gap between current quality and acceptable quality small enough to tolerate.

Once the project gains momentum, you can 'sneakily convert it to something more.' This works especially well when paired with a medium that enables fast iteration and low upfront commitment — digital over physical, notebooks over carved stone. The technique is powerful because it doesn't require you to suppress your quality standards permanently; it temporarily redirects them so they don't strangle the project before it can develop the qualities that would justify higher scrutiny.
