Itzler describes being professionally successful — sold businesses, married, healthy — yet stuck on autopilot. The critical distinction he makes is between routine (productive structure) and rut (the same structure without growth). The two feel identical from the inside because comfort is the default state in both.

This reframes the common narrative that stagnation comes from laziness or lack of ambition. Itzler was neither lazy nor unambitious. The mechanism is subtler: when life is comfortable enough, the absence of acute pain removes the forcing function for change. His solution — deliberately importing extreme discomfort via the SEAL — suggests that growth requires intentional disruption of comfort, not just effort within existing patterns.
