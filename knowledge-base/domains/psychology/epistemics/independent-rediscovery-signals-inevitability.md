The deeper epistemological point generalizes beyond mysticism. Multiple independent discovery is a recurring pattern in science (calculus by Newton and Leibniz, evolution by Darwin and Wallace, oxygen by Scheele and Priestley), in mathematics, in invention. When the same answer keeps coming out of insulated investigators, one reasonable inference is that the problem space has structure that forces the answer — the seekers are not the source of the insight, they're instruments that happened to touch a shared territory. The essay extends this logic from science into phenomenology: if five contemplatives with no contact reported the same inward structure, then perhaps inward looking itself has a shape that produces that report, independent of who is doing the looking. This is not proof, but it's the right kind of inference for this kind of evidence.
