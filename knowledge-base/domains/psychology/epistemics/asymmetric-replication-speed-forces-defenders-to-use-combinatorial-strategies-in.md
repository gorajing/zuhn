The fundamental problem the immune system solves is a speed asymmetry: pathogens evolve and replicate orders of magnitude faster than the host can produce new cells. A bacterium copies in 30 minutes; a virus can produce billions of copies in days. The host cannot out-evolve or out-replicate its enemies.

The solution is to shift from reactive to anticipatory defense — pre-generating a library that covers the entire possible threat space before any specific threat appears. This pattern generalizes beyond biology: any defender facing a faster-iterating attacker must build broad coverage in advance rather than responding to each new variant. It applies to cybersecurity (signature databases), platform trust and safety (pre-built classifiers), and competitive strategy (building broad capabilities before knowing which competitor will emerge).
