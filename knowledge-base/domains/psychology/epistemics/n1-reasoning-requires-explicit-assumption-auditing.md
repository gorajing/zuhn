The Kardashev Scale illustrates a fundamental epistemic challenge: humanity is our only example of a technological civilization, so every assumption about alien civilizations is a projection from n=1. The video explicitly acknowledges this ('we may make incorrect assumptions based solely on ourselves') but proceeds anyway because 'better than nothing.'

This pattern appears everywhere — a founder extrapolating from one successful product, an investor generalizing from one market cycle, a researcher building theory from a single case study. The discipline isn't avoiding n=1 reasoning (often it's all you have), but explicitly auditing which properties you're assuming are universal (physics constrains energy use) versus which might be local (competitive expansion as the only path to advancement). The video itself demonstrates this tension: it assumes curiosity and greed are universal drivers, then in the final moments questions whether 'progress' necessarily means outward expansion.
