Stars must die once in a supernova to compress matter into neutron stars, then those neutron stars must collide and die again in a kilonova to produce heavy elements like gold, platinum, and uranium. A single destruction event only gets you to iron — the truly complex outputs require a second pass through extreme conditions.

This pattern generalizes: many valuable outcomes require iterative destruction-and-reassembly rather than a single transformative event. In startups, a failed first venture often produces the founder who succeeds on the second. In learning, the first breakdown of a mental model creates fragments that only become a deeper framework after a second restructuring. The key insight is that some complexity is simply inaccessible without multiple passes through destruction.
