The video points out that over 1,000 nuclear tests have occurred in the last 70 years without changing Earth's orbit or triggering geological apocalypse. This is massive empirical evidence that should have already eliminated many catastrophic hypotheses — yet the question 'could a nuke destroy Earth?' persists because the narrative is compelling.

This is a specific failure mode in epistemics: accumulated evidence of non-occurrence (absence of evidence at scale) should dramatically update beliefs, but narrative appeal and availability bias preserve catastrophic hypotheses long past their evidential expiration date. The same pattern appears in technology fears, economic predictions, and health scares — repeated non-events fail to update the cultural prior because the scary story is more memorable than the boring base rate.
