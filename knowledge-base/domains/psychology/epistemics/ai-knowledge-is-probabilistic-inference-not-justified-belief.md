Classical epistemology defines knowledge as justified true belief, but LLMs do not possess beliefs, justifications, or truth-values. They predict tokens based on statistical correlations, making their outputs better understood as probabilistic inferences. This creates a Gettier-like problem at scale: AI outputs can be accurate yet fail to constitute genuine knowledge because correctness is coincidental rather than grounded in sound reasoning.

This distinction has practical consequences for anyone consuming AI outputs. The shift from classical to Bayesian epistemology for AI means we should evaluate outputs by statistical reliability and data provenance rather than by logical coherence alone. High-confidence AI outputs may still represent 'confident hallucinations' — low entropy does not equal epistemic accuracy. Users and builders must internalize this gap between linguistic fluency and justified knowledge.
