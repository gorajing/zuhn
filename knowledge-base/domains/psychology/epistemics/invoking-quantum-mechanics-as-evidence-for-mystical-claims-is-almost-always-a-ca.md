Hughes repeatedly reaches for quantum physics as external validation of mystical claims: 'observation changes the behavior of matter,' 'no particle is truly separate, every particle has some entanglement.' Both are misstatements of the physics. 'Observation' in quantum mechanics refers to any measurement interaction that couples a system to its environment, not to a conscious observer looking at it. Entanglement is a correlation between previously interacting particles and cannot be used to communicate information or enforce the kind of holistic connectedness mystics describe. Contemplative claims about non-separation and constructed experience may have real merit on their own terms — but they borrow no actual epistemic credit from quantum mechanics, and any argument that leans on the quantum connection should be downgraded.
