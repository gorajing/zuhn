Quantum mechanics holds that information is indestructible. When paper burns, every bit of information about the original paper persists in the ash, smoke particles, and radiated heat. It's not lost — it's just hard to read. If you could measure every atom and wave of radiation, you could theoretically reconstruct the paper. The information paradox was alarming precisely because black holes seemed to violate this otherwise universal conservation law.

This conservation principle has analogues in organizational and market dynamics. When a company 'fails,' its information — the knowledge, relationships, market insights, and talent — doesn't vanish. It redistributes into the ecosystem through alumni networks, acquired IP, and lessons absorbed by competitors. When a market 'crashes,' the value information transforms into repriced expectations and revealed risk models. Understanding that destruction is always transformation changes how you respond to apparent losses — the question becomes 'where did the information go' rather than 'is it gone.'
