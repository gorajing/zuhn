The Gapyeong UFO photo is a well-known Korean case where a single image is routinely cited as depicting an object moving at kilometers per second. The physicist points out that motion is a derivative quantity: without two points in time, 'speed' is not defined. Worse, the two popular explanations for the image's streaks (shock-wave cloud forming ahead of motion vs. a black trail left behind motion) imply contradictory directions, forcing ad hoc rescue explanations that violate Occam's Razor. Use this as a portable test: if a quantitative claim about motion rests on a single static frame, it is story, not measurement.
