The Egg has 35M+ views and near-universal positive reception despite making zero testable claims. Its power comes from simultaneously satisfying multiple deep psychological needs: the desire for cosmic fairness (every experience is balanced), the fear of death (consciousness continues), the need for meaning (the universe exists for a purpose), and moral motivation (kindness to others is self-interest). This pattern — unfalsifiable claims spreading via emotional resonance — is a general epistemics principle. It explains the stickiness of conspiracy theories, the persistence of religious frameworks, and the virality of self-help content. The practical implication: when evaluating beliefs, notice which ones you adopted because they felt right versus because you examined evidence. Emotional adoption isn't inherently wrong, but recognizing the mechanism prevents mistaking comfort for truth.
