The video highlights that 'dark energy' is essentially a label for something we don't understand — the mysterious force accelerating universal expansion. Yet this naming act is epistemically powerful: it transforms a vague observation into a tractable research program. Einstein conceived and discarded the idea; modern astrophysicists revived it precisely because having a named concept lets communities coordinate around a shared unknown.

This pattern recurs across domains: naming a problem or force — even without understanding it — creates a Schelling point for collective investigation. In startups, labeling a fuzzy market dynamic (e.g., 'product-market fit') enables teams to measure and optimize something they couldn't articulate before. The risk is mistaking the label for understanding, but the benefit of coordinated attention usually outweighs that risk.
