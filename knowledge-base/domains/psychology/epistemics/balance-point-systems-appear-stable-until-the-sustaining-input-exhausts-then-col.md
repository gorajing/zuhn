A star's stability is not inherent — it's an active equilibrium between gravitational collapse and outward fusion pressure. This balance can persist for billions of years, creating the illusion of permanence. But the moment the fuel reaches iron (which cannot fuse), the outward pressure vanishes instantly and the core collapses at 25% the speed of light.

This pattern appears in many domains: businesses sustained by a single revenue stream, relationships maintained by one person's continuous effort, or markets propped up by a policy that could be reversed. The warning sign is that the stability depends on a consumable input. When systems look stable but require active maintenance of balance, the failure mode is not gradual decline — it's sudden collapse once the sustaining resource is exhausted.
