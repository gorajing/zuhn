The entire Venus terraforming plan uses technologies that either exist today or are straightforward engineering extensions: thin foil solar shades, electromagnetic mass drivers, space tethers, and biological organisms that have already proven they can transform a planetary atmosphere. No new physics is required — no fusion breakthroughs, no exotic materials, no theoretical advances.

This challenges the common assumption that impossible-seeming problems require revolutionary breakthroughs. Often the barrier is not knowledge but will and coordination — the engineering is known, the scale is what's daunting. This pattern applies to climate change (existing solar/wind/nuclear at scale), disease eradication (existing vaccines at universal distribution), and software (existing simple algorithms at massive parallelism). Framing a challenge as 'needs a breakthrough' can be a form of learned helplessness that prevents deployment of known solutions at the necessary scale.
