The video presents a striking epistemological scenario: a civilization born in the far future will observe a single galaxy in an apparently empty, static universe. They won't see the cosmic microwave background radiation. They'll have no reason to hypothesize a Big Bang or universal expansion. Crucially, they won't even know they're missing anything — the evidence that evidence once existed will itself be gone.

This generalizes beyond cosmology. In any system where information degrades over time — markets, organizations, ecosystems — there exist windows of observability that close permanently. The dangerous version is when the closure is silent: no residual signal indicates that a signal once existed. This means the strongest epistemic discipline isn't just collecting available evidence, but actively asking what evidence might have already become unobservable.
