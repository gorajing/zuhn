One of the most striking patterns across the 30 sensemaking platforms analyzed is audience willingness to invest significant cognitive effort. Multi-hour podcasts, dense essay series, and technically demanding video explainers are not niche — they command massive and growing audiences. This directly contradicts the dominant narrative that attention spans are collapsing.

The more accurate model is that attention is bifurcating: shallow content competes in a zero-sum attention economy, while deep content operates in a different market entirely where audiences actively seek cognitive challenge. The practical implication is that creators and businesses should not default to simplification — there is a large, underserved market of people who will invest hours if the intellectual payoff justifies the effort. Depth is a viable and defensible content strategy.
