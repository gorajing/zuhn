On Earth, the atmosphere both creates the shockwave AND shields against direct radiation and ballistic debris. We focus on the shockwave as 'the destructive part' without recognizing that the atmosphere is simultaneously preventing worse alternatives. The Moon scenario reveals that removing the atmosphere doesn't just remove the shockwave — it unleashes unimpeded radiation and micro-meteorite bombardment with no safe viewing distance at all.

This maps to how organizations, markets, and societies systematically undervalue protective infrastructure. Regulations, cultural norms, institutional processes — these 'atmospheres' create visible friction (the shockwave equivalent) that people complain about, while silently preventing worse failure modes that would emerge in their absence. The absence of protective mechanisms leaves no evidence trail, making them perpetually undervalued until catastrophically removed.
