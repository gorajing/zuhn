Everyone 'knows' life is short, but the knowledge remains abstract and fails to influence daily decisions. Converting lifespan to weeks (a 100-year life is 5,200 weeks) makes finitude viscerally concrete. At 25, you have ~3,900 weeks. At 50, ~2,600. These numbers are small enough to feel real.

This technique works because it bridges the gap between intellectual knowledge and emotional salience. Abstract mortality awareness produces philosophical reflection; concrete week counts produce behavioral change. The mechanism is similar to why showing calories on a menu changes food choices — the information was always available, but the format makes it actionable. For anyone struggling with prioritization, converting their remaining time to weeks is a cheap, immediate intervention.
