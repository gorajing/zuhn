The simulation argument's statistical core is simple: if posthuman civilizations run billions of simulations each containing billions of conscious beings, simulated minds outnumber biological ones by orders of magnitude. By base rate alone, any randomly selected conscious being is almost certainly simulated. This logic is uncomfortable but structurally identical to reasoning we accept elsewhere — if 99.9% of diamonds are synthetic, a random diamond is probably synthetic regardless of how 'real' it feels. The insight generalizes: when evaluating your own category membership (am I a typical startup? a typical investor? a typical case?), the base rates of the reference class should dominate over your subjective feeling of specialness. Most people overweight their felt experience and underweight the statistical prior.
