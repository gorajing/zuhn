Kurzgesagt's 'Behind the Lies' segment reveals that despite presenting a confident size ranking, the underlying data is remarkably thin: good measurements exist for only a couple dozen black holes. Properties are derived indirectly from gravitational effects on nearby matter, then converted through equations (like Schwarzschild) that assume idealized conditions (no spin, perfect sphericity) that don't actually hold.

This layered uncertainty — observe indirectly, approximate with simplified models, present with false precision — is endemic beyond astrophysics. Investment theses built on limited comparable transactions, startup metrics extrapolated from small cohorts, and medical conclusions drawn from underpowered studies all share this pattern. The sophistication of the presentation often inversely correlates with the robustness of the underlying data.
