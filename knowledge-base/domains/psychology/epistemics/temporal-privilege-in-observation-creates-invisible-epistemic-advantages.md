Humanity occupies a brief window in cosmic history where the universe is old enough to have formed complex structures but young enough that light from the Big Bang and distant galaxy clusters still reaches us. This allows us to reconstruct the full story of cosmic evolution. No future civilization will have this capability.

This pattern — temporal privilege in observation — appears across domains. Early employees at a company witness founding decisions that become invisible institutional assumptions. First-generation researchers in a field see the raw phenomena before they're abstracted into canonical frameworks. Investors who witnessed a bubble's formation understand dynamics invisible to those who arrived after the crash. Recognizing temporal privilege means asking: what am I seeing right now that will be invisible to those who come after me, and how should I preserve or act on that information?
