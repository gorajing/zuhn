Calculus begins with limits — the study of what happens as you approach a point, not what happens at the point. This is a profound epistemological principle: the most informative observations often come from edges and extremes, not from the comfortable middle. A function can be well-behaved everywhere except at a single point, and that single point reveals its true character.

This principle transfers directly to system analysis, product development, and strategic thinking. Stress-testing at boundaries (what happens when traffic spikes 100x? when a customer uses the product in an unintended way?) reveals structural weaknesses invisible during normal operation. The calculus insight is that limits formalize this intuition — you don't need to reach the extreme, you need to understand the trend as you approach it. Edge case analysis is not pedantic caution; it's where the deepest understanding lives.
