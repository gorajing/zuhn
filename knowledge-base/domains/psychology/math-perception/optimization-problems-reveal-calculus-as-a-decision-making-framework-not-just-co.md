The course's progression from abstract derivative rules to concrete optimization problems — maximizing area with fixed perimeter, minimizing cost functions, finding optimal production quantities in marginal cost analysis — reveals calculus's real power: it converts 'what's the best choice?' into a systematic procedure. Set the derivative to zero, check second derivatives, evaluate boundary conditions. This algorithmic approach to finding optima applies far beyond mathematics.

The marginal cost economics section is particularly revealing: the derivative of a cost function gives the marginal cost, and profit maximization occurs where marginal revenue equals marginal cost. This is not a metaphor — it's a direct application. Every business optimization, resource allocation, and engineering design problem is fundamentally a calculus optimization problem. Presenting calculus as 'the mathematics of optimal decisions' rather than 'the mathematics of slopes and areas' would likely transform student engagement and retention.
