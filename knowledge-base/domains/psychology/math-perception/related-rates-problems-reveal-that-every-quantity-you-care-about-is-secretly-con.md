Related rates problems in Calculus 1 take a geometric or physical setup — a ladder sliding down a wall, a balloon inflating, water filling a cone — and ask: if one quantity changes at a known rate, how fast does another change? The method requires identifying the hidden equation connecting the quantities, then differentiating implicitly with respect to time.

This is a powerful mental model beyond mathematics. In business, revenue growth rate connects to hiring rate, which connects to culture dilution rate, which connects to quality metrics. In health, caloric intake rate connects to metabolic rate, which connects to energy levels. The calculus framework teaches that you cannot understand any single rate of change without understanding the constraint equations that tie it to everything else. The practical skill is learning to identify those hidden constraint equations — the implicit relationships — before trying to predict how fast something will change.
