Main sequence stellar brightness scales roughly as mass to the 3.5-4th power. This means Sirius, at just 2 solar masses, shines 25 times brighter than the Sun, while Beta Centauri's components at ~10 solar masses shine 20,000 times brighter. The input ratio is 5:1 but the output ratio is 800:1. Human brains default to linear interpolation and dramatically underestimate these gaps.

This perceptual failure matters everywhere nonlinear relationships govern outcomes: compound interest, viral growth, network effects, talent distributions, and dose-response curves. The practical implication is that whenever a system follows a power law, your gut estimate of the difference between 'good' and 'great' inputs is almost certainly too small by orders of magnitude. Calibrating for nonlinearity requires explicit mathematical reasoning, not intuition.
