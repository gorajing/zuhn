On a linear scale, exponential data creates a hockey stick curve where early variations are crushed into an invisible baseline and recent data dominates perception. A log scale maps each order of magnitude to equal visual distance, turning constant-rate exponential growth into a straight line. Any deviation from that straight line — acceleration, deceleration, inflection — becomes immediately visible as a bend.

This is a practical tool for anyone tracking growth metrics. When your revenue chart looks like a hockey stick on a linear scale, you literally cannot see whether growth is accelerating or decelerating in the recent data. Switch to a log scale and the answer is obvious: a straight line means constant percentage growth, an upward bend means accelerating growth, and a downward bend means you're approaching saturation. The log plot doesn't add information — it reformats the same data to match human visual processing.
