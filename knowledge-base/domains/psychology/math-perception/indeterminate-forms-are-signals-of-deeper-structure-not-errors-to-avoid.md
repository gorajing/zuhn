In calculus, every derivative computation from the definition produces the indeterminate form 0/0, because you're measuring the slope of a secant line as two points converge to the same location — both rise and run approach zero simultaneously. This isn't a bug; it's the entire point. The algebraic tricks needed to resolve 0/0 (factoring, conjugate multiplication, L'Hôpital's rule) are all techniques for extracting the meaningful ratio hiding inside the apparent paradox.

This pattern generalizes far beyond calculus. In many domains, an apparent contradiction or undefined state is actually a signal that you're approaching something interesting from the wrong angle. The productive response isn't to retreat from the paradox but to find a different algebraic or conceptual manipulation that reveals the underlying truth. Training yourself to see 0/0 as 'there's a real answer here, I just need a better approach' builds a transferable problem-solving instinct.
