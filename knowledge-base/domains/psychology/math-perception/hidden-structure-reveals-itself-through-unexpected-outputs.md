The colliding blocks puzzle demonstrates a meta-principle about discovery: nobody set out to find pi in collisions. Someone noticed the digits 3, 31, 314, 3141 and recognized the anomaly demanded explanation. The pattern was discovered by working backward from surprising output to hidden structure.

This applies far beyond mathematics. In any domain — business metrics, health data, creative work — when a simple process produces unexpectedly structured output, that anomaly is a signal. The instinct to dismiss coincidence ('that's weird but probably nothing') fights against the instinct to investigate ('why would this be so clean?'). Cultivating the latter instinct is what separates pattern-finders from pattern-missers. The colliding blocks teach us: if the output looks too clean to be random, there is almost certainly a hidden geometric or structural reason.
