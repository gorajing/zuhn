One of the most practically useful results in Calculus 1 is that to find the highest or lowest value of a function, you look for points where the derivative is zero (critical points). This is deeply counter-intuitive: the peak of a mountain is the one place where the ground is momentarily flat. The maximum profit point is where marginal gain has dropped to zero. The fastest moment in a trajectory is where acceleration crosses zero.

This principle transfers directly to business and life strategy. The optimal pricing point is where marginal revenue equals marginal cost — not where revenue is growing fastest. The best time to stop iterating on a product is when the rate of improvement has flattened, not when it feels most exciting. Training this intuition — that extremes live at the boundary between increasing and decreasing — gives you a powerful mental model for recognizing when you have pushed far enough in any optimization.
