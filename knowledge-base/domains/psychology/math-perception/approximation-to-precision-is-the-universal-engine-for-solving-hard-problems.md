The entire derivation of the circle's area proceeds by accepting an imperfect approximation (treating curved rings as rectangles) and then showing the error vanishes as the slices get thinner. This approximate-then-refine pattern is the conceptual core of calculus and applies far beyond mathematics.

In any domain where exact answers are intractable, the productive strategy is: find an approximation that's 'slightly wrong,' identify the parameter that controls how wrong it is, and show the error shrinks to zero as that parameter improves. This is how integration, numerical methods, and iterative engineering all work.
