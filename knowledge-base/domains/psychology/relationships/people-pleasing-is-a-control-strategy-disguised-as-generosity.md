People-pleasing appears selfless but functions as environmental control: by anticipating and meeting everyone's needs, the pleaser prevents negative emotions in others that would cause them discomfort. This isn't generosity - it's anxiety management through external control.

The cost is enormous: the people-pleaser loses authentic self-expression, builds resentment from unreciprocated investment, and creates relationships based on a false version of themselves. Partners, friends, and colleagues relate to the performed self rather than the real one, meaning the people-pleaser never feels truly known or accepted.
