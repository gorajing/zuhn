Sexton frames love through an existential lens: to love anything is insane because you're accepting you'll lose it. A dog will die in 10-15 years. Every marriage ends in death or divorce. Yet the joy of full commitment is so profound that it's worth the guaranteed pain. As he quotes from Drugstore Cowboy: 'We played a game you couldn't win, to the utmost.'

The practical advice is to stop performing your relationship for social media or measuring it against others. Don't turn monogamy into an endurance event — '50 years married, miserable for 47, but we hung in there.' Instead, go all in. Choose to see the beautiful and brilliant in your partner. Initially this is a conscious practice, like meditation, but over time it becomes automatic. The key is to start this practice when things are good, not once the wheels are coming off.
