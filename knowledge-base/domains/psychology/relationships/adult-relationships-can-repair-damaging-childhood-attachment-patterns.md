Childhood experience sets the stage for what people expect from the world and relationships. Those raised by warm, reliable caregivers develop secure expectations; those from troubled environments often enter adulthood expecting the world to be unsafe and people to be unreliable.

Critically, the Harvard study demonstrates that adult experience can correct for unfortunate childhood lessons. Forming connections with good partners and reliable friends can fundamentally change those gloomy expectations about relationships. This is not merely coping — it represents genuine rewiring of relational expectations. The study also found that all important relationships involve disagreements, and that working through difficulties actually strengthens relationships rather than damaging them.
