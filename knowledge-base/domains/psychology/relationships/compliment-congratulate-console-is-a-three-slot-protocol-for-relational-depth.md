Itzler's three-slot protocol works because each slot exploits a different relational gap: unprompted compliments breach the 'I'm-only-called-when-needed' assumption, congratulations counteract the fact that most people are bad at celebrating others' wins, and showing up for grief (not texting — physically arriving) answers a need that almost no one meets. The mechanism isn't politeness — it's scarcity. Few people do any of the three consistently, so the person who does becomes memorable. Itzler credits this protocol with the referral pipeline that sustained his businesses through downturns.
