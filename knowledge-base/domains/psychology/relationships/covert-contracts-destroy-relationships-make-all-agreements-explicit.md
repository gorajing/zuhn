Huberman draws a critical distinction between overt and covert contracts in all human relationships. An overt contract is explicit: we agree on terms and both honor them. A covert contract looks identical but involves hidden resentment — one party secretly takes compensation through verbal jabs, showing up late, building alliances behind your back, or feigning vulnerability.

This pattern appears in romantic relationships (fear of betrayal causes preemptive betrayal), business partnerships, and even primate hierarchies (chimps groom to signal loyalty while plotting coups). The antidote is radical honesty about needs and expectations upfront. Huberman observes that the best long-term romantic relationships he's seen all share one pattern: establish deep friendship first, defer physical intimacy, and keep all contracts overt.
