Research by psychologist Sonja Lyubomirsky breaks happiness into three components: approximately 50% is a biological set point determined by genes (innate temperament), about 10% is driven by current life circumstances, and the remaining 40% is under voluntary control. This means there is substantial room to move the needle on personal happiness through intentional choices.

The Harvard Study's findings point to relationship investment as the highest-leverage use of that 40%. The practical framework involves three self-assessment questions: Do I have enough connection (not too much, not too little for my temperament)? Do I have relationships that are warm and supportive — people who have my back? Do I have the functional relationships I need — people to have fun with, practical help, emotional support? The study also shows that adult relationships can partially repair damage from difficult childhoods, suggesting it's never too late to build the social connections that promote wellbeing.
