When victimhood becomes a strategic position rather than a genuine state, it creates an unfalsifiable dynamic. Confronting the behavior? 'You're attacking me.' Setting boundaries? 'You're abandoning me.' Expressing frustration? 'This proves how much you hurt me.'

Recognizing victimhood-as-strategy vs. genuine victimhood is one of the most important interpersonal skills. The key differentiator is whether the person's victim narrative leads to growth (processing and moving forward) or to control (using victimhood to manipulate others' behavior).
