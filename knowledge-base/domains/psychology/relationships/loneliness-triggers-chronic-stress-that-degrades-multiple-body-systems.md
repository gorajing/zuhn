The biological mechanism connecting relationships to physical health operates through stress regulation. Daily stressors activate fight-or-flight responses — elevated heart rate, sweating, physiological arousal. Normally, when the stressor passes, the body returns to equilibrium. If you can talk to a trusted friend or partner, you literally feel your body calm down.

Without that social regulation, lonely individuals remain in chronic low-level fight-or-flight mode with elevated cortisol and inflammation. This sustained physiological stress gradually degrades multiple body systems — increasing risk of coronary artery disease, type 2 diabetes, arthritis, and accelerated cognitive decline. MRI studies confirm that holding someone's hand during a stressful medical procedure keeps the body measurably closer to equilibrium than facing it alone. Good relationships function as biological emotion regulators.
