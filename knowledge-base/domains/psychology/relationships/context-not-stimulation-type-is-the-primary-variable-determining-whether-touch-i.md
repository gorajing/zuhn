Nagoski emphasizes that what makes stimulation feel good isn't the stimulation itself but the context in which it occurs. The same touch on the same body part by the same person can feel pleasurable in one moment and annoying or even painful in another. The variables that shift are contextual: stress levels, emotional safety, body image feelings, relationship security, physical environment, and mental presence. This insight has practical implications far beyond sexuality — it's a general principle about how the nervous system evaluates sensory input. In the sexual domain specifically, it means that 'technique optimization' is largely misguided. The higher-leverage intervention is always context optimization: reducing stressors, increasing safety, and creating conditions where the nervous system can interpret stimulation as pleasurable.
