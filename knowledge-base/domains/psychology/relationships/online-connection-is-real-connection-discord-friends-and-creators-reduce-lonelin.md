Paul explicitly validates online social connection as meaningful: finding a creator you resonate with, chatting on Discord servers, having online friends — these all 'matter if they make you feel more connected and less alone.' Rather than treating online interaction as inferior to in-person connection, he positions it as a legitimate source of belonging.

This is particularly relevant for people who are geographically isolated, have mental health challenges that make in-person socializing difficult, or simply find their tribe more easily online. The key qualifier: the connection must make you feel 'not alone' and help you realize 'people out there are actually very similar to you and feel very similar feelings.'
