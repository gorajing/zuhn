De Botton cites research suggesting that roughly 50% of children develop attachment wounds from suboptimal caregiving. These wounds manifest as avoidant attachment (pulling away when intimacy deepens), anxious attachment (constant reassurance-seeking), or disorganized attachment (oscillating between approach and withdrawal).

This 50% base rate is crucial context for dating and relationships. It means that with a coin flip's probability, any potential partner has significant attachment patterns that will shape the relationship. The response isn't to avoid these people but to develop the literacy to recognize and work with attachment styles -- both your own and your partner's. Attachment wounds are not permanent; they can be worked through with awareness and therapeutic support.
