De Botton presents a striking statistic: a person's life satisfaction is determined by up to 70% by the quality of their personal relationships. A rational response would be to dedicate proportional resources -- money, education, time -- to understanding relationships. Instead, we leave it entirely to chance, because romantic culture sees rational planning as antithetical to love.

The therapeutic model de Botton advocates treats love as requiring the same kind of deliberate skill development as any other complex activity: classes in attachment theory, communication, apology, and listening. The Greeks saw love as emotional education; the romantics see it as a feeling. De Botton argues we need to move into a third era -- the therapeutic age -- where we combine the liberation of choosing our own partners with the sophistication of understanding how relationships actually work.
