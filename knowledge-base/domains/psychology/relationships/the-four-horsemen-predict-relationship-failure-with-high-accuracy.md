The Gottman 'Four Horsemen' represent an escalating pattern: criticism (attacking character rather than behavior), defensiveness (refusing responsibility), stonewalling (shutting down entirely), and contempt (mocking from a position of superiority). Each horseman feeds the next -- criticism triggers defensiveness, which triggers stonewalling or contempt.

Contempt is the most destructive because it signals fundamental disrespect. The antidote pattern is equally specific: replace criticism with 'I' statements about your own feelings, replace defensiveness with accepting some responsibility, replace stonewalling with self-soothing breaks, and replace contempt with building a culture of appreciation. The key insight is that these are learnable skills, not personality traits.
