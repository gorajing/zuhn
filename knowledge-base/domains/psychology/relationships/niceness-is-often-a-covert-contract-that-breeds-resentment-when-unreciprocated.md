The 'nice person' often operates on implicit expectations: be nice and people will like you, accommodate you, reciprocate your efforts. When this unspoken contract is violated - someone takes advantage, doesn't reciprocate, or doesn't show sufficient gratitude - the nice person experiences profound resentment.

This resentment reveals that the niceness was transactional rather than genuine. True kindness expects nothing in return. Niceness-as-strategy expects everything in return while pretending to expect nothing. The paradox: the nicest people are often the most resentful because they've invested the most in an unreciprocated transaction.
