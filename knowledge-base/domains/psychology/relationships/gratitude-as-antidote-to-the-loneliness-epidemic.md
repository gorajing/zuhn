In the context of a US loneliness epidemic declared by the Surgeon General, gratitude serves as a relational force that binds people to each other and to something bigger. When people feel genuine gratitude, they help others more and feel less isolated.

The key mechanism is perspective expansion: gratitude forces you to step back and see the bigger picture, recognizing your place within a community or the broader fabric of existence. This makes it a practical, accessible intervention for social isolation — not just a feel-good exercise but a genuine connector.
