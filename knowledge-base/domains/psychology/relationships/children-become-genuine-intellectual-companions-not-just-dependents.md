Both Graham and his mother independently noted that children are surprisingly interesting to talk to. This contradicts the common pre-parenthood assumption that kids are purely dependents who consume energy without providing intellectual stimulation. The relationship is bidirectional — parents don't just love their children, they genuinely enjoy them as people.

This reframes the parenthood trade-off calculation entirely. If children are only costs (time, money, energy), the decision is purely about sacrifice. But if they're also sources of genuine intellectual engagement, novel perspectives, and friendship, the equation shifts dramatically. Adults are sophisticated enough to appreciate the complexity of young children's characters in ways that other children cannot.
