Itzler cites Doug E. Fresh arriving at his door after tragedy with the line 'when you get news like that, you don't call. You show up.' The principle generalizes: costly signals — those that require real time, physical inconvenience, and emotional exposure — function as reliable proof of commitment precisely because they can't be faked by messaging. Grief is a high-stakes attribution moment where people permanently categorize their contacts into 'showed up' and 'didn't,' and that categorization is sticky for decades. The asymmetry: low effort to send a text, massive effort to travel — and the recipient knows the difference.
