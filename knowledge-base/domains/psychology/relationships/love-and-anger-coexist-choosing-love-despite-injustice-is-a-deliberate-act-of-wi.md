When asked about anger versus love, a Palestinian refugee gives a remarkable answer: he loves everyone, including those who destroyed his home, and is against all violence. This is not naivete — he has directly experienced brutality. It is a conscious choice to maintain humanity in conditions designed to destroy it.

This is the deepest form of psychological resilience: the ability to hold anger and love simultaneously, and to choose love as the dominant response. It contradicts the common assumption that trauma necessarily produces hatred. The insight for any domain involving conflict — interpersonal, organizational, geopolitical — is that love and anger can coexist, and the choice between them is always available regardless of external circumstances.
