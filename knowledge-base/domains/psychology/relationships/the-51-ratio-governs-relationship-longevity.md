The Gottman research across thousands of couples found that relationship satisfaction and longevity correlate strongly with maintaining a 5:1 ratio of positive to negative interactions. Critically, 'positive' doesn't mean grand romantic gestures -- it means responding to your partner's bids for connection: laughing at their joke, looking up from your screen, saying 'want to come with me?' These micro-moments of turning toward rather than away accumulate into the fabric of relationship health.

The practical implication is that relationship quality is less about avoiding conflict and more about building a thick enough buffer of positive deposits. Each partner has different bid styles, so the first step is identifying what your partner's bids actually look like, then consistently meeting them.
