After seeing thousands of marriages from the inside, Sexton identifies the pattern that distinguishes lasting relationships: the partners are 'thickest thieves' who never publicly criticize each other. Culture encourages the opposite — the eye-rolling wife, the castrated husband tropes — and this normalization of partner-bashing is toxic.

The successful couples have a steadfast rule: you don't take the piss out of your partner in front of people. You don't talk shit about your partner to anyone. When the world criticizes you (and it will), having one person who always says 'you've got this, I'm with you' is a superpower. This isn't about dishonesty — you can privately acknowledge blind spots — but the public stance is unconditional alliance. Sexton describes it as looking at the groom watching the bride walk down the aisle: pure adoration.
