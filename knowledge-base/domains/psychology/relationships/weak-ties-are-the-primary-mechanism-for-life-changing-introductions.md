Drawing on Granovetter's Strength of Weak Ties theory, the article explains why your closest friends are poor sources of novel introductions — tight network clusters have near-complete overlap, so everyone already knows everyone. Acquaintances bridge between clusters, providing access to entirely different pools of people, ideas, and opportunities.

This has direct tactical implications: when seeking a romantic partner, job, or new perspective, deliberately activate weak ties rather than asking close friends. Identify 'hub' acquaintances who maintain many weak ties across different cliques. The same principle explains why arranged marriages based on kin-network compatibility can outperform love matches — they optimize for network compatibility rather than individual chemistry alone.
