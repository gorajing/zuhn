Reeves identifies a specific empathy gap: when young men are struggling — not in the labor market, not in education, not connected to society — the reflexive response is blame. 'If they could just get off the sofa and stop smoking weed and playing video games.' He notes this blame instinct is rarely applied with the same intensity to other groups.

The word he reaches for is 'lost' — these men are not feckless, they are lost. They are caught in a 'pinball machine' between being told they are too masculine on Tuesdays and not masculine enough on Wednesdays. The question 'what is my place in the world, why am I needed, what is my role?' is genuine and unanswered. The blame reflex is counterproductive because it shuts down the exact conversation that could lead to solutions. Reeves argues these men are 'up for grabs' — which should send alarm bells ringing.
