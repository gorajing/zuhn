Silverglate observes that free speech is one of the few things liberal and conservative Supreme Courts consistently agree on. The reason is strategic self-interest: each side recognizes that while their group may be ascendant today, it may not be tomorrow, and they want objective, clear rules protecting speech when they're in the minority.

This reveals free speech not as an ideological position but as a rational insurance policy against political cycles. Only the extremes — fascists on the right and communists on the left — oppose it, because they never intend to be in the minority. For anyone building institutions or platforms, this means speech protections aren't a political stance — they're a structural safeguard that benefits everyone across time.
