Jung was precise about what qualifies as synchronicity versus mere coincidence. The fish-on-Friday example illustrates the distinction: despite an impressive clustering of fish-related events during his study of fish symbolism, Jung himself dismissed it as chance grouping — fish on Friday is ordinary, April fish references are seasonal, and fish symbols are common in the unconscious.

Contrast this with the scarab beetle incident: a patient dreams of a golden scarab, describes it during a session, and a scarab-like beetle simultaneously taps on the window — displaying unusual behavior for its species. The four criteria are met: internal event (dream/description), external event (beetle appearing), no causal link, meaningful symbolic parallel, temporal proximity. Understanding these criteria prevents the common dilution of synchronicity into 'any weird coincidence.'
