The universe's expansion has no center — like dots on an inflating balloon's surface, every point moves away from every other point at the same rate. But the moment you choose any single dot as your frame of reference, it immediately appears to be the center of all expansion. The 'center' is entirely an artifact of observation.

This maps directly to cognitive biases in everyday reasoning. People naturally assume their vantage point is special or central — in markets, organizations, and social networks. The cosmological principle provides a precise physical demonstration that apparent centrality is often an illusion created by the observer's frame, not an inherent property of the system. Recognizing this helps counteract egocentric bias in strategic and analytical thinking.
