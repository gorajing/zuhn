The video draws an explicit analogy between ocean waves and sound: just as you can hear the bass but not the treble when walking away from a concert, ocean storms produce waves across many frequencies but only the low-frequency swell survives hundreds of miles of travel. High-frequency wind waves dissipate their energy more quickly.

This is a universal principle of wave physics that applies far beyond oceans. In communication, detailed nuance (high-frequency information) is lost over organizational distance while broad themes (low-frequency) persist. In markets, short-term noise dissipates while long-term trends propagate. In memory, specific details fade while general impressions endure. Understanding that distance acts as a natural low-pass filter helps explain why secondhand information always feels 'smoother' and less detailed than firsthand experience.
