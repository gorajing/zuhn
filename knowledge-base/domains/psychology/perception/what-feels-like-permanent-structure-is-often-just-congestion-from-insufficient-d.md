In 3D, a knot is permanent -- the string is genuinely trapped. But in 4D, the knot slides apart effortlessly. Nothing broke. The constraint just stopped existing because there was suddenly enough room for it to resolve. The knot was never real structure; it was congestion caused by insufficient space.

This is a powerful mental model for problem-solving: when a problem feels permanent and inescapable, consider whether you're trapped by the 'dimensions' of your framework rather than by genuine impossibility. Business constraints, creative blocks, and interpersonal conflicts often dissolve when you add a new dimension of thinking -- a new perspective, a new resource, or a new context -- rather than trying harder within the existing frame.
