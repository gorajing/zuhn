General relativity creates a situation where two observers witnessing the same event — someone crossing a black hole's event horizon — have irreconcilably different experiences. The outside observer sees the person slow down, redshift, and freeze forever. The falling person watches the universe accelerate into fast-forward. Neither perspective is wrong; both are complete descriptions from their reference frame. This has direct implications for organizational decision-making and conflict resolution: when two team members or stakeholders report completely contradictory accounts of the same situation, both may be accurate from their positional frame. The instinct to find 'the truth' assumes a privileged reference frame that may not exist. Better to ask: from what position is each account true, and what does that positional difference reveal about the system's structure?
