Manson identifies a modern trap: the over-consumption of psychological self-knowledge without behavioral change. People can name their attachment style, identify their triggers, and map their childhood wounds — yet nothing changes because insight without action is a more sophisticated form of laziness.

This is especially relevant in the age of therapy-speak podcasts and self-help content. The test is not 'do I understand my patterns?' but 'am I doing something differently today because of that understanding?' Watching another self-improvement video creates the feeling of progress without actual progress. Real change is behavioral, not conceptual.
