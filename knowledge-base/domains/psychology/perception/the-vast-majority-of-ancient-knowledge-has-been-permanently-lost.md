Finkel emphasizes that surviving cuneiform tablets represent a tiny fraction of what was written. Clay tablets survived by accident (often by being baked in fires that destroyed the buildings housing them). The selection is non-random: administrative records survive better than literature, public monuments better than private correspondence.

This means our understanding of ancient civilizations is built on survivor bias of the most extreme kind. The actual technological, mathematical, and philosophical capabilities of these civilizations may have been far more advanced than surviving evidence suggests. Every statement about 'when X was invented' should be prefixed with 'the earliest surviving evidence of X.'
