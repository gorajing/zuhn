Bach shares his personal experience of skipping stage 3 (social self) due to neurodivergence, feeling profoundly alone until finding fellow nerds at a math school. He describes eventually building empathy in his 20s through deliberate practice: meditation, physical closeness, vulnerability, and simply paying deep attention to other humans.

The key insight is that empathy is not a binary trait you either have or lack — it is a perceptual skill that can be developed at any age. For neurodivergent builders and engineers, this is liberating: the social interface that feels impossibly hard at 15 becomes buildable at 25 with deliberate practice. The trick is creating safe environments for experimentation rather than trying to force-fit neurotypical social patterns.
