Reeves tracks a dramatic shift: in 1971, men were 56% of psychology undergraduates — a gender-balanced field. Now the figure is 20% and still falling. Similar collapses are occurring in social work and teaching. Only 1 in 5 psychologists and social workers are men, with numbers more than halving in recent decades.

Reeves makes a provocative comparison: the lack of men in psychology and teaching may be more important than the lack of women in tech and engineering. These are professions where representation directly shapes outcomes — boys struggling with mental health may need a male therapist to feel comfortable opening up. The alarm bells should be ringing loudly when critical caregiving professions become gendered 'right in front of us' with no corrective action being taken.
