An Empower survey found Gen Z considers $588,000 annual income the threshold for 'financial success' — top 1% territory and 13x the US median personal income. While survey wording effects may play a role, this aligns with the hypothesis that daily exposure to upper-class influencer lifestyles recalibrates what 'normal success' looks like. The practical consequence is that even objectively successful young people feel like failures. This is a form of perceptual distortion analogous to body dysmorphia: the reference class has been so skewed by curated content that accurate self-assessment becomes impossible.
