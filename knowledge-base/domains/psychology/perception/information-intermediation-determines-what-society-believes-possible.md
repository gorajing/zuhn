Every era has information intermediaries: priests, publishers, broadcasters, search engines. Each controlled access to information and shaped public understanding. AI represents an unprecedented concentration of this power: a few models built by a few companies will mediate billions of people's access to knowledge.

Unlike search engines (which present ranked links you can evaluate), AI models present synthesized answers that are difficult to verify, source, or challenge. The model's biases, training data limitations, and corporate objectives become invisible filters on what billions of people believe. This is the most powerful information gatekeeping mechanism ever created.
