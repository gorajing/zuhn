This Calculus 1 course has a distinctive structure: 87 chapters alternate between calculus content and bracketed [Corequisite] chapters covering algebra and trigonometry. Rational expressions are reviewed right before limits involving rational functions. Trig identities appear right before derivatives of trig functions. Logarithm rules come right before the chain rule with exponentials. The student never asks 'why am I learning this?' because the answer is always 'you'll need it in 10 minutes.'

This just-in-time approach mirrors effective onboarding in software and workplace training. Teaching someone Git branching strategies in an abstract workshop is far less effective than teaching them at the moment they need to create their first feature branch. The principle extends to documentation, skill development, and even AI prompt engineering: context at the point of need beats comprehensive preparation. The prerequisite becomes meaningful because you have an immediate, concrete reason to care about it.
