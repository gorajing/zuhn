Harris challenges the dominant liberal narrative that Capitol police would have responded with overwhelming force had the mob been Black. He presents specific counter-evidence: black officers were among those struggling to defend the building, a white woman was shot and killed (while no BLM protesters were shot by police over months of rioting), and extensive footage exists of cops standing idle during BLM looting. He also notes the security failure may have partly resulted from DC's mayor resisting heavy-handed policing after the BLM federal response.

The broader insight is about how premature narrative framing prevents accurate institutional diagnosis. When every failure is interpreted through the same lens regardless of evidence, you lose the ability to identify actual causes — which could range from deliberate conspiracy to woke policing resistance to simple resource allocation failure. More importantly, this reflexive framing 'convinces everyone right of center that their cynicism and blind partisanship is totally justified,' actively undermining the coalition-building needed to address the real threat.
