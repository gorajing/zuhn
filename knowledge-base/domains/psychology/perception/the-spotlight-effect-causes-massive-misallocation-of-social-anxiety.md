The spotlight effect is one of psychology's most robust findings: people consistently overestimate how much others notice and remember about their appearance, behavior, and mistakes. In experiments, people estimated 50% of observers noticed their embarrassing t-shirt when only 25% actually did.

The practical implication is that a large portion of social anxiety is generated by an inaccurate model of others' attention. Recognizing this doesn't eliminate social anxiety but redirects the energy from impression management (which others largely don't notice) toward genuine engagement (which they do).
