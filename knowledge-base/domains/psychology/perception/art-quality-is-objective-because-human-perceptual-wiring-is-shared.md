The relativist claim that 'taste is subjective' collapses once you recognize that humans share deep perceptual responses — face recognition is literally in our DNA, primary color sensitivity is an artifact of eye mechanics, and edge-finding makes defined shapes more engaging than blur. These aren't cultural preferences; they're hardware-level responses shared across all humans.

This means art quality can be defined functionally: good art is art that consistently engages its human audience. You don't need to pick one person's opinion as 'correct' — you just need to observe that all humans react similarly to certain stimuli. Beauty becomes a property of objects the same way toxicity is: it's defined by consistent effects on humans, not by residing 'in' the object metaphysically.
