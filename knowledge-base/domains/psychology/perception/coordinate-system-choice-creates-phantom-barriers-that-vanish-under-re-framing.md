The Schwarzschild metric produced a fake singularity at the event horizon that confused physicists for decades, including Einstein. The singularity vanished entirely when a different coordinate system was chosen. The underlying reality never changed -- only the map did.

This is a direct analogy for problem-solving in any domain: when you hit an apparent impossibility, the first question should be whether you're using the right coordinate system -- the right frame, assumptions, or variables. Re-framing doesn't change reality, but it can dissolve barriers that were never physically real. Einstein himself was fooled by a coordinate artifact, which shows even the best thinkers get trapped by their initial framing.
