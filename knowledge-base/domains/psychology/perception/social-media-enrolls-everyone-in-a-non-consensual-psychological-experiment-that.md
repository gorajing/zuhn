Harris frames social media not as a communication tool but as an involuntary psychological experiment that fundamentally changes how humans process information. The key mechanism is that social media makes all communication performative — people speak to signal tribal allegiance rather than to reason toward truth. This transforms information from a shared resource into a weapon, where the goal is to win social points rather than to understand reality.

The downstream effect is an epistemological crisis: when communication becomes performance, the shared infrastructure for determining 'what is real' degrades. Harris argues this is visible across institutions — universities, the press, political discourse — where the incentive to signal moral virtue has overwhelmed the incentive to be accurate. The implication is that any institution dependent on honest discourse (which is essentially all of them) is vulnerable to this dynamic.
