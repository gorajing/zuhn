Lukianoff traces the connection between freedom of speech and science to the Enlightenment — what Yuval Harari called 'the discovery of ignorance.' Both movements emerged from the realization that folk wisdom, intuition, and received authority are unreliable. Galileo's demonstration that our intuitions about falling objects are wrong parallels the broader insight that we need open debate to correct our systematic errors.

This frames free speech not as a luxury or political position but as an epistemological necessity. We are creatures of incredibly limited knowledge and incredibly self-deceiving — therefore we need mechanisms to surface and test competing ideas. Viewpoint discrimination (censoring specific opinions while allowing others) is the most dangerous form of censorship because it removes exactly the corrective feedback that open discourse provides.
