Zelazny's Lord of Light depicted original colonists using technology hoarding to establish themselves as gods over later generations. The parallel to modern tech concentration is direct: AI capabilities concentrated in a few companies, data access restricted by platforms, algorithmic power wielded without transparency.

The pattern recurs because it works: controlling access to transformative technology is the most efficient mechanism for maintaining power. The question isn't whether this will happen with AI - it's already happening. The question is whether countervailing forces (open source, regulation, distributed computing) can prevent the same hierarchical outcome.
