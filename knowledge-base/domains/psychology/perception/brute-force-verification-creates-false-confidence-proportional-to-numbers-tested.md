Every number up to 2^68 (roughly 295 quintillion) satisfies the Collatz conjecture. This sounds overwhelmingly convincing until you learn that the Polya conjecture was similarly verified for an enormous range before a counterexample was found at approximately 1.845 × 10^361 — roughly 10^340 times larger than the Collatz search space.

This is a profound epistemological lesson: human intuition about 'large numbers of confirmations' is systematically miscalibrated. In any domain where the search space is infinite or astronomically large, even massive empirical verification can be worthless. The same principle applies to software testing, financial models, and any inductive reasoning — passing a billion tests doesn't guarantee the billion-and-first will pass.
