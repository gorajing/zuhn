Kennedy inverts the censorship argument: it's precisely because the N-word is explosive, volatile, and part of the soundtrack of racial terrorism that people need to understand its history. Refusing to study something because it's powerful is the opposite of taking it seriously.

James Baldwin didn't say 'I am not your negro' — he said the actual word, because he 'wasn't sworn to cover up anything. He wanted people to face the facts of American life.' This principle applies to any domain: the most important topics to study are often the most uncomfortable ones. Avoidance masquerading as sensitivity is actually a form of intellectual cowardice.
