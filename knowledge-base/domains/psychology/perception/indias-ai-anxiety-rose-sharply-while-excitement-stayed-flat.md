The AI Index 2026 Chapter 9 documents an important outlier in global sentiment: India registered the sharpest rise in AI nervousness of any country surveyed between 2024 and 2025, a 14 percentage point increase, with only a 2 point increase in excitement. This decoupling is unusual — the typical pattern is that anxiety and optimism rise together as public awareness grows. India's decoupling matters because India is the second-largest source of global AI talent (behind the US), the largest source of non-resident AI graduate students in the US (now being visa-restricted), and Microsoft and Amazon have both committed tens of billions to India AI infrastructure (Microsoft $17.5B, Amazon $35B through 2030). A country that is being positioned as a destination for AI investment simultaneously becoming the fastest-souring on AI sentiment is a signal that the expected growth story is encountering friction on the ground. Confidence is medium because one-year survey shifts can be noise, but the magnitude (+14 pp is over 4x the global average shift) is too large to dismiss, and the pattern suggests specific domestic triggers — possibly labor market impacts on Indian IT services, or specific AI incidents that changed framing.
