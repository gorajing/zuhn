Cuneiform writing emerged around 3500 BC and was still in use in the first century AD - over three millennia of continuous use. During this period, it was adapted to write multiple languages (Sumerian, Akkadian, Babylonian, Hittite) and served every purpose from accounting to literature to law.

This durability humbles modern information technology: our current digital systems have existed for decades, the internet for about 30 years, smartphones for about 15. Cuneiform persisted for 100x longer than any modern technology. The lesson: durability of information systems depends on simplicity of substrate (clay vs. silicon) and independence from power infrastructure.
