Humans intuitively judge distance on the flat maps they grew up with, causing systematic errors when reasoning about routes on a sphere. The most direct path from New York to Bangkok goes over the North Pole — counterintuitive on a Mercator projection but obvious on a globe.

This is a specific instance of a broader cognitive pattern: we reason using the representation we're given, not the underlying reality. Flat maps distort distance and direction; org charts distort influence and communication flow; financial spreadsheets distort non-linear dynamics. The corrective isn't to 'think harder' on the wrong representation — it's to switch representations entirely. Just as a polar-projection map makes the Bangkok route obvious, choosing the right abstraction makes previously hidden solutions visible.
