Every generation imagines the future as a modified present: flying cars, video phones, robot servants - all extensions of existing technology. The actually transformative changes (internet, smartphones, social media) were largely unimagined because they required conceptual leaps beyond existing frameworks.

This systematic imagination failure has practical implications: long-term planning based on extrapolation reliably misses the most important developments. The most impactful future changes will be things we cannot currently conceptualize, making flexibility and adaptability more valuable than specific predictions.
