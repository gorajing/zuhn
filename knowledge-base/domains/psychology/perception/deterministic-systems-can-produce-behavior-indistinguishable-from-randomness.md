When you take the logarithm of a Collatz sequence and remove the linear trend, the residual fluctuations are statistically indistinguishable from a random coin flip — geometric Brownian motion, the same model underlying stock market behavior. Yet the underlying rule (multiply by 3 and add 1, or divide by 2) is completely deterministic.

This has deep implications for how we interpret apparent randomness in any system. Stock markets, weather patterns, social dynamics, and neural activity may all exhibit 'randomness' that emerges from deterministic processes we simply can't untangle. The lesson: observing random-looking behavior tells you nothing about whether the underlying mechanism is random. Pseudorandomness and true randomness are observationally equivalent from within the system.
