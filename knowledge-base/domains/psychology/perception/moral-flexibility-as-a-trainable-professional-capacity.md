Bustamante draws a sharp distinction most people never consider: ethics are externally defined by professional codes (attorneys, doctors, teachers all have different ethical codes), while morals are internally held personal beliefs about right and wrong. Most people conflate the two.

CIA training teaches that while the organization has its own code of ethics (quite short, as an 'organization of last resort'), officers must develop moral flexibility — the ability to hold one thing as moral today and something different as equally moral tomorrow. This isn't amorality; it's the capacity to adapt personal convictions to mission requirements. The broader insight is that rigid moral frameworks can paralyze action in genuinely ambiguous situations, while the ability to recognize that context shapes what is 'right' enables more effective decision-making under pressure.
