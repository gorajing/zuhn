Donald Hoffman and colleagues used evolutionary game theory to prove that the probability of natural selection shaping any sensory system to perceive true properties of objective reality is precisely zero. This is not an approximation — it's a mathematical limit computed across all possible fitness payoff functions for any generic world structure (total orders, topologies, metrics). The only exception is for probability measures (sigma algebras), which must be preserved for any science to be possible.

The implications are radical: what we perceive is not a degraded or simplified version of reality, but a fitness-maximizing interface completely detached from objective reality — analogous to a computer's desktop GUI hiding the underlying voltages and transistors. Genetic algorithm simulations by Hoffman's graduate students confirmed that truth-tracking organisms never even appeared on the evolutionary stage; they were eliminated within a single generation by fitness-tracking organisms. This reframes all perceptual experience as adaptive fiction rather than approximate truth.
