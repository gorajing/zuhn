When asked whether looking at the Milky Way makes her feel small, Frebel offers a reframe that challenges the default existential response. Instead of smallness, she feels awe and belonging: 'Yes I'm small, but there are many other small things and lots of small things make one big whole.' She describes lying under the southern Milky Way and feeling like she's sitting in a 'galactic merry-go-round,' orbiting the center.

The shift from 'I am insignificant' to 'I am part of something enormous' is a fundamental perceptual reframe with practical applications. In any overwhelming context — a massive market, a complex codebase, a global problem — the default reaction is to feel small and paralyzed. The alternative frame: you are one node in a vast connected system, and your small contribution is part of how the whole thing works. The difference between these frames is the difference between paralysis and participation.
