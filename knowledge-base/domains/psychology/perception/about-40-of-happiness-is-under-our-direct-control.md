Research by psychologist Sonja Lyubomirsky breaks happiness into three components: approximately 50% is a biological set point determined by genes and inborn temperament, about 10% depends on current life circumstances, and the remaining 40% is under our direct control.

This 40% is the actionable insight. We can meaningfully move the needle on happiness by building conditions that promote it — primarily through investing in relationships. The research suggests asking specific diagnostic questions: Do I have enough connection? Do I have relationships that are warm and supportive? Do I have people who would show up in an emergency? The advice to 'never worry alone' — sharing concerns with trusted people — captures the practical application of this finding.
