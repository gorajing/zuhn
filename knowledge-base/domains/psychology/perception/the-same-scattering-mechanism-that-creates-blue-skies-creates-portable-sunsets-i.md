Aerogel's nanoscale structures scatter light according to Rayleigh scattering, where intensity scales as 1/wavelength^4. This preferentially scatters short (blue) wavelengths. Against a dark background, you see the scattered blue light (like looking at the sky). Against the blue sky, you see the transmitted light with blue removed — yellows and oranges, exactly like a sunset where atmosphere has scattered out the blue.

This demonstrates a key perceptual insight: the same physical phenomenon produces opposite visual experiences depending on whether you observe scattered or transmitted light. The mechanism is identical; only your observation angle changes the result. This applies broadly to how framing determines interpretation — the same underlying process can appear completely different depending on which output channel you attend to. What looks like contradictory evidence may be two views of the same mechanism.
