Psychological maturity requires self-awareness, emotional regulation, independent moral reasoning, and the capacity to tolerate uncertainty. These are difficult developmental achievements. What society actually requires is much simpler: showing up to work, paying bills, following rules, and maintaining social acceptability.

The gap between performed adulthood and genuine maturity explains many social phenomena: adults throwing tantrums, leaders making impulsive decisions, widespread inability to have difficult conversations, and the popularity of authoritarian thinking (which removes the burden of independent judgment). The performance of maturity has become so normalized that genuine maturity is barely recognized.
