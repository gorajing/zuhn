Before Torricelli, the question was 'why does nature prevent emptiness?' After him, it became 'why is there something rather than nothing?' This inversion of the default assumption is one of the most consequential reframes in the history of science. Earth is a 'tiny speck of dust floating through a vast expanse of an utterly silent, inhospitable void.'

This principle transfers to many domains: the productive question is often about why the exception exists, not why the default persists. In product development, the default state is that users don't engage -- you need to explain adoption, not disengagement. In organizational design, the default is entropy and disorder -- you need to explain coordination, not chaos. Identifying the true default state is a prerequisite for asking the right questions.
