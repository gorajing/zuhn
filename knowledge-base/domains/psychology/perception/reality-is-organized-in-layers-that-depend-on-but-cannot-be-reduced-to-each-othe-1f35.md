Carroll describes how the stability of a chair ultimately comes down to quantum field theory, which gives rise to chemistry, which gives rise to biology, and so on up the ladder. Each layer genuinely depends on the one below it — there is nothing magical happening.

But the crucial insight is that dependence does not imply reducibility. You cannot usefully study psychology by examining neurons, or biology by studying atoms. Each level requires its own concepts, vocabulary, and methods. This 'layered reality' framework resolves the false dichotomy between reductionism and emergence — both are true simultaneously. The layers depend on each other AND need to be taken seriously for their own sake.
