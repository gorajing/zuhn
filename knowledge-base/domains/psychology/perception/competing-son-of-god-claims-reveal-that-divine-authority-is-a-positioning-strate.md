The video opens with a striking parallel: Roman coins called Augustus 'son of God' at the exact time Jesus was born. Both figures used identical divine framing, but for opposite audiences. Augustus's claim reinforced existing power structures — the emperor's divine mandate justified the status quo. Jesus's claim subverted them — a poor Jewish carpenter as God's son was a radical inversion that resonated with the powerless.

This reveals that authority claims are not absolute but contextual. The same frame ('son of God') can reinforce or subvert depending on who wields it and who receives it. In modern contexts, this maps to how 'industry leader' positioning works differently for incumbents (reassuring) versus insurgents (where 'the underdog who actually understands you' positioning is more effective). The frame that wins depends on whether your audience benefits from or suffers under the current order.
