Seth describes consciousness research as facing a uniquely frustrating measurement problem. Unlike cosmology (things are far away) or particle physics (things are tiny), brains are accessible and human-sized — but overwhelmingly complex. With 86 billion neurons, no single technique captures the full picture.

fMRI measures metabolic byproducts of neural activity at second-scale resolution — far slower than the millisecond timescale of actual neural firing. EEG captures fast electrical signals but with such low spatial resolution it is like dangling a microphone over a city and trying to hear individual conversations. Direct electrode implantation is precise but requires clinical justification and can only sample thousands of neurons out of billions. The mathematical tools to handle even existing data complexity are inadequate — a gap where AI could eventually help.
