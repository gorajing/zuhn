The Banach-Tarski proof begins by assigning every point on the sphere a unique name derived from the sequence of rotations that reach it. This naming scheme — which converts an uncountable surface into a structured, manipulable set — is what makes the entire paradox possible. Without names, the points are an undifferentiated continuum. With names, they become separable, groupable, and rearrangeable.

This is the fundamental move of any knowledge system. An unnamed market is undifferentiated chaos. Segment it — name the segments by the 'rotation sequences' (behaviors, needs, contexts) that reach them — and suddenly you can decompose, rearrange, and recombine. The naming scheme isn't just a label; it's the structure that enables all subsequent operations. Choose the wrong naming scheme and the decomposition is useless. Choose the right one and operations that seemed impossible become mechanical.
