The myth that psychopathy is untreatable causes enormous harm. The main barrier to treatment is not the condition itself but that people with psychopathy often do not believe they need treatment. When they do recognize that their behavior is damaging their own life, validated approaches can help.

For adults, cognitive behavioral therapy helps overcome hostile attribution bias (interpreting ambiguous behavior as threatening) and teaches new behavioral responses. Psychostimulants used for ADHD can reduce impulsiveness and disinhibition. For children at risk, parent management training (including PCIT) coaches parents on managing disruptive behavior. The psychopathyis.org website provides evidence-based treatment guidance for clinicians, parents, and affected adults.
