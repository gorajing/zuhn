Starbucks's pricing has nothing to do with coffee quality. The $6 latte is a $0.50 coffee with a $5.50 experience premium: comfortable seating, wifi, ambient music, the social permission to occupy space, and the ritual of a customized order. Starbucks sells belonging and routine, not caffeine.

For startups, this insight unlocks premium pricing in commodity markets: any product category can support premium pricing if the business creates a compelling 'third place' or experience context. WeWork applied this to office space. SoulCycle applied it to exercise. The key is that the physical product is merely the ticket to the experience — and experiences can command any price the market will bear.
