The straw collapsing, gravity seeming to be suspended, air rushing into empty spaces -- all these phenomena that 'proved' nature abhors a vacuum were actually just atmospheric pressure at work. Torricelli's mercury tube experiment didn't just disprove Aristotle; it provided a completely new framework where the vacuum is nature's default state and it's the presence of air that requires explanation.

This is a masterclass in paradigm shifts: the winning move wasn't to argue against the old framework point by point, but to provide a new framework that explained the same observations plus new ones. The phrase 'we live at the bottom of an ocean of air' instantly made atmospheric pressure intuitive. When challenging an established view, the most powerful approach is often to provide a better frame, not more evidence against the old one.
