The observable universe appears vastly larger than the universe we can physically interact with. Light from galaxies that have already crossed the cosmological horizon continues to arrive because it was emitted billions of years ago when those galaxies were much closer. This creates a perceptual illusion: the night sky suggests a universe far more accessible than it actually is.

This see-but-can't-touch principle generalizes beyond cosmology. In markets, media, and technology, we routinely conflate what we can observe with what we can influence or access. Visibility creates an implicit promise of availability that reality doesn't honor. Recognizing the gap between observable scope and actionable scope is a critical calibration for realistic planning in any domain.
