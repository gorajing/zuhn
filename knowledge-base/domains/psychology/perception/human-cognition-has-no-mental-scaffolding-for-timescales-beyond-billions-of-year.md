The video attempts to convey 10^100 years by constructing an elaborate analogy: an hourglass with one grain per particle in the universe, dropping one grain every 10 billion years — and even after all sand falls, less than 1% of the black hole's lifetime has passed. The fact that even this stacked analogy barely registers emotionally reveals a hard limit in human cognition. We evolved to reason about timescales from seconds to decades; anything beyond ~10,000 years is already abstract. This has practical consequences: climate change operates on century scales and feels abstract; compound interest over 40 years is intellectually known but emotionally unfelt; AI capability timelines of 5-20 years get either dismissed or sensationalized because we lack calibrated intuition for that range. The implication is that for any decision involving long timescales, we must distrust our intuitive sense of urgency and instead rely on structured reasoning and quantitative models.
