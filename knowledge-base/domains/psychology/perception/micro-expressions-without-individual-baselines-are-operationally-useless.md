Bustamante directly contradicts the popular 'read anyone's body language' industry, calling it 'baloney' without individual baselines. He demonstrates this live with Lex: most micro-expression readers would flag Lex's rightward eye movement as referencing facts, but Bustamante recognized it was actually Lex's creative thinking pattern—an eye dominance habit, not a truthfulness indicator.

The CIA's approach combines three elements: facial expressions, body language/nonverbal cues, AND effective elicitation techniques that the interviewer controls. The baseline must account for education, culture, and life experience. Even polygraphs work this way—they don't detect lies but detect variance from physiological baselines, giving interviewers additional data to direct questioning. This framework transforms lie detection from a parlor trick into what Bustamante calls an 'art based in a foundation of science'—useful only when you invest time building the individual model first.
