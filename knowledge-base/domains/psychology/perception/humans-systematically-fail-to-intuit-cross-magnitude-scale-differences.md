The video demonstrates that people cannot intuit how insignificant 7 billion humans are relative to Earth's mass (6.588 sextillion tons). Even aggregating all human mass produces only 1/100th of a hydrogen atom's width of displacement. This isn't just a physics curiosity — it reveals a deep cognitive limitation. Humans evolved to reason about quantities in the hundreds or low thousands, not across 15+ orders of magnitude.

This has practical implications for any domain requiring scale reasoning: startup founders misjudging market sizes, investors miscalculating risk probabilities, or engineers underestimating infrastructure loads. The antidote is concrete anchoring — translating abstract large numbers into relatable comparisons (fitting all humans into Los Angeles, or comparing TNT equivalents to Earth's mass). Any time you encounter a claim involving very large or very small numbers, your intuition is almost certainly wrong without explicit calibration.
