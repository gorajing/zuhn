Orwell's work consistently shows that the most destructive acts are committed by people convinced of their own moral righteousness. Moral certainty eliminates the self-questioning that prevents excess: if you're absolutely sure you're right, any means becomes justified by the righteous end.

The practical application is treating moral certainty as a warning signal rather than a virtue. The person who says 'I know I'm doing the right thing' has eliminated the very doubt that would prevent them from doing the wrong thing. Ethical behavior requires ongoing uncertainty about whether one's actions are truly good.
