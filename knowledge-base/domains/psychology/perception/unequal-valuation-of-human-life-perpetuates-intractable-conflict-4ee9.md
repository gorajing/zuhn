Wright's pivotal question came from a concrete event: Israel traded 1,000 Palestinian prisoners for one captured Israeli soldier, Gilad Shalit. This led him to ask: how does one life come to equal a thousand? He argues this devaluation and overvaluation of human life is at the root of why the conflict endures.

When one side sees itself as a thousand times more valuable, it cannot extend genuine empathy. When the other side experiences itself as radically devalued, it increases the likelihood of violent action. This asymmetry makes peace structurally impossible. The insight extends beyond geopolitics: any system where stakeholders assign radically unequal value to different participants will produce entrenched, irresolvable conflict.
