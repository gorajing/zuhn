Graham resolves the philosophical puzzle of aesthetic quality by drawing an analogy to physics: if every particle a mass interacts with behaves as if it has mass m, then it has mass m. Similarly, if most humans respond to certain art in similar ways, that response pattern constitutes a real property of the art.

This reframing dissolves many false dichotomies. A vaccine's effectiveness isn't dismissed as subjective just because immunity happens inside individual immune systems. The same logic applies to art, design, writing, and any domain where human response matters. The key variable is the degree of commonality among observers — people share enough cognitive architecture that their aesthetic responses aren't random, even though they're not identical.
