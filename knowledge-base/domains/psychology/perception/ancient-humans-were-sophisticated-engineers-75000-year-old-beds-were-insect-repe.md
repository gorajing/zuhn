Kean describes how ancient humans 75,000 years ago built beds with an ash layer (tick-proof mattress) topped with aromatic leaves (mosquito repellent), and carried water in ostrich egg canteens that self-cooled through evaporative pores. Obsidian tools were sharper than modern surgical scalpels. These were not crude survival hacks but multi-layered engineering solutions.

The tendency to underestimate past civilizations mirrors a broader cognitive bias: we systematically underestimate the sophistication of systems we don't fully understand. This applies to legacy codebases, traditional business practices, and cultural traditions -- 'primitive' solutions that have survived a long time often contain encoded wisdom that isn't immediately visible to modern observers.
