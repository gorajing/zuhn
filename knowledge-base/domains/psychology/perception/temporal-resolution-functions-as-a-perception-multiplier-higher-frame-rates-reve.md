At 30fps, an AK-47 firing underwater looks like a single violent event. At 27,450fps, it separates into three distinct phenomena with different physical causes, different spatial patterns, and different temporal signatures. The jump in temporal resolution didn't add detail to a known phenomenon — it revealed entirely new phenomena that could not be inferred from lower-resolution observation.

This is a step-function pattern in observation: resolution improvements don't produce linear improvements in understanding. They're largely useless until they cross a threshold, at which point they reveal categorically new information. This applies to data analysis, scientific instrumentation, and even business metrics. Sampling your conversion funnel daily versus hourly might show the same trend with more noise. But sampling it per-second might reveal that 'gradual decline' is actually three distinct drop-off events caused by different bugs. The actionable insight is to ask not 'can I get more resolution?' but 'what phenomena might exist below my current resolution threshold?'
