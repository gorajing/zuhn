The video introduces a fundamental principle from the origin of life: the 'law of stability.' In the primordial void, random energy jolts caused simple molecules to combine. Most combinations were unstable and fell apart immediately. But occasionally, a combination happened to be more stable than its components. These stable configurations persisted, and when enough accumulated, they themselves could combine into even more complex structures.

This is a powerful mental model for building anything: habits, companies, knowledge systems. Unstable foundations (unclear values, bad unit economics, unreliable processes) collapse before they can compound. Stable ones persist long enough to become building blocks for higher-order complexity. The video's simulation shows that reducing a mega-blob's dissolution rate from 95% to 1% changes the void from mostly empty to mostly full. Small improvements in stability yield exponential increases in what can be built on top.
