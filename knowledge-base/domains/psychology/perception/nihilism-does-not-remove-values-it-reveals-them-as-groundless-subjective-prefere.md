O'Connor corrects the common misconception that nihilism means having no desires or motivation. A nihilist who learns that thirst is merely an evolved biological drive does not stop being thirsty — they still drink. Nihilism is a bird's-eye analysis that recognizes all values, desires, and purposes ultimately trace back either to arbitrary evolutionary drives or to an infinite regress of contingent reasons — neither of which provides objective grounding.

This explains why nihilism correlates with depression but does not necessitate it: the recognition that your actions are ultimately meaningless at the cosmic level does not eliminate the subjective experience of wanting things. You still love, create, and strive — you just recognize that none of it is anchored to anything beyond your own preferences. The practical question becomes: can you find a self-justifying principle (raising children, pursuing truth, serving God) where the question 'but why?' feels inappropriate to ask further?
