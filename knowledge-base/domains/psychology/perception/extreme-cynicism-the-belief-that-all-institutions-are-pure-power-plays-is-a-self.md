Harari identifies a surprising ideological convergence: both the extreme left (Marxism) and extreme right (Trumpism) share the view that all social interactions are fundamentally power struggles, all institutions are conspiracies, and asking 'is it true?' is naive — the only real question is 'who benefits?' This cynicism is not merely pessimistic; it's structurally destructive.

Democracy requires trust to function — you give power to someone for four years on condition they return it. If all trust is destroyed, the only remaining mechanism for social cohesion is terror, which is what dictatorships provide. Would-be dictators therefore systematically attack institutional trust not as a side effect but as a strategy: destroy faith in courts, media, universities, and elections, and dictatorship becomes the only remaining option. The factual error in this cynicism is that people are not purely power-motivated — even powerful people genuinely care about truth — and competing interests prevent the kind of monolithic conspiracies populists imagine.
