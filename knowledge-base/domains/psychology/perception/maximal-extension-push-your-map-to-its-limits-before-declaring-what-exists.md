Schwarzschild only mapped the 'northern hemisphere' of his solution. It took decades for others to realize the math allowed negative coordinates -- a whole 'southern hemisphere' and beyond. The maximally extended diagram revealed white holes, wormholes, and parallel universes hidden inside the simplest possible case.

The principle applies broadly: when you have a model or framework, explore its full mathematical or logical extent before concluding what it does or doesn't contain. Premature boundary-drawing means you miss structure that was always there. The analogy to the northern/southern hemisphere is particularly useful -- it makes the abstract idea of maximal extension immediately intuitive.
