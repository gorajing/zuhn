Hervey Cleckley's 'Mask of Sanity' concept captures psychopathy's core: these individuals appear completely normal, even 'super normal,' while concealing profound deficits in emotional experience and genuine caring. You cannot reliably identify psychopathy from brief interactions — claims about eye contact 'tells' don't hold up scientifically.

What reveals psychopathy is patterns of behavior: exploiting others without remorse, easy and purposeless lying, cheating without caring about betrayal, and especially proactive aggression (deliberate, goal-directed aggression like threatening to reveal secrets to get what you want). Women with psychopathy tend toward emotional aggression and manipulation rather than physical violence. The most unnerving aspect is that these individuals can feign emotions flawlessly — tears that stop like a switch when they realize the display isn't working.
