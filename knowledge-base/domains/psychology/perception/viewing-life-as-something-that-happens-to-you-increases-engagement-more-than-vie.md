The control orientation treats life as a project to manage: plan, execute, optimize. This creates constant anxiety about whether you're managing correctly. The experiential orientation treats life as something to be present for: observe, respond, appreciate. This creates engagement because each moment is interesting rather than evaluated.

This isn't passivity or fatalism - you still make decisions and take action. The shift is in orientation: from 'I must make the right things happen' (anxious control) to 'let me respond well to what's happening' (engaged presence). The irony is that engaged presence often produces better outcomes than anxious control because it allows for flexibility, creativity, and serendipity.
