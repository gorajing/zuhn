Hormozi asks whether the hater hates the target 'for committing to something or yourself for lacking the guts to commit to anything.' He's describing a projection mechanism — the visible commitment of others activates the hater's own unresolved avoidance, and the easiest relief is to label the committed person as foolish. This frame is useful both for receiving criticism (don't absorb it as a data point about your work) and for self-awareness (if you notice yourself mocking someone's commitment, ask what commitment of your own you're avoiding).
