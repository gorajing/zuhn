Graham distinguishes between naughtiness and evil: the best founders aren't amoral, they're selectively rebellious. They care about getting the big moral questions right but delight in circumventing arbitrary systems and proprieties. Sam Altman's suggestion to ask applicants about a time they 'hacked something to their advantage' became one of YC's most predictive application questions.

This trait may be a downstream effect of imagination — if you can see that many rules are arbitrary conventions rather than fundamental truths, you're more likely to find creative solutions that others miss. It also suggests a useful hiring and co-founder screening heuristic: look for people who have a history of finding clever workarounds within systems, not people who either blindly follow all rules or destructively break important ones.
