Hormozi reframes the comparison between average and elite performers. The conventional framing asks 'what do they do that I don't?' — implying you need to add something. The reframe is 'what do they not do that I do?' — implying you need to subtract something.

This is a powerful identity-level shift. It means you don't need more willpower, talent, or information. You need fewer social obligations, fewer meetings, fewer dopamine hits, fewer projects. The 'off switch' metaphor is key: most people have an off switch for intense work (they check their phone, attend events, respond to every message). Champions have removed that switch entirely for defined periods.
