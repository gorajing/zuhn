The Summer Founders Program revealed that young people commonly labeled as lazy worked 'ridiculously hard' when given real ownership. A researcher studying the cohort found exceptional work ethic was the single common trait, and founders consistently met every commitment — contradicting stereotypes about their age group.

Graham's insight is that both laziness and irresponsibility are often environmental artifacts, not character traits. People seem lazy because the work is pointless, and irresponsible because they're given no real power. The implication extends beyond startups: putting people in positions of genuine autonomy may reliably surface capabilities that hierarchical structures suppress. As Graham puts it, 'throw them off a cliff, and most will find on the way down that they have wings.'
