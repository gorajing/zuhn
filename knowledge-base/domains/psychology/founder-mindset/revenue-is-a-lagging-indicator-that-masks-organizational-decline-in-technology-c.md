Graham makes a sharp observation about Apple post-Jobs: revenues may continue to rise, but 'as Microsoft shows, revenue is a lagging indicator in the technology business.' This is a deeper point than it first appears. It means that the most commonly used metric for corporate health — revenue — is actively misleading in technology because it measures the monetization of past innovations, not the capacity for future ones.

This creates a dangerous feedback loop for incumbents. Rising revenue reinforces the belief that the current strategy is working, which reduces urgency to innovate, which further erodes innovative capacity — all while the numbers look great. The implication for founders is that incumbent financial strength is not the barrier to entry it appears to be. A company showing record revenues may already be hollowed out creatively. And for anyone evaluating their own organization, the question isn't 'are we growing?' but 'are we still creating new things?' — a much harder question to answer honestly.
