Hormozi makes a counterintuitive social dynamics argument: the people whose opinion actually matters for your career advancement — those ahead of you — are actively repelled by status spending. They want to see you 'eating shit, living poor, learning and hustling.' A $200K watch impresses nobody in that circle.

This creates an ironic trap: status spending is designed to impress peers, but peer approval has near-zero career leverage. The mentors and investors who could change your trajectory use frugality and learning intensity as screening signals. When they see someone spending on status, they categorize them as 'doesn't get it' and won't associate with them. The very behavior intended to gain respect destroys access to the people whose respect matters.
