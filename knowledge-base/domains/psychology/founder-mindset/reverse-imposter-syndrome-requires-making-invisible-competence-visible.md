Traditional imposter syndrome is feeling inadequate despite being competent. Reverse imposter syndrome flips this: you know you're skilled, but others consistently undervalue your contributions. Wes Kao's framing suggests the fix isn't internal confidence work but external perception management.

The actionable insight is that competence alone doesn't create recognition — you must make your strategic reasoning visible to others. This means narrating the 'why' behind decisions, framing work in terms of impact rather than effort, and proactively aligning others' perception with your actual ability. For founders especially, this is critical when dealing with investors, board members, or co-founders who may default to underestimating quiet operators.
