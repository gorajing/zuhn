Perfectly calibrated self-assessment sounds optimal in theory but fails in practice because creators face skepticism from two directions: other people's dismissiveness and their own internal critic. A slight positive bias in self-confidence compensates for these asymmetric headwinds. Graham also notes that overestimating the importance of what you're working on has a similar corrective effect—if you judge 20% progress toward a goal you believe is twice as important, your expected-value estimate ends up roughly correct despite both components being wrong. This suggests that certain cognitive biases, usually seen as flaws, serve as adaptive error-correction in creative work.
