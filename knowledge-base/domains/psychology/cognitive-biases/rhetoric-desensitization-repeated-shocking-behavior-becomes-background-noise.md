Ben Shapiro articulates a key psychological mechanism: when people first encounter shocking rhetoric, it produces a strong reaction, but years of repetition cause the brain to adjust — treating it as background noise, like tinnitus. This explains why Trump's divisive statements lost their political impact over time while Biden's comparatively milder rhetoric felt more jarring against his unity-branding baseline.

This has broad implications beyond politics. In any domain — startup culture, team leadership, content creation — the shock value of any signal decays with repetition. The person who consistently pushes boundaries eventually gets their behavior 'baked into the cake,' while someone who breaks from an established pattern (even mildly) receives disproportionate scrutiny. This is essentially the hedonic treadmill applied to rhetoric: the reference point shifts, and only deviations from the new baseline register.
