Graham traces a consistent pattern in intellectual history: Copernicus moved Earth from the center of the universe, Darwin removed divine authorship of humanity, Dawkins showed we aren't even the protagonists of our own genes. Each step required discarding the naive assumption that reality is organized around us.

This suggests a generative search heuristic for original ideas: look for places where our current models still implicitly assume human centrality. The resistance these discoveries faced — 'ferocious opposition' in Graham's words — is itself a signal that the idea is onto something real, not a reason to back away.

For thinkers and researchers, this reframes where to direct attention: not into established paradigms but into the places where we've not yet asked 'why do we assume it's about us?'
