The standard Western history narrative says Rome fell in 476 CE when Germanic chieftain Odoacer deposed the last Western emperor. But the Eastern Roman Empire — Byzantium — continued for nearly another millennium, producing Justinian's legal code, preserving Greek learning, and projecting enormous military and cultural power until Constantinople fell in 1453.

This isn't just a historical curiosity — it reveals a deep cognitive bias. We accept collapse narratives because they're dramatic and tidy, while ignoring continuity because it's mundane. The same bias appears in business ('company X is dead') and technology ('framework Y is over'). Recognizing this pattern means questioning every 'death' narrative: what's actually continuing that the dominant story erases? The lesson is to look for the Byzantine continuation in every supposed collapse.
