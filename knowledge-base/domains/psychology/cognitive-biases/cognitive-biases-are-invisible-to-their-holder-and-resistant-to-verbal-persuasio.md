After Destin struggled for 20 minutes on a normal bike in Amsterdam, then suddenly began riding normally, onlookers assumed he had been faking the entire time. Despite witnessing the struggle firsthand, they couldn't accept that an adult could genuinely forget how to ride a bicycle — their cognitive framework ('you never forget how to ride a bike') was so strong it overrode direct observational evidence. This illustrates a meta-level truth about cognitive biases: they don't just distort perception, they prevent you from recognizing the distortion even when someone points it out. As Destin concludes: 'Be very careful how you interpret things because you're looking at the world with a bias whether you think you are or not.' This has profound implications for teaching, persuasion, and debate — you cannot simply tell someone their bias exists and expect them to see it. The bias itself blocks the recognition.
