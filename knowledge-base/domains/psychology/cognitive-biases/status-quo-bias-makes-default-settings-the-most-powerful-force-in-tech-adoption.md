Microsoft's strategy with Bing reveals that status quo bias isn't just a minor cognitive quirk — it's the single most profitable behavioral pattern in consumer technology. Microsoft explicitly acknowledges that 'ignorance and apathy are on their side.' Users don't know how to change defaults, or don't care enough to do so, making the default option the de facto choice for hundreds of millions of people.

This extends far beyond search engines. Internet Explorer dominated for years despite being widely disliked simply because it shipped with Windows. The same pattern applies to every default: email clients, browsers, maps, voice assistants. For product builders, this means the battle for user attention is often won or lost before the user ever makes a conscious choice. Designing for default placement — or designing experiences so compelling they overcome default inertia — should be a first-order strategic concern, not an afterthought.
