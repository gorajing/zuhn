The pre-WWII Western world largely assumed that scientific, industrial, and technological progress naturally carried moral progress with it. Germany — the nation of Goethe, Beethoven, and cutting-edge science — used its industrial capacity for systematic genocide, proving these dimensions are orthogonal.

This cognitive bias persists today in tech discourse: the assumption that more powerful AI, more advanced biotech, or more connected systems inherently make things better. Recognizing that capability and ethics advance on independent tracks is essential for evaluating new technologies honestly. The bias is especially dangerous because it causes societies to under-invest in governance and ethical frameworks precisely when technological power is accelerating fastest.
