The standard Western narrative places the 'fall of Rome' at 476 CE when the last western emperor was deposed. But the Roman Empire — its laws, institutions, culture, and governance — continued thriving in the east as the Byzantine Empire until 1453. The 'fall' narrative persists because humans are biased toward clean decline-and-collapse stories over messy transformation-and-continuation realities.

This cognitive bias appears everywhere: people declared 'the death of PC' when mobile rose, 'the end of retail' when e-commerce grew, and 'the fall of traditional media' when streaming emerged. In each case, what actually happened was geographic or modal shift, not collapse. The original form continued in transformed ways alongside the new. Recognizing this framing bias helps you spot transformation opportunities that others misread as decline — and avoid the mistake of abandoning functional systems just because the narrative says they're 'dying'.
