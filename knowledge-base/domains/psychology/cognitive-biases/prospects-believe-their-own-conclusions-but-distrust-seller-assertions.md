Hormozi frames this as a core psychological asymmetry: 'a prospect believes almost nothing about what you say and almost everything that they say.' This explains why traditional feature-benefit selling underperforms — the seller is the least trusted source of information in the conversation. The implication is that the salesperson's job isn't to tell prospects they're a good fit but to ask questions that lead the prospect to say 'I think I'm a good fit.' This breadcrumbing toward the logical conclusion leverages the well-documented self-persuasion effect, where people are more committed to conclusions they reached themselves. The practical corollary: 'do you have any questions?' is the worst question in sales because it hands control to the prospect and invites objections rather than guiding toward self-discovered commitment.
