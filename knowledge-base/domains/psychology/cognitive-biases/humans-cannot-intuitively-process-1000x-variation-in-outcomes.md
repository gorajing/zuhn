Graham identifies a cognitive limitation that persists despite awareness: humans simply cannot assimilate the reality of 1000x variation in outcomes. Whether this expectation of bounded variation is innate or culturally learned, the result is the same — our intuitions about what constitutes reasonable variation are off by orders of magnitude.

This explains why even sophisticated investors who intellectually understand power laws still behave as if outcomes are more normally distributed. It's not a knowledge gap but a perception gap — similar to how knowing about optical illusions doesn't make them disappear. The practical consequence is that any domain governed by power-law distributions (venture capital, content creation, scientific research) will systematically attract strategies calibrated for the wrong distribution, creating persistent opportunities for those who can override their intuitions.
