When presented with an exponential sequence, humans process absolute values rather than ratios. A country with 60 cases feels '100x better' than one with 6,000 — but on an exponential curve, 60 is just a month behind 6,000. The gap between 'small' and 'catastrophic' is purely a matter of time, not trajectory.

This anchoring failure has direct implications beyond epidemics. In startups, early revenue growth looks trivially small in absolute terms even when the underlying ratio is strong. In AI capability curves, people dismiss small benchmark improvements without recognizing the multiplicative dynamic. The fix is simple but requires discipline: always look at ratios between successive periods, not absolute values.
