Dunning-Kruger operates through a metacognitive deficit: the skills required to produce correct judgments are the same skills required to recognize correct judgments. Someone who lacks understanding in a domain also lacks the ability to recognize their own lack of understanding.

Conversely, increasing expertise reveals the vast landscape of what you don't know, producing appropriate humility. This creates a social problem: the loudest, most confident voices in any discussion are statistically the least informed, while the most informed voices are quieter and more hedged, making them less persuasive in public discourse.
