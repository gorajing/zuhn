Chamath identifies that quantum computing perception has oscillated between two extremes: distant science fiction or imminent all-purpose supercomputer. This binary framing is a general cognitive pattern that applies to any emerging technology — AI, gene therapy, fusion energy all suffer the same distortion.

The gap between 'technically possible' and 'practically achievable' is where most confusion and poor decision-making lives. The antidote is what Chamath calls contextualization: understanding how hardware capabilities, algorithm availability, and realistic timelines interact as a system rather than evaluating any single dimension in isolation. This systems-level assessment resists compression into binary narratives and requires holding multiple constraints in mind simultaneously.
