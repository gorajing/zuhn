The video reveals that consumers and municipal leaders believed plastic consumption was 'guilt-free' simply because they performed the ritual of putting items in a recycling bin. Nobody downstream verified outcomes—most plastic ended up incinerated or landfilled even during the system's supposed golden era.

This is a specific cognitive pattern: when a visible action (sorting trash) is strongly associated with a desired outcome (sustainability), the action itself becomes the proof of the outcome. Verification feels redundant because the ritual feels complete. This same pattern appears in corporate compliance (filling out checklists vs. actual security), health (taking supplements vs. measuring biomarkers), and software (writing tests vs. verifying they test the right things). The fix is always the same: measure outcomes, not inputs.
