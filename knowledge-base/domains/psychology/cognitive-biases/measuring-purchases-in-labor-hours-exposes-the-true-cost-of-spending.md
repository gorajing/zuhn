Hormozi describes a simple reframing: if you earn $20/hour, a $100 shirt actually costs 6 hours of work after taxes. Asking 'would I work all day for this shirt?' changes the emotional calculus of purchasing decisions. He observed this pattern at Smoothie King where coworkers earning $43/shift would spend $15 on lunch — effectively working 6 hours for only $20 of actual take-home.

This mental model works because it converts abstract dollar amounts into concrete time costs, bypassing the psychological disconnect between money and effort. It's more effective than traditional budgeting because it triggers loss aversion at the point of purchase rather than requiring discipline against a spreadsheet. The framework scales with income but remains psychologically grounding at any level.
