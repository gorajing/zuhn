Self-presentation bias causes people to overvalue packaging even when the substance is strong. For founders who default to brevity and depth over spectacle, this creates a systematic misread: investors interpret minimalism as lack of strategy, teams mistake low hype for low ambition, and peers miss the signal entirely.

This is distinct from simple obscurity. The bias actively generates false narratives — silence gets interpreted as stagnation. In opaque systems, the world assumes nothing is happening. This means under-communication isn't a neutral act; it's a negative signal in ecosystems optimized for optics. The antidote isn't performing louder but designing 'authentic visibility' — selecting meaningful signals and showing them with intention.
