Smith observes a recurring pattern: every couple of weeks, someone publishes a big post about how AI is changing everything, it goes viral, everyone discusses it for days, then the cycle repeats. This periodicity suggests the phenomenon is more about collective anxiety processing than information transmission — each post functions as a fresh anchoring event that temporarily coordinates attention and fear, regardless of whether it contains genuinely new information. The Citrini post follows the same template as Matt Shumer's 'Something Big is Happening' weeks earlier. For decision-makers, recognizing this as a social-psychological pattern rather than an evidence accumulation process helps maintain rational evaluation of AI's actual trajectory amid the noise.
