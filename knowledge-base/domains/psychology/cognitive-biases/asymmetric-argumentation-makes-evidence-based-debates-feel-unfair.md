The video identifies a structural asymmetry in the vaccine debate: the pro-vaccine side argues with studies and statistics, while the anti-vaccine side uses gut feeling, anecdotes, and misinformation. This isn't a level playing field because emotional narratives are psychologically stickier than statistical evidence. One parent's story about their child's adverse reaction after vaccination is more persuasive than a table showing 120 serious side effects per 10 million vaccinations. This pattern recurs in any domain where evidence-based positions compete against emotionally compelling narratives — climate change, economic policy, technology risk. The insight that 'screaming at people' doesn't work suggests that effective counter-messaging must meet people on emotional ground rather than simply presenting more data.
