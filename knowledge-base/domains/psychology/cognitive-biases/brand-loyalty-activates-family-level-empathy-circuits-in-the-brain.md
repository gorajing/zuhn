Research by Michael Platt's team at UPenn used MRI to observe iPhone and Samsung users hearing brand news. Apple customers showed brain empathy responses toward Apple identical to how people respond to family members — positive news triggered warmth, negative news triggered distress.

This finding reframes brand loyalty from a marketing phenomenon to a neurological one. It suggests that successful brands don't just earn preference — they literally rewire social bonding circuits. The implication for anyone building products or evaluating their own purchasing decisions is that 'rational consumer choice' is largely a fiction once identity attachment forms.
