Many people protect their beliefs by avoiding opposing arguments, creating a fragile confidence that shatters upon first contact with serious challenge. Genuine confidence requires understanding the strongest counter-arguments and having coherent responses.

The test: can you articulate the opposing position well enough that someone who holds it would say 'yes, that's what I believe'? If not, your disagreement is with a phantom rather than the actual argument, and your confidence is based on ignorance of the challenge rather than mastery of the subject.
