Overt narcissism is relatively easy to identify: grandiosity, attention-seeking, obvious self-centeredness. Covert narcissism achieves the same core goals (attention, validation, control) through opposite-appearing behaviors: victimhood, self-deprecation, passive aggression, and emotional manipulation.

The covert narcissist's victimhood narrative makes confrontation nearly impossible: challenging them confirms their victimhood story. This creates a particularly insidious dynamic in close relationships where the partner or friend is trapped between their own perception (something is wrong) and the narcissist's narrative (they're the victim of your cruelty).
