When people look back at history, they see civil rights and gay marriage as inevitable victories, reinforcing the belief that 'the arc of history bends toward justice.' But this involves a massive selection effect: we only label the wins as 'rights' in retrospect. Abortion rights have stagnated for decades, affirmative action was struck down with broad public support, and immigration policy is far more restrictive than 140 years ago. The losses don't get labeled as 'rights that should have been,' so they vanish from the narrative.

This survivorship bias is dangerous because it encourages passivity — if history is on your side, why strategize? The communist movement made this exact error, treating Marxist historical determinism as quasi-religious prophecy. Movement leaders who actually won (civil rights, gay marriage) combined moral conviction with savvy strategy, compromise, and timing. The arc didn't bend itself; people bent it through deliberate action.
