Human alarm systems for detecting wasteful behavior evolved in pre-industrial contexts where self-indulgence was the primary threat. Spending visibly on luxuries triggers immediate guilt and discomfort — most people would feel deeply uncomfortable watching TV all day.

But the modern world has created new categories of waste that mimic productive or virtuous activity. These traps are more dangerous precisely because they don't trigger our innate alarms. The implication is that anyone relying solely on 'feeling guilty' as their compass for detecting waste is using an obsolete instrument. You need deliberately constructed, learned detection systems for the new category of threats.
