Amazon Prime and Costco both leverage sunk-cost psychology, but with critically different mechanics. Prime users self-sort — each individually calculates whether the subscription saves enough money. This means Prime members are already price-conscious comparison shoppers who opted in rationally. The sunk-cost effect adds loyalty on top of an already-rational decision.

Costco's mandatory fee applies the sunk-cost trigger universally. Every single customer has already paid $60-120 before making their first purchase, creating a uniform psychological pressure to justify the investment by shopping exclusively at Costco. This eliminates the comparison-shopping behavior entirely — 'I've already paid, so I should use it' becomes the default heuristic rather than 'let me check if this is cheaper elsewhere.' The mandatory nature removes the rational opt-in filter, making the behavioral effect broader and less susceptible to being unwound by price comparison.
