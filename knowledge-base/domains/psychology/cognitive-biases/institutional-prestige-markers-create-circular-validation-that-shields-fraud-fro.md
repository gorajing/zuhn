Javice's fraud exploited a fundamental vulnerability in institutional trust networks: credibility markers are treated as transitive. JP Morgan didn't just evaluate Frank — they evaluated a Forbes 30 Under 30 honoree with a Wharton degree, $20M in VC backing, and years of favorable press coverage. Each of these legitimacy markers was itself based on prior unverified claims, creating a circular validation chain where no single institution had actually verified the underlying facts. This is the same mechanism that enabled Theranos and FTX — once you accumulate enough social proof, new institutions substitute that proof for independent verification. The insight for any evaluator is that prestige credentials should increase rather than decrease the rigor of direct verification, precisely because they create a false sense that someone else already did the work.
