Both Costco and Amazon Prime exploit the sunk-cost fallacy: once you've paid $60-120, shopping elsewhere feels like wasting money you've already spent. Customers stop comparing prices and automatically default to the paid-for option. This is psychologically distinct from loyalty points or discounts, which require ongoing calculation of value.

The critical difference is that Costco's fee is mandatory while Prime is optional. Optional fees create self-selection where only price-sensitive customers who've done the math subscribe. Mandatory fees create a universal behavioral anchor — every member experiences the sunk-cost effect regardless of their initial motivation for joining. For anyone designing pricing, the implication is that upfront commitment fees can be more effective loyalty mechanisms than ongoing rewards, because they exploit an automatic cognitive bias rather than requiring rational evaluation.
