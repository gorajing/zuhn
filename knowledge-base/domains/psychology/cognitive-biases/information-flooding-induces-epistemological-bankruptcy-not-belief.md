Harris references Hannah Arendt's insight from 'The Origins of Totalitarianism': sustained lying at scale is not designed to get people to believe specific falsehoods — it's designed to get people to believe nothing. The target state is epistemological bankruptcy, where citizens conclude that understanding reality is impossible or not their responsibility. This is Steve Bannon's 'flood the zone with shit' strategy made explicit.

The mechanism works because humans have finite epistemic bandwidth. When every claim is contested and every fact has a counter-fact, the rational response for a busy person is to disengage entirely. This benefits authoritarians because passive disengagement creates the same political space as active support — it removes opposition. The practical defense is not trying to counter every false claim but maintaining a small number of high-confidence epistemic anchors and recognizing that the feeling of 'who knows what's true anymore' is itself the intended effect, not a neutral observation.
