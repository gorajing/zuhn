Every few months, bright nerds in San Francisco try to 'hack' themselves into becoming charismatic and persuasive, and the attempt almost invariably backfires. The problem is selection bias in techniques: methods that make you feel transformed are far more common than methods that actually transform you. And the most dangerous self-improvement parasites hijack your ability to distinguish real change from performed change.

The uncanny valley is deep — a normal person unconvincingly performing confidence is worse than a normal person being themselves. Adams' constant announcements of 'I just hypnotized you!' undermined rather than demonstrated his claimed persuasive mastery. This suggests that authentic mastery of social skills, if achievable, requires internalizing them deeply enough that you never need to announce them. The announcement itself is the tell that the transformation is incomplete.
