The Selfish Gene framework reveals that behaviors appearing strategic in nature -- like squirrels giving alarm calls or penguins being timid -- aren't the result of conscious planning. They're traits that happened to increase gene survival and were thus copied. As the video states: 'What may look like intention is just simple chemistry that happens to work well and propagates.'

This is a direct challenge to the narrative fallacy in business. We retrospectively construct strategic narratives around successful companies, but much of what worked was accidental -- a feature that happened to resonate, a timing coincidence, a market shift. Genetic drift shows that even less-fit variants can win purely by chance. The lesson: study the environment and incentive structures, not just the stories winners tell about themselves.
