Harris warns that Trump's reelection could cause Democrats to double down on the exact identity politics that cost them the election, because Trump himself is such a provocation to the left that he triggers defensive escalation rather than strategic recalibration. He notes this is 'exactly what Trump and the MAGA cult want because it will be politically suicidal.'

This describes a general feedback loop in adversarial systems: when Party A's behavior is primarily defined by opposition to Party B's cultural positions, Party A's victory provokes Party B to intensify those positions rather than moderate them, which then gives Party A more grievance material. Both sides become trapped in an escalation cycle where moderation is punished and extremism is rewarded by the internal dynamics of each coalition. This same dynamic appears in corporate rivalries, online communities, and any system where identity becomes fused with opposition to an outgroup.
