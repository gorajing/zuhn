The right applies zero-sum thinking to labor markets (immigrants take our jobs) and trade (foreign manufacturing steals our industry), while the left applies it to global wealth (rich countries stole from poor countries) and domestic resources (the economy is a pie to redistribute). Both frameworks assume a fixed pool being divided rather than a growing system driven by production.

This symmetry makes the bias especially persistent because each side can clearly see the other's error while remaining blind to their own version. A conservative who correctly identifies progressive redistribution fantasies may still believe deporting immigrants creates a job bonanza. A progressive who correctly sees that tariffs hurt more than they help may still believe that colonial resource extraction explains modern wealth gaps. The Judgment of Solomon framing captures it precisely: a baby is much more than the sum of two halves.
