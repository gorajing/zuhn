Graham distinguishes between intellectual capacity and intellectual willingness. Unsuccessful founders were fully capable of following the implications of advice — they just weren't eager to. They 'traversed idea space as gingerly as a very old person traverses the physical world.'

This reframes denial not as stupidity or confusion but as a protective conservatism born from weakness. The internal process a YC partner described — where bad founders munge advice into conformity with pre-existing decisions or dismiss it with rationalizations — happens below conscious awareness. Recognizing this pattern in yourself requires noticing when you're filtering input through conclusions you've already reached rather than evaluating it fresh.
