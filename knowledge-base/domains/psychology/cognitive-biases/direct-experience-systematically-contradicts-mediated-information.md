Tucker Carlson describes being repeatedly shocked when direct experience contradicted his media-shaped expectations — Moscow was not a smoldering ruin, Slavic culture was rich rather than backward, and most people he meets are 'way better than you think.' Lex Fridman corroborates this with his Ukraine visit, where he found a functioning, clean, resilient society despite active war.

This pattern suggests a systematic bias: mediated information (news, social media, Wikipedia) consistently distorts our mental models of people and places, usually in the negative direction. The correction almost always runs positive — things are better or more complex than the narrative suggests. This has practical implications for decision-making: when forming strong opinions about distant situations, the confidence penalty for not having direct experience should be much higher than most people apply. The antidote is what Carlson calls 'updating your files' — deliberately seeking unmediated experience before committing to conclusions.
