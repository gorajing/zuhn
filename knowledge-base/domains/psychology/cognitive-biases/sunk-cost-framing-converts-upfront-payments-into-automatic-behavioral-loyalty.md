Both Costco and Amazon Prime use sunk-cost psychology — once you've paid, you feel compelled to get your money's worth. But Costco's version is stronger because the fee is mandatory and gatekeeps physical access. With Prime, customers self-select based on rational calculation of whether the fee saves enough money. With Costco, there is no shopping there without paying, so the sunk-cost trigger activates for every member.

The behavioral result is that members stop comparison shopping. They 'automatically go to Costco' the same way Prime members default to Amazon. But the mandatory nature means the entire customer base exhibits this behavior, not just the subset who opted into a loyalty program. This is a design lesson for any business considering tiered access: mandatory fees with clear gatekeeping create stronger behavioral lock-in than optional premium tiers.
