Graham observes that people think of property as having a single unchanging definition because its definition changes very slowly. This is a general cognitive pattern: when something evolves at a rate below our perceptual threshold, we experience it as static and natural. We then defend the current state as if it were a fundamental law rather than a snapshot of an ongoing process.

This bias has broad implications beyond property. It applies to career norms, institutional structures, social hierarchies, and even scientific paradigms. The antidote is deliberately studying history to see past definitions — how hunter-gatherers viewed land, how medieval guilds viewed trade secrets, how pre-copyright societies viewed creative works. Each shift felt unthinkable to those inside the old definition. Recognizing this pattern helps you identify which 'fixed' assumptions in your own field are actually ripe for redefinition.
