Musk articulates a specific mechanism by which cynicism becomes dangerous: cynical individuals see bad behavior as universal human nature, which psychologically licenses them to engage in the same behavior without guilt. The reasoning follows that if 'everyone cheats,' then cheating isn't a moral failing but simply pragmatism.

This maps to known psychological research on moral disengagement and self-licensing. The practical implication is clear — when evaluating trustworthiness of collaborators or partners, expressed cynicism about human nature should function as a warning signal, not intellectual sophistication. The person who says 'everyone lies' may be telling you about themselves, not about everyone.
