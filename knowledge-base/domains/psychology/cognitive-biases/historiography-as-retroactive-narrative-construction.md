The CrashCourse video highlights how early Chinese historians — particularly Sima Qian and the tradition that followed — framed 2,000 years of dynastic history through a Confucian moral lens. Virtuous founders received the Mandate; decadent last emperors lost it. This wasn't neutral record-keeping — it was deliberate narrative construction that made Confucian philosophy appear to be validated by centuries of evidence.

This is a specific instance of a broader cognitive bias: retroactive coherence. Humans naturally construct narratives that make the past appear to lead inevitably to the present. In business, this manifests as post-hoc rationalizations of success ('we always knew this market was right'), in science as publication bias toward confirming results, and in personal life as hindsight bias. The antidote is to actively seek out the narratives that the winners had incentive to suppress.
