The video illustrates a compensation hierarchy where each level flexes on the one below: junior at $50K → negotiator at $225K → Netflix at $900K → early Uber engineer at $25M → crypto billionaire. Each person in the chain feels inadequate relative to the next, despite objectively high compensation.

This is a textbook demonstration of relative deprivation theory and social comparison bias in tech. The reference frame constantly shifts upward — a $225K salary that would seem extraordinary in most contexts feels inadequate when your peer makes $900K. This has practical implications for retention: companies cannot win the compensation arms race through salary alone because the reference frame always expands. Instead, non-comparative sources of satisfaction (autonomy, craft, impact) are more durable motivators.
