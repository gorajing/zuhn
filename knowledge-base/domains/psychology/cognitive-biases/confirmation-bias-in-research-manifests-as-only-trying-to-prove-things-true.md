Alex Kontorovich describes his paradigm shift after two decades on the problem: 'It's very hard to prove a theorem that's false.' He recounts a separate problem he spent three years trying to prove before finding a counterexample, which immediately clarified the correct statement and led to a proof within a month.

This is confirmation bias operating at the research-program level. When everyone assumes a conjecture is true and only attempts proofs, the collective search is systematically biased. The same pattern appears in startups (only seeking validation for your idea), investing (only reading bull cases), and product development (only testing happy paths). Actively seeking disconfirming evidence isn't just intellectually honest — it's often the fastest path to truth.
