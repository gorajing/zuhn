The pre-WWII Western world largely assumed that technological and industrial advancement was synonymous with civilizational and moral progress. The war shattered this assumption — the most industrially advanced nations used their capabilities for unprecedented destruction, culminating in the atomic bomb.

This cognitive bias persists in technology: the assumption that more powerful AI, faster automation, or more connected systems inherently lead to better outcomes. Each capability breakthrough also unlocks new failure modes and misuse vectors. The pattern suggests that capability development without proportional investment in governance and safety frameworks reliably produces catastrophic misuse — not because builders are malicious, but because the progress narrative makes the downside scenarios feel impossible.
