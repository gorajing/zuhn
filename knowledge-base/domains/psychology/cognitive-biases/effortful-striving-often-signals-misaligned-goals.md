The cultural assumption is that difficulty means you're not trying hard enough. Dostoevsky suggests an alternative: persistent extreme difficulty may signal misalignment rather than insufficient effort. The right path isn't always easy, but the wrong path is consistently and unsustainably hard.

This doesn't endorse laziness - genuine achievement requires sustained effort. The distinction is between productive difficulty (challenging but yielding progress) and unproductive difficulty (exhausting but yielding nothing). Learning to distinguish between the two prevents the common mistake of working harder at the wrong thing.
