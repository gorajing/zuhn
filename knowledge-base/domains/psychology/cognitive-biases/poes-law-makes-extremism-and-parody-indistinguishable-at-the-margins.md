Poe's Law, as discussed in the context of flat Earth communities, states that at their extremes, parody of extremism and sincere extremism are difficult to distinguish. When asked 'are these people for real?' about flat Earthers, the honest answer is 'probably not most of them' — but you genuinely cannot tell.

This has practical implications beyond internet culture. Any community organized around contrarian or extreme positions will naturally attract both sincere believers and ironic participants, and the two groups reinforce each other. The sincere members provide content that looks like parody, and the parodists provide cover that makes sincere belief seem less extreme. This dynamic makes it impossible to assess the true size or seriousness of any extremist movement from the outside, which matters for anyone trying to evaluate information sources or community signals.
