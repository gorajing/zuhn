Both Costco and Amazon Prime exploit sunk-cost psychology — once you've paid, not using the service feels like wasting money. But there's a crucial difference in mechanism. Amazon Prime users self-sort, individually calculating whether the subscription saves enough money to justify the cost. This creates loyalty but leaves the calculation framework intact.

Costco's mandatory fee eliminates the opt-in decision entirely. You cannot comparison-shop at Costco without already being committed. This removes the ongoing mental arithmetic of 'is this worth it?' and replaces it with 'I've already paid, so I should maximize usage.' The behavioral implication is significant: mandatory commitment devices bypass rational evaluation more completely than voluntary ones, which is why Costco achieves 90% renewal rates despite requiring physical store visits in an era of digital convenience.
