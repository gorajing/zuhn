The video notes that no one bothered to document Jesus's life for several decades after his death, because followers expected the new everlasting kingdom to arrive imminently. When it became clear the timeline was longer, the Gospels were written — but by then, decades of oral transmission had shaped the narrative through selection, emphasis, and interpretation.

This 'imminence bias' appears repeatedly: startup founders who expect rapid acquisition don't document their architecture; teams expecting imminent pivots skip writing specs; organizations expecting restructuring don't codify processes. The result is always the same — when the expected transformation doesn't materialize quickly, you're left reconstructing history from imperfect memory. The practical takeaway is that documentation discipline should be inversely proportional to your confidence in imminent change, because that confidence is almost always overestimated.
