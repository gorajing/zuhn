Intuitions are strong feelings that something is true, shaped by evolution to help with survival-relevant tasks: detecting danger, reading social cues, making rapid decisions about apartments or jobs. Studies show gut instinct often outperforms deliberate reasoning for these domains.

But intuitions fail badly when applied to domains evolution never prepared us for — spherical Earth, quantum mechanics, the nature of consciousness. There was no evolutionary pressure to develop accurate intuitions about whether the Earth is round or whether consciousness is fundamental. Recognizing when intuition is trustworthy versus when it is misleading is itself a trainable metacognitive skill.
