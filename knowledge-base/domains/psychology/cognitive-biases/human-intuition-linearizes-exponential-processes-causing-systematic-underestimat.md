When confronted with exponential growth, humans instinctively project linearly from recent data points rather than recognizing the accelerating curve. This isn't a failure of intelligence but a deep cognitive bias — our evolved intuition handles linear change well because most survival-relevant phenomena in ancestral environments were linear or cyclical.

This has profound practical implications: investors underestimate compound returns, founders underestimate viral growth curves, and policymakers consistently act too late on exponential threats like pandemics. The corrective isn't to 'think harder' but to replace intuition with mathematical tools — plot the data on log scales, compute doubling times, and use models rather than gut feel whenever exponential dynamics are in play.
