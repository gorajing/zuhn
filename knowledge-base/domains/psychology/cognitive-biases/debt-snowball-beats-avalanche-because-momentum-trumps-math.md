The mathematically optimal debt payoff strategy (avalanche method — highest interest first) saves more money in pure interest calculations. But Hormozi argues, consistent with behavioral economics research, that the debt snowball (smallest balance first) produces better real-world outcomes because it generates early wins that sustain motivation.

This is a powerful example of a broader principle: systems that account for human psychology outperform systems optimized for theoretical efficiency. The interest savings from avalanche vs. snowball are typically small (a few hundred dollars over years), but the dropout rate difference is massive. A strategy you abandon at month three saves zero interest. This pattern — optimize for behavioral sustainability over mathematical perfection — applies far beyond debt payoff to exercise habits, business systems, and learning protocols.
