The article identifies a specific cognitive trap: spending hours reading about growth loops, saving pricing strategy threads, and watching YC talks creates a felt sense of accomplishment that is neurologically indistinguishable from actual progress. This is more dangerous than obvious procrastination because it resists self-diagnosis — the founder genuinely believes they are working.

The downstream effect is fragmented thinking: a brain full of half-applied frameworks that contradict each other depending on which article was read last. The antidote is not consuming less, but consuming with an execution filter — only engage with resources that address a specific problem you are actively solving. The founders who move fastest spend less time consuming and more time doing, not because they are less curious, but because they have learned to recognize consumption as a substitute for action.
