Sam Bankman-Fried constructed a public identity as an effective altruist driving a modest Toyota while secretly owning a $30M Bahamas mansion. Celebrity endorsements from Tom Brady, Steph Curry, and Larry David — who received equity, not cash — created social proof that further insulated FTX from scrutiny. Fortune and Forbes put him on covers. This narrative shield worked so well that when employees raised concerns about conflicts of interest and fund commingling, Sam's inner circle simply fired dissenters via a loyalist HR head. The cognitive mechanism is halo effect at institutional scale: a compelling founder story creates a category-level judgment ('genius') that makes specific red flags feel like noise rather than signal.
