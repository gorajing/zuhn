The finding that gun injury severity has increased at roughly the same rate as trauma care improvement illustrates a deep pattern in adversarial systems. Whether through explicit calculation, cultural transmission of 'street wisdom,' or selection effects (robberies declining while targeted killings remain), the lethality per violent act has stayed roughly constant despite major medical advances.

This Red Queen dynamic — running faster just to stay in place — appears across many domains: cybersecurity (better defenses produce more sophisticated attacks), academic integrity (better plagiarism detection produces more sophisticated cheating), tax policy (closing loopholes produces more creative avoidance), and biological evolution itself. The practical implication is that one-sided technological improvements in adversarial contexts often produce less benefit than expected, because the other side adapts.
