Harris references Murray's own 'flare' metaphor from the aftermath of October 7th — the idea that crisis events light up a landscape and show exactly where everyone is positioned. Harris then extends this to Trump's memecoin issuance, the Signal group chat scandal, and the Bukele Oval Office meeting, arguing each was a flare that revealed the moral positions of those nearby.

This is a powerful epistemic heuristic: when you want to know what someone truly values, don't listen to their principles in peacetime — watch their responses during crises. Crises strip away the luxury of ambiguity and force binary choices. The pattern applies well beyond politics: in business, team crises reveal who prioritizes customers vs. metrics; in relationships, emergencies reveal true commitment levels. The key insight is that you can deliberately use crisis responses as calibration data for trust.
