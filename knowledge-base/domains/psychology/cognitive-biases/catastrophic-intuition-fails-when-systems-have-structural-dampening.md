When people imagine a nuclear bomb in the Mariana Trench, they picture tsunamis, earthquakes, and apocalypse — extrapolating from surface-level explosions without accounting for the 1,000 atmospheres of pressure that fundamentally changes the physics. The bubble grows to 1km instead of 10km and is crushed back by the medium.

This is a general cognitive pattern: we model disruptions as if they happen in a vacuum, ignoring the structural resistance of the system they occur in. Markets have liquidity that absorbs shocks. Organizations have cultural inertia that dampens change initiatives. Ecosystems have redundancy that contains damage. The insight is to always ask 'what is the medium's resistance?' before predicting cascade effects.
