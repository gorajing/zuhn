Graham identifies a specific cognitive bias: when solutions are delivered informally, people must actually understand them to judge their value, which is more work than evaluating surface-level signals of legitimacy. Complex solutions seem impressive. Thick documents seem serious. Formal presentations seem credible. Simple solutions delivered casually seem like nothing.

This bias explains why successful innovations are consistently dismissed at first and called obvious in retrospect. Reddit's minimal design seemed like no design. Y Combinator's small checks seemed inconsequential. Viaweb's web-based interface seemed like just a website. In each case, the contemptuous response came from people using presentation quality as a proxy for solution quality — a heuristic that systematically undervalues substance and overvalues theater.
