Plants using RuBisCo were so successful that they transformed Earth's atmosphere from CO2-rich to O2-rich. But RuBisCo was designed for low-oxygen conditions, so the very success of photosynthesis degraded the enzyme's effectiveness by roughly 50%. Plants created the environmental conditions that undermined their own core process.

This self-undermining dynamic appears everywhere: a startup's growth attracts competitors who change the market; a popular investment thesis drives up prices until returns disappear; a social platform's success changes user demographics and culture. Recognizing that your strategy will alter the environment it depends on is crucial for planning second-order adaptations before they become urgent.
