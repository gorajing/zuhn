Donald Trump would be worth $13B if he'd passively invested his $400M inheritance in 1988 - nearly double his current estimated wealth. Yet he and most billionaires continue working. The average billionaire age is 63 and rising. No self-made billionaires are under 30.

Beyond a certain wealth threshold, continued work is economically irrational - the time cost exceeds the marginal wealth gain, especially versus passive index investing. The persistence reveals that wealth accumulation has become psychological: identity, status, legacy, and the fear of irrelevance. For many billionaires, their net worth IS their identity, making retirement psychologically equivalent to death.
