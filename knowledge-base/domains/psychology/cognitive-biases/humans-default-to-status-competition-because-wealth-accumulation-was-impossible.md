Naval identifies a deep evolutionary explanation for why people instinctively play status games even when wealth games would serve them better. Before agriculture, hunter-gatherers couldn't accumulate wealth — they carried everything on their backs. In that world, your rank in the social hierarchy was the best predictor of survival, access to resources, and reproductive success.

Farming societies began shifting toward wealth-based hierarchies, and modern industrial economies are heavily wealth-based. But our brains still carry hunter-gatherer firmware that overweights status signals. This explains why people who could be building positive-sum businesses instead spend energy on Twitter arguments, virtue signaling, or tearing down successful people. Recognizing this evolutionary mismatch is the first step to consciously choosing wealth creation over status competition.
