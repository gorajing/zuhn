Native Americans had no written language, so the entire historical record of European-Indigenous contact comes from European sources. Even sympathetic accounts like Bartolomé de las Casas's writings frame indigenous suffering through European moral categories (Christian ethics, property rights, civilization). The Native American experience of contact — their strategic reasoning, cultural interpretation, internal debates — is largely lost.

This is directly analogous to data bias in any analytical system. When your training data comes exclusively from one perspective, even well-intentioned analysis reproduces that perspective's blind spots. In business, the 'historical record' of why a product failed usually comes from the surviving company's postmortem, not from churned users. In AI development, model behavior reflects whose experiences made it into the training set. The lesson isn't that biased sources are useless — it's that you must actively flag whose voice is missing and resist treating the available record as complete.
