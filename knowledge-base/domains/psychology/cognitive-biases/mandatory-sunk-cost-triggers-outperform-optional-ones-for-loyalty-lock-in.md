Both Costco and Amazon Prime leverage sunk-cost psychology — once you've paid, you feel compelled to shop there to 'get your money's worth.' But the mechanism differs critically in one dimension: optionality. Amazon Prime users self-sort by individually calculating whether the subscription saves enough money. This creates loyalty among subscribers but doesn't change the company's fundamental business model.

Costco's mandatory fee eliminates this self-selection entirely. Every customer enters already psychologically committed. This isn't just a pricing trick — it restructures the entire customer relationship from the first interaction. The psychological commitment is uniform across the customer base, which means Costco can build its entire operation around the assumption of committed, repeat customers rather than optimizing for conversion of casual browsers.
