Humans have no evolved customs for evaluating early versions of ambitious projects. We default to judging them as either finished work (finding them lacking) or as less ambitious projects (finding them underwhelming). This is a systematic cognitive error because early ambitious work is a special category — 20% of the way to something worth 100 looks terrible by finished-work standards but represents enormous expected value. Recognizing this as a distinct evaluation category, rather than a quality failure, is the first step to correcting the bias. Silicon Valley's cultural norm of switching polarity — from listing reasons something won't work to imagining how it could — demonstrates that this bias is learnable and unlearnable.
