Graham observes that exclusive institutions function like exclusive clubs: the primary advantage of membership is knowing you aren't missing much. Those excluded can only imagine the benefits, which are invariably larger in imagination than reality. This creates a perverse dynamic where the psychological cost of exclusion exceeds the actual value of inclusion.

This insight has broad application beyond college. Any gatekeeping institution — elite accelerators, prestigious employers, exclusive networks — generates outsized psychological power not from what it delivers to insiders, but from the uncertainty it creates in outsiders. Founders and professionals who recognize this can redirect energy from credential-seeking to skill-building, which Graham argues is increasingly the actual route to success.
