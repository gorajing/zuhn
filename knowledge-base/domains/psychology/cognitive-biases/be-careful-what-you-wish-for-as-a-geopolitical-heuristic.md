Smith identifies a striking pattern where multiple political factions all advocated for the end of Pax Americana but each imagined a different outcome. Leftists and foreign rivals assumed multipolarity would check American power. Rightists assumed America would turn inward and focus on domestic cultural battles. Both were wrong in ways that directly undermine their goals.

This is a recurring cognitive pattern in complex systems: actors optimize for the immediate removal of a constraint without modeling what the system looks like without it. The constraint (rules-based order) was load-bearing in ways that weren't visible until it was gone. This maps to a general principle — when advocating for systemic change, the hardest thing to model is what the removed structure was quietly preventing.
