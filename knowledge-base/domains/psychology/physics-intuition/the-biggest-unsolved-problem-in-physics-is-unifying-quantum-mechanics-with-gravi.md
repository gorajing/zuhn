Physics has four fundamental forces. Three (strong nuclear, weak nuclear, electromagnetic) fit together beautifully in the quantum mechanics framework — they are described using the same mathematical language of quantized particles. But gravity is fundamentally different: it can be made to disappear (via freefall), it appears to be continuous rather than quantized (no graviton has been found), and it conflicts with quantum mechanics' predictions.

The most promising thread connecting the two is AdS/CFT correspondence, the most cited paper in theoretical physics. It suggests gravity and quantum entanglement may be mathematically connected — that spacetime curvature might emerge from quantum particle correlations. This is not solved, but it is a glimpse of how a theory of everything might work. The practical takeaway: our most successful models of the universe are fundamentally incomplete, and whoever bridges this gap could unlock technologies as transformative as what GPS was to navigation.
