For millennia, building tall meant stacking heavy materials — compression structures like pyramids and cathedrals. The tallest buildings remain compression-dominant (Burj Khalifa at 828m). But the tallest *structures* are guyed radio towers supported by tension cables, and the theoretical next leap — space elevators — is entirely tension-based.

This compression-to-tension shift illustrates a powerful mental model: when you hit diminishing returns in one paradigm, the breakthrough often comes from inverting the fundamental approach rather than optimizing within it. The same pattern appears in problem-solving across domains — the biggest gains come from reframing the constraint, not pushing harder against it.
