Two-dimensional creatures would swear that lines are absolute barriers and interiors don't exist. We swear surfaces matter, objects are sealed, time flows forward, and observation is harmless. These beliefs are locally correct -- they hold only because we're confined inside the same dimensional bottleneck.

The existential implication: our sense of order, identity, and narrative may not be features of the universe but survival mechanisms for beings trapped in a constrained projection. Anxiety about existence might not be a flaw in psychology but an accurate response to living inside a limited slice of something much larger. The universe doesn't become supernatural at higher dimensions -- it becomes honest.
