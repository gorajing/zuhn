Weight is a force of mutual attraction: you pull Earth upward with exactly the same force that Earth pulls you down. A 180-pound person exerts 180 pounds of gravitational force on the planet. The asymmetry in motion exists because Earth's vastly greater mass makes it far more resistant to acceleration — the same force moves you dramatically more than it moves Earth.

This confusion is reinforced by everyday scales, which detect force but display kilograms or pounds (mass units). The scale calculates what mass near Earth's surface would produce the detected force, which works fine on Earth but is conceptually misleading. Astronauts in orbit still have ~90% of surface gravity acting on them — they're not 'weightless' but lack apparent weight because everything around them falls at the same rate. The pencil-drop example crystallizes the mutual nature: when you release a pencil, Earth rises toward it by about 9 trillionths the width of a proton.
