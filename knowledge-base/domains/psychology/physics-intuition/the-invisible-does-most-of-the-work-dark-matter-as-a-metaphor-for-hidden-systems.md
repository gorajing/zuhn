Lisa Randall explains that dark matter carries five times the energy of visible matter and was the primary driver of galaxy formation. Ordinary matter merely 'came along' with the dark matter scaffolding. She compares it to the unseen workers who build a building while we focus on the architect.

This is a powerful mental model for any builder: the visible outputs (product, brand, content) get attention, but the invisible infrastructure (systems, processes, culture, relationships) does most of the actual work. Just as physicists had to develop new intuitions to detect dark matter's gravitational effects, leaders need to develop sensitivity to invisible dynamics — team morale, technical debt, cultural norms — that drive outcomes far more than visible metrics suggest.
