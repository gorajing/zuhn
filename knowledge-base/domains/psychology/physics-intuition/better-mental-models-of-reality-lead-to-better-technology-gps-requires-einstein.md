A common objection to re-examining mental models is 'same same but different' — why does the description matter if the outcomes look similar? The gravity example disproves this forcefully. If we still used Newton's force-based model for GPS satellites, positioning would be off by kilometers. Uber could not find you on a street corner. Every satellite, plane, and navigation system depends on Einstein's spacetime curvature corrections.

This principle extends far beyond physics. In any domain, the accuracy of your mental model directly determines the quality of what you can build. A slightly wrong model might work for simple cases but breaks down at scale or precision. The progression from Newton to Einstein to quantum gravity to a potential theory of everything mirrors how any field evolves: each better model unlocks capabilities that were impossible under the previous one.
