One of the researchers explicitly names this as 'one of the big questions': where does quantum mechanics stop and classical physics start? No existing theory draws that line. Every experiment that pushes entanglement into heavier particles, larger distances, or new observables either confirms the theory at a new scale or reveals a crack. Treating the quantum-classical divide as a philosophical curiosity rather than an empirical frontier is the wrong mental posture — it's the single most likely place for a revolution in physics to emerge. The mental model transfers: when you have two incompatible models that both work in their own regime (micro vs. macro, classical vs. relativistic, short-run vs. long-run), the gap between them is not an annoyance to be papered over with hand-waving — it's where the next real insight will come from.
