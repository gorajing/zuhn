The traditional Goldilocks Zone model assumes life requires a planet at just the right distance from a star for liquid water. Europa demolishes this assumption: Jupiter's immense gravity tidally flexes the moon's interior, generating friction heat that maintains a liquid ocean beneath miles of ice — no sunlight needed.

This is a powerful mental model beyond astrobiology. In any system, we default to the obvious energy source (the sun, the market leader, the primary revenue stream) and miss that mechanical stress, friction, or indirect forces can sustain entire ecosystems. The lesson: when searching for where something can thrive, don't just look at the obvious energy gradient — look for hidden mechanical energy inputs that sustain conditions beneath the surface.
