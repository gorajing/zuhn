3Blue1Brown makes a bold claim: whenever pi shows up, there's a path back to circles. The Basel Problem seems to have nothing to do with geometry — it's a sum of simple fractions. But the proof reveals the number line as a limit of circles with doubling circumference, and the π²/6 result comes directly from the original circle's circumference-to-diameter relationship.

This principle extends beyond pi. Hidden structural connections explain why the same constants and patterns recur across seemingly unrelated domains. In complex systems, when you see an unexpected regularity, it often signals a hidden underlying structure. The practical takeaway: instead of dismissing surprising connections as coincidence, trace them — they frequently reveal deep unifying mechanisms that simplify the entire problem space.
