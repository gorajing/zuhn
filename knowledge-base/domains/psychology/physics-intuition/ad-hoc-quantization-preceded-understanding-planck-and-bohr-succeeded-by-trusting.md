Planck introduced energy quantization (E=hf) in 1900 as a purely mathematical trick to solve the ultraviolet catastrophe, calling it an 'act of desperation.' He spent years afterward trying to find a theoretical justification. Bohr similarly quantized angular momentum 'for no good reason' in 1913, yet perfectly reproduced the hydrogen spectrum.

It took de Broglie (1924) to provide the physical explanation: electrons are waves, and standing waves naturally quantize. The pattern repeats across science and engineering — sometimes the right answer arrives before the understanding does. This suggests that when a mathematical model works but intuition resists, trust the math. Understanding may follow decades later, but the predictive power is real and immediate.
