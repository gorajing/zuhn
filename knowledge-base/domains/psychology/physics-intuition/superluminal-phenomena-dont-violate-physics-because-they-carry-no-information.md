A shadow cast on the Moon can sweep thousands of kilometers in a fraction of a second if you move your finger a few centimeters. Scissor blades a light-year long would have an intersection point that crosses a light-year in one second. Destructive interference patterns between two wave sources race through the overlap zone faster than either wave travels.

None of this violates relativity because the actual constraint is on information transfer, not on the apparent motion of geometric abstractions. The shadow doesn't carry a signal from point A to point B — the darkness arrives from you to each point independently at light speed. This distinction between 'things that move' and 'information that propagates' is fundamental to correctly reasoning about physical systems and applies broadly to any domain where apparent patterns seem to propagate faster than their underlying substrate.
