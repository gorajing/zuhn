Special relativity predicts that objects contract along the direction of motion at high speeds. A particle approaching Earth at 99.9999% the speed of light would genuinely measure Earth as flattened to about 11 meters thick. This isn't an illusion — it's a real physical measurement that would be confirmed by any instrument in that reference frame.

This reveals something profound about the nature of physical properties: shape is not absolute but reference-frame-dependent. The question 'Is Earth flat?' doesn't have a single correct answer — it depends on who's asking and how fast they're moving. This is a specific instance of a broader pattern in physics and epistemology: many properties we treat as intrinsic (simultaneous events, lengths, durations) are actually relational. Recognizing frame-dependence is a transferable skill — in business, what looks like a crisis from one stakeholder's frame may be an opportunity from another's.
