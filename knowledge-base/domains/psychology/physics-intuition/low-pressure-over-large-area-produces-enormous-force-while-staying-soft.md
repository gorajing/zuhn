Human intuition about force is calibrated to small contact areas — a finger pressing, a nail point. We equate high force with high pressure. But force equals pressure times area, so an inflatable pillow at just 1-2 PSI over a square meter can lift a person or a car while feeling like a cushion.

This has profound design implications: you don't need rigid, high-pressure hydraulics for heavy lifting. Soft pneumatic structures can replace them in applications where safety matters — rescue operations, medical devices, human-adjacent robotics. The vine robot exploits this by growing a large-area inflatable section that can lift rubble off trapped people without crushing them. The paradox is that gentleness and power aren't opposing forces when you control area.
