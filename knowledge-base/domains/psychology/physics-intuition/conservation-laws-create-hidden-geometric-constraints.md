The punchline of this puzzle is that conservation of energy constrains the two blocks' velocities to lie on an ellipse (which rescales to a circle) in phase space. Each collision corresponds to a reflection off a line in that space, and counting collisions becomes counting how many times a point bounces around a circle — naturally producing pi.

This illustrates a deep principle: conservation laws don't just constrain what can happen, they impose geometric structure on the space of possibilities. When you see a mysterious quantitative regularity in any constrained system, ask what the conserved quantity is and what shape it carves out in the abstract state space. The hidden circle is always there when pi appears — whether in pendulums, quantum mechanics, or bouncing blocks.
