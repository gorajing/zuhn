In photosynthesis, the electron transport chain pumps protons into the thylakoid lumen, creating a concentration gradient. Protons then flow back through ATP synthase like water through a turbine, generating ATP. The thylakoid is literally a rechargeable battery: energy in (photons) → stored as imbalance → released on demand.

This pattern — storing energy as a differential across a selective barrier — is universal. Hydroelectric dams store gravitational potential as water height difference. Capacitors store charge across a dielectric. Even pricing arbitrage stores economic energy as a price differential across a market barrier. Recognizing this template lets you see energy storage opportunities wherever you find barriers and gradients, and diagnose energy loss wherever barriers become leaky.
