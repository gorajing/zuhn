People intuitively think of orbiting objects as somehow 'held up' by an opposing force, but orbits are simply falling with enough sideways speed that you continuously miss the surface. A thrown ball makes a tiny orbit — the only difference from the moon is speed and the absence of air resistance. This reframing dissolves the mystery: there's no anti-gravity force, just geometry. The insight generalizes — many seemingly stable equilibria in complex systems aren't maintained by opposing forces but by momentum in an orthogonal direction, which looks like stability until the velocity changes.
