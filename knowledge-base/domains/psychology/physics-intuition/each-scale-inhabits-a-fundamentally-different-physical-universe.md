At insect scale, surface tension is the dominant lethal force while gravity is irrelevant — you can throw an ant from an airplane unharmed. At elephant scale, the ratio inverts completely: gravity becomes catastrophically destructive while surface tension is imperceptible. The fairy fly lives in a world where air itself behaves like a viscous fluid, requiring swimming motions rather than flight.

This is not merely a difference in degree but in kind. The dominant forces, survival strategies, and engineering constraints are so different at each scale that solutions from one scale are not just suboptimal but physically meaningless at another. This maps to organizational design: the forces that kill a 5-person startup (inability to ship) are completely different from those that kill a 5000-person company (coordination failure), and transplanting solutions across scales often makes things worse.
