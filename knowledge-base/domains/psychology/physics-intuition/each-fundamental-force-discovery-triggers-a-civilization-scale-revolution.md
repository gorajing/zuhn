Kaku traces a clear pattern across physics history: Newton's unraveling of gravity didn't just explain planetary motion — it gave us the mechanics (F=ma) that made steam engines, factories, and the entire Industrial Revolution possible, toppling feudalism across Europe. Faraday and Maxwell's electromagnetic unification didn't just explain light — it gave us electricity, radio, television, the internet, and the entire modern connected world.

The nuclear forces (strong and weak) unlocked the secret of stars and gave us nuclear energy and particle physics. Each time humanity understood a fundamental force, it gained leverage over nature at a scale that reshaped civilization within decades. This pattern suggests that investment in understanding truly fundamental mechanisms — not incremental improvements — produces the highest-magnitude returns, though with unpredictable timelines. The same meta-pattern may apply in other domains: understanding the fundamental 'force' driving a system unlocks disproportionate ability to reshape it.
