Vsauce demonstrates that on a flat disc Earth, gravity wouldn't pull uniformly 'down' — it would pull toward the center of mass, meaning at the edges you'd experience gravity at extreme angles, water would flow toward the center, and the atmosphere would thin dramatically at the periphery. None of this is obvious until you simulate it.

This is the thought experiment method at its best: take a familiar system (gravity), change one assumption (Earth's shape), and observe what breaks. The technique transfers directly to strategy and product thinking. 'What if our biggest customer left?' 'What if our cost structure inverted?' 'What if our market grew 100x overnight?' These aren't predictions — they're stress tests that reveal which of your current assumptions are load-bearing and which are incidental. The flat-disc gravity simulation shows that even physicists carry unexamined geometric assumptions; founders and strategists carry equivalent hidden assumptions about markets, users, and business models.
