Gravity pulls toward center of mass, not perpendicular to a surface. On a flat disc, only someone standing at the exact center would experience gravity pulling straight down. As you move toward the edges, gravity would pull increasingly sideways — at the rim, you'd experience near-horizontal gravitational pull toward the center. Rain would fall diagonally, buildings couldn't stand upright, and oceans would pool at the center.

This is a powerful demonstration of how physics thought experiments reveal hidden assumptions. Most flat Earth arguments implicitly assume gravity works perpendicular to the surface everywhere, but that's actually a property unique to spheres. The flat disc model defeats itself when you rigorously apply the physics it claims to accept. This pattern — where a model collapses under its own internal logic — is a general technique for evaluating any extraordinary claim.
