Al-Khalili argues against the standard view that the arrow of time derives from thermodynamic entropy increasing. Instead, he proposes that time-symmetric equations are idealizations that only apply to truly isolated systems — and the only truly isolated system is the entire universe. Everything within it interacts with its surroundings, and at the quantum level, this interaction produces entanglement and decoherence.

Decoherence — where quantum information dissipates irreversibly into the environment — is the one truly irreversible process in nature. Since it is happening everywhere, all the time, it provides a fundamental directionality to time that does not require thermodynamics as a foundation. This reframing has deep implications: rather than asking 'how does irreversibility emerge from reversible laws,' the answer is that reversible laws are the idealization, not reality.
