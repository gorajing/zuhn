Multiple quotes in this collection converge on the same insight: Asimov's observation that breakthroughs come from 'That's funny...' rather than 'Eureka!', Darwin's systematic attention to exceptions, and Townes noting that great ideas seem obvious in retrospect. The pattern is that discovery is less about raw intelligence and more about a specific attentional discipline.

This reframes expertise development. Most people filter anomalies as noise to maintain cognitive coherence. The discoverer does the opposite — they treat every exception as a potential signal. This is a trainable habit: systematically noting when reality deviates from your model, rather than dismissing the deviation. It's the difference between 'that's weird, anyway...' and 'that's weird — why?'
