Elena Verna's framework, cited by Lenny, reframes career progression: leadership titles validate abilities and impact, but they're means to an end. The real goal is career optionality — the ability to craft your career however you want. For Verna, this materialized as solopreneurship where her brain became her product across multiple monetization streams.

This inverts conventional career advice that treats VP/C-suite titles as finish lines. Instead, each title is a credential that expands your option set. The solopreneur path — advising, speaking, writing, teaching — becomes viable only after accumulating enough experience and reputation. Lenny's own path illustrates this: years at Airbnb built the expertise and credibility that made his newsletter possible. The implication is to pursue titles strategically as optionality-builders, not as identity anchors.
