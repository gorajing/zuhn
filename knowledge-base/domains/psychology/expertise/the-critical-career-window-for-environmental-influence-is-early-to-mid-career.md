Graham identifies a specific temporal window when geographic environment has maximum impact on career trajectory. College students exist in a sufficiently rich micro-environment of a few thousand people, and late-career people have already found their problems and peers. The critical phase is the transition after formal education when you face the hardest challenge: discovering what problems to work on.

This is when you need both encouragement (people around you who care about similar work) and a large intake mechanism for finding peers. The Impressionists exemplify this pattern — born all over France, died all over France, but defined by the years they spent together in Paris during their formative creative period. The practical implication is that you can leave once you've found your peers and your problems — the city is a catalyst, not a permanent requirement.
