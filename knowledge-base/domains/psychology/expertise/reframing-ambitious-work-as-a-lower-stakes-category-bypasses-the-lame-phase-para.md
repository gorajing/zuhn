One of the most effective techniques for surviving the early phase of ambitious projects is to deliberately classify them as a lesser type of work—a sketch rather than a painting, a quick hack rather than real software. This isn't self-deception; it's a strategic mismatch between your internal framing and your actual ambition. You judge initial results by the lower standard of the declared category, which prevents premature abandonment. Once the project builds enough momentum and quality, you 'sneakily convert it to something more.' This technique works best when paired with a medium that enables fast iteration and low upfront commitment—digital over physical, notebooks over carved stone. The speed of initial results matters because focusing on rate of change rather than absolute position makes imperfect early work feel like progress rather than failure.
