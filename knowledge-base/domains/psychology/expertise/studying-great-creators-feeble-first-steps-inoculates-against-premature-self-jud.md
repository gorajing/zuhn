Graham's deepest insight is that the solution to judging early work too harshly is to realize that our attitudes toward early work are themselves early work — crude v1 heuristics we can improve. One concrete method is studying the actual first steps of people who did great things, which are almost always surprisingly feeble.

The internet has made this easier: you can find programmers' first commits, musicians' first recordings, and writers' first drafts. This practice serves as a calibration tool — it shifts your reference class from 'finished masterwork' to 'what great work actually looked like at this stage.' Over time, this builds an intuition for recognizing promise in rough form, making you immune to both external skepticism and your own fear of producing something lame.
