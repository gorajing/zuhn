When the world is static, accumulated experience monotonically increases the reliability of beliefs. But most domains change, and experts who don't account for this become prisoners of outdated mental models. The danger is that expertise feels increasingly reliable over time — the very mechanism that makes experts useful in stable domains makes them fragile in shifting ones.

This has practical implications for how you weight expert opinions: the more confident and experienced the expert, the more you should probe whether their confidence is calibrated to current conditions or to a world that no longer exists. The correction isn't to distrust experts wholesale, but to distinguish between domains where experience compounds (human nature, physics) and domains where it decays (technology, markets, culture).
