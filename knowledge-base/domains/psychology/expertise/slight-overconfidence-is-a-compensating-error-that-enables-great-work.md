Conventional wisdom says accurate self-assessment is optimal, but Graham argues this ignores systematic biases working against ambitious projects. Early work looks worse than it is, and both you and others will undervalue it. Slight overconfidence acts as a compensating error — like deliberately overestimating the importance of your goal to offset underestimating progress. The most successful people across many fields share this trait not because delusion is good, but because the world's default judgment of early-stage work is systematically too harsh. The error corrects for the environment, not for reality.
