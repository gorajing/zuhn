Graham observes that it's much easier to convince yourself something is 'just a sketch' when you're drawing in a notebook than when you're carving stone. This seemingly obvious point has deep implications for creative strategy: the choice of medium directly affects your psychological ability to tolerate ugly early work.

Digital media's advantage isn't primarily the software features — it's that digital work requires little upfront commitment, allowing you to start things cheaply and iterate quickly. This compresses the painful early phase where work looks terrible and abandonment is most tempting. John Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this: when the gap between early and final work is small (as in crude-aesthetic games like Minecraft), innovative concepts become the dominant value, and many more people successfully push through to completion. The strategic implication is that when starting something ambitious, deliberately choose the lowest-fidelity medium that can express your core idea.
