Graham catalogs several mind-game techniques for surviving the lame phase, but the experiment frame is among the most powerful because it changes what counts as success. When a project is an experiment, failure is still informative — if the theorem is false or the structural member breaks, you've learned something. The binary succeed/fail evaluation collapses into a spectrum where every outcome has value.

This connects to the broader principle that curiosity is a more durable motivational fuel than ambition. Graham notes that curiosity — wanting to see how things turn out — was what sustained him through long projects like Bel. The experiment frame harnesses this by making the process itself rewarding regardless of outcome. The practical technique is linguistic: calling early work a sketch, a hack, or a quick experiment lowers the implicit quality bar and lets you start before your internal critic can object.
