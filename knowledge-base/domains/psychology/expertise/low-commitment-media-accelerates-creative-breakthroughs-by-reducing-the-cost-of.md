Graham observes that the gap between early work and finished work varies enormously by medium. Sketching in a notebook feels low-stakes; carving stone feels permanent. Digital tools reduce this gap further — not because of software features per se, but because they lower upfront commitment. This is why game mods flourished in crude engines like Quake and Minecraft: when everything looks rough, gameplay innovation becomes the only axis of competition.

The actionable principle is to deliberately choose your starting medium for minimum friction rather than maximum capability. Write the first draft in a text file, not a typesetting system. Build the prototype with the crudest tools that can demonstrate the idea. The medium shapes your willingness to experiment, and willingness to experiment determines whether you ever reach the phase where the work becomes genuinely good.
