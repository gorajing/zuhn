Graham draws a sharp distinction between where in the decision tree someone refuses to yield. Persistent people are deeply attached to nodes high in the tree — the overarching goal — but will readily abandon lower-level tactical decisions when expected value drops. Obstinate people spray 'don't give up' indiscriminately across the entire tree, clinging to first ideas about methods even as evidence mounts against them.

This maps directly to how founders and problem-solvers fail: they conflate changing their approach with giving up. The persistent person who pivots their strategy while maintaining their mission is fundamentally different from the obstinate person who keeps executing the same failing plan. The practical test is simple — when someone points out a problem, do you listen with predatory intensity (like the Collisons) or do your eyes glaze over?
