SVB's Chief Risk Officer departed in April 2022 and was not replaced until January 2023 — an 8-month gap that coincided precisely with the Federal Reserve's aggressive rate-hiking cycle. This was the period when the bank's bond portfolio most urgently needed rebalancing to account for the new interest rate environment. Without a CRO, no one was overseeing the process. As the video describes it: 'the bank was a plane without a pilot during a storm.'

This failure mode extends well beyond banking. Any organization facing elevated environmental risk — whether market shifts, competitive threats, or technological disruption — that allows its risk oversight function to lapse is running without guardrails at the exact moment they matter most. The lesson is that risk management roles should have mandatory succession plans and interim coverage, especially during periods of macro uncertainty. The cost of an 8-month CRO vacancy was $209 billion in assets and the second-largest bank failure in US history.
