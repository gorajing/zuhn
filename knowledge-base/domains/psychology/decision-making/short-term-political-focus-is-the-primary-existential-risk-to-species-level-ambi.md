The video makes a striking claim: building a Dyson Sphere is 'based on physics alone, not just possible but easy.' The hard part isn't engineering — it's that 'our attention is too often focused on short-term political gains and conflicts that will not matter in the long run.' This reframes existential risk: the threat isn't that we can't solve technical problems, but that we won't allocate attention and resources to them because short-term incentives dominate.

This pattern recurs across domains. Climate change solutions exist but face political gridlock. AI safety research is technically tractable but chronically underfunded relative to capabilities research. The insight suggests that for any long-horizon project, the primary risk model should focus on political and attention economics rather than technical feasibility. The question isn't 'can we build it?' but 'will we choose to?'
