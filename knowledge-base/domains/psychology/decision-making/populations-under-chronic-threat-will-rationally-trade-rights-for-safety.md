This pattern reflects a deep truth about human priority hierarchies: safety is non-negotiable. When Salvadorans faced 18 homicides per day and couldn't walk their streets, abstract democratic rights felt like luxuries. The 'state of exception' that suspended due process, legal counsel, and judicial oversight was overwhelmingly popular because it delivered the one thing people desperately wanted — the ability to live without fear.

The international community's condemnation, while institutionally correct, missed that rational actors under chronic threat will discount long-term systemic risk in favor of immediate survival gains. This isn't irrational — it's a different optimization function. The video frames this as 'short-term gain for long-term sacrifice,' but from the perspective of someone who lived through decades of gang terror, removing that threat IS the long-term play. The tension between institutional integrity and immediate results is real, and the 'correct' answer depends entirely on which risks you weight more heavily — a calculation that outsiders and insiders will almost always disagree on.
