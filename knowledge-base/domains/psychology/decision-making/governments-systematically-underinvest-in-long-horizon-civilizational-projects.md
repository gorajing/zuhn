Kurzgesagt notes that the primary barrier to a moon base isn't technology or cost — it's 'hard to get governments interested in long-term investments in the future of humanity.' The technology exists, the price is manageable, and the payoff is immeasurable, yet no government commits.

This is a structural problem, not an intelligence problem. Electoral cycles of 2-6 years create discount rates that make decade-long projects politically irrational even when they're economically rational. The diffuse nature of civilizational benefits (technology spillovers, species survival insurance, resource access) means no single constituency lobbies for them the way defense contractors lobby for military spending. This explains why private space companies like SpaceX have accelerated progress — they operate on founder-vision timescales rather than electoral cycles, and can capture concentrated returns that justify the investment horizon.
