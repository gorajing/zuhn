The OSIRIS-REx mission's pristine asteroid samples revealed ribose, glucose, and a novel gum-like polymer—compounds that don't survive atmospheric entry and therefore never appear in meteorite studies. Scientists had been analyzing meteorites for decades without finding these molecules, not because they weren't looking, but because their collection method made discovery impossible.

This principle generalizes far beyond space science: the way you collect evidence determines the ceiling of what you can learn. In business, surveying only current customers can never reveal why non-customers reject your product. In investing, analyzing only public filings can never surface the cultural dynamics that drive execution. The Bennu finding is a clean demonstration that upgrading your collection methodology—not just your analytical sophistication—is often the binding constraint on insight generation.
