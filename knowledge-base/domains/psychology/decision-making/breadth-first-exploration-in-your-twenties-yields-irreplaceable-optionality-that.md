Graham frames the startup timing decision using search algorithms: startups are a 'brutally fast depth-first search' while people in their early twenties should still be searching breadth-first. Activities like plunging into projects on a whim and traveling cheaply have a narrow window — they become impossible once a successful startup takes over your life irrevocably.

Critically, Graham argues there is no tradeoff: waiting increases your probability of success while preserving irreplaceable exploration. Mark Zuckerberg can charter jets but will never experience the serendipity of bumming around a foreign country. Success at scale means 'Facebook is running him as much as he's running Facebook.' The practical advice is to delay unless a side project takes off organically — and even successful founders universally report that the total volume of worry never decreases, it only changes in character.
