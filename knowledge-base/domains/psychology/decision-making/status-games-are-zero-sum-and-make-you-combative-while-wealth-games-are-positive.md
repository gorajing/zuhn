Naval draws a fundamental distinction between two life games: wealth creation and status competition. Wealth creation is positive-sum — building a house doesn't prevent others from building houses, and shared knowledge actually makes it easier. Status, however, is strictly zero-sum: for one person to rise in rank, another must fall.

This has profound psychological implications. Status players become angry and combative by necessity, since the game requires tearing others down. Wealth creators can be collaborative because everyone can win simultaneously. When someone attacks wealth creators (journalists attacking tech, virtue signaling against money), they're often playing the status game — trying to elevate themselves by diminishing others. Recognizing which game someone is playing helps you avoid being drawn into zero-sum dynamics when you could be building positive-sum outcomes.
