The video explicitly states: 'To get the energy needed to build a Dyson Sphere, it's almost as if you need the power output of a Dyson Sphere.' This is a universal pattern in ambitious projects — the resources required to build the thing can only come from the thing itself. Startups face this with needing revenue to build the product that generates revenue. AI labs face it with needing compute to train the models that optimize compute usage.

The resolution is always the same: find the smallest viable starting point that generates enough output to fund the next increment. On Mercury, that's one square kilometer of solar panels. For a startup, it's an MVP with one paying customer. The pattern reveals that the real constraint isn't the total resource requirement — it's finding that minimum viable seed and ensuring the feedback loop is positive.
