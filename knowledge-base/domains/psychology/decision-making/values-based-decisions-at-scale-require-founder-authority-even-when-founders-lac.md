When Google faced the China censorship decision in 2010, Schmidt's operator instinct was to preserve market access — a rational optimization for business continuity. But Page and Brin saw it as an identity question: what does Google stand for? Their willingness to exit China despite enormous financial cost reveals a structural difference between founder and operator decision-making.

Operators are hired to optimize within constraints. Founders define what the constraints are. When a decision touches company identity — not just strategy — operator logic breaks down because it treats values as negotiable variables rather than fixed parameters. This suggests that even in companies with professional CEO leadership, founders should retain veto power over decisions that implicate core identity, because no operator has the standing or incentive to sacrifice growth for coherence.
