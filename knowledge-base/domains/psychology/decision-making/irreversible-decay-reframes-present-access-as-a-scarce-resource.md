The video catalogs a series of irreversible losses: total solar eclipses will become impossible when the Moon drifts far enough away, Niagara Falls will erode to nothing, Saturn's rings will dissipate, Mount Rushmore's faces will weather away. None of these are cyclical — they are one-directional decay processes with no reset mechanism.

This creates a framework for thinking about present-moment decisions: some opportunities are renewable (you can always start a new project, learn a new skill) while others are on irreversible decay curves (specific relationships, historical moments, natural phenomena). Standard temporal discounting treats future and present value as interchangeable modulo a discount rate. But irreversible decay means certain present experiences have effectively infinite future discount rates — their future value is literally zero.

Applied to personal decision-making, this suggests a heuristic: when choosing between a renewable opportunity and a decaying one of similar value, strongly prefer the decaying one. The renewable option will still be available; the decaying one won't.
