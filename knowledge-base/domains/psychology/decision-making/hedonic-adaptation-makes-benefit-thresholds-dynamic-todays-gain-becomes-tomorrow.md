The Value Proposition Canvas framework implicitly surfaces a deep psychological phenomenon: hedonic adaptation. Once customers experience a gain consistently, their reference point shifts and the same outcome becomes the new expectation floor. Free two-day shipping was a differentiating gain for Amazon Prime at launch; it is now table stakes that competitors must match to avoid being coded as a pain.

For product strategy, this means gains have a half-life. The question is not just 'how do we create gains?' but 'how do we create gains that are structurally difficult to commoditize or adapt to?' Network effects, personalization that compounds over time, and physical integration (hardware + software) all slow the adaptation treadmill.

This also explains why customer satisfaction scores drift down even when nothing changes: the product didn't get worse, the expectation floor rose. Tracking NPS alongside explicit expectation baselines — asking customers what they now take for granted — gives a more accurate signal of whether you're running to stay still or actually advancing.
