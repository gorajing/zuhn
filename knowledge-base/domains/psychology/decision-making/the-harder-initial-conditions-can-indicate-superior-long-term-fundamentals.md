The video frames Venus as objectively more hostile than Mars — 460°C surface, 93x atmospheric pressure, sulfuric acid clouds — yet argues it's the superior colonization target because of one structural fundamental: near-Earth gravity. Mars has ~38% Earth gravity, which likely causes irreversible health damage over generations.

This pattern recurs in business and investing: the option with higher activation energy but better structural properties (market size, unit economics, network effects) outperforms the easy-entry alternative. The lesson is to separate 'current difficulty of entry' from 'quality of the underlying asset.' Difficulty is a solvable engineering problem; structural deficiencies (like Mars's gravity) are permanent constraints.
