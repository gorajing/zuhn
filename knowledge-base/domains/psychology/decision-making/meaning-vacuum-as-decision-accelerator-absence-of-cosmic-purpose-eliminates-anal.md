Analysis paralysis often stems from the belief that there exists an objectively correct life path and the terror of choosing wrong. Optimistic nihilism dissolves this by removing the external scorekeeper. If the universe has no principles, the only principles relevant are ones you decide on.

This doesn't produce reckless decisions — it produces faster ones grounded in self-knowledge rather than attempts to divine external approval. The framework shifts the question from 'what should I do?' (implying an external answer exists) to 'what do I want to do?' (requiring only self-knowledge). For founders and creators facing constant ambiguity, this shift from cosmic optimization to personal alignment dramatically reduces decision latency.
