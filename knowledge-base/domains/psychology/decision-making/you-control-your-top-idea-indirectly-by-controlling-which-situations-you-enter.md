Graham frames ambient thought management as an indirect control problem. You cannot force your mind to drift toward a particular topic — by definition, if you're controlling the drift, it's not drifting. But you have significant control over the inputs: which commitments you take on, which disputes you engage in, which financial structures you accept. The practical heuristic is to engineer your life so that the things demanding urgent attention are also the things you'd choose to think about. This reframes many career and business decisions — taking on a contentious client, entering a legal dispute, accepting complex financing terms — as attention allocation decisions with compounding downstream effects on creative output.
