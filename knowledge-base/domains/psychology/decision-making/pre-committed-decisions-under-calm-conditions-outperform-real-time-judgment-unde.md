The UK requires each new Prime Minister to write sealed letters instructing submarine commanders what to do if the government is destroyed by nuclear attack. These letters are written during peacetime clarity and destroyed unread when the PM leaves office. This is a powerful model for any high-stakes domain: make your hardest decisions when you have full cognitive capacity, then seal them so they execute automatically under crisis conditions.

This principle applies broadly — founders should pre-commit to shutdown criteria before running low on cash, investors should set sell triggers before market panic, and teams should establish escalation protocols before incidents occur. The key insight is that the moment you most need good judgment is precisely when you're least equipped to exercise it. Pre-commitment bridges that gap.
