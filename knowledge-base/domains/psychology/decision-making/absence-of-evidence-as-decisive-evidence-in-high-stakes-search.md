The video makes a surprisingly rigorous epistemic move: a Type 3 civilization's influence would be 'so all-encompassing' that 'we couldn't miss them — the galaxy should flash with their activity.' Therefore, the absence of such signals is not mere ignorance but positive evidence that no such civilization exists nearby. Scientists looked for harvested stars, decaying megastructures, and scars of interstellar wars — finding none.

This 'absence as evidence' reasoning is directly applicable to competitive analysis, market research, and due diligence. If a competitor had truly achieved product-market fit at scale, you'd see unmistakable artifacts: hiring surges, customer testimonials, industry chatter, infrastructure footprint. When thorough search reveals none of these traces, the rational conclusion is that the threat doesn't exist at the claimed scale — not that it's merely hidden. The key qualifier is 'thorough search' — absence is only informative when your detection capability matches the expected signal strength.
