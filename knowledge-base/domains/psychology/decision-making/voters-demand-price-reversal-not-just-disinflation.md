Polling data shows Americans rank inflation and cost of living far above unemployment as their primary economic concern. More critically, evidence suggests many voters don't just want inflation slowed — they want prices to actually decrease to prior levels. This expectation gap is enormously consequential because deflation is extremely difficult to achieve without triggering recession.

This creates a structural political trap: any leader promising affordability faces a mandate that conventional economic tools cannot easily fulfill. The gap between what voters expect (prices going back down) and what policy can deliver (prices rising more slowly so wages catch up) drives leaders toward increasingly unorthodox measures — exactly the dynamic pushing Trump's 'gangster affordability' approach.
