The thylakoid membrane is 'charged like a battery' by pumping protons to one side, creating a concentration gradient. The protons naturally want to equalize, but they can only escape through ATP synthase — an enzyme that harnesses their movement to produce ATP. The energy comes not from the protons themselves but from the controlled imbalance and the constrained release channel.

This maps to motivation and decision architecture: creating deliberate imbalances (information asymmetry, urgency, scarcity) stores potential energy, but value is only captured when the release is channeled through a specific mechanism. Unfocused equalization (diffusion) wastes the gradient. This explains why open-ended mandates produce less output than constrained channels — the constraint is what converts pressure into work.
