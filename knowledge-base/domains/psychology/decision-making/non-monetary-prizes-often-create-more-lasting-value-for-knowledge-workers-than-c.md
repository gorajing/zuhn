The ACX contest prizes include cash ($2,500/$1,000/$500), but also: free publicity with links to other works, free subscriptions, sidebar blog links, and the right to pitch Scott new articles directly. For a writer building an audience, the non-monetary prizes likely dominate in long-run value — a sidebar link on a high-traffic Substack and a warm introduction channel to an influential editor are compounding assets, while cash is consumed once.

This reveals a general principle about incentive design for intellectual workers: prestige, distribution, and access compound in ways money doesn't. An invitation to pitch a respected platform is a reusable option. A link from a trusted source generates ongoing traffic. A subscription to a respected publication signals community membership. Contest designers who only compete on cash prize size miss this dimension entirely. The corollary: participants who evaluate contests solely on prize money may systematically undervalue the non-monetary upside.
