The article cites that the human brain processes visuals 60,000 times faster than text as justification for why the Business Model Canvas works. While the exact multiplier is debatable, the directional claim is well-supported by cognitive science: spatial and visual processing engages parallel neural pathways that text-based sequential processing cannot match.

This has implications beyond business strategy. Any domain with complex interdependencies—system architecture, investment portfolios, product ecosystems—benefits from visual mapping tools. The key design principle is that the visual framework must balance simplicity (so anyone can use it) with fidelity (so it doesn't oversimplify). The Business Model Canvas hits this sweet spot with exactly 9 blocks—few enough to grasp at a glance, numerous enough to capture real business complexity.
