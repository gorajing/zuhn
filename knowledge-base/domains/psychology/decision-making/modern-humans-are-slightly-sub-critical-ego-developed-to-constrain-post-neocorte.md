Robin Carhart-Harris's broader thesis from the entropic brain hypothesis: the expansion of the neocortex in human evolution massively increased the repertoire of possible brain states (the imagination, language, planning capabilities that make us human). This entropy increase was double-edged — it enabled new capabilities but also risked detachment from reality (psychosis, delusion). The ego (the narrative self, default mode network) evolved to constrain this new entropy, keeping us grounded. But the constraint became excessive. Modern humans, especially in highly-ordered cultures, live in slightly sub-critical states: rigid identity, rumination, anxiety, depression — all symptoms of insufficient entropy in the system. Visiting critical states is necessary for healthy hierarchy in the brain. The mechanisms humans have developed to do this: (1) Psychedelics (LSD, psilocybin, mescaline, DMT) — direct 5-HT2A activation, the documentary's main focus. (2) Meditation — voluntary access to ego-dissolution states. (3) Flow states — task absorption that quiets the default mode. (4) Fasting and extreme cold/heat — physical stressors that activate the 5-HT2A 'pivotal mental state' system. (5) Music and ritual — entrainment patterns that synchronize brain states. (6) Sleep — natural cycling through high-entropy states (REM dreams). The implication: a complete life requires regular visits to criticality. Cultures that suppressed all such practices (the Christian Roman Empire banning the Eleusinian mysteries in 364 AD) produce populations that are progressively over-ordered — and the documentary argues the modern Western 'crisis of meaning' is partly a result of 1,500+ years without mainstream criticality practices. The practical implication for builders and operators: if you feel ossified, depressed, or trapped in rigid thinking, the prescription isn't more analysis (which adds order); it's deliberately destabilizing experiences — long retreats, fasting, meditation, physical extremes, novel environments, real conversations with people who hold opposed priors. Schedule entropy.
