The shade ball experiment perfectly demonstrates the quicksand principle: when stationary on the multi-layer surface, you float comfortably. But movement gives the balls enough energy to shift, and you sink progressively deeper. The more you struggle, the faster you descend.

This maps to high-uncertainty environments like market crashes, organizational crises, or career turbulence. Action bias — the instinct to 'do something' — often accelerates the very collapse you're trying to prevent. Each reactive move destabilizes the surrounding system further, creating a positive feedback loop of deterioration. The counterintuitive optimal strategy is often deliberate stillness until you have a clear, efficient path forward rather than thrashing through multiple panicked adjustments.
