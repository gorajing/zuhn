Naval identifies four distinct types of luck: blind luck (pure chance), hustle luck (generated through volume of activity), skill luck (pattern recognition from domain expertise), and character luck (unique positioning that attracts opportunities to you). The key insight is that these form a controllability gradient — each successive type gives you more agency over outcomes.

The practical implication is that most people's wealth strategy over-indexes on type 1 (hoping to get lucky) or type 2 (pure hustle) while underinvesting in types 3 and 4. Building deep expertise in a specific domain (type 3) and cultivating a unique personal brand or capability combination (type 4) are the highest-leverage moves because they make luck systematic rather than stochastic. The goal is to be wealthy in 999 of 1000 parallel universes — which requires moving beyond luck types that are inherently probabilistic.
