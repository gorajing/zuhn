Destin's reflection distills the training into a universal decision framework: 'Take one decision after another, slowly thinking it through, that gets you closer and closer to the light.' This isn't motivational language — it's describing the actual cognitive strategy that works underwater in zero visibility.

When sensory input is overwhelming and unreliable (inverted, water-filled sinuses, zero visibility), the brain cannot construct an accurate global model of the situation. Attempting holistic assessment leads to paralysis or panic. The trained approach reduces everything to a chain: grab reference → find belt → unbuckle → hand-over-hand to exit → breathe → surface. Each step is a single decision with immediate feedback. This sequential approach maps to startup crisis management, incident response, and any scenario where the full picture is unknowable — stop trying to understand everything and start making the next correct micro-decision.
