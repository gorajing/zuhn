Graham distinguishes between showing and telling confidence. Saying 'we're passionate' is at best ignored and at worst marks you as a bullshitter. This maps to a broader pattern in persuasion: explicit claims about one's own qualities trigger adversarial evaluation, while the same qualities demonstrated implicitly bypass those filters.

The mechanism is that explicit claims are cheap signals—anyone can say them regardless of truth. Implicit signals like speaking with deep knowledge about your problem space, or treating your presentation as doing investors a favor by sharing the opportunity, are expensive to fake. Investors pattern-match on these costly signals because they're more reliable discriminators between genuine capability and performance.
