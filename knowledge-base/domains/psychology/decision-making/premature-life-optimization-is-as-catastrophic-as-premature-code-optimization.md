Paul Graham draws an explicit analogy between premature optimization in software engineering and premature commitment in life planning. Just as optimizing code before understanding the problem leads to brittle, wrong solutions, optimizing your life trajectory before understanding your own interests and the landscape of possibilities leads to suboptimal outcomes.

The 'stay upwind' heuristic operationalizes this: like a glider without an engine, you lose altitude flying into the wind. Choose paths that keep you close to good landing spots — fields like math that open doors to many downstream options rather than narrow specializations that close them. The key insight is that optionality itself has compounding value, and the correct strategy under uncertainty is to preserve it rather than collapse it prematurely.
