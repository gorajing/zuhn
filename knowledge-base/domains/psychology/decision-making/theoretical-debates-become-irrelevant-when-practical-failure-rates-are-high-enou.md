Graham's core rhetorical move is showing that intellectuals debate the death penalty on theoretical grounds (state authority, deterrence, equity) while the practical reality — innocent people being executed — makes those debates irrelevant. You never reach the philosophical question because the system fails at a more basic level.

This pattern applies broadly to decision-making: when implementation is broken enough, optimizing the theory is wasted effort. Before debating whether a strategy is correct in principle, verify that the execution machinery can deliver it without catastrophic failure. A 4% fatal error rate in any system should halt all other discussions until it's addressed.
