Graham identifies three simultaneous forms of ignorance that masquerade as indecision: not knowing what truly makes you happy, not knowing what various kinds of work are actually like day-to-day, and not knowing how well you could perform them. People mistake this ignorance for a balanced trade-off between equally valid paths.

The practical implication is that the correct response to career indecision is not more deliberation but more experimentation. Rather than trying to reason your way to a decision with insufficient information, you should try working on things that interest you to gather direct evidence about your fit. This reframes career uncertainty from a decision problem (which path?) to an information problem (what don't I know yet?).
