The video's conclusion ties kissing back to a broader evolutionary principle: life favors activities that transform uncertainty into knowledge. Kissing reduces mate-selection uncertainty through biological data exchange. Curiosity reduces environmental uncertainty through exploration. Scientific inquiry reduces causal uncertainty through experimentation.

This framing recontextualizes many pleasurable human behaviors as evolved uncertainty-reduction mechanisms. The pleasure isn't incidental — it's the evolutionary incentive for information-gathering. This has practical implications for decision-making frameworks: the discomfort of uncertainty is a signal to seek more information, and the pleasure of resolving it is the reward. In business, the founders and organizations that most systematically convert uncertainty into knowledge (through customer research, rapid experimentation, data collection) gain the same advantage that curious, kissing pre-humans had over their less inquisitive peers.
