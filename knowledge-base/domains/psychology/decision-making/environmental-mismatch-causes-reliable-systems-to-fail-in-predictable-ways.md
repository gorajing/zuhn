RuBisCo was 'designed' for an atmosphere with abundant CO2 and negligible oxygen. When oxygen levels rose, the enzyme didn't stop working — it started systematically grabbing the wrong molecule, producing toxic phosphoglycolate instead of useful products. The failure mode wasn't shutdown but misapplication.

This is the exact pattern behind evolutionary mismatch in human psychology. Our threat-detection systems evolved for predator-rich savannas and now fire on social media notifications. Our calorie-seeking drives evolved for scarcity and now drive overconsumption in abundance. The failure isn't that the system breaks — it's that it confidently processes the wrong input.

The practical implication: when you notice a reliable system producing bad outputs, check whether the environment has changed rather than assuming the system is broken. The fix is often environmental (plants manage oxygen levels inside leaves) rather than replacing the core mechanism.
