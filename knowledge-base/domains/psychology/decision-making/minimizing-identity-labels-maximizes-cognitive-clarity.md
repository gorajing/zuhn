Graham's core prescription is that since identity attachment corrupts reasoning, the strategic response is to let as few things as possible into your identity. Each label you adopt — 'I'm a Mac person,' 'I'm a libertarian,' 'I'm a Python developer' — is a domain where you've traded the ability to think clearly for the comfort of belonging.

This goes beyond tolerance. Tolerating Y while identifying as X still leaves your reasoning about X compromised. The more radical step is refusing the X label entirely. The practical implication for decision-making is that identity-light people have a structural advantage: they can evaluate evidence on more topics without triggering defensive cognition. This is especially valuable for founders and investors who need to update beliefs quickly when evidence changes.
