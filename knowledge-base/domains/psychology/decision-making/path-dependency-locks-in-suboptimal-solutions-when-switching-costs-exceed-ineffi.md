RuBisCo, the most abundant protein on Earth (~40 billion tons), evolved when atmospheric oxygen was negligible. Now that oxygen is abundant, it confuses O2 for CO2 roughly half the time, producing a toxic byproduct plants must actively detoxify. Yet no plant lineage has replaced RuBisCo — they simply produce massive quantities and bolt on repair pathways.

This mirrors organizational and technical path dependency: once a system is deeply integrated (hiring pipelines, codebases, supply chains), the cost of wholesale replacement dwarfs the ongoing cost of working around its flaws. The implication is that early architectural decisions deserve disproportionate scrutiny, because the window for low-cost switching closes faster than most decision-makers expect. Recognizing you're in a lock-in situation shifts the rational response from 'replace it' to 'build the cheapest viable workaround.'
