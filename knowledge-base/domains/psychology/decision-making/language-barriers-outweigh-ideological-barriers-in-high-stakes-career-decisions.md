Stalin personally invited Ferdinand Porsche to become General Director of Development for the entire Soviet automotive industry — an enormous role with virtually unlimited resources. According to Porsche's son, after serious consideration, Porsche declined not because of political objections to the Soviet system, but because of the language barrier. He couldn't imagine managing a massive engineering program without being able to communicate directly with workers and engineers. This reveals something counterintuitive about how humans make consequential decisions: practical, tangible constraints (can I communicate daily?) often dominate over abstract considerations (do I agree with this political system?). For decision-making frameworks, this suggests that when evaluating why people accept or reject opportunities, look first at mundane friction factors before assuming ideological or strategic reasoning.
