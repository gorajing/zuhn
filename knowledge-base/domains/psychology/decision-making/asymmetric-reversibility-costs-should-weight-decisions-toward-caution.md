Dr. Lappert's closing advice — 'think it through because it's a lot harder to get a tattoo off than it is to put it on' — illustrates a fundamental decision-making principle. Tattoo application is a single session measured in hours. Removal requires multiple laser treatments spaced weeks apart, each session painful, with full removal taking 1-2 years and costing 5-10x the original tattoo. The effort asymmetry between creation and reversal is roughly 10:1 to 50:1.

This pattern recurs across domains: it's easier to ship code than to roll back a migration, easier to hire than to fire, easier to regulate than to deregulate, easier to merge companies than to split them. Yet humans consistently treat decisions as if they were symmetrically reversible. Deliberately asking 'what's the reversal cost ratio?' before committing forces a more accurate risk assessment. When reversal costs are 10x+ the initial action, the decision deserves proportionally more upfront analysis.
