In archery, a perfectly rigid arrow cannot hit a target directly in front of the bow because the riser physically blocks the straight-line path. The solution is counterintuitive: make the arrow flexible so it oscillates and snakes around the obstacle. This principle applies broadly to strategy, negotiation, and problem-solving. When facing an immovable constraint (a regulatory requirement, a team member's non-negotiable, a market reality), the instinct to push through rigidly or remove the obstacle often fails. Instead, building controlled flexibility into your approach — adapting your trajectory while maintaining your endpoint — lets you navigate around what cannot be moved. The key word is 'controlled': the arrow's flex is precisely calibrated, not random wobbling.
