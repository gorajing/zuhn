Graham reframes the inequality debate by distinguishing between wealth and the corruption of wealth translating into power. The real objection most people have isn't to someone getting rich from a startup but to construction firms buying government contracts or rich parents buying college admissions. His proposed solution — radical transparency and logging of all power transactions — comes from computer security principles: the single most important deterrent is the knowledge that actions leave a trail.

This is strategically elegant because it targets the actual harm (corruption) without the collateral damage of suppressing wealth creation. Historical evidence supports this — what changed between Reggie Vanderbilt killing pedestrians with impunity and modern accountability wasn't the reduction of wealth but the increased visibility of power exercise. The prescription 'log everything' applies far beyond economics: in any system where illicit connections thrive in secrecy, transparency is more effective than prohibition.
