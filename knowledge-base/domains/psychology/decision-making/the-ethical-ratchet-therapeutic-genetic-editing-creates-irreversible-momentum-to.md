The video maps a clear ratchet mechanism: first, editing embryos to eliminate fatal genetic diseases (hard to argue against). Then, as it becomes normalized, not editing becomes seen as unethical ('condemning children to preventable suffering'). Once the door is open, incremental enhancements — disease immunity, metabolism, intelligence — each feel like small rational extensions. The key insight is that huge societal changes emerge from the accumulation of millions of personal decisions, not top-down mandates. This pattern applies broadly to technology adoption: the ethical frontier advances through a series of individually defensible steps that collectively produce an outcome no one explicitly chose. Understanding this ratchet dynamic is critical for anyone evaluating where emerging technologies will actually land versus where the current Overton window sits.
