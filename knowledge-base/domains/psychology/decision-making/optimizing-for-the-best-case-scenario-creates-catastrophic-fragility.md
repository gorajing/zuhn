Russia's invasion strategy assumed Ukrainian capitulation within 3-5 days—the window their forces could operate self-sufficiently without logistics support. This wasn't a secret flaw; it was a known structural limitation that leadership chose to bet against. When the assumption failed, there was no graceful degradation—fuel ran out, food ran out, communications were disrupted, and forces became disorganized simultaneously. The pattern is universal: systems optimized purely for the expected case, with no margin for deviation, don't degrade proportionally to how wrong the assumption was. They collapse entirely once the assumption breaks. This is distinct from acceptable risk-taking; Russia didn't accept the risk of a longer war with a backup plan—they simply didn't plan for it.
