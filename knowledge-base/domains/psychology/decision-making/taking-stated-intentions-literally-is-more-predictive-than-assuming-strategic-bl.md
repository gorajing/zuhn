Murray points out that Trump talked about tariffs 'rally after rally after rally' yet market participants and commentators like Ben Shapiro assumed it was merely a negotiating ploy. This reveals a systematic forecasting error: sophisticated observers over-index on strategic interpretation and under-index on literal interpretation of stated intentions.

This has broad applications beyond politics. In business, when a competitor announces a strategic shift repeatedly, the base rate prediction should be that they mean it. In relationships, when someone tells you who they are, believe them. The error pattern is consistent: high-context thinkers assume everything is subtext and signaling, when often the signal IS the message. Murray's heuristic — 'you can absolutely gauge what Trump is going to try to do by what he said on the campaign trail' — is a useful corrective to over-sophisticated analysis.
