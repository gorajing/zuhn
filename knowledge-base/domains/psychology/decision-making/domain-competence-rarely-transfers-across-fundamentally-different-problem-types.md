Paine draws a sharp distinction: Mao 'puts Humpty Dumpty back together' militarily when no one else could, then proves 'incapable of running the economy in peacetime.' The failure mode isn't stupidity or lack of effort — it's that wartime leadership (mobilization, loyalty, decisive violence, morale) uses almost none of the cognitive machinery required for price signals, supply chains, and distributed production.

This generalizes well beyond dictators. Founders who excel at zero-to-one often fail at scaling. Brilliant engineers make poor managers. World-class investors stumble as operators. The pattern: a high-salience success in Domain A creates a halo that both the actor and observers mistake for general capability, and the actor is then given authority over Domain B where the underlying skills don't apply.

Application: when evaluating anyone — a founder, a hire, a historical figure — ask which specific muscles their prior wins exercised, and whether the next task uses the same muscles. Past performance predicts future performance only within the same problem class.
