Itzler's father worked 6.5 days a week and still made every family event. The insight isn't that you should optimize for family presence over work — it's that consistency is the mechanism by which presence becomes identity. One missed dinner is forgettable; the absence of any missed dinners creates a pattern that subconsciously shapes how family members describe you for decades. The practical implication: reliability is a more efficient legacy strategy than intensity. A parent who shows up to everything with moderate engagement outperforms a parent who shows up to 60% with peak engagement, because the brain remembers the pattern, not the average.
