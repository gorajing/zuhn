Media amplifies young success stories (tech founders, athletes, musicians) creating the impression that early achievement is the norm. Survivorship bias hides the vast majority of successful people who achieved significance later in life. Research consistently shows that peak creativity in most fields occurs in the 40s, not the 20s.

The artificial urgency created by the '30 deadline' produces counterproductive behavior: taking shortcuts, avoiding necessary learning, choosing quick wins over deep capability building, and experiencing despair when the manufactured timeline isn't met. The people who built the most enduring achievements typically spent their 20s and 30s developing the capabilities they deployed in their 40s and beyond.
