A significant portion of human anxiety is generated by attempting to make things permanent that are inherently temporary: relationships, health, youth, career position, emotional states. Each attempt to freeze a changing situation creates tension between reality (which changes) and desire (which demands stasis).

Mono no aware suggests a different relationship: flow with impermanence rather than resist it. This doesn't mean not caring but caring more fully precisely because you know this moment, this relationship, this experience is temporary. The energy saved from futile preservation efforts becomes available for deeper engagement with what IS present.
