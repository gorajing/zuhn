The SEAL's motto — 'if it doesn't suck, we don't do it' — isn't masochism. It's a systematic calibration protocol. By repeatedly proving that you can endure more than you thought in controlled settings (cold water, extra reps, longer runs), you update your brain's internal model of your limits.

This has a transfer effect: someone who has proven to themselves that they can do 100 pullups after exhaustion carries that updated self-model into business negotiations, creative blocks, and emotional challenges. The suffering isn't the point — the recalibration is. Each voluntary encounter with discomfort provides data that your perceived limits are fiction, making the next encounter with any difficulty less likely to trigger premature quitting.
