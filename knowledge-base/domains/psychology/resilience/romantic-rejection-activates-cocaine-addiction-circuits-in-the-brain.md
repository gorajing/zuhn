Fisher's second study put people recently rejected in love into brain scanners. She found activity in the VTA (the love drive persists), attachment regions (you don't stop loving someone who dumped you), physical pain regions (the same area activated by a toothache — except a toothache goes away after the dentist), and crucially, three brain regions linked specifically to craving and addiction, including the nucleus accumbens.

The nucleus accumbens is the core brain region activated by cocaine, heroin, alcohol, cigarettes, and gambling addiction. This proves romantic love — both happy and rejected — is an addiction. Fisher argues this demands that rejected love be treated with the same seriousness as substance addiction, including potentially adapting principles from addiction recovery programs like AA. Telling someone to 'just get over it' fundamentally misunderstands the neurological reality.
