Hormozi cites the statistic that 52% of 18-year-olds expect to be millionaires by 25, illustrating that goals are commodity inputs with no differentiating power. The Atomic Habits framing is precise: 'the goal of the winner is to commit to the activities; the goal of the loser is to commit to the goal.' This inverts conventional motivation advice. Rather than setting bigger or clearer goals, the leverage point is building the capacity to repeat unglamorous activities without visible progress. The practical implication is that any time spent refining goals, vision boards, or outcome targets has near-zero ROI compared to time spent building systems for consistent execution.
