Modern culture tends to pathologize engagement with dark questions: existential crisis is treated as disorder rather than development. But cultures throughout history have used confrontation with mortality, meaninglessness, and suffering as developmental practices (memento mori, Buddhist death meditation, existentialist philosophy).

The difference between being destroyed by dark philosophy and being strengthened by it is integration vs. identification. Engaging with nihilism as a question to wrestle with is strengthening. Identifying with nihilism as a conclusion is destructive. The practice is to take the question seriously without taking the despair permanently.
