Abigail Marsh debunks several persistent myths: psychopathy is not extremely rare (1% prevalence, same as schizophrenia or bipolar disorder), not exclusively male (men are roughly twice as likely but women are affected too), not purely genetic or purely environmental, and not untreatable.

Effective treatments include psychostimulants (used for ADHD) that reduce impulsiveness, antipsychotics that act on dopamine systems, CBT for adults to overcome hostile attribution bias, and parent management training (PCIT) for children. The biggest barrier to treatment is that psychopathic individuals often don't believe they need it. When they recognize their behavior is 'blowing up their own life,' validated treatment approaches can help both them and the people around them.
