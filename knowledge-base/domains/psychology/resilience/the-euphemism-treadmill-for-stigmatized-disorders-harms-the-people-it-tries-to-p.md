Marsh argues against the proliferation of terms (sociopath, antisocial personality disorder, psychopath) for what is essentially one condition. The term sociopathy has no scientific definition and no mentions in scientific literature. It emerged from a belief that all antisocial traits result from social experiences, which is now known to be incomplete.

The practical harm is real: families of children with psychopathic traits cannot find consistent information when the terminology keeps shifting. Parents are already reluctant to seek help because of stigma and blame. When they do seek help, confusion about which term to search for, which diagnosis to ask about, and what the condition is actually called creates additional barriers. Marsh advocates standardizing on 'psychopathy' as the single scientific term with an established research base.
