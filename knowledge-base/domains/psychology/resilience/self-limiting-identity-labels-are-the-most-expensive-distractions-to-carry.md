Hormozi shares his own example: he believed he was bad at math from ninth grade onward due to a bad teacher. In his 20s, he decided this label no longer served him and stopped using calculators entirely, forcing mental math for years. The people around him now consider him exceptionally good with numbers. The identity was entirely constructed and entirely dismantlable.

The razor he proposes is brutal in its simplicity: does this belief, person, or action make it more or less likely that I hit my goals? Identity is not who you are — it's a description of what you do. 'Doing is being.' Since you control what you do, identity is 100% under your control. The historical naming convention (John the Carpenter, John the Mason) makes this literal — people are named by their actions. If you want a new identity, you need new actions, not new affirmations.
