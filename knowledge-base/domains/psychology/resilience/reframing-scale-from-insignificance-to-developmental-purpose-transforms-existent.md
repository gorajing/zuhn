The Egg reframes every painful human experience — being a victim, being a perpetrator, suffering, dying — as necessary curriculum for growing a cosmic consciousness. This is structurally identical to how effective resilience frameworks work at a personal level: reframing setbacks as training rather than punishment. The story's massive emotional impact suggests this reframe addresses a deep psychological need. People don't just want suffering to end; they want it to mean something. Therapeutic modalities like logotherapy (Frankl), post-traumatic growth research, and even startup founder psychology all converge on this: the narrative wrapper around hardship matters as much as the hardship itself. The practical takeaway is that when helping someone through difficulty, providing a developmental frame ('this is building something') is more useful than providing a comfort frame ('this will pass').
