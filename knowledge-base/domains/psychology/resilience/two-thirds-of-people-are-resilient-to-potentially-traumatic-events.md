George Bonanno's research, now replicated in over 100 independent studies, consistently finds that approximately two-thirds of people exposed to potentially traumatic events maintain a stable trajectory of healthy functioning. They get upset — that is normal and necessary for the stress response to work — but they continue to concentrate, do their jobs, and maintain close relationships.

This finding was initially met not with rebuttal but with indifference and dismissal from clinical communities who insisted it could not be true. Bonanno's colleagues did not even want him to use the word 'resilience' because it seemed too provocative. The persistent myth that trauma inevitably causes lasting damage reflects a cultural blind spot, not the empirical reality.
