Harris frames Trump's presidency as a 'stress test of our democracy that we nearly failed.' The key insight is that many democratic protections were norms — traditions of behavior that previous presidents followed voluntarily — rather than enforceable laws. Trump's willingness to violate these norms (refusing to commit to peaceful transfer of power, spreading election misinformation, inciting mob violence) exposed this vulnerability.

Harris argues Biden should work to 'diminish the power of the presidency' and create 'laws where we only had norms.' This is a general principle of institutional design: systems that depend on good-faith actors will eventually encounter bad-faith ones. The solution is to design systems that constrain worst-case behavior structurally rather than depending on the restraint of the people within them. This applies beyond politics to any institution — companies, platforms, organizations — where the gap between norm and law creates exploitable attack surface.
