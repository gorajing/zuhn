Unprocessed emotions keep cycling back as triggers because they were never properly metabolized. The common response is to reach for coffee, alcohol, phone, or screens to bypass the feeling. Somatic work offers an alternative: when a low-vibration feeling arises, physically locate where that emotion sits in your body and actually feel it for a few minutes.

As you feel these charges, they lose their power. Each processed trigger reduces the overall pull toward lower vibrational states, naturally raising baseline emotional frequency. This is the journaling principle taken deeper — not just writing about feelings, but physically experiencing and releasing them. The counterintuitive insight: spending 3-5 minutes feeling a difficult emotion can dissolve what years of avoidance kept alive.
