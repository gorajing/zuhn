The control paradox: seeking control creates anxiety about losing control, which creates more control-seeking behavior, which creates more anxiety. The person who must control their environment, relationships, and outcomes lives in perpetual tension because perfect control is impossible.

Dostoevsky's insight is that much suffering comes not from life events themselves but from the gap between how life is and how we demand it be. Reducing the demand for control doesn't make you passive - it makes you adaptive. The person who can work with circumstances rather than against them has more effective capacity than the one rigidly demanding specific outcomes.
