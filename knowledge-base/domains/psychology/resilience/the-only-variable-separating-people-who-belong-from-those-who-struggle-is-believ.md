Brown's research divided subjects into two groups: those with a strong sense of love and belonging, and those who struggled for it. The only distinguishing variable was belief in worthiness. The whole-hearted group shared three traits: courage (the original Latin meaning — telling the story of who you are with your whole heart), compassion (toward themselves first, then others), and authenticity (willingness to let go of who they thought they should be in order to be who they were). This is not a motivational platitude — it's a research finding that suggests the primary barrier to connection is not external circumstances but an internal narrative about worthiness that can be deliberately rewritten.
