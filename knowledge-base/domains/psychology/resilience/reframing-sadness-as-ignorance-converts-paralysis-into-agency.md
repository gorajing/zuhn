Hormozi's reframe — 'this is ignorance, not sadness' — is a cognitive intervention that shifts the locus of control from external circumstances to internal capability. When someone feels hopeless, they typically believe their situation is fixed. Reframing the emotion as an information gap implies a clear next action: learn more.

This maps to research on learned helplessness vs. growth mindset. The key mechanism is that ignorance is perceived as temporary and fixable, while sadness about circumstances feels permanent. By relabeling the emotion, you change the implied action from 'endure' to 'investigate.' This is practically useful for founders facing seemingly impossible problems — the reframe suggests that the feeling of being stuck is itself evidence that unexplored solutions exist, not that solutions don't exist.
