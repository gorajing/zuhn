Hormozi identifies a specific skill deficiency behind most failures: frustration tolerance, defined as the number of times you can be rejected and try again. Unlike grit (a trait), he frames this as a trainable skill — you build it by deliberately sitting in discomfort without escaping. The young man who quit sales at $80K didn't lack passion; he lacked the ability to persist through boredom.

This has direct application: instead of searching for work that never gets boring (impossible), build your tolerance for boring repetitive work. Hormozi himself has made ~5,000 ads in a single year after 14 years of weekly ad recording. The insight connects to behavioral psychology — people stop not at the point of maximum difficulty, but at the point of maximum boredom, when the work becomes repetitive and unrewarding.
