Guo explicitly does not 'sit still'—no TikTok, no TV, no movies. Her free time goes into angel investing (100+ companies) and building new ventures. This isn't about work-life balance; it's about ensuring every waking hour contributes to long-term compounding.

The deeper insight from YC's framework: there are only three activities that matter in the early stages—building product, talking to customers, and maintaining your physical health. Everything else is a distraction that feels productive but doesn't compound. This is especially relevant for founders who confuse being busy with being productive.
