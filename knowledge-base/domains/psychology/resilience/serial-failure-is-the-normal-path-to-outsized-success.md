Hormozi's personal trajectory of nine failed businesses before building a $250M+ portfolio challenges the narrative that successful entrepreneurs either get it right quickly or should quit. The failures weren't wasted — each one built domain-specific pattern recognition that eventually combined into the judgment needed for the winning venture.

This has an important implication for how we evaluate our own progress: if the expected number of attempts before success is high, then being on attempt three or four is not evidence of inability — it's expected variance. The dangerous conclusion is quitting at attempt five when the payoff was at attempt six. The key differentiator is whether each failure teaches something new or repeats the same mistake, connecting back to the ignorance debt concept.
