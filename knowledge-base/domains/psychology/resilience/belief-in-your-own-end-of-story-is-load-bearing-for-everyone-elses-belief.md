Itzler argues that outcome-belief isn't optional self-help fluff — it's a coordination signal. Investors, hires, partners, and customers all read a founder's conviction about where they're going, and skeptical founders repel capital and talent. The underlying mechanism is that people are reluctant to bet their own time or money on someone who isn't betting their own belief. The risk is that this principle rationalizes delusion. The honest version is: conviction in the absence of evidence is dangerous, but conviction that outlasts temporary setbacks is a precondition for surviving long enough to generate the evidence.
