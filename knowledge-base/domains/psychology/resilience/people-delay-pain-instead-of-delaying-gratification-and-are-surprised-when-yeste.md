Hormozi identifies a precise inversion of delayed gratification: instead of taking pain now for pleasure later, people take pleasure now and delay pain to tomorrow. The diet starts Monday, the savings start next month, the hard work begins next year. But today is yesterday's tomorrow — the pain you deferred is the reality you're living in right now.

He extends this with an investing analogy: never investing eliminates daily risk of loss but guarantees you never build wealth. Taking a small risk each day (potential for short-term loss) guarantees macro growth over time. The same applies to career building — you have to 'eat the glass' of short-term pain (working double shifts, swallowing pride, living on nothing) to pull future benefits into the present. The visual is a timeline of glass that gets progressively smoother, but most people's hands get too cut to keep pulling.
