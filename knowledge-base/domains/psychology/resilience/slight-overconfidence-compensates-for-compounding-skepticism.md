When working on ambitious projects, you face skepticism from two directions: other people doubt you, and you doubt yourself. These compound — external dismissal feeds internal doubt and vice versa. Graham observes that the most successful people across many fields are slightly overconfident, not because delusion is good, but because this particular error cancels out the systematic bias toward under-valuing early work. The key word is 'slightly' — wild overconfidence leads to ignoring real feedback, but a small calibration error on the optimistic side provides exactly the armor needed to survive the lame phase that every ambitious project passes through.
