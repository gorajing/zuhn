Bessel van der Kolk's 50 years of trauma research reveals a fundamental mismatch in conventional treatment: trauma is encoded in the body's survival brain, yet most treatments target cognition or neurochemistry. His studies found that SSRIs like Prozac and Zoloft 'didn't work very well' for PTSD, while yoga proved 'a more effective treatment for PTSD than any of the drugs we had studied.'

The mechanism is that trauma keeps the body in a state of perceived danger. Effective treatments — yoga, theater, EMDR — work because they give people new bodily experiences of safety, agency, and calm. This reframes the entire treatment question from 'how do we change what people think?' to 'how do we help people live in bodies that feel fundamentally safe?' The implication extends beyond clinical settings: any domain dealing with deep behavioral change (habit formation, performance anxiety, leadership presence) may benefit from prioritizing embodied practice over intellectual understanding.
