Early achievers often optimize for the specific skills that produced early results, creating narrow expertise. Late bloomers who spent years exploring, failing, and developing across domains often produce more integrative and original work because they have broader foundations.

The 'wasted years' of apparent non-achievement are often the most valuable in retrospect: they provide diverse experience, failed experiments that inform later success, and the psychological resilience that comes from sustained effort without reward. The person who succeeds at 45 after 20 years of development is often more capable and more resilient than the person who succeeded at 25 through a combination of talent and luck.
