Taleb draws a sharp line between mechanical systems (washing machines) and organic ones (cats, economies, people). Organic systems require stressors to function. You stress your body at the gym; bones need loading to maintain density; cells that break under fasting stress are replaced by stronger ones. The same Jensen's inequality that kills grandmothers at extreme temperature averages makes humans healthier with temperature variation.

The disease Taleb calls 'interventionism' is the compulsion to over-stabilize organic systems. Greenspan's Federal Reserve tried to eliminate all economic fluctuation — and merely delayed and amplified the inevitable crash. Maxwell's governor showed that over-stabilizing a steam engine causes it to blow up. The principle extends everywhere: governments seeking perfect stability, parents eliminating all childhood risk, and organizations trying to remove all variability from processes. The cure is often worse than the disease.
