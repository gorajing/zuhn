A landmark study at Harvard and Virginia universities found that students preferred experiencing physical pain (electric shocks) over just 15 minutes of boredom. The Mind Field episode replicated this dramatically: subject Jameson explicitly said 'I don't want to do that again' about the shock, yet after only 1 minute and 57 seconds of sitting alone, he voluntarily shocked himself — and then did it a second time.

This reveals something profound about human psychology: stimulus deprivation is perceived as worse than physical harm. The brain's need for input is so fundamental that pain — which at least provides sensation and something to process — becomes preferable to nothing. This has direct implications for understanding why people make seemingly irrational choices (doomscrolling, picking fights, substance use) when the alternative is unstimulating boredom. The intervention isn't willpower against bad choices but providing adequate stimulation.
