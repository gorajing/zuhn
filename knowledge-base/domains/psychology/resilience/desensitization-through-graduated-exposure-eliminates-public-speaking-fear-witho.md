Cain distinguishes between introversion (a temperament preference for lower stimulation) and public speaking fear (a conquerable anxiety). Her prescription is desensitization: start with very small, low-stakes speaking opportunities and gradually build up. This is borrowed directly from clinical psychology's exposure therapy framework.

The critical reframe is that public speaking fear isn't evidence that you're 'not meant' to speak publicly—it's simply a skill that responds to graduated practice like any other. Warren Buffett, whom Cain cites as a famous introvert, took a Dale Carnegie speaking course early in his career. The technique works because each small success recalibrates your threat assessment. The key is making the increments small enough that anxiety never overwhelms—you're training your nervous system, not your willpower.
