Paul Graham observes that most ambitious projects go through a stage where they don't seem impressive even to their creators. This is not a sign of poor quality—it's a structural feature of ambition. The gap between where the project is and where it's going makes the current state look worse than it is.

The practical damage is that most people never even reach the embarrassing stage, let alone push through it. They self-censor before starting. Understanding that early ugliness is a predictable phase—not a quality signal—reframes the emotional experience of creation. You're not failing; you're in the part of the process that filters out everyone who doesn't persist.
