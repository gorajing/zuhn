Paul Yu has an engineering degree he doesn't use, lives with his mom at 35, earns $30-35 every 28 days from YouTube, and does minimal part-time work. By every conventional metric, he is a failure. Yet he is genuinely, verifiably happy — having gone from psychiatric ward stays in his 20s (bipolar disorder) to zero incidents in his entire 30s.

His hierarchy is revealing: 'If you've got your health and a roof above your head, you're doing just fine.' Having experienced actual suffering (psychiatric confinement, forced medication, holiday seasons in the psych ward), he has a calibrated sense of what matters. This challenges the default assumption that career achievement and income are prerequisites for happiness. The people calling him a failure 'aren't living my life.' Internal metrics (mental stability, peace, genuine contentment) can diverge completely from external ones.
