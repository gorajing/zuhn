Most anxiety reduction techniques operate within the frame of 'your actions matter enormously, here's how to cope.' Optimistic nihilism inverts this: if the universe ends in heat death and every humiliation is forgotten, the stakes of any single moment are genuinely low. This isn't apathy — it's liberation from the illusion that mistakes are permanent.

This reframe is especially powerful for perfectionists and high-performers who catastrophize about reputation or legacy. The relief comes not from minimizing the experience of failure, but from accurately calibrating its actual duration relative to cosmic time. You don't remember the 13.75 billion years before you existed; the trillions after will feel the same. The logical conclusion is to act boldly rather than cautiously.
