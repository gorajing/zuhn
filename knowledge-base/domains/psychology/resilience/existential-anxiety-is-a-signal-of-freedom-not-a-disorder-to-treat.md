Modern psychology pathologizes anxiety as something to eliminate. Kierkegaard's angst is fundamentally different: it arises from the awareness of genuine freedom and responsibility. Feeling anxious about life choices isn't a disorder - it's evidence that you recognize those choices matter.

The practical reframe: attempting to eliminate existential anxiety through medication, distraction, or rigid routine doesn't resolve the underlying condition (being free) - it merely numbs the awareness of it. Kierkegaard suggests that engaging with anxiety rather than avoiding it is the path to authentic living.
