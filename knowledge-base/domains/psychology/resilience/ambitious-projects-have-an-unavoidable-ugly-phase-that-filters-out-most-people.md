Every ambitious project passes through a phase where it doesn't seem impressive even to its creator. This is not a sign of failure—it's a structural feature of doing new things. The people who produce great work are not those who avoid this phase but those who push through it. Most people never even start because the fear of producing something embarrassing is stronger than their ambition. Recognizing the ugly phase as universal and temporary reframes it from a signal to quit into a checkpoint on the path to quality.
