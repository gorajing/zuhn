Golf balls elastically deform and fully recover at impacts up to ~300 mph, but somewhere between 300–500 mph they cross into plastic deformation where the internal structure permanently breaks. This threshold is invisible from below — the ball looks fine at 290 mph and destroyed at 400 mph.

This maps directly to human and organizational resilience. People absorb stress and bounce back up to a point, then something breaks permanently — burnout, trust collapse, cultural rot. The dangerous part is that the elastic zone feels sustainable and gives no warning of how close you are to the plastic boundary. Founders who push teams at 90% of breaking point think they have margin because everyone keeps recovering, not realizing the threshold is nonlinear and the next 5% increment causes irreversible damage.
