Popular media and even some professional literature promote the idea of a few magic traits that make people resilient — 'the five traits of highly resilient people.' Bonanno's research finds the opposite: many different factors correlate with resilience, but they all have small effects. No single factor or small set of factors explains much of the variance.

This means resilience is not a fixed personality trait you either have or lack. It is an emergent outcome — a trajectory that depends on the interaction of many small factors with the specific event and context. A person can be resilient to one event and not another. This finding is both humbling (there is no simple formula) and hopeful (resilience is not reserved for a special few).
