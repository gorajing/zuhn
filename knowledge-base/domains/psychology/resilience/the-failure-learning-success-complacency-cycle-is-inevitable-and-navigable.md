Hormozi reframes the entrepreneurial journey from linear (failure → eventual permanent success) to cyclical (failure → learning → success → complacency → failure). This removes the emotional devastation of setbacks because failure simply means the learning phase is about to begin. Conversely, it tempers the high of success because complacency inevitably follows. He describes it as 'beating bosses' — sometimes that boss takes 10 years, not 10 minutes. The practical application is metacognitive: when you feel complacent, you're about to fail; when you're failing, you're about to learn. By recognizing the phase, you can consciously accelerate through complacency back to learning-success loops. This also eliminates the binary labeling of events as 'good' or 'bad' since both lead to the same destination.
