Graham identifies a subtle feedback loop: determination produces achievement, achievement increases temptation (status, comfort, options), and temptation tips the will-discipline balance toward willfulness, causing regression to the mean. This is Shakespeare's insight about 'thin men' being dangerous — they weren't distracted by power's perquisites.

This maps directly to observable patterns like lifestyle creep in successful founders, second-album syndrome in artists, and the general phenomenon of people who achieve one great thing then coast. The actionable takeaway is that discipline must be treated as a scaling challenge — each level of success requires a proportional upgrade in self-regulation systems, not just maintenance of existing habits.
