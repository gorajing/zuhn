Huang argues that low expectations are a strategic advantage because they remove the psychological shock of difficulty. Leaders who expect smooth progress fracture when reality pushes back — they interpret friction as failure rather than as the normal texture of ambitious work. Leaders who assume friction as baseline adjust faster because their mental model already accounts for resistance.

This reframes a common startup failure mode: most founders don't fail because they chose the wrong idea, but because they couldn't stay psychologically invested through the period when the idea produced only friction and criticism. The NVIDIA culture trains people to assume that meaningful work will feel wrong for a long time before it feels inevitable. This tolerance for extended ambiguity — staying committed while signals remain negative — is what allowed NVIDIA to build CUDA over years of skepticism rather than abandoning it at the first sign of market resistance.
