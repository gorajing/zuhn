Bowler draws a precise distinction: happiness is a psychologically relaxed state that is cumulative and contextual — it requires things having gone well, and one bad event can topple it. You cannot be happy and sad simultaneously. Joy is fundamentally different — it engages both dopamine systems AND stress systems, coexisting with despair and pain rather than replacing them.

After her stage four cancer diagnosis, Bowler found that happiness was no longer sufficient. She needed 'something more existentially gritty.' Joy adds a layer on top of reality rather than escaping it — it's the capacity to see everything and still find it good. This makes joy more durable and meaningful than happiness, especially during life's hardest periods. Most people get joy wrong because they think it's a bonus level of happiness rather than a separate, more powerful capacity.
