Perfect calibration sounds optimal in theory, but creative work faces asymmetric headwinds: other people's skepticism, your own harsh self-judgment, and the inherently unimpressive appearance of early-stage ambitious projects. These forces all push toward quitting too early. Slight overconfidence acts as a counterweight — not enough to make you delusional, but enough to keep you moving through the ugly phase where most people abandon ship.

This reframes overconfidence from a cognitive bias to correct into a strategic error to cultivate. The most successful people in many fields share this trait not because they're miscalibrated, but because the environment systematically punishes accurate pessimism more than slight optimism. The practical implication: when you catch yourself thinking 'this isn't good enough yet,' recognize that your internal critic is likely overweighted and push forward anyway.
