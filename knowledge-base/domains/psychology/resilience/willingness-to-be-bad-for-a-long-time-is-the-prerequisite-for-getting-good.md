Hormozi frames skill acquisition as fundamentally constrained not by talent or resources but by tolerance for sustained incompetence in public. The Charlie Munger quote he cites — 'the world is too rational a place to reward the undeserving' — means deserving requires being good, and being good requires a long period of being visibly bad. Most people quit early because they confuse the pain of early incompetence with evidence they're not meant for it, rather than evidence they're on the only path. The practical implication: when evaluating whether you'll succeed at something, ask not 'am I good at this yet' but 'am I willing to be bad at this publicly for years.' The latter is the binding constraint.
