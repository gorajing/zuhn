First-time founders experience every failure as personal — a churned customer questions the entire company, a flopped feature triggers existential doubt. Second-time founders develop what the article calls 'emotional distance without detachment': they still care deeply, but they process setbacks through a diagnostic lens rather than an emotional one. This isn't stoicism for its own sake — it's a practical durability upgrade. Founders who can metabolize bad news without spiraling make better decisions under pressure, pace themselves for the long game, and build better feedback loops because they're not defending their ego. The shift from seeking affirmation to seeking falsification is what makes them faster and more resilient.
