Bowler's most striking claim is that happiness is an emotional state but joy is a story. It is a feeling in your spirit that it feels good to be alive, to be here, to be put together with whatever you have left. Joy binds broken pieces together and still makes you laugh at the same time.

This narrative quality is what gives joy its transformative power. It is not just pleasant; it makes people more grateful, more hopeful, and more able to see reality with new eyes. It is both emotional and existential, which is what makes it feel gritty and real. And mostly what it takes is love and connection, not achievement or optimization.
