Van der Kolk identifies self-compassion — specifically, understanding that your reactions are rooted in getting stuck in the past — as a 'terribly important part of beginning to recover.' This isn't soft encouragement; it's a mechanistic claim. Without this reframe, people remain trapped in a shame cycle where their trauma responses generate more self-blame, which generates more stress, which amplifies the trauma responses.

The practical application extends to how institutions and individuals approach dysfunction. Systems that label and pathologize ('you have disorder X') without contextualizing ('this pattern developed because Y happened to you') may inadvertently reinforce the shame loop. Whether building therapeutic products, managing people, or working on personal growth, creating space for the narrative 'my reactions are understandable given my history' unlocks the capacity for change that pure behavioral correction cannot.
