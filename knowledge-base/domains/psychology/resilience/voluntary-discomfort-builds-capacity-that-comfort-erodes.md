Thoreau's choice to live simply when he could have lived comfortably wasn't asceticism for its own sake - it was training. Voluntary discomfort builds appreciation for what you have, resilience against loss, and freedom from dependency on material comfort.

Hedonic adaptation means increased comfort produces diminishing returns while increasing vulnerability: the person accustomed to luxury suffers more from its loss than the person accustomed to simplicity. Deliberately choosing less creates psychological optionality - the ability to be content across a wider range of circumstances.
