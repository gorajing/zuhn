After being cheated out of the equivalent of $1M by Edison, Tesla didn't retreat to a safe engineering job. Instead, he dug ditches for $2/day (about $50 today) while searching for investors to back his AC vision. He described this period as 'very painful,' yet he continued pursuing his technical vision rather than compromising on a more comfortable but less ambitious path.

This pattern — extreme adversity between achievements — serves as a natural filter for intrinsic motivation. Tesla's willingness to endure degrading manual labor while holding patents for revolutionary technology demonstrates that his drive was about the work itself, not status or comfort. This is a stronger signal of future output than early success, because early success can be partly luck, but sustained effort through humiliation requires genuine internal commitment.
