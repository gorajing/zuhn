Hormozi's Smoothie King example crystallizes this: coworkers earning $43 for a 6-hour shift would spend $15 on lunch, effectively working 2+ hours just to eat next door. A $100 shirt at $20/hour (after 20% payroll tax) costs 6 hours of labor. The reframe — 'would I work all day for this shirt?' — makes the trade-off visceral.

This is a mental model that converts abstract dollar amounts into concrete time commitments. It's particularly powerful for lower earners where the ratio is most dramatic, but scales at every income level. The technique works because humans are loss-averse about time in ways they aren't about money. It also exposes the hidden tax wedge — you need to earn significantly more than the sticker price due to taxes, making the hours-to-earn calculation even more sobering than it appears.
