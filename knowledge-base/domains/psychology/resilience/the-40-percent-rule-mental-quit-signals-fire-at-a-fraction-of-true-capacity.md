The 40 Percent Rule, popularized by Navy SEAL David Goggins, posits that when your mind tells you you're done, you're really only at about 40% of your true capacity. This maps to the central governor theory in exercise science — the brain proactively limits exertion as a protective mechanism, well before actual physiological failure.

The practical implication is that the feeling of being 'done' is a negotiable signal, not a hard limit. Marathon runners who 'hit the wall' at mile 16-20 still finish — meaning 40-60% of the race distance gets completed after the brain declares exhaustion. Recognizing this gap between perceived and actual limits is the first step to expanding what you believe is possible in physical, professional, and creative endeavors.
