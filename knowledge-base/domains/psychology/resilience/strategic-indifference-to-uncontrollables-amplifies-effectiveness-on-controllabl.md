The modern interpretation of 'not caring' is apathy or nihilism. Spinoza's version is surgical: identify what is within your sphere of influence and direct all emotional energy there. Everything outside that sphere receives intellectual attention (understanding) without emotional investment (worry, anger, anxiety).

The result is paradoxically more effective engagement: freed from wasting energy on uncontrollables (weather, others' opinions, market fluctuations), you have more capacity for things you can actually affect (your skills, your relationships, your responses). The person who cares about fewer things cares more effectively about the things that matter.
