Paul Graham observes that most people never do great work not because they lack talent, but because they abandon projects during the inevitable ugly early phase. Ambitious projects by definition start far from their final form, and our instinct is to judge them by the standards of finished work — which makes them look terrible.

The key insight is that this phase is structural, not a signal of failure. Knowing it's coming and expecting it transforms the emotional experience from 'I'm failing' to 'I'm on track.' The people who do great work aren't immune to the discomfort — they've just learned to keep going through it, often by developing specific psychological strategies to manage the gap between their taste and their current output.
