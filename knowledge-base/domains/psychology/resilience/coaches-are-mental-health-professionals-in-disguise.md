Reeves argues that coaches play a vastly underrecognized role in male mental health. About a third of male K-12 teachers are also coaches, meaning they continue working with boys after the school bell rings. Coaches sit shoulder-to-shoulder on benches, work in groups, and naturally notice when a boy is struggling -- creating safe, non-threatening openings for conversation.

For boys without strong father relationships, coaches often serve as the primary male role model. The decline of men in teaching (from 33% in the early 80s to 23% and falling) therefore represents not just an education gap but the erosion of informal mental health infrastructure for boys. Reeves argues that the lack of men in teaching and psychology may be as significant as the lack of women in tech.
