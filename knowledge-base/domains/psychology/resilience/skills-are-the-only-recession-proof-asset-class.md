Hormozi frames skills as the only truly unconfiscatable asset. After losing everything and being down to $1,100 in his bank account, he was able to collect $110,000 in his next 30 days purely on the strength of skills he'd built. No financial asset offers this kind of resilience — real estate can be seized, accounts can be frozen, investments can go to zero.

This reframes the traditional advice of 'invest in assets' to 'invest in yourself first.' The return on skill acquisition has no ceiling, no counterparty risk, and compounds across every future endeavor. It also explains why serial entrepreneurs can recover from bankruptcy — their skills survived even when their balance sheet didn't. For anyone starting from zero, this means the optimal first investment is always in acquiring high-value skills, not in financial instruments.
