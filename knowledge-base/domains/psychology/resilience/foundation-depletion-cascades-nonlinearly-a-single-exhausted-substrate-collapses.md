Health: sleep and nervous system regulation are the highest-leverage daily interventions because they are foundational substrates -- when broken, immune function, hormonal balance, cognitive performance, and emotional regulation all degrade simultaneously. No amount of supplementation or exercise compensates. Psychology: attention is a finite daily reservoir; when depleted, decision quality, relationship quality, creative output, and self-regulation all collapse in parallel. Organizations: when trust (the organizational substrate) erodes, coordination costs spike across every function simultaneously -- hiring, retention, execution speed, innovation. The structural pattern: complex systems have foundational resources that don't degrade linearly. They function normally until the foundation drops below a threshold, then everything built on top fails at once. The implication: protecting the foundation is categorically more important than optimizing any function that depends on it.
