In the light-dependent reactions, plants pump protons into the thylakoid space, creating a concentration gradient. The protons then flow back through ATP synthase, and this flow powers the creation of ATP. The thylakoid is literally 'charged like a battery.'

This gradient-as-energy-storage pattern is universal: it appears in mitochondria (proton motive force), nerve cells (ion gradients for action potentials), hydroelectric dams (water pressure differentials), and even pricing arbitrage in markets. Recognizing this pattern — create an imbalance across a boundary, then harvest energy from the equalization flow — is a powerful mental model for understanding systems that accumulate potential and release it in controlled bursts.
