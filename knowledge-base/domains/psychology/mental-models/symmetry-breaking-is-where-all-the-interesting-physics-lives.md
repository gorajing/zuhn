After the Big Bang, for every billion matter-antimatter pairs, one matter particle survived. That 1-in-10^9 asymmetry is responsible for every atom, galaxy, and life form. This is a powerful mental model: when outcomes appear rich and complex, look for tiny asymmetries in underlying processes, not large ones. The same logic shows up in compound interest, evolutionary selection, and market returns — a small edge ratcheted over enough iterations becomes everything. Apparent symmetry with tiny breaks is where the story actually lives.
