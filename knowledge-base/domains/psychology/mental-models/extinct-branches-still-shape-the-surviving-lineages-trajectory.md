Douglas's 2025 model uncovered something subtle: branches in the evolutionary tree that evolved and then went extinct left measurable traces in the lineages that survived. The living system still carries information about paths that were tried and failed. The present is shaped by the absence of things as much as the presence of things.

This is a powerful mental model against survivorship bias in any complex system. When analyzing why something successful is the way it is, the relevant causes include all the competing alternatives that were tried and lost — and those dead branches often constrained the winner's trajectory in ways that are hard to see without specifically looking. In business, this shows up as 'why did the market standardize on X' being incomplete without understanding the Y and Z that almost won.

Practically: when reverse-engineering why a complex system has its current shape, don't just study what's present. Look for the footprints of what used to exist. The absence of something is often load-bearing for understanding the present.
