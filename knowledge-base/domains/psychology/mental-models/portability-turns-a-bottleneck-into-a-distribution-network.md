The BASE experiment hit a precision wall from CERN's continuously-ramping accelerator magnetic fields. Their solution was to make antimatter shippable: an 800kg trap that loads onto a truck and can go to quieter labs worldwide. This is a distribution pattern worth noticing — often the constraint on a centralized resource isn't its capacity but the environment it's embedded in. Portability turns a single facility into a hub-and-spoke research network, often delivering more total output than capacity expansion would. Applies equally to compute, datasets, and expert knowledge: shipping the resource often beats scaling the central site.
