In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane maintains a concentration gradient of protons—high inside, low outside. This controlled imbalance is what drives ATP synthase, the enzyme that produces the cell's energy currency. Without the gradient, the enzyme has nothing to work with.

This pattern appears everywhere: batteries store energy as chemical potential difference, hydroelectric dams convert height differentials, and even markets function by exploiting information or price asymmetries. The insight is that useful work doesn't come from equilibrium—it comes from deliberately creating and then strategically releasing imbalance. Systems that maintain no gradients are, by definition, dead. This reframes the goal of any system designer: don't seek balance, seek productive imbalance with controlled release mechanisms.
