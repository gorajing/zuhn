Confucius proposed five key relationships — ruler/subject, father/son, husband/wife, elder/younger brother, friend/friend — each with defined mutual obligations. The ruler owed benevolence; subjects owed loyalty. This wasn't merely top-down authority but a web of reciprocal duties that created predictable behavior across millions of people without centralized enforcement.

As a mental model, this suggests that effective coordination in complex systems requires clearly defined roles with explicit mutual obligations rather than vague egalitarianism or pure top-down command. Modern applications include team operating agreements, manager-report contracts, and platform-developer relationships. The key insight is that hierarchy with reciprocity is a coordination technology — it reduces ambiguity about who owes what to whom, enabling large-scale cooperation.
