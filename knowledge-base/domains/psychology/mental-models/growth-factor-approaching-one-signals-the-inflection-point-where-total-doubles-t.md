The growth factor — the ratio of new additions today to new additions yesterday — is a powerful real-time diagnostic. When it's consistently above one (say 1.15), you're on the exponential part and orders of magnitude of growth may still lie ahead. When it drops to approximately one, you've hit the inflection point of the logistic curve, meaning the total will roughly double from wherever you are now and then flatten.

This is deeply counterintuitive: a growth factor of 1.15 versus 1.0 feels like a trivial 15% difference in daily pace, but they imply radically different futures — one means explosive continued growth, the other means you're halfway to the ceiling. This metric applies to any growth context: user acquisition, revenue, market penetration. Tracking the growth factor rather than absolute numbers gives you a leading indicator of when the regime is shifting.
