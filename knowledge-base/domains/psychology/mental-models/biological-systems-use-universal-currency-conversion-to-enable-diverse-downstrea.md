The body cannot use food energy directly for muscle contraction, nerve signaling, or membrane transport. It must first convert glucose into ATP — a universal intermediate that every cellular process accepts. This is identical to how economies require currency conversion: raw resources (oil, labor, goods) must be converted to money before they can participate in diverse transactions.

This 'universal currency' pattern appears across domains: in computing, diverse data formats are converted to standardized APIs; in organizations, varied individual contributions are converted to measurable output metrics. The key insight is that the conversion step is not overhead — it's what enables the system's flexibility. Without ATP, cells would need separate energy pathways for every function. The intermediary simplifies coordination at the cost of conversion efficiency.
