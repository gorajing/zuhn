The video highlights that less than 2% of Earth's biomass lives in the oceans, and 90% of that is in the first 200 meters — yet the ocean represents the largest habitat on Earth by volume. Humanity has concentrated its attention on the thin, accessible surface layer while the vast majority of ocean space remains a mystery.

This is a powerful mental model for decision-making: we naturally gravitate toward domains where observation is easy, creating an illusion of comprehensive understanding. The deep sea serves as a concrete reminder that the hardest-to-reach territories often contain the most novel discoveries. In business, research, and personal development, the highest-value insights frequently live in the zones that require the most effort to access — the metaphorical midnight zones where few competitors venture.
