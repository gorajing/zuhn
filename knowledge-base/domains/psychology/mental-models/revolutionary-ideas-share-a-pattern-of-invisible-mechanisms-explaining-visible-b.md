The video draws an explicit parallel between Freud's theory of the unconscious and Darwin's natural selection — both were 'abstract, invisible, and there was something about them that seemed irrational.' Both posited that observable phenomena (behavior, species adaptation) were driven by mechanisms that couldn't be directly seen.

This pattern recurs across revolutionary science: germ theory (invisible organisms causing visible disease), plate tectonics (invisible convection currents driving visible geography), and even modern AI (invisible weight distributions producing visible intelligence). Recognizing this pattern helps evaluate new theories — genuinely revolutionary ideas often feel irrational precisely because they locate causation in an invisible layer.
