Noah Smith's Napoleon-Trump parallel reveals a useful mental model: personalist leaders operate like mafia bosses, where every policy decision runs through a filter of personal loyalty, revenge on enemies, and entrenchment of authority. This model predicted Fed targeting before it happened and explains the DOJ-as-weapon pattern.

The model's predictive power comes from its simplicity: once you identify the governance style as gangsterist rather than institutional, you can forecast targets (any independent power center), methods (ad hoc legal/executive pressure), and vulnerabilities (the approach creates 'boondoggles and quagmires' because it optimizes for personal power rather than systemic effectiveness). This maps to Napoleon's trajectory: initial tactical wins followed by strategic overextension.
