For decades, the search for extraterrestrial life was anchored to the Goldilocks Zone — the distance from a star where water stays liquid. The implicit assumption was that stars are the only relevant energy source. Europa shattered this by demonstrating that tidal forces from Jupiter's gravity pump enough energy to maintain a subsurface liquid ocean billions of years old.

The reasoning pattern is powerful beyond astronomy: when you have a working framework (life needs liquid water needs heat), breakthroughs come from questioning which inputs to that framework are truly constrained. The Goldilocks Zone framework was correct about needing heat — it was wrong about heat requiring a star. This maps directly to business strategy (what if distribution doesn't require a sales team?), technology (what if computation doesn't require silicon?), and problem-solving generally. The meta-skill is identifying which 'fixed' variable in your model might actually have substitutes.
