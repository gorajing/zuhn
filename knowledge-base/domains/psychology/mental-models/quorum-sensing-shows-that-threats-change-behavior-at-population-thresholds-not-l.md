The video describes how bacteria initially 'fly under the radar' after entering a wound, doubling every 20 minutes. Only when a certain population threshold is reached do they collectively change behavior and begin damaging the body by altering the environment around them. This is quorum sensing — a phase transition triggered by population density rather than any individual bacterium's decision.

This threshold-based behavior change appears across many domains: social movements that simmer quietly until reaching critical mass, technical debt that's manageable until it suddenly isn't, market bubbles that inflate gradually then pop. The mental model matters because humans tend to extrapolate linearly — assuming that twice the bacteria means twice the damage. In reality, the system is stable-stable-stable-catastrophe. Monitoring the population count matters more than monitoring the current damage level, because by the time damage is visible, the phase transition has already occurred.
