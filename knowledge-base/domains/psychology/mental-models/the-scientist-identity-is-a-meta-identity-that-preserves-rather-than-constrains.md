Graham makes one exception to his 'keep identity small' rule: identifying as a scientist may be a net positive. But he argues this isn't really an exception — it's a meta-identity that commits you to a process (follow evidence wherever it leads) rather than to any specific belief. It's like putting a sign in a cupboard saying 'this cupboard must be kept empty.'

This distinction between conclusion-identities and process-identities is powerful. A biblical literalist is committed to a specific conclusion regardless of evidence. A scientist is committed to changing conclusions when evidence demands it. This suggests that when you must adopt an identity, choose process-identities over conclusion-identities. 'I'm someone who updates on evidence' is safer than 'I'm someone who believes X.' The same logic applies to professional identity — 'I'm a builder' is less constraining than 'I'm a React developer.'
