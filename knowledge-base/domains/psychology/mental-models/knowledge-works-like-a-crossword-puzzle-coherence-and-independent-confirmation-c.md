Susan Haack's crossword puzzle metaphor resolves a classic epistemology debate. Foundationalists say knowledge must rest on bedrock axioms; coherentists say beliefs just need to be mutually consistent. Haack's 'foundherentism' says both are partially right: like a crossword, each entry needs independent clues (evidence/foundation) AND must be consistent with crossing entries (coherence).

This maps directly to decision-making in complex domains. A startup thesis shouldn't rely solely on first-principles reasoning (pure foundationalism) or solely on pattern-matching with other successful companies (pure coherentism). The strongest conviction comes when independent evidence supports the thesis AND it's consistent with other things you know to be true. When beliefs fail the crossword test — they have evidence but contradict other established knowledge, or they cohere with your worldview but lack independent support — that's a signal to investigate further.
