The video repeatedly emphasizes that mechanical advantage is not free: 'I'm getting four times the force, but I'm only moving it 1/4th as far.' When pulling the truck onto the trailer with ratchet straps, the force multiplication was enormous but 'it was taking forever' because distance had to be covered in tiny increments.

This is a physical manifestation of conservation laws that appear everywhere: compressing data trades space for computation time, delegation trades direct control for bandwidth, abstraction trades flexibility for simplicity. Recognizing that *every* advantage has a hidden cost in another dimension is a powerful diagnostic tool — when something seems like pure upside, you haven't identified what dimension is paying the price.
