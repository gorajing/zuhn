In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane is deliberately 'charged' by pumping protons to one side, creating a concentration gradient. This stored imbalance is then released through ATP synthase in a controlled way to produce ATP — usable energy. The energy isn't in the protons themselves but in the *difference* between the two sides.

This gradient model applies broadly: competitive advantage comes from information asymmetry (knowing what others don't), pricing power comes from supply-demand imbalance, and learning breakthroughs come from the gap between your current understanding and a new framework. The key insight is that useful work requires maintaining imbalance — equilibrium is death (literally, in biology). Systems that actively create and sustain productive imbalances outperform those seeking balance.
