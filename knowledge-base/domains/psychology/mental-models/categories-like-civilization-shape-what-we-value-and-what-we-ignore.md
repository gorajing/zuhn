The video opens with a critical epistemological point: 'civilization' is an intellectual construct that privileges certain traits (surplus production, cities, writing, centralized government) while implicitly devaluing societies organized differently. The Greek origin of 'barbarian' — meaning anyone who didn't speak Greek — illustrates how categorization schemes encode the values of whoever creates them.

This framing effect operates identically in modern contexts. When VCs define 'real companies' as venture-scale startups, they systematically undervalue bootstrapped businesses. When we categorize 'AI capabilities' by benchmarks, we ignore forms of intelligence the benchmarks don't measure. Recognizing that our categories are constructs — not natural kinds — is the first step toward seeing what they hide.
