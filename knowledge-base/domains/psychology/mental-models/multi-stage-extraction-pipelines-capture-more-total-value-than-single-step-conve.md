In photosynthesis, a single photon's energy is not converted to ATP in one step. Instead, the excited electron passes through Photosystem II, the Cytochrome Complex, Photosystem I, and ATP Synthase — each extracting a portion of energy for different purposes (water splitting, proton pumping, NADPH creation, ATP synthesis). No single step could efficiently capture all the energy.

This maps directly to how effective systems extract value: sales funnels with multiple touchpoints convert better than single asks, content repurposing pipelines extract more value than one-shot publishing, and iterative compression (as in Zuhn's own hierarchy) captures more insight than single-pass summarization. The principle is thermodynamic: smaller energy differentials at each step mean less waste.
