The video opens by noting photosynthesis 'actually rather sucks'—it's complicated, inefficient, and confusing—and closes the RuBisCo section with the phrase 'another example of unintelligent design.' This is not a rhetorical flourish. RuBisCo misfires half the time, requires enormous overproduction to compensate, generates toxic byproducts requiring additional cleanup enzymes, and cannot be replaced because evolution has no mechanism to do a ground-up redesign.

This mental model—'evolved systems are kludges, not designs'—is useful whenever you encounter a biological, organizational, or technological system that seems needlessly complex. The complexity is often load-bearing: it encodes the history of every patch applied to survive a past crisis. The correct response to such complexity is not to assume it can be simplified without loss, but to map it carefully before touching it. Many software rewrites fail because engineers mistake 'unintelligently designed' for 'unnecessary.'
