The CNN interview famously showed a xiangqi board while Kissinger discussed Go — revealing that the metaphor was never about accurately describing Chinese strategic culture. Xiangqi (Chinese chess) is faster-paced, more tactical, and far more popular in China than Go. If board games actually reflected strategic culture, China's approach would look more aggressive and short-term than America's, not less.

Kissinger chose Go because he wanted America to adopt an encirclement strategy toward China — and it worked. This is a masterclass in strategic framing: the most powerful metaphors aren't the most accurate ones, they're the ones that produce the behavioral change you want in your audience. In business communication, product positioning, and leadership, the question isn't 'what metaphor best describes reality?' but 'what metaphor will cause my audience to act in the way I need them to act?'
