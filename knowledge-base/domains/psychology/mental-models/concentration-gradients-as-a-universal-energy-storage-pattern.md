In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane acts as a barrier that maintains a concentration gradient of protons. Energy is stored by pumping protons to one side (creating imbalance), then harvested when protons flow back through ATP synthase (releasing the imbalance). The video explicitly compares this to 'charging a battery.'

This pattern — store energy by creating imbalance across a barrier, harvest energy by allowing controlled equalization — is universal. Hydroelectric dams store gravitational potential across a physical barrier. Batteries store charge across electrochemical barriers. In economics, arbitrage opportunities exist wherever price imbalances persist across market barriers. Recognizing this pattern helps identify energy-storage mechanisms in unfamiliar systems: look for the barrier, look for the imbalance, and look for the controlled channel that harvests the flow.
