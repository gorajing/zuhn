The terraforming plan describes decades of cooling with no visible progress — the atmosphere stays dense, hot, and deadly. Then at 31°C, the CO2 hits its critical temperature and suddenly liquefies, triggering a 30-year global rainstorm that transforms the entire planet. The pressure and temperature drop in unison once the cascade begins.

This is the physical manifestation of a pattern that appears everywhere: startups that grind for years then hit product-market fit overnight, skill acquisition plateaus that break into sudden competence, social movements that simmer then erupt. The critical insight is that absence of visible progress is not evidence of absence of progress — the system may be approaching a phase boundary where small additional input triggers massive state change. Quitting during the 'no visible change' period means abandoning the investment right before the payoff.
