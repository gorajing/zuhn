WWII killed 60+ million people not because the animosities were uniquely intense — comparable ethnic and territorial tensions existed throughout history — but because industrialized nations had unprecedented capacity to project force. The same factories that produced consumer goods produced tanks, bombs, and the infrastructure of genocide.

This is a critical mental model for evaluating modern risks: the danger of any system scales with its operational capacity, not the intensity of intent behind it. A poorly governed AI system with massive compute is more dangerous than a malicious actor with limited resources. In business, this means the biggest risks come not from hostile competitors but from well-resourced organizations making small systematic errors at scale. Monitoring capability growth matters more than monitoring intent.
