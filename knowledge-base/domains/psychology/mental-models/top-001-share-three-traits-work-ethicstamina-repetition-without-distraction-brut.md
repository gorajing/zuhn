Chamath's observed pattern across the top performers he's spent time with:

(1) **Work ethic/stamina** — Kevin Hart plays poker until 4am, gym at 6am, then 4-5 shoots; finds the gear from 'a level of desire' that drives them to pack as much into life as possible. The 'rest when you're dead' adage operationalized.

(2) **Repetition without distraction** — Draymond Green's commitment to thousands of hours of focused practice without being pulled away by distractions; Elon disappearing for weeks living/breathing inside Tesla or SpaceX, sleeping on the ground in a sleeping bag.

(3) **Brutal honesty about failure** — Bill Ackman, Dan Loeb being 'incredibly honest about the trials and tribulations of their job.' Not necessarily honest about everything in their lives, but in the thing they're best at, they have incredible 'taste,' which requires honesty. Without honesty about quality, you can't have taste; without taste, you can't be exceptional.

For builders and operators: this is a useful self-diagnostic. Which of the three are you weakest on? The honest answer (which itself requires honesty) probably points at the next-most-leveraged personal investment. For Zuhn editorially: the converse pattern is also useful — if a leader visibly lacks one of these (chronic burnout-then-recovery cycles, distraction-driven priorities, blame-everyone-else when things fail), it's a strong signal they're not durable at the top tier.
