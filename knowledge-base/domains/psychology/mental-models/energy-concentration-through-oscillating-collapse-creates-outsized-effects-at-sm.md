The Rayleigh-Plesset equation describes how an underwater bubble oscillates — expanding and contracting — with each cycle concentrating more energy into a smaller volume. At the moment of final collapse, temperatures can reach thousands of degrees, producing visible light from a microscopic bubble. The input energy (a shock wave) is modest; the output (photon emission) is extreme.

This oscillating-collapse pattern appears across domains. Financial bubbles expand and contract with increasing amplitude before a final collapse that concentrates losses. Feedback loops in organizations can amplify small signals through repeated cycles until they produce outsized organizational changes. Recognizing the oscillation phase — repeated expansion-contraction cycles with growing amplitude — serves as an early warning that energy is being concentrated toward a disproportionate endpoint.
