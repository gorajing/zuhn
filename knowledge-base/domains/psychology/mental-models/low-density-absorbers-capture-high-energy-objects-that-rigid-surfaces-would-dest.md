In the Stardust mission, comet particles traveling at 6 kilometers per second were captured intact by aerogel. The particles entered the porous material, progressively breaking apart the silica network and losing energy until they stopped. A solid surface would have vaporized the same particles on impact — destroying the very thing you wanted to study.

This is a general principle about reception systems: matching impedance between incoming energy and the receiving structure determines whether you capture information or destroy it. In management, this means absorbing critical feedback gradually through structured processes rather than rigid defensive responses. In startups, it means building flexible onboarding that absorbs diverse customer needs rather than forcing a rigid workflow. The insight is that the receiver's structure must yield proportionally to preserve what matters in the incoming signal.
