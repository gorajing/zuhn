In the Calvin Cycle, 3 molecules of RuBP are processed through carbon fixation and reduction to yield 6 molecules of G3P. But 5 of those 6 G3P molecules must be recycled to regenerate the starting RuBP. The net yield is just 1 G3P per full cycle turn — an 83% reinvestment rate that's invisible if you only look at gross production.

This is a mental model for any cyclical system: SaaS businesses that spend 80% of revenue on retention to maintain ARR, content creators who produce 5 pieces to stay relevant for every 1 that grows the audience, or teams that spend most of their sprint maintaining existing systems. When evaluating any cycle's efficiency, always ask: what fraction of gross output is consumed by regeneration? The answer is usually much higher than intuition suggests, because cycle maintenance costs are structurally invisible.
