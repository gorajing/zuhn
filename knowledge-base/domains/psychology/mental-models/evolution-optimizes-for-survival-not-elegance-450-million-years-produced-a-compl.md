Photosynthesis converts sunlight to glucose through a Byzantine chain of four protein complexes, electron carriers, proton gradients, and a three-phase carbon fixation cycle. After 450 million years of evolutionary pressure, it's still described by biologists as inefficient and confusing. This isn't a failure of evolution — it's a fundamental property of systems optimized by selection rather than design.

The mental model applies broadly: any complex system shaped by iterative survival pressure (codebases, organizations, markets, legal systems) will converge on messy-but-robust solutions that resist clean redesign. The impulse to 'fix' such systems by replacing them with elegant alternatives often underestimates why the messiness persists — it encodes solutions to problems you can't see from the outside. Herbert Simon's 'satisficing' applies at the deepest biological level.
