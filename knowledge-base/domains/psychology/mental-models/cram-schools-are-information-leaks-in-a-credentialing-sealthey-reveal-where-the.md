Graham frames credentials as a 'seal' preventing direct transmission of power between generations, and cram schools as leaks in that seal where wealth converts into credentials. This is a broadly applicable mental model: any gatekeeping system will develop parasitic industries that exploit its weaknesses, and those industries serve as diagnostic tools showing you where the system is most vulnerable.

The model extends beyond education. Regulatory arbitrage firms reveal where financial regulations leak. SEO farms reveal where search algorithms are hackable. Interview prep services reveal where hiring processes are gameable. In each case, the existence and popularity of the 'cram school equivalent' is a precise signal: it tells you both where the system fails and, by its decline, when you've succeeded in fixing it. The actionable takeaway is to study your system's parasites rather than trying to eliminate them directly.
