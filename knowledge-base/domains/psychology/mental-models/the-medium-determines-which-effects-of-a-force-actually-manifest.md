A nuclear explosion on the Moon versus Earth doesn't just produce a 'weaker' version of the same destruction — it produces an entirely different set of effects. On Earth, the atmosphere creates the shockwave (the most destructive element) and the mushroom cloud. Remove the atmosphere and you don't get a smaller shockwave — you get zero shockwave, but gain unimpeded radiation and ballistic debris instead.

This pattern applies broadly: the same startup strategy in different markets doesn't produce weaker/stronger versions of the same outcome — it activates entirely different dynamics. The same management technique in different cultures doesn't scale linearly. When transferring a proven approach to a new context, the critical question isn't 'how much weaker will this be?' but 'which effects will exist at all and which new ones will emerge?'
