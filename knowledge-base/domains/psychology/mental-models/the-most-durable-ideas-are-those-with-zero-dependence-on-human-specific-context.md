The alien truth framing maps cleanly onto the concept of idea durability: truths that any intelligent being would share are maximally portable across time, culture, and context. Truths that depend on human evolutionary history, cultural assumptions, or biological substrate are inherently more brittle — they may stop being useful as circumstances change. This isn't just philosophical — it's practical for anyone building knowledge systems or making long-term bets.

Applied concretely: when evaluating whether to invest deeply in an idea or principle, ask how much of its validity depends on features specific to humans circa 2024. Ideas about incentive structures, empirical reasoning, and feedback loops tend to score well; ideas about status, aesthetic preference, or social norms tend to score poorly. The alien truth test is a fast heuristic for flagging which parts of your worldview are load-bearing versus culturally contingent.
