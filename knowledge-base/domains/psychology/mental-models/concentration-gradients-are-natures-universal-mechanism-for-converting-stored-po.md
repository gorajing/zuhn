In photosynthesis, the electron transport chain doesn't directly create usable energy. Instead, it pumps protons across the thylakoid membrane, building up an artificial concentration gradient. When those protons naturally push back toward equilibrium through ATP synthase, that flow drives the mechanical production of ATP.

This gradient-harvesting pattern appears everywhere: batteries store charge differentials, hydroelectric dams store water height differentials, and market arbitrage exploits price differentials. The deeper principle is that raw energy is useless — it must first be converted into a structured imbalance, and then the system's natural tendency to resolve that imbalance is channeled through a mechanism that captures work. This mental model applies to business (create information or access asymmetries, then harvest them) and personal productivity (build up focused preparation, then release it through structured output).
