There is a crucial psychological difference between 'house prices are up since the pandemic, so there are more homeless people and downstream effects' and 'civilization is collapsing.' The specific version identifies a concrete cause, implies concrete solutions, and is falsifiable. The vague version triggers omnipresent despair and learned helplessness. Crucially, the specific version isn't optimistic spin — it's just more precise. This reframing doesn't require ignoring evidence or being a pragmatist who endorses useful lies. It's the opposite: vague catastrophism actually ignores evidence by collapsing many distinct phenomena into one emotional narrative. Specificity is simultaneously more epistemically honest and more compatible with psychological functioning and taking productive action.
