The video illustrates that 4 classical bits can be in one of 16 configurations, but 4 qubits in superposition occupy all 16 simultaneously — and this scales exponentially (20 qubits = ~1 million parallel states). Humans systematically underestimate exponential growth because our evolved cognition is calibrated for linear, additive reasoning. This mental model failure extends far beyond quantum computing: it explains why people underestimate compound interest, viral spread, network effects, and AI capability scaling. Recognizing 'exponential state space' as a named concept helps identify situations where naive intuition will be orders of magnitude wrong.
