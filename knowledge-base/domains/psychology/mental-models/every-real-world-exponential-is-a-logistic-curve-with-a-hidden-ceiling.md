The distinction between exponential and logistic growth is one of the most important mental models for forecasting. Pure exponentials (constant percentage growth forever) don't exist in nature or economics — they always hit some constraint: a virus runs out of susceptible hosts, a startup saturates its addressable market, a technology approaches physical limits.

The practical implication is that when you observe exponential-looking growth, the most important analytical question isn't 'how fast is it growing?' but 'where is the ceiling?' Early-stage exponentials and early-stage logistics look identical, but their long-term trajectories are completely different. This matters enormously for AI scaling debates, market sizing, and epidemic modeling — the math of the early phase tells you the growth rate, but understanding the constraints tells you the outcome.
