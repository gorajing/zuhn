Kurzgesagt demonstrates that the current nuclear arsenal can destroy every city on Earth and still have 1,500 warheads left over — yet detonating all of them at once barely registers as an extinction event. The jump from civilizational catastrophe to actual species extinction requires energy comparable to the dinosaur-killing asteroid. Humans collapse these wildly different scales into one mental bucket labeled 'total destruction.' This overkill illusion is a specific instance of scope insensitivity: once damage exceeds personal comprehension, all further magnitudes feel equivalent. Recognizing this bias matters for any domain involving large-scale risk assessment — from climate modeling to startup failure scenarios — where the difference between 'very bad' and 'terminal' is precisely the margin that matters most.
