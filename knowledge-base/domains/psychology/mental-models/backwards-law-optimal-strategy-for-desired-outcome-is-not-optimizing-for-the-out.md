This is a restatement of Alan Watts' backwards law and related observations in happiness research (directly pursuing happiness tends to reduce it). Bayer's extension is practical: when the desired outcome has a strong affective/identity component, direct pursuit activates its psychological opposite. The correct optimization target is the set of internal conditions (state, identity, attention) that would naturally produce the outcome. This doesn't apply to every goal — technical skills, pure execution tasks can often be directly pursued — but applies reliably to goals where the pursuer's state is part of the production function.
