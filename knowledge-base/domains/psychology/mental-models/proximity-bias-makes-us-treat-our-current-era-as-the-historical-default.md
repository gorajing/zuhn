The video opens and closes with the observation that our current lifestyle feels 'normal' and 'ordinary' — yet it has existed for less than 0.001% of human history. For 99.99% of the human experience, life was fundamentally different: small bands, no agriculture, no surplus, no specialization.

This is a specific instance of proximity bias — the tendency to treat whatever is immediately present as the default state. It distorts risk assessment (we underestimate fragility), gratitude calibration (we take extraordinary infrastructure for granted), and strategic thinking (we assume current conditions will persist). Recognizing the anomaly of the present is a prerequisite for thinking clearly about its fragility.
