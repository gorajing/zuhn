In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane acts as a dam. Light-dependent reactions pump hydrogen ions (protons) into the thylakoid interior, creating an ever-growing concentration difference across the membrane. When protons push their way out through ATP synthase, that flow powers the creation of ATP — the cell's energy currency. The energy isn't stored in any single molecule; it's stored in the asymmetry itself.

This gradient pattern appears everywhere: batteries store energy as charge gradients, knowledge advantages work as information gradients, and even pricing power exists as a value-perception gradient between what something costs to produce and what customers will pay. The mental model suggests that building sustainable advantage means maintaining asymmetry across a boundary — and that energy dissipates the moment the gradient equalizes. Defensibility comes not from what you have, but from the differential you maintain.
