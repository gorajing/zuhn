When stars above ~150 solar masses form, they burn so hot and bright that their radiation pressure blows away any surrounding gas that could add more mass. The limit isn't set by how much material exists — it's set by the star's own output creating a negative feedback loop against further growth.

This pattern appears across domains: successful products that grow so fast they overwhelm their own support infrastructure, companies whose culture degrades as they scale, or social movements whose visibility triggers backlash. The insight is that the binding constraint on growth is often internally generated rather than externally imposed. Recognizing self-limiting feedback early lets you design around it — stars can't, but systems designers can.
