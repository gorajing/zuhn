Photosystem II is often described simply as 'the thing that absorbs light,' but it contains at least 99 distinct chemicals — including over 30 individual chlorophyll molecules — all coordinated to accomplish what appears to be a single, simple task: capturing a photon's energy and transferring it to a mobile electron carrier. The apparent simplicity of the input-output relationship (photon in, energized electron out) conceals enormous molecular coordination.

This pattern recurs in software, organizations, and products. A Google search query returns results in 200ms, hiding millions of coordinated processes. A one-click checkout masks payment processing, fraud detection, inventory management, and fulfillment coordination. When estimating the difficulty or cost of building something, the 'PSII principle' suggests multiplying your complexity estimate dramatically — what looks like one step often requires 99 coordinated components to execute reliably.
