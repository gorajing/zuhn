In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane is 'charged like a battery' by pumping hydrogen ions to one side, creating a concentration gradient. When protons flow back through ATP synthase, that stored potential converts to usable energy (ATP). The membrane's role is maintaining the differential — without it, equilibrium destroys the energy store.

This gradient pattern appears everywhere: market arbitrage exists only while price differentials persist between venues. Expertise creates value precisely because of the knowledge gap between expert and non-expert. Competitive moats work by maintaining a differential (cost, switching, network) that prevents equilibrium. Even attention economics runs on information gradients — content is valuable when it bridges what the audience knows and what they need to know.

The actionable insight is that value creation often means building and maintaining differentials, not achieving equilibrium. And like the phospholipid bilayer, the barrier that maintains the gradient is as important as the gradient itself. Remove the membrane and the protons equalize instantly — remove barriers to entry and competitive advantages evaporate.
