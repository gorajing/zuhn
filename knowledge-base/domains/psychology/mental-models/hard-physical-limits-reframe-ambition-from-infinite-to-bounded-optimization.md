Kurzgesagt frames the Local Group — our gravitationally bound cluster of galaxies — as both a hard limit and a spectacular playground. With a trillion stars and billions of years to explore, it's simultaneously the smallest possible answer to 'how much universe do we get' and an incomprehensibly vast resource.

This reframing from unbounded to bounded is psychologically powerful. Infinite possibility breeds paralysis and diffuse effort; known boundaries enable focused optimization. The same principle applies to startups (TAM as a bound), personal careers (finite working years), and resource allocation generally. Accepting a hard limit isn't defeatist — it's the precondition for strategic clarity. You stop asking 'what if we could do everything' and start asking 'given our actual constraints, what's the highest-value use of what we have.'
