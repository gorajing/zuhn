ALPHA-g already answered 'does antimatter fall down?' (yes) in 2023, but with ±13% error bars. GBAR's elaborate pipeline exists to reach 1% then 1-in-100,000 precision — because at 0.5 Kelvin, antiatoms jitter too fast to time a 20cm fall accurately. The lesson: when someone takes a much harder route to the same nominal output, they're usually climbing a precision curve where the 'easy' approach has a built-in floor. Judging research (or products, or processes) only by output counts misses the precision dimension. Always ask: what's the precision target, and what's the floor of the simpler method?
