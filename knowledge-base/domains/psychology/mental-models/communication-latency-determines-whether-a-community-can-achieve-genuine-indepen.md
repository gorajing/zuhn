Vitalik frames the Moon vs. Mars question through communication latency: the Moon's 2.3-second round-trip is effectively synchronous with Earth — conversations are possible in real time. Mars's 5-15 minute one-way latency makes every interaction asynchronous, more like email than a phone call. That asynchrony isn't just a UX inconvenience — it's a civilizational structuring force.

Organizations that must operate asynchronously develop independent decision-making, local institutions, and their own norms. Communities that remain synchronously coupled to a central authority remain governed by it. This is why Mars independence is a recurring sci-fi trope and Moon independence is rare: Mars's physics require self-sufficiency, and self-sufficiency breeds autonomy. The principle extends beyond space: latency, bandwidth, and communication friction between a node and its center determine how much institutional independence that node can realistically develop. Remote teams, diasporas, and offshore subsidiaries all face versions of this constraint.
