The video opens by calling photosynthesis a process that 'rather sucks — complicated, inefficient and confusing.' Yet this inefficient process powers virtually all life on Earth. RuBisCo fails half the time. The Calvin Cycle requires elaborate regeneration phases. Plants waste energy processing toxic byproducts. None of this matters because photosynthesis achieved escape velocity first.

This is the QWERTY principle at planetary scale: once a mediocre solution becomes the foundation that everything else depends on, its inefficiency becomes irrelevant. The mental model to internalize is that competition is often won at the 'good enough + fast' stage, not the 'optimal + late' stage. When evaluating strategies, ask not 'is this the best possible approach?' but 'can this approach accumulate dependencies faster than alternatives can mature?'
