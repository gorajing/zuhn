In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane maintains a proton concentration gradient — high inside, low outside. When protons flow back through ATP synthase to equalize, that flow powers the creation of ATP. The membrane's ability to resist equalization is what makes energy storage possible.

This pattern generalizes far beyond biology. Market inefficiencies are concentration gradients: when information, capital, or talent is unevenly distributed across a boundary, energy (profit, influence, advantage) can be extracted as the gradient equalizes. The critical insight is that the boundary matters as much as the gradient — without a membrane that resists free flow, no energy can be captured. In business, moats serve the same function as phospholipid bilayers: they maintain the imbalance that makes value extraction possible.
