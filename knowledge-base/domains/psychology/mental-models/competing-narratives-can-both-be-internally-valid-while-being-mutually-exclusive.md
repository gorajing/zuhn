The video explicitly states both narratives are 'true': Palestinians have been denied statehood for over a century and live under military occupation; Israelis need a homeland, were granted one by the UN, and face active threats from neighbors. Neither narrative is fabricated — both are supported by historical evidence. Yet they justify incompatible outcomes.

This is a powerful mental model for any conflict: the failure mode isn't that one side is lying, but that both sides are selecting from the same set of facts to construct coherent but irreconcilable stories. Resolution requires moving beyond 'who is right' to 'what framework can accommodate both legitimate claims.' In organizational settings, this means treating competing departmental narratives (engineering vs. sales, for instance) as both valid and asking what structure satisfies both, rather than adjudicating truth.
