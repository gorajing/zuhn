In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane acts as a battery: protons are pumped inside to create a concentration gradient, and when they flow back out through ATP synthase, that flow drives the creation of ATP. The energy isn't stored as a molecule — it's stored as an imbalance.

This pattern — create an imbalance, then extract work from its resolution — is one of the most universal mechanisms in nature and human systems. Batteries store electrical potential differences. Dams store gravitational potential via water height differentials. In economics, arbitrage profits come from price gradients across markets. In information theory, surprise (and therefore information content) comes from probability gradients.

Recognizing this pattern helps identify where energy or value is being stored in any system: look for maintained imbalances. It also reveals vulnerabilities — if the membrane (or market barrier, or information asymmetry) breaks down, the stored energy dissipates instantly without doing useful work.
