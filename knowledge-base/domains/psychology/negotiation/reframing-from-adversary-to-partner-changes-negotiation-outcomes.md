Shapiro's third barrier — affiliation — addresses the structural framing of conflict. Most people default to an adversarial frame (my position vs. yours), which creates a zero-sum dynamic where both sides butt heads like rams. The fix is a deliberate reframe: turn the other person from an adversary into a partner facing the same shared problem.

The specific technique is asking: 'What's your advice on how we can get as many of our interests met at the same time?' This question does three things simultaneously — it signals respect by asking for advice, it redefines the problem as shared rather than competitive, and it shifts both parties' optimization target from 'winning' to 'mutual interest satisfaction.' This structural reframe is the most leveraged move because it changes the entire game being played, not just how it's played.
