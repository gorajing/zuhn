Walter emphasizes that for stakeholders to share genuine intelligence about decision-making dynamics, they need to feel safe. He opens conversations with explicit framing: 'This is meant to be an informal chat. I'd like to better understand your world and get your advice.' He also commits to not using shared information against them later.

This deliberate separation of intel-gathering from persuasion is counterintuitive — most people try to gather information and persuade simultaneously, which makes the other party guarded. By cleanly separating these phases, Walter gets access to honest assessments of objections, decision-maker preferences, and political dynamics that would remain hidden in a more adversarial or transactional framing. The trust built in the intel phase also becomes a foundation for the later persuasion phase.
