For early-stage founders without polished presentation skills, the single biggest piece of evidence investors use to evaluate the company is the founders' own confidence in it. But this must be shown, not told—saying 'we're passionate' gets ignored or marks you as a bullshitter.

The litmus test Paul Graham offers is genuine: if you don't believe you're doing investors a favor by letting them invest, why are you investing your own time? This reframe turns confidence from performance into authentic signal. Nervousness and apologetic tone are the real killers, not lack of polish.
