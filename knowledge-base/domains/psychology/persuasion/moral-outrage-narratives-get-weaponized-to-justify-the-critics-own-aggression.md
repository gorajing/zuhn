The Black Legend — English propagandists amplifying Spanish atrocities against Native Americans — wasn't born from humanitarian concern but from imperial rivalry. England used Spain's documented cruelty as justification for its own colonization: 'We must expand to save the Indians from the Spanish.' Yet English colonization proved equally devastating to indigenous populations.

This pattern recurs constantly: competitors denounce a market leader's practices not from genuine concern but to position themselves as the ethical alternative while pursuing the same extractive goals. In geopolitics, business, and even interpersonal dynamics, moral outrage about others' behavior is frequently a strategic tool rather than a principled stance. The diagnostic question is whether the critic's proposed alternative actually protects the victims or simply replaces one exploiter with another.
