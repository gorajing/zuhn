When audiences lack the technical background to evaluate substance directly, they fall back on the presenter's own confidence as a proxy signal. But explicit confidence claims ('we're passionate,' 'our product is great') get filtered out or worse, coded as bullshit.

The effective approach is implicit signaling: deep knowledge of the problem space, genuine enthusiasm visible in how you describe what you've built, and the framing that you're doing investors a favor by sharing this opportunity. This is a general principle of persuasion—stated claims about yourself are discounted, while demonstrated qualities are weighted heavily. The asymmetry exists because audiences are better at detecting performed confidence than generators realize.
