Graham's essay is a masterclass in persuasion through reframing. He doesn't engage with the standard death penalty arguments (morality of state killing, deterrence, racial bias). Instead, he shifts the question from 'Is the death penalty justified?' to 'Is it acceptable to execute innocent people?' — a question with only one defensible answer.

This technique works because it sidesteps entrenched positions. People with strong priors on the moral question are unlikely to change their minds from another moral argument. But reframing to a different, more concrete question (error rates and innocent deaths) bypasses those defenses entirely. The pattern: when a debate is stuck, don't argue harder within the existing frame — find a frame where the answer is obvious.
