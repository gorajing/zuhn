Sequoia calls this the 'proxy for demand' — observable behavior that proves people need what you're building. When people pay a lot for something, cobble together workarounds, or spend hours on manual processes, that's hard evidence of need. Market size numbers (is it $500M or $5B?) are abstract and forgettable — like an aspiring actor citing Tom Hanks' salary instead of demonstrating their own talent.

The most powerful version is when the founders themselves experienced the need. Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, and Google all grew from founders scratching their own itch. Experienced investors recognize this pattern and weight it heavily. The next best thing is needs observed in people you know personally. Both work because they're specific and verifiable, unlike theoretical TAM calculations that exist only on slides.
