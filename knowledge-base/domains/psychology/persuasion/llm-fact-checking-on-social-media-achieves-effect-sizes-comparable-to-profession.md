Renault et al. (2026) analyzed 1.67 million English-language fact-checking requests made to Grok and Perplexity on X and found that API-access versions of Grok achieved agreement with human fact-checkers that did not significantly differ from inter-fact-checker agreement rates. In a preregistered experiment with 1,592 participants, exposure to LLM fact-checks meaningfully shifted belief accuracy with effect sizes comparable to professional fact-checking studies.

Critically, despite Elon Musk's efforts to make Grok less 'woke,' the AI was more likely to correct Republican posts than Democratic ones — demonstrating that LLM creators have difficulty imparting political bias to their models. Costello et al. (2024) separately found that talking to AI reduces belief in conspiracy theories. This suggests LLMs can serve as scalable, always-available expertise dispensers that are more patient and less condescending than human experts, addressing two key barriers to effective public communication of expert knowledge.
