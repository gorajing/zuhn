Most people default to presenting their strongest arguments when trying to influence others. Jules Walter's approach inverts this: before any persuasion attempt, he conducts structured intelligence gathering through one-on-ones, asking about the stakeholder's goals, OKRs, prioritization process, key decision-makers, and anticipated objections.

This pre-work transforms influence from a single high-stakes pitch into an informed campaign. At YouTube, this approach revealed that a partner team needed detailed forecast models tied to their specific metric — something Walter would never have known to prepare without the intelligence phase. The insight generalizes beyond product management: any time you need buy-in, the preparation phase of understanding the other party's world is where the real leverage is created.
