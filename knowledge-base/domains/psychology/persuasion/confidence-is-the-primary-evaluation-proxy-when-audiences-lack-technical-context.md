Graham identifies that most investors at Demo Day lack both the time and technical background to properly evaluate early-stage products. In this information vacuum, the founder's own confidence becomes 'probably the single biggest piece of evidence.' This isn't about bluster — saying 'we're passionate' or 'our product is great' triggers the opposite reaction, marking founders as bullshitters.

The mechanism is implicit signaling: confidence must be shown through how you present, not stated explicitly. This creates a genuine filter — founders who truly believe they're doing investors a favor by sharing their work radiate different energy than those performing confidence. Graham suggests that if you can't genuinely project this belief, the problem may be with what you're building, not your presentation skills.
