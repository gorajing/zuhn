Greg McAdoo from Sequoia coined the phrase 'proxy for demand': what are people doing now, using inadequate tools, that proves they need what you're building? This bypasses the biggest investor fear at the early stage—that you've built something from a priori theories rather than observed need.

This framework is powerful because it shifts the burden of proof from projection to observation. Market size numbers ('$5 billion TAM') are abstract and unverifiable at pitch time. But specific stories of users suffering with current solutions constitute revealed preference—behavioral evidence that demand exists. Graham notes the strongest version is when the founders themselves experienced the pain (Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all started this way), because it simultaneously validates the problem and the founders' domain expertise.
