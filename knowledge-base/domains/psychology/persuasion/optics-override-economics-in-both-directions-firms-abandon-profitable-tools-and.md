The private aviation industry was hit hard by the 2008 financial crisis and still hasn't fully recovered. While some cuts were genuine cost reduction, many companies eliminated jets that were economically justified because they wanted to signal fiscal discipline. The video notes this was 'all about optics' — narrative pressure overrode spreadsheet logic.

This bidirectional irrationality is revealing: optics push firms to abandon profitable tools during downturns (austerity theater) and adopt unprofitable perks during booms (status signaling). The same social force operates in both directions, always against shareholder interest. For strategists, this suggests that companies willing to make unpopular-but-correct decisions during narrative-heavy moments (keeping jets during a crisis, cutting them during a boom) have a structural governance advantage.
