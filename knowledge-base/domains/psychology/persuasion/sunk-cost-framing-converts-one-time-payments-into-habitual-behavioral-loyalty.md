Both Costco memberships and Amazon Prime exploit the same cognitive bias: once you've paid $60-120 upfront, you feel compelled to use the service to 'get your money's worth.' This is more powerful than traditional loyalty programs (points, punch cards) because the loss is concrete and immediate — you've already spent real money. The result is that customers stop comparing prices altogether and default to the platform they've invested in. Costco's version is more potent because membership is mandatory, not optional like Prime, meaning every customer experiences this psychological lock-in. For any business designing retention mechanisms, an upfront commitment fee may outperform reward-based loyalty programs precisely because losses loom larger than gains.
