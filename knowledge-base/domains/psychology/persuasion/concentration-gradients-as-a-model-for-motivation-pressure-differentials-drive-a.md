In photosynthesis, ATP isn't created by raw energy input. The thylakoid membrane is charged like a battery by pumping protons to one side, creating a concentration gradient. Protons then push through ATP Synthase driven by their natural tendency to equalize, and that flow generates ATP. The membrane's job is maintaining asymmetry, not adding energy.

This reframes motivation design: rather than trying to inject more willpower or enthusiasm (adding protons), effective behavioral systems create and maintain pressure differentials — making the desired action the path of least resistance while building barriers on the other side. Commitment devices, environment design, and accountability structures are all membrane equivalents that maintain the gradient so action flows naturally.
