The Polarity Principle — where uncertainty strengthens attachment — is not a unique relationship phenomenon but an instance of variable-ratio reinforcement, the most extinction-resistant conditioning schedule known. This is the same mechanism that makes slot machines, social media feeds, and loot boxes compelling.

The cross-domain application is significant: any system designer (product, organizational, educational) can either exploit or guard against this mechanism. In product design, variable rewards create engagement loops. In management, inconsistent feedback creates anxious attachment in reports. In personal relationships, recognizing this pattern allows distinguishing between genuine deep connection and reinforcement-schedule-driven compulsion. The actionable insight: if attraction feels compulsive and anxiety-laden rather than calm and secure, it's likely a reinforcement artifact, not genuine compatibility.
