Professional investors hear so many pitches they blur together. The first filter isn't quality — it's memorability. Graham recommends a single descriptive phrase, often in the form 'the X of Y' (Viaweb's was 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce'), launched early in the presentation.

The exercise of distilling your startup into one compelling phrase also serves as a diagnostic: if you can't do it, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. This makes the soundbite both a communication tool and a strategy test.

This pattern appears across persuasion domains — political slogans, brand taglines, academic paper titles. The constraint of compression forces clarity. When you have limited attention budget, a single sticky frame that anchors all subsequent detail beats a balanced but forgettable survey of features.
