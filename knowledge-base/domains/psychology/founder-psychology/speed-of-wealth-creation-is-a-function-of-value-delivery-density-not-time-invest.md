Hormozi's title deliberately invokes the discomfort many founders feel when revenue accelerates rapidly. This psychological friction — the sense that fast money must be illegitimate — is a cognitive bias rooted in the labor theory of value: the belief that compensation should correlate with time and effort expended.

In reality, value creation is non-linear. A single insight about pricing or offer structure can 10x revenue overnight with zero additional effort. The psychological trap is that founders who hit these inflection points often self-sabotage by raising their cost base, adding unnecessary complexity, or doubting their model — because the speed feels 'wrong.' Recognizing this pattern is important because the correct response to rapid value capture is to systematize and scale it, not to slow down to a pace that feels more proportional to effort.
