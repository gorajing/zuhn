Masayoshi Son — famous for his legendary $20M Alibaba investment — met Adam Neumann for just 12 minutes before committing $4.4B and later comparing WeWork to Alibaba. This validation from arguably the most celebrated tech investor alive created a psychological shield around Neumann. When the most successful investor in history tells you to 'think bigger,' every other voice — board members, executives, employees — becomes noise. The pattern extends beyond WeWork: founder hubris rarely emerges in isolation. It's typically reinforced by a specific external validator whose status makes their endorsement unfalsifiable in the founder's mind. The corrective mechanism (board oversight, executive pushback) fails not because it doesn't exist, but because it can't compete with the psychological weight of legendary-figure validation.
