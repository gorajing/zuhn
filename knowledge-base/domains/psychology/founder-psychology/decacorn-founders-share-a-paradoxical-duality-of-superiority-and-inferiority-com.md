After interviewing almost every $10B+ founder, Harry Stebbings identified a disturbing pattern: they all carry both a superiority complex (inherently believing they are more capable than others) and a deep inferiority complex (constantly feeling they're not moving fast enough or achieving enough). Harry describes this as 'deep masochism or sadism towards themselves, which is deeply psychologically scarring' but which drives relentless improvement.

This is not a motivational insight — it's a clinical observation. The duality creates an internal engine that never turns off: you're always better than everyone else yet never good enough for yourself. Harry acknowledges having both traits 'in spades' and links it to 'a lack of self-love in any way.' The founders who survive channel this psychological tension into systems rather than letting it manifest as burnout. Recognizing these traits in yourself means understanding both your competitive advantage and your Achilles heel.
