Multiple credible voices — including Kevin Zhao of Galeos Capital and a prescient 2019 conference speaker — predicted exactly how Terra would fail. Do Kwon publicly mocked critics, bet $10 million that Luna wouldn't collapse, and dismissed warnings while simultaneously dissolving Terra's Korean entity days before the crash. According to insiders, he micromanaged decisions, verbally abused staff, and drove away virtually all core team members within two years.

The pattern is instructive: Terra's algorithmic mechanism had known vulnerabilities, but these were theoretically addressable through design changes (withdrawal delays, dynamic yield rates, larger reserves). What made the failure existential rather than recoverable was the founder's psychological inability to acknowledge flaws. When all feedback channels are blocked by ego, the organization loses its immune system. This is distinct from mere overconfidence — it's a structural failure where the decision-maker's identity becomes fused with the product's infallibility, making any admission of weakness psychologically impossible.
