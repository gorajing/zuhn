Hormozi reframes failure as debt repayment: every mistake pays down 'ignorance debt' — the gap between your current knowledge and what's required to succeed. Unlike financial debt where you can avoid taking it on, ignorance debt accrues automatically from the moment you pursue a goal. The only question is whether you pay it down quickly through deliberate action and fast failure, or slowly through years of avoidance.

This mental model inverts the typical fear calculus. Instead of asking 'what's the cost of failing?' the question becomes 'what's the cost of NOT failing?' — of staying ignorant because you refused to take the bet that would have taught you the lesson. The willingness to 'pay in looking bad, in money lost, in time lost, in people judging me' becomes an investment strategy rather than a risk profile. It also explains why serial entrepreneurs succeed faster on subsequent ventures — they've already paid down significant ignorance debt.
