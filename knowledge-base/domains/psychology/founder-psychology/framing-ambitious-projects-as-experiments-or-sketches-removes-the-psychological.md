The 'just a sketch' frame works because it resets the implicit success criteria. If you announce to yourself that something is just a hack or experiment, you are judged (by yourself) against sketch-level standards. Once momentum builds, you can silently upgrade the project's status. Lisa Randall's version: treat new things as experiments so there is no such thing as failing—you either succeed or learn a boundary condition. This framing is especially powerful for first steps because it separates the question 'can I produce something?' from 'is what I produce good enough?'
