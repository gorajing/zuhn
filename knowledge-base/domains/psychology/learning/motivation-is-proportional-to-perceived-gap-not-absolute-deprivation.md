Physical deprivation (hunger, thirst, fatigue) creates motivation through physiological signals, but financial and professional motivation operates on a purely psychological mechanism: the perceived gap between current state and desired state. A person earning $5K/month wanting $10K is at 50% of their goal; a person earning $1M/month wanting $10M is at 10% of their goal — the wealthier person feels more deprived relative to their target.

This explains the counterintuitive finding that the #1 predictor of wealth isn't IQ, gender, or race — it's the ZIP code you're born in. Your environment sets your reference standards. Hanging around people with 10x your net worth will make you feel poorer than you did when surrounded by people with less. The actionable version: you can engineer your motivation by deliberately choosing your comparison group. Compare yourself to the person you want to become rather than to peers, and the gap creates sustainable drive without requiring external pressure.
