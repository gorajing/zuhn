In photosynthesis, the first protein complex in the electron transport chain is called Photosystem II, while the second is Photosystem I—named in order of discovery rather than function. The video explicitly acknowledges this causes confusion, and even offers the alternative name 'plastoquinone oxidoreductase' as a joke about how unhelpful naming can be.

This illustrates a broader principle in knowledge design and technical communication: naming that reflects historical accident rather than functional logic creates a permanent tax on every learner. The same pattern appears in programming (JavaScript vs Java, HTTP status codes, 0-indexed arrays explained to 1-indexed thinkers). When designing APIs, taxonomies, or any system others must learn, optimizing names for functional clarity over historical accuracy reduces cognitive load for every future user.
