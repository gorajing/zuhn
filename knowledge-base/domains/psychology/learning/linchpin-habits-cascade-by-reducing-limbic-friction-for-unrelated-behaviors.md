Huberman introduces 'linchpin habits' — behaviors you already enjoy that make a cascade of other, harder habits easier to execute. The mechanism is not motivational or psychological willpower; it's neurochemical. Exercise, for example, elevates norepinephrine and dopamine, which directly reduces the limbic friction barrier for subsequent behaviors like healthy eating, focused work, or good sleep hygiene.

The key insight is that linchpin habits must be things you genuinely enjoy — forced 'keystone habits' don't produce the same neurochemical cascade. This reframes habit strategy: instead of attacking your hardest habit first, identify which enjoyable habits naturally shift your neurochemical state toward lower limbic friction, then sequence your harder habits to follow them. The linchpin doesn't build discipline; it changes your brain's chemical environment so that discipline becomes less necessary.
