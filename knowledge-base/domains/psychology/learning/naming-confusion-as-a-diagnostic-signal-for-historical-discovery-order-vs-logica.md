Photosystem II was named second but acts first in the electron transport chain — the naming reflects discovery order, not functional order. This isn't just a biology trivia point; it's a meta-learning signal. Fields built through incremental discovery (biology, networking protocols, programming languages) accumulate naming debt where labels reflect historical accident rather than logical structure.

Recognizing this pattern accelerates learning in any discovery-built field: when you encounter a naming inversion, you can predict that adjacent concepts will have similar historical baggage. Instead of memorizing arbitrary labels, build a functional mental model first, then map the names onto it. This is why experienced learners in new fields ask 'why is it called that?' — the answer reveals the field's archaeological layers and helps predict where other confusions hide.
