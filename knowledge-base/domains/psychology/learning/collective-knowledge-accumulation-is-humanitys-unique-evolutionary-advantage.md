Before advanced language emerged roughly 50,000 years ago, information passed between generations primarily through genetics — an extremely slow channel. The development of complex language enabled three compounding capabilities: rapidly expanding knowledge, preserving it across generations, and building on past discoveries to generate deeper insights.

This reframes individual genius as secondary to systemic knowledge infrastructure. The implication for any knowledge-intensive endeavor is that the system for capturing, preserving, and building on prior work matters more than any single contributor's raw ability. A mediocre team with excellent knowledge infrastructure will outperform brilliant individuals who rebuild from scratch each cycle.
