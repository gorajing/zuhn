The tutorial's 31-chapter structure follows a strict pattern: each chapter introduces exactly one new concept (e.g., pointers, inheritance) while using only previously mastered constructs in examples. This mirrors cognitive load theory — working memory can only handle ~4 novel chunks at once.

This graduated complexity approach is why the tutorial has 13M+ views despite being 4 hours long. When learners feel they understand each step before the next one arrives, they maintain momentum. The opposite approach — introducing classes, pointers, and templates in one lesson — causes the 'wall of confusion' where students abandon. This principle applies to onboarding users to complex products, not just teaching programming.
