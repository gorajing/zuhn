Naval identifies the key differentiator of AI as a learning tool: not that information is abundant (it already was), but that AI can adaptively match the level of explanation to your current understanding. If you have eighth grade vocabulary but fifth grade mathematics, it speaks to you at precisely that intersection. This eliminates the two failure modes of traditional learning — incomprehension (too advanced) and boredom (too basic).

This maps directly to Vygotsky's zone of proximal development: learning happens most effectively at the boundary between what you know and what you don't. Naval's practical approach is to have AI generate diagrams, analogies, and illustrations, then drill down with follow-up questions until he understands at the fundamental level. His claim that 'the means of learning are abundant; it's the desire to learn that's scarce' reframes the bottleneck from access to motivation — AI has solved the supply side of education, but the demand side remains entirely human.
