In the light-dependent reactions, the thylakoid membrane maintains a proton concentration gradient — high inside, low outside. Protons naturally flow back through ATP synthase, and that flow generates ATP. The membrane's role is purely to maintain the imbalance.

This 'charge then discharge through a controlled channel' pattern is one of the most universal in nature and engineering. Batteries store chemical gradients. Dams store gravitational potential. Capacitors store charge differentials. In economics, arbitrage exploits price gradients. In information theory, compression exploits probability gradients. Recognizing this shared structure helps transfer solutions across domains — any system that creates and maintains an imbalance has harvestable energy.
