Light-dependent reactions don't directly build sugar. Instead, they convert unpredictable photon energy into two standardized energy currencies: ATP and NADPH. The Calvin Cycle then draws on these currencies independently of whether the sun is currently shining at full intensity. This decoupling is what makes the system robust.

This is the same pattern behind money (decoupling production from consumption), APIs (decoupling services), and even note-taking systems (decoupling learning from application). The insight is that introducing an intermediate representation between raw input and final output adds a step but dramatically increases system flexibility. When designing any multi-stage process, the question isn't whether to add an intermediate currency — it's what the right intermediate representation is.
