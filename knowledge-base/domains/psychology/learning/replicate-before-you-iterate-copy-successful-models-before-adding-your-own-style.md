Hormozi advocates becoming 'the number one student' by doing exactly what a mentor says before adding personal modifications. This mirrors deliberate practice research: novices who copy expert form before developing personal style reach mastery faster than those who immediately improvise.

The psychological barrier is ego — people want to put their 'sauce' on things immediately, which introduces uncontrolled variables before they understand what makes the base system work. By replicating first, you build a mental model of cause and effect within the system. Only after you can reliably produce the expected output do you have enough understanding to make intelligent modifications. This is essentially the scientific method applied to skill acquisition: establish the control before running experiments.
