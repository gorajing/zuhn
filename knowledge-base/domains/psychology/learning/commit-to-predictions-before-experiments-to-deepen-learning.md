In the video, the team makes explicit predictions before each cannon shot — how many gloves the ball will penetrate, whether the dummy will survive, how the different weave patterns will perform. This transforms passive watching into active hypothesis testing. When Trent correctly predicts the ball will go through all 9 gloves while others predict 2-4, it reveals who has the best mental model of the physics involved.

This technique generalizes far beyond physics experiments. In any domain — product launches, hiring decisions, investment outcomes — writing down your prediction before seeing the result creates a feedback loop that calibrates your intuition over time. Without the prediction, you experience hindsight bias and learn nothing about where your mental models are wrong. The prediction-first approach is the difference between accumulating experience and accumulating expertise.
