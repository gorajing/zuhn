The medium you choose determines how much identity and effort you stake on early output. Calling something 'just a sketch' or 'a quick hack' lowers the standard you apply to initial results, which lets you produce them without the paralyzing fear of failure. Once the project gains momentum, you can quietly convert it into something more serious.

This explains why digital media has unleashed so much more creative output than physical media—not primarily because of software capabilities, but because digital work requires far less upfront commitment. You can start, discard, and restart with near-zero cost, which means the psychological barrier to beginning is dramatically lower. The same principle applies to game modding: when the aesthetic is explicitly crude (Minecraft, Roblox), innovative gameplay concepts flourish because creators aren't paralyzed by production quality expectations.
