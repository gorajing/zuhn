The backwards bicycle experiment demonstrates a profound gap between declarative knowledge ('I know the steering is reversed') and procedural competence ('I can ride with reversed steering'). Every participant who understood the mechanism still failed to ride the bike, because bicycle riding is controlled by a deeply trained algorithm involving balance, force, and proprioception that runs below conscious awareness.

This has broad implications: expertise cannot be transferred by explanation alone. Training someone on 'how to think differently' through lectures or documentation fails when the skill requires rewiring procedural pathways. Real behavior change demands deliberate, repeated practice against the old pattern — Destin needed 8 months of daily 5-minute sessions. This applies to any domain where practitioners must unlearn deeply grooved habits: coding styles, management reflexes, musical technique, or investment instincts.
