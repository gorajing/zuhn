3Blue1Brown's core pedagogical insight is that mathematical operations become intuitive when visualized as physical movements rather than taught as symbol-manipulation rules. Matrix-vector multiplication, when presented as 'scale and add the transformed basis vectors,' feels obvious — but when presented as 'multiply row by column and sum,' it feels arbitrary.

This has broad implications for learning any abstract system. The principle is: find the concrete spatial or physical metaphor that makes the formal rules feel inevitable rather than arbitrary. The formal notation then becomes a compact recording of something you already understand, rather than a mysterious recipe to memorize. This is why 3Blue1Brown's approach works — he teaches the geometry first and derives the algebra from it, inverting the traditional curriculum.
