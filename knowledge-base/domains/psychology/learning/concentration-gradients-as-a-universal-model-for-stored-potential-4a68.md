The thylakoid membrane acts as a battery by maintaining a proton concentration gradient — high inside, low outside. When protons flow back through ATP synthase to equalize, that flow does useful work (making ATP). The energy isn't in the protons themselves but in the imbalance between the two sides.

This gradient model appears everywhere once you see it. In learning, a knowledge gap between what you know and what a problem requires creates cognitive tension that drives understanding. In markets, information asymmetry creates trading opportunities. In motivation, the gap between current state and desired state drives action. The key insight is that the useful work happens at the point of equalization, not accumulation. Stockpiling knowledge without tension against problems produces no insight, just as protons without a membrane produce no ATP.
