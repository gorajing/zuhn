Graham observes that 99% of people reading Ulysses are thinking 'I'm reading Ulysses' as they do it. The feeling of virtue in consuming prestigious work contaminates your ability to evaluate it honestly. Guilty pleasures bypass this contamination because there's no status payoff — the enjoyment is pure.

The practical test: what do you read and feel sad there's only half left, rather than impressed you're halfway through? That sadness signals genuine engagement. For anyone trying to develop their craft — writing, coding, design — this distinction matters enormously because you'll only sustain the thousands of hours of deliberate practice needed for mastery if you're working in a domain that genuinely captivates you, not one you chose for its prestige.
