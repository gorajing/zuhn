The most consistently praised feature across top innovation programs is hands-on application — working on real projects, running simulations, and getting feedback on actual business challenges. This pattern appears in every highly-rated program: Strategyzer's learn-while-doing platform, BCG's work-on-your-own-projects approach, and McKinsey's behavioral science-driven application.

This reflects a deeper principle: the gap between knowing innovation frameworks and actually innovating is enormous. Traditional executive education creates knowledge without capability. Programs that minimize the lag between learning and execution — by making execution the learning medium — close this transfer gap. The implication for any organizational training is to bias toward formats where the output of learning is real work product, not certificates.
