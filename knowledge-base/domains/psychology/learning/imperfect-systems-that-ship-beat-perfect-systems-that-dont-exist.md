The CrashCourse episode opens by noting photosynthesis 'actually rather sucks — it's complicated, inefficient and confusing.' Modern photosynthesis converts only about 3-6% of incident solar energy into chemical energy. Yet this inefficient process is the foundation of nearly all food chains and atmospheric oxygen.

The reason it dominates isn't quality — it's that it existed and compounded. Every day it ran, it created conditions (oxygenated atmosphere, organic carbon) that made alternatives less likely and its own continuation more entrenched. This maps directly to product development and learning: an imperfect habit practiced daily, or a shipped product with known flaws, accumulates compound returns that a hypothetical perfect version never can. The bias toward perfection before shipping is a bias toward zero impact.
