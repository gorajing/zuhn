Goggins describes a brute-force learning strategy born from necessity: with ADHD preventing normal information retention, he writes the same material by hand over and over until each page becomes a photographic image he can mentally flip through during exams. His process is layered — first learn the bulk concept, then return for granular details (medication names, dosages, mechanisms). During tests, he literally 'flips pages in his head' to locate answers. This approach is notable because it rejects the common ADHD accommodation model (medication, reduced expectations, alternative assessments) in favor of extreme repetition. While not universally applicable, it demonstrates that learning disabilities can sometimes be compensated through volume of effort rather than optimized technique — a counter-narrative to the efficiency-obsessed study hack culture.
