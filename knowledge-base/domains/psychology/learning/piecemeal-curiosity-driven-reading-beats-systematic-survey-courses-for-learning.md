Graham recommends learning history 'piecemeal' — reading books about specific topics that excite you, even if you don't understand everything on first pass, rather than grinding through comprehensive surveys like Roberts's History of the World. He then provides a curated list spanning medieval technology, Roman history, art, and trade.

This mirrors research on intrinsic motivation and learning retention: engagement with material predicts retention far better than systematic coverage. The gaps in knowledge from a piecemeal approach fill themselves over time as topics interconnect, while the boredom of forced chronological reading often causes people to abandon the project entirely. The strategy optimizes for sustained curiosity over completeness.
