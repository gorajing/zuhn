Graham identifies medium selection as an underappreciated strategic lever for creative output. It's easier to convince yourself something is 'just a sketch' when drawing in a notebook than when carving stone. Digital mediums specifically reduce the gulf between early work and final work, lowering the activation energy for starting.

John Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this: Quake's crude technical limitations meant quick hacks weren't far from the official game, spawning many careers. When commercial quality rose, modding became a full-time job and creativity dropped. Minecraft reversed this by making crudeness the aesthetic, restoring the low-commitment environment. The principle generalizes: any environment that reduces the distance between 'just trying something' and 'real work' will produce more ambitious experimentation.
