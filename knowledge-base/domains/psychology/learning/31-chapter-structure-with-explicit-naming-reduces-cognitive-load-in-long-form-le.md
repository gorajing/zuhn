The tutorial uses 31 chapters across 4 hours — averaging about 7-8 minutes per chapter. This is significantly more granular than most educational content, which typically uses 8-12 sections for equivalent length. The high granularity serves multiple cognitive purposes.

First, each chapter completion triggers a small dopamine hit from progress — 31 completions vs 10 means 3x more motivation signals. Second, explicit chapter names (not just 'Part 1, Part 2') let learners preview what's coming, reducing uncertainty anxiety. Third, granular chapters create natural re-entry points — a learner who stops mid-course can resume at the exact concept they left off, rather than rewatching a 30-minute section to find their place. This principle applies to any long-form content: documentation, onboarding flows, even product tours benefit from more frequent, explicitly-named waypoints.
