When Huygens observed his two pendulum clocks spontaneously synchronizing in 1665, he systematically eliminated possible causes: it wasn't air currents (blocking with a board didn't help), and separation destroyed the effect. The coupling medium — the shared wooden beam — was the critical variable. The clocks' individual properties (slightly different frequencies) were secondary to the channel connecting them.

This principle scales across nature: gravitational coupling locks moons to planets, chemical coupling synchronizes BZ reactions, visual coupling synchronizes fireflies, and mechanical coupling synchronized the Millennium Bridge pedestrians. The universality suggests that when trying to create alignment in any system (teams, markets, ecosystems), designing the coupling mechanism is more leveraged than optimizing individual elements. A team of average performers with strong coupling channels may outperform a team of stars with weak ones.
