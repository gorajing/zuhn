Destin Sandlin could fully describe the physics of how a reversed-steering bicycle works — pedal forces, lean angles, gyroscopic precession — yet could not ride it for even 10 feet. This demonstrates a deep gap between declarative knowledge (being able to explain how something works) and procedural understanding (being able to actually do it). The implication extends far beyond bicycles: in any domain with complex motor or cognitive algorithms, understanding the theory is insufficient for performance. You can know the rules of chess without being able to play well, or understand music theory without being able to improvise. True understanding requires building the neural pathways through practice, not just memorizing the map.
