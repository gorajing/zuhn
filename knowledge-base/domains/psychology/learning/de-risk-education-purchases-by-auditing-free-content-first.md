Hormozi recommends a simple heuristic for education purchases: consume someone's free content first. If their free YouTube videos, podcasts, or blog posts teach you useful things and resonate with your learning style, their paid content is very likely worth the investment. If their free content is thin or hype-driven, paying more won't fix that.

This inverts the common anxiety around education spending. People agonize over course purchases but skip the free due-diligence step. The framework also protects against marketing-heavy educators whose sales pages promise transformation but whose actual teaching is shallow. In an era of abundant free educational content, the barrier to evaluating teachers before paying them is nearly zero.
