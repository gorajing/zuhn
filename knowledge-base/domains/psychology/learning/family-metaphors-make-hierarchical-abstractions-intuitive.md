The HTML tutorial teaches DOM nesting by calling elements 'parents,' 'children,' and 'siblings' rather than using abstract graph theory terminology. This works because family relationships are among the most deeply internalized conceptual structures humans possess — everyone understands that children belong to parents, siblings are peers, and nesting implies containment.

This pattern generalizes beyond HTML to any hierarchical domain: file systems, organizational charts, inheritance in OOP, even taxonomy in biology. The key insight is that the best teaching metaphors aren't just 'simpler' — they map onto structures the learner has been building since childhood. When introducing any nested or tree-like concept, reaching for family metaphors gives learners instant structural intuition before they encounter any technical complexity.
