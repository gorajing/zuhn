Photosynthesis appears overwhelmingly complex: Photosystem II, cytochrome complex, Photosystem I, ATP synthase, NADPH production — each with dozens of molecules and sub-reactions. But the entire light-dependent stage reduces to a single repeating mechanism: absorb photon energy → excite electron → use that energy to move protons across a membrane → let the gradient drive useful work.

This is a general principle of learning: complex multi-step processes usually have one core mechanism repeated with variations. Students who search for the underlying pattern comprehend faster than those who try to memorize each step independently. When facing a complex system, the first question should be 'what is the one thing happening here over and over?' rather than 'what are all the steps?'
