3Blue1Brown demonstrates that matrix-vector multiplication — typically taught as a rote row-column dot product — becomes intuitive when reframed as 'where do the basis vectors land?' This geometric lens transforms a mechanical formula into a visual story about how space deforms. The insight generalizes: when an abstract concept resists understanding, the bottleneck is often the representation, not the learner's capacity. Finding the right visual or spatial metaphor can collapse complexity into intuition. This applies beyond math — any domain where practitioners memorize procedures without understanding the underlying transformation benefits from representational reframing.
