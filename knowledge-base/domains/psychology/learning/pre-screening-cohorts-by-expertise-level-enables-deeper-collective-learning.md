Most learning events waste the first 20–30% of time establishing shared foundations. The Strategyzer Bootcamp model inverts this by requiring demonstrated mastery as a prerequisite — everyone enters at an advanced level. This means facilitators can immediately engage with hard, context-specific problems rather than introductory content.

The implication extends beyond workshop design: any learning environment — mastermind groups, internal training, hiring cohorts — benefits from ensuring participants share a baseline. The bottleneck in peer learning is often not the curriculum but the spread of experience levels. Homogeneous expert groups generate insights that mixed groups never surface.
