The heat equation reveals that when you decompose a temperature distribution into frequency components, each decays exponentially but at rates proportional to the square of their frequency. This means jagged high-frequency detail vanishes almost instantly while broad low-frequency patterns persist. 'All of the complexity in the evolution of this heat distribution is captured by this difference in the decay rates for the different pure frequency components.'

This is a powerful mental model beyond physics. In investing, daily price volatility (high-frequency noise) decays from relevance while fundamental business value (low-frequency signal) dominates long-term returns. In skill development, trendy techniques (high-frequency) become obsolete quickly while foundational principles (low-frequency) compound over decades. In idea evaluation, the flashy novel framing fades while the core insight persists. The practical implication: systematically invest in low-frequency fundamentals because time itself acts as a filter that destroys everything else.
