The author describes the emotional trajectory: total incompetence → feeling like a genius after your first program → realizing you suck again → repeating forever. This maps to the Dunning-Kruger curve but extends it into an infinite series rather than a single peak-and-valley. Each new skill level brings a new 'peak of mount stupid' followed by a new 'valley of despair.'

This reframing is powerful because the most common reason people quit is misinterpreting a competence dip as evidence of permanent inability. If you expect the cycle, each dip becomes a signal of growth rather than failure. The guitar callus metaphor reinforces this: pain is not a bug in the learning process, it's the mechanism. The practical takeaway is that metacognitive awareness of the cycle itself—knowing that feeling incompetent after a period of competence is normal—provides the emotional resilience needed to persist through the painful phases.
