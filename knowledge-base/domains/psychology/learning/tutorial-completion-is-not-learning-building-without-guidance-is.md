The course instructor explicitly states that after completing a tutorial, the next step is building projects on your own — not finding another tutorial. This reflects the well-documented 'tutorial hell' problem where learners consume endless instructional content without developing the ability to solve novel problems.

The key mechanism is that tutorials provide a false sense of competence. Following along activates recognition memory ('I understand this') rather than recall and synthesis ('I can produce this'). Independent building forces the learner into productive struggle, where they must decompose problems, search for solutions, and integrate knowledge across domains. This applies far beyond programming to any skill acquisition domain.
