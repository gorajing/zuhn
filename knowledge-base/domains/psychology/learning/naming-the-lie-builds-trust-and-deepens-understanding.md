Multiple times in the video, 3Blue1Brown explicitly says things like 'this is a little bit of a lie, but it's in the direction of truth' or distinguishes his 'almost Fourier Transform' from the real one. Rather than confusing learners, this transparency does two things: it builds trust (the teacher isn't trying to sneak past the hard parts) and it creates productive tension (the learner knows a refinement is coming and is primed to receive it).

This contrasts sharply with the common pedagogical approach of presenting simplified versions as if they were complete, then later saying 'well actually...' which feels like a betrayal. By naming the simplification upfront, you turn it into a deliberate teaching tool rather than a discovered omission. The pattern applies beyond math education — in technical communication, product demos, and strategy presentations, naming what you're leaving out is more persuasive than pretending completeness.
