Every skill follows a learning curve where the early phase involves high effort and low results. Hormozi's point is that ambitious people are particularly vulnerable to quitting during this phase because the gap between their standards and their current performance feels intolerable. They'd rather start something new (where expectations are low) than endure visible incompetence.

This creates a paradox: the higher your standards, the more painful the incompetence phase, and the more likely you are to abandon ship prematurely. The solution is compartmentalizing — maintaining high standards as a destination while accepting current incompetence as the price of admission. The people who build extraordinary skills are those who made peace with being terrible first.
