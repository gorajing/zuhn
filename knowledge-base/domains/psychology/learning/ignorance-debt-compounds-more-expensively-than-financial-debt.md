Hormozi frames 'ignorance debt' as worse than financial debt because it's invisible and compounds in the wrong direction — the less you know, the worse your decisions, which keeps you poor in both money and knowledge. Financial debt at least has a known interest rate; ignorance debt has an unknown and often exponential cost.

The prescription is aggressive investment in learning: courses, mentors, books, and especially proximity to people who have what you want. The ROI on eliminating a single critical knowledge gap can dwarf years of incremental effort. This reframes 'spending money on education' from an expense to debt repayment on the most expensive loan you carry.
