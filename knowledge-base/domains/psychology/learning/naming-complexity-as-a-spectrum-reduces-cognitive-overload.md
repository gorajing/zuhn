Photosynthesis involves dozens of chemical reactions across multiple cellular compartments. The CrashCourse approach makes it learnable by aggressively naming every stage and sub-component — Photosystem II, Cytochrome Complex, Calvin Cycle, RuBisCo — and giving each a mini-narrative. Even confusing names (why is it called Photosystem II if it comes first?) become memorable anchors because the confusion itself creates a cognitive hook.

This reflects a broader principle in learning design: breaking continuous processes into named, discrete stages leverages chunking — the brain's tendency to group information into manageable units. Each named stage becomes a mental 'room' the learner can revisit independently. The technique works even when the names are jargon-heavy because the act of naming creates boundaries in what would otherwise be an undifferentiated stream of reactions. For anyone designing educational content, technical documentation, or onboarding flows: name every stage, even if the names seem redundant.
