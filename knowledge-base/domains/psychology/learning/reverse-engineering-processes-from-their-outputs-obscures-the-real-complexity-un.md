The equation for photosynthesis fits on one line, yet the actual mechanism involves photosystem II splitting water, an electron transport chain charging thylakoids like batteries, photosystem I re-energizing electrons, and the Calvin Cycle running three phases just to net a single G3P molecule. Each step has dozens of proteins and precise molecular choreography.

This pattern recurs everywhere: successful companies look simple from outside (input money, output product), but internally run enormously complex coordination. When learning any system, resist the temptation to stop at the I/O summary. The real understanding—and the real leverage for improvement—lives in the intermediate transfer steps that clean abstractions hide.
