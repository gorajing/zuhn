Graham identifies a practical trick used by many creative professionals: start by explicitly labeling the work as belonging to a lower-stakes category. A painting is 'just a sketch,' software is 'just a quick hack,' an essay is 'just notes.' This reframe lowers the internal quality bar just enough to allow starting without the weight of full ambition.

The deeper mechanism is that perfectionism is not a fixed property of a person but a function of the declared category of the work. By temporarily reassigning the category, you change the standards being applied. Once the project has momentum — once there's real material to evaluate — you can silently upgrade it to its true ambition without fanfare.

This is related to the lean startup principle of MVP, but applied at the psychological rather than product level. The goal is not to ship something low-quality but to give yourself permission to work on something high-ambition without the full weight of that ambition bearing down on every early step.
