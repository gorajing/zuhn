The video elegantly reframes observation as an active process: 'seeing is touching.' To observe a particle, you must hit it with electromagnetic waves, and at quantum scales, those waves carry enough energy to change the particle's state. This isn't a technology limitation to be solved — it's a fundamental feature of reality. The principle extends far beyond physics. In startups, measuring team performance changes team behavior (Goodhart's Law). In psychology, being observed alters subjects' actions (Hawthorne effect). In investing, publishing a thesis moves the market. The deep lesson is that in any complex adaptive system, the observer and the observed are entangled. Pure objectivity is structurally impossible — the best you can do is account for your measurement's impact and design observation methods that minimize distortion.
