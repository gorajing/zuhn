RuBisCo evolved in an atmosphere with abundant CO2 and almost no oxygen. It worked well enough. But as photosynthetic organisms pumped oxygen into the atmosphere, RuBisCo started grabbing O2 instead of CO2 up to half the time, producing a toxic byproduct (phosphoglycolate) that plants must expend energy to neutralize.

The enzyme didn't change — the environment did. This is a powerful mental model for personal and organizational adaptation: skills, habits, and processes optimized for one context carry hidden assumptions about that context. When the environment shifts (new job, market change, technology disruption), previously invisible dependencies suddenly become critical failures. The antidote is periodically stress-testing your core assumptions against changed conditions, not just optimizing within current ones.
