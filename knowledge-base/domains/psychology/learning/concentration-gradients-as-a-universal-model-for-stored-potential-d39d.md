In photosynthesis, raw photon energy doesn't directly produce ATP. Instead, the light reactions use photon energy to pump protons across the thylakoid membrane, building a concentration gradient. ATP synthase then harvests this gradient as protons flow back through it — like a hydroelectric dam. The actual energy carrier (ATP) is produced by the gradient, not the original input.

This pattern generalizes powerfully to learning and skill development. Raw information consumption (reading, watching) is the photon — necessary but not directly useful. The real cognitive energy comes from creating deliberate knowledge imbalances: identifying specific gaps, building tension between what you know and what you don't, then letting that gradient drive focused inquiry. Spaced repetition, the Feynman technique, and Socratic questioning all work by maintaining productive cognitive gradients rather than attempting direct information-to-understanding conversion.
