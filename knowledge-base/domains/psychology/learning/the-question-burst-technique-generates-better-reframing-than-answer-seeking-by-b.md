Gregersen's 'question burst' technique has two strict rules: no answering questions, and no explaining why you're asking them. These constraints are the mechanism, not a limitation. When people are freed from the obligation to justify or resolve their questions, they bypass the cognitive shortcut of anchoring on familiar solutions.

The technique works because most problem-solving sessions fail at the framing stage — people solve the wrong problem efficiently. By generating a multitude of questions without filtering, participants surface hidden assumptions, uncover blind spots, and often discover that the real challenge is different from the one they started with. This applies at individual, team, and organizational levels.
