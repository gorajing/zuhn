3Blue1Brown's entire pedagogical thesis is explicit: 'My goal is for you to come away feeling like you could have invented calculus yourself.' He contrasts this with the standard approach where 'calculus has a lot of rules and formulas which are often presented as things to be memorized.' The discovery frame works because it forces the learner to trace the causal chain — not just WHAT the formula is, but WHY it must be that way given the problem constraints. This has direct application to how we learn and teach any complex system. When studying a codebase, framework, or business model, asking 'what problem would have led someone to invent this?' produces much deeper understanding than simply memorizing the API or reading the docs. It also serves as a powerful bullshit detector — if you can't reconstruct why something exists, you may not actually understand it.
