Hormozi argues that the hard/soft skill distinction is misleading. Hard skills are simply easy to measure; soft skills are hard to measure — but both are fully trainable. 'Patience' decomposes into dozens of specific behaviors: letting someone finish their sentence, not checking your phone during conversation, waiting before responding. Each of these can be isolated, practiced, and reinforced. This reframing is powerful because it turns vague self-improvement goals ('I need better people skills') into concrete training programs. It also means that entrepreneurial 'character traits' like persistence, charisma, and leadership aren't genetic gifts — they're skill stacks that can be deliberately built. The practical implication: instead of saying 'I'm not a people person,' identify the specific micro-behaviors you're missing and train them one at a time.
