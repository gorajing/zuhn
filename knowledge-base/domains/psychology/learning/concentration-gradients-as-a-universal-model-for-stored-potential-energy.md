In photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane acts as a battery. By splitting water and pumping protons via the cytochrome complex, plants create a high concentration of protons inside the thylakoid. When protons flow through ATP synthase to equalize the gradient, that flow powers the creation of ATP. The energy isn't in the protons themselves — it's in the asymmetry of their distribution.

This gradient model applies broadly as a mental model. Information asymmetry is the gradient that powers profitable trades. Skill gaps between what's needed and what's available create career gradients. Motivation flows from the tension between current state and desired state. The insight is that energy and value don't exist in static pools — they exist in differences between pools. Building value means deliberately creating and maintaining asymmetries, then positioning yourself at the channel where the gradient releases.
