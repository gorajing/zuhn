Several effective techniques exist for surviving the lame phase, and most work by reframing the category of effort. Telling yourself a painting is 'just a sketch' or software is 'just a quick hack' lets you judge initial results by a lower standard. Once the project gains momentum, you can sneakily convert it to something more serious. This is not self-deception—it's strategic category management.

The experiment framing adds another layer: if you treat new work as an experiment, there is no such thing as failure, because you learn something regardless of outcome. This is especially powerful when combined with choosing fast, low-commitment media. Digital tools beat carving stone not because the software is better, but because the low upfront commitment makes it psychologically easier to start. The gap between early work and final work should be as small as possible to keep the creator from being overwhelmed by the distance remaining.
