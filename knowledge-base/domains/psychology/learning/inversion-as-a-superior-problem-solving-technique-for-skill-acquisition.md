Hormozi uses Charlie Munger's inversion technique as his primary strategic tool: instead of asking 'how do I win?' he asks 'what are the fewest moves I could make to absolutely guarantee I lose?' This exploits a cognitive asymmetry — humans are neurologically better at finding threats and problems than solutions. Our survival programming makes us natural critics but poor optimists.

The power of inversion is in its specificity. When you ask 'how do I succeed?' you get vague answers like 'work hard.' When you ask 'how do I guarantee failure?' you get sharp, actionable items: don't follow up, don't advertise, be impatient, show up unprepared. The smaller the failure list, the more potent each item — a single-item failure guarantee becomes your north star for winning. He demonstrated this with a salesperson who 2x'd the previous top performer by simply asking for referrals after every sale — an inverted version of 'never ask for referrals.'
