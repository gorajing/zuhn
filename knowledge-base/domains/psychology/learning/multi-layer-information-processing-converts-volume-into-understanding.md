Chamath describes a four-stage knowledge pipeline: broadly consume content (news, papers, social media, videos), write quick 1-2 page essays on topics that seem important to develop a point of view, conduct month-long deep dives with a team on the most promising topics, and finally stress-test conclusions with domain experts and key opinion leaders.

This mirrors deliberate practice principles applied to intellectual work. Each tier serves a different function: skimming builds situational awareness and pattern recognition, essay-writing forces articulation of a point of view (exposing gaps in understanding), team deep dives create rigorous analysis, and expert review challenges assumptions. The key insight is that most people either stay at the skimming layer or try to go deep on everything—the tiered approach is what makes high-volume learning sustainable and high-quality simultaneously.
