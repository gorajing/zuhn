The fear of failure is one of the strongest inhibitors of ambitious work. Reframing a project as an experiment dissolves this fear by redefining the success criteria: you're not trying to produce a finished product, you're trying to learn something. If the theorem turns out to be false or the structural member fails under stress, you've gained real knowledge—just not the knowledge you originally sought. This reframe is especially powerful for sharply defined problems where negative results are genuinely informative. It lowers the activation energy to start and sustains motivation through the ugly early phases by guaranteeing a non-zero return on effort regardless of outcome.
