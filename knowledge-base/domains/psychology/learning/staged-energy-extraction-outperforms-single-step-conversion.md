In photosynthesis, an excited electron doesn't dump all its energy at once. Instead, it passes through a chain of protein complexes (PSII → Cytochrome → PSI), losing a bit of energy at each step. Each step captures that energy for useful work — pumping protons, generating ATP, producing NADPH. A single large energy release would be wasteful and destructive.

This principle applies to learning and skill development: trying to master an entire complex domain in one pass leads to overwhelm and poor retention. Breaking the challenge into staged extractions — where each stage captures usable understanding before passing to the next — mirrors how biological systems efficiently convert raw inputs into stored value. Spaced repetition, progressive overload in training, and iterative product development all embody this staged-extraction pattern.
