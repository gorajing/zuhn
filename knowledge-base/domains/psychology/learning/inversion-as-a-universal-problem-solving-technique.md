The inversion technique (attributed to Einstein, popularized by Charlie Munger) exploits a cognitive asymmetry: humans are evolutionarily programmed to detect threats and problems far more readily than to generate solutions. By asking 'What would guarantee I lose?' you engage this stronger neural pathway, producing a more comprehensive and honest list than asking 'What should I do to win?'

The practical application is to create the shortest possible list of guaranteed-failure behaviors (the shorter, the more potent each item), then invert each into its positive counterpart. This becomes your action plan. The technique transfers across domains — product strategy (how would we guarantee users churn?), system design (how would we guarantee downtime?), and relationship building (how would we guarantee nobody trusts us?). The constraint of finding the *fewest* moves to guarantee failure forces you to identify the highest-leverage behaviors.
