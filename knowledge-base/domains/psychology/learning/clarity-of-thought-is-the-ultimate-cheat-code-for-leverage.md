Naval claims that 99% of effort in life is effectively wasted — not that you learn nothing, but that the outcomes are inconsequential. Only about 1% of decisions actually matter. Clear thinking improves your ratio from 99:1 to perhaps 80:20, which is a 5x improvement in life outcomes. The mechanism is that modern leverage (code, media, capital, labor) magnifies the impact of good decisions, so judgment quality has nonlinear returns.

Practically, Naval has restructured his life around protecting clarity: keeping a clear calendar so he focuses on important things rather than urgent ones, and judging communication quality as a proxy for thinking quality ('if you cannot communicate clearly, you cannot think clearly'). He treats a cluttered mind the way you'd treat a cluttered desk — as a signal of tolerating disorder that will leak into decision-making everywhere.
