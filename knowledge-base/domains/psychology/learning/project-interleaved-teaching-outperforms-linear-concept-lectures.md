The freeCodeCamp tutorial structure interleaves three mini-projects (calculator, mad libs, guessing game) between concept blocks rather than saving all projects for the end. This mirrors research on interleaved practice — switching between concept acquisition and application forces the learner to retrieve and apply knowledge in context, strengthening encoding.

This pattern is directly applicable to any technical curriculum design: teach a cluster of 3-4 concepts, then immediately build something small that uses them. The project serves dual purpose — it validates understanding (feedback loop) and provides intrinsic motivation to continue. The 13M+ view count on a 4-hour video suggests this format sustains engagement far better than pure lecture approaches.
