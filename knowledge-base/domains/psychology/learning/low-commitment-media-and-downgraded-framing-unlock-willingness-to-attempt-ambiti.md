Graham catalogs a powerful technique: deliberately downgrade the category of what you're making. Start a painting by calling it a sketch. Start software by calling it a quick hack. This lowers the internal quality bar enough to get initial results, and once momentum builds, you can 'sneakily convert' the project into something more serious. The choice of medium reinforces this—digital tools and notebooks impose less upfront commitment than stone carving or oil painting, making it psychologically easier to begin. John Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this: when the medium is explicitly crude (Minecraft, Roblox), innovative gameplay concepts become the dominant value because the gap between early and finished work is small. The practical implication is to always choose the lowest-commitment medium that can express your idea, and frame early work as exploration rather than production.
