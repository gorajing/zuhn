The tutorial includes 31 chapters across its 4.5-hour runtime — roughly one chapter every 8-9 minutes. This granularity transforms the viewing experience: instead of passively watching a monolithic video, learners can jump to specific topics, revisit tricky sections, and skip material they already know.

This maps to the psychological concept of metacognitive regulation — learners perform better when they can monitor and control their own learning process. Chapter markers are the minimal viable implementation of this principle. For anyone creating long-form technical content (documentation, courses, onboarding materials), the lesson is clear: invest in granular navigation structure. The marginal cost is near-zero, but the impact on learner agency is substantial.
