Graham's deepest insight is that our poor judgment of early work is itself early work — a crude version 1 of a skill humanity is still developing. The most direct way to upgrade this skill is empirical: study the actual first steps of people who went on to do great things. First commits, first paintings, first papers. These are almost always embarrassingly feeble, and seeing that pattern repeatedly recalibrates your internal quality meter.

The internet has made this easier than ever — you can see programmers' first commits, musicians' first videos, writers' first blog posts. The more of these you study, the better your intuition becomes for separating 'this is bad' from 'this is early.' This is a learnable perceptual skill, not a fixed personality trait, which means the current epidemic of creative paralysis is a solvable problem, not an inherent feature of human psychology.
