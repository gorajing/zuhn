A revealing moment in the video occurs when Destin notes that 'anyone who knows the game well can tell what's going on simply at the crack of the bat.' He could distinguish grounders (bottom barrel contact), fly balls (top barrel), and foul balls (late rotation contact) purely by sound. This represents a form of tacit knowledge that develops through thousands of hours of exposure — the auditory cortex learns to map sound signatures to physical events.

This pattern appears across many expert domains: experienced mechanics diagnose engine problems by sound, musicians identify tuning issues by ear, and surgeons report 'feeling' when a procedure is going wrong before instruments confirm it. The implication for learning is that immersive, multi-sensory exposure to a domain builds diagnostic capabilities that purely visual or analytical training cannot replicate. Expertise isn't just knowing — it's having trained perceptual systems that extract signal from ambient data.
