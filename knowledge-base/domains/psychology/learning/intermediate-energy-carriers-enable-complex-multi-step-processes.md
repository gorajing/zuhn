The electron transport chain is a masterclass in decomposition. A photon's energy doesn't magically become sugar. Instead, it excites an electron, which loses energy in controlled steps: pumping protons across a membrane, creating a concentration gradient, driving ATP synthase, producing ATP and NADPH, which finally power the Calvin Cycle to fix carbon. Each intermediate carrier stores energy in a form suited to the next step.

This pattern applies to learning and problem-solving. Trying to go directly from ignorance to mastery (or from raw input to final output) usually fails. The most robust approach is building intermediate representations — notes become outlines become drafts become finished work. Each transformation is small enough to execute reliably. The 'inefficiency' of multiple steps is actually what makes the system resilient and debuggable.
