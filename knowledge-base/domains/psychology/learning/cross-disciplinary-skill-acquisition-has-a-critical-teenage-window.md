Paul Graham observes that painters rarely hack and hackers rarely paint because both appear as 'great abstruse mysteries' from the outside. Teenagers can learn either because everything is mysterious at that age, and ignorance breeds boundless self-confidence. But adults who mastered one discipline develop enough awareness to fear the other.

This has practical implications for career strategy and personal growth. The barrier to cross-disciplinary mastery isn't cognitive capacity — it's psychological. Recognizing that fear, not ability, is the bottleneck suggests that adults pursuing a second discipline should deliberately cultivate beginner's mindset and tolerate incompetence longer than feels comfortable.
