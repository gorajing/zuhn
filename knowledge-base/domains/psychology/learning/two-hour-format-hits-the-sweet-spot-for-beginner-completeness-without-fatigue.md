The tutorial spans 2 hours and covers HTML from first tag to complete webpage — long enough to be comprehensive but short enough for a single sitting. This format succeeds because it respects two competing psychological needs: the need for closure (finishing something produces satisfaction and motivation to continue) and the need for sufficient depth (skimming too fast produces the illusion of learning without real understanding).

For educational content design, this suggests a structural principle: scope each learning unit so that a motivated beginner can complete it in one session and have a tangible output. Splitting the same material across 10 short videos loses the closure effect. Stretching it to 8 hours risks abandonment. The 1.5-3 hour range appears to be the sweet spot for technical tutorials — long enough for depth, short enough for completion, producing a 'I built something real' reward signal that drives continued learning.
