The prefrontal cortex—the 'CEO of the brain' responsible for planning, impulse control, and long-term thinking—develops considerably earlier in girls than boys, with the biggest gap occurring during adolescence. This means education systems that reward turning in homework, maintaining GPA, and preparing for college structurally favor the group whose executive function matures first.

Reeves argues this was invisible when sexism capped women's aspirations. Only after the women's movement removed those barriers did the natural female advantage in education become apparent. The gender gap in college degrees is now wider than in 1972 (15 points favoring women vs 13 points favoring men then). His proposed solution—boys starting school a year later than girls—directly addresses the developmental timing mismatch rather than trying to change the education system itself.
