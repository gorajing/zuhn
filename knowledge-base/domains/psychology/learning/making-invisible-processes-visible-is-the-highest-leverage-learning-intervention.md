The entire video exists because carburetors are opaque metal boxes where invisible fluid dynamics happen too fast to see. By combining transparency (3D-printed clear housing) with temporal magnification (28,546 fps slow motion), Destin transformed an abstract textbook concept into direct observation. He could literally see the Venturi vacuum pulling fuel droplets, watch turbulence break them apart, and observe the pulsing rhythm of intake strokes.

This principle generalizes powerfully: most complex systems are hard to understand because their critical processes are hidden. Software developers use debuggers and logging. Business operators use dashboards and metrics. Scientists use microscopes and sensors. The common pattern is the same — making the invisible visible is often the single highest-leverage intervention for understanding. When you're stuck understanding a system, ask: what process is happening that I can't currently see, and how can I make it observable?
