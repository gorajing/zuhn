Hormozi frames skill deficiency as a recurring financial liability rather than a one-time gap. If you earn $50K but could earn $1M with the right skills, you're paying $950K/year in ignorance debt. Unlike financial debt, this compounds negatively because each year without the skill is another year of the full cost. Conversely, investing in skill acquisition (even $1,000 in a sales course that takes you from $40K to $220K) generates returns that dwarf any stock market investment — and the returns are permanent and compounding because skills only improve over time. This reframing makes skill investment feel urgent rather than optional, and quantifies the 'invest in yourself' platitude into concrete ROI math. His father's story reinforces this: education is the one asset that can't be taxed, seized by governments, or lost in divorce.
