The secularization analogy reveals a specific failure mode of institutions: they can maintain structural integrity (offices, meetings, hierarchies) while losing the animating belief that made those structures meaningful. This creates a zombie-institution phase where participation is driven by economic necessity rather than genuine conviction, and everyone knows it but continues performing.

This pattern has predictive value. Secularization didn't end with empty churches — it ended with new meaning-making systems (secular humanism, political identity, wellness culture). Similarly, the death of corporate belief won't end with empty offices but with new frameworks for organizing productive work. The parallel economy of side projects may be the early signal of what replaces corporate faith.
