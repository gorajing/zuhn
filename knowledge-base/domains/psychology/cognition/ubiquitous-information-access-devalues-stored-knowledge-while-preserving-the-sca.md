Smith describes humanity's creation of a 'single unified exocortex' — the combination of Wikipedia, YouTube tutorials, Google, and smartphones that makes virtually any factual knowledge available on demand. This has fundamentally changed what makes a person valuable: the cabinet-fixer and the fact-knower are less distinctive when YouTube and Google can substitute for their stored knowledge.

However, understanding and practiced skill remain scarce. YouTube can't make you a great violinist in 30 minutes, and Wikipedia can't give you the ability to do difficult math proofs. Direct experience still produces superior knowledge quality. This creates a clear hierarchy: factual recall has been commoditized, procedural knowledge is being eroded by tutorials, but deep understanding and embodied skill retain their full value. For anyone investing in personal development, this suggests optimizing for depth of understanding and practiced capability rather than breadth of factual knowledge.
