Under special relativity, a sphere moving at high velocity relative to an observer is literally measured as an oblate disc — not as an illusion, but as a physical measurement fact. Vsauce uses this to show that Earth is 'actually flat' to a muon or any observer moving fast enough relative to it.

This has profound epistemological implications beyond physics. If even shape — the most seemingly objective property — depends on the observer's frame, then claims about 'objective truth' in business, strategy, and decision-making deserve similar scrutiny. What appears true from one stakeholder's frame may be literally false from another's, not because of bias, but because of structural position. The lesson is to always ask 'from whose frame?' before accepting any claim as settled fact.
