Flat Earth proponents have surprisingly good explanations for individual pieces of evidence: circumnavigation is a flat circle path, lunar eclipse shadows could come from a disc, time zones work with a spotlight sun, and apparent gravity is just upward acceleration. Each explanation, in isolation, is internally consistent. Yet they are predominantly ad hoc — excuses made up on the spot that only address one issue and don't fit together into a coherent whole.

This is a critical distinction for evaluating any competing framework. The scientific method doesn't just require that a theory explain individual observations — it requires that a single theory coherently explain ALL observations better than alternatives. The ability to generate a plausible story for each data point is a much weaker epistemic standard than unified explanatory power. This applies directly to evaluating business strategies, investment theses, or any domain where multiple explanations compete: ask whether the explanation works as a system, not just per data point.
