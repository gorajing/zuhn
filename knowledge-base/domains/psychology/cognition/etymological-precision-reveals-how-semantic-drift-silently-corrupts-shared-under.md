The video's aside on 'decimate' — which etymologically means to remove one-tenth, not to destroy completely — illustrates how semantic drift creates invisible miscommunication. Two people using the word 'decimate' may have completely different mental models of severity, yet never realize the discrepancy because they're using the same word.

This pattern extends to high-stakes domains where precision matters. In business, words like 'strategy,' 'platform,' 'scalable,' and 'MVP' have drifted so far from their original meanings that teams routinely talk past each other while believing they're aligned. The fix isn't pedantic insistence on etymology but rather explicit operationalization: when a term is central to a decision, define what you mean by it in concrete, measurable terms. Semantic drift is most dangerous precisely when everyone assumes shared understanding.
