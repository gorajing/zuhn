Harris asks meditators to 'forget about the objects, forget about the boundaries between objects and just encounter the raw data of scene — just light.' He's pointing to a specific claim in perceptual psychology: our moment-to-moment visual experience is heavily structured by top-down conceptual processing — genetically determined patterns and learned categories that carve continuous sensory input into discrete objects. This has cross-domain implications for anyone working in design, AI, or creative fields: what we call 'seeing' is already interpretation. The ability to temporarily suspend this machinery and access a less filtered perceptual layer is a trainable skill with applications in artistic perception, scientific observation, and debugging assumptions baked into how we parse information.
