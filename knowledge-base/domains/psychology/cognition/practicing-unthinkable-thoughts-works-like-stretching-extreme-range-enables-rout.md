Graham draws an analogy between physical stretching and intellectual boundary-testing. A runner who stretches into extreme positions before a race finds the actual running positions comfortable. Similarly, a thinker who habitually entertains truly radical, socially unacceptable ideas will find that conventional 'innovative thinking' requires almost no effort — those ideas are well within their already-expanded range of motion.

This reframes heretical thinking from a moral stance into a practical cognitive training regimen. Darwin's natural selection was 'so simple' that the mystery is why nobody thought of it sooner — the answer is that the idea was unthinkable, not unthought. Scientists who make breakthroughs are precisely those who 'go looking for trouble' by deliberately questioning assumptions. The application extends to any competitive field: the US auto industry couldn't fix its obvious problems because the diagnosis ('they make bad cars') was unsayable within the organization.
