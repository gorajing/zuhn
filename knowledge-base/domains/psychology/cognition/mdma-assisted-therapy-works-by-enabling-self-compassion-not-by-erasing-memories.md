Van der Kolk identifies the core barrier to trauma recovery as 'the basic sense of defectiveness and self-loathing' that trauma instills. MDMA doesn't erase memories or simply make people feel good — it allows people to 'see themselves with compassion' while confronting the worst things that happened to them. Measures of self-perception and self-awareness 'just shoot up.'

This reframes psychedelic therapy from a pharmacological intervention to a self-relationship intervention. The drug creates a temporary window where the inner critic quiets enough for genuine self-compassion to emerge. The insight generalizes: in any domain where self-judgment blocks performance or growth (perfectionism in creative work, imposter syndrome in leadership, fear of failure in startups), the bottleneck may not be skill or knowledge but the inability to view oneself with compassion. Tools that lower the self-criticism barrier — whether chemical, social, or experiential — may unlock capabilities that were always present but suppressed.
