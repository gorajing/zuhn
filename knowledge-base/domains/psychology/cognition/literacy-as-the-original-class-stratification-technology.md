Before writing, foraging societies were relatively egalitarian because knowledge was oral and distributed. Writing changed this fundamentally: it created a specialist class (scribes) who controlled information flow, enabling rulers to codify laws, track debts, and administer taxes. Mesopotamian society went from egalitarian foraging to a hierarchy that included slavery and sport-like entertainment where humans rode other humans.

This pattern — a new information technology creating rather than dissolving class boundaries — has repeated with every major communication shift: printing press, internet, AI. Each promises democratization but initially concentrates power among those who master the new medium first. The insight isn't that technology is bad, but that the transition period between invention and widespread literacy in any new medium is when the sharpest inequalities emerge.
