Asked what he's thinking about that no one is discussing, Hassabis pivots past economics (well-covered) to the philosophical questions that arrive after the technical and economic problems are solved. 'What is meaning, what is purpose, what consciousness is, what does it mean to be human' — these become urgent once labor and survival are decoupled. His explicit call: 'we need some great new philosophers to help us navigate that.' This reframes the intellectual opportunity: the contested ground isn't model architecture or economic policy, it's the philosophy of meaning for a post-labor civilization. Practical implication: for people thinking about what to work on, philosophy of meaning and consciousness is an underserved, high-leverage domain relative to its importance.
