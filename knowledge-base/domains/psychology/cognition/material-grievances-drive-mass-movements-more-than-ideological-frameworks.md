The video emphasizes that while Enlightenment thinkers like Kant provided intellectual scaffolding for the revolution, the actual mass mobilization came from food shortages and economic pain. The Women's March on Versailles was triggered by rumors of Marie Antoinette hoarding grain, not by Article 6 of the Declaration of Rights of Man.

This distinction matters for understanding any movement — political, social, or commercial. Intellectuals construct narratives, but material conditions drive behavior. In product and business contexts, users adopt new tools because of concrete pain points (cost, speed, reliability), not because of abstract value propositions. The implication: if you want mass adoption, solve a material problem first and attach ideology second. The ideology provides staying power and identity, but it can't substitute for solving real pain.
