The common complaint — 'it's been 50 years, why haven't we been back?' — assumes the cadence of the 1960s was normal and everything since has been failure. Hank Green's reframe is the opposite: the fact that humanity reached the Moon with 1960s computers, materials, and manufacturing was the anomaly, powered by Cold War urgency and near-limitless budgets. The baseline for crewed lunar travel was 'not yet possible with the physics we can afford.' Apollo jumped the curve. Artemis is arriving approximately on the curve.

This reframe is useful any time you feel disappointed by 'decline.' Ask whether the golden age you're comparing against was a sustainable baseline or a one-time surge driven by unusual conditions. Often the present isn't late; the past was early.
