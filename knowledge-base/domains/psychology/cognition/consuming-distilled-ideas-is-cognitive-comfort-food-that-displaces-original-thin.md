Graham's mountain-walking revelation — that he could entertain himself with his own ideas instead of reading — mirrors a broader pattern where consumption crowds out creation. He frames books as 'jam' versus the 'fruit' of original thought: palatable and easy but nutritionally inferior for intellectual development.

This doesn't argue against reading, but against the reflexive reaching for others' ideas whenever boredom or discomfort arises. The dependency on external intellectual stimulation can prevent the generative boredom that produces original insights. Many knowledge workers fill every idle moment with podcasts, articles, or social media, never allowing the uncomfortable gap where their own thinking would emerge. Periodically fasting from consumption may be as valuable for creative output as the consumption itself.
