Kross identifies four distinct functions of the inner voice: (1) verbal working memory — keeping information active for processing, (2) simulation and planning — mentally rehearsing scenarios before they happen, (3) self-control — the voice that talks you out of impulsive decisions, and (4) narrative construction — building the story of who you are.

Understanding these as separate functions matters because chatter doesn't disable all of them equally. Rumination primarily corrupts the simulation function (replaying past events instead of planning future ones) and the narrative function (constructing stories of helplessness rather than agency). Recognizing which function is being hijacked points toward more targeted interventions rather than a generic 'quiet your mind' approach.
