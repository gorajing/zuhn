The official narrative from DHS Secretary Noem called Good a 'domestic terrorist' who 'weaponized her SUV.' Trump claimed she 'violently ran over the ICE officer.' Frame-by-frame analysis by the Washington Post and Bellingcat — using publicly available video from multiple angles — showed these claims were false. The agent fired his second and third shots from the side of a vehicle driving away from him.

This represents a structural shift in the relationship between institutions and public trust. Previously, official narratives had hours or days of unchallenged primacy. Now, independent forensic analysis can falsify institutional claims within hours, and the analysis itself becomes shareable content that spreads faster than the original narrative. For any organization operating in public — startups, corporations, or agencies — the assumption must be that every public action will be recorded, analyzed frame-by-frame, and compared against official statements. The cost of narrative dishonesty has increased by orders of magnitude.
