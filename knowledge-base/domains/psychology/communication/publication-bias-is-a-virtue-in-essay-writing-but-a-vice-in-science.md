In science, publication bias is a serious problem: only publishing positive results distorts the literature and wastes resources on already-disproven hypotheses. But Graham argues that in essay writing, the same behavior is exactly correct. You generate many ideas loosely, then ruthlessly filter to only publish the ones that survive scrutiny.

This is a powerful example of how identical strategies produce opposite outcomes in different domains. The underlying variable is whether your audience needs your complete exploration history (science) or just your best conclusions (essays). This domain-dependence of strategy quality is itself an insight worth internalizing — before importing a best practice from one field to another, check whether the information needs of the audience are the same.
