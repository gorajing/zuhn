Graham argues that the first cut in fundraising is simply being remembered. Professional investors hear pitch after pitch until they all blur together. A descriptive phrase — 'the x of y' pattern like Viaweb's 'the Microsoft Word of ecommerce' — functions as a cognitive anchor that survives this blur.

The deeper insight is that this exercise is diagnostic, not just tactical. If you can't describe your startup in one compelling phrase, your plans may not be sufficiently focused. The soundbite forces clarity of thought — it's a compression test for your entire strategy. This connects to the broader pattern that constraints (ten-minute pitches, one-phrase descriptions) don't just limit communication, they reveal whether the underlying thinking is sharp enough to survive compression.
