Graham identifies a paradox in presentation delivery: you need a completely different voice and manner for speaking to a group versus conversation, yet the goal is to make this artificial mode sound conversational and spontaneous. He draws a parallel to writing — 'good writing is an elaborate effort to seem spontaneous.'

The practical implication is that founders shouldn't try to 'just be natural' when presenting — that produces mumbling and lost audience attention. Instead, treat it as a technical skill to acquire. The actor's rule Graham cites is useful calibration: if you feel you're speaking too slowly, you're probably at about the right speed. This reframes presentation anxiety from a personality problem to a skills gap, which is solvable through deliberate practice rather than personality change.
