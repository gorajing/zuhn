Steve Jobs' three-word 'Stop doing this' to Google resulted in a recruiter being fired within an hour. Bezos' single-character '?' emails became legendary accountability tools at Amazon. Larry Page's one-sentence suggestion led to the $1.65B YouTube acquisition. The pattern is consistent: leaders with the clearest thinking produce the shortest messages, and those messages carry disproportionate weight. This works because brevity eliminates ambiguity about intent and forces the sender to distill their actual position. The inverse is also true — bloated communications signal unresolved thinking and dilute authority. For founders, this means if you can't compress your message to a single clear line, you haven't finished thinking about what you actually want.
