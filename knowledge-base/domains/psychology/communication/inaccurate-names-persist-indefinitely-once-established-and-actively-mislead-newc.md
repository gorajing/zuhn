In photosynthesis, Photosystem II was named after Photosystem I despite operating first in the electron transport chain, and the Calvin Cycle is still called 'dark reactions' despite occurring during daylight. These misnomers persist decades after the confusion was identified because the cost of renaming in textbooks, curricula, and professional discourse exceeds any individual's incentive to fix it.

This is a coordination failure pattern that applies broadly: API parameter names, product terminology, team role titles, and technical jargon all suffer from the same inertia. The practical insight is to invest heavily in naming accuracy at creation time, because renaming later requires global coordination that rarely materializes. For existing misnomers, the best mitigation is explicit disambiguation at point of introduction rather than hoping the name will eventually be corrected.
