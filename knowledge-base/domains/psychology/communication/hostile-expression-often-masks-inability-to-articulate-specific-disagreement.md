Graham draws an analogy between trolling and graffiti: both occur at the intersection of ambition and incompetence. People want to make their mark but lack the skill to do so constructively. Hostile one-liners serve a dual purpose — they're easier to produce than reasoned critiques, and they're immune to counter-argument because there's no specific claim to refute.

This reframes hostile communication as a skill deficit rather than a character flaw. In practice, this means that improving discourse quality is partly an education problem — teaching people how to disagree specifically and constructively — not just a moderation problem. It also suggests that forums which make structured disagreement easier (templates, required reasoning) may reduce hostility as a side effect.
