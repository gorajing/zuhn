There's a common defense: 'It's too complex to explain simply.' But this usually means the thinker hasn't yet achieved clarity themselves. Richard Feynman's teaching method operated on the same premise — if you can't explain it to a beginner, you don't actually understand it.

In startup pitching this manifests as jargon-heavy intro slides. The fix isn't better writing — it's clearer thinking. Airbnb's intro slide ('Book rooms with locals, rather than hotels') works because the business concept is genuinely clear in the founders' minds. The slide reflects that clarity, not the other way around.

The broader principle: communication quality is a lagging indicator of thinking quality. Improve the thinking first, and the communication becomes easy. Trying to fix communication without fixing thinking produces polished confusion.
