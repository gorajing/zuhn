Dr. Gilbert's insight reveals that Houdini's physician likely failed not because the medical recommendation was wrong, but because it was framed in a way that a strong personality would reject. The surgeon suggests that with the right bedside manner — perhaps framing surgery as a strategic move rather than an admission of defeat — Houdini might have consented.

This is a universal persuasion principle: the same message framed differently produces opposite outcomes depending on the recipient's identity structure. In startups, telling a founder 'your product is failing' produces defensiveness, while 'the market data suggests a higher-value opportunity' produces openness. The information content is identical; the framing determines whether it passes through the identity filter or gets rejected by it.
