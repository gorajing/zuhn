Susan Cain argues that introverts have a structural disadvantage in spontaneous verbal communication but a potential advantage in written and prepared communication. Rather than trying to become better at impromptu speaking, introverts should create content—blogs, newsletters, written analysis—that showcases their depth of thinking.

This strategy works because written communication plays to introvert strengths: deep reflection, careful word choice, and thorough analysis. It also creates compounding assets—a body of written work continues to build reputation long after publication, unlike a comment in a meeting that's forgotten by end of day. The approach shifts the game from one where extroverts have natural advantages to one where introverts can excel.
