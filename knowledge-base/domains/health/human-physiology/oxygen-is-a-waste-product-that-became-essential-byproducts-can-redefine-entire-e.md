When Photosystem II splits water to replace a lost electron, it produces hydrogen ions and oxygen as byproducts. The oxygen is useless to the plant. Yet this 'waste' fundamentally transformed Earth's atmosphere, enabled aerobic respiration, and made all animal life possible. The video calls it 'the most useful useless byproduct in history.'

This pattern recurs everywhere: the internet was a byproduct of military communication research, SMS was a byproduct of unused signaling bandwidth, and many billion-dollar companies started as side projects. The implication is that monitoring and leveraging byproducts — rather than discarding or ignoring them — can yield outsized returns. When evaluating any process, ask: what is the oxygen here?
