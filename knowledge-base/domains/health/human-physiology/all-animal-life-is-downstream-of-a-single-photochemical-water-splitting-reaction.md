Photosystem II performs what the video calls one of the most amazing reactions in biology: splitting the ultra-stable H2O molecule to steal a single electron, releasing hydrogen ions and oxygen as byproducts. This reaction — happening continuously in every photosynthetic organism — is the sole source of atmospheric oxygen that powers all aerobic respiration.

The framing matters: oxygen was originally a waste product, not a goal. The entire aerobic biosphere — every animal, every fungus, every aerobic bacterium — is built on top of another organism's garbage output. This is a profound example of how the most consequential outcomes in complex systems often emerge as side effects of processes optimized for something else entirely. Understanding this dependency chain (sunlight → electron excitation → water splitting → oxygen → respiration → your existence) grounds human physiology in its actual biochemical foundation.
