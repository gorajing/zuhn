The light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis begin when a photon — born from hydrogen fusion in the Sun's core and traveling 93 million miles — strikes a chlorophyll molecule and excites an electron. That electron's energy is then laundered through an elaborate transport chain: Photosystem II splits water (producing the oxygen we breathe), the Cytochrome Complex pumps protons to create a concentration gradient, ATP Synthase harvests that gradient to make ATP, and Photosystem I re-energizes electrons to make NADPH.

This ATP and NADPH then power the Calvin Cycle to build glucose. Animals eat plants (or eat animals that ate plants) and run cellular respiration — essentially photosynthesis in reverse — to extract that stored solar energy back into ATP.

This means every breath you take, every calorie you burn, and every thought you think is powered by nuclear fusion happening 93 million miles away, with plants serving as the intermediary energy-laundering system. This first-principles view connects nutrition, exercise physiology, and environmental sustainability into one continuous energy flow rather than treating them as separate domains.
