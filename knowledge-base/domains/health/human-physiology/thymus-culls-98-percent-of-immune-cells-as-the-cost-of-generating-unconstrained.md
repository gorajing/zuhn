Because the adaptive immune system generates receptors randomly, many inevitably match the body's own proteins. The thymus acts as a quality filter — presenting self-proteins to new immune cells and ordering any that react to self-destruct. Only 2% survive this 'murder university' and enter circulation.

This reveals a deep pattern: when you need maximum diversity from a generative process, it's cheaper to generate without constraints and filter aggressively than to constrain generation upfront. The same logic appears in creative processes (brainstorm freely then ruthlessly edit), startup ecosystems (fund many, expect most to fail), and evolutionary algorithms (random mutation plus selection). The 98% death rate isn't waste — it's the mathematically necessary cost of covering 10 billion possible threats.
