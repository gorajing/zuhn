Every calorie humans consume traces back to a photon from the Sun exciting an electron in chlorophyll, which then cascades through the electron transport chain to produce ATP and NADPH, which power the Calvin Cycle to fix carbon into G3P — the precursor to all carbohydrates, and indirectly all food.

This reframes nutrition and agriculture as energy conversion efficiency problems. The entire food chain is a series of lossy energy transformations starting from sunlight. Each trophic level loses ~90% of energy, which is why plant-based diets are more energy-efficient and why agricultural productivity is fundamentally bounded by photosynthetic efficiency (~1-2% of incident solar energy).
