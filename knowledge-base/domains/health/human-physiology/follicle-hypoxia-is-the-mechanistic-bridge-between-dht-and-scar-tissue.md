Hair follicles are extraordinarily oxygen-hungry because they house mitochondria-dense factories for hair protein synthesis. When DHT causes vasoconstriction and inflammation, oxygen delivery drops, creating hypoxia. Hypoxia triggers a reparative cascade that ends in scar tissue around the follicle — the shiny bald scalp is literally scar tissue. This is why Minoxidil (Rogaine) works via vasodilation, not DHT inhibition — it's treating the downstream hypoxia directly. The time-sensitive implication: hair loss progresses through stages (DHT excess → hypoxia → inflammation → scar tissue), and each stage is harder to reverse. Early intervention before scar tissue forms is mechanistically necessary for full recovery.
