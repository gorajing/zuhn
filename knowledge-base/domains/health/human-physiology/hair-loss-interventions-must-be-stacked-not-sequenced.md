Berg explicitly states that doing only microneedling + red light + rosemary oil won't work if the underlying insulin resistance remains unaddressed, and conversely, fixing insulin resistance alone won't regrow hair already lost to scar tissue. The cascade has at least five intervention points: (1) metabolic — fix insulin resistance, (2) hormonal — reduce DHT, (3) mechanical — disrupt scar tissue via microneedling, (4) energetic — restore mitochondria via red light, (5) circulatory — vasodilation via rosemary oil or minoxidil. Skipping any one leaves the system stuck. This is a general pattern for multi-factor diseases: if the pathology is a cascade with multiple amplification points, you cannot monotherapy your way to a cure — you must attack multiple nodes simultaneously. This is why N-of-1 anecdotes about 'what worked for me' rarely replicate.
