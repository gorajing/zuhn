The standard model of androgenic alopecia treats it as a DHT overexpression problem — inhibit 5-alpha reductase, preserve hair. But Berg argues this misses the downstream cascade: excess DHT starves the follicle of oxygen, which creates inflammation, which creates scar tissue, which then persists independently of DHT levels. Hair transplants work because they move the entire follicle ecosystem, not the hair shaft. This reframes interventions from 'block the hormone' to 'restore the environment' — meaning any protocol that addresses DHT without also restoring oxygen delivery, mitochondrial function, and stem cell activity will plateau. The practical implication: finasteride alone is a partial solution; full recovery requires environmental repair (microneedling, red light therapy, circulation boosters).
