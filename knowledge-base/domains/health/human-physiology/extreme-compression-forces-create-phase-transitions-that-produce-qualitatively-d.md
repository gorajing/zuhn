At neutron star densities, matter does not simply get denser — it undergoes qualitative phase transitions. Electrons and protons are crushed together into neutrons. Deeper in, nuclei are squeezed until they touch and reorganize into exotic shapes called nuclear pasta (spaghetti, lasagna), which may be the strongest material in the universe. At the core, matter may dissolve entirely into a quark-gluon plasma — a fundamentally different state.

This illustrates a general principle: sufficient quantitative pressure eventually triggers qualitative transformation. Resistance to compression is not linear — systems have breakpoints where they reorganize into something with entirely new properties. This applies to organizations under extreme resource constraints, materials under stress, and even ideas under sustained intellectual pressure.
