The Rayleigh-Plesset equation describes how a bubble in fluid oscillates and eventually collapses — the same mathematics applies to bubbles formed by an AK-47 bullet traveling through water and bubbles induced by focused ultrasound in medical treatments (lithotripsy for kidney stones, histotripsy for tissue ablation). In both cases, the collapsing bubble generates extreme local temperatures and pressures.

This cross-domain transfer is non-obvious: military ballistics research and medical ultrasound engineering share a common mathematical foundation. Understanding one domain's bubble dynamics directly informs the other. The video's observation that bubbles oscillate before collapsing (rather than simply popping) is the Rayleigh-Plesset equation in action — the bubble overshoots equilibrium, rebounds, and oscillates with decreasing amplitude until violent collapse.
