Big Think framing: memory peaks surprisingly early at age 30 and declines gradually thereafter. The mechanism: loss of brain's ability to form new connections, or to easily modulate the weights of connections between nerve cells (synaptic weights). This is normal aging — short-term memory degrades more than long-term memory. Dementia is structurally different: plaques and tangles in the brain (visible post-mortem in Alzheimer's patients) representing cell death rather than connection-strength changes. The distinction matters because they have different prognoses, different interventions, and different rates of progression.

Application: in self-monitoring or family member monitoring of cognitive decline, distinguish 'forgot a detail' from 'forgot a category.' Detail forgetting (keys, names, lunch) is in the normal-aging cluster. Category forgetting (forgot they wear glasses, forgot what a relative's name refers to) signals something else. Use this distinction in clinical conversations.
