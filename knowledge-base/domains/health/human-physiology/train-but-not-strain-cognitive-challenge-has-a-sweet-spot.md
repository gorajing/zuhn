Big Think's training principle: cognitive activities should be mentally challenging but not too difficult. Whether crossword puzzles, learning languages, or playing musical instruments — vary the activities and find what's enjoyable. The 'train but not strain' framing suggests there's a Goldilocks zone for cognitive load that drives plasticity. Below it, no challenge. Above it, frustration and disengagement.

Application: when designing personal cognitive practice (or training programs for others), explicitly calibrate difficulty. Activities should require focused effort but be completable. As skill develops, difficulty must increase to maintain the training zone. This is the same principle as physical exercise progressive overload, applied cognitively. Memric application: same logic for user-facing AI tools — the audit insights should challenge the firm's existing beliefs but in a digestible way, not be so far outside their priors as to be dismissed.
