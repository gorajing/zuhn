Photosynthesis evolved to fix carbon and produce glucose for plant energy storage. Oxygen is generated as a byproduct of water-splitting in Photosystem II, essentially molecular waste that plants need to expel. Yet this 'waste' product fundamentally transformed Earth's atmosphere and enabled the evolution of all aerobic life.

This pattern — where a system's most transformative impact comes from its byproducts rather than its intended output — recurs across domains. Internal combustion engines produce heat as waste that we harness for cabin warming. Social media platforms produce behavioral data as a byproduct that became more valuable than the social features. Recognizing byproduct potential is a powerful lens for identifying hidden value in any system.
