Berg argues the primary driver of elevated DHT — beyond aging and genetic receptor sensitivity — is insulin resistance. The mechanism has two arms: (1) insulin resistance upregulates 5-alpha reductase, the enzyme that converts testosterone to DHT, and (2) insulin resistance damages liver function, which impairs the buffering of sex hormones and increases free (unbound) testosterone, providing more substrate for DHT conversion. This connects hair loss to the same root cause driving NAFLD, Type 2 diabetes, and metabolic syndrome. The implication: topical treatments and 5-AR inhibitors are treating a symptom; without metabolic intervention, the problem persists and recurs. Fixing insulin resistance is non-negotiable for long-term results.
