Rather than encoding a specific defense for every possible threat, the adaptive immune system uses V(D)J recombination — shuffling a small library of gene segments into random combinations to produce billions of unique receptor configurations. Each immune cell gets one unique receptor, and collectively they cover the entire protein shape space.

This is the biological equivalent of a generative algorithm: a small input set produces astronomically large output diversity. The same principle appears in machine learning (random initialization creating diverse feature detectors) and startup strategy (combinatorial innovation from limited components). The key insight is that pre-programming for every scenario is impossible when the enemy space is effectively infinite — you need a generative process instead.
