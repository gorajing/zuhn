A March 2021 JAMA editorial analyzed four major trials: STRENGTH (13,000 patients, 4g/day omega-3), REDUCE-IT (8,000 patients), OMEMI (1,027 patients), and VITAL Rhythm (12,000 participants). At 4g/day, there was a statistically significant near-doubling of atrial fibrillation risk. At 1.8g/day, risk increase was present but not statistically significant. Fish oil also increases lipid peroxidation (measured by plasma MDA) which was not suppressed by vitamin E.

This directly contradicts popular recommendations of 4-8g daily fish oil as a longevity hack. The polyunsaturated fatty acids in fish oil are highly susceptible to oxidation, creating the very oxidative stress that drives cardiovascular disease.
