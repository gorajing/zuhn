Emulsifiers are added to processed foods to blend oil and water, improve texture, and extend shelf life. They appear in 60%+ of packaged foods. In the gut, they act exactly like detergent — dissolving the protective mucus layer that separates gut bacteria from the intestinal wall. When this barrier breaks down, bacteria translocate across the gut wall, triggering immune responses and chronic low-grade inflammation (the precursor to metabolic syndrome, autoimmune disease, and cancer). Common culprits: polysorbate 80, carboxymethylcellulose (CMC), carrageenan, and soy lecithin in large doses. The fix is simple: eat whole foods where emulsifiers do not exist.
