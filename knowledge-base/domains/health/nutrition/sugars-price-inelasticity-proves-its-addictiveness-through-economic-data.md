Lustig presents an elegant cross-domain argument using economics to prove sugar addiction. Price elasticity measures how consumption responds to price changes: eggs are highly elastic (0.32 — consumption drops significantly when price rises). The most price-inelastic foods are fast food (0.81), soft drinks (0.79), and juice (0.77) — all sugar-laden. Consumers will pay almost any price for them.

This maps to Jeffrey Sachs' three economic actor types: (1) the rational actor (Keynesian — can determine value as utility/cost), (2) the irrational actor (Kahneman/Tversky — risk-averse, overweights cost), and (3) the hedonic actor (Sachs — cannot determine value because the fix overrides cost calculation). Sugar consumers behave as hedonic actors. The food industry knows this, which is why 73% of grocery items have added sugar — it's not about taste, it's about creating a consumer who cannot rationally evaluate whether to buy. This framing turns a nutrition argument into an economic/regulatory one.
