When oxygen is unavailable, cells reroute pyruvates from glycolysis into fermentation rather than the Krebs Cycle and electron transport chain. This yields only 2 ATP per glucose molecule compared to the 38 from full aerobic respiration — a 95% efficiency loss. In human muscles, this produces lactic acid as a byproduct, causing the soreness associated with intense exercise.

This is a fundamental biological design pattern: the system prioritizes continuity of operation over efficiency. Rather than shutting down when the optimal pathway is blocked, cells accept dramatically worse performance to keep functioning. The lactic acid buildup serves as a signal that the system is operating in degraded mode, creating the discomfort that motivates returning to aerobic conditions.
