Coffee and caffeine increase urinary excretion of magnesium, calcium, and other minerals. Regular coffee drinkers may need to supplement with magnesium or increase dietary intake through animal foods, coconut water, and milk. This mineral-depleting effect is compounded if combined with alcohol or proton pump inhibitors.

A critical practical note: do not take creatine with coffee. Recent evidence suggests caffeine may impair creatine absorption. Separate them by several hours. If drinking coffee, choose organic, shade-grown, mold-free beans to minimize mycotoxin and pesticide exposure. Coffee is also a dehydrating fluid that should not count toward the daily 2.3L hydration target.
