Prominent figures like Tony Robbins, Howard Stern, and Joe Rogan have documented elevated heavy metal levels from fish-heavy diets. Shellfish (oysters, mussels, shrimp) are bottom-dwelling organisms with even higher heavy metal concentrations. Being a pescatarian is described as a quick route to heavy metal toxicity.

The alternative: when seed oils are removed from the diet, the body efficiently converts ALA from animal fat (tallow, butter) into EPA, DPA, and DHA. Blood work confirms adequate omega-3 levels without any fish consumption. Anyone eating regular fish should test heavy metal levels (mercury, arsenic, lead, cadmium) periodically.
