Fast food restaurants are not required to disclose full ingredient lists on menus. A typical fast food meal contains seed oils as the primary cooking fat, anti-foaming agents like dimethylpolysiloxane, preservatives like TBHQ, artificial colors, and MSG derivatives listed under various names. The total ingredient count for a simple burger and fries can exceed 50 chemicals.

Whole food alternatives from a grocery store -- grass-fed ground beef, organic potatoes cooked in tallow, and fresh vegetables -- contain 3-5 total ingredients. The cost difference is often minimal when comparing per-meal prices, especially at budget stores. The health cost difference is immeasurable.
