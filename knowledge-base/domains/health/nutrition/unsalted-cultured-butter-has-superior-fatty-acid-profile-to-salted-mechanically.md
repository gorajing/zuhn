The manufacturing difference matters: unsalted butter is typically cultured/fermented, which produces beneficial odd-chain fatty acids (C15, C17) linked to longevity and ferroptosis protection. Salted butter is usually mechanically separated without fermentation, missing these compounds. The taste difference is noticeable.

Grass-fed butter contains significantly more vitamin K2, vitamin E, CLA (conjugated linoleic acid), and beta-carotene (visible as deeper yellow color) compared to grain-fed. It also has a better omega-3 to omega-6 ratio. Kerrygold is a widely available grass-fed option, though not 100% grass-fed year-round. For maximum quality, seek small-farm butter from 100% grass-finished cows.
