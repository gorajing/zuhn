Three magnesium mistakes: 1) Wrong form — magnesium oxide is the cheapest and most common but only 4% bioavailable. Use magnesium glycinate (calming, well-absorbed), threonate (crosses blood-brain barrier for cognition), or malate (for energy and muscle). 2) Missing cofactors — B6 is required to transport magnesium into cells, and vitamin D activates the magnesium receptors. Without these, magnesium stays in the blood but does not enter tissues. 3) Taking calcium simultaneously — calcium and magnesium compete for the same absorption channels. Take them at different times. Also: taking magnesium with high-phytate foods (grains, beans) blocks absorption.
