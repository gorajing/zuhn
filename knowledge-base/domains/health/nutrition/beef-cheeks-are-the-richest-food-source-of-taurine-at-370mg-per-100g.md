Taurine concentrations in muscle tissue correlate with slow-twitch fiber composition because taurine is involved in calcium handling and osmolyte regulation during endurance activity. The masseter muscle in beef cheeks is used for constant chewing in ruminants, making it an exceptionally rich taurine source at 370mg/100g.

Other high-taurine cuts include flat iron steak and skirt steak (slow-twitch muscles) and dark meat chicken. Organs (liver, heart) are also good sources. This explains why taurine supplementation improves endurance exercise specifically -- it enhances the same calcium handling and electrolyte regulation that slow-twitch muscles rely on. Cooking method matters: grilling preserves taurine, while boiling leeches it into broth.
