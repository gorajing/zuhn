Lustig reframes the entire diabetes paradigm: insulin is not 'the diabetes hormone' but 'the energy storage hormone.' The PADIRKO mouse is the key evidence — this transgenic mouse lacks insulin receptors only in the kidney. It has normal blood glucose, normal glucose tolerance, normal weight, but develops devastating diabetic nephropathy. Since glucose levels are normal, the kidney damage cannot be caused by glucose — it must be caused by circulating insulin acting elsewhere in the body.

The broader principle is that every cell has two modes: burning (mitochondrial, requires oxygen) and growing (driven by insulin, doesn't require oxygen). This is why Otto Warburg won the Nobel Prize in 1931 — cancer cells don't need oxygen to grow because no cell needs oxygen to grow. A fetus develops at oxygen partial pressures of 6-31 mmHg (vs. 100 mmHg normal), enabling 36 of the 41 cell doublings from zygote to adult to occur in utero. Insulin drives the growth pathway; when chronically elevated by sugar consumption, it promotes storage and growth at the expense of mitochondrial burning.
