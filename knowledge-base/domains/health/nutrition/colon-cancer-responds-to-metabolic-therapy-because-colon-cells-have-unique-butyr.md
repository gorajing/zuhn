Colon epithelial cells get 70% of their energy from butyrate, produced by gut bacteria fermenting fiber. When the microbiome is disrupted (antibiotics, low fiber diet, processed food), butyrate production drops, and colon cells lose their primary fuel AND their tumor suppression mechanism. Butyrate inhibits histone deacetylase, a key cancer pathway. Restoring butyrate through: sauerkraut and fermented foods (direct butyrate), resistant starch (feeds butyrate-producing bacteria), and intermittent fasting (promotes beneficial microbiome composition). Combined with ketogenic diet (cancer cells cannot use ketones) and fasting-induced autophagy, this creates a multi-pronged metabolic approach.
