The chronic disease epidemic is driven by a systemic feedback loop: food manufacturers use seed oils, processed sugars, and additives that are cheap, shelf-stable, and appetite-disrupting. These ingredients drive metabolic dysfunction, obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease. Pharmaceutical companies then treat these conditions with lifetime medications rather than addressing root dietary causes.

Neither industry has financial incentive to break the cycle. University research funding from food corporations (like Coca-Cola's hundreds of millions to study 'energy balance' rather than food quality) ensures the scientific narrative supports the status quo. The solution is individual agency: eating single-ingredient whole foods (meat, fruit, vegetables) eliminates the need for most chronic disease medications.
