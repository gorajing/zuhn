The strict carnivore position against fruit is contradicted by human interventional data. Whole fruit consumption does not cause insulin resistance even in large quantities. The fructose in fruit is paired with glucose (required for co-transport across the gut lining), fiber, polyphenols, and hundreds of bioactive compounds that prevent bacterial overgrowth.

Isolated fructose feeding causes problems because free fructose without glucose gets stuck in the gut, feeding pathogenic bacteria and producing endotoxin. Whole fruit, with its complete matrix of compounds, does not trigger this pathway. The fruit-avoiding position relies on animal studies (different metabolism) and isolated fructose studies that don't apply to whole food consumption.
