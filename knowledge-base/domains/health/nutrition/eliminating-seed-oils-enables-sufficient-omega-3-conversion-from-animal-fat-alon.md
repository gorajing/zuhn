Omega-6 and omega-3 fatty acids share the same desaturase and elongase enzymes for conversion. When seed oils flood the body with omega-6 linoleic acid, these enzymes are monopolized, crippling omega-3 conversion. This is why vegetarians and the general population show poor ALA-to-EPA/DHA conversion.

Saladino's NutriEval blood work shows high EPA, excellent DPA and DHA levels eating only animal fat (tallow from fatty steaks/burgers) with no fish, no eggs, and no fish oil supplements. His linoleic acid is very low. The implication: fish oil supplementation is a band-aid for a seed oil problem. Fix the root cause (eliminate seed oils) and the body handles omega-3 conversion naturally. Fish consumption also carries significant heavy metal risk (mercury, arsenic, cadmium).
