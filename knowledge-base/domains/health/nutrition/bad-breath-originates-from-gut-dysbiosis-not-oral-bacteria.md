The conventional approach to bad breath focuses on killing oral bacteria with fluoride toothpaste and mouthwash. However, chronic halitosis is often a downstream signal of gut dysbiosis -- the same bacterial imbalance that drives metabolic dysfunction. Addressing gut health through proper diet (whole foods, avoiding processed sugars that feed pathogenic bacteria) resolves the root cause.

Fluoride in toothpaste and tap water raises separate concerns: excess fluoride has been associated with decreased IQ in children in some studies. Saladino maintains oral hygiene through mechanical brushing without toothpaste and reports no dental issues. Similarly, eliminating processed foods and seed oils from the diet appears to reduce body odor, making conventional deodorant unnecessary for some people.
