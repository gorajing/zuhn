Tallow (rendered beef fat) is predominantly saturated and monounsaturated fatty acids, making it extremely stable when heated — unlike seed oils whose polyunsaturated fats oxidize into toxic aldehydes. Grass-fed tallow specifically contains: CLA (conjugated linoleic acid — anti-cancer, anti-inflammatory), fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K, and stearic acid (which improves mitochondrial function). Historically, tallow was the primary cooking fat worldwide until it was replaced by industrially-produced seed oils in the mid-20th century. The switch from animal fats to seed oils correlates precisely with the rise of chronic disease. For practical use: render your own from suet (cheap from butchers) or buy pre-rendered grass-fed tallow. Use for all high-heat cooking.
