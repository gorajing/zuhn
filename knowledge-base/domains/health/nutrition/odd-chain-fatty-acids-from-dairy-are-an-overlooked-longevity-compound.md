Odd-chain fatty acids C15 (pentadecanoic acid) and C17 (heptadecanoic acid) are found primarily in dairy fat, especially fermented/cultured dairy products. Observational research consistently associates higher blood levels of both with improved overall lifespan metrics. C15 levels are a reliable biomarker of dairy fat consumption.

Despite the longevity community supplementing with creatine, taurine, CoQ10, and many other animal-derived nutrients, no one has focused on odd-chain fatty acids yet. Cultured butter (unsalted, fermented) is a superior source compared to mechanically separated (salted) butter. This represents another case where vilified animal fats contain protective compounds that the recommended replacement oils (seed oils) lack.
