Odd-chain fatty acids C15 and C17, found primarily in dairy fat (especially fermented/cultured butter), are inversely correlated with ferroptosis -- iron-dependent, lipid peroxidation-induced cell death. C15 levels in cell membranes are a strong biomarker of dairy consumption and are associated with better longevity outcomes.

The optimal C15 level is 0.4-0.64% of cell membrane fatty acids. Unsalted Kerrygold butter is fermented/cultured and contains these odd-chain fats, while salted Kerrygold is mechanically separated without fermentation. This represents another paradox: dairy fats have been vilified, yet they contain a fatty acid that specifically protects against the oxidative damage caused by the seed oils that replaced them.
