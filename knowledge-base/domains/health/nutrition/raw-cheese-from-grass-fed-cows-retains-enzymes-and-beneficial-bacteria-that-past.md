Pasteurization (heating to 161°F for 15 seconds) kills pathogenic bacteria but also destroys: lactase enzyme (why lactose-intolerant people can often eat raw cheese), beneficial Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains, heat-sensitive vitamin K2 (the vitamin that directs calcium to bones instead of arteries), and CLA (conjugated linoleic acid). Raw cheese from grass-fed cows provides: probiotics comparable to fermented foods, K2 levels up to 10x higher than conventional cheese, naturally occurring CLA for anti-cancer and anti-inflammatory effects, and superior protein bioavailability from intact enzymes. Costco carries raw cheese selections. Aged raw cheese (60+ days) has an excellent safety record — aging itself is antimicrobial.
