The 4-step hierarchy matters because most people 'argue about the roof when the foundation is cracking.' Step 1: Cut the crap (one-ingredient rule). Step 2: Lock in protein (most satiating nutrient, triggers GLP-1 fullness signal naturally). Step 3: Adjust carb/fat ratio based on activity level. Step 4: Fill gaps with targeted supplements based on blood work.

Critically, steps 3 and 4 are useless without steps 1 and 2. This resolves the 'diet wars' -- keto, paleo, Mediterranean are all just different ways of managing the energy dials in step 3. The real question is: are you eating foods that look like they came from nature? If yes, most dietary problems self-correct. Protein at every meal (targeting 30g to activate muscle protein synthesis via leucine) physically crowds out junk food, making willpower less necessary.
