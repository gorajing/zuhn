Coca-Cola has funded hundreds of research studies and given hundreds of millions of dollars to universities promoting the narrative that junk food is acceptable as long as energy balance is maintained. This 'Global Energy Balance Network' approach deliberately shifts focus from food quality to calorie counting.

This framing protects the processed food industry by implying that a Coca-Cola in calorie deficit is equivalent to whole foods in calorie deficit. The reality: food quality determines hormonal signaling, gut microbiome health, mitochondrial function, and satiety in ways that pure calorie math cannot capture. The institutional capture of nutritional science by food industry funding has delayed public health progress for decades.
