Modified food starch is mechanically ground, chemically treated (bleach, vinyl acetate, sodium tripolyphosphate), and stripped of all fiber armor so it breaks down into glucose faster than table sugar. It is in 55-75% of ultra-processed food calories, baby formulas, gluten-free products, and even hot dogs as a filler at $0.20/lb. The nutrition label shows total carbs minus fiber minus sugar, but the remaining unlisted grams are starch — invisible unless you read ingredients. The average American consumes 65-250 lbs of industrial starch annually.
