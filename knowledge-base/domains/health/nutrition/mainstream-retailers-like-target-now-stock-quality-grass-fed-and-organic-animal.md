The increasing availability of grass-fed beef, pasture-raised eggs, organic dairy, and quality butter at mainstream retailers like Target represents a significant market shift. Consumers no longer need specialty stores like Whole Foods or local farms to access nutrient-dense animal foods.

This accessibility is critical for broadening adoption of whole-food animal-based eating. When grass-fed ground beef and pasture-raised eggs are available in the same store where people buy everyday items, the barrier to dietary change drops dramatically. Quality still varies by brand, so label reading remains important.
