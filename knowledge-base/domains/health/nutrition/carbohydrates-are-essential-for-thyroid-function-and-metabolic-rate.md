Thyroid hormones set the body's metabolic rate -- essentially a thermostat for energy expenditure. Carbohydrate restriction lowers T3 (active thyroid hormone), signaling the body to conserve energy. This is an evolutionary adaptation to perceived scarcity.

For optimal body composition and energy, carbohydrates from fruit, honey, squash, and tubers maintain thyroid output, keeping metabolic rate high. Athletes show improved recovery and performance metrics when adequate carbohydrates are included. The key distinction is source: whole food carbohydrates with their companion nutrients vs. processed carbohydrates that cause metabolic dysfunction.
