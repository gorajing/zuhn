Hunter-gatherer populations consumed roughly 2% of calories as linoleic acid. Modern Americans consume 10-15% due to refined seed oils in processed foods. To naturally obtain 5-7 tablespoons of sunflower seed oil, you would need to eat 2.5 pounds of sunflower seeds daily -- an amount no ancestral population ever consumed.

Seed oils undergo harsh industrial processing including heating to 485F, hexane extraction, bleaching, and deodorization. Studies show seed oil consumption increases oxidized LDL, Lp(a), and LpPLA2 -- all cardiovascular risk factors. Replacing seed oils with olive oil, tallow, or other traditional fats may significantly reduce these markers.
