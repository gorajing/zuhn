Retinal cones (responsible for daylight vision color and fine detail) have the highest mitochondrial density of any cell. By age 60 you need 3x more light to see than a 20-year-old. The decline follows stages: loss of contrast sensitivity then needing more light to read then increased glare at night then macular degeneration. Red light therapy at 670nm wavelength for 3 minutes once per week with eyes closed can optimize remaining mitochondria. Exercise intermittent fasting and ketogenic diet promote mitochondrial biogenesis. NAC (N-acetyl carnosine) eye drops dissolve glycated proteins in cataracts. Saffron improves age-related macular degeneration. The 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min look 20 ft away for 20 sec with deliberate blinking) counters screen-induced accommodation cramp.
