Dr. Lustig identifies three specific enzymatic pathways fructose disrupts: (1) AMP kinase — the liver's fuel gauge that signals mitochondrial biogenesis, irreversibly inhibited when fructose metabolite methylglyoxal binds to arginines in its active site; (2) ACAD-L (acyl-CoA dehydrogenase long-chain) — needed to cleave fatty acids for metabolism; (3) CPT1 (carnitine palmitoyltransferase 1) — the shuttle mechanism for getting fatty acids into the inner mitochondrial membrane for beta-oxidation.

The key insight is that fructose is not acutely toxic like cyanide (parts per billion) but is a weak chronic toxin like alcohol — it's not one exposure that kills you, it's 10,000 exposures over years. This explains why metabolic disease develops gradually. The irreversible nature of the AMP kinase inhibition (covalent bonding) means the enzyme is permanently destroyed, requiring new enzyme synthesis to restore function. This makes chronic fructose consumption a ratchet that progressively degrades mitochondrial capacity.
