When evaluating dairy alternatives, the nutritional gap is larger than marketing suggests. Oat, almond, rice, and coconut milks all require artificial enrichment to approach the vitamin and calcium levels of cow milk, and most still fall short on protein. Only soy milk naturally delivers comparable protein levels and overall nutritional density.

This matters for anyone switching away from dairy for environmental or ethical reasons — the choice of alternative isn't nutritionally neutral. Lab-grown milk produced through fermentation by gene-modified bacteria may soon close this gap entirely, as it can replicate the casein and whey proteins that give dairy its structure and taste, something plant-based alternatives fundamentally cannot do.
