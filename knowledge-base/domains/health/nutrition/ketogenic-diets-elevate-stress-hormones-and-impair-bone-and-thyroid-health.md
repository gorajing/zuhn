Multiple studies show ketogenic diets elevate cortisol, epinephrine (adrenaline), and glucagon. While cortisol and epinephrine may partially attenuate over time, glucagon remains chronically elevated on low-carb diets. A paper on short-term ketogenic diets showed impaired markers of bone remodeling.

Adding carbohydrates improves cortisol metrics, exercise recovery, and thyroid hormones (T3/T4) -- the thermostat controlling metabolic rate. Suppressed thyroid from carb restriction leads to lower energy expenditure, making weight loss harder long-term despite the initial insulin-lowering effect. The optimal approach is adequate carbohydrates from fruit, honey, and tubers rather than chronic restriction.
