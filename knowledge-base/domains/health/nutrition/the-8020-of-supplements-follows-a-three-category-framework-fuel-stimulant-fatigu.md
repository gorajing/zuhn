Rather than building complex supplement stacks, Galpin argues for a minimalist three-bucket framework that captures the majority of supplementation benefits. The categories map to distinct physiological mechanisms: fuel (creatine monohydrate, 3-7g/day — provides phosphocreatine for ATP regeneration), stimulant (caffeine or beetroot juice for those avoiding stimulants — enhances alertness or vasodilation), and fatigue blocker (beta-alanine or sodium bicarbonate — buffers acid buildup that causes muscular fatigue).

This framework is powerful because it prevents the common failure mode of complex supplement stacks where ingredients counteract each other. Each category targets a different performance limiter, so there's no redundancy. The framework also makes it easy to periodize supplementation — you might use all three for high-performance training phases but only the fuel category (creatine) as a baseline. Importantly, both beta-alanine and creatine require consistent daily intake over weeks to reach tissue saturation; they don't work as acute doses.
