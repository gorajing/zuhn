Hormozi structures every meal around a single constraint: maximize protein per calorie. Breakfast is 94g protein from Greek yogurt in 500 calories. Lunch is open-face lox sandwiches targeting 90g protein with minimal bread. Snacks are jerky (60g protein per bag) and string cheese.

This single-variable optimization simplifies meal design dramatically. Instead of balancing macros, you solve for one thing — protein density — and let everything else float. The approach treats carbs as a deliberate addition (dessert, cereal topping) rather than a default component of every meal. This mirrors how effective systems design works: optimize for the one metric that matters most, and accept reasonable trade-offs on everything else.
