The standard story is menopause equals low estrogen, treated with hormone replacement therapy. But HRT side effects include breast cancer, endometrial cancer, clots, stroke risk, and gallstones. The actual mechanism: progesterone drops approximately 90% at menopause while estrogen only drops about 50%, creating relative estrogen dominance. This ratio imbalance drives hot flashes, weight gain, depression, joint pain, bone loss, and muscle loss. Women end up on polypharmacy — Ozempic for weight, SSRIs for depression, NSAIDs for pain, plus bone and BP medications — for symptoms that share one root cause. Natural progesterone, cruciferous vegetables (DIM for estrogen metabolism), and insulin control address the actual problem.
