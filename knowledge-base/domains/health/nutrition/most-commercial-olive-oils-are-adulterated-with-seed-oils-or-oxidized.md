Olive oil fraud is one of the most common food adulterations globally. Many brands labeled as extra virgin olive oil are diluted with cheaper seed oils (soybean, sunflower, canola) or are oxidized due to improper storage, transport in hot conditions, or extended shelf time. Some studies suggest up to 70-80% of olive oil sold in the US fails authenticity tests.

To ensure quality: buy from brands with third-party testing and certificates of analysis, choose olive oil in dark glass bottles, check harvest dates (should be within 18 months), and store away from heat and light. Even authentic olive oil contains 12-25% linoleic acid, making it less optimal than butter or tallow for those actively trying to reduce PUFA intake.
