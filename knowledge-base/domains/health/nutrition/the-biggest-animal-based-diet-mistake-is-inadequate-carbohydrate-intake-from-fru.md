Many people adopting an animal-based diet carry over the low-carb mentality from keto or carnivore, eating primarily meat without sufficient fruit and honey. This suppresses thyroid function (lowering metabolic rate), elevates cortisol (an aging hormone), and reduces testosterone (signaling evolutionary scarcity).

The fix: include 200-300g of carbohydrates daily from fruit, honey, squash, and raw milk. These carbohydrates don't cause insulin resistance when eaten as whole foods. They signal abundance to the body, supporting reproductive hormones, thyroid output, and recovery. The animal-based diet is not a low-carb diet -- it's a diet that eliminates processed food while including both animal foods and whole-food carbohydrates.
