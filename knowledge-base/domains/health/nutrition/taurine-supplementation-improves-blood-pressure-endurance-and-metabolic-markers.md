Multiple randomized controlled trials show taurine supplementation (1-6g/day) improves: fasting glucose, fasting insulin, blood pressure (systolic and diastolic), endothelial function in diabetics, and multiple metabolic syndrome markers. For exercise, 1-6g pre-workout extends time to exhaustion by 10%+, lowers blood lactate 16.5%, and attenuates body temperature rise.

Best food sources: beef cheeks (370mg/100g -- highest due to constant chewing), dark meat chicken (100-150mg/100g), regular beef (50-100mg/100g), organs (liver, heart). Cooking in water leeches taurine into broth. Rare or medium-rare cooking preserves more taurine. Taurine also activates GABA-A and glycine receptors, providing anxiolytic effects at 1-3g/day. Unlike creatine, no loading phase is needed.
