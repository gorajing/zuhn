Honey contains over 300 bioactive compounds and has been shown in medical literature to improve insulin sensitivity, metabolic function, and testosterone levels in humans. This stands in stark contrast to isolated sucrose, which does not confer the same benefits. Review papers specifically document honey's positive effects on metabolic illness.

The key insight is that blood glucose elevation is a symptom of metabolic dysfunction, not the cause. Honey does not cause insulin resistance -- if someone with diabetes sees blood sugar spikes from honey, the underlying dysfunction was caused by other factors. Focusing on blood glucose as the primary metric leads to wrongly condemning beneficial whole foods.
