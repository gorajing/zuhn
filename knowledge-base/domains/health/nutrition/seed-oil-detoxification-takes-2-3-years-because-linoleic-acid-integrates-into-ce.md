Linoleic acid (the primary fatty acid in soybean, corn, canola, and sunflower oils) doesn't just pass through the body — it integrates into cell membranes and adipose tissue. With a half-life of approximately 680 days, even after completely eliminating seed oils from your diet, your cells remain contaminated for years. During this period, the stored linoleic acid continues to oxidize (especially during exercise or illness), producing inflammatory byproducts. Saladino recommends: replace all cooking oils with tallow, butter, ghee, or coconut oil; avoid restaurant food (nearly all restaurants use seed oils); read every label (seed oils are in 60%+ of packaged foods); and supplement with vitamin E to protect against oxidation of stored linoleic acid during the clearance period.
