Erling Haaland's publicly discussed diet includes organ meats (liver, heart), grass-fed beef, and ancestral eating principles. This represents a growing trend among elite athletes returning to nutrient-dense whole-food approaches rather than relying solely on synthetic supplements and processed sports nutrition.

The performance rationale: organ meats provide concentrated CoQ10, heme iron, B12, copper, creatine, taurine, and carnosine in forms far more bioavailable than supplements. Elite athletes with extreme physical demands benefit from the nutrient density that only whole-food animal nutrition can provide. This trend may influence broader public adoption of organ meat consumption.
