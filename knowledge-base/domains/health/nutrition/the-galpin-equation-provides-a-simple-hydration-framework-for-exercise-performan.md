Galpin's hydration framework has two components: baseline daily intake (half body weight in ounces — so a 200 lb person targets 100 oz) and exercise replacement (125-150% of weight lost during training, measured by pre/post weigh-in). The overshoot compensates for the fact that plain water is not isotonic — without electrolytes, the body triggers baroreflexes and excretes excess water before it fully rehydrates. Galpin pushes back on the caffeine-as-diuretic myth: natural caffeine in coffee and tea only causes net fluid loss if intake is extreme (12+ cups). At normal consumption, caffeinated beverages count toward total fluid intake. The flip side is also important — over-hydration causes hyponatremia (dangerously low sodium concentration), which is more common than people realize. Both under- and over-hydration follow a hormetic curve, so the goal is finding the optimal middle range rather than maximizing water intake.
