Cooking oil safety depends on fatty acid composition and oxidation resistance. Saturated fats (tallow, butter, ghee) are the most stable at high heat because their saturated bonds resist oxidation. They are ideal for pan frying, grilling, and high-temperature cooking.

Olive oil is acceptable for low-to-medium heat cooking but contains 12-25% linoleic acid and can oxidize at high temperatures. Avocado oil (often adulterated) contains ~20% linoleic acid. Seed oils (corn, soybean, canola, sunflower, safflower) should be completely avoided -- they oxidize readily at cooking temperatures, producing aldehydes and other harmful compounds. Coconut oil is another safe option due to its high saturated fat content. The general rule: the more saturated the fat, the more stable it is for cooking.
