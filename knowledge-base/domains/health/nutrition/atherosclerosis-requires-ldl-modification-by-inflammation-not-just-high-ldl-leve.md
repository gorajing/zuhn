Animal studies demonstrate that knocking out inflammatory signaling pathways eliminates atherosclerosis even with very high LDL levels. This proves that LDL alone is insufficient -- it must be modified by oxidative stress and inflammation, then recognized by immune cells whose macrophages have altered uptake behavior.

The clinical implication: aggressively lowering LDL with drugs while ignoring metabolic health (insulin resistance, seed oil intake, inflammatory markers) addresses one ingredient in a multi-ingredient cake. The paradox of people on keto with very high LDL showing improved CAC scores further suggests that metabolic context matters more than absolute LDL number. A more nuanced approach would assess metabolic health comprehensively rather than treating LDL as the sole target.
