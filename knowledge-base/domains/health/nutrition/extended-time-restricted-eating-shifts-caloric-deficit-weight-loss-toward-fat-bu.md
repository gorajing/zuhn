When time-restricted eating (8-10 hour window) is maintained for 60+ days, two metabolic shifts occur that bias weight loss toward fat: hepatic lipase (LAPC) — a liver enzyme that metabolizes fat for energy — increases, while CIDEC — a lipid droplet-associated lipolysis inhibitor — decreases. This is effectively removing the brake and pressing the accelerator on fat burning simultaneously.

This finding matters because not all weight loss is equal — losing muscle, water, or glycogen is metabolically counterproductive. The mechanism only activates under caloric restriction (you still can't out-fast overeating), but for people already in a caloric deficit, sustained time-restricted feeding appears to preferentially direct the body toward fat stores for energy compensation. This is one of the stronger arguments for time-restricted eating beyond simple calorie counting.
