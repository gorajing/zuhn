Many people who are lactose intolerant with pasteurized milk can drink raw milk without digestive problems. This is likely not due to a lactase enzyme in the milk itself, but rather the naturally occurring bacterial cultures in raw milk that modify gut microbiota to restore lactose-digesting capability.

Pasteurization kills these beneficial organisms along with pathogens. It also changes the conformation of whey proteins, affecting how milk interacts with the immune system. Studies (Gabriella study and others) show children who drink raw milk -- whether on or off farms -- have lower rates of asthma, eczema, and seasonal allergies. The distinction matters: raw milk is not inherently dangerous but requires proper sanitary conditions (healthy grass-fed cows, clean collection, rapid chilling).
