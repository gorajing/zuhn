Canonical research shows complete remodeling of cell membranes, fatty tissues, and mitochondria from seed oil-derived linoleic acid takes approximately 4 years after cessation. Monkey studies demonstrated acceleration to 30 days on very low-fat diets, though this is impractical for humans long-term.

Practical acceleration strategy: focus exclusively on low-linoleic acid fats. Beef tallow and butter contain only 1-2% linoleic acid. Olive oil contains 12-25% and avocado oil ~20% -- still significant sources. For maximum membrane turnover speed, even olive and avocado oil should be temporarily eliminated. The C15 fatty acid (pentadecanoic acid) found in dairy fat, especially cultured/fermented butter, protects against ferroptosis (oxidative stress-induced cell death from polyunsaturated fats in membranes).
