Bryan Johnson publicly takes over 100 supplements daily on his vegan longevity protocol. Analysis reveals most compensate for nutrients naturally found in animal foods: B-complex vitamins (liver), CoQ10 (heart), vitamin E (butter/tallow), EPA (animal fat), iodine (meat/dairy), vitamin K2 MK4/MK7 (animal foods), taurine (meat), zinc (meat), and more.

Despite this supplementation, the protocol still misses creatine (perhaps the most studied ergogenic aid), carnosine, anserine, and animal-based collagen. US beef consumption declined from 95 lbs/person in 1976 to 60 lbs in 2017, while chronic disease rates rose -- a correlation worth noting alongside increased grain and seed oil consumption.
