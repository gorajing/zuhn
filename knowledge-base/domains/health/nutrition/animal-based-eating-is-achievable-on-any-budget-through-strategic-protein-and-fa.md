The cost myth around healthy eating is largely false. Ground beef is the most affordable quality meat ($5-8/lb grass-fed at Costco/Aldi). Whole chickens are $1-2/lb. Pasture-raised eggs are $5-7/dozen. Seasonal fruit is $1-3/lb. Whole milk is $4-6/gallon. Total daily cost: $10-15 for nutrient-complete meals.

Strategies: buy in bulk and freeze, prioritize fattier cuts (cheaper and more satiating), use desiccated organ capsules instead of expensive fresh organs, buy seasonal fruit, and consider raw milk cooperatives. This often costs less than a processed food diet when comparing per-meal costs including the soda, snacks, and fast food that processed diets normalize.
