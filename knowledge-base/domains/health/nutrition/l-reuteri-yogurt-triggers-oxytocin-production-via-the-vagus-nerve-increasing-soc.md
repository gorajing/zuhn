L. reuteri is a missing keystone microbe depleted by antibiotics and absent in people who were not breastfed. When reintroduced via homemade yogurt it sends signals through the vagus nerve to the brain triggering increased oxytocin production. Oxytocin is one of the most powerful stress-lowering hormones and drives social bonding behavior. The microbe does not provide oxytocin directly — it triggers endogenous production. Also helps with SIBO (small intestinal bacterial overgrowth) muscle mass and sleep. Only half a cup daily is needed. Berg reports noticeable behavioral changes within one week.
