As men age, testosterone drops while estrogen rises (partly from aromatase enzyme converting testosterone to estrogen in fat tissue). Excess estrogen stimulates prostate growth. The conventional focus on testosterone or DHT blockers misses this. Zinc is a natural aromatase inhibitor — pumpkin seeds, shellfish, and red meat are top sources. Cruciferous vegetables contain DIM which helps metabolize excess estrogen. Saw palmetto blocks DHT. Stinging nettle root blocks estrogen receptors in prostate tissue. Losing visceral fat reduces aromatase activity. The worst foods for prostate: soy (phytoestrogens), beer (hops are estrogenic), and dairy from hormone-treated cows.
