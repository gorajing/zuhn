After eating, your body remains in a 'fed state' for 5-6 hours regardless of when you stop putting food in your mouth — blood glucose stays elevated, insulin remains active, and digestive processes continue. This distinction between 'not eating' and 'actually fasted' is critical for understanding fasting benefits.

A simple 20-30 minute walk after your last meal can significantly compress this transition window by increasing gastric emptying time and mobilizing glucose into muscles via GLUT4 transporters. This is especially valuable for people whose last meal falls close to bedtime — the walk helps ensure more of their sleep occurs in a genuinely fasted state, which is when the most important cellular repair processes (autophagy, circadian gene alignment, liver restoration) occur.
