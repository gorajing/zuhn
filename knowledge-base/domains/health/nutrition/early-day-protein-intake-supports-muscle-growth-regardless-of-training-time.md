A 2021 Cell Reports study (Aoyama et al.) in both mice and humans found that protein ingestion early in the day — before approximately 10 AM for someone waking at 7 AM — supports muscle maintenance and hypertrophy more effectively than later protein intake. This effect is driven by the clock gene BMAL, which regulates protein synthesis pathways within muscle cells on a circadian schedule.

Crucially, this benefit is independent of when resistance training occurs. Whether you train in the morning or evening, the protein synthesis advantage from early eating persists. This creates a genuine tension for intermittent fasting practitioners interested in muscle building: the ideal fasting protocol (eating window later in the day, extending the sleep-fast) conflicts directly with the ideal muscle-building protocol (protein early in the day). The practical resolution is to shift the feeding window earlier if hypertrophy is a primary goal, even at the modest cost of some fasting-related health benefits.
