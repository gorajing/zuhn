Seed oils serve a dual commercial purpose: they are far cheaper than alternatives like tallow or olive oil, and they function as effective preservatives extending shelf life. The food industry has strong financial incentives to maintain seed oil usage.

There is growing evidence that seed oils may sabotage satiety signaling, making people hungrier and driving overconsumption. This creates a perverse cycle where the same ingredient that makes food cheaper also makes consumers eat more of it. Unlike cigarettes, processed foods containing seed oils carry no warning labels about their potential metabolic effects despite accumulating evidence of harm.
