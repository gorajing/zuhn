Cellular respiration breaks glucose into ATP across three stages: glycolysis (2 ATP net), Krebs Cycle (2 ATP), and the electron transport chain (34 ATP). This extreme back-loading means the final stage — which depends on oxygen and healthy mitochondria — produces roughly 17x more energy than the initial glucose breakdown.

This has practical implications for understanding fatigue and energy. Interventions that support mitochondrial function (exercise, sleep, avoiding mitochondrial toxins) have outsized impact on energy levels compared to simply consuming more glucose. The bottleneck is rarely fuel supply — it's the efficiency of the final conversion stage.
