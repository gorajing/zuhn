The mechanism: pure sucrose or high fructose corn syrup feeds pathogenic bacteria in the gut, causing dysbiosis and elevated lipopolysaccharide (endotoxin). Endotoxin binds TLR4 receptors, triggering TNF-alpha, IL-6, and inflammatory cytokines that directly cause insulin resistance. Cultures eating 95% carbohydrate from whole foods do not develop diabetes.

Whole fruits contain hundreds of bioactive chemicals that prevent bacterial overgrowth. Orange juice, bananas, honey -- these sugar-containing foods don't cause dysbiosis because their companion compounds suppress pathogenic bacteria. The processing of sugar strips these protective compounds. This explains why food quality, not macronutrient ratios, determines metabolic health.
