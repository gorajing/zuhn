When blood sugar drops too low during sleep, the adrenals release cortisol as an emergency response to mobilize glucose. This cortisol spike causes alertness and anxiety, waking you at 2-3AM with a racing mind. The fix is not sleep medication but metabolic: eat more fat at dinner (MCT oil, butter, fatty meat) to provide slow-burning fuel through the night. Also helpful: magnesium glycinate before bed (relaxes muscles and supports sleep), vitamin B1 (supports the autonomic nervous system), and potassium (from avocados or leafy greens). Reducing carbs at dinner prevents the blood sugar roller coaster entirely.
