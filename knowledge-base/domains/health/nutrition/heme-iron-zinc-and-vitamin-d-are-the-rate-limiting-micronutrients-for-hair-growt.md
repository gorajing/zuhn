Berg identifies zinc (30-40mg daily) and vitamin D3 (10,000 IU daily) as the 'two most important things to stimulate stem cells or growth factors' for hair. Heme iron — the highly bioavailable form found in liver — is the substrate for hair protein synthesis. Non-heme iron from plants is dramatically less bioavailable. The implication: a vegetarian or low-animal-protein diet can bottleneck hair regrowth even if hormones, circulation, and mitochondria are all addressed. This is why 'best meal for hair' protocols center on liver + onions (heme iron + quercetin anti-inflammatory) and why plant-heavy diets sometimes show worsened hair quality. The dosing suggests upper-end supplementation, which carries some risk (zinc >40mg chronically can cause copper deficiency) but signals the level of deficiency Berg sees in his clinical population.
