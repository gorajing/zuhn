The liver produces bile continuously but at low concentration. The gallbladder concentrates it 5-20x for release during meals. Without a gallbladder, dilute bile trickles continuously — inadequate for digesting fat-heavy meals. Consequences: poor absorption of vitamins A, D, E, K (all fat-soluble), digestive discomfort after fatty meals, increased cholesterol issues (bile is the primary cholesterol elimination pathway), and altered gut microbiome (bile regulates bacterial populations). Most gallstones form from bile sludge caused by insulin resistance, low bile salts, and excess estrogen. Natural dissolution: bile salt supplements (TUDCA or UDCA), apple cider vinegar, low-carb diet, and addressing insulin resistance.
