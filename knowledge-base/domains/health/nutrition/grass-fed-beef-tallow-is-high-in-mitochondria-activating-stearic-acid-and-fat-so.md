Beef tallow from grass-finished cattle has a uniquely beneficial fatty acid profile: high in stearic acid (C18:0), a saturated fat that research suggests activates mitochondrial function and promotes fat burning. It contains only 1-2% linoleic acid (vs 50%+ in seed oils), making it extremely low in inflammatory PUFA.

Additional benefits: naturally contains vitamins E and K2 (fat-soluble vitamins absent from seed oils), extremely stable for high-heat cooking (high smoke point, resistant to oxidation), and was the traditional American cooking fat before Crisco and seed oils replaced it in the early 1900s. Grass-fed tallow has a superior fatty acid profile compared to grain-fed, with more CLA and omega-3s.
