Independent heavy metal testing of popular salt brands reveals surprisingly high levels of lead, mercury, cadmium, and arsenic in many sea salts. The contaminants come from ocean pollution concentrating in evaporated salt. This is particularly concerning given that optimal hydration requires 8-9 grams of salt daily.

The solution is to choose salt brands that publish certificates of analysis showing heavy metal levels, and to favor brands with documented low contamination. Kosher sea salt and certain specific brands test significantly lower than generic sea salts. This applies to mineral waters as well -- the heavy metal content varies dramatically between brands and should be verified before regular consumption.
