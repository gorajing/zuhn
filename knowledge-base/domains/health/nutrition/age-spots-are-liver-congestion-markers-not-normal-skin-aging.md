Age spots contain lipofuscin — oxidized fat and protein debris that the liver should process and eliminate. When the liver is congested (fatty liver, toxin overload, sluggish bile flow), these waste products accumulate in the skin instead. The spots correlate with internal liver health, not just sun exposure. They improve with: milk thistle (silymarin supports liver regeneration), cruciferous vegetables (support phase 2 liver detoxification), intermittent fasting (triggers liver autophagy), bile salt supplementation (improves bile flow), and reducing seed oil consumption (less oxidized fat for the liver to process). Berg argues that if you have age spots, you likely have an internal liver problem worth investigating.
