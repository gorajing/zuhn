Selenium is depleted from modern soils and not replenished by conventional fertilizers making widespread deficiency likely. It is essential for thyroid function (T4 to T3 conversion — many hypothyroid cases are selenium deficiency not iodine), immune system (T-helper cells cannot function without it), testosterone and fertility (protects ovaries and testes), and hair retention (alopecia is autoimmune and selenium supports immune regulation). Toxicity only occurs at approximately 20 nuts daily for months. More than 2 per day is unnecessary.
