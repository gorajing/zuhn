Testosterone follows a circadian rhythm, peaking in the morning. The pituitary sends FSH and LH signals to the testicles -- sleep disruption directly impairs this signaling cascade. Seven to eight hours at consistent times is non-negotiable.

Practical protocol for maintaining testosterone naturally: (1) Prioritize sleep -- same bedtime, 7-8 hours, stop eating 2 hours before bed, avoid alcohol and screens. (2) Eat adequate carbohydrates from fruit, honey, and tubers. (3) Eliminate endocrine disruptors from food and environment. (4) Eat nutrient-dense whole foods including organs. (5) Monitor with morning fasted testosterone blood tests. Target 600-800+ total testosterone. Low testosterone manifests as low libido, erectile dysfunction, depression, difficulty gaining muscle, and poor motivation.
