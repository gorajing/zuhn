Carroll reveals that the immune system is not just analogous to evolution — it IS evolution operating inside your body. When a pathogen enters, immune cells with surface molecules complementary to the invader activate and multiply from one cell to billions in about a week, producing antibodies.

Then a process called hypermutation specifically mutates the antibody-encoding genes, generating variants. The variants that recognize the pathogen better are selected for — climbing the staircase of self-defense. This is why your immune system can recognize virtually anything nature throws at it, including entirely novel pathogens. The same principle explains why cancer is fundamentally an evolutionary disease — cancer cells mutate and are selected for survival advantage within the body.
