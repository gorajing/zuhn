During photosynthesis, the thylakoid membrane accumulates protons through two mechanisms: water splitting (which releases H+ ions) and active pumping by the Cytochrome Complex. This creates a concentration gradient — high proton concentration inside the thylakoid, low outside. The protons naturally want to equalize, and the only exit is through ATP synthase, a turbine-like enzyme that converts the kinetic energy of proton flow into chemical energy (ATP).

This is literally a biological battery: energy input (electron transport) charges it by creating imbalance, and energy output (ATP synthesis) discharges it by allowing equalization. The phospholipid bilayer membrane is critical — it must be impermeable enough to maintain the gradient. This pattern recurs in neurons (sodium-potassium gradients for action potentials), mitochondria (proton motive force), and muscle cells. Understanding this mechanism explains why membrane integrity is so fundamental to health — a leaky membrane is a dead battery.
