Berg cites a 2025 Journal of Neurology study showing 12,772 adults followed for 8 years had 62% faster cognitive decline if consuming artificial sweeteners — equivalent to 1.6 years of accelerated brain aging vs the avoidant group. The implication: the common substitution strategy (sugar → diet soda) doesn't deliver the cognitive benefits of sugar elimination. Substitution-fallacy: assuming any version of a craved item delivers the benefit of avoiding it. The substitution that works: real food substitutes for processed foods (keto-version of comfort food using real ingredients), not artificial-substitute versions of processed foods.

Application: when designing substitutions for any harmful pattern (alcohol, processed food, social media, gambling), verify that the substitution doesn't carry the same underlying harm via different mechanism. Diet soda is not zero-sugar — it's different-mechanism-of-harm. Generalizes: 'low-tar cigarettes' didn't reduce lung cancer; 'lite' alcoholic beverages don't reduce alcohol's costs proportionally; etc.
