While direct human confirmation is pending, the 2025 exercise study in mice shows a clean causal chain: paternal training produces specific sperm microRNAs that alter offspring metabolic and muscle phenotypes. Earlier mouse work on diet, stress, and toxins supports a general pattern where paternal state signals forward via RNA.

Given that the intervention cost is essentially zero — be reasonably fit, avoid binge drinking, eat well, manage stress in the weeks/months before conception — and the potential benefit is a marginally healthier child, this is asymmetric: small downside, potentially meaningful upside. The 'we don't know for sure in humans yet' caveat doesn't change the expected value math for a risk-averse decision.

The prescription: men planning conception should treat the pre-conception window as deliberately as pregnant mothers treat pregnancy. Exercise regularly, avoid substances, eat deliberately, and manage chronic stress. This is a prototypical case where absence of human confirmation is not grounds for ignoring compelling mechanistic evidence when the intervention cost is near-zero.
