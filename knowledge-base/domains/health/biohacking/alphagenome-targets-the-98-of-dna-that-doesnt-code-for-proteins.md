AlphaGenome represents a paradigm shift in genomics by targeting the 98% of DNA that was previously considered 'junk' but actually regulates everything from gene expression to disease susceptibility. It outperformed existing models in 24 of 26 evaluations covering gene expression, splicing, and mutation-disease links.

The practical impact is enormous: researchers demonstrated how specific non-coding mutations trigger T-cell leukemia, revealing mechanisms that were invisible to traditional protein-focused analysis. By simulating genetic variant effects computationally before lab testing, AlphaGenome could compress personalized gene therapy timelines by years. This follows the same pattern as AlphaFold — DeepMind applying transformer architectures to biological sequence data and achieving step-function improvements over traditional methods.
