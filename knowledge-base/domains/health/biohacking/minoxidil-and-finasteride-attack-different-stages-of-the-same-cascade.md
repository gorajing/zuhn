Most hair loss medication discourse frames minoxidil and finasteride as alternatives — which one works better? The mechanistic truth: they are sequential interventions. Finasteride blocks 5-alpha reductase, suppressing DHT production at the hormonal source, which addresses the upstream driver. Minoxidil is a vasodilator that increases scalp circulation, which addresses the downstream hypoxia that DHT creates. Using them in combination attacks two distinct steps in the cascade and should theoretically produce additive results. This also explains why each alone is only partially effective — they leave the other half of the pathology untreated. The side effect profile is also different: finasteride has systemic hormonal effects (libido, erectile dysfunction) because it shuts down DHT body-wide; minoxidil is primarily local.
