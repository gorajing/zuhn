Finnish cohort studies show that regular sauna use (4-7x/week) is associated with 40-50% lower cardiovascular disease and all-cause mortality. However, a recent analysis by Connekt found that temperatures above 100C (212F) are associated with a 2x increase in dementia risk.

The optimal protocol is 60 minutes total sauna time per week at 80-100C (175-200F), broken into sessions of 7-20 minutes. Wearing a sauna hat protects the brain from excessive heat. For men concerned about fertility and testosterone, testicular cooling during sauna is important since heat impairs spermatogenesis and may affect hormonal health.
