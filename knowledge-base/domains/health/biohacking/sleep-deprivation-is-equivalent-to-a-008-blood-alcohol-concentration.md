The sleeping brain is highly active metabolically — consolidating memory, clearing waste products, processing emotions. Shortchanging sleep produces impairment equivalent to 0.08 blood alcohol concentration (legal intoxication). The common belief that you can stay up all night and 'catch up on weekends' is neurologically false.

Sleep follows 90-minute cycles through four stages, with REM dreaming at the top. This cycle architecture means you can't compress or reschedule sleep. Cytowic advises: if you need to perform in the morning, sleep early and wake at 5am to prepare — your tanks will be full and memory primed. Sleep hygiene basics: cool room (68F), no bedroom TV, consistent schedule, no stimulating content before bed.
