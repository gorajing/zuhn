Myopia may be the most common health issue globally, with rates reaching 80-95% in parts of East Asia for people under 20 and 40-50% in the US. This epidemic coincides with the shift to indoor living and technology use over the past 30-40 years. Warmer climates with more outdoor living (like Costa Rica) show dramatically lower myopia rates.

Practical recommendations: spend as much time as possible outdoors looking at long distances, especially in morning sunlight. Gradually reduce eyewear prescription strength. Remove glasses during walks when safe to do so. Age-related macular degeneration is linked to both seed oils (via mitochondrial dysfunction in retinal pigmented epithelium) and blue light exposure -- the same pathology as skin cancer but in the inner eye.
