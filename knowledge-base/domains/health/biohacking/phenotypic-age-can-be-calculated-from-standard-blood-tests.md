Phenotypic age captures how your physiology is aging relative to your chronological age, using biomarkers from standard blood panels: liver function, kidney function, metabolic health, lipids, inflammation, and immune markers. Free online calculators accept these nine values and output your phenotypic age. Most people fall within plus or minus five years of their chronological age, though outliers of 10-20 years exist.

The practical advantage is that this is already being measured in annual physicals. While doctors typically only flag abnormal values (binary high-risk/low-risk), phenotypic age extracts continuous spectrum information from the same data. You can track year-over-year changes, correlate lifestyle modifications with biological age shifts, and catch accelerated aging before traditional risk thresholds are crossed. Ideally, your phenotypic age should increase at less than one year per chronological year.
