Shinya Yamanaka discovered four factors that can revert adult cells to an embryonic stem cell state. Scientists noticed this also resets the aging clock. The catch: continuous activation kills mice via organ failure because stem cells cannot function as specialized tissue. But transient activation — essentially turning the genes on only at weekends — reverses aging without destroying cell identity.

Altos Labs has $3 billion (from Bezos and others) to turn this into a human therapy. However, Steele notes a critical limitation: epigenetic reprogramming is only one of 12 hallmarks of aging. It cannot fix DNA mutations accumulated over a lifetime, nor damage to extracellular proteins like collagen. We might solve aging inside cells while leaving the scaffolding of the body to deteriorate.
