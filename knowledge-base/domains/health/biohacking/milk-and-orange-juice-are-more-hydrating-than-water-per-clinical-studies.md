Multiple studies confirm that milk and orange juice are more hydrating than water due to their nutrient and electrolyte content. The optimal daily hydration protocol: ~2.3L (77oz, ~10 glasses) of hydrating fluid regardless of body size, paired with 8-9g of low-heavy-metal sea salt. Coffee and alcohol are dehydrating and don't count toward the target.

Critical timing: consume the majority of fluid in the first 10 hours after waking (before 5pm if you wake at 7am), limiting intake to 5-8oz after that to minimize nighttime urination. For exercise, use the Galpin equation: body weight in pounds / 30 = ounces of fluid every 15-20 minutes during exercise. Ketogenic dieters need significantly more salt due to insulin-driven electrolyte wasting. Filter tap water to remove fluoride, pharmaceuticals, and chlorine derivatives.
