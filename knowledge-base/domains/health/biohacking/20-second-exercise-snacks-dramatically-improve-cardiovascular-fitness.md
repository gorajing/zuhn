Research from Canadian laboratories demonstrated that 20-second bouts of all-out exertion (like running up ~60 stairs), repeated about once every four hours during a workday, 3 times per week for 6 weeks, produced statistically significant improvements in cardiorespiratory fitness (VO2 max) and cognitive benefits. A separate study showed the same intervention improved postprandial glucose control, insulin response, and other metabolic markers after a high glycemic index meal.

Galpin emphasizes the protocol details are not what matters — it doesn't have to be exactly 20 seconds, exactly every 4 hours, or exactly stairs. The principle is: get your heart rate up really quickly, multiple times a day, with any form of maximal exertion. No warm-up needed, no sweating required. This makes it extraordinarily accessible for desk workers and removes the most common exercise barriers (time, equipment, shower access).
