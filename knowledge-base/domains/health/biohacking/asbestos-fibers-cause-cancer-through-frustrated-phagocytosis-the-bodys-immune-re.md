When asbestos fibers lodge deep in lung tissue, macrophages attempt to engulf them — but the fibers are too long and stiff. This is described as 'trying to eat a toothpick sideways.' The frustrated macrophages release reactive oxygen species that damage surrounding cell DNA, eventually triggering uncontrolled cell division (cancer). Fibers can also migrate through lymphatic vessels to reach virtually every organ — brain, bone marrow, spleen, intestines, pancreas, prostate, ovaries, thyroid, and liver.

The key biological insight is that the immune system itself becomes the damage vector. The body's defensive mechanism — inflammation and phagocytosis — is what causes the disease. Asbestos-related deaths from shipyard exposure during WWII (14 per 1,000) actually exceeded combat deaths (8.6 per 1,000). Asbestos has been detected in consumer products as recently as 2017 in children's makeup sold at Claire's stores worldwide.
