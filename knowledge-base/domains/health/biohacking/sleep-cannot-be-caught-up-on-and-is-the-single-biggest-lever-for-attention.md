During sleep, the brain is highly active: consolidating memory, clearing waste products, and processing emotions. Shortchanging sleep produces cognitive impairment equivalent to a blood alcohol concentration of 0.08. You cannot compensate by sleeping in on weekends because the circadian cycle of 90-minute sleep stages does not work that way.

Practical sleep hygiene: keep the bedroom at 68 degrees, maintain regular sleep and wake times, remove TVs from the bedroom, avoid stimulating content (politics, social media) in the hours before bed. If you need to prepare for a morning presentation, go to bed and set an early alarm rather than staying up late. Morning preparation after sleep produces better performance than late-night cramming.
