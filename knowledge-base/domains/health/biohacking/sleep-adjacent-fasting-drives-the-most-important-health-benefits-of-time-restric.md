The core insight from Huberman's consultation with Satchin Panda is that sleep-related fasting is uniquely powerful — not just because you're unconscious and not tempted to eat, but because sleep activates specific cellular repair processes (autophagy, circadian clock gene coordination, liver restoration, gut microbiome maintenance) that are amplified by the fasted state. The two foundational pillars are: no food for at least 60 minutes after waking, and no food (not even 1 gram of sugar) for 2-3 hours before bed.

This reframes fasting strategy entirely. Rather than obsessing over a narrow daytime eating window, the priority should be ensuring deep fasting during sleep. Eating dinner at 6 PM and sleeping at 10 PM means you enter sleep already transitioning to fasted — far better than eating at 9 PM and sleeping at 10 PM, even if both achieve the same total fasting hours. The ideal (though socially difficult) protocol: 8 hours in bed + 1 hour buffer after waking + 2-3 hours buffer before bed = roughly an 8-hour feeding window in the middle of the day.
