Nicotine is an endogenous neurotransmitter in the human body, which has led some biohackers to promote nicotine supplementation for cognitive enhancement. However, introducing exogenous nicotine creates downstream effects similar to how exogenous cannabinoids disrupt the endocannabinoid system.

Nicotine pouches can raise blood pressure, increase heart rate, and create strong dependency. While some data suggests potential benefits for dementia prevention and alertness, the net effect of regular nicotine supplementation is likely negative. The framing of nicotine as a health supplement obscures its nature as an addictive drug.
