Morgan Levine's research shows that the number on your driver's license is essentially a proxy for what actually matters: your biological age. Using nine biomarkers from standard annual blood tests, her phenotypic age algorithm reveals how fast your biology is actually deteriorating relative to chronological time.

In the US population, the standard deviation is about 5 years — meaning most people are within 5 years of their chronological age, but extreme outliers can appear 10-20 years older or younger biologically. The critical insight is that biological age is malleable. Unlike chronological age, it responds to behavioral and lifestyle changes. Free online calculators already exist to compute your phenotypic age from standard lab results, making this actionable immediately.
