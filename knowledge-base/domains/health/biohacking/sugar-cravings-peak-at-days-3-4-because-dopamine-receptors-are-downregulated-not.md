Berg's day-by-day breakdown: by day 3-4 the brain demands more dopamine but receptors are dysfunctional from sugar history. Cortisol and adrenaline spike to pressure the person back into sugar. Symptoms include brain fog, irritability, headaches, fatigue, anxiety, pressure, agitation. By day 5-7 dopamine receptors begin upregulating; cravings ease; cognitive function improves; mood elevates. Day 14 typically yields full receptor reset and 'food tastes better.' Pre-warning people of the trough is a strong adherence intervention because it converts confusing symptoms into expected ones.

Application: when designing any behavior-change protocol involving neurochemistry (sugar, caffeine, smoking, social media), explicitly map the trough timing and warn participants. The pattern generalizes: 'this is the day you'll want to quit; here's why; here's how long until it's better.' Same logic for change management in organizations — name the trough in advance and adherence improves.
