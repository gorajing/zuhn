Every cell has the same DNA but functions differently because the epigenome — particularly DNA methylation patterns — determines which parts of the genome are accessible. Skin cells, brain cells, and liver cells all have distinct methylation patterns that give them their identity.

With aging, these patterns degrade due to stress and random errors. Cells lose their functional identity and stop performing their intended tasks. This molecular-level deterioration cascades upward: dysfunctional cells produce dysfunctional organs, which produce the visible symptoms we associate with aging. By the time you see wrinkles or experience disease, the molecular damage has been accumulating for years. Machine learning applied to methylation data produces 'epigenetic clocks' that can detect this invisible aging process.
