Hair follicle mitochondria break down in pattern baldness, leaving follicles without the energy to produce hair even if other problems are solved. Red and near-infrared light at 630-850nm penetrates skin and is absorbed by cytochrome c oxidase in mitochondria, boosting ATP production — this is photobiomodulation. The same mechanism is being applied to retinal cells for vision improvement. For hair loss, this explains why red light therapy is synergistic with microneedling and insulin repair — microneedling delivers oxygen, insulin repair restores metabolic inputs, but the mitochondria themselves must be functional to convert those inputs into hair growth. This reframes red light therapy from 'skin treatment' to 'mitochondrial repair' — a fundamental cellular energy intervention.
