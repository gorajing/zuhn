Standard creatine supplementation of 5g/day is well-established for strength training, but higher doses unlock additional benefits. The 10g/day dose has been studied for bone health, making it particularly valuable for older women with osteopenia who have never heard of creatine. The 20g/day dose crosses the blood-brain barrier at sufficient levels to provide cognitive benefits, especially after sleep deprivation.

Creatine monohydrate is the most studied form with the strongest evidence base and is extremely affordable. Creatine HCL is an alternative for those who experience GI issues or water retention with monohydrate, though at significantly lower doses (1-3g/day) due to higher bioavailability. The main side effect of monohydrate is water retention, typically around the midsection.
