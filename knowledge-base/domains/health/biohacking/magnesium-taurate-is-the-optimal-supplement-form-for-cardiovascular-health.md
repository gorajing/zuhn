Among magnesium supplements: Taurate is well-absorbed and provides cardiovascular benefits from taurine (an amino acid found in meat with strong evidence for endothelial and heart health). Malate is well-absorbed and may support energy via Krebs cycle. Glycinate is calming and good for sleep but can deplete methyl groups in people with MTHFR polymorphisms. Threonate has one small study suggesting better blood-brain barrier crossing. Citrate is poorly absorbed and primarily causes diarrhea.

Topical forms (chloride oil, Epsom salt baths) feel good but lack evidence for meaningfully raising blood magnesium levels. Test with both serum/plasma magnesium and RBC magnesium before supplementing. People with kidney disease should be cautious as they may not excrete excess magnesium properly.
