Aging is the single biggest risk factor for heart disease, cancer, diabetes, and most other diseases people worry about. The current medical paradigm treats each disease as a separate problem, but scientists increasingly argue this is backwards: if you slow the underlying aging process, you reduce risk across all age-related diseases simultaneously.

This 'geroscience hypothesis' reframes health optimization from disease-specific prevention (statins for heart disease, screening for cancer) to a single upstream target: biological aging rate. The practical implication is that interventions proven to slow biological aging -- exercise, caloric modulation, sleep optimization, stress reduction -- may be the highest-leverage health investments available, producing benefits across every disease category rather than just one.
