Isolated cold plunging after resistance training has been associated with decreased muscle mass gains. If building muscle, cold exposure should be separated from training by 6-8 hours. Contrast therapy (alternating sauna and cold) has not been studied for this effect and may be a reasonable alternative.

The current consensus suggests approximately 11-12 minutes per week of total cold exposure at 50-55F. Excessive cold plunging may negatively affect immune function based on anecdotal evidence. The primary benefits of cold plunging are mental -- dopamine and catecholamine release creating alertness and a sense of wellbeing rather than direct physical recovery benefits.
