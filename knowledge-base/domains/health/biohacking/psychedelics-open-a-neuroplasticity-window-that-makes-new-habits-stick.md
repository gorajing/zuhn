Johnson describes a multi-level mechanism for psychedelics' lasting effects. At the receptor level, psilocybin activates serotonin 2A receptors. At the network level, brain areas that normally never communicate suddenly become highly correlated. Psychologically, this manifests as radical perspective shifts and ego dissolution.

Critically, animal research shows increased neuroplasticity in the days following use — the brain becomes more primed to learn new things. This creates a sticking power for insights gained during the experience. If the new patterns are reinforced, they become the new normal, which is why people show improvements six months later in depression and smoking cessation. The therapeutic model is: psychedelic breaks old patterns, neuroplasticity window allows new ones to take root, follow-up integration solidifies the change.
