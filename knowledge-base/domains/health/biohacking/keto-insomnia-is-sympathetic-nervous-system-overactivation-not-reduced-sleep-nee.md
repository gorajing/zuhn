A common claim in ketogenic communities is that the diet reduces sleep requirements. Saladino argues this is a misinterpretation: the sympathetic (fight-or-flight) nervous system becomes overactive on very low-carb diets due to elevated cortisol and catecholamines. People feel more wired, not more rested.

Carbohydrates are critical for sleep quality. Adding carbohydrates from fruit and honey to a ketogenic or carnivore diet typically improves sleep scores significantly. The complete sleep optimization protocol: consistent sleep/wake times, sunlight in the morning, grounding (especially grounding mats at night), blue light avoidance, no caffeine/alcohol/marijuana, no screens, good food not eaten too close to bedtime, and adequate carbohydrate intake.
