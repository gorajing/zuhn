The kidney concern stems from creatinine, a breakdown product of creatine, being a standard kidney function marker. When supplementing creatine, creatinine naturally rises as a benign lab artifact, not because kidneys are damaged. Multiple long-term studies confirm creatine safety for kidney function.

The hair loss myth comes from a single study in rugby players showing elevated DHT, but this hasn't been reliably replicated. Creatine is arguably the most studied ergogenic supplement in existence, with an extensive safety profile across all age groups. The primary side effect is water retention with monohydrate, which creatine HCL (1-3g/day) may mitigate for sensitive individuals.
