Van der Kolk describes neurofeedback as placing electrodes on the skull and rewarding brainwave patterns associated with calm and focus. The brain learns to produce these patterns more frequently, effectively reshaping its default configuration. His clinical results were strong enough to include it among his six primary trauma treatments.

The mechanism is operant conditioning at the neural level — the brain gets immediate feedback when it produces desired states, and over time gravitates toward those states by default. This is significant because it works below conscious awareness, making it useful for people who struggle with meditation or cognitive techniques. For the biohacking community, neurofeedback represents a trainable knob on brain state that doesn't require pharmacological intervention or years of contemplative practice.
