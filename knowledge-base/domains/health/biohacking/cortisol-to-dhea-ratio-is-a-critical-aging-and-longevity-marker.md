Research strongly links the cortisol-to-DHEA-S ratio with longevity and morbidity outcomes. Cortisol functions as an aging hormone -- published review papers specifically ask whether cortisol drives aging, and evidence supports this. Fasting and ketogenic diets tend to elevate cortisol between meals.

The practical protocol: get both fasting cortisol and afternoon cortisol-to-DHEA-S ratios tested regularly. Target a ratio below 3. Adding carbohydrates from fruit and honey can lower cortisol without worsening insulin sensitivity -- fasting insulin often remains low or even decreases when adding 200-300g of carbs from whole food sources. This challenges the keto/fasting orthodoxy by showing that signals of nutritional abundance may be more anti-aging than deliberate restriction.
