Art Winfree's research connected the spiral wave patterns observed in BZ chemical reactions to identical patterns found in fibrillating hearts. In a healthy heart, electrical signals synchronize muscle contractions to pump blood. In fibrillation, these signals fragment into rotating spiral waves — the heart muscle contracts chaotically, no blood is pumped, and death follows within minutes.

This cross-domain transfer from chemistry to cardiology illustrates how synchronization theory has life-or-death applications. The insight that fibrillation is a synchronization problem (not just an electrical problem) opens the door to designing gentler defibrillators that restore synchrony through targeted intervention rather than the current approach of overwhelming the heart with massive electrical shocks. It also demonstrates that both too little synchronization (fibrillation) and too much (the Millennium Bridge) can be destructive — optimal systems need calibrated synchronization.
