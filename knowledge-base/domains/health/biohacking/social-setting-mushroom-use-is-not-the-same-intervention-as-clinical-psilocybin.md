The claim is that most of the 'bad trips,' 'life-altering decisions,' and 'psychotic break' cases in the cultural discourse come from unquantified, unsupervised use in social settings — not from the clinical protocols that are being studied in trials. This doesn't mean clinical psychedelics are risk-free; the conversation acknowledges that a non-zero fraction of users are predisposed to schizophrenic or psychotic reactions regardless of setting.

But it does mean that aggregate risk estimates need to be stratified by context. A friend eating an unknown dose at a party is a fundamentally different experimental condition than a screened participant taking 25mg of measured psilocybin with a trained sitter. The practical implication: when evaluating psychedelic safety research, always ask which condition is being described. When making personal decisions, understand that the protocol is most of the intervention.
