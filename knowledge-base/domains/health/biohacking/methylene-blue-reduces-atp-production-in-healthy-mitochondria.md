Methylene blue shuttles electrons along the mitochondrial electron transport chain, which can help when the chain is broken (as in Alzheimer's). However, in healthy mitochondria, it actually decreases total ATP production by short-circuiting the normal process. This has been reproducibly demonstrated.

As an MAOI (monoamine oxidase inhibitor), methylene blue alters neurotransmitter levels, creating subjective feelings that users mistake for increased energy. This makes it impossible to self-assess whether the benefit is metabolic or merely psychoactive. Alzheimer's trials showed no improvement and increased falls. The correct approach is fixing mitochondria foundationally through diet (CoQ10 from organ meats, reducing linoleic acid) rather than using a pharmaceutical band-aid.
