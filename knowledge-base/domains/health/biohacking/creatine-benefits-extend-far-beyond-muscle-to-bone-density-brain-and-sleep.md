Creatine's benefits are dose-dependent across different tissues: 5g/day is well-established for strength and muscle recovery. 10g/day has been studied for bone density, particularly important for women with osteopenia. 15-20g/day achieves sufficient brain levels for cognitive enhancement and sleep quality improvement -- especially valuable after poor sleep.

Creatine is found exclusively in animal foods but even heavy meat eaters rarely get enough from diet alone (~1-2g/day from food). Monohydrate is the gold standard with the most research support. For those with GI issues or water retention, creatine HCL at 1-3g/day is more bioavailable per gram. Pennies per day makes this one of the most cost-effective supplements available.
