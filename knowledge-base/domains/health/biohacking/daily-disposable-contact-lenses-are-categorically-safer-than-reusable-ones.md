Contact lens-related corneal infections, while rare, can be devastating — leaving permanent corneal scarring even after successful treatment. The risk factors compound: reusable lenses trap bacteria and fungus, overnight wear creates a low-oxygen environment where anaerobic bacteria thrive, and human compliance with cleaning protocols is unreliable.

Daily disposables eliminate the two highest-risk variables: cleaning compliance and extended wear temptation. Dr. Goldberg recommends them despite the higher cost and environmental concern, framing it as a risk-elimination strategy rather than convenience. This follows a general principle in preventive health: when the downside of failure is severe and irreversible (permanent corneal scarring), it's worth paying more for a system that removes the failure mode entirely rather than relying on consistent human behavior. As people age and tear production decreases, contact lens tolerance naturally declines — most people transition from 12-hour wear to only occasional use over decades.
