Current gene therapies cost millions of dollars per dose, making them inaccessible for most patients and economically unviable for rare diseases with only a handful of patients worldwide. Kelsic argues that Wright's Law — with every doubling of cumulative production, costs decrease by a fixed percentage — will apply to gene therapy as it has to semiconductors (200x improvement) and solar (200-fold cost reduction over five decades).

The vision is AI-designed personalized gene therapies: an AI system designs a therapy customized to a specific patient's genome, charts the development pathway, production process, and safety testing — all at near-zero marginal cost. This could address the 'long tail' of 7,000+ genetic diseases, including ultra-rare conditions with only a single patient in the world. The concept of 'genetic agency' — choosing to upgrade or remove gene therapies over time, treating genetics as a modifiable layer rather than fixed identity — represents a fundamental shift in how humans relate to their own biology.
