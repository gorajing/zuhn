Carbohydrates serve as an evolutionary signal of abundance. Historically, fruit availability meant seasons of plenty -- time for reproduction and fertility. Without carbohydrates, the body interprets the situation as winter or famine, downregulating reproductive hormones including testosterone.

Saladino's personal data: strict carnivore dropped testosterone from ~800 to ~500 (still in range, but a 37% decline). Adding fruit and honey restored levels. The optimal carbohydrate sources for testosterone: fruit, honey, squash, milk, sweet potatoes, and tubers. White potatoes work for those without nightshade sensitivity. Grains are the least preferred carbohydrate source due to antinutrient content.
