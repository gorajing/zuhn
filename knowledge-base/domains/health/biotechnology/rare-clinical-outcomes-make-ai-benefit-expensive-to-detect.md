Primary care creates a measurement bottleneck because serious near-term outcomes such as hospitalization or death are rare, and many visits involve self-limiting conditions. The release notes that detecting modest effects may require trials involving more than 100,000 patients, far beyond many AI pilots.

This keeps the lesson grounded: lack of a significant outcome lift is not automatically proof that the AI has no value, but it does mean claims must be calibrated to what the study could detect. For AI deployment teams, choose endpoints whose base rates match the expected effect size, or explicitly frame early wins as process, safety, or cost evidence rather than patient-outcome proof.
