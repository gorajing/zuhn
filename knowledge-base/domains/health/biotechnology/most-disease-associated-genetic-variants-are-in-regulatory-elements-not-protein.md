Anshul Kundaje's framing: the human genome encodes about 20,000 protein-coding genes but ~3.5-4 million control elements that regulate when and where those genes are expressed. The vast majority of genetic variants associated with complex disease are NOT in the coding regions — they're in these regulatory elements, often hundreds of kilobases from the gene they affect. Implication: therapeutic targeting of the proteome (the dominant pharma strategy of the last 50 years) misses the largest fraction of where disease genetics actually lives.

Application: the next decade of therapeutics will require regulatory-genome interventions (CRISPR edits to control elements, antisense to enhance/suppress regulatory RNAs) at scale. Drug discovery teams that only look at coding-region variants are operating on the smaller fraction of the actionable variant space.
