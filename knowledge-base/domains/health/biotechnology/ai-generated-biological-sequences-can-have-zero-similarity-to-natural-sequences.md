Brian Hie's striking result: EVO-1 generates anti-CRISPR genes when prompted with anti-CRISPR-associated genes. Some of these AI-generated sequences have NO significant sequence similarity to any gene that has ever been discovered from nature. Yet experimental validation confirms they function. This is evidence that generative biology models are doing more than retrieval or shallow interpolation — they're learning the underlying sequence/function manifold and generating in regions evolution simply hasn't explored.

Application: design pipelines should explicitly seek 'low sequence similarity to known' generations as candidates for novel mechanisms. The model is mining unused regions of biological possibility — those novel-by-construction candidates may have advantages (e.g., evading immune memory in therapeutic contexts) that close-to-natural variants can't offer.
