The workflow: take skin or blood cells, reprogram them with Yamanaka factors into induced pluripotent stem cells, differentiate those into heart/liver/lung organoids, then test drug candidates and combinations in those patient-specific tissues. The value is not just efficacy prediction — it is idiosyncratic toxicity detection, drug-drug interaction screening, and combinatorial dose-finding in a system that shares the patient's genome.

This is still exploratory ('TBD if it actually works') but it is the most plausible path to personalized pharmacology at scale. The current limits are: organoids don't capture whole-organism pharmacokinetics, immune interactions, or tissue crosstalk. But as a first-pass filter on 'is this drug likely to hurt me or help me,' it is substantially better than the current standard of 'take it and measure biomarkers for 90 days.' The technology is also the right architecture for AI-accelerated drug discovery at the individual level.
