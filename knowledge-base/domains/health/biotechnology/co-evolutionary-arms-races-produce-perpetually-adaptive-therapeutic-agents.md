The fundamental advantage of phage therapy over antibiotics is that phages are living systems locked in an evolutionary arms race with their targets. While antibiotics are static chemical compounds that bacteria can permanently outrun through mutation, phages continuously counter-adapt. This makes them 'smart weapons that are constantly getting better at killing.'

This distinction between static and adaptive solutions has broad implications for biotechnology. The most durable therapeutic approaches may be those that harness existing biological arms races rather than introducing novel static interventions. The billions of years of R&D that evolution has invested in phage-bacteria warfare represents an optimization depth that no pharmaceutical development timeline can match.
