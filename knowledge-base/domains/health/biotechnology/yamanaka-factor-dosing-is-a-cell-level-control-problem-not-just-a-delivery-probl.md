The core technical constraint on using Yamanaka factors for rejuvenation is that the dose window is very narrow: too little and nothing happens, too much and the cell fully dedifferentiates into a pluripotent state, which is functionally equivalent to a cancer cell. Any whole-organism or whole-tissue dosing strategy is going to hit cells with heterogeneous sensitivity, and you only need one dedifferentiated cell to start a tumor.

The proposed solution is to put the control loop inside the cell: engineered cellular switches that sense the local expression level of reprogramming targets and shut down protein synthesis when thresholds are approached. This is a bet that the problem can only be solved with synthetic biology feedback circuits, not with better delivery or dosing protocols. It is a useful architectural principle — any therapy with a cliff-edge failure mode at the cell level eventually needs cell-level closed-loop control, not just systemic titration.
