Bryan Johnson reframes psychedelics from a psychiatric therapy (treating depression, anxiety, PTSD) into a candidate longevity intervention measured by inflammation markers, blood glucose, microbiome composition, and default mode network plasticity. His n=1 psilocybin protocol (3 doses at 25mg) reportedly moved his blood glucose from the 99.5th to 99.9th percentile without metformin-style direct glucose action, suggesting a whole-body metabolic reset rather than a CNS-only effect.

This is a genuinely novel framing worth tracking but the evidence is thin: a mouse study on inflammation, one human (Johnson himself) with extreme confounds, and no controlled replication. Treat as a hypothesis that could unlock a new therapeutic modality if replicated, not as established biology. The falsifiable claim is that measured biomarkers (HbA1c, CRP, microbiome diversity, DMN connectivity on fMRI) improve in a dose-dependent way across a cohort after psychedelic protocols.
