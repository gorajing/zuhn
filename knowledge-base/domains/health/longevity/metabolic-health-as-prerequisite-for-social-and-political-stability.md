The claim is that external conflict is significantly downstream of internal unhappiness, and that internal unhappiness is significantly downstream of metabolic dysfunction, sleep deprivation, and chronic disease. The cited figures — 84% of Americans with some form of metabolic disorder, 40%+ obesity, widespread mental health decline — are roughly consistent with published epidemiology.

The causal step from 'population metabolic dysfunction' to 'political and social dysfunction' is the speculative part. It could be true (unhealthy people have less psychological bandwidth for constructive civic engagement) or a just-so story that over-weights a favored lens. Mark as low confidence and as a hypothesis worth testing rather than an established fact. The useful frame regardless of truth value: if you believe this story even 30% you should weight GLP-1s, metabolic interventions, and sleep improvement more heavily in any policy portfolio because the downstream effects are much larger than the direct health benefit.
