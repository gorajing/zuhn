The video documents Bitcoin's early social reception: Reddit's first Bitcoin post was downvoted. The first TV reference (The Good Wife, 2012) treated it as a novelty at $3.41. A 2013 conference talk about Bitcoin at $100 played to an empty room. Mainstream media called it 'worthless artificial gold' and a 'Ponzi scheme.'

This pattern challenges the common heuristic that social consensus provides useful signal about asset quality. In Bitcoin's case, unanimous dismissal coexisted with the asset's most asymmetric return period. The few early believers (Chamath Palihapitiya at $135) identified adoption drivers — developing-world currency instability, desire for unfettered capital access — that the dismissive majority couldn't see because they evaluated Bitcoin through developed-world, status-quo assumptions. The insight isn't that all dismissed assets succeed, but that early social rejection provides essentially zero information about long-term trajectory.
