The video documents the progression from gold-backed currency to pure fiat, and the consequence: since 1971, all currencies are backed only by trust in governments that have structural incentives to inflate. This isn't a conspiracy — it's the logical outcome of a debt-based system that requires money supply growth. Inflation isn't a bug; it's the operating temperature of the system.

This creates permanent demand for assets that can't be diluted by central bank policy: gold (finite supply, 5000-year track record), Bitcoin (algorithmically capped supply), and other scarce assets. The Russia-China pivot to gold reserves isn't ideological — it's balance sheet management against a depreciating reserve asset. For investors, the question isn't whether to hold non-sovereign stores of value, but what allocation reflects the pace of fiat dilution versus the volatility risk of alternatives.
