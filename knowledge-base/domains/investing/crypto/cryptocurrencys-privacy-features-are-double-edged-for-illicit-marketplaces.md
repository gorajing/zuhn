Bitcoin was instrumental in enabling Silk Road's anonymous marketplace, but the same blockchain that provided pseudonymity also created an immutable transaction ledger. When the FBI arrested Ross Ulbricht, they seized approximately 170,000 bitcoins — worth ~$100M at the time but ~$2.8B at 2017 peak prices. This illustrates a fundamental asymmetry: cryptocurrency provides enough anonymity for small transactions to feel safe, but large-scale illicit operations accumulate forensic surface area that blockchain analysis can eventually unravel. The permanence of blockchain records means that even currently-anonymous transactions can be de-anonymized retroactively as analysis tools improve, making cryptocurrency a surprisingly poor choice for sustained criminal enterprise.
