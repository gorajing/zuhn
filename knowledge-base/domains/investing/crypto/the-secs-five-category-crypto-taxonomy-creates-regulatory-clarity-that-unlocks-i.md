The March 2026 SEC/CFTC joint interpretive release represents a structural shift in US crypto regulation. By introducing a formal taxonomy (digital commodities, collectibles, tools, stablecoins = not securities; digital securities = securities), the SEC eliminated the ambiguity that forced companies to either avoid the US market or operate under constant enforcement risk.

The guidance also clarifies edge-case activities—airdrops, staking, mining, asset wrapping—that had been regulatory gray zones. While courts can deviate from interpretive releases, the SEC's commitment to follow up with a proposed rule signals this is durable policy direction, not an enforcement pause.

The simultaneous Nasdaq/DTC approval for tokenized Russell 1000 stocks and major ETFs is the immediate commercial implication: traditional equity markets are beginning to adopt blockchain rails while preserving existing structures (same ticker, CUSIP, rights). This is not crypto displacing TradFi—it's TradFi absorbing crypto's settlement infrastructure, which may be the more durable and larger market opportunity.
