Harry Markopolos identified Madoff's fraud in five minutes by noticing returns that were positive regardless of market direction — a mathematical impossibility for any legitimate strategy. Real investments, no matter how sophisticated, show some correlation to market conditions and experience periodic losses. When an investment product claims steady 1-2% monthly returns through bull and bear markets alike, it violates basic financial physics. Due diligence should treat unusually smooth return curves as a primary disqualifier, not a selling point. The Madoff case proves that consistency itself can be the lie.
