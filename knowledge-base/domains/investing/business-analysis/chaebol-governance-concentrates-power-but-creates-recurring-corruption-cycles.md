Samsung exemplifies the chaebol paradox: every leader of Samsung has been accused, convicted, or jailed for tax evasion, bribery, embezzlement, or perjury, yet the company's 20% share of South Korea's GDP makes it effectively untouchable. Lee Jae-yong received bribery charges, served jail time, and was back as executive chairman by 2022 because the Korean government explicitly stated Samsung was too important to disrupt.

This creates a governance doom loop — concentrated family power enables corruption, corruption leads to prosecution, but systemic importance guarantees restoration. For investors, this means chaebol stocks carry a permanent governance discount that periodically spikes during scandal cycles but never fully resolves. The pattern is structural, not incidental.
