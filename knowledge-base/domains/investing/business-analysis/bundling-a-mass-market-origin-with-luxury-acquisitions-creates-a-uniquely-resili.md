Volkswagen's growth strategy reveals a pattern: use a high-volume, affordable brand to generate cash flow and manufacturing scale, then acquire prestigious marques (Bentley, Lamborghini, Bugatti, Porsche) that share platforms and engineering but command premium margins. This dual structure creates resilience — the mass-market side provides volume stability during downturns while luxury brands contribute disproportionate profit margins. The strategy also creates engineering synergies: Porsche's racing expertise feeds back into VW's consumer vehicles, while VW's manufacturing scale reduces per-unit costs for low-volume luxury brands. This pattern generalizes beyond automotive — any conglomerate anchoring on volume distribution while layering premium brands benefits from the same cash-flow-to-prestige flywheel.
