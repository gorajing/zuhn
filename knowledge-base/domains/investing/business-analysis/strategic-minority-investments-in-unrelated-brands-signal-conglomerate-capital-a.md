Samsung's 1995 rescue investment of $2M into clothing brand FUBU — just as it was about to collapse — produced $6B in downstream sales. This isn't just a fun fact; it reveals a pattern where diversified conglomerates develop capital allocation instincts that function like internal venture capital arms.

The ability to spot undervalued assets across completely unrelated industries (electronics to fashion) and deploy small amounts of capital with outsized returns is a signal of organizational intelligence that's hard to see from the outside. For investors analyzing conglomerates, these small bets are leading indicators of management quality — they show the leadership can evaluate opportunities beyond their core competency.
