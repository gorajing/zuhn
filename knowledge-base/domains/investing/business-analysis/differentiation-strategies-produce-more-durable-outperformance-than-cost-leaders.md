ROIC decomposes into NOPAT margin times invested capital turnover (via DuPont analysis). Companies can achieve the same ROIC through very different paths: Marathon Petroleum earns 18% ROIC through 8% margin and 2.3x turnover (cost leadership), while Devon Energy earns the same 18% through 26% margin and 0.7x turnover (differentiation). The isoquant curve at ~8% cost of capital cleanly separates value creators from value destroyers.

Raynor and Ahmed's study of 25,000+ U.S. public companies from 1966-2010 found that truly exceptional performers shared two rules: 'better before cheaper' (compete on differentiation, not price) and 'revenues before cost' (grow the top line, not just cut expenses). Mauboussin's own analysis of 643 firms that maintained top-quintile ROIC for 10+ consecutive years from 1963-2023 confirmed that NOPAT margin was the dominant driver of sustained outperformance, suggesting differentiation strategies are more durable than cost leadership.
