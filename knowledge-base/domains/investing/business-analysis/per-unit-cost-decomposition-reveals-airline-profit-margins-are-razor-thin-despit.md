Breaking down a $70-80 NYC-DC ticket reveals fuel costs only $2.50 per person, crew costs $1.50, airport fees $13.50, taxes $15, aircraft depreciation $11.50, and maintenance $14. The airline's actual profit is roughly $10 on an $80 ticket — and that assumes a fully loaded plane. This cost structure explains why airlines are perennially low-margin businesses despite appearing expensive to consumers: most of the ticket price flows to airports, governments, and capital replacement rather than to the airline itself. Investors evaluating capital-intensive transport businesses should distinguish between gross revenue and the portion the operator actually controls.
