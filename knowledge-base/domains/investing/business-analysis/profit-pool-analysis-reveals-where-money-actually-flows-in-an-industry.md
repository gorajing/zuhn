The profit pool framework plots each industry participant with the ROIC-WACC spread as height (value creation percentage) and invested capital as width (how much money is deployed). A company's economic profit is the area of its rectangle (spread times capital). The aviation industry example is striking: airlines and airports absorb the majority of invested capital but have negative economic profit, while freight forwarders and fuel producers earn positive spreads on relatively small capital bases. The aggregate economic profit for aviation was negative $69 billion in 2022.

Periodic snapshots reveal competitive dynamics over time. The U.S. airline profit pool from 2013 to 2023 shows how consolidation shifted value creation: in 2013 only United had positive economic profit among the big four, but by 2018 three of four were positive as consolidation benefits accrued. Bezos's maxim 'your margin is my opportunity' captures both the opportunity and threat of large profit pools.
