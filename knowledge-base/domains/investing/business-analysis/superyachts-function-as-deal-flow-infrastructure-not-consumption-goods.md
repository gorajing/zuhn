The video quotes a yacht owner telling the New Yorker that 'one deal secured on board will pay it all back many times over.' While this could be rationalization, it reveals how ultra-wealthy individuals reframe consumption as investment. Yacht clubs require sponsorship and committee approval, berth reservations must be booked months ahead, and prime positioning at events like the Monaco Yacht Show requires years of relationship-building with event directors.

This reframing matters for business analysis: assets that appear to be pure consumption goods may actually function as critical deal-flow infrastructure with high switching costs and network effects. The yacht club membership model — sponsor-required, committee-approved, relationship-dependent — creates exactly the kind of exclusive network that generates outsized returns for members. This pattern applies beyond yachts to any high-cost asset that serves as a coordination mechanism for deal-making among wealthy networks.
