Hormozi argues that a $500, two-day certification can triple someone's hourly rate from minimum wage to $25/hour — a permanent 3x multiplier on lifetime earnings. Meanwhile, $500 in an index fund at 10% annual returns takes roughly 11 years to double. The asymmetry is stark: skill investments can 2-10x earning capacity within months, while financial assets compound slowly.

This doesn't mean financial investing is wrong — it means there's a sequencing problem. The optimal strategy is to maximize earning capacity first (through education investment), then redirect growing surplus income into financial assets. The S&P 500 is most powerful when you have large amounts to invest, which requires high earnings, which requires skills. Hormozi frames the choice as: would you rather compound $1,000 at 10% annually, or compound your ability to generate $1,000 every week?
