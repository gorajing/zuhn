Coffeezilla provides a concrete natural experiment in deterrence theory: when the DOJ released its first-ever NFT fraud indictment, several dead NFT projects immediately came 'back to life' because their founders realized law enforcement was now in the space. This reveals that many fraudsters are not ideological — they run an explicit cost-benefit calculation where prosecution probability is a key variable.

The implication is that enforcement has a massive multiplier effect. One prosecution doesn't just punish one fraudster — it shifts the math for every potential fraudster watching. During the 2020-2022 crypto boom, the absence of enforcement created what Coffeezilla calls a 'nihilistic landscape' where not committing fraud was almost irrational for amoral actors. This suggests that in any emerging unregulated market, early enforcement actions have outsized deterrent value compared to later ones.
