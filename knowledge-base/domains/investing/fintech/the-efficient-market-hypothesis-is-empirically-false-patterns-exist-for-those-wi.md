Andrew Lo of MIT published research in 1988 showing the EMH is statistically false — predictable patterns exist in stock markets. Jim Simons' Medallion fund proved this practically with 66% annual returns for 30 years (turning $100 into $8.4B).

The crucial nuance is that markets are 'efficient enough' that casual prediction fails — the very act of predicting and trading on predictions eliminates the patterns being exploited. But with Hidden Markov models, massive data collection, and scientific talent (physicists, mathematicians, astronomers — never traditional finance people), Renaissance Technologies found persistent edges. Ironically, if all patterns were eventually discovered and exploited, the market would finally become truly efficient and perfectly random.
