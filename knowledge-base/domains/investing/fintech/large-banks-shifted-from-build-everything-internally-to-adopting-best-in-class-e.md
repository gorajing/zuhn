David Goldberg traces a cultural arc at large financial institutions: Goldman Sachs once built its own email client (Orbit) rather than use Outlook. Then came a period of trying to become fintech companies themselves (Marcus). After a humbling, they became more open to external technology.

AI is accelerating this shift because unlike cloud migration (an esoteric question for a bank CEO), any board member can try a prompt and intuitively understand the productivity impact. This bottom-up adoption pressure combined with top-down strategic recognition is creating the fastest technology adoption cycle these institutions have ever experienced. The implication: selling AI-powered software into large financial institutions is currently easier than it's ever been.
