The core innovation of Real Estate 3.0 is reducing the minimum viable ownership unit — from an entire home ($300K+) to a $5 share of a property. This pattern (exemplified by Landa, Pacaso, and similar platforms) mirrors what Robinhood did for equities: the asset class itself doesn't change, but the access mechanism transforms when you reduce the minimum investment by orders of magnitude.

The critical enabler is not just splitting ownership, but making those fractions tradeable. Without liquidity — the ability to buy and sell fractions easily — fractional ownership is just a gimmick. The companies that win must solve both the fractionalization problem AND the marketplace liquidity problem. This is why smart contracts and automated legal paperwork matter: they reduce the per-transaction cost enough to make micro-ownership economically viable. The pattern applies to any illiquid asset class — art, commercial real estate, infrastructure, intellectual property.
