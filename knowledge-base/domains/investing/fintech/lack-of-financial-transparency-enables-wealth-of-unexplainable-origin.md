The Epstein case exposes a systemic vulnerability: the financial system allowed someone with no formal credentials (no CPA, no Series 7, no CFA, no college degree) to charge tens of millions in fees for services of unverifiable value. Ultra-wealthy clients either didn't perform due diligence or didn't care about credentials.

This isn't just about one individual - it reveals that high-end financial services operate on networks of trust and social proof rather than verifiable competence. The 'follow the money' investigative approach fails when the money trails are deliberately structured to be untraceable, which is trivially easy in a system that prioritizes privacy over transparency.
