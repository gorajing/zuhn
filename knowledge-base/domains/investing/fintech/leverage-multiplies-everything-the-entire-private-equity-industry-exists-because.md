Srivatsaa demonstrates leverage with a simple example: buying a $1M house all-cash and getting 10% appreciation = 10% return. Buying with $200K down and $800K loan on the same house = 50% return on your capital. This multiplier effect created the entire private equity industry and commercial real estate investing.

The tax dimension is equally important: Elon Musk borrowed against Tesla stock to buy Twitter rather than selling shares and triggering capital gains. Borrowing against collateral generates no taxable income. Four principles of leverage: (1) it's the number one economic growth engine globally, (2) educate yourself on available risk, (3) it's a game of collateral — the asset backs the loan, (4) since leverage isn't income, it creates massive tax advantages. The key nuance: leverage magnifies what you've already built, so the prerequisite is having something worth leveraging.
