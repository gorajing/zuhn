Most people try to increase returns without paying attention to risk. Srivatsaa's friend bet $700K of life savings — 15 years of systematic saving — on a single oil and gas deal that collapsed. The lesson isn't about evaluating the deal; it's about sizing the bet. You don't risk the empire for a pot of gold.

Ray Dalio's framework reframes investing: given two investments with similar returns but different risk profiles, the goal is to reduce risk while keeping returns the same. Move from risk-150/return-15% to risk-60/return-11.5% — that becomes the best investment. Two rules: (1) protect the machine that produces opportunities (your capital base), (2) swing for upside only when downside is capped. Diversification follows knowledge: concentrate where you understand risk and have control, diversify only where you have neither. This is why entrepreneurs hold their entire net worth in their business — they have insider knowledge and control.
