Kalshi's valuation jump to $11B following a $1B financing round illustrates the prediction market category's explosive growth alongside its fundamental tension. Surging volumes and competitive dynamics with Polymarket are driving massive value creation, but this growth coincides with increasing legal friction because regulators struggle to categorize these instruments.

The core challenge is that prediction markets exist in a genuinely ambiguous space between financial derivatives and wagering. As volumes grow, they attract more regulatory scrutiny precisely because they become systemically relevant. For investors, this means the category offers asymmetric upside if regulatory clarity emerges favorably, but the risk of adverse regulation scales proportionally with the market's success — a dynamic that makes timing and jurisdiction critical factors in any investment thesis.
