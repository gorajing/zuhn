Before the 2024 U.S. election, prediction markets were a niche product with roughly $50 million in weekly trading volume. Within 24 months, that figure surged past $6 billion — a 100x increase that signals a fundamental shift in how markets price information and uncertainty.

This growth trajectory mirrors early-stage adoption curves of other financial innovations like ETFs and crypto exchanges. The implication for investors and builders is that prediction markets are becoming a legitimate asset class and information layer, not just a novelty. The infrastructure being built around them (liquidity, regulation, UX) suggests durable growth rather than a speculative bubble.
