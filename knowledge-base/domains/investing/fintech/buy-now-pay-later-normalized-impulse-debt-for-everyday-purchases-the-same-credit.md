Buy Now Pay Later brought short-term unsecured personal loans from furniture/appliances into everyday impulse shopping. The innovation: instant credit in a few taps, splitting any purchase into tiny installments, instant gratification with delivery before first payment. By 2021: 173M global users, $97B in BNPL purchases (2% of global e-commerce). The 2008 parallel: credit expansion that makes borrowing frictionless always increases default risk. When anyone can split a $20 purchase into 4 payments, the line between 'manageable installment' and 'compounding micro-debts' disappears. Missing payments triggers fees, collections, and credit score damage — but the UI hides this friction behind seamless checkout flows.
