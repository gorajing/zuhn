Through his Wealth Coach seminars, Ramsey interviewed hundreds of millionaires and found a consistent pattern: they didn't get rich from a particular asset class. Car dealers invested in classic cars. Farmers bought land. Business operators bought businesses. 67% of Forbes 400 are first-generation, and over 90% of those are business-driven. Hormozi applied this principle after his $40M liquidity event — every time he almost wrote a $5M check for real estate, he felt uncomfortable because it wasn't his sandbox. When he shifted to buying businesses (his domain expertise), he felt confident. The lesson: the information advantage from deep domain knowledge produces better risk-adjusted returns than diversification into unfamiliar sophisticated instruments.
