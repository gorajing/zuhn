Hormozi highlights that the average American sends 35% of every paycheck to banks and lenders for debt service, yet simultaneously tries to earn 10% returns in the stock market. This is negative arbitrage — you're paying 24% guaranteed on credit cards while hoping for 10% uncertain returns on investments. The framing as 'guaranteed returns against you being wealthy' is powerful because it repositions debt payoff from a boring obligation into an exciting guaranteed-return investment. Compound interest is working against you on both sides: the debt compounds negatively at a higher rate than your investments compound positively. The math is straightforward but people fail to see it because they mentally separate 'debt payments' from 'investment returns' into different categories rather than viewing them on the same financial balance sheet.
