Traditional financial services companies relied on decades of accumulated data to build underwriting models for insurance, loans, and credit. This data moat was their primary competitive advantage. But two shifts have eroded it: ML algorithms that achieve greater accuracy from smaller datasets, and the explosion of publicly available or scrapeable alternative data sources.

This means a marketplace startup can now build underwriting models that match or exceed incumbent accuracy by leveraging its own transaction data plus alternative data sources. The result is lower cost of underwriting through automation and improved loss ratios. A B2B ecosystem of companies packaging alternative data for underwriting (identity, behavior, transaction history) further lowers the barrier. For investors, this means the moat in financial services is shifting from data accumulation to data utilization—favoring technology-native companies over traditional financial institutions.
