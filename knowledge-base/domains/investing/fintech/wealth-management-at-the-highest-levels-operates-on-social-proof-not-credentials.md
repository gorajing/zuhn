In traditional finance, credentials serve as trust proxies: a CFA or Series 7 tells clients the advisor has demonstrated baseline competence. At the ultra-high-net-worth level, these credentials become irrelevant. Access, exclusivity, and social proof replace formal verification.

This creates an environment where genuine talent, mediocre networkers, and outright fraudsters are structurally indistinguishable from each other. The same opacity that protects legitimate wealth also enables illegitimate wealth, and the social dynamics of elite networks actively resist the scrutiny that would distinguish between them.
