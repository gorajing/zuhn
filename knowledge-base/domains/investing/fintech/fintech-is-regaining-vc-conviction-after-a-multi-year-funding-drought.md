After the post-2021 correction gutted fintech valuations and dried up deal flow, Q1 2025 data shows VCs actively re-entering the sector. The pullback was severe—rising interest rates, BNPL defaults, and neobank struggles spooked institutional capital. The renewed conviction appears driven by leaner, more defensible business models and AI-enabled unit economics that couldn't exist in the prior cycle.

For founders, this reopening represents a narrow window: the funds re-entering fintech are looking for companies that survived the drought with strong retention and proven monetization, not growth-at-all-costs pitches. The bar is higher than 2021, but capital is available for operators who demonstrate margin discipline.

For investors, fintech re-entry requires distinguishing genuine recovery from reflexive mean-reversion. The winners in this cycle will likely be infrastructure and embedded finance plays rather than consumer neobanks that dominated the prior wave.
