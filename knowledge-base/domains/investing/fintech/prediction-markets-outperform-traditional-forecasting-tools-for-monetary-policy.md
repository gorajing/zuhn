A new Federal Reserve working paper validated prediction markets as superior forecasting instruments. Kalshi's median/mode predictions have accurately matched FOMC decisions the day before meetings since 2022, outperforming fed funds futures. More notably, Kalshi's CPI forecasts beat Bloomberg consensus — the industry standard for macro forecasting.

The asymmetric finding is particularly valuable: upside CPI surprises influenced expected policy paths more than downside surprises, revealing a market-embedded bias that traditional point estimates miss entirely. The fact that the Fed itself is now recommending monitoring these markets suggests a feedback loop where prediction market liquidity and accuracy will compound. For individual investors and decision-makers, incorporating prediction market distributions into macro views provides probabilistic framing that consensus forecasts structurally cannot offer.
