Goldman Sachs found 40% of $500K+ earners live paycheck to paycheck, with less than a third consistently saving. This has shifted from rare cautionary tales (athletes, celebrities) to the majority of high earners. The cause isn't declining financial literacy but an economy increasingly optimized to capture high-income spending.

Luxury has been democratized through financing: premium cars, private school, high-end real estate, and exclusive experiences are now accessible through leverage rather than savings. Status competition in high-income professions means spending signals professional success. The industries targeting high earners have become more sophisticated at capturing income than the earners are at retaining it.
