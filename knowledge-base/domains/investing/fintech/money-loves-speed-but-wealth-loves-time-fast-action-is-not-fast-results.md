Srivatsaa contrasts his own path (flipping 100 homes in 5 years for cash) with a friend who bought one property, then a fourplex, then a 20-unit complex. After 5 years, the friend's net worth was 5x larger because he was working on time while Srivatsaa was working on speed. Fast action is not equal to fast results.

Speed means shortening the gap between seeing and acting on an opportunity. Time means making a good decision and allowing compounding to work. Berkshire Hathaway's track record is the proof: 20% annual compounding from 1965-2024, beating the S&P 500 by double, with total returns hitting 5 million percent. Most people confuse fast action with fast results. The best partner in wealth creation is time — but time only works if you got the initial decision right. The framework: use speed to seize the right opportunity, then use time to let it compound.
