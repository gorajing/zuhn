Auto loans and vehicle depreciation have created a particularly vicious debt trap. Vehicles lose value rapidly (especially EVs with extreme depreciation), but auto loans are longer than ever (7+ year terms common). The result: millions of borrowers owe more than their vehicle is worth within months of purchase.

Negative equity removes the normal escape valve from bad debt: selling the asset to pay off the loan. A homeowner underwater can at least wait for appreciation. Vehicles only depreciate. The only exits are: continue paying on a depreciating asset, refinance at worse terms, or default. For many borrowers, default has become the rational economic choice.
