Traditional consumer debt metrics (credit card balances, loan delinquencies) miss BNPL entirely because these companies aren't required to report. A consumer with maxed credit cards AND multiple BNPL obligations appears less leveraged in official statistics than they actually are.

This unmeasurability is systemic risk: if we can't accurately measure total consumer leverage, we can't assess systemic risk or design appropriate policy responses. The $500B+ in BNPL debt sits in a regulatory and measurement blind spot that makes the consumer balance sheet look better than it is.
