Democratized Prime runs hourly Dutch auctions where lenders submit minimum acceptable rates and borrowers submit maximum payable rates, with smart contracts clearing at the intersection. This replaces the traditional warehouse lending model where rates are negotiated bilaterally between banks and originators behind closed doors.

The mechanism has several structural advantages: it aggregates distributed price signals in real time, ensures all accepted lenders receive the same clearing rate (eliminating information asymmetry), and provides continuous liquidity windows rather than locked-in terms. The base yield on YLDS (~3.85% via SOFR minus 50bps) means unfilled bids still earn, reducing the opportunity cost of participation. This auction-based approach could become a template for how other credit products — auto loans, student debt, SMB receivables — get priced on-chain.
