BNPL was designed as short-term purchase financing (8-week payoff), but users treat it as revolving credit - a way to make their monthly budget stretch further. Companies aren't required to report to credit agencies or follow traditional lending regulations, creating a shadow credit system of unknown true size.

The regulatory gap is by design: BNPL companies argue they're not making 'loans' but splitting payments. This semantic distinction allows them to avoid credit checks, interest rate disclosures, and reporting requirements that apply to credit cards. When these 'not-loans' default at rising rates, the systemic impact is harder to measure and manage than traditional credit defaults.
