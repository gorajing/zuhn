Srivatsaa distinguishes the two financial pillars: cash flow pays today's bills (salary, revenue, distributions), while equity builds tomorrow's wealth (ownership stakes, real estate, business value). Most people optimize only for cash flow — higher salary, more clients — while neglecting equity accumulation.

McDonald's illustrates perfectly: their burger business generates cash flow, but their real wealth comes from $1.6B in franchise royalties and nearly $45B in real estate. The best way to own equity is to own your business; second best is to own a piece of someone else's (public stocks). The strategic question is: how can you use your cash flow to acquire equity? Either build equity in your own business or systematically convert cash flow into ownership stakes. The trap is chasing cash flow stability and calling it wealth — they're fundamentally different assets.
