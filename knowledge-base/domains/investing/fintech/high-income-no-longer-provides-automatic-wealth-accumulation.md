Historically, earning in the top 1% virtually guaranteed wealth accumulation: even with poor financial decisions, the sheer surplus income would build wealth over time. This is no longer true because the cost of maintaining a top-1% lifestyle has outpaced income growth in that bracket.

Housing in professional centers, private school tuition, healthcare, and the social spending required for career advancement have all escalated faster than high-end incomes. The 'brute force wealth building' threshold has risen above what most six-figure earners actually earn, meaning deliberate financial strategy is now required at every income level.
