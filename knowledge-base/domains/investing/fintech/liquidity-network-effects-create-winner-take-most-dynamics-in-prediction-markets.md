Without sufficient liquidity, small trades cause large price swings and wide bid-ask spreads, degrading the market's core value proposition of accurate price discovery. As volume increases, spreads tighten, signals sharpen, and the market becomes more useful — pulling in additional participants.

This dynamic explains why two architecturally different platforms (centralized Kalshi and decentralized Polymarket) can both grow: they serve different liquidity pools. Polymarket grew through structural advantages — near-instant settlement, global access without banking relationships, and rapid contract creation. Kalshi leveraged the CFTC ruling to attract institutional capital. The insight for investors is that liquidity network effects in prediction markets mirror those in exchanges and marketplaces — early moats compound, and the market will likely consolidate around a few dominant platforms.
