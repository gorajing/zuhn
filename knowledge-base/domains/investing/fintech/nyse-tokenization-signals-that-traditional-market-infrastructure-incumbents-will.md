NYSE's design is revealing: rather than rebuilding from scratch, ICE is layering blockchain settlement onto its existing Pillar matching engine, with BNY and Citi handling tokenized deposits. The hardest problems — fungibility between tokenized and traditional shares, dividend rights, governance rights, regulatory compliance — are problems incumbents are best positioned to solve. Crypto-native platforms can build the blockchain layer, but they cannot replicate decades of regulatory relationships and institutional trust. Larry Fink's Davos quote ('democratization by reducing fees if we had all investments on a tokenized platform') signals that the investment thesis is fee compression through efficiency, not speculative token appreciation. For investors evaluating the RWA space, the signal is to weight incumbents with existing regulatory moats over pure-play crypto infrastructure plays.
