The standard PE fee structure (2% annual management fee + 20% of gains above hurdle) guarantees substantial revenue regardless of performance. A $10B fund generates $200M annually in management fees before a single investment is made. This makes fundraising (increasing assets under management) more profitable than investing well.

The structural misalignment explains the industry's relentless fundraising: PE firms are incentivized to raise as much capital as possible because management fees scale with fund size. Performance fees (carry) are bonus income on top of already-lucrative base fees. The investor's primary goal (returns) and the manager's primary income source (fees on assets) are fundamentally different motivations.
