Analyzing the Forbes 400, Srivatsaa identifies three paths: earned income (salary), selling a business, or buying and building continuously. The result: zero people made it on salary alone. A few made it by selling (Mark Lore sold Jet.com, Dan Gilbert sold Quicken Loans), but the vast majority — Musk, Bezos, Buffett, Zuckerberg — are buyers and builders. Even Dan Gilbert bought Quicken Loans back and took it public again.

The principle 'he who gives the money has the power' is validated by every major acquisition: Facebook bought Instagram for $1B (now worth $45B+), Google bought YouTube for $1.6B (now core to their business). Buyers unlock value because they control the terms. This doesn't require having all the cash — it requires understanding the strategy. The wealth creation hierarchy is clear: buying and building > selling a business > earned income.
