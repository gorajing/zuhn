The $1,000 newborn investment accounts originated from Brad Gerstner opening custodial accounts for his sons, then his son asking why other kids didn't have the same access. This personal narrative carried the idea from a non-profit (Invest America, 2021) to Michael Dell to the President to federal policy. The $6.25B Dell pledge then triggered a cascade of corporate commitments.

This pattern reveals how large-scale economic policy often depends on a relatable origin story rather than policy analysis. The tax-advantaged structure (tracking diversified US stock index funds, $5K annual contribution limit, conversion to IRA at 18) is sound policy design, but it was the emotional narrative chain—not the policy mechanics—that moved it through political channels and attracted bipartisan support.
