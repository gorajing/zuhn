Tarter clarifies that SETI should really be called SETT — Search for Extraterrestrial Technology — because we have no way to define or detect intelligence at a distance. What we can detect are technosignatures: electromagnetic signals that nature cannot produce but technology routinely does.

In radio, this means frequency compression — power concentrated at a single channel on the radio dial rather than spread across many channels as natural sources produce. In optical, it means time compression — bright pulses lasting nanoseconds or microseconds that nature cannot generate but lasers can. The instruments split incoming signals into many channels or sample very rapidly to detect these compressions. A key challenge is distinguishing genuine signals from interference, requiring careful verification that detected patterns match sidereal motion (the way stars move across the sky). This methodological clarity matters because it defines what a 'detection' would actually mean and what it wouldn't.
