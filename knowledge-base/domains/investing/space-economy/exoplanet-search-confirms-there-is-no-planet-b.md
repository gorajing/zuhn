Despite discovering thousands of exoplanets, no true Earth twin has been found, and even if one were, the distances involved make travel or colonization practically impossible with current or foreseeable technology. Sara Seager, who has devoted her career to finding other Earths, concludes: 'ultimately, there is no Planet B.'

This is not an argument against space exploration but rather a reframing: the primary value of exoplanet science is understanding our own origins and appreciating Earth's extraordinary rarity. The all-the-pieces-fit-together nature of Earth's biosphere is not something we can recreate or relocate to. This has direct implications for how we allocate resources between space exploration and Earth preservation -- both matter, but only one planet can sustain us.
