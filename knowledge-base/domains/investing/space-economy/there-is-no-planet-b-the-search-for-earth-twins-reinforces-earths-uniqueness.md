Seager's conclusion after decades of searching for Earth twins is sobering: there is no planet B. Exoplanets are so far away that reaching them is infeasible with current or foreseeable technology. Even when habitable planets are found, they will likely differ significantly from Earth.

But the search has produced a profound side effect: it has revealed how remarkably well-configured Earth is. All the pieces — atmosphere, oceans, temperature, chemistry — evolved together and fit in ways that are extraordinarily rare. The practical lesson is that the search for extraterrestrial life, rather than providing an escape route, is actually the strongest argument for Earth stewardship. Understanding what makes a planet habitable makes our own planet's fragile habitability impossible to take for granted.
