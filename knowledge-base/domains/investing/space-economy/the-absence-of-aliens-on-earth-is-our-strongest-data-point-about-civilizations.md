Kipping highlights Hart's Fact A as 'the strongest data point we have' in astrobiology: there are no aliens on Earth right now. While we cannot prove a distant exoplanet lacks civilization, we can be highly confident that no alien civilization cohabits our planet. This seemingly modest observation has profound implications.

Even at Voyager-speed travel, the galaxy could have been colonized many times over in its 13 billion year history. Self-replicating Von Neumann probes could accomplish this even more efficiently. Yet clearly this hasn't happened. This means either: civilizations are extremely rare, they never develop the capability or will for aggressive expansion, or there is some consistent barrier that prevents galaxy-wide colonization. This constraint eliminates entire classes of hypotheses about abundant, expansionist alien civilizations.
