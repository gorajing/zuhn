The Artemis program follows a rigorous phased approach: Artemis 1 tested unmanned re-entry, Artemis 2 tests crewed orbit without landing, Artemis 3 attempts first landing in 55 years, Artemis 5 deploys a lunar rover (LTV), Artemis 7 sends a habitable mobility platform (Lunar Cruiser), and Artemis 8 installs a permanent base camp near the South Pole. Each odd-numbered mission delivers a major capability increase.

This pattern — validating safety before adding humans, proving orbit before attempting landing, deploying mobility before establishing permanence — is a masterclass in phased risk management. The South Pole location for the base camp was chosen for two practical reasons: potential water ice in permanently shadowed craters and continuous solar exposure for power generation. The lesson extends beyond space: large infrastructure projects succeed when each phase both delivers standalone value and de-risks subsequent phases.
