Unlike Apollo's single-use capsules, the Orion spacecraft is designed to be returned and reused across multiple Artemis missions. This shift from disposable to reusable space hardware mirrors what SpaceX achieved with rocket boosters but extends it to the crewed vehicle itself. The economic implications are significant: amortizing development costs over many missions makes each subsequent mission dramatically cheaper.

Artemis 1 also reused Space Shuttle engines in the SLS rocket, demonstrating that even legacy hardware can be repurposed into next-generation systems. This reusability principle — building durable, multi-use infrastructure rather than one-shot solutions — is the economic foundation that makes sustained space presence viable. It transforms space from an expedition model (high cost, infrequent trips) to an operations model (amortized cost, regular activity).
