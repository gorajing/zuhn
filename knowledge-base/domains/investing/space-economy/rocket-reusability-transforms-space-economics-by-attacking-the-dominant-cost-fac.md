The Falcon 9's fuel cost of $200,000 is essentially a rounding error on an $80M launch price, meaning the rocket hardware itself accounts for nearly all the cost. This reveals why reusability is transformative: if you can reuse the first stage for dozens of launches, you amortize the dominant cost factor across many flights. SpaceX projected reusability would cut prices to $40M (half current), with a long-term goal of $10M (an 8x further reduction). The broader principle is about identifying and attacking the dominant cost factor rather than optimizing minor ones. Many industries have analogous 'hidden dominant costs' where conventional wisdom focuses optimization efforts on the wrong variable—understanding the actual cost structure breakdown is prerequisite to meaningful disruption.
