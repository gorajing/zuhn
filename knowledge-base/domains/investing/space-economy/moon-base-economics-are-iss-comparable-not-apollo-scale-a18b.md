The video casually assumes 'there's already a moon base that serves as a hub for Mars missions' — burying a massive economic implication. A permanent lunar staging facility transforms space economics from Apollo-style one-shot mission budgets into ISS-style sustained operational spending. This means recurring revenue streams for suppliers, long-duration contracts, and infrastructure that justifies private investment because it has ongoing utility beyond any single mission. For space economy investors, the signal is that the path to Mars runs through lunar infrastructure, and lunar infrastructure investment profiles look more like building airports than launching rockets — high upfront capital, but decades of operational revenue. The companies positioned to build and supply sustained lunar operations have a fundamentally different business model than traditional launch providers.
