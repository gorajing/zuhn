Recent dating places the emergence of life on Earth at 4.2 billion years ago, just 200 million years after oceans formed at 4.4 billion years ago. Kipping argues this rapid start is genuinely significant: even accounting for the anthropic argument that complex life needs roughly 4 billion years to evolve (forcing an early start), 200 million years is so short it overwhelms that reasoning.

This represents what Kipping calls the first strong (though not definitive) evidence that life is an easy process under Earth-like conditions. The implication is that simple microbial life could be quite common in the universe — assuming Earth-like conditions are not vanishingly rare. However, the gap between simple life and intelligent civilization remains enormous, with Earth taking 4 billion years to bridge it.
