The cost per pound to orbit is dropping exponentially as a side effect of SpaceX's success, creating dynamics similar to Moore's Law in semiconductors. This declining cost is enabling entirely new categories of space startups, just as cheap transistors enabled new computing applications. YC is actively seeking space startups as a result.

Michael's provocative take: people in the space industry who say 'we finally have cheap launch' are wrong -- we'll look back at this era and think it was still expensive. He draws an iPhone analogy: if Starlink is the iPhone moment for space apps, Starship may be the real iPhone moment. However, space companies face a practical constraint: SpaceX itself competes for its own launch capacity (Starlink missions bump paying customers), creating a platform-monopoly dynamic where the infrastructure provider is also its own biggest customer.
