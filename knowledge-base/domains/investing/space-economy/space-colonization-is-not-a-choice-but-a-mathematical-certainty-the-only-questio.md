Bezos's space vision is rooted in a simple energetic argument: civilization benefits from growing energy usage per capita (antibiotics, literacy, lower infant mortality all correlate with energy access), but this is incompatible with living on a finite planet long-term. The solution is moving heavy industry to space while preserving Earth as a pristine gem.

His specific vision: a trillion humans in the solar system, living in giant O'Neill-style space colonies made from asteroid and lunar materials. This would mean a thousand Mozarts and a thousand Einsteins alive at any time. Planetary surfaces are too small — the future is space stations. Most people initially will live near Earth, visiting it like Yellowstone National Park. The key insight for investors: 'you can argue about when you have to do that, but you can't credibly argue about whether you have to do that.'
