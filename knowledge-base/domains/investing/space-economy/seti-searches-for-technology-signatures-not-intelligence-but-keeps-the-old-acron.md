SETI's name is a misnomer that Tarter openly acknowledges. We have no way to define intelligence rigorously, let alone detect it at interstellar distances. What we actually search for are technosignatures: electromagnetic signals that nature cannot produce but technology routinely does.

In radio, this means frequency compression — power concentrated at a single channel rather than spread across many. In optical, it means time compression — nanosecond or microsecond bright pulses that only lasers can produce. The search then becomes a signal-processing challenge: distinguishing these engineered patterns from terrestrial interference (satellites, equipment noise) using techniques like dual-telescope verification separated by hundreds of kilometers.
