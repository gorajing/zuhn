Carlson identifies a general-purpose heuristic: when someone pushing a major initiative refuses to define what success looks like precisely, deception is almost certainly involved. He applies this to Ukraine war policy ('victory was never defined precisely') but the principle is universal.

In business contexts, vague success metrics serve the same function: they allow the narrative to be retroactively adjusted to whatever outcome occurs. When a startup says they're 'winning' without defining what winning means in measurable terms, or when a product team claims success without precise KPIs, the same dynamic applies. The antidote is insisting on precise definitions upfront, even when -- especially when -- the other party resists providing them.
