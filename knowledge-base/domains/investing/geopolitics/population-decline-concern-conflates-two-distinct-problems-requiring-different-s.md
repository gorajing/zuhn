There's a critical distinction between two framings of population decline. One is genuinely concerned about sustainability: who funds pensions, who provides eldercare, how does an economy adapt to fewer workers? The other is concerned about labor supply: maintaining a stream of available workers to keep wages low.

These require opposite solutions. The sustainability concern calls for automation, immigration, and restructured pension systems. The labor supply concern wants more births to maintain a labor surplus that keeps wages low. Conflating these under 'population crisis' rhetoric allows corporate interests to hijack genuinely important demographic policy discussions.
