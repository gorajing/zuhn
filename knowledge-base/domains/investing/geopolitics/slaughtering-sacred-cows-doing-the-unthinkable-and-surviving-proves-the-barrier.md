Kushner describes how the Abraham Accords became possible only after Trump systematically violated diplomatic 'sacred cows' that everyone said would be catastrophic: recognizing Jerusalem as Israel's capital, moving the embassy, recognizing the Golan Heights. Each time, the predicted catastrophe didn't materialize — 'the next morning the sun rose, the next evening the sun set.'

This reveals a negotiation pattern: many apparently immovable barriers in complex negotiations are actually preference falsification maintained by everyone's fear of being first to challenge them. Once someone does challenge them and nothing happens, the entire framework shifts. For investors and strategists: identify which 'impossible' constraints in a system are genuinely structural versus merely conventional. The ones that are conventional can be challenged — and doing so creates massive opportunity.
