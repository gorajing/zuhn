While the US strategic goal is to have Middle Eastern countries bear primary regional responsibility, Iran represents a persistent wildcard. The risk of Iran acquiring nuclear weapons extends beyond Iran itself — the concern is proliferation to Hezbollah and the Houthis.

Israel perceives an existential threat and believes it must resolve the Iran situation while it still has relative strength, creating a timing pressure that can drag the US back into regional engagement despite its preference to pivot toward Asia. This tension between strategic withdrawal and emergency intervention defines the current Middle East dynamic.
