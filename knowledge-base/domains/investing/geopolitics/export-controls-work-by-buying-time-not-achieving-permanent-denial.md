The article demonstrates that US chip export controls have successfully maintained a 21-49x AI compute advantage over China by slowing domestic chip development — China's 7nm yields remain poor, 5nm is delayed to 2026+, and over 22,000 semiconductor companies have shut down. However, the goal was never permanent denial but rather buying time during which deterrence conditions could improve.

This framing matters because critics attack export controls for failing to permanently stop China, which was never the realistic objective. The strategic value lies in the delay window: if China can be kept from military parity for 5-10 more years, demographic shifts, leadership changes, or allied capability development could reduce conflict risk. This time-buying logic applies broadly to any technology denial strategy.
