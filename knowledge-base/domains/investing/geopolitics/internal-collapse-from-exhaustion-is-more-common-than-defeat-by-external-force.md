The Russian Revolution of 1917 wasn't caused by military defeat — Russian armies were still fighting. It was caused by internal exhaustion: bread shortages, millions of casualties with no end in sight, a incompetent government, and a war-weary population that lost faith in both the war and the regime. The French army mutinies of 1917 showed the same pattern — soldiers didn't defect to the enemy, they simply refused to attack.

In business, most market leaders don't lose to competitors — they collapse internally. Key talent leaves, organizational culture degrades, decision-making calcifies, and the leadership loses connection to reality. The external competitive threat is often secondary to the internal decay. The lesson: monitor internal health metrics (employee morale, talent retention, decision quality, cultural vitality) as carefully as external competitive threats.
