When Bezos, Griffin, Musk, and dozens of other billionaires concentrate in a single metropolitan area, they create a political power center that can influence policy at state and national levels. Their combined economic weight exceeds many countries' GDP.

This concentration has self-reinforcing dynamics: political influence attracts more favorable policy, which attracts more billionaires, which creates more political influence. South Florida is becoming a billionaire company town where wealth concentration shapes everything from real estate to regulation.
