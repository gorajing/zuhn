Rwanda's 29th-place ranking on the World Bank's Ease of Doing Business Index is a masterclass in regulatory signaling. By focusing legislative reform on the ten specific factors the index measures — starting a business, construction permits, electricity access, property registration, credit, investor protection, taxes, cross-border trade, contracts, and insolvency — Rwanda engineered a ranking that places it above countries with 50-100x its GDP per capita.

This creates a powerful asymmetric signal: foreign investors scanning for emerging market opportunities see Rwanda outranking established economies and infer competence and stability. Foreign direct investment grew from $10.5M in 2005 to $293M in 2017, a 28x increase. The insight for investors is that countries explicitly optimizing for rankings indices are making a calculated bet that the signaling value outweighs the opportunity cost of reforms — and this bet appears to pay off disproportionately for the poorest nations where the baseline expectation is worst.
