Noah Smith argues that Pax Americana was not just a projection of American power but a set of self-imposed constraints: the US accepted limitations on military force, eschewed territorial conquest, and treated smaller nations as equals in international bodies. Advocates of multipolarity — leftists, foreign rivals, and detractors — celebrated its decline as a diminution of American power.

What they failed to account for is that removing the normative framework doesn't remove American capability — it removes the restraints on how that capability is used. Trump's threats against Greenland, unilateral strikes on Iran, and alienation of allies demonstrate that an unbound America is more aggressive and unpredictable, not weaker. The multipolar world they wished for arrived, but without the guardrails they assumed would persist.
