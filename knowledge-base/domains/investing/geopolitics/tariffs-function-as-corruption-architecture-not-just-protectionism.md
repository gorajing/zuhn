Goldberg argues that tariffs are historically the biggest driver of political corruption because they force every industry to either seek exemptions or request that competitors be tariffed. This creates a system of 'special pleading' where access to executive power becomes the primary determinant of business success.

This framing recontextualizes tariffs beyond the standard 'bad economics' critique. Rather than merely distorting markets, tariffs construct an architecture of dependency where industries must continuously court political favor. Trump's attraction to tariffs, in this analysis, isn't about protectionism but about creating leverage — a bottleneck he controls that forces supplicants to come to him. This is the same dynamic as a mob boss controlling access to a market.
