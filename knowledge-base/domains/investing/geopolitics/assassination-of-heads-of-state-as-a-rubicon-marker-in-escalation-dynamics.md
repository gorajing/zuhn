Smith identifies the Israeli strike killing Ayatollah Khamenei as qualitatively different from previous military actions against Iran. Prior strikes — even significant ones — could theoretically be absorbed and followed by diplomatic de-escalation. Eliminating a head of state crosses a threshold that makes return to the status quo structurally impossible.

This is an important pattern in escalation theory: certain actions function as one-way doors regardless of the actor's stated intentions. Whether Trump intended this as a serious geopolitical commitment or stumbled into it, the consequences are path-dependent. For forecasting and risk assessment, identifying which actions are reversible versus which cross Rubicon thresholds is critical for understanding where conflicts are actually heading versus where participants claim they're heading.
