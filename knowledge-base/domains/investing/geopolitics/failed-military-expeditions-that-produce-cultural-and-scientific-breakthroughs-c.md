Napoleon brought 160 scientists to Egypt alongside his army. The military expedition failed — the army was stranded and eventually surrendered. But the scientific expedition produced extraordinary results: the Description de l'Egypte (a comprehensive survey of Egyptian civilization), the discovery of the Rosetta Stone, and the founding of modern Egyptology. The venture that failed militarily succeeded intellectually.

In business, failed ventures that produce valuable secondary outputs — talent development, technology insights, market knowledge, intellectual property — can be net positive over longer time horizons. Google's failed social networking attempts informed their successful messaging products. Amazon's failed phone informed their successful Echo. The key is capturing and redeploying the secondary outputs rather than writing off the entire venture as a loss.
