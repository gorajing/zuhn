Dr. Kim identifies three foreign policy camps within the Republican Party: Reagan-era world police, MAGA non-interventionism, and selective engagement (Elbridge Colby's approach). She places Trump squarely in the third camp.

The strategy is consistent across administrations — Obama, Trump 1.0, and Biden all wanted Middle Eastern countries to take primary regional responsibility. Trump simply executes this more visibly. The goal is freeing resources to project power in the Indo-Pacific, where the US most wants 'nothing to happen' — which requires credible deterrence against China.
