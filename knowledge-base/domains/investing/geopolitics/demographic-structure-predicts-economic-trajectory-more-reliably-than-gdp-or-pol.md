While individual behavior is unpredictable, demographic groups behave with remarkable consistency. The number of 18-year-olds in 30 years is already determined by births today — no country has figured out how to 'manufacture' them. This makes population pyramids one of the most powerful forecasting tools available.

For investors and strategists, this means demographic data should be weighted heavily alongside traditional economic indicators. China's current massive economic growth is partly explained by two large population bulges currently in peak spending and peak earning years — but those same bulges will create an enormous retiree burden within decades. Countries with similar inverted pyramids (Japan, Italy, Germany) offer preview cases of the economic consequences.
