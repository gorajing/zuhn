The UN described the 2022 developing-world crisis as a 'perfect storm' — three massive shocks arriving simultaneously. COVID eliminated tourism revenue and remittances (the buffers against trade deficits). Fed interest rate hikes increased the cost of servicing existing debt (forcing countries to choose between debt payments and domestic spending). The Ukraine war spiked food and energy prices (increasing the cost of essential imports precisely when foreign reserves were depleted).

Each shock individually would have been manageable. But each one also eliminated the buffer that would have absorbed the others. This non-linear compounding is a critical concept for risk analysis: the probability of three simultaneous shocks may be low, but the damage function is exponential rather than additive. Pakistan was identified as a potential next domino, with the added dimension of being a nuclear state — illustrating how economic fragility can cascade into geopolitical instability.
