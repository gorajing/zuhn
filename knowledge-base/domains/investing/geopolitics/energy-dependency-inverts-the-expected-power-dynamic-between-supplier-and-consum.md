The video makes a striking empirical point: Germany's muted criticism of Russia versus the UK's willingness to confront it maps almost perfectly onto gas dependency levels. Countries like Bulgaria, Lithuania, Latvia, Estonia, and Finland are nearly 100% dependent on Russian gas. This dependency functions as a political leash—the supplier doesn't need to threaten military action when they can threaten economic devastation by closing a valve. For investors and strategists, this pattern generalizes: whenever a single supplier controls 40%+ of a critical input (energy, rare earths, semiconductors), the consumer's foreign policy becomes structurally constrained. The US attempt to export LNG to Europe is explicitly a strategy to break this leverage, even at higher cost—showing that nations will pay a premium to escape dependency-based political control.
