The American system of employment-tied healthcare means that every economic disruption - layoffs, gig economy transitions, career changes - creates healthcare gaps. During the exact periods when people face the most stress (job loss, financial uncertainty), they also lose medical coverage.

This creates a mortality risk multiplier: economic downturns don't just cause financial hardship, they directly cause deaths through healthcare access interruption. In countries with universal healthcare, economic recessions don't produce the same mortality spikes because medical access is decoupled from employment status.
