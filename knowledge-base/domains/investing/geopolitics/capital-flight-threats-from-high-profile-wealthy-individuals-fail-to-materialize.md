When Massachusetts passed the Fair Share Amendment in 2022 (4% surtax on income over $1M), spokespeople for the wealthy predicted a mass exodus to Florida. In the two years after passage, the number of Massachusetts millionaires actually grew by close to 40%. The revealed preference of billionaires like Jensen Huang is consistent with this: when asked about the California wealth tax, Huang said he hadn't thought about it even once because Silicon Valley is where the talent pool lives. The mechanism: ultra-wealthy individuals are anchored to place-specific agglomerations (talent, suppliers, networks, universities) whose replacement cost dwarfs any realistic tax delta. High-profile 'I'm leaving' statements are negotiating theater, not revealed preference. For forecasting tax-policy effects, the priors should strongly favor 'the threatened exodus does not happen' — and by extension, models that treat capital-flight elasticities as large are systematically overcalibrated on headline risk.
