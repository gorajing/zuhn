The American Revolution followed a textbook escalation spiral: Britain imposed taxes, colonists protested, Britain imposed harsher measures, colonists organized resistance, Britain sent troops, colonists formed militias. At each stage, moderates on both sides lost influence to hardliners. By 1775, the accumulated grievances and commitments on both sides made compromise politically impossible for either party.

In business relationships, escalation spirals between companies and their partners, employees, or regulators follow the same pattern: each aggressive action triggers a proportional response that escalates the conflict. Employment disputes that start with a performance review and end in lawsuits, vendor negotiations that start with a pricing discussion and end in court, regulatory interactions that start with a request and end in enforcement actions. The key is to recognize and interrupt the escalation cycle before positions harden beyond compromise.
