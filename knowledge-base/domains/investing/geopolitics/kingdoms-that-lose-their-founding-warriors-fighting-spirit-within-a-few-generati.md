The Vandals who conquered North Africa were formidable Germanic warriors. But a century of comfortable Mediterranean living — Roman baths, villas, easy wealth — transformed their descendants into an ineffective military force. When Belisarius arrived with 15,000 professional Byzantine soldiers, the Vandal army of 80,000+ collapsed quickly because its fighting spirit and discipline had atrophied through generations of comfort.

This is the innovator's dilemma applied to martial capability: the generation that conquers (or founds a company) has the hunger, resilience, and skill that comes from fighting for survival. Subsequent generations inherit the rewards without the struggle. By the third generation, the competitive edge is gone. This pattern affects family businesses, inherited wealth, and corporate cultures — the founding generation's advantages erode as comfort replaces hunger.
