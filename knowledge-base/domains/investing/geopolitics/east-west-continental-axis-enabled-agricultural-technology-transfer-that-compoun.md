Eurasia's east-west orientation meant that domesticated plants and animals could spread thousands of miles across similar climates without requiring re-domestication. The four largest early empires (Qing, Abbasid, Umayyad, Mongol) all expanded horizontally along this axis. Vertically oriented continents like the Americas forced civilizations to re-domesticate from scratch when crossing climate zones, severely limiting expansion speed. This geographic lottery compounded over millennia — the agricultural surplus enabled specialization, which enabled technology, which enabled colonization. For modern investors evaluating emerging markets, this reminds us that structural geographic advantages (trade routes, climate corridors, resource access) compound over centuries and shouldn't be dismissed as historical accidents.
