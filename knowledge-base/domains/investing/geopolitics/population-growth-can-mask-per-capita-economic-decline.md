Canada demonstrates how aggregate economic metrics mislead: GDP grew because population grew through high immigration, but GDP per capita didn't improve for a decade. This creates a political problem where leaders cite GDP growth while citizens experience stagnation or decline.

The population growth also intensifies housing pressure, further eroding living standards. More people competing for a limited housing stock in desirable cities drives prices beyond affordability. The 'solution' of population growth to support economic growth actually worsens the per-capita experience.
