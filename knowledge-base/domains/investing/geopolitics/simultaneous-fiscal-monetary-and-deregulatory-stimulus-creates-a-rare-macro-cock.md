David Solomon describes a rare convergence of four stimulative forces: (1) continuing fiscal stimulus amplified by new legislation, (2) monetary stimulus from a rate-cutting cycle, (3) a capital investment super cycle where the four largest companies contributed 1% to GDP growth with $400 billion in spending, and (4) a deregulatory unwind from the previous administration's regulatory surge.

This cocktail makes it very hard to slow the economy. While average Americans feel the 25-30% cumulative price increases, the financial leverage keeps the economy resilient. For owners of monetary assets, investable assets, or anything attached to growth and technology, this is a prime environment. The main risk is geopolitical — the shift to a multipolar world creates higher probability of disruptions than the past 30 years since the Berlin Wall fell.
