Alexander's conquests extended across multiple cultures: Greek, Persian, Central Asian, Indian. Each step further from Macedonia increased the difficulty of governance, the resistance of local populations, and the homesickness of his troops. At the Hyphasis River in India, his veteran army simply refused to march further. The cultural distance between Greek Macedonia and the Indian subcontinent was too great for any one organizational system to span.

In business, cultural distance creates the same expansion ceiling: companies that expand across cultural boundaries (Western tech companies in Asia, consumer brands across very different markets) face exponentially increasing complexity. What works in the home market doesn't just need translation — it needs fundamental rethinking. The further from your cultural base, the more your assumptions become liabilities. This is why 'going global' is orders of magnitude harder than 'going national.'
