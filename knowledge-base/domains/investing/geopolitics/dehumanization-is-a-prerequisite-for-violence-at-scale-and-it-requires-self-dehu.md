Youssef cites Holocaust survivor Maior Meyer's insight from Auschwitz: 'In order for a group of people to dehumanize another group, they need first to dehumanize themselves.' This framework explains how ordinary people participate in atrocities — the psychological cost of dehumanizing others requires numbing your own humanity first.

Youssef extends this to modern technology: remote killing via drones, AI-generated target lists, biometric surveillance — each layer of technology between the human and the act of killing reduces the psychological barrier. He draws an analogy to dating apps: swiping left and right makes human connections feel disposable, just as pushing a button to launch a strike makes human life feel disposable. The insight is that technological mediation doesn't just enable violence at scale — it actively facilitates the self-dehumanization that Meyer identified as the prerequisite.
