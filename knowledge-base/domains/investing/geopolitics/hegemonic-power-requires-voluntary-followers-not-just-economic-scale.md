Dr. Kim makes a crucial distinction: hegemonic power is 'not something that happens alone — someone around you has to make it for you.' While many discuss the Global South's alignment with China, she notes the depth of these relationships is shallow — they are options, not commitments.

True hegemony requires sacrifice and public goods provision. The US built its post-WWII order by creating systems others could benefit from. China, under Xi Jinping, revealed its intentions too early (particularly through Hong Kong), alarming potential followers like Taiwan. A country that doesn't show up during major events or offer genuine partnership cannot sustain hegemonic leadership regardless of GDP.
