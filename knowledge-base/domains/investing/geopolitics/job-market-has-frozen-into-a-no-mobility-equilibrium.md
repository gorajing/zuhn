The job market has entered a frozen state: low hiring, low quitting, low internal mobility. This appears stable but is actually fragile. Workers stay in suboptimal positions because alternatives have disappeared. Companies maintain staff because replacement is uncertain. Nobody advances, innovates, or takes risks.

The danger of a frozen market is that it masks deterioration. Workers losing real income to inflation without mobility to find better compensation. Skills atrophying in static roles. Innovation stalling as people afraid to leave cushy positions. When the freeze eventually thaws, the pent-up transition could be chaotic.
