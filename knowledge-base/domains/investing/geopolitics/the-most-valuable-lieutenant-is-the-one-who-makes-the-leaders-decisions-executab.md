Marshal Berthier was neither a great strategist nor a brilliant battlefield commander. But he was the indispensable translator between Napoleon's mind and the army's execution. He converted Napoleon's rapid-fire ideas into precise written orders, managed the movement of 200,000+ men across a continent, and ensured the logistics worked. When Berthier was absent (as at Waterloo), the army's execution quality noticeably degraded.

In business, this is the COO/chief of staff role: the person who makes the CEO's vision operationally real. These operators are chronically undervalued compared to revenue-generating or product roles, but their absence immediately degrades organizational performance. The lesson: great strategy without great operational translation is just ideas. The person who converts strategy into executable process is often more valuable than the person who creates the strategy.
