When a16z launched in 2009 in the wake of the financial crisis, they faced widespread criticism: 'Why are you raising money now? What are you stupid?' The answer proved to be that raising capital when nobody else has it is structurally advantageous — less competition for deals, better pricing, and founders who are more serious about building.

This principle extends beyond venture capital. The natural human tendency is to invest when markets are hot and retreat when they're cold, but the best opportunities emerge during capital scarcity. a16z went from that contrarian start to capturing 18.3% of all venture capital raised in the US in 2025 — from an upstart to the largest player in the industry.
