Sellers of Russian crude have resumed requesting yuan payment from Indian state refiners as sanctions pressure and counterparty risk rise. This routing had appeared before but lapsed — its return is pragmatic, driven by the need to clear sanctioned barrels rather than a strategic push for yuan dominance. China's currency still handles only ~4% of international payments by value versus ~50% for the dollar.

The pattern matters because pragmatic adoption can be more durable than ideological campaigns. Each sanctions regime that forces parties into alternative settlement currencies builds infrastructure, relationships, and muscle memory for non-dollar transactions. The India-China relationship's instability limits this particular channel, but the broader trend of sanctions creating workarounds that slowly build parallel financial plumbing is a structural force that compounds over years regardless of any single bilateral relationship.
