The 'supply chain risk' designation was designed to protect against foreign espionage threats like Huawei. Using it as a bargaining chip in contract negotiations with a domestic company is what Alexander calls 'insane Third World bullshit' — it transforms a legitimate security tool into an instrument of arbitrary economic destruction with no legal review.

The chilling effect extends far beyond Anthropic: every company considering Pentagon contracts must now price in the risk that the government will unilaterally renegotiate terms and threaten corporate destruction if refused. This makes future defense contracting more expensive, less competitive, and concentrated among companies willing to accept any terms — precisely the opposite of what produces good technology.
