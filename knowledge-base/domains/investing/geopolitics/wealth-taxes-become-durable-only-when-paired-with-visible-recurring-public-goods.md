Massachusetts' 2022 Fair Share Amendment was hypothecated to universal free school meals, tuition-free community college, and transit improvements. A principal interviewed said he now 'breathes a sigh of relief' each year the tax is reaffirmed — because the services have become normal expectations. This is Olson's logic of collective action applied to tax policy: concentrated losers (billionaires) have strong incentive to organize against, while diffuse winners (millions of families benefiting from free meals) have weak incentive unless the benefit is visible and memorable. Hypothecation to recurring, tangible services creates that memorability, building a defense coalition that persists across election cycles. Taxes whose revenue disappears into a general fund lack this defense and are politically fragile. Implication for wealth-tax design: the revenue destination is not separable from the tax's survival probability. Massachusetts also needed five failed attempts over decades before success, suggesting the political construction of a durable beneficiary coalition takes multiple election cycles.
