Huberman draws an analogy to how auto credit democratized nice cars: GLP-1 drugs are democratizing healthy body weight. Retatrutide (GLP-3) passed Phase 3 at Eli Lilly with up to a third of body weight loss and some degree of muscle sparing, while bypassing side effects of earlier GLPs. But the real disruption is distribution: compounding pharmacies and gray market sources already sell it at much lower doses and costs than pharma pricing.

Lilly is fighting to prevent compounding pharmacies from selling retatrutide, but Huberman notes there's been no major adverse event from compounding sources, making enforcement difficult. People have also discovered that much lower doses than prescribed are effective, including novel uses like reducing alcohol cravings and 'cognitive noise.'

The investment angle is dual: (1) pharma companies face margin compression as gray market distribution expands, and (2) the societal impact of near-eradication of obesity is massive — healthcare cost reduction, insurance model changes, food industry disruption. Huberman's prediction that over 50% of Americans will be on GLPs within 5 years represents one of the largest shifts in consumer health behavior in history.
