Both Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan pursued territorial expansion primarily to secure food and raw materials, not just to fulfill ideological ambitions. Germany's Lebensraum doctrine and Japan's Greater East Asia Co-Prosperity Sphere were fundamentally resource-acquisition strategies dressed in ideological language.

This pattern recurs throughout history and remains relevant for geopolitical analysis today. When evaluating state behavior, tracking resource dependencies (food imports, energy sources, critical minerals) often provides better predictive signal than analyzing official rhetoric or ideology. Modern parallels include China's Belt and Road Initiative securing supply chains and Russia's leverage of energy exports. For investors, monitoring resource chokepoints and food security metrics offers early warning of geopolitical escalation.
