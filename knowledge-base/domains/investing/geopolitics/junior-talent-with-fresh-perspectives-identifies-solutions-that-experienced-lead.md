The experienced generals at Toulon thought in terms of their training: infantry assault, frontal attack, siege warfare. Napoleon, trained as an artillerist, thought in terms of fire positions and indirect effects. His different professional background gave him a completely different analytical lens that revealed solutions invisible to the infantry-trained generals.

In business, this is why cross-functional hires and outside perspectives are valuable: someone from a different industry, discipline, or background sees your problem through a different analytical framework. The solution to a marketing problem might be obvious to an engineer, and vice versa. The most valuable thing a junior employee can offer is not experience but a perspective unconditioned by institutional assumptions.
