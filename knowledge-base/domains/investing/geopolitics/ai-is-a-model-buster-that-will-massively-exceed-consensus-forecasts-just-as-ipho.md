David George introduces the 'model buster' concept: companies that grow faster and longer than anyone would have modeled in any scenario. The iPhone is the canonical example — consensus models for Apple were off by 3x over four years despite Apple being the most extensively covered public company at the time. He argues AI will produce many such model busters.

Supporting data: OpenAI and Anthropic combined added almost half as much net new revenue in 2025 as the entire public software industry ($46B). By 2026, AI model companies are projected to add 75-80% as much new revenue as the entire public software industry including SAP and legacy vendors. Current total AI revenue is roughly $50B but growing well over 100% YoY.

The investment framework: value concentrates in outliers following power laws. The top 10 unicorns comprise 40% of the total $5.5T unicorn valuation (doubled since 2020). The average lifespan of S&P 500 companies has declined 40% over 50 years, meaning disruption happens faster each cycle. For investors, this means the winners will dramatically outperform any reasonable model, while the losers will be disrupted faster than historical precedent suggests.
