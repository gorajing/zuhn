Because America buys far more from China than it sells, container ships return to China nearly empty, making eastbound trans-Pacific shipping rates a fraction of westbound rates. This backhaul economics made it viable to ship essentially zero-value MRF Residual Bales 8,000 miles across the Pacific.

An entire global industry—worth billions—was built on this logistics anomaly rather than on fundamental economics. When China banned imports, there was no alternative destination with the same trifecta (cheap shipping + high demand + low wages), and the system collapsed. The lesson: any business model whose viability depends on a logistics subsidy from an unrelated trade imbalance is fragile. The dependency is hidden because it looks like 'cheap shipping' rather than a structural anomaly that could reverse.
