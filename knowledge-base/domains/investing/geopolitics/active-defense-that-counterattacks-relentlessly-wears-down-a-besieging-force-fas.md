Rather than passively defending Rome's walls, Belisarius launched daily cavalry sorties against the Gothic siege lines. These raids inflicted steady casualties, disrupted supply operations, and demoralized the attackers. The Goths could never settle into a comfortable siege because they were constantly under threat. Eventually, the cumulative attrition and psychological effect of ceaseless counterattacks caused the Gothic army to withdraw.

In business, this is the counterattack defense: when a competitor attacks your market, responding with aggressive moves in their market (or their supply chain, talent pool, or partner network) rather than just defending your position. Active defense that imposes costs on the attacker is more effective than passive defense that only absorbs attacks. Companies that just play defense lose slowly; companies that counterattack force the aggressor to divide their resources.
