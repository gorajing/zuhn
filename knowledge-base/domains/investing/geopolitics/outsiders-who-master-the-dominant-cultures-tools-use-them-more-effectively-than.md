Napoleon was Corsican, spoke French with an accent, and was bullied as an outsider at French military school. This outsider status drove him to master French culture deliberately: he read voraciously, studied military science obsessively, and analyzed French politics with the detachment of an observer. Native French officers absorbed these things passively; Napoleon studied them actively. The result was a more intentional, systematic mastery.

In business, immigrant founders and cultural outsiders often exhibit the same pattern: they study market dynamics, customer behavior, and business culture deliberately rather than absorbing them unconsciously. This deliberate study produces deeper understanding and more creative application. First-generation immigrants are disproportionately represented among successful founders precisely because their outsider status forces deliberate mastery of systems that insiders take for granted.
