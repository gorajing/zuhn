Jiang's geographic-chokepoint framework: 'Trump didn't realize how vulnerable the global economy is because the Iranians control the Strait of Hormuz. It's only about 33 kilometers across. Iran can sink ships, mine the strait, attack tankers — and instantly disrupt 25% of global oil flow.' The chokepoint inventory: (1) STRAIT OF HORMUZ — 33km between Iran and Oman, ~25% of global oil flows through it. Iran can close it via missile/mining within hours. (2) STRAIT OF MALACCA — between Malaysia/Singapore/Indonesia, 80% of China's energy imports flow through it. The US Navy patrols this. China explicitly building Belt-and-Road routes overland to reduce dependence (China's 'Malacca dilemma'). (3) BOSPHORUS — Turkish strait connecting Black Sea to Mediterranean. All Black Sea grain exports (Russia + Ukraine, ~33% of global wheat) must transit it. (4) SUEZ CANAL — connects Mediterranean to Indian Ocean. Houthi attacks 2024-2025 demonstrated its fragility. (5) PANAMA CANAL — connects Atlantic to Pacific for the Americas. Trump's 2025 threats to retake control. The structural implication: 'whoever controls these chokepoints holds disproportionate leverage' — the leverage is asymmetric because the cost of disrupting traffic is low for the controller but catastrophic for the global economy. Russia threatens grain via Bosphorus/Odessa control → Africa and Middle East starve. Iran threatens oil via Hormuz → global recession. China feared losing Malacca → built Belt-and-Road. The historical pattern: 19th century British naval dominance was built on controlling these chokepoints (Singapore, Gibraltar, Suez, Cape Town). 20th century US naval dominance inherited this control. 21st century is the moment regional powers contest each chokepoint individually. The corollary for investors: monitor chokepoint stability as a leading indicator of commodity-price volatility. Hormuz tension → energy price spikes. Malacca tension → semiconductor supply chain disruption (since Taiwan logistics route through). Bosphorus closure → grain inflation. Each chokepoint has identifiable instruments (oil futures, semiconductor stocks, grain futures) that respond. The corollary for businesses dependent on global supply chains: identify which chokepoints your supply chain depends on. The default assumption that 'shipping works because it's always worked' is breaking down. Build redundancy where you can — multiple sourcing routes, alternative suppliers, inventory buffers. The corollary for understanding seemingly disconnected conflicts: many conflicts that look ideological are really chokepoint-control. Iran war is about Hormuz, not theology. Ukraine war includes Black Sea control. East Asia tensions include Malacca. Reading conflicts through chokepoint lens makes them more predictable.
