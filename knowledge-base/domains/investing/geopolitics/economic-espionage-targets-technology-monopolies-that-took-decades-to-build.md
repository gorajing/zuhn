Jet engine technology represents one of the hardest-to-replicate industrial capabilities, developed primarily during WWII with the US and Europe holding monopolies for decades. China's MSS intelligence apparatus specifically targets these irreplaceable technology gaps through systematic recruitment of students with technical backgrounds and clean travel visas.

The playbook — 'rob, replicate, replace' — reveals that espionage risk is directly proportional to a technology's replication difficulty. For investors and entrepreneurs, this means sectors with deep technical moats (aerospace, semiconductors, advanced materials) face persistent state-level IP theft threats, making trade secret protection a material business risk rather than just a legal concern.
