Because there is no official government definition of a digital US dollar, anyone can create a token branded as USD without any backing requirements. This is digital counterfeiting made trivially easy — the author notes you could launch a fake USD stablecoin in 15 minutes. At the nation-state level, this means China could theoretically create an RMB-backed stablecoin branded as USD, connecting the American brand to Chinese monetary policy.

This is a novel geopolitical attack vector that most people haven't considered. The solution is surprisingly simple — trademark USD in digital contexts and define minimum requirements for using the brand. The asymmetry between the severity of the threat and the simplicity of the defense makes this a high-leverage policy action. For investors and founders, it signals that stablecoin regulation is likely inevitable and will favor projects that already meet these basic standards.
