The pattern is clear: Gulf states pay tens of billions annually for US military protection, yet the US failed to defend Saudi Aramco facilities in 2019 (Houthi attack), then launched strikes on Iran without consulting Gulf allies, and Israel attacked Qatar — a US ally — without consequence. Meanwhile, these same Gulf states invest heavily in US companies (SpaceX, OpenAI).

The response has been systematic: Saudi Arabia signed a security treaty with Pakistan, introduced Chinese language education in schools, gave Xi Jinping a state welcome while giving Biden a cold fist bump, and is the last Arab country to grant China a trade agreement. UAE businessmen openly criticized Trump on social media. The journalist's assessment: if the US doesn't cleanly resolve this war, 'tectonic shifts' in Middle East alignment are coming. This is not a temporary disagreement — it's a structural erosion of the post-WWII US security umbrella in the Gulf.
