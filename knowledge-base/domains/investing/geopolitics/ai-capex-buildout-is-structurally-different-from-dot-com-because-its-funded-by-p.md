David George presents data showing the AI infrastructure buildout differs fundamentally from the dot-com bubble: capex is supported by cash flows (not speculation), capex as a percentage of revenue is considerably lower than dot-com levels, and public market multiples are priced on actual earnings growth rather than loss-making growth. The leading tech companies are described as 'the best businesses in the history of the world' based on their margin improvement trajectories.

The 'no dark GPU' analogy is particularly powerful: during the fiber buildout, dark fiber sat unused for years. Every GPU deployed in data centers gets fully utilized immediately, with even 7-8 year old TPUs running at 100% utilization and secondary market prices for A100s and H100s holding up well.

However, two warning signs merit monitoring: (1) Private credit is beginning to enter the data center buildout — not all counterparties are equally creditworthy. (2) Oracle's pivot to becoming a cloud company has them going cash-flow negative for years with credit default swap costs rising to 2%. The 10% hurdle rate on cumulative ~$5T hyperscaler capex by 2030 requires annual AI revenue hitting roughly $1 trillion (about 1% of global GDP) — ambitious but plausible over a 2030-2040 horizon.
