The AI Index 2026 Chapter 4 documents that China installs more industrial robots than the rest of the world combined, and the gap widened in 2024: 54% of global installations, up from 51.1% in 2023. Global year-over-year growth in industrial robot installations was flat. Several major markets — the United States, Germany, and Italy — saw declines. Taiwan was an exception at +33% YoY. Combined with Chapter 1's finding that China produces 30 notable AI models annually (second only to US at 50) and now leads in publication volume, citations, and patent grants, the strategic picture is that China is not racing the US in AI — it is racing the US in AI and also accumulating the physical automation base to deploy that AI at scale. The US has frontier model leadership by 2.7% and a declining manufacturing base. This is not an analogy to the semiconductor race (where the US conceded fab capacity while retaining design); it is more consequential, because AI deployment at scale requires exactly the industrial robotics, sensor arrays, and physical feedback loops that China is monopolizing.
