Normally when investors sell Treasuries, they redeploy capital into other U.S. assets like stocks or real estate. But when yields rise AND the dollar falls, it means investors are converting dollars to foreign currencies to buy assets abroad. This two-signal pattern is the definitive marker of capital flight versus domestic portfolio rebalancing.

This distinction matters enormously for portfolio positioning. A simple yield spike might mean inflation expectations or Fed policy shifts — both manageable. But the yield-plus-dollar pattern signals a fundamental loss of confidence in the country as an investment destination, which has cascading effects across all domestic asset classes simultaneously.
