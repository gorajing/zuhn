Russia's strategy in 1812 was not to defeat Napoleon in battle but to deny him the decisive engagement he craved. Barclay de Tolly retreated, burning crops and supplies. When Kutuzov finally fought at Borodino, it was to satisfy Russian honor, not strategic necessity. The real war was fought by distance, disease, hunger, and eventually winter. Napoleon won every battle but lost the campaign because the enemy refused to play his game.

In business, competitors who refuse to engage in head-to-head competition and instead play a different game entirely are the most frustrating opponents. The startup that doesn't compete on features but on business model. The company that doesn't match your price but changes the value proposition. The platform that doesn't build competing products but changes the rules of the marketplace. The most effective competitive strategy is often not competing at all in the way the opponent expects.
