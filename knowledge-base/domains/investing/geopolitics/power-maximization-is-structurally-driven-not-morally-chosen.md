Mearsheimer's offensive realism argues that the international system is anarchic — there is no higher authority states can turn to when threatened. In this structure, states must maximize their relative power to ensure survival. This is not a moral choice but a structural imperative: even peaceful democracies behave aggressively when the balance of power demands it.

This framework is critical for understanding geopolitical investment risk. Analyzing a nation's behavior through the lens of structural incentives rather than ideology or leadership personality produces more reliable predictions. China's rise, Russia's actions in Ukraine, and US foreign policy all follow the same structural logic regardless of their political systems.
