Marshal Davout was unique among Napoleon's marshals: he could command an independent force and win against the odds without Napoleon's guidance. At Auerstedt, his single corps (27,000) defeated the main Prussian army (50,000) on the same day Napoleon fought the secondary Prussian force at Jena. This level of independent competence was extraordinarily rare — most marshals needed Napoleon's direct oversight to perform well.

In business, autonomous leaders who deliver results without constant CEO involvement are the rarest form of talent. Most executives need direction, approval, and course correction. Finding people who can independently solve problems, make strategic decisions, and execute without supervision is the most valuable hiring outcome possible. These people are force multipliers — they effectively clone the CEO's strategic capability across multiple fronts.
