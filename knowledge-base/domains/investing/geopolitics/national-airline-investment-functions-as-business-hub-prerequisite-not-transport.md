Singapore Airlines, Emirates, and Qatar Airways are not incidental to their host cities' business hub status — they are prerequisites. Geographic centrality is worthless without actual connectivity, and commercial airlines follow demand rather than create it. A government-backed national airline can artificially create connectivity before organic demand justifies it, bootstrapping the flywheel of business traffic → more routes → more business traffic.

Rwanda's investment in RwandAir — expanding from regional jets to intercontinental routes including London, Brussels, and planned service to Guangzhou and New York — follows this exact playbook. Critics question spending on an airline when average income is $750/year, but the investment thesis is that connectivity is the bottleneck to foreign direct investment, not the other way around. This pattern suggests evaluating emerging market hub plays by their airline strategy as a leading indicator.
