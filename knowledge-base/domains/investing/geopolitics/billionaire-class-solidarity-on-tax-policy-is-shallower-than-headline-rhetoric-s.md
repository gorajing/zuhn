Billionaire-backed opposition PACs project unanimous class opposition to wealth taxes, but revealed preferences are more heterogeneous. Jensen Huang, CEO of Nvidia and one of the wealthiest individuals who would be directly subject to California's wealth tax, publicly said the tax had never crossed his mind and he was 'perfectly fine with it' because he works in Silicon Valley for the talent pool. This is consistent with rational decision-making for founders whose business value depends on geographic agglomeration: a one-time 5% tax is trivially smaller than the value destruction from relocating a company's hiring pipeline. Implication for political economy: 'the billionaires oppose it' is often 'the politically active subset of billionaires whose wealth is already liquid or geographically footloose opposes it.' Founders of agglomeration-dependent operating businesses have different incentives than heirs, finance billionaires, and retirees. Coalitional support for wealth taxes should target this split rather than assume a monolithic opposition.
