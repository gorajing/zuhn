Russia's vast size gave it a unique defensive advantage: it could retreat almost indefinitely, drawing invaders deeper into hostile territory while their supply lines lengthened and their forces attrit. This strategy — trading space for time — defeated both Napoleon (1812) and Hitler (1941-45). The territory itself was the weapon; no tactical genius could overcome the simple mathematics of distance.

In markets, 'strategic depth' takes the form of diversified revenue streams, deep cash reserves, or broad product portfolios that allow a company to absorb attacks in one area while maintaining overall viability. Amazon can absorb losses in new ventures because AWS provides strategic depth. Berkshire Hathaway can hold through market crashes because its diversified operations provide cash flow. The principle: the entity with more space to retreat has the ultimate defensive advantage.
