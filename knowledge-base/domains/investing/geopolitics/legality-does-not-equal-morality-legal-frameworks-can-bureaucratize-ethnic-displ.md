El-Kurd presents a crucial framework for understanding power structures: legal systems are not neutral arbiters of justice but tools that can be shaped by those in power. In the Palestinian context, the judge ruling on home demolitions is himself a settler, and 95% of Palestinian building permit applications are rejected by authorities with explicit political agendas.

The broader insight transcends this specific conflict: throughout history, Jim Crow, apartheid, and various forms of ethnic cleansing have operated through legal mechanisms. When evaluating any legal framework or regulatory system, the critical question is not 'is this legal?' but 'who wrote the law, who enforces it, and whose interests does it serve?' This applies equally to business regulation, technology governance, and international policy.
