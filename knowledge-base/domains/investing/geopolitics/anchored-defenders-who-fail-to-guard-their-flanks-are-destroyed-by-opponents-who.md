The French fleet anchored in a defensive line at Aboukir Bay, with their port (landward) side ungunned because they assumed the shallow water between them and shore was impassable. Nelson's captains found enough depth to sail between the French ships and the shore, attacking from both sides simultaneously. Ships designed to fight on one side were attacked on two, and the fleet was annihilated.

In business, companies that build defenses against the expected direction of competition leave themselves vulnerable to attacks from unexpected directions: retailers who fortified against other retailers were blindsided by e-commerce, cable companies who competed with each other were disrupted by streaming, banks who watched other banks were outflanked by fintech. The most dangerous attack always comes from the direction you assumed was safe.
