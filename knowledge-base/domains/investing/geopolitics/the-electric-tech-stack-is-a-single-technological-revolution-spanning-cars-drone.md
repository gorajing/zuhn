Noah Smith and the a16z analysis he cites argue that lithium-ion batteries, rare-earth electric motors, and power electronics have created a convergent technology stack. An electric vehicle is fundamentally the same components as a drone, a robot, or a data center backup system — just arranged differently. This means that manufacturing scale in one product category (e.g., EVs) directly reduces costs and improves capability in all others.

This convergence explains why Chinese firms like BYD can move from cars to buses to ships to trains, and DJI from drones to cameras to robotics. They aren't diversifying — they're redeploying the same electro-industrial stack. Countries that opt out of one category (like the US retreating from EVs) lose scale economies that cascade across the entire stack, including defense applications like military drones.
