At Arcole, the French attack stalled at a heavily defended bridge. Troops refused to advance. Napoleon grabbed a tricolor flag and personally led the charge across the bridge. Though he was nearly killed (he fell into a ditch and had to be rescued), the gesture electrified his troops. His willingness to die alongside them transformed their psychological state from fear to fanaticism.

In business, founder/CEO intervention at crisis moments — personally calling key customers, working the support lines during outages, leading the product sprint during a make-or-break launch — creates the same psychological transformation. When the team sees the leader sharing the hardest work at the most critical moment, it shifts organizational energy from self-preservation to total commitment. The leader's personal risk at the crisis point is a signal that cannot be faked.
