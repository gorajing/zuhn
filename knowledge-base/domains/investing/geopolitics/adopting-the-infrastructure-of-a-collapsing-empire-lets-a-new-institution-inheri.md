Christianity's spread was inseparable from Roman infrastructure: it traveled along Roman roads, organized around Roman cities, adopted Roman administrative geography (bishoprics matching Roman provinces), and eventually became the Roman state religion. When Rome collapsed, the Church inherited the entire administrative infrastructure, becoming the only institution with continent-wide reach.

In business, this is the 'build on the declining platform' strategy: institutions that embed themselves deeply within a dominant but declining platform's infrastructure can inherit that platform's network effects. Microsoft built on IBM's corporate relationships, Apple built on the music industry's catalog, and new social platforms build on Facebook's social graph. The successor inherits the predecessor's network.
