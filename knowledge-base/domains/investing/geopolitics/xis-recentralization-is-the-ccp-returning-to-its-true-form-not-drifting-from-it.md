Paine is emphatic: don't ever kid yourself that the Communist Party of China doesn't believe in communism. They view the Deng Xiaoping reforms as a modification of economic mechanisms — a pragmatic adjustment — not a renunciation of the underlying political-economic model. Xi is now recentralizing 'right back the way it was.'

She also notes Putin is pulling the same move in Russia, mimicking Soviet-style centralized economic control. The implication is that Western investors and policymakers who assumed the 1990s–2010s liberalization was a one-way ratchet misread the situation entirely. The ratchet was always reversible because the party apparatus never dismantled the tools of control.

Investment implications: assume continued tightening on private capital, tech, and foreign ownership in China; price in regulatory risk as structural rather than cyclical; treat any temporary liberalization signals as tactical rather than directional. The same framework applies to Russia and potentially to any state where a single party retains the full toolkit of centralized control — the option to recentralize is always on the table.
