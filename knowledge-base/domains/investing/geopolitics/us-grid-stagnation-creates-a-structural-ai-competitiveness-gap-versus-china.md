From 1900 to 2000, US grid capacity grew 10x every 30 years. Since then, generation has flatlined at 4,000-4,200 TWh while China surged to roughly 10,000 TWh. This matters because AI data centers are inelastic power consumers—they cannot simply throttle down during shortages.

The implication is that the binding constraint on US AI dominance may shift from semiconductor access to raw electricity availability. While the US focuses on chip export controls and model capabilities, the physical energy substrate is quietly becoming the harder bottleneck to solve, given that major transmission projects take a decade to approve and critical hardware has 2028-2030 delivery queues.
