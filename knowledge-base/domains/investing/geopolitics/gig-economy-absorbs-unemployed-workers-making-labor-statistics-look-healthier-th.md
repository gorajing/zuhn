Bureau of Labor Statistics shows unemployment falling by roughly 3 million over 5 years while Uber driver counts increased by roughly 3 million over the same period. This isn't coincidence - it's the same people moving from 'unemployed' to 'self-employed' in official statistics. The job situation didn't improve; it was reclassified.

This has massive implications for economic policy: if low unemployment is partially an artifact of displaced workers entering gig platforms, the economy is weaker than metrics suggest. Gig income is typically lower, more volatile, and provides no benefits, meaning these 'employed' people are worse off than traditional unemployment statistics imply.
