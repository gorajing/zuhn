Napoleon's Egyptian campaign achieved brilliant tactical success — he conquered Egypt rapidly. But Nelson's destruction of the French fleet at the Battle of the Nile cut off the entire army from France. Napoleon was stranded with no reinforcements, no supplies, and no way home. The military conquest was strategically meaningless because he couldn't maintain the position without sea control.

In business, this is the 'landlocked expansion' problem: entering a market that requires ongoing support from a distribution channel, supply chain, or platform you don't control. If the controlling entity cuts off access, your market position becomes untenable regardless of your product's success. Companies dependent on a single app store, marketplace, or distribution partner face the same vulnerability: success in the market means nothing if your access to that market can be severed.
