Horowitz describes a framework for evaluating GP performance that avoids the classic VC trap of waiting for outputs that take 10-15 years to materialize. Instead, he evaluates 'at the point of attack': how good is the GP at finding opportunities, how good are they at winning those opportunities, and what is the perceived quality at the time of investment.

The logic is that some investments work and some don't regardless of quality, but you can assess whether a GP is winning deals with literally the best entrepreneurs in the world. Winning a deal with someone like Ilya Sutskever means something whether or not that specific company works out. The input quality — the caliber of entrepreneurs attracted and deals won — is a more reliable and timely signal than waiting for the full cycle to play out. This parallels the broader principle of evaluating by leading rather than lagging indicators.
