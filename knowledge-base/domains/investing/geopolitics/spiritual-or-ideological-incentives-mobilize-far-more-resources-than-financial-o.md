Pope Urban offered crusaders something no temporal ruler could: complete remission of all sins — essentially a guaranteed ticket to heaven. This spiritual incentive mobilized not just soldiers but entire families, who sold their possessions and marched thousands of miles at their own expense. No amount of financial payment could have raised this army at this cost; the ideological incentive did what gold could not.

In business, this is why mission-driven companies attract talent at below-market compensation and why open-source communities outproduce commercial teams: purpose-driven motivation scales differently than financial motivation. Companies that genuinely embed meaning into their work — not as marketing but as organizational reality — access a human resource pool that purely financial competitors cannot. The key word is genuinely: manufactured purpose is quickly detected and rejected.
