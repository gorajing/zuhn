Britain and France won WW1, but at what cost? Britain's national debt increased 14x. A generation of young men was killed or traumatized. The British Empire began its terminal decline. France lost 1.4 million dead and vast productive territory was devastated. The 'victors' were too exhausted to enforce the peace, setting the stage for WW2 just 21 years later.

In business, prolonged competitive battles between market leaders often produce the same outcome: the 'winner' of an Uber-Lyft style price war, a Samsung-Apple patent battle, or a streaming wars content-spending race may capture the market but emerge financially and organizationally depleted. The real beneficiary is often a third party who avoided the fight entirely — just as the US and Soviet Union emerged as the true winners of the European exhaustion of WW1 and WW2.
