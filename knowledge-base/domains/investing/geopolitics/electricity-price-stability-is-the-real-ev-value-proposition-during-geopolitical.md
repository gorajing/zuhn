Oil demand is extremely inelastic in the short run — people can't suddenly stop commuting or shipping goods. This means even modest supply disruptions (like 20% of global oil flowing through the Strait of Hormuz) can nearly double prices. Electricity prices, by contrast, are far less volatile because generation sources are diversified and local.

This reframes the EV value proposition from 'cheaper per mile on average' to 'structurally insulated from recurring geopolitical shocks.' At 5 cents per mile vs 12 cents for gas (pre-war), EVs were already cheaper — but the real advantage is that EV owners don't experience the periodic 50%+ cost spikes that gas drivers face every time a conflict erupts near a maritime chokepoint.
