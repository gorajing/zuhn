HMS Victory was one of the most expensive objects Britain ever built. Its maintenance consumed enormous resources. But as Nelson's flagship, it became an irreplaceable symbol of British naval supremacy. Its presence in the fleet inspired British sailors and intimidated opponents. The symbolic value exceeded the material value many times over.

In business, flagship products, offices, or initiatives often cost more than their direct ROI justifies. But as organizational symbols — demonstrating ambition, capability, and seriousness — they generate disproportionate returns in talent attraction, customer confidence, and competitive intimidation. Apple's flagship stores, Google's campus, and SpaceX's starship are modern equivalents: their symbolic value exceeds their direct utility.
