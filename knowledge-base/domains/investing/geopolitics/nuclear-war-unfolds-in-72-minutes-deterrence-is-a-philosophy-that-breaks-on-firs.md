Annie Jacobsen's investigation reveals the terrifying mechanics behind nuclear war. The US has 1,770 deployed nuclear weapons that can launch in 60 seconds. Russia has 1,674 on similar readiness. Satellites detect a launch within fractions of a second, and the president has exactly 6 minutes under 'sole presidential authority' to decide on a counter-strike — with no required approval from Congress, the Secretary of Defense, or anyone else.

The critical insight is that deterrence — the mutual assumption that nobody will launch because it means everyone dies — is a philosophy, not a physical law. Every national security advisor Jacobsen interviewed said it 'could happen.' Once a first launch occurs, there is no 'battle for Moscow' or negotiation — it's push-button warfare that kills hundreds of millions in 72 minutes, followed by nuclear winter killing billions through starvation. Reagan himself called the 6-minute window 'irrational' but could not change it.
