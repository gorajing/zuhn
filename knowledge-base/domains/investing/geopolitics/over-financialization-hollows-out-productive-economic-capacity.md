The UK demonstrates financial Dutch Disease: when one sector (finance) offers outsized returns, investment flows away from manufacturing, technology, and other productive sectors. The finance sector grows by extracting value from existing assets rather than creating new ones.

Privatization accelerated this by converting public infrastructure into financial assets. Rail, utilities, and healthcare became vehicles for financial engineering rather than public services. The short-term efficiency gains privatization promised were consumed by the financial extraction the new ownership structure enabled.
