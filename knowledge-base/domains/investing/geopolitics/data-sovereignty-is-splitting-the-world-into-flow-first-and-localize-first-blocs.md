The AI Index 2026 Chapter 8 documents that data localization measures have increased across nearly every region since 2000, but the distribution is sharply uneven. East Asia and the Pacific have 77 cumulative data localization measures, sub-Saharan Africa 71, Europe and Central Asia 66. Middle East and North Africa 44, Latin America and the Caribbean 36, South Asia 24. North America remains a striking outlier with just 3 measures, reflecting a long-standing 'flow-first' policy orientation. The steepest rise in localization adoption began around 2016, coinciding with GDPR implementation and the subsequent 'Brussels Effect' whereby other nations adopted similar frameworks. The strategic implication is that global data flows are not a neutral technical fact — they are a contested policy variable, and the US/North American 'flow-first' position is increasingly isolated. Any AI product that processes user data globally needs to architect around the assumption that East Asia Pacific, Africa, and Europe will have strict localization requirements while North America will not, creating compliance fragmentation by default. The three-cluster world is not going away.
