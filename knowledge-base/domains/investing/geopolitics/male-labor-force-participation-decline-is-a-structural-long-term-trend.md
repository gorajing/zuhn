Male labor force participation has been declining steadily since the 1960s at approximately 0.1% per month. This predates COVID, social media, and most commonly blamed factors. Over 10 million men are now not working, not studying, not retired, and not institutionalized.

The trend suggests something structural about how the economy rewards male labor has fundamentally shifted. Manufacturing decline, credential inflation, rising disability claims, and the availability of low-cost entertainment all contribute. The pandemic accelerated the trend but didn't cause it, meaning post-pandemic recovery won't reverse it.
