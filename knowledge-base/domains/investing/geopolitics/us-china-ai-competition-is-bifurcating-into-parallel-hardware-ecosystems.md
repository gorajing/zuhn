Multiple signals point to accelerating bifurcation in AI infrastructure: China banned foreign AI chips from state-funded data centers, Nvidia's Jensen Huang warned that China 'will win' the AI race with the US, and rare-earth magnet startups sealed a $1.4 billion deal with the Trump administration to secure domestic supply.

These moves suggest the AI hardware ecosystem is splitting into two self-contained stacks rather than following the globalized semiconductor model of the past few decades. Each side is building redundancy in the other's chokepoints — China reducing dependence on Nvidia chips, the US reducing dependence on Chinese rare earths. For investors, this implies that companies positioned in only one ecosystem face asymmetric risk, while those bridging both (or building stack-independent software) may capture premium valuations.
