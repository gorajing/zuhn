The AI Index 2026 Chapter 8 reports that between 2013 and 2024, the United States invested approximately $20.4 billion in AI-related federal contracts and grants. That is a decade of public investment. In 2025 alone, US private AI investment reached $285.9 billion. The single-year private-to-decade-public ratio is roughly 14 to 1. By comparison, European AI public commitments reached approximately $3.7 billion in contracts from 2013-2024, with UK $1.6B, Germany $505M, France $320M — and recent European spending is accelerating (UK committed $454.4M in 2024 alone, 28% of its decade total). The implication is that US AI direction is essentially a private-sector phenomenon with public-sector rhetoric, while Europe's public spending is a larger fraction of a much smaller total. The strategic consequence: public goods in AI (open evaluations, safety research, academic infrastructure, assistive technologies for underserved populations) will be systematically underprovided in the US market because the public-sector share of spending is too small to shape the direction of the private-sector investment. The Genesis Mission executive order (November 24, 2025) is an attempt to address this but runs against the underlying ratio.
