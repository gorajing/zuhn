    never control countryside, 20-year quagmire ending in withdrawal. (3)
    Vietnam 1955-1975 — jungle, US technological superiority neutralized by
    terrain that hid Vietnamese forces. (4) Korean War 1950-1953 — mountainous
    peninsula, war stalemated. (5) Iran 2025-? — mountains, expected to be
    Afghanistan-class quagmire. The general principle: technological/air
    supremacy works on flat terrain because the defender can't hide. Air
    supremacy is neutralized on mountainous/jungle terrain because the defender
    can disperse, hide, and conduct asymmetric warfare. No amount of US air
    capability changes this physical reality. The corollary for predicting
    conflict outcomes: look at the map first. If you see mountains/jungle,
    expect long protracted asymmetric conflict regardless of relative military
    strength. If you see flat plains/desert, expect short decisive conflict
    where the dominant power wins quickly. The corollary for any country
    thinking about defense: invest in geographic-leverage assets. Mountain
    bases, underground facilities, distributed command structures, multiple
    operating bases. The US itself uses Cheyenne Mountain (NORAD) for this
    reason. The corollary for capital allocators on defense spending: countries
    with mountainous terrain that develop missile capabilities (Iran, North
    Korea, China) get disproportionate strategic deterrence per dollar spent.
    Countries with flat terrain need different defense investments. The
    implication for geopolitical analysis: stop predicting war outcomes from
    military spending ratios. Use topography first.
