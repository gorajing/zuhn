China's relationship with North Korea reveals a counterintuitive power dynamic: the stronger party is actually constrained by its own interests. China's worst-case scenario is a collapsed North Korea flooding 25 million refugees into weak northeastern provinces and advancing US troops to its border. Because China prioritizes stability above all else, it has no reason to deploy its overwhelming leverage, leaving it with limited fine-grained influence. North Korea exploited this same pattern historically by playing the Soviet Union and China against each other - both knew they were being manipulated but preferred it to ceding influence. This generalizes beyond geopolitics: any entity whose primary goal is preserving a status quo becomes vulnerable to exploitation by those willing to threaten disruption.
