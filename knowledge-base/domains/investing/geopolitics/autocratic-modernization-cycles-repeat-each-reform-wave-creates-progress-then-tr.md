Russian history exhibits a clear reform-reaction cycle: Peter the Great modernized, his successors retreated. Catherine expanded and reformed, Paul reversed course. Alexander II freed the serfs, Alexander III cracked down. Each modernizer had to restart because the previous wave's gains were partially rolled back by reactionaries threatened by change.

In business, organizations exhibit the same reform-reaction cycles: a new CEO implements transformative changes, their successor (or the institutional antibodies) roll them back, the next leader must re-reform. Companies that can't lock in institutional gains from each reform wave waste enormous energy cycling between improvement and retrenchment. The key is making reforms structural (embedded in processes, systems, and incentives) rather than personal (dependent on the leader who initiated them).
