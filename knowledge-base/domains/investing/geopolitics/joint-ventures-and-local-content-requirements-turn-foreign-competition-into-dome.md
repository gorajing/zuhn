The article proposes a specific policy toolkit: allow Chinese EVs in with low tariffs and import quotas, but require joint ventures with US companies and tax imported components (especially batteries, motors, and power electronics) used in Chinese-owned US factories. This forces companies like BYD — which is vertically integrated and could easily build US battery plants — to help construct America's Electric Tech Stack rather than just ship finished goods.

This is notably the exact playbook China used to build its own automotive and manufacturing sectors over the past three decades. Europe is now considering similar requirements for Chinese factories. The irony is that the most effective response to Chinese industrial strategy may be to adopt China's own industrial strategy in reverse — using market access as leverage for technology transfer.
