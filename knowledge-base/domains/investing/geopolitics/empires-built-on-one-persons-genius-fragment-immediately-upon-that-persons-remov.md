Alexander conquered the largest empire in history by age 32. When he died at 32, it fragmented within years into four warring successor kingdoms. There were no institutions to maintain unity: no administrative system, no shared governance, no succession plan. The empire was held together by Alexander's personal presence alone. Without him, his generals immediately fought each other for pieces.

This is the ultimate founder-dependency warning: organizations that achieve extraordinary things through a single leader's genius but fail to institutionalize their capabilities face the same instant fragmentation upon founder departure. Companies where the CEO is the strategy, the product vision, and the cultural glue simultaneously have the highest potential upside and the highest succession risk. The test of organizational maturity is whether it can function at 80% capability without its founder.
