Napoleon's Italian campaign was financially self-sustaining: defeated Italian states paid enormous war indemnities, and captured wealth funded the army. He sent millions back to Paris, making him politically invaluable to the Directory. This self-funding model meant he didn't depend on government funding (which was unreliable) and could maintain operational tempo indefinitely.

In business, this is the self-funding growth model: companies that generate enough revenue from each expansion phase to fund the next (bootstrapping, capital-efficient growth) have a structural advantage over those dependent on external funding rounds. The self-funded company maintains operational independence and tempo, while the externally funded company must pause to fundraise and accept governance conditions. Each customer acquisition that funds the next acquisition creates a compounding growth loop.
