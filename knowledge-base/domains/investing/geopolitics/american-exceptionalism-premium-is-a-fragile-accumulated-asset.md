Foreign investors have poured capital into U.S. assets for decades based on institutional stability rooted in winning WWII and the Cold War. This created a self-reinforcing cycle: inflows strengthen the dollar, which attracts more inflows. But trust-based equilibria are inherently fragile — they depend on shared beliefs that can shift suddenly.

The asymmetry between accumulation speed (decades) and potential unwinding speed (months or weeks) is the core risk. During the 2008 crisis, U.S. bonds were still the safe haven despite the crisis originating here. But the April 2025 and January 2026 episodes showed the opposite pattern — U.S.-originated crises driving money OUT of America. This inversion suggests the exceptionalism premium is eroding, and the next crisis could accelerate the shift rather than trigger flight-to-safety.
