The traditional political-economic framework where leaders prioritize job creation is being displaced by affordability politics. Gallup data shows that while 'the economy' tops concern lists generically, inflation and cost of living specifically dominate over unemployment by a wide margin. This shift fundamentally changes what economic success means politically.

For investors and strategists, this means policy actions will increasingly target prices directly — through administrative measures, supply-side interventions, or institutional pressure — rather than the traditional employment-focused stimulus. Understanding this shift is critical because it predicts which sectors face political risk (housing, credit, energy, food) and which unconventional policy levers will be pulled.
