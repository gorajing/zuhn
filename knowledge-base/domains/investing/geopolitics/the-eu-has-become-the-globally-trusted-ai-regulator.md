The AI Index 2026 Chapter 9 reports that globally, the EU is trusted more than the United States or China to regulate AI effectively. Across 25 countries in Pew's 2025 survey, a median 53% said they trust the EU, compared to 37% for the US and 27% for China. Combined with the timeline evidence that the EU AI Act's first prohibitions took effect in February 2025, general-purpose AI obligations in August, and Italy becoming the first EU member state to pass its own national AI law, the EU is accumulating regulatory legitimacy at exactly the moment the US is shedding it. The Brussels Effect — whereby EU rules become de facto global standards because multinational companies comply with the strictest jurisdiction — is already observable in data localization (GDPR-driven) and privacy, and is now extending to AI governance. For product strategy, this means building to EU AI Act compliance gives a product global trust even in markets that haven't adopted the rules, while building to US federal compliance gives it minimal trust even in the US. The regulatory value proposition has flipped geographies.
