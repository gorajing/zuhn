The Rurikid dynasty that founded Russia started as Viking merchants trading along the river routes from the Baltic to Constantinople. They established fortified posts to protect their trade, which attracted settlers, which required governance, which created the first Russian states (Novgorod, Kiev). The state literally grew out of commerce infrastructure.

In the digital economy, the same pattern repeats: platforms that start as marketplaces (eBay, Amazon, Alibaba) create governance infrastructure to protect transactions, which creates a de facto state-like entity with rules, enforcement, and dispute resolution. The platform-as-state analogy is not metaphorical — commerce creates the need for governance, and whoever provides governance captures the value.
