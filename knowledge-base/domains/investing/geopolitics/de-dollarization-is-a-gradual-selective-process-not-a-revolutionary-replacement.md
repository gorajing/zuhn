The BRICS alliance aims to reduce global dollar dependence, but the reality is more nuanced than revolutionary. Russia increased yuan-denominated oil trade with China and reduced Treasury holdings, but primarily under sanction pressure. China promotes yuan oil contracts and trade settlements but selectively, not comprehensively.

The analogy is apt: switching from the dollar is like getting everyone to move from Twitter to Threads -- the network effects are enormous and the infrastructure costs of switching are real. Dollar liquidity (ability to move billions without crashing markets), legal protections, and established contract frameworks create massive inertia. The investment implication: dollar decline is real but operates on a decades-long timeline of gradual erosion, not a sudden regime change. Watch for accelerating signals (more bilateral non-dollar trade agreements, central bank reserve composition shifts) rather than expecting a single breaking event.
