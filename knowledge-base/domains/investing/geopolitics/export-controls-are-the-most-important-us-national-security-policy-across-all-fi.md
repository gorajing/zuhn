Dario makes the strongest possible case for export controls: 'Whoever establishes dominance in this technology will have military and economic dominance everywhere.' The logic chain: Nvidia is ~4 years ahead of Huawei in chip capability. While that gap may close over 10-20 years, the next 10 years are the critical window for establishing AI dominance. During this window, preventing China from building datacenters with millions of frontier chips is both feasible (hard to smuggle at scale) and decisive.

The diffusion rule (Biden-era) divided countries into three tiers. Tier-two countries can host large numbers of US chips if companies provide security affidavits guaranteeing they're not fronts for China. Chinese chips (Huawei) remain significantly inferior, making the alternative less attractive.

Dario also raises a concern that gets less attention: industrial espionage of US AI companies. He notes that some of Anthropic's algorithmic secrets are '$100 million secrets that are a few lines of code' and that Chinese espionage likely targets these. He calls for more government help defending companies against this risk.

This geopolitical framing is essential context for enterprise AI sales — it explains why government and defense sector customers are particularly motivated to buy from US frontier labs like Anthropic.
