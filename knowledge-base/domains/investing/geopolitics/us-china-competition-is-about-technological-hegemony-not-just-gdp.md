Dr. Kim argues that among all forms of hegemony (financial, integration, military), technological hegemony plays the most decisive role. The current battleground is AI and whoever sets the technological standards first gains lasting advantage.

China's prospects have dimmed significantly over the past decade due to demographic decline, questions about the sustainability of its political system, and its inability to attract genuine allies. Unlike the US, which built a post-WWII order that other nations voluntarily joined, China has struggled to make countries say 'I want to go along with China.' The competition will be fierce and protracted, but hegemonic transfer is not instantaneous — we are in the messy middle.
