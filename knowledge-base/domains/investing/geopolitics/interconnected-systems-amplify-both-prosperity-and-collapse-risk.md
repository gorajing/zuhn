The Late Bronze Age Mediterranean operated as a 'small world network' where no civilization was self-sufficient — Egypt supplied gold, Greece silver, Cyprus copper, Afghanistan tin. This interdependence drove unprecedented prosperity but meant that when trade routes were disrupted, the entire system collapsed within decades. The parallel to modern global supply chains is direct: the same integration that enables efficiency creates fragility. This suggests that any assessment of systemic risk must account for the degree of coupling between nodes, not just the health of individual nodes.
