Chamath reflects that focusing on narrow aspects like startup momentum in India was 'necessary but insufficient' for investment success. His 2016 bets on India around UPI launch didn't pay off despite the thesis being correct, because the broader structural conditions weren't yet aligned.

This suggests a framework for emerging market investing: map all structural layers (governance stability, demographic profile, financial infrastructure, physical infrastructure, geopolitical positioning) and assess whether they're converging. A single breakthrough like UPI can be a leading indicator, but returns only materialize when multiple structural pillars align simultaneously. The lesson generalizes beyond India — any emerging market bet should pass a multi-dimensional structural readiness test, not just a momentum check.
