The 'just build more houses' solution fails on composition: record new construction is almost entirely luxury properties because rising costs make affordable housing unprofitable to build. This creates a paradox: hundreds of thousands of luxury units sit empty while an estimated 4 million affordable homes are needed.

Companies raising hundreds of millions to build affordable housing are almost certainly going to fail because the economics don't work at current construction costs. The fundamental problem isn't insufficient construction - it's that the market only incentivizes building housing that the people who need housing can't afford.
