Russia sits between Europe and East Asia—regions that together account for half the world's GDP. By refusing to sign the ICAO Transit Agreement, Russia retains unilateral control over which airlines cross its airspace. This geographic monopoly lets it charge overflight fees estimated at up to $100 per roundtrip ticket. The leverage scales with the economic density on both sides: the more trade and travel these regions generate, the more valuable the chokepoint becomes. This pattern generalizes—any entity controlling the sole efficient path between two high-value nodes can extract rents proportional to the traffic between them, not proportional to any value it creates.
