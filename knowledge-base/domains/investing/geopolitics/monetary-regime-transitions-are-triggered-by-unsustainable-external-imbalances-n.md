The essay reveals a pattern: the 1907 Panic (triggered by a failed copper corner) exposed banking fragmentation and forced the Fed's creation. France's gold redemptions in the 1960s — driven by skepticism about the U.S. balance of payments deficit from Vietnam War spending — forced Nixon to abandon gold convertibility in 1971. In both cases, the existing system wasn't reformed proactively; it broke under external stress.

This pattern suggests the current fiat/Fed regime won't evolve through gradual reform either. Investors should watch for analogous external pressure points — whether from de-dollarization by BRICS nations, cryptocurrency adoption, or sovereign debt sustainability concerns — as signals of the next regime transition. The transition itself is likely to be abrupt and politically framed as a 'shock' even when the underlying pressures are visible years in advance.
