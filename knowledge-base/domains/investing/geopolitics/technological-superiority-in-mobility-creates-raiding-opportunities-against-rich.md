The Viking longship was a revolutionary technology: shallow draft for river navigation, ocean-capable for sea voyages, fast under sail and oar, and beachable on any shore. This mobility meant Vikings could appear without warning at any point along thousands of miles of coastline, raid wealthy but undefended monasteries and towns, and disappear before slow-moving defending armies could respond.

In business, superior speed creates the same raiding opportunities: fast-moving startups can 'raid' the most profitable customer segments of slow incumbents, capturing high-value accounts before the incumbent's sales organization can respond. The startup's advantage isn't better product or lower price — it's speed of engagement. By the time the incumbent realizes what's happening and organizes a response, the raider has moved to the next target.
