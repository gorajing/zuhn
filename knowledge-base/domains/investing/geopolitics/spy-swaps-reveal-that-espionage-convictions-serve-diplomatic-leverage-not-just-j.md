The FBI explicitly wanted to 'set an example' with severe sentences. Yet both convicted spies were ultimately returned to China in a prisoner swap for three Americans held by Beijing. China publicly denied all allegations while privately lobbying for their return. This dual-track behavior — public denial, private negotiation — is the standard playbook.

For geopolitical analysis, espionage cases function as diplomatic currency. The conviction itself is the asset; the sentence length sets the exchange rate. This means high-profile trade secret theft cases should be evaluated not just for their security implications but for their diplomatic leverage value. The pattern also suggests that 'everyone does it' is the operational reality — the asymmetry is in what gets prosecuted, not what gets done.
