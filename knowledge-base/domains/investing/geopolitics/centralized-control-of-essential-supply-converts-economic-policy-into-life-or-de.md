Paine's framing is precise: the commune system didn't just collectivize agriculture, it transferred the life-death decision from weather and local markets to a single political body. The Great Famine was the only nationwide famine in Chinese history because only a centralized policy apparatus can produce a failure synchronized across an entire country — weather-driven famines are always regional.

The generalizable principle: whenever a single entity becomes the sole distributor of something essential (food, information, capital, compute, identity), its routine operational mistakes stop being inconveniences and start being existential events for the dependent. Platform risk in startups (Twitter API lockouts, App Store delistings), single-supplier exposure in hardware, and sovereign dependence on a single reserve currency all have the same topology.

Application: when evaluating a system, count the single points of control over essentials. Each one is a lever where a policy error becomes a mass casualty event — in businesses, in supply chains, or in nations.
