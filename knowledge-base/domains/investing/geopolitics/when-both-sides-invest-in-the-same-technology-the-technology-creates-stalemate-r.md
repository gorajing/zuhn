The Somme exemplified technological stalemate: machine guns made defense lethal, artillery made positions untenable, and barbed wire made movement impossible. Both sides had identical technology, so neither could gain advantage. The result was mutual attrition with no breakthrough — hundreds of thousands of casualties for negligible territorial gains. Technology hadn't created advantage; it had created mutual destruction.

This is the Red Queen effect in business: when all competitors adopt the same technology (AI, cloud computing, data analytics), the technology becomes table stakes rather than competitive advantage. The investment was necessary to avoid falling behind, but it doesn't create differentiation. Companies in technology arms races (ad tech, fintech, social media features) often discover that matching each other's capabilities just raises the cost of competition without improving anyone's position.
