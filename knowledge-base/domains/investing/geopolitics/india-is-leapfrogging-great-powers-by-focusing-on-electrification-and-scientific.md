While the US, China, and Russia inflict self-harm through ideological crusades, military purges, and demographic destruction respectively, India is executing a disciplined growth strategy. Its electronics industry surged nearly sixfold from $22 billion (FY2015) to $130 billion (FY2025), mobile phone production went from 2 million to 300 million units, and solar module production hit 120 GW—a twelvefold increase making India self-sufficient. India's well-cited scientific publications have overtaken the UK, Japan, and Germany.

The strategic logic mirrors China's earlier trajectory: electronics manufacturing capabilities spill over into solar panels, batteries, and EVs because the underlying technologies share more DNA than traditional energy infrastructure. With Sino-American tensions driving supply chain diversification and the OECD projecting India's economy to surpass China's by century's end due to demographic trends, India's domestic market growth creates a self-reinforcing manufacturing advantage. The key insight is that in a period of great-power self-harm, the country that simply focuses on growth and electrification wins by default.
