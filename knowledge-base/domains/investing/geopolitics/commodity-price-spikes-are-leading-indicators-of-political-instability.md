The video overlays 15 years of agriculture, commodity, and grain prices against major geopolitical events. The Arab Spring (2010-2012) coincided with a commodity price spike. The Ukraine war pushed prices even higher — Russia and Ukraine together supply 28% of global wheat and 15% of global fertilizer.

For investors, this creates a two-layer signal: commodity prices predict instability in food-import-dependent nations, and that instability feeds back into further supply disruption as affected countries ban food exports. What the rich world experiences as inconvenience (high gas prices, supply chain friction) translates to existential crisis for developing economies — undoing decades of healthcare, education, and prosperity gains.
