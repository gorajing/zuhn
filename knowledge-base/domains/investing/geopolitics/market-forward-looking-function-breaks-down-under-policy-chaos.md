Markets theoretically aggregate information about future economic conditions into current prices. This function requires participants to be able to form reasonable expectations. When tariffs are announced, cancelled, re-announced, and litigated within weeks, market moves stop reflecting economic fundamentals and instead reflect pure sentiment oscillation.

This breakdown has practical implications: technical analysis, fundamental analysis, and economic models all assume some baseline policy predictability. When that assumption fails, traditional risk management frameworks become unreliable. The most dangerous market environment isn't one with clear bad news - it's one where nobody can form coherent expectations.
