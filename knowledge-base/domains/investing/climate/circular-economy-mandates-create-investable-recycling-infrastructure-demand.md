The EU law mandates 50% lithium recovery from waste batteries by 2027, 63% portable battery collection rates, and minimum recycled content in EV batteries (16% cobalt, 6% lithium, 6% nickel, 85% lead). These aren't aspirational targets — they're legal requirements with compliance deadlines. This creates structural, regulation-guaranteed demand for battery recycling infrastructure, collection logistics, and materials recovery technology. The investment thesis is straightforward: companies building this infrastructure have a guaranteed market backstopped by law. The risk lies in execution — the collection target especially will be hard to enforce at the consumer level, and recycling technology must scale dramatically to hit these numbers.
