The fundamental mismatch between solar generation (daytime) and peak household demand (nighttime) means that without adequate storage, solar cannot displace baseline power generation regardless of how cheap or efficient panels become. Tesla's Powerwall and similar battery systems address this, but their economics depend on the parallel cost curve of lithium-ion batteries — which has been driven down by electric vehicle manufacturing scale. This reveals a cross-industry dependency: solar's viability as a dominant energy source is downstream of the EV industry's ability to drive battery costs down. The broader principle is that in any system with temporal mismatches between supply and demand, the storage layer — not the production layer — determines the economic ceiling.
