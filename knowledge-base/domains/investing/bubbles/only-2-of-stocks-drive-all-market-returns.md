Professor Hendrik Bessembinder at Arizona State University found that approximately 2% of stocks are responsible for all the returns seen over 75 years of market history. Most stocks are up a little, down a little, or flat — they cancel out. A handful get crushed to zero. The remaining tiny percentage of giant winners drive all returns.

This has profound implications for stock pickers: even identifying a promising company is only step one. You must also buy at the right price, hold through extreme volatility, and correctly distinguish temporary setbacks from permanent decline. The Microsoft vs. Intel example illustrates this perfectly — both added to the Dow 25 years ago, yet their performance diverged dramatically. Index investing bypasses this challenge entirely by guaranteeing you own the winners.
