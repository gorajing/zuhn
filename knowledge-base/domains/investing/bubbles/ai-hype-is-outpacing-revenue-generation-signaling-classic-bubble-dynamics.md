As of mid-2024, analysts are flagging a widening gap between the enormous capital flowing into AI infrastructure and applications versus the actual revenue those investments are generating. This mirrors the classic bubble pattern where narrative and expectation run far ahead of business fundamentals.

The practical implication for investors and founders is that companies without a clear, near-term path to revenue will face a painful reckoning when capital becomes more selective. AI companies that can show repeatable monetization and unit economics that improve with scale have a durable advantage over those selling on promise alone.
