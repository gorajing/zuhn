Dr. Ruja walked with two bodyguards, was extremely punctual, and then vanished precisely when the FBI sealed charges against her — suggesting she was tipped off. Her mother was reportedly 'unnerved but not panicked' by the disappearance, suggesting prior awareness or at least unsurprised acceptance. She had pre-booked flights through Vienna to Athens, indicating a planned extraction route rather than a panicked flight.

For investors and regulators, the pattern is actionable: when a founder of a high-risk venture begins escalating personal security, reduces public appearances, and arranges international travel contingencies, these are leading indicators of a planned exit. The combination of heightened security awareness with operational withdrawal from the business is particularly diagnostic — legitimate founders who fear threats increase security AND engagement, while fraudulent founders preparing to flee increase security while reducing exposure to situations that might constrain their mobility.
