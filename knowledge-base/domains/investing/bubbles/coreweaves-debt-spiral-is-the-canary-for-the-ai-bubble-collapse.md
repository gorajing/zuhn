Zitron identifies Coreweave as the key indicator precisely because it's the strongest borrower: backed by Nvidia (who bought $2B in shares), Blackstone, JP Morgan, Goldman Sachs, and others. If Coreweave has trouble raising debt, it signals that Nebius, Nscale, Iron, and every smaller data center company will face the same wall.

The mechanism: Coreweave's revenues depend on hyperscaler contracts (Meta, Microsoft) that only exist because those companies want to keep capex off their balance sheets. These contracts require completing data centers on unrealistic timelines. Any delay means more obsolete hardware, more debt interest, and less customer willingness to pay. The Bernstein analysis projecting $23B negative free cash flow in 2026 (2.5x worse than 2025) suggests the treadmill is accelerating, not decelerating. Watch for whether Blue Owl or other private credit firms balk at new Coreweave facilities.
