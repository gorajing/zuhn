Chinese households save approximately 40% of their disposable income — roughly 10x the European rate. But with a volatile domestic stock market and banks perceived as unpredictable, real estate becomes the default store of value, accounting for 70% of all household wealth. A 0% property tax further incentivizes buying and holding indefinitely rather than renting or selling.

This creates a dangerous feedback loop: high demand pushes prices up, rising prices validate the investment thesis, and the lack of alternatives means there's no natural rebalancing mechanism. The insight generalizes beyond China — whenever a large population has high savings but artificially constrained investment options (whether through regulation, distrust, or underdeveloped capital markets), capital will concentrate into whichever asset class is accessible, pushing it far beyond fundamental value. The bubble isn't caused by greed but by the absence of choice.
