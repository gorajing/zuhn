Oracle exemplifies the data center trap: spending $100B+ on AI data centers with negative gross margins, funded by $100B+ in debt, for customers like OpenAI that burn billions annually. The timeline mismatch is fatal — data centers take years to build, GPUs must be bought upfront, and Nvidia's product cycle (Blackwell -> Vera Rubin -> Kyber) means hardware is outdated before deployment.

The construction analogy is apt: no construction project finishes on time or budget. Every delay makes the hardware more obsolete. And Nvidia's upcoming Kyber rack architecture will be incompatible with current data center designs, requiring entirely new builds. This creates a structural trap where companies can't stop building (the market would punish them) but can't profit from what they build either.
