Noah Smith cites Ernie Tedeschi's analysis showing that data centers and computing equipment together are contributing to GDP growth at rates last seen during the dot-com boom. This is striking because the dot-com boom is the canonical example of infrastructure investment that ran well ahead of productive utilization — fiber was laid that sat dark for years, and server farms were built that were never filled.

The parallel doesn't mean a crash is inevitable. The dot-com infrastructure eventually did get used — the internet economy that emerged in the 2010s ran on fiber laid in 1999. But it does suggest that current data center investment may be front-running AI demand by years. Investors evaluating AI infrastructure plays should model scenarios where utilization rates stay low for longer than current valuations imply, similar to how telecom infrastructure investors fared poorly even though the underlying technology eventually proved transformative.
