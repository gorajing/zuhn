Meta's $230B single-day loss wasn't caused by a proportional revenue decline — it was triggered by the first-ever report of declining daily users on Facebook. The market had priced in indefinite user growth, and the revelation of saturation forced a complete repricing of the growth narrative.

This pattern recurs across growth stocks: the valuation premium depends on the belief that the growth curve continues. When evidence emerges that the S-curve is inflecting, the repricing isn't gradual — it's a step function. 42 of 52 Wall Street analysts had buy ratings going into the print, showing how consensus can be completely blindsided when a growth narrative breaks. The lesson for investors is that growth stock positions carry embedded binary risk around saturation signals, and consensus bullishness is itself a contrarian warning sign.
