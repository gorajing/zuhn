Ritholtz exposes the financial media business model: they are not selling information, they are selling your attention to advertisers. This creates perverse incentives: sensational headlines, decontextualized scary numbers, and constant urgency that triggers exactly the evolutionary threat-response biases that destroy investment returns.

He introduces 'denominator blindness' — presenting a numerator without context. '10,000 layoffs' sounds terrifying but is meaningless without knowing the company size (Walmart's 2 million employees vs. a 25,000-person manufacturer). 'Market drops 500 points' could be an 8% S&P crash or a sub-1% Dow fluctuation. The antidote: evaluate information sources by asking who the person is, what they are selling, what their conflicts of interest are, what their track record is, and whether their advice is suitable for your specific situation.
