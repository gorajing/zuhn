The AI industry is experiencing what may become one of the largest capital expenditure booms in history, with OpenAI alone attracting potential investments of $60B from Nvidia/Microsoft/Amazon, $50B from Amazon independently, $30B from SoftBank, and $50B from Middle Eastern investors — plus a planned IPO. Smith warns that this concentration creates systemic risk: if OpenAI stumbles despite AI succeeding, the fallout could cause an unwarranted chill in U.S. AI investment, potentially allowing Chinese competitors like DeepSeek to gain ground.

For investors, this is a classic lesson in distinguishing between thesis-level conviction (AI will transform the economy) and vehicle-level conviction (this specific company will capture the value). The railroad boom made railroads essential infrastructure while bankrupting most railroad companies. The same pattern could repeat in AI.
