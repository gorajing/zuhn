The data on active management is damning at every time horizon: fewer than 50% of mutual fund managers beat their benchmark in a single year, 80% fail over five years, over 90% fail at ten years, and virtually none outperform over twenty years — all net of fees and taxes.

This evidence makes index investing the rational default. If the best-resourced professional stock pickers in the world cannot consistently beat a passive index, the idea that individual investors can do so is a dangerous fantasy. Dollar-cost averaging into low-cost index funds guarantees you'll outperform 99% of your fellow investors over the long haul.
