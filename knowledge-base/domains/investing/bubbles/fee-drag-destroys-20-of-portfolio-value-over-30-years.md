The 'Vanguard effect' research shows that Jack Bogle's fee pressure through Vanguard saved investors approximately $1 trillion by 2016 and another trillion in the decade following. Transaction costs have fallen from hundreds of dollars per trade to zero, and fund expense ratios dropped from 1-2% to under 5 basis points for broad indexes.

The math is simple but powerful: higher fees compound against you just as surely as returns compound for you. A portfolio charging 1.5% annually will generate roughly 20% less terminal wealth over 25-30 years compared to one at 5 basis points. As Ritholtz notes, the lower your costs, the more you keep at the end of your investing life. This is pure math, not opinion, making fee reduction one of the few guaranteed improvements investors can make.
