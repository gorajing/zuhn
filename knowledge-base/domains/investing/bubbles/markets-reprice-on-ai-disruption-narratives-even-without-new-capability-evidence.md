When Citrini Research published a speculative scenario about AI disrupting software and finance companies, stocks of named companies fell meaningfully — despite the post containing no new model capability announcements. This contrasts with legitimate repricing events like IBM falling on Anthropic's COBOL capabilities. The pattern suggests markets are increasingly reactive to AI disruption narratives as coordination devices for sentiment, where traders read, get spooked, and sell in concert on the specific names mentioned. For investors, this creates both risk (holding named stocks during viral posts) and opportunity (buying the dip when sell-offs are narrative-driven rather than fundamentals-driven).
