The numbers tell the entire story. Total AI compute revenue including Coreweave, Nebius, and all providers is approximately $55 billion. Bain Capital estimates $2 trillion in revenue is needed to justify current and planned AI infrastructure spending. That's a 36x gap. Deutsche Bank's analysis is blunt: the only way to sustain the AI investment thesis is continued parabolic spending increases, which they describe as 'very unlikely.'

Meanwhile, OpenAI — the largest consumer of AI compute — has plateaued at 700M weekly active users for over a month with no sign of the exponential growth needed. Venture capital returns since 2018 average 0.8-1.2x per dollar invested. The system feeding AI investment is itself in decline. There is no historical precedent for a 36x revenue-to-spending gap closing in a technology market. This isn't a bull-vs-bear debate; it's arithmetic.
