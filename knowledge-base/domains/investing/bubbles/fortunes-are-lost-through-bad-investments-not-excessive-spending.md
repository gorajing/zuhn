The popular narrative of fortunes lost to luxury spending is largely fiction. In reality, most wealth destruction comes from bad investments — particularly complex instruments like derivatives where you can lose millions instantly without the visceral discomfort of spending.

This happens because humans have deeply ingrained alarms against self-indulgent consumption (possibly even genetic), but lack equivalent alarms for investment risk. When you invest, you tell yourself you're just moving money between assets, not spending it. This is why salespeople frame expensive purchases as 'investments' — it bypasses your spending alarms. Protecting wealth requires deliberately developing new, learned alarms for investment risk that don't come naturally.
