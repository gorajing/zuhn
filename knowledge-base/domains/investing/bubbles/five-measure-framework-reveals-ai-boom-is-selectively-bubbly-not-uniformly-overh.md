Azeem Azhar and Nathan Warren propose evaluating the AI boom against five historical bubble measures: economic strain, realized returns, valuation levels, funding quality, and speculative behavior. Unlike the dot-com era where all five metrics flashed red simultaneously, AI appears to be selectively overheated.

This uneven risk profile suggests that rather than a single catastrophic pop, AI will experience rolling corrections — overvalued pure-play AI companies may crash while AI-integrated businesses with real revenue hold up. The framework is useful because it moves beyond the binary 'bubble or not' debate toward understanding which specific pockets of AI investment carry genuine bubble risk and which represent durable value creation.
