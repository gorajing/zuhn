Market history shows that true bubble pops come from unanticipated risks, not widely known ones. When 'AI bubble' becomes consensus, sophisticated investors face a choice: follow the crowd (and potentially miss a genuine technological revolution) or recognize that universal bubble awareness is itself a form of insurance against the worst outcomes.

The devil's advocate case for AI includes: infrastructure investments retain physical value even if AI disappoints, geopolitical competition makes de-investment strategically unacceptable, and the capital has no better alternative destination. The most dangerous thing isn't what you don't know - it's what you know for sure that simply isn't true.
