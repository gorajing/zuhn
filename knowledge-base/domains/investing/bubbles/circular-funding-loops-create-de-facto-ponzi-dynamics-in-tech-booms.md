Graham describes the late-1990s dynamic where investors funded internet startups because Yahoo's revenue was growing, and those startups used the funding to buy Yahoo ads, which grew Yahoo's revenue further. This circular flow made Yahoo's business look fundamentally healthy when it was actually dependent on continued capital injection from outside the system. The insight generalizes: any time a company's revenue growth is primarily driven by spending from entities that are themselves funded by enthusiasm about that revenue growth, you have a Ponzi structure. The cycle breaks when external capital stops flowing in — which in Yahoo's case meant the dot-com crash. Investors should trace the ultimate source of a company's revenue to check whether it originates from real economic value or from recycled investment capital.
