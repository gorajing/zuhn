Charlie Ellis's 'loser's game' concept reveals that investing mirrors amateur tennis: professionals win by scoring points, but amateurs win by making fewer unforced errors. The vast majority of investors — including professionals — underperform simple index funds over time.

This reframes the entire investing mindset. Instead of searching for the next Nvidia, the winning strategy is to automate contributions, diversify broadly, minimize fees, ignore forecasts, and resist the urge to react to market volatility. Every decision is another opportunity for error, and with only 2% of stocks driving all market returns, the odds of picking winners are vanishingly small.
