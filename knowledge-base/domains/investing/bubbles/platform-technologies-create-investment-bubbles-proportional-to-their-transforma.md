The electrification of America created billion-dollar industries from nothing and launched decades of enhanced economic growth through quantum leaps in manufacturing, household technology, and work efficiency. The video argues this bubble dwarfed even the dot-com fever because electricity was a true platform technology — it didn't improve existing workflows, it created entirely new categories of possibility.

The key insight for investors is that platform technologies (electricity, internet, possibly AI) generate bubbles proportional to their transformative scope. The bubble itself isn't irrational — it's a rational response to genuinely unbounded TAM. The irrationality is in which specific companies capture value, not in the aggregate capital deployed. Most electricity-era startups failed, but the aggregate bet on electrification was correct.
