Ritholtz cites Philip Tetlock's research showing that thousands of expert market forecasts performed no better than average predictions. The specific, confident forecaster ('Dow will hit 53,575 next year') is psychologically persuasive but empirically less reliable than the honest analyst who says 'I don't know, but historically 8-10% is typical.'

Recent examples prove the point: nobody forecast the 2020 pandemic and subsequent 69% market rally, the 2021 Ukraine invasion, or the 2022 Federal Reserve raising rates by 500 basis points — causing the rare simultaneous crash of stocks and bonds. Yet investors consistently follow confident forecasts over humble uncertainty. The prescription: ignore all market forecasts, maintain a diversified portfolio, automate contributions, and accept that the future contains unknowable events that no expert can predict.
