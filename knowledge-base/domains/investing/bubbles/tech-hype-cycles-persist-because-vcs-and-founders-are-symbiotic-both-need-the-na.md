Modern MBA identifies the structural reason why tech hype cycles are self-perpetuating: VCs and founders are symbiotic organisms. VCs need radical ideas to generate outsized returns. Founders want to build businesses with other people's money. Both sides must maintain the narrative to reach the same payday -- an IPO or acquisition. The inspirational speeches, generous salaries, magazine spreads, and motivational essays aren't accidental; they're designed to keep the talent pipeline flowing.

Most VCs are former bankers or celebrities who have never worked in tech -- 'it's the blind leading the blind.' But the math works because you only need to win once. One Facebook or Google returns more than hundreds of failures combined. This creates a near-infinite pool of aspiring founders and VCs, and both are quick to forgive, reconcile, and pivot to the next growth story. When big data died, everyone pivoted to AI within months. When AI eventually faces its reckoning, another narrative will emerge. The cycle is structural, not cyclical.
