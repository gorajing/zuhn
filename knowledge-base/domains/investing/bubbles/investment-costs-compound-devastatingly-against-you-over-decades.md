The math on investment fees is stark: over 25-30 years, a high-cost portfolio produces roughly 20% less value than an identical low-cost portfolio. The 'Vanguard effect' — the competitive fee pressure created by Vanguard's low-cost funds — saved investors approximately $1 trillion by 2016 and another trillion in the decade since.

The fee landscape has transformed dramatically: stock trades went from $300-500 to free, and fund expense ratios dropped from 1-2% to as low as 5 basis points for broad indexes like the S&P 500. Unlike market returns, costs are the one variable investors fully control. Every basis point saved compounds in your favor over decades, making fee awareness the simplest and most reliable path to better outcomes.
