Studies show that roughly 30% of people who sell during market lows never return to equities. Those who panic-sold in early 2009 and stayed out missed a 15-year bull run. This is the ultimate unforced error in the loser's game.

The behavioral cascade is devastating: emotional selling locks in losses, fear prevents re-entry, and the compounding opportunity cost grows exponentially. This is why automation matters — removing the human from the decision loop during volatile periods is not laziness, it's the single most effective risk management strategy available to individual investors.
