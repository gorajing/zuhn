The math on AI data centers is straightforward and alarming: ~$30M/MW in servers and GPUs, ~$14M/MW in construction, totaling ~$44M per megawatt. Data centers range from hundreds to thousands of megawatts. None are built without debt, mostly junk-grade. The customers are unprofitable AI startups whose revenue depends on other unprofitable companies.

Zitron identifies three collapse scenarios that would cascade: (1) a project that never starts because financing falls through (Lancaster, PA/Coreweave), (2) a project in progress that stalls mid-construction, and (3) a completed center that goes dark because customers disappear. Any one of these would signal to the entire debt market that AI data center investments are unstable. The comparison to the 2008 crisis is specific: this is a test of debt markets, not technology markets. Insurance funds and pensions are invested in data center debt, creating potential contagion.
