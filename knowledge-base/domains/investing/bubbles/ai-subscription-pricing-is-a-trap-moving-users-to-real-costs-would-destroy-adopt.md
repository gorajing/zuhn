The Uber comparison that AI bulls love actually proves the bears' case. Uber's price hikes worked because users had an existing mental model for what a cab ride costs — the subsidized price was low but the real price was within the same order of magnitude. AI subscriptions have no such anchor.

A Claude Code user on $20/month burning $150 in compute has never experienced metered AI costs. Moving them to API pricing means every hallucination, every circular reasoning loop, every unnecessary token costs visible money. The psychological shift from 'all you can eat' to 'pay per mistake' would be devastating to usage. And even the $200/month subscribers allowing $2,700 in compute burn — that's not a 2x price increase to fix, it's 13x. No consumer product has survived that kind of repricing. The AI companies have no viable path between their current pricing (hemorrhaging money) and sustainable pricing (losing all users).
