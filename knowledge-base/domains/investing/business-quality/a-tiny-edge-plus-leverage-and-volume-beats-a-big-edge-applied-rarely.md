Bob Mercer's quote — 'we're right 50.7% of the time, and being right 100% right 50.7% of the time can make you billions' — is the practical inversion of how most investors think. Traditional investing philosophy prizes conviction: wait for the fat pitch, size up, hold. Rentech's approach is the opposite: accept that your edge per trade is tiny, then compensate through volume (millions of short-held positions), disciplined Kelly-style bet sizing, and leverage (basket options let them run $60B of positions on $5B of capital).

The architectural implications are profound. You need clean tick-level historical data, low-latency execution, sophisticated risk infrastructure, and models that can find and re-find marginal edges faster than they decay. You cannot run this strategy on conviction bets or concentrated positions — the math only works when variance is tamed by volume.

The counterintuitive takeaway for non-finance contexts: in any optimization problem, a reliable tiny edge applied at scale can dominate occasional large edges. This is why A/B testing beats executive intuition at scale, why machine learning beats expert systems on high-volume tasks, and why slot machines are more profitable than poker rooms. If you can find a 0.5% edge and apply it 10,000 times cheaply, that's a better business than a 20% edge you can only apply once a quarter.
