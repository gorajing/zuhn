Mauboussin presents Bruce Greenwald's market share instability metric: observe market shares at two points (3-5 years apart), calculate each company's absolute change, and average them. The data across four industries is telling: U.S. search engines show just 1% instability (Google's dominance is rock-solid), auto manufacturing shows 1% (oligopoly stability), while web browsers and social media show 3% each (more dynamic competitive landscapes).

The U.S. airline industry's instability analysis from 1974-2023 shows that periods of low instability (mid-1990s, 2014-2019) corresponded with relatively good industry profitability. Spikes in instability followed deregulation in 1978, 9/11, and COVID-19, each corresponding to periods of poor industry economics. This is a simple, replicable metric that any investor can calculate from public data to assess moat durability.
