Mauboussin emphasizes that sustainable value creation has two dimensions: the magnitude of the ROIC-WACC spread and how long a company can maintain it. A company earning 5% ROIC when its WACC is 7% is destroying value despite having positive returns. The stock market tends to reflect this spread, with approximately one-third of S&P 500 market cap historically representing anticipated future value creation.

This reframing matters because many investors and executives fixate on ROIC in isolation. Even within value-destroying industries (negative aggregate ROIC-WACC), individual companies can still create value, and vice versa. The analysis of 63 U.S. industries in 2023 shows wide variance in spreads, with the distribution within each industry mirroring the overall population. Industry alone does not seal a company's fate for value creation.
