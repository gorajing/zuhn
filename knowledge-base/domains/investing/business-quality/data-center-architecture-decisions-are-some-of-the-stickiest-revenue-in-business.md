NZS Capital's insight via the Acquired podcast: data center revenue is among the stickiest forms of revenue known to humankind because the organizational switching costs involved in procurement and architectural standardization are enormous. Fortune 500 CIOs don't change their data center architecture more than once a decade, and decisions being made in 2023 around generative AI infrastructure will persist through at least 2028-2033.

This reframes Nvidia's posture: the apparent supply constraint is not a feature they love — they are pushing TSMC for every ounce of capacity because they know this is a window to lock in architecture decisions. Every DGX or H100 cluster installed now creates a 5-10 year dependency on CUDA, NVLink, InfiniBand, and the Nvidia systems stack. Even if generative AI enthusiasm cools, the architectural lock-in remains.

For investors evaluating infrastructure companies during platform transitions, the key question isn't 'is the current hype sustainable' but 'whose architecture is being standardized on during this window'. The answer determines the durable revenue base independent of whether AI workloads triple or halve from here. This is the same pattern that made AWS's cloud 1.0 lead so durable — the switching cost is organizational, not technical.
