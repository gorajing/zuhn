Victoria Dickinson's model classifies companies into Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Shake-Out, and Decline stages based on cash flow patterns. Growth companies show positive operating cash flow, negative investing (spending on growth), and positive financing (raising capital). Mature companies show positive operating, negative investing, and negative financing (returning capital). The key advantage is that companies can move forward or backward through stages, unlike rigid age-based models.

Empirical data from 1985-2023 validates the framework: Growth stage companies average 10.6% ROIC with 12.7% sales growth, while Maturity shows peak ROIC at 11.2% but only 6.3% sales growth. Decline companies show -12.0% ROIC. Nearly three-quarters of companies are in Growth or Maturity at any given time. This framework is immediately actionable for classifying any public company's current strategic position.
