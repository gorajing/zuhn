When Dwarkesh asks about the Moore's Law analog for quantum now that the 'first transistor' exists, Nadella gives an unusually concrete window: 2027, 2028, or 2029 to build an integrated, fault-tolerant quantum computer. He frames this as the logical next step now that the fabrication technique is in hand.

This matters for tracking claims. Most quantum timeline statements from major labs have historically been hedged into the 2030s or beyond. A sitting Microsoft CEO putting a 2-4 year window on fault tolerance is either a significant forecast update or a marketing statement — and either way it is a concrete, falsifiable prediction that can be checked.

For investors and forecasters, the useful move is to treat this as a calibration signal on Microsoft's internal confidence, and to watch for whether they ship an integrated Majorana chip in that window. If they miss by 2025-level 'fusion is 30 years away' margins, it devalues the Majorana bet; if they hit it, the implications for drug discovery, materials, and cryptography timelines all move forward.
