The heat death scenario illustrates the second law of thermodynamics at cosmic scale: the universe trends inexorably toward maximum entropy, where all energy is evenly distributed and no work can be done. Stars exhaust gas clouds, black holes evaporate via Hawking radiation, and eventually even photons decay.

This maps directly to economic and organizational systems. Companies, markets, and institutions are all low-entropy structures that require continuous energy (capital, talent, innovation) to maintain. The default trajectory for any business is decay — not because of competition, but because of entropy. This reframes competitive strategy: the primary battle isn't against competitors but against the natural tendency toward disorder. Moats are really entropy-resistance mechanisms.
