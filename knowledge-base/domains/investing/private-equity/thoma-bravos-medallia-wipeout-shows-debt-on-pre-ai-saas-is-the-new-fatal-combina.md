Tasch's diagnostic of Medallia: 2021 deal, $6B transaction, $5B equity, ~$1.6B debt — not wildly overleveraged. But Medallia's revenue eroded (sales QoQ retention reportedly 21%) because CIOs are consolidating vendors and Medallia's 'measure-customer-engagement-via-survey' product is exactly what gets cut first. With $200M EBITDA and now ~$2B debt, the $300M debt service smothers the business — it's not worth Thoma Bravo putting more equity in to service the debt.

The scary aha for the entire 2021 LBO vintage: this isn't an over-leverage failure, it's an over-pay-then-AI-disruption failure. The same pattern applies to potentially Coupa, New Relic, Anaplan, Zendesk, Avalara, SmartSheet, Cornerstone — all 2021 deals at high multiples on pre-AI assets. The classic LBO playbook (muddle along at 10% operating income, refinance at low rates) doesn't work when the underlying asset has negative terminal value momentum. For Zuhn editorially this is the strongest single data point that Issue 3's 'SaaS apocalypse' thesis is operationally real — not as headline collapse but as PE firms quietly handing keys to creditors as 2021 vintage debt comes due against AI-eroded revenue. The number to watch: refinancing cliffs in 2026-2027 for the debt structures from those vintage deals.
