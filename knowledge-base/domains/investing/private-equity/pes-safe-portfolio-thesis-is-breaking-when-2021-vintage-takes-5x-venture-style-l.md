Tasch's analysis of LP psychology: the historical mental model was 'venture is risky 4-5x return, PE is safe consistent 2x return — diversify accordingly.' That model breaks if Medallia ($5B wipeout) is followed by similar collapses across the 2021 PE vintage (potentially Coupa, New Relic, Anaplan, Cornerstone, Avalara, SmartSheet, Zendesk all at risk). When the 'safe' part of the portfolio takes significant losses, LPs reduce overall risk appetite, hurting venture allocations even though venture-vintage 2021 was already written off.

The corollary for venture firms: even if your fund performance is fine, your fundraising environment isn't, because your LPs are nursing PE losses. Calacanis adds that LPs had already internalized the 2021 venture vintage as a write-off ('mulligan'), but they hadn't internalized PE losses, which they expected to be the safe ballast. For Zuhn editorially: this is the under-discussed second-order effect of the AI-vs-pre-AI-SaaS bifurcation. The pain shows up not just in product markets (SaaS apocalypse) and labor markets (job displacement) but in capital markets (LP allocation shifts) — and the capital reallocation is the part that determines what gets funded over the next 3-5 years.
