BEA data shows information processing equipment contributed ~1.11 percentage points and software ~0.99 points to real GDP growth in H1 2025, together explaining over half of the 3.8% annualized growth print while other components were mixed. This means AI-driven infrastructure spending is no longer a niche tech story — it's a macroeconomic force propping up headline GDP numbers.

The implication is double-edged: AI capex is currently subsidizing GDP growth, but this creates concentration risk. If AI investment sentiment shifts (due to overcapacity, funding dry-up, or regulatory shock), the GDP impact would be significant. It also raises questions about whether this growth is productive investment or a bubble-phase misallocation — the answer depends on whether AI applications generate returns that justify the infrastructure spend within a reasonable timeframe.
