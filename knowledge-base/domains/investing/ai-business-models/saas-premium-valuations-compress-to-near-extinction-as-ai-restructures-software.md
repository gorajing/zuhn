Public SaaS valuation multiples have compressed dramatically, with very few companies maintaining above 10x revenue multiples in 2026. The survivors share common traits: they own infrastructure layers rather than application features, solve mission-critical problems where switching costs are high, and sustain growth at enterprise scale.

This compression reflects a structural shift, not just a cyclical downturn. AI is commoditizing many application-layer SaaS functions — if an AI agent can replicate what a SaaS tool does, the tool's pricing power evaporates. The companies that retain premium valuations are those where the value is in the data network effects, the integration depth, or the regulatory moat rather than the software functionality itself. For founders building SaaS, this means defensibility must come from something AI cannot easily replicate — proprietary data, deep workflow integration, or regulated infrastructure positioning.
