The supply side of tech hype (startups producing narratives) is well understood. But the demand side is equally important and less discussed. Fortune 500 executives are terrified of being disrupted. During the big data era, no CEO wanted to be caught without a data strategy. Every Wall Street analyst asked how companies were going to stop tech from eating their lunch. The conversation devolved into a pissing match of who had the most data and best data culture.

This corporate FOMO creates a willing buyer for every overblown narrative Silicon Valley produces. Companies hired Chief Data Officers, bought analytics platforms, and invested in data science teams -- most of which produced no meaningful ROI. The same pattern is now repeating with AI: every Fortune 500 needs an AI strategy, every executive's LinkedIn mentions AI, and the fear of being left behind drives massive spending on unproven technology. This demand-side dynamic is what transforms a genuine technology advance (which benefits a few) into a market-wide mania (which benefits VCs and consultants).
