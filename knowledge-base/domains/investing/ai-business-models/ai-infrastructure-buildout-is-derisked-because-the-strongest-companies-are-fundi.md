The current AI infrastructure buildout looks superficially similar to the dot-com broadband overbuild, but the risk profile is fundamentally different. The companies bearing the burden — Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta — are arguably the most profitable companies ever created. They can absorb potential capacity overbuild in a way that leveraged 2000-era telecoms could not.

Additionally, the demand side is already validated: ChatGPT reached 365 billion searches in 2 years versus Google's 11 years. Over a billion monthly active users are already on AI products, and daily users spend 28-30 minutes per day on ChatGPT. This demand visibility, combined with the financial strength of the supply-side builders, makes this buildout fundamentally different from prior technology infrastructure cycles where demand was speculative.
