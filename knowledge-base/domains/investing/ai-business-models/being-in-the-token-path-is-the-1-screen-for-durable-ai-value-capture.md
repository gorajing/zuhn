a16z's number-one criterion for assessing an AI company's place in the world is now 'are you in the token path?' — does the product sit on the actual flow of model inference, where consumption and pricing are happening, rather than wrapping it. The logic is downstream of cost pressure: enterprise buyers already can't even cover the growth in their AI costs, so they will not increase budgets for previous-generation software. Anything that is merely a skeuomorphic layer on top of the old workflow gets disintermediated as buyers consolidate spend onto things that are load-bearing in the inference path.

This reframes moats away from classic SaaS defensibility (lock-in, integrations, brand) toward positional control over where tokens are consumed and priced. It also explains why model companies are 'legging up' into the application layer — owning more of the token path is their biggest lever for stickiness — and why pure application wrappers are structurally exposed from both directions: squeezed by buyer cost pressure below and by labs moving up into apps above.

For operators and investors, the practical test is mechanical: trace the dollar of inference spend and ask whether removing this company breaks the flow or merely the interface. If it's the interface, the position is weak.
