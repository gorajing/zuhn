Marcus and Eisman identify alarming financial patterns in the AI ecosystem. OpenAI has spent tens of billions more than it has earned. Nvidia invested $30 billion in OpenAI, which then buys Nvidia chips — a pattern critics call vendor financing or circular financing. If OpenAI needed chips and had the money, they'd just buy them; Nvidia subsidizing them suggests the organic demand isn't there.

Nvidia stock has been flat for 6 months after years of rocket-like growth — a transition most haven't noticed. Oracle has fallen 35% since September. Around 70% of fund managers now believe AI is overvalued, up from nearly zero three years ago. Marcus's biggest fear is 'too big to fail' — that AI companies become so enmeshed with the financial system through bank loans and leverage that taxpayers end up bailing them out, as in 2008. The market can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent, but the signs of correction are mounting.
