The distinction between genuine and illusory data moats is critical for evaluating tech businesses. First-movers like Facebook, LinkedIn, Netflix, and Google had organic adoption and network effects -- users came for the product, generated data passively, and the data made the product better, attracting more users. This is a genuine flywheel.

The thousands of consumer startups that followed did not have this organic adoption. They needed to spend millions on advertising to acquire users. The 'data' they collected was from paid users, not organic ones. The users didn't stick around once the subsidies ended. The data didn't compound because the users weren't loyal. Yet every one of these companies cited data as their competitive moat in investor presentations. The difference is fundamental: earned data from organic network effects compounds; purchased data from paid acquisition does not. This lens cleanly separates genuine AI/data businesses from those merely adopting the narrative.
