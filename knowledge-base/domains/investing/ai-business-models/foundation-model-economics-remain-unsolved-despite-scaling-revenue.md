The foundation model market is experiencing explosive revenue growth, but the fundamental question of where durable margins emerge remains unanswered. Training costs are astronomical, inference costs are declining but still significant at scale, and competitive moats are unclear as open-source alternatives narrow the gap.

This creates an interesting investment dynamic: the market rewards scale and capability improvements, but the businesses may ultimately look more like infrastructure utilities with thin margins than the high-margin software companies investors are pricing in. The winners may not be the model builders themselves but the companies that build defensible applications and workflows on top of commoditizing model capabilities.
