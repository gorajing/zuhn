The Q3 2025 earnings pattern reveals a maturing investor framework for evaluating AI bets. Microsoft dropped ~3% despite beating Azure growth expectations because management admitted being 'short on capacity'—implying missed near-term revenue and continued capex pressure. Meta's stock plunged ~11% as $97B in planned capex (nearly double prior year) overshadowed record $51B quarterly revenue.

Meanwhile, Alphabet hit $100B quarterly revenue for the first time and rose to all-time highs, with Google Cloud's $155B backlog demonstrating monetization maturity. Amazon jumped 13%+ on strong AWS plus operational efficiency from cost cuts. The divergence is instructive: the market has moved past rewarding AI ambition and now demands proof that capex translates to margin-accretive revenue within visible timeframes.
