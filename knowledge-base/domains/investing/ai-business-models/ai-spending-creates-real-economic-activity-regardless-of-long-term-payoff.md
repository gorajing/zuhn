The AI investment cycle is currently self-reinforcing: companies spend $400B+ annually on data centers, creating construction and engineering jobs. Rising stock prices from AI hype create wealth effects that boost consumer spending. This keeps economic indicators positive, which justifies more AI investment.

This means the economy becomes dependent on continued AI capital expenditure regardless of whether the technology delivers commercial returns. If spending stops or significantly slows, both the direct employment effects and indirect wealth effects reverse simultaneously, creating a potential cascade. The economy has essentially placed a leveraged bet on AI's success.
