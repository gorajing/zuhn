Ramp data reveals Anthropic now captures 73% of new AI spending among companies buying AI tools, up from 50/50 ten weeks prior. The catalyst: Opus 4.5 and subsequent models created a step function in quality that was 'under-discussed.' Meanwhile, OpenAI exhibits strategic whiplash: Sora being folded into ChatGPT, hardware ambitions deprioritized, headcount plans reversing from flat to doubling to 8,000. The 20VC hosts observe that OpenAI has shifted from an air of invincibility to an 'air of desperation.' The deeper lesson: in enterprise, consistency of ICP and product vision builds trust, while rapid pivots signal the company doesn't know its own direction. OpenAI still owns the consumer market (ChatGPT muscle memory) but coding/enterprise lock-in to Claude is hardening.
