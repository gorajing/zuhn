Horowitz argues that AI should be viewed not as an internet-like cycle with a few dominant winners, but as a new computing platform comparable to computers themselves. The internet produced a 'reasonably large number' of spectacularly huge winners — Meta, Netflix, Amazon, Google. AI products are having even bigger economic impact, and the design space is enormous and unprecedented.

On the bubble question, Horowitz pushes back directly: valuations have risen fast, but demand has risen equally fast. Even Nvidia's multiples are not historically outrageous when you factor in growth rates and earnings. The customer adoption, revenue growth rates, and demand intensity are unlike anything he has seen in his career. The key risk is not overvaluation but rather that people are so worried about a bubble that they underestimate the scope of value creation happening underneath.
