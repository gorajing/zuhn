The economy faces a lose-lose dependency: AI capital expenditure ($400B+/year in data centers alone) provides construction jobs and stock market wealth effects that sustain consumer spending. If this spending stops (AI disappoints), those jobs and wealth effects vanish. If AI succeeds, it automates the remaining jobs.

This creates an economic trap: we can't afford to stop investing in AI (the spending itself sustains the economy) and we can't afford for AI to fully succeed (it replaces the workers who are consumers). The only viable path is a perpetual middle ground where AI is always promising enough to justify spending but never quite capable enough to fully displace workers.
