The current AI investment landscape is sufficiently complex that early-stage funds ($100-500M) need explicit frameworks rather than broad AI thesis. The landscape spans infrastructure, models, tooling, and vertical applications—each with different risk profiles, capital requirements, and time horizons. A structured framework helps funds avoid the trap of chasing every AI deal and instead build conviction in specific layers of the stack or application domains. This mirrors the maturation pattern of previous technology waves: early generalist investing gives way to specialized thesis-driven funds as the ecosystem develops clear segments. Founders benefit from understanding which fund archetype they're pitching to, as framework-driven funds have clearer criteria and faster decision-making.
