When Facebook eliminated WhatsApp's $1/year subscription, the logic was straightforward: a user's data — phone numbers bridging offline/online identity, behavioral patterns, contact lists, purchase signals — generates far more than $1/year when combined with Facebook's advertising infrastructure. The phone number specifically was described as 'the missing link' connecting WhatsApp users to their Facebook profiles.

This illustrates a fundamental asymmetry in platform economics: users dramatically underestimate the value of their data because they can't see the cross-referencing that makes it valuable. A single data point (your phone number) is nearly worthless. But that phone number linked to your Facebook profile, Instagram activity, browsing history, and purchase behavior creates a composite identity worth hundreds of dollars per year to advertisers. This is why 'free' products backed by data extraction consistently outcompete paid alternatives — the indirect monetization ceiling is orders of magnitude higher than what users will voluntarily pay.
