While Coinbase's headline announcements focused on consumer-facing products (stock trading, prediction markets, AI advisor), the most consequential move is infrastructural: standardizing USDC as the unified settlement layer across all product verticals. This creates a self-reinforcing flywheel where each new product increases USDC transaction volume, which strengthens Coinbase's position as the primary on/off ramp between traditional and decentralized finance.

This pattern — where the infrastructure decision underneath a product launch matters more than the products themselves — recurs across tech history. AWS mattered more than any individual Amazon product. Stripe's payment API mattered more than any merchant feature. When evaluating platform expansions, the analytical lens should focus on which layer of the stack the company is standardizing, not which products they're shipping. Coinbase is effectively building the settlement infrastructure for a converged financial system.
