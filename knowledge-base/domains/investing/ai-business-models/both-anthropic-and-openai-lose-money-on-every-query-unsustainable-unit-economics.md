Marcus presents stark financial data: Anthropic has earned $5 billion in total revenue but spent $10 billion, losing $5 billion net. OpenAI's numbers are worse — perhaps $50-80 billion spent against $10-20 billion in revenue. Both companies fundamentally lose money every time a user poses a query. This is not a scaling problem to be solved later; it's a structural cost issue exacerbated by rising energy prices.

Valuing OpenAI at $700-800 billion against these fundamentals is irrational by any traditional metric. Marcus argues the bubble's core is simple: unreliability limits profitability, and the technology is expensive to run. The psychological question is what triggers the wake-up. With 70% of fund managers now viewing AI as overvalued (up from near zero three years ago), Nvidia flat for 6 months, and Oracle down 35%, the correction may already be underway.
