Herjavec's investment thesis mirrors the classic 'picks and shovels' framework applied to AI: while consumer apps like chatbots and image generators attract attention, the companies building the underlying data pipelines, compute infrastructure, and tooling layers will capture more long-term value. This echoes historical patterns from cloud computing where AWS captured more value than most apps built on it.

For investors and founders, this suggests evaluating AI opportunities through an infrastructure lens. Companies that become essential plumbing — data labeling, model serving, vector databases, evaluation frameworks — may have more defensible positions than those competing in crowded consumer app categories where switching costs are low.
