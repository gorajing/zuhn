Tesla shareholders approved the largest executive compensation package in history: $1 trillion in stock options across twelve escalating performance tranches. Each tranche requires both revenue and market-cap targets, culminating at $8.5T valuation, plus operational metrics like 20M vehicle deliveries, 10M FSD subscriptions, 1M Optimus robots, and 1M robotaxis.

The structure is notable because it converts the principal-agent problem into a shared upside bet. If milestones aren't hit, Musk gets nothing from the package. The 75% shareholder approval signals that investors prefer dilution risk (13% to 25% ownership) over the risk of losing Musk's focus to SpaceX, xAI, and X. This model suggests that for founder-CEOs running multiple ventures, milestone-gated compensation may be the only credible retention mechanism.
