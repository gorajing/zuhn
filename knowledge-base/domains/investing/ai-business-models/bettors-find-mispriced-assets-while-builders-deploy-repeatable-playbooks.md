Hormozi distinguishes two fundamental investing philosophies: Bettors (Buffett-style) who find companies trading below intrinsic value and wait for appreciation, versus Builders who have a specific operational hold code — templatized SOPs and playbooks — that they deploy into companies to directly create value. Most private equity combines both, but Hormozi's acquisition.com leans toward building.

The Builder model is notable because it transforms investing from a prediction problem (will this asset appreciate?) into an execution problem (can we deploy our playbooks?). This means deal selection criteria shift from 'is this company undervalued?' to 'will our specific playbooks work here?' — rejecting 95% of deals not because they're bad companies but because the operational fit isn't there. This is particularly relevant in the current AI era where operational playbooks around automation, content, and sales systems can be increasingly templatized.
