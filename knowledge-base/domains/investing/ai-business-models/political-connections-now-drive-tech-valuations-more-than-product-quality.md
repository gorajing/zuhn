Oracle gained more market cap in 8 hours than McDonald's total value despite reporting lower-than-expected earnings. The surge wasn't driven by product innovation but by AI promises, control of TikTok's US operations, and deep connections to political power.

This represents a shift in what drives tech valuations: government relationships and regulatory capture are becoming more valuable than engineering excellence. Oracle's historical reputation for mediocre enterprise software hasn't prevented it from becoming one of the most influential companies in tech, suggesting that in the AI era, the winners may be determined more by political access than technical capability.
