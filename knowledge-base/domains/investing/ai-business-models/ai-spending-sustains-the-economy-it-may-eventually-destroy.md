The AI economic paradox is self-referential: trillions in data center investment creates construction jobs, drives stock gains that boost consumer spending, and maintains economic activity. But this spending is building tools designed to automate the very workforce whose spending sustains the economy.

The danger is that treating this as a 'foregone bubble' is as risky as ignoring the bubble. If AI achieves its stated goals, displacement is massive. If it doesn't, the wasted investment collapses the economic activity it was sustaining. The economy needs AI to be perpetually promising but never quite transformative enough to fully displace workers.
