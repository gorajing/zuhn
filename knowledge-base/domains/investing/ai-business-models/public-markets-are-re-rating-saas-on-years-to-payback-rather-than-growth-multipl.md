Chamath argues the 'SaaS apocalypse' is not just tactical but a deep reset in capital markets. Snowflake in 2023 would have taken ~100 years of free cash flow to recoup a single share; that number has been cut in half as the stock price fell. ServiceNow, Workday, and similar have all rerated. Meanwhile the Mag 6 (ex-Nvidia) have been rerated up because the market believes their cash flows are 'monopolistically durable forever.' The central question is: if AI makes every business disruptable every 5-6 years, what forward multiples make sense? This has implications for Silicon Valley employees — if equity upside becomes unreliable, the rational response is to demand more cash compensation, which would further change startup financing and valuations. This is a major regime-change thesis.
