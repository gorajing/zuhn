VC valuation methodology is fundamentally different from traditional corporate finance. Rather than discounting future cash flows, 65% of VCs anchor valuation on the likelihood and quality of exit opportunities — whether IPO or acquisition. Another 60% benchmark against comparable companies. Traditional metrics like IRR (target ~31% for early stage) and NPV take a backseat, especially for earlier rounds.

This exit-first valuation framework means founders can significantly influence their valuation by crafting a compelling exit narrative. Showing that acquirers in your space are active, that comparable companies have achieved strong exits, or that the IPO market is favorable for your sector directly impacts what VCs will pay. The 5.5x target cash-on-cash multiple also reveals the return threshold — founders should understand that VCs need to believe your company can deliver at least this multiple to justify the risk.
