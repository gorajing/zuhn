After a prolonged drought in venture exits (2022-2024), Q1 2025 broke M&A records with $54.5B in billion-dollar startup acquisitions before the quarter even ended, alongside a heating IPO market. This matters enormously for the venture ecosystem because exit liquidity drives the entire flywheel: LPs need distributions to re-commit to new funds, GPs need realized returns to raise successor funds, and founders need exit validation to justify the venture path.

The surge appears structural rather than cyclical: corporations sitting on large cash reserves feel urgency to acquire AI capabilities rather than build them, and the regulatory environment has shifted to be more permissive of large tech acquisitions. For founders currently fundraising, this reopening means later-stage investors are more willing to pay higher valuations because they can now model realistic exit timelines rather than indefinite holding periods.
