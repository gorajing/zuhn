The VC evaluation process follows a remarkably consistent pattern across firms. After initial deal sourcing, investors assess five dimensions in increasing depth: market size and problem urgency (is this a billion-dollar opportunity?), traction and growth (are users adopting and paying?), business model and unit economics (is this scalable and profitable?), team strength (do founders have execution ability?), and competitive moat (how defensible is this?). A startup that excels across all five becomes a high-priority investment.

This predictability is actually useful for founders. Rather than treating fundraising as mysterious or relationship-dependent, you can audit your startup against these five factors and identify your weakest dimension before approaching investors. Pre-traction startups can still raise by compensating with exceptional team credentials, strong letters of intent, or breakthrough technology—but they need to consciously address the traction gap rather than ignore it.
