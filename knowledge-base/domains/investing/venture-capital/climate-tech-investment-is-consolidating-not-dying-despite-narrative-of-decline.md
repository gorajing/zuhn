Despite narratives declaring climate tech 'dead,' the evidence shows continued significant funding — including a €285M European sustainability fund and a $125M climate and health tech fund. What's actually happening is a maturation and consolidation of the sector, not an abandonment.

The shift is from broad, optimistic cleantech bets toward more targeted investments in specific infrastructure, adaptation technology, and climate-adjacent sectors like insurance for climate-impacted properties. This pattern mirrors previous tech sector consolidations where early hype capital retreats but serious, thesis-driven capital moves into the validated subsegments. Founders in climate tech should position around specific, defensible niches rather than broad sustainability narratives.
