Most analysis of VC performance focuses on deal selection — which companies to invest in at entry. But fund-level mechanics like reserve allocation and capital recycling can be equally deterministic of returns. Reserves allow funds to maintain or increase ownership in their best-performing companies through follow-on rounds, directly amplifying the power law effect. Capital recycling — reinvesting proceeds from early exits back into new deals — effectively increases the fund's investable capital beyond committed amounts. Together, these mechanics mean that two funds with identical initial portfolios can produce dramatically different returns based purely on capital management discipline. The best fund managers treat reserves as a strategic weapon, not an afterthought.
