The nine-area diligence framework (team, tech/IP, regulatory, GTM, competition, market, financials, exit potential, legal/cap table) reveals an important weighting: at seed stage, financials are the least reliable signal because there's minimal history and maximum uncertainty in projections. The areas that actually predict outcomes—founder integrity and execution history, IP ownership clarity, regulatory readiness, and whether the competitive moat is real—are all non-financial. This has implications for how investors allocate diligence time: spending 40% of effort on financial modeling for a pre-revenue company is misallocated versus spending that time on backchannel founder references or technical architecture review. The framework also explains why the best seed investors often have operating backgrounds rather than finance backgrounds—they know which non-financial signals matter.
