Graham observes that VC offices on Sand Hill Road are 'boringly uniform'—same buildings, minimal exteriors, confusing layout—and calls this 'a pretty accurate reflection of the VC business.' This is a sharp insight: capital itself is a commodity, and VC firms that try to differentiate through surface-level branding are fighting the wrong battle.

The contrast with the bustling energy of University Ave startups is telling. The value creation happens in the cafes where founders pitch, not in the quiet offices where checks are written. This suggests that the real differentiator for VCs is access to deal flow and judgment in selecting founders, not the capital itself. The physical environment literally embodies this dynamic—founders lean forward eagerly while investors sit back with 'slightly pained expressions.'
