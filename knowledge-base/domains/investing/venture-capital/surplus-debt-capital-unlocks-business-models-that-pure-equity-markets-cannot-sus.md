When institutional debt capital doubled from $400B to $800B post-2009, it didn't merely inflate asset prices — it enabled fundamentally new business models like iBuyers (Opendoor) and vertically-integrated short-term rentals (Sonder, Lyric). These companies' unit economics depend on access to cheap leverage.

This pattern generalizes beyond real estate: any industry where a sudden influx of a specific capital type occurs can birth startup categories that are structurally dependent on that capital regime. The implication for investors is that these businesses carry hidden regime risk — when the capital environment normalizes, the entire category may become unviable, not just individual companies. Evaluating proptech or similar debt-dependent startups requires stress-testing against capital cost scenarios, not just market demand.
