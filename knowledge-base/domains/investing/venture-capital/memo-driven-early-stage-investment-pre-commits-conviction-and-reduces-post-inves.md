The standard VC decision process involves partner meetings, term sheet negotiation, and post-signing rationalization — a flow that lets investors drift during adverse conditions because the original reasoning was never explicit enough to hold them accountable. The memo-driven alternative forces the investor to articulate the full thesis in writing, publish it (or at least commit it to a dated file), and then hold themselves to that thesis through the post-investment journey. This has two effects. First, it surfaces weak reasoning that verbal diligence hides — you cannot write 'he's got all the boundary conditions to build something incredibly iconic' without actually knowing what those boundary conditions are and testing whether the founder has them. Second, when the investment enters its dark years (inevitable for deep tech), the original memo serves as an anchor that prevents panic-driven exits. The technique is asymmetric in reputation cost (public memos look foolish if wrong) but that asymmetry is itself a discipline.
