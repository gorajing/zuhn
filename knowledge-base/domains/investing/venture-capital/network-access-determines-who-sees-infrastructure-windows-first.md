NFX candidly describes how their space investment thesis originated not from top-down analysis but from network access — specifically, getting an early meeting with Stoke Space's founder. That single relationship opened a cascade: understanding launch economics revealed satellite manufacturing opportunities, which revealed on-orbit compute possibilities, which revealed navigation and application layers.

This pattern — where one infrastructure relationship unlocks an entire adjacent stack — suggests that infrastructure investing is fundamentally a network-access game rather than an analytical one. The information asymmetry exists not because the data is hidden, but because the implications of technical capabilities are only legible to people embedded in the domain. For founders seeking infrastructure-stage investors, this means targeting VCs already invested in adjacent infrastructure layers rather than generalists.
