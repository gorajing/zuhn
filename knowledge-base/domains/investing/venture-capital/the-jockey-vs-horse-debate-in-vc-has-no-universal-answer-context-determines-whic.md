The classic VC debate of jockey (founder quality) vs. horse (market/idea quality) resurfaces constantly because both matter and neither is sufficient alone. The nuanced answer is that stage determines the weighting: at pre-seed and seed, the idea is often still malleable, so founder resilience, learning speed, and domain insight dominate. At Series A and beyond, market size and traction evidence take precedence because pivots become more expensive.

A practical heuristic: back the jockey when the market is uncertain but the founder has unfair insight into it; back the horse when the market is clearly large but execution risk is the main variable. Great investments typically have both, but forced to choose, early-stage investors should almost always prioritize the jockey.
