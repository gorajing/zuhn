An early investor in ElevenLabs (now a billion-dollar AI company) shared two specific red flags that cause him to walk away from deals: teams that lack deep technical capability, and founders chasing crowded, hype-driven markets. These two factors interact—without technical depth, teams can't build defensible products, and in hype-driven markets, the lack of differentiation means they compete primarily on capital rather than innovation.

The compounding effect is that shallow technical teams in hot markets face both slower product velocity and inflated hiring/infrastructure costs, creating a death spiral of burning capital without building moats. This aligns with the broader pattern that the best venture outcomes come from technically deep teams working on problems where their specific expertise creates an unfair advantage, not from teams riding waves they don't deeply understand.
