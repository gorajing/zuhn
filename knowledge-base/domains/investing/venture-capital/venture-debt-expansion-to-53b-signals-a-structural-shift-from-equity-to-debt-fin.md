Venture debt reached $53B in 2024, driven by companies delaying IPOs and seeking non-dilutive growth capital. This represents a significant shift in the startup financing stack — mature companies that would historically have gone public are instead layering debt on top of equity rounds to fund operations and growth.

The implications extend beyond individual companies. This trend concentrates more value in private markets longer, changes the risk calculus for equity investors who now share the capital structure with debt holders, and creates a new class of venture lenders with significant influence over late-stage company decisions. For founders, understanding debt covenants and maintaining metrics that satisfy lenders becomes as important as managing equity investor relationships.
