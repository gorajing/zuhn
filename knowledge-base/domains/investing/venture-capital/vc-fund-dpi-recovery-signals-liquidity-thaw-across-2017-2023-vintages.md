Q2 2025 data shows a meaningful shift in VC fund performance: DPI (distributions to paid-in capital) is rising across 2017-2023 vintages, meaning LPs are actually receiving money back. TVPI and IRR also climbed while LP check sizes grew larger, signaling renewed confidence.

The simultaneous decline in dry powder suggests managers are deploying faster rather than sitting on committed capital. This combination — returns going up, fresh capital flowing in, reserves declining — points to an inflection in the VC cycle where the post-2021 distribution drought is finally breaking. For fund managers, the pressure shifts from raising to returning capital efficiently.
