Q3 2025 venture data shows funding surpassing $90B for the fourth consecutive quarter, fueled by mega-rounds to decacorns and AI startups. This sustained cadence — rather than a single spike — suggests the venture market may have structurally expanded rather than simply cycling through a peak.

The drivers are mutually reinforcing: AI companies require massive capital for compute and talent, successful AI companies reach decacorn status faster creating more mega-round targets, and LP appetite for AI exposure keeps fund sizes growing. Exit activity beginning to rebound adds the return-of-capital component that sustains LP reinvestment. The question is whether this represents genuine value creation or capital recycling within an increasingly top-heavy market.
