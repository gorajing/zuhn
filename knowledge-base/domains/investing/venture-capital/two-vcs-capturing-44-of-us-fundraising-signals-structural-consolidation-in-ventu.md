When two firms capture nearly half of all LP capital in a year, it is not an anomaly — it is a structural signal. Mega-funds benefit from brand flywheel effects: they attract the best founders, which produces strong returns, which pulls in more LP capital. Smaller managers are squeezed not because they perform worse necessarily, but because LPs increasingly concentrate commitments for operational efficiency and relationship simplicity.

For founders, this matters because it shapes the negotiating landscape: a smaller number of funds control a disproportionate share of available capital. For emerging managers, it underscores the importance of differentiation in strategy, not just returns. The 44% figure also raises systemic questions about whether VC is converging toward a structure more like asset management than its traditional partnership model.
