The essay uses naked mole rats — which maintain constant, low mortality risk regardless of age — as the biological proof that negligible senescence is achievable. This creates a powerful investment filter: does a longevity company aim to flatten the human mortality curve (constant low risk at any age) or merely push the curve a few years to the right?

This distinction matters because Gompertz-law exponential mortality means small rightward shifts yield diminishing returns — a 90-year-old still faces 1-in-6 annual death odds regardless of marginal improvements. Companies targeting the three 'hardcore longevity' categories (replacement via stembroids, bioengineering via gene therapy and reprogramming, and biostasis as backup) are working on curve-flattening rather than curve-shifting. For VC portfolio construction, this suggests concentrating in platform technologies that address aging's root mechanisms rather than diversifying across incremental therapeutic approaches.
