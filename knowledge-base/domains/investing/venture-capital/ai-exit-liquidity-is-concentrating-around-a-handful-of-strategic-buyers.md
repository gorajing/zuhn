The Nvidia-Groq deal fits a broader pattern where Nvidia has become one of the most consistent sources of real liquidity in AI infrastructure. This isn't a wide-open exit market — outcomes increasingly cluster around a handful of strategic buyers. Firms like Tiger Global and Insight Partners have each had multiple portfolio companies acquired by Nvidia recently.

This concentration has practical implications for both founders and investors. For founders, strategic relevance to specific acquirers under pressure matters more than generic market traction. For investors, the narrowing of exit paths means portfolio construction should account for which companies are most likely to face the kind of control-erosion anxiety that triggers defensive acquisitions. Safety has become easier to defend than belief, and big wins feel nostalgic not because opportunity disappeared, but because fewer people are willing to endure the path.
