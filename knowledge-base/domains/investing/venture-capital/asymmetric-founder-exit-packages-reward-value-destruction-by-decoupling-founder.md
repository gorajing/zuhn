Despite WeWork's near-bankruptcy, Adam Neumann's exit package guaranteed him $1.7B — including consulting fees, stock sales, and a credit line. Meanwhile, thousands of employees faced job losses. This asymmetry reveals a structural flaw in how venture-backed companies negotiate founder terms: the exit package decouples founder wealth from company health. Once Neumann had locked in personal liquidity through years of share sales and below-market-rate loans from the company, his incentive to preserve long-term value evaporated. The pattern suggests that investor due diligence should weight founder liquidity events as leading indicators — a founder who has already extracted hundreds of millions has fundamentally different incentives than one whose wealth is locked in equity. SoftBank's $15.4B total investment (more than Jamaica's GDP) was the cost of learning this lesson.
