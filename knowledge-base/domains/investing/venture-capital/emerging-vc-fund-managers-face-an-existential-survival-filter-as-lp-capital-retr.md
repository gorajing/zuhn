The emerging manager landscape has undergone a dramatic contraction, with fundraising dropping 75% from 2021 peaks. This isn't merely a cyclical correction — it reflects LPs consolidating into established managers with proven track records after being burned by the 2021 vintage. New fund managers now need what the article describes as 'a killer edge, perfect timing, or a powerful story' to survive.

This consolidation has second-order effects on the entire startup ecosystem. Emerging managers historically took the earliest, riskiest bets that established funds avoided. As these managers disappear, the pre-seed and seed ecosystem loses its most adventurous capital allocators. The startups that would have been funded by a first-time fund manager with domain expertise now face a gap — too small for large funds, too risky for the remaining cautious emerging managers.
