Historically, secondary transactions in venture were treated as distress signals or last-resort liquidity events. Selling LP stakes or founder shares on secondary markets carried stigma. But as IPO timelines stretched and venture fund lifecycles extended well beyond 10 years, secondaries became a necessary and eventually desirable part of the ecosystem.

Now major funds are raising dedicated secondaries vehicles, and LPs actively use them for portfolio rebalancing rather than just desperate exits. This legitimization creates a more liquid and efficient private market, which paradoxically makes primary venture investments more attractive by reducing the 'locked capital' risk that has historically deterred institutional allocators.
