Andreessen Horowitz closed $7.2B in new funds in April 2024, continuing the trend of established mega-funds commanding outsized LP allocations. When top-tier firms can raise at this scale, they crowd out emerging managers who must compete for the same institutional LP dollars. The math compounds: larger funds require larger exits to return meaningful multiples, pushing these firms toward late-stage and growth deals where they can deploy capital at scale. This structurally changes where innovation capital flows and which founders get backed.
