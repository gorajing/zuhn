The power law dynamics of venture capital mean that fund returns are dominated by a small number of outsized winners. As fund sizes expand into the billions, the mathematical bar for what constitutes a fund-returning investment rises dramatically — a $3B fund needs 3x the exit magnitude of a $1B fund just to match the same multiple.

This creates a paradox: the largest funds must concentrate on later-stage, larger deals where outcomes are more predictable but upside is capped, or take massive early-stage bets where the hit rate is low. Neither approach reliably produces the 3x+ net returns LPs expect from venture. Founders should recognize that mega-fund VCs face different incentive structures — management fees on large AUM can make the fund profitable for GPs even without strong returns, potentially misaligning GP and LP interests.
