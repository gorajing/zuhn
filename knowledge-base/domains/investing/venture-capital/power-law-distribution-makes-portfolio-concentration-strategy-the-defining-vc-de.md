Venture capital returns are not normally distributed — they follow a power law where a tiny fraction of investments generate the vast majority of returns. This has profound implications for fund strategy: the primary job of a VC is not to avoid losers but to be in the winners. Funds that spread capital too thinly across many bets without adequate reserves for follow-on investment in breakout companies systematically underperform. The power law also explains why top-quartile VC firms dramatically outperform median firms — access to and concentration in outlier deals compounds over multiple fund vintages.
