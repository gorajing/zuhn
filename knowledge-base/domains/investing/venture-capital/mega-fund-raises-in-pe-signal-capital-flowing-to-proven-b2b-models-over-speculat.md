Thoma Bravo closing a record $34.4B fund represents a broader trend of institutional capital gravitating toward predictable, recurring-revenue business models. This is a flight-to-quality signal—LPs want exposure to software economics but with the downside protection of growth-stage or buyout structures rather than seed-stage risk.

For B2B/SaaS founders, this creates favorable exit dynamics: there is deep buyer demand at growth and late stages. However, it also means the bar for early-stage B2B funding is rising, as investors can point to later-stage alternatives with better risk profiles. The implication is that early-stage B2B founders need to demonstrate capital efficiency and path-to-profitability earlier than in previous cycles.
