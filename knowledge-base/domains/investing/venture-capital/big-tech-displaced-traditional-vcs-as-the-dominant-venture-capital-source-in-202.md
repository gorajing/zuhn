In 2023, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, and Nvidia collectively deployed over $25 billion in venture deals, representing approximately 8% of total North American venture capital. This is not opportunistic participation — it reflects a structural shift where incumbents with platform distribution, cloud credits, and strategic motives are outcompeting financial VCs for access to the most promising AI and infrastructure startups.

For founders, this changes the calculus of which investors to pursue. Big Tech capital often comes with distribution leverage (Azure credits, AWS Marketplace, Google Cloud integrations) that pure financial VCs cannot offer. But it also introduces strategic risk: investors with product roadmaps of their own have motives beyond financial return, which can create conflicts as the startup scales. The 2023 data suggests this dynamic will intensify, not reverse.
