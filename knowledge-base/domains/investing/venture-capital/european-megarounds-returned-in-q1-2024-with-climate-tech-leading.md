After a prolonged post-2022 VC downturn, European megarounds staged a comeback in Q1 2024 with 42 rounds exceeding $100M — the highest count since the peak of Q2 2022. Notably, climate tech companies were the primary beneficiaries of these large checks, suggesting that deep-tech and infrastructure themes are driving recovery rather than the consumer AI frenzy dominating US headlines.

For founders and investors, this signals that Europe's capital concentration is shifting toward long-cycle, capital-intensive bets. Climate tech's dominance of megarounds may reflect both LP mandate pressure around ESG and the longer commercialization timelines that require large upfront capital. It also implies that European AI startups may still be competing for smaller rounds while climate infrastructure attracts the largest allocations.
