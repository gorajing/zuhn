Traditional fund formation requires a track record of returns, which creates a chicken-and-egg problem for first-time managers. Stebbings inverted this by spending years building distribution first — interviewing 1,000+ founders created relationships where guests began sending him deals unprompted. When a multi-billionaire founder offered to back him, 50 others followed within a week, each writing $100K-$250K checks.

This model works because media solves the hardest venture problem simultaneously: sourcing, winning deals, and servicing portfolio companies. One LinkedIn post generated $3-4M ARR for a portfolio company within 72 hours. The implication for emerging managers is that distribution is not a nice-to-have but a structural fundraising advantage — LPs can see tangible, measurable value-add rather than vague promises of 'network and expertise.'
