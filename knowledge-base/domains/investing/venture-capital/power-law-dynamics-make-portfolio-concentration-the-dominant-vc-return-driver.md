Equal Ventures highlights that venture fund returns are governed by the power law — a mathematical distribution where a handful of investments produce the vast majority of gains while most return little or nothing. This has profound implications for portfolio construction: rather than diversifying broadly to reduce risk (as in public markets), VCs must concentrate capital in their highest-conviction bets and aggressively follow on into winners. The power law also explains why top-quartile funds dramatically outperform median funds — a single breakout company can define an entire fund's returns, making access to and selection of outlier companies the only strategy that matters.
