Venture capital return distributions are highly skewed: the top 1-2 investments in a fund typically return more than all others combined. This has a counter-intuitive implication—the analytical rigor applied to the median investment in a portfolio has almost no effect on fund-level returns. What drives outcomes is whether the fund got into the eventual 100x company at all, and how much of it they own.

This shapes rational VC strategy in two ways. First, it justifies taking more shots at potentially large outcomes even at lower per-deal conviction, since missing the eventual winner is catastrophically costly. Second, it argues for aggressive reserve deployment into winners as they emerge (follow-on rounds) rather than spreading reserves evenly. Managing reserves and recycling capital from early exits specifically to concentrate into emerging winners is the execution layer through which power law dynamics are captured.
