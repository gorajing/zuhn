The AI Index 2026 Chapter 4 documents that 2025 broke every previous AI investment record. Global corporate AI investment reached approximately $581.69 billion in 2025, a 129.9% increase from $253.02B in 2024, with mergers and acquisitions rising 132.6% year over year. Private investment alone grew 127.5% to $344.66B. Generative AI companies accounted for $170.87B of that — nearly half of all private investment and more than 200% growth over 2024. The number of newly funded AI companies reached 3,499, up 70.8% year over year. Average AI investment event size grew 46% to $66.5 million. Funding events exceeding $1B nearly doubled: 28 in 2025, up from 15 in 2024. Notable events include OpenAI raising $40B at $300B valuation, Anthropic raising $13B at $183B valuation, Anysphere/Cursor at $29.3B, and Thinking Machines Lab's $2B seed at $12B. This is not a 'AI winter' data point in any direction — the investment surge is broad (more deals, more companies) and concentrated (larger rounds, more mega-events) simultaneously.
