Conventional VC wisdom held that smaller funds outperform because they can move faster, take larger ownership stakes, and aren't constrained by the need to deploy massive capital. The emerging counter-narrative—that mega funds are now outperforming middle-market funds—challenges this assumption and has real implications for LP allocation strategy.

Possible explanations include: mega funds' ability to lead later rounds and protect ownership, preferential access to the most competitive deals, and their capacity to provide portfolio companies with strategic resources beyond capital. If this trend holds, it suggests the VC industry is experiencing winner-take-more dynamics similar to the portfolio companies they fund.
