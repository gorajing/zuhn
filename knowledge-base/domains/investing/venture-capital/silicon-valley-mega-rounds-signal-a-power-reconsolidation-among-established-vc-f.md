The pullback of aggressive crossover investors — Tiger Global and SoftBank — has not reduced available capital but rather reconsolidated it among established VC firms like General Catalyst, Andreessen Horowitz, and Lightspeed. Single rounds now exceed $40B, raising questions about whether valuations reflect genuine innovation or a supply-demand imbalance in late-stage capital.

This reconsolidation matters because it changes the power dynamics for founders. When capital was fragmented across many eager investors, founders had leverage. Now, with fewer dominant players writing larger checks, the negotiating dynamics shift. The debate over whether these valuations are sustainable echoes prior cycles, but the concentration of decision-making power in fewer firms is a structural change worth watching.
