The emergence of dedicated funds targeting the AI-longevity intersection — including Menlo Ventures and Anthropic's $100M AI fund and companies like Brenig Therapeutics raising $65M for cancer treatments — signals this is evolving from a speculative thesis to a recognized investment category. AI's ability to simulate molecular interactions and predict therapeutic outcomes compresses traditional drug development timelines.

For investors, this convergence creates a new evaluation framework: longevity startups are increasingly judged not just on their biological insights but on the sophistication of their AI infrastructure. The companies that will win are those that treat AI not as a marketing buzzword but as core computational infrastructure for accelerating discovery.
