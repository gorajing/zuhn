The data showing that 44% of US unicorn founders are immigrants reveals a structural dependency in the venture ecosystem that most investors underweight. This isn't a marginal effect—nearly half of the companies generating outsized returns are founded by people who chose to relocate to build their companies.

For VCs, this has direct portfolio implications: funds that systematically source from immigrant founder networks or have partners who understand cross-cultural founding dynamics may have a structural edge. It also means that immigration policy changes represent a genuine macro risk to US venture returns, not just a political abstraction. India's dominance in the data suggests that specific diaspora networks and educational pipelines (particularly IIT → US tech → founding) represent identifiable deal flow channels.
