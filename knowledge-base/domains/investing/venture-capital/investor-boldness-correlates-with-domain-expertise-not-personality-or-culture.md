Graham observes that Silicon Valley investors consistently outmaneuver Boston investors not because they are cowboys, but because they understand startups deeply enough to act decisively. The Facebook example illustrates this perfectly: Boston VCs saw the deal first but passed, while west coast investors recognized the asymmetric upside and moved fast.

This reframes boldness as a competence signal rather than a personality trait. Conservative investing in startups often masks insufficient domain knowledge — investors who don't deeply understand the space default to caution because they can't distinguish promising risk from reckless risk. The practical implication is that founders should evaluate investors not by their stated risk appetite but by the depth of their domain understanding, since the two are correlated.
