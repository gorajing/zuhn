Despite every firm pitching proprietary pipelines and unique lenses, the data tells a collective story. According to PitchBook data, roughly 40% of all deals led by top-20 firms are co-invested with at least one other top-20 firm. Even historically independent firms like Benchmark and Redpoint now syndicate a significant share of their deals.

This coordination is rational at scale: syndication helps top firms spread risk, validate pricing, maintain access to follow-on opportunities, and satisfy LPs who prefer familiar logos. In an environment where downstream capital is king, signaling matters as much as conviction—nothing signals like brand-name VCs moving together.

The structural insight is that the incentive to syndicate creates a self-reinforcing loop: LPs want dependable exposure, GPs want predictable downstream capital, and founders optimize for the language that passes through both gates. The system doesn't chase risk—it absorbs and smooths it.
