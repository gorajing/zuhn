The 2025 global startup ranking reveals a structural shift: companies like SpaceX ($210B+), Stripe ($70B+), and Revolut ($75B) remain private despite valuations that would make them top-tier public companies. This is not a temporary anomaly but a mature ecosystem where secondary markets, crossover funds, and sovereign wealth provide the liquidity founders once needed IPOs to access. The implication for investors is that the highest-returning companies may never enter public markets, forcing a rethinking of portfolio construction that historically assumed eventual public exits as the primary liquidity event.
