The data reveals a stark divergence in venture capital: AI startups absorbed 64% of U.S. deal value in the first half of the year, while traditional VC funds are experiencing a prolonged fundraising drought. This is not simply AI being hot — it represents a structural reallocation of capital that is starving non-AI ventures of funding.

The concentration creates a self-reinforcing cycle: LPs see AI returns and allocate more to AI-focused funds, which drives up AI valuations, which creates more LP interest. Meanwhile, non-AI founders face a capital desert regardless of business quality. The Mira Murati example ($12B valuation, no product) illustrates how extreme this concentration has become — capital is flowing to AI team pedigree rather than product-market fit.
