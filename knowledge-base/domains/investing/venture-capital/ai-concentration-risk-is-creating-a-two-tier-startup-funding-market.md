The venture capital market has split into two distinct economies: AI startups swimming in capital and everyone else struggling to raise. This concentration mirrors previous hype cycles (crypto, cleantech) but at unprecedented scale — 40% of all VC capital flowing to a single sector creates massive misallocation risk.

The 'zombiecorn' phenomenon — companies with unicorn valuations but deteriorating fundamentals — suggests the AI funding boom is producing quantity over quality. When the correction comes, the damage won't be limited to AI; it will further reduce LP appetite for venture as an asset class, compounding the emerging manager crisis and reducing overall startup formation rates.
