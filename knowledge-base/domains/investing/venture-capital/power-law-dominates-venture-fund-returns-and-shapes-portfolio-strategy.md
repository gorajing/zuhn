The power law in venture capital means that a tiny fraction of investments — often just 1-2 deals — drive the majority of a fund's returns. This has profound implications for how VCs construct portfolios and make follow-on decisions. Unlike traditional investing where diversification smooths returns, VC requires concentration in winners.

This means the optimal VC strategy is not about avoiding losers but about ensuring exposure to the rare massive winners. Fund managers must size positions, structure reserves, and make follow-on decisions with the understanding that most investments will return little, and the fund's success depends on catching and doubling down on outliers. This also explains why top-tier VC firms consistently outperform — access to the best deals compounds the power law advantage.
