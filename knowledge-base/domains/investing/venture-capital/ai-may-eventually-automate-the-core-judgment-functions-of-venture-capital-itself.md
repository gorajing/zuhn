There is a deeply ironic dynamic emerging in venture capital: investors are enthusiastically funding AI companies whose capabilities could eventually automate core VC functions like deal sourcing, market analysis, and investment judgment. The question is whether algorithmic insight can compete with human judgment in venture investing.

The bull case for human VCs is that venture investing requires relationship depth, founder psychology reads, and contrarian conviction that resist quantification. The bear case is that most VC returns come from a small number of outlier bets, and AI pattern recognition across thousands of data points might identify those outliers more reliably than human intuition. Either way, the reflexive nature of VCs funding their own potential replacement creates a fascinating strategic tension — they can't afford not to fund AI, even if AI eventually commoditizes their judgment.
