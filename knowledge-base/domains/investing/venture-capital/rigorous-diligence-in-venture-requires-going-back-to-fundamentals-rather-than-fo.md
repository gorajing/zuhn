The TDK Ventures/6Pages piece on 'Doing Diligence Well' advocates a return-to-fundamentals approach at a time when AI hype has compressed diligence timelines and inflated valuations. In bull markets, narrative momentum substitutes for analysis — investors fear missing out more than they fear overpaying, so checks move faster with less scrutiny. The hangover is overfunded companies with weak fundamentals that struggle to grow into their valuations.

Structured diligence — examining customer concentration, cohort retention, gross margin trajectory, and founder-market fit — is less glamorous than pattern-matching on team pedigree and market buzz, but it's the skill that separates consistent performers from tourists. Investors who build and maintain repeatable diligence frameworks compound their judgment over time; those who rely on market temperature tend to perform well only when momentum is broadly favorable.
