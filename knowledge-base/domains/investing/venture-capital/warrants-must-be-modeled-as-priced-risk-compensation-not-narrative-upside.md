Mezzanine lenders attach warrants as an equity kicker to compensate for the higher risk of subordinated debt. The coverage percentage, strike price, and target ownership are all negotiable inputs that mechanically determine the number of warrant shares and their value across exit scenarios. When founders or advisors treat warrants as a minor sweetener or narrative promise of alignment, they fail to calculate the actual dilution and its sensitivity to exit outcomes.

The modeling discipline here is critical: warrant value should be calculated across low, base, and high exit cases to show how sensitive the equity kicker is to company performance. In strong exit scenarios, warrants can represent a substantial portion of the lender's total return — meaning the founder gave away significant equity that they may not have priced into their mental model of the deal. Conversely, in weak exits, warrants may be worthless, concentrating all lender return into debt terms. This asymmetry means both parties need scenario analysis, not point estimates, to understand what they actually agreed to.
