Traditional multi-tab financial models accumulate complexity that works against their purpose — formulas reference wrong cells, partners use different versions, and founders can't trace the assumptions. A single-tab return model that connects revenue, margins, raise sizes, valuation, and exit multiples forces both founders and investors to agree on the same inputs and see the same outputs.

The practical advantage is speed under pressure: you can toggle a pre-money valuation live in a partner meeting without risking a cascade of broken references. This matters because most investment decisions are made in time-constrained settings where model opacity becomes a liability. The constraint of one tab acts as a forcing function for clarity — if it doesn't fit on one sheet, you're probably modeling noise rather than signal.
