A survey of nearly 300 private-capital professionals reveals that firms are doubling down on data consolidation and AI to sharpen sourcing and evaluation capabilities. As fundraising conditions improve, managers emphasize efficiency and demonstrable value creation to differentiate themselves from competitors.

This signals a structural shift in how private capital operates. Traditional relationship-driven deal sourcing is being augmented — and in some cases replaced — by systematic, data-driven approaches. Firms that invest in consolidating their data infrastructure and applying AI to evaluate opportunities will have a compounding advantage over time, similar to how quantitative approaches transformed public markets. For startups, this means investor due diligence is becoming more data-intensive, making clean metrics and transparent reporting increasingly important for fundraising success.
