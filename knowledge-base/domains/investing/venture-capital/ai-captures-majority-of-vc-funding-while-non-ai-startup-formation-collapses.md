The venture capital market has undergone a dramatic structural shift where AI startups now absorb more than half of all global venture funding. In the US specifically, AI captures 62.7% of VC dollars at $192.7B, creating what amounts to an 'AI or bust' funding environment.

The most striking signal is the collapse in total startup count from 4,430 in 2022 to just 823 — an 81% decline. This suggests the funding concentration isn't just tilting toward AI but actively starving non-AI ventures of capital. For founders outside AI, the implication is stark: either integrate AI into your value proposition or face a dramatically harder fundraising environment. For investors, this concentration creates both herd risk and potential contrarian opportunity in overlooked non-AI sectors.
