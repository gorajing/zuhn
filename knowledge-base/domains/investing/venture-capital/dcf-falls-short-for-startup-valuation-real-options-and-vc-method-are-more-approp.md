Discounted Cash Flow analysis requires reasonably predictable future cash flows — a condition startups violate by design. Startups are better understood as bundles of real options: the option to expand into new markets, pivot the product, or be acquired at a premium. Real Options valuation, borrowed from derivatives pricing, explicitly prices these strategic choices. The VC Method, which back-calculates today's valuation from an expected exit multiple, aligns incentives with how investors actually think. Founders who internalize these frameworks can engage more credibly with investors and push back when they are being undervalued by investors using simplistic models.
