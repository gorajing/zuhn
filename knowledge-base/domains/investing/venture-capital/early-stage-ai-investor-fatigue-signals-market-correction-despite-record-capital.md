While AI startups collectively raised nearly $30 billion in the first half of 2024, indicators suggest early-stage investors are developing AI fatigue. This divergence between capital volume and investor enthusiasm is significant — it suggests the easy-money phase of AI investing is ending while growth-stage deals continue to attract capital.

This pattern mirrors previous tech cycles where early-stage investors, being closest to the raw deal flow, are first to recognize saturation in a category. The implication is that AI startups now need stronger differentiation and clearer paths to revenue rather than riding the AI wave alone. For founders, this means the bar for seed and pre-seed AI rounds is rising even as headline funding numbers remain strong.
