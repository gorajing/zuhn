Data from investors who backed at least 3 companies per YC Demo Day from 2018-2020 shows striking return distributions: bottom quartile achieved 3.3x TVPI, median 5x, top quartile 8x, and top decile 15x. The bottom quartile of Demo Day investing outperforms the top quartile of the broader venture capital market.

This validates a portfolio construction thesis: at the accelerator stage, broad exposure to a high-quality dealflow filter (YC's selection process) matters more than individual company picking. The implication extends beyond YC — any sufficiently selective filter applied at the earliest stage should produce similar dynamics where systematic deployment beats selective conviction. The key variable isn't investor skill in picking winners, it's access to a pre-filtered pipeline where the base rate of success is already elevated.
