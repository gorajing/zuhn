The evolving VC landscape shows a clear trend toward polarization between mega-funds (like ARCH Venture Partners closing $3B+ and Headline raising $865M) and smaller specialized funds ($100-200M range targeting specific sectors or geographies). This barbell distribution means mid-size generalist funds face increasing pressure to differentiate or specialize.

For founders, this bifurcation means matching fund size to stage and sector matters more than ever. Mega-funds provide follow-on capacity and brand, while specialized smaller funds offer deeper domain expertise and more partner attention. The competitive dynamics between these tiers create different incentive structures around deal terms, board involvement, and portfolio support.
