The MSCI Emerging Markets Index returned only 3.01% annualized over 10 years versus the S&P 500's 12.39%, largely because currency depreciation erodes gains. Top-quartile VCs, however, delivered 25.6% IRR over a similar period. This power-law return profile means that even after accounting for severe depreciation (e.g., Nigerian Naira losing 80%+ of its USD value), a winning VC portfolio can still deliver compelling absolute returns. The implication is that VC is not just one option for EM exposure — it may be the only asset class where the math works after currency adjustment.
