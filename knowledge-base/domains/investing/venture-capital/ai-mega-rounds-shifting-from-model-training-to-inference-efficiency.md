The shift in AI mega-rounds toward infrastructure, inference optimization, and vertical specialization marks a maturation of the AI investment thesis. Investors are moving capital away from the expensive and increasingly commoditized model training race toward companies that make existing models cheaper, faster, and more specialized to deploy.

This reflects a broader pattern in technology platform shifts: once the foundational layer stabilizes, value creation moves up the stack to efficiency and application layers. For founders and investors, this signals that building another frontier model is no longer the highest-return bet—instead, the opportunity is in making AI deployment practical and economical at scale through better inference, specialized vertical solutions, and cost optimization.
