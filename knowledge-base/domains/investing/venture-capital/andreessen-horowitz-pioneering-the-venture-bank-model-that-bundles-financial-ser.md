Andreessen Horowitz has evolved from a traditional venture fund into what's being called a 'venture bank' — a platform that combines venture investing with broader financial services, media operations, and strategic advisory. This model goes beyond the standard GP/LP structure by creating multiple revenue streams and deeper portfolio company lock-in.

The venture bank model has significant implications for the VC ecosystem. It raises barriers to entry for smaller funds who can't replicate the full-service offering, while simultaneously creating stronger network effects around the platform. For founders, it means choosing a lead investor increasingly means choosing an entire operating system for your company. The question is whether this consolidation benefits or constrains the broader startup ecosystem's diversity.
