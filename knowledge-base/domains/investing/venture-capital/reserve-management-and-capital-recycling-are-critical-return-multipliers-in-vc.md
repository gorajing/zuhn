Reserve strategy — how much capital a fund holds back for follow-on investments in winners — directly impacts whether a fund can maintain ownership in its best companies through later rounds. Under-reserving forces a fund to get diluted in its winners, while over-reserving means less capital deployed into new opportunities.

Capital recycling adds another lever: by reinvesting proceeds from early exits or distributions back into the fund before the investment period closes, GPs can effectively deploy more than 100% of committed capital. This magnifies returns when deployed well but requires careful management to avoid overextension. The interplay between reserves and recycling is a key differentiator between top-quartile and median fund performance.
