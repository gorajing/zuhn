The power law in venture capital means that a tiny fraction of investments generate the vast majority of returns. This mathematical reality has a critical operational implication: the ability to double down on winners through reserves matters far more than optimizing initial check sizes or portfolio breadth. Funds that under-reserve miss their chance to increase ownership in breakout companies, while funds that over-reserve deploy too little capital into the discovery phase.

Capital recycling — reinvesting early proceeds back into the fund before the investment period closes — amplifies this dynamic by effectively increasing the total deployable capital without raising a larger fund. The combination of disciplined reserves and recycling creates a compounding advantage that separates top-quartile funds from median performers.
