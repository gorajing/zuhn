European VC funds that emerged between 2012 and 2015 are now attempting to raise their third vehicles while simultaneously deploying their second. They are hitting resistance from LPs because the first two funds have not demonstrated realized returns—exits are scarce, portfolio companies remain unprofitable, and valuations from boom-era rounds are too high for strategic acquisitions.

This creates a trapped-capital problem: startups are too expensive for PE acquirers (negative EBITDA), too large for additional VC rounds from small funds, and too early for IPO. The underlying cause is insufficient rigor on operational metrics like CAC, CAC/LTV ratio, and EBITDA during the investment period. Funds are now scrambling to force profitability across portfolios, but the structural damage to LP trust may take a full fund cycle to repair.
