Index Ventures is already realizing $11 billion in returns from companies like Figma, Wiz, and Scale AI—while most VC firms are still waiting for exits. The newsletter argues Index is becoming 'the new Sequoia' by backing category-defining companies with high conviction and riding them to massive outcomes.

This signals a shift in VC power dynamics. While legacy firms struggle to adapt to a changing market, Index's portfolio concentration and willingness to back companies through extended private periods is paying off dramatically. For founders, this means the most value-added VC partnerships may increasingly come from firms willing to take concentrated positions rather than those managing the largest AUMs.
