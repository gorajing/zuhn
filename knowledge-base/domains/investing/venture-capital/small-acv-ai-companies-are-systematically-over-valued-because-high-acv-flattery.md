Venture capital pattern-matches on topline revenue growth and rarely on real TAM shape. In the 2026 AI environment, this produces a specific pathology: businesses like OpenRouter (5% take rate on LLM API calls) show impressive growth but require capturing 100% of their theoretical market to justify their valuations. In contrast, high-ACV businesses (Harvey, Lora, enterprise AI coding tools) at $50-100k+ ACV per customer can support aggressive valuations even capturing 5-10% market share. Comparison reference: Revenue Cat with 50% mobile-subscription management share achieves only modest revenue at half-percent take rates, so the theoretical-TAM math for small-take-rate businesses rarely survives contact with reality. Practical investment rule: when evaluating AI investments in 2026, separately assess ACV and required market share; a high-ACV business at 10% capture is a better investment than a low-ACV business at the same valuation requiring 80% capture, because the execution risk of 80% capture is dramatically higher in competitive environments.
