The conventional criticism of mega funds — that they're too large to generate meaningful returns — misunderstands how power law dynamics scale. As the ceiling for startup outcomes rises (potential trillion-dollar companies), the optimal fund size rises proportionally. A $10B fund needs a $100B+ outcome to return the fund; these outcomes are now plausible in AI and platform businesses.

This reframes the entire VC landscape: top quartile performance is meaningless in a power law distribution because returns concentrate in the extreme tail. The funds positioned to capture those outliers — through larger check sizes, deeper reserves for follow-ons, and multi-stage strategies — are structurally advantaged. The implication is that venture capital itself is consolidating around power law logic, not just at the startup level but at the fund level.
