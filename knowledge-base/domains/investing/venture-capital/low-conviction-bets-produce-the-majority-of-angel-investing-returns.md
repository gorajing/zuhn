Lenny Rachitsky tracked his confidence level (OK, Good, or Great) for each of his 140+ angel investments. Reviewing the data, only a third of his biggest winners were rated 'Great' at the time of investing. This means a strategy of only backing high-conviction deals would have eliminated two-thirds of his best outcomes.

This aligns with AngelList's research showing seed-stage investors maximize expected returns by broadly indexing into every credible deal rather than trying to pick winners selectively. The math of power-law returns means a single 100x or 1000x outcome dominates portfolio performance, and the inability to predict which deal will produce that outcome makes broad deployment of capital the rational strategy. The practical implication: invest uniform check sizes and lower your conviction threshold for participation.
