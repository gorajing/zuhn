A comprehensive pre-seed due diligence checklist looks fundamentally different from Series A or later frameworks. At pre-seed, there are no meaningful financial metrics, no proven unit economics, and often no product-market fit to evaluate. The relevant signals are almost entirely about the founders: their domain expertise, speed of learning, ability to attract talent, and clarity of insight about the problem.

This creates an interesting information asymmetry — VCs who apply later-stage frameworks to pre-seed deals either pass on great opportunities (because the 'data' isn't there yet) or develop false confidence from analyzing metrics that are essentially noise at that scale. The best pre-seed investors develop pattern recognition around founder qualities and market timing rather than trying to force quantitative rigor onto inherently qualitative decisions.
