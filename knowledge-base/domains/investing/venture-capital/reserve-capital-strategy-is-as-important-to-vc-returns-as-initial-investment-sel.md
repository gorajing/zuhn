Many VC funds deploy too much capital upfront and lack reserves to participate in later rounds of their best companies. As winners raise larger rounds at higher valuations, funds without reserves get diluted out of meaningful ownership. The recycling dimension — returning early capital to reinvest — compounds this further.

A fund with strong reserve discipline can maintain or increase its ownership percentage in breakout companies through Series B, C, and beyond, while funds without reserves watch their ownership percentage fall from 15% to 3% by the time a company IPOs. Since power law returns are driven by a small number of companies, preserving ownership in those companies is one of the highest-leverage decisions in fund management.
