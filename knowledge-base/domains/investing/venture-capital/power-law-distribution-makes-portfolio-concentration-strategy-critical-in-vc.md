Venture capital returns are uniquely governed by the power law — unlike most asset classes where returns approximate a normal distribution, VC fund performance is dominated by a small number of outsized winners. This has profound implications for fund strategy: managers must construct portfolios that maximize exposure to potential outliers rather than optimizing for median outcomes.

This means that reserves (capital set aside for follow-on investments in winners) and recycling (reinvesting early returns into the fund) become critical levers. A fund that deploys all capital in initial checks without reserving for winners will systematically underperform, because it cannot double down when the power law reveals which companies are breakout successes. The best-performing firms consistently maintain 40-60% of fund size in reserves.
