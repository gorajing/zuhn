Deeptech startups differ fundamentally from traditional tech in their return profile. While they require longer time to market and larger early capital, they build defensible IP moats, secure non-dilutive grants (SBIR, Horizon Europe, DARPA), and get acquired at strategic premiums by pharma, defense, energy, and government buyers. Traction looks different — published research, LOIs from utilities, government lab validation — rather than ARR.

This means traditional VC evaluation frameworks break down for deeptech. Investors need deep technical literacy to evaluate synthetic biology or fusion, and must structure funds with longer horizons. The growing ecosystem of deeptech-focused firms (Lux Capital, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Deep Science Ventures) suggests the asset class is maturing, but the evaluation gap still creates opportunities for technically-literate investors willing to hold through long R&D cycles.
