UK fintech Onfido — an identity verification company — was in acquisition talks in early 2024, with its first backer reportedly set to make a 320x return. This is notable because it challenges the implicit assumption that venture-scale returns require IPO exits. M&A can produce just as extreme multiples when entry valuation is low enough and the company occupies a durable position in infrastructure.

The Onfido case also highlights the compounding advantage of being a first institutional backer. A 320x return means a $100K check becomes $32M — the magnitude of outcome is entirely a function of entry price and longevity of hold, not the exit vehicle. For early-stage investors, the lesson is to prioritize getting into durable infrastructure bets at the earliest possible stage rather than optimizing for liquidity path.
