The conventional wisdom that heavy YC exposure in a venture portfolio represents dangerous concentration is challenged by performance data. YC startups outperform peers on survival rates, scaling velocity, and exit outcomes due to compounding network effects — alumni hiring alumni, batch-mates becoming customers, and shared institutional knowledge reducing common failure modes.

This reframes portfolio construction for seed and early-stage investors. The 'over-concentration' concern applies standard diversification logic from public markets where correlation is the enemy, but in venture the question is whether a signal source (like YC) has persistent alpha. If YC systematically improves startup outcomes through network effects rather than just selection, then limiting exposure is leaving returns on the table disguised as prudent risk management.
