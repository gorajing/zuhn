PitchBook's analysis of VC horizon IRR by first-year deployment speed challenges the common pressure on fund managers to deploy capital quickly. The data suggests that patience — taking time to find the right deals rather than rushing to put money to work — produces better long-term returns.

This has practical implications for LP due diligence and GP fund management. LPs should be cautious about penalizing GPs for slow early deployment, and GPs should resist the temptation to deploy quickly just to show activity. The finding aligns with the broader value investing principle that discipline and selectivity compound over time, even in venture's power-law distribution where a single outlier can define fund performance.
