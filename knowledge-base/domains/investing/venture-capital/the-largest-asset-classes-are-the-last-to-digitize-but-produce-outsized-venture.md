Real estate at $228T globally dwarfs equities and debt combined, yet remained largely analog through multiple waves of tech disruption that transformed smaller categories. Despite this late start, proptech has already produced more billion-dollar companies in 15 years than almost any consumer services category except e-commerce.

This pattern suggests a venture capital heuristic: look for the largest industries with the lowest tech penetration. The combination of massive TAM and low digitization creates a long runway of compounding opportunity. The counterintuitive insight is that the very factors making these industries slow to adopt tech — regulatory complexity, fragmented ownership, physical asset dependency — also create deeper moats for the startups that do break through. Early entrants in these massive, slow-moving categories capture disproportionate value precisely because the barriers to entry that delayed digitization also protect the pioneers.
