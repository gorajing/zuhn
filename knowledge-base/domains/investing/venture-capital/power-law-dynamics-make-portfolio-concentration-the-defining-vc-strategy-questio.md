Equal Ventures highlights how the power law fundamentally shapes venture capital strategy. Unlike normal distributions where average performers matter, VC returns are dominated by extreme outliers — a single investment can return an entire fund. This mathematical reality means that portfolio construction must optimize for maximum upside exposure rather than loss avoidance. The implication is that reserves (capital held back for follow-on investments in winners) become the critical strategic lever, because doubling down on power law winners compounds the effect rather than diluting it across mediocre performers.
