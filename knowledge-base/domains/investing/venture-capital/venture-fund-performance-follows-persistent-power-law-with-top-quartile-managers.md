Performance data across recent fund vintages confirms the persistent power law in venture capital returns. Top-quartile managers don't just outperform in good years — they maintain their edge across entire market cycles including downturns. This creates a self-reinforcing dynamic: the best managers attract the best deal flow, which generates the best returns, which attracts more capital.

For LPs, this means manager selection dominates all other allocation decisions. Timing the venture market matters far less than getting into the right funds. For founders, it means the signaling value of your lead investor compounds — a top-quartile firm on your cap table opens doors that capital alone cannot. The practical implication is that founders should optimize for investor quality over valuation in early rounds.
