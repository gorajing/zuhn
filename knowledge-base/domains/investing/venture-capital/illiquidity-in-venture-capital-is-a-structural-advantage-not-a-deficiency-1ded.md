Illiquidity in venture capital is commonly viewed as a drawback — capital is locked up for 7-10+ years with no easy exit. However, this constraint actually serves as a behavioral forcing function that prevents investors from selling too early.

Public market investors routinely sell winners prematurely due to loss aversion, rebalancing rules, or short-term thinking. VC's structural illiquidity removes this option, effectively forcing LPs and GPs to hold through the exponential growth phase of their best investments. The compounding that occurs during years 5-10 of a breakout company is precisely when most of the value is created. Funds that attempt to engineer early liquidity through secondary sales often capture only a fraction of the terminal value.
