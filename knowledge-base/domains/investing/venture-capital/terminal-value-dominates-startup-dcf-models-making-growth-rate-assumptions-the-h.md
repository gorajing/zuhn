The DCF model calculates terminal value as Final Year FCF × (1 + g) / (WACC − g), where small changes in g (terminal growth rate) or WACC produce enormous swings in output. For startups with compressed 5-year forecast windows and hockey-stick growth assumptions, terminal value often represents 70-90% of total enterprise value.

This mathematical reality means that sophisticated investors will interrogate terminal growth assumptions more aggressively than near-term revenue forecasts. Founders should understand that their valuation argument is essentially a terminal value argument—and they need to justify why their long-term growth rate is sustainable relative to the industry and economy. A 1% change in terminal growth rate can shift valuation by 20-30%, making this the highest-leverage negotiation point in any DCF-based fundraise.
