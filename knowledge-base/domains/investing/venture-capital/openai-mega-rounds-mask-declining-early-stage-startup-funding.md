Q1 2025 saw US startup funding hit $85.5B, nearly doubling previous periods. But this headline is almost entirely an artifact of OpenAI's record $40B round. Strip that out and the picture is bleak for early-stage founders — deal volume declined and capital concentrated further into late-stage AI companies.

This concentration effect is important for founders and smaller fund managers to understand. The 'venture is booming' narrative driven by mega-rounds creates a false sense of market health. Early-stage founders should plan for a tighter funding environment regardless of what aggregate numbers suggest, and VCs evaluating market conditions need to look past the top-line figures.
