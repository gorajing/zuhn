In Q1 2026, every confirmed fund close—regardless of sector—framed its strategy around AI. Infrastructure funds cited AI's demand for energy and compute. Critical minerals funds cited AI's need for raw materials. Climate funds cited AI-enabled grid optimization. This represents a phase shift: AI is no longer a differentiator but table stakes in LP conversations. The practical consequence is that funds without an AI narrative angle are experiencing harder conversations with capital allocators, even in traditionally non-tech categories. For founders and fund managers alike, the ability to credibly articulate an AI connection has become a prerequisite for fundraising.
