---
The QSBS provision (Section 1202) allows investors to exclude up to $10 million or 10x their cost basis in capital gains from federal taxes when selling stock in qualifying small businesses held for more than 5 years. For VC funds, this creates a powerful structural advantage that compounds across portfolio returns.

Sophisticated funds structure investments specifically to maximize QSBS eligibility: ensuring portfolio companies are C-corps with under $50M in gross assets at time of investment, holding positions through the 5-year requirement, and using stacking strategies across entities. The difference between a fund that systematically optimizes for QSBS and one that ignores it can be 15-20% in after-tax returns to LPs. This is a durable edge because tax code complexity creates information asymmetry that benefits knowledgeable managers.
