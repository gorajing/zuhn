Peter Walker's data shows that venture funding in 2025 is concentrating into fewer companies than ever before. AI-focused startups and those based in the Bay Area are capturing a disproportionate share of available capital, talent, and media attention.

This concentration effect creates a self-reinforcing cycle: the best talent gravitates toward well-funded AI companies, which produce better results, which attract more capital. Startups in smaller markets or non-AI sectors face an increasingly steep climb for visibility and funding. For founders, this means geographic and thematic positioning matters more than ever — and for investors, the herd effect may be creating both opportunity in overlooked markets and risk in overcrowded ones.
