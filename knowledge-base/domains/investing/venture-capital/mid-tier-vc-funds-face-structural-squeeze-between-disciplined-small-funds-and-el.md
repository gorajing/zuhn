Venture capital returns are increasingly concentrating at two extremes: small, disciplined funds that maintain ownership efficiency, and elite, large funds with privileged deal flow. Funds stuck in the middle — without a clear structural advantage in either direction — face a compounding disadvantage.

This polarization means that fund strategy must be intentional about positioning. A $50-100M fund competing for the same deals as $500M funds without the brand, network, or follow-on capacity is structurally disadvantaged. Conversely, a small fund that stays disciplined on check size and ownership targets can generate outsized returns through concentration. The implication for founders is that the fund's structural position increasingly predicts the quality of support and follow-on dynamics they can expect.
