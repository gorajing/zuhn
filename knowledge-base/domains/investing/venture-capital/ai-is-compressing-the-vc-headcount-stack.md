The structural labor of venture—deal sourcing, market mapping, reference checks, due diligence memos—is being automated by AI tools. Firms that built large analyst and associate benches to handle volume work are now shrinking those teams dramatically. This isn't cyclical headcount reduction tied to a down market; it's a permanent compression of the labor-per-deal ratio.

The implication for the industry is a shift toward leaner firms running larger portfolios with fewer people. The competitive moat migrates from team size to model quality: who has better proprietary data, better AI tooling, and better judgment at the top of the funnel. For emerging managers, this cuts both ways—less overhead to compete, but also harder to differentiate on operational depth when incumbents can match it with software.
