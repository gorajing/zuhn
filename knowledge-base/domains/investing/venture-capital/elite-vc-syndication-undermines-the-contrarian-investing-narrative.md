The VC industry heavily markets itself on contrarian conviction — backing founders others overlook. But PitchBook data reveals that nearly 40% of elite VC deals include at least one other top-tier firm, suggesting syndication and consensus play a much larger role than the narrative admits.

This matters for founders because it reframes fundraising strategy. If top VCs frequently co-invest, getting one marquee firm on board may trigger a cascade of interest from peers rather than requiring you to find a lone contrarian believer. It also means the 'contrarian bet' framing in pitch meetings may be more performative than operational — VCs spread risk through syndication while claiming conviction positioning.
