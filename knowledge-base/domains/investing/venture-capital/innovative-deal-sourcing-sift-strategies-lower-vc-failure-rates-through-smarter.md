Sharon Vosmek's 'sift' strategy at Astia Fund challenges the dominant VC deal sourcing model, which relies heavily on warm introductions and network effects — a method that systematically biases toward pattern-matching on founder demographics rather than business fundamentals. The sift approach introduces more structured evaluation criteria earlier in the pipeline, aiming to lower failure rates by making smarter selections rather than relying on volume. This matters beyond Astia because it points to a broader industry recognition that traditional sourcing leaves alpha on the table. For founders, the practical implication is that funds using systematic sourcing may evaluate pitches on fundamentally different criteria than network-driven funds, making it worth understanding a fund's sourcing methodology before tailoring your approach.
