The wave of fund launches in Q4 2024—including Faber Deep Tech (€60M, Iberian deeptech), Dawn Capital V (€400M, European B2B), Chemistry Capital ($350M, sustainability), and Seedcamp VII (€200M, fintech/healthtech/climatetech)—reveals where LPs and GPs see the next decade of returns. The pattern shows capital flowing toward sectors with defensible intellectual property, regulatory moats, and longer development cycles.

This shift has practical implications for founders choosing where to build. European deep tech startups now have access to dedicated capital pools that didn't exist five years ago, while US consumer tech founders face a more competitive fundraising environment with fewer new funds targeting their space. The trend also suggests that LPs have recalibrated their return expectations, accepting longer hold periods in exchange for more defensible portfolio companies.
