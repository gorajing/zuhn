In 2025, AI startups absorbed $192.7B — the first time a single category captured more than half of global VC dollars. But this capital pools dramatically at the top: Anthropic, OpenAI, xAI, and Mistral together captured nearly one-third of all global venture money. Late-stage AI rounds surged 66% YoY while seed rounds held flat but grew far more selective.

This marks what investors call Phase 2 of AI venture investing — the shift from broad FOMO-driven experimentation to disciplined consolidation around proven scale. For founders, this means the fundraising environment rewards demonstrated revenue momentum and deep technical moats over novelty. The era of raising on AI hype alone is over; capital now flows to companies that can deploy it at venture speed with clear paths to dominance.
