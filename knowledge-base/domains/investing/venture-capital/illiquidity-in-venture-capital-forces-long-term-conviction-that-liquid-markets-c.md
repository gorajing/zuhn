In liquid markets, investors can exit at any moment — and behavioral biases like loss aversion and panic selling routinely cause them to exit at the worst times. Venture capital eliminates this option entirely: once committed, capital is locked for 7-10 years regardless of how scary the macro environment becomes. This illiquidity acts as a pre-commitment device, forcing investors to ride out downturns and give companies time to compound. The implication extends beyond VC: any investment vehicle that reduces optionality-to-exit may deliver better returns precisely because it removes the investor's ability to make fear-based decisions. The fee structure and LP agreements are not just commercial terms — they are behavioral guardrails.
