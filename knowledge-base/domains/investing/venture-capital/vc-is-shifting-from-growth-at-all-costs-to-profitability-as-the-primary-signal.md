After years of near-zero interest rates enabling growth-at-all-costs investing, the VC landscape has recalibrated. Forbes and others note that investors now scrutinize unit economics and profitability timelines far more aggressively than during the 2020-2021 bubble peak.

For founders, this changes the fundraising playbook: burning cash to capture market share is a harder sell than demonstrating efficient growth. The strategic implication is that startups optimizing for burn multiples and gross margin rather than pure ARR growth will find more receptive investors in the current climate. This shift may be semi-permanent as institutional LPs demand better risk-adjusted returns.
