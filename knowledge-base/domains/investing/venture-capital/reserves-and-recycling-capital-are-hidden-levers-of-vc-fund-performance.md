Beyond picking great companies, how a VC fund manages its reserves (capital set aside for follow-on rounds) and recycles capital (reinvesting early exits or distributions back into the fund before the commitment period ends) can significantly affect realized returns. A fund that under-reserves may miss the chance to maintain ownership in its biggest winners during later rounds, diluting the very power-law returns it needs. Conversely, over-reserving locks up capital that could have been deployed into new high-potential deals. Capital recycling amplifies deployed capital beyond the committed fund size, effectively increasing exposure without additional LP commitments — but it also introduces timing risk and can obscure true fund economics if not disclosed transparently.
