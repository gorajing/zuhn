Kirsten Green's account of scaling Forerunner Ventures into a tier-1 firm highlights that the durable competitive advantage of top venture funds is organizational, not informational. Any firm can access information about hot deals; few can build decision-making structures that consistently back category-defining consumer companies before consensus forms.

The key design choices Green emphasizes—who gets hired, how partners are developed, which decisions get decentralized—compound over fund cycles. A strong team culture becomes self-reinforcing: it attracts better founders, generates better learning loops, and sustains conviction through market volatility.

For emerging managers, this reframes the priority stack. Early hires and partnership dynamics matter more than fund size or LP pedigree. The team IS the product, especially in the first two funds before the brand can carry deal flow independently.
