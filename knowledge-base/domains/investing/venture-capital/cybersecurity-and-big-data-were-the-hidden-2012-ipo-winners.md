While media attention in 2012 focused on Facebook's IPO, the more instructive investments were Palo Alto Networks ($3.7B valuation) and Splunk ($2.9B). Both companies addressed problems that were growing in importance as enterprises digitized: network security threats and the need to extract value from machine-generated data.

These IPOs validated a broader investing principle: structural tailwinds matter more than headline hype. Cybersecurity spend has grown consistently as digital threats evolve, and big data analytics became foundational infrastructure. Investors who identified these secular trends early — rather than chasing the consumer social media narrative — were rewarded with companies that grew into their valuations and beyond. This pattern repeats: the best IPO investments are often in the sectors solving problems that are getting worse, not the ones generating the most press.
