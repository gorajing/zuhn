The newsletter catalogs 80+ non-dilutive capital sources—grants, credits, alternative financing—that can stack into real runway without equity loss. The insight is that availability is not the bottleneck; execution is. Most founders default to equity rounds because that's the visible path, not because alternatives don't exist.

This reframes fundraising as a portfolio problem rather than a binary equity-or-nothing decision. Founders who systematically layer non-dilutive capital before or alongside equity rounds retain significantly more ownership through early stages. The compounding effect of retained equity becomes enormous by Series B and beyond.
