PitchBook projects Europe's VC direct secondaries market will reach $77.2 billion, representing a fundamental shift in how venture-backed companies provide liquidity to shareholders. This growth is driven by constrained traditional exit paths — IPO markets remain selective and M&A activity hasn't fully recovered.

The rise of secondaries changes the calculus for both founders and investors. Founders can offer partial liquidity to early employees and investors without needing a full exit event, reducing pressure to sell prematurely. For investors, secondaries create a new asset class within venture that offers shorter hold periods and more predictable returns. This trend suggests the traditional 'build to IPO or M&A' binary is being replaced by a more nuanced liquidity spectrum.
