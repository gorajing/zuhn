Most organizations default to structures that minimize friction and suppress conflict, but this leads to critical issues being buried or discovered too late. The better approach is to deliberately design reporting lines, meeting structures, and escalation paths so that the conflicts most important to your strategy naturally surface to decision-makers.

This principle applies across VC firms, startups, and large organizations alike. In a VC context, this might mean structuring partnership meetings so that investment disagreements are surfaced rather than avoided through consensus-seeking. In startups, it means ensuring product-engineering tensions reach founders rather than being resolved at lower levels where the strategic context is missing. The key insight is that organizational structure is a conflict-routing system, and the best leaders design it intentionally.
