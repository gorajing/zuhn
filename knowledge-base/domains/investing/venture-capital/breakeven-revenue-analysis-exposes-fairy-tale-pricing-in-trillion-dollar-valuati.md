Aswath Damodaran proposes replacing multiple-based valuation shortcuts with a breakeven-revenue framework: for any given market cap, calculate the minimum revenue a company must generate to justify that price, given reasonable assumptions about margins, growth rates, and reinvestment needs. This approach forces investors to confront whether the implied revenue targets are physically achievable.

The most powerful insight is the '3P test' and big-market delusion detection: when you aggregate the breakeven-revenue requirements of all companies claiming to dominate a sector, the total often exceeds plausible market demand. This is a systematic check against the tendency to evaluate each company's narrative in isolation. For founders, this framework helps set realistic expectations about what valuation milestones actually imply about required business performance, and for investors it provides a disciplined filter against hype-driven pricing.
