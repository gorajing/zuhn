PitchBook data shows a significant decline in the number of active European VC investors between 2022 and 2024. This matters beyond the headline number because VC markets exhibit strong network effects — fewer active investors means fewer competitive term sheets, less price discovery, and slower deal velocity across the ecosystem.

This contraction likely reflects both the global funding downturn's delayed impact on Europe and a structural shakeout where tourist investors (corporates, family offices, crossover funds that entered during the 2021 boom) have retreated. For European founders, this means fundraising requires targeting a smaller pool of committed, thesis-driven VCs rather than relying on market momentum to attract capital.
