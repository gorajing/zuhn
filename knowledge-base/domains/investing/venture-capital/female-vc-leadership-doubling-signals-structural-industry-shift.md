Women now hold nearly 20% of top venture capital roles in the US, doubling from roughly 9% in 2018. This isn't just a diversity metric — it changes the composition of investment committees making the biggest capital allocation decisions in tech.

The practical implication is twofold: first, research consistently shows diverse investment teams surface different deal flow and evaluate risk differently, which means the types of companies getting funded are shifting. Second, this creates a compounding effect as senior women become mentors and sponsors for the next generation of female investors, suggesting the trend will accelerate rather than plateau.
