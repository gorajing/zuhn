Paul Graham reveals that of YC's ~$10 billion in portfolio value, roughly three quarters came from just two companies (Dropbox and Airbnb). This extreme concentration means that in each YC batch, at most one company meaningfully affects returns — the rest are a cost of doing business.

This has profound implications for investment strategy: evaluating startups on their probability of modest success is the wrong metric entirely. The only question that matters is whether a startup could become a massive outlier. This means investors should be willing to fund many apparent failures to avoid missing the one that returns 10,000x, since a single hit at that scale covers a thousand zeros.
