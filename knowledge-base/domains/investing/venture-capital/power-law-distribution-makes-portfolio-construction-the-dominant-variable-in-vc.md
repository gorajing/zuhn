Venture capital returns are governed by the power law: a small number of outsized winners drive the vast majority of fund performance. This mathematical reality has profound implications for investment strategy — it means that missing even one breakout company can dramatically impact fund returns, while individual losses on failed bets matter relatively little.

This shifts the strategic emphasis from deal-by-deal selectivity toward ensuring broad enough coverage to capture the rare outliers. Funds that optimize for avoiding losses (high selectivity, conservative bets) may actually underperform funds that optimize for capturing the right tail of the distribution. The implication for founders is that VCs rationally prefer high-variance, winner-take-all business models over steady-growth companies, because only the former can produce the 100x+ returns that drive fund performance.
