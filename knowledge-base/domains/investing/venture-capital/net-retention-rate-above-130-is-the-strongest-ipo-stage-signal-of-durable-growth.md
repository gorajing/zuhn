Rubrik's 133% net retention rate was arguably the single most important metric in its IPO story. While 47% ARR growth is impressive, high net retention proves something deeper: existing customers are expanding their spend faster than any churn can offset. This means growth is partially self-funding — the company doesn't need to spend proportionally more on sales and marketing to maintain its trajectory.

For VCs evaluating late-stage investments, net retention above 130% is the clearest signal that a company has genuine product-market fit at the enterprise level. It indicates that the product becomes more embedded over time rather than being commoditized. Rubrik's data security platform naturally expands as enterprises generate more data, creating a built-in growth engine that investors can underwrite with confidence.
