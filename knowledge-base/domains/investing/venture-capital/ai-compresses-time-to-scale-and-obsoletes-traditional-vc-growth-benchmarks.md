The traditional venture playbook relied on well-established benchmarks: T2D3 growth rates, standard fundraising thresholds at each stage, and precision pricing models calibrated over decades. AI-driven automation is compressing the time it takes startups to reach scale, which means companies can hit milestones faster with fewer resources than historical baselines assumed.

This shift rewards conviction and speed over methodical analysis. VCs who wait for traditional proof points risk missing compressed windows, while those who move with concentrated follow-ons based on early signal capture outsized returns. The implication is that the entire evaluation framework—from due diligence timelines to round sizing—needs recalibration for an era where a 12-person team can achieve what previously required 200.
