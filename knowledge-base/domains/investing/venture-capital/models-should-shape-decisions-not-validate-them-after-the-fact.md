Most investors and founders reach for models after a decision is emotionally made — to validate assumptions or patch errors. This inverts the entire purpose of modeling. A model used post-decision becomes a confirmation bias amplifier: you'll tweak inputs until the output matches your gut.

The correct workflow is to model before the pitch, during IC review, and when repricing. The model should surface tensions — like an aggressive P&L paired with an unreasonable exit multiple — before capital moves. This means the model needs to be simple enough to use in real-time decision contexts, not so complex that it only gets opened when someone needs to justify a wire transfer.
