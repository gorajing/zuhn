The 'Liberation Day' tariffs hit US regions asymmetrically, with the Midwest seeing a 32% drop in PE deal activity and the Southeast declining 26%. These regions' economies are more exposed to trade-sensitive industries like manufacturing and agriculture, making their deal pipelines directly vulnerable to tariff policy.

This geographic disparity in deal flow is notable because it demonstrates how macroeconomic policy can redirect private capital away from entire regions. For founders in trade-sensitive regions, this means fundraising headwinds that have nothing to do with company quality. For investors, regional tariff exposure becomes a new risk factor in portfolio construction.
