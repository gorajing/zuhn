The 2025 IPO class reveals a structural disconnect: public market indices can rise while newly public VC-backed companies still underperform. This challenges the conventional wisdom that a rising market lifts all IPO boats. Companies like Figma, Chime, and Navan debuted to initial excitement that faded quickly as macro uncertainty persisted.

For founders and investors planning exits, this means IPO timing cannot simply track broad market health. The gap suggests that public market investors are applying stricter scrutiny to newly public tech companies than the headline indices would suggest — potentially reflecting lingering skepticism from the 2021 vintage of overpriced IPOs. Late-stage investors face real liquidity risk even in seemingly favorable windows.
