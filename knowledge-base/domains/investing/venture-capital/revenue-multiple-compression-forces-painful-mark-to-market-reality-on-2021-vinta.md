The most concrete manifestation of the VC correction is revenue multiple compression. Companies that commanded 9x revenue valuations in 2021 are now valued at 3-4x, and nearly 1 in 5 funding rounds is a down round. This repricing is structural rather than cyclical because it reflects a fundamental shift in how investors value growth versus profitability. The Rule of 40 framework that previously justified high multiples for fast-growing but unprofitable companies has been replaced by investor ruthlessness around sustainable unit economics. For founders, this means raising at peak-era valuations creates a trap: the higher your last round, the more painful your next raise will be. For investors, 2021-vintage portfolios likely need significant markdowns that many have been slow to take, creating a transparency problem with LPs.
