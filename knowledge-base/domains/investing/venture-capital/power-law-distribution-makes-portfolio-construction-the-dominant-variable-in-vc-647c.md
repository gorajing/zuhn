Venture capital returns are not normally distributed — they follow a power law where a small number of outsized winners drive the bulk of fund performance. This has profound implications for portfolio construction: the optimal strategy is not to avoid losers but to ensure exposure to potential outliers. Funds that optimize for downside protection (heavy due diligence, conservative bets) may systematically miss the tail outcomes that define top-quartile performance.

This means the meta-skill in venture investing is not picking winners per se, but constructing a portfolio with enough shots on goal in categories where power-law outcomes are possible. Reserve strategy, follow-on allocation, and sector concentration all become downstream consequences of this single distributional fact.
