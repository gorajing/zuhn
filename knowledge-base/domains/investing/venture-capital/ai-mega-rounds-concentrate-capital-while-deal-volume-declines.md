AI venture funding reached a record $225.8B in 2025, but the distribution reveals extreme concentration: mega-rounds made up 79% of total investment, and just three companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI) captured 38% of all funding. Meanwhile, deal activity actually declined, meaning fewer companies are absorbing more capital.

This pattern suggests a winner-take-most dynamic in AI infrastructure, where the capital requirements for frontier model training create natural monopolies. For startups outside the frontier model layer, the implication is that differentiation must come from applications, vertical specialization, or novel go-to-market—not from competing on model scale. The 782 M&A acquisitions suggest consolidation is already accelerating, particularly in robotics and corporate AI agents.
