The GSER 2025 reveals meaningful shifts in global startup power centers. London's drop to #3 and the rise of Beijing, Shanghai, Hong Kong, and Bengaluru signal that innovation gravity is shifting eastward. Meanwhile, Boston's return to the Top 5 suggests mature ecosystems with deep research institutions retain durable advantages.

For investors and founders, this has practical implications: talent pools, regulatory environments, and funding availability are becoming more distributed globally. The concentration of startup activity in Silicon Valley and a handful of Western cities is giving way to a more polycentric model where multiple regions compete for startup talent and capital.
