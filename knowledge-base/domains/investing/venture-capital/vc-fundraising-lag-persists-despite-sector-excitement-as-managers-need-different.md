Despite intense interest in cutting-edge sectors like AI, new VC vehicle formation and capital closes are falling behind prior cycle benchmarks. Managers without differentiated access to deal flow or demonstrable exit pathways face extended fundraising timelines and increasingly punitive terms from LPs.

This creates a bifurcation in the VC market: established managers with track records and proprietary deal flow continue to raise effectively, while emerging and undifferentiated managers struggle. For the broader startup ecosystem, this means fewer new fund managers entering the market, potentially concentrating capital among existing players. For founders, it reinforces the importance of targeting investors who have recently closed funds and have dry powder to deploy, rather than those still in fundraising mode.
