Venture capital returns are typically quoted as MOIC (multiple on invested capital), which is time-agnostic. A 5x return sounds impressive whether it takes 3 years or 10. But IRR — which accounts for the time value of money — tells a very different story. A 5x in 3 years is roughly 71% IRR; the same 5x in 10 years is only 17% IRR.

This makes venture returns uniquely sensitive to exit timing. When exits take 2-3 years longer than projected (which is common — median time to exit has stretched from ~5 years to ~7-8 years), the IRR impact is severe even if the valuation holds. Sensitivity tables that model these timing scenarios expose how many investments that look strong on MOIC are actually mediocre on a time-adjusted basis.

For investors, this argues for building exit timing scenarios into every investment memo rather than assuming a single expected timeline. For founders, it means understanding that investor pressure to exit isn't just impatience — delayed exits materially destroy fund-level returns.
