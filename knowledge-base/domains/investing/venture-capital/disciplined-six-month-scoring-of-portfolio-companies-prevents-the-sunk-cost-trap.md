Corcoran gives every Shark Tank investment a report card every six months, evaluating momentum, cash position, and actual customer feedback. When a company drifts, she stops — not after one more quarter, not after giving them another chance, but immediately. This systematic approach is what separates capital that scales from capital that bleeds out slowly.

The insight applies beyond venture investing to any resource allocation decision. Pre-committing to quantitative evaluation intervals and explicit red lines removes the emotional negotiation that keeps people funding losing propositions. The critical implementation detail is defining the red lines before you start, not after problems emerge — because once you're emotionally invested, you'll rationalize moving the goalposts.
