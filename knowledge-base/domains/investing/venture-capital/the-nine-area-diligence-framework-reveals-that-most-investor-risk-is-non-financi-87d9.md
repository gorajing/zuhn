The checklist's nine diligence areas—team, technology/IP, regulatory, GTM, competition, market, financials, exit potential, and legal/cap table—reveal that only two (financials and market) are primarily quantitative. The remaining seven are qualitative assessments that financials alone cannot surface: Is the CEO truly backable? Are IP assignments properly executed? Are contractors misclassified? Is the competitive moat real or a pitch deck fiction?

This distribution reflects a deeper truth about early-stage investing: at seed and Series A, the financial model is largely speculative. The real risks are structural—a missing IP assignment that unravels Series A, a misclassified contractor creating six-figure liability, or a vague data policy creating compliance exposure. Investors who over-index on financial analysis at early stage systematically miss the risks most likely to kill their deals.
