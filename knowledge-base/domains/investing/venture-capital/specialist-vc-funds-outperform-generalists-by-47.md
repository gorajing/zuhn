Data shows specialist venture funds outperform generalist peers by 47%. As markets mature and competition for deals intensifies, the information advantage that comes from deep sector knowledge—pattern recognition, network density within a vertical, and ability to provide genuine operational help—creates compounding returns that generalist breadth cannot match.

This has implications beyond venture capital. In any domain where information asymmetry drives returns, specialization beats diversification of attention. The pattern mirrors what we see in startup strategy: narrow focus on a specific problem space tends to outperform broad horizontal plays, especially in competitive markets.
