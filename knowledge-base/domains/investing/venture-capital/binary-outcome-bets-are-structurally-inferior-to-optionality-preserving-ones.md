Pure asset-focused biotech is fundamentally binary: you either get FDA approval or you don't. If you don't, the entire investment is lost. Platform companies mitigate this by generating multiple independent candidates — if one fails, the platform still has value through other pipeline assets and licensing revenue.

This maps to a broader investing principle: optionality has compounding value. Each additional viable asset generated by a platform represents a call option with independent probability of success. Even modest platform advantages in hit rate, when multiplied across many candidates, produce dramatically better expected outcomes than concentrating all capital behind a single clinical bet. The key qualifier is that the platform must actually produce differentiated assets — a nominal platform that only advances one candidate anyway provides no real optionality.
