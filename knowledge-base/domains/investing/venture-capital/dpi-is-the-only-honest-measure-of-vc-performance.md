TVPI includes unrealized paper gains that may never convert to cash, making it unreliable during market downturns. IRR can be artificially inflated through financial engineering that shortens the period between capital calls and markups. DPI—distributions to paid-in capital—measures only actual cash (or distributed securities) returned to investors versus what they contributed, making it the most honest performance signal.

The critical follow-on metric is TVPI-to-DPI conversion efficiency. From 1997 to the mid-2000s, top-quartile VCs converted ~97% of TVPI to DPI. But 2010-2020 vintages have only converted ~25% so far—roughly a third of the historical mean. This suggests ~$1 trillion in global paper value may be at risk of evaporating as markets normalize. For founders and employees evaluating VC backers, asking about a fund's DPI track record across multiple vintages reveals whether the firm actually generates real returns or rides market momentum.
