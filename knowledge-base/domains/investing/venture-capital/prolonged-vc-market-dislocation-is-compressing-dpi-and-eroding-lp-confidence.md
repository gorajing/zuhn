Distributions to Paid-In Capital (DPI) has become the critical metric for LP evaluation of venture fund performance, and the current market is producing historically low DPI numbers. With fewer exits, compressed valuations on paper markups, and a sluggish IPO market, LPs are seeing delayed or diminished cash returns from their venture allocations.

This creates a cascading effect: LPs with poor DPI from recent vintages become reluctant to commit to new funds, which constrains fundraising for emerging and mid-tier GPs. The result is a bifurcation where top-tier funds with strong track records continue to raise while newer managers face existential fundraising challenges. This dynamic will likely persist until the exit environment meaningfully improves.
