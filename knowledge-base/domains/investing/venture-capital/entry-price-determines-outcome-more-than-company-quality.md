The Brex acquisition perfectly illustrates how the same outcome produces radically different results depending on cap table position. Early investors who entered at low valuations saw massive multiples on a $5.15B exit. Late-stage investors who entered at the $12.3B peak lost capital. Employees who received options priced at peak valuation saw them become worthless.

This is a fundamental lesson that gets repeatedly ignored during bull markets: a great company is a bad investment at the wrong price. The discipline of entry valuation is the single most controllable variable in venture returns, yet it's the first thing abandoned when FOMO takes over. For founders, this means understanding that accepting sky-high valuations doesn't just affect their own optionality — it sets up their later employees and investors for potential disappointment.
