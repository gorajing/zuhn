Venture capital has historically maintained an informal ethics norm against defense investment, particularly after controversies at major tech companies. The explicit, public leadership of a16z and Founders Fund in defense VC deals represents more than an investment thesis—it's a cultural declaration that changes what's acceptable in the ecosystem.

When flagship funds lead in a category, they send a signal to founders (it's okay to build this), to other VCs (this is a viable category), and to engineers (this is a legitimate career path). This reflexive legitimacy effect compounds: more capital attracts more talent, which produces better companies, which attracts more capital. The defense tech wave in 2024 is partly a genuine response to geopolitical conditions and partly this self-reinforcing legitimacy cascade.
