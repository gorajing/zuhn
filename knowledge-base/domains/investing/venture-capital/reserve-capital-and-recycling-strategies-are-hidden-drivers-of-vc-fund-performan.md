Most analysis of VC performance focuses on deal selection — which companies a fund invests in. But reserve strategy (how much capital to hold back for follow-on rounds) and recycling (reinvesting early distributions back into new deals) are equally critical and less discussed levers.

Reserves allow funds to maintain or increase ownership in their winners during subsequent funding rounds. Given the power law, doubling down on the top 1-2 performers can dramatically improve fund returns. Too little reserved means getting diluted in your best companies; too much means fewer initial bets and lower chance of finding an outlier. Recycling — taking early exits or distributions and redeploying them — effectively increases the total capital invested beyond committed capital, amplifying returns. Both strategies require disciplined judgment about which companies merit additional investment, making them a quiet but powerful differentiator between top and median funds.
