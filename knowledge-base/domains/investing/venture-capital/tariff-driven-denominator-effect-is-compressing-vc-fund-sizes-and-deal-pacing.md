When public portfolios shrink—as they did with the 4%+ S&P 500 drop after the tariff announcement—LPs' private market allocations become a larger percentage of their total portfolio, triggering rebalancing away from venture. This denominator effect is already manifesting as smaller fund sizes, slower deal pacing, tighter internal benchmarks, and a shift toward structured deals with more downside protection.

The practical implication for founders is that fundraising timelines are stretching and valuation pressure is increasing. Investors are demanding cleaner metrics, stronger unit economics, and 18-24 month runway plans. Bridge rounds are returning not as desperation moves but as strategic tools. European VCs are advising portfolio companies to delay fundraising until 2026, and over 76% of Canadian startups expect tariff impact on their businesses.
