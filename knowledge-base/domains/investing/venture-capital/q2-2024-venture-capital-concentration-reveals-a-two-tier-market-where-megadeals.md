Q2 2024 venture deal value hit an eight-quarter high of $55.6 billion, but the headline number is misleading. CoreWeave and xAI alone absorbed $14.6 billion—over 26% of total capital deployed. Strip those two deals out and the remaining market tells a story of continued constraint rather than recovery.

This concentration pattern has structural implications for founders: the capital supply that matters to most startups (sub-$50M rounds) has not recovered proportionally. Founders interpreting headline numbers as a return to easy fundraising conditions will miscalibrate their runway planning and negotiation posture. The practical takeaway is to benchmark against stage-specific and sector-specific data rather than aggregate venture totals.
