Cherry Ventures securing $500M for early-stage European startups represents a broader trend of capital consolidation in European venture. When established firms can raise funds of this magnitude for early-stage deals, they crowd out smaller managers competing for the same deals.

This mirrors the US pattern where mega-funds have increasingly dominated seed and Series A rounds. For founders, this means fewer but better-capitalized lead investors. For emerging managers, the bar to raise a competitive European fund keeps rising, requiring either exceptional track records or highly differentiated strategies to attract LP capital.
