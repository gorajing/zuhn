Paine shows that Germany's U-boat program was tactically excellent — wolfpack tactics, four-rotor Enigma, captured British codes, Hitler's Happy Time, 850,000 tons sunk. What broke it was arithmetic: in 1943 the US tripled naval hulls and personnel, quadrupled merchant ship construction, and started sinking U-boats at rates Germany couldn't replace. The divergence between construction and destruction curves in mid-1943 is the whole story.

The lesson for investors and strategists: in any prolonged competitive contest — wars, platform battles, capital-intensive industries — the structural question is 'who has the bigger factory?', not 'who has the better trick?' Clever tactics matter early, but they asymptote. Production capacity compounds. When analyzing which side of a protracted fight to back, weight production capacity and supply chain depth far above current battlefield scoreboard.
