When Panama spent $5.4 billion expanding its canal locks to fit 15,000 TEU ships, the state of New Jersey alone had to spend $1.7 billion raising the Bayonne Bridge — and every major east coast port from Miami to New York invested millions to billions in deeper berths and larger facilities. The total downstream spend likely exceeded the canal expansion itself. This pattern is investable: identify the upstream chokepoint expansion, then map the mandatory downstream capital deployments. The participants who must spend are not choosing to — they're compelled by competitive necessity ('in order for the port of Newark to stay competitive, they just had to raise that bridge'). This creates predictable demand for construction, engineering, and port equipment companies positioned along the cascade.
