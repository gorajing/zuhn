Because post-Bretton Woods currencies are ultimately backed by US dollar reserves, Federal Reserve money printing doesn't just dilute domestic purchasing power — it dilutes the reserves of every central bank on Earth. This is why Russia and China have been systematically reducing dollar reserves and accumulating gold: they're opting out of a system where another nation can unilaterally devalue their savings.

This dynamic creates a fundamental geopolitical tension. Countries benefit from dollar liquidity for trade but resent the 'exorbitant privilege' that lets the US fund deficits by exporting inflation. The move toward alternative reserve assets (gold, yuan, potentially crypto) is a rational response to this asymmetry, not merely political posturing. For macro investors, tracking de-dollarization flows is tracking the slow unwinding of a 80-year structural advantage.
