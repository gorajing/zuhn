Dimon highlights that high asset prices 'certainly feel good in the short run' but 'create additional risk if anything goes wrong.' Household net worth as a percentage of GDP is 560%, far above the 460% peak during the 2006 housing bubble. Credit spreads are at levels that leave minimal margin for error. This setup means anything less than the positive scenarios being priced in could cause rapid repricing.

The self-reinforcing mechanism is key: rapidly decreasing asset prices can create a self-reinforcing loop where falling prices trigger selling, which causes more price declines. Prices are set by the marginal buyer and seller — which on any given day is only a small fraction of asset owners. Market makers are less able to intermediate in volatile markets than before, with brokerage inventories smaller as a percentage of investments than ever.

Foreigners own almost $30 trillion of U.S. equities and bonds. While U.S. assets remain safe havens, a change in sentiment driven by geopolitical realignment, trade wars, or loss of confidence in U.S. fiscal trajectory could affect the global flow of investments into securities, including Treasuries.
