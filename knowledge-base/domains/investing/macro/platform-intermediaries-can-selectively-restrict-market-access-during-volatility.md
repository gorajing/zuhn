Robinhood's decision to halt buying (but not selling) of GameStop stock was the most revealing moment of the saga. The platform earned $700M selling user data and order flow to hedge funds like Citadel — the same Citadel that bailed out Melvin Capital's losing short position. Whether the restriction was due to capital requirements (the stated reason) or institutional pressure, the structural conflict is identical: when your revenue comes from institutional counterparties, your incentives during a crisis align with institutions, not retail users. This extends beyond Robinhood to any platform where the user is the product. The lesson is that access to markets through intermediaries is not a right but a privilege that can be revoked precisely when it matters most.
