Most investors and analysts treat ChatGPT's 2022 release as the starting gun for the AI era and expect mature market structure to emerge on a 3-5 year timeline. Chamath's view — that we're still in early stages as of early 2025 — implies a much longer runway before competitive dynamics, application winners, and return profiles become legible.

Historical precedent supports this. The internet went mainstream in the mid-1990s but its most economically significant applications (cloud infrastructure, mobile internet, social media) emerged 10-15 years later. The PC was commercially available in 1981 but its transformative economic effects took a decade to show up in productivity statistics. If AI follows a similar trajectory, investors and builders anchoring on short-horizon milestones may misprice both the duration of opportunity and the patience required to capture it.
