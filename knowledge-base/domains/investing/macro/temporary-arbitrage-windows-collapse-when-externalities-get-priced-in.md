For decades, private Chinese companies profited from sorting Western MRF Residual Bales, but China as a nation was losing money once healthcare costs from hazardous exposure and environmental cleanup from illegal dumping were factored in. When the government recognized this externality gap, they banned imports overnight—plastic waste imports dropped 99.1% in one year.

This pattern generalizes: any industry where private profit depends on socializing costs to a host government is a ticking clock. The arbitrage looks stable until the host country's governance capacity catches up. Investors should treat 'cheap labor + lax regulation' business models as carrying embedded regulatory risk that compounds over time, not as durable competitive advantages.
