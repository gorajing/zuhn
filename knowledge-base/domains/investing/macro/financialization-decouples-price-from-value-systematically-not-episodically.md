The video traces the structural roots of the GameStop saga back to the Commodity Futures Modernization Act of 2000, which enabled self-regulation of financial products and triggered the age of financialization. The key insight is that GameStop's price-value disconnect was not an anomaly caused by Reddit — it was a concentrated, visible instance of the same dynamic that pervades the entire market. Companies buying back stock to inflate prices, cheap borrowing eliminating risk consciousness, and 23% of all US dollars being printed in a single year all produce the same fundamental disconnect between price and value that GameStop made obvious. Warren Buffett's principle — 'price is what you pay, value is what you get' — has been systematically violated not by retail traders but by the architecture of the financial system itself. GameStop simply made the invisible visible.
