In a free-market regime, alpha comes from identifying undervalued fundamentals. In a state-directed regime, alpha comes from identifying which sectors the government has decided to subsidize with below-market credit. Napier's winners list for the next cycle: re-industrialization (manufacturing coming back onshore), rearmament (defense spending), energy infrastructure, robotics and automation, and broadly the 'old economy' — manufacturing, raw materials, defense industry. These are the sectors governments are funneling cheap capital into because they align with national security and industrial sovereignty. The implication: index-level exposure to US large-cap growth will underperform targeted exposure to government-favored sectors, regardless of the fundamental merit of either.
