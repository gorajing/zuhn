Rogoff points out that economists are terrible at forecasting, and the specific failure mode is trend extrapolation. Japan was going to eat the world in 1989; its GDP per capita is now at the bottom of the developed rung. The USSR was projected to overtake the US in the 1970s. Goldman Sachs projected China would be to the US what the US is to Canada by 2030 — Rogoff now thinks China may never have a larger economy than the US in absolute terms, even with 4x the population.

The same failure is happening in real time with AI projections. The lesson isn't that the extrapolated trend is definitely wrong in direction, but that the rate systematically decelerates as countries hit the middle-income trap, demographics bite, and institutional constraints (like Xi's centralization) bind. Applied: when you see a confident hockey-stick projection 15+ years out — China GDP, AI capability, crypto adoption, EV penetration — heavily discount the magnitude. The probability distribution has a much wider variance than the projecting institution admits.
