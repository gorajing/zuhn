In a debt-based monetary system, 97% of money exists as digital bank credit created through loans. This means the money supply is literally composed of outstanding debt. When borrowers repay loans without new loans being issued, money is destroyed — the money supply contracts. This creates an inherent growth imperative: the system needs continuously expanding debt to maintain a stable or growing money supply.

This structural dependency explains why central banks panic during deleveraging events (2008, COVID) and immediately flood the system with liquidity. It also explains why government debt levels only ever increase in the long run — sovereign deleveraging would catastrophically shrink the money supply. For investors, this means betting on sustained deleveraging in a fiat system is betting against the system's survival mechanism.
