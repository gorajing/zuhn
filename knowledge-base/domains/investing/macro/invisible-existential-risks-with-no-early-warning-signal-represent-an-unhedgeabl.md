The strangelet scenario presents a pure tail risk: a subatomic particle, invisible and undetectable, drifting through space for billions of years before striking a planet and converting it entirely. This maps to a class of investment and strategic risks where traditional risk management (monitoring, early warning, hedging) is structurally impossible. Unlike market crashes or pandemics which give leading indicators, some risks have zero observable precursors. The rational response to undetectable existential risks isn't paralysis — it's recognizing that survival so far constitutes meaningful Bayesian evidence against the risk (Earth hasn't been consumed in 4.5 billion years), and that portfolio construction should focus on risks that ARE detectable and hedgeable rather than spending resources on those that aren't.
