Every heavy element in our technological civilization — the gold in circuits, the uranium in reactors, the platinum in catalytic converters — was forged in neutron star collisions billions of years ago. These atoms scattered through the galaxy, mixed into gas clouds, and eventually coalesced into our solar system. The catastrophic destruction of neutron stars became the foundation materials for all subsequent complexity.

This maps directly to economic and technological cycles: the infrastructure, talent, and intellectual property scattered by one era's collapse become the building blocks for the next. The dot-com bust dispersed engineers and ideas that became the foundation of Web 2.0. Demolished industrial infrastructure gets recycled into new construction. The pattern suggests that tracking where destruction products land is more predictive of future value creation than tracking the destruction itself.
