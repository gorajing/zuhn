The video illustrates that Vietnam can manufacture shoes, ship them across the entire Pacific Ocean, and still profit on a $20 sale price because pre-pandemic container rates were roughly $1,000 for a 20-foot container holding 3,500 shoeboxes — about 30 cents per box. This extraordinary cheapness is what 'ocean shipping enabling globalization' actually means in concrete terms.

This near-zero transport cost is the hidden foundation of the entire global manufacturing arbitrage model. It means labor cost differentials between countries dominate location decisions, while physical distance is essentially free. For investors analyzing manufacturing shifts, reshoring trends, or trade policy impacts, the key question is whether any disruption (tariffs, carbon pricing, persistent congestion) can raise shipping costs enough to materially change this calculus. The 2021 crisis temporarily did — rates spiked 5-10x — but the structural cheapness of ocean shipping makes permanent cost increases hard to sustain.
