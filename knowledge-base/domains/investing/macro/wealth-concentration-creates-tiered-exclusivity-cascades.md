The video documents a clear cascade: as more people become millionaires, they flood ultra-luxury hotels and private jet charters, pushing the ultra-wealthy toward superyachts. As superyacht ownership grows, the ultra-wealthy move to expedition yachts and bespoke experiences. This pattern reveals a structural dynamic in luxury markets — they are never in equilibrium because wealth concentration continuously pushes new entrants into each tier.

This has investment implications: luxury goods companies that can continuously create new tiers of exclusivity (like Aman hotels or bespoke travel firms) capture more value than those stuck at a fixed price point. The capacity crunch across private aviation, ultra-luxury hotels, and superyachts simultaneously suggests the entire luxury infrastructure is structurally undersupplied relative to growing demand from wealth concentration.
