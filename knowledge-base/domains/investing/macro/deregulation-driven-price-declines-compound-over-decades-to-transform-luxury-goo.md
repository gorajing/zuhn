Before the Airline Deregulation Act of 1978, the Civil Aeronautics Board set minimum fares — a NYC-LA ticket couldn't legally cost less than $1,442 in today's dollars. After deregulation, real airfares fell 50% over 30 years and continue declining. This wasn't a one-time adjustment but a sustained multi-decade trend driven by competition, efficiency gains, and the elimination of regulatory price floors. The pattern repeats across industries: telecommunications, trucking, and financial brokerage all saw similar post-deregulation deflation. For investors, regulated industries with artificial price floors represent potential for massive value creation (or destruction) upon deregulation — the key is identifying which side of the disruption you're positioned on.
