St Kitts and Nevis offers citizenship through a $400,000 real estate investment, and Bulgaria has similar programs. These citizenship-by-investment (CBI) programs effectively create a market for sovereignty access, and their pricing encodes real information.

The price floor reflects the issuing country's calculation of marginal cost (infrastructure, services) versus revenue. The demand side reflects buyers' assessment of passport utility (visa-free travel), tax optimization potential, and geopolitical hedging value. When CBI prices rise, it signals increasing demand for mobility optionality — often preceding political instability in buyer-origin countries. When programs proliferate, it signals fiscal stress in small nations. For macro investors, CBI program dynamics are a leading indicator of both capital flight pressure and sovereign fiscal health in small states.
