Hawking radiation demonstrates that even black holes — the most extreme concentrations of mass-energy in the universe — slowly evaporate through quantum effects at their boundary. Virtual particle pairs form at the event horizon; one falls in, the other escapes, gradually draining the black hole's energy. The process accelerates as the black hole shrinks, ending in a violent explosion. This maps to dominant market entities: monopolies, empires, and market leaders all leak value at their boundaries through interactions they can't fully control. Each individual loss seems negligible — a small competitor wins one niche customer, a regulation chips away at one advantage, a talented employee leaves for a startup. But these boundary losses compound. And critically, the process accelerates as the entity shrinks: a weakening monopoly loses talent and customers faster, not slower. The investment implication: when evaluating dominant companies, examine boundary health — where are the micro-losses happening, and are they accelerating?
