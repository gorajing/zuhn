Mody frames 'model behavior' as the function that defines, evaluates, and ships desired model behavior: behavior design (the style, tone, and shape of responses), data collection for measurement and training, and model steering (prompting, model selection, context-window contents, parameters). The process is deeply iterative—build, refine, create evals, ship, collect internal and external feedback, repeat.

The analogy offered is the evolution of the web: websites began merely functional, then design and craft grew as ambitions and technology advanced. Model behavior is following the same arc—from functional 'instructions in, output out' toward agent behaviors: goal-directed reasoning, shaping autonomous tasks, self-correction and learning, and even crafting the personality of the model itself.

The forward-looking claim is that model behavior will become a specialized and prevalent function of its own, even inside product companies—not something engineers do on the side. Teams building AI products should plan to invest in this as a distinct craft with its own people, tools, and processes.
