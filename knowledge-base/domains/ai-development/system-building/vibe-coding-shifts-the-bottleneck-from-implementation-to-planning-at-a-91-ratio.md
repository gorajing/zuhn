Manifast CEO Heo Jaehyeok reports that from their agency experience, the time allocation between planning and development has fundamentally shifted. What used to be 2:8 (planning:development) has become 5:5 on average and up to 9:1 in complex projects. AI coding tools execute development tasks more precisely when requirements are precisely defined, so developers now spend the majority of their time defining what they want rather than building it.

This is a structural shift in how software gets made. The implication is that investment in planning tools, specification frameworks, and requirement-gathering processes now has dramatically higher ROI than investment in implementation tools. For anyone doing vibe coding, the quality ceiling is determined entirely by how well you can articulate what you want — the AI will faithfully implement whatever you specify, including your mistakes.
