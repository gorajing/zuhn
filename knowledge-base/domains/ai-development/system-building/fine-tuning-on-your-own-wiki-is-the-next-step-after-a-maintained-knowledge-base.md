The fourth principle of the LLM-wiki pattern is bring-your-own-AI: because everything is in universal formats, you can plug in any LLM — Claude, GPT, Codex, open-source models — and even fine-tune a model on your wiki so it knows your data in its weights, not just in its context window. This is explicitly flagged as the probable next step once a wiki is mature enough to be worth training on. The implication for Zuhn: the 5-level compression hierarchy is not just a navigable knowledge base, it is also a high-signal fine-tuning dataset, and the principle layer in particular is exactly the kind of compressed, curated, deduplicated content that small models benefit most from.
