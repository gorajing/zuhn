Theoretical physicists don't approach problems through forces or energy — they write down a Lagrangian (kinetic energy minus potential energy) and the principle of least action generates the correct equations of motion. The same framework works for classical mechanics, special relativity, electrodynamics, and quantum field theory.

This is a powerful meta-lesson for system building: instead of encoding domain-specific rules, find the right abstraction layer where a single framework generates correct behavior across many domains. The hunt for a 'theory of everything' in physics is literally the search for one Lagrangian that spits out all physical laws. Analogously, the best software architectures find one core abstraction that unifies seemingly disparate features.
