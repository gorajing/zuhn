Justinian's Corpus Juris Civilis (534 CE) took centuries of accumulated Roman legal precedent and codified it into a structured, teachable system. This codification outlasted the Byzantine Empire itself — it became the foundation for European civil law systems that persist today, nearly 1500 years later.

The principle generalizes: when you formalize tacit institutional knowledge into explicit, structured systems, that knowledge becomes portable and self-propagating. It no longer depends on the survival of the original institution or the people who held it. This is directly relevant to AI system-building — models trained on well-structured knowledge bases, codified decision frameworks, and formalized reasoning patterns create value that persists beyond any individual project or team. The act of codification itself is often more valuable than the institution doing the codifying.
