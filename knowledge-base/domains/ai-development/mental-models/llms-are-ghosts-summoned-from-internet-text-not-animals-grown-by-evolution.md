Karpathy rejects Sutton's 'build animals' framing because animals come with enormous evolutionary priors baked into their weights (a zebra runs minutes after birth). We don't know how to run evolution, so we imitate internet documents instead — which produces a different kind of intelligence entirely, digital ghosts rather than biological animals.

The practical consequence: reasoning by analogy from animal learning (especially reinforcement learning from scratch) misleads LLM research. Pretraining is doing two distinct things at once — absorbing knowledge AND bootstrapping algorithmic primitives like in-context learning. The second is what we actually want; the first is mostly dead weight that makes models rely on memory instead of intelligence.

The goal should be extracting the 'cognitive core' — stripping knowledge while keeping the algorithms of problem-solving. This reframing matters because it tells researchers to stop trying to build animal-like systems and instead develop techniques specific to imitation-trained digital entities.
