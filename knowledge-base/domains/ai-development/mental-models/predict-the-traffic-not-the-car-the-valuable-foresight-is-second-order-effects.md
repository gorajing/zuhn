Rodriguez relays a quote from a Midjourney friend: back in the age of horses, predicting the car was trivial — swap the energy source, an engine for a horse, done. What was genuinely hard to predict was traffic. The primary substitution is legible because it's a direct swap; the emergent, systemic consequences are where the surprise and the opportunity live. He turns it on the AI field: everyone is focused on the obvious capability ('you can transform JSON to YAML'), and missing the 'traffic' — the structural shifts that the new capability will unleash.

As a mental model this is a prompt to redirect strategic attention: when a capability lands, don't just ask 'what does this directly replace?' — ask 'what new systems, frictions, and behaviors emerge once everyone has it?' For founders, researchers, and engineers, the differentiated bets are downstream of the obvious substitution, in the second-order effects most people aren't yet pricing in. The model generalizes well beyond AI to any platform shift.
