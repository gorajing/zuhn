Special relativity shows that for an observer moving at near-light-speed, Earth would literally be a flat disc due to Lorentz contraction in the direction of motion. This isn't a perceptual trick or measurement artifact — in that reference frame, Earth genuinely has those dimensions. All physics experiments conducted within that frame would confirm the contracted measurements.

This has deep implications for how we think about models and representations. The flat-Earth question has a technically correct answer: Earth IS flat in certain reference frames. The lesson isn't relativism but rather that the right answer depends on precisely specifying your frame of reference. This principle transfers directly to system design and AI: a model's outputs are only meaningful relative to the frame (training distribution, evaluation criteria, deployment context) in which they were produced. Changing the frame doesn't just change how you see the output — it changes what the output actually means.
