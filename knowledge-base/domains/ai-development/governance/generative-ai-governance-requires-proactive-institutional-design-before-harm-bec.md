The World Economic Forum's work on generative AI governance highlights a structural mismatch: AI capabilities are advancing faster than the institutions designed to oversee them. Traditional regulatory approaches — waiting for harm, then legislating — break down when technologies can cause rapid, diffuse, or hard-to-attribute harm at scale.

The implication for builders and policymakers alike is that governance needs to be co-developed with the technology, not retrofitted after deployment. This includes both internal governance (how AI systems are tested, deployed, and monitored within organizations) and external governance (standards bodies, audit frameworks, liability regimes). The companies and jurisdictions that build robust governance infrastructure now will have a structural advantage as regulatory scrutiny intensifies.
