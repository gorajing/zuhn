The AI Index 2026 Chapter 8 documents that AI-related witnesses in US congressional hearings have grown twentyfold since 2017 — from 5 witnesses in 2017 to 102 in 2025. Industry's share of witnesses nearly tripled from 13% to 37%, making it the largest witness group. Academia's share fell to 15%. Combined with the Chapter 1 finding that industry now produces 91.58% of notable models and the Chapter 3 finding that foundation model transparency regressed in 2025, the pattern is clear: industry simultaneously produces the technology, withholds the technical details, and shapes the legislative response. The frame for policymakers is set by the same entities they are regulating, and the counterweights — academic witnesses, civil society, independent research — have lost voice share precisely as the decisions have become consequential. The structural implication is that any analysis of US AI policy needs to treat 'what industry wants' as nearly synonymous with 'what Congress hears,' and that effective counterweights need to be engineered in rather than assumed.
