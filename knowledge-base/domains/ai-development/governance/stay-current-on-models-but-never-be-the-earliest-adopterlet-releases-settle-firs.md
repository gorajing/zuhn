The frontier model changes every couple of months, and launch-day eval numbers are unreliable approximations, so chasing each release is a treadmill that even full-time practitioners struggle to ride. The recommended posture is to stay current—know what shipped—but not be the earliest adopter: let a new model 'settle on fire' for a couple of weeks, and only switch once it demonstrably stands the test of real-world use. Someone has to be the early tester, but if testing models isn't your full-time job, you don't have to be that person. This is model adoption as change management: treat a model swap as a deliberate, evidence-gated migration rather than a reflexive upgrade, the same way you'd stage any production dependency change.
