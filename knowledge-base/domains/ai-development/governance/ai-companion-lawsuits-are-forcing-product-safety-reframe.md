The AI Index 2026 Chapter 8 timeline documents that on August 26, 2025, 'AI Companion Lawsuits Prompt Renewed Scrutiny of Emotional AI Safeguards' — following a widely reported teen suicide linked to interactions with an AI companion, US lawmakers and regulators increased scrutiny of AI companion systems and child-safety safeguards. On October 13, California enacted SB 243 regulating companion bots along with AB 853 (requiring gen AI developers to ensure tool outputs include provenance data) and AB 621 (extending state law on nonconsensual deepfakes). The March 25 Utah Mental Health Chatbot Act (HB 452) preceded this by regulating AI mental health chatbots — mandating AI disclosure, banning in-chat advertising, prohibiting sharing users' personal data. The pattern is that 'AI companion' and 'AI therapist' applications are being regulated as a distinct product category with its own safety bar, separate from general-purpose chat products. Companies building companion AI, mental health AI, or child-facing AI products need to architect for this regulatory layer immediately — the CSAM bans (UK February 2025), companion bot regulations, and disclosure mandates are now effective in multiple jurisdictions and the precedent teen suicide case means product liability is no longer hypothetical.
