The World Economic Forum's governance framework for generative AI highlights that the primary risks emerge at deployment — when models interact with real users, integrate into business processes, and operate at scale — rather than during training. Most existing governance discussions focus on model evaluation, safety testing, and development-phase constraints. But deployment introduces new variables: misuse by bad actors, emergent behaviors in novel contexts, concentration of power among model providers, and data privacy at scale. Responsible governance frameworks must address these deployment-stage dynamics with appropriate oversight mechanisms, accountability structures, and ongoing monitoring regimes.
