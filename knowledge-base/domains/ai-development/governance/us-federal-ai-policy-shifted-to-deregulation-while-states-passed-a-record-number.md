The AI Index 2026 Chapter 8 timeline shows the US moving in two directions at once in 2025. Federal actions point to deregulation: January 23 Executive Order 'Removing Barriers to American Leadership in AI,' February 11 refusal to sign the Paris AI Action Summit inclusive AI declaration, July 1 Senate strike of a 10-year federal moratorium attempt on state AI regulation, July 23 America's AI Action Plan and three executive orders, November 24 Genesis Mission executive order (compared in ambition to the Manhattan Project), December 11 Pax Silica Declaration, December 12 executive order to curb state-level AI laws. State actions point to substantive regulation: March 25 Utah Mental Health Chatbot Act, April 16 Montana Right to Compute Act, June 22 Texas TRAIGA, September 29 California SB 53 (safety/transparency/whistleblower), October 13 California SB 243/AB 853/AB 621 (companion bots, provenance, deepfakes). The result is a fragmented enforcement landscape where product decisions must satisfy California's disclosure requirements, Texas's high-impact AI bans, Utah's mental health chatbot rules, and federal 'dominance' framing simultaneously. Vendors will gravitate toward California rules as the effective national standard (Brussels-effect style), while the federal-state conflict heads to courts. Confidence is high because the chronology is on record; uncertainty is about how the conflict resolves.
