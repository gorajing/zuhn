The AI Index 2026 Chapter 9 reports that across all 50 US states, concern about too little AI regulation outweighs concern about too much. Nationally, 41% of respondents said federal AI regulation will not go far enough, compared with 27% who said it will go too far, though more than one-third were unsure. This holds in every state, making the federal deregulation push under the January 2025 Executive Order, the July 2025 America's AI Action Plan, and the December 2025 executive order to curb state AI laws structurally misaligned with public opinion — not just blue-state opinion, not just coastal opinion, but every-state opinion. The implication for state AI regulation is that the public mandate is strong and geographically uniform, which means state AI bills have political cover that federal bills lack. Expect state-led regulation to continue and expand regardless of federal preemption attempts, because the underlying voter preference structure supports it everywhere.
