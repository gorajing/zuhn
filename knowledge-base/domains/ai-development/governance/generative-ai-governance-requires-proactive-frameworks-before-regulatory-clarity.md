The World Economic Forum's report on generative AI governance argues that responsible development can't be retroactively designed. The governance challenges—bias, hallucination, misuse, concentration of power, accountability gaps—are most tractable when addressed during system design and deployment planning, not after incidents surface.

For enterprises deploying GenAI, this means building internal review processes, establishing accountability chains, and creating documentation practices now, even in the absence of regulatory mandates. The organizations that treat governance as a competitive advantage—using it to build trust with customers and regulators—will outperform those that treat it as compliance overhead. The cost structure also favors early movers: retrofitting governance onto deployed systems is exponentially harder than designing it in from the start.
