    'R&D progress multiplier' — a measurable variable that quantifies how
    AI-assisted research compares to baseline. 'How many months of progress
    without the AIs do you get in one month of progress with all of these new
    AIs helping with the intelligence explosion. So 2027, we start with a five
    times multiplier for algorithmic progress.' Why coding is the bottleneck
    (and the source of acceleration): 'Our scenario focuses on coding in
    particular because we think coding is what starts the intelligence
    explosion. We are less interested in questions of how do you mop up the last
    few things that are uniquely human, compared to when can you start coding in
    a way that helps the human AI researchers speed up their AI research, and
    then if you've helped them speed up the AI research enough, is that enough
    to, with some ridiculous speed multiplier — 10 times, 100 times — mop up all
    of these other things.' The implication is that the most consequential AI

    use internally to build their next-generation models. The labs whose

    — capability gains in your domain depend on whether your experts can use AI

Daniel Kokotajlo's central framework in AI 2027: rather than vague 'intelligence explosion will happen sometime,' the scenario introduces the 'R&D progress multiplier' — a measurable variable that quantifies how AI-assisted research compares to baseline. 'How many months of progress without the AIs do you get in one month of progress with all of these new AIs helping with the intelligence explosion. So 2027, we start with a five times multiplier for algorithmic progress.' Why coding is the bottleneck (and the source of acceleration): 'Our scenario focuses on coding in particular because we think coding is what starts the intelligence explosion. We are less interested in questions of how do you mop up the last few things that are uniquely human, compared to when can you start coding in a way that helps the human AI researchers speed up their AI research, and then if you've helped them speed up the AI research enough, is that enough to, with some ridiculous speed multiplier — 10 times, 100 times — mop up all of these other things.' The implication is that the most consequential AI capability development is happening NOT in chat applications, consumer agents, or even productivity tools — it's in coding assistants that AI labs use internally to build their next-generation models. The labs whose internal coding tooling improves fastest get a compounding lead on the labs whose tooling doesn't. The corollary for builders and capital allocators: track 'AI helping with AI research' as a leading indicator. If the R&D multiplier in labs starts hitting 5x for real (vs the current ~1.5-2x range), the lab leadership rankings will reshuffle quickly. The implication for non-AI work: this same multiplier applies to any domain where 'experts using AI to help with expert work' creates feedback loops. Software engineering (developers using AI to write better dev tools), drug discovery (researchers using AI to design better experiments), education (teachers using AI to design better curricula). Watch for the multiplier specifically — capability gains in your domain depend on whether your experts can use AI to amplify their expert output.
