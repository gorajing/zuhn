The speaker notes a trend of labs releasing their LLMs with vision capabilities on day zero rather than bolting them on later. Examples cited include Gemini 4 (an omni model), Qwen 3.5, and Chimera 2.5—all shipped as VLMs. She forecasts that over time essentially all new models will release multimodal from the start. Crucially, vision LLMs double as computer-use agents: they can operate over screenshots and know where to click.

For anyone designing agent architectures, this means planning for vision as a baseline assumption rather than a separate capability to integrate. The merging of 'LLM' and 'VLM' into a single category simplifies the model-selection space and makes screenshot-driven computer-use agents a default-available pattern. The marked time-sensitivity reflects that this is a stated near-term trajectory, with specific model versions that will date quickly even as the underlying direction holds.
