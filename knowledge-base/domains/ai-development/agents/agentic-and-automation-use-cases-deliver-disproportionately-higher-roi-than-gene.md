Most enterprise use cases today sit in a first tier of content productivity — increasing output volume, improving quality, winning back five hours a week. Time savings alone represents about 35% of all use cases and clusters around 5 hours/week (which, framed annually, is 7-10 reclaimed work weeks per person — meaningful, even if it's not the ultimate prize).

But the data shows a clear performance gap: use cases that mention automation or agents specifically wildly outperform on self-reported ROI, on both the automation and agent dimensions. This is the leading edge of where enterprise value is migrating — from assisting humans on existing tasks to automating workflows end-to-end.

For builders and operators, this is a directional signal: time savings is the on-ramp, but the disproportionate returns sit in the next layer of more advanced agentic and automation use cases. Teams that stop at productivity assistance leave the larger prize on the table. Standard caveat: the ROI here is self-reported by an AI-enthusiast audience, so absolute magnitudes should be discounted even though the relative gap is informative.
