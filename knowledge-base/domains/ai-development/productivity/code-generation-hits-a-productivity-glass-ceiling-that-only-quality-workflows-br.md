Friedman frames AI-assisted development as a stack of glass ceilings. Out-of-the-box autocomplete gives a modest boost. Agentic code generation (gen 2.0) with rules and investment raises the ceiling further. But the McKinsey/Stanford-hyped 2x-10x for the *entire* software lifecycle never materializes from generation alone — because generation only addresses one phase of the SDLC.

The ceiling lifts only when AI breaks out of the IDE into agentic quality workflows: automated review, testing, verification, and the many quality-related organizational workflows that a 100+ developer org accumulates. Critically, these workflows must be *dynamic* — standards and rules that keep learning — because quality itself is dynamic.

The actionable reframe: stop measuring AI ROI by code-writing speed and start measuring it by lifecycle throughput. The teams that hit the promised multiplier are the ones that invested in quality automation, not the ones that simply adopted more code-generation tools. AI is a tool, not a solution.
