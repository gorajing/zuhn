The format design of 'How I AI'—capped at ~30 minutes, single use case, live demo—is a deliberate bet on a specific theory of behavior change: people adopt new tools when the cognitive distance between 'watching someone do it' and 'trying it myself' is small. Long-form content creates a sense of having learned without triggering the try-it impulse. Short demos do the opposite.

This mirrors research on instruction design: massed, comprehensive instruction is often less effective at producing behavior change than distributed, focused exposure. For AI tools specifically, where the barrier is usually psychological ('I don't know where to start') rather than technical, a 30-minute demo that leaves viewers with one specific workflow to try is likely more effective than a comprehensive overview that leaves them better informed but no more activated.
