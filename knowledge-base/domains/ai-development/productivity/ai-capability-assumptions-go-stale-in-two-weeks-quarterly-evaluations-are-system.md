The standard enterprise evaluation cadence for new technology is quarterly: a team tries a tool, decides it does not yet meet requirements, and revisits in 90 days. For AI tools in 2026, this cadence is structurally too slow. Capability frontiers move every few weeks (model releases, harness improvements, prompting techniques, integration patterns), and any assumption older than two weeks about what the tools cannot do is probably wrong. Logan repeats this point twice in the conversation, which is a tell that he sees teams making the same mistake repeatedly. The practical implication for leaders: institute a two-week capability re-evaluation cadence for AI tools relevant to the team's workflow. Specifically, every two weeks, take the failed-AI-task list from the previous cycle and re-test the top three. The cost is roughly half a day per cycle; the upside is catching capability inflections within weeks rather than quarters. Teams that sustain this cadence consistently outpace teams that don't, because the gap compounds — and the gap is invisible until results diverge.
