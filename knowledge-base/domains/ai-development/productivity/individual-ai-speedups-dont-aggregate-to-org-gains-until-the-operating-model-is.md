McKinsey surveyed ~300 enterprises and found a stark disconnect: individuals can rattle off ten tasks that went from days to minutes, but company-wide productivity improvement averages only 5-15%. The gap is not a tooling problem — it is that organizations layered AI on top of an operating model built for the constraints of human development (eight-to-ten person teams, two-week sprints, manual review). Speeding up one stage simply relocates the constraint: more code generated means more manual review; uneven AI impact across tasks and across people makes work allocation 'highly non-trivial'; and generated code amplifies tech debt and complexity (citing a Carnegie Mellon report). The practical implication is that buying tools and measuring adoption will keep you at marginal gains. Realizing the latent value requires re-wiring the product development lifecycle around the new bottlenecks, not just inserting AI into the old one. Treat sub-20% gains as a signal that the operating model, not the model, is the ceiling.
