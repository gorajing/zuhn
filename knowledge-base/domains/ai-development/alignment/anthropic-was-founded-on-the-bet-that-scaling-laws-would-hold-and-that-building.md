Dario explains Anthropic's origin story with unusual specificity: in 2019-2020, a group within OpenAI recognized scaling laws — the empirical finding that more computation and data with simple algorithms reliably improves cognitive task performance. They measured these trends when models cost $1K-$10K to train and predicted they'd hold at $100M, $1B, $10B — which they have.

The second realization was that technology of this significance needed to be built correctly. 'We grow them more than we build them — they're like a child's brain developing.' This unpredictability made safety not optional but essential. Anthropic's concrete safety commitments include: investing early in mechanistic interpretability (no commercial value for 4 years), creating constitutional AI (training models from principles rather than just data), delaying Claude's release ~6 months for safety testing (before ChatGPT launched — a deliberate commercial sacrifice), and creating the first responsible scaling policy with ASL levels modeled on biosafety levels.

For someone seeking to sell Anthropic's products, understanding this founding narrative is essential. Dario positions Anthropic as the company that consistently led on safety standards and forced competitors to follow — this is the core brand differentiation that enterprise buyers with safety concerns respond to.
