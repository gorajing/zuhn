Moderna's CEO stated the company will not invest in new late-stage vaccine trials because growing opposition from US officials makes return on investment impossible. Regulatory delays, narrowed eligibility populations, and government-sowed confusion about vaccine safety have shrunk the addressable market. This represents a concrete mechanism by which ideological movements destroy scientific capacity: not through banning research, but by making it commercially unviable.

The cascading consequences extend far beyond measles—mRNA cancer treatments use the same vaccine-based platform technology. Anti-vaccine ideology that began on the leftist fringe migrated to conservative mainstream during COVID and now threatens to condemn Americans to unnecessary cancer deaths by defunding the therapeutic pipeline. Combined with the broader Trump administration assault on scientific institutions, this creates a compounding effect where reduced funding leads to brain drain, which leads to fewer breakthroughs, which further erodes public trust in science. The article notes this is especially alarming given AI-assisted amateurs' increasing ability to create bioweapons, making vaccine readiness a national security concern.
