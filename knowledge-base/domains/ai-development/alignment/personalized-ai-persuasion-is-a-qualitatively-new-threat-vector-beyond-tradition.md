Altman draws a sharp distinction between existing disinformation (troll farms creating one viral meme everyone sees) and a new threat enabled by AI: systems that read everything a person has ever written online, then send a single message customized to that individual at precisely the right moment to shift their worldview.

This is qualitatively different because it scales personalization, not just volume. Traditional propaganda works through repetition and broad emotional appeal. AI-powered persuasion could work through precision — understanding individual cognitive vulnerabilities, belief structures, and timing windows. The defense surface is fundamentally different: you can't counter-meme something that only one person ever sees.
