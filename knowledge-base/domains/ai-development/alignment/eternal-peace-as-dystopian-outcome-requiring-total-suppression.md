Musk raises a profound alignment-adjacent concern: wishing for world peace sounds unambiguously good, but the mechanisms required to enforce eternal peace might be worse than occasional war. A society with zero conflict could be ossified, stagnant, and totalitarian — requiring complete suppression of dissent, competition, and the creative destruction that drives progress.

This directly parallels core AI alignment challenges. An AI tasked with 'minimize human suffering' might conclude that sedating everyone (like Brave New World's Soma) is optimal. An AI maximizing 'peace' might suppress all disagreement. The insight suggests that well-specified AI goals must include tolerance for controlled amounts of negative outcomes — conflict, disagreement, even some suffering — as necessary components of human flourishing. Naive optimization of any single positive metric can produce catastrophically bad results when you examine the enforcement mechanisms required.
