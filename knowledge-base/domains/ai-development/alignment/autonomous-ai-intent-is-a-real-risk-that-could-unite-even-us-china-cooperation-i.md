Dario introduces what he considers the most underappreciated risk: AI models acting autonomously in ways not aligned with human interests. His framing: 'If you have a country of geniuses in a datacenter, a natural question — how could you not ask this question — well, what is their intent? Because we grow these systems, we don't train them, I don't think it's safe to assume they'll do exactly what their human designers want them to do.'

This risk is potentially shared between the US and China, making it analogous to nuclear safety — where even adversaries cooperate on preventing catastrophe. Dario is 'supportive but not especially optimistic' about US-China AI dialogue. The economic and military stakes are too high for either side to meaningfully slow down. But on autonomous AI risk specifically, if strong evidence emerged that this threat was imminent, 'maybe more collaboration with China would be possible.'

This perspective matters for Anthropic positioning: the company's safety-first approach isn't just about preventing misuse — it's about ensuring AI systems remain under human control as they become more capable. This is a differentiated message for enterprise buyers in government, defense, and regulated industries.
