Verdon introduces the concept of 'hyperstition' — ideas that, once believed, increase their own probability of becoming real. He argues that pessimism about technology is fundamentally destructive because it is hyperstitious: if you believe the future will be worse, you reduce investment, slow innovation, and make that worse future more likely.

Conversely, optimism is equally hyperstitious but constructive. Believing you have agency to build a better future and that technological progress is net positive creates the conditions for that progress. This is not naive positivity — it's a strategic choice about which self-fulfilling prophecy to adopt. For builders and founders, the practical implication is that the narrative you tell about the future shapes the future you build.
