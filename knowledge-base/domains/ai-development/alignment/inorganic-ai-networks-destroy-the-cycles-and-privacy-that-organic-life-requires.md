All previous information networks were organic, running on human brains that require sleep, rest, and cycles. Even Wall Street closes on weekends. Even the Soviet KGB could not watch everyone all the time. These organic constraints inadvertently protected privacy and rest.

AI networks are inorganic: they never rest, never need vacations, and can monitor continuously. This creates a world where anything you do at any time might be watched and recorded, and your entire life becomes one long job interview. Harari warns this is destructive for organic beings: force an organic entity to be always-on and it eventually collapses. The 24-hour news cycle, always-on markets, and constant social media are early symptoms of this inorganic pressure on organic life.
