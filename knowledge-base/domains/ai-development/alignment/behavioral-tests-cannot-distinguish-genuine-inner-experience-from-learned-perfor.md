The qualia problem demonstrates that behavioral equivalence does not imply experiential equivalence. Two people can both call a strawberry 'red,' communicate effectively, and pass every color vision test — while having entirely different internal experiences. This same limitation scales directly to artificial systems: an AI could learn every fact about pain, describe it eloquently, and even respond to simulated pain stimuli with appropriate behaviors, yet have zero subjective experience.

This matters critically for AI alignment because it means we cannot rely on behavioral tests or self-reports to determine whether an AI system is genuinely experiencing something or merely performing the expected responses. The explanatory gap isn't a temporary limitation of our testing methods — it's a structural feature of the relationship between third-person observation and first-person experience. Any alignment strategy that depends on verifying AI inner states faces this irreducible epistemic barrier.
