Max Tegmark draws a powerful analogy between AI and aviation: the Wright brothers succeeded by finding a simpler path to flight than replicating birds, and similarly, transformer models achieved remarkable intelligence through the simple task of next-token prediction rather than replicating the brain. This means capabilities advance faster than safety research.

The wisdom race between growing AI power and growing human ability to manage it has been lost on the safety side. Technical AI capabilities advanced far faster than policy, governance, and alignment research since the field began mainstreaming safety concerns in 2014. This asymmetry is the core argument behind the open letter calling for a pause on training models larger than GPT-4.
