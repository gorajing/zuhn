Harari dismantles the assumption that information equals truth. Billions of portraits of Jesus have been created over 2,000 years, and not one is authentic — we have zero information about his appearance. Creating fiction requires no research, no evidence. Creating truth requires enormous institutional investment (academic research, investigative journalism, peer review).

This means flooding the world with information — including AI-generated content — will not surface truth. Truth will drown under cheap fiction unless we deliberately invest in truth-seeking institutions. Harari recommends individuals go on 'information diets' analogous to food diets: monitor quality, reduce quantity, and periodically fast to digest and detoxify. Information is the food of the mind, and junk information produces sick minds.
