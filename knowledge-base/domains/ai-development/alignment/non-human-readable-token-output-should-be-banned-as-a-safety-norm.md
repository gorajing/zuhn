When asked what his 'magic wand' for AI safety would do, Hassabis named this specific concrete restriction: don't let AI systems communicate or output in machine languages humans can't read. This would kill the efficiency gains from letting agents talk to each other in learned compressed protocols, but preserve auditability. Combined with his call for international AI Safety Institutes (UK, US, others) as the technical bodies that benchmark systems for undesirable properties like deception, this gives a concrete governance playbook: (1) national safety institutes staffed with researchers, (2) international coordination via an IAEA-like body, (3) certification/kite-mark process for models, and (4) hard interpretability constraints on output modalities.
