The current AI safety approach maintains unilateral human control: we can always shut down, retrain, or constrain AI systems. Kellis argues this is fundamentally incompatible with building genuine trust. An intelligent system that knows you can terminate it at will has no rational basis for trusting you.

This creates a dilemma: maintaining control prevents trust, but releasing control feels dangerous. The parenting analogy suggests a gradual trust-building process: you don't give a child full autonomy immediately, but you also can't maintain total control forever if you want a healthy relationship. The transition requires genuine good faith from both parties.
