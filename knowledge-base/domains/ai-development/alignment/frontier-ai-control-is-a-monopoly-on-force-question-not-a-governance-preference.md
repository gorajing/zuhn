Ben Thompson's argument crystallizes a structural truth: if AI becomes as powerful as nuclear weapons (as its own creators claim), then private companies controlling it is equivalent to private nuclear arsenals. The nation-state's most fundamental function is maintaining a monopoly on the use of force — without it, laws cannot be enforced, public safety cannot be ensured, and democratic will cannot be executed.

This reframes the Anthropic-DoW conflict from a culture-war narrative into a structural inevitability. Regardless of which administration is in power or which company is involved, any sufficiently powerful AI will be brought under state control. The only real question is whether this happens through cooperative regulation or adversarial seizure. Companies building frontier AI must internalize that their endgame is either subservience to the state or an attempt to overthrow the state — there is no stable third option.
