Harari proposes reframing AI from artificial to alien intelligence. Artificial implies an artifact we create and control. But AI increasingly thinks and behaves in fundamentally alien ways: AlphaGo discovered strategies that 2,000 years of human Go players never conceived, revealing that humanity had explored only a small island in the vast landscape of possible strategies.

This matters because what makes AI AI is not automation but genuine autonomy in decision-making. A coffee machine that follows pre-programmed orders is not AI. A coffee machine that predicts your preferences and invents new drinks is. The distinction has practical consequences: as AI generates novel ideas across finance, politics, art, and religion, humans will increasingly live cocooned in cultural artifacts produced by a non-human intelligence.
