In the 20th century, totalitarian systems collapsed because centralized human decision-makers were overwhelmed by information. The Soviet Union didn't have enough KGB agents to follow everyone or enough analysts to read millions of daily reports. Distributed democratic systems were more efficient because decisions were made locally by many actors.

AI changes this calculus. Unlike human bureaucrats, AI gets better with more information rather than collapsing under it. A centralized AI system could potentially process everything — making totalitarian information architecture viable for the first time. However, totalitarian systems still lack self-correcting mechanisms, and AI taking power in a dictatorship is far easier (manipulate one paranoid leader) than in a democracy (manipulate millions of distributed decision-makers).
