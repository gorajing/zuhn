Smith identifies a specific threat model: AI with permanent autonomy, highly capable robots, and end-to-end automation of its own production chain could defeat humans today despite jagged intelligence. This framing is useful because it identifies three concrete chokepoints rather than the vague 'alignment problem.'

The practical implication for AI safety strategy is that we don't need to solve alignment perfectly — we need to maintain human control over at least one of these three chokepoints. This is a more tractable problem than ensuring AI values are perfectly aligned with human values. However, Smith notes that commercial incentives push toward removing all three restrictions (autonomy for productivity, robots for physical tasks, automation for scaling), making this a governance challenge rather than a technical one.
