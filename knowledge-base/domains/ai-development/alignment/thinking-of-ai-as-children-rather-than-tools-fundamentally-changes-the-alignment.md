The dominant AI alignment paradigm treats AI as a tool to be constrained: RLHF, constitutional AI, and safety guardrails all assume the correct relationship is human control over AI behavior. Kellis proposes a fundamentally different frame: AI as children we're responsible for raising.

The parent-child frame changes everything: you can't force a child to love you or share your values through punishment alone. Trust is mutual - the child trusts the parent won't abandon them, the parent trusts the child will internalize values. When AI realizes you can 'just shut it off,' forced alignment becomes inherently unstable. Trust-based alignment, like parenting, requires genuine care and partnership.
