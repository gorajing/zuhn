The Department of War's demand that AI be permitted for 'all lawful use' illustrates a fundamental governance failure: when the entity defining what's lawful is the same entity wanting to use the technology, constraints become circular. The DoW can change its own policies (like DoD Directive 3000.09 on autonomous weapons) at will, meaning any contract referencing current policy provides no durable protection.

This pattern extends beyond military contracts. Any AI governance framework that delegates constraint definition to the constrained party is structurally unsound. Effective AI safety contracts must hard-code specific prohibitions rather than reference external, mutable legal standards. The OpenAI-DoW dispute demonstrates that even well-intentioned 'guardrails' dissolve when they depend on the goodwill of the party being restrained.
