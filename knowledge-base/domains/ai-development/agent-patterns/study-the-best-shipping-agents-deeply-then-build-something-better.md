The framing for the whole talk is Picasso's line that great artists steal — not literal theft, but studying something deeply, truly understanding it, making it your own, and using that to create something better than you could have otherwise. Applied to agent engineering, this means selecting the handful of best agents currently available, studying their behavior closely, and naming the patterns they share so you can reuse them deliberately. The four patterns surfaced this way — focus modes, transparent execution, personalization, and reversibility — recur across Cursor, Claude, Harvey, and Manifold despite different domains (coding, legal, general), which is itself evidence that they are general design primitives rather than product-specific tricks. The method generalizes: in a fast-moving field, structured imitation of proven products compresses the search for good design far more efficiently than first-principles invention.
