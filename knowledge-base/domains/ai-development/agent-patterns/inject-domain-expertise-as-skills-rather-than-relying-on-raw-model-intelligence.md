The talk frames the gap with a vivid analogy: for your taxes you want Barry the experienced tax professional, not Mahesh the 300-IQ mathematician deriving the 2025 tax code from first principles. Today's agents are like Mahesh — brilliant but missing expertise, unable to absorb your specific context up front, and they don't learn over time. The fix is not waiting for a smarter model but supplying expertise externally: skills are organized collections of files that package composable procedural knowledge. This is why the highest-traction adopters are large enterprises teaching agents their organizational best practices and bespoke internal software, and why non-technical functions (finance, recruiting, legal) can extend general agents. The directional takeaway: when an agent underperforms in a domain, the lever is captured procedural knowledge, not raw intelligence.
