At a Sequoia AI event, Jensen Huang argued there should be orders of magnitude more security agents than productive agents in an enterprise. Lahav agrees with the directional thesis: in a world of non-deterministic autonomous AI systems, you need continuous monitoring agents watching for behavioral anomalies, policy violations, and emergent misbehavior.

The debate is whether secure-by-design principles embedded in the models themselves can reduce this ratio. Lahav believes significant progress on secure-by-design is possible, which would reduce but not eliminate the need for monitoring agents. Either way, the market for AI security infrastructure will be enormous, potentially larger than the productive agent market itself.
