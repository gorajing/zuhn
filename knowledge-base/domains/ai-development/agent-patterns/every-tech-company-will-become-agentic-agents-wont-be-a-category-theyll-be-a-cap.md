Olivia Moore draws a direct parallel between agents today and internet companies in 1990: initially, being a 'dot com company' was its own designator, but eventually every company became an internet company. Similarly, every AI company — and eventually every tech company — will be agentic because models are headed toward delivering outcomes rather than just inputs.

The practical implication is that standalone 'agent' companies may face the same compression that standalone 'internet' companies did. The more interesting plays are products that use agentic capabilities to unlock new consumer use cases: complex shopping, healthcare navigation, financial planning, and travel. These were impossible pre-agents because they required reliable cross-system data gathering.

Voice is highlighted as the most information-dense input medium that's about to cross from early adopters to mainstream consumers within 6-9 months, with voice dictation and AI meeting transcription already becoming normalized in tech companies.
