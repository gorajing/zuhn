Ron from Open Router observed that the typical technology adoption pattern — where innovations start as toys and take years to reach regulated enterprises — is being dramatically compressed for AI agents. He predicted the adoption curve will be measured in months, not years.

Several forces drive this compression. First, bottom-up adoption: Kilo Code's Eric noted that business-side employees (not just engineers) are now engaging with enterprise coding tools, asking about risks, and pushing for adoption. Second, security coalitions like the Toronto Alliance are forming to create industry-specific standards for secure agent deployment. Third, the cost of not adopting is becoming visible — companies see competitors deploying agents and moving faster. The panel cautioned that liability questions (who is responsible when an autonomous agent causes damage?) remain unresolved, but the consensus was that security solutions will mature faster than expected because the economic incentive is so strong.
