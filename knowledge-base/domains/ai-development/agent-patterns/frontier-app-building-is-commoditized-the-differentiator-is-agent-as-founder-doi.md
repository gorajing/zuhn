Liu's product positioning for HyperAgent: any Frontier agent can build a clean v1 app from a prompt — that's a commoditized capability now. What HyperAgent does that's different: take an idea, research the landscape, validate the problem on Reddit, do competitive analysis, build the business case, AND build the v1 product informed by all that context. The agent plays the founder role, not the developer role.

The demonstrated example: Liu fed it 'hyperlocal real estate market reports for agents generated from public data' and it returned market validation (Reddit posts of real estate agents asking for this), competitive analysis, business case for a 'medium-sized market' (not Fortune 500 size, not micro), and a v1 of the actual product. The founder activities (problem-validation, competitive scan, business case authoring) are the value-add layer above commodity coding.

For builders: design your agent product to do the work your customer's founder would do, not just the work their engineer would do. Engineering capability is increasingly free; founder judgment + execution is the durable wedge. For Zuhn editorially: this matches the evolution from 'AI helps you code' to 'AI is your full-stack founder.' The next category of multi-billion-dollar AI tools will be agent-as-founder products, not agent-as-developer products.
