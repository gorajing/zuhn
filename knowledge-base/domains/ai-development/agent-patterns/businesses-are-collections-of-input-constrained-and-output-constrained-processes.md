Mike Cannon-Brookes argues that the 'system of record' mental model is as outdated as the floppy disk save icon. Modern knowledge businesses are actually collections of processes — some input-constrained and some output-constrained — and this distinction determines how AI transforms each.

Input-constrained processes (customer service, legal review, HR compliance) have fixed demand: making them 10x faster doesn't generate 10x more questions. AI here is pure efficiency — do the same work cheaper and faster. Output-constrained processes (software development, marketing, creative work) are limited only by human creativity. AI here enables doing more output rather than limiting input.

This framework has immediate practical value: when analyzing any business function for AI automation, first classify it as input-constrained or output-constrained. The automation strategy, ROI calculation, and impact on headcount are fundamentally different for each. Input-constrained automation reduces costs; output-constrained automation expands capability. Both are valuable, but they require completely different approaches to deployment, pricing, and change management.
