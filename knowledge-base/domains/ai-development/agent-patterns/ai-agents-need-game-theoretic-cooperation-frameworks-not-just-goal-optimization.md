As AI agents proliferate in real-world environments, they will inevitably interact with other agents — both AI and human. Pure goal optimization without cooperation mechanisms leads to adversarial dynamics, resource conflicts, and emergent zero-sum behavior that harms all parties.

Game theory provides frameworks (Nash equilibria, mechanism design, cooperative games) that can be embedded into agent architectures to ensure stable multi-agent outcomes. This is a fundamental design constraint for agentic AI systems that goes beyond single-agent capability benchmarks.
