The talk repeatedly frames the engineer's job as externalizing implicit human knowledge: the environment a decision operates in, what 'important' means to the stakeholders, the reference class that determines whether average behavior applies, and the soft rules that live informally in Slack channels and Google Docs. Humans handle these effortlessly because they experience them; agents need them stated explicitly to reason at all.

The caveat is honest and important: the general decision framework is easy to draw but 'very hard to generalize' — the particulars of every step end up deeply domain-specific. This means the leverage is not in a universal decision module but in the patient, domain-by-domain work of surfacing and encoding what experts know implicitly. It reframes agent capability gains as a knowledge-elicitation problem more than a model-capability problem.
