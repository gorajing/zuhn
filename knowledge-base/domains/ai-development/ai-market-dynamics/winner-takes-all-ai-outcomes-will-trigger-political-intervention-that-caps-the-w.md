Gary's political economy prediction: the 'AI produces 9 winners and leaves 8 billion behind' scenario is not a stable equilibrium because political pressure would force redistribution well before it arrived. He speculates about a three-day work week with the other four days subsidized by the dominant companies, more leisure, return to arts and family, and side projects producing 'micro pennies.' The forecast is less confident than his consumer-behavior calls but the reasoning is load-bearing: anyone planning their career or investments on a pure winner-takes-all trajectory is implicitly betting governments will stay passive, which is historically not how extreme capitalism concentrations have ended. The practical implication for individuals: plan for a future where your agency matters (micro-entrepreneurship, side projects, analog value) rather than one where you're either a supercaler employee or destitute.
