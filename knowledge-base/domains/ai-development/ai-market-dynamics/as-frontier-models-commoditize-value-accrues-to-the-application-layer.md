Ubl presents two futures. In one, a single model lab wins, AI stays expensive, all value accrues to that company, and 'AI engineers' degrade into forward-deployed engineers for whichever lab dominates. He considers this unlikely. The more probable future is commoditization: Claude, Codex, and Google's models are all strong and converging, and Google in particular will keep pushing prices down because it has the cheapest infrastructure, driving the cost of intelligence toward zero.

In the commoditized world, the application layer is where power concentrates. AI engineers can build a stable layer on top of interchangeable models — sometimes model X is better, sometimes Y — so innovation no longer requires working at a model lab. He cites Europe (Vercel's AI SDK, the Pi coding agent, Open Claw) as evidence that application-layer leadership is decoupled from model-side leadership. The strategic takeaway: bet on building agents and the application layer, treat models as a swappable commodity input, and expect the next AI paradigms to be discovered at the application layer rather than inside the labs.
