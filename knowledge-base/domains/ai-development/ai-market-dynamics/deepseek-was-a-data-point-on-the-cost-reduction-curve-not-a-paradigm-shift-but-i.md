Dario provides the clearest framework for understanding DeepSeek: two simultaneous dynamics drive AI economics. First, algorithmic efficiency improves roughly 4X per year — meaning last year's billion-dollar model can be replicated for $250M today. Second, economic demand for intelligence means total spending grows roughly 10X per year, because smarter models have higher value. The 4X efficiency gain is consumed and exceeded by the desire for ever-smarter models.

DeepSeek was 'literally just another data point on the cost reduction curve.' They spent a few million on the final model (consistent with the curve), but billions on R&D and infrastructure (consistent with other frontier labs). Their chip inventory is roughly on par with expectations.

What IS notable: DeepSeek is the first time a Chinese company has gone 'toe to toe' with US companies on engineering innovation at the frontier. This is significant because it validates export control urgency rather than undermining it — if China can match US engineering with fewer chips, then preventing them from getting billions of chips becomes even more critical, because a billion-dollar model is now more capable than ever.
