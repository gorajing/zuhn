The enterprise world spent 20 years doing massive, all-or-nothing technology rollouts — Salesforce implementations that cost a quarter million dollars, took three years, and ended with nobody using the system because it wasn't built iteratively. AI has an advantage here: its distributed architecture allows training in layers, building multiple specialized agents, and getting quick ROI on narrow scopes. Rather than 'train AI on everything,' start with 'train AI on project documents, tell the team exactly what it knows, and iterate.' Scope carefully — you can't put in data about topic A and expect answers about topic B. This phased approach directly contradicts the 'big bang AI transformation' pitch that many consultants sell, but it's what actually works.
