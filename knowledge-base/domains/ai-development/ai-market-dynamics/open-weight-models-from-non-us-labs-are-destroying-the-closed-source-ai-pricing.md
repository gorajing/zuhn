DeepSeek R1 is open-weight, meaning anyone can download, modify, and self-host it — unlike OpenAI and Anthropic's closed models. Combined with training costs of $5.57M versus OpenAI's rumored $100M+, this creates a two-pronged competitive threat: the model is both cheaper to build AND free to use.

For Western AI companies charging premium API prices, this fundamentally undermines the business model. If a near-equivalent model is available for free self-hosting, the premium must be justified by something other than raw capability — likely trust, enterprise support, data privacy guarantees, and ecosystem integration. This mirrors the open-source disruption pattern seen in databases, operating systems, and cloud infrastructure. The strategic implication is that AI companies need to build defensible moats around applications, workflows, and trust rather than model quality alone.
