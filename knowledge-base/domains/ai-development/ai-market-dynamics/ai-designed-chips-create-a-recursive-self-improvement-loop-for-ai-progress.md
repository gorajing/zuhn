Recursive Intelligence's thesis is that chips are the fuel for AI scaling laws, and there's a fundamental mismatch between how fast AI methods evolve (months) versus how long chips take to design (years). By dramatically accelerating chip design with AI, they can enable co-design and co-evolution of models and hardware together.

Their Alpha Chip work at Google demonstrated this concretely: across four generations of TPUs, AI-generated layouts achieved increasingly superhuman performance, with the delta between AI and human baselines growing each generation -- a property of AI scaling with data. The company's vision is to move the industry from 'fabless' to 'designless,' where companies don't need hundreds of in-house chip designers to get custom silicon optimized for their specific workloads. This could trigger a Cambrian explosion of custom chips for everything from AR/VR to space computing.
