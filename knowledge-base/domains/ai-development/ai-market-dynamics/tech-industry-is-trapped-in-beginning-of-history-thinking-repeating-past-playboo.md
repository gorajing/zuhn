Zitron's 'beginning of history' concept (a Fukuyama inversion) describes how the tech industry keeps assuming what worked before will work again. Amazon Web Services took 11 years and $39B to become profitable — Oracle's AI bet already dwarfs that timeline and investment. But AI data centers are not fiber optic cables from the dot-com era; they don't last as long, aren't as broadly useful, and serve unprofitable customers.

This is a powerful mental model for technology investors: when an industry's dominant players can't articulate a new thesis and instead default to 'spend more money,' they're revealing that they have no real strategy. The pattern of Oracle, Meta, Microsoft, and Amazon pouring hundreds of billions into infrastructure while AI application revenue remains tiny ($55B total) against trillions in spending is historically unprecedented and should be treated as a warning signal, not a sign of confidence.
