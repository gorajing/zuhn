Sankar introduces a rubric for evaluating which SaaS survives the AI wave: alpha vs beta software. Beta software made you more similar to everyone else — standardized ERP, generic project management, commodity CRM. The feedback loop was 'can I sell it?' not 'did it add value?' Alpha software expresses how you're different from competitors — it encodes your strategy, your unique workflows, your competitive advantage.

AI and vibe-coding make it trivial to recreate beta software. If Monday.com's value is a Kanban board with integrations, that can be rebuilt in a weekend. But alpha software — the kind that lets you express and operationalize what makes your company uniquely competitive — becomes more valuable because AI makes it easier to build custom, specific-to-you tools. The COVID stress test proved this: nobody's $5B ERP implementation saved their supply chain. What CEOs talked about was Zoom and Teams. The alpha platforms (like Palantir's Ontology) helped customers adapt at the speed of disruption.
