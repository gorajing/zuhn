HBS professor Rem Coning argues that just as Warren Buffett's edge was capital allocation and McKinsey's was talent allocation, the defining advantage in the AI era is intelligence allocation. This means deciding what Claude does, what Lovable does, what GPT does, and critically what humans still do.

The key is not doing things better but doing things differently. Humans still think differently from models — and in strategy, differentiation beats optimization. Founders who master the orchestration of multiple AI models plus human judgment will outperform those who simply use one model for everything. This shifts the strategic question from 'how do we use AI?' to 'how do we allocate intelligence across our entire operation?'
