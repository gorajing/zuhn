Semiconductor companies operate on 5-8 year chip lifecycles. They need partners who will still exist and still care about the same problem in 5 years. Nota's proudest feedback: 'It's evident how much your company has done this work. It's visible in the product.' The semiconductor companies valued this 'dirt on the hands' signal more than any benchmark result.

The practical approach: regular meetings (January, March, May cadence), demonstrating new projects and programs each time, and extremely clear communication about what each side wants and what the next action items are. The CEO explicitly warns against the 'I want to give it a try' mindset for global expansion -- you must approach it as your only option, not an experiment. This 'all-in' commitment signal is what enables trust with partners who think in decade-long timescales.
