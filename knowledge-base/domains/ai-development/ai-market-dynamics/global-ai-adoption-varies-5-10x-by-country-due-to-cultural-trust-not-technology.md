a16z's first per-capita AI adoption heat map reveals surprising global patterns. Singapore ranks #1, followed by Hong Kong, UAE, and South Korea. The US sits at #20 despite being where the biggest AI products originate. Russia and China are sub-50 but have built entirely parallel AI ecosystems out of necessity (sanctions and censorship respectively).

The key driver isn't technology access — it's cultural trust. An Edelman survey showed US AI trust at 32%, while top-adopting countries range from 50-70%. The top-4 countries share demographics of tech-first, white-collar, high-skill workforces, while the US has a massive base of jobs AI hasn't touched yet (retail, transportation). China's AI favorability is at 80%.

This creates opportunities for founders targeting high-adoption markets outside the US, and suggests that the 'AI anxiety' narrative common in American media may actually be a competitive disadvantage for US-based products trying to reach mainstream adoption.
