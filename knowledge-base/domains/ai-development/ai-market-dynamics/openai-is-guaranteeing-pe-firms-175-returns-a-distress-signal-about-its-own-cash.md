Jason flags that OpenAI is offering private equity investors a guaranteed minimum return of 17.5% as part of a joint venture that helps PE firms deploy AI across portfolio companies and offset high upfront costs. Guaranteed returns are non-standard and generally unattractive from the issuer's perspective — you take on the downside while capping your upside. Such structures typically signal either (a) extreme confidence in outcomes or (b) capital-raise desperation. Combined with OpenAI cancelling the $1B Disney Sora deal and the 'panic mode' narrative, this reads as a distress signal about capital needs. Low confidence because we don't know the full deal terms, but it's a useful data point for tracking OpenAI's financial position.
