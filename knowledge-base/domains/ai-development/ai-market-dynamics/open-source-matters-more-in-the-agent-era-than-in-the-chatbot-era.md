Kimi's Sarah articulated a strategic shift: in the chatbot era, openness was nice-to-have because you were just optimizing a single response. In the agent era, you're optimizing entire multi-step workflows where each step's accuracy compounds. Enterprise users and developers need the ability to inspect, tune, and control individual steps in the pipeline.

This makes open-source models and open infrastructure more strategically critical than before. When your agent executes 100 sequential steps (as Kimi's agent swarm does), you need visibility and control at each step. Closed models create black boxes at every step in the chain, making debugging and optimization much harder. This argument suggests the open-source model ecosystem may actually gain strategic importance rather than lose it as AI moves from chatbots to agents.
