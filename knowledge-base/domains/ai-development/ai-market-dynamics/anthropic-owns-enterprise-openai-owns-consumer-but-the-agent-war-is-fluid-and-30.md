20VC's snapshot: Anthropic turned down $800B funding offers; secondary market trades them at $1T; European family-office LPs say 'all our families want only Anthropic, every dollar wants its home in Anthropic.' But Sam Altman just announced Codex usage up 50% in one month and OpenAI's autonomous agents are launching the same week. Even Calacanis, who has been running workflows through both APIs, says he can't honestly predict 30 days out.

The operational implication: capital deployment decisions based on current market share are dangerous. Three things are true simultaneously — (1) Anthropic has won enterprise developer mindshare for now, (2) the autonomous-agent war is restarting from zero with new product surfaces, (3) compute access (where OpenAI may have the edge via Microsoft) becomes the binding constraint as both labs scale. For Zuhn editorially: avoid declaring winners in print. The story is the structural fact that two entities are now plausibly trillion-dollar AI labs with different architectures of competition (Anthropic = enterprise-of-record + alignment narrative; OpenAI = consumer + agent product velocity), and the durable observations are about how the war is shaped, not who's winning this week.
