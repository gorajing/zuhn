The WEF report on governance in the age of generative AI highlights that traditional regulatory approaches — designed for slower-moving technologies with clearer boundaries — struggle with AI systems that are general-purpose, rapidly evolving, and capable of both beneficial and harmful applications simultaneously. Key challenges include the speed of deployment outpacing regulatory cycles, the difficulty of auditing opaque foundation models, and the global nature of AI development creating regulatory arbitrage opportunities. The report advocates for multi-stakeholder governance models that combine industry self-regulation, government oversight, and civil society input, rather than relying on any single approach. For investors and builders, this signals that regulatory risk in AI is not a binary pass/fail but an evolving landscape where companies with proactive governance practices will have durable competitive advantages.
