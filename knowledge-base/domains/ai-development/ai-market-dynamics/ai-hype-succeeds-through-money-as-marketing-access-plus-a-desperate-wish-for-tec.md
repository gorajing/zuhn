Bender identifies three mechanisms driving AI hype acceptance. First, enormous money behind AI translates directly into marketing power and access to corridors of political power — the ability to shape narratives at the highest levels. Second, there's a deep human desire to believe that technology can solve existential crises: 'it would be lovely to think that with just a little bit more compute, climate crisis solved.' This desire for technological salvation makes people receptive to inflated claims.

Third, and most subtle: language itself is the vehicle. Because LLMs produce coherent text, and because human cognition automatically projects understanding behind coherent text, every interaction with a chatbot reinforces the illusion of intelligence. This creates a self-reinforcing cycle: impressive-seeming demos attract investment, investment buys marketing, marketing shapes narratives, narratives drive more investment. Breaking this cycle requires recognizing that the people telling us AI is revolutionary are exactly the people who profit from us believing it.
