Epoch AI frames the bubble question precisely: if AI labs stopped developing new models today and just ran inference on existing ones, they would be profitable. The money being spent is on building even larger future models, which is the actual gamble. This means the 'bubble' framing is misleading — current AI is clearly generating real value (people are paying for it), but the massive capex on next-generation models could become a bubble if scaling returns diminish.

The useful heuristic they offer is to track Nvidia revenue growth as a proxy for real demand. If revenue keeps growing, the spending is justified. If a bubble exists, it will burst suddenly rather than deflate gradually. Their GDP forecast extrapolation suggests AI could add ~1% of GDP by 2030 based on current revenue trends alone (without assuming AGI), which would be significant by economic standards. The extreme scenario — AI that can do any remote job — would produce either 30%+ GDP growth or catastrophic outcomes, with little middle ground.
