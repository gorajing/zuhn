A frontier-tech VC with 11 years of experience went on the record: every impressive humanoid robotics demo on social media is paired with no commercial deployment at scale. The biggest deals in market are still POCs — collaborative discovery processes with a single customer to find out what's possible — not contracted volume orders.

The diagnostic: if humanoid robotics had crossed the chasm, we would expect to see at least one publicly-disclosed >100-unit commercial deployment. We do not. The valuations being assigned to humanoid robotics companies are therefore underwriting a future state, not a current revenue trajectory.

This is the demo-deployment gap manifesting at the venture level: capital is being deployed on the strength of capability demos, but the field has not produced the boring evidence (signed contracts, repeat orders, deployment counts) that would normally precede this valuation level. For investors, the implication is that humanoid robotics is more like a 5-10 year category bet than a 12-24 month exit thesis.
