Dario is remarkably direct: 'AI is going to be better than almost all humans at almost all things. We have to reckon with that world as soon as possible.' His timeline: 2-4 years to models as intelligent as Nobel Prize winners across fields, able to do anything on a computer — 'any remote work that humans do, any modality, tasks that take days, weeks, months.'

On coding specifically, he expects AI writing 90% of code in 3-6 months and essentially all code in 12 months, with humans still needed for design decisions, collaboration, and common sense — but those 'little islands' will eventually be picked off too.

His most provocative insight on economic disruption: randomly displacing 50% of workers is worse than displacing everyone, because it sends the message 'we're picking half of people and saying you are useless.' Universal displacement at least forces society to redefine value and meaning. He draws an analogy to chess — Deep Blue beat Kasparov 30 years ago, yet Magnus Carlsen is a celebrity and fashion model. Human excellence retained meaning even after AI surpassed it.

Dario proposes the 'crazy town' scenario: if AI causes 10% annual economic growth, the tax base grows enough to erase deficits and fund massive transition programs. He released the Anthropic Economic Index to measure whether AI usage is augmentative or replacing in various fields.

This framing is critical for enterprise sales: customers worried about AI replacing their workforce need to hear that Anthropic's own CEO advocates for augmentation-first deployment and policy preparation.
