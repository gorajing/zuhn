Generative AI presents governance challenges that differ qualitatively from previous technology waves. Unlike social media or cloud computing, generative AI systems exhibit emergent capabilities that are difficult to predict, can be repurposed for harmful uses without modification, and are being deployed at a pace that outstrips regulatory capacity. The WEF report argues that bolting AI provisions onto existing regulatory frameworks is insufficient.

What's needed are purpose-built governance structures that can adapt to rapid capability changes, involve multi-stakeholder input (governments, industry, civil society, technical experts), and operate internationally given AI's borderless nature. This has implications for AI companies building products, investors evaluating regulatory risk, and startups positioning in the AI stack — the governance layer is still being defined, creating both uncertainty and opportunity for those who engage proactively with emerging frameworks.
