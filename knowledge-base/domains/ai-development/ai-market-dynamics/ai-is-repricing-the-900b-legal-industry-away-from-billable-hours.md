The legal industry's $900B market has been built on the billable-hour model for decades, but AI is fundamentally repricing how legal work gets delivered. By automating research, document review, and contract analysis, AI tools can offer faster, outcome-based services at scale rather than charging for time spent.

This shift opens a $70B+ market for more efficient legal delivery. The implication extends beyond legal — any professional services industry built on time-based billing (consulting, accounting, architecture) faces similar repricing pressure as AI commoditizes the labor component and forces a shift toward value-based pricing.
