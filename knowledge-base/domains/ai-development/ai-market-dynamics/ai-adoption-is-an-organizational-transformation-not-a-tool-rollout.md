DORA's guidance is operationally conservative: clarify AI policies, connect AI to internal context, prioritize foundational practices, fortify safety nets, invest in internal platforms, and focus on end users. That list challenges a recurring AI-economics belief that the next wave is mostly about replacing headcount with code and maintaining ultra-lean cores.

The stronger synthesis is a tension: AI does enable smaller, faster teams in some contexts, but the durable advantage may accrue to organizations that can redesign their work system around AI rather than those that merely buy tools or hire fewer people. This warrants creating or updating a tension between 'AI collapses headcount and lead durability' and 'AI raises the premium on organizational delivery substrate.'
