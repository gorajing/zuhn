The World Economic Forum's exploration of generative AI governance highlights that traditional tech regulation — built around specific, predictable use cases — fails when applied to systems with emergent, general-purpose capabilities. Generative AI can produce novel outputs across text, code, images, and reasoning, making it impossible to enumerate all potential harms in advance.

Effective governance must address the dual-use nature of foundation models (the same model that assists research can generate misinformation), the speed of capability advancement (regulations risk being obsolete before implementation), and the concentration of compute resources among a few actors. For startups building on generative AI, this means anticipating regulatory shifts and building governance into products proactively rather than retrofitting compliance later.
