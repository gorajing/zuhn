The explicit statement that AI compute demand is driving a utility-sector CAPEX cycle is noteworthy because it comes from the operator of Berkshire Hathaway Energy, which serves markets 24% below the national retail electric rate. BHE is not an AI speculator — it is a conservative utility that is nevertheless acknowledging that the load forecast has shifted materially and requires infrastructure investment. Two substantive details worth extracting: (1) the cycle is real enough that a cost-disciplined utility is planning for it, not dismissing it; (2) BHE's position is that hyperscalers should pay for the infrastructure they require, not have it socialized to residential ratepayers. This matters for ai-development/ai-market-dynamics because it's a concrete signal that the AI compute boom is translating to real-world utility capex and real-world cost-of-capital discussions, and for investing more broadly because it identifies utilities and grid infrastructure as a potentially undervalued downstream beneficiary of the AI investment wave. Marked time-sensitive because the cycle is dependent on continued AI demand growth — if demand plateaus, the CAPEX forecast collapses.
