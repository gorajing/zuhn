OpenAI's o1 launched as a premium $200/month reasoning model, yet within months DeepSeek released R1 — a free, MIT-licensed alternative matching or exceeding o1 on math and software engineering benchmarks. This pattern of rapid open-source catch-up has strategic implications.

For builders, this means proprietary model access is a shrinking advantage. Product differentiation must come from application-layer innovation (data, UX, workflows) rather than model access. For investors, it signals that pure model companies face commoditization pressure unless they build durable advantages beyond raw capability.
