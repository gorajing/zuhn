The pattern: Musk → xAI. Amodei (and sister Daniela + core Anthropic cofounders) → Anthropic. Sutskever → Safe Superintelligence. Murati → Thinking Machines Lab. Greg Brockman has returned but the orbit continues spinning off. All of them worked together at OpenAI. Hao's observation: 'It's not just don't get along, they end up hating each other after working together.'

The underlying reason isn't personality clashes — it's that imperial ambition to create AI in your own image is structurally incompatible with sharing decision rights. If you believe the technology will be civilization-defining and you believe your vision is correct, delegating to anyone else is an existential risk. So every time one of these figures accumulates the capital, talent, and conviction to run their own company, they do — and the company they left becomes an enemy, not a peer.

Implication: the industry will not converge on shared alignment approaches, shared safety standards, or shared governance, because the structure selects against convergence. Every time one figure tries to establish the standard, the others must compete with an alternative. 'Safe Superintelligence' is an indirect shot at OpenAI. 'Anthropic' was founded to build AI the way OpenAI wasn't. This is why regulatory approaches based on voluntary industry coordination will fail — the founders are structurally selected against cooperating.
