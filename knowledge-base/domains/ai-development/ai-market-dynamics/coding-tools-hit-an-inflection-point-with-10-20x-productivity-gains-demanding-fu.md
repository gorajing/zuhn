David George describes a concrete case study: a pre-AI company founder, frustrated with a product, assigned two deeply AI-skilled engineers to rebuild it from scratch using Claude Code, Codex, and Cursor with unlimited tool budgets. Progress was 10-20x faster than their existing team. The tool bills were high enough that it forced the founder to reconsider what his entire org should look like — concluding he needs every product and engineering team working this way within 12 months.

Another portfolio CEO has adopted the framework of asking for every task: 'Can I do this with electricity or do I need blood?' This represents the extreme mindset shift happening among the most aggressive adopters. December 2024 was identified as the turning point for coding tool capabilities.

The practical warning: companies that don't fully embrace AI coding tools within the next 12 months will move dramatically slower than peers. This isn't optional optimization — it's a structural competitiveness requirement. The org design implications are profound: the boundaries between product, engineering, and design are blurring when two engineers with AI tools can outproduce traditional teams.
