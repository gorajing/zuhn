The World Economic Forum and similar bodies continue producing governance frameworks for generative AI, but the core challenge is structural: regulatory institutions operate on multi-year cycles while AI capabilities and deployment advance on monthly timescales. This mismatch means that by the time governance frameworks are adopted, the technology landscape has already shifted.

This governance gap creates an asymmetric advantage for large incumbents (OpenAI, Google, Anthropic) who have the resources to establish credible self-governance programs, participate in standards bodies, and shape regulatory conversations. Smaller AI companies and startups face a double bind — they lack resources for compliance theater but also lack the market power to influence governance frameworks in their favor. The practical effect is that governance becomes a competitive moat rather than a public good.
