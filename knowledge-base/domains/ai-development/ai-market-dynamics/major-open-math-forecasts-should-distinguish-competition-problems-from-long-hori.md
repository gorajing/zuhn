PRED-260325-5C39 is not directly falsified: the Apple tasks are puzzles, not theorem proving, and the paper still acknowledges a medium-complexity advantage for reasoning models. But the evidence is a warning against treating current reasoning traces as reliable long-horizon proof search.

The confidence recommendation is modest downward pressure, not reversal. AI progress in formal math may still come through proof assistants, search, external verifiers, and domain-specific scaffolds, but current language-model reasoning alone shows scale-dependent brittleness that matters for open problems requiring sustained exactness.
