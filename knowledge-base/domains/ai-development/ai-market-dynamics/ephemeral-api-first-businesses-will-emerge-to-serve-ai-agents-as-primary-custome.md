Stripe's machine payment protocol demo showed agents autonomously paying third-party services (BrowserBase for a browser session, Parallel AI for web search, Postal Form for physical mail) as part of completing a task. The total cost for planning a birthday party across multiple services was roughly $5.47. This points to a nascent business model category: companies that exist primarily to serve AI agents, with no need for traditional SaaS infrastructure like dashboards, admin panels, or landing pages — just a hyper-useful single API endpoint.

The key insight is that these businesses can offer ephemeral, per-use access rather than subscription relationships. An agent doesn't need a BrowserBase account — it just needs one browser session for 30 seconds. This micropayment model dramatically lowers the barrier for agents to access specialized capabilities, and creates opportunities for highly focused, single-purpose services that would be too niche for human-facing products. The convergence of tokens-as-currency and dollars-as-currency (both representing 'cost of intelligence or service') suggests these economic models will feel increasingly natural.
