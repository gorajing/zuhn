Alibaba Cloud's Jeff offered a vivid economic analogy for the open vs closed model debate: closed models are like mega manufacturing plants where many customers share GPU capacity, achieving economies of scale. Open models are like home manufacturing — highly customizable to your specific needs, but you bear the full infrastructure cost alone.

This framing cuts through the ideological debate about open vs closed by grounding it in economics. Open source wins when you need data privacy, low latency, or deep customization. Closed source wins when you want shared infrastructure costs and don't need full control. Jensen Huang's 'token factory' concept maps directly to this: the future will have both mega token factories (closed API providers) and boutique home operations (open model deployments), each serving different market segments.
