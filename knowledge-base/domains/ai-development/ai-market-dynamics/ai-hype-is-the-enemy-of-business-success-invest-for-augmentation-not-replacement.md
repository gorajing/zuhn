Acemoglu warns that most executives invest in AI blindly, driven by pressure from consultants and media telling them competitors are spending big. This fear-driven investment pattern produces waste, not innovation. He notes that historically, no business became the 'jewel of their industry' by just cutting costs.

The better approach: start with your human resources and ask where technology can augment their capabilities to create new goods and services. The real promise of AI is not automating existing work but enabling entirely new offerings — financial inclusion services, elder care innovations, climate adaptation tools. Business leaders should resist the hype cycle and focus on strategic deployment that leverages human-AI complementarity rather than substitution.
