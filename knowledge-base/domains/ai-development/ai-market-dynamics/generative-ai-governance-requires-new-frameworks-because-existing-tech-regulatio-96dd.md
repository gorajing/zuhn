The World Economic Forum's exploration of generative AI governance highlights a fundamental mismatch: existing technology regulation was built for deterministic systems where you can audit inputs and outputs, trace decisions, and reproduce results. Generative AI violates all three assumptions — the same prompt can produce different outputs, the reasoning process is opaque, and the outputs are contextually generated rather than retrieved from a fixed database.

This means governance strategies must shift from output auditing to process governance: evaluating training data provenance, deployment guardrails, use-case restrictions, and organizational accountability structures. For startups building in this space, the governance uncertainty creates both risk and opportunity — companies that develop robust self-governance frameworks early will have regulatory moats when formal rules eventually arrive.
