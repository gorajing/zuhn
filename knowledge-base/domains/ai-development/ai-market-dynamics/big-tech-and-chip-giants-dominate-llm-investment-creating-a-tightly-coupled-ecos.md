The LLM startup ecosystem has attracted $148.5B across 32 developers from 630+ investors, but the capital structure reveals a deeply entangled network. The largest checks come from companies — NVIDIA, Amazon, Microsoft, Alibaba, Salesforce, Google — that simultaneously act as investors, compute suppliers, distribution partners, potential acquirers, and direct competitors.

This creates a uniquely conflicted ecosystem where the same entities providing essential infrastructure (cloud compute, chips) are also funding and competing with their customers. For startups in this space, this means navigating relationships where your investor is also your largest cost center and may be building the same product. The tight coupling introduces systemic fragility: if one major player changes strategy, it ripples through investment, supply, and competitive dynamics simultaneously.
