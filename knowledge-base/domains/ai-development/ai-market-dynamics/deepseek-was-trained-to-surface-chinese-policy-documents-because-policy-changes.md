Victor Shih, a China political economy expert, has inductively observed that DeepSeek is the best model for surfacing Chinese government policy documents and high-level official statements. Baidu's models return social-media-flavored content; American models are 'okay'; only DeepSeek reliably returns policy-weighted results. His hypothesis: because DeepSeek was built by quant hedge fund High-Flyer, and because in the Chinese market the largest alpha source is anticipating policy changes (replacing the old insider-info model of calling ministry contacts), High-Flyer deliberately upweighted policy documents in training.

This is a rare concrete example of how commercial incentives shape what an open-weights model is secretly good at. The same weights are now used globally, meaning every downstream application of DeepSeek inherits this China-policy bias whether users want it or not. It also implies that frontier Chinese model capabilities may be optimized for surveillance-and-forecasting use cases first, with general-purpose reasoning as a byproduct.

For research or investment analysis involving Chinese policy signals, DeepSeek is not just 'another option' — it's specifically trained for this domain. Conversely, for consumer or general reasoning tasks, its alpha is lower than its benchmarks might suggest.
