Against the wall-to-wall agentic-commerce headlines, the practitioner reality check: actual agent-driven traffic to retail sites is currently a very small fraction of total, and it converts worse than direct visits. Two reasons: (1) consumer trust in delegating purchases to agents is still nascent (see the autonomy/trust insight), and (2) major platforms like Amazon actively block agents from calling their sites (see the margin-defense insight).

This matters as a calibration against overcommitting to a channel that doesn't yet move volume. The trajectory is real and worth preparing for (the SEO→agent-optimization and funnel-collapse insights are about getting ready), but the present-tense volume does not justify reallocating the bulk of commerce investment to agent channels today.

The time-sensitive nature is explicit: this is a 'right now' snapshot. The honest framing — prepare for the shift, but don't mistake the hype curve for the adoption curve. The gap between them is exactly where premature investment gets burned. Track agent-traffic share and its conversion rate as the leading indicators of when this flips from trajectory to channel.
