Bret Taylor identifies a paradigm shift from selling productivity tools to selling outcomes. Traditional SaaS companies like Salesforce and ServiceNow sell enhanced productivity to attorneys or customer service agents, creating a TAM limited by the number of workers and software budgets. Vertical AI agents sell the work itself: not a tool to help attorneys do antitrust review faster, but the antitrust review itself at a fraction of the labor cost.

This reframes the addressable market dramatically. Current top SaaS companies plateau around $200-300B market cap. But when you're replacing high-cost labor rather than selling it $20/month software subscriptions, the TAM expands by orders of magnitude. Taylor believes this could create the first trillion-dollar applied enterprise software company. The value chain has three layers: foundation models (consolidating, low margin, high scale like cloud infrastructure), tooling (threatened by model providers, closer to the sun), and vertical agents (largest opportunity, furthest from model provider competition).
