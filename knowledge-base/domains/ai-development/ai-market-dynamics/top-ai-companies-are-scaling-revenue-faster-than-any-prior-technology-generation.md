The current AI growth cycle is outpacing prior technology waves — including mobile, cloud, and SaaS — in terms of revenue scaling speed. Top AI companies are pulling far ahead of competitors, which forces incumbents into defensive positions and reshuffles market leadership faster than historical precedent would suggest.

This has cascading effects: faster scaling means shorter windows for competitive entry, higher capital requirements to stay relevant, and market definitions that shift before traditional analysis can keep up. For founders, the implication is that timing and speed of execution matter more than in previous cycles. For investors, it means traditional valuation frameworks anchored to prior technology adoption curves may systematically undervalue early AI leaders.
