a16z reports a significant disconnect between Fortune 500 CEO rhetoric ('we have to adapt, we're dying to understand AI tools, we're ready to change') and actual organizational change. The biggest barrier isn't technology readiness — it's change management. Getting thousands of employees to adopt AI tools, changing established business processes, and putting AI into production workflows is extremely hard.

Concrete proof that it works when done right: Chime reduced support costs by 60%. Rocket Mortgage saved 1.1 million hours in underwriting (6x YoY improvement), translating to $40M in annual run rate savings. Navan's AI handles 50% of travel booking interactions, delivering a 20 percentage point gross margin expansion over 3 years while competitors stood still.

The prediction: a major reckoning over the next 5 years between companies that can push through change management and those that can't. The productivity gap between adopters and non-adopters will compound year after year. Many corporations are currently doing the latent work of orienting their backend systems to be AI-ready, and results will accelerate as this foundation work completes.
