IBM's 2026 tech-leader study pressures the belief that AI-native organizations can mainly win by replacing human-to-human workflows with agent operating systems. Surveyed CIOs and CTOs expect 38% more AI agents by 2027, but only 11% say they are fully ready for that scale and 77% say AI adoption is already outpacing governance. The counterevidence is strong because it comes from 2,000 C-level technology executives across 33 geographies and 19 industries, not pundit skepticism. The belief should move from 'replacement is the winning architecture' toward 'replacement only works when control, visibility, and governance are designed into the architecture first.'
