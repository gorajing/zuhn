When asked about AI infrastructure, most leaders think cloud credits, data pipelines, and GPU capacity. Kozyrkov argues the critical missing piece is human infrastructure: Have you managed user expectations? Do users accustomed to narrow, correct answers understand they'll get probabilistic outputs? Have you trained staff on how to think about AI tools? If you need humans in the loop, are they actually alert or asleep at the wheel? For high-stakes tasks, have you considered consensus AI with multiple human reviewers? Even highly performant AI systems will encounter the long tail — outlier situations nobody anticipated. How flammable is your organization when that inevitable mistake touches a user? The counterweight to the tech-first AI narrative: the hardest part of AI deployment isn't technical — it's preparing your organization's humans for a fundamentally different relationship with their tools.
