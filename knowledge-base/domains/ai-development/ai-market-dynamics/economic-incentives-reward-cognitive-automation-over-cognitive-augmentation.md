Appleton notes that OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and the other foundation labs are investing heavily in 'autonomous, agentic workflows like the recent Deep Research hype' and 'reasoning models where the models themselves are trained to be the critical thinkers.' Both directions focus on getting the model to do cognitive work INSTEAD of the user, rather than helping the user become a better thinker. The economic reason is straightforward: automating a knowledge worker's job captures 100% of the labor cost, while augmenting a knowledge worker's thinking captures only a fraction. Appleton accepts the economic logic but pushes back: 'the labs have enough resources to pursue both.' This is one of those situations where the default Silicon Valley optimization target (automate maximum labor) creates a blind spot for a different high-value category (tool-for-thought interfaces) that requires a different financial model to pursue seriously.
