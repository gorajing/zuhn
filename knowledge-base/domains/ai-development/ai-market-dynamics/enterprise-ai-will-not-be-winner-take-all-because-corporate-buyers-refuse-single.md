Nadella's core framing from his Oracle/IBM client-server experience: consumer markets can go winner-take-all through network effects, but enterprise markets cannot, because corporate buyers systematically demand two or more qualified suppliers to preserve negotiating leverage and continuity risk. He saw investors declare Azure dead against Amazon's lead and was right to ignore them.

The implication extends to foundation models. Even if one lab reaches AGI first, open-source alternatives plus state actors will ensure a competitive floor — open-source acts as a 'governor' on closed-source dominance, the same dynamic Linux imposed on Windows. A separate consequence: at scale, nothing is commodity. The know-how of running 60+ region hyperscale compute is not replicable by 'racking and stacking servers,' so the supposed commoditization risk gets absorbed into operational moats.

Practical application: when evaluating AI markets for investment, competitive analysis, or product strategy, distinguish consumer categories (where ChatGPT-style network effects may entrench a single winner) from enterprise categories (where assume 2-4 winners by default). Betting on winner-take-all in B2B AI is the same mistake VCs made against Azure.
