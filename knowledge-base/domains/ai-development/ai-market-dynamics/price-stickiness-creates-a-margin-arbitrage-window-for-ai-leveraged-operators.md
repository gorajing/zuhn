The observation is that prices in professional services embed the cost of human labor as a historical anchor, and those anchors don't update in real time as AI collapses the delivery cost. A marketing agency charging $10K/month for work that now takes a solo operator and an agent stack 3 hours still gets paid $10K — because the buyer's reference price is based on the old world. This gives AI-native operators a transient but large margin advantage, and also lets them invest the margin into better agents and capture more market. The window is inherently time-sensitive: as the market learns, prices compress, and the arbitrage converts into a volume game. Operators should maximize the early-window margin to fund the volume-game phase.
