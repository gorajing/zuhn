Tomasz Tunguz's analysis shows AI isn't just accelerating startup development cycles — it's fundamentally restructuring the cost basis. When AI copilots replace junior engineering hires, automate customer support, and generate marketing content, the resulting margin profile looks more like a mature software company than an early-stage startup.

This creates a counterintuitive dynamic: AI-native startups can command higher valuations not because they grow faster, but because they're structurally more profitable at every stage. Investors are essentially paying for operating leverage that previously only existed at scale. The risk is that this margin advantage erodes as AI tools become table stakes and competitors achieve the same cost structure.
