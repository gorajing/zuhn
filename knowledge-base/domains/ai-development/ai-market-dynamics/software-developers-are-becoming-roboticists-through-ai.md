Thomas Wolf identifies a three-phase developer evolution: traditional software development, then AI-aware development (where most developers now understand LLMs and want to train models), and next, robotics-aware development. The LeRobot library bridges this gap: it's just Python code, so with vibe coding tools, even non-roboticists can control robots and create new behaviors.

The three persona types already using LeRobot confirm this trend: traditional roboticists who want AI capabilities, AI developers who see robotics as physical AI, and curious people (including investors) who buy $100 robot arms just to understand the technology. This expanding developer base is exactly what made transformers succeed: when every developer wants to use the tools, the ecosystem growth becomes exponential.
