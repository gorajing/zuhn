Altman: 'worst case lights out for everyone, best case we cure cancer, solve climate change, abundance.' Amodei: 'worst case catastrophic existential harm, best case mass human flourishing.' These are not opposing positions inside the industry — they are the same two-sided myth deployed by all the major lab CEOs.

The function is anti-democratic: if the stakes are this extreme, broad participation in developing the technology is too risky, and the few companies that understand the technology must be the ones controlling every step. Altman literally manufactured this exact framing in 2015 by mirroring Elon Musk's doom language ('greatest existential threat,' 'summoning the demon') in blog posts that were designed to convince Musk to co-found OpenAI — and internal documents from the Musk lawsuit show Altman had been exclusively writing about engineered viruses as the greatest threat just weeks earlier.

Hao invokes a Dune analogy: Paul Atreides knows the Messiah prophecy on Arrakis is a seeded myth, but living and breathing inside it for long enough blurs the line between performing the myth and believing it. She argues Dario Amodei has crossed this line — if asked whether he genuinely believes in 10-25% catastrophic risk, he would say yes, because he's been required to believe it to continue the work.
