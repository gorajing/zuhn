The Pentagon already had access to Grok via classified systems but considered it insufficient. Anthropic's Claude was the only frontier model integrated with classified infrastructure (via their Palantir partnership). Rather than undertake the 'annoying thing' of integrating a competitor's model, Hegseth chose to threaten Anthropic's existence.

This reveals that classified system integration creates an extraordinarily powerful moat — one where the switching costs are so high that a government customer will attempt to force compliance rather than switch vendors. For AI companies, this suggests that early classified integration creates not just revenue but a defensive position that even sovereign power struggles to circumvent through normal means.
