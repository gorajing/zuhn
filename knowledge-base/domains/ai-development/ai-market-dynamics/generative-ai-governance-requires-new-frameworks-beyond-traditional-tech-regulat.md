The World Economic Forum's exploration of generative AI governance highlights a structural mismatch: traditional regulatory frameworks operate on multi-year cycles with clear jurisdictional boundaries, while generative AI capabilities evolve in months and operate globally by default. This creates a governance gap where regulations are outdated before they take effect.

The challenge is compounded by generative AI's dual-use nature — the same model that writes marketing copy can generate misinformation, and the same code assistant that boosts productivity can produce malware. Effective governance likely requires adaptive frameworks that can evolve with the technology, international coordination mechanisms, and industry self-governance standards that move faster than legislation. For startups building in AI, this means regulatory risk is both a threat and an opportunity — companies that proactively build governance into their products may gain competitive advantage as regulation inevitably tightens.
