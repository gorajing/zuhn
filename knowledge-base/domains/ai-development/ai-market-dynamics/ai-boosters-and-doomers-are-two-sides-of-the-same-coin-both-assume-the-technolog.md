Bender and Hanna argue the standard AI discourse frames boosters and doomers as extreme ends of a continuum, forcing everyone to find their position in between. But these aren't opposite ends — they're two sides of the same coin, both predicated on the idea that AI is a real, inevitable, imminent thing. They only diverge on implications. The real dissent isn't about whether powerful AI is good or bad, but whether it's actually powerful at all.

This reframing is strategically important. By accepting the booster-doomer spectrum, skeptics get positioned as 'somewhere in the middle' when their actual position rejects the shared premise entirely. Bender and Hanna's concerns aren't about what AI will do but about what's being done in the name of artificial intelligence — the harms to people and the planet as companies chase a fantasy. This is a fundamentally different axis of concern that gets obscured when forced onto the booster-doomer spectrum.
