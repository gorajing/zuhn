Sequoia frames the new production function as flops per knowledge worker. Today a worker might use one AI assistant; tomorrow they might manage dozens or hundreds of AI agents, each consuming compute. Their portfolio survey shows minimum 10x compute increase per worker, with optimistic projections of 1,000-10,000x.

This has cascading implications: inference companies face massive demand growth, security companies must protect this inference at scale, and companies leveraging this new production function gain enormous leverage -- with correspondingly less certainty. The analogy to the industrial revolution's specialization imperative is apt: just as factories required combining general-purpose and specialized components, the AI revolution requires combining general-purpose models with domain-specialized agents. Sequoia estimates this cognitive revolution can compress what took 200+ years industrially into just a few years.
