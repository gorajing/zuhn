Visual Capitalist's mapping of newly funded AI startups from 2013 to 2023 — filtering for companies that received over $1.5 million in private investment — reveals a highly uneven global landscape. While AI discourse is global and AI tools are accessible worldwide, the formation of venture-backed AI companies remains heavily concentrated in specific geographies.

This concentration pattern matters for multiple reasons: it suggests that factors beyond technical talent drive AI company formation — access to capital networks, regulatory environments, market proximity, and existing tech ecosystem density all play outsized roles. For founders outside these concentrated hubs, this implies either relocating to access capital or developing strategies that leverage local advantages (domain expertise, regulatory arbitrage, underserved markets) rather than competing head-on with Silicon Valley AI startups for generalist VC attention.
