Meta's willingness to open-source its frontier AI models (LLaMA series) is a rational competitive strategy rather than a philosophical commitment to openness. Meta does not have a foundation model monetization business — it makes money from advertising and social platforms. By releasing powerful models for free, Meta raises the floor of available AI capabilities for everyone, which disproportionately hurts OpenAI and Anthropic whose business models depend on model access fees. It also accelerates adoption of Meta-aligned AI infrastructure and tooling, creating ecosystem lock-in at a different layer. This is the same logic as Google open-sourcing Android — commoditize what your competitors sell, monetize what you uniquely own. Founders building on AI infrastructure should recognize that the base model layer is trending toward free, pushing value up-stack to applications and data.
