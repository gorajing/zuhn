Google Cloud's summary says 90% of respondents use AI at work and more than 80% believe it increased their productivity, but 30% still report little or no trust in AI-generated code and DORA continues to find a negative relationship with delivery stability. That combination is a warning against treating adoption and self-reported speed as sufficient proof of real software-output gains.

This connects to prior METR-style pressure on AI coding optimism without duplicating it. The contradiction target here is the business-scaling claim: if review burden, distrust, and instability remain material, AI-assisted teams may not realize the full headcount collapse implied by revenue-per-employee anecdotes. Confidence should move from 'AI broadly reduces required team size' toward 'AI reduces team size only where governance, review, platform, and feedback systems absorb the extra change volume.'
