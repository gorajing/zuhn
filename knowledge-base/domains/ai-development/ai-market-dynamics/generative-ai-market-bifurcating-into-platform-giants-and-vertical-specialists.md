The 2025 generative AI market is crystallizing into two tiers: horizontal platform companies (OpenAI, Anthropic) competing for general-purpose dominance, and specialized startups capturing defensible niches in verticals like legal and medical AI.

This bifurcation pattern is significant because it suggests the 'middle ground' of general-purpose AI startups without platform-scale resources is becoming unviable. Startups that succeed will either need to go deep into a specific domain with proprietary data and workflows, or compete at the infrastructure layer. Elad Gil's analysis points to vertical specialization as the more realistic path for most founders.
