Ryan McIntosh at a16z identifies the 'electro-industrial stack' as the foundation of 21st century economic and military power. This shared technology layer powers electric vehicles, drones, data centers, and modern manufacturing. The US can match China's engineering capability for any individual technology, but China's advantage lies in its multi-tier supplier ecosystem that enables industrial scale at low cost.

Companies like SpaceX vertically integrate by necessity, not strategy — there simply isn't a domestic supplier ecosystem to support them. Building this ecosystem requires blending Silicon Valley software talent with industrial domain veterans, co-locating engineering with manufacturing, and building mission prestige to attract top talent. As AI and automation increase their presence in industrial and military applications, controlling these supply chains becomes increasingly critical. The companies and countries that build this infrastructure today will compound their advantage over decades.
