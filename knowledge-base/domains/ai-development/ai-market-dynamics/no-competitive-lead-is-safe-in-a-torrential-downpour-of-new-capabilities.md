Classical competitive analysis treats market position as a slow-changing variable: incumbents have moats, challengers chip away over years. The 2026 AI environment compresses this dynamic dramatically. Foundation model releases (every few months) create capability inflections that can reverse a competitive position within a quarter. A product that was state-of-the-art in December may be commoditized by April because the underlying model improved or a competitor wrapped a better harness around the same capability. The dual implication: incumbents cannot rely on prior position for durable advantage (the rain is washing away the lead), but challengers should not expect incumbents to be permanently exposed (the rain stops eventually and prior moats partially reassert). The right disposition for both incumbents and challengers in 2026: treat every quarter as a fresh competitive evaluation, ship aggressively in the rain, and lock in customer relationships during the disruption window because they will be harder to win once the rain slows. Strategic patience is the wrong frame; strategic urgency without strategic confusion is the right frame.
