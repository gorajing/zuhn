Venture investment in AI reached $150 billion in 2024 alone, with cumulative global investment exceeding $1 trillion over 15 years. Yet the only successful consumer product category remains AI chatbots. The gap between prototyping and production is the core bottleneck — while building an AI demo is easier than ever, achieving the 99.9%+ reliability enterprises demand in regulated sectors like healthcare and banking remains unsolved.

This creates a paradox for AI investors: returns ultimately depend on customers paying for AI applications, but the reliability gap prevents enterprises from adopting AI products at scale. The implication is that the next wave of AI value creation won't come from better foundation models but from solving the reliability and integration challenges that bridge the gap between impressive demos and production-grade systems that enterprises will actually pay for.
