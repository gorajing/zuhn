A key revelation from the Anthropic-Pentagon episode: the government's leverage over AI labs is vastly greater than direct contract leverage. Even if an AI lab refuses to do business with the government at all, the government controls: (1) permitting for power generation, which every data center needs; (2) antitrust enforcement; (3) contracts with every major cloud, chip, and infrastructure partner the lab depends on.

An explicit or even unspoken condition attached to Amazon's, Nvidia's, or Google's federal contracts — 'stop working with the company drawing red lines' — would be catastrophic to a frontier lab without requiring any direct regulatory action. The supply-chain risk designation is just the visible tip; the coercive surface area is much larger.

This matters for how AI labs should think about independence. Corporate courage (refusing to sell to the military on moral grounds) assumes a direct bilateral relationship. The actual relationship is mediated through every infrastructure dependency the lab has, all of which are themselves government-contract-dependent. True independence is structurally almost impossible for frontier labs at current compute intensities — which is itself an argument against concentrating safety decisions at the lab level.
