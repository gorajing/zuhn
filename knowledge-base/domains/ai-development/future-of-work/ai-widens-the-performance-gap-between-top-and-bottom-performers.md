A randomized control experiment with BCG junior consultants found that when given access to LLMs, top performers improved significantly while lower performers actually declined. Gonzalez calls this the 'selective upgrade puzzle.' The implication is that naive AI deployment — giving everyone the same tools — does not equalize performance but widens the gap. This is because domain expertise is a prerequisite for effectively evaluating and directing AI output. Using AI with zero domain knowledge is 'a very dangerous proposition.' Organizations deploying AI need guardrails and minimum expertise thresholds, not just universal tool access.
