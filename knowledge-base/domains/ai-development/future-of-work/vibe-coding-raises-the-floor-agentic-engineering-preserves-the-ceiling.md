Karpathy's clean separation: vibe coding raises the floor — anyone can vibe code anything, and that's incredible. Agentic engineering is about preserving the quality bar of professional software while going faster — you're still responsible for security, reliability, and correctness. The agents are spiky, fallible, stochastic, but extremely powerful, and the engineering discipline is about coordinating them without sacrificing quality.

The practical implication for the 10x-engineer trope: agentic engineering magnifies the gap. Karpathy says people who are very good at this 'peak a lot more than 10x' — the spread between mediocre and excellent operators is widening, not narrowing. For builders, this maps onto two distinct product strategies: tools that lower the floor (Replit, Lovable, V0) serve the long tail; tools that lift the ceiling (Claude Code, Codex, agent-native IDEs) serve professional teams. They are not the same market, and a single product trying to be both will likely fail at both.
