Alexander pushes back on the framing that 'nothing happens' is the safe default. The actual trend line has been steady algorithmic progress doubling annually, compute scaling, and capability benchmarks falling faster than expert surveys predicted. Metaculus AGI timelines have compressed from 2050 (2020) to near 2030 (2025). To predict stagnation, you have to name a specific mechanism that breaks the trend — and any such mechanism is itself a positive claim with high out-of-model error.

This is the same error as assuming the stock market will crash, or that a fast-growing startup will slow down — people frame these as cautious positions when they're actually strong contrarian claims. 'Conservative' in the sense of 'assume trends continue' is different from 'conservative' in the sense of 'assume nothing dramatic happens.' The two meanings get conflated and the second gets free epistemic credit it hasn't earned.

Application: when someone invokes 'but things usually don't change that much' as their argument, ask what specific mechanism they expect to halt the current trajectory. Absence of a specific reason is not evidence for stasis.
