Bender identifies a critical blind spot in the 'AI replaces junior workers' narrative. The point of internships and junior positions isn't just cheap labor — it's the training ground where people gain experience to become senior professionals. When companies say 'we don't need that layer anymore because senior folks can just review AI output,' they create an unanswered question: where do the next senior folks come from?

This is already manifesting as 'slopification' — once-solid career roles atomized into gig work that can be sent overseas. The damage isn't just to current workers but to the entire pipeline of professional development. A company that replaces all junior developers with AI code generation will find in 5-10 years that it has no senior developers who understand the codebase at a deep level, because no one went through the learning curve. This is a slow-moving crisis that won't be visible until the talent pipeline is already broken.
