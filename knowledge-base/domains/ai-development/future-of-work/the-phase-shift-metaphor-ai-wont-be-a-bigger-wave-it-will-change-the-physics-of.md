Borrowed from Brian Johnson: humans train their careers like swimmers getting progressively better at harder conditions (pool → lake → ocean → cross-border). This model assumes the medium stays the same. A phase shift — water to gas — breaks the assumption, and the accumulated skill becomes irrelevant because the environment obeys different rules. Applied to AI, the implication is that career capital built on labor-substitutable skills may not transfer to the post-AI economy at all, even for the most skilled practitioners. This is an inspirational frame rather than a precise prediction, useful for jolting people out of 'I'll just work harder' thinking into 'I need to redesign what I'm doing from scratch.' The frame is falsifiable if AI turns out to be more gradual and continuous than the phase-shift metaphor suggests — which is plausible.
