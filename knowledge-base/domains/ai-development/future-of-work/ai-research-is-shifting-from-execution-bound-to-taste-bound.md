Chen describes 'vibe researching' as the near-term analogue of vibe coding, and says OpenAI and other labs are moving toward it 'very quickly.' The work is becoming mostly orchestration: the researcher comes up with ideas and the model is 'great enough to do the implementation execution by itself.' This is a market shift — execution, historically the rate-limiter, is being commoditized, and the differentiator becomes the volume and quality of ideas a researcher can generate.

The binding constraint is taste. Models don't yet have the taste to pick which research directions are promising, which is precisely why human researchers are still needed to originate ideas — and Chen notes taste is 'going to be hard to teach.' But the acceleration on the execution side already shows 'clear tangible benefits,' so the asymmetry is live now even though full parity is on a multi-year roadmap whose end state is end-to-end model-driven research.

The practical implication for anyone doing research-like work: invest in idea-generation throughput and judgment, and treat implementation as increasingly delegable to models. It's marked time-sensitive because it describes an in-flight transition with an explicit horizon (models reaching taste parity), not a stable equilibrium.
