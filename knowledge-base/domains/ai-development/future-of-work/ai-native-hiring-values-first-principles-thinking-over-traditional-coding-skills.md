Linda from Minimax shared a sharp framework for AI-era talent: the people thriving are first-principles thinkers who are versatile and use AI to fortify themselves. Those being displaced are order-takers who execute predefined solutions. In Minimax job interviews, candidates can vibe-code their way through technical assessments — the method matters less than whether they solve the problem.

This extends beyond individual hiring to organizational design. Linda described the 'AI-native organization' where even receptionists use AI for room booking — AI adoption must be ground-up, not just an engineering initiative. Minimax operates with only 400 people across coding, video, audio, music, and image models by organizing diverse first-principles thinkers who collaborate across disciplines. Ron from Open Router echoed this: non-engineers at their company (sales, support) are building agents to multiply themselves, doing in hours what used to take days.
