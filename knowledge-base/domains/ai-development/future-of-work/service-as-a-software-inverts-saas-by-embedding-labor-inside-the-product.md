Traditional SaaS digitized workflows but always required humans to operate the software — the labor budget ($5T US) dwarfed the software budget ($230B) because software couldn't self-execute. AI breaks this by letting software both organize and execute tasks. The implication is a fusion of the two markets: companies now buy outcomes, not tools. Founders building in this space should frame their product as a headcount replacement, not a productivity multiplier, because the unit economics and pricing leverage are fundamentally different. Service margins (sub-30%) converge toward software margins (80%+) when AI delivers the service.
