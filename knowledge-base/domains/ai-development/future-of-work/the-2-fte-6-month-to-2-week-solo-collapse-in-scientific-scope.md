Sonia's concrete data point from her work as a spatial-tech biotech advisor: in mid-2025 she estimated that pulling in all spatial transcriptomics data across the company's top three indications, reprocessing it consistently, and overlaying the founder's proprietary models would take 2 FTEs × 6 months. When she re-estimated the same exercise in November during due diligence, her estimate had collapsed to 'I could do this myself in 2 weeks.' That's a 12-24x productivity multiplier in roughly 5 months, driven mostly by agentic coding maturity and tooling improvement. The implication for capital allocation is dramatic: if 'a project' that justified hiring two engineers in Q2 can now be done by one operator in two weeks, the gating constraint shifts from headcount to taste / direction / verification ability. Investment theses should explicitly account for this when modeling burn rate — assume the AI-amplified solo operator can absorb scopes that previously needed teams. The flip side: if your team is the same size as last year and the work hasn't compressed similarly, you should be asking why your team isn't capturing the leverage. Sonia's personal investment thesis: 'if you have something where AI can really accelerate a meaningful step 100x, that's part of your whole investment thesis.'
