Using OpenAI occupational exposure metrics, Yale Budget Lab finds the employment share of low-, medium-, and high-exposure occupations remains stable since ChatGPT's launch, and unemployed workers do not show a clear upward trend in exposure. This weakens the simple mechanism behind AI job-loss predictions: if exposure alone were enough to drive displacement, high-exposure occupations should already be shrinking or overrepresented in unemployment. The evidence argues for treating exposure as a risk map, not an employment forecast.
