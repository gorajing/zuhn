The traditional mature-product engineering team is a pyramid: a leader, a few senior engineers, and a large base of junior people writing code and fixing bugs. Reynold Xin argues that well-harnessed AI agents now do most of that base-of-the-pyramid coding work — and in some cases the design work — which removes the structural reason to staff a large junior tier.

The replacement shape is top-heavy (an 'eye' or inverted pyramid): the scarce, valuable roles are the people who can specify what needs to be built and understand how to build it, while the grunt work is fully automated. The implication for hiring and career paths is significant — the apprenticeship rungs that produced senior engineers may erode, and orgs need a deliberate answer for how judgment and architectural understanding get developed when the junior coding tier is automated away.
