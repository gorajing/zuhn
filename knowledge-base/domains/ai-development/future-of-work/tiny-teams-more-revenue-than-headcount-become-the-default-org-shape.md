swyx defines a tiny team as one with more millions in revenue than number of full-time employees, and points to AI Engineer itself as proof: nine full-time people running a business doing more than $9 million, soon scaling event operations from a 1,000-person London conference to 6,000 people in San Francisco without growing headcount.

The key reframe is that the tiny-team idea is too often hijacked by the fantasy of the one-person billionaire. swyx argues every company — small or large — can operate as a tiny team by pushing leverage through agents. The constraint that lifts is not the number of people but the amount of routine knowledge work a small team can absorb once workflows are 'ironed out' and people are onboarded onto agents.

The strategic implication: revenue-per-employee, not absolute headcount, becomes the metric of organizational health, and hiring plans should be re-examined against what agents can now absorb before adding people.
