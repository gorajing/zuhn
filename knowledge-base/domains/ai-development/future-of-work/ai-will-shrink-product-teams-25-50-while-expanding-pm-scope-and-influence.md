Lenny endorses Marty Cagan's prediction that AI will shrink product teams by 25-50%, with the reduction coming primarily from the engineering side. PMs will have more influence and broader scope, but their role shifts toward discovery (figuring out what to build) and GTM (driving adoption) rather than the design-and-build phase that AI increasingly handles.

This reframes the 'will AI replace PMs?' debate: PMs become more important, not less, but the job description changes substantially. The skills that matter—prioritization, buy-in, taste, adoption—are exactly the ones AI struggles with. This aligns with the broader trend of AI handling execution while humans retain judgment and strategy roles. The time-sensitive nature reflects that this transition is actively unfolding and the exact ratios will become clearer.
