The speaker's 25-year career arc demonstrates how optimizing for fewer than four dimensions creates predictable failure modes. Profit without passion (Accenture consulting) led to misery. Passion without prowess (early labs) felt hollow. Prowess without purpose (innovation director) triggered the Peter Principle. The breakthrough came only when all four aligned simultaneously.

Critically, 'profit' doesn't mean maximizing income -- it means enough money to keep doing you at scale. This reframe is important because many people sacrifice the other three dimensions chasing profit, while the most engaged people (who never want to retire) have enough profit to sustain the other three. The actionable framework: make four independent lists, then use combinatorial analysis (or AI) to find the intersections.
