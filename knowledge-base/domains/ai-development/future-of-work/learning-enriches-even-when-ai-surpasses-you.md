Dario Amodei makes a philosophical argument that goes beyond pure
economic utility: even if AI permanently surpasses your ability in a
domain, the act of learning that skill still has intrinsic value. You
enrich yourself intellectually by understanding how things work. This
pushes back against the purely utilitarian view that if AI does it
better, humans shouldn't bother. Learning builds mental models,
develops taste, and enables the kind of critical evaluation that makes
human-AI collaboration effective. It's also a defense against the
deskilling problem — maintaining active engagement with skills prevents
cognitive atrophy even when AI handles the heavy lifting.
