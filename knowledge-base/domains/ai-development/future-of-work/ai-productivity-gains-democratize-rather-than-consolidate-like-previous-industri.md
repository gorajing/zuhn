The auto industry consolidated sharply after Ford's assembly line created efficiency advantages that crushed smaller competitors—hundreds of manufacturers collapsed into a handful. AI coding assistants deliver comparable 55-81% productivity gains, but the structural effect is inverted: rather than concentrating power, these tools democratize building capacity so any developer can produce at scale.

This distinction matters for market structure predictions. If AI productivity tools follow the assembly line pattern, we'd expect consolidation (the 83% figure some predict). But because the tools are universally accessible rather than proprietary infrastructure advantages, the more likely outcome is an explosion of new software businesses. The barrier to building has dropped, not the barrier to competing. This suggests the software market will fragment further, creating more niche players rather than fewer dominant ones.
