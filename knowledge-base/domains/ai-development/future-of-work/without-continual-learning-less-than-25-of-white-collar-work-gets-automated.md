The concrete example carries the argument: Claude 4 Opus can technically rewrite autogenerated transcripts, but Dwarkesh still pays a human editor because the human accumulates a mental model of his voice, his audience, and what he cares about. The LLM resets every session. Even within a session the LLM does get smarter — by paragraph 4 of a co-written essay it's finally giving good suggestions — but that entire subtle understanding dissolves when the session ends.

This generalizes: any white-collar role whose value comes from accumulated context about a specific person, team, or codebase is effectively un-automatable until continual learning is solved. Generic task completion isn't enough. This is a counterweight to the common view that 'even spiky models will eat white-collar work because the tasks are collectable.' Dwarkesh says: collect all the data you want, without the learning loop you're not replacing employees.
