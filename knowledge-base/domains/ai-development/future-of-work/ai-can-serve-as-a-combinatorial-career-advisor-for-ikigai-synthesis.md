The traditional Ikigai exercise asks vague questions like 'what do you love?' The speaker refines this with specific prompts: 'Where do you lose track of time?' 'What have you done for free and would keep doing?' 'What are you complimented on?' These concrete inputs feed an AI tool that uses combinatorial reasoning to find intersections across all four dimensions.

The sailing metaphor is key: you can identify your destination (true north) but can't sail directly into the wind. The AI generates not just destinations but waypoints -- a series of 45-degree tacks that represent realistic career steps. This is a concrete example of AI augmenting human self-knowledge rather than replacing human judgment, using the machine's ability to process combinations that exceed human working memory.
