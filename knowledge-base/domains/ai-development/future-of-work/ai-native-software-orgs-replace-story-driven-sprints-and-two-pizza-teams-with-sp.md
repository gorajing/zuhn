In McKinsey's survey, the enterprises pulling away shared two structural shifts. On workflow: they moved from quarterly to continuous planning and from story-driven to spec-driven development — PMs iterate on specs (and even direct code prototypes) with agents rather than authoring long PRDs and stories whose fuzzy prose acceptance criteria produce downstream rework. They scaled AI across four or more use cases in the lifecycle rather than buying point solutions for just code review or just code generation (7x more likely). On talent: they shrank the 'two-pizza' team into 'one-pizza' pods of three to five people, consolidating separate QA, front-end, and back-end roles into product builders who orchestrate agents with full-stack fluency and a strong grasp of the whole architecture (6x more likely to have AI-native roles). With the same headcount you create more pods, each expected to perform at the prior team's level, yielding reported 5-6x increases in delivery speed alongside higher, more consistent quality. Marked time-sensitive because the specific pod sizes and ceremony details will evolve as agents grow more capable, even if the direction holds.
