A study by Aldasoro et al. across the EU found that AI adoption causally increases labor productivity by 4% while showing no evidence of employment reduction. The mechanism appears to be capital deepening — AI augments worker output by enabling faster task completion and better decisions without displacing labor. This mirrors historical technology waves where complementarity dominated substitution.

Interestingly, a survey reveals a perception gap: corporate executives predict AI will cut employment by 0.7%, while employees themselves predict a 0.5% increase. This divergence suggests executives may be over-indexing on the substitution narrative. The assumption that AI is primarily a human-remover is widespread but not yet supported by firm-level data — the economy reallocates resources in surprising ways that simple automation narratives fail to capture.
