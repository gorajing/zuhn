Figure closed a $1B Series C at a $39B post-money valuation, a 15x jump from its $2.6B Series B just 18 months prior. The company's master plan frames manual labor compensation as ~50% of global GDP (~$42 trillion/yr) and positions humanoid robots as the mechanism for humans to opt out of physical work while sharing in the resulting abundance.

This framing is strategically powerful for fundraising but elides enormous practical barriers: regulatory approval, unit economics at scale, reliability in unstructured environments, and the social disruption of displacing manual workers faster than new roles emerge. The capital earmarked for BotQ manufacturing, GPU infrastructure for the Helix AI platform, and human-video data pipelines suggests Figure is betting on a vertically integrated approach rather than licensing the AI stack. The valuation implies investors are pricing in a winner-take-most outcome in general-purpose robotics.
