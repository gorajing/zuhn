Yale Budget Lab uses Anthropic task-usage data as a present-focused proxy for where AI is actually being used, and finds employment in high-automation or high-augmentation occupations remains stable under their aggregation methods. This is strong counterevidence to claims that visible AI usage has already become broad labor displacement, but weaker for coding-specific claims because Claude usage is heavily skewed toward computer and mathematical occupations and the analysis is still a static snapshot.
