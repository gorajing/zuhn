The 'learn to code' decade (2010s-early 2020s) was framed as democratization but encoded a hidden assumption: that the ability to write programs was the bottleneck preventing creators from building products. The Replit data shows this assumption was wrong for most builders. Solo entrepreneurs are now building multi-million-dollar businesses without learning to code; what they need is the ability to articulate what they want built and to evaluate whether the AI built the right thing. The skill stack that matters for an AI-augmented creator: product clarity, taste, articulation precision, evaluation discipline, market sense — not syntax fluency. The implication for educators, parents, and career-curious adults: the most valuable course in 2026 is not Python; it's whatever teaches you to make a coherent product with rigorous taste. Schools and bootcamps still pushing 'learn to code first' are anchoring on a 2015 mental model. Some level of programming literacy still matters for AI engineers, infrastructure engineers, and embedded systems work; but for the vast majority of would-be creators, going deep on the create skill stack beats going deep on syntax.
