Alfred Lin at Sequoia observes that the best builders have shifted from writing every line of code to directing fleets of AI coding agents working simultaneously. This is not just a productivity hack — it's a fundamental role transformation analogous to the shift from individual contributor to strategic commander.

The implications cascade through hiring, skill development, and startup strategy. When taste, direction-setting, and product vision become the bottleneck rather than typing speed, the profile of a 10x developer changes dramatically. Founders who can clearly articulate what to build and why — then orchestrate AI agents to execute — gain disproportionate leverage over those who are fast coders but lack strategic clarity.
