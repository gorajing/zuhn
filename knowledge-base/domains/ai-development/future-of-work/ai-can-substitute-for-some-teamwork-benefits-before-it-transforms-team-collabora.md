The experiment suggests AI's first-order workplace effect may be individual capability expansion rather than team-level transformation. Teams without AI beat solo controls by 0.24 standard deviations, validating the ordinary collaboration baseline. But individuals with AI gained 0.37 standard deviations, while Team+AI gained 0.39, not significantly different from Individual+AI on average. The organizational implication is not simply 'add AI to every team'; it is to ask which teamwork benefits are being substituted, which are still uniquely human, and where smaller team structures can preserve quality while reducing coordination cost.
