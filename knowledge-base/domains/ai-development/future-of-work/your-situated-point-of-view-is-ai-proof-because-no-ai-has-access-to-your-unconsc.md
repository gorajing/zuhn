The AI-replacement anxiety for knowledge workers centers on 'can AI do my job?' The more useful framing: can AI reproduce the specific outputs that distinguish you from any other practitioner of your job? For most people doing most work, the answer is increasingly yes — generic practitioners are replaceable. What remains irreducible is the situated point of view: the opinions, taste, and intuitions produced by a specific human's lived experience. An AI can simulate a persona but cannot originate it, because origination requires access to the unconscious, the memories, and the ongoing stream of novel experience. The practical implication for career longevity: invest in your own situated point of view through novel experience, deep engagement with specific domains, and the accumulation of a written track record that expresses that point of view over time. Creators who stay indoors on Claude 24/7 extract their situated-point-of-view asset without replenishing it. The counter-practice is to spend significant time off-desk having experiences that generate original material, and to treat the written expression of that material as the career moat AI cannot erode.
