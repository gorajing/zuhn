Yale Budget Lab's tracker directly challenges broad near-term AI-displacement predictions such as PRED-260407-85BF by finding no discernible disruption in the broader labor market 33 months after ChatGPT. Occupational mix is changing somewhat faster than earlier comparison periods, but not markedly, and key shifts predate generative AI adoption. This should lower confidence in claims that AI is already eroding economy-wide cognitive-labor demand, while leaving longer-deadline displacement predictions active because widespread technological disruption often takes years or decades to appear in employment aggregates.
