The standard AI productivity discourse stops at the individual level: an engineer with Claude is X times faster, a PM with AI Studio prototypes Y times faster, etc. The collaboration-fracture framing surfaces what happens at the next level up — teams.

When each individual is 10x faster, the team's bottleneck moves to coordination: alignment meetings, decision arbitration, dependency management between agent outputs from different roles. None of these scale with model capability. So the team's actual ship rate stays roughly flat while individual idle/standby time goes up, manifesting as the unsettling 'I have 6 hours of slack today but we're still launching the same thing next month.'

This predicts where org-design innovation will happen next: teams will need explicit coordination tooling (better async decision-making, agent-mediated arbitration, structured PRD/spec exchange) before they can absorb the individual productivity gains. Companies that solve this layer ship faster than competitors with the same AI tools.

For Zuhn this maps onto the same compression-vs-collection distinction visible at the knowledge layer — capture/individual-productivity is the well-funded part of the market; coordination/compression layers are structurally undervalued.
