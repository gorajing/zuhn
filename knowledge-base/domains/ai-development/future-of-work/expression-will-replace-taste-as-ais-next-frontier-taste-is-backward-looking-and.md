The essay proposes a three-stage depreciation ladder: craft → taste → expression. AI first commoditized execution skill (coding, writing, design). The current consensus is that 'taste' — the ability to evaluate and curate quality — is the new differentiator. But the essay argues taste is fundamentally backward-looking: it evaluates what has worked before and copies it, making it a learnable signal that AI companies are already working to codify.

Expression, by contrast, is forward-looking — it makes a statement about what could be. It requires a genuine point of view and an internal world to externalize. Picasso's journey from academic realism to the Blue Period (taste) to Les Demoiselles d'Avignon (expression) illustrates this progression. The painting that critics hated became one of the most important works in history precisely because it was expression, not taste.

For builders, this means optimizing for 'good taste' is a temporary moat at best. The real competitive advantage lies in having an original perspective and the courage to manifest it.
