The 'Python for Everybody' title and pedagogical approach — teaching programming to non-CS students as a general-purpose skill — reflects a broader trend where programming shifts from specialist craft to universal literacy. The course explicitly targets social scientists, biologists, and other non-technical professionals who need data processing capabilities.

This parallels the historical adoption of written literacy: initially a specialist skill for scribes, it became universal but stratified. Most people read emails and news (functional literacy) while few write novels or academic papers (deep literacy). Similarly, most professionals will write scripts to process data and automate tasks (functional programming literacy) while fewer will architect distributed systems. This has implications for how we think about AI replacing coding — AI may replace the deep literacy tier while the functional tier persists as a thinking tool.
