The AI Index 2026 Chapter 9 reports that US adults and AI experts show strong consensus on a few high-risk categories but sharp disagreement on others. Both groups rank cashiers at 73% AI-risk and factory workers in the 60% range. For truck drivers, AI experts project 62% risk versus the public's 33% — experts think autonomous driving will displace truckers at double the rate the public expects. For lawyers, experts say 38% versus public 23%. Conversely, the public projects higher risk for teachers (31% public vs 43% experts — closer here) and for medical doctors (18% vs 28%). The structural pattern is that the public underestimates risk in categories where AI capabilities are already strong but deployment is bottlenecked on regulation or infrastructure (autonomous trucking), and overestimates risk in categories where AI is visibly capable but deployment is bottlenecked on liability, licensing, or trust (teaching, medicine). The implication for individual career decisions is that the risk premium on 'AI-safe' professions is mispriced — regulation-protected jobs may be safer than they look, while physical/trust-adjacent jobs may be more at risk.
