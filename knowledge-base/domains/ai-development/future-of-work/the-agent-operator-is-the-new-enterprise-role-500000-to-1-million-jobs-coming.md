The emerging role combines technical AI fluency (MCPs, CLIs, skills, agent configuration files) with business-process literacy for a specific function. Unlike pure engineers (who can build the agent but do not know the marketing or legal workflow) or pure domain experts (who know the workflow but cannot configure the agent), agent operators sit at the intersection. Their job involves: (1) redesigning existing business processes for agent-first execution rather than retrofitting agents to human workflows, (2) configuring agent permissions, data connections, and callback hooks specific to the function, (3) maintaining agent configurations when model updates break prompt templates, (4) producing agents.md and skills documentation that captures domain-specific context. Enterprise employees from IT, operations, or engineering will transition into this role; a significant fraction will be hired externally. The career implication: any knowledge worker worried about AI displacement should evaluate whether they can transition into being the agent operator for their own function — that is the defensive move available inside existing employers. The staffing implication: enterprises without agent operators cannot actually deploy agents successfully, which is why so many AI pilots fail to produce sustained value.
