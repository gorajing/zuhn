Felicis and RunwayML frame the core strategic question as: 'Every abundance creates a new scarcity. The task for builders is to figure out which resources AI makes abundant, which are rendered scarce, and where an edge can be formed.' The article identifies specific scarcities: focused attention, original art and writing, trusted news and facts, data protection skills, customer empathy, green computing, AI ethics governance, and complex problem solving.

This inverts the typical AI strategy of 'automate everything possible.' Instead, the winning approach is to identify what becomes more valuable as AI commoditizes adjacent capabilities. A company that combines AI-driven scale with genuinely scarce human capabilities (deep empathy, original creativity, trustworthy curation) creates a defensible position. This framework applies equally to individual career strategy—skills that AI makes abundant (basic content creation, data analysis) decrease in value, while skills that remain scarce (judgment under uncertainty, relationship building, ethical reasoning) increase.
