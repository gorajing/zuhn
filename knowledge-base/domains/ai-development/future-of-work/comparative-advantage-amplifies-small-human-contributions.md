Dario Amodei argues that comparative advantage is "surprisingly powerful"
in human-AI collaboration. Even when AI handles 95% of a task, the human
contribution of the remaining 5% gets dramatically amplified. The human
provides the judgment, direction, taste, and contextual understanding
that the AI cannot fully replicate — and this small slice of human input
multiplies total output by roughly 20x. There is "surprisingly much" in
that zone of comparative advantage. This means the right response to AI
capability isn't despair about being outperformed, but strategic focus
on the high-leverage parts only humans can do well.
