The AI Index 2026 Chapter 7 directly contradicts the prevailing 'brain drain' narrative about AI academia. New AI PhDs in the United States and Canada increased 22% from 2022 to 2024, reaching 442 in 2024. But the absolute number going to industry has stayed flat while academic hires doubled. The industry share of new AI PhD graduates declined from a peak of 77% in 2022 to 65% in 2024, while academia's share nearly doubled. Government jobs rose from 0.7% in 2021 to 2% in 2024. The structural interpretation is that industry already has the AI talent it needs — the frontier labs scaled up hiring aggressively 2020-2022 and have now saturated — while universities are absorbing a growing supply of freshly-minted PhDs. This is the reverse of the story told in the public discourse, and it carries a sharp implication: if industry AI hiring has plateaued, then compensation-driven talent wars may be ending, and academic AI labs (for the first time in a decade) are actually gaining bench strength. Capital allocation and founder hiring strategies that assume scarce AI talent need to be re-examined.
