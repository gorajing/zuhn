The US economy is $31 trillion, with labor costs accounting for roughly 60% of that value. Chamath argues that the heated disagreement about AI's impact on work exists because different commentators are examining different layers of the labor market and talking past each other.

This is a powerful framing insight: rather than asking 'will AI replace jobs?' as a binary question, decomposing the labor market into layers (routine cognitive, creative, physical, management, etc.) reveals that AI impacts each layer differently. Some layers face near-term displacement, others augmentation, and others are largely unaffected. A first-principles framework that acknowledges these layers produces much more useful predictions than blanket optimism or pessimism about AI and employment.
