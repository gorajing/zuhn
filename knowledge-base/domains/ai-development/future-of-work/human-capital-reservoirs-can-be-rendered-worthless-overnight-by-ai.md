Smith highlights a particularly cruel economic dynamic: human capital — the skills and expertise workers build over entire careers — can become worthless overnight. Unlike physical capital that depreciates gradually, human capital in a domain hit by AI automation can go from extremely valuable to nearly worthless in the time it takes for a new AI tool to ship.

This creates a novel form of economic risk that traditional career planning doesn't account for. The conventional wisdom that investing in hard technical skills provides career security is being inverted: the harder and more technical the skill, the more precisely AI can replicate it, and the more suddenly it can be automated. The paradox is that the skills that took the longest to build may be the most vulnerable, not the most protected.
