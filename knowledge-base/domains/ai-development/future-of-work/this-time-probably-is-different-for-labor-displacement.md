Hassabis acknowledges the historical pattern (new tech disrupts old jobs, new higher-quality jobs appear) and says this 'definitely is going to happen' again. But he cautions against the simple 'this time isn't different' line that Andreessen takes. His argument: AGI is bigger than the internet or mobile in magnitude, and faster in timeline. The Industrial Revolution itself caused 'huge upheaval' before its benefits diffused — and we should try to 'mitigate some of the downsides a bit better' this time. His mitigation ideas: pension funds buying into AI companies so everyone holds equity, sovereign wealth funds, and redistribution infrastructure. This is a substantive disagreement with Silicon Valley techno-optimism: Hassabis agrees outcomes may be good but rejects the 'no preparation needed' implication.
