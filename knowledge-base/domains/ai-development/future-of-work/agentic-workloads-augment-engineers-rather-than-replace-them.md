The case study is concrete: ten engineers each run their own agent in Kubernetes that periodically checks in on model evaluations, and one engineer estimated the setup did the work of six. The speaker's framing is deliberately not 'we're all losing our jobs' — the displaced work is the tedious, repetitive eval-running, and what it enables is the high-value, outside-the-box, creative work the team would otherwise never reach.

The supporting anecdote is that the speaker stopped hand-writing code months ago because the model became reliably better at it, and openly told skeptical senior engineers that not using AI for everything means missing out. The directional claim — that agents reallocate human effort up the value chain rather than zeroing it out — is plausible but contested, and the per-team multiplier figures are anecdotal, so treat them as illustrative rather than benchmarked.
