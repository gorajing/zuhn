DeepSeek built two models: R1-Zero, trained with pure reinforcement learning and no human supervision, and R1, a refined version with additional human data. The remarkable finding is that R1-Zero independently learned to reason, verify its own work, and discover new problem-solving strategies — without any human-provided examples of reasoning.

This validates a powerful hypothesis about AI development: that reinforcement learning alone can produce emergent cognitive capabilities. When an AI system is given a clear objective and allowed to explore solution spaces freely, it can discover reasoning patterns that human trainers might never think to demonstrate. The addition of human data in R1 improved usability but the core reasoning emerged independently. This has profound implications for the path to more capable AI — it suggests that the next frontier isn't bigger datasets of human reasoning but better reward signals that let models discover their own reasoning strategies.
