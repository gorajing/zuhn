Gwern's framing, relayed by Dwarkesh and sharpened by Handmer: Dario's 'data center of geniuses' shows up in GDP only as its inputs (chips, energy) and outputs (tokens) — none of which are astronomical. Yet the value it produces could replace a meaningful chunk of the $60T global wage pool for human labor. The result is nominal GDP shrinking while the civilizational surplus explodes, much like how the internet makes enormous consumer surplus invisible to GDP by distributing zero-priced goods.

Energy already shows this pattern: oil is ~1% of GDP but a double-digit oil shock cuts GDP by double digits. Per-joule, gasoline is 100x cheaper than food, yet removing it collapses the economy. Fraction-of-GDP is a poor proxy for importance when elasticity and substitutability diverge. Handmer argues civilizational scale should be measured in raw energy use — which points toward a Kardashev-style framing where the goal is 'convert sunlight to cognition' and the limit is planetary insolation.

This has concrete implications: (1) macro indicators will mislead during the AGI transition, (2) traditional productivity measures will look bad when AI creates value faster than it shows up in priced transactions, (3) the long-run AI thesis is 'paving the earth in solar panels to power quadrillions of AI souls' — land-per-unit-cognition becomes the binding economic variable.
