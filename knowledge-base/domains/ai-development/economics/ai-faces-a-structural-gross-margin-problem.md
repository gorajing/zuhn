Traditional software has gross margins of 70-90% because the marginal cost of serving an additional user is near zero — you write the code once and distribute it infinitely. AI products break this model: every user query incurs real compute cost (GPU time, API calls), which scales with usage rather than amortizing away.

This creates a ceiling on margin expansion that SaaS never faced. Companies building on top of foundation model APIs face a particularly acute version: they're reselling compute at thin spreads. Even companies training their own models face enormous ongoing inference costs. The question for AI investors is whether cost curves fall fast enough (via hardware improvements, distillation, quantization) to eventually reach software-like margins, or whether AI is structurally a lower-margin business category.
