The AI Index 2026 reports that the estimated value of generative AI tools to US consumers reached $172 billion annually by early 2026, with the median value per user tripling between 2025 and 2026. Generative AI also reached 53% US population-level adoption within three years, faster than the PC or the internet, though adoption correlates strongly with GDP per capita (Singapore 61%, UAE 54%, US ranks 24th at 28.3%). The strategic implication is that revenue-based measures of AI economic impact — the ones cited in investor calls and GDP statistics — radically undercount real welfare value because most of the consumer benefit flows through free tiers, not paid subscriptions. Any business model or policy analysis built on revenue data is missing the dominant economic effect. Conversely, if free-tier value dwarfs paid value, then the monetization question is not 'can we extract more?' but 'can we extract any without collapsing the surplus that drives adoption?'
