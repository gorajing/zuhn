The traditional brand-creative pipeline routed through agencies: brief the agency, wait weeks, get a small set of polished assets, run them. GenAI inverts the economics — a tiny in-house team (the speaker says '1 or 2 percent' of the prior headcount) generates a far larger volume of creative variations directly, cheaper and faster, and feeds them straight into performance channels.

The structural consequence is disintermediation of the creative-agency layer for performance marketing specifically. Agencies added value through production capacity and craft; when production capacity collapses to near-zero marginal cost and craft becomes 'good enough' for performance ads, the agency's value proposition erodes for that use case. (The companion insight — premium brands resisting GenAI — marks where this does NOT hold.)

The second-order effect is volume-driven optimization: because creatives are now cheap to generate, the bottleneck moves from production to selection — which variation actually performs. That makes performance measurement and creative-testing infrastructure the new scarce capability, not production. This mirrors the broader 'capture is commodity, the selection/compression layer is the moat' pattern.

For marketing operators: reallocate budget from production to testing/measurement, and bring high-volume performance-creative in-house; reserve agencies for brand work where craft still differentiates.
