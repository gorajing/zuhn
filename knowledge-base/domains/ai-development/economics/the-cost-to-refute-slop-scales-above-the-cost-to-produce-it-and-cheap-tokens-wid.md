swyx maps the slop problem onto Brandolini's law — the energy to refute nonsense is an order of magnitude greater than the energy to produce it — and coins 'Fix's law of anti-slop': the amount of taste needed to fight slop is an order of magnitude bigger than that needed to produce it. This is an asymmetric war, and it is getting harder to win because the cost of generating tokens drops by 100x to 1000x every year while the supply of taste does not.

The strategic implication is that defenders cannot win on volume — they will always be outpaced by ever-cheaper generation. The only durable counter is to raise and concentrate taste: elevate high-quality work, curate ruthlessly, and reserve scarce human attention for judgment rather than production. The same dynamic that makes generation nearly free makes discrimination and curation the bottleneck resource, which is exactly where human effort and competitive advantage should be reallocated.
