This is the strongest mechanism-level counterargument to the outcome-pricing thesis: even if outcomes are philosophically closer to delivered value, customers and vendors still need understandable contracts, billing, forecasting, and sales motions. SBI reports that more than 80% of respondents still use some sort of seat-based metric, with only 8% using seats as their only value metric.

The right update is not that seats are permanently dominant. It is that the transition may be hybrid and slower than the clean agent-economics story implies. The existing belief should become a tension: AI may make seat pricing less theoretically correct while commercial simplicity keeps seats embedded as primary or secondary metrics.
