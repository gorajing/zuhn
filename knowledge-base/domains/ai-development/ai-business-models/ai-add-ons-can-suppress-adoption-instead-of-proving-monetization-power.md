SBI reports that 20% of net-new customers buy AI add-ons and only 38% of those purchasers use them, implying only 8 of 100 net-new customers ultimately use the AI feature. This challenges a simple monetization-first view where vendors can bolt AI on top of existing SaaS packaging and immediately capture premium pricing.

The implication is not purely anti-monetization. It is that packaging AI as a paid add-on may be a bad fit when the strategic priority is adoption, feedback, and model/product improvement. This supports a tension between near-term AI revenue capture and long-term AI capability compounding.
