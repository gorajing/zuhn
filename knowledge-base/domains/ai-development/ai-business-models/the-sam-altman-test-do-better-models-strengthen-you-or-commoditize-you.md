The Sam Altman test is a litmus for model-proof positioning: as frontier models improve, does your service get stronger, or does the model itself commoditize you? Founders want to be in the first camp, where rising model capability is tailwind rather than existential threat. This requires model fluency — knowing what frontier models can do today and designing the product to ride the curve as they improve — plus a second honesty check: are you using humans because the work genuinely needs judgment, or because you're papering over product gaps? Be honest, because compensating for product shortcomings with headcount destroys the operating leverage the whole model depends on. The test generalizes well beyond services: any business built on a foundation model should ask whether improving capability accrues to them or erodes their differentiation. Areas to avoid include anything involving equipment and on-site physical labor, where software-margin math doesn't apply and real leverage is hard to create.
