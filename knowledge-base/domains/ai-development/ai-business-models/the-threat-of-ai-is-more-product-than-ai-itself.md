Mo Bitar's central claim: the productivity narrative around AI is more impactful than AI's actual productivity gains. The doomer framing of AI displacement creates labor compliance, surveillance acceptance, and willingness to operate at lower pay. Meta laid off 8K people in the same month they announced screen-recording surveillance — the doomer narrative made the surveillance acceptable. Tech executives like Dario Amodei pushing 'we're going to take your job' messaging are functionally doing labor extraction work for their entire industry.

Application: when you hear 'AI will replace X workers' framing from CEOs or VCs, ask what concession that framing is being used to extract right now. The framing is rarely free; it's almost always serving an extractive function. For Memric specifically: the AI-replace-analysts framing extracts willingness from family offices to outsource judgment work — but Memric's actual positioning should be that AI augments judgment, not replaces it.
