A surprising adoption signal: even on college campuses, far fewer people have used a coding agent than you'd expect given how fast young people usually adopt new tech — and the gating factor is cost. Smart models stay expensive while cheap 'rush' models get cheaper but aren't free. Amp's response is a contrarian monetization experiment: a mini ad network delivering subtle, non-intrusive ads for other developer tools inside the terminal and editor, used to sponsor inference for the cheaper agent tier so more people can experience agents on side projects. This reframes agent monetization around removing the cost floor for the lowest-intent users rather than only charging high-intent professionals. As inference costs keep declining, the economics of ad-subsidized free tiers shift from 'never works' toward viable — making this a time-sensitive bet on a moving cost curve.
