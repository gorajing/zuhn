SBI argues that the rarity of outcome pricing is not mainly a billing-infrastructure problem. Their respondents regard implementing pricing changes in financial and billing infrastructure as comparatively easy; the hard part is value delivery, because truly discrete and self-contained customer outcomes remain rare.

For AI builders, this reframes the pricing decision as a product-design constraint. If the agent cannot prove it completed a clean unit of work, outcome pricing is likely premature. Confidence movement warranted: strengthen the operational precondition around the existing outcome-pricing belief rather than treating outcome pricing as the default destination for all AI products.
