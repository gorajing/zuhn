This challenges the existing Zuhn belief that agent-era software shifts from per-seat SaaS to selling completed work. SBI's survey found only 2 of 321 SaaS pricing decision-makers using outcome or performance-based pricing, which is fewer than 1% of respondents. That is strong counterevidence against any present-tense version of the claim.

The evidence is weaker against the 2028 prediction that at least five $100M+ AI startups will primarily charge per outcome or task, because the sample is broad SaaS rather than only AI-native agent companies and the report itself says agentic AI may eventually change the pattern. Confidence movement warranted: reduce confidence in near-term or generalized outcome-pricing adoption, but keep the longer-horizon AI-startup prediction active pending AI-native cohort data.
