Harrer's proof point: Insilico Medicine's AI agents facilitated the entire end-to-end drug discovery and development pipeline. In a world-first, they brought a new drug candidate from initial conception to clinical trial stage in approximately half the time conventional methods take. The savings are hundreds of millions of dollars and years of work. Important detail: the drug candidate was conceived BY AI in the first place — AI didn't just accelerate human-conceived ideas, it generated novel ones that humans validated. This is a different category than 'AI makes drug screening faster.'

Application: when modeling time-to-market or time-to-result for any AI-augmented scientific process, look at end-to-end pipelines, not just the AI-eligible step. The Insilico case is significant because it's the full pipeline (conception → validation → trial), not just one step (e.g., target identification). This is a leading indicator for what 'AI agents in your industry' could compress.
