Humeau observes that voice cloning now needs only a few seconds of reference audio, making it trivial to impersonate or configure a voice. He predicts that the concept of 'vocal identity' — already taken seriously by large companies in advertising and branding — will move mainstream: just as companies define how their website and visual identity appear, they will deliberately define how their brand sounds.

This is a forward-looking, cross-domain prediction connecting AI capability to business/marketing practice. The capability inversion (voice generation going from expensive/scarce to cheap/abundant) creates both an opportunity (consistent branded voices across all touchpoints, multilingual voice consistency) and a threat (easy impersonation, the reason Mistral withheld its cloning encoder from open release).

Marked time-sensitive and medium confidence because it's a directional forecast about how branding norms will evolve, falsifiable by observing whether 'brand voice guidelines' become a standard deliverable alongside brand style guides over the next few years.
