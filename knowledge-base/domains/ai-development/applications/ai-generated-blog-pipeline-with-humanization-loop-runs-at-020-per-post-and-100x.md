Parsa's content pipeline: feed Hrefs top 10 competitor keywords + competitor top-content, generate first draft, then run two evaluator agents that iterate on humanization (rewrite m-dashes, remove LLM-isms, restructure for readability) until the AI-detection score falls below threshold. 15-20 minutes per post, ~$0.20 cost. The economics fundamentally change content marketing: previously a high-quality blog post cost $200-1000 of contractor time; now it costs $0.20 + 5-minute human polish. A founder can ship 3-5 high-quality posts per week instead of one per month. Combined with proper GEO strategy, this changes the math on content marketing as a customer acquisition channel.

Application: for any product with a content-marketing-friendly customer (B2B SaaS, technical tools, professional services), the AI-blog pipeline is now an operational must-have. The 5-minute human polish is the irreducible quality gate. For Memric specifically: the Memric Brief Substack should be produced via this exact pipeline — competitor analysis (Doctrine.ai, Hebbia, AlphaSense content) → keyword targeting (CIO decision-making queries) → AI draft → humanization loop → final founder polish. 3x/week cadence becomes feasible vs the 1-2x/month constraint of pure-founder-written content.
