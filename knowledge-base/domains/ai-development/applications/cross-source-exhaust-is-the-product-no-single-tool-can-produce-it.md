Podhajský coins 'cognitive exhaust fumes' for the digital byproducts of your cognition — browsing history, journal entries, tasks, messages. Any single stream is just waste, but analyzing the combined exhaust diagnoses the underlying 'engine.' The defining capability is cross-source: no individual tool can tell you about intention-action gaps, attention drift, or relationship decay, because each source is blind to the others. Asking 'given my recent reading, who in my network should I discuss this with?' fuses browser history, a CRM, and reading lists into an insight no single app could surface.

For builders, this reframes where to invest: the moat is not a better single-source feature but the integration layer that lets uncoordinated sources be queried together. It also reframes data you already throw away — the most underused dataset you own is the exhaust you generate passively. The product job is to make that latent cross-source signal legible and reviewable, not to add another siloed tool.
