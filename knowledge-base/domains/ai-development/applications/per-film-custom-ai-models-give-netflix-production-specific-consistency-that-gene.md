Netflix acquired InterPositive for up to $600M primarily for its architectural approach: ingesting raw production dailies and training a bespoke model on that specific film's footage before handing filmmakers an autonomous post-production engine. The result is 10x efficiency gains on VFX tasks (relighting, wire removal, background generation, continuity fixes) while staying stylistically consistent with the director's vision.

The key insight generalizes well beyond filmmaking. Generic foundation models are trained on everything and optimized for nothing in particular. Project-specific fine-tuning on domain-relevant data produces outputs that feel native to that context. This is a pattern we should expect to see replicated across industries: architecture, fashion, game development, and anywhere else where brand or style consistency is load-bearing.

The acquisition also signals that Netflix is betting on owning proprietary production infrastructure rather than licensing commodity AI tools. Specialized capability compounds over time; commodity tools commoditize margins.
