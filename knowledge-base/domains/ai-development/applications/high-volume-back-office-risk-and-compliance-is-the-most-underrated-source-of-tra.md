When forced to pick a single primary ROI benefit across eight categories (time savings, increased output, quality, new capabilities, better decisions, cost savings, revenue, risk reduction), respondents chose risk reduction least often — just 3.4%. On its face, it looks like the weakest category.

The distribution inside that category inverts the story: 25% of risk-reduction use cases report transformational impact, the highest transformational rate of any category. The mechanism is intuitive once stated — compliance, risk, and back-office functions are defined by sheer volume and quantity of repetitive, high-stakes work, exactly the shape of problem where AI scales leverage dramatically. NLW's contacts in compliance and risk functions independently confirmed this pattern.

The takeaway for anyone allocating AI effort: the loudest, most-attempted use cases (time savings) are not where the asymmetric upside lives. Underexplored, volume-heavy back-office functions are a contrarian bet with a higher hit rate for transformational outcomes. Where the crowd isn't looking is where the transformational ROI is hiding.
