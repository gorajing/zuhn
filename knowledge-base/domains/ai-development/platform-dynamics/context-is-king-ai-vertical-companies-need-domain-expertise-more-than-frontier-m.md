The pattern for successful vertical AI: founders who are technically fluent in frontier models but hire for deep domain context early. Eve (legal AI for plaintiff law) exemplifies this — technical founders from Rubric who hired plaintiff attorneys on staff so that every new model release gets evaluated through the lens of actual legal workflows.

The business model alignment matters too: in contingency-based plaintiff law, making lawyers 5x more efficient means taking on 5x more clients (unlike billable-hour firms where efficiency erodes revenue). OpenAI won't build dental care management or auto-loan servicing voice agents — these are 'gold bricks' 100 feet away while they have gold bricks at their feet. The Cambrian explosion of newly-interesting markets (labor markets that were never interesting for software) is the opportunity.
