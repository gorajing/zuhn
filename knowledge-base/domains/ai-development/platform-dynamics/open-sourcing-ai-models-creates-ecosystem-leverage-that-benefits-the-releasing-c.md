Zuckerberg explains Meta's open-source AI strategy through two reinforcing loops. First, top AI researchers want to work at places where they can share their work — so openness is a talent acquisition strategy. Second, open-sourcing infrastructure (they did this with data center designs via Open Compute Project) drives community innovation that makes Meta's own systems more efficient.

The strategy is deliberately designed to prevent a small number of companies from monopolizing state-of-the-art AI. By making LLaMA available, Meta ensures a broader ecosystem of startups, researchers, and hackers build on their foundation. This isn't altruism — it's platform strategy. The more the ecosystem relies on Meta's models, the more Meta learns from community usage patterns and improvements.
