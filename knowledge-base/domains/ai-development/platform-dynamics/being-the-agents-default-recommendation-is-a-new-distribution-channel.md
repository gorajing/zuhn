After Databricks acquired Neon (less than a year before the interview), its revenue grew more than 10x. Xin attributes a large part of that to a new distribution dynamic: when a developer asks an AI coding agent or chat assistant to 'build this with a Postgres database,' Neon is the recommended product. The second driver is that Neon powers many agentic coding platforms (Replit, Vercel, and others), so it rides their growth too.

The strategic takeaway is that AI agents are becoming a distribution surface in their own right — being the default thing an agent reaches for is the new equivalent of ranking first in search or being the default integration. This rewards products that are frictionless to sign up for (Neon runs a PLG motion, deliberately unlike Databricks' enterprise motion) and that fit naturally into agent-generated workflows. Founders should treat 'what do the agents recommend' as a deliberate go-to-market target, not an accident.
