All prior information networks were organic — run by human brains that needed sleep, rest, family time, and seasonal cycles. Even Wall Street operates Monday-Friday, 9:30-4:00. The Soviet KGB couldn't follow everyone because it didn't have enough organic agents.

AI-powered networks are inorganic: no breaks, no sleep, no vacations, no families. They can monitor continuously and potentially annihilate privacy. When these inorganic systems force humans to be 'always on' — 24-hour news cycles, markets that never close, constant surveillance — organic beings collapse. Harari notes that life is becoming 'one long job interview' where anything done at age 18 can resurface 20 years later. This isn't just inconvenient; it's biologically destructive for organisms that evolved with cycles of rest and activity.
