Naval argues that when anyone can code via AI, the app market follows the same pattern as Amazon (bookstores) and YouTube (TV networks): one or two giant aggregators at the head, a massive long tail of niche apps filling every conceivable need, and the destruction of medium-sized firms in between. The 5-10-20 person software companies that served enterprise niches get either vibe-coded away or absorbed by the lead app expanding its scope.

This has direct strategic implications: if you're building a mid-tier SaaS product, you're in the kill zone. Either move toward becoming the category-defining app with engineering depth that vibe coders can't match, or embrace the long tail by serving hyper-specific niches that were previously uneconomical. The middle ground — 'good enough for a segment' — ceases to be defensible when the cost of building competitive alternatives drops to near zero.
