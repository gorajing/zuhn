The video explicitly frames open-source models as preferable because they lack the 'unwanted safety layers' of Midjourney and DALL-E. This reveals a structural dynamic in AI platform competition: any proprietary safety constraint that restricts commercially valuable outputs drives demand toward open-source alternatives where those constraints don't exist.

This creates an asymmetric competitive landscape. Proprietary platforms must balance safety with user retention, knowing that overly restrictive filters push power users to self-hosted alternatives. Meanwhile, open-source ecosystems benefit from a steady stream of fine-tuned checkpoints (hosted on platforms like CivitAI) that are specifically optimized for the use cases proprietary platforms restrict. The implication for AI governance is that platform-level content moderation has a hard ceiling on effectiveness as long as capable open-source base models exist.
