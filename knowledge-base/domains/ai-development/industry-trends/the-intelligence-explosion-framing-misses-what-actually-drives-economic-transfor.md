Tamay Besiroglu argues that the intelligence explosion frame is misleading because the Industrial Revolution wasn't primarily about raw horsepower—it was about simultaneous innovations in agriculture, transportation, law, finance, and urbanization. Similarly, getting very smart AI systems is only one moving part among many needed for broad economic transformation.

The practical implication is that even if you fully automate AI R&D, the world economy is not bottlenecked by 'not enough good reasoning.' Technological acceleration requires the economy as a whole growing, supply chains upgrading, demand forming for new products, and complementary innovations across many sectors. Having brilliant reasoning tokens alone doesn't substitute for this broader buildout.

For investors, founders, and researchers this is a corrective against narratives that assume 'superintelligence → overnight transformation.' Even in fast scenarios, the physical buildout and cross-sector integration dominate the timeline.
