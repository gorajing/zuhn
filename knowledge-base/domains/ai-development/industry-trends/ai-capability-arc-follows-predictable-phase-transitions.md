From the 2017 transformer paper through multi-modal LLMs, reasoning models capable of advanced math and coding, and now agentic systems, AI has undergone roughly one major phase transition per year. Each phase didn't just add capabilities — it shifted what the technology fundamentally was. Chatbot intuitions don't translate well to reasoning models; reasoning model intuitions don't cleanly apply to agents.

The practical consequence is that expertise accumulated in one phase can become a liability in the next if held too rigidly. Teams that built deep intuitions about GPT-3-era prompt engineering found some of it irrelevant for chain-of-thought reasoning models. Practitioners need to periodically audit which beliefs were phase-specific versus which will persist. The discipline of distinguishing durable principles from cycle-specific tactics is more valuable than raw expertise in any single phase.
