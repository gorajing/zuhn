Intuition says small, nimble companies should adopt new technology fastest. The data repeatedly shows the opposite for AI at scale: in McKinsey's study only 7% of organizations see themselves as fully at scale while ~62% are still piloting or experimenting, and larger organizations are generally further along the scaling curve than smaller ones. This has held across the multi-year trajectory of AI adoption — the biggest organizations make the biggest, most systematic efforts.

There is a nuance at the extreme low end. The very smallest organizations (and especially the 1-50 person band) appear to capture more transformational benefit early — a 3-person company and a 40-person company may behave radically differently, unlike a 1,000 vs 2,000 person org which look similar. NLW flags this band as worth a dedicated follow-up study.

Marked time-sensitive because it describes the current shape of the adoption curve, which will shift as tooling commoditizes. The strategic read: large incumbents' scale, budgets, and systematic programs are an underrated AI advantage right now, while the smallest firms compete on a different axis — speed to transformational rather than breadth of scaling.
