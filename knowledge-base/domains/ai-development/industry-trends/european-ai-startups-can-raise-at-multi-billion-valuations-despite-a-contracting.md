Mistral's €468M equity round at a €5.8bn valuation — led by DST Global with General Catalyst — illustrates that AI-native companies can attract global capital regardless of regional market conditions. This stands in stark contrast to the simultaneous 50%+ drop in active European VC investors. The divergence suggests capital is not evenly contracting: it is concentrating into perceived high-conviction bets, particularly in AI foundation models and infrastructure. For European founders, the implication is that AI positioning increasingly determines access to global capital pools, while non-AI companies face a much harder environment as marginal investors exit the market.
