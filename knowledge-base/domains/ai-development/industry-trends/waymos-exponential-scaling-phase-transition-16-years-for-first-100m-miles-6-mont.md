Dolgov's metrics: 16 years from Waymo's start to 100M autonomous miles; another 100M in just 6 months. 20M total rides delivered; half of those in the last 7 months. 8 years to launch in 4 cities; 4 more cities in a single day a few weeks ago. The technology has crossed from R&D phase (slow capital-intensive iteration) to scalable product phase (replicable city launches).

Why this matters beyond Waymo: this exponential pattern is what 'crossing the production-readiness threshold' looks like for any AI capability. Software engineering with frontier agents may be undergoing the same transition (Karpathy's December 2025 inflection); customer service AI is approaching it; coding agents have crossed it. The signature: time-per-additional-unit-of-deployment collapses by an order of magnitude or more. Investors and operators who recognize the phase transition early capture massive value; those who wait until the exponential is obvious miss the inflection.

For Zuhn editorially: this is the operational form of 'AI capability matures in steps, not curves.' The right question for any AI domain isn't 'how fast is progress?' but 'has it crossed the production-readiness phase transition yet?' If yes, expect 5-10x compression in deployment timelines. If no, the long tail still applies.
