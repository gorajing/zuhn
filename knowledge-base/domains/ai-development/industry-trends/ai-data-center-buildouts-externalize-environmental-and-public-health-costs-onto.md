Specific facilities Hao documents: OpenAI Stargate in Abilene, Texas (Central Park-sized, 1M chips, >1 gigawatt, >20% the power demand of New York City). Meta Louisiana (4x the size of Abilene, half NYC's average power demand, roughly 1/5 the area of Manhattan). xAI Colossus in Memphis (35 methane gas turbines erected without community notification in a predominantly Black and working-class neighborhood already facing environmental racism and some of the highest lung cancer rates in the US; residents discovered it from the smell of gas leaks in their living rooms).

These facilities increase power utility costs and decrease grid reliability for existing residents; they compete with communities for freshwater in regions already under drought; and when labs can't get grid connections fast enough, they pop up their own fossil plants next door. The key claim for epistemics: the per-token marginal cost claims of frontier labs tacitly assume these externalized costs don't count. When they're internalized — via community protest, data center bans, and air quality lawsuits — the unit economics of 'just scale to AGI' look very different. Dozens of grassroots protests against data centers are already stalling and banning projects.
