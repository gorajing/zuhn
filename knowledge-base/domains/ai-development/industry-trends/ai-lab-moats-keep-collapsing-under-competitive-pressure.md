The historical record from 2020 onward shows no AI lab has been able to convert any supposed flywheel — engagement data, synthetic data generation, scale economies — into a sustainable lead. Instead, the big three labs rotate around the podium roughly monthly, with the next tier never far behind. This pattern suggests that whatever advantages exist are leaking through talent poaching, the SF rumor mill, or normal reverse-engineering of released models.

This matters for forecasting intelligence explosions: even if one lab cracks continual learning, the breakthrough is unlikely to translate into a winner-take-all dynamic because the implementation will be reverse-engineered and replicated within months. Expect a broadly deployed intelligence ramp-up rather than a single-lab takeoff.

Apply this when evaluating any AI lab's strategic positioning or investor pitch — flywheels in this market have a poor track record and the burden of proof should sit with whoever claims a durable moat.
