Surya's framing in the introduction: AI is an incredibly powerful tool that expands the space of solvable scientific problems, but it cannot answer 'what problems matter' or 'what they mean to us.' Those are irreducibly human judgments. This is a counter to naive optimism that AI will simply 'do science better.' It will solve more problems, but the curation of which problems deserve attention — what's worth a decade of work, what serves humanity, what's intellectually beautiful — remains in human hands.

Application: institutional design for AI-augmented science needs explicit mechanisms for problem-selection that don't depend on AI scoring functions. Otherwise the field optimizes for solvable problems rather than important ones. Same logic applies to investment-firm AI: model can audit decisions, but selecting which decisions to audit and what verdicts to act on remains the IC's judgment, not the model's.
