Universe Panel: panelists describe pattern abstraction as a deliberate cognitive skill. The mental move: 'Let me identify what's unique about this problem, then articulate it in language that abstracts away from my specific domain (e.g., wave magnetic, AI), so it can be picked up and applied in some other area.' Examples cited: ideas in astrophysics initially developed for one problem proved applicable to drug discovery and material science via the same simulation-based-inference framework. The skill takes time to develop but is teachable. Particularly valuable in AI for science because so many domains are simultaneously discovering similar patterns.

Application: graduate student training should explicitly include cross-disciplinary pattern recognition as a skill, not leave it to chance. For knowledge management systems (like Memric/Zuhn): cross-domain transfer detection is itself a feature, surfacing this kind of pattern explicitly to scientists who would otherwise have to do it manually.
