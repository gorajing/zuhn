Yale Budget Lab's strongest methodological contribution is its discipline: compare occupational-mix change against historical technology baselines, separate theoretical exposure from actual usage, test unemployment and cohort slices, and update regularly rather than infer displacement from anecdotes or adoption headlines. For Zuhn predictions, this means confidence updates should be tied to observed movement in preregistered indicators, not executive rhetoric, layoffs with ambiguous causes, or tool-usage growth alone.
