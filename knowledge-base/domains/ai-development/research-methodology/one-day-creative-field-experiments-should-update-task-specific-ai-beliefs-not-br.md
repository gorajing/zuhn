This paper earns more weight than generic pro-AI commentary because it used random assignment, a real company setting, 776 professionals, expert evaluation of 550 solutions, and preregistered design elements. But its external validity is still bounded. The tasks were one-day virtual early-stage product-development exercises, not months-long product execution, safety-critical decisions, or deep operational coordination. The right confidence movement is targeted: raise the probability that AI helps creative cross-functional teams produce exceptional ideas, while preserving skepticism about naive human-AI teaming in decision tasks and long-horizon workflows.
