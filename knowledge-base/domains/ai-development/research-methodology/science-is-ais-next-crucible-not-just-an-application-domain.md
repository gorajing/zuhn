David Gouli's framing argument at the conference opening: AI for science is fundamentally different from consumer AI (which can generate fun videos and songs without rigor). Scientific applications demand precision — physics measures the electron magnetic moment to 13 decimal places. To meet this rigor, the next generation of AI must be more explainable, trustworthy, data efficient, causally aware. Science is therefore a forcing function on AI quality, not just a consumer of AI capability.

Application: AI researchers seeking high-impact problems should work in scientific domains rather than consumer applications. Quality requirements there will force better fundamental advances. Same logic applies to other high-rigor domains: investment-firm decision audit also demands precision (false positives on retired principles have real consequences) — same crucible logic applies.
