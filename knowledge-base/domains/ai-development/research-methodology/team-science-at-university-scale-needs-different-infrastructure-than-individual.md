James Lennon's second commitment for the merged HAI+SDS institute: team science 'at a scale and a form that no one else will do.' Long-horizon scientific questions, public-interest evaluations, AI for fields with messy data and slow returns require larger teams, sustained funding, real engineering capacity, and shared compute. The traditional academic structure of individual PI labs competing for grants isn't matched to this. Universities have to evolve their internal structures or cede this work to industry.

Application: when evaluating 'who can do AI-for-X research,' ask whether they have the team-science infrastructure (>10 people, multi-year funding, dedicated engineers, shared compute) — not just smart individual PIs. The institutional substrate matters more than individual talent at this scale.
