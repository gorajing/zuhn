The counterevidence is credible because it comes from a large McKinsey Global Survey with 1,993 participants and explicit high-performer comparisons, including a relative weights analysis across management practices. But it is still survey evidence and can suffer from selection, self-reporting, and reverse causality. The warranted confidence move is to narrow the bottom-up principle: bottom-up adoption reveals where AI matters; top-down transformation decides whether the value survives enterprise scaling.
