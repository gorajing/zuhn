The core tension in modern physics is that quantum mechanics (governing small things) and general relativity (governing gravity and spacetime) are individually the most successful theories ever created, yet their mathematics literally breaks down when combined. This pattern recurs across domains: when two independently validated frameworks produce contradictions at their interface, the answer isn't to patch one or discard the other — it's to find a deeper abstraction that contains both as special cases. In AI development, this mirrors tensions between symbolic reasoning and neural approaches, or between scaling laws and architectural innovation. The history of physics suggests these boundary contradictions are the most productive places to look for breakthroughs — they're not bugs but signposts pointing toward missing conceptual layers.
