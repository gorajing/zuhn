Gil's response to a question about productivity measurement: the productivity definition itself is a scientific question DOE wants to engage the community on. But the broad direction is clear: NOT doubling the number of papers (Gil explicitly says this is not the goal). Instead, mission-level outcome measurement — programs targeting specific national challenges (fusion, advanced materials, biotech) measure productivity against the outcome (e.g., delivering fusion energy by 2032 vs. 2035, reducing material design from a decade to under a year). Combined with granular workload-level improvements (e.g., 6-month X-ray reconstruction down to 10 hours via AI). Macro-level productivity is mission-completion-rate; micro-level productivity is workload-throughput.

Application: in any scientific or engineering organization, the productivity definition shapes behavior. 'Number of papers/PRs/features' optimizes for volume. 'Mission outcome delivered N years earlier than baseline' optimizes for impact. Universities specifically need to evolve from output counts to outcome attribution if AI is going to multiply output without multiplying impact.
