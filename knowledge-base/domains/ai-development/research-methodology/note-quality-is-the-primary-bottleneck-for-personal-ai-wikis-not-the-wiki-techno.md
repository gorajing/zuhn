Kim Moon-jeong's most important observation from building her LM Wiki: 'I think the original quality of the notebook is becoming much more important. The key point is not the act of building the LM wiki itself, but rather what kind of notes — how good and high-quality, how insightful — were used as the foundation to build the LLM wiki. Because we aren't using LM Wiki solely for the purpose of construction itself.' This is the personal-AI analogue of the Benchling 2026 report's finding (AI killer-app categories sit on clean verifiable data; AI struggles where data is scattered). Your personal AI wiki will only be as smart as your notes are insightful. If your notes are 'meeting happened, attendees were X, agenda was Y' — your wiki will surface that exact shallow content. If your notes are 'meeting happened, the surprising thing was Z because everyone expected W; this connects to my January thinking about Q' — your wiki can surface those connections and propose related claims. The implication: before spending the weekend setting up Alda/Obsidian/MLX, spend a month upgrading your note-taking discipline — write principles not summaries, write stances not topics, write what surprised you not what was confirmed, and link aggressively to other notes. The wiki is the multiplier; the notes are the multiplicand. Garbage notes × great wiki = slightly-organized garbage. This generalizes to any AI system layered on personal data: email AI, journal AI, research AI — invest in the upstream quality before the downstream amplification.
