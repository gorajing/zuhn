Brian Hie on the panel addressing dual-use concerns: AI in biology has dual-use potential, but the marginal benefit calculation favors defenders. Nature already constantly produces viruses with pandemic potential without any human intervention; the bottleneck on biosecurity isn't 'are pathogens scarce?' but 'can we detect, rapidly respond, and cure?' AI accelerates the detect/respond/cure side dramatically more than it accelerates the design-a-novel-attack side, because attack designs already exist in nature in abundance. Nuance: this doesn't mean dual-use risks are zero, just that the cost-benefit favors aggressive AI development for biology over restriction.

Application: when evaluating biosecurity policy proposals, ask not 'does this AI capability enable harm' but 'does the marginal benefit of this capability for defenders exceed the marginal benefit for attackers given the natural baseline.' Most generative biology capabilities pass this test.
