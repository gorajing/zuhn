Sung argues physics is built from simple principles and intuition, while biology is an irreducibly complex system without a single governing equation; therefore AI — which reads very complicated patterns — is the most suitable 'language' for biology. He pairs this with the practical point that AI compresses the literature-reading and organizing burden and, if grounded to reduce hallucination, frees the researcher's scarce resource (mental energy and attention) for judgment. A useful framing for where AI-for-science pays off most.
