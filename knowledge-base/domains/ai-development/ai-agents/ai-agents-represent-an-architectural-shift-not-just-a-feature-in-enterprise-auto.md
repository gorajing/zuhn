Traditional enterprise automation (RPA, workflow tools) handles structured, deterministic processes. AI agents differ architecturally: they can reason about ambiguous goals, use tools dynamically, and complete multi-step tasks without human handoffs at each step. Menlo Ventures' analysis positions this as a platform shift, not an incremental improvement.

The implication for enterprise software is significant: the value chain for workflow automation is being disrupted. Companies building on agent infrastructure (orchestration, memory, tool-calling) are likely to capture outsized returns, while traditional RPA vendors face structural headwinds. For founders, the design principle is to build systems that give agents the right tools and context, rather than hardcoding workflow logic.
