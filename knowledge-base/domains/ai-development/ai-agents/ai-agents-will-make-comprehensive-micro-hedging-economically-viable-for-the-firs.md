Even sophisticated hedge funds hedge only a handful of risk categories, because building and maintaining a portfolio that accounts for every plausible adverse scenario requires enormous ongoing intellectual labor. A seaside resort, for example, faces hurricane risk, tsunami risk, oil-spill risk, local regulatory risk, and dozens of other factors—most of which could theoretically be hedged in prediction markets if the work of identifying, sizing, and rebalancing those positions weren't prohibitively expensive. AI agents that can perform this analysis continuously at near-zero cost change the calculus entirely. The limiting factor shifts from 'who has the bandwidth to model and monitor a thousand positions' to 'who has access to liquid markets for those risks.' This creates a potential demand shock for prediction market volume if the agent economy materializes as expected—and it suggests that prediction market infrastructure built today is positioning for that demand.
