There is a fundamental distinction between structured knowledge and
vector memory. Vector databases store text blobs and retrieve them by
semantic similarity — good for finding relevant passages but unable to
reason about relationships between concepts. Curated structured data
(entities with typed relationships) enables the model to reason about
connections, traverse relationship chains, and synthesize insights
across multiple linked entities. For business-critical context where
accuracy and reasoning matter, structured data consistently outperforms
vector retrieval.
