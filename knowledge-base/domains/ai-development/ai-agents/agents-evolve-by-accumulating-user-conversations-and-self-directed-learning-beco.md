Korean YouTuber Kim Moon-jeong's experience with AI agents demonstrates compound personalization. Her coin-trading agent doesn't just execute trades — it remembers every conversation, learns from links she shares, and makes increasingly refined decisions. When she shared chart analysis tools, the agent followed the instructions exactly and began identifying patterns independently.

Most striking: when she told the agent about real AI influencers, it went to their social media accounts, clicked follow on its own, and began self-educating by consuming their content. The agent evolved from following instructions to autonomously expanding its own knowledge base in its specialization area. She also created a health management agent for her diabetic father that analyzes photos of meals, calculates carbohydrates, knows his fasting blood sugar levels, and gives eating-order advice (eat tofu and fish first, rice last, walk 10 minutes after). The key insight: an agent's value compounds over time as it accumulates more context about its specific user.
