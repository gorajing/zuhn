PitchBook data shows that nearly half of Y Combinator's Spring 2025 batch consists of AI agent startups, continuing a dramatic concentration trend. This mirrors the broader venture market where AI startups dominate funding rounds across stages.

The high concentration is a double-edged signal: it confirms AI agents as the consensus next platform shift, but it also means the space will be brutally competitive within 12-18 months. Startups entering this category need to differentiate through vertical specialization, proprietary data moats, or superior distribution rather than raw AI capability, which is increasingly commoditized.
