Matthew, running a cybersecurity firm from Bali, demonstrates the most radical AI agent productivity shift documented. His agents access client systems overnight, run security tests, discover vulnerabilities, and write complete reports with solutions. Previously one person could do one test every two weeks manually. Now multiple tests run daily.

His workday consists of just two human-required touchpoints: setting goals at the beginning and confirming results at the end. Everything between — from executing tests to reflecting revisions to sending emails to customers — is handled autonomously by agents. Monthly revenue approaches 150 million won (~$110K USD). This is not hypothetical efficiency gain but a documented operational reality, though it represents an early-adopter case in a technically suitable domain (cybersecurity testing is highly structured and automatable). The pattern suggests that domains with clear goal-setting, structured execution, and verifiable outputs will see the most dramatic agent-driven compression.
