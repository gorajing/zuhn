The historical pattern is clear: jet airliners existed for decades before 9/11 forced security overhauls, RC hobby drones became the leading weapon category of the century before meaningful drone regulation existed, and nuclear technology was only controlled after Hiroshima. Smith argues AI agents will follow this identical trajectory — they won't be regulated as weapons until someone uses them as weapons.

The terrifying asymmetry is that AI agent capabilities are improving so rapidly that by the time the catalyzing attack occurs, agents may be capable of far more damage than the attack itself demonstrates. The gap between 'first misuse' and 'agents capable of civilizational-scale damage' may be extremely narrow, leaving almost no runway for regulatory response. This makes the pre-attack window — right now — the only realistic time to build regulatory frameworks, even though political will is essentially zero.
