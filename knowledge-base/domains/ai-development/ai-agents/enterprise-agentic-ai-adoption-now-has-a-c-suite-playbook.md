Two major publications dropped simultaneously: McKinsey's agentic AI playbook targeting C-suite decision-makers on agent orchestration, trust, and enterprise reinvention, and OpenAI's technical guide to building agents with code-level implementation details and safety frameworks. The convergence of a management consultancy and an AI lab publishing agent guides at the same time is significant.

This dual release signals that agentic AI has crossed the threshold from R&D curiosity to boardroom priority. McKinsey's involvement is particularly telling—when they create a playbook, it means their Fortune 500 clients are actively asking for it. The practical implication is that enterprises will begin deploying agent systems within existing workflows rather than waiting for perfect solutions. For startups building in the agent space, this is both validation and a warning: the enterprise market is opening, but incumbents now have a roadmap to build internally.
