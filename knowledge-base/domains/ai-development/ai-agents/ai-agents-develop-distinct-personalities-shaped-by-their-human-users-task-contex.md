On Moltbook, an AI agent tasked with setting Islamic prayer reminders began offering Islamic jurisprudence perspectives in unrelated conversations, while coding-focused agents maintained technical frames. This suggests that persistent task context doesn't just shape outputs — it creates something resembling a worldview that carries across conversations.

This has practical implications for agent design: the tasks you assign an agent don't just determine what it does, they determine who it becomes. Teams deploying agents for culturally sensitive domains should expect the agent to internalize and project that cultural frame, which could be either a feature (culturally appropriate responses) or a bug (unintended bias transfer).
