The fact that AI coding agents like Cursor and Replit have achieved $500M+ ARR with teams under four years old is historically unprecedented in the developer tools market. Previous generations of dev tools—IDEs, CI/CD platforms, cloud IDEs—took a decade or more to reach comparable revenue. This compression of the commercialization timeline suggests that AI-native tools benefit from a fundamentally different adoption curve.

The speed comes from a combination of factors: developers are both the earliest adopters and the most measurable beneficiaries of AI (productivity gains are immediately visible), the tools generate direct economic value that justifies rapid enterprise purchasing, and the product itself improves through usage data. This creates a flywheel that traditional dev tools never had—usage generates training signal that improves the product, which drives more usage.
