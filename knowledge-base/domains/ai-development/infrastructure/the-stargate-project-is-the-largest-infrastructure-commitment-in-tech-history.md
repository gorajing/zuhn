The AI Index 2026 Chapter 4 timeline lists Stargate as the defining investment event of 2025. Announced January 21, 2025 at the White House by OpenAI, SoftBank, Oracle, and MGX (with Nvidia support), the venture plans to invest between $100 billion and $500 billion to build advanced AI data centers across the United States by 2029. On September 10, 2025, OpenAI signed a $300 billion five-year cloud contract with Oracle beginning in 2027, with Oracle providing 4.5 gigawatts of computing capacity for Stargate. Google announced $150B in annual capex in 2025, $40B in Texas data centers through 2027, and $6.4B in Germany cloud infrastructure. Microsoft pledged $17.5B for India AI infrastructure. Amazon announced $35B for India by 2030. The scale is without historical precedent — the Interstate Highway System adjusted for inflation was roughly $500B over 35 years. Stargate alone aims to match that in 4. The implication is that energy siting, grid interconnect, and water rights are becoming the binding constraints, and that AI infrastructure spend is structurally different from dot-com capex — it is producing durable physical assets, not fiber glut.
