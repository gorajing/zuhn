Gil's announcement: the Genesis Mission, formally launched by the Secretary of Energy, is structured as a coherent national platform connecting high-performance computing, AI, quantum technology, and experimental facilities (including robotic laboratories) across the 17 DOE national labs and 28 user facilities. Goals: 10-100x acceleration in materials science, fusion, biotech, energy systems. Initial response: 8K+ applications from 800+ institutions across all 50 states (3x previous DOE program records). Initial 26 national science/technology challenges defined with measurable outcomes (e.g., reducing material design from decade to under one year). Components include the 'American Science Cloud' (distributed AI models + scientific data), the 'self-improving AI for science' research program, and a Genesis consortium with 30+ companies.

Application: this is the institutional vehicle through which large-scale AI-for-science investment will flow in the US. Researchers and companies positioning for AI-for-science work should align with Genesis Mission challenges and consortium structure rather than building parallel infrastructure.
