Gil cites fusion ignition (achieved at the National Ignition Facility in 2022) as a precedent for the Genesis Mission's integration model. It wasn't 'AI suddenly solved fusion' — it was the result of decades of investment in integrated science: high-fidelity simulation development, experimental design refinement, theoretical advances all working together, supported by federal infrastructure that no commercial entity would have built. The new piece is the speed and scale: AI makes the integration tighter and faster, but the fundamental model (simulation + experiment + theory in coordination) is what produced fusion ignition. Genesis aims to scale that model from one mission outcome to many.

Application: when arguing for the value of long-horizon scientific investment, fusion ignition is a clean precedent — measurable outcome, decades of patient integrated work, dependent on federal funding nobody else would provide. Useful for both Genesis-style policy arguments and for justifying long-horizon corporate research programs.
