swyx uses his own AI News as the proof point: the PyTorch lead told him plainly it 'is not an agent' — it's a workflow — and swyx concedes he's right. Yet it delivers daily value to over 70,000 readers. His conclusion is that builders should 'deliver value instead of arguable terminology.' The fact that something is 'just a bunch of scripts in a trench coat' running on a fixed daily schedule does not diminish the value it creates.

The strategic warning is that 'is it really an agent?' is a status game that distracts from the only question that matters to users: does this do something valuable. Teams that gate launches on whether a system is sufficiently agentic, or that add agentic complexity purely to earn the label, are optimizing for vocabulary over outcomes. The same instinct shows up in over-branding — swyx jokes about why every conference track isn't relabeled as some flavor of 'agent.'

This connects to the simplicity lesson and the input/output-ratio framing: a simple, non-agentic workflow that reliably ships value is a legitimate and often superior endpoint, not a way station you must evolve past to become a 'real' agent.
