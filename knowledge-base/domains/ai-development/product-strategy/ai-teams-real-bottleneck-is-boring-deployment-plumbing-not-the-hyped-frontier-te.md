The public AI conversation centers on flashy capabilities — voice agents, MCP, agentic workflows — but those teams are mostly grappling with a far more fundamental and boring problem: how to use more models in more places without rebuilding or extending infrastructure each time. Trying a new open-source model today means writing a Dockerfile, spinning up a container, hosting it, and wiring it in as yet another tool in the agent's context. Complexity creeps in and compounds.

The strategic implication is that the highest-leverage product opportunities often sit in the unglamorous substrate the hype skips over. A tool builder who reduces 'try a new model' from a multi-step infra project to a one-line model-argument change is solving the problem teams actually feel daily. The pattern generalizes: when evaluating where to build, discount the discourse and instead ask what repetitive friction practitioners absorb silently because no one has bothered to remove it.
