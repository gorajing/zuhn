Fan projects beyond current robotics to the 'Physical API' -- analogous to the LLM API but for manipulating atoms instead of bits. On top of this layer, he envisions physical prompting (instructing robots through language and demonstration), a physical app store, and a skill economy where a Michelin chef could teach a robot and deliver Michelin dinners as a service.

The progression mirrors LLM development: from digital twins (manually crafted, limited) to digital cousins (hybrid generative/classical) to digital nomads (pure neural world models dreaming in video space). The end state is robots that 'fade into the background like ambient intelligence' -- you pass the Physical Turing Test and it's remembered as just another Tuesday. For builders, the near-term opportunity is in simulation infrastructure; the long-term opportunity is in the platforms that broker physical skills.
