The current product landscape distinguishes 'AI products' (chatbots, agents, AI assistants) from 'traditional products' (Gmail, Google Docs, search). Logan's prediction collapses this distinction: every product becomes agentic, meaning the user interaction shifts from clicking through fixed UI to dispatching tasks to an agent that operates the product on the user's behalf. Gmail-as-agent triages, drafts, and sends with user approval. Search-as-agent runs multi-step research and delivers compressed conclusions. Google Docs-as-agent writes the first draft and proposes revisions. The implication for product strategy: the question for any product is not 'should we add an agent feature' but 'what does this product look like as an agent?' Products that maintain non-agentic versions while competitors ship agentic ones face the same fate as desktop software that resisted cloud — slow erosion as users migrate to the more leveraged interaction. The implication for builders: design current products with the assumption that the agent-shaped version will arrive within 2-3 years, and ensure your data model and API surface support that shape rather than locking into UI-clicking patterns that will become obsolete.
