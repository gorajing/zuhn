Wattrus maps Flatfile's AI stack into four buckets that have nothing to do with the model and everything to do with where the AI sits relative to the user. Invisible AI runs entirely in the background (e.g. signing up triggers agents that build a personalized demo app from your company's domain — the user never knows AI is involved). Ambient AI is present in the workspace but not directly operated — sparkles appear on columns where an agent has found a fixable problem. Inline AI is embedded in the active workflow, letting the user invoke it directly on the data they're already editing. Conversational AI is the chat-driven 'build mode' everyone argues about.

The strategic point is that the industry's reflex is to express every AI capability as a chatbox, which is only one of four surfaces and often the worst fit. Most of the highest-leverage AI in a product is invisible or ambient — it does work without demanding attention or a conversation. Treating 'add AI' as 'add a chat panel' leaves three of the four form factors on the table.

For practitioners, the taxonomy is a design checklist: for any AI capability, ask which form factor minimizes user friction. Background personalization wants invisible; opportunistic suggestions want ambient; data manipulation the user is already doing wants inline; open-ended construction wants conversational.
