XBOW's autonomous hacker improved dramatically just by swapping in newer foundation models: it topped HackerOne with a Sonnet/Gemini alloy, and de Moor estimates GPT-5 alone would have made it roughly three times better than the best human, with benchmarks now saturating so fast they must be replaced. The strategic lesson is to position your product as orchestration on top of frontier models rather than as bespoke model engineering. When the underlying models improve, your product gets better for free while competitors who hard-coded around an older capability ceiling have to re-engineer. The risk this trades for is commoditization, so the orchestration layer (here, reconnaissance, prioritization, and exploit selection) must carry the defensibility — but the default posture is to ride the curve, not fight it.
