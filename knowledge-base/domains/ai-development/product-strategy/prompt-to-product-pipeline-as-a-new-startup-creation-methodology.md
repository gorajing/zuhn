The article introduces a new product discovery methodology: observing what prompts people repeatedly use with ChatGPT, recognizing that each repeated prompt represents an unmet need, and building a dedicated tool that solves that specific job better than the general-purpose AI. The fashion-assistant example shows this pipeline—someone prompts ChatGPT for personalized style advice, recognizes the pattern has broad appeal, and builds Style DNA as a dedicated product.

This 'prompt-to-product' pipeline is notable because it inverts traditional startup methodology. Instead of starting with a hypothesis and searching for validation, founders start with observed behavior (people already using AI for this task) and build a more polished solution. The prompts serve as natural demand signals—if thousands of people are crafting similar prompts, there's a proven market. The risk is that the general-purpose models keep improving and absorb these vertical use cases, making the dedicated tool redundant. Speed to market and deep workflow integration become essential moats.
