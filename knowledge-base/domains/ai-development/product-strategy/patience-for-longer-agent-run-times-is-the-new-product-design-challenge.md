Ng draws from his experience at Google where he failed to push for 'big box search' (long-prompt, slow-response search) because web search conditioned users to expect sub-second responses. The same challenge faces agentic workflows: humans instinctively want immediate feedback, but agents need time to iterate through reflection, tool use, and planning loops.

His analogy to management is apt: 'novice managers delegate something and check in 5 minutes later -- that's not productive.' The design challenge is creating UX patterns that allow users to delegate tasks, receive progress signals, and intervene when needed, rather than demanding instant completion. This maps directly to Harrison Chase's concept of 'ambient agents' and the 'agent inbox' pattern. For product builders, designing graceful async interaction is as important as the agent's capability.
