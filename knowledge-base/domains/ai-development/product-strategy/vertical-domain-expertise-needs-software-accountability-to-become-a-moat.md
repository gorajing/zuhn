The GitLab evidence suggests a useful correction to the vertical-software thesis. Domain experts can now prototype or even build more of the product themselves, but the moment the tool becomes production infrastructure, the winning capability includes traceability, security, compliance, review, deployment, and maintenance. This does not falsify the belief that domain expertise matters more when code gets cheaper. It lowers confidence in the version that treats software engineering skill as mostly obsolete and raises confidence in hybrid teams or platforms that package engineering accountability for domain experts.
