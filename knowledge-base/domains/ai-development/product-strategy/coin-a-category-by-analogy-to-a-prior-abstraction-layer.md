Recursive frames its ambition as enabling a 'designless' era, deliberately echoing the 'fabless' revolution that TSMC enabled, where Nvidia and Apple could design chips and send them elsewhere for fabrication. By positioning themselves as the platform that abstracts away chip design, they let customers focus on application, modeling, and higher layers while Recursive owns the compute-and-hardware substrate.

The move is a reusable positioning tactic: anchor a novel platform to a well-understood prior abstraction layer the industry already accepts as inevitable. The analogy does two jobs at once—it makes an unfamiliar value proposition instantly legible ('we are the next layer of the same trend you already believe in') and it borrows the prior layer's perceived inevitability, reframing the new platform as the obvious continuation of history rather than a speculative bet. Founders in deep-tech categories that lack existing vocabulary can manufacture a category name and a sense of inevitability by finding the closest historical abstraction and appending the same suffix.
