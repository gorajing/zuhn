The Sora team explicitly frames their release strategy around co-evolving society with technology rather than dropping bombshells. They position Sora 2 as the 'GPT-3.5 moment for video,' a usability threshold that makes the world aware of what's possible and starts building comfort with the technology.

This is strategic positioning for a much more ambitious long-term vision: autonomous copies of users running in virtual environments, performing tasks and reporting back to the physical world. By releasing incremental improvements and letting society develop norms and rules, they aim to avoid the backlash that could come from deploying the full vision without preparation.
