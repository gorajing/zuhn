Casas warns that 'floating windows' is the too-obvious future, the equivalent of early TV being radio shows with cameras pointed at them — we lack the imagination for the genuinely new medium, and we're still in its 'radio era.' His bet is that the future moves beyond components toward human-agent collaboration on a shared artifact.

The Excalidraw MCP app is his proof of direction: it isn't just diagram output, it's a shared canvas where human and agent co-edit — you ask the agent to change something, but you can also click around and modify the UI directly, going back and forth in one space. The takeaway for designers: the unit of interaction shifts from 'agent generates UI, user consumes it' to 'human and agent jointly manipulate a living, personalized artifact.' This is explicitly speculative (he rates his own certainty low), but it reframes generative UI as a collaboration medium rather than an output channel.
