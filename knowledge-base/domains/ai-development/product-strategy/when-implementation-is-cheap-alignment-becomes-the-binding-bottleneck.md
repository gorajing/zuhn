Implementation is rapidly becoming a solved problem: writing code is fast, cheap, and rising in quality. That shifts the binding constraint. The hard question is no longer 'how do we build it?' but 'should we build it?' — and getting a team aligned on what's worth building is the new bottleneck. Alignment was always a bottleneck, but agents have made the cost of being unaligned dramatically higher, because misaligned work now gets produced and shipped far faster.

The sharpest reframe is economic: when production is cheap, opportunity cost becomes the real cost. You can't build everything, so whatever you pick comes at the cost of everything else you didn't. This means the highest-leverage activity moves upstream — to rigorously deciding what deserves the now-abundant implementation capacity. Teams should reallocate the time agents free up toward research, exploration, and deciding what the right thing to build actually is, rather than simply producing more.
