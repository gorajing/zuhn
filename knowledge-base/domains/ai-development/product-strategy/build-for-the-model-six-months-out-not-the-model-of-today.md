Cherny's repeated advice to LLM founders is to never build for the model of today: identify the frontier where the current model is weak but improving, build there, and let the next model release make the product work. Claude Code itself wrote ~10% of his code in February 2024 and was 'not very good at it' — they shipped anyway because the bet was on where the model was going.

The failure mode he warns against is the inverse: spend effort finding PMF for a product that works on today's model, then get leapfrogged when a new model arrives every few months and someone else built for it. The discipline is uncomfortable because you can't find PMF on a product that doesn't yet work — but feeling out the boundary of current capability and aiming just past it is the durable strategy. The corollary for evaluation: weak demos on frontier capabilities are a feature, not a disqualifier, if the capability is on an obvious improvement trajectory.
