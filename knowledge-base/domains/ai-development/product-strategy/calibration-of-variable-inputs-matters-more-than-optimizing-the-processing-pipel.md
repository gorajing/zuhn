Byron Ferguson doesn't just practice his aim — he spine-tests each arrow by measuring its flex characteristics and selecting only arrows within a tight tolerance band. This pre-processing calibration step is what enables his legendary accuracy. The direct parallel to AI and software systems is profound: garbage in, garbage out. Teams obsess over model architecture, prompt engineering, and inference optimization while neglecting input normalization. In ML pipelines, data cleaning and feature normalization routinely contribute more to model performance than architectural changes. In production systems, input validation and sanitization prevent more bugs than sophisticated error handling downstream. The archer's lesson: invest disproportionately in calibrating your inputs.
