When asked about the shape of AI-generated chip placements, the founders note that human experts produce very aligned, regular-looking layouts, while the AI produces curved, organic-looking placements that minimize wire length and improve performance—something that 'was kind of shocking to physical design engineers when they first saw them.' The AI is not imitating human style; it is optimizing the underlying objective and arriving at configurations human aesthetics never would.

This is a recurring tell across domains where AI reaches superhuman performance (AlphaGo's move 37 is the canonical analog): the breakthrough outputs look unnatural, even unsettling, to domain experts precisely because human convention encodes shortcuts, legibility preferences, and historical habit rather than the true objective. For builders, two implications follow. First, resistance to AI output that 'looks wrong' is weak evidence against it—measure the objective, don't trust the expert's aesthetic gut. Second, if an AI system only ever produces human-conventional outputs, it probably hasn't exceeded human performance yet; genuine superhuman capability tends to announce itself as strangeness.
