Most early AI startups are API wrappers relying 100% on OpenAI, Anthropic, or another foundation model provider. This is fine for a prototype but fatal for a company. You don't control pricing (rate increases collapse your margins), performance (latency or downtime breaks your product), or differentiation (the same API is available to everyone).

Jasper is the cautionary tale: once valued at $1.2B as the poster child of AI writing tools, it lost ground rapidly once ChatGPT launched because it had no structural moat beyond a UI wrapper. The barrier to entry for any GPT-powered feature is essentially zero. Winners diversify dependence by routing across multiple providers, training domain-specific models, and owning infrastructure when scale demands it.
