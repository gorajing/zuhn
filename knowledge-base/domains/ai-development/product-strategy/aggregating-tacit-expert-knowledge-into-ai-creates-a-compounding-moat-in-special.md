Open Evidence has reached a remarkable scale: 25% of US practicing physicians as monthly active users, with the average user engaging daily. This scale unlocks a unique opportunity: for every piece of medical knowledge written down in papers and guidelines, there's as much or more that 'only exists distributed across millions of wet brains.' Because of their scale, Open Evidence can now encode, aggregate, and redistribute this tacit collective wisdom.

Their first example augments answers with 'the aggregate clinical wisdom of some of the best GI doctors in the world.' This creates a flywheel: better answers attract more physician use, which generates more tacit knowledge data, which further improves answers. The in-flight chickenpox demo powerfully illustrates the core value: every patient is a long-tail case with unique comorbidities and context, making it impossible for any single doctor to hold all relevant knowledge. The AI acts as 'a shoulder physicians can lean on' at a rate of 10 queries per second across the US.
