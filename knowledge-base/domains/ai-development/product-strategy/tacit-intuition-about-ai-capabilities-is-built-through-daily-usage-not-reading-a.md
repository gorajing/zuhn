Predicting which problems AI can solve well, which it can solve approximately, and which it cannot solve at all requires tacit intuition that only comes from daily hands-on use. Reading about AI, watching tutorials, and tracking model releases does not produce this intuition — it produces an illusion of it. Founders who want to build real businesses on AI should work directly with the models for several hours every day across diverse tasks (writing, idea organization, strategy) and let the failure modes and unexpected successes calibrate their expectations. This intuition compounds into the ability to spot problems worth building products around.
