The Pitch Deck GPT crossed 10K+ conversations not because it accessed a better model, but because it was trained on real pitch decks from YouTube, Facebook, and Uber plus VC frameworks. The author's domain expertise as a VC determined what training data to include and how to structure the step-by-step interaction.

This pattern generalizes: AI product moats come from knowing which knowledge to curate, how to structure the interaction flow, and what domain-specific guardrails to enforce. Anyone can call the OpenAI API, but not everyone knows which pitch deck frameworks actually matter or how VCs evaluate narratives. The curation layer is the product.
