Seo generalizes a pattern from the AI revolution: things seem impossible without scale but become inevitable with it. He applies this directly to Neuralink's roadmap. Even at only ~20 participants, fine-tuning state-of-the-art transformer/LLM architectures on neural data is already producing interesting and counterintuitive results — an early 'neural foundation model.' The bet is that nothing fundamental prevents these architectures from learning the latent manifolds of neural systems; the missing ingredient is simply scale of data.

The deeper framing is that Neuralink's entire origin was a scaling bet that sounded insane when made. In 2016 the core insight was the IO bottleneck between human output and AI capability — a gap that seemed crazy to try to close, but which Seo says 'seems more real and real' with every passing week. The strategic posture is to commit to a capability that is not yet feasible, on the conviction that the scaling curve will arrive, rather than waiting for present-day proof.

The grounding counterweight Seo keeps returning to is the world of atoms: scale is necessary but the physical work to generate and clean the data is unforgiving and slow. The inevitability is real but not automatic — it is earned by doing the on-the-ground work that lets scale accumulate. For AI-native founders, the takeaway is to pick bets whose feasibility is a function of scale you can plausibly reach, then do the unglamorous physical and data work that compounds toward it.
