A survey of 1,750 tech professionals reveals a telling split: product managers want AI for research, founders want it for ideation, and engineers are shifting from coding to documentation, code review, and testing. The common thread is that all roles are moving AI upstream—from execution tasks toward strategic thinking support.

This signals a significant market gap. Most AI tooling today optimizes execution (code generation, content creation, data processing), but the demand is for AI as a thinking partner that enhances decision quality. The compounding effect matters: a 50% improvement in strategic research quality compounds through every downstream decision, while a 50% improvement in coding speed only saves linear time. Startups building AI tools for research synthesis, competitive analysis, and strategic ideation are targeting the higher-leverage layer.
