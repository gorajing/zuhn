Photosynthesis doesn't try to convert photon energy directly into glucose. Instead, it runs excited electrons through a four-stage pipeline (PSII → Cytochrome Complex → PSI → NADPH production), extracting energy at each step to charge different carriers (ATP via proton gradient, NADPH via electron transfer). Each stage is specialized for a different type of energy conversion.

This staged extraction pattern maps directly to AI and data pipeline design. Rather than building a single monolithic model or process that attempts to extract all value from raw input, decomposing into specialized stages — each producing intermediate representations optimized for the next consumer — yields higher total value capture. The biological proof: 450 million years of evolution converged on this multi-stage pattern rather than optimizing a single-step conversion.
