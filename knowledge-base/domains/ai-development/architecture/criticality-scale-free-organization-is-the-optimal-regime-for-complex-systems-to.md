The criticality principle from Robin Carhart-Harris's entropic brain hypothesis, extending to all complex systems: 'Criticality refers to certain properties that you'll see in certain complex systems when they inhabit what we call a critical point. You see it throughout the natural world during phase transitions — sand pile in the midst of an avalanche, water transitioning from liquid to gas. In these moments, systems balance the dynamism of a chaotic system with the robustness of an orderly one.' At criticality, complex systems exhibit scale-free / fractal organization — the same hub-node-connected-to-multiple-disparate-nodes pattern repeats at multiple hierarchical scales. This produces: (a) maximum sensitivity to inputs (any signal can propagate through the network), (b) information flows easily (computations are facile), (c) structural stability (the hub-spoke pattern is robust to local failures). The implications for organizations: an org too rigid (excessive top-down control, no information flow from edges) becomes 'sub-critical' — unresponsive to market signals, depression-shaped. An org too flat/chaotic (no hierarchy, no coordination) becomes 'super-critical' — falls apart under any stress. The sweet spot is hierarchical-but-flexible, where leaders are wise old kings (in the documentary's metaphor) — upholding order while remaining responsive to constituencies. Applies to AI agent design: too-rigid prompt-and-instruction stack = unresponsive. Too-flexible (no priors, no constraints) = falls into random behavior. The sweet spot is hierarchical priors with adjustable precision weights. Applies to learning: ossified knowledge (excessive certainty) blocks updating. Volatile knowledge (no priors) can't predict. The sweet spot is held-confidently-but-updatable priors. Carhart-Harris's entropic brain places different states of consciousness on this spectrum: deep sleep (excessive order) → ordinary waking (slightly sub-critical) → psychedelic states (critical or supra-critical) → seizure / psychosis (chaotic). The same spectrum applies to organizations, markets, and AI systems.
