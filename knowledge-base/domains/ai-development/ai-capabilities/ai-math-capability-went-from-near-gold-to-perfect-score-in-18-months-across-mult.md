Carina Hong's timeline: July 2024 — DeepMind announces AlphaProof, scores one point shy of IMO gold (silver medal). December 2024 — Putnam undergraduate exam, no full attempts. Summer 2025 — multiple labs (US, China, hyperscalers, startups) all hit IMO gold (35/42). December 2025 — Putnam: two organizations (Axia, Numina nonprofit) achieve perfect scores on the hardest undergraduate exam in the world, where the median student score is zero. This 18-month progression went from 'shocking the world with a silver' to 'multiple perfect scores' — a clear phase transition in formal/competition math capability.

Application: when forecasting AI capability arrival in a structured-task domain, expect non-linear transitions rather than smooth progress. Two-year-old projections of 'when AI will reach X math benchmark' have been systematically underestimated. Plan for capability arrival earlier than benchmarks suggest.
