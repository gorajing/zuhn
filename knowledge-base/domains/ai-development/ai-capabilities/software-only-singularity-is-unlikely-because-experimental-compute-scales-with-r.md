Epoch AI argues against the software-only singularity (AI automating AI research to produce rapid recursive improvement) based on a key empirical observation: experimental compute — the compute needed to actually test ideas — is receiving similar or more investment than final training runs. This suggests you can't just throw more AI researchers at the problem without also scaling up the physical compute for experiments.

If research progress depended primarily on researcher time and ideas, you'd expect most spending to go to researchers with minimal experimental compute. Instead, the data shows the opposite. This means even if AI fully automates the cognitive work of research, progress would still be bottlenecked by the physical build-out of compute infrastructure, which scales at hardware-limited speeds rather than software-unlimited speeds. The counterargument — that Anthropic and others believe software-only takeoff is possible — remains a legitimate position with reasonable differences, but the current data leans toward hardware-bottlenecked progress.
