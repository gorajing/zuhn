Playter describes how early walking robots failed by fighting their own physics — constantly trying to prevent tipping. Dynamic stability means going with the fall, catching yourself with the next foot. When the physics of the machine inherently want to do the right motion, the result is more efficient, more stable, and more beautiful.

This is a deep engineering principle that transcends robotics: in any system design (software architecture, organizational design, product design), working with the natural grain of the system rather than overpowering it produces more elegant, more resilient solutions. The gymnast metaphor applies: the best athletes let their body's inertia and momentum do the work.
