The transformer architecture achieves human-level performance on many tasks through a fundamentally simple mechanism: a feed-forward neural network trained to predict the next token. This is analogous to how airplanes fly using fixed wings and fuel rather than flapping like birds.

However, this simplicity also creates architectural limitations. Unlike the human brain's recurrent loops that enable rumination and self-reflection, transformers process information in a single pass with fixed depth. This means they can fail on problems requiring more reasoning steps than their architecture allows. Understanding both the power and limitations of this simple-yet-effective approach is crucial for predicting where AI will advance quickly versus where breakthroughs are still needed.
