Oxman draws a direct parallel: just as large language models compress human knowledge from the internet, 'large molecule models' could compress nature's design intelligence from biological data across all five kingdoms of life. The challenge is that we don't yet have the biological equivalent of the internet's text corpus — quantifying and digitizing nature's 'language' is the prerequisite.

This vision of giving nature access to computational bandwidth ('neuralink for nature') represents a potential paradigm shift: instead of human-designed sustainability interventions, nature itself could make adaptive decisions with computational augmentation. The infrastructure challenge is building the biological data corpus, which could be as transformative as digitizing human text was for AI.
