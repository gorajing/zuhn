The Cursor team traces their founding conviction to the 2020 scaling laws papers from OpenAI, which showed predictable improvement with more compute and data. But it was early access to GPT-4 in late 2022 that converted theoretical belief into action — 'the step up in capabilities felt enormous.' One founder even had a bet that AI would win an IMO gold medal by 2024, which seemed delusional at the time but turned out to be prescient.

The lesson for founders: there's a critical difference between intellectually believing a technology trend and viscerally experiencing it. The scaling laws paper was public knowledge — anyone could have read it. But the founders who got hands-on with each capability jump (Copilot beta, GPT-4 early access) developed conviction others lacked. Timing your startup to the moment theoretical progress becomes tangible is the founder skill that created Cursor.
