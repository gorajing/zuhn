Assembly Theory makes explicit something often missed about evolution: it's not random search. It's a process that accumulates successful sub-assemblies (functional protein folds, regulatory mechanisms, body plan elements) and recombines them in new ways. Each successful assembly becomes a building block for more complex assemblies.

This 'memory' of past successful constructions is what makes evolution so powerful: it doesn't start from scratch each generation but builds on accumulated complexity. The same principle explains why innovation in technology and business is combinatorial rather than from-scratch: successful components are recombined into new configurations.
