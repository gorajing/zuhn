Marcus calls the scaling hypothesis the 'trillion-pound baby fallacy' — just because your baby doubles weight in month one doesn't mean it'll weigh a trillion pounds at college. The AI industry made this exact naive extrapolation: pour more data and GPUs, and intelligence emerges. Early leaps were dramatic — GPT-1 to GPT-2 to GPT-3 each showed obvious improvements within minutes of use. But GPT-5 vs GPT-4 required formal benchmarks to detect improvement.

This is not merely an academic observation — it has trillion-dollar implications. When Sam Altman released GPT-5 on August 7, 2025, the public needed about an hour to realize it still hallucinated and made stupid errors. That single day was the turning point: even AI enthusiasts started admitting Marcus was right. An AAAI presidential survey showed 85% of AI researchers already believed LLMs would not achieve AGI. The gap between marketing narrative and technical reality has never been wider.
