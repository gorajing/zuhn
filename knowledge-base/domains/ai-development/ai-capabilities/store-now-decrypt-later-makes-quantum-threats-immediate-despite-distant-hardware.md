The Store Now Decrypt Later (SNDL) strategy means the quantum computing threat is not a future problem but a current one. Industrial research, pharmaceutical data, government intelligence, and long-lived personal data intercepted today will still be valuable when quantum decryption becomes feasible.

This is why US Congress has mandated all agencies begin transitioning to post-quantum cryptography now, despite functional quantum computers being years away. The required qubit count for breaking RSA has dropped from 1 billion (2012) to 20 million (2019), and IBM's qubit progress appears exponential. Organizations with sensitive long-duration data should treat post-quantum migration as urgent.
