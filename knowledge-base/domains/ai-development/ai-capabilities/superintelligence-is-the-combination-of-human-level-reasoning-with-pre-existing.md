The conventional framing of superintelligence focuses on when AI will surpass human reasoning ability across all cognitive tasks. Smith argues this misses the point: computers were already superhuman at memory, calculation speed, and tireless processing before 2022. LLMs simply filled the 'last mile' gap by adding human-level language understanding, pattern recognition, and conceptual reasoning.

This reframing matters because it shifts the timeline from 'superintelligence is coming someday' to 'superintelligence is already here.' The practical implication is that AI systems today can already do things no human can — reading entire scientific literatures in minutes, being expert-level across all domains simultaneously, and working without fatigue. The superhuman capabilities come not from superior reasoning but from combining adequate reasoning with computational superpowers.
