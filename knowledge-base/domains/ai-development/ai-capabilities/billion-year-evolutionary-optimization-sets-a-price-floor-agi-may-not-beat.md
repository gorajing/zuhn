We've spent hundreds of billions trying to replicate human intelligence because evolution only spent tens of millions of years on it. But evolution has been optimizing calorie-to-meat conversion for a billion years — every system from immune response to growth factors to texture has been under selection pressure. A modern broiler chicken has a 2x feed conversion ratio on grain that is already incredibly cheap, and the industry has stripped away every cost category (welfare, comfort, space) that isn't grain.

This means cultivated meat, plant-based alternatives, and even hypothetical nanotech proteins are trying to beat a target that has had orders of magnitude more optimization pressure than human cognition. Add cultural preferences for real meat plus political barriers (cultivated meat already banned in seven US states and possibly the EU), and the assumption that AGI will automatically disrupt factory farming looks unfounded.

The broader lesson is that evolutionary optimization time is a criminally underrated benchmark for technological disruption. When you're trying to replace a product of billions of years of optimization, the cost floor is much lower than it appears, and throwing more compute at the problem may not be enough.
