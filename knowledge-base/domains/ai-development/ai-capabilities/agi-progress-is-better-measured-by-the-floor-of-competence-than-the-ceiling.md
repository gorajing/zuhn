David Cahn's 5% Rule proposes that AGI progress should be measured not by headline-grabbing benchmark scores but by whether AI can reliably handle the hardest, messiest bottom 5% of tasks in any given field. These are the edge cases, ambiguous situations, and judgment calls that currently require deep human expertise.

This reframing matters because it shifts evaluation from cherry-picked demonstrations to real-world robustness. An AI that handles 95% of coding tasks brilliantly but fails on the critical 5% requiring architectural judgment isn't transformative — it's a better autocomplete. The fields most likely to be transformed first (coding, writing, customer service) are those where the floor of AI competence is rising fastest, not where peak performance is highest.
