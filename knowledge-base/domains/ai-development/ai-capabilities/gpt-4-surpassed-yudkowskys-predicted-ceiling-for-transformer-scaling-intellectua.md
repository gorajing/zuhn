Yudkowsky states plainly: 'GPT-4 has passed where I thought this paradigm was going to take us... whoops, I was incorrect about what happens if you keep on stacking more transformer layers, and that means I don't necessarily know what GPT-5 is going to be able to do.' He frames this through the lens of calibration: 'oops is the sound we make when we improve.' The goal isn't to never be wrong but to be well-calibrated — when you say 90%, it happens 9 times out of 10.

The meta-insight for anyone making predictions about AI: if one of the world's most careful AI thinkers had his ceiling predictions broken, most people's intuitions about AI limits are likely wrong too. The practical takeaway: maintain probability distributions over outcomes rather than point predictions, and actively notice when reality exceeds your expectations. Yudkowsky's intellectual honesty here — publicly saying 'I was wrong' — is itself the key capability that matters more than being right about any specific prediction.
