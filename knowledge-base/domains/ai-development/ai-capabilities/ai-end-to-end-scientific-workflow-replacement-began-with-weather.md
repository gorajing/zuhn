The AI Index 2026 Chapter 5 flags weather forecasting as the first area where AI has fully replaced a scientific workflow rather than augmenting it. Aardvark Weather replaced the traditional numerical weather prediction pipeline — decades of physics-based modeling — with a single ML system, and multiple AI weather models reached operational deployment at institutions like the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather. FourCastNet 3 generates a 60-day global forecast in under 4 minutes, running 8 to 60 times faster than prior approaches. This matters beyond weather: it establishes a pattern where a sufficiently large training dataset (decades of historical weather observations) and a well-defined prediction target (next 60 days of atmospheric state) can produce an ML system that fully substitutes for first-principles physical modeling. Other scientific domains with similar data density and target structure — climate, fluid dynamics, materials simulation, protein folding — are candidates for analogous full-workflow replacement. The threat to scientific software businesses built on 'AI-assists-physics' is that 'AI-replaces-physics' becomes the better product.
