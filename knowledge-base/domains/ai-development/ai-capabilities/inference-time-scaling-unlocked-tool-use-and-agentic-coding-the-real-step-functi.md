Nathan Lambert identifies that the most transformative development of the past year was not a new architecture but the combination of RLVR (reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards) training and inference-time scaling. During extended thinking, models naturally learn to try tools, check results, iterate -- behaviors that emerge from the RL training process. This is what enabled Claude Code, Deep Research, and similar agentic products. The models generate hidden reasoning tokens (seconds to hours) before responding, and within that reasoning they orchestrate tool calls, CLI commands, and Git operations. Lambert notes that a year ago, nobody expected models to do this. The low-hanging fruit in RLVR has been picked, and the next step function is unpredictable, but this paradigm -- training models to reason longer and verify their own outputs -- represents the single biggest capability unlock since ChatGPT.
