While the immediate impact of generative AI is cost reduction (Azra Games cut concepting costs by >50%), the essay argues the transformative value lies in what becomes newly possible: infinite unique in-game items, personalized storylines that never repeat, and AI companions that adapt to individual players over time. This is creative leverage — the ability to produce n-of-1 content at scale.

This distinction matters beyond gaming. When creation cost drops dramatically, innovation increases not linearly but combinatorially. The shift from 'one experience for all players' to 'unique experience per player' mirrors a broader pattern: generative AI doesn't just automate existing workflows, it enables entirely new product categories built on mass personalization. The essay frames this as moving from scripted worlds to open worlds to fully unscripted worlds — each transition enabled by a step-change reduction in content creation cost.
