The original AI project at Dartmouth in the 1950s tried to reduce human intelligence into symbolic logic: if X then Y rules that a computer could execute. This worked for toy problems but failed catastrophically as complexity increased. Even defining 'is this email spam?' generates an infinite regress of exceptions and sub-definitions.

This matters beyond AI history because it reveals a fundamental limit of explicit reasoning. Psychology is in a replication crisis. Social sciences struggle to produce universal laws. Medical diagnosis resists rule-based reduction. The pattern is consistent: domains involving human behavior and complex systems resist the Socratic project of reducing knowledge to explicit definitions.
