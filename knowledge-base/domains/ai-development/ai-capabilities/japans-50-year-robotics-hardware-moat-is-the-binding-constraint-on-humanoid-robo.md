James Riney of Coral Capital identifies the critical distinction between Short-Duration Peak Performance (impressive demos) and industrial deployment requiring 5,000-10,000 hours Mean Time Between Failures. Most software-first robotics companies and low-cost Chinese clones hit a 'Reliability Cliff' around 1,000 hours as gears develop backlash, lubricants break down, and positional accuracy drifts. Companies like Harmonic Drive Systems and Nabtesco have spent 50 years mastering the 'black art' of tribology, metallurgy, and heat treatment.

This has major investment and geopolitical implications. The robotics competition is not US vs China — it's US+Japan vs China. China dominates robot adoption volume due to cheap bank loans and factory scale, but most Chinese robots are low-end mass-market products. Japan holds 70% of global industrial robot market share according to METI. As AI pushes into physical embodiment, Japan's hardware expertise becomes the binding constraint, not software intelligence. This makes Japanese precision manufacturing companies potential beneficiaries of the AI-robotics convergence.
