Assembly Theory quantifies an object's complexity by finding the minimum number of steps required to construct it from basic parts, combined with the number of copies that exist. Objects that are both complex (high assembly index) and abundant (many copies) can only exist through evolutionary-like processes - random chance cannot produce them.

This provides the first rigorous framework for detecting life that doesn't depend on carbon chemistry or DNA. A mass spectrometer on Mars could measure the assembly index of molecules in soil: above a threshold with sufficient copies, the sample was almost certainly produced by living systems. This works regardless of whether the life is anything like Earth biology.
