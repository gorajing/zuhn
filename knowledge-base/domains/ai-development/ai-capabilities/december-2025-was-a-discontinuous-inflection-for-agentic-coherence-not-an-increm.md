Karpathy reports a stark transition in December 2025: with the latest models, code chunks 'just came out fine' and he stopped correcting them, eventually trusting the system enough to vibe code freely. He explicitly warns that anyone whose AI experience is 'ChatGPT-adjacent' from earlier in 2025 needs to look again — things changed fundamentally on the agentic coherent workflow dimension.

The practical consequence: priors set before December 2025 are unreliable. If you tried Claude Code or Codex in mid-2025 and bounced off, you are operating on stale calibration. The inflection point is a real discontinuity, not a smooth curve, and re-evaluation is required for anyone making capability bets, hiring decisions, or product roadmap choices that depend on what agents can actually do today.
