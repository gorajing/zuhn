Hormozi illustrates this with a thought experiment: a superintelligent AI could build a trillion-dollar company from a single prompt — writing code, creating ads, buying media, building landing pages, and transacting autonomously. If that capability exists, the two critical governance questions become: who gets access, and what queries are permitted.

This frames AI governance not as an abstract policy debate but as the most consequential resource allocation question in history. Unlike previous scarce resources (oil, land, capital), superintelligent AI could concentrate unprecedented productive capacity in whoever controls the access layer. This is why AI companies' governance structures, open-source vs. closed-source debates, and regulatory frameworks aren't peripheral concerns — they're the central economic question of the coming decades.
