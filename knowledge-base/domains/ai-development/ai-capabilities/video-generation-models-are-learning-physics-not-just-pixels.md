The AI Index 2026 flags video generation as having crossed a qualitative threshold in 2025: Google DeepMind's Veo 3 was tested across more than 18,000 generated videos and demonstrated abilities like simulating buoyancy and solving mazes without being trained on those tasks. This is significant because it suggests video models are beginning to internalize implicit world-models — the same property that makes robotics foundation models promising. If video gen is learning physics as a byproduct of next-frame prediction, then video datasets become a training substrate for embodied AI, not just a media product. The caveat is that emergent physical understanding is still selective and unverified — 'works on buoyancy' does not mean 'works on friction, momentum, or deformable materials,' and the jagged-frontier pattern applies here too.
