Epoch AI's researchers converge on 5-10% of current jobs being automated away over the next decade, which is roughly comparable to what software already did. The critical variable is not the total magnitude but the speed of transition. A 5% increase in unemployment over 6 months would trigger massive political and social reactions — comparable to COVID-era responses with trillion-dollar stimulus packages in weeks.

The key distinction is between task automation (which happens across many jobs, reducing some tasks within each role) and occupation automation (which eliminates entire job categories). Mainstream economics suggests automation happens at the task level, but some jobs will be 'really hit' by having most of their tasks automated simultaneously. The practical implication is that the social response to AI will be driven by whether people perceive it as gradually shifting job descriptions or suddenly eliminating roles. Revenue trends in AI over the next 1-2 years will provide much clearer signal about which scenario is unfolding.
