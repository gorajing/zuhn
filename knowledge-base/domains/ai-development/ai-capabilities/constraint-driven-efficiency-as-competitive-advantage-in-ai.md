Musk draws a striking comparison: the human brain's thinking functions operate on roughly 10 watts, yet produce better novels than a 10-megawatt GPU cluster — a six-order-of-magnitude efficiency gap. Tesla's autonomous driving system, constrained to approximately 100 watts of onboard compute, was forced to develop extremely efficient approaches to world understanding.

This 'necessity is the mother of invention' dynamic suggests that compute-constrained systems may discover architectural innovations that brute-force approaches miss entirely. The pattern echoes throughout technology history — mobile computing constraints drove innovations that eventually improved desktop computing. As AI matures, the field will likely shift from 'make it work with unlimited compute' to 'make it efficient,' and systems like Tesla's that were efficient from the start may hold fundamental advantages in that transition.
