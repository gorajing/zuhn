Bessemer Venture Partners argues that the next wave of AI value creation will come from vertical integration — AI solutions built deeply into specific industries rather than general-purpose horizontal platforms. This mirrors historical patterns where vertical SaaS eventually captured more total market value than horizontal tools.

The implication is that AI startups should resist the temptation to build broadly applicable tools and instead go deep into a single industry, accumulating proprietary training data, domain-specific fine-tuning, and workflow integration that becomes nearly impossible to replicate. The moat isn't the model — it's the vertical-specific data flywheel and deeply embedded workflows that make switching costs prohibitive.
