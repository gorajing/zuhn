Leyden maps four American inflection points at roughly 80-year intervals. The Founding era (1787-1810) created democratic institutions. The post-Civil War period (1865-1890) built the transcontinental railroad, land-grant universities, and the Homestead Act. The post-WWII era (1945-1970) produced the interstate highway system, GI Bill, Great Society, and the golden age of capitalism with 90% top tax rates.

Each cycle shares the same structure: an old system breaks down, extreme political polarization erupts (America First movements in the 1930s parallel today), and then new technologies enable a dramatically different civilization to be built in roughly 25 years. The current cycle's technologies — AI, clean energy, bioengineering — may enable a shift from financial capitalism to sustainable capitalism and from representative democracy to digital democracy.
