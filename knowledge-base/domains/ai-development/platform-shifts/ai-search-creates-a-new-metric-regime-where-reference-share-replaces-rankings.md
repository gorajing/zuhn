The article identifies an emerging metric framework for AI-era content visibility: featured citations in SGE or AI summaries, brand mentions in AI chatbot references, engagement depth metrics (time on page, scroll depth, return visits), search visibility across multiple channels, and zero-click visibility (impressions without clicks). This represents a fundamental shift from position-based metrics to recognition-based metrics.

The mechanism behind this shift is Google's Knowledge Graph and entity SEO. Brands that establish themselves as recognized 'entities'—through structured data, schema markup, consistent directory listings, and even Wikipedia/Wikidata entries—are more likely to be surfaced in AI-powered search experiences. The competitive advantage moves from 'who ranks highest' to 'who does AI trust to explain things clearly,' which favors brands with demonstrated topical authority and consistent, well-structured content across the web.
