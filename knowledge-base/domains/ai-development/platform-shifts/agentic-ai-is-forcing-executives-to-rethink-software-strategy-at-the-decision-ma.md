The emergence of agentic AI—systems that autonomously plan and execute multi-step tasks—is no longer a purely technical consideration. Executives are now being handed playbooks for how to navigate its impact on business operations and decision-making, signaling that AI has crossed into strategic territory that requires C-suite ownership.

This shift matters because agentic systems can replace or fundamentally alter entire software workflows, not just individual features. Companies that treat agentic AI as an IT project rather than a strategic transformation risk being outmaneuvered by competitors who reorganize around it. The executive playbook framing implies that the competitive window for adaptation is already open.
