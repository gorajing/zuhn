PolyMatter argues that Facebook and Google are not merely competing within the internet but attempting to become the internet itself. Facebook's Instant Articles hosted content directly on Facebook rather than linking to external sites. Google's AMP project similarly cached and served web content through Google's infrastructure. Both reduce the open web to a content source for proprietary platforms.

This pattern accelerates with each platform generation. Mobile apps already bypass the web entirely. AI assistants (which didn't exist when this video was made but validate the thesis) synthesize web content into answers without users ever visiting source sites. The hierarchy of control framework predicts this: platforms at higher layers will always seek to absorb the value created at lower layers. For builders, this means that depending on the open web for distribution becomes riskier over time — strategic survival requires either owning a higher platform layer or building content/services that platforms cannot easily replicate or absorb.
