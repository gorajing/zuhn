Google partnered with Polymarket and Kalshi to display real-time prediction market probabilities directly in Search and Google Finance results. When users search questions about future events — elections, inflation, GDP — they now see market-implied probabilities sourced from active trading.

This is architecturally significant because search has historically indexed backward-looking information: documents, news, facts. Prediction markets inject forward-looking probabilistic knowledge derived from the aggregation of belief and capital. By choosing regulated, liquid platforms as data sources, Google is legitimizing prediction markets as an information primitive on par with news articles or Wikipedia entries. The timing aligns with Polymarket's CFTC re-entry and Kalshi's expansion into macroeconomic contracts, suggesting prediction markets are crossing from niche crypto curiosity to mainstream information infrastructure.
