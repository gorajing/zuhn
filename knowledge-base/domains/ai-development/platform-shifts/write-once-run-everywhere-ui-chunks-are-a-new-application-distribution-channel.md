The closing argument reframes the whole talk: 'this isn't some protocol, this is a new way to distribute applications.' A single MCP App codebase runs on every host — 'write your app once and it will run everywhere.' They size the prize against history: the internet took ~13 years to reach the 800M weekly users ChatGPT hit (now ~1B), and the potential audience is claimed at ~160x the iPhone install base when the App Store launched. Decades-old branding and user journeys port in without a per-host rewrite.

The inspiration here is to watch for the moment a rendering/integration standard quietly becomes a distribution standard — that is the App Store-style inflection where the platform, not the individual app, captures the network effect. The time-sensitive bet the speakers stake is that 2026 is the year MCP Apps standardizes globally; whether the 'write once, run everywhere' promise holds across competing hosts (and against rival protocols like Google's A2UI and WebMCP) is the open question that will determine if this becomes the distribution layer or fragments.
