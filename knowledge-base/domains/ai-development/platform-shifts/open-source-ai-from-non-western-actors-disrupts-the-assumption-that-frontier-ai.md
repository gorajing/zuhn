The prevailing narrative in Western tech has been that frontier AI development requires the kind of capital and talent concentration only available to companies like OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic. DeepSeek R1—developed as a side project by a Chinese hedge fund whose stated goal is 'long-term social value'—directly contradicts this narrative.

This has profound implications beyond the stock market. If frontier-quality models can be produced cheaply and released as open source, the competitive moat for proprietary AI companies narrows dramatically. Marc Andreessen calling it 'one of the most amazing and impressive breakthroughs' and 'a profound gift to the world' signals that even Silicon Valley insiders recognize this as a structural shift, not an anomaly. The comparison to Sputnik is apt: the shock isn't that someone else did it, but that they did it under constraints that were supposed to make it impossible.
