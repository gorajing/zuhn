YC's six target areas — agent infrastructure, AI-native enterprise workflows, workforce retraining, video generation, lean mega-companies, and public-sector consulting automation — share a common thread: they all assume capable AI models already exist and focus on turning that capability into durable products and revenue.

This is a phase transition signal. The experimental period where novel AI capabilities alone attracted funding is closing. The new edge belongs to teams that can operationalize AI into specific verticals with clear distribution and revenue models. For founders, this means the question shifts from 'what can AI do?' to 'where can AI reliably deliver value that customers will pay for repeatedly?' The inclusion of public-sector consulting and workforce retraining suggests YC sees massive TAM in applying AI to traditionally slow-moving, labor-intensive sectors.
