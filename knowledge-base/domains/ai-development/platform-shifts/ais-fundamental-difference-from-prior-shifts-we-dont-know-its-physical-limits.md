Evans identifies what genuinely distinguishes AI from every previous platform shift: with the internet, you knew telcos wouldn't deploy gigabit fiber next year; with iPhones, you knew battery life wouldn't last a year. You could build spreadsheets modeling bandwidth consumption and make bounded predictions. With AI, we can't.

We don't have a good theoretical understanding of why large language models work so well, nor do we have a good theoretical understanding of human intelligence. This makes forecasting 'vibes-based' — Sam Altman says we have PhD-level researchers, Demis Hassabis says we don't. Nobody knows. This uncertainty is why the bubble analogy is apt (Andreessen: '97 was not a bubble, '98 was not, '99 was') but not predictive. The pragmatic response is to invest based on current demonstrated value while staying aware that the capability curve could flatten or steepen dramatically.
