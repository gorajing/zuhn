The article proposes a 5-level AI deployment spectrum from AI-enhanced (using AI for internal efficiency) to AI-first (building entirely new markets). Level 4 — 'AI is the product' — is identified as the most dangerous position because it's simultaneously powerful and fragile. Companies here (AI copywriting tools, chatbots, video editors) are easy to copy and often create workflows that take more time to learn than they save.

The critical distinction is between selling a workflow (fragile) and selling a complete outcome (defensible). EvenUp doesn't sell 'AI-assisted demand letter writing' — it sells the instant, complete generation of the demand letter. This outcome-oriented framing makes the product harder to replicate because competitors must match the entire end-to-end quality, not just the AI capability. Founders should ask: 'Am I selling access to AI, or am I selling a solved problem?'
