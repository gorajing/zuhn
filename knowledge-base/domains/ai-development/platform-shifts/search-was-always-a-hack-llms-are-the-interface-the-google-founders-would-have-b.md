Andreessen argues that Google Search's 10 Blue Links format was never the ideal — it was the best available hack given 1990s technology. Google has been trying to move beyond it for 15 years (Knowledge Graph, one-box answers). The counterfactual is clear: if Brin and Page had LLMs, they would have built an answer engine from day one.

This framing dissolves the 'will AI disrupt search?' debate. The answer is that search was always a waypoint toward the real goal: direct knowledge access. This pattern — where a technology's successor was always the actual intended destination — recurs throughout tech history. It means incumbents who recognize this (as Google has) are better positioned than they appear, while startups need to leapfrog the successor, not just replace the current paradigm.
