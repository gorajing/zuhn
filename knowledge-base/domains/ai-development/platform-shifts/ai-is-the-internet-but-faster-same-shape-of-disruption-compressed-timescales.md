Multiple AI adoption phenomena map cleanly onto early-internet phenomena, but compressed by approximately 4x. Web infrastructure shifted from indexing-dominant (training-analog) to serving-dominant (inference-analog) over 15-20 years; AI compute is doing the same shift in 4 years. Web business models shifted from 'put existing newspapers online' (proof of concept phase) to 'invent native social/feed/marketplace formats' (transformation phase) over 10-15 years; AI products are still in the 'put existing forms into AI' efficiency phase and have not yet found their native agentic-app format. Web went from professional-developer-only to mass creator-economy over 20 years (HTML hand-coding → Squarespace/Wix → social media); AI is repeating this pattern (Python ML notebooks → frameworks → vibe coding) at compressed pace. The strategic value of the analogy is to know what to expect next: the AI equivalent of the Snapchat-style native-format application has not yet emerged but the analogy predicts it will, and identifying it early is the alpha. Founders building 'AI versions of existing products' (the 2026-equivalent of putting newspapers online) are in the efficiency phase and will be displaced by founders who build the AI-native equivalent of the scrollable feed.
