The author draws an architectural analogy: ethics is the rebar, not the paint. You cannot pour the concrete of AI capability first and then thread the rebar through later. OpenAI's addition of a 'no U.S. civilian surveillance' clause after the boycott is exactly the kind of after-the-fact patch that fails -- notably, it says nothing about surveillance of non-U.S. citizens.

The broader pattern mirrors social media's trajectory: technology that can wield power will inevitably face attempts to use it for surveillance and control. The difference is that AI's capabilities for monitoring, analyzing, and profiling vastly exceed social media's. The most convenient AI service can become the most perfect surveillance system the moment civic vigilance relaxes. This makes ongoing public scrutiny -- not one-time boycotts -- the only durable defense.
