Zuckerberg identifies a key insight about VR adoption: many experiences don't need to be fully immersive, and forcing full immersion creates anxiety (hitting lamps, bumping into furniture) that limits usage. Mixed reality solves this by letting users see their physical environment while interacting with digital overlays.

The strategic implication is significant: VR headsets are advancing at a rate where each generation delivers a major new capability (unlike smartphones which barely change year to year). Quest 3 ships mixed reality at $500 — a third the cost of Quest Pro — with a better chipset. This pattern of capability leaps with price drops suggests the adoption curve for spatial computing will be steeper than many expect. The technology enables fundamentally new use cases: remote colleagues appearing as photorealistic holograms in physical meeting rooms.
