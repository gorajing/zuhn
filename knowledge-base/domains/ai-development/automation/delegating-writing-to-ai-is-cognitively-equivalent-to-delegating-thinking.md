The productivity framing of AI writing tools treats writing as a separable output step: you think the thoughts, AI renders them into prose. Graham's essay challenges this framing directly. Writing is not the downstream product of thinking — it is thinking itself. The struggle to express an idea precisely IS the process of understanding it.

This means AI-written memos, emails, and documents may pass review while representing no actual thinking by the author. Organizations that normalize AI writing may find themselves with polished outputs and hollow reasoning underneath — confident-sounding artifacts produced by people who never worked through the problem. The quality of thinking degrades invisibly.
