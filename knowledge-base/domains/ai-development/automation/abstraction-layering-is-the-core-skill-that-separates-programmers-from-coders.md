Malan's course progression reveals a deliberate pattern: each module introduces a new abstraction layer (functions → libraries → classes → decorators) that encapsulates previous complexity. The key insight is that the skill being trained isn't Python — it's the meta-skill of recognizing when to create a new abstraction boundary.

This has direct implications for AI-augmented development. Vibe coders who can't think in abstraction layers will produce brittle, unmaintainable systems regardless of how good their AI tools are. The programmers who thrive in the AI era will be those who can architect abstraction hierarchies and delegate implementation to AI — the same skill CS50 trains through progressive complexity layering.
