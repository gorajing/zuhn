Frenkel draws a crucial distinction: physical theories evolve (Newton superseded by Einstein), but mathematical truths are permanent. x^2 + y^2 = z^2 means the same thing to anyone in the world regardless of culture, religion, or era, and will still be true in a thousand years. This permanence comes from proof by logical deduction rather than empirical observation.

For AI development, this distinction matters when evaluating LLM reasoning capabilities. AI systems trained on empirical data can approximate but never guarantee mathematical certainty. The fact that AI can pass math competitions doesn't mean it 'understands' in the way that produces permanent truth — it may be pattern-matching on the surface form. Frenkel says mathematics is 'an antidote to divisiveness' precisely because its truths are universal and unchanging. For AI alignment, mathematical reasoning may be the one domain where we can truly verify AI outputs — because the answers are provably correct or incorrect.
