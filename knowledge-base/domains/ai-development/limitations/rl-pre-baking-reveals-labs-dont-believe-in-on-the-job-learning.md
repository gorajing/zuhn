Short-timeline AGI bulls and the existence of a multi-billion-dollar RL environment supply chain are in tension. If models were close to humanlike learners, you wouldn't need to pay PhDs and consultants to write training examples for crafting PowerPoint slides — the model would pick that up on the job like any new hire.

The labs' actions describe their actual worldview better than their marketing does. They are behaving as if generalization and continual learning are far away, which is why they need to bake in every economically-useful skill in advance. This is a useful frame for evaluating any AGI-imminent claim: ask what the speaker's capital allocation would look like if they truly believed it.

Apply this lens whenever you hear bullish capability claims from a lab — check whether their training pipeline and hiring patterns match the claim or contradict it.
