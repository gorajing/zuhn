The stochastic parrot argument claims AIs can't have any real understanding because they exhibit 'hallucinations' — a term that implies bizarre, incomprehensible failure. Alexander argues this framing is doing heavy rhetorical lifting. Rename the behavior 'shameless guessing' and it becomes immediately recognizable as something humans do constantly.

The terminological shift matters because it changes the inference people draw. 'Hallucination' suggests a fundamentally alien cognitive process incompatible with understanding. 'Guessing' suggests a goal-directed agent making rational choices under uncertainty — exactly what we'd expect from something that does have an internal model of the world. The word choice isn't just semantic; it shapes whether people take AI capabilities (and risks) seriously.
