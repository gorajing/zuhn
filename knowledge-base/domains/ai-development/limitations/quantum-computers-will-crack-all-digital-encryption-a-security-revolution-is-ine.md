Kaku highlights the security implications of quantum computing: current encryption relies on the computational infeasibility of factoring very large numbers (50+ digits). Digital computers might need hundreds of years to crack such codes. Quantum computers, in principle, could do it almost instantly.

While this capability doesn't exist yet at practical scale, every national intelligence agency is following the development closely. The implication is that a post-quantum encryption standard must be developed and deployed before quantum computers reach practical code-breaking capability. Organizations that wait until quantum computers arrive to address this will find their entire historical data archive potentially compromised.
