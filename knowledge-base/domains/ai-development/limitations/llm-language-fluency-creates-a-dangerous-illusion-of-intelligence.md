Yann LeCun frames current LLMs as 'encyclopedias that can talk' -- impressive in language manipulation but fundamentally lacking the ability to be inventive or approach novel situations creatively. What LLMs actually do is produce the most plausible answer based on massive collections of solved problems. Within the boundaries of their training data, this appears intelligent. But as soon as problems move outside those boundaries, the illusion breaks.

This distinction between simulated reasoning (pattern-matching plausible outputs) and real reasoning (building mental models to solve novel problems) is critical for anyone building on AI. LeCun identifies four capabilities current AI systems lack: understanding the physical world, persistent memory, genuine reasoning, and planning complex action sequences. The 'fourth wave' of AI will need to create systems with internal models of the world or logical processes, moving beyond statistical plausibility to something closer to how humans actually solve problems.
