During cosmic inflation, random quantum fluctuations — the smallest possible variations in energy density — were stretched to galactic scales in fractions of a second. These became the seeds of all large-scale cosmic structure. Dense regions collapsed under gravity into galaxy clusters; sparse regions became voids. The structure is now permanent.

This is a powerful example of how initial conditions at microscopic scales, when amplified by rapid expansion, lock in macro-level architecture that constrains everything downstream. In AI development, early architectural decisions (attention mechanisms, tokenization schemes, training data composition) made when systems were small get amplified as models scale by orders of magnitude. The resulting structure — what the model can and cannot represent — becomes effectively permanent. Similarly in organizations: decisions made during a startup's 'inflationary' growth phase (first hires, initial culture, technical architecture) get stretched into permanent institutional structure that constrains all future possibilities.
