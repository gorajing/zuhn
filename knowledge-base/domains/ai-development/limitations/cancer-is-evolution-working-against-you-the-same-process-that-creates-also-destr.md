Carroll frames cancer as truly an evolutionary disease — cancer cells mutate and those mutations that give cells a survival or replication advantage are selected for, exactly like the staircase of evolution. This means the same mechanism that built all the complexity of life can also destroy it from within.

This is a profound insight for anyone building complex adaptive systems. The same feedback loops that make a system powerful and self-improving can also produce emergent behaviors that work against the system's goals. Evolution is amoral — it optimizes whatever gets selected for, whether that serves the host organism or not. Any system with variation, selection, and replication can evolve toward outcomes its designers never intended.
