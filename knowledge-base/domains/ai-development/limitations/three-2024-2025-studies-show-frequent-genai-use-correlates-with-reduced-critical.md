Appleton cites three independent studies converging on the same finding. Lee 2025 (Microsoft Research, 319 knowledge workers) found 'higher confidence in GenAI is associated with less critical thinking, while higher self-confidence is associated with more critical thinking.' Ye 2024 (UW Social Futures Lab) asked philosophers to use models as critical thinking tools and found they were 'too neutral, incurious, and passive to be helpful.' Gerlich 2025 interviewed 666 participants across educational and age groups and found 'a significant negative correlation between frequent AI tool usage and critical thinking abilities, mediated by increased cognitive offloading. Younger participants exhibited higher dependence on AI tools and lower critical thinking scores compared to older participants.' Three independent methodologies, same directional finding, with Gerlich adding the age gradient. This is real empirical signal against the 'every new medium triggers the same panic and humanity adapts' framing — at least for the current generation of tools. Marks as time-sensitive because the studies test current chatbot designs, not hypothetical future critical-thinking interfaces.
