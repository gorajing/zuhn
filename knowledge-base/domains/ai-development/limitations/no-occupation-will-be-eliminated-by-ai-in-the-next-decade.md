The 5% automation / 1% GDP prediction stands in stark contrast to Silicon Valley's AGI-around-the-corner narrative. Acemoglu's reasoning: only ~20% of the economy is even in the crosshairs of AI automation, and within that, many tasks are in small companies where automation isn't profitable, or require the kind of judgment and social intelligence AI lacks.

The key challenge is the 'hard to learn' tasks within every occupation — the tacit knowledge, checking, and multi-type intelligence that resist automation. Even impressive leaps in AI capability don't resolve the deployment gap: getting AI tools reliably integrated into production processes with zero human supervision remains a much higher bar than benchmark performance. This is a useful counterweight for anyone making hiring or career decisions based on AI displacement fears.
