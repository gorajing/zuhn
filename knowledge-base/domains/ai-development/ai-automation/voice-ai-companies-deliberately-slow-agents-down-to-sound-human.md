An interesting signal from the voice AI space: accuracy and latency have improved so much that some companies are deliberately degrading their agents' performance — adding pauses and background noise — to make them sound more human. This reveals a gap between technical capability and user acceptance.

This is a classic uncanny valley dynamic applied to audio. It suggests that the adoption bottleneck for voice AI is not technical quality but psychological comfort. Builders should consider that 'too good' can be a product problem, and that the optimal AI experience may involve intentional imperfection to maintain trust.
