If you automate something you don't understand, you create uncertainty,
not efficiency. Max (founder of Gumloop, processing 4M workflows/day)
draws a sharp line: AI should speed up what you already know how to do,
not skip the understanding phase entirely. "Vibe coding" — writing code
via AI prompts without understanding the output — produces the software
equivalent of malware. The same principle applies to any automation:
without a mental model of the underlying process, you cannot evaluate
whether the automation is working correctly. You are pulling a slot
machine lever and hoping for the best. Apply AI to accelerate known
workflows, not to bypass learning them.
