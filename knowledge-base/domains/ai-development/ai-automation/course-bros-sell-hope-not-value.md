Every hype cycle (crypto, NFTs, AI) creates a vulnerable audience of
"wantrepreneurs" who buy the dream of skipping hard work. The pattern
is consistent: someone promises a shortcut ("50 AI agents run my
company"), packages it as a course or community, and sells hope to
people who want to believe the hard parts can be automated away. The
tell is simple: if the magic solution actually worked, they would be
using it to generate value, not selling it on Twitter. The people
generating real value with AI are applying it to things they deeply
understand — they are not buying courses about it. Critical thinking
is the filter: when someone's business model is selling the tool
rather than using the tool, the tool probably does not work as
advertised.
