AI is creating a bifurcation in engineering talent, not a uniform
leveling up. People who pause to understand fundamentals and use AI
as a teaching accelerator become exceptional faster than any previous
generation. People who skip understanding and rely on AI to generate
code they cannot evaluate fall to producing "slop" — low-quality
output they cannot debug, improve, or verify. The split widens over
time, not narrows. The critical variable is not access to AI tools
(everyone has that) but the discipline to build understanding before
automating. This suggests the last generation of truly great engineers
may already be born — the ones who learned fundamentals before AI
made it easy to skip them.
