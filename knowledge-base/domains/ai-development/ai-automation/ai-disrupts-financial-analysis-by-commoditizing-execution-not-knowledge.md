The article reveals an important pattern in AI disruption: financial valuation models, risk methodologies, and screening criteria all live in textbooks, MBA curricula, and CFA study guides. The intellectual property was never the moat. The value was having someone who could consistently execute these analyses week after week.

This suggests a broader principle for identifying AI-vulnerable roles: any job where the methodology is well-documented but the value comes from consistent, rigorous execution is prime for LLM automation. The analyst's edge wasn't knowing how to do a DCF — it was actually doing it thoroughly every time. LLMs excel at exactly this kind of structured, repeatable analytical work.
