Anthropic's Claude Sonnet 4.6 demonstrated the ability to complete complex workflows — logging into systems, navigating multi-page flows, fixing errors autonomously — with up to 94% accuracy at roughly $0.30 per task. This represents a 100x cost reduction compared to the $30/hour contractors who currently perform equivalent work on reports, monitoring, and email management.

The implications extend beyond simple cost savings. At sub-dollar pricing, businesses can run these workflows continuously and at scale rather than batching them into human work sessions. The economic gap is so large that even accounting for error rates and oversight costs, the ROI is immediate. This will compress the timeline for widespread adoption because the decision is not whether AI is better — it is whether paying 100x more for a human to do the same task is justifiable to any rational business operator.
