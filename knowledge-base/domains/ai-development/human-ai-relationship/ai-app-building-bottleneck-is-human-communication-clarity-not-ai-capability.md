Most people blame the AI tool when their generated app is generic or unusable, but the actual constraint is the quality of human direction. Vague prompts like 'build me a social media app' produce vague results — not because the AI lacks capability, but because it lacks constraints to optimize against.

This inverts the traditional software bottleneck. In conventional development, the constraint is engineering capacity — you know what you want but can't build it fast enough. With AI app builders, the constraint shifts entirely to specification quality. The 'top 1%' of AI-assisted builders aren't better at using the tool; they're better at thinking clearly about what they want before they touch the tool. Communication and problem definition skills become more valuable than technical execution skills.
