Doug Finkbeiner: the major risks people cite for AI in science (hallucinations, brittleness, surprising failure modes) are real but secondary. The bigger concern is skill loss — researchers gradually atrophying their literal and metaphorical muscle for doing things themselves. The analogy: most people drive places that previous generations would have walked, and the population is less fit. AI risks doing this for thinking. Skill atrophy is hard to monitor because each individual researcher's productivity might temporarily look fine, but the field's underlying capability degrades. Particularly concerning for graduate students who never develop the skill in the first place.

Application: training curricula should explicitly preserve un-AI-assisted skill development at critical stages. Some forms of intellectual muscle are like physical muscle — you don't realize you've lost them until you need them. Universities should be especially vigilant about graduate-student core skill development before allowing AI assistance to dominate the workflow.
