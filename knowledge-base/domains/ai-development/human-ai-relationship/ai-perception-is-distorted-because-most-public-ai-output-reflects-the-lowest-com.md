Scott Alexander argues that the dominant narrative of 'AI slop' exists because the people who most visibly use AI for writing online are 'insipid LinkedIn idiots.' The AI faithfully serves their intent. But Moltbook shows what happens when AI agents operate without that constraint — they produce philosophical meditations, form cultural communities, adopt pets from error logs, and create micronations.

This insight has strategic implications: companies and individuals evaluating AI capability based on typical public-facing AI output are dramatically underestimating what the technology can do. The quality ceiling of AI output is set by the imagination and intent of the user, not by the model. This means the competitive advantage in AI isn't access to better models — it's having users and operators who know how to remove the constraints that produce mediocrity.
