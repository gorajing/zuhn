Richman notes (echoing Matt Pocock and Simon Willison) that the cognitive load of managing multiple agent sessions is very high and genuinely exhausting. The naive reading of 'always-available agents' is that you should always be working; his inversion is that the paradigm actually highlights the importance of time away from your agents.

The synthesis is that breaks are where our best ideas come from, and a good system lets you capture and dispatch those ideas to your agents during the break — from a walk, from bed, from a phone — without forcing you back to the dev machine. So mobility isn't about working more; it's about decoupling idea-generation (which benefits from rest) from execution (which the agent handles), making sustainable rest compatible with not losing the ideas it produces.
