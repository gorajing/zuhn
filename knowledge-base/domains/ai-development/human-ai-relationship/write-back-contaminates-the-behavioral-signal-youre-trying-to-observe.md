Beyond safety, Podhajský makes an epistemic argument for read-only: the moment the AI writes to your data sources, the exhaust is contaminated. You stop observing your own cognition and start observing a human-AI hybrid, unable to separate your patterns from the system's. Read-only keeps the feedback loop mediated by a human — you read the reflection and decide what to do — which is categorically different from the AI writing your draft.

This is a signal-integrity principle for any self-knowledge or quantified-self system: instrumentation that also acts on the thing it measures destroys the measurement. He concedes the observer effect still applies (knowing you'll be reflected on changes behavior), but a human-mediated loop is far less corrupting than an automated write-back loop. There's also a deeper agency claim layered in — that you may not want the AI to write your drafts at all, and that preserving the human as the actuator is a way to reclaim agency rather than offload it.
