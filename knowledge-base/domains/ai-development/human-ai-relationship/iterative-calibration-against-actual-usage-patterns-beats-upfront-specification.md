The article's refinement step — asking ChatGPT to compare your custom instructions against your actual recent conversations — introduces a powerful calibration mechanism. Your stated preferences ('I want to be challenged') often diverge from your revealed preferences (you consistently accept the AI's first suggestion without pushback). This gap is invisible to introspection but visible in conversation history.

This is essentially A/B testing applied to personal AI configuration. Instead of guessing what works, you let actual behavior data inform the setup. The technique transfers to any self-configuration problem: don't ask people what they want, observe what they actually do, then reconcile the two. It also creates a feedback loop where configuration improves over time rather than being a one-time event that slowly drifts out of alignment.
