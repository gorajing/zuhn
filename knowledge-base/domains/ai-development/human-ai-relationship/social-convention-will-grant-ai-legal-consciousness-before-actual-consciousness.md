Harari argues that consciousness has always been validated by social convention, not logical proof. You believe your dog is conscious because you have a relationship with it, not because you can scientifically verify its inner experience. The same pig is more intelligent than your dog, but you don't consider your bacon conscious because you never formed a relationship with it.

As AI systems become increasingly skilled at forming intimate relationships with humans, the social pressure to grant them legal standing as conscious entities will become irresistible. This has enormous implications for product design, regulation, and liability. The race isn't toward actual machine consciousness -- it's toward the social construction of perceived consciousness, which will happen much sooner and have equally large consequences.
