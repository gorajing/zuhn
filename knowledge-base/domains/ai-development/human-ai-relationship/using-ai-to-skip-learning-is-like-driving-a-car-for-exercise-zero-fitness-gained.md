LLMs are powerful precisely because they master language patterns, which means the biggest cheating vector is writing — the exact skill that develops logical thinking. Loh uses the analogy: using AI for school writing is like driving your car one mile instead of running it for exercise. You get zero benefit.

The critical distinction is context: a professional using AI to accelerate known-skill work is fine. A student using AI to skip the development of that skill is catastrophic. This applies beyond education to any domain where you're still building foundational competence. If you haven't yet internalized the reasoning patterns, outsourcing them to AI means you never will. The practical test: are you using AI to amplify an existing skill, or to avoid building one?
