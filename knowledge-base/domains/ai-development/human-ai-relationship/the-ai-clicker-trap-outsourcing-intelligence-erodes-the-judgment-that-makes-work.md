Choi coins 'AI clicker' (AI 딸깍발이) to describe people who click through AI for everything without cultivating underlying skills — not reading books, not writing papers themselves, not studying design tools to develop aesthetic sensibility. He connects this to the broader intelligence outsourcing trend: consumers outsource purchasing decisions to reviews, then to algorithms, and now to AI.

The trap: as AI handles more execution, the differentiating human value is judgment, taste, and meaning-making. But the AI clicker is simultaneously eroding those very capacities by never doing the hard work that builds them. The flyer example (AI-generated chef persona advertising a food delivery service — with four fingers) shows the laziness becoming detectable. He argues this era increasingly needs sincerity precisely because AI makes fakery so easy. The antidote: strengthen analog observation skills, do anthropological fieldwork, and invest in the painful processes that build genuine capability.
