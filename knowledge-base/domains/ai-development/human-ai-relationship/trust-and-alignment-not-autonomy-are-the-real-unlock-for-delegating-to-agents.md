Korevec describes three escalating levels of proactivity: level one fixes things opportunistically (missing tests, unused deps, unsafe patterns) while doing assigned work; level two becomes contextually aware of the whole project and your stack; level three understands not just context but consequence — how choices affect users, performance, and outcomes — with multiple agents (Jules for code, Stitch for design, Insights for analytics) coordinating across boundaries.

Crucially, she argues level three 'isn't about autonomy anymore — it's about alignment.' The human stays firmly in the loop: observing what agents do, refining when intervention is needed, and redirecting when an agent is misdirected. Trust is the named unlock — you can only let a system fill gaps and keep progress moving if you trust it understands your goals. This reframes the maturation of agents away from 'more autonomy' and toward tighter human-agent alignment across the full project lifecycle.
