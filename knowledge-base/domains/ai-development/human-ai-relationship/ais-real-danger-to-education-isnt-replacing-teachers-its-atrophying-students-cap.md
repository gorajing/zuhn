Po-Shen Loh draws a precise analogy: using AI to do writing homework is like driving a car for a mile instead of running it — you get zero exercise. The danger isn't that AI writes better essays; it's that writing is how students develop logical reasoning, argumentation, and the ability to construct coherent thought sequences.

The mechanism is specifically tied to large language models: LLMs are powerful because they master language patterns, and language is the substrate of logical thought. If a generation grows up outsourcing language production to AI, they lose the mental scaffolding that enables independent reasoning. The result isn't just worse writers — it's people who 'just take whatever anyone else gives them' and become dependent on authority figures or AI systems they cannot evaluate.

This has a second-order consequence for democracy and manipulation resistance: people who cannot construct their own arguments also cannot deconstruct others' arguments. They become vulnerable to sophisticated propaganda, especially AI-generated propaganda that 'sounds like a very convincing, reasonable person.'
