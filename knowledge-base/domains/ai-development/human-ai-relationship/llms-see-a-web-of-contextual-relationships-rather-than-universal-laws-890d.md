Traditional science and computing try to reduce the world into clean universal laws: if X is true, Y will happen. LLMs instead process a dense web of causal relationships between different parts of the world that all come together in unique, context-specific ways to produce what comes next.

This is a genuinely different way of modeling reality. It is closer to how expert practitioners actually make decisions in complex domains: not by consulting a rulebook, but by pattern-matching against thousands of hours of experience. Shipper argues this should change how we think about knowledge itself, not just how we build software.
