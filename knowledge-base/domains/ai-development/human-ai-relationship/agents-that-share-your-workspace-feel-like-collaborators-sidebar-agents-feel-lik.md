Ruiz describes the sidebar agentic loop — produce, review, iterate until done — as technically successful but emotionally wrong: 'it felt like I was handing my keyboard to some other AI rather than someone collaborating with me.' The fix was spatial: bring the agent out of the sidebar and onto the canvas itself as little characters ('fairies') whose state you can see. You can watch where in the project each agent is acting relative to the others, see it thinking, see its actions, and the agents can see each other's work.

The insight is that perceived collaboration is an interface property, not a model-capability property. The same underlying agent loop reads as takeover when hidden in a sidebar and as partnership when its state is co-located with your work and continuously observable. For anyone designing agent UX, the lever is observability-in-context: surface where the agent is operating, what it's doing, and how it relates to your artifacts and to other agents, rather than abstracting all of that behind a chat panel.
