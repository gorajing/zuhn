The traditional education model trained people to perform tasks: solve problems, write essays, produce outputs. AI now handles execution at superhuman levels. The new bottleneck is evaluation — can you assess whether AI output is correct, complete, and aligned with the actual goal?

Loh frames this as the fundamental pedagogical shift: everyone needs to learn how to grade homework. This means developing taste, judgment, and the ability to detect subtle errors or misalignments. It requires deeper understanding than execution does, because you need to know not just how to do something but why a particular approach is right or wrong. For knowledge workers, this translates to: your value increasingly lies in your ability to evaluate, curate, and direct AI outputs rather than produce them yourself.
