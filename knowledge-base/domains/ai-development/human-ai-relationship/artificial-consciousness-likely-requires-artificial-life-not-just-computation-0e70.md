Seth challenges the dominant metaphor of brain-as-computer. While it is a powerful map for exploring some brain functions, every era has used its dominant technology as a metaphor for the brain — and every time, assuming the metaphor IS the thing has caused scientists to stop looking for what else might be there.

The difference between conscious systems and non-conscious systems, he argues, may go down to the level of cells that regenerate their own components, that transfer energy into matter and back again. We project consciousness onto things that seem human-like, but the dimensions that actually matter for consciousness may have nothing to do with apparent intelligence. This suggests that no one should be actively trying to create a conscious AI — and that doing so would likely require creating something closer to artificial life than artificial intelligence.
