A hidden tax on understanding a large codebase is the friction of asking humans: you wait for a colleague in another timezone, you ration questions to avoid seeming incompetent, and the answer arrives the next day. Priscila reframes AI as the teammate who never gets tired of your questions and is available instantly, which removes both the latency and the social cost — 'there is no dumb question, it's the cheapest senior engineer out there.' The behavioral consequence is that you ask more, and asking more is exactly what builds the comprehension that prevents shipping code you do not understand. Her framing aligns mental model before prompting: understand first, then the code flows naturally, rather than shipping 'slop' you can't reason about.
