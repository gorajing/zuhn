Where voice is the product (luxury hotel, high-end real estate, a wedding venue), a single wrong sentence can cost more than a refund, and the users are exactly the kind of people who notice. The danger is that the voice layer wants to be warm — which for an AI means first person — but warmth plus a constraint the AI ignores produces a lie: 'I can't wait to show you around' from software with no body. The moment the user realizes they've been performing a relationship with someone who was never there, trust doesn't dip, it inverts. A warm confident voice offering something that isn't real is worse than a cold one, because the user now believes they have the thing — you've handed them a disappointment with a 48-hour delay on it. This is why the venue's AI discloses it is AI in its very first response, before being asked: a product bet that a couple who knows from the start trusts more than one who finds out on turn seven. Users aren't testing your brand voice, they're trusting it — and treating that trust as a one-time prompt-engineering problem loses the very thing you were protecting.
