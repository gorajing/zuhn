Tegmark highlights a disturbing trend: people using ChatGPT to rewrite angry messages in nicer language, or Microsoft demoing a parent using GPT-4 to write a graduation speech for their child. While there are efficiency gains, this outsources the very friction that makes communication human.

The deeper concern is that if AI mediates all our emotional expression, we lose the incentive for self-improvement. The person who uses AI to filter their anger never learns to manage their temper. The parent who outsources a speech never does the emotional work of reflecting on their child's journey. Challenge and struggle are not bugs in human experience — they are core features that create meaning, growth, and authentic connection.
