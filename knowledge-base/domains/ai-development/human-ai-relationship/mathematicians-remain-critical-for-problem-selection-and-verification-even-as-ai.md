Carina Hong's framing: there's no clear instance of AI generating new mathematical definitions or significantly contributing to defining the objects that future proofs operate on. AI is strong at proving theorems within an established formal library (Mathlib for Lean), but selecting which problems are worth attempting and verifying that the problem falls within the library's coverage requires mathematician judgment. Definition-generation, formalizing new mathematical objects, and conceptual frameworks remain human work for now. Carina explicitly says: 'mathematicians are not often able [to do verification] — in fact they help enable AI to do better.'

Application: this is a useful template for human-AI collaboration in research domains generally. AI handles the structured work (proof search, verification within established frameworks). Humans handle the conceptual work (problem selection, framework expansion, verification of informal arguments). The collaboration is asymmetric and stable for the foreseeable future.
