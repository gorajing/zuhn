Choi Jang-soon draws on Walter Benjamin's concept of aura — 'the unique sense of presence, the existence of something far away no matter how close it is' — to explain what AI-generated content structurally lacks. Aura emerges from three types of distance: spatial (the original exists only here and now), temporal (connected to history and tradition), and symbolic (evoking awe and reverence).

AI can produce outputs more colorful than the originals (he demonstrates with miniature artist Tanaka Tatsuya's work), but these lack accumulated pain, time investment, and the possibility of failure. The Ophelia painting example: the model held her pose in cold water 10 hours at a time for 6 months, risking hypothermia. AI could generate a more realistic image, but it cannot replicate that narrative of suffering. For anyone building with AI: the output is the episode; the human story behind it is the narrative. Brands must capture the narrative, not just produce episodes.
