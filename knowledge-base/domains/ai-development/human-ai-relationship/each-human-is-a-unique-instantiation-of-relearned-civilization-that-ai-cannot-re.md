Kellis argues humans are irreplaceable not despite but because of their 'baggage' - each person's unique genetic variants plus unique life experiences create a singular lens through which all of civilization has been independently rediscovered. No two humans have the same perspective on the same knowledge.

AI trained on human data has access to a statistical aggregate of perspectives but not to the unique embodied experience of any individual. The richness of human civilization comes from the diversity of these independently relearned versions - compressing this into a single AI model necessarily loses the variation that makes human knowledge valuable.
