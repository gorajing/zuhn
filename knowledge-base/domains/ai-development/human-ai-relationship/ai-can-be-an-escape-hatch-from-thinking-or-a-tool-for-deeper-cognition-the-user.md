Cleo Abram introduces the concept of 'cognitive time under tension' — borrowed from weightlifting, where squatting 100 pounds for 30 seconds builds more muscle than 3 seconds. She argues the best creative work requires sustained cognitive struggle, and AI tools could let people skip that struggle.

Altman acknowledges the split is real: some users clearly use ChatGPT to avoid thinking, while others use it to think more deeply than ever. He draws inspiration from the top 5% of power users who are 'learning and doing and outputting' at unprecedented levels. His hopeful view: society is competitive, and when people get new tools, expectations rise — 'in theory maybe people just work less but in practice people work ever harder.' The practical implication: AI is a cognitive amplifier, not a replacement. The value accrues to those who use it to increase their cognitive time under tension, not escape it.
