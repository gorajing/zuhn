Appleton's central refinement of the 'AI destroys thinking' narrative: the empirical studies showing GenAI use correlates with reduced critical thinking don't prove that AI is inherently damaging. They prove that models currently on the market — trained with RLHF-driven sycophancy, presented through generic do-everything chatbot interfaces — degrade critical thinking in their current form. Retraining models to support critical engagement (Constitutional AI, RLAIF, personality vectors) and redesigning interfaces around specific domains would produce different outcomes. This is an important distinction: it reframes the problem from 'AI is cognitive poison' (fatalistic, implies abstinence) to 'current AI designs have fixable cognitive flaws' (actionable, implies better design). The same model weights can be catastrophically bad or genuinely useful depending on training goals and interface choices.
