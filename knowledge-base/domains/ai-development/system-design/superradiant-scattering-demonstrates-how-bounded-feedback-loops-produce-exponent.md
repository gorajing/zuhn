In the black hole bomb, electromagnetic waves bounce between a mirror shell and the ergosphere, gaining energy on each pass. The exponential growth depends on two components: the black hole's rotational energy (the amplifier) and the mirror (the containment that forces repeated interaction). Remove the mirror and waves escape; remove the spin and there's nothing to amplify.

This maps directly to system design patterns where feedback loops drive exponential behavior — viral growth needs both a sharing mechanism (amplifier) and a closed network (containment), compounding returns need both reinvestment (amplifier) and a moat (containment). The insight is architectural: exponential effects aren't properties of single components but of the boundary conditions between components.
