The video notes that 'humans are expensive to keep alive and very sensitive to the environment' and proposes a small crew of controllers overseeing autonomous machines for the actual work. This isn't just a space constraint — it's a fundamental scaling law. Any system operating at planetary or stellar scale must be overwhelmingly automated because human labor doesn't scale linearly with project scope.

This maps directly to current AI system design: the most effective architectures use humans for high-level goal-setting and exception handling while autonomous agents handle execution. The ratio of controllers to autonomous workers decreases as scale increases. This suggests that the future of work isn't human-AI collaboration at every level, but rather humans setting objectives for increasingly autonomous AI systems operating at scales humans physically cannot.
