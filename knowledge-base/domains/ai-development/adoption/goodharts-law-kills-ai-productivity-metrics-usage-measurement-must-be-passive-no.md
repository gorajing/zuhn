Goodhart's Law: 'When a measure becomes a target, it is no longer accurate as a measure.' If you judge engineers by lines of code, they write gobbledygook. If you survey employees about whether Harvey (legal AI) made them productive, they'll say yes because their boss bought it.

The only viable approach combines three signals: (1) passive behavioral data showing who actually uses the tools and how much, (2) traditional productivity survey research (the McKinsey/Towers Watson gold standard), and (3) objective output tonnage (hours worked, work completed). The Cursor leaderboard example illustrates this: a founder found his best engineer spending nothing on Cursor — behavioral data revealed a usage gap that no survey would have caught. This three-signal approach replaces surveys with ground truth.
