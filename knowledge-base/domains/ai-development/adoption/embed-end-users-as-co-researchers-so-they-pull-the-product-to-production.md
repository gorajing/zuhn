The default enterprise AI failure mode is to build in a lab, then have to convince skeptical users to adopt the result. Northwestern Mutual inverted this by working with the people who handle the data day in and day out from the outset. This bought them two things: (1) subject-matter expertise that supplied the real questions and real answers needed to build evals and validate correctness, and (2) genuine ownership — the end users became bought into the idea because they helped shape it. When the system matured, the business was already pulling it to production, asking how to package it quickly, rather than being sold on it. Co-development converts the adoption problem into a demand problem.
