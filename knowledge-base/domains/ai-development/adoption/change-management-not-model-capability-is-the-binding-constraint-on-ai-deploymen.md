Nadella's analogy: pre-PC corporate forecasting ran on faxes and interoffice memos; Excel+email didn't just speed it up, it changed the work artifact and workflow. AI in the enterprise needs the same depth of process redesign — what he calls 'Lean for knowledge work.' The implication is that even fully capable agents won't produce productivity gains until companies figure out new patterns of how knowledge gets produced, reviewed, and decided on.

His concrete vision: a 'new inbox' that is an agent manager, not a chat interface — a scaffolding for orchestrating millions of agents that raise exceptions and notifications to a human knowledge worker overseeing many agent knowledge workers. Copilot becomes the UI for AI. SaaS applications with narrow schematized processes and browser UIs are structurally threatened because the business logic migrates into the agentic tier, where agents interrogate CRM, graph, and canvas artifacts on demand.

The practical consequence for timing any AI adoption forecast: bottleneck on legal, compliance, and — most importantly — the humans figuring out new workflows. Expect the capability-to-impact lag to be measured in years of management and process iteration per industry category, with big incumbents both most threatened and best positioned (because they have the data asset and change-management muscle).
