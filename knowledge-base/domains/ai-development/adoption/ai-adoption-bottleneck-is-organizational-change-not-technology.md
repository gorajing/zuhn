According to Gallup, only 8% of US workers use AI daily despite widespread tool availability. The companies seeing real results (Zapier saving 10hrs/week per sales rep, Duolingo going from 100 to 150 courses in 12 months) succeed not because they have better AI tools, but because they translate abstract AI mandates into specific behavioral changes.

The pattern across all 6 companies studied is the same: leaders who say 'we are AI-first' without showing employees what that means for their Tuesday afternoon get nowhere. Tobi Lütke's Shopify memo worked because it included concrete tactics like making AI prototyping part of their GSD process. Intercom's CTO spent a week per month embedded with individual teams to identify specific 2x opportunities. The lesson is that AI adoption is a change management problem, not a technology procurement problem.
