swyx is candid that he has 'the most psychosis' about replacing SaaS and automating with agents, and that this creates friction with employees who are less bought in. His explicit warning to anyone in a position of management: do not talk down to or ignore the concerns of less-enthusiastic staff, because those concerns are valid and those people are the ones who will have to deal with the mistakes when — not if — the AI gets it wrong.

His method is concrete: identify the top three concerns and systematically reduce them, one by one. He describes this as the active process his team is going through while arguing over whether to kick out a SaaS tool and build it themselves.

The broader lesson for AI rollouts is that adoption is a change-management problem, not just a tooling problem. Treating skeptics' objections as a prioritized backlog to retire — rather than resistance to override — both improves the system (the objections often point at real failure modes) and brings the team along the journey.
