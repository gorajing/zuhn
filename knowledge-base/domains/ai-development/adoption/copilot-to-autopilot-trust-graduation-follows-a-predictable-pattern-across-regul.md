The pattern first emerged in radiology, where AI started as a second-opinion tool for detecting mammogram abnormalities, proved it could match or exceed trained radiologists on specific tasks, and is now increasingly seen as necessary infrastructure. This same trust graduation is now repeating across real estate compliance, legal document generation, and construction project management.

The key insight is that routine compliance tasks are the ideal entry point for this transition. They're repetitive, rule-based, and high-consequence when errors compound — exactly where AI excels and where human fatigue causes the most damage. Founders building in regulated industries should target these routine compliance checkpoints as their beachhead, knowing the copilot-to-autopilot transition is not a question of if but when, driven by accumulating evidence of reliability.
