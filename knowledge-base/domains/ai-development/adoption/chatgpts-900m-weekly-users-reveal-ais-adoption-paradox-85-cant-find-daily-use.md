Benedict Evans highlights a crucial signal: ChatGPT's usage data shows massive awareness but thin daily engagement. Only about 5% pay, 10-15% use it daily, and another 20-30% weekly. The remaining majority have accounts, understand the product, and simply can't think of what to do with it.

This isn't an awareness or access problem — it's a use-case discovery problem. Evans compares it to spreadsheets: an accountant seeing Excel for the first time instantly recognized it could do a month of work in 10 minutes. A lawyer saw it and thought 'maybe for my billable hours table.' The implication for AI builders is that the massive opportunity lies not in better models but in wrapping AI into specific workflows that make the value obvious for the 85% who haven't found their daily use case.
