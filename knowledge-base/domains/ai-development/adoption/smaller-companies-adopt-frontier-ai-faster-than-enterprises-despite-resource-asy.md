The conventional wisdom says enterprises adopt slowly because of compliance/legal/integration friction. The panel evidence suggests something stronger: enterprises are also self-bottlenecking on a stale mental model of what AI does — specifically, they're still in 'chat with our internal documents' deployment, which is a 2024-era use case.

Meanwhile small companies are deploying autonomous workflows (agents handling end-to-end processes) because they don't have an established legacy AI deployment to compare against. They evaluate AI tools against the next 6-12 months of capability rather than against their current pilot.

This has product-strategy implications for AI-native sellers: SMB go-to-market produces faster adoption signal than enterprise pilots, even though enterprise pilots produce larger ACVs. Sellers optimizing for proof-of-deployment-velocity should weight SMB; sellers optimizing for revenue dollars should still chase enterprise but expect 6-18 month cycles.

This also implies that enterprise AI adoption is not currently capability-limited — it is imagination-limited. The unlock isn't a better model, it's exposing enterprise buyers to autonomous-workflow demos that they cannot reframe as another chatbot.
