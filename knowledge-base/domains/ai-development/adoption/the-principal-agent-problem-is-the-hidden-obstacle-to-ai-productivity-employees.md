The principal-agent problem in AI adoption: every individual employee is an agent whose rational self-interest is to work as little as possible while getting paid as much as possible. If AI lets a lawyer draft contracts in 4 hours instead of 8, the lawyer gets a productivity gain (golf time) but the company may see zero improvement.

The solution requires both parties to benefit. In Silicon Valley, equity compensation and competitive culture mean many workers will simply work 4 more hours. At larger enterprises, this subset is much smaller. The company needs to either (a) reduce headcount (which CFOs actually hate doing — nobody wants to fire Tina), (b) expect more output per employee, or (c) find new metrics like interdepartmental responsiveness that capture the value without creating Goodhart's Law problems.
