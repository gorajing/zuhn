Dario identifies two distinct exponentials: the capability curve (model performance improving at research speed) and the adoption curve (economic deployment moving at institutional speed). Both are fast, but neither is instant, and there's always a lag between what AI can do and what organizations actually use it for.

This gap is the strategic insight. The companies that build the bridges — tools, workflows, integration layers, training programs — capture disproportionate value. It's analogous to the early internet era where the biggest winners weren't the infrastructure builders but the companies that made the infrastructure usable. Right now, that means products that translate raw model capability into deployed business workflows. The gap is closing fast, which means the window for these bridge-building opportunities is measured in months, not years.
