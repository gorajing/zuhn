The AI Index 2026 Chapter 9 documents Southeast Asia as a distinct global cluster of AI optimism. Across Ipsos' 30-country annual AI Monitor, China, Malaysia, Thailand, Indonesia, and Singapore consistently show over 80% of respondents agreeing that AI will profoundly change their lives in the next 3-5 years. Malaysia posted the largest increase from 2024 (+9 percentage points). Germany (+12), France (+10), and Great Britain (+5) also rose meaningfully but from lower bases. China, Indonesia, and SE Asian countries cluster at the high-excitement/low-nervousness corner of the survey; North America and Europe cluster at low-excitement/high-nervousness. Japan is an outlier — low excitement and low nervousness. The structural interpretation is that AI optimism correlates with two variables: (1) emerging-economy growth trajectory (AI seen as leapfrog opportunity), and (2) cultural/political relationship to technology rather than GDP per capita. This matters because the 'AI is good for growth' political coalition is structurally stronger outside the US and Europe, which is where most safety research and governance infrastructure sits — producing a mismatch where the restrictions come from the skeptical regions and the adoption happens in the optimistic ones.
