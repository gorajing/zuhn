This is the single most damning data point for the AI adoption narrative. Microsoft has the strongest possible position to sell AI features: a complete monopoly on business software, 440 million captive users, and the ability to bundle AI into existing subscriptions. Yet only 8 million — under 2% — are actively using Copilot.

At ~$30/seat/month, that's approximately $2.9B annually. Microsoft has spent over $200B in capex on AI infrastructure. The return is microscopic. If Microsoft can't sell AI features to its own captive user base, the thesis that enterprise AI adoption will drive trillions in revenue is fundamentally broken. Apollo's data showing AI adoption rates have peaked and are declining reinforces this: people tried AI, didn't find lasting utility, and reverted to their previous workflows. This is the demand-side evidence that matches the supply-side economic problems.
