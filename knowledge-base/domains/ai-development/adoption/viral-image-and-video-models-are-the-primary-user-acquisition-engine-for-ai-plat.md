The a16z team identifies that the most impactful consumer AI moments of 2025 were image and video models, not text capabilities. The Ghibli moment for ChatGPT, Nano Banana/Pro for Gemini, VO3 for Google, and Sora 2 for OpenAI all drove massive user spikes. Gemini grew desktop users 155% YoY largely on the strength of these visual model launches.

The key insight is that image/video goes viral in ways text cannot — each new capability spawns trends that spread across TikTok, Instagram, and X. There's 'nearly infinite demand for the best-in-class image or video model' because professionals always want the frontier and consumers follow viral trends. This creates an acquisition flywheel where model breakthroughs drive viral moments that bring users into the ecosystem. The underhyped aspect: search integration within image models (like Nano Banana's web research for accuracy) creates a new dimension beyond just aesthetics and reasoning.
