The visible AI deployment pattern in 2026 is replacement and acceleration: AI does customer service that humans used to do, AI generates code that engineers used to write, AI summarizes meetings that assistants used to attend. This is the efficiency phase: existing workflows running cheaper or faster. The transformation phase, by analogy with the internet, is when new business models become possible that were not previously economically viable. The internet's transformation phase produced the social network, the marketplace, the streaming service, the gig economy. The AI equivalent has not yet appeared. Speculative candidates: the personal AI tutor that produces measurable learning outcomes at scale, the autonomous research-lab-as-a-service, the AI-mediated marketplace where agents negotiate on behalf of human principals. None of these exist as durable businesses in early 2026. The strategic implication for founders and investors: the next Google-scale AI company will not be a faster version of an existing workflow; it will be a business that did not previously exist. Looking for current AI products that would be economically impossible without AI capabilities is a useful filter for identifying transformation-phase candidates vs efficiency-phase plays.
