The anecdote of a 28-year-old at an investment bank who was so good at using ChatGPT that the bank did a global call for him to walk everyone through it — but that's an absurd way to diffuse world-changing technology. The real diffusion pattern: motivated individuals (driven by wanting to be 'lazier and richer') discover massive productivity gains organically.

The worst outcome is that these discoverers keep it secret out of fear ('Am I allowed to use this?'). The fix requires making the discoverer a hero, memorializing the workflow, and pushing it through the entire company. All interests are actually aligned: the go-getter wants recognition, the scared employee wants training and safety, and the company wants more productivity. The Nexus-style product approach makes employees feel safe using AI tools without worrying about looking dumb or accidentally uploading wrong data.
