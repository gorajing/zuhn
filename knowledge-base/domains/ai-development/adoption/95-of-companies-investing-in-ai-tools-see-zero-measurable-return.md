A reported MIT study found that 95% of companies that had invested in AI tools were seeing zero measurable return on that investment. While this headline figure likely reflects a specific moment in the adoption curve rather than a permanent verdict on AI's value, it highlights a critical deployment maturity gap.

The issue is less about whether AI technology works and more about whether organizations have the workflows, data infrastructure, change management, and problem-scoping discipline to extract value from it. This mirrors historical patterns with previous technology waves (cloud, big data, mobile) where early adopters often saw poor returns before deployment practices matured. The practical takeaway is that successful AI adoption requires investing as much in organizational readiness and workflow redesign as in the tools themselves.
