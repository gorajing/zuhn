When Tesla gave Lemonade access to previously unavailable vehicle telemetry, Lemonade launched an 'Autonomous Car' product cutting premiums ~50% for miles driven in FSD mode. This happened before robotaxis are mainstream — the repricing preceded mass adoption. The mechanism is that high-quality behavioral data collapses actuarial uncertainty, forcing premium compression even when the technology is still early. Investors and founders should watch for industries with embedded actuarial uncertainty (insurance, credit, liability) as early repricing targets whenever verifiable behavioral data flows from a new technology platform. The insurance repricing also acts as a legitimizing feedback loop: lower premiums signal to consumers and regulators that AV risk is genuinely declining, accelerating adoption further.
