If you're not going to fire lawyers (and most CFOs don't want to), the question becomes: what DID the AI investment buy? The most promising metric is interdepartmental responsiveness — a behavioral SLA measuring whether departments that adopted AI tools respond faster and handle more requests from other departments.

This works because it captures genuine productivity gains without creating perverse incentives. Lines of code can be gamed. Survey responses are biased. But if your legal department starts responding to product's questions faster, if engineering's turnaround on cross-departmental requests drops, that's a real signal. It also addresses the giant coordination problem that plagues every large company — the reason small companies innovate faster is precisely because they don't have these interdepartmental bottlenecks.
