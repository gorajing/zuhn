McKinsey reports that 88% of surveyed organizations regularly use AI in at least one business function, yet only about one-third are scaling AI enterprise-wide and only 39% report any EBIT impact. That pressures the high-confidence principle that bottom-up user adoption reveals AI product-market fit faster than top-down deployment. The better split is discovery versus scaling: bottom-up usage exposes real work patterns, but enterprise value requires leaders to rewire workflows, budget, governance, and process ownership around those patterns.
