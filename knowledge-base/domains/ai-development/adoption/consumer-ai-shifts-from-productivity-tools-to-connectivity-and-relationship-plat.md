Brian Kim at a16z argues that 2026 is when consumer AI moves beyond productivity into connectivity — helping users see themselves clearly and build relationships with people they love. The core emotion being addressed is wanting to be seen by others.

This creates opportunity for startups because net new user interaction models and creative outlets look fundamentally different from what incumbents offer. The key technical enabler is AI's ability to understand users deeply — through digital footprint ingestion, conversation history, photo analysis — and then facilitate connections between people (imagine your AI reaching out to a friend's AI to suggest checking in). Startups can win here because these interaction models don't natively fit into existing platform architectures.
