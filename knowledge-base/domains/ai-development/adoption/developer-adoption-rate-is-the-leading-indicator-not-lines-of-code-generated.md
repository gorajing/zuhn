The metric that dominates media coverage — what fraction of code is AI-generated — is the one Jellyfish's head of research explicitly distrusts. It tracks output volume, not behavior. The metric that correlates most directly with good productivity outcomes is the developer AI adoption rate: the fraction of coding time a developer actually uses AI tools, averaged across a team to get a company rate.

This matters because adoption captures the behavior change you're trying to drive, and it's the gating variable for gains — you don't see throughput or cycle-time improvements until people use the tools at scale. The data shows median company adoption rising from ~22% (summer 2024) to near 90% (2026), and PR throughput scaling roughly linearly with adoption from 0 to 100%.

For engineering leaders, the actionable move is to instrument adoption rate as the primary KPI of an AI transformation, treat sub-90% rates as a signal of remaining technical/organizational/cultural barriers, and stop optimizing the vanity 'percent of code written by AI' number.
