The AI Index 2026 Chapter 7 captures one of the sharpest governance failures in the report: four out of five US high school and college students use AI for schoolwork (research, essay editing, brainstorming are the top uses), but only half of middle and high schools have AI policies in place, and just 6% of teachers say those policies are clear. The ratio of student usage to teacher-perceived policy clarity is roughly 13 to 1. China and the United Arab Emirates both mandated AI education starting with the 2025-26 school year, while the US has no federal K-12 AI standard. The implication is that the norms for how AI is used in education are being set by students' private choices, not by institutional policy — and once norms form through practice, retroactive policy has limited traction. This is a narrow case of a broader pattern: wherever AI usage precedes institutional governance by a large ratio, the institutional policy ends up formalizing existing student behavior rather than shaping it. The window for proactive policy has largely closed.
