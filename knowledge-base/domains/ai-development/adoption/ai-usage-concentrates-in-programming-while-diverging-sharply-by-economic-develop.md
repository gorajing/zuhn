The Anthropic Economic Index reveals that AI usage is not uniformly distributed globally. Programming dominates across all regions, but the second-order use cases diverge sharply by income level. Advanced economies deploy AI for professional collaboration, workflow optimization, and knowledge work augmentation. Emerging markets lean heavily toward academic support and learning tasks.

This bifurcation suggests that AI's economic impact will compound existing advantages — countries where professionals already have AI in their workflows will accelerate productivity gains, while countries using AI primarily for education may see benefits on a longer time horizon. For founders, this means the addressable market for professional AI tools is concentrated in wealthy economies, while edtech-AI has broader geographic reach but lower willingness to pay.
