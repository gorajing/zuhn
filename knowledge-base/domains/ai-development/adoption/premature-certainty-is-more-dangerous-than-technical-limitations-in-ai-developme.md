The dominant AI discourse frames risk as either 'superintelligence destroys us' or 'AI fails to deliver.' But there's a third, more immediate risk: premature certainty. When an entire ecosystem—investors, builders, regulators, media—converges on the belief that a particular outcome is inevitable, the space for honest inquiry collapses. Skepticism sounds like resistance, caution looks like cowardice, and nuance gets flattened into a for-or-against binary.

This epistemic closure has concrete consequences. Capital flows based on assumed trajectories rather than demonstrated results. Regulation chases momentum rather than evidence. Alternative technical approaches get defunded because they don't fit the consensus narrative. The technology itself may be genuinely impressive, but when certainty travels faster than clarity, the decisions made in that gap can lock in suboptimal paths for years. Maintaining productive uncertainty—staying curious about what we don't yet know—isn't a brake on progress; it's what ensures the foundation is solid.
