Tan argues that the requirements for a 1,000x-superintelligent organization are an egalitarian, trust-by-default culture plus a willingness to spend $10K–$100K+ (even up to $1M) per year per team on tokens. The strategic payoff is a non-repeatable arbitrage: what costs $100K–$1M today will cost ~$10K next year and a couple hundred dollars the year after, by which point everyone does it and 'this is just how companies are.' So there is a one-time time warp where heavy early token spend lets an organization operate as if it were in 2028, leapfrogging every incumbent — Fortune 500s and existing startups alike. The analogy offered is companies in the '90s buying expensive, flaky computers for employees: a superpower precisely because competitors didn't yet have them. This window is explicitly temporary, which is what makes it a time-sensitive opportunity rather than an evergreen one.
