The a16z team identifies multimodal convergence as the defining model trend for 2026. The progression: initially models took text in and produced images/video out. Then image-to-image editing arrived (Nano Banana, Flux). Next comes video-in-to-video-out editing, and ultimately 'mega models' that merge text reasoning, image generation, and video production into unified systems.

This has massive implications for design tools — since design is fundamentally about combining images, text, video, and other elements in creative ways, unified multimodal models could reshape the entire creative software landscape. Labs are actively working on this convergence, combining their previously separate LLM and image/video efforts. For startups, this creates both threat (labs getting more capable) and opportunity (building opinionated interfaces on top of these expanding capabilities).
