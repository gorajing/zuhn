Evans draws a direct line from enterprise SaaS history to the AI future. The typical large company today uses 400-500 SaaS apps, all basically doing things you could do in Oracle, Excel, or email — but wrapped in industry-specific workflows with curated UI choices that encode institutional knowledge.

AI software companies are doing the same to ChatGPT. A raw chatbot asks you everything from first principles. A purpose-built AI product has thousands of clever people who spent years determining which 7 buttons should be on that screen. The value isn't the AI capability — it's the workflow design, the guardrails, the domain expertise baked into the product. This is why 'people buy solutions, not technologies' and why dedicated AI companies will thrive despite model providers moving up the stack.
