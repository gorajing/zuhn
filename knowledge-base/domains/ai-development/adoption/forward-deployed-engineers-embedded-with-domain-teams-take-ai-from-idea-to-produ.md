10DS deployed the first forward-deployed engineers in No. 10's history, embedding them with teams of policy advisers, lawyers, comms people, and pollsters. The engineers observe actual workflows and pain points, co-design solutions in situ, and take ideas to implementation in a couple of weeks — versus projects that 'might be in discovery for a year or more' under normal processes. Concrete results: an engineer embedded with in-house lawyers for two weeks replaced a planned £1.5M external contract to analyze the entire UK statute book, and the resulting tool can be re-run on demand (solving the staleness problem of a one-off paid analysis).

The insight is that proximity to the real workflow is the bottleneck for useful AI, not model capability. By co-locating technical builders with domain experts, you compress the requirements-gathering, build, and feedback loop, and you surface the low-hanging fruit ('lots of simple AI use cases you can do in a few days') that an outside vendor would never see. The FDE model also produces reusable, ownable, in-house capability rather than a consultant deliverable that decays the moment the engagement ends.
