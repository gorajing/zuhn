The standard 'technology takes time to diffuse' argument used to explain why AI hasn't shown up in firm-level productivity is backwards. A genuinely AGI-level model would be vastly easier to deploy than hiring a human: it could read your entire Slack and Drive in minutes, instantly inherit skills from other deployed instances, and avoid the lemons-market problem that makes human hiring so risky and expensive.

Firms spend tens of trillions per year on knowledge-worker wages. If models could substitute for that labor at parity, firms would be willing to spend trillions buying tokens — the fact that lab revenue is orders of magnitude below this is the simplest explanation: the capability isn't there yet.

When someone invokes diffusion lag to defend current AI revenue, ask: would adoption really be slower for a drop-in human replacement than for hiring an actual human? If not, the lag is the capability gap.
