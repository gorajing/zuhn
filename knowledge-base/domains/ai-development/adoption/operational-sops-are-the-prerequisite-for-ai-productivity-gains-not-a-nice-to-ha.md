The data-cleaning bottleneck is widely-discussed; the panel surfaced a stronger claim — even after data is clean, AI cannot deliver consistent enterprise productivity until the underlying processes are documented as SOPs. The reason is that AI can pattern-match, but it pattern-matches to whatever its inputs show; if the same process has 5 different unwritten ways of being done across the team, the AI inherits the variance and reproduces noise.

The sequencing implication: an enterprise's AI-readiness work is (1) document SOPs, (2) clean data, (3) deploy AI. Skipping step 1 and going straight from (2) to (3) produces a fast-but-inconsistent agent that hurts trust in AI internally — the worst possible outcome because it then takes 6-12 months to recover from the 'we tried AI, it didn't work' organizational memory.

This reframes the AI consulting/services market: a non-trivial fraction of what looks like 'AI deployment work' is actually operational-design work that should have been done before any AI was procured. Firms that surface this honestly to clients will compound trust; firms that pretend the AI alone is the answer will burn through pilot budgets.

The Zuhn-internal corollary: the same logic applies to personal knowledge work. AI on a chaotic personal KB pattern-matches to the chaos; the upstream investment is in structured capture (sources, stance discipline, topic ontology) before any extraction can deliver consistent value.
