Gary frames the core AI-era career question as 'architect or mason?' — architects design systems (including systems that use AI), masons execute repeatable work (which AI progressively automates). The mason-to-architect jump is hard because it requires patterns that don't yet exist in the person. His recommended method is the same he used to quit sugary drinks: hire a babysitter-level external enforcer for one year to suffocate the old pattern until the new pattern becomes automatic. Applied to AI adoption, this means using tools heavily enough that they become default, reallocating leisure time to learning, changing who you hang out with, and reshaping your algorithmic newsfeed toward optimistic/offensive content rather than negative news. The insight is that discipline starts mentally but must be backed by environmental constraints; pure willpower loses.
