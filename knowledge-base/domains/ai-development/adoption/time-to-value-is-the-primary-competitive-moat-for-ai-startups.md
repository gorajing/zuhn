The AI market is moving fast enough that incumbency advantages are weak and switching costs are low. In this environment, the startup that gets users to their 'aha moment' fastest builds habitual usage before competitors can catch up. Slow onboarding is especially lethal because users have many AI alternatives to try immediately.

This reframes product prioritization: features that reduce time-to-first-value (better defaults, shorter setup, faster outputs) should rank above features that increase total value ceiling. A user who sees results in 2 minutes is far more likely to stay than one who sees better results after 20 minutes of configuration. Time-to-value is the top-of-funnel metric that all other metrics depend on.
