Nadella is careful to distinguish two breakthroughs bundled into Majorana 1: the physics existence proof of Majorana zero modes in a topological phase, and the fabrication technique that makes them manufacturable. He explicitly says 'now that we have the fabrication technique' as the pivot to building integrated circuits and eventually a fault-tolerant machine.

This mirrors the transistor analogy he leans on. The first transistor was a physics demonstration; the thing that changed the world was the process by which transistors could be laid down repeatably on silicon. Without repeatable fabrication, device physics is a curiosity.

For anyone tracking deep-tech timelines, the useful heuristic is to ask not 'does it work in a lab' but 'is there a manufacturing path'. The gap between those two questions is often where decades of a technology's life are spent.
