The pattern of new AI hardware devices failing — despite significant hype and investment — points to a structural problem: the compelling capability improvements in AI are happening at the model and application layer, not in specialized hardware form factors for consumers. Devices like the Humane AI Pin and Rabbit r1 were premised on the idea that a new device category would unlock AI in ways a phone couldn't, but users found software apps on existing hardware just as capable.

This suggests a durable principle for AI product strategy: hardware differentiation only wins when it unlocks genuinely new sensing modalities (cameras, microphones in new contexts) or compute that cannot be replicated in the cloud or on existing devices. General-purpose AI inference does not clear that bar for consumers.
