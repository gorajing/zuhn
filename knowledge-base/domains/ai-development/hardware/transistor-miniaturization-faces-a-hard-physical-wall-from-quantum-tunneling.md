Since the 1960s, exponential gains in computing power came from shrinking transistors — packing more switches into less space. But as transistors approach the size of individual atoms (currently ~3nm process nodes), quantum tunneling allows electrons to pass through barriers that are supposed to block them. This isn't an engineering problem to be optimized around; it's a fundamental physical constraint. The implication for AI and compute-intensive fields is that raw classical transistor density can no longer be the primary driver of performance gains — architectural innovation (chiplets, 3D stacking, specialized accelerators) and entirely new paradigms (quantum, photonic, neuromorphic) become the necessary paths forward.
