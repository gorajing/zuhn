Nadella frames Microsoft's quantum strategy as a deliberate rejection of the scaling path taken by competitors. Instead of trying to error-correct away the noise of existing qubit types, Microsoft waited on a physical substrate whose reliability comes from the physics itself — Majorana zero modes, theorized in the 1930s and only now fabricated as a new topological phase of matter.

The strategic lesson generalizes beyond quantum: when a field's dominant approach hits a wall that scale alone cannot solve, the correct bet can be to sit out the scaling race and wait for a foundational breakthrough. This is an expensive strategy with binary outcomes — you either get the breakthrough or you get nothing — but for problems like utility-scale quantum, Nadella argues there is no other way.

The implication for AI hardware and deep-tech investing is that 'more of the same with better engineering' is not always a path to the goal. Some goals require waiting for a different substrate.
