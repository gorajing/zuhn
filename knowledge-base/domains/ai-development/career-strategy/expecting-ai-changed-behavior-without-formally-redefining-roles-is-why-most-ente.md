McKinsey identifies whether you have actually changed the roles involved in software development as one of the biggest differentiators between outsized and marginal AI gains. The expected shift is concrete: engineers move from execution and simply writing code to being orchestrators who think through how to divide work among agents, and the PM role shifts toward direct prototyping and real-time customer feedback. But about 70% of surveyed companies have not changed the roles at all — they carry a background expectation that people will behave differently while the role is still defined, understood, and incentivized exactly as it was a couple of years ago. The lesson for both leaders and individuals: behavioral change does not follow from tool access plus vague expectation. You must formally rewrite role definitions, expectations, and incentives. The companies that consolidated roles into smaller pods saw maintained or improved code quality and large output speedups — the gain came from redefining the job, not from the tool alone.
