Rick Beato draws a direct parallel between musical pitch and language acquisition, citing Patricia Kuhl's research on linguistic development. Just as babies can distinguish phonemes of all 6,500 languages at birth but become 'culturally bound listeners' by nine months, Beato theorizes that perfect pitch follows the same trajectory.

He tested this with his son Dylan: playing 'high-information music' (Bach fugues, sophisticated jazz by Aydin Esen, Keith Jarrett) on his wife's stomach starting at 15 weeks, then sitting with Dylan for an hour every morning after birth while listening to music and engaging face-to-face (activating the 'social brain' required for language acquisition). Dylan developed perfect pitch — identifying complex polychords at age eight — despite no family history of it. The key insight: it requires both the auditory exposure AND social brain engagement, just like natural language acquisition.
