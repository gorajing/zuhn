Konyi's pedagogical philosophy is explicit: 'The words are just to guide you about where to focus. But then as soon as you're there, just bathe in that feeling.' Descriptions like 'melancholy' or 'expansive' are pointers, not definitions. If a word doesn't resonate with your subjective experience, ignore it — the feeling state itself is the only accurate representation of itself.

The practical method: play degrees against a drone, feel each one, repeat until recognition is instant. 'If you don't just know, you're just not familiar enough yet.' This maps to a broader learning principle: conceptual understanding is scaffolding for direct recognition, and the mark of mastery is when the scaffolding becomes unnecessary — you simply perceive the thing itself without intermediate cognitive processing.
