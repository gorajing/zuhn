Konyi's core framework redefines how to understand scale degrees: instead of thinking of them as pitch intervals or note names, treat them as distinct feeling states. Degree 1 feels 'perfect, complete, whole, resolved.' Degree 5 feels 'pure, expansive, neutral.' Degree 3 feels 'sweet, warm, glowing, vital.' Flat 3 defines 'minorness' — somber and serious. Flat 2 is 'hopeless, desperate, intense.'

This reframe is practically powerful because it shifts ear training from intellectual categorization to visceral recognition. You don't identify degree 7 by counting intervals — you feel its 'melancholy strained quality' and know what it is instantly. The drone-based practice method (playing degrees against a sustained tonic) is the training mechanism: it isolates the feeling state from melodic context.
