This is perhaps the most counterintuitive insight in Konyi's framework: what sounds close is not what feels close. Degree 5 (pure, neutral, expansive) and sharp 4 (strange, alien, intensely bright) are only a half step apart in pitch, but occupy completely different emotional worlds. Conversely, degrees 3, 6, and 7 share a 'warmth/sweetness' dimension despite being spread across the pitch range.

Konyi organizes degrees on a circle where adjacent positions share feeling-state similarity: 1-5-2 share a 'pure/neutral' quality; 3-6-7 share 'sweetness/warmth'; flat 7 - flat 3 - flat 6 - flat 2 increase in darkness/intensity. This circle model is a practical tool for composition: to shift emotional color gradually, move to adjacent circle positions; for dramatic contrast, jump to opposite positions.
