When you hear a guitar tone, drum pattern, or production texture you love, the next step is credit tracing: finding who performed it, then exploring everything else they have played on. This uncovers creative networks that no algorithm can surface because it follows human collaboration patterns rather than sonic similarity.

The practical chain is: check album credits on the streaming platform, search the musician on Discogs or Genius for their full participation list, and as a last resort search their Instagram feed for project mentions. Artist interviews are described as the single most accurate reference material because they capture the creative intent behind each track. For Korean music, Melon provides comprehensive song-level credits (lyricist, composer, arranger, performer). This methodology transforms passive listening into active research, building a mental map of who makes what and how creative scenes interconnect.
