Wilkenfeld's philosophy is to stand 'on the edge of the cliff' during every performance -- playing at the boundary of the unknown rather than retreating to safe, rehearsed patterns. She argues that playing safe turns you into 'a clown showing skills you studied in your bedroom' rather than creating genuine real-time expression.

The tradeoff is explicit: you'll occasionally play a 'clunker,' but you wouldn't have played half the brilliant stuff either if you weren't constantly at the edge. The key insight for any creative practitioner is that the gap between 'technically competent' and 'genuinely moving' is exactly this willingness to risk failure. Jeff Beck gave her a bass solo at Eric Clapton's guitar festival precisely because her edge-playing created more compelling music than safe perfection would have.
