Being advanced at mixing means being really good at the basics. Volume is the primary tool: louder = more focus, quieter = less important. Getting volume relationships correct before touching EQ, compression, or spatial effects gives you a clear picture of what actually needs processing.

Many beginners reach for plugins immediately when the real problem is a volume imbalance. Correct levels first, then apply EQ to carve space, compression for dynamic control, and spatial mixing for stereo placement as secondary refinements. Sound selection also feeds into this: choose sounds with contrasting characteristics (frequency, stereo width, dynamics) so they sit together with less masking and less processing needed.
