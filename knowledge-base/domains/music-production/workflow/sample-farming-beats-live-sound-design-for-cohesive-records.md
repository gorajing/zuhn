The video argues Aphex Twin kept folders of CDP renders and pulled from them in Player Pro to compose Drukqs. The separation matters: when sound design and arrangement collapse into one session, the producer makes compromised choices on both fronts. Doing them in separate passes — an exploratory generation session with no arrangement pressure, then a curation/composition session with a locked palette — aligns with the broader principle that distinct cognitive modes deserve distinct sessions. This also explains the sonic unity of records where every sound feels like it came from the same weird planet.
