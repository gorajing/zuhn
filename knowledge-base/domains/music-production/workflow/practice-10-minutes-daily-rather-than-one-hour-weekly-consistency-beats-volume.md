Rick Beato's advice to his son Dylan when learning guitar: 'It's better to practice 10 minutes a day, seven days a week than to practice one day for an hour.' While the total time is roughly equivalent, daily practice builds muscle memory and neural pathways more effectively than weekly sessions.

The practical trick that makes this work: keep the guitar on a stand, not in a case. When you walk by it, you pick it up 'for a second' — and that second turns into 10 minutes, then an hour. This removes the friction of setup and leverages casual moments. The inverse is also true: taking a day off turns into a week, which turns into months without touching the instrument. The principle extends beyond music to any skill that requires consistent motor learning.
