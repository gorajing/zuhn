Wilkenfeld's evolution as a performer tracks a clear arc: early in her career, she sat on a road case crying over one bad note at Montreux Jazz Festival. Now she doesn't get depressed about performances at all. The change wasn't that she stopped making mistakes -- it's that she became so fast at recovery that mistakes became invisible to the audience.

The meta-skill isn't perfection but recovery velocity. This maps directly to any high-stakes creative or professional context: the best operators aren't the ones who never make mistakes, but the ones who recover so quickly that errors become imperceptible. The path to this skill is counterintuitive -- you have to fall off the cliff many times to develop the reflexes. Avoiding the cliff keeps you safe but never develops the recovery muscles.
