Recording single-hit samples from analog synthesizers into digital
samplers combines the best of both worlds: the warm, unpredictable
character of analog sound generation with the precise sequencing,
pattern management, and recall capabilities of digital hardware. The
Moog DFAM into Elektron Digitakt pairing is a common example in the
techno community. Record raw DFAM hits at 48kHz/16-bit (Digitakt has
24-bit converters but auto-converts to 16-bit), then use Digitakt's
sequencer for complex patterns, parameter locks, and conditional
triggers that the DFAM's simple 8-step sequencer cannot achieve. Free
DFAM sample packs exist on Elektronauts for those without the hardware.
