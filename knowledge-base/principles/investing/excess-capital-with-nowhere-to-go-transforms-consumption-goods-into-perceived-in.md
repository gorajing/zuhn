When real estate is unaffordable, bonds unattractive, and savings accounts lose to inflation, money floods into equities and speculation regardless of fundamentals -- creating a TINA (there is no alternative) dynamic. This excess liquidity then spills into non-financial assets (watches, sneakers, trading cards, luxury goods) which get reframed as 'investments' despite being pure consumption. The bubble pops reveal the distinction: market crashes destroy notional value that never existed as liquid capital, reflexivity causes self-amplifying loops in both directions, and the VC ecosystem perpetuates the cycle because both founders and investors are incentivized to keep the game running.
