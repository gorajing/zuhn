The Bronze Age collapse, the Great Depression, and modern supply-chain crises all show that tightly interconnected systems fail not from a single shock but from multiple stressors compounding through the same linkages that created prosperity. No civilization in these networks was self-sufficient, and scapegoating a single cause (Sea Peoples, capitalism, one bad actor) masks the systemic fragility. Recovery from true systemic collapse takes centuries, not decades — the Bronze Age took 400 years to rebuild comparable trade networks.
