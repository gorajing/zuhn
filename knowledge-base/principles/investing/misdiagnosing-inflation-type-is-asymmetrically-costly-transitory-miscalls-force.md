When central banks misidentify inflation as transitory and delay action, the catch-up hiking cycle becomes so rapid that institutions holding long-duration assets have no time to adjust. SVB's $80B long-bond bet was safe in isolation but lethal when paired with flighty short-term deposits in a rising-rate environment — the mismatch was structural, but the Fed's pace made it terminal. The asymmetry cuts both ways: calling inflation transitory too long forces emergency tightening, while calling it persistent too quickly damages growth unnecessarily. Governments compound the error by changing measurement methodologies to report lower inflation and unemployment, which delays acknowledgment until the correction arrives violently. For investors, the tell is not the inflation data itself but the gap between official measurement and street-level reality.
