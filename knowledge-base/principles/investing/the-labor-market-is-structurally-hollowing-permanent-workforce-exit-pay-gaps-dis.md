About 0.1% of American men have permanently left the workforce every single month since the 1960s — a trend COVID accelerated but did not create. 10 million American men are not working, studying, retired, or institutionalized. The 'skills gap' narrative is a pay gap: 8.2 million openings vs 7 million seekers looks like mismatch, but most unfilled positions simply pay below market rates. Over 5 years, formal unemployment dropped by 3 million while Uber drivers increased by 3 million — jobs did not improve, they migrated to less visible categories. Job mobility has frozen across industries, 30 million will retire by 2030 with less than $250K, and tying healthcare to employment means every career transition directly increases mortality risk. Fast food sales plummeting signals distress has reached the absolute bottom of the spending pyramid.
