The most dangerous position in conflict is winning every battle while losing the war. Napoleon won at Borodino but lost 30,000 irreplaceable veterans. Germany's 1918 Spring Offensive gained more territory than any operation since 1914 but exhausted its last reserves. Britain and France 'won' WWI but emerged so devastated they couldn't maintain their empires. The Somme continued for months because commanders couldn't justify stopping without results. This pattern transfers precisely to business: companies that win every competitive skirmish while burning through cash reserves, talent, or goodwill are on the same trajectory. The question is never 'are we winning?' but 'can we sustain the cost of winning?'
