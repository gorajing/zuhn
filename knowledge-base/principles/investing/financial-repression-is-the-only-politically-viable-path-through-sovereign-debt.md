The five ways to deal with unserviceable debt are grow, inflate, default, restructure, or repress. At current debt levels, only financial repression is politically viable — it extracts wealth from savers via negative real rates without visible expropriation. Napier's thesis is that this regime has already started and will run roughly 15 years, driven by the same pressure Arthur Burns admitted in 1979 (the political cost of stopping inflation via unemployment is unacceptable under geopolitical rivalry). Partial capital controls are already accumulating — tariffs, pension mandates, 401(k) rule changes — and Larry Fink's 'democratization of investment' is the same thesis dressed in reform language. The winners under repression are re-industrialization, rearmament, defense, energy, and old-economy manufacturing; the losers are whoever is excluded from cheap credit.
