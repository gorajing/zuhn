Clear, credible deterrence often outperforms nuanced diplomatic strategies in preventing adversary escalation, because predictability enables rational calculation. When billionaires or states exercise power unpredictably, it destabilizes more than consistently aggressive use would, since allies and markets cannot hedge against random shifts. This principle unifies nuclear brinksmanship, tariff volatility, and autocratic governance: each creates disproportionate damage not through severity but through the impossibility of adaptation.
