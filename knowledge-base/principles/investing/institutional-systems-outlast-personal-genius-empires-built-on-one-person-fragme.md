Alexander's empire — the largest in the world — fragmented immediately upon his death into warring successor states because he built no institutions, only personal loyalty. Napoleon's marshals defected when he fell because their loyalty was personal, not institutional. In contrast, Venice endured as a republic for over 1,000 years with 117 successive doges. The Bolsheviks captured the state with a tiny force by seizing infrastructure nodes, then built institutional control that lasted decades. Christianity outlasted Rome by inheriting its institutional infrastructure. For investors and founders, this means personal charisma and individual brilliance create fragile organizations — the only durable competitive advantage is an institutional system that functions independent of any single person.
