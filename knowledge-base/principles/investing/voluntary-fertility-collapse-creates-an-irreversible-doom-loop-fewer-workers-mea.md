Unlike historical population declines from war or plague, today's fertility collapse stems from voluntary structural forces: urbanization turns children from assets to liabilities, and economic conditions that enabled the baby boom have been systematically dismantled. The doom loop compounds because fewer workers means fewer researchers means lower productivity growth means less ability to offset the shrinking labor force. Population age structure today reveals economic reality 20-30 years out with surprising accuracy, but demographic shifts happen so slowly they're ignored until retirement waves cascade.
