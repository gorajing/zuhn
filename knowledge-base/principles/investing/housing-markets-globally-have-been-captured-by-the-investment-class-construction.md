Canada, Australia, UK, and US have all independently developed housing crises through different mechanisms but identical root cause: treating housing as an investment vehicle. Developers hold hundreds of thousands of luxury units vacant rather than reducing prices because vacancy preserves asset values. New construction is near record highs but almost entirely luxury apartments, creating hundreds of thousands of empty high-end units alongside a 4-million-unit affordable housing shortage. Australia's negative gearing lets individuals write off real estate losses against income, driving prices to 8x median income. The economic conditions that enabled the baby boom — cheap homes, stable jobs, affordable childcare — have been systematically dismantled. If developers cannot sell luxury inventory, they go bankrupt, reducing construction of the affordable homes actually needed.
