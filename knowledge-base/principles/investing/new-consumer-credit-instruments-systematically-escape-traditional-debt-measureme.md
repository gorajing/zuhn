BNPL ($500B+ unrecorded), fragmented auto lending (leases, subprime, buy-here-pay-here, cross-collateralized products), and private credit ($2T+ with minimal oversight) have created a shadow layer of consumer and corporate debt that doesn't appear in traditional credit metrics. Even regulators admit they can't track total exposure. The result is that every published consumer debt figure is structurally understated, and the real leverage in the system is invisible until it unwinds. Record corporate bankruptcies outside a recession, zombie companies surviving on cheap refinancing, and cars worth less than their loans all signal that the true debt load is already unsustainable.
