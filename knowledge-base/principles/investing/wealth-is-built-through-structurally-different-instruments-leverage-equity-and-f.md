The Forbes 400 consists of buyers and builders who compound businesses, not high earners. Real estate leverage turns a 10% appreciation into a 50% return. McDonald's cash flow funds operations but its $45B real estate portfolio is the actual wealth engine. Meanwhile, 40% of people earning $500K+ live paycheck to paycheck because income alone cannot overcome poor instrument selection. The academic evidence is equally clear: after Fama-French, most apparent alpha dissolves into replicable factor exposures (size, value, profitability, investment), and the Efficient Market Hypothesis has been empirically rejected -- but exploiting predictabilities requires sophisticated models and scientific training, not just capital. The wealth gap is fundamentally a financial literacy gap about which instruments to use.
