Across thousands of years of military history, the single most repeated pattern of victory is concentrating superior force against one isolated enemy segment before others can arrive. Napoleon at Castiglione, Rivoli, and Austerlitz; Alexander at Issus and Gaugamela; Belisarius against the Vandals — all defeated numerically superior opponents by engaging fractions sequentially. The inverse is equally consistent: when enemies finally coordinate simultaneous attacks (Leipzig, Waterloo, Trachenberg Plan), even genius commanders lose. This principle transfers directly to business and investing: attack one market segment with overwhelming focus before incumbents coordinate a response.
