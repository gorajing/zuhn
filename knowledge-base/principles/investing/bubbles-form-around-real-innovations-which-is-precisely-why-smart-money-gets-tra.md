Every major bubble is anchored by genuine technological value -- railroads, dot-com, AI -- which makes early dismissal look foolish and late entry look justified. The real innovation provides intellectual cover for irrational capital allocation, trapping sophisticated investors who correctly identify the technology but incorrectly assume valuation must follow. Circular financing chains, sunk-cost defensiveness, and the impossibility of admitting failure compound the trap.
