Markets have indiscriminately punished software stocks, but the actual vulnerability is determined by moat depth across 8 dimensions, not sector membership. SaaS models that charge for access to human-engineered software are being repriced as AI makes equivalent software trivially cheap to produce, while infrastructure layers with deep integration complexity retain lock-in. The discriminating investor must score moat dimensions individually -- four or more durable dimensions means the stock is likely oversold; two or fewer means AI disruption is fairly priced.
