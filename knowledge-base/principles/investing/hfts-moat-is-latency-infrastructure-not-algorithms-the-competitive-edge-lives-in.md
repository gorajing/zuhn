High-frequency trading firms profit through extreme volume at fractions of a cent per trade. The competitive edge is not algorithmic cleverness but physical infrastructure: kernel-bypass networking (DPDK), exchange colocation, FPGAs executing trading logic at hardware speed in sub-microsecond latency, and lock-free queues that eliminate thread contention. Real-time latency monitoring is a profit driver, not operational overhead, because microseconds of degradation directly cost money.
