Energy is the current AI infrastructure bottleneck driving renewed nuclear interest, but once solved, cooling becomes the next constraint, creating a wave of innovation opportunity around thermal management. This sequential bottleneck pattern means infrastructure costs are structurally higher than projections that only model the current constraint, and each resolution exposes a new capital-intensive problem. Investment opportunities exist at each bottleneck transition, but the total cost of AI infrastructure scales with the full chain of constraints, not just the one everyone is currently focused on.
