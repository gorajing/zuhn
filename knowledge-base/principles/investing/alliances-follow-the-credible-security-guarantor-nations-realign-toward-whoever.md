Geopolitical alliances are not driven by ideology, religion, or historical ties — they follow whoever provides credible security and economic integration. Saudi Arabia and Israel aligned despite decades of enmity because IMEC offered mutual benefit; Gulf states are pivoting toward China because the US repeatedly failed to protect their oil infrastructure. The US strategy of delegating Middle East security via economic corridors (IMEC) to free resources for the Indo-Pacific only works if regional powers believe the arrangement actually protects them.
