Private equity's 2-and-20 model makes fund size more important than fund performance -- managers earn billions in management fees before generating any returns. PE firms borrow against acquired companies, putting debt on the target's books while collecting fees regardless of outcome. Pension funds enable this because PE's opaque accounting lets them report hitting 7-8% return targets even when actual returns don't justify fees. The pattern extends beyond PE: Visa and Mastercard maintain an unspoken harmony where neither encroaches on the other's territory, prediction markets use academic information theory as regulatory cover for what is functionally gambling, and Epstein charged hundreds of millions for wealth management without credentials for decades because nobody questioned the opacity. When you can't see through the intermediary, the intermediary is extracting from you.
