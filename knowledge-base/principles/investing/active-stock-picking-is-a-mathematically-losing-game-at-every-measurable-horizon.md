Only 2% of stocks account for all net market gains over 75 years, making individual stock selection a near-impossible needle-in-a-haystack problem. Fewer than 10% of professional fund managers beat their benchmark at 10 years, dropping to virtually zero at 20. Meanwhile, fee drag destroys 20% of portfolio value over 25-30 years, and financial media and retail-targeted products serve advertisers and institutions rather than investor returns. The cumulative weight of concentration risk, professional failure rates, and compounding costs makes passive indexing the only mathematically defensible default strategy.
