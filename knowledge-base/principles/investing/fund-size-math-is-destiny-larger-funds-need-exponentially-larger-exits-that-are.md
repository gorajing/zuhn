A $1B fund needs ~$17B in aggregate exits for 3x returns, mechanically forcing it toward consensus. Smaller, disciplined funds can own more of fewer outliers and generate better multiples because the math works with smaller outcomes. The structural advantage flips only when the very largest exits become trillion-dollar events.
