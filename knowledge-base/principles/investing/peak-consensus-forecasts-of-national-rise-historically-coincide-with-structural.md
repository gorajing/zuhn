Britain in 1870-1914 produced more at war's outbreak than in 1870, yet felt weaker because America and Germany had grown faster. China's peak-growth consensus coincides with demographic crisis, reform stagnation, and military purges suggesting internal crisis rather than war preparation. Historical precedent across empires shows that relative decline -- competitors growing faster -- consistently blindsides powers still measuring absolute output, because the metrics that defined the ascent stop reflecting the structural reality.
