Across semiconductors, logistics corridors, and commodity markets, the entity that controls the physical bottleneck dictates terms to everyone else. NVIDIA locked up 70% of TSMC's 3nm capacity and customers literally beg for chips. The US designs IMEC to route global trade through allied chokepoints, bypassing China's Belt and Road. Iran is targeted not for ideology but because it physically blocks a logistics highway. AI demand shifted DRAM production lines, making whoever controls fab allocation the price-setter for the entire memory market. The pattern: in every domain, the scarce physical layer — fabs, shipping lanes, energy supply — is where power concentrates.
