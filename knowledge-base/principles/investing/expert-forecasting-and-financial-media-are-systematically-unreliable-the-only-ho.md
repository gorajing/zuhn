Tetlock's research shows expert financial forecasts perform no better than chance, professional fund managers fail to beat benchmarks over any meaningful horizon, and financial media optimizes for attention rather than accuracy. Since nobody reliably predicts market-moving events, the actual edge comes from controlling behavioral errors: avoiding panic selling during routine drawdowns, resisting social-media-amplified FOMO, and recognizing that 30% of crash sellers never return to equities.
