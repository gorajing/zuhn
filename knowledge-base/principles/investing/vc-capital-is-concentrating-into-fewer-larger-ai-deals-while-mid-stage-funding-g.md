Q2 2025 venture funding hit $94.6B (up 53% YoY), but capital is concentrating into fewer, larger deals in AI, hard tech, and defense. A16Z reports a 60% surge in AI-first startup funding while early-stage SaaS investment declines. Funding, talent, and attention are clustering around fewer startups, with AI and Bay Area companies dominating capital flows. Capital concentrates at extremes — large late-stage rounds and small seeds — while mid-stage funding gets squeezed, creating a barbell distribution that founders must navigate strategically.
