Democracy's stability relies on unwritten norms rather than enforceable laws — a shameless norm-violator reveals how fragile the system really is. Jefferson's 'tree of liberty' and RFK's 'inches from barbarity' mean democratic freedoms degrade the moment each generation stops actively protecting them. Liberal IR theories (democratic peace, economic interdependence, institutional cooperation) each fail under the structural pressures of anarchy. States pursue power not because they're inherently aggressive but because the anarchic system leaves no alternative. President Clinton avoided engaging with nuclear issues; Reagan publicly misstated that submarine missiles could be recalled. The pattern: systems appear robust precisely because nobody stress-tests the load-bearing assumptions.
