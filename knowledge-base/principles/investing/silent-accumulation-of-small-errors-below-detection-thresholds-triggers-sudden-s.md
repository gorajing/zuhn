DNA replication errors that escape proofreading accumulate silently until a critical threshold triggers disease — the same dynamic applies to institutional governance failures that prosperity masks. Canada's GDP grew while GDP per capita stagnated for a decade because population growth masked declining individual prosperity. Britain in 1870-1914 produced more in absolute terms yet felt weaker because competitors had grown faster. The pattern is consistent: systems that look healthy on aggregate metrics are quietly degrading at the per-unit level, and the crisis arrives suddenly when the accumulated drift crosses a threshold.
