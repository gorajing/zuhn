When you invest in a country's stock market, you're betting on its future economic power relative to rivals. The US enforces a 70% GDP ceiling on vassals (Japan via Plaza Accord, China via trade war). IMEC binds Middle Eastern nations economically so they self-manage their region. Saudi-Israel alignment proves economic interests override ideological enmity. Even in a US-loses scenario, the S&P returns ~5% annually because China needs the US consumption market. Simultaneously, the sweetest macro spot in 40 years (fiscal + monetary + deregulatory stimulus) creates rare opportunities, while bubble mechanics mirror every previous platform shift with unknowable timing. The principle: own the structural winners across multiple power scenarios, and time your capital deployment to when others are scared.
