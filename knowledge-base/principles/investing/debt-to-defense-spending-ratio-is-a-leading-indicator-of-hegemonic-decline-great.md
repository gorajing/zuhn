Louis XVI spending half of France's national budget on debt service was a leading indicator of regime collapse that no incremental reform could fix. With rates near zero in 2021, the US could have refinanced cheaply but didn't, now facing 5%+ rates on trillions in rolling maturities. When massive fiscal deficits meet a shrinking buyer base for Treasuries, Fed rate cuts no longer reliably lower long-term yields, and presidents fear bond market signals more than stock drops because rising yields directly threaten the government's ability to finance itself.
