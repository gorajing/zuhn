The Qing dynasty's late reforms abolished the exam system and created a constitutional monarchy but these changes destroyed the existing power structure without creating a viable alternative. Russia's cyclical pattern of aggressive reform followed by reaction (Peter, Catherine, Alexander II, each followed by reactionaries) repeatedly destabilized without resolving. The Decembrists' crushed uprising radicalized the next generation from constitutional reform into revolutionary socialism. The 1848 revolutions failed because each national movement pursued different goals and conservative governments crushed them individually. The pattern: partial reform is more dangerous than either full commitment or no change, because it destroys the legitimacy of the old system while failing to establish the new one.
