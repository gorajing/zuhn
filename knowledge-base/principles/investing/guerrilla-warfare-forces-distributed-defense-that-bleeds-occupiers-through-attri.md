Napoleon seized Spain easily but faced six years of guerrilla war that drained more French resources than any conventional campaign. France had hundreds of thousands of troops in Spain but could never concentrate enough because guerrilla warfare forced them to garrison everywhere. Spanish guerrillas could not defeat French armies in open battle but made occupation so costly through constant harassment that France bled out. Iran fights conflicts exclusively through proxies rather than directly — the pattern applies from Hezbollah to Houthis to Hamas. At Bunker Hill, untrained militia behind earthworks inflicted 1000+ casualties on elite regulars, teaching Americans they could fight and teaching the British nothing about how to win. The colonial intelligence network tracked every British movement while the British had no intelligence on colonial preparations. Marshal Suchet was the only French commander who succeeded in Spain — not through brilliance but through methodical pacification that won over the local population, suggesting the only counter to guerrilla warfare is legitimacy.
