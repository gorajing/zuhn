The absence of legacy banking infrastructure in Africa enabled direct adoption of mobile payments (M-PESA), bypassing checks and credit cards entirely. Safaricom's telecom monopoly solved the cold-start problem that fragments US competitors. The pattern generalizes: where incumbents are absent due to infrastructure gaps, new entrants can establish dominant platforms without displacement cost. The gap itself is the advantage.
