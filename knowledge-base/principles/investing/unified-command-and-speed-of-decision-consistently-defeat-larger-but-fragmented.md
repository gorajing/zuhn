Alexander conquered from Greece to India with 40,000 troops against millions by moving faster than enemies could react. Napoleon's primary weapon was speed — crossing Alps considered impossible, arriving before expected, preventing retreats, defeating coalitions one at a time before they could unite. His corps system gave France 10 years of military dominance because it took that long for others to adopt it. Austrian co-equal commanders who could not agree on strategy lost to Napoleon's sole authority decided in minutes. Belisarius defeated armies 5-10x his size because his forces were under unified command while opponents were tribal coalitions. The Bolsheviks captured Russia by seizing key infrastructure with a tiny disciplined force while larger factions deliberated. The advantage is temporary: Austria copied Napoleon's corps system and beat him at Aspern-Essling; the Coalition's Trachenberg Plan neutralized his personal genius by attacking only his marshals.
