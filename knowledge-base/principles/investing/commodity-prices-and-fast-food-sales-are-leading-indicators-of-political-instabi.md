Commodity price charts correlate strongly with political instability in the developing world — the Arab Spring and post-Ukraine spikes both preceded widespread unrest. Fast food sales plummeting signals consumer distress has reached the absolute bottom of the spending pyramid. Japan's and Germany's expansion was fundamentally driven by food insecurity, making agricultural dependency a leading indicator of geopolitical risk. Canada's GDP grew while GDP per capita stagnated for a decade because population growth masked declining individual prosperity. DNA replication errors accumulate silently until a critical threshold triggers disease — the same dynamic applies to institutional governance failures that prosperity masks. The pattern: track the indicators people can't fake (spending on necessities, commodity prices, per-capita vs aggregate metrics).
