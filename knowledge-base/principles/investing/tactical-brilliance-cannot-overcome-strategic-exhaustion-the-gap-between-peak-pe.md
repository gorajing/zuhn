Napoleon's 1814 campaign was tactically brilliant, winning multiple battles against larger forces, but couldn't prevent defeat because France was exhausted after 20 years of war. Germany's March 1918 Spring Offensive was its greatest success of the war; by November it had surrendered. The eight-month gap between peak success and total collapse was invisible to observers. This pattern -- brilliant tactical performance masking strategic depletion -- repeats because observers mistake local wins for systemic health, and the final collapse appears sudden despite being structurally inevitable.
