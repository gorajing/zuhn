US electricity generation has flatlined at 4,000-4,200 TWh since 2000 after a century of exponential growth, while China expanded to 10,000 TWh, creating a physical infrastructure gap that limits AI deployment capacity. The US leads in advanced chips while China leads in energy production -- whoever integrates both first wins the AI race. Solar's 99.9% cost decline since 1975 and China's 278GW annual installation versus 177GW US cumulative demonstrate that energy dominance is shifting faster than chip dominance, and near-zero marginal cost solar potentially removes resource scarcity as the fundamental driver of international conflict.
