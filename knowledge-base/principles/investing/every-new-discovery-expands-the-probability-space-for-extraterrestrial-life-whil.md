The universe has proven far more bio-friendly than assumed even a generation ago: there are more planets than stars, life emerged on Earth almost immediately after oceans formed, and organisms thrive in environments once considered sterile. Yet each expansion of possibility comes paired with epistemic humility -- Venus phosphine is a clue not a conclusion, absence of life on Mars can never be proven, the Copernican principle fails for life-dependent features due to anthropic bias, and SETI actually searches for technology not intelligence. The Fermi paradox (no aliens cohabit Earth despite 13 billion years of opportunity) constrains the optimism. For investors, this means the astrobiology and space-detection sector will grow on expanding search space, but breakthrough confirmation will be slow and forensic rather than a single eureka moment.
