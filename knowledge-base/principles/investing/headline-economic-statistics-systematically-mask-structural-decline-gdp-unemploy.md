Canada's GDP grew while GDP per capita stagnated for a decade because immigration expanded the denominator. US unemployment looks low at 3-4% but labor force participation tells the real story — millions have stopped being counted. 3.8% GDP growth coexists with record defaults because GDP increasingly measures wealthy economic activity. Record apartment construction is almost entirely luxury units alongside a 4-million affordable housing shortage. Consumer spending funds 69% of GDP but is increasingly debt-funded, meaning GDP growth measures debt accumulation rather than genuine economic health. For investors, this means the most important analysis is finding the structural reality beneath the headline numbers — the divergence between reported and experienced economic conditions is itself a leading indicator.
