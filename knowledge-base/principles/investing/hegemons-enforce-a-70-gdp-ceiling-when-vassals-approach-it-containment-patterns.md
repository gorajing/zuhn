The US has a repeating playbook for managing rising powers: when a vassal economy reaches roughly 70% of US GDP, containment mechanisms activate. Japan hit this threshold and got the Plaza Accord (35 years of stagnation). China hit it and got the trade/tech war. The mechanisms vary (currency manipulation, tariffs, export controls, corridor-building) but the pattern is consistent. IMEC binds Middle Eastern nations economically so they self-manage their region, freeing US focus for the primary rival. Saudi-Israel alignment — historically unthinkable — becomes rational once both want to be hub stations on the corridor. When investing in any country's stock market, you're making a geopolitical bet on that nation's position in this power structure. Even in a US-loses scenario, the S&P maintains ~5% returns because China needs the US consumption market.
