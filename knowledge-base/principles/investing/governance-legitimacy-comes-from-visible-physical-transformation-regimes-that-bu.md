China's authoritarian governance persists partly because citizens watch their physical world transform — 1,000 parks in Shanghai, highways from zero, cleaner air — while Americans in static cities lose optimism despite democratic freedoms. China's leadership of trained engineers who build compulsively creates a fundamentally different governance dynamic than America's lawyer-dominated elite who optimize for blocking. The insight extends beyond China: any regime (democratic or authoritarian) that delivers visible, tangible improvement buys legitimacy, while stagnation erodes even the strongest institutional frameworks.
