600 tariff changes in months created paralysis in industrial construction spending because businesses need predictable rules to commit capital. When nobody knows what policies will be in effect next week, asset market predictions become meaningless noise, and the 'Trump Always Chickens Out' trade paradoxically forces ever-larger provocations. Research shows 96% of tariff burden falls on American importers and consumers, not foreign sellers, and blanket tariffs plus selective exemptions concentrate enormous discretionary economic power in a single office.
