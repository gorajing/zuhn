Space ventures do not scale like software -- they advance through deliberate phased deployment where each completed milestone (unmanned test, crewed orbit, landing, base) de-risks the next and unlocks new economic value. Reusable hardware like Orion shifts the cost curve from disposable to durable, SpaceX proves value arrives in 5-7 year technical leaps rather than quarterly growth, and strategic assets like the Lunar Gateway create force-multiplying chokepoints (low lunar gravity for Mars departure) that justify the entire preceding investment chain. The investment implication is that space infrastructure rewards patient capital deployed at phase boundaries, not momentum chasing.
