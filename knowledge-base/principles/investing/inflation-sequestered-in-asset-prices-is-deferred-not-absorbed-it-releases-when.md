Post-2008 money printing inflated assets faster than wages, making housing and investments progressively unreachable for wage earners. This wasn't disinflation — it was inflation being held in a reservoir above the real economy. When asset holders eventually liquidate to spend, money velocity spikes and consumer inflation follows. Stock market cap exceeding GDP is the visible marker of this disconnect: the market is reflecting monetary policy, not economic fundamentals. Household net worth at 560% of GDP combined with low credit spreads means any less-than-positive outcome triggers dramatic repricing — and the inflation that was hiding in asset prices has to go somewhere.
