Corporate debt hit $14T not from productive investment but from financial engineering -- companies borrowed to buy back stock while hollowing out operations. 2025 saw record corporate bankruptcies outside a recession, with companies going bankrupt for the second time within months, revealing that cheap debt masked structural insolvency. PE firms compound this by loading acquisition debt onto portfolio companies while extracting fees regardless of outcomes, creating a system where rising rates trigger simultaneous exposure across thousands of otherwise-unrelated businesses.
