Dominant technologies almost always grew through word-of-mouth from devoted early adopters, not splashy launches. Manual recruitment works because compound weekly growth transforms tiny absolute numbers into massive scale, and early users come from consistent community presence, not one-time launch spikes. Big launches and partnerships appeal to founders because they promise scale without the emotional labor of individual recruitment, but they almost never deliver.
