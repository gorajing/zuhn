Most founders unconsciously filter out ideas that require more than two years, have been tried before, or look structurally intractable -- creating artificially empty competitive spaces for those willing to look. Tar pit ideas trap founders who skip basic research, but ideas discarded by others or born as byproducts of unrelated work (Slack from a failed game, GPS from rocketry experiments) represent systematically underexplored territory. The winning ideation strategy is to invert the default filters: seek long timelines, study discarded concepts, and build domain expertise that makes structurally hard problems approachable.
