Skills that can be mass-taught get mass-produced, driving wages toward training cost plus minimal return. Airbnb spent 5 months hiring their first employee and only hired two people in year one because early hire quality is existential. Weak hires don't just underperform — they force you to hire more people, making the organization structurally slower. Optimize your first job for the quality of people you'll work with, not the paycheck or prestige. The implication: the most valuable hires have skills that are fundamentally hard to acquire quickly.
