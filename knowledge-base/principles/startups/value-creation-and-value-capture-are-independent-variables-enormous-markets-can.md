Airlines created $195B in revenue but cumulative profits approximate zero over 100 years, while Google captured far more from a smaller market. Many major companies require monopoly status just to be profitable at all. The strategic lesson is that market size and growth are insufficient without structural mechanisms for capturing the value created -- pricing power, switching costs, or supply-side lock-in.
