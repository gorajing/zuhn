Intimidating startup ideas have less competition because most founders self-select out of them. Sears, Toys-R-Us, and Payless didn't die because of Amazon — they died because they stopped adapting, and Amazon is a conveniently blameable scapegoat. Costco stocks 4,000 SKUs versus Walmart's 140,000, making each slot fiercely contested by suppliers. The pattern: difficult-looking competitive landscapes often have fewer real competitors than easy-looking ones, because difficulty is itself a filter that most players cannot pass.
