Nike and Adidas hit record revenue at the exact moment consumer demand had already shifted. Sneaker shops printed money during the bubble while building zero actual expertise or moat. Burger King's margins jumped to 69% under PE ownership while store revenue stayed flat for five years. GoPro flooded the market with 20 models in 10 years while Insta360 quietly grew to $10B. The pattern repeats: when revenue is rising, organizations stop examining the structural health underneath -- and by the time revenue finally declines, the decay is already terminal.
