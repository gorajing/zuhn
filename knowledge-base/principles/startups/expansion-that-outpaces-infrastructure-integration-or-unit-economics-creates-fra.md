Webvan expanded to multiple cities before understanding unit economics. Krispy Kreme's ubiquity killed the novelty driving demand. The Mongol Empire splintered because conquest outpaced institutional development. Rome collapsed when expansion outpaced cultural integration. The Assyrians extended beyond their road network. The pattern is universal: growth beyond your operational, cultural, or logistical reach creates a brittle structure that cannot self-correct.
