AI-native founders already grow 20% faster while raising less capital, and tiny teams (2 people, 50M won/month) can generate revenue that previously required large organizations. But AI only works if complete data architecture is in place first -- without it there is nothing for AI to operate on. The scarce skill shifts from writing code to orchestrating agent teams and from ML expertise to deep domain knowledge about what 'good' looks like in a given profession. Junior engineering roles are not coming back, and the speed pressure on incumbents is existential: if a startup can ship in weeks, you cannot ask competitors to wait 9 months.
