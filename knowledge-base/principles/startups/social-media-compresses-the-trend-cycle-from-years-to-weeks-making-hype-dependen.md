TikTok and Instagram have compressed the discovery-adoption-saturation-backlash cycle from years to weeks, turning any trend-dependent product or business into a ticking clock. The Stanley tumbler went from coveted status symbol to symbol of overconsumption in 18 months; sneaker resale shops that seemed invincible collapsed when the bubble burst; collectibles follow financial bubble dynamics at social media speed. Businesses built on hype-driven demand face structural fragility because the same viral mechanics that drive adoption equally drive rejection, and revenue during the boom masks the absence of any durable competitive advantage.
