The most resilient business models generate revenue regardless of economic direction because both conditions trigger demand. Self-storage profits during booms (overconsumption creates overflow) and busts (downsizing creates displacement) -- America has 50,000 facilities serving 300M people vs. Europe's 5,000 for 750M, a direct bet on American consumption patterns. Diners survive unchanged since the 1940s because their customers actively want processed cheese and frozen hash browns at any hour -- recession makes them affordable havens while prosperity makes them nostalgic comfort. Gun retail thrives on fear-of-regulation cycles that spike demand regardless of the broader economy. The common thread is serving a need that is amplified by change itself rather than by any particular direction of change, making the business immune to the cycle-timing problem that kills most operators.
