Founders should decompose their venture into testable hypotheses, rank them by catastrophic potential, and test the most dangerous ones first with minimal spend. LA validated shade balls with three $5 kiddie pools before spending $34M. DoorDash launched with a landing page in under an hour. The principle is to spend the least when you know the least, increase stakes only as validated learning reduces uncertainty, and never build a prototype before mapping business risks.
