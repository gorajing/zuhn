History's most successful empire-builders (Genghis Khan, Toussaint L'Ouverture) converted conquered enemies into their own officers because they valued capability over loyalty. Loyalty-first hiring filters for liars or zealots, destroying an organization's capacity for honest self-assessment, while merit-based promotion systems enable organizations to absorb talent from anywhere. The pattern applies directly to startups: hire geniuses when you find them regardless of whether you have a defined role, because great people create their own value. When loyalty becomes the primary selection criterion, fear-based compliance replaces principled dissent.
