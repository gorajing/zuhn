Costco pays $21/hour (2x retail average) and gets employees three times more productive than competitors. One A+ hire at $170K outperforms five B-players at $100K each in knowledge work. Moving up the leverage hierarchy from labor to code/content multiplies output per unit of input exponentially. Elite developer talent follows power-law distributions, not normal curves. The implication: talent premium is not a luxury — it's the highest-leverage investment available, and underpaying is the most expensive mistake a scaling company can make.
