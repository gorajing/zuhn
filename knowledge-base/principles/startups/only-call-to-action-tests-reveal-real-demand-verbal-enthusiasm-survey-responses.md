If users won't put down five dollars for your idea, their verbal enthusiasm is not real validation. Friends give positive feedback because they want to be nice, not because they want your product. Everyone recognizes desktop clutter as a problem, but nobody's in enough pain to pay for a solution — recognizable problems and painful problems are completely different categories. Customer interviews reveal preferences but only pre-orders, sign-ups, and payments predict demand. Showing what people currently do with bad tools proves demand more convincingly than market size numbers. The optimal validation cadence is 3-5 customers per day with 24-hour follow-up — not 3-5 per week. At least 70% of YC's top 100 came from ideas noticed organically because brainstorming systematically produces tar pit ideas.
