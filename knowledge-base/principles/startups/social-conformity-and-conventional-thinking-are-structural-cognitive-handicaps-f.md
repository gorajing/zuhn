The social skills that help you fit in — reading consensus, calibrating to group opinion — actively work against generating unique startup ideas. Independent-mindedness gives founders access to true insights that conventionally-minded people are structurally blocked from perceiving. The beliefs you do not even think to question, not the ones you debate, are what truly limit potential. Dismissing new ideas is a cultural habit, not human nature. The antidote is surrounding yourself with people working on similarly ambitious projects who can distinguish ugly ducklings from bad ideas.
