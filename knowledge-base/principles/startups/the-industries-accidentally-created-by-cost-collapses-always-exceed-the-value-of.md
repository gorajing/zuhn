Modern finance from sail, consumer credit from automobiles, global manufacturing from containers — the secondary industries created when a technology collapses costs are always larger than the original. Carnegie's steel made more from railroads than railroad companies themselves. When an industry's value chain has cascading dependencies, optimizing one link compounds gains through every downstream step.
