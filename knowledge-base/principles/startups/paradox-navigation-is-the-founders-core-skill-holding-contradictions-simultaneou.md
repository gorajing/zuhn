Great founders hold contradictions that would paralyze normal thinkers: fully believing in the mission while planning for failure, being insanely impatient to start while accepting that hard problems take years, maintaining both optimism and paranoia. Founder excellence is not single-dimension optimization but the ability to oscillate between dream and pragmatism, boldness and humility, speed and patience. This paradox-navigation capacity distinguishes founders from both professional managers (who optimize one dimension) and dreamers (who refuse tradeoffs).
