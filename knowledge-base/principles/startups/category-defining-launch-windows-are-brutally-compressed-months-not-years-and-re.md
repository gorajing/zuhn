Historical data shows viable launch periods for category-defining products are extremely narrow — 11 months for social networks, 30 months for mobile social. Companies that capture these windows (like Rubrik completing its subscription transition before the 2024 IPO window) succeed because they were operationally ready before the window opened. The implication is that founders must prepare for timing windows they anticipate but cannot control, matching the gap between what is technically possible and how things are still being done.
