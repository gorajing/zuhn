LVMH and Kering dominate through an illusion of brand independence -- consumers buy from 'different' brands that all feed the same parent. Carnival and Royal Caribbean own dozens of sub-brands, isolating disasters like Costa Concordia. The strategic principle is that extreme brand diversification creates a firewall where failure in one brand cannot cascade to the portfolio, while the invisible parent captures all the economics.
