Almost every execution failure traces back to diffused ownership. Having six DRIs on something is the same as having zero -- like asking two friends to water your plant guarantees it dies. The antidote is single-threaded ownership paired with bias for action: making a wrong decision and adjusting a week later beats three months of deliberation while the market shifts. Organizational structure must match this reality -- fluid 90-day resource pools where 10-15% of people move between teams each cycle, no fixed timelines for research when breakthroughs require 24-hour integration, and functional orgs over GM structures when transformation demands unified capability. Culture enforces this through collective habits and feedback loops, not wall posters, and teams bleed critical information daily when members silently manage impressions instead of speaking up.
