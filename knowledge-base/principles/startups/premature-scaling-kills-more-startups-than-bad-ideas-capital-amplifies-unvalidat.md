Quirky raised $185M and went bankrupt because capital scaled an unvalidated assumption, not a proven market. Fireship got 3,121 free signups but 0.3% paid conversion, proving free distribution masks PMF failure. The most expensive period of a failing startup is the denial phase after the founder already knows it won't work. Having a great product is necessary but nowhere near sufficient — scaling prematurely without validation evidence converts runway into waste.
