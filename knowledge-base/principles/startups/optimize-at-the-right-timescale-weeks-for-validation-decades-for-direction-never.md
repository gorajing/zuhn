A week is long enough to validate ideas and ship products. Ten years captures true strategic direction. Quarterly planning is a deeply useless middle ground where cohorts don't bake and real outcomes aren't visible. This creates a barbell approach to time horizons: rapid iteration on the short end (spending months making an app 'perfect' is self-deception) and patient directional bets on the long end. The correct operating sequence is not 'plan quarterly, review quarterly' but 'validate weekly, course-correct monthly, reaffirm direction annually.' The fatal error is optimizing at the quarterly timescale — too slow for learning, too fast for strategy.
