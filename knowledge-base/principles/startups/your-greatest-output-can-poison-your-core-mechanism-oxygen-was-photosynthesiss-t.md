Oxygen was originally just a toxic waste product of plants splitting water. Plants produced so much of it that their key enzyme RuBisCo started malfunctioning, since it evolved for a low-oxygen world. Plants couldn't swap out RuBisCo — they'd built everything around it — so they evolved entire metabolic pathways to manage the damage instead. This maps directly to startups: rapid growth creates customer support burden that slows development, viral content attracts bad-faith users that poison community, and successful features create technical debt that constrains the next iteration.
