Bill Anderson argues bureaucracy is produced by the composition of layers, functional silos, and annual budgets. Every organizational check has a hidden cost that proposers never justify. Larger organizations face exponentially higher compliance stakes that create proportionally greater innovation friction. The rising importance of software in every industry means this cost is increasing over time. Eliminating bureaucracy requires restructuring, not motivational speeches.
