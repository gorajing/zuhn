The founders who win are not smarter or better resourced but faster at converting signal into action. The 3-minute rule (if it seems interesting and takes less than 3 minutes, do it now), YouTube shipping experiments overnight without knowing if they would work, and Genspark pivoting to $36M ARR in 45 days all demonstrate the same pattern. The biggest founder regret is universally 'I should have moved three months earlier' -- not 'I moved too fast.' Executing quickly to gather real information consistently beats prolonged analysis because the information landscape changes faster than any plan can accommodate.
