Johns-Manville acquired companies with asbestos-free alternatives to silence anyone who could expose the danger. Tudor maintained his ice monopoly by systematically undercutting competitors until bankruptcy. The US government politically decides which foreign manufacturers can sell to civilians. Taco Bell outperforms peers because Yum Brands concentrated resources on its winner while letting weaker brands stagnate. In each case, the incumbent's defense isn't building a superior product — it's eliminating or reshaping the competitive playing field itself.
