The strongest competitive advantages come not from solving one hard problem but from solving many interdependent problems simultaneously. ASML's monopoly requires mirrors smooth to 2.3 atoms AND tin droplets hit 50,000 times/second AND 13.5nm light generation; jet engine blades must optimize temperature, force, oxidation, creep, weight, and manufacturing precision all at once. Robinhood stacked three individually contrarian bets into a single offering no incumbent could quickly replicate. The integration itself -- not any single component -- is what creates defensibility.
