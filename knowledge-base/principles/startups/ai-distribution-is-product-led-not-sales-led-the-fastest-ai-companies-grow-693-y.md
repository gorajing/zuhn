The fastest AI companies reach $100M revenue faster than any SaaS predecessor, driven by product demand rather than sales spend, running at $500K-1M ARR per FTE versus the old $400K benchmark. Horizontal consumer AI agents grow explosively but face acquisition by big tech because once agentic capabilities commoditize, distribution becomes the deciding moat. Meanwhile, incumbents are more vulnerable than they appear — Amazon's search quality degraded enough for DoorDash to compete on trust, and Robinhood's zero-commission gave them 3-4 years before incumbents could respond because free is categorically different from cheap. AI products follow a predictable distribution ladder: X small communities → AI news → Instagram → creators → Telegram. The pattern: let the product create the pull, then exploit the incumbent's structural inability to match your pricing or quality.
