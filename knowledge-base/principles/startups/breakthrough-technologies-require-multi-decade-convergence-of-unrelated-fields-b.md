Transformative technologies consistently require 30-200 years between initial concept and commercial viability because they depend on convergence of advances in unrelated domains. EUV lithography took 30 years from proof-of-concept to production because it needed fusion mirror research, laser plasma, and precision manufacturing to mature independently. This convergence pattern means being right about a technology's potential but wrong about its timing turns you into free R&D for competitors — the 'why now' question that separates viable ventures from premature ones is really asking which prerequisite fields have finally converged.
