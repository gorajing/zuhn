Entry-level delegation assigns tasks; advanced delegation exports the leader's internal preferences and decision criteria as repeatable algorithms that others can execute independently. The tactical stack includes voice notes (2-3x faster than text, captures nuance), hiring dedicated 'process people' to compensate for personal weaknesses rather than fixing them, and training yourself to count to 30 before intervening when others struggle. The upfront cost of delegation is always real -- it IS faster to do it yourself -- but accepting that cost is the only path to compounding leverage, because leaders who derive identity from being indispensable cap the entire team's capability.
