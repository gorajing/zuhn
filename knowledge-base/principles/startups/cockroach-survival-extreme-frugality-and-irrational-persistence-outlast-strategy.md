Startups die from running out of money, not from running out of ideas. Burn rate is the only runway lever fully in the founder's control -- pay yourself less, get the worse office, read the bank statement monthly. Nota AI survived 7-8 years of market mismatch; Presto's founder lived with mice on under $100. Conrad had zero doubt Rippling would work, so his only job was preventing the team from quitting during two years of nothing working. But cockroach survival is not blind stubbornness: when the world changes, change the plan transparently with investors and team, and check whether investors even want you to keep grinding before assuming they do.
