Streaming killed piracy not by being cheaper but by being easier. Amazon Go solved a checkout problem nobody had. Self-checkout failed because it transferred labor to customers without compensating convenience. When streaming services fragment and raise prices, consumers return to piracy because the convenience equation reverses. The pattern is universal: whichever option requires the least effort from the customer wins, and every competitive strategy that increases friction -- even in pursuit of cost savings or quality -- is fighting against the strongest force in consumer behavior.
