Products that empower users to create and share build self-reinforcing distribution loops at zero marketing cost. Sweeney shipped a game editor alongside ZZT, turning players into creators and establishing the tools-plus-product pattern that sustained Epic for decades. YouTube's embed feature turned every MySpace profile into a free distribution channel where the logo spread and curiosity converted viewers into uploaders. Insta360 invested in mobile-first editing because users with content trapped on hard drives could not become evangelists. The principle: lower the creation barrier and your users become your distribution engine.
