With 64% of people mistrusting journalists and 74% trusting 'someone like me,' the trust advantage has shifted from institutional mastheads to individual creators who show their work. Creators who demonstrate expertise through doing-and-sharing, maintain intellectual honesty (publicly listing bad takes), and expose their reasoning process build deeper trust than branded media hiding behind editorial boards. This is not a temporary trend but a structural realignment driven by audiences recognizing PR fingerprints in institutional content.
