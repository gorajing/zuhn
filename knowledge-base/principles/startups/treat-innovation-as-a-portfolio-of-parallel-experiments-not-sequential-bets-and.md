Oxygen — the gas that enabled all animal life — was just a waste byproduct of photosynthesis. Newton's alchemy obsession was 'wasted' time, but the same disposition produced his physics — you can't separate the wins from the losses. Innovation engines should prototype and test business models to reduce uncertainty, not write business plans that create false confidence. Business models expire faster than CEO tenures, forcing systematic invention capabilities. Industry-centric strategy tools like Porter's Five Forces break down when companies span multiple industries. Startup success requires being right when everyone else is wrong, which demands aggressive independent-mindedness. Cohort networks from innovation programs often outvalue the curriculum. The pattern: diversify experiments, accept that most will fail, and watch for the byproduct that's more valuable than the intended product.
