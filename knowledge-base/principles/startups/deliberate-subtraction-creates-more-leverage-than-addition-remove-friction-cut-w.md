Stop doing low-value activities rather than adding new ones to unlock outsized productivity. Don't just push harder — also remove friction on the other side of the equation. From eliminating unnecessary meetings to questioning whether a task should exist at all before automating it, the leverage of subtraction is systematically underestimated because the human bias toward action makes removal feel like inaction.
