For reversible decisions, the cost of deliberation almost always exceeds the cost of being wrong and correcting. Phrases like 'let's revisit later' and 'we need more data' feel reasonable but systematically introduce hesitation that calcifies into slow-moving culture. After you know what needs to be done, more information-gathering is socially acceptable procrastination — and in a startup crisis, waiting for perfect information narrows options faster than making an imperfect early decision.
