Once a minimum IQ threshold is met, determination predicts startup outcomes far better than raw intelligence. Success and failure look identical during the middle years, and the only differentiator is who keeps going. Smart founders make better stories but determined founders make better outcomes -- investors should weight grit over brilliance. The 'never give up' principle has a crucial exception: persistence in the wrong domain is destructive, signaling the need to redirect rather than quit entirely.
