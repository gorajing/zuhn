Manual execution reveals which steps and questions actually matter, knowledge that spec-writing and upfront design systematically miss. Start with a small useful hack and let it grow; most big programs started as throwaway scripts. Prototyping a subset beats spec-writing because it keeps the problem small enough for one mind to encompass. Stay in low-fidelity block frames as long as possible because the first detailed design becomes an anchor that limits all subsequent exploration. In early startups, product quality is redefined as total user experience quality, where personal attentiveness compensates for product incompleteness.
