When facing important decisions, explicitly telling people you need to pause and think it through from all perspectives produces better outcomes than the instinct to demonstrate decisiveness. Leaders face a structural paradox: easy decisions never reach the CEO desk, and the value of experience lies in navigating close calls where intelligence alone is insufficient. Developing the discipline to stop, say 'I'm taking this seriously but I need to think,' and then respond after genuine reflection is a core leadership skill that compounds with practice.
