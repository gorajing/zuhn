Oxygen was a waste product of photosynthesis that became the atmosphere enabling all animal life. This pattern repeats across innovation: the most valuable output of a system is often unintended. Java's flaws spawned five successful languages; Newton's alchemy obsession came from the same disposition that produced his physics. The implication is that you cannot separate the wins from the losses, and filtering for only 'productive' exploration eliminates the conditions that produce breakthroughs.
