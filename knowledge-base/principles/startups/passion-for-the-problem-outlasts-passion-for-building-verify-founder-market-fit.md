Many AI founders fail because they build impressive demos that don't solve real painful jobs. Before chasing product-market fit, verify founder-market fit: domain expertise and personal stakes that make the problem sustainably compelling. Founders who define themselves by a life mission rather than a specific product can pivot repeatedly without existential crisis, while those attached to their solution enter the most expensive phase of a failing startup -- denial.
