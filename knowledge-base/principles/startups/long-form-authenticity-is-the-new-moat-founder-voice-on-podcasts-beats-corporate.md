The 80-year reign of synthetic corporate communication is over. Professional CEOs trained to say nothing lose to founder CEOs interesting enough to fill 3 hours on Joe Rogan. Podcasts and essays operate on two axes simultaneously — distribution AND credibility — which is why their returns feel disproportionate. The internet's 12-hour OODA loop causes traditional media's 24-hour cycle to perpetually chase, making new media inherently offensive. Each platform requires a native expert (a16z hired an 18-year-old for Instagram, up 35% MoM) because crossposting is malpractice. Say everything you think, but say it long-form where context survives.
