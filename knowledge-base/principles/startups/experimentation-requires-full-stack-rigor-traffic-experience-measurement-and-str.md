Business experiments need all three layers — traffic, experience, and measurement — to produce trustworthy signals. Change only one variable per A/B test. Ensure your call-to-action directly measures what your hypothesis claims. Account for environmental conditions. Most critically, without a structured process to capture what experiments taught you, you run the same tests twice. Test cards and learning cards formalize the experiment-learn loop into a repeatable system.
