Effective feedback cultures are built on a specific sequence: lead with empathy (articulating where someone is coming from, not agreeing), then assert standards clearly. Most leaders fail by being too nice (ruinous empathy) or by stalling on feedback while resentment compounds. The structural fix is to frame work as learning, invite criticism of yourself first, replace manager-driven reviews with peer feedback loops, and paint the future vision rather than dwelling on past mistakes -- because ironic process theory means highlighting errors causes repetition.
