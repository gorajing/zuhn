Spending less money gives you more time, more negotiating power, and more freedom to let ideas evolve. Financial constraints force companies to fix dysfunctions that abundant capital makes invisible. From living on under $100 to survive 5 years with zero revenue, to Bob Klein's principle of tight group, little money, little time — constraints create focus that abundance destroys, and extreme frugality is the best default operating mode regardless of market conditions.
