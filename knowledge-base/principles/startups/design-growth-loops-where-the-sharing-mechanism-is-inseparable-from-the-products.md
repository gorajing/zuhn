Exponential growth comes from stacking multiple self-reinforcing growth loops, not from optimizing individual acquisition channels. Sean Parker's virality framework breaks viral potential into payload, frequency, and conversion rate, with different products winning on different axes. YouTube's embed feature turned MySpace into a free distribution channel at zero marketing cost. AI products should build self-distributing features in three archetypes: outputs that spread awareness (viral artifacts), features that make users show off usage (status loops), and systems that improve with more data (data flywheels). Owned channels like virality and UGC should be prioritized over rented channels like paid social, because competitors can always outbid you on rented platforms.
