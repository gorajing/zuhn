YC speakers independently and consistently advise the same things: launch fast, listen to users, iterate. These converge not because they're clichés but because they're the empirical truths that survive contact with reality. The Calvin Cycle produces 6 G3P but must reinvest 5 to keep running — 83% overhead is the structural cost of a self-sustaining system. Startups have the same economics: most of your effort maintains the cycle, and the 'net output' that drives growth is a thin slice of total work. Culture is the collective habits that dictate day-to-day execution, and it changes only through iterative feedback loops, never by decree. Real entrepreneurs don't need to ask what to build — they can't stop thinking about the problem.
