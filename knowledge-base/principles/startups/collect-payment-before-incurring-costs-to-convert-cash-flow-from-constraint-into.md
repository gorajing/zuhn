Businesses that collect revenue before delivering value (subscriptions, membership fees, annual agreements) achieve structural cash flow advantages that compound into competitive moats. Netflix's pivot from per-rental to flat monthly subscriptions achieved 90% conversion on day one by eliminating per-use decision friction. Costco's membership model filters for affluent customers who spend more and renew at 90%. The principle extends beyond pricing: mandatory membership fees force companies to continuously justify value, aligning long-term incentives with customer satisfaction rather than transaction extraction.
