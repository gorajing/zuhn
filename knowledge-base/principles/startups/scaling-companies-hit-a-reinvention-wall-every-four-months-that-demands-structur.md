Fast-growing startups do not scale linearly -- they hit periodic walls where the current structure, team, and strategy simultaneously break, forcing a complete reinvention of how the company operates. Harvey's CEO reinvents every 4 months because AI shifts make plans obsolete; Weinberg hits the same wall requiring new leadership structures; Genspark's $36M ARR pivot happened in 45 days. The founders who survive treat these walls as predictable phase transitions rather than crises.
