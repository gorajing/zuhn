The winning attack on a monopoly is to make its business model unviable rather than blocking specific attacks. Combining multiple individually contrarian bets (Robinhood: zero commissions + mobile-first + populist brand) creates an offering no incumbent can replicate quickly because it requires abandoning their own proven model. AI-native startups win most reliably at greenfield moments — new company creation or inflection points where customers must switch systems anyway. Industries whose profitability depends on making processes boring, difficult, and time-consuming are most vulnerable to LLM disruption. A barbershop's locality limits growth but also limits competition — removing constraints exposes you to the whole world. Some products are too cheap to bother replacing ($50/month janitorial services problem) — nobody optimizes them, creating zombie markets that are stable but uninvestable.
