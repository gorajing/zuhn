Punishment-based cultures cause employees to exert only the minimum effort needed to avoid being fired, forfeiting all discretionary effort above that bar. Discretionary effort is the delta between what people must do and what they choose to do, and it flows exclusively toward leaders who recognize contributions. Formal evaluation cycles that reward performance (rather than rewarding those who lobby for raises) protect meritocratic culture, while publicly recognizing when team members present better ideas builds a culture where the best idea wins regardless of position.
