Across multiple sources and frameworks, the single most consistent finding is that sales effectiveness is 80% conviction and energy, not methodology. A convicted salesperson naturally sounds right; perfect technique rings hollow without genuine belief. This applies at every stage: cold outreach (pattern interrupts work because authenticity is rare), discovery (leading with perspective beats leading with product), demos (personalization signals genuine care), and pricing (Starbucks-cashier energy works because certainty transfers). The implication is that sales training should focus first on building genuine conviction in the product and only second on technique.
