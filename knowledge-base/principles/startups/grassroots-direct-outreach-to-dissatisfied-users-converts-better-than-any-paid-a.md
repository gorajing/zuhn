Finding people who publicly complain about competitors, building genuine trust, then offering your alternative outperforms traditional ad campaigns. Combined with building-in-public, sponsoring niche communities, daily cost-per-customer experiments, and even offline tactics like handing out flyers, these scrappy direct methods compound because they build trust rather than just awareness. The key insight is that social distribution costs zero — the asymmetry most businesses fail to exploit is that the cheapest acquisition channel is almost always some form of authentic, direct human connection.
