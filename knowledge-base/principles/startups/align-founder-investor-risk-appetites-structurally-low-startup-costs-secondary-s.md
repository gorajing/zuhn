VCs invest in startups over ordinary businesses because equity-based returns through IPO/acquisition prevent the agency problem of founders self-dealing through inflated expenses. Letting founders take some money off the table during fundraising aligns their risk appetite with VCs rather than opposing it — a founder with personal financial security makes better long-term decisions. Low startup costs let founders diversify across multiple ventures over time. There are no successful value investors in startups — trying to find the lowest-valuation, highest-revenue deals is a proven losing strategy because early-stage investing is fundamentally an art. A full-time internal fundraiser after Series B outperforms investment bankers at half the cost and dilution. There's a massive gap between $20K seed checks and $2M Series A where promising startups can't find capital.
