The cost of reaching audiences on social platforms is zero for distribution — an unprecedented asymmetry most businesses fail to exploit. Frame your product as a solution to a problem, not a toy — a video framed as fun got 41M views but terrible conversion, while solution-framed videos converted far better. Target the person who pays, not the person who uses. Ask customers how they describe your product, then use their exact words as marketing copy. Hire content creators directly (5-10K followers, email in bio) — agency creators have the arbitrage already captured. A clown gets attention but has no fan club — trusted small creators outperform viral large ones. Before launching, work for free in exchange for testimonials because proof makes selling unnecessary.
