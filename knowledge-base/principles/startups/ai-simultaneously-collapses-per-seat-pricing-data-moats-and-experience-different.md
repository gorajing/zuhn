AI breaks per-seat SaaS because the new model charges for outcomes, not users provisioned. AI makes it trivial to port data and clone product experiences, eroding brand as a meaningful B2B moat. Adding a GPT wrapper to an existing product is a dead end — the entire user experience must be rebuilt around what AI can now do. Five-year plans are impossible when capabilities change monthly. Lower barriers to build simultaneously lower barriers for competitors, making speed the primary differentiator. There is no moat in AI — the best defense is offense, constantly pushing out new products faster than anyone else. The biggest AI opportunity is not building agents but building the infrastructure every agent will need. Systems of record must choose: charge for data and give away agentic workflows, or charge for workflows and make data free. Enterprises are now buying AI faster than digital natives because board pressure creates top-down urgency that bypasses careful testing cycles. AI startups either help professionals do their jobs, replace tasks entirely, or make previously impossible work feasible.
