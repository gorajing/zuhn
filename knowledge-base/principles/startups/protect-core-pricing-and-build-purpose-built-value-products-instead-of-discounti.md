Discounting core products destroys long-term pricing power and trains customers to wait for sales, yet the pressure to compete on price is constant. The companies that sustain premium positioning create architecturally separate value offerings that are profitable at their price point rather than discounting flagship products. Taco Bell never discounts the Crunch Wrap but builds 79-cent cheese roll-ups that are profitable at that price. Under Armour's premium pricing built retailer confidence for years, while competitors who discounted core items destroyed their ability to ever charge full price again. The principle is structural: value-tier products must be designed from scratch for their price point, not derived from marking down premium offerings.
