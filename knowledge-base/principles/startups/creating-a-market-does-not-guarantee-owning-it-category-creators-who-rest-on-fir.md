GoPro created and cornered the action camera category, had strong margins and brand recognition — stock still dropped 95% because the market was too small for a standalone company, and Insta360 stayed quietly product-first and grew to $10B. GoPro launched 9 generations and 20 models in 10 years, flooding the market with trivial improvements while opening the door. The Stanley tumbler went from coveted status symbol to symbol of overconsumption within 18 months as the same social dynamics that drove adoption drove rejection. During the sneaker bubble, revenue masked the absence of any real expertise or moat — when the market crashed, most stores went bankrupt. Salesforce succeeded not by being the first SaaS company but by defining 'software is over' and becoming permanently associated with the cloud movement. AI-native startups win most reliably at greenfield moments rather than trying to displace incumbents. The lesson: category creation is a one-time event that provides a window, not a wall.
