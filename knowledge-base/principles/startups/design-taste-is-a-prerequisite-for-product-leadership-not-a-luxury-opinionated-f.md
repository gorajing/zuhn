You cannot manage a process intended to produce beautiful things without knowing what beautiful is. Real taste means having an honest internal barometer for whether your product creates genuine user value, not just whether it looks good in a demo. Design tenets must be opinionated and falsifiable statements that guide real trade-off decisions, unlike vague principles no one would disagree with. Even early adopters now expect a baseline of design quality, so MVPs that look half-baked get abandoned before they can generate useful feedback. Apple never A/B tested button colors -- a taste-based judgment from one executive settled it.
