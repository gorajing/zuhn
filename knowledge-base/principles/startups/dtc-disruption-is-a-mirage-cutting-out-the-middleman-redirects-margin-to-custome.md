Casper, Warby Parker, and Dollar Shave Club promised that eliminating retail would lower costs. Instead, they replaced wholesale margins with massive advertising budgets and ended up spending MORE on customer acquisition than retailers spent on shelf space. Casper spent 73-91% of gross profit on advertising and lost $200 per mattress. The final irony: Casper found retail growing 74% YoY — the channel they set out to destroy became their only hope. Every dessert fad (cupcakes, froyo, cookies) follows the same arc: trendsetter creates category, copycats flood, saturation crashes.
