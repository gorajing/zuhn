What you say about a terminated employee at the company meeting becomes their reputation, because every person in your company will eventually be called as a reference for that person. The silent audience watching how departures are handled determines whether management decisions strengthen or erode organizational trust. Demotions create structural resentment from peers who earn less for doing more, and never apologizing creates compounding credibility debt. The principle extends to all leadership moments: the team's interpretation of how you treat individuals defines culture more than any stated values.
