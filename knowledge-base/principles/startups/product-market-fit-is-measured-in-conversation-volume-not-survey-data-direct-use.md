PMF is not a strategy document -- it is the volume of genuine user conversations you are having. Face-to-face interviews reveal pain points that forms and surveys cannot surface. A thousand complaints from dedicated users is healthier than silence or universal satisfaction, because complaints mean engagement. The best startups originate from founders solving their own daily friction, not market analysis. Even in deep tech, conviction transfers through sustained direct evidence: Alpha Chip won over angry skeptical chip designers by showing data weekly for years, not through one-time demos. Months of intensive user research before writing code consistently produces faster PMF than build-fast-iterate approaches.
