The most resilient companies grow not by entering unrelated markets but by systematically expanding into adjacent problems that share the same customer relationship, data, or infrastructure. Intuit went from accounting to invoicing to payroll to credit, each step naturally next to the last. Palo Alto Networks asked which adjacent billion-dollar markets could be consolidated onto their existing infrastructure. Disney owns the full pipeline from film through theme parks, merchandise, and streaming. The pattern is consistent: each expansion leverages existing trust, data, or distribution in a way that a new entrant cannot replicate, creating compounding moats from sequential adjacencies.
