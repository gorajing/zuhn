Costco stocks 4,000 SKUs versus Walmart's 140,000, making each slot fiercely contested by suppliers willing to cut prices. Apple's refusal to make large acquisitions is its competitive advantage. In-N-Out's real product is consistency, not burgers. The counterintuitive principle is that strategic constraint -- deliberately offering less -- creates more pricing power, quality control, and supplier leverage than breadth.
