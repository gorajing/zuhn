Bureaucracy is not a cultural failure -- it is the inevitable structural output of layers, silos, and professional managers who import the processes they were trained in. The antidote is deliberate org design: delay hiring until demand is undeniable, hire people you already trust to reduce vetting risk, build recruiting pipelines with the same rigor as sales funnels, and balance leadership teams 50/50 between experienced operators and high-slope generalists. Growing headcount without proportionally growing the number of independent initiatives creates bloat, not leverage.
