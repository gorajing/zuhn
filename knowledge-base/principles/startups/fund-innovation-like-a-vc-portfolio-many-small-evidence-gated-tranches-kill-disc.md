Internal innovation should follow venture capital portfolio logic: many small parallel bets filtered by evidence at each stage, not executive intuition or annual lump-sum budgets. The portfolio math only works if you do not pre-filter by apparent promise and if you exit failures fast — without kill discipline, zombie projects consume resources and produce nothing. Framing innovation spend as a call option rather than a cost center makes it harder for CFOs to cut because it reuses their own valuation logic.
