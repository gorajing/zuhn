Companies that remove all friction from consumption create switching costs based on effort aversion rather than product superiority. Subscriptions decouple payment pain from consumption moments, making customers unable to evaluate per-use value. Once inertia replaces active choice, the business model shifts from delivering value to exploiting the gap between retention rates and actual satisfaction. This pattern appears in streaming (subscription numbness), BNPL (debt that does not feel like debt), captive venues (airports, theme parks inflating prices), and auto-renewal traps where revenue depends on inability to cancel rather than desire to continue.
