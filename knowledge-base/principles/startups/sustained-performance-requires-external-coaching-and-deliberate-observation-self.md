There is a fundamental distinction between teaching (depositing knowledge) and coaching (observing real performance and walking through iterative change). Even world-class performers like Gawande hit invisible plateaus after a decade that only an external observer can identify -- 20 minutes of outside feedback after a single operation he thought went perfectly. The structural fix is deliberate practice with external feedback loops: educate, exercise in real settings, then reflect with accountability. Leaders must also resist the instinct to intervene (count to 30 before grabbing the instruments) so that the people they develop can actually learn through struggle.
