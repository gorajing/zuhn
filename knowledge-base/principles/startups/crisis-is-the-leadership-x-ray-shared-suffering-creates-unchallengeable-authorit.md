Crisis does not build leaders so much as reveal what was already there. Alexander could strip and show his scars at Opis because shared suffering created authority no speech alone could manufacture. When COVID cut Bolt's revenue by 85%, choosing salary cuts over layoffs kept the team intact and enabled tripling market share while competitors were paralyzed. During GameStop, Tenev succeeded by focusing on the two CEO-only tasks (public communication and emergency fundraising) because he already knew exactly who his best people were -- the crisis didn't create that knowledge, it tested it. Altman's proudest moment during the OpenAI board crisis was discovering his executive team could run the company without him. The real challenge is never the acute crisis itself but the psychological rebuilding on day 60 that nobody talks about.
