Franchisors extract value through ingredient markups, mandatory promotions, and royalty structures that optimize corporate revenue at the expense of franchisee unit economics. Burger King mandated $1 Whoppers that generated royalties for HQ but forced negative margins on operators, while Domino's makes most of its corporate profit selling cheese and dough at markup to its own network. This misalignment means franchise brand quality degrades as operators cut corners to survive, creating a doom loop where the brand promise of consistency erodes from within.
