Leaders who operate on autopilot normalize bad decisions, entrench assumptions, and lose the ability to course-correct. The antidote is a set of deliberate rituals: annual strategy blow-up sessions where the explicit goal is to destroy your own declared strategy (Intuit), asking for criticism of yourself before criticizing others to build trust and credibility, framing work as learning and requesting input to create psychological necessity for voice, painting future visions instead of pointing out past failures (ironic process theory means telling someone what they did wrong makes them repeat it), and gradually shaping belief systems over months so major decisions feel inevitable rather than shocking. Confronting cost directly -- reading every combat injury name, never letting policy become abstract -- prevents the bureaucratic indifference that creeps in when leaders insulate themselves from consequences.
