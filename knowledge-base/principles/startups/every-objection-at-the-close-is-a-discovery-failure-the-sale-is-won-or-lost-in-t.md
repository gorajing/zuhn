The doctor frame (diagnose before prescribing), the breadcrumb technique (drilling into vague answers), pre-handling objections through early qualification, and BANT all point to the same principle: the quality of your discovery determines your close rate. Asking 'have you talked to your partner?' and 'have you set budget aside?' in the first conversation eliminates the objections that kill deals in the last conversation. The deprivation technique (surfacing everything they've tried and failed at) creates urgency that no closing technique can manufacture from scratch. Sales conversations should be 80% questions and 20% answers, not the reverse.
