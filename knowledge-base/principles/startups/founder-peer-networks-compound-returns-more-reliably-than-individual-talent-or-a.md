Tight-knit groups of founders and early employees at breakout companies go on to create the next generation of billion-dollar companies, forming collaborative networks that compound over decades. Structured peer groups of 6-8 founders at similar stages consistently outperform any individual advisor, and the loyalty cycle between team sacrifice and founder persistence creates a self-reinforcing engine that sustains companies through years of zero revenue. Your probability of success correlates directly with whether your closest relationships actively push you toward your goals.
