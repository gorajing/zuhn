Choosing monthly active users over registered users prevented the vanity-metric trap that killed Facebook's competitors, while a misselected North Star Metric causes teams to optimize the wrong behavior entirely. The users who drive growth metrics are on the edge of churning or reactivating, not your most engaged users -- building great product means optimizing for power users, but driving growth means optimizing for the marginal user who barely engages. Finding the one early action retained users share and rebuilding onboarding around it is the highest-ROI growth intervention.
