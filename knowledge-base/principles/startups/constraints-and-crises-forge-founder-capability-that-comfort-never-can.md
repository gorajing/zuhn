Tight budgets, existential crises, competitive backgrounds, and even personal vendettas build the muscles that separate founders who endure from those who quit. Giertz's best inventions came from impossible constraints; Open Secret's marketplace failure catalyzed profitable discipline; Parker Conrad's revenge fantasy after Zenefits fueled Rippling's brutal early years. The pattern is consistent: adversity is not an obstacle to founder development but the primary mechanism of it.
