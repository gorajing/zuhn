Leaders who derive identity from being busy and indispensable prevent team members from growing and cap the team's overall capability. The misconception that leaders must be the most technically knowledgeable person leads to hiring weaker people. Deep work and genuine leadership drain the same finite resource — you cannot maximize both on the same day. The resolution: accept that delegation is slower the first time as a deliberate investment, not a failure of efficiency. A startup is like an egg (find customers, build product, don't die) but must grow into a chicken — and that transition requires the founder to release control at the exact moment they're best at executing.
