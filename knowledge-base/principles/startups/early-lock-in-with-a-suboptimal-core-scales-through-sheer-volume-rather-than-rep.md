RuBisCo is a terrible enzyme that makes errors 50% of the time in today's oxygen-rich atmosphere, yet it became the most abundant protein on Earth (40 billion tons) because plants went all-in before conditions changed and couldn't switch away. Rather than evolving a replacement, plants evolved entire compensatory pathways. This mirrors startup dynamics: companies build on early technical choices that become legacy constraints, scaling volume to compensate for per-unit inefficiency, and constructing workarounds rather than replacing the core. The lesson: first-mover lock-in with adequate-but-imperfect systems beats waiting for optimal ones.
