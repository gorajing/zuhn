When revenue comes from membership rather than markup, the company is structurally incentivized to keep prices low and quality high because renewals depend on perceived value. Costco's 15% markup cap, warehouse layout, and limited 4,000 SKUs create interlocking efficiencies that compound into an unreplicable cost advantage. Mandatory membership filters for affluent households averaging $100K income, with business owners generating two-thirds of sales despite being one-third of members. The model turns what seems like a barrier (paying to shop) into a selection and alignment mechanism.
