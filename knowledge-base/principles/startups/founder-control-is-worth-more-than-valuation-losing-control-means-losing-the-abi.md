Mark Pincus argues control matters more than valuation because losing it means losing the ability to make the unpopular pivots that save companies. Letting founders take some money off the table aligns risk appetites. Board votes essentially never happen — founder power scales directly with company performance. Raising money is an obligation, not an achievement; owning more of your company is the only path to outsized wealth.
