When a founder's self-worth becomes entangled with their company's performance, negative signals trigger defensiveness and narrative distortion instead of adaptation. This pattern explains Casper rejecting a $900M exit because it wasn't a billion, Cox maintaining his 'helping people' story through escalating fraud, and athletes avoiding competition to protect their brand. The fix is structural: founders must practice separating 'I made a mistake' from 'I am a failure' so that feedback loops remain open and self-protection doesn't destroy the thing it claims to protect.
