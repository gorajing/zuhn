When the core asset is human talent that can walk out the door (barbers, taco vendors, BBQ pitmasters), markets resist consolidation because corporate structures can't retain what makes the business work. The winning strategy isn't being the best — it's showing up at the same place, same time, year after year. Texas has the most BBQ per capita yet lower failure rates than less competitive states, suggesting dense competition breeds operational discipline. Independent smashburger shops now outperform chains on the very things — speed, consistency, value — that used to differentiate them.
