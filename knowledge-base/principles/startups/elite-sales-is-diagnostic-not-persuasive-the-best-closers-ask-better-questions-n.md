Elite salespeople let the prospect talk 70% of the time, using questions to understand problems better than the prospect does. Training should orient around deep prospect understanding rather than product knowledge — reps shouldn't be talking much about the product anyway. The best closers don't have better arguments — they ask for the sale more times by using reframing to maintain rapport. Answer objections with questions about the objection to stay in control. Build genuine conviction in the product rather than training tonal techniques — correct tone follows automatically from belief. The paper process (NDAs, contracts, procurement) is the single biggest cause of deal slippage, which is why separating it from the decision process is critical.
