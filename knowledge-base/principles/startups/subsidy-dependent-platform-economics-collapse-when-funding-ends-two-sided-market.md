Platform businesses that subsidize both sides of their marketplace create artificial network effects that collapse when external funding stops. Uber raised $25B+ and operated for 13+ years without profit because the platform economics that were supposed to make ride-sharing infinitely scalable never materialized. Micro-mobility businesses discovered that distances too short to walk are also too short to generate fares covering unit costs. VCs proclaimed scooters would 'disrupt walking,' raised billions with no moat in hardware or software, and the entire category collapsed within 4 years. The pattern reveals a structural flaw: subsidizing price below unit cost generates volume that masquerades as product-market fit.
