RuBisCo is a terrible enzyme but plants produce 40 billion tons of it and dominate Earth. Similarly, early-stage hiring (Pincus at Zynga), biological systems, and brute-force approaches succeed by flooding the system with quantity when optimizing individual units hits diminishing returns. The tradeoff is that abundance strategies lock in the weakness permanently, making later optimization nearly impossible.
