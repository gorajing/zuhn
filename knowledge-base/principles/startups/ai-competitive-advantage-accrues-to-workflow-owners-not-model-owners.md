Owning the full workflow from intake to outcome generates proprietary data that public models can't replicate, creating a compounding moat. AI labs and big tech are structurally incapable of building opinionated consumer products because their incentive structures reward safe features over risky bets. Traditional moats (system of record, network effects) still apply in AI, but the pricing model shifted — even narrow features can charge $20K/year because they replace labor, not just software seats. The highest-leverage entry points are greenfield moments where customers must switch systems anyway.
