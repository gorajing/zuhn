A top VC invested in a company specifically knowing their business model would change three times. Smart investors expect early-stage business models to be wrong, so spending pitch time on revenue plans is a double penalty: it crowds the genuinely impressive parts (problem, product, team) out of investors' memory AND replaces them with skepticism about guesswork. The correct approach: describe your startup narrowly and concretely, mention monetization to show you've thought about it, but don't elaborate. Replace market-size slides with concrete stories of specific users — investors' deepest fear is that you built something nobody wants, and specific user stories counter that fear directly. Approach your description like successive approximation: start grippingly narrow, then flesh out.
