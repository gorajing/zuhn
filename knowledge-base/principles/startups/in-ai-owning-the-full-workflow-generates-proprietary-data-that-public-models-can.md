Owning the full workflow from intake to outcome generates proprietary data that creates a compounding competitive advantage. Data network effects are like gravity — 4 customers vs 3 means nothing, but 4 billion vs 1 billion is decisive. Traditional moats (system of record, network effects, workflow ownership) still apply in AI, but even 'features' can charge $20K/year because they replace labor, not just software seats. Unlike Windows' 95% monopoly, having 5+ model providers means AI startups face taxation risk, not existential platform risk. a16z's platform model worked because its founders had been VC customers — perspectives from being inside the customer's shoes are structural advantages career incumbents can't acquire.
