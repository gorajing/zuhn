Revenue growth during rapid expansion masks fundamental quality and operational problems that only become visible when growth slows. GoPro flooded the market with 20 models in 10 years but opened the door for Insta360; Crumbl's franchise expansion outpaced quality control; Under Armour's founder-driven expansion into fitness apps burned $700M chasing hype. The pattern is consistent across franchising, product launches, and geographic expansion: during the boom, every metric looks healthy because growth covers operational sins. The companies that survive are those that deliberately constrain growth velocity to maintain the quality and consistency that created demand in the first place.
