The journey from 1-to-10 and 10-to-100 is where real value is created, and it requires a fundamentally different operating mode than 0-to-1. Wartime CEOs make fast irreversible decisions with incomplete information while COOs multiply cross-functional bandwidth by absorbing initiative overload. Acquired founders must lead incumbent teams because if the existing structure were sufficient, the acquisition would not have been necessary. At hypergrowth, infrastructure will fail catastrophically -- YouTube maxed out its entire hosting provider and had to self-build overnight -- and companies must plan for breakage at every headcount doubling rather than treating it as a surprise.
