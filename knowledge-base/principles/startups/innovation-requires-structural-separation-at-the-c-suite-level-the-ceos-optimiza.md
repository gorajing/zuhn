Innovation programs reliably fail unless a dedicated C-level role (Chief Entrepreneur) owns them with the power and prestige to protect new ventures from the execution engine's organizational antibodies. CEO time allocation to innovation is the single strongest predictor of innovation program outcomes, but CEOs structurally default to exploitation. The solution is splitting the role: a chief execution officer running the current business and a chief entrepreneur building the next one, each with independent metrics, culture, and reporting lines.
