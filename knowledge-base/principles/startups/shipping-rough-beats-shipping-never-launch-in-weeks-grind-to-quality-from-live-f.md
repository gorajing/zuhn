The most expensive startup mistake is iterating in a vacuum. YC's core advice is simply 'launch' -- if your MVP takes more than two months, you're polishing phantoms. Nine iterations found Marco Polo, not one perfect attempt. But shipping rough is not shipping lazy: most AI startups die at demo-quality 60% because they stop grinding after the first version works. The discipline is launching fast AND relentlessly improving from real-world signal, building the hardest part first while motivation is highest, and treating zero failures as proof you are not placing enough frontier bets.
