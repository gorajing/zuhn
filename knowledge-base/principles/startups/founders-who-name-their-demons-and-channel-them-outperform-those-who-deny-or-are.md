The highest-performing founders share a counterintuitive trait: intimate self-knowledge of their psychological weaknesses, which they consciously convert into drive rather than suppressing or being destroyed by. Musk's addiction to intensity becomes a value-creation engine when channeled into surge-mode productivity. Early hardship instills an unshakeable agency belief. Even negative role models serve as via-negativa templates for who not to become. The danger is not having demons — it's the overcorrection cycle where a founder swings from one extreme to another (like Altman moving from default-trust to paranoia) instead of integrating the lesson.
