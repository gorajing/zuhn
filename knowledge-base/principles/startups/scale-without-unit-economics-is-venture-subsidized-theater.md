Uber raised $25B+ and operated for 13+ years without profit — the platform economics that were supposed to make ride-sharing infinitely scalable never materialized. Scooter companies raised billions to 'disrupt walking' and collapsed within 4 years with zero moat. Esports collectively burned billions — no team ever turned a profit at peak. BuzzFeed ($1.7B valuation to penny stock) and VICE (bankruptcy) proved millions of views are worthless without subscription revenue. Shrinkflation follows the same logic — Kraft Heinz generated phantom growth for years until the $15B ceiling hit. In each case, metrics that don't pay the bills masked the absence of a viable business model.
