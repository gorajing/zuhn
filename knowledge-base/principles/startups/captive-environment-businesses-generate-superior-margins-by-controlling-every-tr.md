Businesses that physically contain customers within a controlled environment -- cruise ships, nightclubs, theme parks, arcades -- convert commodity products into high-margin experiences by eliminating outside alternatives during the visit. Cruise lines earn higher margins than airlines because the ship is a captive-market platform where all spending occurs within their controlled environment, while nightclubs sell status through bottle service and VIP areas rather than the actual drinks. The key is stacking multiple revenue streams (food, drinks, prizes, bookings, experiences) into a single visit where each interaction reinforces willingness to spend.
