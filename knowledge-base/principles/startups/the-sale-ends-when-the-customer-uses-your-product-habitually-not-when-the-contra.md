Optimizely's cautionary tale — six-figure deals where customers never ran a single A/B test — shows that treating implementation as the customer's job is revenue destruction. The fix is owning implementation like a high-priority internal project. Joint sales-CS game tape reviews (sales hears onboarding, CS hears sales) saves the 5-10% of deals that churn from expectation misalignment. And the referral timing insight — ask at the first 'aha moment' when gratitude peaks, not months later — can increase total sales volume 30%. These post-close activities are systematically under-invested because commission structures reward signing, not succeeding.
