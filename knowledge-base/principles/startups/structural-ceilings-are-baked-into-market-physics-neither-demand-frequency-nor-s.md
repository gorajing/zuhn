Some constraints are physics, not strategy. On the demand side: Americans replace mattresses every 9-10 years and no advertising budget changes that cycle; cannabis companies cannot deduct business expenses under Schedule I; European football clubs spend everything on salaries to survive; Unilever ice cream grew only 4% in 20 years despite world-class brands. On the supply side: Napoleon's army did not lose to the Russian military — it lost to the distance between Paris and Moscow exceeding supply chain reach; Alexander's troops mutinied in India at the limit of operational reach; the Bronze Age collapsed when trade route disruption hit the logistics ceiling. In business, market expansion, hiring velocity, and product launches all have a structural ceiling that is invisible until you hit it — and hitting it converts every prior advantage into a liability. Execution cannot compensate for structural constraints on either side.
