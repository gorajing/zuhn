Costco's $60 membership fee, warehouse layout, and bulk-only format appear to limit market size but actually filter for affluent households ($100K average income) and business customers who generate two-thirds of revenue. IKEA's DIY assembly model, Trader Joe's limited selection, and similar 'obstacle-as-feature' designs convert cost-cutting constraints into emotional loyalty and demographic selection mechanisms. The principle: frictionless funnels maximize volume but dilute quality. Deliberate friction that selects for high-value customers creates structural advantages that competitors offering pure convenience cannot replicate.
