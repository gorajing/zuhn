Startup hubs form if and only if both technical talent and experienced investors are co-located; no other input is sufficient. Startup culture is a social phenomenon requiring community expertise, making it fundamentally harder to transplant than industrial technology. Founders in smaller markets should build local ecosystem gravity through conferences, academies, and talent attraction rather than relocating. Founders who leave for top-tier experience then return create a compounding talent flywheel that bootstraps entire startup ecosystems, as Creandum demonstrated by using US credibility to unlock European markets.
