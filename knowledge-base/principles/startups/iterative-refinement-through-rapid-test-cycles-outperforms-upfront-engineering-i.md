The most effective execution pattern is tight loops of build-test-learn with systematic variable isolation. Testing baseline, then single unit, then stacked units in sequence reveals failure modes that a single end-state test would obscure. Teams that broke equipment and redesigned between each attempt reached goals faster than careful upfront engineering, from Nakamura's daily MOCVD cycles to biology's proofreading mechanisms.
