Industries where the core value is an individual human's skill, reputation, or presence create fundamentally inverted business economics. FaZe Clan's cost of revenue exceeded 70% because content creators demanded generous revenue-sharing to stay exclusive -- the company captured the least value while spending beyond its means. European football clubs generate billions but are barely cash-flow positive because every dollar goes to retaining on-field talent. Esports teams burned billions collectively and no team ever turned a profit because players could move freely and demanded competitive salaries. When any person in the value chain can easily leave to start their own shop, no amount of capital, brand, or scale can consolidate the market. The only escape is building value that exists independent of any individual -- library syndication (Lionsgate's 8,000+ titles), infrastructure, or platform economics that survive talent departure.
