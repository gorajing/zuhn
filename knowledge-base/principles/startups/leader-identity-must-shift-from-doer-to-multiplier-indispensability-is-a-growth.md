Novice leaders derive identity from being busy and indispensable, which prevents team members from growing and caps overall capability. The misconception that leaders must be the most technically knowledgeable leads to hiring weaker people and lowering talent density. Instead of working on weaknesses, leaders should ask whether being bad at something is the reason they are good at everything else, then hire complementary people. Optimizing for stewardship over status, shaping belief systems gradually, and starting with high trust that depletes fast all shift the leader from bottleneck to multiplier.
