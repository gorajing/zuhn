Companies routinely misidentify their most valuable asset. McDonald's owns $30B+ in real estate and collects rent from franchisees, making it functionally a real estate company disguised as a fast food chain. 23andMe turned its genetic database from a customer education tool into a drug discovery asset. Lionsgate's acquisition of 8,000+ titles generated steady recurring revenue from syndication licensing, cushioning the inherent volatility of new film production. The pattern extends to data, content libraries, and installed bases: once a primary business creates a concentrated asset, the derivative monetization of that asset frequently exceeds the original business in both margins and durability.
