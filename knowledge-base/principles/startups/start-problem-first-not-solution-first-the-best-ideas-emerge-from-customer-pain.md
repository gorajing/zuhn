Starting with a solution and searching for a problem leads to high acquisition costs, poor fit, and stagnant growth. YC's top companies overwhelmingly came from problems noticed organically. The discipline is to start with who has the problem, not what technology to build. Spend 80% of PMF effort on understanding customer, job, problem, alternatives, and flaws before mentioning your product. Separate customer observation from solution design by temporarily forgetting what you offer.
