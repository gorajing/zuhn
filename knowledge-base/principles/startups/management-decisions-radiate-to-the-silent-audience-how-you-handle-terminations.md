When you fire someone, what you say at the company meeting becomes their reputation because every person in your company will get a reference call. Raises given on request incentivize lobbying over performance and teach the silent performers that results don't matter. Culture is shaped more by what leaders exclude than what they celebrate. The silent audience — everyone who wasn't directly involved — determines whether management decisions strengthen or erode culture. Every email a CEO sends could be the one that gets screenshotted and defines them forever.
