Stan Lee tested Spider-Man in a dying magazine's last issue because nobody cared what went in it -- and his publisher's three 'logical' objections were all wrong. Simone Giertz's intentionally shitty robots taught her more about engineering than trying to build perfect ones, because removing the pressure of perfection unlocked creative exploration. Raibert buried his real idea (pogo stick robot) in the middle of a boring list to let his advisor 'discover' it. The pattern is consistent: ideas that would be killed by formal evaluation processes thrive when tested in environments where failure carries no consequences, and the resulting discoveries are systematically more original than what gatekeepers approve.
