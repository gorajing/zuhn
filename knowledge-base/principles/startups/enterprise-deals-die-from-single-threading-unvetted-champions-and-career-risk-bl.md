Enterprise sales has a distinct failure mode: the deal that looks alive but is politically dead. Three patterns dominate: single-threading (one champion goes silent, months wasted, no backup contacts), unvetted champions (enthusiastic junior without buying authority gets shut down by CTO), and career-risk blindness (ignoring that the VP of IT sees your solution as a threat to their relevance). The fix for all three is the same: multi-thread aggressively, vet champion's actual authority and political cover, and understand that career risk overrides ROI in enterprise decision-making. Being prescriptive about the buying process when champions don't know it is the consultative move that separates strategic AEs from order-takers.
