The biggest startup ideas trigger fear and identity threat, causing founders to unconsciously filter them out before rational evaluation begins. Your unconscious mind prevents you from seeing ideas that involve difficult or unpleasant work. People dismiss ambitious ideas partly because they unconsciously hope you will fail, since your success threatens their relative status. Attractive-sounding ideas are poor bets because many people pursue them, while ideas that look bad initially are valuable precisely because good-sounding ones attract too many competitors. The best ideas are such outliers that your conscious mind rejects them as company ideas, which is why Yahoo, Google, Facebook, and Apple all started as side projects.
