Year 4 of your current business will almost always outperform year 1 of a new one because compounding requires sustained effort. Serial starters repeatedly abandon projects at the valley of despair and restart with uninformed optimism, destroying the compounding effect. The fix is committing long enough for it to be unreasonable to still be bad, and recognizing the boring middle as a feature of ambitious paths.
