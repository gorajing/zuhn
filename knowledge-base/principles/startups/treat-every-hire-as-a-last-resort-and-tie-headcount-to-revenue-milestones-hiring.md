Hiring too fast is by far the biggest killer of startups that raise money, as large teams are the effect of growth, not the cause. People costs are 60-80% of early-stage burn, making headcount planning the single most important financial lever founders have. Weak hires do not just underperform — they force you to hire more people, making the organization bigger and thus structurally slower. Treat every hire as a last resort after exhausting automation, templatization, and temporary outsourcing. Headcount growth should be tied to revenue milestones, not fundraising events or growth ambition.
