Different buyer archetypes require fundamentally different sales approaches: gatekeepers buy to do the same thing cheaper, business users buy to deliver more, enablers buy to help others, and leaders buy for growth. A single deal may simultaneously need candy messaging (delight) for users, vitamin messaging (benefits) for evaluators, and painkiller messaging (prescriptive facts) for executives. Personalization compounds at every touchpoint — demos built with the prospect's logo and data, outreach referencing specific shared context rather than generic flattery, and targeted emails to 100 right people outperforming 1,000 untargeted ones — because the pattern interrupt of genuine specificity is what makes prospects actually pay attention.
