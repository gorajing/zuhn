The 'can't not do it' test is the only reliable predictor of founder endurance because a decade of startup building requires irrational persistence that no financial incentive can sustain. As stakes increase, power struggles intensify — resilient governance structures must be built early, before the pressure peaks. Confidence is necessary to start but owners who think they can do it all fail at much higher rates. Even after a major win, the correct behavior is to assume your competitor has achieved the same advantage and continue at maximum intensity.
