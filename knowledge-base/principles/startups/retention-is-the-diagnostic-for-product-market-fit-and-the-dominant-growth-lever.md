Two businesses with 300 customers have radically different trajectories depending on retention: one with perfect retention grows automatically while one with high churn needs exponentially increasing acquisition spending. Working on retention forces the question 'am I actually helping users?' while top-line growth work tempts extraction through dark patterns. Prioritize retention measurement over acquisition volume to diagnose whether you actually have PMF, because subscriptions only work if the product genuinely delivers and customer feedback is too fast and too public for mediocre products to survive.
