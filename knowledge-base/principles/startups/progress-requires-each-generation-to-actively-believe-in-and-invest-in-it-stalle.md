Progress is not automatic; it depends on self-reinforcing feedback loops where belief drives investment, investment produces results, and results sustain belief. For most of human history, people viewed the past as a golden age and the future as decline — the idea that tomorrow could be better only emerged in the 1500-1600s. The 50-year slowdown from 1970-2020, where only computing advanced meaningfully compared to the five simultaneous revolutions of 1870-1920, demonstrates that when a generation stops actively believing in and funding progress, the flywheel stalls.
