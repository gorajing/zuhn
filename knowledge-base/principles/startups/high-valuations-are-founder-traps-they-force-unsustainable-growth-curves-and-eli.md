Giant rounds inflate valuations to levels where only IPOs satisfy investors, eliminating good-but-not-huge exits. A $300M acquisition is worthless when your last round was at $500M with 2x liquidation preferences. Taking a high valuation narrows the acquirer field and turns a likely win into an unlikely moonshot. Down-rounds are becoming the new normal as companies go public below private peaks.
