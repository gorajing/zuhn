Growth often comes not from steady execution but from capturing displacement when competitors or markets fracture. EncoWithers grew from under 100 to 7,000+ hosts by spending a year preparing before Airbnb expelled Korean hosts. A share-house business pivoted quarantine facilities during COVID and grew beyond its pre-crisis trajectory. Frederic Tudor created an entirely new market by giving free ice to bartenders until customers could never go back. These follow Barabasi's preferential attachment model: a small initial positioning advantage compounds into hub dominance once disruption redirects network flows, and power-law dynamics mean optimizing for exposure to these extreme outcomes matters more than optimizing for averages.
