In early stages, doing more reps at lower quality beats fewer reps at higher quality because volume generates the feedback data needed to know what to optimize. Skill equals repetitions times improvement-per-rep, and most people skip the improvement multiplier of reviewing their top and bottom 10% of outputs. This lesson must be relearned independently in each new domain because the instinct to optimize prematurely is universal.
