Founders who think through failure scenarios before committing, plan their exit conditions, and build structured learning protocols around mistakes consistently outperform those who only optimize for the upside. The critical insight is that only one of three failure responses works: forgive-and-remember (not forgive-and-forget which learns nothing, nor blame-and-humiliate which prevents honesty). This extends to knowing your quit signals in advance — like Fireship recognizing he'd rather make YouTube content than build a startup — because delayed self-awareness burns months of irreplaceable time.
