People show far more originality in solving problems than in choosing them, yet problem selection determines startup success more than execution quality. Twenty years of schooling teaches problem-solving but never problem-choosing. The exercise of picking a startup idea should be reframed from 'find a good startup idea' to 'find a good idea for your team' since founder-market fit is the single most predictive evaluation criterion. Shifting which problem you solve produces order-of-magnitude improvements that execution refinement cannot match. Starting from a genuine edge or puzzling question produces far better results than choosing an important-sounding topic and forcing insights from it.
