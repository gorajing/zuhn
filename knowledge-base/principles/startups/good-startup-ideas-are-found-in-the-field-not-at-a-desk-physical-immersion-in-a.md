The highest-signal startup ideas come from physically going to where users work, live, and struggle -- not from brainstorming sessions, VC podcasts, or survey data. The AtoB founders went to truck stops, Dumpling founders sold below cost at farmers markets, and multiple sources confirm that real pain points only surface through direct environmental immersion. Choosing a fertile idea space first, then systematically mapping it through physical presence, is a replicable method that collapses the false distinction between 'finding' and 'validating' ideas.
