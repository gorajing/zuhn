Inexperienced founders routinely outperform veterans by seeing solutions that experts have unconsciously ruled out. Big tech experience is actively anti-helpful for startups because the culture, speed, and processes are the exact opposite of what founders need. The real advantage is not knowing what is impossible -- entering unfamiliar domains with open acceptance of what you do not know, combined with youth's naivete and stamina, unlocks creative exploration that polished credentials prevent.
