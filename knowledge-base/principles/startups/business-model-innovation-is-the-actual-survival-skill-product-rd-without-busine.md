Kodak, Nokia, and Blackberry were technologically innovative companies that died because they invested in product R&D without exploring new business models. Business models expire faster than CEO tenures, and turnaround plans that fix execution cannot address fundamental model expiration. New business model creation follows a search loop of hypothesis-test-pivot, not a linear execution plan, and organizations that default to efficiency innovation must deliberately shift resources toward transformative categories.
