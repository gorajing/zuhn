Most of the world's most successful companies made their real money from a first derivative of their core product -- Union Pacific from land sales, Google from ads on search, Shopify from payments. Similarly, TwoSpace discovered 80% of members already had offices and were paying for community, not workspace. The correct strategic sequence is pricing optimization, then freed cash flow, then attribution, then paid ads -- not the reverse. Founders who fixate on the obvious product miss where the real margin lives.
