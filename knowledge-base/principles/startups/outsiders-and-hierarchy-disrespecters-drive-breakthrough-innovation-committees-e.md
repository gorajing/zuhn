The next generation of technology is more often built by outsiders tinkering than by insiders planning the official successor. Committees produce safe, lumpy designs because no group will agree to bold risks. Standard education optimizes for convergent thinking, systematically destroying creative capacity. The same trait that makes hackers disrespectful of hierarchy is what makes them capable of breakthrough innovation — and when everyone has the team and the tech, the scarce resource is a strong enough point of view.
