When PE firms or spreadsheet-driven management optimize for quarterly metrics, they systematically destroy the organizational muscle that creates new products. Burger King fell from clear #2 because PE reduced the business to numbers; Hershey, Kellogg, and General Mills had to acquire emerging brands because they'd lost the ability to invent; and the Moneyball mindset gave intellectual cover to rebrand cost-cutting as innovation. Revenue is a lagging indicator — by the time Nike/Adidas hit peak sales, the collapse was already baked in.
