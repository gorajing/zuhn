Most co-founders are chosen like college roommates -- by proximity and familiarity -- rather than through deliberate evaluation of strategic fit, stress compatibility, and alignment on sacrifice. The evidence is clear: co-founders who discover the idea together feel true ownership while those recruited into someone else's vision always have one foot out the door, skills matter far less than how someone behaves under extreme stress, and the right match feels obvious from the first conversation. Structural safeguards like near-equal equity with one CEO tiebreaker share, a third co-founder for deadlock prevention, and the builder-dreamer complementarity test should be non-negotiable evaluation criteria.
