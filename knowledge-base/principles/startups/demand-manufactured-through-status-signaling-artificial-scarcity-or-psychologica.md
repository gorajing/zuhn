Businesses that generate revenue by manufacturing artificial demand -- nightclubs selling status through bottle service rather than the actual product, shrinkflation extracting 3-5% stealth revenue until customers stop buying entirely (Kraft Heinz's $15B write-down), subscriptions forced onto products that don't need them (heated car seats, printer ink), BNPL psychologically decoupling payment pain from purchase pleasure -- all hit the same wall. The mechanism works until customers develop awareness of the extraction, at which point trust erosion is sudden and irreversible. Cost-cutting disguised as innovation generates the same backlash. These models share a common failure mode: they optimize for extraction speed rather than value delivery, and the ceiling arrives not gradually but as a cliff.
