Leaders who model values through action -- reading casualty lists daily, sharing meals, showing scars from shared hardship, picking up homeless people on holidays -- transmit those values far more durably than any speech or values document. Belief systems must be shaped gradually over months through consistent demonstrated behavior so that major decisions feel inevitable rather than shocking. Cultural empathy expressed through genuine behavior (Hawaiian soldiers throwing shakas, sharing tea) produces measurable operational outcomes that top-down directives cannot replicate.
