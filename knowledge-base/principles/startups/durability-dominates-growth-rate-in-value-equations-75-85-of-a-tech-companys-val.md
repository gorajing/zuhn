Silicon Valley overvalues growth rates and undervalues durability. At PayPal in 2001, 75% of the company's value came from projected 2011+ cash flows. Omega died chasing performance against quartz while Patek survived by pivoting to brand — doubling down on a commoditized advantage is fatal. 3G Capital's PE ownership of Burger King pushed margins from 15% to 69% but average store revenue stayed flat for 5 years — zero improvement in actual customer demand. The focus that builds a monopoly prevents the company from destroying it, even when survival requires exactly that. Companies that die (Sears, Toys-R-Us) die from strategic stasis, not from competition — the competitor is a conveniently blameable scapegoat.
