Linux dominates because anyone can tweak it for their specific use case. Kilo Code grew to 1.5M users in under a year by being fully open-source and model-agnostic. Companies invest in OSS to influence standards, attract developers, and commoditize competitors' advantages. The pattern is consistent: open platforms win breadth while closed platforms win depth, and in most markets breadth eventually dominates.
