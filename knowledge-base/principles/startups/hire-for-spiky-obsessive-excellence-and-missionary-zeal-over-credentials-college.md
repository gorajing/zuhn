Hire people who are 'animals' — so obsessively good at their job that they cross from professional into obsessive. Hire for being literally the best in the world at one thing that matters, not for lacking weaknesses. Y Combinator found that college prestige adds almost no predictive value against direct assessment. Academic failures driven by project obsession are often better hires than straight-A students because obsession is a stronger signal of builder aptitude than compliance. Hire undervalued talent — people early in careers or overlooked by the market — rather than competing for proven stars at big companies.
