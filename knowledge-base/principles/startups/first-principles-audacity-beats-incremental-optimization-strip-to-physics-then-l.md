Instead of optimizing from 74 days to 72, strip every problem to what physics allows and discover that 6 days is possible. Founders who define themselves by a life mission rather than a specific product can pivot without existential crisis, and post-PMF growth requires strategic leaps (Facebook beyond .edu, DoorDash beyond food) rather than continuous improvement of the original wedge. The common thread is that incremental thinking anchors you to the current frame, while first-principles reasoning reveals order-of-magnitude possibilities that were always there.
