Some of the most durable business advantages come not from product superiority but from structural asymmetries in regulation, information access, or cross-border policy that shield incumbents from normal competitive forces. Cannabis companies cannot access banking or tax deductions that every other business takes for granted, Chinese e-commerce platforms exploit de minimis trade exemptions and state-subsidized postal services, and gun retailers thrive precisely because advertising bans and lack of price transparency make dealer expertise the only viable competitive advantage. These asymmetries persist because they are embedded in legal and political structures that move slower than markets.
