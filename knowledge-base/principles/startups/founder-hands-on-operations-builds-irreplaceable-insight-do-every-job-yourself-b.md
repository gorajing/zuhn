Manually doing every job in your startup gives founders the operational insight needed to build the right automated systems later. Successful founders personally handle customer support, obsess over tiny product details, and maintain the tightest possible feedback loop with users. This unscalable founder involvement is a moat that should be defended, not a phase to outgrow, because the extreme operational stress can only be sustained by founders, giving startups a structural edge large companies cannot replicate.
