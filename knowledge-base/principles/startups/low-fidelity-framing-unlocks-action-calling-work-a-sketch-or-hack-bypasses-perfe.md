Labeling ambitious work as 'just a sketch', 'just a hack', or 'just an experiment' lowers the psychological commitment threshold enough to actually start. Once momentum builds, the frame drops away and genuine output accumulates. The key insight is that the framing trick works because it makes imperfect early output feel acceptable rather than threatening.
