Making membership the revenue source (not a loyalty add-on) structurally forces a company to keep prices low and quality high because renewals depend on customer satisfaction, not transaction extraction. Contrast this with franchising models where corporate mandates $1 Whoppers that generate royalty revenue for headquarters but force franchisees to sell at negative margins. McDonald's is more real estate investor than restaurant chain ($30B+ in property). Credit card economics depend on the minority who carry balances subsidizing rewards for everyone. A product's true cost is purchase price minus resale value. The pattern: business model architecture determines behavior more reliably than stated values or company culture. Design the incentives correctly and good behavior follows automatically.
