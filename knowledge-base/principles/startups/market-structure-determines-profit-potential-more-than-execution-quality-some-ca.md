  generate sustainable profits when they achieve hub dominance that reduces
  competition on specific routes. 80% of US barbershops remain independent after
  40 years of corporate attempts because the core asset (barber talent) can walk
  out. In LA's saturated taco market, the winning strategy is not being the best

  year after year. Texas has the most BBQ per capita but a lower failure rate

  bother replacing — nobody optimizes a $50/month line item. The error is
  assuming execution can overcome structural constraints: a category that only
  supports monopolies will bankrupt every player except the eventual winner,
  regardless of how well they run their business.

For an alarming number of major companies, becoming a monopoly is not profit maximization — it is the only way they can make a profit at all. Airlines only generate sustainable profits when they achieve hub dominance that reduces competition on specific routes. 80% of US barbershops remain independent after 40 years of corporate attempts because the core asset (barber talent) can walk out. In LA's saturated taco market, the winning strategy is not being the best but being the best for your specific neighborhood, showing up at the same spot year after year. Texas has the most BBQ per capita but a lower failure rate than less competitive states. Donut economics work because the product cost is trivially low relative to the selling price. Some products are too cheap to bother replacing — nobody optimizes a $50/month line item. The error is assuming execution can overcome structural constraints: a category that only supports monopolies will bankrupt every player except the eventual winner, regardless of how well they run their business.
