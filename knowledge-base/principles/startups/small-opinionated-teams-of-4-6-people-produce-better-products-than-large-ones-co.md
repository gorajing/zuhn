The best products come from small teams with a coherent vision, not from large groups that negotiate compromises. The original Mac was 20 people, the iPhone was 24, and Gamma built with a team one-third designers. A design that represents one person's coherent vision will be coherent for the user; committee designs fragment into incoherence. Programmers acquired by big companies have said they would trade half the acquisition price to regain the ability to release code immediately, revealing that organizational overhead is the primary cost of scale in product development.
