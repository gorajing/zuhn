Tech culture applies the software beta-testing mindset to synthetic peptides and experimental therapies -- accepting 'bugs' in exchange for frontier access. But biology is not software: without clinical trials, users cannot distinguish real effects from placebo, especially when paying hundreds of dollars per dose creates strong confirmation bias. The somatic-vs-germline distinction compounds this risk: editing your own cells affects only you, but germline edits permanently alter all descendants, demanding fundamentally higher evidence thresholds as intervention scope widens.
