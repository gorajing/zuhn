fMRI studies show psychedelics cause massive cross-talk between brain regions that normally never communicate, and animal research confirms they increase neuroplasticity for days afterward — creating a window where new behavioral patterns stick more easily. This explains why psychedelics are the only drug class where a single dose routinely produces life transformations that people reference decades later. However, psychedelics are non-specific amplifiers: the same substance can produce euphoria or terror, with the outcome determined almost entirely by set (mindset) and setting (environment), not the drug itself. The therapeutic leverage is real but conditional — it requires deliberate preparation and integration.
