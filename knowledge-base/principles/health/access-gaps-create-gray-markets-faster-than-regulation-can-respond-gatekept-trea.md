When insurance rejects a BMI-34.7 patient for $1000/month Ozempic, she turns to $15 Chinese-sourced peptides within weeks. Synthetic peptides beyond GLP-1s spread through tech culture using the same early-adopter logic as beta-testing software — willingness to accept unknown risks in exchange for frontier access. Meanwhile, confirmation bias from paying hundreds per shot makes self-experimenters unable to distinguish real effects from placebo, and breakthrough treatments like Casgevy remain globally inaccessible despite being curative. The pattern: legitimate gatekeeping creates demand pressure that routes around barriers, producing a parallel system with no quality control or clinical validation.
