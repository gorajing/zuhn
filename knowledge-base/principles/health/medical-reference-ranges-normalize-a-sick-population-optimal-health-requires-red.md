Standard medical 'normal' ranges are bell-curve averages derived from a metabolically sick population, so 'normal' literally means average, not healthy, and the ranges shift as the population gets sicker. Fasting insulin elevates 15-20 years before glucose becomes abnormal, but standard blood panels only test glucose, missing the earliest and most actionable disease signal. Biological age diverges from chronological age by up to 5 years and actually predicts disease risk, yet most people rely on body weight which cannot distinguish fat from muscle from water.
