  The gap between food marketing and food reality is systematic: most commercial
  olive oils are adulterated with seed oils or oxidized, sea salts contain
  undisclosed heavy metals, egg labels (cage-free, free-range) describe
  meaningless distinctions, grass-fed often means grain-finished, and premium
  fast-casual restaurants use the same seed oils as fast food despite quality
  positioning. Navigating this requires specific retailer knowledge (Costco for
  bulk value, Aldi for budget, farmers markets for traceability), third-party
  testing verification (olive oil polyphenols, salt certificates of analysis),
  and visual inspection skills (fat color for grass-finished beef). The insight
  is that food quality is not a spectrum you buy your way up — it requires
  active investigation at every price point.

The gap between food marketing and food reality is systematic: most commercial olive oils are adulterated with seed oils or oxidized, sea salts contain undisclosed heavy metals, egg labels (cage-free, free-range) describe meaningless distinctions, grass-fed often means grain-finished, and premium fast-casual restaurants use the same seed oils as fast food despite quality positioning. Navigating this requires specific retailer knowledge (Costco for bulk value, Aldi for budget, farmers markets for traceability), third-party testing verification (olive oil polyphenols, salt certificates of analysis), and visual inspection skills (fat color for grass-finished beef). The insight is that food quality is not a spectrum you buy your way up — it requires active investigation at every price point.
