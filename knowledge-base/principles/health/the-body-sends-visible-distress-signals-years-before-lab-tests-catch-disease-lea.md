The liver (right shoulder pain, spider angiomas, jaundice), kidneys (foamy urine, ankle swelling), cardiovascular system (age spots, leg weakness), and eyes (contrast loss, needing more light) all display visible external warning signs years before standard blood tests show abnormal values. Lab reference ranges are derived from an increasingly sick population, so 'normal' results provide false reassurance while the body's own signals are more sensitive early indicators.
