RuBisCo confuses O2 for CO2 up to 50% of the time, yet plants maintain ~40 billion tons of it rather than evolving a replacement, because replacing it would require redesigning the entire Calvin Cycle. This pattern — compensating for a broken core component through brute-force scaling rather than architectural redesign — recurs across biological and engineered systems. The cost of replacement exceeds the cost of compensating indefinitely.
