Menopause symptoms, BPH (enlarged prostate), cellulite, hair loss, and certain cancers are driven not by estrogen deficiency or testosterone excess but by estrogen dominance relative to progesterone (women) or testosterone (men). Cruciferous vegetables (DIM), zinc (aromatase inhibition), and reducing visceral fat (aromatase site) address the ratio rather than supplementing hormones, which can worsen dominance.
