We already know how to design genetic fixes for thousands of diseases, but natural AAV capsids deliver only 0.1% of payload to target neurons and dump most into the liver — the real challenge is a postal system problem, not a knowledge problem. Similarly, AlphaFold only succeeded because scientists spent decades building a $21 billion Protein Data Bank, and AI-driven longevity breakthroughs require the same foundational biological data investment before they can accelerate. The optimistic counter: like semiconductors and solar panels, gene therapy costs should follow Wright's Law as AI designs personalized therapies on-demand, potentially dropping costs from millions to thousands — but only after the delivery and data bottlenecks are solved.
