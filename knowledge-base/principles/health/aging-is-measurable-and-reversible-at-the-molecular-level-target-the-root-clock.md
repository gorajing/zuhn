DNA methylation patterns degrade predictably with age, and epigenetic clocks can now measure biological age from routine blood work — revealing that your real disease risk diverges from your birth certificate by up to 5 years. Crucially, this molecular clock is malleable: Yamanaka factor reprogramming reverses cellular aging in mice without destroying cell identity, senolytic drugs clear zombie cells to reduce cancer and heart disease, and rapamycin extends lifespan via autophagy even when given late in life. The paradigm shift is that slowing biological aging prevents multiple diseases simultaneously, outperforming the current whack-a-mole approach of treating each disease individually — and one extra healthy year across the population is worth $38 trillion.
