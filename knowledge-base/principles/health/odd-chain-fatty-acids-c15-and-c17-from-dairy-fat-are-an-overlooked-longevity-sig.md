Observational research consistently links higher C15 (pentadecanoic acid) and C17 odd-chain fatty acid levels from dairy fat with better longevity outcomes, yet virtually no one in the longevity space supplements or tracks them. C15 specifically protects cell membranes from seed oil-induced oxidative damage through anti-ferroptosis activity, with optimal levels at 0.4-0.64% of cell membrane fatty acids. Unsalted butter provides these as part of its fermented/cultured profile with beneficial odd-chain fatty acids, and grass-fed sourcing further improves the fatty acid composition. This represents a gap between epidemiological signal strength and clinical attention — a nutrient hiding in plain sight in the dairy fat that mainstream nutrition spent decades demonizing.
