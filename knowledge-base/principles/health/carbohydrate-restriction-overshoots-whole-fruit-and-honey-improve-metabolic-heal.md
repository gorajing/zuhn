The most common animal-based diet mistake is eating too few carbohydrates from fruit and honey, causing elevated cortisol, suppressed thyroid, and lower testosterone. Short-term ketogenic diets impair bone health markers, elevate stress hormones, and suppress thyroid function. Human interventional studies confirm whole fruit does not cause insulin resistance — its hundreds of bioactive compounds prevent the gut dysbiosis that isolated fructose triggers. Honey improves insulin sensitivity and testosterone levels, distinguishing it from isolated sucrose which lacks honey's 300+ bioactive compounds. Refined sugar causes insulin resistance through gut dysbiosis and endotoxin-driven inflammation, not blood glucose spikes — while whole fruits containing the same sugars with bioactive compounds do not cause dysbiosis. Former keto advocates are increasingly adding fruit and carbohydrates back due to hormonal and thyroid concerns. The optimal ancestral frame is balanced macros around 30-35% each from protein, fat, and carbohydrates.
