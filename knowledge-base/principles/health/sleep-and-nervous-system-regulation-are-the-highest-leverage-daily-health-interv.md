Sleep deprivation impairs cognition equivalent to a 0.08 BAC (legally drunk), weekend catch-up sleep is a myth because circadian rhythms do not allow debt repayment, and consistent sleep hygiene is the single most powerful attention intervention available. Beyond sleep, the nervous system responds to deliberate regulation: 5.5-second nasal breathing cycles produce deep relaxation comparable to a muscle relaxant, rotating exercise modalities in 3-month cycles optimizes both joints and hormonal cascades that affect cognition, and chronic loneliness keeps the body in low-level fight-or-flight that gradually degrades multiple organ systems through inflammation. These are free, daily-actionable levers that compound — and they must be in place before any supplement, drug, or biohack can deliver its promised effect.
