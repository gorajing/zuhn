Colon cells uniquely depend on butyrate for energy and tumor suppression — fermented foods and fiber restore this critical fuel. Food emulsifiers in 60% of processed foods and polysorbate 80 act as gut detergents, stripping the mucosal barrier and causing systemic inflammation. L. reuteri bacteria signal the brain via the vagus nerve to produce oxytocin, establishing the gut-brain axis as a physical pathway not a metaphor. Gut decline after 40 is accumulated microbiome damage from medications and diet, not aging — it is reversible. Even healthy unprocessed plant foods contain defense chemicals that trigger immune activation in susceptible people, causing eczema, psoriasis, and gut inflammation. A 3-4 week elimination diet of meat and fruit followed by sequential reintroduction is the single most valuable diagnostic intervention for autoimmune conditions. Bad breath, anxiety, and brain fog are often gut dysbiosis signals rather than local or psychological problems.
