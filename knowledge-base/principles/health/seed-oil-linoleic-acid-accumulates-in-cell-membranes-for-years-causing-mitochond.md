Linoleic acid from seed oils has a 680-day half-life in cell membranes, meaning full detox takes 2-4 years of complete avoidance. At the molecular level, accumulated linoleic acid in mitochondrial membranes causes proton leak across the electron transport chain, directly reducing ATP production — the fundamental energy currency of every cell. This is not merely inflammation or oxidation but a structural impairment of the energy-generating machinery itself. Seed oils persist in the food supply because they are cheap preservatives that likely undermine satiety, benefiting manufacturers. The practical implication is that eliminating seed oils is necessary but not immediately sufficient — membrane remodeling requires years of sustained avoidance with tallow and butter (1-2% linoleic acid) replacing all cooking fats.
