Reducing meals to 1-2 per day lowers insulin more than food quality changes alone because every eating event spikes insulin regardless of macronutrient content. Constant snacking prevents metabolic flexibility by keeping insulin chronically elevated and blocking the fat-burning switch. Frequent small meals — the standard diabetic advice — worsen blood sugar by maintaining chronic insulin elevation. Starting mornings with juice, cereal, or coffee on an empty stomach spikes insulin and stresses the liver. High-carb breakfasts force the liver to convert fructose to fat first thing. The practical principle is that when you eat (and how often) matters as much or more than what you eat — and fasting insulin is the most important blood test most doctors never order.
