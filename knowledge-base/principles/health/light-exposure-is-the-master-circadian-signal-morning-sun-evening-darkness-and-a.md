The body's circadian system is primarily entrained by light timing and spectrum: morning sunlight within 30 minutes of waking sets the cortisol-melatonin cycle for the entire day, while evening blue light from LEDs and screens suppresses natural melatonin production far more effectively than supplemental melatonin can replace it. Winter depression is driven more by insufficient full-spectrum light than vitamin D deficiency, and modern LED lighting causes chronic circadian disruption through invisible flicker and blue-shifted spectra. Managing light exposure across the day is a zero-cost intervention with outsized downstream effects on sleep, mood, and hormone regulation.
