Commercial supplements repeatedly fail because isolated synthetic compounds behave differently than their whole-food counterparts. Synthetic B vitamins (cyanocobalamin, folic acid) can block nerve receptors and worsen pain. Synthetic ascorbic acid lacks the bioflavonoids and copper activators that make vitamin C functional. Magnesium oxide has only 4% absorption. Most commercial multivitamins contain inadequate doses and harmful fillers. The pattern is consistent: isolated vitamins without cofactors create imbalances and deplete companion nutrients. Whole food sources — sauerkraut for vitamin C, Brazil nuts for selenium, organs for B12 — deliver nutrients in the bioavailable matrix the body evolved to use.
