De-extinction scientists deliberately make the fewest genetic edits possible because each change carries unpredictable risk in a complex system. CRISPR enables precise targeted edits but somatic changes affect only one person while germline edits permanently alter all descendants — scope of intervention determines magnitude of irreversibility. Even at the cellular level, asbestos damage occurs because the body cannot process a single type of foreign fiber, triggering cascading inflammatory failures. The principle applies beyond biology: in any complex system, minimize the surface area of intervention and prefer reversible, scoped changes over broad irreversible ones.
