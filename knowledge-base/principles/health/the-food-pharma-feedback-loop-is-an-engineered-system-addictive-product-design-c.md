Ultra-processed foods combine fat and sugar in ratios that never exist in nature, hitting a mathematically optimized bliss point that short-circuits satiety and creates drug-like dopamine responses. Food companies engineer these addictive products, pharmaceutical companies treat the resulting chronic diseases symptomatically, and both fund research and influencers to protect the cycle. Coca-Cola funds hundreds of millions in research promoting 'a calorie is a calorie' to distract from metabolic harm. Pharmaceutical companies pay social media influencers millions to promote drugs disguised as authentic health content. Medical schools train doctors in pharmaceutical symptom management with virtually no nutrition education. The system is not a conspiracy but an emergent trillion-dollar feedback loop where each participant's financial incentives reinforce the others, with no structural incentive to cure.
