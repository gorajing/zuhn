Creatine monohydrate is the most evidence-based supplement for both physical and cognitive performance, with distinct dose-dependent thresholds: 5g/day for muscle, 10g/day for bone density critical in elderly women with osteopenia, and 15-20g/day to cross the blood-brain barrier for cognitive and sleep benefits. It does not harm kidneys (elevated creatinine is a benign byproduct), the hair loss scare rests on a single flawed study, and the main user errors are underdosing, combining with coffee, and inconsistent daily use.
