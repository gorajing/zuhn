The PADIRKO mouse (kidney-specific insulin receptor knockout) develops diabetic nephropathy with normal blood glucose, proving insulin itself drives metabolic organ damage independently of blood sugar. This overturns the glucose-centric model of metabolic disease. Separately, a 3-4 week elimination diet of meat and fruit followed by sequential food reintroduction is the single most valuable diagnostic intervention for autoimmune conditions because it establishes a clean baseline from which individual triggers become identifiable. Both findings reframe treatment from managing symptoms to identifying root causes.
