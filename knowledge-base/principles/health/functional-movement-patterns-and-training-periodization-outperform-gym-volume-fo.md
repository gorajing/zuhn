The evidence points away from maximizing training volume and toward smarter movement programming: rotating between heavy and light cycles in 3-month blocks protects joints while varying hormonal cascades, short HIIT sessions plus daily walking capture 80% of exercise benefits, and combining intermittent fasting with resistance training maximizes body composition. Corrective exercises for modern postural dysfunctions like upper cross syndrome and flat feet produce rapid results through targeted muscle activation rather than general training. The highest-leverage physical practice is sustainable functional mobility, not peak performance programming.
