The calorie-in/calorie-out model fails because each macronutrient has a radically different metabolic fate: fiber blocks 20% of almond calories for microbiome use, protein's thermic effect wastes 25% of energy as heat, and fructose routes directly to fat synthesis regardless of caloric balance. Weight loss plateaus are primarily driven by unconscious NEAT reduction rather than metabolic slowdown, while subtle fidgety movements trigger local fat-burning through epinephrine release worth 800-2500 daily calories. Exercise helps maintain weight loss mainly by making the brain more responsive to fullness signals, not by burning significant calories directly.
