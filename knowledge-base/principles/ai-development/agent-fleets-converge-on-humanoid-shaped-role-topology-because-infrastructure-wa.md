The 2020-era intuition that AI would arrive as one omnipotent superintelligence has been falsified empirically — production agent deployments converge on role-shaped specialization (content marketer agent, customer responder agent, market researcher agent) for two structural reasons. First, the workflows agents must slot into were designed for human roles, so role-shaped agents integrate with less friction. Second, finite context windows produce the same partitioning logic that drives human org charts — no single attention budget can hold the entire organization's context. The operational consequence: design agent products around clear role identities, scope their context windows like job descriptions, and expect the org chart of an AI-native company to look more like a human one (with three archetypes — IC builders, DRIs, AI founders) than like a single-superagent topology.
