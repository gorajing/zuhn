Humans abandoned wikis because cross-referencing maintenance grew faster than the value retrieved from them. LLMs flip that ratio: they cross-reference, summarize, and lint the graph for free, so a maintained markdown vault that you can grep, audit, and edit by hand outperforms opaque vector stores at personal-to-medium scale (under ~thousands of documents). The right move is to start with the simple vault and let scale failures force a cutover, rather than pre-arguing for embeddings before there's a problem to solve.
