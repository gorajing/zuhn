The systematic pattern across enterprise AI adoption is that projects fail on human and organizational factors, not on model capability. Companies assign their least technical people to critical data science roles, executives mandate 'more AI' as abdication rather than strategy, 83% of companies plan AI agent releases while only 13% have supporting infrastructure, and 95% of AI projects fail — driven equally by skills gaps and employee resistance. The unglamorous work of cultural readiness, staff training, and accurate data pipelines determines outcomes far more than which model is deployed.
