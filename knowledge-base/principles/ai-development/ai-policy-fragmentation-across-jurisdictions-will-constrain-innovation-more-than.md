The most dangerous regulatory threat to AI is not strict rules from any one authority but the patchwork of incompatible regulations emerging simultaneously across jurisdictions. The US faces 1,200+ state-level bills that could create 50 incompatible AI regulatory regimes; the EU AI Act's foundation model provisions could outlaw competition with frontier labs while providing no real safety benefit. Meanwhile, China strategically releases open-weight models for global distribution and influence, nations compete in a zero-sum race the labs themselves don't acknowledge, and cultural trust differentials (32% US vs 50-70% in leading countries) drive adoption gaps independent of technology availability.
