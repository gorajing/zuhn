A leading psycholinguist confirms LLMs have mastered syntax and patterns but not semantics, and the gap between form and meaning may be far larger than assumed. Humans interpret language by modeling speaker intent, so when LLMs produce coherent text we involuntarily attribute intelligence that isn't there. This means hallucination and creativity are the same mechanism, and the form-meaning gap represents a structural ceiling distinct from scaling limitations.
