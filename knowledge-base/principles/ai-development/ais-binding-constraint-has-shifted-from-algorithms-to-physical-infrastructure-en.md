Data center buildout is limited by energy permitting (multi-year delays that capital cannot solve), network quality determines effective GPU cluster size, and globalized GPU supply chains are fragile. Orbital compute proposals and the Fed citing AI as an investment driver both confirm that physical infrastructure, not algorithmic breakthroughs, is now the bottleneck. Every facility built today locks in capacity that will be irreplaceable if supply chains break, making infrastructure decisions strategic bets with decade-long consequences.
