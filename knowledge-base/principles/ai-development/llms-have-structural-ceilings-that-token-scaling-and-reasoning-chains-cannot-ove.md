LLMs generate output one token at a time without forming an abstract plan first, pattern-match across surface statistics rather than building deep logical chains, and hallucinate as an inherent consequence of probabilistic generation that worsens with longer reasoning chains. These are not bugs to be patched but architectural properties. Meanwhile, the cost reality undermines the scaling narrative: reasoning models burn dramatically more tokens per useful output, actual inference costs per task have risen, and users would abandon services if charged per error. People routinely mistake linguistic fluency for genuine intelligence, but AI's real wins remain confined to domains with verifiable ground truth, not the sensory and tacit knowledge that dominates human cognition.
