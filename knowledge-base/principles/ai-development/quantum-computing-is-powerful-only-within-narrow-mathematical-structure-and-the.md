Quantum computers exploit superposition and quantum Fourier transforms to find periodic patterns in specific mathematical structures like modular arithmetic -- they do not brute-force all possibilities. Measurement collapses superposition to one random result, so only problems with special extractable structure (like factoring) yield useful answers, and decoherence means a 100-qubit computation may need a million physical qubits for error correction. Despite these limitations, the threat is present-tense: nation states are already intercepting and storing encrypted data, betting quantum decryption arrives within 10-20 years, while NIST has published post-quantum lattice-based standards as countermeasures.
