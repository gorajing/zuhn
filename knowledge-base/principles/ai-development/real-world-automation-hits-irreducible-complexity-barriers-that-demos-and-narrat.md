AI automation narratives -- both optimistic and pessimistic -- share a blind spot: they describe what happens after AI works without addressing how it would handle the irreducible complexity of real occupations. Enterprise systems require SOC-2 compliance, multi-currency handling, legal accountability, and ongoing maintenance that prompted LLMs cannot reliably provide. Every occupation embeds tacit knowledge that resists decomposition into automatable subtasks, which is why rigorous economic analysis predicts only 5% of tasks will be profitably automated within a decade. Even the physical substrate is hitting limits as transistors approach quantum tunneling thresholds, and the most productive path forward may be searching program space rather than designing from scratch.
