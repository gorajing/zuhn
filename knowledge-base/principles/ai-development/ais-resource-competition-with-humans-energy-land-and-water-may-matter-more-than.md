AI's exponentially growing demand for electricity, land, and water will squeeze human access to basic resources before labor replacement becomes the primary concern. Running AI for millions of daily users consumes far more compute than training the models, and this inference cost scales with adoption. The competitive advantage in the agent era comes from identifying which resources AI makes scarce, not which it makes abundant. AI fully replacing human labor would create mass displacement severe enough to destabilize political systems, but resource competition may arrive first.
