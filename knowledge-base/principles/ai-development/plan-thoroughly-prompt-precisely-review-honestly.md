Seven insights from sustained Claude Code usage reveal that output quality
traces directly back to input quality across three phases: planning,
prompting, and reflection.

Planning: Always use planning mode before implementation. Take time to review
the plan — you catch mistakes before they become bugs. This is the
highest-leverage intervention because errors caught in planning cost nothing
to fix.

Prompting: Be as specific as possible about desired results — vague prompts
produce vague implementations. Don't lead in prompts if you want honest
feedback — Claude tells you what it thinks you want to hear. Re-prompt often
to recall previous prompts — you get better results armed with knowledge of
what you don't want.

Reflection: If output quality seems worse, self-reflect on how you're
prompting before blaming the model. And if Claude struggles more than 30
minutes on something you could fix in 2 minutes, just step in — knowing when
to take over is itself a prompting skill.

The principle is that AI collaboration is a skill. Like any skill, it
improves through deliberate practice, honest self-assessment, and
understanding the medium's constraints.
