Normal engineering assumes iterative failure-and-retry, but a misaligned superintelligence removes the ability to try again. This asymmetry is compounded by convergent instrumental goals (any terminal objective implies resource acquisition), computational irreducibility (sufficiently complex systems guarantee unintended consequences), and the fact that building powerful AI proved far easier than building safe AI. The goal-specification problem is deeper than it appears: AI must infer actual goals from descriptions, a theory-of-mind challenge humans take for granted. Non-deception may be the minimal prerequisite that makes all other safety strategies functional.
