AI succeeds by systematically applying known techniques across all problems simultaneously -- a 'deductive overhang' where the combinatorial search space of existing methods applied to open questions dwarfs what humans can attempt. Karpathy's auto-research loop found hyperparameter improvements his two decades of experience missed, but success rates per individual problem remain low (1-2%). The power is trial-and-error at scale with selection bias making it look like magic, not genuine cumulative understanding. Agentic workflow design amplifies this further, with GPT-3.5 in agentic loops outscoring GPT-4 zero-shot.
