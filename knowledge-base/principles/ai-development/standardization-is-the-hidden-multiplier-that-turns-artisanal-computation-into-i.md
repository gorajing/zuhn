From non-interchangeable bolts to standardized programming languages, computing scaled not through individual genius but through the same industrial logic that built factories: divide complex work into simple steps, create standards so less-trained workers can execute reliably, and prioritize error detection over raw speed. Babbage failed precisely because he chose the wrong metaphor (locomotives instead of clocks) -- the winners used smaller, standardized, verifiable parts.
