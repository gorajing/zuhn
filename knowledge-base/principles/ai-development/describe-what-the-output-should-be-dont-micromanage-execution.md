The paradigm shift in prompting: describe the desired output (role, audience, format, constraints, success criteria) rather than issuing step-by-step instructions. Expert prompters write shorter prompts (150-300 words) that compress the problem space through structural clarity. Adding instructions to fuzzy tasks creates noise. The four-question test: Who is this for? What will it be used for? What constraints matter? What does success look like?
