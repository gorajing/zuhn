Unlike language models that scaled on internet text, robotics and physical AI face a fundamental data scarcity problem -- there is no internet-scale robot action data. The path to physical AI scaling runs through cheap commodity hardware (sub-$300 robots), community platforms aggregating diverse datasets from thousands of contributors, video diffusion models generating synthetic training data from real robot footage, and deployed robots producing training data as a byproduct of economically useful work. This creates a compounding flywheel where each generation of robots generates the data that trains the next, a dynamic unavailable to pure-software AI.
