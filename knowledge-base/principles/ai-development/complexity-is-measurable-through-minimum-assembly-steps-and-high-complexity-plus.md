Assembly Theory provides a substrate-independent method for detecting whether an object was produced by an evolutionary process: measure its assembly index (minimum steps to create it) and its copy number (abundance). Random chance can produce complex one-offs or abundant simple things, but not both -- high complexity plus high abundance is a reliable signature of evolution accumulating and recombining successful building blocks. This framework applies to any substrate, not just biology, and its controversial Nature publication generated more productive scientific scrutiny than conventional papers.
