Every major platform shift (PCs, web, mobile) succeeded by creating entirely new categories of behavior rather than incrementally improving what came before. AI follows this same pattern -- Google's 10 blue links were always a compromise, and NVIDIA's CUDA was a speculative infrastructure bet that paid off only when an unforeseen use case (deep learning) emerged. Unlike previous shifts where physical limits were known (modem speeds, battery life), AI lacks a theoretical ceiling because we don't fully understand why it works, making deterministic predictions about its trajectory uniquely unreliable.
