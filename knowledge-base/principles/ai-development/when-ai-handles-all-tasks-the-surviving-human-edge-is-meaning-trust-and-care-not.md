As AI automates strategy, design, copy, and prototyping, the irreplaceable human roles shift to orchestrating meaning, embodying trustworthiness, and defining scope of care. Employers increasingly hire for qualities visible in character rather than resumes. Human creative work carries an 'aura' of authenticity -- accumulated suffering, time, and narrative -- that AI episodes cannot replicate. The durable career moat is being the person others actively want to collaborate with, not being the person who executes tasks fastest.
