Like steam engines making coal cheaper and thereby increasing total coal consumption, AI lowering the cost of knowledge work will likely expand total demand for that work. Finite compute forces allocation tradeoffs that keep humans economically relevant regardless of AI capability, and cheaper AI models produce more value by expanding demand rather than compressing margins. Individual task speedups are real (55-81% productivity gains) but haven't appeared in aggregate statistics yet, suggesting demand expansion is absorbing the gains.
