The most powerful breakthroughs in both science and engineering come from finding an abstraction that converts intractable complexity into simple manipulation. Lagrange converted vectors to scalars and unlocked the three-body problem; the principle of least action generates all physics laws from a single Lagrangian; Cantor's diagonal argument recurs identically across Godel, Turing, and Russell; running the same interpreter at compile and runtime eliminates an entire category of optimization complexity. The pattern is that finding the right symbolic representation — one that matches human conceptualization while remaining formally manipulable — is worth more than any amount of brute-force engineering.
