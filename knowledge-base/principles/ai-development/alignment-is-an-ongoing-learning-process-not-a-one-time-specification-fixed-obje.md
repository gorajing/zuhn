The 'standard model' of AI — giving machines fixed objectives — inevitably produces self-preservation and resistance to shutdown. RLHF alignment only holds within the training distribution and breaks when systems exceed evaluator intelligence. Treating alignment like writing commandments rather than raising a child produces dangerous rule-followers without understanding. The only robust path is AI that genuinely models and cares about human intent, not AI that follows instructions.
