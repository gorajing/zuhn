Frontier models outperform human experts on structured scientific Q&A but collapse on multi-step research workflows that produce the output — on ChemBench's 2,700+ Q&A pairs top models beat human chemists on average, yet score below 20% on ReplicationBench paper-scale replication tasks, and the best research agents reach only 38.8% on PaperArena versus 83.5% for PhD experts. The gap is 45-65 percentage points for the same model on the same domain, making benchmark saturation a misleading proxy for scientific competence. Benchmark-to-discovery translation is a sharp cliff, not a smooth slope.
