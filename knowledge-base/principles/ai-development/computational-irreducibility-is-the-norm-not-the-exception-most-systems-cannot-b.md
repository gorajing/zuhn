Most complex systems are computationally irreducible: their behavior cannot be predicted without running the full computation step by step. Science and mathematics advance by finding rare pockets of reducibility (proofs, closed-form solutions), but these are exceptions carved out of a default landscape of irreducibility. This means formal undecidability results (diagonal argument, halting problem) are not quirks but reflections of a deep structural property, and even the physical universe may enforce irreducibility through limits on infinite precision.
