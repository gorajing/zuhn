The biggest risk of AI isn't failure — it's success that causes skill atrophy. When AI handles tasks perfectly, processes become rigid, humans stop developing, and organizations lose the ability to innovate. Workers report building software without understanding how it works. The antidote: choose a lower level of automation than possible to keep humans engaged, reorganize around problem-solving rather than tool domains, and treat learning as a first-class system requirement. Trust in AI adoption depends more on trust in employers than trust in the technology.
