Video generation models trained purely on next-frame prediction are beginning to develop implicit physical understanding — Google DeepMind's Veo 3 demonstrated buoyancy simulation and maze solving across 18,000 generated videos without being explicitly trained on those tasks. This suggests video datasets function as a training substrate for embodied AI, not just a media product, extending the compression-forces-intelligence pattern from text to physics-in-pixels. The caveat is that the jagged frontier applies — 'works on buoyancy' does not mean 'works on friction, momentum, or deformable materials.'
