Unix pipes, 300 rotating arrows tracing arbitrary shapes, single-threaded event loops handling thousands of connections, and layered caching systems all demonstrate the same principle: combine many simple operations to produce complex behavior. This beats building clever one-off solutions because each component remains testable, replaceable, and understandable in isolation. The power comes from the composition protocol, not from the sophistication of individual parts.
