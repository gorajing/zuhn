As AI coding tools handle implementation, the planning-to-development ratio has inverted to as high as 9:1, making structured planning the primary constraint on software quality. This creates new roles like FDE (translating vague requirements into AI-ready specs) and new disciplines like harness engineering (using documentation, linting, and architectural constraints to force consistent AI outputs). Text-only planning is insufficient -- parallel visual artifacts and progressive decomposition from ambiguity to precision are required to give AI agents the contextual consistency they need.
