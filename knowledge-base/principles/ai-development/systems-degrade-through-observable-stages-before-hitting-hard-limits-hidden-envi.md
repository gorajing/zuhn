Minecraft displays five distinct degradation zones before total failure, from buggy terrain to void to infinity, demonstrating how systems fail gradually through observable stages. RuBisCo was evolved for a low-oxygen atmosphere and breaks in today's conditions — a direct analogy for software systems whose implicit environmental assumptions become bugs when contexts shift. Hard limits like 64-bit double boundaries or transistor tunneling at 5 atoms create absolute ceilings, but the path to those ceilings produces diagnostic signals that attentive builders can catch.
