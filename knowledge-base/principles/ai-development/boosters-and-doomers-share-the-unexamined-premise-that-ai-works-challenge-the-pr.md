The dominant AI debate frames the only question as whether powerful AI is good or bad, while both camps assume the technology delivers on its promises. The belief that AGI is inevitable and imminent is a community assumption masquerading as scientific consensus. When the AI marketing machine has massive capital buying corridor-of-power access, and people desperately want to believe technology can solve existential crises, critical evaluation of whether the technology actually works reliably gets systematically suppressed.
