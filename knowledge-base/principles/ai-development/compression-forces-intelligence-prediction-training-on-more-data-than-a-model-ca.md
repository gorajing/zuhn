When neural networks must compress vastly more data than they can store, they are forced to discover abstract patterns and algorithmic shortcuts rather than memorizing examples. Embedding spaces develop meaningful geometric structure — with directions encoding concepts like gender and nationality — as a side effect of prediction training, not explicit instruction. Multi-layered optimization produces emergent capabilities irreducible to any single layer's objective, suggesting that the compression bottleneck is the mechanism through which statistical training produces something resembling understanding.
