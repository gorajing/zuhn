Karpathy's coding workflow flipped from 80% manual to 80% agent-driven in weeks, not months. The Cursor founders saw scaling laws as abstract until early GPT-4 access made the theoretical gains concrete enough to bet careers. Each major AI leap — next-word prediction, scaling, and RL reasoning — seemed 'too simple to work' before proving right. The pattern suggests enterprises still in the 'looking for use-cases' phase are sitting before a phase transition, not on a gentle slope.
