The largest measured AI wins come from replacing entire workflows with a single ML system rather than assisting individual steps — Aardvark Weather replaced the traditional numerical weather prediction pipeline with a single ML model and FourCastNet 3 runs 8-60x faster than prior approaches. Figure AI's Helix 02 replaces over 100,000 lines of C++ with a three-layer neural network, and Karpathy's coding workflow flipped from 80% manual to 80% agent-driven in weeks once capability crossed a threshold. The pattern is that workflow replacement consistently outperforms step-level augmentation in measured impact, making 'which workflow can be replaced end-to-end' the right question for AI deployment, not 'which task can be assisted.'
