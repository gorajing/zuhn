  Biology's electron transport chain extracts energy across four protein
  complexes progressively, producing intermediates (ATP, NADPH) usable by many
  downstream processes, rather than attempting one catastrophic energy capture.
  Photosynthesis uses G3P as a universal intermediate convertible to glucose,
  cellulose, or starch. This staged-intermediate pattern recurs in effective
  system architecture: tiered model pipelines (filter cheap, process expensive),
  compiler intermediate representations, and multi-agent workflows that pass
  structured intermediate states. Systems that try to go from raw input to final
  output in one step are fragile; systems that produce reusable intermediates at
  each stage are resilient and composable.

Biology's electron transport chain extracts energy across four protein complexes progressively, producing intermediates (ATP, NADPH) usable by many downstream processes, rather than attempting one catastrophic energy capture. Photosynthesis uses G3P as a universal intermediate convertible to glucose, cellulose, or starch. This staged-intermediate pattern recurs in effective system architecture: tiered model pipelines (filter cheap, process expensive), compiler intermediate representations, and multi-agent workflows that pass structured intermediate states. Systems that try to go from raw input to final output in one step are fragile; systems that produce reusable intermediates at each stage are resilient and composable.
