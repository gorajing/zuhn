Two dynamics run simultaneously: the cost per unit of AI intelligence drops roughly 4x per year (DeepSeek sat perfectly on this curve), while total industry spending rises roughly 10x per year. DeepSeek replicated frontier capabilities from a hedge fund in months as a free open-source model. Meta giving away a $10B model forces commoditization while costing nothing since it doesn't sell AI. The result is a market where individual model pricing power erodes rapidly while the total investment needed to stay at the frontier keeps climbing — creating a paradox where AI gets cheaper and more expensive simultaneously.
