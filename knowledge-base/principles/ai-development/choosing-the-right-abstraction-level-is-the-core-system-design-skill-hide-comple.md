Programming languages, functions, libraries, and data types all exist as abstraction layers that hide complexity. Data types teach the separation of representation from operation. Surrogate keys prevent breakage when business rules change. The recurring pattern: the best systems are not the simplest — they are the ones where each layer handles exactly its own complexity and exposes a clean interface to the next.
