Biology builds elephants at room temperature from grass with zero waste using self-replicating molecular machines -- a feat no human manufacturing process approaches. Gershenfeld argues that Turing and von Neumann made a fundamental mistake by separating software from hardware, since biology computes with its physical structure. Digital fabrication is now following the PC democratization trajectory from institutional tools to personal capability, and self-replicating machines are the key to exponential manufacturing capacity.
