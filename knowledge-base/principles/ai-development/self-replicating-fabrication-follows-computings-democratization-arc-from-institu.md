Digital fabrication is tracing the same trajectory computing followed: from expensive institutional tools to community fab labs to eventual personal capability. The key insight is that biology already solved this — ribosomes build elephants one molecule at a time through self-replication, at room temperature with zero waste. Gershenfeld's work at the bits-atoms boundary reveals that life itself is the original digital-physical hybrid, and Turing and von Neumann's separation of software from hardware was a detour from biology's more powerful approach of computing with physical structure directly.
