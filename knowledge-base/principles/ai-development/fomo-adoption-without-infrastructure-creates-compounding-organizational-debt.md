95% of AI projects fail — twice the rate of any other emerging technology — because organizations skip data science fundamentals, assign their least technical people to AI training, and mandate 'more AI everywhere' as a substitute for strategy. MIT research documents $291B wasted because the ecosystem trained people to sell AI services rather than build reliable systems. The gap between AI adoption plans (83% of companies) and actual infrastructure readiness (13%) compounds over time, creating organizational debt that gets harder to unwind.
