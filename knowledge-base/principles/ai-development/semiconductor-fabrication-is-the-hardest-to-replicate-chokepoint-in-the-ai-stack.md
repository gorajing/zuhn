Advanced chip manufacturing requires a globally distributed supply chain spanning dozens of countries and technologies so extreme that only three companies can fabricate cutting-edge processors. Unlike nuclear weapons which spread to even small nations, chip fabrication demands precision at sub-viral scales using machines with no competitor, making it economically and physically irreplaceable. Moore's Law persists not through physics breakthroughs but through an economic flywheel where each transistor shrink unlocks larger markets that fund the next round of R&D — and AI demand is the current force sustaining this cycle.
