AI generates hypotheses far faster than labs can test them, making physical verification the binding constraint in experimental sciences. AlphaFold solved protein structure prediction in seconds where wet lab took years, but drug development remains gated by clinical trials that no computation can skip. Tao's papers now contain more code and deeper literature searches thanks to AI, but the hardest parts still happen on pen and paper. More simulated George Churches in data centers would not multiply biology progress because the true bottleneck is wet-lab experimentation, not idea generation. Theoretical fields based on logical implications can be nearly push-button accelerated by AI, while experimental fields remain gated by the speed of physical testing. The implication for AI-for-science strategy: invest in robotic lab automation and regulatory reform, not just smarter models.
