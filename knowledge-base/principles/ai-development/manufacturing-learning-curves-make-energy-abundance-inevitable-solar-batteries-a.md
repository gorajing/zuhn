Unlike coal and oil that must be extracted from finite deposits, solar panels and batteries are manufactured technologies that follow learning curves — every doubling of cumulative production drops cost by approximately 20%. This is the same dynamic that drove Moore's Law in semiconductors. AI, clean energy, and bioengineering are simultaneously hitting their adoption tipping points, suggesting a civilizational convergence where cheap abundant energy becomes as inevitable as cheap abundant computation.
