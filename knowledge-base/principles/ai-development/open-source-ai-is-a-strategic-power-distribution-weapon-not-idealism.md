Major tech companies open-source AI models primarily as a competitive strategy to prevent power concentration by rivals, not out of altruism. Meta frames open-source as ecosystem capture that improves their own infrastructure while attracting researchers, while Zuckerberg and LeCun explicitly argue that concentrated proprietary AI is the real existential danger. OpenAI's governance crisis proved that when nonprofit mission authority clashes with financial stakeholders, money wins — validating the structural argument for open distribution as the primary check on AI power concentration.
