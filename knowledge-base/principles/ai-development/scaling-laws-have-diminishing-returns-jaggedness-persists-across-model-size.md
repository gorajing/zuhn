The leap from GPT-4 to GPT-5 required benchmarks to detect, unlike earlier leaps that were immediately obvious, suggesting pre-training scaling has hit inflection points. Models remain simultaneously superhuman in RL-trained domains and mediocre outside them, and this jagged capability profile does not smooth out with scale. Betting everything on continued exponential improvement from larger models ignores theoretical ceilings from Goedel's incompleteness and the compute-proportional requirements of genuine research.
