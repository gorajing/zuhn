A fully occupied infinite hotel can always accommodate more guests. A single twenty-sixth of an infinite dictionary contains every possible word. A sphere can be decomposed and reassembled into two identical spheres. Perfect squares become vanishingly rare yet remain infinite and perfectly predictable. Real numbers between 0 and 1 outnumber all whole numbers. These are not paradoxes — they are the correct arithmetic of infinity. The common error is importing finite conservation rules (parts must be smaller than wholes, removing items makes sets smaller) into a domain where they do not apply. This pattern generalizes: systems operating at different scales often have fundamentally different rules, and the most persistent errors come from assuming familiar rules hold everywhere.
