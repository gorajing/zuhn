When decisions become fused with personal identity, rational evaluation shuts down. This manifests across scales: Houdini refused life-saving surgery because accepting help contradicted his self-image, politicians cling to failing policies that define their brand, and prestige biases people toward careers they would like to want rather than ones they actually want. The remedy is keeping your identity small -- refusing to label yourself preserves the ability to update across domains.
