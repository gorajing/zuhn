A nihilist still feels thirsty and still drinks water — nihilism removes metaphysical grounding but not subjective motivation. Ask 'why' enough times about any action and you reach either God, a self-justifying value like family, or the uncomfortable realization that reasons simply run out. Meaning is found when you reach a motivation so fundamental that asking 'but why?' feels like a category error. The cherry blossom is beautiful because it falls, not despite it — awareness of impermanence deepens experience rather than diminishing it. Hindu cyclical time resolves existential anxiety by framing all experience as phases in an eternal rhythm, while Western linear time creates urgency because every moment is unique. When you stop trying to control your life and start experiencing it as something unfolding, anxiety drops and engagement increases. The most fundamental question — what would remain if everything were taken away — reveals what you actually value versus what you maintain out of habit.
