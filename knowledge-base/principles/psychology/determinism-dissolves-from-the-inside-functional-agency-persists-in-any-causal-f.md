Even in a fully deterministic universe, the inability to compute the future from within the system makes free will functionally real. Nihilism removes metaphysical grounding but not thirst — you still drink the water. Compatibilism, hierarchical causation, and interconnected chaos all converge on the same operational truth: modeling yourself and others as choice-making agents is the correct strategy regardless of the underlying metaphysics, because no finite being can be Laplace's demon. The debate over determinism is philosophically interesting but practically inert.
