Your total capacity to focus resets each day. Every input — email, podcast, notification, social media — takes permanent mental space until the next reset. The first thing you focus on each morning gets prime mental real estate with zero competition, so that slot should go to your single most important task. Delay checking non-essential inputs as long as possible each day. Large dopamine peaks trigger proportional drops below baseline, creating hidden burnout from seemingly healthy activities. Doing your most exciting task first makes boring subsequent tasks feel worse — start with moderately interesting work instead. Three minutes of deliberately doing nothing resets cognitive overload better than any productivity technique. The brain needs both exercise (hard thinking) and rest (deliberate boredom) — AI outsourcing eliminates the exercise, digital stimulation eliminates the rest.
