Agriculture was adopted through millions of small, locally rational choices that collectively created an irreversible civilizational transformation — despite making individuals shorter, sicker, and more overworked than foragers. Revolutions result from accumulated small decisions, not single dramatic moments. Humans systematically linearize exponentials, under-reacting when early action is cheapest and over-reacting when the situation is already determined. The pattern applies directly to AI adoption: individually rational decisions to use AI tools collectively create a transformation whose aggregate consequences no individual chose.
