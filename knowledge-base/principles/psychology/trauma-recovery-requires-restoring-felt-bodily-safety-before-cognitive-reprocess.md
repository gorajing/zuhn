Trauma recovery begins when a person can say 'this is what happened to me and my reactions make sense' rather than 'something is wrong with me.' Body-based interventions outperform medication and pure talk therapy because trauma is stored in survival circuitry, not narrative memory. Research across 100+ studies shows two-thirds of people exposed to potentially traumatic events show resilient trajectories. When a low-vibration emotion surfaces, locate it physically in your body and feel it for a few minutes — this diffuses the charge permanently. The healthiest people in isolation self-stimulate through counting, exercise, and narration; passive endurance leads to cognitive decline. Kierkegaard: anxiety is the dizziness of freedom, and numbing it eliminates the compass for meaningful action. Happiness is fragile; joy is existentially gritty and can coexist with suffering.
