Humans privilege emotionally vivid individual cases over statistical patterns because stories activate empathy circuits that numbers cannot reach. One identified victim moves people more than 500,000 statistical deaths; a single dramatic anecdote dominates perception even when aggregate data contradicts it. This creates systematic distortion in policy, media, philanthropy, and personal risk assessment.
