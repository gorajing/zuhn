Education systems explicitly aim to transmit knowledge faithfully and produce behavioral conformity — the opposite of the creative knowledge generation that defines human uniqueness. Schools adapt subjects so heavily for teaching that students never encounter real work in those fields, delaying career discovery. Traditional emphasis on obedience produces wisdom at the expense of intelligence by suppressing idiosyncratic interests. The corrective is parents encouraging preposterously deep pursuit of genuine fascinations.
