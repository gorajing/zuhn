Most smart people produce nothing original because new ideas demand a combination of obsessive interest, independent-mindedness, and willingness to explore directions that look unpromising by definition. False popular beliefs create predictable blind spots where novel insights cluster, but only intellectually honest thinkers can find them because dishonesty produces boring conclusions by preventing the discovery of surprising ideas. Great discoverers like Darwin succeeded by refusing to let anomalies pass unnoticed rather than through raw intelligence.
