Every ambitious project looks embarrassingly bad early on, and most creators quit before pushing through this inevitable phase. Applying finished-work standards to early-stage work is a category error that destroys ambition. The practical counter-strategies are: framing ambitious work as sketches or experiments to lower the psychological barrier, maintaining slight overconfidence to armor against self-doubt, and surrounding yourself with colleagues who can distinguish ugly ducklings from genuine failures.
