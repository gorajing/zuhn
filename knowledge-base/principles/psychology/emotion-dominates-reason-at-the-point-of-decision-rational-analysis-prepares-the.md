Even when people can articulate the rational choice, emotional intuitions still dominate actual decisions. Romantic love activates ancient drive systems while shutting down planning regions; personal moral dilemmas shift processing from prefrontal cortex to amygdala; psychopaths' ability to bypass emotional interference makes them more effective in certain high-stakes scenarios. The practical implication is that decision systems must be designed to either harness or structurally bypass emotional override, not merely inform against it.
