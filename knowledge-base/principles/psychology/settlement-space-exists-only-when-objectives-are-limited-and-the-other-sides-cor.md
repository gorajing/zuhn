Negotiations succeed when both sides can walk away with their identity intact, and fail when one side signals total elimination. Limited objectives ('you lose a province but stay in power') create room for compromise; unlimited objectives ('you're out of power') eliminate it entirely. Whether compromise is wisdom or suicide depends entirely on the other side's real objective — territorial concessions can be traded, but regime change and extermination cannot be appeased without empowering the executioner. The same dynamic operates at every scale: arguments escalate when they shift from issues to threatening identity, and avoiding triumphalism after winning buys decades of integration time that gloating would foreclose.
