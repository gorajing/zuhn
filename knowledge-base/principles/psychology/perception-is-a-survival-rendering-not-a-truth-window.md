Across evolutionary biology, neuroscience, and physics, the evidence converges: what you perceive is not reality but a fitness-optimized interface constructed by your brain. Hoffman's interface theory shows evolution selects for useful illusion over truth; the brain renders a real-time simulation filtered for survival; meaning-making runs as a default mode generating false positives; your subjective 'now' is a 200-300ms stitched fabrication; and even your sense of a stable world is an artifact of confinement in a narrow dimensional slice. Accepting that perception is always a rendering -- never a recording -- is the prerequisite for questioning any deeply held assumption about what is real.
