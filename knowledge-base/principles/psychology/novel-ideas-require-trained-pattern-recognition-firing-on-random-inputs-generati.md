Ideas form when domain-trained mental habits involuntarily fire on random stimuli, which is why expertise is a prerequisite for creative insight. Removing passive consumption (reading, scrolling) forces the mind to generate its own entertainment through original ideas. Writing ideas down matters not because you will re-read them but because holding one idea in memory blocks the next from forming. The practical implication is that idea generation is a pipeline problem — load the training, clear the buffer, then let the pattern-matcher run.
