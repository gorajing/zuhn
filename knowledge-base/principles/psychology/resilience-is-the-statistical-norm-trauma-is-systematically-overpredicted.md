Research across over 100 studies consistently shows approximately two-thirds of people exposed to potentially traumatic events continue functioning normally. Events are potentially traumatic, not inherently traumatic — trauma lives in the response, not the event. During mass crises, social contagion and affective forecasting bias create collective blind spots where people cannot imagine their distress is temporary, leading to systematic overprediction of lasting damage.
