Destin's backwards bicycle experiment and related evidence show that cognitive biases are not bad habits you can decide to stop; they are structural features of neural architecture. Even after learning a new pattern, the old algorithm persists and can resurface. Every participant who understood the bias intellectually still could not override it through conscious effort. The practical implication is that bias mitigation requires environmental redesign and structural safeguards, not willpower or awareness alone.
