The inner voice handles working memory, future simulation, self-control, and identity construction. Chatter occurs when this system locks into repetitive stress loops, keeping the stress response active long after the trigger has passed. Childhood lies compound into silent cognitive debt, and unprocessed past experiences hijack present reactions. The intervention is not silencing the inner voice but restoring its normal operating mode through deliberate processing.
