The most insidious form of bias is not arriving at wrong conclusions but unconsciously adjusting the reasoning process itself to protect desired beliefs. People retroactively neutralize their own clear assessments, deploy circular definitions, stack artifact explanations, and apply scrutiny asymmetrically — all while experiencing themselves as objective. The only reliable safeguards are structural: name what would change your mind before investigating, argue both sides, and check if you would accept the same logic from an opponent.
