Stuart Firestein argues science begins where facts run out. Gödel proved that any consistent system contains true statements it cannot prove — there are always unreachable truths that must be axiomatically declared. Confusion about advanced topics is usually weak prerequisites, not genuine complexity. The implication: the frontier of knowledge is defined by the quality of questions you can ask, not the quantity of answers you've memorized.
