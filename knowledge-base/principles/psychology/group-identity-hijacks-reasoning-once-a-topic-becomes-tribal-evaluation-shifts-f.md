When a belief becomes entangled with group membership, cognitive evaluation switches from 'is this true?' to 'does this support my tribe?'. The same person who reasons clearly about neutral topics becomes systematically irrational about identity-charged ones. This manifests as swapping actors to test consistency, the left-right mirror where both sides deploy identical fallacies against different targets, and the social pressure that makes people misrepresent their own beliefs.
