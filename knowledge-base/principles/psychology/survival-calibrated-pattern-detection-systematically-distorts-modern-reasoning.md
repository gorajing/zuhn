Evolution optimized human cognition for false positives over false negatives — seeing a tiger that isn't there is survivable, missing one that is there is fatal. This ancient calibration now generates compulsive false causal narratives, meaning-in-noise hallucinations, and gut instincts that fail systematically on questions evolution never needed to answer: statistics, probability, long-term planning, and institutional design. The same mechanism that made ancestors excellent threat detectors makes modern humans terrible at distinguishing signal from noise in complex systems.
