Scott Adams, self-proclaimed persuasion expert, was destroyed by a manipulation technique straight from his own playbook that he failed to recognize. Smooth-tongued liars succeed by method-acting belief in false premises, producing internally consistent but externally ungrounded reasoning. Vague predictions appear accurate because our brains apply the 'law of near enough' — making almost any outcome confirmatory. The pattern: metacognitive confidence in your resistance to persuasion lowers your actual resistance, because you stop applying the skepticism that would catch the techniques you 'already know.' Persuasion offers short-term advantages but cannot indefinitely substitute for factual grounding.
