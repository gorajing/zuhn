Equations, proofs, and formal frameworks consistently generate valid predictions their authors did not put in and often resisted. Dirac's equation was 'smarter than he was' -- predicting antimatter from negative energy solutions that were initially condemned as 'the saddest chapter in modern physics,' only for the positron to be found one year later. Schwarzschild's simplest solution to Einstein's equations already contained black holes, white holes, and parallel universes, visible only after mathematicians pushed the coordinate map to its full extent. Mathematicians who publicly rejected the axiom of choice were found unknowingly using it in their own proofs. The pattern: when a formal system you trust produces an output that offends your intuition, bet on the system -- your intuition is the part shaped by evolutionary heuristics and social consensus, while the math has no such constraints.
