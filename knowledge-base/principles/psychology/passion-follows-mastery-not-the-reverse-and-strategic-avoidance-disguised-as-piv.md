Passion is a byproduct of getting good at something, not the prerequisite for starting. Entrepreneurial 'strategy sessions' about new directions often mask emotional avoidance of difficult execution work. When money arrives faster than feels normal, the correct response is to scale, not question legitimacy. The pattern: founders who chase passion find nothing; founders who chase competence find passion on the other side.
