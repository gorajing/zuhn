Great work begins as embarrassingly rough attempts that look worse than they are because we lack cultural norms for evaluating unfinished work. Successful creators overcome this by deliberately downgrading perceived stakes — calling work 'just a sketch' or 'just an experiment' — which disarms perfectionism while preserving the option to scale up later. Studying the humble origins of great creators trains you to judge early efforts accurately rather than abandoning them prematurely.
