Sadness comes from perceived lack of options (solve with knowledge). Anxiety comes from too many options without priorities (solve with a decision). Hopelessness reframes as 'I don't know enough yet' rather than 'my situation is impossible' — ignorance is solvable, fate is not. Expecting cyclical setbacks as normal growth features prevents premature quitting. The healthiest people in isolation self-stimulate actively rather than enduring passively.
