Staring at a wall for 10-15 minutes, the Dutch practice of niksen (three minutes of looking out a window), and simply doing nothing all reliably produce flow states and reset cognitive overload better than any productivity technique. The mechanism works by subtraction rather than addition: reducing inputs clears the processing queue that continuous stimulation keeps permanently full. Musashi's 'empty mind' and Thoreau's deliberate simplification describe the same principle -- total present-moment engagement requires creating space by eliminating, not by adding another tool or technique.
