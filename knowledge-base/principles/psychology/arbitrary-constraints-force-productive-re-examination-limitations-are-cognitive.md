Imposing restrictions on creative or intellectual work forces re-examination of assumptions and gravitates toward improvement. Imagining constrained environments reveals normalized dependencies; editing by cutting words forces you to discover what you actually mean; and training within extreme constraints (like the backwards bicycle) reveals how deeply procedural knowledge is wired. The value of constraints is not discipline — it is that they make invisible structure visible.
