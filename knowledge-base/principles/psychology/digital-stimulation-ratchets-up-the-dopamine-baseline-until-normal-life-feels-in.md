Every scroll, notification, and short-form video trains the brain to expect that level of stimulation as the new normal, progressively making books, conversations, and deep work feel unbearably slow. This is not a willpower problem but a neurobiological one: heavy screen use does not just exploit weak impulse control, it actively stunts the brain's ability to develop that control, creating a vicious cycle. The wanting circuit is easy to trigger but impossible to satisfy, and tech companies deliberately exploit this asymmetry between the diffuse wanting system and the small, satiable liking system.
