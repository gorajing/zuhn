When factual rebuttals would fail, moral labels (-ist, -phobic) replace them by signaling that truth-evaluation is no longer the relevant frame. This works because people's default response to being told they're breaking a norm is to believe the accuser, especially when norms shift rapidly. The pattern scales from personal rhetoric to geopolitical propaganda (the Black Legend) and produces a reliable failure mode: speakers who deploy delegitimization rhetoric lose the ability to credibly claim constructive leadership.
