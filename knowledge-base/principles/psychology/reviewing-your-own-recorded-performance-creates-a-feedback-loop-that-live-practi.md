Hormozi's requirement that sales teams record and review their own calls, the 130,000 fps camera revealing failure dynamics invisible to the naked eye, and the principle of mastering systems through exact replication before iteration all point to the same mechanism: the quality of the feedback loop determines the rate of skill acquisition more than the quantity of practice. Changing observation speed (slow motion, replay, re-reading) reveals patterns that real-time experience systematically hides. The structured debugging process from programming — isolate, reproduce, trace, fix — is a universal diagnostic framework that works because it forces systematic observation before intervention.
