When facing high uncertainty, the dominant strategy is to choose actions that keep the most future paths open rather than optimizing for any single predicted outcome. This applies to career timing (exploring in your twenties), financial sizing (investments small enough to lose painlessly), and life architecture (picking cities for network optionality). The cost of premature commitment is systematically underweighted because the options you foreclose are invisible.
