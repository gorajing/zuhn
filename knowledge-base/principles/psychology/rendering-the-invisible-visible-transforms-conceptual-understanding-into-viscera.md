Many phenomena remain mysterious not because they are inherently complex but because the critical dynamics happen outside human perceptual range. High-speed cameras revealing arrow flex, slow-motion exposing failure dynamics, transparency making hidden processes observable — all demonstrate that changing observation speed or adding instrumentation frequently provides more insight than adding theory. The pedagogical implication is to invest in making invisible dynamics perceptible before investing in explanation.
