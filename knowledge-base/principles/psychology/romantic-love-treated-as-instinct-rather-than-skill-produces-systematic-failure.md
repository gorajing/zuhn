De Botton argues we have been in a 'romantic age' for 250 years where love is treated as instinct and feeling, and it has been a disaster. Research shows we do not seek what makes us happy but what feels familiar — children of alcoholics seek partners who struggle with alcohol. Personal relationships determine up to 70% of life satisfaction yet we spend zero educational time on attachment theory, communication, or conflict resolution. Marriages fail like bankruptcy — slowly then suddenly — through accumulated small disconnections. The 5:1 positive-to-negative ratio, reflective listening, and separating behavior from identity are all learnable skills with enormous empirical backing. Treating love as a skill to learn rather than an instinct to follow would transform outcomes at scale.
