Living in multiple countries trains assumption-questioning because existing mental models repeatedly fail; psychedelics suppress the default mode network, breaking entrenched neural patterns; and forcing predictions before revealing answers deepens learning by exposing the gap between current models and reality. The common mechanism is disrupting automaticity — any intervention that forces the brain off its default predictions produces lasting cognitive flexibility that comfortable expertise cannot.
