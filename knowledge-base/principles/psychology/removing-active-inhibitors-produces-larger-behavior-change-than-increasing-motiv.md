When behavior is blocked by active inhibitors (stress, fear, friction), removing those brakes produces bigger results than pushing harder on motivation. Psychedelics appear to work therapeutically by temporarily destabilizing rigid neural patterns, creating a window of flexibility where new behavioral repertoires can form. Johnson's pilot study showed 80% smoking cessation at 6 months with psilocybin versus 25-35% for the best existing treatments. The early Islamic empire didn't require conversion but taxed non-Muslims more, creating a persistent economic nudge that drove widespread voluntary conversion over generations. The alcoholic refuses to change because changing means surrendering his defiant self-image — the inhibitor is identity, not capability. Write down small achievable goals nightly; the check-mark dopamine hit builds an addiction to the learning process itself.
