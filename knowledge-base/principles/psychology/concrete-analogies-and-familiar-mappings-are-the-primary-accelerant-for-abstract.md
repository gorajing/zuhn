Reframing intimidating concepts through familiar analogues — boxes for variables, restaurants for functions, family relationships for hierarchies, neural networks as 'just a function' — removes psychological barriers and provides cognitive scaffolding that formal definitions cannot. The pattern is consistent: teach core intuitions before precise definitions, use physical and relational metaphors, and reduce new concepts to already-understood categories. This works because human cognition naturally extends from concrete to abstract.
