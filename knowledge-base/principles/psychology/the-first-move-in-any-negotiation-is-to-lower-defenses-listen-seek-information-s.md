Across VC fundraising, M&A, and geopolitical diplomacy, the optimal opening is non-threatening curiosity rather than persuasion. Listen consciously for the first 10 minutes and acknowledge the other side's logic before countering. Frame early conversations as information-seeking ('what's the landscape?') rather than pitches. Start with something personal you researched about the human, not the capital interest. Ask 'what's your advice on how we can both get our interests met?' to convert an adversary into a co-problem-solver. These moves work because they address the counterparty's status and autonomy needs before any substantive negotiation begins.
