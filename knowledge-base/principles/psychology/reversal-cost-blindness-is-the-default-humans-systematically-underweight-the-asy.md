People consistently misjudge the difficulty of undoing decisions relative to making them. Getting a tattoo takes hours but removing one takes years; bullet loans feel cheap today but concentrate all risk into a single exit event; the freedom sacrificed for commitments was mostly unused anyway. This asymmetry is structural, not just psychological -- reversal costs grow nonlinearly as systems adapt around the commitment, making late exits exponentially more expensive than early ones.
