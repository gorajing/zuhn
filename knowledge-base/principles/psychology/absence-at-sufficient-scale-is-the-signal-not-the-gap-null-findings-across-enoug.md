When you've searched thoroughly enough and still find nothing, the absence itself becomes the most important data point. The empty universe is the central fact of the Fermi Paradox, not a gap waiting to be filled. 1,000+ nuclear tests with no civilization-ending catastrophe should update priors on catastrophic risk, not be dismissed as 'we got lucky.' The inverse also holds: future civilizations looking at a cosmically expanded universe will have no visible evidence of the Big Bang and will rationally but wrongly conclude a static, solitary cosmos — meaning the presence or absence of evidence must always be weighed against what the detection apparatus can actually see.
