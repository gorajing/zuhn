When moderates leave online discourse, remaining participants are more extreme than the population they claim to represent. Social media functions as a status marketplace where divisive hostility is the most reliable path to visibility. More disagreement online is a structural artifact of conversational media, not evidence that people are angrier. Anonymous communication degrades behavior like windshields degrade driving courtesy.
