Confident conclusions frequently dissolve when you trace them back to measurement problems rather than real effects. Temperature-dependent sensors produced a fake diurnal cycle in speed-of-light measurements because the lab was outdoors. Taking a vote on art quality is like finding north with a compass next to a magnet. China's one-child policy is widely credited with reducing fertility that was already declining. The discipline of separating instrument effects from real signals is the single most underrated skill in evaluation, and proxy metrics systematically distort the phenomena they claim to capture.
