Rich people don't take big risks — they structure deals to eliminate risk while preserving upside. The core mental model shift is from hourly compensation to value-capture percentage. Study what successful people did at your stage, not what they do now. Choose roles that maximize skill acquisition over immediate compensation during growth years. The differentiator for top performers is absence of distractions, not presence of special abilities. Masa Son's explicit 50-year plan gave him a framework to absorb $70B losses without changing course. The real obstacle to your first customers is your discomfort with asking people you know to buy from you — not market conditions. Redefine success as daily activity completion rather than outcome milestones to break the feast-famine cycle.
