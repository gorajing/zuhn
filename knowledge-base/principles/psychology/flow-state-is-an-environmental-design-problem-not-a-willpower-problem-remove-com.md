Focus emerges naturally when all competing stimuli are removed, not from trying harder to concentrate. Physically separating work and internet computers forces conscious awareness of distraction that software tools cannot replicate. Engineers who protect mornings from all interruptions achieve 65% above-average productivity, and coders knocked out of flow need 15+ minutes to return. The practical architecture is: block first 4-6 hours for deep work, physically separate distraction sources, and design systems so interruptions require deliberate effort rather than prevention.
