Across mathematics, physics, and problem-solving, choosing the right representation transforms intractable problems into obvious ones. Drawing the right picture turns abstract formulas into geometric facts. Decomposing along natural symmetries yields simpler subproblems. Recognizing that 'sum of many small things' equals 'area under a curve' unlocks a universal template. Discovering inverse operations gives you one skill for free. The common thread: difficulty is usually an artifact of representation, not intrinsic to the problem. This applies beyond math — Torricelli dissolved the vacuum debate by reframing it as atmospheric pressure, and Lagrange replaced 3D vector mechanics with scalar energy to solve previously impossible problems.
