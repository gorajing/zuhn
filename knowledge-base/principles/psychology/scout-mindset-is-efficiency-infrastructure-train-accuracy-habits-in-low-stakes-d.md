Scout thinking (Galef's motivation to see what's actually there vs. what you wish was there) is not a moral posture — it's an efficiency strategy. Sacrificing accuracy for comfort has compounding costs across thousands of life decisions that usually exceed the emotional benefit. But scout mindset can't be summoned in high-stakes moments where identity is threatened; it has to be trained in advance on ideologically low-stakes domains (politics you don't care about, arguments you aren't emotionally invested in) so the habits are there when you need them. A large fraction of apparent belief defense is actually identity defense against positions that were never consciously chosen — bet against the explanation where you are the protagonist, and look for the honest-comforting intersection before jumping to self-deception.
