Multiple sources converge: confidence follows action, not the reverse. Duckworth's grit data shows grit is unrelated or inversely related to talent — the most gifted often lack perseverance. The tech sales insight that confidence builds from doing big things while scared, not from thinking yourself into readiness, maps to the same pattern. Montaigne's observation that honest self-examination produces deeper insight once you stop caring about outcomes, and Spinoza's claim that rational clarity comes from caring less about what you can't influence, both support the same structure: the attempt to manufacture confidence before action is backwards. Act first, survive, and confidence follows as evidence of competence.
