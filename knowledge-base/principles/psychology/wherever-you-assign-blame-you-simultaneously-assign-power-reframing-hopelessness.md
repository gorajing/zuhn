Blame is pain management disguised as accountability -- when people blame, they are discharging discomfort, not seeking truth. But the cost is structural: whatever you cast blame to is where you also cast power over your future outcomes, whether that's your parents, the economy, or politics. The antidote is reframing: interpreting hopelessness as 'I don't know enough yet' rather than 'my situation is impossible' converts despair into a knowledge deficit that is solvable by definition, and spelling out worst-case scenarios in concrete steps consistently reveals that feared outcomes are manageable rather than catastrophic.
