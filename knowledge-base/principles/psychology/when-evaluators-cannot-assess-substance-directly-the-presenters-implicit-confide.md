Under time pressure and information asymmetry, audiences default to using the presenter's apparent confidence as a proxy for quality. This works because confidence is cheap to fake but expensive to maintain under questioning, creating a rough credibility filter. However, there's a critical split: unsophisticated evaluators reward polish regardless of substance, while expert evaluators (VCs, experienced hiring managers) actively penalize polish that masks inexperience — they want to see raw talent, not corporate presentation skills. The optimal strategy flips depending on your audience: polish for laypeople, candor for experts.
