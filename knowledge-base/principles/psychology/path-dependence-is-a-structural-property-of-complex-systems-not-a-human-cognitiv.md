RuBisCo — an enzyme that confuses oxygen for CO2 half the time — has persisted for 450 million years because plants compensate with volume (40 billion tons) rather than evolving a replacement. This is not a bug in evolution; it's the fundamental arithmetic of lock-in. Replacement requires simultaneously maintaining the old system while building the new one, and the switching cost always exceeds the inefficiency cost at any single decision point. The same pattern drives human sunk-cost behavior, organizational inertia, and civilizational lock-in (agriculture, QWERTY, internal combustion). Recognizing path dependence as structural, not irrational, changes the intervention: don't fight the lock-in, design around it.
