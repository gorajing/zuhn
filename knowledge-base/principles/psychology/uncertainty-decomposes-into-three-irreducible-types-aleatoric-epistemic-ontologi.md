Not all uncertainty is the same. Aleatoric uncertainty is irreducible randomness inherent to the phenomenon (quantum events, genuine noise) — collecting more data doesn't help, you must propagate it through the decision. Epistemic uncertainty is a knowledge gap you can close with better evidence, training, or grounding. Ontological uncertainty arises from ambiguous or contested concepts — you must negotiate definitions before measurement is meaningful. Conflating these three guarantees wrong mitigation strategies and false calibration.
