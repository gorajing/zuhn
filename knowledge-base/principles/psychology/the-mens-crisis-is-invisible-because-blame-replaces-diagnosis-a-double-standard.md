Men account for 80% of US suicides and 70% of drug poisoning deaths. Since 2010, the rise in suicide has been almost entirely among young men under 30. Male psychology undergraduates dropped from 56% to 20%. A third of male K-12 teachers also coach, functioning as disguised mental health infrastructure. Yet the instinct when young men struggle is to blame them for laziness and video games — a response not applied to any other demographic group facing comparable challenges. Reactionary influencers exploit this gap by telling boys 'nobody wants you to watch my content,' and slamming the laptop shut proves their point. The pattern: when a demographic's struggles are met with blame rather than diagnosis, the crisis deepens while remaining invisible to those who could help.
