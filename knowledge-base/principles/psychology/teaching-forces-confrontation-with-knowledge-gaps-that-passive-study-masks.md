Explaining a concept to others — whether formally or through self-test — reveals precise boundaries of understanding that reading and listening conceal. The mechanism is that passive consumption creates an illusion of comprehension while teaching demands reconstruction from first principles. This applies from classroom teaching to Feynman-technique self-study to building working models that expose every gap.
