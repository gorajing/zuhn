The mind compulsively generates narrative explanations and confident frameworks as its default operating mode, producing false positives constantly. But this drive toward certainty consistently underperforms uncertainty tolerance: Tetlock's most confident experts predicted worse than chance, 'nature abhors a vacuum' persisted for a millennium on intuitive appeal alone, and consensus routinely defaults to the 'least bad' story even with known fatal flaws. The skill is not pattern-finding (the mind does that automatically) but pattern-skepticism.
