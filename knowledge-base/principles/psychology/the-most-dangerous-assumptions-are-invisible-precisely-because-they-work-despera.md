Planck's quantum and Bohr's angular momentum quantization were 'acts of desperation' with no physical justification — they worked spectacularly before anyone understood why. Mathematicians who publicly rejected the axiom of choice were found using it unknowingly in their own proofs. 'Nature abhors a vacuum' dominated for a thousand years because the framing was so intuitively compelling that contradictions were dismissed. The giant impact hypothesis became consensus not because it was proven, but because it was 'the least bad option.' Correct answers often arrive through desperation before understanding, and the assumptions most worth questioning are the ones so natural you don't notice them.
