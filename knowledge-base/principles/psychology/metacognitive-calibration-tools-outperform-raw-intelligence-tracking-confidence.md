The gap between good and bad decision-making is less about cognitive horsepower and more about metacognitive discipline. Recording confidence ratings at decision time and comparing to outcomes calibrates intuition over time. Actively trying to convince yourself a problem is not real surfaces evidence gaps that enthusiasm misses. Evaluating advice by watching whether you give logical reasons for disagreement or unconsciously munge it reveals your actual decision process. These are trainable skills, not personality traits.
