Destin knew the reversed-steering rule but couldn't ride the backwards bicycle for 8 months of daily practice — then one day it clicked as a discrete phase transition. His 6-year-old son learned the same skill in 2 weeks (16x faster), and even after mastering the new skill, a phone ringing could crash his brain back to the old algorithm. This proves that knowledge is stored declaratively while skills are stored procedurally, and the transition between stored programs is abrupt, fragile, and age-dependent.
