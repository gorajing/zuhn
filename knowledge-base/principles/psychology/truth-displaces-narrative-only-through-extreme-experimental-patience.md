Even when a new idea is correct, displacing an entrenched narrative requires decades of progressively tighter experimental designs that systematically eliminate every alternative explanation -- not a single decisive experiment, but an exhausting campaign of precision. The Aharonov-Bohm effect required 27 years and experiments escalating from iron whiskers to superconducting donuts before consensus shifted. Lunar isotope ratios matching Earth at parts-per-million precision invalidated the most elegant Moon formation model, showing that measurement precision can overturn theoretical beauty. Michelson and Morley built the most precise instrument of their era and still doubted their own results. Mathematical geometry must be separately validated against physical reality. The implication: if you have a correct but unpopular idea, your job is not to argue louder but to design experiments so airtight that every objection is pre-eliminated.
