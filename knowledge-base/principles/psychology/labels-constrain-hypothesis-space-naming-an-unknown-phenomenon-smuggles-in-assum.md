The words we attach to concepts silently restrict what we can think about them. Calling unknown gravitational effects 'dark matter' biases toward particle explanations; semantic drift causes people to talk past each other using identical vocabulary; and relabeling your own work ('sketch' vs 'draft') changes the internal standards you apply. Naming is not neutral description — it is the first and most invisible act of theory selection.
