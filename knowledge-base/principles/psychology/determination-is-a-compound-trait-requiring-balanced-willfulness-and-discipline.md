Sustained high performance requires a specific cocktail of traits that interact multiplicatively: energy, imagination, resilience, good judgment, and goal focus, where missing any one degrades the whole. Determination itself has two axes — willfulness (refusing to give up) and discipline (choosing the right thing to work on) — and excess of either without the other produces local maxima or paralysis. Slight overconfidence empirically outperforms perfect calibration, and specific knowledge is found by looking backward at what you have already done rather than forward at what you plan to learn.
