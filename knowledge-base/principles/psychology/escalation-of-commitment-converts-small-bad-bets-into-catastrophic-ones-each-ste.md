Once committed to a course of action, people rationalize continuation rather than reverse course, because admitting error is psychologically more costly than doubling down. Each incremental commitment raises the exit price further, creating an escalation trap. This pattern appears in Silk Road's moral decay, SafeMoon cult behavior, sunk-cost retail loyalty, and the when-then fallacy that locks people into circular inaction.
