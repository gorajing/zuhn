Confirmation bias in the justice system doesn't just affect one detective or one prosecutor — it compounds across multiple actors into a reliable wrongful conviction pipeline. The same pattern applies in organizations: each person's bias is small, but when a biased analyst feeds a biased manager who briefs a biased executive, the cumulative distortion is massive. Public outcome data enables bias audits that institutions cannot suppress. Schools optimize for intelligence because it's measurable, creating a lifelong illusion that intelligence is the destination — years of grade optimization create a subconscious pattern of seeking tricks over substance. Believing all options are equivalent is a cognitive shortcut that eliminates evaluation of dimensions the decision-maker cannot understand.
