Letting learners hit predictable mistakes — wrong references, negative overtime, cognitive dissonance with misconceptions — before introducing the fix produces stronger retention than delivering the right answer upfront. The mechanism is that errors create an emotional and cognitive gap that the subsequent correction fills more durably. This error-first pedagogy works across programming, science education, and conceptual learning.
