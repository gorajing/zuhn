Busywork that feels productive, browsing the internet at your desk, and the Dark Playground of leisure during work hours are all more destructive than obvious procrastination because they don't trigger the guilt signals that would prompt correction. Unlike watching TV on a couch, browsing at your desk looks and feels like work, which delays recognition of the problem. The most insidious variant is 'grab coffee' meetings that are nearly free for managers but devastatingly expensive for makers, with neither side recognizing the asymmetry.
