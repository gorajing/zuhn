Focus capacity resets each day and every input (email, notification, podcast, commitment) permanently occupies bandwidth until the next reset. The dominant strategy is therefore defensive: give the first slot to your most important work before competition exists, delay non-essential inputs as long as possible, and actively cut commitments rather than adding productivity systems. When focus is already depleted, reset it through subtraction — wall-staring or exhausting exercise that forces presence by emptying the mind, not by filling it with a new technique.
