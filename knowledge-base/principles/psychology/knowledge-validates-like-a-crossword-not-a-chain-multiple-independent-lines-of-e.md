Susan Haack's foundherentism shows that beliefs are justified by mutual support among independent evidence lines, not by any single chain of reasoning or coherent narrative. Like crossword answers that must fit intersecting words, claims gain credibility when they interlock with established knowledge from different angles. A single compelling argument is weaker than three mediocre arguments from independent sources.
