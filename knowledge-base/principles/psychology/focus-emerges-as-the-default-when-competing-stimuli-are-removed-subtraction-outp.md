Focus is not a muscle to strengthen but a natural state that appears when distractions are eliminated. Removing obligations frees more capacity than adding urgines. The Season of No — saying no to everything — creates an environment where intense work becomes the only option. Physical separation of work and internet computers forces conscious awareness of distraction that software blockers cannot replicate. Even scheduled interruptions (knowing you have a meeting in an hour) prevent deep work more than random ones, because anticipation consumes background bandwidth.
