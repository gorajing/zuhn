Expertise produces a recurring cycle of feeling competent then incompetent again, and this loop never ends. Most people quit during the incompetence phase, mistaking a normal oscillation for evidence of inability. The productive response is to tolerate being bad long enough to become good, focus on improvement rate rather than absolute position, and recognize that adults avoid new disciplines from fear that replaced teenage confidence, not from actual inability.
