Censorship does not eliminate hostile views; it pushes them into echo chambers where they intensify without challenge, accelerating group polarization. Free speech protections exist precisely because humans are self-deceiving creatures of limited knowledge who need open discourse to approach truth -- the same epistemological humility that drives scientific inquiry. Both liberal and conservative courts defend speech because each recognizes they may need its protection tomorrow. The marketplace of ideas must entertain all hypotheses, including hateful ones, because knowing who harbors hostility is more critical than comfort, and words powerful enough to harm are powerful enough to demand more engagement, not less.
