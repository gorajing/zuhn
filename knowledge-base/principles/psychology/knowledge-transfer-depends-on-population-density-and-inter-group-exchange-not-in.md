Cultural complexity is determined by population density and inter-group knowledge transfer, not by individual genius. Isolated groups reliably degenerate in capability (Easter Island). The Mongols became siege experts by absorbing techniques from conquered peoples. Watson and Crick synthesized others' work into a model nearly a century after Miescher discovered DNA. The implication is that learning infrastructure — networks, exposure, peer exchange — matters more than raw talent.
