Obscure writing about important ideas creates a closed loop: it attracts ambitious novices seeking status, repels critical thinkers who demand clarity, and has a built-in defense because you cannot easily prove text is meaningless. Idea restrictions propagate backward — forbidding conclusions in one domain suppresses exploration in every connected domain. The same pattern appears in historical information gatekeeping, where control over access to writing created persistent class structures. Clarity is not just a communication virtue — it is an epistemic necessity.
