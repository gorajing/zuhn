The feeling of moral certainty tricks the brain into treating outrage as evidence, bypassing genuine analysis. This manifests across politics, social media, and interpersonal judgment: people who feel strongly about an issue systematically overestimate how well they understand it. The antidote is to notice when emotional intensity is doing the work that evidence should do.
