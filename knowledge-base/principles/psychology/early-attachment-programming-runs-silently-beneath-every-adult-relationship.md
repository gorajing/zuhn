Caregivers let down roughly 50% of children, and those children absorb an invisible emotional grammar — patterns of trust, fear, and communication — that governs adult relationship choices without conscious awareness. People do not seek what makes them happy but what feels familiar, which is why children of dysfunction recreate dysfunction. Secure adult relationships can reprogram these defaults, but only after the unconscious pattern is surfaced.
