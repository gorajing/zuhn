The dominant failure pattern is cycling through excitement, difficulty, quitting, and restart on new projects, never staying long enough for compounding returns to kick in. Work begins once motivation stops -- the period after excitement fades is where most people quit and where champions pull ahead. The capacity to do the same unrewarding thing repeatedly is learnable and is the main separator between success and failure, because effort compounds non-linearly: quitting destroys disproportionately more future value than the past effort would suggest.
