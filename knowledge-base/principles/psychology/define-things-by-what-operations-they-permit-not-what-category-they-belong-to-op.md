Mathematicians define vectors by what you can do with them (add, scale) rather than what they are, yielding maximum generality. Asking 'what can I reach by combining these primitives' reveals true system power. Adding something that simplifies the problem and then redefining everything in terms of the addition dissolves difficulty. Linearity constraints eliminate most transformations, making the survivors tractable. The alien truth test filters for universals by stripping human-specific framing. Across all these cases, shifting from categorical identity ('what is it?') to operational capability ('what can I do with it?') consistently produces more powerful, more transferable, and more precise thinking.
