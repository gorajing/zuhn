Agriculture wasn't adopted in one decision but through millions of small, locally rational choices that collectively created an irreversible civilizational transformation. Hong Kong's autonomy eroded through individually reasonable integration steps. Revolutions result from accumulated small decisions, not dramatic moments. The pattern is universal: each step makes sense in isolation, but the cumulative trajectory is invisible until it's irreversible. This creates a systematic forecasting failure — we look for the dramatic trigger and miss the quiet accumulation. The intervention is to track trajectory, not just evaluate individual decisions.
