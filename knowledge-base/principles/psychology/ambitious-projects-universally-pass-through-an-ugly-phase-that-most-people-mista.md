Great work almost always looks lame at first, and the fear of that lameness is the primary killer of ambitious projects. The ugly duckling phase is not a risk factor but a universal structural feature of any project worth doing. Applying finished-work standards to early-stage projects is a category error. Framing ambitious work as 'just a sketch' or 'just an experiment' lowers the psychological barrier enough to start, and momentum converts it into real work. The right colleagues don't just encourage you — they help you see which ugly ducklings are actually swans.
