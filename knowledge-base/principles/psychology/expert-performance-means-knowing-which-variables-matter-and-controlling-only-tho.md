Byron Ferguson controls arrow spine precisely but rides the wobble — knowing which variables to control is the essence of expertise. The gap between good and great hackers is not intelligence but empathy for how users actually think. Knowing programming syntax creates a dangerous illusion of competence because the real skill is algorithmic thinking. Elite developer talent follows power-law distribution, not normal curves. Across wildly different fields, Graham's heroes shared exactly two traits: excessive care about their work and absolute refusal to pander.
