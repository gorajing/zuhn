  Teaching concepts at the moment a project demands them creates natural

Teaching concepts at the moment a project demands them creates natural motivation and deeper retention than front-loading theory. The optimal structure is a sequence of complete mini-projects, each introducing exactly one new concept while keeping everything else familiar. This prevents working memory overload while building cumulative competence. Interleaving projects throughout a course produces better retention than separating theory from practice. The ~2 hour marathon tutorial format exploits commitment psychology — sunk cost and flow state reduce abandonment. Real learning solidifies when learners build without a tutorial, forcing independent problem-solving.
