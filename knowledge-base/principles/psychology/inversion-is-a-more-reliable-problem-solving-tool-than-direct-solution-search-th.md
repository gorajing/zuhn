Defining failure modes first and then inverting each into a winning behavior consistently outperforms trying to generate solutions directly, because human threat-detection circuits are stronger than solution-finding circuits. This applies from Munger's 'invert, always invert' to debugging processes to safety planning. Making predictions before seeing results forces engagement with underlying models rather than just outcomes.
