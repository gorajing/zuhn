Across archaeology, physics, and craft traditions, physically recreating a process consistently uncovers knowledge that decades of theoretical analysis and artifact examination missed entirely. Roman concrete 'defects' turned out to be self-healing mechanisms, ancient beds encoded sophisticated pest-control chemistry, experimental archaeology ruled out ramp theories for pyramids, and practitioners like hairdressers have corrected academic experts. The body of knowledge embedded in practice is fundamentally inaccessible to pure observation — you must do the thing to know what the thing knows.
