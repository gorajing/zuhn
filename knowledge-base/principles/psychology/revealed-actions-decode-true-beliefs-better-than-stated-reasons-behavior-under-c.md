The most reliable signal of someone's actual position comes not from what they say but from structural behavioral evidence. If an opponent lobbies against your strategy while claiming it helps them, the lobbying reveals the strategy is working. Judging character by counterfactual behavior under reversed roles is more revealing than judging by actual behavior. People who repeatedly state intentions usually mean it -- treating explicit commitments as bluffs is a forecasting error. The pattern is that actions under cost or constraint reveal preferences that costless speech systematically obscures.
