Explicit claims of passion, quality, or certainty activate audience skepticism filters and destroy the credibility they attempt to establish. Genuine credibility emerges from concrete metrics, specific examples, acknowledged limitations, and the implicit confidence of someone who knows their material deeply enough to enumerate failure modes. The paradox is that restraint in claims signals deeper knowledge, while overclaiming signals the opposite.
