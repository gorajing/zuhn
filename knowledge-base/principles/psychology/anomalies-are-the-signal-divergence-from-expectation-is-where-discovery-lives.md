The most productive response to data that contradicts your model is excitement, not dismissal. SLAC researchers get most excited when simulated electron behavior diverges from experimental measurements, because the gap is where new physics lives. Dirac's 'saddest chapter in modern physics' -- the negative energy solutions everyone wanted to discard -- turned out to predict antimatter, confirmed within a year. Torricelli didn't just create a vacuum; he reframed the entire question by recognizing that atmospheric pressure, not nature's abhorrence of emptiness, explained all the phenomena. Edgerton's strobe technique achieves superior results by deliberately sacrificing temporal resolution for spatial resolution -- an anomalous design choice that outperforms the 'obvious' approach. A network poised on an edge of instability means one person's anomalous choice can tip an entire system. The principle: train yourself to feel curiosity rather than discomfort when reality deviates from prediction, because the deviation is the most information-rich signal available.
