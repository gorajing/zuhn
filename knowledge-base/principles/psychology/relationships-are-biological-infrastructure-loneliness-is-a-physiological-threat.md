The 85-year Harvard Study and converging research show that warm relationships are the single strongest predictor of health, happiness, and longevity. Good relationships regulate the body at a biological level, keeping cortisol and inflammation in check and slowing brain decline. Conversely, loneliness keeps the body in chronic fight-or-flight, and chronic relationship acrimony may be more harmful than being alone — making relationship quality a medical variable, not merely an emotional preference.
