Across Jung's synchronicity, dimensional geometry, and creative work, the same pattern emerges: problems that seem permanent or inescapable are often artifacts of operating within an insufficient dimensional framework. In high-dimensional geometry, congestion that seems like solid structure dissolves when you add dimensions. In creative work, there is no right answer — but the director's job is to make their choice become the right answer through total commitment. Our meaning-making machinery is so powerful it generates false positives constantly — we see patterns in noise because meaning-making is our default mode. The practical implication: when stuck, question whether the constraint is real or an artifact of the lens you're using to view it.
