Religious oaths were most taboo when the church held power, bodily words when Victorian propriety reigned, and identity slurs now that social equality is the dominant moral framework. English profanity largely descends from lower-class Saxon vocabulary stigmatized when Norman French speakers held social power. Taboo words become taboo through class-based avoidance — higher-status groups abandon terms associated with lower-status groups, creating a euphemism treadmill where today's polite term becomes tomorrow's slur. Harvard Business School's largest cohorts systematically catch the last wave because competition feels like validation rather than warning. Developer pecking orders reward complexity theater over actual delivery. The pattern: group status dynamics determine which signals are enforced, and the enforcement itself becomes the signal.
