Taleb's antifragility applied to psychology: organic systems (bodies, economies, psyches) communicate with their environment through stressors and require variability to remain healthy. The stress response is a magnificent multiphasic system designed for episodic activation, not chronic suppression. Foundation depletion (sleep, attention, trust) cascades nonlinearly because these substrates are interconnected homeostatic systems. Removing all volatility — through overprotection, emotional numbing, or chronic stress avoidance — atrophies the system's adaptive capacity. The two-thirds resilience finding (most people function normally after trauma) is evidence that the system is designed to handle variability; the problem is chronic suppression, not acute exposure.
