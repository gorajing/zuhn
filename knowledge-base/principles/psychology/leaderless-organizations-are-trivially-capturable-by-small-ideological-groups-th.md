Organizations without strong leaders default to consensus and cannot delay when accused of impropriety. When moral rules become elaborate enough, following them substitutes for actually being good, attracting people who weaponize compliance. Enforcers of orthodoxy compete with each other, creating a race to the bottom where borderline ideas get banned and opposition gets recast as the evil being hunted.
