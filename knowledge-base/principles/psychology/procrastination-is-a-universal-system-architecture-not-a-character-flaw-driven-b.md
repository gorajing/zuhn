Procrastination is not a failure of discipline unique to certain people. It is a universal cognitive architecture in which an Instant Gratification Monkey consistently overrides the Rational Decision-Maker, producing the Dark Playground where leisure brings no real enjoyment because it is unearned. The only reliable override is the Panic Monster, activated by imminent deadlines or public embarrassment. Since non-procrastinators do not actually exist — everyone has domains where the absence of deadlines lets the Monkey win — the distinction that matters is between contained deadline-based procrastination and the open-ended, deadline-free variety that silently consumes life goals.
