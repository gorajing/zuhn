fMRI studies show romantic love activates the VTA dopamine system next to circuits for thirst and hunger — it is a mating drive that evolved to focus energy on a single partner, not a feeling that can be reasoned away. Rejection activates the nucleus accumbens (cocaine/heroin addiction center) and physical pain regions, explaining why heartbreak can drive obsession and self-destruction. Bipedalism 4.4 million years ago created evolutionary pressure for pair-bonding, hardwiring this drive into human brain architecture.
