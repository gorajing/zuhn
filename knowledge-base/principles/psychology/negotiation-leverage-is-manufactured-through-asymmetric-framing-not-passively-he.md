Power in negotiations comes from controlling the frame, not from the objective position. SEIU weaponized ballot initiatives as destruction threats to extract concessions worth 16x their investment — creating a crisis first makes your offer to stop look like a concession, giving leverage you never earned. Calibrated irrationality (seeming rash enough to execute threats but patient enough to deal) is the optimal bilateral monopoly strategy. At the micro level, the party that introduces a formal model (quantitative terms) irreversibly shifts the conversation from narrative to numbers, and whoever controls the frame controls the outcome. Even VC deal dynamics follow this pattern: VCs resist founder control not from conviction but because conceding would signal weakness to their own partners.
