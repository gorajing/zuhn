Every generation discovers overconsumption is a problem, declares a movement, and then resumes consuming through the branded products of that movement. Anti-consumption trends are immediately co-opted by brands selling anti-consumption-branded products, decluttering creates psychological permission to buy replacements, and reusable tote bags and water bottles become status symbols rather than practical alternatives. The consumption engine metabolizes its own critics, making genuine cultural exit from consumerism structurally impossible through market mechanisms alone.
