Clinical psychedelic outcomes depend overwhelmingly on controllable protocol variables: adequate dose to achieve full dissolution rather than anxious partial states, prepared mindset and trust in guides, safe physical environment, and the patient's capacity to surrender rather than resist the experience. The molecule is necessary but not sufficient. Single sessions produce lasting change only when the protocol enables mystical-type experiences, which breaks the pharmacological assumption that psychiatric treatment requires continuous intervention. This makes psychedelic therapy a design problem, not a drug problem.
