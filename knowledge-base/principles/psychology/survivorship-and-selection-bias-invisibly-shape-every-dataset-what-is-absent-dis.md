The most dangerous bias in reasoning is not misinterpreting evidence but never seeing the missing evidence. Survivorship bias makes us study successes and ignore the vastly larger pool of failures with identical starting conditions. Selection effects make post-filtered data look like talent rather than luck, historical records narrate only the victors' perspective, and base-rate neglect makes rare events look meaningful. The antidote is always asking 'what would the data look like if the opposite were true?'
