RuBisCo was perfectly adapted to a low-oxygen atmosphere, but plants' own photosynthetic success raised oxygen levels until RuBisCo became self-sabotaging. This reflexive environment-shifting pattern recurs across scales: successful startups change their market dynamics, successful social movements change the cultural landscape they were adapted to, and successful cognitive heuristics change the information environment. The key insight is that the threat isn't external disruption — it's the strategy undermining its own operating conditions. The corrective is building environmental monitoring into any strategy, not just execution metrics.
