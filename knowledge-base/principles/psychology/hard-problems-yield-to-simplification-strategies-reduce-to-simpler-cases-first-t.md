Across mathematics, physics, and engineering, the most productive problem-solving strategy is to knock hard problems down to simpler cases, find structure there, and generalize back up. Schwarzschild solved Einstein's equations by imagining the simplest possible universe. Fourier decomposition reframes complex functions into sums of simple waves. The meta-skill is treating approximation-then-refinement as a deliberate strategy rather than a compromise.
