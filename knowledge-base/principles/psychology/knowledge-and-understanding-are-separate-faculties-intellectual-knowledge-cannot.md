Knowing the rules of a system intellectually does not confer the ability to perform within it. The backwards bicycle experiments prove that declarative knowledge ('turn left to go right') provides zero transfer to procedural skill, and that deeply trained motor/cognitive patterns resist override even when you understand them perfectly. True understanding requires embodied practice that rewires neural pathways, not just information acquisition.
