Across quantum mechanics, contemplative traditions, neuroscience, and philosophy of mind, a convergent signal keeps appearing: consciousness may not be produced by brains but may be the substrate in which brains -- and all matter -- arise. Multiple quantum consciousness frameworks consistently model the brain as a transceiver or interface rather than a generator of awareness. In deep sleep, experience ceases entirely yet something registers that nothing happened, suggesting awareness persists beneath all states. In transcendental consciousness, thought dissolves but 'I am' remains, indicating awareness is the field in which thoughts appear rather than their product. Jung's unus mundus posits psyche and matter as expressions of a single unified order. Quantum measurement creates properties rather than revealing pre-existing ones, and the vacuum -- pure emptiness -- is the universe's default state, with matter being what needs explaining. This principle doesn't prove idealism, but the convergence across independent domains is too strong to dismiss.
