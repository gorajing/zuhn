Reading great books repeatedly until you can teach them produces compounding returns that consuming many books superficially cannot match. A single deep book digested over years upgrades your entire evaluative framework for all future information. The mechanism is that rereading reveals layers invisible on first pass because the reader has changed between readings — the book you read at 25 is literally not the same book at 45.
