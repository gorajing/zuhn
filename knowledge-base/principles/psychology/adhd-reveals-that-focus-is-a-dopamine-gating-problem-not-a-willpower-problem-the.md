ADHD hyper-focus on enjoyable tasks proves the focus circuitry is intact -- the deficit is specifically in the dopamine system that should enable focus on non-preferred tasks. Dopamine controls blink rate, which resets time perception, explaining why low dopamine causes both poor time management and poor focus simultaneously. The default mode network and task-directed networks fire together instead of alternating in ADHD, and dopamine acts as the conductor that should keep them anti-correlated. Caffeine uniquely upregulates dopamine receptors rather than depleting stores, making it the only common stimulant that enhances focus without crash dynamics.
