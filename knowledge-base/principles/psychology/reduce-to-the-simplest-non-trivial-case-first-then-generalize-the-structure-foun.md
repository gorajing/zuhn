Schwarzschild solved Einstein's equations by imagining the simplest possible universe (one point mass, no rotation) and still found black holes and wormholes. Knock hard problems down to simpler cases and the structure generalizes back up. Treating approximation-then-refinement as a deliberate skill rather than a compromise unlocks problem-solving across domains — from Fourier decomposition to startup strategy. The simplest case reveals the skeleton that complexity drapes over.
