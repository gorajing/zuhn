Leslie Lamport's dictum: if you're thinking without writing, you only think you're thinking. Writing generates ideas rather than merely recording them — 90% of essay content only emerges during the writing process. Good-sounding writing and correct thinking are structurally coupled through rhythm matching idea shape and revision forcing truth-convergence. Sleeping on a stuck problem reliably produces solutions that extended conscious grinding cannot — the unconscious continues processing. Even after learning a new skill, any small distraction can crash you back to the old algorithm (Destin's backwards bicycle). Living in multiple countries trains you to question assumptions because your existing mental models repeatedly fail. The common thread: cognition is not a passive state — it requires active externalization (writing), environmental challenge (travel), and oscillation between focused effort and deliberate rest.
