Humans reason about assumption chains additively, so a conclusion resting on five 80%-confident assumptions feels like an 80% conclusion. The actual math is 0.8^5 ≈ 33%. This arithmetic error is the hidden mechanism behind overconfident forecasts, policy predictions, and business strategies that quietly depend on long chains of contingent assumptions. Trend extrapolation that is 86% accurate on individual predictions (Kurzweil) can still miss every major discontinuity because discontinuities break multiple assumptions at once. The rule: enumerate your assumption chain and multiply.
