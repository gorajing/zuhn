Across programming, creative fields, and knowledge work, elite talent is qualitatively different from average, following power-law rather than normal distributions. A great programmer does not do the same work faster — they invent solutions an ordinary programmer would never conceive. As skills become mass-teachable they commoditize, but the highest performers escape this because their advantage lies in pattern recognition and creative leaps that resist standardization. Increasing specialization widens this gap further over time.
