Neither a single resilience strategy nor a fixed philosophical framework reliably handles the variety of challenges people face. The most effective approach is an iterative cognitive loop: narrow the problem to what is bothering you right now, choose one strategy from your available toolbox, monitor whether it is working, and switch strategies if it is not. Shifting self-talk from declarative statements to diagnostic questions activates problem-solving circuitry rather than emotional comfort, and this flexibility is what separates resilient outcomes from rigid ones.
