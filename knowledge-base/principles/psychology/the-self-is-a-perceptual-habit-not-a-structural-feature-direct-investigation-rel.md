The feeling of being a thinker behind your thoughts, located behind your eyes, authoring your decisions is not a discovery about reality but an unexamined cognitive habit. Multiple contemplative and neuroscientific lines converge: thoughts arise unbidden, the sense of authorship is the absence of noticing spontaneous arising, and self-knowledge has a built-in uncertainty principle because examining the self changes the self. Meditation works by subtracting these illusions rather than adding capabilities, and the dissolution is not a loss but a correction.
