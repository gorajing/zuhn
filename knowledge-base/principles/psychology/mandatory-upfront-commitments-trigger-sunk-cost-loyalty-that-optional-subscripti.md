Once customers pay a membership fee, sunk-cost psychology makes them default to that retailer without comparing prices, converting a one-time payment into persistent behavioral change. Mandatory fees trigger universal loyalty across all customers, while optional subscriptions only lock in the subset who already calculated it worthwhile. Optional subscriptions self-select for rational evaluators who keep comparing prices; mandatory ones commit everyone equally. The mechanism is that an upfront payment shifts the internal question from 'is this the best option?' to 'how do I justify what I already spent?' — a fundamentally different cognitive frame that suppresses comparison shopping. This is why gym memberships, Costco fees, and Amazon Prime all use the same structure.
