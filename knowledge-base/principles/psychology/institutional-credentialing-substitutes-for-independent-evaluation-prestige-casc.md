When evaluating claims, investments, or people, decision-makers routinely substitute institutional credentials (Ivy League, Goldman Sachs, celebrity endorsements) for independent investigation. This creates cascading failures: each endorser assumes the previous one did the diligence, producing a chain of unchecked assumptions. The pattern repeats from Madoff to 1MDB to college admissions to art valuation.
