Happiness is a fragile state requiring things to go well; joy is existentially gritty and can coexist with the deepest suffering. Joy requires emotional openness, surprise, and connection — precisely the conditions that productivity optimization and screen dependency destroy. The day-to-day quiet enjoyment of ordinary life contributes more to annual well-being than highlight-reel experiences, and meaning is found when you reach a motivation so fundamental that asking 'but why?' feels like a category error.
