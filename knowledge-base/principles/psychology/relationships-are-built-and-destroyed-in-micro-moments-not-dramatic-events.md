The fate of any relationship is determined by the accumulated weight of small, daily interactions -- bids for connection answered or ignored, covert resentments silently compounding, the ratio of positive to negative exchanges. Marriages fail like bankruptcy: slowly then all at once. The 5:1 positivity ratio, unconditional advocacy, and vulnerability in routine moments matter infinitely more than grand gestures. Covert contracts (secret resentment taken as indirect sabotage) and Gottman's four horsemen (criticism, defensiveness, stonewalling, contempt) are the micro-erosion mechanisms, while choosing love as a renewable daily act and going all-in despite guaranteed eventual loss are the micro-building mechanisms.
