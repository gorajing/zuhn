Flooding the zone with contradictory claims makes people give up on knowing truth, which is more useful to authoritarians than convincing them of a specific lie. Novel contrarian revisionism gets algorithmic boost while settled truth does not. Each moral compromise raises the psychological cost of reversing course, creating escalation traps. Bad decisions trigger rationalization rather than course-correction. The structural asymmetry means truth requires active, expensive maintenance while fiction propagates cheaply — and this applies equally to personal beliefs, organizational narratives, and information ecosystems.
