Abstract knowledge that life is finite rarely changes behavior, but concrete framings do. Counting the finite instances of recurring experiences makes time scarcity actionable. Cultivating aggressive impatience about doing things that matter reframes mortality as urgency rather than dread. The gain of today becomes the expectation of tomorrow through hedonic adaptation, so present experiences are worth more than they feel. Irreversible geological decay means some experiences are genuinely non-renewable. The pattern is that decision quality improves dramatically when finite time is made vivid rather than abstract.
