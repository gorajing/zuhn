When projects are framed as experiments, sketches, or hacks, failure becomes impossible because even negative results yield knowledge. This mental reframe — calling work 'just a sketch' or treating it as a learning exercise — disables premature quality judgment long enough for momentum to build. The biggest killer of potentially successful projects is never starting them, and the primary mechanism is excessive quality standards applied too early.
