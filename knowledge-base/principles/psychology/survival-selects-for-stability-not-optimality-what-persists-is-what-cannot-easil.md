Across physics, evolution, game theory, and strategy, the same selection principle operates: what endures is not what is best but what is stable enough to resist perturbation. In the primordial void, unstable configurations fall apart and vanish while stable ones endure long enough to combine into increasingly complex structures. What looks like intentional strategy in evolution and business is actually 'simple chemistry that happens to work well and propagates.' The Mashco Piro's default violence toward outsiders is a rational multigenerational strategy born from centuries of hostile contact -- not optimal in isolation, but stable across generations. Pre-committing to principled behavior yields better outcomes than optimizing each decision, because commitment is a stability strategy that removes the attack surface of per-decision weakness. Even in physics, only near-classical paths survive because the crazy alternatives destructively interfere and cancel. The practical rule: when designing systems, careers, or habits, optimize for what survives disruption, not for theoretical peak performance.
