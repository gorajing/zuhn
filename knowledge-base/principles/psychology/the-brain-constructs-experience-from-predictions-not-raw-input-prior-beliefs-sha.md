The brain does not passively receive sensory data; it actively constructs meaning through predictive models. The same nerve signals produce pleasure or irritation depending on context; religious crowds seeing the same solar phenomenon interpret it through their priors; and the High Place Phenomenon shows the brain inventing intentions to explain mismatched survival signals. Neuroscience's predictive coding framework reveals this is not a bug but the core operating principle shared by both biological and artificial intelligence.
