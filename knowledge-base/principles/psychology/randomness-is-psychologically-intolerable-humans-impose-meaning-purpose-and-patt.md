The human mind compulsively generates explanations for random events, sees purpose in accidents, and detects patterns in noise. This is not a bug but a calibrated survival feature: false positives (seeing a tiger in the bushes) are cheaper than false negatives (missing an actual tiger). The result is that true randomness looks 'designed' to us, vague predictions seem prophetic, and products must introduce artificial structure to satisfy our pattern-seeking minds.
