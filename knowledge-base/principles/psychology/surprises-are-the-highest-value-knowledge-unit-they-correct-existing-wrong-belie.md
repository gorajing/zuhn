Surprises are not just new information but corrections to existing wrong beliefs, making them disproportionately valuable for learning. Not knowing what you don't know is the most expensive form of ignorance because it compounds through repeated mistakes. The expanding circle metaphor — as knowledge grows, its boundary of contact with the unknown grows even faster — means learning systematically increases your awareness of ignorance. Productive learning therefore maximizes surprise-seeking rather than confirmation-gathering.
