Research consistently shows approximately two-thirds of people exposed to potentially traumatic events continue functioning normally. Events are not traumatic — they are potentially traumatic — because the same event produces resilience in most people and lasting harm in a minority. During mass events like 9/11 or COVID, social contagion and affective forecasting bias make people unable to imagine their distress is temporary. Resilience is not a fixed character trait but an emergent outcome of many interacting variables, which is why lists of '3 traits of highly resilient people' are myths.
