The skills that produce good speakers and the skills that produce good ideas are not just different — they're often opposed. Speaking rewards fluency, charisma, and real-time social calibration. Writing rewards precision, logical completeness, and sustained reasoning. Because writing removes the audience's real-time presence, less can go unsaid, forcing the writer to confront gaps in their thinking that conversation would skip over. This makes writing the superior medium for idea generation and complex reasoning, while speaking remains superior for motivation, emotional impact, and rapid social coordination.
