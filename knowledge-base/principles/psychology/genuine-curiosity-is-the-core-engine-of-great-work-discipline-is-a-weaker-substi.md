Across fields, sustained excellence emerges from obsessive, disinterested curiosity pursued for its own sake rather than from forced discipline or external reward. Deep interest makes hard work feel like natural pull, surfaces authentically novel ideas that audience-optimized strategies miss, and produces better creative output because boredom in the maker shows up in the result. Discipline can sustain effort temporarily, but curiosity sustains it indefinitely.
