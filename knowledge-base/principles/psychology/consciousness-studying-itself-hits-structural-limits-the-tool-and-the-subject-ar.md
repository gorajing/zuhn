Consciousness investigating consciousness encounters paradoxes that are not failures of method but inherent structural features. You cannot see your own eyes directly; subjective experience transcends complete physical description (the knowledge argument); language is structurally incapable of encoding peak conscious states; and some mysteries are sources of wonder that analysis would destroy rather than illuminate. These limits are not temporary gaps awaiting better instruments but permanent boundaries created by the self-referential nature of awareness examining itself.
