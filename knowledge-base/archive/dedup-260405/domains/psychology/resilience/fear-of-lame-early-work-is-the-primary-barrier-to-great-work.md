Paul Graham identifies that great projects universally pass through an ugly duckling stage where they look unimpressive even to their creators. The critical insight is that most people never reach this stage — they're too frightened to start at all, let alone push through. This means the primary bottleneck to great work is emotional, not intellectual or resource-based.

The practical implication is that building tolerance for producing bad early work is more valuable than improving raw skill. Anyone attempting ambitious projects should expect and plan for a phase where results look embarrassingly poor, and develop specific strategies to persist through it rather than treating embarrassment as a signal to quit.
