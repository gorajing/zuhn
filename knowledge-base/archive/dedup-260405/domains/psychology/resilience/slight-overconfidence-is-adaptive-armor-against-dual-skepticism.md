Most people assume perfect calibration of one's abilities is optimal. Graham argues this ignores that ambitious creators face skepticism from two directions simultaneously: other people's dismissal and their own internal critic. Slight overconfidence acts as armor against both, keeping you in the game long enough for the work to mature past its ugly-duckling phase.

This reframes a cognitive bias as functionally adaptive in creative contexts. The key word is 'slightly' — gross overconfidence leads to Theranos-style delusion. But a modest bias toward believing in your work compensates for the systematic undervaluation that early-stage ambitious projects receive from everyone, including their creators.
