Rational calibration seems optimal in theory, but in practice creators face skepticism from two directions simultaneously: external dismissal and internal self-doubt. These compound to create an asymmetric drag that pure accuracy cannot overcome. Being slightly overconfident acts as a counterweight—not delusional, but enough to keep you moving through the inevitable ugly duckling phase of ambitious work. This is why the most successful people across many fields tend to be mildly overconfident rather than perfectly calibrated. The error is functional, not irrational.
