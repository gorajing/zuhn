Great projects universally pass through an early stage where they look unimpressive even to their creators. The critical differentiator between people who do great work and those who don't is often not ability but willingness to push through this embarrassing phase. Most people never even reach the point of making something they're embarrassed by — they're too frightened to start at all.

This suggests that interventions targeting creative fear (reframing failure, lowering initial stakes, surrounding yourself with people who can distinguish ugly ducklings from baby swans) may have outsized returns compared to interventions targeting skill or resources. The fear is not deeply rooted — it's a relatively recent cultural artifact that can be unlearned, as Silicon Valley's evolution demonstrates.
