Graham observes that across many fields, the most successful people are slightly overconfident — not delusionally so, but enough to counterbalance two forces pulling in the opposite direction: external dismissiveness and internal harsh judgment of early work. The error in self-assessment is not a bug but a feature, because it compensates for other systematic errors (people undervaluing incomplete work, creators judging drafts against finished-product standards). The implication is that perfect calibration is suboptimal in creative domains — you need a bias toward action to survive the inevitable ugly-duckling phase of ambitious projects.
