Most people apply the same quality bar to a project's first prototype as they would to a polished final product. This is a category error — early work and finished work are structurally different things. A 20% complete version of a 100x-impact project looks worse than a finished version of a trivial project, yet has far higher expected value.

The practical fix is to switch polarity when evaluating early work: instead of listing reasons it won't work, force yourself to imagine ways it could work. This isn't naive optimism — it's a calibration correction. Silicon Valley's outsized returns come partly from institutionalizing this mental switch, treating rough ideas as challenges to imagination rather than targets for critique.
