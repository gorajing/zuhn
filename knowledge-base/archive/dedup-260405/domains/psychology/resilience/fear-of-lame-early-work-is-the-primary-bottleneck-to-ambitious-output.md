Most people never even reach the stage of producing embarrassing early work, let alone push past it. This fear is disproportionate to the actual risk because early versions of ambitious projects are genuinely a special case—they look worse than they are relative to their eventual trajectory. The key realization is that this judgmental reflex is culturally learned (humans had no customs for evaluating novel work until recently), which means it can be unlearned. Recognizing that harsh early-stage judgment is a bug in our evaluation firmware, not a feature, is the first step to overriding it.
