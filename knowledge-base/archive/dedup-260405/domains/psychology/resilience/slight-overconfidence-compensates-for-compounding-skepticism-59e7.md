When pursuing ambitious work, you face skepticism from two directions simultaneously: other people dismissing your early efforts, and your own internal critic judging rough drafts against finished-work standards. Slight overconfidence doesn't make you delusional—it corrects for these compounding negative biases that would otherwise stop you before the work matures.

This explains why the most successful people across many fields tend to be slightly overconfident rather than perfectly calibrated. Perfect calibration sounds optimal in theory, but in practice it leaves you defenseless against the asymmetric costs of abandoning promising work too early versus persisting slightly too long.
