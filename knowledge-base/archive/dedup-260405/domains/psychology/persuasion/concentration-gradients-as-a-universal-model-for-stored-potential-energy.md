The light-dependent reactions work by pumping protons into the thylakoid space, creating a concentration gradient. This stored potential energy is then harvested as protons flow back through ATP synthase, converting ADP to ATP. The thylakoid membrane's phospholipid bilayer is critical — it maintains the gradient by preventing leakage.

This gradient-and-gate pattern is a powerful mental model beyond biology. Pricing arbitrage works because price gradients exist across markets with barriers. Information asymmetry stores persuasive potential that's released through strategic disclosure. Sales funnels concentrate attention on one side of a conversion boundary. In each case, the value comes not from the gradient alone but from controlling the gate through which flow occurs. The membrane matters as much as the concentration difference.
