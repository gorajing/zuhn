When founders say 'we're passionate' or 'our product is great,' audiences either ignore it or actively write them off as bullshitters. These explicit claims have been so overused they've become negative signals. The same message delivered implicitly—through visible excitement about the problem, deep knowledge of users, and calm certainty in the demo—bypasses skepticism entirely.

This applies well beyond pitching. In any high-stakes persuasion context, the audience's BS detector is calibrated to explicit self-promotion. The paradox is that the more directly you assert a quality, the less credible it becomes. The most convincing founders act as though they're doing investors a favor by letting them invest—and genuinely believe it.
