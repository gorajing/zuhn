It seems paradoxical that having a miscalibrated self-assessment could be advantageous—surely accuracy would be optimal. But this ignores the asymmetry of the environment. Creators face skepticism from two directions simultaneously: external doubt from others, and internal doubt from their own quality standards. These are systematic biases toward pessimism about early work.

Slight overconfidence corrects for these biases. If you're 20% through a project worth 100 but believe you're 10% through a project worth 200, your estimate of expected value is actually correct despite both components being wrong. The same compensatory math applies to self-belief: being slightly overconfident doesn't make you delusional, it brings your effective confidence to the level that accurately reflects the project's true expected value after discounting for environmental pessimism.
