Graham observes that the most successful people across many fields are slightly overconfident, which seems paradoxical since perfect calibration should be optimal. The resolution is that creators face systematic negative bias from two directions: other people's skepticism and their own harsh self-judgment of early work.

Slightly overestimating your abilities acts as armor against these compounding headwinds. The math works out: if you're 20% too generous in evaluating your work but facing 30% too-harsh external judgment, your net assessment is actually closer to truth than perfect self-calibration would be. This suggests the 'right' amount of confidence is context-dependent — in fields with high ambient skepticism, more self-belief is rational.
