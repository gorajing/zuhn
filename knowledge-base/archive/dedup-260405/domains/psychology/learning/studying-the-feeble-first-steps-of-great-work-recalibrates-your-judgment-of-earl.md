Graham suggests that the ultimate solution to harsh self-judgment of early work is learning to see it accurately — recognizing that a lame-looking early version of an ambitious project truly is more valuable than it seems. The practical method is studying the histories of people who've done great work, focusing specifically on their very first steps. These beginnings are almost always feeble, though often hard to find because creators are embarrassed by them and don't publish early versions. The internet has helped by making first commits, first videos, and other early artifacts visible. Graham frames this meta-insight beautifully: our attitudes toward early work are themselves early work — a crude version 1 that we're still evolving better customs around.
