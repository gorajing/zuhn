Graham identifies a concrete technique: label early work as a lesser category (sketch, hack, experiment) and choose media that support this framing. Drawing in a notebook feels lower-stakes than carving stone; writing a quick script feels safer than architecting a system. This isn't self-deception — it's a deliberate reduction of psychological activation energy.

John Carmack's observation about game mods reinforces this: when the medium is crude enough (Quake mods, Minecraft), the gap between early work and finished work shrinks, and innovation flourishes. The shift from physical to digital media amplified this effect by making it trivially cheap to start something new. The practical takeaway is to bias toward tools and formats where version 0.1 doesn't feel like a permanent commitment — then sneakily upgrade the ambition once momentum builds.
