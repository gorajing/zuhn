In photosynthesis, the electron transport chain pumps protons into the thylakoid interior, creating a concentration gradient. These protons 'want' to equalize, and the only exit is through ATP synthase — an enzyme that harnesses their flow to produce ATP. The thylakoid functions as a biological battery: charge it by creating asymmetry, discharge it through a constrained channel that captures useful work.

This gradient model appears everywhere. Information asymmetry in markets creates arbitrage opportunities (the 'flow' of capital toward equalization). Skill gaps between teams create consulting value. Pressure differentials in attention (viral content vs. low-awareness niches) drive audience flow. The model's power is in its two requirements: you need both the asymmetry AND the constrained channel. Without the constraint (ATP synthase, a marketplace, a funnel), the gradient dissipates without producing useful work.
