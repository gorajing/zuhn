Montaigne's key discovery was that expressing ideas helps form them — but 'helps' drastically understates it. Most of what ends up in essays only gets thought of during the writing process itself. This inverts the common model where you first think, then write to communicate. Instead, writing is the thinking tool.

This has practical implications: if you want to figure something out, write about it. Don't wait until you have clarity to start writing. The writing itself is what produces clarity. The audience component matters too — writing just for yourself tends to peter out, but writing for others forces you to think well, much like inviting guests forces you to clean your apartment.
