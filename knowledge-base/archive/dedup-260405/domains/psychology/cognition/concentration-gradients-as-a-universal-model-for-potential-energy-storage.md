The light-dependent reactions don't directly convert photon energy into ATP. Instead, they use photon energy to pump protons across the thylakoid membrane, building a concentration gradient. ATP synthase then harvests this gradient as protons flow back through it, like water through a turbine. The energy storage is in the asymmetry itself.

This gradient model appears everywhere: venture capital accumulates in Silicon Valley while opportunity gradients exist elsewhere, creating arbitrage. Information asymmetry between experts and novices creates consulting markets. Skill gaps between industries create cross-pollination opportunities. Recognizing where gradients exist — where something is concentrated on one side of a barrier — reveals where potential energy is stored and where value can be captured by building the equivalent of ATP synthase: a channel that lets the gradient equalize while extracting useful work.
