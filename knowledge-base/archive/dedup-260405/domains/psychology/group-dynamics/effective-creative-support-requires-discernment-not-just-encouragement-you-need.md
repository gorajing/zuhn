Graham makes a crucial distinction between encouragement and discernment. Surrounding yourself with people who are always positive is counterproductive because you learn to discount their feedback, making it worthless as a signal. What you actually need are peers who can evaluate early-stage work with genuine judgment — recognizing real promise in rough form.

The best source of such peers is people working on similar ambitious projects of their own, which explains why university departments and research labs are so effective. These people have calibrated intuitions about what 'promising but ugly' looks like in your domain. This insight reframes networking: the goal isn't finding cheerleaders but finding people whose judgment you can trust precisely because they're capable of honest negative assessment.
