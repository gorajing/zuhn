Graham warns that simply collecting encouraging people around you doesn't work — you'll learn to discount unconditional praise. What you actually need are colleagues who can distinguish between early work that's ugly-but-promising and work that's genuinely mediocre. The people best equipped for this are those working on similar ambitious projects of their own, which explains why university departments and research labs are so effective at incubating great work. These colleagues naturally coalesce given opportunity, but actively seeking them out accelerates the process dramatically. Teachers are a special case — their job is specifically to see promise in early work and encourage continuation — but good ones are rare, so finding one is high-leverage.
