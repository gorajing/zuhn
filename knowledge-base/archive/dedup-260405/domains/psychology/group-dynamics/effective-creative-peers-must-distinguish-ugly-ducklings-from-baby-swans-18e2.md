Generic encouragement is useless because you learn to discount it. What you need is informed optimism: people who understand the domain well enough to distinguish a genuinely promising but ugly prototype from a dead end. The best source of such judgment is people working on similarly ambitious projects of their own, because they've calibrated their intuition through firsthand experience with the gap between early and finished work.

This is why university departments, research labs, and startup hubs like YC work: they naturally concentrate people with both the domain expertise to evaluate early work and the personal incentive to be constructively optimistic. You don't need formal institutions for this—such groups coalesce naturally—but actively seeking them out dramatically accelerates creative progress by creating a local eddy against the social headwind of dismissal.
