Creating a supportive environment for ambitious work is not about collecting people who are always encouraging—you'd quickly learn to discount unconditional praise. The real value comes from colleagues who can actually evaluate early-stage work and distinguish genuine potential from dead ends. The people best equipped to make this judgment are those working on similar ambitious projects of their own, because they have calibrated intuitions about what early progress looks like in that domain. This is why university departments and research labs are so effective: they naturally concentrate people with the right discriminative ability. The practical takeaway is to actively seek out others attempting similarly ambitious work, not just supportive friends.
