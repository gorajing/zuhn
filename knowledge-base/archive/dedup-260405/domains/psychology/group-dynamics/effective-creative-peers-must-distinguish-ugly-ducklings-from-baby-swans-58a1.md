Graham makes a crucial distinction between two types of supportive environments. Generic encouragement gets discounted quickly and provides no signal. What creators actually need are colleagues who can distinguish between work that looks bad because it's early and work that looks bad because it's bad. This evaluative skill is rare and most commonly found among people working on similar ambitious projects themselves.

This is why university departments, research labs, and startup hubs like YC function as creative accelerators—not because they provide resources or encouragement per se, but because they concentrate people who have developed the specific skill of reading early-stage potential. The implication for founders and creators is that peer selection should optimize for judgment quality, not emotional warmth. Seek out people attempting similarly ambitious things in adjacent domains.
