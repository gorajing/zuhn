Creating an 'eddy in the social headwind' against new ideas requires more than collecting cheerleaders. You'd quickly learn to discount unconditional encouragement, making it useless. What you actually need are colleagues who can tell the difference between early work that's ugly because it's unfinished and early work that's ugly because it's bad. The people best equipped for this judgment are those working on similarly ambitious projects of their own.

This is why university departments and research labs work so well, and why Silicon Valley's density of ambitious founders creates compounding advantages. The mechanism isn't just emotional support—it's accurate evaluation at a stage when most people can't tell promise from failure. This has direct implications for how founders should choose their social environment: optimize for judgment quality, not encouragement quantity.
