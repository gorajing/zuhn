The World Economic Forum's report on governance in the age of generative AI highlights that traditional regulatory approaches — built for deterministic software with predictable outputs — break down when applied to foundation models that generate novel, unpredictable content. Key challenges include attribution of responsibility when AI outputs cause harm, the global nature of model deployment versus jurisdiction-bound regulation, and the speed of capability advancement outpacing legislative cycles. The report advocates for multi-stakeholder governance frameworks where industry self-regulation, government oversight, technical standards bodies, and civil society input operate in parallel rather than sequentially. This mirrors how internet governance evolved but must move faster given AI's pace of advancement and broader societal impact.
