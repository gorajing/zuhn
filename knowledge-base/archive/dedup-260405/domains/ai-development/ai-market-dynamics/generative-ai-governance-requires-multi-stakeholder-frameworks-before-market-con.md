The World Economic Forum's exploration of generative AI governance highlights a critical timing dynamic: the window for effective governance is during the technology's rapid expansion phase, not after market consolidation. Once a few dominant players emerge with massive revenue bases, they gain disproportionate influence over regulatory processes. History from social media and search shows that post-consolidation regulation becomes primarily defensive (protecting incumbents) rather than protective (safeguarding public interest). The current moment, where many AI companies are competing and business models are still forming, represents the optimal intervention point for establishing safety standards, data rights frameworks, and accountability mechanisms that can shape the industry's trajectory.
