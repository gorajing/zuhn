The World Economic Forum's examination of generative AI governance highlights that traditional regulatory approaches designed for specific software products or platforms are inadequate for foundation models that can be applied across virtually any domain. The challenges are unique: rapid capability advancement outpaces regulatory cycles, dual-use potential means the same model can serve beneficial and harmful purposes, and the global nature of AI development creates jurisdictional gaps. Effective governance will likely require hybrid frameworks combining industry self-regulation, government oversight, and international coordination — a multi-stakeholder approach that moves beyond the binary of regulation vs. innovation. For builders and investors, this means governance capability is becoming a competitive moat rather than merely a compliance cost.
