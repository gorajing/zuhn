The World Economic Forum's exploration of generative AI governance highlights a fundamental mismatch: existing technology regulation assumes systems produce predictable outputs from given inputs. Generative AI violates this assumption — the same prompt can produce different outputs, outputs can be novel and creative, and the boundary between tool-generated and human-generated content blurs.

This means governance frameworks need to shift from regulating outputs (which are unpredictable) to regulating processes, access controls, and accountability structures. The challenge is creating governance that enables innovation while preventing harm, without the false precision of trying to predict or constrain specific AI outputs. Early governance decisions will likely create path dependencies that shape the AI industry for decades.
