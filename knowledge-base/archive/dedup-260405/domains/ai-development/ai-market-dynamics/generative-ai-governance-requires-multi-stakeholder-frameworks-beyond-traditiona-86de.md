The World Economic Forum's analysis highlights that generative AI introduces governance challenges fundamentally different from previous technology waves. Unlike social media or cloud computing, gen AI's ability to generate novel content, code, and reasoning creates risks that span intellectual property, misinformation, labor displacement, and safety — simultaneously across every industry.

This means governance must be multi-stakeholder by design: governments set guardrails, companies implement responsible development practices, and civil society provides accountability. Single-actor regulation (purely government or purely self-regulation) has proven insufficient. For investors and builders, this signals that companies building with governance-first approaches will have durable competitive advantages as regulation inevitably catches up to capability.
