Generative AI differs from previous technology waves in that the development stack spans multiple jurisdictions, involves both open-source and proprietary models, and affects virtually every industry simultaneously. No single government can regulate what they cannot fully observe or control, and no single company can self-regulate when competitive dynamics incentivize capability races.

The World Economic Forum's governance framework recognizes this by advocating for coordination between governments, industry, civil society, and technical communities. The practical challenge is that governance frameworks tend to lag capability development by years, creating a window where norms are set by whoever moves fastest rather than by deliberate design. Organizations that proactively engage in governance discussions gain disproportionate influence over the rules that will eventually constrain them.
