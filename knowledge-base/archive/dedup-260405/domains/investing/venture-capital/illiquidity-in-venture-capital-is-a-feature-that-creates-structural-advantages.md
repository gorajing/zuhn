Illiquidity is often framed as a downside of venture capital, but it functions as a structural moat. Because capital is locked up for 7-10+ years, only investors with genuine long-term conviction participate, filtering out short-term speculators and momentum traders who distort pricing in public markets.

This illiquidity premium also enables founders to pursue ambitious, multi-year roadmaps without quarterly earnings pressure. The inability to sell creates alignment between investors and founders on long-horizon outcomes. For LPs evaluating VC allocations, the illiquidity should be priced as a feature that enables access to returns uncorrelated with public markets, not merely tolerated as a cost.
