Reserve strategy — how much capital a fund holds back for follow-on rounds — directly determines whether a fund can fully capitalize on its winners. Funds that under-reserve get diluted in their best companies' later rounds, while over-reserving means too little capital deployed into new opportunities. Capital recycling (reinvesting proceeds from early exits before the fund's investment period ends) effectively increases the fund's deployable capital beyond what LPs committed. Together, these mechanics mean that two funds with identical initial investments can produce dramatically different returns based purely on capital management discipline. This is an underappreciated operational edge that separates consistently top-performing firms from one-hit wonders.
