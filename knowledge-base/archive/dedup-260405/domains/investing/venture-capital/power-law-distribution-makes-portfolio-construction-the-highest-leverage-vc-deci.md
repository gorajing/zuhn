The power law in venture capital means that a small number of outsized winners drive nearly all fund returns. This mathematical reality has profound implications for how VCs should construct portfolios — it favors larger portfolios with adequate reserves to follow on into winners, rather than concentrated bets.

This shifts the strategic emphasis from 'picking the best deals' to 'ensuring you have enough shots on goal and enough reserves to double down when winners emerge.' Funds that under-reserve or over-concentrate systematically underperform because they can't participate in the follow-on rounds of their breakout companies, leaving returns on the table precisely when the power law compounds most aggressively.
