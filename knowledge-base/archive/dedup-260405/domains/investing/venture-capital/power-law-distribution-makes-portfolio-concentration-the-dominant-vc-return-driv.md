The power law in venture capital means that fund performance is overwhelmingly driven by a small number of extreme winners rather than consistent moderate returns across the portfolio. This has profound implications for investment strategy: optimizing for average outcome quality matters far less than maximizing exposure to potential outliers.

This structural reality shapes everything from initial check sizes to follow-on reserve strategies. VCs who optimize for batting average (avoiding losses) systematically underperform those who optimize for slugging percentage (maximizing upside exposure). The implication for founders is that VCs are rationally incentivized to push for massive outcomes rather than comfortable exits, because only the former meaningfully moves fund-level returns.
