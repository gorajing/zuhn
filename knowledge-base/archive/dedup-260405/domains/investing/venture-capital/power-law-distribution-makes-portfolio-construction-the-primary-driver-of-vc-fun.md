The power law in venture capital means that fund returns are not normally distributed — a tiny fraction of investments produce exponential returns while most return modest or negative outcomes. This has profound implications for how VCs should construct portfolios: the goal is not to avoid losers but to ensure exposure to the rare massive winners. Funds that optimize for loss avoidance by being overly selective may actually underperform funds that take more bets with sufficient reserves to follow on into winners. This dynamic also explains why top-quartile VC funds dramatically outperform median funds — the difference between having one or two power-law winners in a portfolio is enormous at the fund level.
