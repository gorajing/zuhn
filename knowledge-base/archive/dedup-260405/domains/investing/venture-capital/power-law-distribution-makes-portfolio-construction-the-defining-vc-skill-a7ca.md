Venture capital fund returns follow a power law distribution rather than a normal distribution. This means a small number of investments — often just one or two per fund — generate the vast majority of returns. The implication is profound: VC firms cannot succeed by avoiding losses alone. They must ensure they are invested in the rare outlier companies that return 50-100x.

This shapes every aspect of VC strategy: portfolio size (enough bets to catch an outlier), ownership targets (enough stake to matter when one hits), and follow-on reserves (ability to double down on winners). Firms that optimize for average deal quality rather than outlier exposure systematically underperform. The power law also explains why top-quartile VC firms capture a disproportionate share of industry returns — access to the best deals compounds over time.
