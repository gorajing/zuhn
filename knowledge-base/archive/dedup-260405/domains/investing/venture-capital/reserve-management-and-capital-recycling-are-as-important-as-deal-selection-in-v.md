Most attention in venture capital goes to deal sourcing and selection, but reserve management — how much capital to hold back for follow-on rounds and when to deploy it — is equally critical. Poor reserve management can mean missing the ability to double down on winners in later rounds or over-investing in underperformers. Capital recycling (reinvesting realized gains from early exits before the fund closes) can meaningfully extend a fund's effective deployment and boost DPI. These operational mechanics are less glamorous than sourcing but often separate top-performing funds from median ones.
