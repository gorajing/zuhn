Venture capital fund returns follow a power law rather than a normal distribution — the best investments don't just outperform, they generate returns that dwarf the rest of the portfolio combined. This means the traditional diversification logic from public markets doesn't apply. A VC fund's success depends almost entirely on whether it captured one or two massive outliers.

This has deep implications for fund strategy: GPs should optimize for maximum upside exposure rather than downside protection. Passing on a potential outlier is far more costly than losing money on a failed bet. Fund construction, follow-on reserves, and deal selection should all be oriented around maximizing the probability of capturing and riding power-law winners to their full potential.
