Beyond initial investment selection, the mechanics of how a VC fund manages its capital through reserves and recycling can dramatically affect net returns to LPs. Reserves — capital set aside for follow-on investments — allow funds to double down on winners, which is critical in a power-law environment. Recycling involves reinvesting capital returned early (from quick exits or failed investments) back into new or existing portfolio companies, effectively increasing the fund's deployed capital beyond its committed amount. These operational levers are often invisible to outsiders but represent a meaningful edge for sophisticated fund managers who understand capital velocity and timing.
