Venture capital fund returns are fundamentally shaped by power law dynamics — a small number of investments in a portfolio generate outsized returns that dwarf the rest. This mathematical reality has profound implications for investment strategy: it means that avoiding losses matters far less than ensuring exposure to the biggest winners. The practical consequence is that VCs must construct portfolios large enough to capture tail outcomes, maintain sufficient reserves for follow-on investments in winners, and resist the urge to over-optimize individual deal selection at the expense of portfolio breadth. Funds that understand this deploy capital differently than those treating each deal as independent.
