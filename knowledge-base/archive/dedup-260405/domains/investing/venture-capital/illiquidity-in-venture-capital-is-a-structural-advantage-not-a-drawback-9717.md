Illiquidity in venture capital is often cited as a disadvantage, but it functions as a feature that benefits disciplined investors. Because VC fund stakes cannot be easily traded, investors are structurally prevented from the behavioral mistakes that plague public market investors — panic selling during downturns, chasing momentum, or over-trading based on short-term noise.

This forced patience aligns capital with the multi-year timelines required for startups to build real value. The illiquidity premium — the excess return investors demand for locking up capital — also means that VC investors are compensated for this constraint. For LPs with long time horizons (endowments, pension funds, family offices), this structural lock-up is net positive: it harvests behavioral alpha by removing the temptation to act on short-term market sentiment.
