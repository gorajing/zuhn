The narrative that Amazon killed retail is factually wrong. During the same period that Sears lost 142 stores and Toys-R-Us went bankrupt, companies like Costco, Ross, Aldi, and Dollar General grew significantly. Each failed retailer had specific, identifiable failures: Sears tried to do everything poorly, J.C. Penney lost touch with its customers, and Toys-R-Us dismissed the internet.

This pattern recurs in every industry facing technological change. The disruption narrative conveniently externalizes blame, but survivors and thrivers in the same market prove the cause is internal. The actionable lesson for any company facing industry disruption: the threat is not the new technology itself but the complacency it exposes. Companies with clear, differentiated value propositions and willingness to evolve tend to survive category-level disruptions.
