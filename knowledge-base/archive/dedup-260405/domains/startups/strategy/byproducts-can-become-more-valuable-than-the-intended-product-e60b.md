When early photosynthetic organisms evolved water-splitting to fuel their electron transport chains, oxygen was literally a waste gas — a 'useless byproduct' in CrashCourse's words. It was so reactive that it poisoned most existing life forms in the Great Oxidation Event. Yet this byproduct became the single most important enabler of complex multicellular life, aerobic respiration, and ultimately human civilization.

This pattern recurs constantly in business and technology. Slack was a byproduct of a failed video game. Flickr emerged from a game's photo-sharing feature. AWS started as Amazon's internal infrastructure. The insight for builders: pay close attention to what your system produces incidentally. The side effect that seems irrelevant or even problematic may contain more value than your primary product — but only if you're watching for it rather than discarding it.
