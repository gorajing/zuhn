Sri Lanka's government had been rolling old loans into new ones (a 'pyramid scheme' per the World Bank), cut taxes in 2019 when it needed revenue most, and banned all chemical fertilizers to go 'all-organic' — immediately halving crop yields. None of these individually caused collapse, but each removed a buffer.

The insight for organizations: the quality of governance is only testable under stress. In good times, poor decision-making is subsidized by growth and favorable conditions. The real question isn't 'are we growing?' but 'how many simultaneous shocks can we survive?' This applies directly to startups and businesses — rapid growth can mask poor unit economics, technical debt, and organizational dysfunction until a downturn or competitive shock reveals the fragility. Building resilience means stress-testing governance quality before the shocks arrive.
