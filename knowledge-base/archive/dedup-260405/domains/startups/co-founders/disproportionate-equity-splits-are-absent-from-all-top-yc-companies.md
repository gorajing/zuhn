YC's internal data shows that none of their top companies (by valuation) had significantly unequal equity splits among co-founders. Disproportionate ownership signals deeper problems: founders not fully committed, one founder overvaluing past contributions (idea, prototype, credentials) over future execution, or a lack of honest conversation about expectations.

The key reframe is forward-looking: if all founders are 100% committed for the long haul, nothing that happened before incorporation should matter — not who had the idea, not who wrote the first code, not who has the MBA. Execution is what creates value, and execution requires the full team. Unequal splits create resentment that compounds over the years a startup takes to succeed.
