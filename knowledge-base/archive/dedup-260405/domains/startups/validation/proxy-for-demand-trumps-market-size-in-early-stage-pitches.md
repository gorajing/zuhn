Graham relays Sequoia's concept of 'proxy for demand'—evidence that people are already struggling with inadequate tools to solve the problem you're targeting. This is fundamentally more persuasive than market size figures because it shows revealed preference rather than theoretical opportunity. People paying a lot for an inferior solution is the strongest signal.

The hierarchy of user-need evidence is instructive: best is your own need (Apple, Microsoft, Google all started this way), next is needs of people you know personally, and weakest is abstract market research. This maps to the broader startup principle that the most reliable demand signals come from observed behavior, not surveys or TAM calculations. Experienced investors pattern-match on this, so founder-as-user stories get disproportionate attention.
