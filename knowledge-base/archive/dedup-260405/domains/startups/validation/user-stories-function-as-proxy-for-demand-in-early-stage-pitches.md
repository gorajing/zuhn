Graham identifies the core investor anxiety at early stage: that founders built something from a priori theories about what the world needs rather than observed demand. The antidote is what Sequoia's Greg Mcadoo calls the 'proxy for demand'—evidence that people are already doing something painful with inadequate tools, proving the need exists independent of your solution.

The hierarchy of user stories matters: best is when founders built it for themselves (Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all started this way, and experienced investors know it), next best is needs of people you know personally. Abstract market-size arguments ('$5B TAM') are nearly worthless by comparison—Graham compares citing market size to a beginning actor citing Tom Hanks' salary. The gap between 'people need this' and 'here's a specific person struggling without it' is the gap between theory and evidence.
