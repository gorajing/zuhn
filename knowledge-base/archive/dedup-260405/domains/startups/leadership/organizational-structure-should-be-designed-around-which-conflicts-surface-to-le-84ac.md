Most organizational design focuses on efficiency, reporting lines, and role clarity. But a more powerful lens is to ask: which conflicts does this structure surface to the people who can resolve them? Every org structure creates its own pattern of tensions — cross-functional friction, resource competition, priority disagreements — and the key is ensuring the right conflicts reach the right decision-makers.

This reframes org design from a static efficiency problem to a dynamic information-routing problem. A structure that suppresses all conflict appears smooth but often hides critical tensions until they become crises. The goal is to intentionally design for productive conflict at the leadership level while resolving routine friction at lower levels.
