Every organizational structure creates information channels that amplify certain conflicts while muting others. Functional orgs surface technical trade-offs but hide customer-facing conflicts. Product-oriented orgs surface customer conflicts but hide technical debt. The key insight is that this filtering is a design choice, not an accident.

Leaders who understand this can deliberately structure their organization to ensure the conflicts most critical to their strategy reach decision-makers. This means accepting that some friction is valuable signal — the goal isn't to minimize conflict but to ensure the right conflicts are visible. Organizations that suppress all conflict lose their ability to detect and respond to the threats that matter most.
