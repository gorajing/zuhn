The frictionless globalization that enabled startups to sell worldwide from day one is being replaced by a patchwork of trade blocs with 10-100% tariff walls between them. A European startup can now win the EU market while U.S. and Chinese rivals are effectively locked out by duties. This creates regional moats—but also caps total addressable market.

The strategic implication is profound: 'best in the world' matters less than 'best inside your wall.' Founders must now treat geography as a strategic variable on par with product-market fit. This means exploring licensing and joint ventures instead of direct export, building region-specific product variants, and sequencing market entry based on trade treaty alignment rather than pure market size. Go-to-market planning now requires a trade policy layer that most startups have never built.
