The biggest investor fear at the early stage is that you've built something from a priori theories that nobody actually wants. Specific user stories counter this directly. Sequoia looks for what they call 'proxy for demand'—evidence that people are currently using inadequate tools to solve the problem you're addressing.

The hierarchy of story strength matters: best is when the founders themselves had the need (Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all started this way), next is needs of people you know personally, and weakest is abstract market research. Another strong signal is when people pay a lot for an existing solution—a cheaper alternative to something popular is an easy sell if you preserve the qualities that made it popular. Numbers compound stories: if you can say the median visitor generates 12 page views, that sticks in investors' heads far better than market-size statistics.
