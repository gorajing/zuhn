Founders resist narrow descriptions because their product could become many things. But as a description approaches 'could be anything,' its informational content approaches zero. Investors pattern-match against boilerplate and discard vague claims immediately.

PG's framing is algorithmic: start with a description that's gripping but perhaps overly narrow, then broaden it. The goal at pitch time is not to describe the full vision but to get a follow-up meeting. A working mental model in the investor's head — even an incomplete one — beats a fog of possibility.

This mirrors good product strategy: launch with a narrow wedge, expand later. The pitch is the MVP of the story.
