Paul Graham observes that founders resist narrow descriptions because early-stage startups could become anything. But as a description approaches 'could be anything,' its information content approaches zero. Investors discard vague statements as meaningless boilerplate.

The counterintuitive fix is to treat your pitch like incremental development: start with a working prototype description that's perhaps too narrow but immediately gripping, then expand. 'Easy web-based database' beats 'system to collaboratively leverage information value' every time. The goal isn't to describe everything your system might become — it's to create a working mental model in the investor's head that compels further conversation.
