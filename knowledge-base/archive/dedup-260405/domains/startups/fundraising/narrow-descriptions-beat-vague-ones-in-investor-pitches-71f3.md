As a startup's description approaches 'a system to allow people to collaboratively leverage the value of information,' its actual information content approaches zero. Investors will discard vague statements as boilerplate and wait impatiently for you to explain what you actually built.

The better approach is successive approximation: start with a gripping but perhaps overly narrow description, then flesh it out. This mirrors incremental development — at every point you have 'working code,' meaning a working mental model in the investor's head. The fear of sounding too narrow is unfounded because your primary goal isn't to describe everything the system might become, but to convince investors you're worth a follow-up conversation.
