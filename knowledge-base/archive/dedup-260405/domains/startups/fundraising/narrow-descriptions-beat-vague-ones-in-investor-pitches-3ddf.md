Founders resist narrow descriptions because their product feels like it could become anything. But describing something that could be anything conveys nothing — investors mentally discard sentences like 'a system to collaboratively leverage information' as boilerplate. The investor's working model of your product is the actual product you need to ship in their mind.

PG's framing is algorithmic: start with a gripping but overly narrow description, then add precision. This mirrors incremental software development — you always want working code at each step, and here 'working code' means a clear, accurate model in the investor's head. Your goal in the pitch is not to describe future possibility space but to earn the next conversation.
