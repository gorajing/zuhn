Most organizational design focuses on efficiency, clear ownership, and reducing friction — all of which inadvertently suppress the conflicts that leaders most need to hear about. When reporting lines, team boundaries, and incentive structures are optimized purely for smooth execution, disagreements about strategy, resource allocation, and technical direction get resolved at levels too low for leadership to notice until they become crises.

The counterintuitive move is to intentionally design organizational structures that surface specific types of healthy conflict. This means creating overlapping responsibilities where strategic trade-offs live, ensuring cross-functional teams have direct access to decision-makers, and building feedback loops that reward surfacing problems rather than hiding them. The conflicts an organization is designed to hear about reveal its actual priorities far more than any mission statement.
