Revenue and cash are not the same thing. A startup can be growing quickly on paper while running out of actual cash because of working capital timing mismatches. If you owe suppliers in 30 days but collect from customers in 90 days, every new sale actually increases your short-term cash deficit before it eventually improves it.

This is especially dangerous for startups because growth amplifies the gap — more sales mean more upfront costs before collections arrive. The financial model must account for collection timelines, payment terms, and the resulting cash conversion cycle. Founders who only model revenue and expenses without modeling when cash actually moves will be blindsided by liquidity crunches that have nothing to do with product-market fit or profitability.
