Top-down modeling starts with a revenue goal and reverse-engineers what's needed, which feels intuitive but often masks unrealistic assumptions. Bottom-up modeling traces the path from marketing spend through MQL-to-SQL conversion rates, win rates, and sales cycle length to arrive at revenue — every number must be justified by observable data or explicit assumptions.

The practical power is that bottom-up models reveal which lever is broken. If you need 400 MQLs to close 10 deals (10% MQL-to-SQL × 25% win rate), you can immediately see whether your marketing budget and channels can actually produce 400 qualified leads. Most financial modeling combines both approaches, but the bottom-up layer is what makes the model useful for operational decisions, not just fundraising slides.
