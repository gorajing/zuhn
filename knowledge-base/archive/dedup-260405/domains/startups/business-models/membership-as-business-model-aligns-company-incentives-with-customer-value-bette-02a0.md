Costco derives 75% of its revenue from membership fees, not product markups. This fundamentally restructures incentives: raising prices on any item gains pennies today but risks losing the $60-120 annual renewal. The result is a self-imposed 15% maximum markup (vs. Walmart's 24% or Home Depot's 35%) that customers can feel and trust.

This is structurally different from Amazon Prime, where membership is optional and self-selecting — it creates loyalty but doesn't change the underlying business model. Costco's mandatory membership means every operational decision flows from one question: will this make members renew? This single-metric alignment produces coherent strategy across pricing, selection, store design, and employee compensation. The 90% renewal rate is evidence that the alignment works.
