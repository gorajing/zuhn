Costco generates 75% of its profit from membership fees, not product markups. This fundamentally restructures its incentives: rather than maximizing margin per item, Costco maximizes renewal rate by consistently delivering low prices and high quality. The self-imposed 15% max markup rule is not charity—it's rational when $60-120/year renewals at 90% retention are the real revenue engine.

This model creates a flywheel: low prices drive high renewal rates, predictable membership revenue funds further price reductions, and the cycle reinforces itself. Amazon Prime triggers similar sunk-cost psychology but doesn't change Amazon's core incentive to maximize product margins—Prime is a loyalty layer, not the business model itself. The distinction matters: Costco's mandatory membership makes incentive alignment structural, not optional.
