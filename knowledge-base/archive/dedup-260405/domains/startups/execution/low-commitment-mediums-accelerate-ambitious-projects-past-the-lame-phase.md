The lame phase of ambitious projects is psychologically dangerous — every day you spend in it, your motivation erodes. The strategic response is to choose a medium that minimizes time-in-lame-phase. Sketching in a notebook versus carving stone, writing a quick hack versus architecting a full system, digital versus physical prototyping — each reduces the commitment threshold and accelerates initial results. Graham and Carmack both note that the gap between early work and final work matters enormously: when Quake mods were crude, innovation flourished because a quick hack wasn't far from the real thing. The practical lesson is to actively choose your starting medium for speed, not fidelity, and to frame early versions as sketches or hacks to give yourself permission to be rough.
