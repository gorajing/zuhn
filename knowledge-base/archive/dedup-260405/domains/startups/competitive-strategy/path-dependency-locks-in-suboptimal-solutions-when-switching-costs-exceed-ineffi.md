RuBisCo, the enzyme responsible for carbon fixation in all plants, confuses oxygen for CO2 roughly half the time, producing toxic byproducts that plants must neutralize with additional enzymes. Yet plants produce ~40 billion tons of it rather than evolving a replacement, because every downstream process has been built around RuBisCo's outputs and quirks.

This mirrors how startups and platforms become locked into early architectural decisions. The cost of maintaining workarounds (plants making glycine from phosphoglycolate) stays lower than the cost of replacing the core system. The lesson for founders: your earliest technical and strategic choices will calcify fastest, so invest disproportionate care in foundational decisions — but also recognize that 'good enough and entrenched' reliably beats 'optimal but unadopted.'
