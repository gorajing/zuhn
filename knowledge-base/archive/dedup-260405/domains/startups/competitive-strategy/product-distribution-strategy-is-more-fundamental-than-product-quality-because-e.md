Hoffman presents a counterintuitive strategic hierarchy: financing is more fundamental than distribution, which is more fundamental than product. The logic cascades downward — if you run out of money, even a great product dies; if you can't reach customers, even a great product with funding fails. Only when financing and distribution are secured does product quality become the decisive factor.

This means great founders think about their next fundraise while executing the current one, and architect product decisions around distribution mechanics rather than the reverse. When raising money, you're already establishing relationships for the next round. When building product, you're asking 'how does this feature affect our distribution?' rather than 'how do we distribute this feature?' This hierarchy explains why many technically superior products lose to inferior ones with better distribution.
