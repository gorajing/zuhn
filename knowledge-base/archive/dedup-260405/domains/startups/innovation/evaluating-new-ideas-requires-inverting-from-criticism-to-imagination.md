Graham distinguishes between two modes of evaluating early-stage ideas: the default mode lists reasons something won't work, while the productive mode tries to imagine how it could work. This is not about being less critical—it's about redirecting critical thinking toward finding viable paths. Silicon Valley's cultural advantage isn't naivety but this specific inversion, trained by the power-law distribution of startup outcomes where missing one outlier is catastrophically expensive. This practice has become self-reinforcing: because optimistic evaluation produces better returns, it spreads as a cultural norm rather than remaining a specialist technique.
