Most people evaluate early-stage ideas the same way they evaluate finished products, which systematically kills ambitious projects. Graham, from his YC experience sorting thousands of startup pitches, argues the right approach is a full polarity switch: instead of lowering your standards slightly, completely reverse the frame from 'why this won't work' to 'how could this work?' In a power-law world where missing one outlier is catastrophic, this optimistic evaluation becomes urgent rather than naive. Silicon Valley's culture of encouraging strange ideas from unknown people isn't just niceness — it's a high-performance evaluation heuristic calibrated to a world where the biggest wins come from the least obvious places.
