Graham describes several psychological reframing techniques that reduce the sting of early-stage ugliness: calling work 'just a sketch,' treating a project as 'just a hack,' or framing it as an experiment. These aren't self-deception — they're scope management for your inner critic.

The experiment framing is especially powerful because it makes failure literally impossible: an experiment that disproves your hypothesis still produced knowledge. Lisa Randall's version is to treat new things as experiments where ruling something out counts as success. This pairs well with choosing fast, low-commitment media so you can get to your first results quickly and iterate before your inner critic catches up.
