Customer surveys, NPS scores, and even usage metrics can all mislead founders because they measure sentiment or activity rather than commitment. Revenue tells you what customers actually do with their wallets: pay, renew, expand, downgrade, or churn. Each of these actions is an unambiguous signal about whether your product delivers real value.

This means the fastest iteration loop in early-stage startups runs through the billing system, not the feedback form. Founders who instrument revenue flows (new MRR, expansion, contraction, churn) get a real-time readout on whether their product decisions are working. This is especially critical pre-Series A where you can't afford to spend months acting on misleading qualitative signals.
