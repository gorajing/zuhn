Missing IP assignment agreements, misclassified contractors, vague data policies, legacy shareholder issues, and convertible note ambiguities are individually small oversights. But they interact and compound: a fuzzy cap table creates dilution disputes, which create founder conflicts, which create governance questions, which create investor walk-aways. The critical insight is that these issues are cheaply fixable at formation but exponentially expensive to resolve under the time pressure of a closing deal. VCs who check these items early in diligence aren't being pedantic—they're avoiding the scenario where a signed term sheet unravels over a $2,000 legal cleanup that should have happened two years earlier. For founders, the implication is that legal hygiene is not a fundraising expense but a fundraising prerequisite.
