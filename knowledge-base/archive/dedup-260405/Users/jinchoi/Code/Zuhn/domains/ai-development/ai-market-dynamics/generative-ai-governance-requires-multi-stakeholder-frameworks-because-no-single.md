The World Economic Forum's report on generative AI governance highlights that the technology creates challenges that traditional regulatory models cannot address in isolation. Generative AI's risks — from misinformation to IP infringement to job displacement — cross national borders, industry boundaries, and technical layers simultaneously. A model trained in one jurisdiction serves users globally, the training data comes from countless sources, and the downstream applications span every sector. This means governance must involve coordinated efforts between governments, technology companies, civil society, and international organizations rather than top-down regulation from any single body. The report outlines strategies for responsible development that balance innovation incentives with risk mitigation, recognizing that overly restrictive governance in one jurisdiction simply pushes development elsewhere without reducing global risk.
