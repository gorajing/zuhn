The article highlights that deeptech foodtech investing demands understanding of long development cycles, technical risk assessment, and the ability to project future market needs—skills that generalist VCs typically lack. Standard VC evaluation frameworks (traction metrics, unit economics, growth rates) are designed for software and consumer businesses with shorter feedback loops.

This creates a structural advantage for specialized funds: they can access deals that generalist funds either can't evaluate or systematically undervalue. The insight generalizes beyond foodtech—in any sector where the technology cycle is long and the science is complex (biotech, materials science, energy), specialization creates an information edge that compounds over time as deal flow and domain reputation build.
