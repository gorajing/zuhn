Centivax's founder repeatedly resisted pressure to fill seats quickly, instead holding out for people 'so good they're scary' — world-class experts like a CMO who had more approved vaccines than anyone alive. His reasoning: in a small organization of 10-30 people, individual impact is dramatically non-linear. One luminary changes the trajectory of the entire company.

This is the biotech version of the 'talent density' principle. The key insight is that the pressure to compromise on hiring is strongest exactly when it matters most — during the intense operational push where every unfilled role feels painful. But the cost of a mediocre hire in a small team isn't just their underperformance; it's the drag on every exceptional person around them.
