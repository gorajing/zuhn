Spotify's core structural weakness was that record labels captured ~70% of all revenue and held the power to pull licenses at any time. Every minute a user spent listening to licensed music cost Spotify money to the labels. The podcast strategy was explicitly designed to solve this: every minute spent on Spotify-owned podcast content (Joe Rogan, Obama deals, Anchor platform) was a minute not generating royalty obligations to labels. This simultaneously reduced label leverage (less dependence on their catalog), created exclusive content competitors couldn't offer, and improved margins on user engagement time. The pattern mirrors Netflix's shift from licensed content to originals, and Amazon's move into private-label products. When your suppliers capture most of your value and can credibly threaten to walk away, backward integration into content ownership becomes an existential strategic imperative, not just a nice-to-have.
