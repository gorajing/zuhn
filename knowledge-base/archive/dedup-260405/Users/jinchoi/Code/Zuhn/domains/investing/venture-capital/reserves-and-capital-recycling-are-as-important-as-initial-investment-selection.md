Most analysis of venture performance focuses on deal sourcing and initial selection — finding the right companies before others do. But reserve strategy is equally critical and far less discussed. Funds typically reserve 40-60% of their committed capital for follow-on rounds, allowing them to maintain ownership in breakout companies through later dilutive rounds. Recycling — redeploying returned capital from early exits back into new investments — can extend a fund's effective deployment period and compound returns. A fund that runs out of reserves cannot protect its ownership in its winners when they need the most capital. This means fund managers must forecast which portfolio companies will need follow-on rounds and size reserves accordingly from day one.
