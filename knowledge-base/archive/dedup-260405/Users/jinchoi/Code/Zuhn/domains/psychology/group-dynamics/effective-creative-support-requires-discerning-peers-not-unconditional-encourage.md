The social environment around creative work matters enormously, but the common advice to 'surround yourself with supportive people' misses a crucial distinction. People who are always encouraging become noise—you learn to discount their feedback, and it loses its motivational power. What you actually need are colleagues who can tell the difference between early work that has genuine promise and early work that doesn't.

The people best positioned to make this judgment are those working on similar ambitious projects of their own. This is why university departments and research labs are so effective—they create natural concentrations of people with calibrated judgment about early-stage work in a specific domain. You don't need institutions to find these colleagues; they naturally coalesce given the chance. But actively seeking out other people trying to do new things dramatically accelerates this process and creates a local eddy that counteracts the broader social headwind against ambitious early work.
