De Botton traces romantic culture from its 18th-century origins through its central tenets: follow instinct, recognize soulmates by a special feeling, communicate without language, never criticize your partner, and resist any attempt to change each other. He argues bluntly that this has been a disaster -- we are not appreciably happier than in the dynastic marriage era.

The romantic framework treats any rational analysis of relationships as unromantic. Talking about money, discussing childhood patterns, evaluating compatibility -- all are seen as cold and calculating. De Botton argues this is exactly backward: these are the activities that build workable partnerships. The ancient Greeks understood love as a process of emotional education where lovers help each other grow. Romantic culture reversed this entirely.
