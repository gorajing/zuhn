The conventional view treats venture capital illiquidity as a cost investors bear in exchange for higher returns. But reframing illiquidity as a feature reveals its structural benefits: it prevents panic selling during downturns, forces LPs to commit to long time horizons, and shields portfolio companies from the short-termism that plagues public markets.

This illiquidity premium also creates a natural selection mechanism — only investors with genuine long-term conviction and sufficient capital reserves enter VC, which aligns the investor base with the multi-year timelines required to build transformative companies. For founders, having patient capital means they can pursue ambitious technical bets without quarterly earnings pressure.
