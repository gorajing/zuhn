Al-Khalili emphasizes that quantum computers are not faster classical computers — they are fundamentally different machines suited to specific problem types. Expected applications include drug discovery, climate modeling, financial transaction optimization, and simulating quantum systems in physics and chemistry.

Only a handful of quantum algorithms exist (Grover's for search, Shor's for factoring), and developing more is an active research frontier. This means even when hardware matures, the software ecosystem must catch up. The framing matters for investors and strategists: quantum computing is a complementary technology layer, not a wholesale replacement of existing computing infrastructure.
