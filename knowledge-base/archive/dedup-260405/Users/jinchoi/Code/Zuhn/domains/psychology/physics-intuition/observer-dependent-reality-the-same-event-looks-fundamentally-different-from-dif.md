This is perhaps the deepest insight from general relativity applied to everyday thinking: the same event can look completely different depending on your reference frame, and both perspectives can be simultaneously valid. The outside observer genuinely sees time freeze at the horizon. The falling observer genuinely crosses it without incident. Neither is wrong.

In team dynamics, strategy debates, or any multi-stakeholder situation, conflicting accounts of the same situation aren't necessarily evidence that someone is wrong. They may simply be observing from different reference frames. The productive move is not to determine which observer is 'right' but to understand what each frame reveals and conceals.
