Multiple books in the resilience category (Reboot, The Resilience Plan, The Resilient Founder) converge on the same insight: resilience is not a mood or a trait but a muscle that strengthens through deliberate practice. Pelletier's work explicitly bridges neuroscience, psychology, and leadership into a daily resilience toolkit. This reframing matters because founders who believe resilience is innate will not invest in building it, leaving them vulnerable when inevitable crises hit. The practical implication is that resilience protocols should be part of founder operating systems alongside OKRs and sprint planning—preventive infrastructure rather than emergency response.
