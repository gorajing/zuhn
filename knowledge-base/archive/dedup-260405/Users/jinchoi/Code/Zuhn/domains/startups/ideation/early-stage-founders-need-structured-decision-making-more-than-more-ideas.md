The conventional wisdom is that startups begin with a great idea. But the article reframes the early-stage bottleneck: 'Early-stage startups don't lack ideas. They lack clarity.' The templates for the idea stage focus not on brainstorming but on comparison, market analysis, business model design, and structured exploratory calls.

This suggests the real value for pre-product founders is decision infrastructure — tools and frameworks that help them make decisions with less noise. The goal is not to overthink but to create enough structure that founders can move from exploration to commitment faster. This is particularly relevant in an AI era where the cost of building an MVP is dropping rapidly, making the 'which idea to pursue' decision even more consequential than the 'can we build it' question.
