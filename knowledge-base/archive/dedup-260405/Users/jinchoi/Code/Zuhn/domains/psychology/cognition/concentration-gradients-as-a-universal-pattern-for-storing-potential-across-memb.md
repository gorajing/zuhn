ATP synthase works by letting protons flow down a concentration gradient through an enzyme—the chloroplast literally charges itself like a battery by pumping protons to one side of a membrane. The energy isn't in the protons themselves but in the differential between the two sides of the boundary.

This gradient model applies far beyond biology. Information asymmetry between markets creates arbitrage opportunities. Knowledge gaps between expert and novice create teaching leverage. Attention differentials between saturated and underserved channels create marketing advantages. The insight is that energy (or value) doesn't reside in things—it resides in maintained imbalances. Identifying where gradients exist and building 'synthase-like' mechanisms to harvest them is a transferable strategic pattern.
