Given brief time and limited technical background, many investors can't deeply evaluate your technology. The single biggest piece of initial evidence becomes your own confidence in what you've made. But this must be shown, not told — saying 'we're passionate' or 'our product is great' gets discarded as noise or worse, marks you as a bullshitter. The framing shift that unlocks authentic confidence: if you've truly made something good, you're doing investors a favor by telling them about it. If you don't believe your startup has enough promise that you'd be doing them a favor by letting them invest, the problem isn't your pitch — it's what your company is doing.
