The SpaceX-xAI merger at $1.25T valuation represents a bet that the future of AI compute is off-planet. By combining xAI's model capabilities with SpaceX's launch infrastructure and Starlink's revenue ($8B profit), Musk is building a vertically integrated stack where one company controls the AI models, the satellites, the launch vehicles, and the orbital infrastructure.

This mirrors historical patterns where the winner in capital-intensive industries is often the company that controls the entire value chain. The merger also solves xAI's immediate cash-burn problem by pairing it with SpaceX's profitable operations. The strategic question for investors and competitors is whether this vertical integration creates an insurmountable advantage or whether terrestrial AI compute will continue to scale faster than orbital alternatives.
