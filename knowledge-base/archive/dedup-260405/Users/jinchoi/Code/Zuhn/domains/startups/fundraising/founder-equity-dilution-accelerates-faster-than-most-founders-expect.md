Carta's data reveals that founder equity erodes much faster than most first-time founders anticipate, with structural dilution accelerating through each successive round. The compounding effect of option pools, SAFE conversions, and multiple priced rounds means founders often hold far less than they expected by Series B.

The emerging counter-pattern is that AI-native founders—especially solo builders who can ship product with minimal headcount—are retaining significantly more equity than historical norms. This isn't just a lifestyle choice; it's a structural advantage enabled by AI tooling that reduces the need for early hiring. The implication is that the optimal founding strategy may be shifting: delay headcount, use AI to extend what a small team can build, and preserve equity through the highest-dilution phases.
