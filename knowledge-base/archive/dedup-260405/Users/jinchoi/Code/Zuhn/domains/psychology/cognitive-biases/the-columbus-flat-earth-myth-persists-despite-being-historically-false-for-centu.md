The popular story that Columbus proved Earth was round by sailing west is a fabrication. Eratosthenes calculated Earth's circumference around 240 BC with remarkable accuracy. Medieval scholars universally accepted a spherical Earth. The flat-Earth Columbus myth was largely popularized by Washington Irving's 1828 fictionalized biography.

This matters beyond historical trivia because it reveals how progress narratives get manufactured retroactively. We construct stories where a lone genius overcomes ignorant masses, because that narrative template is emotionally satisfying and ideologically useful. Recognizing this pattern helps you spot similar myth-making in other domains — the garage startup myth, the lone inventor myth, or the idea that past practitioners in your field were naive compared to current understanding.
