Russia's February 1917 revolution was genuinely popular -- millions participated, the Tsar abdicated in 10 days. The Provisional Government and the Petrograd Soviet (dominated by Socialist Revolutionaries and Mensheviks) both had far more popular support than the Bolsheviks. But the Bolsheviks had three advantages: (1) a simple, unforgeable message -- 'bread, peace, and land', (2) superior organizational discipline under Lenin, and (3) willingness to act while others deliberated. When Kornilov's coup attempt discredited the Provisional Government, the Bolsheviks' Red Guards were ready. By October, they seized power with minimal resistance. This pattern -- organized minorities capturing power during transitions -- recurs from the French Revolution to modern tech platform shifts. The window of opportunity is always smaller than it appears.
