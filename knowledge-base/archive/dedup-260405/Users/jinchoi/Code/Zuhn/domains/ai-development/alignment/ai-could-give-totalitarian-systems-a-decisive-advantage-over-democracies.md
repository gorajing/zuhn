In the 20th century, totalitarian systems failed because centralized decision-making overwhelmed human bureaucrats with information they could not process. Democratic distributed systems performed better. But AI inverts this dynamic: flooding a human with information causes collapse, while flooding AI with information makes it better.

This means the 20th-century victory of distributed democracy over centralized totalitarianism may not hold in the 21st century. AI enables constant surveillance without the bottleneck of human analysts. The Soviet Union could not have enough KGB agents or analysts; AI faces no such constraint. However, totalitarian AI still lacks self-correcting mechanisms, making catastrophic errors more likely. The risk is that AI makes totalitarianism more efficient while also more brittle.
