RuBisCo fails roughly 50% of the time under current atmospheric conditions, grabbing oxygen instead of CO2. Plants compensate purely through volume — producing so much RuBisCo that the successful half still fixes enough carbon to sustain all plant life on Earth.

This challenges the optimization bias in decision-making. Sometimes the winning move isn't to find a better approach but to massively scale a known-mediocre one. The compounding advantage of an established, scaled system (network effects, institutional knowledge, infrastructure) can make it unbeatable even when theoretically superior alternatives exist. This is why incumbents with 'worse' products often beat startups with 'better' ones.
