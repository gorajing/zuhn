Graham distinguishes between two types of supportive environments: one filled with people who are always encouraging (which you quickly learn to discount), and one filled with people working on similar ambitious projects who can actually evaluate early-stage work. The second is far more valuable because their feedback carries signal — they know what promising early work looks like because they're producing it themselves.

This explains why university departments, research labs, and startup ecosystems like YC work: they concentrate people with calibrated judgment about early-stage work. The implication for anyone doing creative or ambitious work is that environment design — actively seeking out peers attempting similarly ambitious things — is not a luxury but a core strategy. The quality of judgment in your immediate circle directly determines whether you'll persist through the ugly phase or abandon work that had genuine potential.
