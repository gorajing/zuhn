After hearing hundreds of pitches, investors experience them as a blur — the first filter isn't whether they understood you but whether they remember you at all. A compact descriptive phrase ('the x of y' pattern) creates a durable mental anchor that persists after the details fade. Graham notes this should be launched 'clearly but apparently casually' near the beginning, so it frames everything that follows.

The exercise of creating this phrase also functions as a strategic diagnostic: if you can't compress your startup into one compelling phrase, your plans may lack focus. This mirrors the general principle that the inability to simplify is often a symptom of unclear thinking rather than genuine complexity. The soundbite doesn't replace depth — it creates the mental slot into which depth can later be filed.
