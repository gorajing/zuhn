A cohort that loses 47.5% of customers by Month 6 can still show NRR above 100% if the surviving customers expand their spending enough. This creates a dangerous false signal: the revenue line looks healthy while the customer base is hollowing out. The critical distinction is between Gross Revenue Retention (GRR), which measures pure contraction without expansion, and Net Revenue Retention (NRR), which includes upsells. If GRR trails logo retention, the business is losing its heavier spenders first—a particularly toxic pattern. Operators need to read GRR and NRR together with logo retention to get the true picture. Strong GRR with flat NRR indicates a different problem: limited expansion motion, meaning the product isn't deepening value over time.
