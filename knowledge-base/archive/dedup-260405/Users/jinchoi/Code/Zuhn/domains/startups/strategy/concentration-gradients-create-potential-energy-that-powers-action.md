The light-dependent reactions don't directly make ATP. Instead, they spend energy pumping protons into the thylakoid, building a concentration gradient — an imbalance. ATP synthase then harvests this imbalance as protons rush back through, converting the gradient into usable energy. The intermediate step of creating deliberate asymmetry is what makes the whole system work.

In business, the most powerful strategies similarly involve creating and exploiting asymmetries. Information asymmetry drives venture capital returns. Attention concentration (brand) creates pricing power. Talent density in a small team creates disproportionate output. The pattern suggests that the highest-leverage strategic move isn't doing work directly, but creating conditions of imbalance that make productive work inevitable. Build the gradient first; the energy flows naturally.
