In the 2010s, sneakers became investment assets with unprecedented appreciation and resale value. Low interest rates fueled speculation. When the market crashed, media blamed Nike's product strategy, but the real cause was macro: interest rates rose, speculation ended, and revenue (a lagging indicator) was still climbing when demand had already turned. Sneaker conventions that once drew thousands are now rare events with volatile attendance. Most resellers went bankrupt or moved to the next speculative asset. The lesson: when revenue is still growing but the underlying behavioral shift has already happened, you're looking at the last innings of a bubble, not sustainable growth.
