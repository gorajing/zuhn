One of the most practical techniques for surviving the ugly duckling phase is to deliberately miscategorize your work as a lower-stakes format—a sketch rather than a painting, a quick hack rather than production software. This works because it changes the standard you judge yourself against. Once the project gains momentum, you can sneakily upgrade its status. The technique is amplified by choosing media that support fast iteration with low upfront commitment (digital over physical, notebooks over stone carving). Patrick Collison extends this further: making the tenuousness itself a feature ('I'm just seeing how far this naive approach can get') turns the hack framing from a defensive posture into an offensive one that invites experimentation.
