Most organizational design focuses on efficiency, clear ownership, and reducing friction. But a more powerful lens is to design your organization around the conflicts you want to hear about. Every org structure creates information channels that amplify certain tensions and muffle others — the question is whether those filters are intentional or accidental.

For example, separating product and engineering into independent reporting lines ensures that technical debt vs. feature velocity conflicts reach leadership. Combining them under one leader may resolve that tension locally but hide it from executive view. The key insight is that organizational silence on a topic doesn't mean the conflict doesn't exist — it means you've structurally chosen not to hear about it. Founders and leaders should explicitly map which conflicts their current structure surfaces and which it suppresses, then redesign to hear the ones that matter most for their stage and strategy.
