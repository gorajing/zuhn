Most discussions of VC performance focus on deal sourcing and picking winners, but the operational mechanics of reserve management and capital recycling are equally critical. Reserves — capital set aside for follow-on rounds in portfolio companies — determine whether a fund can double down on its emerging winners or gets diluted in later rounds.

Capital recycling, where a fund reinvests proceeds from early exits (often acqui-hires or small M&A) back into new investments, effectively increases the deployed capital beyond the committed fund size. Together, these mechanics mean that two funds with identical initial portfolios can have dramatically different returns based purely on how they manage capital deployment over the fund's life. Founders benefit from understanding this because it explains why existing investors sometimes cannot or will not participate in follow-on rounds.
