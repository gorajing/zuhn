China is systematically using export controls to prevent India from developing competitive manufacturing industries — blocking Foxconn equipment transfers, restricting solar equipment exports, pressuring automakers not to invest, and now withdrawing lithium iron phosphate battery technology partnerships. This goes beyond traditional trade barriers because it targets industrial capability development rather than finished goods trade.

The battery technology case is particularly revealing: Reliance Industries paused lithium-ion cell manufacturing plans after Chinese supplier Xiamen Hithium withdrew under Beijing's technology transfer restrictions. This demonstrates how control over a single critical supply chain node (battery chemistry IP) can stall an entire industrial development program. Despite BRICS rhetoric, China clearly views India as a strategic manufacturing rival whose rise must be contained.
