Graham distinguishes between two kinds of supportive environments: those that are generically encouraging, and those where colleagues can genuinely evaluate early-stage work because they are doing similar work themselves. The former you discount after a while; the latter provides signal. University departments and research labs work well because everyone is engaged in early-stage ambitious work and has calibrated expectations. The implication for founders: don't just seek cheerleaders—seek people who understand the domain well enough to give meaningful early feedback without applying finished-product standards.
