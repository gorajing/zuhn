While deal sourcing and selection get the most attention in VC, reserve management and capital recycling are critical operational levers that determine realized returns. Reserves — capital set aside for follow-on investments in winning portfolio companies — allow funds to double down on their best performers, amplifying the power law effect. Under-reserving means missing the chance to increase ownership in breakout companies; over-reserving means deploying less initial capital and potentially missing opportunities.

Capital recycling — reinvesting early distributions (from quick exits or secondaries) back into new investments — effectively increases the fund's deployable capital beyond committed amounts. This technique can significantly boost net returns but requires careful management to avoid over-committing. Together, these mechanics mean fund operations and capital strategy are as important as investment thesis in driving top-quartile performance.
