Graham argues that the most valuable form of intelligence for founders is imagination, specifically the ability to generate ideas that sit at the right level of craziness. This is a non-obvious selection filter: ideas that seem obviously good are already being pursued, so the remaining valuable ideas must by definition seem bad to most people.

Airbnb perfectly illustrates this principle — even YC thought the idea of strangers sleeping in each other's homes was too crazy, and funded the team based on founder quality rather than idea quality. The founders' creative resourcefulness (selling branded breakfast cereal to fund themselves) was a stronger signal than the idea itself. This implies that idea evaluation should focus less on whether an idea sounds good and more on whether it occupies unexplored territory that incumbents would dismiss.
