The obvious advice for pushing through the lame phase is 'surround yourself with supportive people,' but Graham identifies a crucial refinement: indiscriminate encouragement is useless because you'll learn to discount it. The real value comes from people who can actually distinguish promising early work from genuinely bad work.

The best source of such discernment is people working on their own ambitious projects in similar domains—which is why university departments and research labs are so effective. They provide calibrated feedback: honest enough to be credible, informed enough to see potential others miss. This happens naturally when ambitious people cluster, but actively seeking out such communities dramatically accelerates the process.
