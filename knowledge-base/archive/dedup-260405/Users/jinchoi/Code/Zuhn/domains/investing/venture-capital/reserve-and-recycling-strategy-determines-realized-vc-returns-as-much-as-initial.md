Initial deal selection gets most of the attention in venture capital, but the mechanics of reserves (capital set aside for follow-on rounds) and recycling (reinvesting early exits or distributions back into the fund) are equally critical to realized returns. A fund that picks great companies but under-reserves for follow-ons will get diluted out of its winners. A fund that over-reserves sits on dry powder that drags overall returns.

Recycling adds another dimension: when a fund receives early proceeds (from quick acquisitions or secondary sales), reinvesting those proceeds rather than distributing them effectively increases the deployed capital without raising a larger fund. This can meaningfully boost net multiples. The interplay between initial check size, reserve ratios, and recycling strategy is a core competency of top-performing fund managers that's invisible to most outside observers.
