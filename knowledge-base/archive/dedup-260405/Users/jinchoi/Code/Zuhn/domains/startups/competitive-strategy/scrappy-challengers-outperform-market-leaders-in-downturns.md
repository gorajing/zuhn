Brex built its entire operation for an era where growth was the only metric that mattered — large teams, aggressive spending, broad product surface. When capital became expensive in 2022, this infrastructure became dead weight. Ramp, forced to be efficient from birth, had already internalized the discipline that Brex had to painfully learn mid-flight.

This pattern extends beyond fintech. Companies that achieve dominance during easy-money periods often embed that abundance into their cost structures, hiring practices, and strategic assumptions. When the cycle turns, the challenger who never had those luxuries can move faster because they have less to unwind. The lesson is that early dominance purchased with capital is fragile; dominance earned through operational efficiency compounds.
