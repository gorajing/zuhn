Graham makes a crucial distinction in the type of social support that sustains ambitious work. Simply collecting encouraging people fails because you learn to discount unconditional positive feedback—it carries no information. What you actually need are peers working on similarly ambitious projects who can tell when early-stage ugliness conceals genuine promise versus genuine mediocrity.

This explains why university departments, research labs, and startup ecosystems work: they concentrate people with calibrated judgment about early-stage work in specific domains. The practical implication is that seeking out other people trying to do new things—and whose judgment you respect precisely because they're not always positive—is one of the highest-leverage actions for sustaining ambitious projects through the inevitable discouraging early phase.
