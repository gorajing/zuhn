The 'janitorial services problem': if you tell a CEO you can get their toilets 9% cleaner and save 1% on spend, they won't even exercise the mental energy to find the person who cares. This Goldilocks zone of irrelevance is actually more protective than any technical moat.

Payroll companies like ADP are the perfect example — collectively worth hundreds of billions, operating in a zone where the per-employee cost is so low relative to total payroll that nobody ever switches. Conversely, if you're consuming 90% of a company's profits, they'll do RFPs relentlessly to replace you. The strategic implication: for AI startups targeting incumbents in the Goldilocks zone, don't try to displace them. Instead, target greenfield (new company creation) where there are no incumbents, though this requires patient entrepreneurs and high enough rates of new business formation.
