AI data centers are inelastic consumers: they need continuous, reliable power and cannot easily shift load. The current grid has aging hardware (25-40 year service life), multi-year equipment delivery queues, and decade-long approval timelines for major transmission projects.

This creates an underappreciated constraint on AI scaling. The industry narrative focuses on GPU supply and model architecture, but the physical energy layer may become the binding constraint. Companies that secure power purchase agreements and co-locate near generation sources will have a structural advantage over those competing for scarce grid capacity.
