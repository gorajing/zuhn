A sketch in a notebook feels low-stakes; carving stone feels permanent. This difference in perceived commitment profoundly affects whether ambitious projects get started at all. Digital mediums have a massive advantage here—not because the software is better, but because they reduce the gulf between first attempt and final product, lowering the psychological barrier to entry. The same principle explains why game modding exploded when platforms like Minecraft made the aesthetic deliberately crude: when the gap between a quick hack and a polished product is small, more people experiment, and more careers are born. The practical implication is to deliberately choose tools and formats that minimize upfront commitment when exploring new ideas.
