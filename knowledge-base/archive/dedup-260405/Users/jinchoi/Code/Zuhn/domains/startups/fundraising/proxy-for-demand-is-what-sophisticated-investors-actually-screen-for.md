The biggest investor fear with early startups is that founders built something from a priori theories that nobody actually wants. The antidote is showing proxy for demand: what are people doing right now, with inadequate tools, that proves they need what you're making?

This works at multiple levels. People paying a lot for something proves demand for a cheaper alternative (if you preserve what made it popular). Founders solving their own problem is the strongest signal of all—Apple, Microsoft, Yahoo, Google all started this way. Experienced investors know this pattern and actively look for it, making personal-need stories disproportionately effective.
