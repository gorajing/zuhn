The pattern of early leaders losing to later entrants repeats across technology waves: Yahoo lost search to Google, BlackBerry and Nokia lost smartphones to Apple, Studebaker lost automobiles. Noah Smith applies this pattern to OpenAI's position in AI, arguing that despite creating the first widely-used LLM with ChatGPT, OpenAI faces the same structural risks that toppled previous first movers.

The implication for founders and investors is that sustainable competitive advantage must come from business fundamentals — profitability, vertical integration, cost structure, market positioning — not from the ephemeral advantage of being first. Incumbency without structural moats is a wasting asset, especially in markets experiencing rapid commoditization.
