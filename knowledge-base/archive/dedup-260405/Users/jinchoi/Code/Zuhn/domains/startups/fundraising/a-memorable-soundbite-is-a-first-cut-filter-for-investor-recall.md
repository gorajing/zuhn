After a demo day or investor meeting series, pitches genuinely blur. The investor's ability to recall and advocate for a specific startup in partner meetings or follow-up conversations depends on having a handle — a phrase that encodes the startup's essence and sticks.

The structural forms PG identifies ('the X of Y', 'X meets Y') work because they borrow existing mental models. 'The Microsoft Word of ecommerce' (Viaweb, later acquired by Yahoo) instantly communicates scope, category, and aspiration without a slide.

This exercise also serves as a forcing function for founders: if you can't describe what you're building in one compelling phrase, your own strategy may not be sufficiently focused. The inability to produce the soundbite is diagnostic — it often reveals that the product vision hasn't been fully resolved internally.
