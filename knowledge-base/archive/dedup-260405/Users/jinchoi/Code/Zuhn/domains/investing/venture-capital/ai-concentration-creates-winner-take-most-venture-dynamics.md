The 2025-2026 venture rebound is not evenly distributed — capital is concentrating into AI-focused companies through increasingly large rounds, while the broader startup ecosystem sees tighter conditions. This creates a two-tier market: AI startups competing for massive checks from eager investors, and everyone else facing a structurally harder fundraising environment. The pattern echoes previous technology concentration cycles but is more extreme because AI's perceived winner-take-all dynamics drive investor FOMO into fewer bets. For founders, this means the fundraising strategy itself must account for whether you're positioned inside or outside the AI capital magnet.
