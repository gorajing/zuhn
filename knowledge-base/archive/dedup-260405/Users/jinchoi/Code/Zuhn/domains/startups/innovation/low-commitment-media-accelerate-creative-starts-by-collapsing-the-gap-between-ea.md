Graham identifies medium selection as a critical but underappreciated lever for creative output. When the gap between early work and final work is small — as in game mods for crude engines like original Quake, or digital sketches versus stone carving — creators start more freely because the early version doesn't feel embarrassingly far from 'done.' John Carmack's observation about Minecraft and Roblox is striking: by making crudeness the aesthetic, these platforms made innovative gameplay the only differentiator, and single authors routinely outperformed massive professional teams. The practical implication is that when starting something new, deliberately choose the fastest, least-committal medium available — a notebook sketch, a quick hack, a rough prototype — and only convert it to something more polished once momentum is established.
