Beyond deal selection, the mechanical decisions of reserve allocation and capital recycling materially impact VC fund returns. Reserves — capital set aside for follow-on rounds in winning companies — allow funds to maintain ownership in their best performers rather than getting diluted. Recycling — reinvesting early distributions (from quick exits or secondary sales) back into the fund — effectively increases the fund's investable capital beyond the committed amount.

These operational levers are often invisible to LPs evaluating funds but can represent the difference between a 2x and 3x net return. A fund that recycles 20% of early proceeds effectively has 120% of committed capital to deploy, amplifying the power law effect if that capital flows to winners.
