Apple's experience in India demonstrates a hard ceiling on premium-only market entry. With 75% of Indian smartphones selling under $250 and 95% under $500, the cheapest iPhone at $1,100 addresses a tiny sliver of the addressable market. Even selling older models (iPhone 6, SE) at relatively high prices failed because consumers expect new devices at those price points, not 3-5 year old hardware.

This contrasts sharply with Chinese competitors like OnePlus, Oppo, and Vivo who specifically designed low-cost, locally-manufactured devices for the Indian market. The lesson extends beyond phones: when entering markets where per-capita GDP is a fraction of your home market, brand prestige alone cannot overcome a 5-10x price mismatch. Companies must either create market-specific product lines or accept marginal presence.
