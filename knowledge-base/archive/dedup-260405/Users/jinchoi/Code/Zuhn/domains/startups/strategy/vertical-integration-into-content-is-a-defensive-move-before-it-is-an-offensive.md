In 2015, when this analysis was written, Amazon was already licensing significant amounts of content from studios that had every incentive to eventually launch their own streaming services (as HBO, Disney, and NBC Universal later did). Original production was Amazon's hedge against that dependency: owned content cannot be repriced or withdrawn by a competitor.

This is a recurring pattern in platform strategy: vertical integration into adjacent layers is often framed as expansion but is structurally defensive. It reduces the leverage that suppliers in that layer have over the platform's core value proposition.

For founders building on top of third-party content, data, or services, this suggests treating key supplier dependencies as risks to be gradually internalized as the business scales. The right trigger for vertical integration is when a supplier gains enough leverage to threaten the unit economics or continuity of the core business — not when it looks like an attractive revenue opportunity.
