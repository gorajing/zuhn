Costco's inventory discipline — roughly 4,000 SKUs versus 30,000 at a typical supermarket or 140,000 at Walmart — is not a limitation but a moat. When a supplier knows there are only one or two slots per category, they will lower prices, adapt their product specifications, and work to meet Costco's requirements to secure the slot. Costco used this leverage to re-engineer a cashew container to fit more per pallet, saving 24,000 pallets a year and passing the savings to customers.

This is the opposite of the 'more choice is better' assumption that dominates most retail and marketplace thinking. Fewer choices concentrate buying power, reduce operational complexity, and paradoxically improve customer satisfaction by eliminating decision fatigue.

For product builders and marketplace operators, the principle extends beyond retail: constraining the option space in a category can increase your leverage over suppliers or vendors while improving the customer experience. Curated > comprehensive when the curator has skin in the game.
