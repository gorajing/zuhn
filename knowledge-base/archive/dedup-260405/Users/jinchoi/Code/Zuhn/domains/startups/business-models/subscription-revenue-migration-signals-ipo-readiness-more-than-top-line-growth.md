Rubrik's IPO success was driven less by raw revenue growth and more by the composition of that revenue. Moving from 59% subscription to 91% in just two years transformed how investors valued the business — from a lumpy hardware-adjacent vendor to a predictable SaaS platform. This mirrors a broader pattern where public markets heavily discount non-recurring revenue streams.

For founders planning eventual public exits, the lesson is that revenue quality matters more than revenue quantity. A company with $500M in 90%+ subscription ARR will command a higher multiple than one with $800M in mixed revenue. The transition itself also signals management discipline and product-market fit in the cloud-native model.
