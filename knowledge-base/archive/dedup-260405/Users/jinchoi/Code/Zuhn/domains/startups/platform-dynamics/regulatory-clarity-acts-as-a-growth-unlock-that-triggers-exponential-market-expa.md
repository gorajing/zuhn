Before 2024, prediction markets were stuck at ~$50M weekly volume — not because the product was bad, but because regulatory ambiguity kept institutional money and major distribution partners away. The Kalshi v. CFTC decision removed this constraint, and within 24 months volume exploded 100x to $6B/week.

The pattern is instructive for any startup operating in regulatory gray zones: clarity — even imperfect clarity — is the unlock. Once legitimized, Robinhood, Coinbase, DraftKings, and FanDuel integrated prediction markets, instantly exposing the product to 100M+ retail accounts with linked bank accounts. This is the distribution leverage that no amount of organic growth could replicate. The lesson: in regulated markets, the regulatory moat is both the biggest obstacle and the biggest accelerant.
