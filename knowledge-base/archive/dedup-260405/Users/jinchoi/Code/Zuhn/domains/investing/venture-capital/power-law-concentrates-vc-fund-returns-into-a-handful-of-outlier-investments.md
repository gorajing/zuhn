The power law distribution of startup outcomes means that most VC fund returns come from a tiny fraction of portfolio companies. This isn't a quirk — it's the structural reality of high-variance, winner-take-most markets. A fund with 30 investments may generate 80%+ of its returns from 2-3 companies.

This shapes everything from portfolio sizing (concentrate enough to own meaningful stakes in winners) to follow-on strategy (reserve capital to double down when signals are clear) to LP expectations (median performance is a misleading metric). Understanding the power law reframes 'diversification' in VC — too much diversification dilutes the upside from the rare outliers that actually matter.
