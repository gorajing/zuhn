The AI infrastructure investment wave has triggered familiar debates about whether the industry is overbuilding capacity ahead of actual demand. Historical precedents from fiber optics, cloud computing, and mobile infrastructure suggest that transformative technology shifts tend to see initial overbuilding followed by demand catching up to and exceeding supply.

However, this cycle has unique characteristics: the concentration of capex among a few hyperscalers, the rapid commoditization of inference costs, and the uncertain timeline for AI-native applications to generate proportional revenue. The key question is not whether AI infrastructure will be utilized, but whether the current investors in that infrastructure will capture the returns, or whether value will flow to application-layer companies that benefit from cheaper compute.
