The common objection to nihilism is that it leads to 'rotting away in bed.' O'Connor counters with a simple example: explaining that thirst is merely an animalistic drive with no cosmic purpose does not make you less thirsty. You still want water and will still drink it. The same applies to art, music, relationships, and ambition. Camus captured this in the Myth of Sisyphus — you can recognize the absurdity of the repetitive structure of daily life while still engaging fully with it. The practical implication is that philosophical recognition of meaninglessness is compatible with vigorous, purposeful action. The motivation comes from within, not from external validation of cosmic significance.
