Anton Zaides' observation that founders fire people too late runs counter to the typical founder fear of seeming impulsive. In practice, the cost of retaining an underperformer compounds: team morale erodes as high performers watch low-accountability persist, the underperformer gets entrenched in processes and relationships, and the founder invests emotional energy into coaching instead of building. The data pattern from experienced founders is nearly universal — when surveyed in hindsight, almost none say 'I wish I had kept them longer.' The actionable implication is to set a clear performance horizon and act when it passes, not to keep extending it.
