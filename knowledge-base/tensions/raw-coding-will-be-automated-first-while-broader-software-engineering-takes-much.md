**Side A:** "Raw coding will be automated first while broader software engineering takes much longer to automate"
**Side B:** "AI should enable humans rather than replace them in important tasks"
**Reason:** One predicts coding automation as inevitable, the other advocates for AI as enabler not replacer — tension between automation-as-destination vs augmentation-as-philosophy
**Resolution:** Side A (coding automated first, engineering later) is a factual prediction about automation sequence. Side B (AI should enable not replace) is a normative philosophy about how to deploy automation. These answer different questions and are not in conflict. The synthesis: raw coding WILL be automated first (A is correct as prediction), AND the correct response is to use that automation to enable engineers to focus on harder engineering problems rather than to eliminate engineering roles (B is correct as strategy). Dario Amodei himself, the source for Side A, advocates for exactly Side B's approach. The real tension is between A's implication and the organizational response to it. Companies that treat coding automation as replacement will destroy their talent pipeline (per INS-260323-DA85). Companies that treat it as enablement will produce engineers who operate at a higher level of abstraction.

**Prediction:** Companies that redefine developer roles around architecture, system design, and AI orchestration (enablement) during the coding automation wave will retain more senior engineering talent and ship higher-quality systems than companies that simply reduce engineering headcount (replacement).
