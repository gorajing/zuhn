**Side A:** "All inputs to AI are falling in cost faster than Moore's law, creating hyperdeflation in per-unit intelligence costs"
**Side B:** "While per-token costs have decreased, reasoning models burn dramatically more tokens per task, so actual inference costs per useful output have increased"
**Reason:** Andreessen argues all AI input costs are collapsing faster than Moore's law, creating hyperdeflation and massive elastic demand. Zitron argues the headline metric (cost per token) is misleading because cost per correct output is rising. The disagreement: which denominator matters — cost per token or cost per useful output?
**Resolution:** Synthesis — Jevons paradox applied to intelligence: cheaper tokens don't reduce total spend, they enable more ambitious workloads. Cost per token IS falling faster than Moore's law. Cost per useful output HAS increased for reasoning-heavy tasks. The right denominator depends on use case.
