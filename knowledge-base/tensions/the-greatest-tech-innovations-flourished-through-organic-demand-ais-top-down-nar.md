**Side A:** "The greatest tech innovations flourished through organic demand — AI's top-down narrative is the red flag"
**Side B:** "AI adoption is accelerating faster than any previous technology wave with genuine productivity gains"
**Reason:** One claims AI adoption is fear-driven not pull-driven (red flag), the other shows AI adoption accelerating with real gains — opposing views on whether AI demand is organic or manufactured
**Resolution:** Side A is correct about the pattern: past tech revolutions (PC, internet, mobile) were pull-driven by organic demand, while AI adoption has significant push-driven components (billionaire evangelism, FOMO-based corporate purchasing, usage-based KPIs). However, Side A overstates by implying AI has no organic demand. Note: Side B reference (INS-260322-8BC5) is mislinked -- it maps to an SMB pricing insight, not an AI adoption insight. Regardless, evidence within the knowledge base (AI companies growing 693% YoY, genuine developer productivity gains, real cost reductions) demonstrates pockets of genuine organic demand. The resolution: AI adoption is a mix of both -- real productivity gains in specific domains (coding, content, data processing) surrounded by a much larger bubble of FOMO-driven adoption theater. The red flag is not AI itself but the gap between actual utility and capital deployed.

**Prediction:** AI companies serving developers and technical users (organic pull) will retain 70%+ of customers through a market correction, while AI tools sold top-down to enterprises on FOMO (push) will see 40%+ churn when budgets tighten.

**Note:** Side B insight reference INS-260322-8BC5 appears mislinked -- it corresponds to the SMB pricing death zone insight, not an AI adoption acceleration insight. This should be corrected in the tension metadata.
