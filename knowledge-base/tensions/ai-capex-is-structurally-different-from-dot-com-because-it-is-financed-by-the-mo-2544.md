**Side A:** "AI capex is structurally different from dot-com because it is financed by the most profitable companies in history with zero idle GPUs"
**Side B:** "Data center credit deals total $178.5B, much of it junk-grade debt for unprofitable AI startups"
**Reason:** Both factually accurate. Hyperscalers are financially healthy, but the broader financing layer carries systemic risk with junk-grade debt. Is AI capex sound because the top funds it, or risky because the periphery is fragile?
**Resolution:** Synthesis — AI capex has a bifurcated risk structure: the core (hyperscalers spending from cash flows) is structurally sound, while the periphery ($178.5B in junk-grade data center debt) carries systemic fragility. The correct model is 'core is safe, periphery is a contagion vector' — the same structure as 2008 housing.
