**Side A:** "AI's economic impact and design space exceeds the internet era, meaning more companies will reach $1B+ and $10B+ valuations"
**Side B:** "Oracle and the wider tech industry are applying the 'spend big, growth follows' playbook from the cloud era to AI, but the underlying economics are fundamentally different"
**Reason:** Horowitz argues AI's design space is unprecedented and the key risk is underestimating value creation. Zitron argues trillions in infrastructure spending dwarfs $55B in actual AI revenue. AI companies grow at unprecedented rates AND the spend-to-revenue ratio is historically unprecedented — both facts are true.
**Resolution:** Synthesis — AI will create more $1B+ companies than the internet, but most current infrastructure spending won't be recouped by the spenders. The winners will be application-layer companies exploiting cheap infrastructure built by others who went bankrupt — the exact dot-com-to-Google pattern.
