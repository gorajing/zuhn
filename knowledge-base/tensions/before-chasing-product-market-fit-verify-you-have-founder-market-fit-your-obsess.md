**Side A:** "Before chasing product-market fit, verify you have founder-market fit — your obsessions and expertise determine whether you can sustain the PMF search"
**Side B:** "The fastest path to product-market fit is having an audience you can survey, test MVPs with, and iterate against — not building in isolation"
**Reason:** Two competing origin stories: start from who YOU are (founder-market fit as prerequisite) vs start from who your CUSTOMERS are (audience-first as method). Without audience validation, founder-market fit builds products nobody wants. Without founder-market fit, audience-first builds products the founder can't sustain. The tension is whether internal conviction or external signal should be the primary compass.
**Resolution:** Synthesis — these are sequential steps, not alternatives. Founder-market fit is a prerequisite filter (which arena to enter), audience-first is the execution method (how to find PMF within that arena). The Pensive case proves the sequence: 8 pivots failed in domains without founder-market fit, then education (where both founders had deep expertise) became the domain where audience feedback could actually converge on a product. Barman's 20+ failed businesses confirm the corollary: without EITHER founder-market fit OR audience feedback, you are building blind. The practical framework: (1) Choose a domain where you have expertise, personal stakes, and decade-long motivation. (2) Build an audience in that domain. (3) Let audience pain points guide product iteration. Skip step 1 and you pivot endlessly. Skip step 2 and you build in isolation.
