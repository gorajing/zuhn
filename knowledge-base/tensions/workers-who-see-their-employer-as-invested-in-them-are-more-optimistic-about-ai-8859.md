**Side A:** "Workers who see their employer as invested in them are more optimistic about AI, suggesting adoption succeeds through trust and genuine value"
**Side B:** "Companies adopt AI because competitors are adopting it, not because ROI is proven, with employees measured on chatbot usage rather than productivity"
**Reason:** Trust-driven adoption produces value. FOMO-driven adoption produces theater. Are these different populations, or does the same organization exhibit both patterns simultaneously?
**Resolution:** Both patterns coexist within the same organization, often simultaneously. Executive leadership adopts AI due to competitive FOMO and measures employees on chatbot usage (Side B). Meanwhile, individual workers' actual engagement with AI tools correlates with whether they feel their employer is investing in them (Side A). The determining variable is organizational trust level: high-trust companies convert even FOMO-initiated adoption into genuine productivity; low-trust companies produce adoption theater regardless of the technology's capability. Side B is the more common pattern today, but Side A describes the necessary condition for any adoption to produce real value.

**Prediction:** Companies that measure AI adoption by productivity outcomes rather than usage metrics will show 3-5x higher ROI on AI tooling within 2 years compared to those using chatbot usage KPIs.
