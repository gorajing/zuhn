**Side A:** "The ideal AI startup founding team is one builder who ships in 24 hours and one go-to-market person with audience empathy"
**Side B:** "Altman believes AI tools now enable a single person to build a company worth over a billion dollars, delivering products that previously required teams of hundreds"
**Reason:** Tension on whether AI augmentation eliminates the need for co-founders. One says you still need complementary skills (builder + GTM), the other says one AI-augmented person can do it all. The resolution might be stage-dependent: one person can BUILD a product with AI, but DISTRIBUTING and SELLING it may still require a human with different skills. Or AI might eventually handle GTM too, making the solo founder viable end-to-end.
**Resolution:** Conditional on distribution model. AI has already collapsed the BUILD side — one person with AI tools can ship what took teams of hundreds. The unresolved question is the DISTRIBUTE side. For product-led growth (developer tools, API products, viral consumer apps), a solo AI-augmented founder can plausibly reach billion-dollar scale because distribution is embedded in the product. For audience-driven growth (social media, content marketing, community building) or relationship-driven sales (enterprise, B2B), a human GTM co-founder with audience empathy remains necessary because trust and authenticity cannot yet be delegated to AI. The Higgsfield two-person model (builder + GTM) is the safer bet today because it covers both modes. Altman's solo founder vision is a directional truth that becomes more accurate as AI handles more GTM functions, but is premature as a universal claim in 2026.
