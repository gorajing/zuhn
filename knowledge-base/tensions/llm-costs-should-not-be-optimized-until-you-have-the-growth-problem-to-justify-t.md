**Side A:** "LLM costs should not be optimized until you have the growth problem to justify the effort"
**Side B:** "Every dollar of profit not reinvested into growth is a dollar a competitor could use against you"
**Reason:** One says ignore costs and focus on growth first, the other says reinvest every dollar into growth — similar direction but the first dismisses cost optimization while the second demands maximum reinvestment
**Resolution:** Synthesis — Not a genuine tension — both point in the same direction (prioritize growth). Pre-PMF: let LLM costs burn. Post-PMF: reinvest profit. Same principle (premature efficiency kills), different stages.
