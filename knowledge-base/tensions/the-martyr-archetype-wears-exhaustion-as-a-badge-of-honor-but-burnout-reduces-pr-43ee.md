**Side A:** "The martyr archetype wears exhaustion as a badge of honor, but burnout reduces productivity by 10%+ and early burnout can cost $300K/year"
**Side B:** "Camus's Sisyphus finds happiness not by winning but by embracing the insurmountable task with full commitment"
**Reason:** Is sustained intense effort destructive (A) or generative (B)? Resolution may hinge on whether effort is chosen vs imposed, and driven by meaning vs obligation.
**Resolution:** The resolution hinges on whether intense effort is chosen or imposed, and whether it is driven by meaning or obligation. Side A (burnout destroys productivity) describes the martyr archetype: effort driven by external obligation, identity-attachment to suffering, or fear of stopping. Side B (Sisyphean commitment creates meaning) describes effort driven by intrinsic meaning and willing acceptance of difficulty. The same observable behavior -- 80-hour weeks, intense focus, sacrificing comfort -- is destructive when externally imposed or ego-driven (martyr), and generative when freely chosen and meaning-driven (Sisyphus). The suffering audit from Side A is the diagnostic tool: if 70% of your sacrifices are self-imposed obligations no one asked for, you are the martyr. If you push the boulder because you chose the hill, you are Sisyphus.

**Prediction:** Founders who report high meaning/purpose scores but moderate stress scores will outperform founders who report high stress but low meaning on 5-year business outcomes.
