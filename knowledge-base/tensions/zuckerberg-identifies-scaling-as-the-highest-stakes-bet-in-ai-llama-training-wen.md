**Side A:** "Zuckerberg identifies scaling as the highest-stakes bet in AI: Llama training went from 10-20K to 100K+ GPUs, with hundreds of billions riding on continued returns"
**Side B:** "The leap from GPT-4 to GPT-5 was subtle enough to require benchmarks, unlike earlier leaps that were obvious in minutes — scaling has hit a wall"
**Reason:** Zuckerberg bets hundreds of billions that transformers haven't plateaued. Marcus argues the GPT-4→5 gap proves they have, and 85% of AI researchers believe LLMs won't achieve AGI. Perhaps the highest-stakes disagreement in the AI industry.
**Resolution:** Conditional. The two sides operate on different success criteria. Zuckerberg's infrastructure bet pays off even if Marcus is right about the pre-training ceiling, because GPU-scale compute has value across inference scaling, fine-tuning, agentic orchestration, and can host future architectures. Marcus is correct that qualitative reasoning leaps have stalled, and 85% of AI researchers agreeing LLMs won't reach AGI is a strong signal. But Zuckerberg isn't betting solely on AGI — he's betting on commercially dominant AI, which plateau-era models already deliver. The bet fails only if a fundamentally different compute paradigm (not just architecture) renders GPU clusters irrelevant, which no credible alternative currently suggests.

**Prediction:** Meta's AI infrastructure investment will generate positive ROI by 2028 through AI-driven ad revenue and product features, regardless of whether transformer scaling produces qualitative capability leaps — validating the "infrastructure is architecture-agnostic" thesis.
