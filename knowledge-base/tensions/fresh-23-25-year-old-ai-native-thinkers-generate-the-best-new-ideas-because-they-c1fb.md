**Side A:** "Fresh 23-25 year old AI-native thinkers generate the best new ideas because they think differently about what's possible"
**Side B:** "Your 20s should be spent accumulating expertise, network, and readiness — enthusiasm without domain knowledge burns runway"
**Reason:** Zuckerberg, Jobs, and many AI founders were very young. But domain-specific startups show deep experience matters. The tension maps to whether the current AI wave rewards beginner's mind or expert's eye.
**Resolution:** Conditional on domain type. Both insights correctly identify real patterns but in different contexts. AI-native young thinkers (Higgsfield's 23-25 year olds) excel in paradigm-creation domains where the absence of legacy thinking is an advantage — they imagine products that experienced people would dismiss as impossible. But the gaming studio example (wanting to build AAA games without knowing 78% of the Indian gaming market was mobile) proves that domain-expertise startups punish enthusiasm without knowledge. The synthesis is not "young vs old" but "what kind of knowledge matters." In AI-native product categories, fresh thinking beats experience. In regulated, operationally complex, or domain-deep markets, experience beats enthusiasm. The practical implication: a 24-year-old should found an AI tool company, not a healthcare startup. A 35-year-old healthcare veteran should found a healthcare AI startup, not a consumer AI app.
