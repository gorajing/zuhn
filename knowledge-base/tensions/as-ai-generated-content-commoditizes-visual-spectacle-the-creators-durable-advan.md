**Side A:** "As AI-generated content commoditizes visual spectacle, the creator's durable advantage becomes narrative authenticity — story, emotion, and philosophy win"
**Side B:** "Using AI to rewrite emotional messages or generate personal speeches eliminates the meaningful human struggle from communication"
**Reason:** AI may simultaneously increase the market VALUE of authentic human expression (by making generic content worthless) while decreasing the number of people who PRACTICE it (by outsourcing emotional labor). Creators benefit from scarcity of authentic work, but consumers may lose the capacity for authentic expression through disuse. The tension resolves differently for producers vs consumers of creative work.
**Resolution:** Both sides are simultaneously true for different populations. AI commoditizing visual spectacle increases the market value of authentic human expression (Side A) while simultaneously reducing the number of people who practice it (Side B). For professional creators, Side A dominates: the scarcity premium on authentic narrative rises as AI floods the market with generic content. For ordinary consumers and casual communicators, Side B dominates: outsourcing emotional expression to AI atrophies the capacity for authentic communication. The tension resolves as a producer/consumer split -- AI creates a wider gap between those who cultivate authentic expression (who become more valuable) and those who outsource it (who lose the skill entirely).

**Prediction:** Within 5 years, "authenticity verification" or "human-created" labels will emerge as a significant market premium in creative industries, analogous to organic food labeling.
