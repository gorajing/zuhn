**Side A:** "Software that makes you similar to everyone else (beta software) will be replaced by AI and vibe-coding"
**Side B:** "Vibe coding won't replace enterprise SaaS because decades of accumulated edge-case knowledge are embedded in deterministic rules that can't be easily replicated"
**Reason:** Sankar (Palantir) argues commodity enterprise software is existentially threatened by vibe coding. Cannon-Brookes (Atlassian) argues hidden complexity like Indiana maternity leave rules embedded in payroll systems makes replacement far harder than it appears. Both have merit: commodity tools ARE increasingly rebuildable, but deep domain-specific enterprise software has genuine hidden complexity.
**Resolution:** Both sides are correct but apply to different software categories. Beta software (commodity SaaS that standardizes you) is genuinely vulnerable to vibe-coding replacement -- a Kanban board can be rebuilt in a weekend. But enterprise SaaS with deep domain complexity (payroll with Indiana maternity leave rules, regulatory compliance across 50 states) embeds decades of edge-case knowledge that vibe-coding cannot replicate. The determining variable is domain complexity depth: shallow-domain tools get replaced, deep-domain systems of record get extended. Cannon-Brookes's strongest point is that vibe coding actually makes these platforms stickier by enabling the long tail of customization (e.g., a 20-person conference room booking app built on Workday APIs).

**Prediction:** By 2028, at least 3 major commodity SaaS tools (project management, basic CRM, or simple analytics) will lose 30%+ market share to AI-generated alternatives, while enterprise platforms like Workday and SAP will grow their extensibility revenue.
