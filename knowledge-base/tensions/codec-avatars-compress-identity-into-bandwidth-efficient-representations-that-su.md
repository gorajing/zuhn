  Codec avatars compress identity into bandwidth-efficient representations that

**Side A:** "Meta's codec avatars encode facial expressions into a compact model rather than transmitting full video, achieving photorealism while using less bandwidth than traditional video calls."
**Side B:** "The codec avatar system crosses the uncanny valley by preserving human imperfections like freckles, asymmetry, and wrinkles rather than smoothing them out."
**Resolution:** These insights are not in tension -- they describe complementary aspects of the same technology. Side A describes the technical architecture (encoding expressions into compact models for bandwidth efficiency). Side B describes a key design principle that makes the architecture succeed (preserving imperfections to cross the uncanny valley). Together they form a complete insight: codec avatars achieve both bandwidth efficiency AND emotional fidelity because the compact encoding preserves the human imperfections (freckles, asymmetry, wrinkles) that make digital humans feel real. This should be reclassified as a complementary pair rather than a tension.
